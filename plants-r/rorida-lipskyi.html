Rorida lipskyi

Rorida lipskyi, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a mat-forming growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in spring and summer.

Rorida lipskyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
