Rubus fuscus var. adamsii

Rubus fuscus var. adamsii, commonly known as the Adams' blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has compound leaves with serrated edges.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruits are edible, though often tart and less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.
