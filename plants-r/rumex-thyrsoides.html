Rumex thyrsoides

Rumex thyrsoides, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other damp habitats. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, and its flowering stems bear small, clustered greenish-white flowers. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Rumex thyrsoides is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
