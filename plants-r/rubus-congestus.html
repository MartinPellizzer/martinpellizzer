Rubus congestus

Rubus congestus, commonly known as the clustered blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to dark purple to black berries in the fall. These berries are edible and often used in jams and wines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
