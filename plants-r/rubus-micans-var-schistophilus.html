Rubus micans var. schistophilus

Rubus micans var. schistophilus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its dark, glossy berries and dense, thorny growth habit. It is native to parts of eastern North America and thrives in woodland and shaded environments. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the edible fruits in the summer.

Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge and a slightly hairy texture.
