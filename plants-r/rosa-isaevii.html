Rosa isaevii

Rosa isaevii, commonly known as the Isaev's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa isaevii prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its hardy nature allows it to thrive in cooler climates, though it may require protection in harsh winters.
