Rubus fissifolius

Rubus fissifolius, commonly known as the fragrant raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its compound leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its flowers are white with five petals and are borne in clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
