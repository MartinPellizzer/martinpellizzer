Ranunculus columnae

Ranunculus columnae, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

Ranunculus columnae is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it prefers specific environmental conditions to flourish.
