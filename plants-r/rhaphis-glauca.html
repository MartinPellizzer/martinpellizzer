Rhaphis glauca

Rhaphis glauca, commonly known as the blue fan palm, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, which eventually develop into small, round berries. Rhaphis glauca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
