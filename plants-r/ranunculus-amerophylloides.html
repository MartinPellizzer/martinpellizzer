Ranunculus amerophylloides

Ranunculus amerophylloides, commonly known as the American water crowfoot, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in streams, ponds, and wetlands. The plant features finely divided, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface or emerge from the water. Its flowers are bright yellow with five petals and numerous stamens, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
