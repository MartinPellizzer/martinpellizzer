Rubus nudistylus

Rubus nudistylus, commonly known as the naked-styled blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and have five petals, with a distinctive naked stamen structure.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
