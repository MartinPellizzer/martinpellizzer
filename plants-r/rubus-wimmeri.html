Rubus wimmeri

Rubus wimmeri, commonly known as the Wimmer's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Chile and Argentina. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters, followed by edible blackberries that ripen in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and fruit production in suitable climates.
