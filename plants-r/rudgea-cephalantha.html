Rudgea cephalantha

Rudgea cephalantha, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and grows in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
