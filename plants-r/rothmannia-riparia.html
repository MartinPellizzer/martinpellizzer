Rothmannia riparia

Rothmannia riparia, commonly known as the riverbank rothmannia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic environments. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, aiding in photosynthesis in waterlogged conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

Rothmannia riparia plays an important role in stabilizing riverbanks and supporting local biodiversity.
