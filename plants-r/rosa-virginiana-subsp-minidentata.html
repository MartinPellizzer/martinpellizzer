Rosa virginiana subsp. minidentata

Rosa virginiana subsp.

minidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It features thorny stems and simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins.

The flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters of five, and are followed by oval, red-orange hips. This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils in open woods or along streambanks.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including pollinators and birds.
