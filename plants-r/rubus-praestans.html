Rubus praestans

Rubus praestans, commonly known as the showy raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, juicy berries in the summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant has a trailing or climbing habit.

Rubus praestans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
