Ribes rubrum var. parvum

Ribes rubrum var. parvum, commonly known as the dwarf redcurrant, is a compact variety of the redcurrant plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, red, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has slender, arching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in jams and preserves.
