Rubus trivialis var. serosus

Rubus trivialis var. serosus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its botanical features include thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus.
