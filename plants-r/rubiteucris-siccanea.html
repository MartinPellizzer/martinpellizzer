Rubiteucris siccanea

Rubiteucris siccanea, commonly known as the desert orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and a waxy cuticle that reduces water loss. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, storing moisture efficiently.

Despite its beauty, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered endangered due to habitat loss and over-collection.
