Rosa pimpinellifolia var. coreana

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to Korea and parts of northeastern China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and woodland borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
