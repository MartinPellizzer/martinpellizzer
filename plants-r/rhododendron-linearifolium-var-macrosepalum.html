Rhododendron linearifolium var. macrosepalum

Rhododendron linearifolium var. macrosepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southeastern China.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are typically pink to red, with large, showy sepals that are often more prominent than the petals. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its role in native ecosystems.
