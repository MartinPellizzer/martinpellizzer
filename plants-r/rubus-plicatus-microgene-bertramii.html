Rubus plicatus microgene bertramii

Rubus plicatus microgene bertramii, commonly known as the Bertram's raspberry, is a rare and small-fruited member of the Rosaceae family.

The plant is native to specific regions in the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, shaded habitats. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.
