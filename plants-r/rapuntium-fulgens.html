Rapuntium fulgens

Rapuntium fulgens, commonly known as the golden cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its seeds are small and contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Rapuntium fulgens is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
