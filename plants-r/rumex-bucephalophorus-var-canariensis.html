Rumex bucephalophorus var. canariensis

Rumex bucephalophorus var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, reddish leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
