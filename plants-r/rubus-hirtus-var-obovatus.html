Rubus hirtus var. obovatus

Rubus hirtus var. obovatus, commonly known as the prickly rose or brambles, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
