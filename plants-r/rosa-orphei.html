Rosa orphei

Rosa orphei, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, single or double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a yellow center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Rosa orphei is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in Persian horticulture.
