Ranunculus filiformis var. ovalis

Ranunculus filiformis var. ovalis, commonly known as the ovalleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to eight petals and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually arranged alternately along the stem and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.
