Rubus macilentus var. macilentus

Rubus macilentus var. macilentus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thin, thorny stems and small, dark purple leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
