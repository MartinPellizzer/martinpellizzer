Rhododendron neoglandulosum

Rhododendron neoglandulosum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high elevations. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhododendron neoglandulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
