Ranunculus lappaceus var. scapigerus

Ranunculus lappaceus var. scapigerus, commonly known as the scapigerous buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, glandular stem that distinguishes it from other ranunculus species. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
