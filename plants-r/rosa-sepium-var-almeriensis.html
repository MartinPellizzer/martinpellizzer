Rosa sepium var. almeriensis

Rosa sepium var. almeriensis, commonly known as the Almeria rose, is a variety of the rose species Rosa sepium.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula, particularly Spain, and thrives in Mediterranean climates. This plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and profuse flowering, producing clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and oval-shaped, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and is relatively low maintenance once established.
