Rubus melanoxylon var. amphistrophos

Rubus melanoxylon var. amphistrophos, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a species of bramble native to parts of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible berries and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
