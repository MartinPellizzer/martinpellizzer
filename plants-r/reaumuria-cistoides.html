Reaumuria cistoides

Reaumuria cistoides, commonly known as the sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It features small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Reaumuria cistoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
