Rubus nagasawanus

Rubus nagasawanus, commonly known as the Nagasawan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their edible berries. The plant typically grows in forested areas and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential for use in local agriculture.
