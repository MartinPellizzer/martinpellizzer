Rhodanthe polycephala

Rhodanthe polycephala, commonly known as the clustered sand-verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with multiple flowering stems that produce clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It blooms during the autumn and winter months, attracting various pollinators.

Rhodanthe polycephala is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in full sun and well-drained conditions.
