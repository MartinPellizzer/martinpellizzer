Rubus fuscus var. longiglandulosus

Rubus fuscus var. longiglandulosus, commonly known as the long-hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its hairy stems and leaves are distinctive features that aid in its identification.
