Ranunculus hyperboreus subsp. intertextus

Ranunculus hyperboreus subsp.

intertextus, commonly known as the Arctic buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It typically grows in open, rocky, or sandy habitats, often in tundra or alpine environments.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually palmately divided, and it produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.
