Ranunculus auricomus subsp. acutiserratus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

acutiserratus, commonly known as the sharp-toothed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals.

The leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive serrated edge, which gives the subspecies its name. The plant prefers moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
