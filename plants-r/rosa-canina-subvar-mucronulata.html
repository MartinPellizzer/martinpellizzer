Rosa canina subvar. mucronulata

Rosa canina subvar. mucronulata, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, orange-red hips that are rich in vitamin C and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are pinnate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
