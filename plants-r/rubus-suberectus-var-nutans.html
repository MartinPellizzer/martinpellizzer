Rubus suberectus var. nutans

Rubus suberectus var. nutans, commonly known as the drooping blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
