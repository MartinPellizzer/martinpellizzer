Rosa arvensis f. adenocarpa

Rosa arvensis f. adenocarpa, commonly known as the glandular-fruited dog rose, is a horticultural form of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its glandular pith in the stems and a distinct, often more compact growth habit compared to the typical species. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, hairy, orange-red hips. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
