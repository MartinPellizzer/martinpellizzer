Rubus hoatiensis

Rubus hoatiensis, commonly known as the Hoatiensis blackberry, is a species of bramble native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically clustered together. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
