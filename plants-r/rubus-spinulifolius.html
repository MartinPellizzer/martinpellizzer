Rubus spinulifolius

Rubus spinulifolius, commonly known as the prickly bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Rubus spinulifolius is typically found in woodland edges and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.
