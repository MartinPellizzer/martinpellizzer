Rubus chloocladus

Rubus chloocladus, commonly known as the green-leafed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often dark purple or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
