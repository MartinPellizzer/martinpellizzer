Rubus hebecarpos subsp. doranus

Rubus hebecarpos subsp.

doranus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries, which are usually dark purple to black, are edible but often have a tart flavor.
