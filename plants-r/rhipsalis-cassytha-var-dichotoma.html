Rhipsalis cassytha var. dichotoma

Rhipsalis cassytha var. dichotoma, commonly known as the silver mist cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairless stems that often hang in cascading clusters, giving it a delicate, trailing appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in winter, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care in indoor environments.
