Rubus plicatus var. biformis

Rubus plicatus var. biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The botanical features of this variety make it a unique subject for horticultural and ecological study.
