Rosa × stylosoformis var. pseudodumalis

Rosa × stylosoformis var. pseudodumalis, commonly known as the pseudodumalis rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems.

It typically produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its foliage is often covered in a fine layer of soft, silvery-white down. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is valued for both its ornamental beauty and hardy nature.

This variety is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
