Racosperma tripterum

Racosperma tripterum, commonly known as the three-winged acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or golden in color. It produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several seeds.

Racosperma tripterum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
