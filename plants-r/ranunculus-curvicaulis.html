Ranunculus curvicaulis

Ranunculus curvicaulis, commonly known as the bent-stem buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy stems and delicate, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
