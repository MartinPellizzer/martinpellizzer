Rosa nuda

Rosa nuda, commonly known as the naked rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often thornless stems and simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in early spring. Rosa nuda is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and attractive species within the rose family.
