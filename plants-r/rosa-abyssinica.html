Rosa abyssinica

Rosa abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian rose, is a species native to Ethiopia and Eritrea.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and a woody structure typical of many rose species.

Rosa abyssinica is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
