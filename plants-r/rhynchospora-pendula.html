Rhynchospora pendula

Rhynchospora pendula, commonly known as the hanging spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Rhynchospora pendula thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
