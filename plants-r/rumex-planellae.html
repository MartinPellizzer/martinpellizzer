Rumex planellae

Rumex planellae, commonly known as the narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a wavy margin and a prominent midrib, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also known for its role in ecological systems as a food source for various insects and animals.
