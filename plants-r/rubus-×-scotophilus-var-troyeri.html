Rubus × scotophilus var. troyeri

Rubus × scotophilus var. troyeri, commonly known as the Troyer raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate climates, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor, making it popular for culinary use. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a bushy growth habit, which contribute to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued both for its fruit production and its role in ecological habitats.
