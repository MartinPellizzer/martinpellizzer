Rhexia spicata

Rhexia spicata, commonly known as swamp candle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rhexia spicata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
