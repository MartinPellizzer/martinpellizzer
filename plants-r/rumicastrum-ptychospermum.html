Rumicastrum ptychospermum

Rumicastrum ptychospermum, commonly known as the broadleaf burrobrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features silver-gray foliage with small, spiny leaves, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in drought conditions.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is a component of desert scrub ecosystems.
