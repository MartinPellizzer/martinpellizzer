Rumex nemorosus

Rumex nemorosus, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being heart-shaped with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Rumex nemorosus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
