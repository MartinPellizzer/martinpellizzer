Rubus rusticanus f. obovatus

Rubus rusticanus f. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-ovate blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive leaf shape.

It is a shrub native to parts of North America, typically found in moist, wooded areas. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they often have a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
