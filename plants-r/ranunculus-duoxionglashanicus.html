Ranunculus duoxionglashanicus

Ranunculus duoxionglashanicus, commonly known as the Chinese buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
