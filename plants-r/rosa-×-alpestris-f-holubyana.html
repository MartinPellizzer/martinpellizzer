Rosa × alpestris f. holubyana

Rosa × alpestris f. holubyana, commonly known as the Holubyana rose, is a botanical hybrid that originates from the cross between Rosa alpestris and Rosa multiflora.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant typically features fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various garden conditions.
