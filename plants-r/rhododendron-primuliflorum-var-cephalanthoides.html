Rhododendron primuliflorum var. cephalanthoides

Rhododendron primuliflorum var. cephalanthoides, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with acidic soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
