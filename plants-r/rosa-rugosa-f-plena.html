Rosa rugosa f. plena

Rosa rugosa f. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered rugosa rose, is a cultivar of the rugosa rose that features densely packed, double blooms.

It is a hardy shrub native to coastal regions of East Asia, characterized by its vigorous growth and thorny stems. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season.

Rosa rugosa f. plena is valued for its resilience, adaptability, and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
