Rapuntium fervens

Rapuntium fervens, commonly known as the fiery cress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers and serrated, edible leaves that have a spicy, peppery flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
