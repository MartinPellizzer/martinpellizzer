Rhamnus colmeiroi

Rhamnus colmeiroi, commonly known as the Spanish buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean scrublands.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
