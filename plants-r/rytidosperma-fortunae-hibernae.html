Rytidosperma fortunae-hibernae

Rytidosperma fortunae-hibernae, commonly known as the Irish fortuna grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in moderate climates and its relatively low maintenance requirements.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local insect populations.
