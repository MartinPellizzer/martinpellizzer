Rhytidostemma viride subsp. mcphersonii

Rhytidostemma viride subsp.

mcphersonii, commonly known as the McPherson's green stemmed plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green in color, with a smooth, waxy surface.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
