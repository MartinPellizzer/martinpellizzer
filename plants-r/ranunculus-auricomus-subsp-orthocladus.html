Ranunculus auricomus subsp. orthocladus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

orthocladus, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a broad, shield-shaped center.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and meadow habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with palmate lobes, and the stems are hairy.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
