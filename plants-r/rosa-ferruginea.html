Rosa ferruginea

Rosa ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it typically grows in woodlands and rocky areas. The plant is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and small, fragrant pink flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
