Rosa rubiginosa pubescens

Rosa rubiginosa pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent rose or the rough rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, with serrated edges and a pubescent texture.

Rosa rubiginosa pubescens is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and arid environments.
