Rubus × concinnatus

Rubus × concinnatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, shady environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
