Rubus sengorensis

Rubus sengorensis, commonly known as the Sengorensis blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Rubus.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Uganda and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable food source for both wildlife and humans.
