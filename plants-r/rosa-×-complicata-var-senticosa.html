Rosa × complicata var. senticosa

Rosa × complicata var. senticosa, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are edible and contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species suitable for various climates.
