Rubus hirtus var. platyphyllus

Rubus hirtus var. platyphyllus, commonly known as the flatleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in horticulture.
