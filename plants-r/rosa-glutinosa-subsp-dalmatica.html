Rosa glutinosa subsp. dalmatica

Rosa glutinosa subsp.

dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian rose, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
