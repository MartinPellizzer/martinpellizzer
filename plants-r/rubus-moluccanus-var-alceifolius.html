Rubus moluccanus var. alceifolius

Rubus moluccanus var. alceifolius, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and somewhat tart in flavor. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its use in local cuisines.
