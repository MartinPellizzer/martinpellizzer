Rhododendron chrysanthum var. senanense

Rhododendron chrysanthum var. senanense, commonly known as the Senanese yellow rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, with leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower color and compact growth habit.
