Rubus questieri var. interpolatus

Rubus questieri var. interpolatus, commonly known as the Alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
