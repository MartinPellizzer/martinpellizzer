Rosa × dumetorum f. subamblyphylla

Rosa × dumetorum f. subamblyphylla, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa, characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, trifoliate, and often covered in a fine layer of white down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler, high-altitude environments.
