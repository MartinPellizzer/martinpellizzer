Roeslinia angustifolia

Roeslinia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf roeslinia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild diuretic properties.

Roeslinia angustifolia thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
