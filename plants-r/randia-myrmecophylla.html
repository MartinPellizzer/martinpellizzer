Randia myrmecophylla

Randia myrmecophylla, commonly known as the ant-plant, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique structure, featuring specialized leaves that provide shelter for ant colonies. These ants, in turn, protect the plant from herbivores and help in nutrient cycling. The plant belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is known for its small, fragrant flowers.

Its ecological role is significant in maintaining the balance of its tropical habitat.
