Ranunculus × gutermannii

Ranunculus × gutermannii, commonly known as the German buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with a hairy center. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

Ranunculus × gutermannii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
