Roegneria striata

Roegneria striata, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Asia and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Roegneria striata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
