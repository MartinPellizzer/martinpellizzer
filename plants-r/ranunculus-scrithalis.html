Ranunculus scrithalis

Ranunculus scrithalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus scrithalis is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.
