Rivea pomacea

Rivea pomacea, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are edible and resemble sweet potatoes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Rivea pomacea is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it a popular choice in gardens and as a houseplant.
