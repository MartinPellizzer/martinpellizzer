Rhododendron blandulum

Rhododendron blandulum, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant.

Rhododendron blandulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging alpine environments.
