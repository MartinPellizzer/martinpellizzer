Rubus humifusus var. napobius

Rubus humifusus var. napobius, commonly known as the Napo raspberry, is a low-growing shrub native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its flowers are pink to white and bloom in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local cuisine.
