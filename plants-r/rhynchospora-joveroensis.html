Rhynchospora joveroensis

Rhynchospora joveroensis, commonly known as the Jovero sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. Rhynchospora joveroensis thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
