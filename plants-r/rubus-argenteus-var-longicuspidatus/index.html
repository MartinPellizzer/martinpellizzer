Rubus Argenteus Var. Longicuspidatus

Rubus argenteus var. longicuspidatus is a unique variant of the evergreen bramble known for its striking silver-white undersides on its leaves. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Its long, pointed leaf tips and dense growth habit make it easily distinguishable from other Rubus species. Despite its ornamental appeal, it is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife. The plant's hardy nature and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience.
