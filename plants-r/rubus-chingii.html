Rubus chingii

Rubus chingii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
