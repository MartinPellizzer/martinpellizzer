Rubus crebrispinus

Rubus crebrispinus, commonly known as the dense-fruited blackberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in culinary applications or for making jams and wines.
