Rosa plumbea

Rosa plumbea, commonly known as the gray rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale grayish-green leaves and pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas, and thrives in temperate climates. Rosa plumbea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant blooms.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and botanical collections.
