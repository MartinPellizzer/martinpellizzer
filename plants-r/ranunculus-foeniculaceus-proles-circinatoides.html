Ranunculus foeniculaceus proles circinatoides

Ranunculus foeniculaceus proles circinatoides, commonly known as the cirnoid water crowfoot, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of fennel. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for wetland habitats.
