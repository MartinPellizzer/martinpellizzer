Rosa aciculata

Rosa aciculata, commonly known as the needleleaf rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, dry climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa aciculata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
