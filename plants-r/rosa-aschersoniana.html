Rosa aschersoniana

Rosa aschersoniana, commonly known as the Chilean rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double blooms that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Rosa aschersoniana is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
