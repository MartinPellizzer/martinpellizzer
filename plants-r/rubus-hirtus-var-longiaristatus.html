Rubus hirtus var. longiaristatus

Rubus hirtus var. longiaristatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
