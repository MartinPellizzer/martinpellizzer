Rosa mollis f. inalbata

Rosa mollis f. inalbata, commonly known as the white-flowered form of the soft rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its pale to white petals, which contrast with the typical pink or red hues of the parent species. The plant produces fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
