Renanthera × maingayi

Renanthera × maingayi, commonly known as the hybrid orchid or Maingay's orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Renanthera imschoiana and Renanthera atrovaginata.

It is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its large, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, and it blooms during the warmer months of the year.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under suitable conditions.
