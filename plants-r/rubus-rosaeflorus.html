Rubus rosaeflorus

Rubus rosaeflorus, commonly known as the roseflower raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The berries are often used in jams and desserts due to their tart flavor and high antioxidant content.
