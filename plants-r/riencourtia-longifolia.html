Riencourtia longifolia

Riencourtia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved riencourtia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its ecosystem.
