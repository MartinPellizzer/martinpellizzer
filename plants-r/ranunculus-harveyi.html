Ranunculus harveyi

Ranunculus harveyi, commonly known as Harvey's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, radially symmetrical flowers. The plant typically features bright yellow petals and a central cluster of stamens, with leaves that are palmately divided. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
