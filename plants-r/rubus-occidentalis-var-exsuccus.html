Rubus occidentalis var. exsuccus

Rubus occidentalis var. exsuccus, commonly known as the thimbleberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are usually thorny. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
