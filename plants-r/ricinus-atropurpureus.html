Ricinus atropurpureus

Ricinus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple castor oil plant, is a striking ornamental species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dark purple to almost black foliage. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a velvety texture and a deep, rich coloration. It produces striking red or orange flowers that are followed by spiny capsules containing toxic seeds.

The plant is valued for its dramatic appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
