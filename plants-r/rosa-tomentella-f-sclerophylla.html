Rosa tomentella f. sclerophylla

Rosa tomentella f. sclerophylla, commonly known as the woolly rose or the shrub rose, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dense, greyish-white pubescent foliage that provides a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and narrow, adapted to withstand dry conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
