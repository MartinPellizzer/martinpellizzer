Rubus oegoacanthus

Rubus oegoacanthus, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a species of brambles native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

The berries are edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter taste.
