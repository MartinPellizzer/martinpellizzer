Rosa rubus

Rosa rubus, commonly known as the wild raspberry or bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Rosa rubus thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

It plays an important role in both ecological systems and human agriculture.
