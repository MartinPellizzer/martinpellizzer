Rosa webbiana var. winterbottomii

Rosa webbiana var. winterbottomii, commonly known as the Winterbottom's rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It features upright stems with thorny branches and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the branches. It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in sunny, open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
