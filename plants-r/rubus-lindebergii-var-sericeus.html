Rubus lindebergii var. sericeus

Rubus lindebergii var. sericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, making it a popular choice for gardens and small-scale cultivation.
