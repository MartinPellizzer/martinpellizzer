Rubus moluccanus var. arcuatus

Rubus moluccanus var. arcuatus, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a red or orange color. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces hairy stems and thorny branches.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its edible fruits in some regions.
