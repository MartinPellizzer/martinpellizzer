Rhamnus lycioides var. edetana

Rhamnus lycioides var. edetana, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and wind exposure.

This variety is often found in maritime chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
