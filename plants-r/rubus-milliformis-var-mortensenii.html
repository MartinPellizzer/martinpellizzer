Rubus milliformis var. mortensenii

Rubus milliformis var. mortensenii, commonly known as the Mortensen's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
