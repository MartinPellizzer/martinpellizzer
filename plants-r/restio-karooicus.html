Restio karooicus

Restio karooicus, commonly known as the Karoo restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and unbranched stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Restio karooicus produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers.

It plays a significant role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
