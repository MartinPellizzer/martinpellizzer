Rubus pallidus subsp. fuscus

Rubus pallidus subsp.

fuscus, commonly known as the pale bramble or dark-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
