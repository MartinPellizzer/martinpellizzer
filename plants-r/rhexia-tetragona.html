Rhexia tetragona

Rhexia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled rhexia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rhexia tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
