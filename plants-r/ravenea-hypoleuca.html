Ravenea hypoleuca

Ravenea hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-veined Ravenea, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that are often adorned with white veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking yellow flowers followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its aesthetic appeal.
