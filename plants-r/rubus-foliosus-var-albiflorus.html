Rubus foliosus var. albiflorus

Rubus foliosus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually serrated and have a matte green upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit, a red or black berry, is edible but typically tart.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.
