Rhynchospora unisetosa

Rhynchospora unisetosa, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and pond margins, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it relies on wind pollination.

Rhynchospora unisetosa plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
