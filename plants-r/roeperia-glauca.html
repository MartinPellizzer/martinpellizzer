Roeperia glauca

Roeperia glauca, commonly known as the bluebells of the rock, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery blue bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Roeperia glauca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
