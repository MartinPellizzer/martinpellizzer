Rosa glauca var. opacifolia

Rosa glauca var. opacifolia, commonly known as the opacifolia rose, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are slightly hairy and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in woodland gardens.
