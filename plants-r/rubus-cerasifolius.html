Rubus cerasifolius

Rubus cerasifolius, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Rubus cerasifolius is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.
