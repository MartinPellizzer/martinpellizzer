Rubus tereticaulis subsp. ellipticus

Rubus tereticaulis subsp.

ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic blackberry, is a shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its edible berries and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
