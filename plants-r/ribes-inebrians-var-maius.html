Ribes inebrians var. maius

Ribes inebrians var. maius, commonly known as the giant gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, edible berries that can grow up to 3 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broadly ovate with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various regional cuisines.
