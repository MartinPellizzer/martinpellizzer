Ruellia incana

Ruellia incana, commonly known as hairy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and produces clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ruellia incana is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in some regions.
