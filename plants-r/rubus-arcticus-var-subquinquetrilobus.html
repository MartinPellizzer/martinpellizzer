Rubus arcticus var. subquinquetrilobus

Rubus arcticus var. subquinquetrilobus, commonly known as the Arctic bramble, is a variant of the arctic raspberry found in cold, northern climates.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tundra and alpine regions, characterized by its low, spreading growth habit. The leaves are palmately lobed, with five to seven lobes, and are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a red, juicy berry that ripens in late summer.

This plant plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.
