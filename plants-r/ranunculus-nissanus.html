Ranunculus nissanus

Ranunculus nissanus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five to eight petals. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
