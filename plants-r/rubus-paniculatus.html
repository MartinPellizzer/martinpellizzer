Rubus paniculatus

Rubus paniculatus, commonly known as the purple-leaf bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually three to five lobes and often have a purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in panicles during the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in thickets and along stream banks.

Rubus paniculatus is a member of the Rosaceae family and is known for its thorny stems and vigorous growth habit.
