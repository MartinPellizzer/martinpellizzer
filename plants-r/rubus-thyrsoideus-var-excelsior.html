Rubus thyrsoideus var. excelsior

Rubus thyrsoideus var. excelsior, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
