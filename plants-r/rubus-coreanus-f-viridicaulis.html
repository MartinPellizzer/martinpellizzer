Rubus coreanus f. viridicaulis

Rubus coreanus f. viridicaulis, commonly known as the green-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, green stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
