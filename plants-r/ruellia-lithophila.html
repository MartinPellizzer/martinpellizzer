Ruellia lithophila

Ruellia lithophila, commonly known as the rock ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to violet tubular flowers. Ruellia lithophila is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
