Rubus menkei var. lacroixii

Rubus menkei var. lacroixii, commonly known as the lacroix's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that produces small, dark berries, which are typically used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor, making them valuable both nutritionally and commercially.
