Reaumuria kuznetzovii

Reaumuria kuznetzovii, commonly known as Kuznetsov's reaumuria, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and its ability to thrive in harsh, saline soils. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, red or orange berries that are edible for some bird species.

Reaumuria kuznetzovii is well adapted to extreme environmental conditions, making it an important component of desert and steppe ecosystems.
