Rosa berberifolia var. velutina

Rosa berberifolia var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and sometimes medicinal properties.
