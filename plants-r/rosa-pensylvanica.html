Rosa pensylvanica

Rosa pensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania rose, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, hairy fruits called hips. Rosa pensylvanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
