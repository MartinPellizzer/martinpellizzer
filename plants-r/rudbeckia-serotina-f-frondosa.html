Rudbeckia serotina f. frondosa

Rudbeckia serotina f. frondosa, commonly known as the late flowering rough coneflower, is a horticultural form of the late coneflower.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy yellow-orange ray flowers with a dark brown central disk. The plant typically reaches heights of 3 to 5 feet and produces foliage that is broad, heart-shaped, and often slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
