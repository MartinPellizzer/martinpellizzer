Ranunculus rigoi

Ranunculus rigoi, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus rigoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
