Rosa tomentosa var. ravaudiana

Rosa tomentosa var. ravaudiana, commonly known as the Ravaud's hairy rose, is a variant of the hairy rose species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, white to greyish pubescence covering the stems, leaves, and flowers, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a serrated margin.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
