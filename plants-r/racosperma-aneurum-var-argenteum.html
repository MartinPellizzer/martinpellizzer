Racosperma aneurum var. argenteum

Racosperma aneurum var. argenteum, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically pollinated by bees and other insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.

The silver wattle is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
