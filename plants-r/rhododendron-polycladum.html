Rhododendron polycladum

Rhododendron polycladum, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often found in forested areas. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and dark green in color. Its flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron polycladum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
