Rosa canina f. subverticillacantha

Rosa canina f. subverticillacantha, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rosa genus.
