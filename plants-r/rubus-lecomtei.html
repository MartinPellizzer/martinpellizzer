Rubus lecomtei

Rubus lecomtei, commonly known as Lecomte's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a deep black color. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is valued for its flavor and is often used in jams and desserts.
