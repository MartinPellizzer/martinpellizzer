Ranunculus divergens

Ranunculus divergens, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is hairy and can grow up to 30 cm in height. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, each adorned with a distinct central mark. Its flowers typically bloom in spring, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Ranunculus divergens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, daisy-like appearance.
