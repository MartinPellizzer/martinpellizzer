Rhamnus frangula var. laevifolia

Rhamnus frangula var. laevifolia, commonly known as whitebeams, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
