Rhexia latifolia

Rhexia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf rhexia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and woodland edges.

Rhexia latifolia is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
