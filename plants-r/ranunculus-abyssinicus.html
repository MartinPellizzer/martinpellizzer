Ranunculus abyssinicus

Ranunculus abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
