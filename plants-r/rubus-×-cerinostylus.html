Rubus × cerinostylus

Rubus × cerinostylus, commonly known as the smooth-chinned blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thornless stems and large, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

This cultivar is prized for its sweet flavor and is often used in jams and desserts.
