Rubus colemannii

Rubus colemannii, commonly known as the Colemann's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in local cuisine and has a tart, acidic flavor.
