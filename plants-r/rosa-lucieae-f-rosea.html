Rosa lucieae f. rosea

Rosa lucieae f. rosea, commonly known as the rose of Lucie, is a hybrid rose with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and abundant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has dark green, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rosa genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular watering and pruning for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
