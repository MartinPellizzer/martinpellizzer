Rhododendron thomsonii subsp. thomsonii

Rhododendron thomsonii subsp.

thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
