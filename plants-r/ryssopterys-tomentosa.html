Ryssopterys tomentosa

Ryssopterys tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy texture on the fronds, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive appearance that makes it easily identifiable.

Ryssopterys tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
