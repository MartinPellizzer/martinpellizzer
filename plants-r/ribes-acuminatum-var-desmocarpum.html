Ribes acuminatum var. desmocarpum

Ribes acuminatum var. desmocarpum, commonly known as the Desert Currant, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries that are edible. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

The berries are used in local cuisines and have historical significance among indigenous communities.
