Ruellia dissimilis

Ruellia dissimilis, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue or violet tubular flowers that bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Ruellia dissimilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
