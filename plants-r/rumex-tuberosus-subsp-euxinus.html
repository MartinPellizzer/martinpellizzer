Rumex tuberosus subsp. euxinus

Rumex tuberosus subsp.

euxinus, commonly known as the Armenian rhubarb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features upright, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish-purple hue.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
