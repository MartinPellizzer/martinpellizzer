Ranunculus abortivus var. micranthus

Ranunculus abortivus var. micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America.

It features basal leaves that are palmately divided with three to five lobes, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
