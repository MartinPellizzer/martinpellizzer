Rosa amblyphylla

Rosa amblyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in coastal and Mediterranean climates. The plant features slender, often thornless stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to white, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa amblyphylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty and adaptability.
