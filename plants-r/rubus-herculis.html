Rubus herculis

Rubus herculis, commonly known as Hercules' club, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, raspberry-like fruits in the summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in clay soils.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
