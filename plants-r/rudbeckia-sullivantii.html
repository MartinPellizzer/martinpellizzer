Rudbeckia sullivantii

Rudbeckia sullivantii, commonly known as the prairie coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features bright yellow to orange petals surrounding a central cluster of brown, cone-like flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often covered in fine hairs.

Rudbeckia sullivantii is well adapted to prairie and dry woodland environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
