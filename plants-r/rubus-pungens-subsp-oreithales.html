Rubus pungens subsp. oreithales

Rubus pungens subsp.

oreithales, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and hairy, thorny stems.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a pubescent surface.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
