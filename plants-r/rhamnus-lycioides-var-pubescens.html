Rhamnus lycioides var. pubescens

Rhamnus lycioides var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark, shiny berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its resilience in arid environments.
