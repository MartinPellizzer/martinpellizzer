Ranunculus bullatus proles semicalvus

Ranunculus bullatus proles semicalvus, commonly known as the semi-calyx buttercup, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It features a yellow or orange flower with five to seven petals, often with a prominent central stigma. The plant has a short, hairy stem and basal leaves that are deeply lobed. Its sepals are reduced and partially calyx-like, giving it the specific epithet "semicalvus.".

This species is typically found in moist, temperate habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
