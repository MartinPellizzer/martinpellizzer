Rosa pouzinii var. puberula

Rosa pouzinii var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, slightly hairy leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
