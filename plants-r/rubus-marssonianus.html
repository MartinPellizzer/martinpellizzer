Rubus marssonianus

Rubus marssonianus, commonly known as the Marsson's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that mature to a deep purple-black color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces berries that are edible but often tart.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
