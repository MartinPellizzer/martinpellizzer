Ranunculus gouanii

Ranunculus gouanii, commonly known as the Gouan's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. Its leaves are palmately divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
