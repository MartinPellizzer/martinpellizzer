Raphanus courtoisii

Raphanus courtoisii, commonly known as the Courtois's radish, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known vegetables like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, and it produces round, edible roots similar to those of the common radish.

Raphanus courtoisii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.
