Ruschianthemum gigas

Ruschianthemum gigas, commonly known as the giant sunflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its massive, yellow-orange flower heads that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant features large, rough, and hairy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it thrives in full sunlight with well-drained soil.

Due to its size and visual impact, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as an ornamental plant.
