Rubus × heterocolor

Rubus × heterocolor, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications due to its tart flavor.
