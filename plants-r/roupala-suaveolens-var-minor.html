Roupala suaveolens var. minor

Roupala suaveolens var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf roupala, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 2 meters, making it a compact and ornamental species. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
