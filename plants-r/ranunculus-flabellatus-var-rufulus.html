Ranunculus flabellatus var. rufulus

Ranunculus flabellatus var. rufulus, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are alternate along the stem. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
