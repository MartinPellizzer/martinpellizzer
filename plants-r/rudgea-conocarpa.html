Rudgea conocarpa

Rudgea conocarpa, commonly known as the yellow rudgea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Rudgea conocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
