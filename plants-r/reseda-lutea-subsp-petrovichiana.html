Reseda lutea subsp. petrovichiana

Reseda lutea subsp.

petrovichiana, commonly known as the Caucasian dyer's rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its upright growth habit and slender stems.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, linear to lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is valued for its historical use in dyeing fabrics due to the presence of flavonoids in its flowers.
