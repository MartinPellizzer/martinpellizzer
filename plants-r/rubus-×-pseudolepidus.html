Rubus × pseudolepidus

Rubus × pseudolepidus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry or black raspberry, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are typically dark red to black in color. These berries are edible, with a tart flavor and a high antioxidant content.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
