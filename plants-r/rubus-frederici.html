Rubus frederici

Rubus frederici, commonly known as the Frederic's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is prized for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in jams and pies.
