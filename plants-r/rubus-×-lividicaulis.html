Rubus × lividicaulis

Rubus × lividicaulis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the western United States and thrives in a variety of soil types.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex hybrid origins and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
