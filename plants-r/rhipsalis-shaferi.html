Rhipsalis shaferi

Rhipsalis shaferi, commonly known as the Shafer's mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its soft, green, leaf-like stems that are often mistaken for foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its habitat and appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in shaded, humid environments.
