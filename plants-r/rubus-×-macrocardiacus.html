Rubus × macrocardiacus

Rubus × macrocardiacus, commonly known as the giant blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its large, thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests and open fields. Its fruit is highly valued for its intense flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, Rubus ursinus and Rubus ellowii, making it a notable example of hybrid vigor.
