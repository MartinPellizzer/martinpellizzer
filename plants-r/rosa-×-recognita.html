Rosa × recognita

Rosa × recognita, commonly known as the double-flowered rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically have multiple layers of petals, giving them a full, fluffy appearance. The plant is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant blooms in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rosa × recognita is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
