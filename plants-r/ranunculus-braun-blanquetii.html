Ranunculus braun-blanquetii

Ranunculus braun-blanquetii, commonly known as the Braun-Blanquet's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are often borne on long, slender pedicels.

It is a relatively rare species, with limited distribution and specific ecological requirements.
