Rhodomyrtus locellata

Rhodomyrtus locellata, commonly known as the red sandalwood or small-fruited rhatany, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and small, red or purple berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a reddish underside.

Rhodomyrtus locellata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
