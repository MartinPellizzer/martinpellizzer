Rubus semicinereus

Rubus semicinereus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features include thorny stems, palmate leaves, and a generally shrub-like growth habit.
