Rosa micrantha f. pauciglandulosa

Rosa micrantha f. pauciglandulosa, commonly known as the low-gland rose, is a cultivar of the species Rosa micrantha.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are often borne in clusters and are noted for their delicate appearance.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
