Rubus alaskensis

Rubus alaskensis, commonly known as the Alaskan raspberry, is a shrub native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and alpine meadows. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable resource for both wildlife and humans.
