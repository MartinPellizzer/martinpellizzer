Ranunculus lucidus

Ranunculus lucidus, commonly known as the glossy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow, spurred petals that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus lucidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season blooms.
