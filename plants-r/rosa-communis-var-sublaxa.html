Rosa communis var. sublaxa

Rosa communis var. sublaxa, commonly known as the soft-stemmed rose, is a cultivar characterized by its slender, flexible stems and delicate foliage.

It produces fragrant flowers that are typically pink to white in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is known for its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscape design and cut flower arrangements.
