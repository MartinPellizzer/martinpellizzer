Rhaphiolepis indica var. indica

Rhaphiolepis indica var. indica, commonly known as the Chinese rose or evergreen cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that provide dense foliage, making it suitable for hedges or ornamental use. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, exhibiting good drought tolerance once established.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
