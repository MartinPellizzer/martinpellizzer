Ranunculus oresterus

Ranunculus oresterus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and trifoliate, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
