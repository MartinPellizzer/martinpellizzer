Rapistrum rugosum var. nemausense

Rapistrum rugosum var. nemausense, commonly known as the Nemausense thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It has a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
