Rubus × collinus var. romanus

Rubus × collinus var. romanus, commonly known as the Roman blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows. It produces small, edible berries that are popular in local cuisines.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
