Rubus sulcatus var. rosulentus

Rubus sulcatus var. rosulentus, commonly known as the Rosy Raspberry, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-prostrate growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and develops red, hairy fruits that are edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its attractive flowers and fruit.
