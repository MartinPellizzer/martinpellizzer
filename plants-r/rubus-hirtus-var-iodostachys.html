Rubus hirtus var. iodostachys

Rubus hirtus var. iodostachys, commonly known as the iodine-stem blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are often used in jams and preserves. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its unique stem coloration, which can take on a bluish or iodine-like hue.

Its growth habit and morphological features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
