Rubus macrophyllus microgene fuxeensis

Rubus macrophyllus microgene fuxeensis, commonly known as the Fuxian blackberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, dark green leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
