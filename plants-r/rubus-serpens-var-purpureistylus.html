Rubus serpens var. purpureistylus

Rubus serpens var. purpureistylus, commonly known as the purple-styled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, often with a sweet and tart flavor. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth habit.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
