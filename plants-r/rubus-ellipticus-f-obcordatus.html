Rubus ellipticus f. obcordatus

Rubus ellipticus f. obcordatus, commonly known as the obcordate blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, obcordate (heart-shaped at the base) leaves and produces small, dark berries. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats. Its flowers are white or pale green, and it blooms in the spring.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and edible fruits.
