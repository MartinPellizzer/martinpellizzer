Rubus sanguinolentus

Rubus sanguinolentus, commonly known as the bloodberry bramble, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red berries that turn a striking crimson when ripe. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has a distinctive, aromatic scent. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in early summer.

The berries are not only ornamental but also have historical uses in traditional medicine.
