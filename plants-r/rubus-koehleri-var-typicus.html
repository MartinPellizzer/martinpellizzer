Rubus koehleri var. typicus

Rubus koehleri var. typicus, commonly known as the Koehler's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and dark purple to black berries that ripen in the fall. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant thrives in temperate to subtropical climates.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
