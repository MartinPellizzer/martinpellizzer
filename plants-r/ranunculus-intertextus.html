Ranunculus intertextus

Ranunculus intertextus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish stems that support its showy blooms.

Ranunculus intertextus is often found in meadows and along stream banks at high elevations.
