Rubus bouvetianus

Rubus bouvetianus, commonly known as the Bovet's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
