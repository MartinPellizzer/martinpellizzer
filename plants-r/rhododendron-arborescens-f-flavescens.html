Rhododendron arborescens f. flavescens

Rhododendron arborescens f. flavescens, commonly known as the golden flame azalea, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range from pale yellow to bright orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that turn red or bronze in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various garden conditions.
