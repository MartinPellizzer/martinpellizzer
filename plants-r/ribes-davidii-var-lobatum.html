Ribes davidii var. lobatum

Ribes davidii var. lobatum, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are ovate to elliptic in shape and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible and rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the development of modern cultivated kiwifruit.
