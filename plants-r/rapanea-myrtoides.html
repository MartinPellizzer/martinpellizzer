Rapanea myrtoides

Rapanea myrtoides, commonly known as the African myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

The tree is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
