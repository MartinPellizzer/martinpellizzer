Reseda fruticulosa subsp. attenuata

Reseda fruticulosa subsp.

attenuata, commonly known as the slender reseda, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, with four petals, and bloom in early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
