Rosa jundzillii var. speciosa

Rosa jundzillii var. speciosa, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of northern India and Pakistan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a strong, pleasant fragrance and a preference for well-drained, alkaline soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
