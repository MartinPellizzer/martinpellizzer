Rubus sabaudus

Rubus sabaudus, commonly known as the Sabaudian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, often with a slightly tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
