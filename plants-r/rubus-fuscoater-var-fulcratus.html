Rubus fuscoater var. fulcratus

Rubus fuscoater var. fulcratus, commonly known as the blackberry variant, is a species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its foliage is compound, with serrated leaflets, and it blooms with white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
