Rubus parvulidens

Rubus parvulidens, commonly known as the small-fruited blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
