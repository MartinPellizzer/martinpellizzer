Rosa phalacropoda

Rosa phalacropoda, commonly known as the purpleleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple foliage and fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. Native to the mountainous regions of Central America, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant's stems are covered in thorns, and its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa phalacropoda is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
