Rhododendron potaninii

Rhododendron potaninii, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to China and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

It typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. The plant is part of the Ericaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its flowers, which range in color from white to deep pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron potaninii is also valued for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and its resistance to certain pests and diseases.
