Reseda phyteuma var. ligustica

Reseda phyteuma var. ligustica, commonly known as the Ligustic Reseda, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are linear and slightly hairy, often arranged oppositely along the stem. Native to southern Europe, this plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
