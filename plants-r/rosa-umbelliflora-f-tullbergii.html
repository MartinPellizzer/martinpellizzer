Rosa umbelliflora f. tullbergii

Rosa umbelliflora f. tullbergii, commonly known as the Tullberg's sweet briar, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
