Restio major

Restio major, commonly known as the tall restio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Restio major thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in fynbos vegetation.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
