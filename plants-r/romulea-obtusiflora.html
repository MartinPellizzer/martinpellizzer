Romulea obtusiflora

Romulea obtusiflora, commonly known as the blunt-flowered romulea, is a perennial geophyte native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its corm-like underground storage structures. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a slight greenish tinge. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically evergreen.

Romulea obtusiflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
