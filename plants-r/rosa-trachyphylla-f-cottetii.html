Rosa trachyphylla f. cottetii

Rosa trachyphylla f. cottetii, commonly known as the Cottet's rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, making it suitable for gardens in USDA hardiness zones 6 through 9.

This variety is also valued for its historical significance in horticultural breeding programs.
