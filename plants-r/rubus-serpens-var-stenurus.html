Rubus serpens var. stenurus

Rubus serpens var. stenurus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for local ecosystems and wildlife.
