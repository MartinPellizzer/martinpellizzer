Rubus thyrsoideus microgene aciodontus

Rubus thyrsoideus microgene aciodontus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex inflorescence and its role in local ecosystems as a food source for various wildlife species.
