Raphia insignis

Raphia insignis, commonly known as the giant reed palm, is a tall, robust palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces long, slender fruit clusters that contain fibrous, edible fruits. Its trunk is typically unbranched and covered with a fibrous sheath.

Raphia insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in construction and crafts.
