Rubus pararosifolius

Rubus pararosifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, often forming dense thickets.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family, sharing features with other Rubus species such as raspberries and blackberries.
