Rosa gallicoarvensis

Rosa gallicoarvensis, commonly known as the Gallic wild rose, is a species of shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rosa gallicoarvensis is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
