Roldana heracleifolia

Roldana heracleifolia, commonly known as the giant rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
