Rhododendron flavidum var. psilostylum

Rhododendron flavidum var. psilostylum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dense, evergreen foliage, with leaves that are elliptical and often hairy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
