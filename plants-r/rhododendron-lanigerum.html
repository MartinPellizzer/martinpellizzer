Rhododendron lanigerum

Rhododendron lanigerum, commonly known as the woolly rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and parts of southern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with cool climates. Its flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron lanigerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
