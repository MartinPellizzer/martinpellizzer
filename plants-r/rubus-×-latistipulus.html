Rubus × latistipulus

Rubus × latistipulus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its large, broad leaves and dark purple to black fruit. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, wooded areas. It produces edible berries that are often used in jams and pies.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex hybrid origin and variability in morphology.
