Rosa afzeliana f. ostensa

Rosa afzeliana f. ostensa, commonly known as the Persian rose or the white rose of Persia, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, often fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has glossy, dark green foliage and produces hips that are smaller and less showy than those of other rose species. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

This form is a natural variation of Rosa afzeliana, known for its unique floral characteristics and adaptability to various climates.
