Rhododendron cinnabarinum subsp. cinnabarinum

Rhododendron cinnabarinum subsp.

cinnabarinum, commonly known as the crimson rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded environments. Its leaves are dark green and elliptical, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens.
