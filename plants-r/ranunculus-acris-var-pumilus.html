Ranunculus acris var. pumilus

Ranunculus acris var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches heights of only a few inches, with a rosette of basal leaves and delicate, yellow flowers. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, showy blooms in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
