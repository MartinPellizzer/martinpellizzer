Rourea striata

Rourea striata, commonly known as the striated rourea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oppositely arranged and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Rourea striata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
