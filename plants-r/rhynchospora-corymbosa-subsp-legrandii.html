Rhynchospora corymbosa subsp. legrandii

Rhynchospora corymbosa subsp.

legrandii, commonly known as the Florida marsh sedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
