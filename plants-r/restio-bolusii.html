Restio bolusii

Restio bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in fynbos vegetation.
