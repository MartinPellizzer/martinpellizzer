Rubus castrolinensis

Rubus castrolinensis, commonly known as the Castrolinensis blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries, raspberries, or dewberries. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like units, typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the plant has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its edible fruits and ornamental qualities.
