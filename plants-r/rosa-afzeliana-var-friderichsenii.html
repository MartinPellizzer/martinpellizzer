Rosa afzeliana var. friderichsenii

Rosa afzeliana var. friderichsenii, commonly known as the Friderichsen's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features upright, thorny stems and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has oval, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its bark is smooth and reddish-brown. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
