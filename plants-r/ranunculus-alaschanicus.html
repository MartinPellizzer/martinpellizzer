Ranunculus alaschanicus

Ranunculus alaschanicus, commonly known as the Alaska buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, herbaceous species. The plant produces delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the genus Ranunculus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
