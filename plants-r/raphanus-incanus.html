Raphanus incanus

Raphanus incanus, commonly known as the hairy radish, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lobed leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by round, fleshy fruits called siliques.

The hairy radish is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
