Rubus alterniflorus var. bagnallii

Rubus alterniflorus var. bagnallii, commonly known as Bagnall's bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters and blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.
