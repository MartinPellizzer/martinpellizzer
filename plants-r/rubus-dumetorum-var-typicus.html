Rubus dumetorum var. typicus

Rubus dumetorum var. typicus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are dark purple to black in color and are edible when fully mature.
