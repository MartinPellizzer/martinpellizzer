Rubus × coriifolius

Rubus × coriifolius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny bushes and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple or black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species, Rubus and Coriifolius.
