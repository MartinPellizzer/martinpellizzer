Ranunculus heterophyllus

Ranunculus heterophyllus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and sometimes pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are usually deeply lobed and vary in shape depending on the variety.

Ranunculus heterophyllus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
