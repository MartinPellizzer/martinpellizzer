Rubus × subrotundiformis

Rubus × subrotundiformis, commonly known as the subrotundiform blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
