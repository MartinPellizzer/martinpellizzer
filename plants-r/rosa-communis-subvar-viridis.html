Rosa communis subvar. viridis

Rosa communis subvar. viridis, commonly known as the green rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose characterized by its unique foliage.

This subvariety features dark green, velvety leaves that are broader and more serrated compared to the typical rose. The flowers are similar to those of the common rose, with a fragrant scent and five to seven petals. It is typically found in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural displays.
