Rosa anceps

Rosa anceps, commonly known as the New Zealand tea tree, is a species of shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in coastal and mountainous regions, often forming dense thickets.

Rosa anceps is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world.
