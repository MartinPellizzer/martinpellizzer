Rubus cineraceus

Rubus cineraceus, commonly known as the gray bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its berries, which are typically red or black, are edible but often have a tart flavor.

Rubus cineraceus is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions.
