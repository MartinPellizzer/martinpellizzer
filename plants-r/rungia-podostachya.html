Rungia podostachya

Rungia podostachya, commonly known as the water spinach or kangkong, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves and stems that are often used in various cuisines. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and can thrive in shallow water. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color.

Rungia podostachya is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its adaptability to different aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
