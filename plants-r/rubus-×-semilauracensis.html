Rubus × semilauracensis

Rubus × semilauracensis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry-loganberry.

This plant is a hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a unique combination of traits. It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The fruit is aggregate, consisting of multiple small drupelets, and is usually dark purple to black in color.

It is cultivated for its flavor and is often used in jams and desserts.
