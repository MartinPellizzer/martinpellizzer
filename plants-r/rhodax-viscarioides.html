Rhodax viscarioides

Rhodax viscarioides, commonly known as the sticky leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, waxy leaves that often have a sticky or glandular surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Rhodax viscarioides thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.

It prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
