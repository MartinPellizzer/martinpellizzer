Rubus lahidjanensis

Rubus lahidjanensis, commonly known as the Lahijan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
