Rhynchospora chinensis

Rhynchospora chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and floodplains, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in dense spikes, and are pollinated by wind.

Rhynchospora chinensis is native to East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is often found in wetland habitats.
