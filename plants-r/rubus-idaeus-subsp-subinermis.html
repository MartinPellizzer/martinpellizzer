Rubus idaeus subsp. subinermis

Rubus idaeus subsp.

subinermis, commonly known as the smooth raspberry, is a subspecies of the red raspberry that is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though some varieties may have yellow or orange berries. It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The subspecies is valued for its fruit, which is used in culinary applications and can be harvested in late summer.
