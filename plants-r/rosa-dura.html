Rosa dura

Rosa dura, commonly known as the hardy rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorny projections.

Rosa dura is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
