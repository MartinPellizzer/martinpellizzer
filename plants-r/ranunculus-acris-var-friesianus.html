Ranunculus acris var. friesianus

Ranunculus acris var. friesianus, commonly known as the Friesian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a basal rosette of deeply lobed, palmately divided leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
