Rhynchosia rhomboidea

Rhynchosia rhomboidea, commonly known as the broad-leafed cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods. The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
