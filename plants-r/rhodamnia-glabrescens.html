Rhodamnia glabrescens

Rhodamnia glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth rhodamnia, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons.

The species is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in coastal areas and dry woodlands.
