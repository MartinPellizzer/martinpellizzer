Rhadamanthus calcicola

Rhadamanthus calcicola, commonly known as the limestone rhadamanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
