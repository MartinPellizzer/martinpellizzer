Rubus corylifolius subsp. ostenfeldii

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

ostenfeldii, commonly known as the Ostenfeld's bramble, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
