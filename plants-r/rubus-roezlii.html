Rubus roezlii

Rubus roezlii, commonly known as the Rösel's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1–2 meters, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are small and white, and it produces edible berries that are similar to blackberries in taste and texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
