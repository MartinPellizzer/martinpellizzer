Rubus rosaceus var. grandicuspidatus

Rubus rosaceus var. grandicuspidatus, commonly known as the large-spined blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and large, thorny stems. The leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and have a dark green upper surface. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by dark purple to black berries that are edible.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental qualities.
