Rebutia euanthema f. fricii

Rebutia euanthema f. fricii, commonly known as the Frici's Rebutia, is a unique hybrid of the genus Rebutia, native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of succulent plants. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in the spring. Its foliage is usually green with a slight bluish tint, and it is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
