Rosa uncinella var. subatrichostylis

Rosa uncinella var. subatrichostylis, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a variant of the hairy rose species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and stems, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet being hairy and oval in shape.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
