Rapanea cacuminum

Rapanea cacuminum, commonly known as the Malabar boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Rapanea cacuminum is often cultivated as a hedge or topiary plant due to its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate pruning.
