Racosperma lentigineum

Racosperma lentigineum, commonly known as the spotted wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive spotted pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are typical of the wattle genus. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma lentigineum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.
