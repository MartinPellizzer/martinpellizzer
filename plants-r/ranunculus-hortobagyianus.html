Ranunculus hortobagyianus

Ranunculus hortobagyianus, commonly known as the Hungarian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Hungary and parts of Central Europe.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has a short, stout stem that branches near the top, bearing several leaves that are palmately divided. Its leaves and flowers are covered in fine, soft hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks.
