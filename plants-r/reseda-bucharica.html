Reseda bucharica

Reseda bucharica, commonly known as the Bukharan reseda, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Reseda bucharica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
