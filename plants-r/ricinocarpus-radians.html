Ricinocarpus radians

Ricinocarpus radians, commonly known as the broadleaf sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive red throat. The plant is typically found in coastal areas and brackish water environments. It has a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
