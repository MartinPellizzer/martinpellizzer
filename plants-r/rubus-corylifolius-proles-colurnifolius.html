Rubus corylifolius proles colurnifolius

Rubus corylifolius proles colurnifolius, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
