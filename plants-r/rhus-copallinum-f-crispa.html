Rhus copallinum f. crispa

Rhus copallinum f. crispa, commonly known as the roughleaf sumac, is a cultivar of the staghorn sumac.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated, and rough-textured leaves that are typically bright green in summer and turn red in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
