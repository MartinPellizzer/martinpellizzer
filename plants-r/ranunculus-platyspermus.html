Ranunculus platyspermus

Ranunculus platyspermus, commonly known as the broad-seeded buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring, and it produces small, rounded seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus platyspermus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, daisy-like appearance.
