Rubus muenteri var. pusillus

Rubus muenteri var. pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has a dense, thorny structure with compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
