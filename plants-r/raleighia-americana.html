Raleighia americana

Raleighia americana, commonly known as the Florida anise tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and emit a licorice-like scent when crushed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, capsule-like fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a pleasant fragrance.

Raleighia americana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
