Rosa viscaria var. belnensis

Rosa viscaria var. belnensis, commonly known as the Belnensian rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are dark green, oval-shaped, and arranged alternately along the stems. This variety is known for its hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
