Rhododendron vialii

Rhododendron vialii, commonly known as the Vial's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its floral display ranges from pink to purple, depending on the specific variety.

Rhododendron vialii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
