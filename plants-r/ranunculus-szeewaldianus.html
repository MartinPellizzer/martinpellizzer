Ranunculus szeewaldianus

Ranunculus szeewaldianus, commonly known as the Szeewald's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
