Rubus obscurus var. pulchrifactus

Rubus obscurus var. pulchrifactus, commonly known as the beautiful blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its botanical features include hairy stems and flowers with five pink petals.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.
