Rubus corylifolius subsp. wahlbergii

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

wahlbergii, commonly known as the Wahlberg's blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dark, prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that develop into edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and shrubland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit, which is used in traditional foods and beverages.
