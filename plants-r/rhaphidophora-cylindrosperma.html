Rhaphidophora cylindrosperma

Rhaphidophora cylindrosperma, commonly known as the spindle-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves and distinctive inflorescence. The plant features a rhizomatous stem system, which allows it to spread and form dense clumps. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.
