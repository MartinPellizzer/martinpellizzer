Rubus hadrocarpus f. adenophorus

Rubus hadrocarpus f. adenophorus, commonly known as the hairy-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale pink and are clustered in racemes.

The hairy-fruited blackberry is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated in home gardens.
