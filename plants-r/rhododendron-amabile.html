Rhododendron amabile

Rhododendron amabile, commonly known as the sweet azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhododendron amabile is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
