Rubus latissimus

Rubus latissimus, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thick, prickly stems and large, broad leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The broadleaf blackberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
