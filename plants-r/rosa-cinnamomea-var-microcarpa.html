Rosa cinnamomea var. microcarpa

Rosa cinnamomea var. microcarpa, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a compact shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in short, grayish hairs. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

This variety is particularly hardy and adaptable to a range of soil types and climates.
