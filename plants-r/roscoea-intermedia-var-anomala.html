Roscoea intermedia var. anomala

Roscoea intermedia var. anomala, commonly known as the purple-veined roscoea, is a rhizomatous perennial native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves with prominent purple veins, and its flowers are showy, with pale green petals and a deep red center. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Roscoea intermedia var. anomala is valued for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
