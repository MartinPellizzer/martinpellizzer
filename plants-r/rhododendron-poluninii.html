Rhododendron poluninii

Rhododendron poluninii, commonly known as the Siberian rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high altitudes. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in subalpine or alpine zones.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
