Rubus neopyramidalis var. neodumnoniensis

Rubus neopyramidalis var. neodumnoniensis, commonly known as the Neodumnonian raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry genus characterized by its unique floral and fruit morphology.

It exhibits a compact growth habit with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, contributing to its ornamental value. The fruits, which are aggregate berries, develop from multiple ovaria and are typically red when mature.

This variant is noted for its adaptability to a range of soil types and its potential for cultivation in temperate climates.
