Rhododendron nivale subsp. australe

Rhododendron nivale subsp.

australe, commonly known as the Australian snowbell, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are white to pale pink, blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after plant in horticulture and conservation efforts.
