Rubus commersonii var. simpliciflorus

Rubus commersonii var. simpliciflorus, commonly known as the simpleflower blackberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local culinary traditions.
