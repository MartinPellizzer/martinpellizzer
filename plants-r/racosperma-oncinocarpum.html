Racosperma oncinocarpum

Racosperma oncinocarpum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, which makes it beneficial for soil fertility. It is often found in sandy or loamy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its ecological and ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various parts of the world.
