Rottboellia hordeoides

Rottboellia hordeoides, commonly known as false barley, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Rottboellia hordeoides is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
