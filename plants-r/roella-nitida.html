Roella nitida

Roella nitida, commonly known as the slender roella, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Roella nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
