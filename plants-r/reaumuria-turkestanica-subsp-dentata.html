Reaumuria turkestanica subsp. dentata

Reaumuria turkestanica subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the toothed reaumuria, is a shrub native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval leaves with serrated edges, which are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry climates.

The subspecies is notable for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
