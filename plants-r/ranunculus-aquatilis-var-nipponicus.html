Ranunculus aquatilis var. nipponicus

Ranunculus aquatilis var. nipponicus, commonly known as the Japanese water buttercup, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features delicate, floating leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It has a short stem and produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that disperse by water.

This variety is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
