Rhynchosia buchananii

Rhynchosia buchananii, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a climbing legume native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by bean-like pods. The plant is characterized by its twining habit and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Rhynchosia buchananii is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
