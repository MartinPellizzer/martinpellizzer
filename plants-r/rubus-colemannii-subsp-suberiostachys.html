Rubus colemannii subsp. suberiostachys

Rubus colemannii subsp.

suberiostachys, commonly known as the California blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by its edible fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and adaptability to different climates.
