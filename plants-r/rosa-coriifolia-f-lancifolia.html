Rosa coriifolia f. lancifolia

Rosa coriifolia f. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. This plant is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates.

Its stems are often covered in thorns, and it produces small, edible rosehips that are rich in vitamin C. The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
