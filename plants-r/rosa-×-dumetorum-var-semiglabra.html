Rosa × dumetorum var. semiglabra

Rosa × dumetorum var. semiglabra, commonly known as the creeping shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit, with thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are covered in a fine layer of hairs. This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.

It is also used in landscaping for its ability to form dense ground cover.
