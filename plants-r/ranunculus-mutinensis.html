Ranunculus mutinensis

Ranunculus mutinensis, commonly known as the Italian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features delicate, showy flowers with 5-10 petals, typically yellow or orange in color, and a distinctive yellow center. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Ranunculus mutinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
