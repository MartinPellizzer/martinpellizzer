Ruta angustifolia var. attenuata

Ruta angustifolia var. attenuata, commonly known as narrowleaf rue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, pungent odor. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by narrow, elongated fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
