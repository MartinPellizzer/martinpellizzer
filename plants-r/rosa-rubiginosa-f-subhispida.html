Rosa rubiginosa f. subhispida

Rosa rubiginosa f. subhispida, commonly known as the rusty rose or subhairy rose, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a reddish-pink hip, which contains achenes surrounded by a fleshy, hairy covering.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
