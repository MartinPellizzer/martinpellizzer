Rhododendron redowskianum

Rhododendron redowskianum, commonly known as the Redowskian rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This species is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
