Rubus foliosus var. viscosus

Rubus foliosus var. viscosus, commonly known as the sticky leaf raspberry, is a shrub native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of sticky hairs on its stems and leaves. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow when ripe.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
