Rosa seidlii

Rosa seidlii, commonly known as the Himalayan rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and temperate climates. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of downy hair.

Rosa seidlii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
