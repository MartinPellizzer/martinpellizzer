Rubus oenogynus

Rubus oenogynus, commonly known as the wineberry, is a species of brambles native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and wines. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Rubus oenogynus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
