Rubus acaracis

Rubus acaracis, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
