Rubus × pseudoancophilus

Rubus × pseudoancophilus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges.

The fruit, which is usually dark purple to black, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
