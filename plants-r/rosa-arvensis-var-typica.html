Rosa arvensis var. typica

Rosa arvensis var. typica, commonly known as the field rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces pink to red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often thorny, and it is commonly found growing in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is adaptable to various soil types and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
