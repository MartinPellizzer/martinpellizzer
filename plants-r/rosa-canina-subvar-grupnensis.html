Rosa canina subvar. grupnensis

Rosa canina subvar. grupnensis, commonly known as the Crimean rose, is a subspecies native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rosa genus. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in spring, followed by orange-red hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in local culture.
