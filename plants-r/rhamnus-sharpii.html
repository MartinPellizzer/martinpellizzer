Rhamnus sharpii

Rhamnus sharpii, commonly known as the Chinese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Rhamnus sharpii is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
