Ratibida columnifera var. pulcherrima

Ratibida columnifera var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy yellow-orange ray flowers that resemble sunflowers. The plant produces numerous small, disc-shaped florets surrounded by the prominent, petal-like bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features include alternate, lance-shaped leaves and a long flowering period from summer to fall.
