Rosa canina var. caesia

Rosa canina var. caesia, commonly known as the pale dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it is valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
