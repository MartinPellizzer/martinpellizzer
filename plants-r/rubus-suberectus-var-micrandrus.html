Rubus suberectus var. micrandrus

Rubus suberectus var. micrandrus, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated margins and a hairy underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
