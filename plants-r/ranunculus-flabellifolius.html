Ranunculus flabellifolius

Ranunculus flabellifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a distinctive white or yellow center. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are broad and lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Ranunculus flabellifolius is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
