Rapuntium capillifolium

Rapuntium capillifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are arranged in a rosette and its clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed soils. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread across various habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
