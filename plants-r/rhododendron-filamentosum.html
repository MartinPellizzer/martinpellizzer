Rhododendron filamentosum

Rhododendron filamentosum, commonly known as the filamentous rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, thread-like appearance, giving it its common name.

Rhododendron filamentosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
