Rhodiola bouffordii

Rhodiola bouffordii, commonly known as the Chinese rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Rhodiola bouffordii is valued for its adaptogenic properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
