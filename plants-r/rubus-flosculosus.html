Rubus flosculosus

Rubus flosculosus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits, which are typically blackberries, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
