Ranunculus alejandrei

Ranunculus alejandrei, commonly known as the Alexander's buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive black central spot. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, cool environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique and ornamental plants.
