Rhaponticum dahuricum

Rhaponticum dahuricum, commonly known as Siberian rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features a thick, fleshy rhizome and large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer, which are followed by small, greenish fruits. It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
