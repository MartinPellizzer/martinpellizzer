Rhododendron cavaleriei var. chaffanjonii

Rhododendron cavaleriei var. chaffanjonii, commonly known as the Chaffanjon rhododendron, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade. Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and a compact growth habit, making it a desirable specimen for specialized gardens.
