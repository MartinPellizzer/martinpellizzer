Rhytiglossa laevilinguis

Rhytiglossa laevilinguis, commonly known as the smooth-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often clustered in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
