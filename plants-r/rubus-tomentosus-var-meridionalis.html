Rubus tomentosus var. meridionalis

Rubus tomentosus var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern brambles, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It features prickly stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are broad and serrated.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
