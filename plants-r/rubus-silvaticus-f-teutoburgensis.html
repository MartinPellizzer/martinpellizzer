Rubus silvaticus f. teutoburgensis

Rubus silvaticus f. teutoburgensis, commonly known as the Teutoburgensis raspberry, is a shrub native to central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it flowers with white or pinkish inflorescences.

This subspecies is adapted to woodland and heathland environments, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
