Rorippa microcarpa

Rorippa microcarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It typically grows in shallow water, wet soil, or along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, with four petals.

Rorippa microcarpa is often found in temperate regions and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
