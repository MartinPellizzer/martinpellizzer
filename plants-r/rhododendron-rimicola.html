Rhododendron rimicola

Rhododendron rimicola, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron rimicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat.
