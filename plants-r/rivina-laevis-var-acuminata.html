Rivina laevis var. acuminata

Rivina laevis var. acuminata, commonly known as the Acuminata Rivina, is a variant of the Rivina laevis species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
