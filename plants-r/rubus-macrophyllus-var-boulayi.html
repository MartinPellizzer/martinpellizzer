Rubus macrophyllus var. boulayi

Rubus macrophyllus var. boulayi, commonly known as the Boulay's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus family, valued for both its ecological role and historical use in traditional medicine.
