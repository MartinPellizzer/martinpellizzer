Racosperma lobulatum

Racosperma lobulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters, typical of many wattles. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma lobulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
