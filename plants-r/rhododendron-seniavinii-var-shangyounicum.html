Rhododendron seniavinii var. shangyounicum

Rhododendron seniavinii var. shangyounicum, commonly known as the Shangyouni rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features lanceolate leaves that are dark green and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
