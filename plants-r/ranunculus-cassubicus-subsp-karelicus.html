Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. karelicus

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

karelicus, commonly known as the Karelian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern Europe and western Siberia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has a short, stout rhizome and grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are trifoliate, with lobed divisions, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
