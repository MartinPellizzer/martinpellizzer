Racosperma congestum subsp. wonganense

Racosperma congestum subsp.

wonganense, commonly known as the Wongan wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter, followed by narrow, pod-like fruits. It is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with a Mediterranean climate.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
