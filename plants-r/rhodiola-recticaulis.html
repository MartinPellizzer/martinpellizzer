Rhodiola recticaulis

Rhodiola recticaulis, commonly known as the slender rhodiola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its upright, succulent stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in cold, rocky soils with low nutrient content.

Rhodiola recticaulis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported adaptogenic properties.
