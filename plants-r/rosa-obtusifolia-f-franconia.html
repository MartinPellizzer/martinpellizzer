Rosa obtusifolia f. franconia

Rosa obtusifolia f. franconia, commonly known as the Franconian rose, is a compact, shrubby plant native to central Europe.

It features upright stems covered in dense, thorny bracts and produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The foliage is dark green and glossy, with slightly toothed leaves. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often used in gardens for its visual interest and ability to attract pollinators.
