Rubus cuspidiferus var. oegacanthus

Rubus cuspidiferus var. oegacanthus, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to wildlife.
