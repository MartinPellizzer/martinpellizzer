Restrepia xanthophthalma

Restrepia xanthophthalma, commonly known as the yellow-flowered restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive orange lip. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil. It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its attractive blooms and relatively easy care requirements.

Restrepia xanthophthalma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both greenhouse and outdoor settings.
