Ranunculus monspeliacus var. saxatilis

Ranunculus monspeliacus var. saxatilis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and bright green, often with a hairy texture. The flowers are typically yellow with dark centers, and they bloom in early spring. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in crevices and on slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, mountainous conditions.
