Rhododendron piercei

Rhododendron piercei, commonly known as the Pierce's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron piercei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as part of native plant conservation efforts.
