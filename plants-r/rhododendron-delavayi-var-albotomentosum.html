Rhododendron delavayi var. albotomentosum

Rhododendron delavayi var. albotomentosum, commonly known as the albotomentous rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features dense, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, white tomentum, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
