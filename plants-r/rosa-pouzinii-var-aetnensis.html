Rosa pouzinii var. aetnensis

Rosa pouzinii var. aetnensis, commonly known as the Aetna rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the volcanic regions of Sicily.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge. This variety is well adapted to harsh, rocky environments and thrives in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in Mediterranean horticulture.
