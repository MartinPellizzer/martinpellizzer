Rebutia kupperiana

Rebutia kupperiana, commonly known as the Kupper's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, colorful flowers. The plant has a short, thick stem with areoles that bear clusters of spines. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in the spring and are often tubular in shape.

Rebutia kupperiana is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
