Rubus × adenocladus

Rubus × adenocladus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
