Rubus caesioidaeus

Rubus caesioidaeus, commonly known as the blueberry rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, edible berries that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

The berries are not only ornamental but also have potential culinary uses.
