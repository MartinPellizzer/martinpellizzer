Rubus × hemilasiothyrsus

Rubus × hemilasiothyrsus, commonly known as the blackberry-boysenberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccatus (boysenberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a large, dark purple to black aggregate fruit with a distinctive, sweet-tart flavor.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in jams and desserts.
