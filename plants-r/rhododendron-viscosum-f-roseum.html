Rhododendron viscosum f. roseum

Rhododendron viscosum f. roseum, commonly known as the Roseum Azalea, is a compact, broadleaf evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, semi-double flowers that range from pale pink to deep rose, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has leathery, dark green foliage that provides an attractive backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense, bushy growth that can be shaped for aesthetic appeal.
