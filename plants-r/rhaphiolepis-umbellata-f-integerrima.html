Rhaphiolepis umbellata f. integerrima

Rhaphiolepis umbellata f. integerrima, commonly known as the compact form of the Japanese rose.

It is a flowering shrub native to Japan, characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and showy pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features small, glossy evergreen leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.

Its compact form makes it suitable for use as a hedge or container plant.
