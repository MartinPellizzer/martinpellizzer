Rosa × collina var. sylvestris

Rosa × collina var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild mountain rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
