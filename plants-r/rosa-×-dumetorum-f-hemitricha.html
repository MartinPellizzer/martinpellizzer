Rosa × dumetorum f. hemitricha

Rosa × dumetorum f. hemitricha, commonly known as the alpine rose or mountain rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that helps it withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine, white trichomes, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
