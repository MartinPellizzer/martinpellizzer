Rubus flagellaris var. inermis

Rubus flagellaris var. inermis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thornless stems and small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white and borne in clusters, and the plant is valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
