Rosa glauca subvar. archetypa

Rosa glauca subvar. archetypa, commonly known as the blueberry rose, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by wildlife. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
