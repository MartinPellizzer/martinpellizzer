Rhynchospora biceps

Rhynchospora biceps, commonly known as the two-armed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, three-petaled flowers that are typically arranged in dense spikelets. Rhynchospora biceps thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various insect and bird species.
