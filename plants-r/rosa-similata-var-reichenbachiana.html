Rosa similata var. reichenbachiana

Rosa similata var. reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Reichenbach's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are covered in fine pubescence. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
