Rosa afzeliana var. briquetii

Rosa afzeliana var. briquetii, commonly known as the Briquet's rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late summer, which are typically borne in clusters. Its botanical features include hairy stems and stipules, and it is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
