Rhynchosia malacotricha

Rhynchosia malacotricha, commonly known as the hairy bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. The plant bears pods that contain several angular, edible seeds. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions.

Rhynchosia malacotricha is valued for its nutritional seeds and is sometimes cultivated for forage or ornamental purposes.
