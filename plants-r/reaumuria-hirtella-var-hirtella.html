Reaumuria hirtella var. hirtella

Reaumuria hirtella var. hirtella, commonly known as the Desert Holly, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its woody stems are often reddish or grayish, providing structural support in harsh conditions.

This species is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive with minimal water, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
