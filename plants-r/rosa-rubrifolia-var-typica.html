Rosa rubrifolia var. typica

Rosa rubrifolia var. typica, commonly known as the red-leaf rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage, which becomes more pronounced in cooler weather. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in temperate climates.
