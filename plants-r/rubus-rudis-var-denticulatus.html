Rubus rudis var. denticulatus

Rubus rudis var. denticulatus, commonly known as the toothed raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its distinctive toothed leaf margins.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, similar to other raspberry species. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary applications.
