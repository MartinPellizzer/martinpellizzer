Rubia valantioides

Rubia valantioides, commonly known as the Valantoides bittersweet, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
