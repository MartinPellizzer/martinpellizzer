Rosa rubra lucida

Rosa rubra lucida, commonly known as the radiant red rose, is a species characterized by its striking, deep crimson petals that reflect a luminous quality in sunlight.

The plant is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a rich, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The botanical structure of Rosa rubra lucida includes a woody stem covered in a layer of bark, and its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
