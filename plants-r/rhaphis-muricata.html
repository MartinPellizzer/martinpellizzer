Rhaphis muricata

Rhaphis muricata, commonly known as the spiny rush, is a member of the palms family (Arecaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
