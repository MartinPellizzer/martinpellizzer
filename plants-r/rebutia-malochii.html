Rebutia malochii

Rebutia malochii, commonly known as the Ghost Cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and pale green to bluish-gray stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. Rebutia malochii is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems and having a shallow root system.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
