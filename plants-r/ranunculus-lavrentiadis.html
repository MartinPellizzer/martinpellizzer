Ranunculus lavrentiadis

Ranunculus lavrentiadis, commonly known as the Lavrenti's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green, oval fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
