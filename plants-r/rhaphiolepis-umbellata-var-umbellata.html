Rhaphiolepis umbellata var. umbellata

Rhaphiolepis umbellata var. umbellata, commonly known as the Chinese holly or winter jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, glossy, oval leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
