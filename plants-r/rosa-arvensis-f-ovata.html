Rosa arvensis f. ovata

Rosa arvensis f. ovata, commonly known as the ovate form of the field rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its rounded, ovate-shaped leaves.

It is a variant of Rosa arvensis, which is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its stems are covered with thorns, and it typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
