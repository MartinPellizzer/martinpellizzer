Rhododendron kasoense

Rhododendron kasoense, commonly known as the Kasoense rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and flowers that range in color from pink to red, making it a notable species in its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often studied for its role in local ecosystems.
