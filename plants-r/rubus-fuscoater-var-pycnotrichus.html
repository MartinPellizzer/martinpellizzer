Rubus fuscoater var. pycnotrichus

Rubus fuscoater var. pycnotrichus, commonly known as the dark-leaved bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green foliage that often appears almost black in shade. The plant produces small, aggregate berries that are typically red or black when mature, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within the Rubus genus.
