Rubus godronii var. belophoroides

Rubus godronii var. belophoroides, commonly known as the Godron's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species native to specific regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
