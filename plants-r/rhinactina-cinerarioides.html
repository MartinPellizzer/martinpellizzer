Rhinactina cinerarioides

Rhinactina cinerarioides, commonly known as the ash-leaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, ash-gray foliage. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
