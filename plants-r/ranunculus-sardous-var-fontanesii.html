Ranunculus sardous var. fontanesii

Ranunculus sardous var. fontanesii, commonly known as the Sardinian buttercup, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Sardinia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions. It has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
