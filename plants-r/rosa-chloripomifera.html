Rosa chloripomifera

Rosa chloripomifera, commonly known as the green-leafed rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
