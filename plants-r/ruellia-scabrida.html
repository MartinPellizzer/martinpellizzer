Ruellia scabrida

Ruellia scabrida, commonly known as rough ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators. Ruellia scabrida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
