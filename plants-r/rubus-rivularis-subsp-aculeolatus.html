Rubus rivularis subsp. aculeolatus

Rubus rivularis subsp.

aculeolatus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
