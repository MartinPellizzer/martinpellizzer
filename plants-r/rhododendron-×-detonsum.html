Rhododendron × detonsum

Rhododendron × detonsum, commonly known as the purpleleaf rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that often turns a striking purple or reddish hue in the fall. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late spring. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in suitable climates.
