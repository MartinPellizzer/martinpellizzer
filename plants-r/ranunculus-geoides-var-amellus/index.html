Ranunculus Geoides Var. Amellus

Ranunculus geoides var. amellus is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa. This variant of the ranunculus family is known for its small, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered by conservationists. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
