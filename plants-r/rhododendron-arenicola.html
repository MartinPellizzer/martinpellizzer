Rhododendron arenicola

Rhododendron arenicola, commonly known as the sand rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils, often found in high-altitude environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Rhododendron arenicola is adapted to harsh climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience.
