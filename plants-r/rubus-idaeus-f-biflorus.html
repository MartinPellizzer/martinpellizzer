Rubus idaeus f. biflorus

Rubus idaeus f. biflorus, commonly known as the double-flowered red raspberry, is a horticultural form of the common raspberry.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which features two distinct flower clusters on a single peduncle. This variety produces small, bright red berries that are typically smaller and more acidic than standard raspberries. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and unique floral structure.
