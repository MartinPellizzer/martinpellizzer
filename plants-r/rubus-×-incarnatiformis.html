Rubus × incarnatiformis

Rubus × incarnatiformis, commonly known as the hybrid raspberry or red raspberry, is a species resulting from the crossbreeding of various Rubus species.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red when ripe. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
