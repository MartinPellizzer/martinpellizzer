Ranunculus cymbalaria var. americanus

Ranunculus cymbalaria var. americanus, commonly known as the American water buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and roadside ditches. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often surrounded by two rows of sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps repel water.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and sometimes toxic compounds.
