Rubus lipovensis var. orsovensis

Rubus lipovensis var. orsovensis, commonly known as the Orsova blackberry, is a variant of the lipovensis species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the Balkan region, particularly in parts of Serbia and Bosnia and Herzegovina. This plant is characterized by its dark purple to black fruits, which are typically small and have a firm texture. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its edible berries, which have a rich flavor and are used in local cuisines and traditional beverages.
