Rorippa hybosperma

Rorippa hybosperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Its seeds are small and numerous, adapted for dispersal by water, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
