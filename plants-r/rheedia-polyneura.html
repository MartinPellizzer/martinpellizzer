Rheedia polyneura

Rheedia polyneura, commonly known as the mountain balsam, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features striking, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. Rheedia polyneura is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to high-altitude ecosystems and its role in local biodiversity.
