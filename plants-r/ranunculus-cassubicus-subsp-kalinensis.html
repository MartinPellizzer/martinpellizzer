Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. kalinensis

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

kalinensis, commonly known as the Kalinensky buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is characterized by its short stature and early flowering period, often blooming in late spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
