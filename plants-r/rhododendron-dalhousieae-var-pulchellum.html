Rhododendron dalhousieae var. pulchellum

Rhododendron dalhousieae var. pulchellum, commonly known as the Dalhousie's Rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool, moist environments with well-drained acidic soil. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to white, bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
