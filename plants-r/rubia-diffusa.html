Rubia diffusa

Rubia diffusa, commonly known as common bugleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces dark blue or black berries that are used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Rubia diffusa is also valued for its historical use in dye production and as a remedy for various ailments.
