Rosa spinosissima var. euspinosissima

Rosa spinosissima var. euspinosissima, commonly known as the Spanish pink or the Spanish rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
