Rubus nigrobaccus var. sativus

Rubus nigrobaccus var. sativus, commonly known as blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically white or pale green, and the plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits.
