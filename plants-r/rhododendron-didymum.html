Rhododendron didymum

Rhododendron didymum, commonly known as the twinleaf rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Rhododendron didymum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
