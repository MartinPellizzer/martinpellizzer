Rubus reichenbachii

Rubus reichenbachii, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which are typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The species is often found in forest understories and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
