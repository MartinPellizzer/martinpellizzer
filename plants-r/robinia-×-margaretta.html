Robinia × margaretta

Robinia × margaretta, commonly known as the Southern catalpa, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit consists of long, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
