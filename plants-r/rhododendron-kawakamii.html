Rhododendron kawakamii

Rhododendron kawakamii, commonly known as Kawakami's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Taiwan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
