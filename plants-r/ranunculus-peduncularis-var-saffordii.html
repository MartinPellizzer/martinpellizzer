Ranunculus peduncularis var. saffordii

Ranunculus peduncularis var. saffordii, commonly known as the Safford's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb with basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
