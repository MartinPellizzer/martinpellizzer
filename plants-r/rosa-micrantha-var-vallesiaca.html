Rosa micrantha var. vallesiaca

Rosa micrantha var. vallesiaca, commonly known as the Vallesian rose, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pink to reddish blooms in spring, which are followed by oval, hairy fruits. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
