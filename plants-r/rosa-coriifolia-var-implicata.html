Rosa coriifolia var. implicata

Rosa coriifolia var. implicata, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that have serrated edges. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
