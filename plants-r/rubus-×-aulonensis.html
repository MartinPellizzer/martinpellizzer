Rubus × aulonensis

Rubus × aulonensis, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many bramble species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are commonly referred to as blackberries, and are known for their sweet to tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its adaptability and fruit production, it plays an important role in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
