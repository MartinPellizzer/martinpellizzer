Rubus glandulosus var. coburgianus

Rubus glandulosus var. coburgianus, commonly known as Coburg raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in local cuisine.
