Rosa urbica var. globata

Rosa urbica var. globata, commonly known as the globular dog rose, is a rare and compact variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical growth habit and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and thorny stems typical of the Rosa genus. It is native to rocky and alpine regions in Europe and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its rarity and unique form, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
