Rubus multisetosus var. acuminatus

Rubus multisetosus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-toothed blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and arranged in clusters, typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.
