Ranunculus bingoeldaghensis

Ranunculus bingoeldaghensis, commonly known as the Bingoeldagh buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive central blotch. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in grassy or rocky habitats. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
