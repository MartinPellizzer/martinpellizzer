Ranunculus sublaetevirens

Ranunculus sublaetevirens, commonly known as the pale-green buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous stamens. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in moist, temperate climates. The leaves are typically hairless and have a smooth texture, while the flowers bloom in early spring.

It is often found in meadows and forest clearings, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
