Rubus perdeclinatus

Rubus perdeclinatus, commonly known as the declining bramble, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small and tart, though they are not as widely cultivated as other Rubus species. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its declining population, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in some regions.
