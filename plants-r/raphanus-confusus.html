Raphanus confusus

Raphanus confusus, commonly known as the confused radish, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers. Its root system is not as economically important as that of the common radish, but it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
