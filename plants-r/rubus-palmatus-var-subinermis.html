Rubus palmatus var. subinermis

Rubus palmatus var. subinermis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
