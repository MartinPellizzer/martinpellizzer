Rosa glandulicaulis var. solitaria

Rosa glandulicaulis var. solitaria, commonly known as the solitary glandular rose, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thornless stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with glandular dots along the midrib. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
