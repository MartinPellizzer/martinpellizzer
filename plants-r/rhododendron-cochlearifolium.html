Rhododendron cochlearifolium

Rhododendron cochlearifolium, commonly known as the spiral-leaved rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with elliptical leaves that are often spirally arranged along the stems. The plant produces showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
