Rubus mecocarpus

Rubus mecocarpus, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, edible berries. Its berries are often used in jams and desserts, though they can be quite tart.

The plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different climates.
