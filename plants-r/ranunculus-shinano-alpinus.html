Ranunculus shinano-alpinus

Ranunculus shinano-alpinus, commonly known as the Japanese mountain buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Honshu, Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30–60 cm, with trifoliate leaves and a single, upright stem bearing a single flower. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with 5–10 sepals that resemble petals, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus shinano-alpinus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded forested areas.
