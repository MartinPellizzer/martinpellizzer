Rosa tomentosa var. attenuatocarpa

Rosa tomentosa var. attenuatocarpa, commonly known as the woolly rose or the creeping rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in clusters, typically in spring. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a soft, woolly texture, and the fruit is a red, hairy rose hip.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
