Rosa biformis

Rosa biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive, almost heart-shaped appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Rosa biformis is known for its thorny stems and is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.

It is a hardy species that can thrive in partial to full shade and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
