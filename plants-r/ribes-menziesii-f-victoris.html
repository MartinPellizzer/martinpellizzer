Ribes menziesii f. victoris

Ribes menziesii f. victoris, commonly known as the Victoria gooseberry, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring arching branches covered in small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green, edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its foliage is often densely covered with glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Native American medicine.
