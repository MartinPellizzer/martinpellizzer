Rhododendron lapponicum var. parvifolium

Rhododendron lapponicum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the dwarf mountain rhododendron, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features dense, leathery foliage with elliptical, dark green leaves that are small and narrow. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in harsh, northern environments.
