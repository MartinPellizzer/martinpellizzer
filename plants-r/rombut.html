Rombut

Rombut, commonly known as the wild passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Passiflora genus and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Rombut thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
