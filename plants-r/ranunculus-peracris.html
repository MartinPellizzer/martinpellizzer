Ranunculus peracris

Ranunculus peracris, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. The plant features a short, hairy stem that supports a cluster of bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Ranunculus peracris is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season, blooming in late spring to early summer.
