Rosa × dumetorum var. oblonga

Rosa × dumetorum var. oblonga, commonly known as the creeping Rosa, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with stems that often lie close to the ground and can spread over large areas. The leaves are compound, typically consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets that are slightly hairy. The flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
