Restrepia nicolasii

Restrepia nicolasii, commonly known as the Nicolas's restia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, high-altitude environments. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
