Rubus prasinus f. pseudopsis

Rubus prasinus f. pseudopsis, commonly known as the green raspberry or false raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and edible, tart fruits that resemble tiny raspberries. The plant typically grows in forested areas and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary use in jams and preserves.
