Rosa dumalis f. dentatistipulata

Rosa dumalis f. dentatistipulata, commonly known as the serrated leaf rock rose, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, giving it its specific epithet "dentatistipulata.". Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, and it produces small, aggregate fruits known as rosehips.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
