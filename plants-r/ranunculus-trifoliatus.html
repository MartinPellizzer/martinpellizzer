Ranunculus trifoliatus

Ranunculus trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of trifoliate leaves, each leaf composed of three oval or heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellow or orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Ranunculus trifoliatus is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
