Ranunculus acetosellifolius

Ranunculus acetosellifolius, commonly known as the heartleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive heart-shaped appearance, and its flowers have five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, buttercup-like blooms.
