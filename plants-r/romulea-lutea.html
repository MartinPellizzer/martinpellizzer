Romulea lutea

Romulea lutea, commonly known as the yellow romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a spike. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Romulea lutea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
