Rosa trachyphylla var. jundzillii

Rosa trachyphylla var. jundzillii, commonly known as the Jundzillii rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dark green, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, alpine conditions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its fragrant blooms appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in colder climates.
