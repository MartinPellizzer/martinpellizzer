Rosa tianschanica

Rosa tianschanica, commonly known as the Tian Shan rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces hips that are orange-red in color. It is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine soils.

Rosa tianschanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
