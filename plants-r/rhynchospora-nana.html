Rhynchospora nana

Rhynchospora nana, commonly known as the dwarf spikerush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. Rhynchospora nana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.

It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates with high humidity and moderate temperatures.
