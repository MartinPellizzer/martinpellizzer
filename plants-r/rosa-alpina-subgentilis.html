Rosa alpina subgentilis

Rosa alpina subgentilis, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval and hairy, adapting to cold, high-altitude environments. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Rosa alpina subgentilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
