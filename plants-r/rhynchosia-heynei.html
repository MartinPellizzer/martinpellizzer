Rhynchosia heynei

Rhynchosia heynei, commonly known as the yellow velvetbean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Rhynchosia heynei is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and is sometimes used in soil enrichment.
