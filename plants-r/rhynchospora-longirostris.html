Rhynchospora longirostris

Rhynchospora longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and three-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense tussocks, contributing to soil stabilization in its environment.

Rhynchospora longirostris is often found in nutrient-rich, moist soils and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
