Rhododendron theiochroum

Rhododendron theiochroum, commonly known as the Greek Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its showy blossoms.

Rhododendron theiochroum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
