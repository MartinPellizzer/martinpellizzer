Rosa × novae-angliae

Rosa × novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England rose, is a hybrid species of rose native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with arching stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing semi-double to fully double petals. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a dense canopy.

Rosa × novae-angliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
