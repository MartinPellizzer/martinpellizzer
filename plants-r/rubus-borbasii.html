Rubus borbasii

Rubus borbasii, commonly known as the Borbas's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruit, a type of raspberry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Rubus borbasii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
