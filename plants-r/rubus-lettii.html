Rubus lettii

Rubus lettii, commonly known as the Hawaiian raspberry, is a species of shrub native to the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forest habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Rubus lettii is considered a rare and endangered species.
