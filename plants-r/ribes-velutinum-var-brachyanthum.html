Ribes velutinum var. brachyanthum

Ribes velutinum var. brachyanthum, commonly known as the smooth gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.
