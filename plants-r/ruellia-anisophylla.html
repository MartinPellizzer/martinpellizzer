Ruellia anisophylla

Ruellia anisophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Ruellia anisophylla is often found in moist, shady environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
