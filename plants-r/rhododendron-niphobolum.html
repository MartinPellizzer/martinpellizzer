Rhododendron niphobolum

Rhododendron niphobolum, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and alpine landscapes.
