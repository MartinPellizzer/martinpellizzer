Rhododendron × tatuoi

Rhododendron × tatuoi, commonly known as the Tatuoi rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, broad, and often dark green foliage, which provides a striking contrast to its showy floral displays. The flowers are typically large, funnel-shaped, and come in a range of colors from deep red to pink and white. This species is native to the Appalachian region of the United States and thrives in moist, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
