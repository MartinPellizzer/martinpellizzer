Rosa crispa

Rosa crispa, commonly known as the rough rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, thorny stems and deeply lobed, serrated leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and numerous stamens. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
