Rhododendron × fuchsii

Rhododendron × fuchsii, commonly known as the Fuchs' rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features dense, evergreen foliage and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sun.

Rhododendron × fuchsii is relatively disease-resistant but may be susceptible to pests such as aphids.
