Rottboellia altissima

Rottboellia altissima, commonly known as the tall grassveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas, often forming dense stands.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
