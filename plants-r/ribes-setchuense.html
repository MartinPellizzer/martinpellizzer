Ribes setchuense

Ribes setchuense, commonly known as the Siberian gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, which are rich in vitamin C. Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
