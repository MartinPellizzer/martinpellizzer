Rubus saladiensis

Rubus saladiensis, commonly known as the Saladenis blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
