Rhododendron makinoi var. basirosaceum

Rhododendron makinoi var. basirosaceum, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, leathery foliage with elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant blooms and compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.
