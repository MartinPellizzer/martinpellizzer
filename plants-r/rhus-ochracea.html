Rhus ochracea

Rhus ochracea, commonly known as the yellow sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with 11 to 31 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fuzzy berries in the fall. Rhus ochracea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods, fields, and along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
