Rubus × jaquetii

Rubus × jaquetii, commonly known as the European blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces edible berries that are similar to blackberries.

This species is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
