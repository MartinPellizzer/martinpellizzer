Rubia triflora

Rubia triflora, commonly known as lesser blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Rubia triflora is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grassy areas and along woodland edges.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
