Rosa tomentosa var. serotina

Rosa tomentosa var. serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering shrub rose, is a variant of the woolly rose.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom later in the growing season. It is a deciduous shrub native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
