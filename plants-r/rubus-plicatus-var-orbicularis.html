Rubus plicatus var. orbicularis

Rubus plicatus var. orbicularis, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, rounded leaves and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its flowers are white and clustered, typically appearing in early spring.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or slightly bitter flavor.
