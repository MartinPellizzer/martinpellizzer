Ranunculus imparidens

Ranunculus imparidens, commonly known as the purple buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to nine petals, typically ranging in color from yellow to deep purple. The plant has a short, upright stem with basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Ranunculus imparidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
