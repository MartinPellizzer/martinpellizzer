Ranunculus sarntheinianus

Ranunculus sarntheinianus, commonly known as the Sarntheinian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmately divided leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
