Rubus rosifolius pinnatus

Rubus rosifolius pinnatus, commonly known as the roseleaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive rose-like appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates.

The fruit, a type of aggregate berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.
