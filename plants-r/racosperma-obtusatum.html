Racosperma obtusatum

Racosperma obtusatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Its foliage and seeds are also used in traditional Aboriginal practices.
