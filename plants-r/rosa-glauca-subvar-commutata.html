Rosa glauca subvar. commutata

Rosa glauca subvar. commutata, commonly known as the blue rose or blue musk rose, is a subspecies of the blue rose native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue-pink flowers, which are a result of a unique combination of pigments and genetic traits. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a distinctive color to the landscape.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
