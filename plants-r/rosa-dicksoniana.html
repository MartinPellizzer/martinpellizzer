Rosa dicksoniana

Rosa dicksoniana, commonly known as the Australian swamp rose, is a species of flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in wetland environments, such as swamps and riverbanks. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves and produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. Its stems are often covered in fine, velvety hairs, and it has a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor.

Rosa dicksoniana is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
