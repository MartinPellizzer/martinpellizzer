Rosa × collimitata

Rosa × collimitata, commonly known as the double-flowered shrub rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its dense, thorned stems and compact growth habit, making it suitable for hedging or small garden spaces. The plant produces abundant clusters of semi-double to fully double pink or white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and slightly serrated, providing a lush backdrop for its showy blooms.

Rosa × collimitata is relatively hardy and adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates.
