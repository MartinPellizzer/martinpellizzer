Rubus horridulus var. pinipetus

Rubus horridulus var. pinipetus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry.

This variant is a shrub native to North America, characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. It produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

It is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
