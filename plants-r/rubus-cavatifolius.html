Rubus cavatifolius

Rubus cavatifolius, commonly known as the hollow-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America and is characterized by its distinctive hollow, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries in the summer. Its thorny stems and vigorous growth habit make it a common weed in disturbed habitats.

The botanical features of Rubus cavatifolius make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
