Rubus koehleri var. horridiformis

Rubus koehleri var. horridiformis, commonly known as the roughleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
