Rubus fruticosus var. thyrsoideus

Rubus fruticosus var. thyrsoideus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features arching stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats or thickets. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
