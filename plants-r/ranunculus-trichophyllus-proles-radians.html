Ranunculus trichophyllus proles radians

Ranunculus trichophyllus proles radians, commonly known as the hairy leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its delicate appearance.
