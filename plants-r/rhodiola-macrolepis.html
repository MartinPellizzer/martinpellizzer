Rhodiola macrolepis

Rhodiola macrolepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhodiola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Rhodiola macrolepis is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal medicine for its adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.
