Rubus × dilatatifrons

Rubus × dilatatifrons, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
