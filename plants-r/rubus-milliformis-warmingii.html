Rubus milliformis warmingii

Rubus milliformis warmingii, commonly known as the Warming's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in China and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces red or black berries that are edible.

This species thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.
