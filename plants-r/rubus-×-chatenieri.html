Rubus × chatenieri

Rubus × chatenieri, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (raspberry). This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate of small drupes that can be red or black in color.

It is often cultivated for its flavor and is used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
