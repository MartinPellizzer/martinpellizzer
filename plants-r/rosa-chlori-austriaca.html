Rosa chlori-austriaca

Rosa chlori-austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian yellow rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant typically produces fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Rosa chlori-austriaca is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticultural history.
