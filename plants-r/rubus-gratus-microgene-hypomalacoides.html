Rubus gratus microgene hypomalacoides

Rubus gratus microgene hypomalacoides, commonly known as the small-fruited raspberry, is a species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
