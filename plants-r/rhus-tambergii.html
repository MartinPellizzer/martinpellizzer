Rhus tambergii

Rhus tambergii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have a bright green color during the growing season. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into distinctive, red, velvety pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Rhus tambergii is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
