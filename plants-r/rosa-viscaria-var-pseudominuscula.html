Rosa viscaria var. pseudominuscula

Rosa viscaria var. pseudominuscula, commonly known as the dwarf climbing rose, is a compact, low-growing variety of the shrub rose.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has thornless stems that allow it to climb or trail along surfaces, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing an attractive backdrop for the blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care in garden settings.
