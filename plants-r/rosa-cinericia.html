Rosa cinericia

Rosa cinericia, commonly known as the ash-gray rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Rosa cinericia is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with thorny stems and elliptical leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh, arid environments.
