Ruellia repanda

Ruellia repanda, commonly known as the spreading ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the tips of the stems, with tubular corollas that are usually blue or purple in color.

Ruellia repanda is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.
