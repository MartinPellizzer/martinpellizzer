Rubus × deltoideus

Rubus × deltoideus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in moist, acidic soils. It produces edible berries that are often used in jams and pies.

Its ornamental value comes from its dense foliage and vibrant autumn foliage.
