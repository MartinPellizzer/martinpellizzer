Ribes quercetorum

Ribes quercetorum, commonly known as the redcurrant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, glossy leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, red berries that are edible and often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and hedgerows.

Ribes quercetorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support local wildlife.
