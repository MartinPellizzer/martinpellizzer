Restrepia jesupiana

Restrepia jesupiana, commonly known as the Jesup's restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or humus-rich soils at high elevations. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip. It has a sympodial growth habit, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
