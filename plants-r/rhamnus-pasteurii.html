Rhamnus pasteurii

Rhamnus pasteurii, commonly known as the Pasteur's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, egg-shaped berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various climates.

Rhamnus pasteurii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
