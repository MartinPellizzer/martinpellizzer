Rosa bonhominis

Rosa bonhominis, commonly known as the "Man's Rose," is a species of shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces hip fruits that are red and ornamental. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

Rosa bonhominis prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many landscapes.
