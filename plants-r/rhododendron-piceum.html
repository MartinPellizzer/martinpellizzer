Rhododendron piceum

Rhododendron piceum, commonly known as the black rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of deep purple to almost black flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhododendron piceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
