Rosa × dumetorum f. subuncinelloides

Rosa × dumetorum f. subuncinelloides, commonly known as the creeping shrub rose, is a compact, low-growing plant with dense, thorny stems that often form mats on the ground.

Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a serrated margin. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
