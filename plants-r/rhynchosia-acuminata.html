Rhynchosia acuminata

Rhynchosia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant bears pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are typically yellow or brown in color. Rhynchosia acuminata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
