Rosa micrantha var. gaillardii

Rosa micrantha var. gaillardii, commonly known as the Gaillard's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in rocky soils and dry environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the shrub can grow up to 2 meters in height.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
