Rosa frutetorum

Rosa frutetorum, commonly known as the tree rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces rose hips that contain seeds.

Rosa frutetorum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
