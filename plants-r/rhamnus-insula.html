Rhamnus insula

Rhamnus insula, commonly known as the island buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, exhibiting a compact, multi-stemmed structure. The plant produces small, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are clustered in small inflorescences and are followed by small, oval berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
