Rubus sinuolatus

Rubus sinuolatus, commonly known as the wavyleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with wavy margins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Rubus sinuolatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its fruit and ecological benefits.
