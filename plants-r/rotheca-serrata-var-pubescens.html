Rotheca serrata var. pubescens

Rotheca serrata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy rotheca, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal or sandy environments, showcasing its adaptability to saline and well-drained soils. The plant features small, serrated leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters, and attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
