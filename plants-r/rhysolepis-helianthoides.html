Rhysolepis helianthoides

Rhysolepis helianthoides, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often hairy, while its flowers are yellow with a distinct central disk.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
