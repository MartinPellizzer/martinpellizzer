Ranunculus mancus

Ranunculus mancus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar botanical features.

Ranunculus mancus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is known for its resilience in harsh climates.
