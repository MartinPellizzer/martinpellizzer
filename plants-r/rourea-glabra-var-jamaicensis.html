Rourea glabra var. jamaicensis

Rourea glabra var. jamaicensis, commonly known as Jamaican rourea, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
