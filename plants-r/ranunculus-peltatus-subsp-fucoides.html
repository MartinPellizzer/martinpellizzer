Ranunculus peltatus subsp. fucoides

Ranunculus peltatus subsp.

fucoides, commonly known as the sea ranunculus, is a coastal plant adapted to saline and rocky environments. It features distinctive palmate leaves with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are typically yellow with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. It blooms in spring and is found along the Atlantic coast of Europe and North Africa.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, maritime conditions.
