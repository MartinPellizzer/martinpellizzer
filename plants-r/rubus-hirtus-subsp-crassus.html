Rubus hirtus subsp. crassus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

crassus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its prickly stems and large, usually biennial, compound leaves.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is an important species for pollinators and wildlife.
