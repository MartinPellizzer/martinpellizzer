Rubus × subcalviformis

Rubus × subcalviformis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like structures called achenes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavorful berries.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
