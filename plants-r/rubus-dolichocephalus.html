Rubus dolichocephalus

Rubus dolichocephalus, commonly known as the long-headed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed areas and along forest edges.
