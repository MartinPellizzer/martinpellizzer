Rubus serpens var. neoserpens

Rubus serpens var. neoserpens, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, dark purple to black berries, which are typically tart and used in jams and wines. The plant has prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
