Rubus haynaldianus

Rubus haynaldianus, commonly known as the Haynald's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
