Rosa glabrata var. breynina

Rosa glabrata var. breynina, commonly known as the Breyn's smooth rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces oval, glossy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal blooms.
