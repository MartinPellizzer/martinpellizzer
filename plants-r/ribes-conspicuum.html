Ribes conspicuum

Ribes conspicuum, commonly known as the showy gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants and berries. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring. Its fruit, a small, tart berry, ripens in late summer and is used in jams and wines.

Ribes conspicuum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
