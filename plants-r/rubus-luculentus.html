Rubus luculentus

Rubus luculentus, commonly known as the shiny blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in the summer. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and pies.

The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is an important food source for wildlife.
