Rosa grevillei

Rosa grevillei, commonly known as the Greville rose, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub-like plant with woody stems and thorny branches, typical of many rose species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets, and it prefers well-drained soil in a sunny location.

Rosa grevillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
