Rhynchosia himalensis var. craibiana

Rhynchosia himalensis var. craibiana, commonly known as the Himalayan cowpea, is a climbing legume native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant is characterized by its long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to cool, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture and as a source of protein-rich seeds.
