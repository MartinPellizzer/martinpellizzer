Rhamnus alaternus subsp. alaternus

Rhamnus alaternus subsp.

alaternus, commonly known as the Italian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries that are rich in anthocyanins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
