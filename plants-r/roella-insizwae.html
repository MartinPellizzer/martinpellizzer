Roella insizwae

Roella insizwae, commonly known as the Zulu thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and coarse, fibrous leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or inland grassland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

Roella insizwae is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
