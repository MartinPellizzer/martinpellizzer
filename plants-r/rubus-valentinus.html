Rubus valentinus

Rubus valentinus, commonly known as Valentine's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruit, a red, raspberry-like berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.

Rubus valentinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in floral symbolism.
