Rubus arundelanus

Rubus arundelanus, commonly known as the Arundel blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its horticultural potential and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
