Rubus subintegratus

Rubus subintegratus, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The berries are edible but often have a tart flavor and are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
