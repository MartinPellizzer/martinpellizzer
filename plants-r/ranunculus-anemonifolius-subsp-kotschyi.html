Ranunculus anemonifolius subsp. kotschyi

Ranunculus anemonifolius subsp.

kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features delicate, showy flowers with five to nine petals, typically in shades of yellow or white.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, lace-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
