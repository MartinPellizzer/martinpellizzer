Rubus × nesteinus

Rubus × nesteinus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus allegheniensis (blackberry) and Rubus strigosus (raspberry). The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
