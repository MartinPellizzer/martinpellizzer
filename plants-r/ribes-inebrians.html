Ribes inebrians

Ribes inebrians, commonly known as the purple-flowered currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Ribes inebrians is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
