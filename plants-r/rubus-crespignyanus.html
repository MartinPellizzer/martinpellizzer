Rubus crespignyanus

Rubus crespignyanus, commonly known as the Crespigny blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, specifically Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries in the summer.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
