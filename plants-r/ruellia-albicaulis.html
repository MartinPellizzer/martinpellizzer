Ruellia albicaulis

Ruellia albicaulis, commonly known as white-stemmed ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Ruellia albicaulis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
