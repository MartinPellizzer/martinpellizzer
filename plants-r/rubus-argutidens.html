Rubus argutidens

Rubus argutidens, commonly known as the roughleaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China and is characterized by its prickly stems and broad, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for foraging purposes.
