Rosa quasilindstroemii

Rosa quasilindstroemii, commonly known as the Quasilindstroem's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a shrub with arching stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces hip fruits that are red and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
