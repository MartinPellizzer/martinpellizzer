Rhododendron × docimum

Rhododendron × docimum, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, clustered flowers.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in early to mid-spring. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Rhododendron × docimum is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects in suitable climates.
