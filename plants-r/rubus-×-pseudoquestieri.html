Rubus × pseudoquestieri

Rubus × pseudoquestieri, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many bramble species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are composed of small, drupe-like berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
