Rosa tomentosa var. ucrainica

Rosa tomentosa var. ucrainica, commonly known as the Ukrainian woolly rose, is a shrub native to eastern Europe and parts of Russia.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, grayish stems and foliage, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for hedges and decorative planting.
