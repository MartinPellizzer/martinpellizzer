Ranunculus auricomus var. procerior

Ranunculus auricomus var. procerior, commonly known as the tall buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a distinctive orange or reddish hue at the base. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and covered in a dense layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
