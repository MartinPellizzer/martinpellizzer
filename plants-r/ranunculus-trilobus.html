Ranunculus trilobus

Ranunculus trilobus, commonly known as the three-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven sepals that resemble petals, and it typically blooms in early spring. The plant has alternate, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus trilobus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.
