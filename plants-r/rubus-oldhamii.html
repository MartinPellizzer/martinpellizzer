Rubus oldhamii

Rubus oldhamii, commonly known as Oldham's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is edible but has a tart flavor, making it a valuable resource in local cuisines.
