Rubia longipetiolata

Rubia longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base, and small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties.
