Ranunculus bulbosus var. minor

Ranunculus bulbosus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a short rhizomatous rootstock and produces showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are deeply divided and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are often borne in clusters.

It is an important species for pollinators and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
