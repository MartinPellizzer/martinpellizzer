Rosa crenatula

Rosa crenatula, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in the spring. Rosa crenatula is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
