Rosa woodsii subsp. woodsii

Rosa woodsii subsp.

woodsii, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain rose, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, often at high elevations. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and slightly serrated.

The fruit is a red, hairy rose hip that contains seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
