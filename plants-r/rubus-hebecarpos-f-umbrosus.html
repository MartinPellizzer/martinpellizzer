Rubus hebecarpos f. umbrosus

Rubus hebecarpos f. umbrosus, commonly known as the shadowy blackberry, is a shrub characterized by its dark, thorny stems and dense, evergreen foliage.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are typically elliptical with serrated edges, and it has a compact growth habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
