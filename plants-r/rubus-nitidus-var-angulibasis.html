Rubus nitidus var. angulibasis

Rubus nitidus var. angulibasis, commonly known as the angular-leaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are sharply angled and have a distinctive, almost serrated appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
