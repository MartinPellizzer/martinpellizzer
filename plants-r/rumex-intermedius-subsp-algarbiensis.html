Rumex intermedius subsp. algarbiensis

Rumex intermedius subsp.

algarbiensis, commonly known as the Algarbi dock, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, reddish-veined leaves and stout, reddish stems.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in Mediterranean wetland ecosystems.
