Reseda calicinalis

Reseda calicinalis, commonly known as the rock reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features linear to lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish or white flowers in clusters. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
