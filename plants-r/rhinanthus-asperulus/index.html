Rhinanthus Asperulus

The rhinanthus asperulus is a unique flowering plant known for its striking appearance and ecological significance. Found primarily in grasslands and open habitats, it thrives in areas with well-drained soil and full sunlight. This plant is often mistaken for other members of the orchid family due to its intricate flower structure. Its name derives from the Greek words for nose and sharp, referring to its distinctive, spiky flowers. Despite its beauty, it faces threats from habitat loss and changes in land management.
