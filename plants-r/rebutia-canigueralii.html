Rebutia canigueralii

Rebutia canigueralii, commonly known as the Canary Island cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Rebutia canigueralii is well adapted to arid environments, with spines that provide protection and reduce water loss.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements and ornamental appeal.
