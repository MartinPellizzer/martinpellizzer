Rosa afzeliana var. grandis

Rosa afzeliana var. grandis, commonly known as the large-flowered rock rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
