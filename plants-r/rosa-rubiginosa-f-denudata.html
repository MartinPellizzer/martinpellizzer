Rosa rubiginosa f. denudata

Rosa rubiginosa f. denudata, commonly known as the naked-stemmed dog玫瑰, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and reduced leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits are rose hips, which are typically red and contain numerous seeds.

This form is adapted to rocky or dry soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
