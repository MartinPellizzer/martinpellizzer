Rubus hirtus var. irrufatus

Rubus hirtus var. irrufatus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
