Rubus koehleri var. scoliacanthus

Rubus koehleri var. scoliacanthus, commonly known as the Hainan blackberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
