Rosa schmidtii

Rosa schmidtii, commonly known as Schmidt's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include horticultural value, making it a sought-after plant in gardens and botanical collections.

Rosa schmidtii is also noted for its adaptability to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
