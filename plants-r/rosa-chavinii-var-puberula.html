Rosa chavinii var. puberula

Rosa chavinii var. puberula, commonly known as the Chavin rose, is a species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from pink to white, depending on the variety. Its leaves are dark green, ovate, and covered with a fine layer of pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
