Rubus thyrsoideus var. grabowskii

Rubus thyrsoideus var. grabowskii, commonly known as the Grabowsky blackberry, is a variant of the thyrsoideus blackberry species native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and potential use in both ornamental and edible landscaping.
