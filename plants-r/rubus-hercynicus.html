Rubus hercynicus

Rubus hercynicus, commonly known as the Hercynian bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple drupelets.

Rubus hercynicus is native to Europe and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.
