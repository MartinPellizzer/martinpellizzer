Rubus consanguineus

Rubus consanguineus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces edible berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit develops from the flower and is typically harvested in the summer months.

The plant is valued for both its culinary uses and its role in ecological habitats.
