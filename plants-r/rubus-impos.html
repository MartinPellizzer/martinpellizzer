Rubus impos

Rubus impos, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
