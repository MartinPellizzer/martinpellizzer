Ranunculus aequilaterus

Ranunculus aequilaterus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight broad, petal-like sepals that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces a short, stout flowering stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, buttercup-like blooms.
