Ranunculus similis

Ranunculus similis, commonly known as the similar buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, yellowish flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus similis is an herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring.
