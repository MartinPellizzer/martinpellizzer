Rubus niveus var. niveus

Rubus niveus var. niveus, commonly known as the white-flowered evergreen raspberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red, hairy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Rubus genus.
