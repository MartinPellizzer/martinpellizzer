Rosa submoschata

Rosa submoschata, commonly known as the musk rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets.

Rosa submoschata is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfume-making due to its strong, musky fragrance.
