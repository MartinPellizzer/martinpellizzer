Rubus bicknellii

Rubus bicknellii, commonly known as Bicknell's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species.

Bicknell's blackberry is native to the eastern United States and is considered a rare and endangered species.
