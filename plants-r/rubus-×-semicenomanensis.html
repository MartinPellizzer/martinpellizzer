Rubus × semicenomanensis

Rubus × semicenomanensis, commonly known as the Semicenomanensis blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, woodland habitats. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
