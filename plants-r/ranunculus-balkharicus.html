Ranunculus balkharicus

Ranunculus balkharicus, commonly known as the Balkh poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

Ranunculus balkharicus is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native habitats.
