Randia echinocarpa

Randia echinocarpa, commonly known as the spiny randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
