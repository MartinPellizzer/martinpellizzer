Ribes multiflorum var. urceolatum

Ribes multiflorum var. urceolatum, commonly known as the smooth currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, edible berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.
