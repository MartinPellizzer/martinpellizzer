Rubus calycinus f. dalibardoides

Rubus calycinus f. dalibardoides, commonly known as the Dalibard's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local cuisery.
