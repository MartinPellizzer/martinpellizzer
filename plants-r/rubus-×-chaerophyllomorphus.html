Rubus × chaerophyllomorphus

Rubus × chaerophyllomorphus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas where it forms dense thickets.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
