Rapuntium caeruleum var. glabrescens

Rapuntium caeruleum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered rapunculus, is a variant of the rapunculus species characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers.

It is a biennial plant native to Europe, typically growing in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that become smoother as it matures. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety glabrescens is distinguished by its reduced leaf hair compared to other rapunculus species.
