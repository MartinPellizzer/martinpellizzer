Rafnia cuneifolia var. rhomboidea

Rafnia cuneifolia var. rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid-leafed Rafnia, is a variant of the Rafnia cuneifolia species, characterized by its distinctive rhombus-shaped leaves.

This plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge, while its stems are woody and often branched. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by small, fleshy fruits.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
