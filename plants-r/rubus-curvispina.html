Rubus curvispina

Rubus curvispina, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in the wild, though it can be challenging to cultivate due to its aggressive growth habit.
