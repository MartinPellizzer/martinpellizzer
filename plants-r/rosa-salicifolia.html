Rosa salicifolia

Rosa salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are lance-shaped and have a distinctive silvery-white underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Rosa salicifolia is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
