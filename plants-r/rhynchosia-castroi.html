Rhynchosia castroi

Rhynchosia castroi, commonly known as the Brazilian bean or castro's cowpea, is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain small, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its nutritional value.

Rhynchosia castroi is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
