Rosa afzeliana var. parcepilosa

Rosa afzeliana var. parcepilosa, commonly known as the dense-piled rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
