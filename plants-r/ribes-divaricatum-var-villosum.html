Ribes divaricatum var. villosum

Ribes divaricatum var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in the Pacific Northwest.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
