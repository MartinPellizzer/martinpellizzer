Ranunculus intramongolicus

Ranunculus intramongolicus, commonly known as the Mongolian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, radially symmetrical flowers. The plant typically has simple, alternate leaves and produces yellow or white petals. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
