Rubus micans microgene micantiformis

Rubus micans microgene micantiformis, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red, hairy berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its hirsute foliage and ornamental berries make it a subject of interest in both ecological studies and horticultural practices.
