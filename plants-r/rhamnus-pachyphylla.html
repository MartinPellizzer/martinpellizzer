Rhamnus pachyphylla

Rhamnus pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its desert environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. Its bark is often covered in a thick, corky layer, which helps protect it from extreme temperatures.

Rhamnus pachyphylla plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing food for local wildlife.
