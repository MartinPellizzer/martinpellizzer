Racosperma ataxiphyllum

Racosperma ataxiphyllum, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive, often twisted appearance. It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.

Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.
