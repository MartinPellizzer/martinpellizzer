Racosperma williamsonii

Racosperma williamsonii, commonly known as the Williamstown acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually yellow or pale green, attracting nectar-feeding insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
