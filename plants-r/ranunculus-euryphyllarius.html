Ranunculus euryphyllarius

Ranunculus euryphyllarius, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight broad, petal-like sepals, giving it a distinctive cup-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Ranunculus euryphyllarius is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
