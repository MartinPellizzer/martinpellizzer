Rymia

Rymia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rutaceae.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and leathery, silver-green leaves. The plant is native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia. Rymia species often grow in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
