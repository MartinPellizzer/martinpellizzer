Rosa × dumetorum f. coazii

Rosa × dumetorum f. coazii, commonly known as the Coazii form of the shrub rose, is a hybrid species that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Rosa rugosa and Rosa multiflora.

It is a compact, thorny shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, thriving in a variety of soil conditions.
