Rhamnus dalanta

Rhamnus dalanta, commonly known as the African buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible for some bird species. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Rhamnus dalanta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
