Rhamnus alaternus var. clusii

Rhamnus alaternus var. clusii, commonly known as the Clusii buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable plant for rocky or dry gardens.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and resistance to pests and diseases.
