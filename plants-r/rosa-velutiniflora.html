Rosa velutiniflora

Rosa velutiniflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. Its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.

Rosa velutiniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
