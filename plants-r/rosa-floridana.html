Rosa floridana

Rosa floridana, commonly known as the Florida rose, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa floridana is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.
