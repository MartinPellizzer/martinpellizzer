Rhipsalis biolleyi

Rhipsalis biolleyi, commonly known as the silver mist cactus, is a unique species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and often pendulous stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are modified into short, cylindrical structures that help reduce water loss.

Rhipsalis biolleyi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and ease of care.
