Ribes parishii

Ribes parishii, commonly known as the Parry gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, forested areas and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, tart berries in the summer.
