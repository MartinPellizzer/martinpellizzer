Rubus ellipticus var. obcordatus

Rubus ellipticus var. obcordatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its elliptic, obcordate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
