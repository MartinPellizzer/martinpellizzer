Rubus vigorosus

Rubus vigorosus, commonly known as the vigorous blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Rubus vigorosus is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
