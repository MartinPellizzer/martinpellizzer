Rubus montanus var. grabowskii

Rubus montanus var. grabowskii, commonly known as the Grabowskii raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to eastern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing multiple druplets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
