Racinaea steyermarkii

Racinaea steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's racinaea, is a rare and striking member of the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, with a prominent midrib and a waxy texture. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of many aroid species. Native to the understory of humid tropical forests in Central and South America, it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare tropical plants.
