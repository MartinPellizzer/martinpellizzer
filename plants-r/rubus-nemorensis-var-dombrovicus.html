Rubus nemorensis var. dombrovicus

Rubus nemorensis var. dombrovicus, commonly known as the Dombrovic raspberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruits are dark red to black when ripe.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
