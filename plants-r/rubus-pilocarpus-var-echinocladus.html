Rubus pilocarpus var. echinocladus

Rubus pilocarpus var. echinocladus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests. Its leaves are typically three-lobed with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
