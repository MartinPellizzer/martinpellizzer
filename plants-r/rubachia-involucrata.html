Rubachia involucrata

Rubachia involucrata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, purple-pink petals and a central cone-like structure composed of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubachia involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
