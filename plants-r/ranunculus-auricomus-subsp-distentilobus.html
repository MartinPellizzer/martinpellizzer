Ranunculus auricomus subsp. distentilobus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

distentilobus, commonly known as the golden shield or broadleaf buttercup, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five broadly rounded petals that resemble a shield, hence its common name.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, calcareous soils. It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
