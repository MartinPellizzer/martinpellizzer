Reseda anomala

Reseda anomala, commonly known as yellowrocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in elongated clusters.

Reseda anomala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
