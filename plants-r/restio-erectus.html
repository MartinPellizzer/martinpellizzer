Restio erectus

Restio erectus, commonly known as the upright restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in open, sunny environments. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening for its architectural form and drought tolerance.
