Rhynchospora oaxacana

Rhynchospora oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flower heads that are often inconspicuous.

Rhynchospora oaxacana plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
