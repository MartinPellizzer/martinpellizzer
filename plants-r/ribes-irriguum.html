Ribes irriguum

Ribes irriguum, commonly known as the Sierra gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of shrubs and vines commonly found in North America. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are clusters of white to pinkish blossoms.

Ribes irriguum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
