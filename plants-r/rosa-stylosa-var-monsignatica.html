Rosa stylosa var. monsignatica

Rosa stylosa var. monsignatica, commonly known as the Monsignac rose, is a climbing shrub native to southern China.

It features fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a notable crimson edge. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, red hips in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticultural collections.
