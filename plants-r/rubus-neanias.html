Rubus neanias

Rubus neanias, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically serrated and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils.

Rubus neanias plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
