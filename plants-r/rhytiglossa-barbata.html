Rhytiglossa barbata

Rhytiglossa barbata, commonly known as the barbed thistle, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround the flower head, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged texture.

Rhytiglossa barbata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
