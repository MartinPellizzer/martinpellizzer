Rabdosia umbrosa f. albescens

Rabdosia umbrosa f. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. This particular form of Rabdosia umbrosa is distinguished by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple or blue hues of the species. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
