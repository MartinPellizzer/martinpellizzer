Rhododendron caucasicum var. album-lilacinum

Rhododendron caucasicum var. album-lilacinum, commonly known as the Caucasian white-lilac rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to lilac in color. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas with acidic, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
