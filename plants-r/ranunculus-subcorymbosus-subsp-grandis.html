Ranunculus subcorymbosus subsp. grandis

Ranunculus subcorymbosus subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the large-corymbous buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, often arranged in a corymb-like cluster.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive, rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
