Rubus lindebergii f. typicus

Rubus lindebergii f. typicus, commonly known as the Lindberg's raspberry.

It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible red berries. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in the spring, followed by the development of its berries in late summer.

The species is relatively rare and is often found in rocky or forested habitats at higher elevations.
