Rhododendron kaempferi var. lusidusculum

Rhododendron kaempferi var. lusidusculum, commonly known as the silver-leaf rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which has a distinctive silvery-blue hue on the underside of the leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a valued addition to horticultural collections.
