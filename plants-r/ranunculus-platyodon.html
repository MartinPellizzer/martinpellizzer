Ranunculus platyodon

Ranunculus platyodon, commonly known as the flatleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ranunculus platyodon is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates.
