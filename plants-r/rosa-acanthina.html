Rosa acanthina

Rosa acanthina, commonly known as the thorned rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, while its fruits are aggregate fruits known as rosehips.

Rosa acanthina is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its historical use in herbal medicine.
