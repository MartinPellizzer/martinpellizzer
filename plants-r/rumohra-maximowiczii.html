Rumohra maximowiczii

Rumohra maximowiczii, commonly known as the giant horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, jointed stems and whorled arrangement of leaves, which are typically dark green and narrow. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stem, a feature typical of fern allies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with significant botanical interest.
