Rubia velutina

Rubia velutina, commonly known as velvet borage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in woodland edges or scrublands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
