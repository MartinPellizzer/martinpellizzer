Rhapis siamensis

Rhapis siamensis, commonly known as the Siam reed palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a clumping palm with slender, arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Rhapis siamensis is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
