Ribes uva-crispa subsp. glabra

Ribes uva-crispa subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth currant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright stems covered with smooth, glabrous bark, distinguishing it from other subspecies.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible red berries in late summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a glossy surface.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
