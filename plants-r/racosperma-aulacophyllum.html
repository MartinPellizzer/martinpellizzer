Racosperma aulacophyllum

Racosperma aulacophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
