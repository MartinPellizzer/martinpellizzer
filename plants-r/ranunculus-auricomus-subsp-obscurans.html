Ranunculus auricomus subsp. obscurans

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

obscurans, commonly known as the dark-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals, typically surrounded by a ring of sepals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker, more leathery leaves compared to the broader, lighter leaves of the typical form. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
