Rubus × largus

Rubus × largus, commonly known as the large-fruited blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically sweet and juicy. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and pinnate leaves, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its fruit is valued for culinary use and is also used in jams and wines.
