Rosa pomifera f. semproniana

Rosa pomifera f. semproniana, commonly known as the Sempronian rose, is a unique botanical variant of the pomiferous rose.

It is characterized by its large, edible fruits, which resemble a fuzzy, pinkish fruit with a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a fragrant scent.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses.
