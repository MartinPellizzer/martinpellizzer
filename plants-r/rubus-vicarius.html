Rubus vicarius

Rubus vicarius, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, palmate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are widely used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
