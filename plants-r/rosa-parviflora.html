Rosa parviflora

Rosa parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf garden rose, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive, flat bloom. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
