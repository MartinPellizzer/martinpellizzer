Rubus caesius f. vulgaris

Rubus caesius f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets that turn dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and used in culinary and medicinal applications.
