Rhus quartiniana var. acutifoliolata

Rhus quartiniana var. acutifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated, lance-shaped leaflets that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by red, fuzzy berries that persist into winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
