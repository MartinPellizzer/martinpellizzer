Rubus moluccanus var. hasskarlii

Rubus moluccanus var. hasskarlii, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible and have a tangy flavor, making the plant both ornamental and potentially useful in local cuisines.
