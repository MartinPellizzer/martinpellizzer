Rosa communis var. aurosica

Rosa communis var. aurosica, commonly known as the golden rose, is a cultivar prized for its vibrant, golden-yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorned stems and broad, dark green leaves. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand mild frosts.
