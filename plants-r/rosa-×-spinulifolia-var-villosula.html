Rosa × spinulifolia var. villosula

Rosa × spinulifolia var. villosula, commonly known as the woolly shrub rose, is a hybrid species characterized by its dense, thorny stems covered in a soft, woolly layer of hair.

It produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is well adapted to harsh, arid conditions. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered with a fine, silvery down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a landscape shrub.
