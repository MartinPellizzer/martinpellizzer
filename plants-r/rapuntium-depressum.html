Rapuntium depressum

Rapuntium depressum, commonly known as the broadleaf campion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of bright pink or red flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and early summer.

Rapuntium depressum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
