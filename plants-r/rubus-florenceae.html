Rubus florenceae

Rubus florenceae, commonly known as the Florence raspberry, is a species of shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in early summer.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are often used in local culinary traditions and have a tart, aromatic flavor.
