Rhipsalis brevibarbis

Rhipsalis brevibarbis, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a climbing cactus native to Brazil.

It features slender, wiry stems that often branch out into clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and holiday blooming cycle, it is a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
