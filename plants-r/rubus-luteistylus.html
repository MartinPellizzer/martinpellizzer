Rubus luteistylus

Rubus luteistylus, commonly known as the yellow-styled raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to New Zealand, characterized by its distinctive yellow stamens and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest margins and shrublands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
