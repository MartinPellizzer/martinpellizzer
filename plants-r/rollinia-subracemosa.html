Rollinia subracemosa

Rollinia subracemosa, commonly known as the red-berried tropical fruit tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green fruits that mature to a deep red color. The fruits are edible, with a sweet and tart flavor, and are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and fruit-bearing qualities.
