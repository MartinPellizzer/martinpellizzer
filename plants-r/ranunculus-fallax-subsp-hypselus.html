Ranunculus fallax subsp. hypselus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

hypselus, commonly known as the tall meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to central and eastern Europe. It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy, with a distinctive yellow flower that has five petals and numerous stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy, cup-shaped flowers that attract pollinators.

This subspecies is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
