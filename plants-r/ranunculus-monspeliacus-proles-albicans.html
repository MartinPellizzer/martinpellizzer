Ranunculus monspeliacus proles albicans

Ranunculus monspeliacus proles albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered meadow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, pale green leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces showy, bright white flowers with five petals, often adorned with a yellow center. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is commonly found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
