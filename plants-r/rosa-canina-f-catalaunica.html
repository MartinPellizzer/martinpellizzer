Rosa canina f. catalaunica

Rosa canina f. catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalaunian rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in herbal medicine.
