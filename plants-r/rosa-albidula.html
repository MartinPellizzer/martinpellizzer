Rosa albidula

Rosa albidula, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa albidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.
