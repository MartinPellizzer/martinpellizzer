Ranunculus eumorphus

Ranunculus eumorphus, commonly known as the European buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has palmate leaves that are divided into five to seven lobes, and its stems are hairy.

Ranunculus eumorphus is often found in meadows and along the edges of forests, where it thrives in cool, damp conditions.
