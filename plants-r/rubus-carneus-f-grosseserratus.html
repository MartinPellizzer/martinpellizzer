Rubus carneus f. grosseserratus

Rubus carneus f. grosseserratus, commonly known as the thick-leaved blackberry.

This plant is a shrub with woody stems and large, hairy leaves that are deeply lobed and serrated. The leaves are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy surface, and they are often described as being thick and fleshy. It produces clusters of small white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple or black berries in the summer.

The plant is native to parts of North America and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
