Ranunculus paralbertsonii

Ranunculus paralbertsonii, commonly known as the Albertson's buttercup, is a rare flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several states.
