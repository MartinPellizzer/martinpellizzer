Ranunculus palaestinus

Ranunculus palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb with smooth, hairy, or glandular stems that often grow from a short rhizome. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are palmately divided, with three to five leaflets that are usually lobed or deeply cut.

Ranunculus palaestinus is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.
