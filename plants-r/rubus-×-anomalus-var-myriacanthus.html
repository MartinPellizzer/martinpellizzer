Rubus × anomalus var. myriacanthus

Rubus × anomalus var. myriacanthus, commonly known as the many-thorned blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
