Robsonodendron eucleiforme

Robsonodendron eucleiforme, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the rainforests of South America.

It possesses small, clustered leaves and produces yellowish-green berries that are an important food source for certain bird species. The plant's roots penetrate the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients, while it also photosynthesizes to some extent. Its unique ecological role contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in some regions.
