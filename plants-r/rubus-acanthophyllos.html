Rubus acanthophyllos

Rubus acanthophyllos, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny environments.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and strawberries.
