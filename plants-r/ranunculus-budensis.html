Ranunculus budensis

Ranunculus budensis, commonly known as the Butea ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes various species of buttercups and other showy flowers. The plant is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are lobed and hairy.

Ranunculus budensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
