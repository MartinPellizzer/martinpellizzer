Rosa pseudoscabriuscula

Rosa pseudoscabriuscula, commonly known as the roughleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly in the western United States. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. It typically grows in open woodlands and rocky slopes, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

The fruit is a red, hairy rose hip that contains seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
