Rubus jacquemontii

Rubus jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
