Rondeletia microphylla var. lythroides

Rondeletia microphylla var. lythroides, commonly known as the small-leaved rondeletia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

This variety is often appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant.
