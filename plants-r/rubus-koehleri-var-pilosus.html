Rubus koehleri var. pilosus

Rubus koehleri var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a firm, slightly tart flavor. The plant features thorny stems and serrated leaves, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential use in horticulture or traditional medicine.
