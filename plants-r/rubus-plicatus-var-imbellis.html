Rubus plicatus var. imbellis

Rubus plicatus var. imbellis, commonly known as the soft-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and small, dark purple to black berries that are typically softer and less tart than those of other blackberry varieties. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

This variety is valued for its ease of cultivation and pleasant flavor profile.
