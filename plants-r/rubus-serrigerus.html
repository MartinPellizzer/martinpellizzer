Rubus serrigerus

Rubus serrigerus, commonly known as the prickly rose or brambles, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart and acidic. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it has a widespread distribution across the Northern Hemisphere.

Rubus serrigerus plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
