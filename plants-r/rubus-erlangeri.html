Rubus erlangeri

Rubus erlangeri, commonly known as the Erlanger blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, often forming dense thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
