Rubus glaucifolius var. ganderi

Rubus glaucifolius var. ganderi, commonly known as Gander's evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
