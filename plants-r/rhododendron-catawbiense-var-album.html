Rhododendron catawbiense var. album

Rhododendron catawbiense var. album, commonly known as the Catawba white rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the pale blossoms.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
