Rubus glandulosus var. rotundifolius

Rubus glandulosus var. rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaf bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its stout, thorny stems and compound leaves with rounded, lobed leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red, juicy berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in riparian habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
