Ranunculus polyanthus

Ranunculus polyanthus, commonly known as the garden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, and red. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early autumn, and its flowers are often used in floral arrangements. Ranunculus polyanthus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

It is also known for its ability to naturalize and spread in suitable conditions.
