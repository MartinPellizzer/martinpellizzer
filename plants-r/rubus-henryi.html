Rubus henryi

Rubus henryi, commonly known as Henry's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to China.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark red to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in woodland and rocky areas, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
