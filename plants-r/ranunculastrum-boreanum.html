Ranunculastrum boreanum

Ranunculastrum boreanum, commonly known as the Arctic buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow flowers and palmate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

Ranunculastrum boreanum is an important component of Arctic ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and providing nectar for pollinators.
