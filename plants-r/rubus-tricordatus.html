Rubus tricordatus

Rubus tricordatus, commonly known as the three-ribbed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which have three distinct leaflets with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and can be found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
