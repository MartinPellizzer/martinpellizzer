Rubus divaricatus

Rubus divaricatus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries in the summer. Its berries are typically dark purple to black when mature and are often used in jams and pies.

The trailing blackberry is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
