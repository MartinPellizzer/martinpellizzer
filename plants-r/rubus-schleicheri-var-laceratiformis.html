Rubus schleicheri var. laceratiformis

Rubus schleicheri var. laceratiformis, commonly known as the fringed-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
