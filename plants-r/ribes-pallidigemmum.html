Ribes pallidigemmum

Ribes pallidigemmum, commonly known as the pale-stem gooseberry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, cool environments such as forests and streamside habitats. The plant features slender, pale-green stems that are often covered with fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible berries, which are an important food source for wildlife.
