Roubieva anthelmintica

Roubieva anthelmintica, commonly known as the African wormwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its anthelmintic effects, which help in expelling parasitic worms. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Its chemical composition includes various sesquiterpene lactones, contributing to its pharmacological significance.
