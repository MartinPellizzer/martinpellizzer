Rosa pimpinellifolia f. sirei

Rosa pimpinellifolia f. sirei, commonly known as the Sirens rose, is a rare and ornamental cultivar of the rock rose.

It is characterized by its fragrant, single pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a compact shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its unique floral scent and historical association with the sirens have made it a prized addition to botanical collections.
