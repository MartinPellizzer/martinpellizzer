Rubus opiparus

Rubus opiparus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub or brambles, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. Rubus opiparus thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

It is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas, and is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative growth.
