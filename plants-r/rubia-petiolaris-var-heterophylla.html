Rubia petiolaris var. heterophylla

Rubia petiolaris var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable petioled gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in racemose inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
