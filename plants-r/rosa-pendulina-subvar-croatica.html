Rosa pendulina subvar. croatica

Rosa pendulina subvar. croatica, commonly known as the Croatian climbing rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnate, with serrated edges and a glossy green color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and horticultural gardens.
