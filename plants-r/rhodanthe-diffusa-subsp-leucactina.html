Rhodanthe diffusa subsp. leucactina

Rhodanthe diffusa subsp.

leucactina, commonly known as the white-flowered swamp everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
