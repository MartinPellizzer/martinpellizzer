Rhododendron anthopogon var. album

Rhododendron anthopogon var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
