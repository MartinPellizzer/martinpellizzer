Rhynchosia quadricallosa

Rhynchosia quadricallosa, commonly known as the four-lobed cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain small, round seeds, which are used as a food source in some regions. It has a climbing habit and is often found in disturbed habitats such as fields and along roadsides.

Rhynchosia quadricallosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
