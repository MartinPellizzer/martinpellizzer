Rubus × erinaceiformis

Rubus × erinaceiformis, commonly known as the brambleberry or thimbleberry, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often yellow or orange in color, turning red as they ripen. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
