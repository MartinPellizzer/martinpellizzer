Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp. subthyrsiflorus

Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp.

subthyrsiflorus, commonly known as the purple-leaf bramble, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dark purple leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically small and tart. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
