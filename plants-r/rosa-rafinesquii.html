Rosa rafinesquii

Rosa rafinesquii, commonly known as the Florida rose, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, spiny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of swamps, adapting well to moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
