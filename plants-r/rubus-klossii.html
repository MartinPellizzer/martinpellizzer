Rubus klossii

Rubus klossii, commonly known as Kloss's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Rubus klossii is considered a rare and endangered species.
