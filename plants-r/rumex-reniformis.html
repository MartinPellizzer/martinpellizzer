Rumex reniformis

Rumex reniformis, commonly known as the broadleaf sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are often reddish underneath, and its clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which gives them a tart flavor and makes them useful in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
