Rubus nemorosus var. ferox

Rubus nemorosus var. ferox, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bush, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, and is known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through vegetative growth.

The berries of this variety are particularly valued for their flavor and nutritional content.
