Ranunculus distentilobus

Ranunculus distentilobus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with broad, lobed segments and yellow flowers with five to nine petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy, buttercup-like flowers.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, though its sap can be toxic.
