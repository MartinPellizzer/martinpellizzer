Ranunculus procerus

Ranunculus procerus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and vibrant yellow flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus procerus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
