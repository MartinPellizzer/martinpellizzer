Ranunculus plesiolobus

Ranunculus plesiolobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features compound leaves with finely divided lobes, giving it a lacy appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus plesiolobus is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its early spring blooming habit.
