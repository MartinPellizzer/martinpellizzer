Rhamnus anatolica

Rhamnus anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, temperate climates.

Rhamnus anatolica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
