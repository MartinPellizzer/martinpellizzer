Rhododendron kingianum

Rhododendron kingianum, commonly known as the King's rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pink, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant produces leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green and densely arranged along the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Rhododendron kingianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
