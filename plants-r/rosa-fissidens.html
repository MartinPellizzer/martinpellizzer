Rosa fissidens

Rosa fissidens, commonly known as the fissured rose, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a high degree of ornamental value.

Rosa fissidens is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
