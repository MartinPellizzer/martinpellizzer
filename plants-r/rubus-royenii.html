Rubus royenii

Rubus royenii, commonly known as the royal blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its dense foliage and showy flowers.
