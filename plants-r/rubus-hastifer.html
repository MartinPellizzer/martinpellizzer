Rubus hastifer

Rubus hastifer, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy foliage and its role in supporting local wildlife.
