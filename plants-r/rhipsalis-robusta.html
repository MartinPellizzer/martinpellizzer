Rhipsalis robusta

Rhipsalis robusta, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, waxy leaves. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Rhipsalis robusta is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
