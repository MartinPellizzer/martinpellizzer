Rumex integrifolia

Rumex integrifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved dock, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are smooth along the edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and the plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Rumex integrifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
