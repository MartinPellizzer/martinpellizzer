Rubus × praevenustus

Rubus × praevenustus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in woodland and moist environments.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, Rubus hirtellus and Rubus argutus.
