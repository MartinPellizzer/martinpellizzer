Rosa sanguinea

Rosa sanguinea, commonly known as the blood rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its deep red, semi-double flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Rosa sanguinea produces fragrant blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
