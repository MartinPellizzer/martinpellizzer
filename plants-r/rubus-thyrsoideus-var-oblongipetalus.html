Rubus thyrsoideus var. oblongipetalus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. oblongipetalus, commonly known as the broad-petaled blackberry.

This variant is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible blackberries. The plant thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges or thickets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
