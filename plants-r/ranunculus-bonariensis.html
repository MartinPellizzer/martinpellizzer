Ranunculus bonariensis

Ranunculus bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink, typically with 5 to 15 petals. The plant has alternate, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ranunculus bonariensis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
