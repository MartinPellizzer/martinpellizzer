Racosperma truncatum

Racosperma truncatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and have a distinctive truncate or notched tip. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in spherical or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.
