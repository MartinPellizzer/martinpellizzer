Rumex kaschgaricus

Rumex kaschgaricus, commonly known as the Karakorum sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Its leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor, making it a traditional food source in some regions.
