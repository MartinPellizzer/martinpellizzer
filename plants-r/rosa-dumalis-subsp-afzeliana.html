Rosa dumalis subsp. afzeliana

Rosa dumalis subsp.

afzeliana, commonly known as the Afzelia rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
