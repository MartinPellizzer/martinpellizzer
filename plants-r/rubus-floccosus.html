Rubus floccosus

Rubus floccosus, commonly known as the whitebillberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, often found in open woodlands and grassy areas. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a hairy texture that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The fruit, a small, red berry, is edible but typically not cultivated for commercial use.
