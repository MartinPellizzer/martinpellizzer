Rosa × calendarum var. multiplex-alba

Rosa × calendarum var. multiplex-alba, commonly known as the white multibract rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its ornamental value and unique botanical features.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with multiple stems and a spreading habit. The plant produces large, showy white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnate, consisting of 5 to 7 oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Rosa × calendarum var. multiplex-alba is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively disease-resistant compared to other rose varieties.
