Ranunculus gelidus

Ranunculus gelidus, commonly known as the glacier buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and occasionally pink. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

Ranunculus gelidus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
