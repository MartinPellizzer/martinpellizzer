Ramatuela latifolia

Ramatuela latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ramatuela, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Ramatuela latifolia is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
