Rhynchosia balansae

Rhynchosia balansae, commonly known as the Balansae cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Rhynchosia balansae is valued for its nutritional content and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both agricultural and ornamental purposes.
