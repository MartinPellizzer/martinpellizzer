Rubus arduennensis var. vestii

Rubus arduennensis var. vestii, commonly known as the Arduennian bramble, is a shrub native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is known for its hardy nature.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
