Racosperma acanthocladum

Racosperma acanthocladum, commonly known as the mountain wattle, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in sclerophyllous vegetation. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Racosperma acanthocladum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
