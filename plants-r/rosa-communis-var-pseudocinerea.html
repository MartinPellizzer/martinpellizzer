Rosa communis var. pseudocinerea

Rosa communis var. pseudocinerea, commonly known as the silver-leaf rose, is a cultivar characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its unique leaf coloration and blooming habit make it a popular choice for both landscape design and cut flower arrangements.
