Rubus cruentiflorus

Rubus cruentiflorus, commonly known as the bloodyflower raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, hairy fruits in the summer. Its botanical features include a woody shrub structure and a preference for moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
