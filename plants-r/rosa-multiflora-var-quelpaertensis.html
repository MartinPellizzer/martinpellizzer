Rosa multiflora var. quelpaertensis

Rosa multiflora var. quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Korean rock rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a popular choice in horticulture.
