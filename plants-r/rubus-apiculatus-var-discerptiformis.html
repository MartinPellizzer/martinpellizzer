Rubus apiculatus var. discerptiformis

Rubus apiculatus var. discerptiformis, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
