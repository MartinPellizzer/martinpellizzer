Ranunculus subsimilis

Ranunculus subsimilis, commonly known as the similar buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves and produces flowers with five to ten petals, often in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered an invasive species in some regions.
