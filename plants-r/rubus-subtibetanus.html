Rubus subtibetanus

Rubus subtibetanus, commonly known as the Tibetan raspberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing or upright growth habit. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges.

Rubus subtibetanus is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
