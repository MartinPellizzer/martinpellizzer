Rosa stupenda

Rosa stupenda, commonly known as the "stupendous rose," is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorns. Rosa stupenda thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
