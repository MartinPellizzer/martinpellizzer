Rubus schleicheri subsp. irrufatus

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

irrufatus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
