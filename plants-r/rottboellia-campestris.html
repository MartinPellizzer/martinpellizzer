Rottboellia campestris

Rottboellia campestris, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in many parts of the world.
