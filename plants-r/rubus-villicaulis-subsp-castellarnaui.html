Rubus villicaulis subsp. castellarnaui

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

castellarnaui, commonly known as the Castellana raspberry, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink to white, and the fruits are small, red, and edible.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
