Rosa elymaitica var. typica

Rosa elymaitica var. typica, commonly known as the Elymaian rose, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
