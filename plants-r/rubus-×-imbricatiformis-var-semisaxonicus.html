Rubus × imbricatiformis var. semisaxonicus

Rubus × imbricatiformis var. semisaxonicus, commonly known as the saxonic blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
