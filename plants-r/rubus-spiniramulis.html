Rubus spiniramulis

Rubus spiniramulis, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white and clustered in umbels.

This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.
