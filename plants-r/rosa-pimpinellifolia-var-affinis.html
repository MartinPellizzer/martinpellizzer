Rosa pimpinellifolia var. affinis

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. affinis, commonly known as the creeping rose or the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets, and the plant is adapted to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. affinis is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it contributes to the biodiversity of mountainous ecosystems.
