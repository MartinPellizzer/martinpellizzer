Rindera corymbulosa

Rindera corymbulosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rindera corymbulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
