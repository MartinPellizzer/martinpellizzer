Rosa gallica proles provincialis

Rosa gallica proles provincialis, commonly known as the Provencal Gallica rose, is a historic cultivar originating from the Provence region of France.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to temperate climates and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its historical significance in perfumery and as a parent plant in many modern rose cultivars.
