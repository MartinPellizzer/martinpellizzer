Rubus celtidifolius

Rubus celtidifolius, commonly known as the Scottish blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, known for its ecological and horticultural significance.
