Roegneria melanthera var. tahopaica

Roegneria melanthera var. tahopaica, commonly known as the Tahopea grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are often tinged with a reddish or brownish hue. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
