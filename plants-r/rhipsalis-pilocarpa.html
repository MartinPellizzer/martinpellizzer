Rhipsalis pilocarpa

Rhipsalis pilocarpa, commonly known as the white-leafed mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its soft, leaf-like stems that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Unlike typical cacti, it lacks spines and instead relies on its dense foliage for photosynthesis. The plant is often found growing on trees in humid, shaded environments.

Its flowers, which are typically white or pale green, bloom during the rainy season and are pollinated by various insects.
