Rubus pseudincisior

Rubus pseudincisior, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
