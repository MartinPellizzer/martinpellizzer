Rubus flexuosus var. rubicundus

Rubus flexuosus var. rubicundus, commonly known as the red-veined blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are often streaked with red veins. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or astringent flavor.
