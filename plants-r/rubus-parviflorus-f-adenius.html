Rubus parviflorus f. adenius

Rubus parviflorus f. adenius, commonly known as the腺叶黑莓, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It is native to regions of North America and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its foliage features glandular dots, which give it its scientific name, and it has a compact growth habit.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
