Rubus arabicus var. schimperi

Rubus arabicus var. schimperi, commonly known as the Arabian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

The botanical features of this species make it an important plant in local ecosystems and traditional agriculture.
