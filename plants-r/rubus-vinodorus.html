Rubus vinodorus

Rubus vinodorus, commonly known as the purple-blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is often used in jams and preserves due to its tart flavor.

The botanical features of Rubus vinodorus make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
