Rubus debeauxii

Rubus debeauxii, commonly known as the DeBeaux blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
