Rhabdocaulon strictum

Rhabdocaulon strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf rhabdocaulon, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in open woodlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhabdocaulon strictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
