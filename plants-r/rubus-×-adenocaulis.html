Rubus × adenocaulis

Rubus × adenocaulis, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit, which helps it thrive in high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems that provide structural support.

This species is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
