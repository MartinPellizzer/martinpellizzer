Restrepia maculata subsp. robledorum

Restrepia maculata subsp.

robledorum, commonly known as the Robledor orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that produce multiple stems and leaves.

The plant produces inflorescences bearing showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves and flowers that are typically yellow with purple or red markings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
