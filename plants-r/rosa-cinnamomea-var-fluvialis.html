Rosa cinnamomea var. fluvialis

Rosa cinnamomea var. fluvialis, commonly known as the riverbank rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
