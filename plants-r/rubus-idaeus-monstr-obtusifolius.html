Rubus idaeus monstr. obtusifolius

Rubus idaeus monstr.

obtusifolius, commonly known as the blunt-leaf raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry species characterized by its distinctive, broad, and rounded leaves. It is a deciduous shrub that produces aggregate fruits, which are typically red and juicy, with a sweet-tart flavor.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries. Its botanical features include thorny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and a flowering period in late spring.

This cultivar is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
