Rosa villosa f. gaudinii

Rosa villosa f. gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's hairy rose, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a hybrid form of Rosa villosa, characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight. Its botanical features include thorny stems, compound leaves, and showy blooms that attract pollinators.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in garden settings.
