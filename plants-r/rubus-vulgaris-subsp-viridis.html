Rubus vulgaris subsp. viridis

Rubus vulgaris subsp.

viridis, commonly known as the green bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves that are usually a vibrant green.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, hairy berries in the autumn. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
