Rosa primula

Rosa primula, commonly known as the English rose or garden rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Rosa primula is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
