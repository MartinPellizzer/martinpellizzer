Rauvolfia littoralis

Rauvolfia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal rosy spurge, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, saline soils along shores and estuaries. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in mangrove ecosystems and is adapted to tolerate high salinity and tidal conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal habitats.
