Rosa amurensis

Rosa amurensis, commonly known as the Amur rose, is a species of wild rose native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has thorny stems and pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Rosa amurensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in horticulture.
