Rubus concolor

Rubus concolor, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. These berries are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

Botanically, Rubus concolor is part of the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
