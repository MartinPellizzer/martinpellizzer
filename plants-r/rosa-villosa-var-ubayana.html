Rosa villosa var. ubayana

Rosa villosa var. ubayana, commonly known as the Ubayana rose, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It features dense, thorny stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets that are oval and slightly serrated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
