Rauvolfia glabra

Rauvolfia glabra, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stem and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cymes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Rauvolfia glabra is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in traditional medicine.
