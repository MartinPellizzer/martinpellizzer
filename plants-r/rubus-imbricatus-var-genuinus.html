Rubus imbricatus var. genuinus

Rubus imbricatus var. genuinus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruits are dark purple to black when mature.

The species is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for wildlife and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian areas.
