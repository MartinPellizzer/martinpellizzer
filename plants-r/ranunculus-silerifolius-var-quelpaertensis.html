Ranunculus silerifolius var. quelpaertensis

Ranunculus silerifolius var. quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Korean buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
