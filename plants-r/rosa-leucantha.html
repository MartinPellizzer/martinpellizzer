Rosa leucantha

Rosa leucantha, commonly known as the white everglen rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their delicate appearance.

Rosa leucantha is also valued for its fragrance and is sometimes cultivated for its cut flowers.
