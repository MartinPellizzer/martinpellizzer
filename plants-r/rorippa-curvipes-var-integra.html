Rorippa curvipes var. integra

Rorippa curvipes var. integra, commonly known as the smooth watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, alternate leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots are adapted for water uptake, allowing it to thrive in moist or submerged soils.

This variant is distinguished by its smooth leaf surfaces, setting it apart from other varieties within the species.
