Rosa vosagiaca var. pilosula

Rosa vosagiaca var. pilosula, commonly known as the Pilosula rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a prominent central pistil. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
