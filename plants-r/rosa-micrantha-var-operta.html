Rosa micrantha var. operta

Rosa micrantha var. operta, commonly known as the Chinese rose or everblooming rose, is a species native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and a compact, bushy growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
