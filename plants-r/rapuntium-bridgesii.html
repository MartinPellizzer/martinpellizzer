Rapuntium bridgesii

Rapuntium bridgesii, commonly known as the bridge's rapunzel, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. The plant features erect, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Rapuntium bridgesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
