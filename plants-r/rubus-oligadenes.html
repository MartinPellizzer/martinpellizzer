Rubus oligadenes

Rubus oligadenes, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
