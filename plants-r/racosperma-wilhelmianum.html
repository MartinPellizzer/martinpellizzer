Racosperma wilhelmianum

Racosperma wilhelmianum, commonly known as the Wilhelmine acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in Indigenous Australian medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
