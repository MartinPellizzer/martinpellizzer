Rubus milliformis var. transiens

Rubus milliformis var. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, reddish stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated and covered in fine hairs.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
