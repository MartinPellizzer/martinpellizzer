Rubus aurora

Rubus aurora, commonly known as the dawn raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in late summer, typically a shade of red or orange. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are white with five petals and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
