Ranunculus kunmingensis var. hispidus

Ranunculus kunmingensis var. hispidus, commonly known as the hairy Chinese buttercup, is a perennial herb native to China.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem that distinguishes it from other ranunculus species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
