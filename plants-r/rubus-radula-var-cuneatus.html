Rubus radula var. cuneatus

Rubus radula var. cuneatus, commonly known as the slender bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry species found in North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
