Rhododendron × variolosum var. hybridogenum

Rhododendron × variolosum var. hybridogenum, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It typically blooms in late spring with flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the specific variety. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, where it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

The species is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to a variety of growing conditions.
