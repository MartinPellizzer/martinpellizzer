Reilia eryngioides

Reilia eryngioides, commonly known as the spiked everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Reilia eryngioides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
