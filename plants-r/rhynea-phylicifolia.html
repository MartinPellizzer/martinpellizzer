Rhynea phylicifolia

Rhynea phylicifolia, commonly known as the sweetleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain sticky, edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
