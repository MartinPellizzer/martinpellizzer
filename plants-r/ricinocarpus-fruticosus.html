Ricinocarpus fruticosus

Ricinocarpus fruticosus, commonly known as the broad-leafed acanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences of purple or pink flowers. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
