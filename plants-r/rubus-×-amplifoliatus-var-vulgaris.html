Rubus × amplifoliatus var. vulgaris

Rubus × amplifoliatus var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
