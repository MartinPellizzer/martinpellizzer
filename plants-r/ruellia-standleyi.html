Ruellia standleyi

Ruellia standleyi, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia standleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
