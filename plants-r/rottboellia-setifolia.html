Rottboellia setifolia

Rottboellia setifolia, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Rottboellia setifolia is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
