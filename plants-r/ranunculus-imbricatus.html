Ranunculus imbricatus

Ranunculus imbricatus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in moist, often wet soils.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
