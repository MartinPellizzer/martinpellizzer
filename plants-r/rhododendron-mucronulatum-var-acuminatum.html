Rhododendron mucronulatum var. acuminatum

Rhododendron mucronulatum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alpine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist environment.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine settings.
