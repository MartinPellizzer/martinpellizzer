Rosa coriifolia var. gothica

Rosa coriifolia var. gothica, commonly known as the Gothic rose, is a variation of the shrub rose characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often pendulous flowers.

This plant typically features thorned stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five to seven petals, and bloom in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in horticulture.
