Ranunculus bicolor

Ranunculus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive dark red or brown center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves divided into five to seven lobes, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, delicate blooms.
