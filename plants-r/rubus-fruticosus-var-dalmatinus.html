Rubus fruticosus var. dalmatinus

Rubus fruticosus var. dalmatinus, commonly known as the Dalmatian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and use in both ornamental and culinary applications.
