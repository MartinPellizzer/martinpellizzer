Rubus × dumalis f. ferocior

Rubus × dumalis f. ferocior, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a vigorous, thorny shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are typically large and flavorful. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has a strong, woody structure that supports its climbing and spreading growth habit. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, appearing in clusters and blooming in early summer.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and is often found in disturbed soils and along forest edges.
