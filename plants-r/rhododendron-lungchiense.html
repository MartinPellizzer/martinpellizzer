Rhododendron lungchiense

Rhododendron lungchiense, commonly known as the Yunnan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. It is often found in forest understories at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
