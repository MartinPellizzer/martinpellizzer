Rubus schmidelyanus

Rubus schmidelyanus, commonly known as Schmidel's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural settings.
