Ramtilla oleifera

Ramtilla oleifera, commonly known as the African oil bean tree, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its fruit, which contains a heavy, oily seed used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its economic value.

The botanical features of Ramtilla oleifera make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
