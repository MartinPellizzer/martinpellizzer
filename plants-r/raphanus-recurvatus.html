Raphanus recurvatus

Raphanus recurvatus, commonly known as the curly root radish, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its root system consists of a single, swollen, and often twisted taproot, which is the primary edible part.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.
