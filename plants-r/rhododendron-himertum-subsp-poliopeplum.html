Rhododendron himertum subsp. poliopeplum

Rhododendron himertum subsp.

poliopeplum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a subshrub native to the eastern Himalayas. It typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, thriving in cool, moist, and shaded environments.

The plant features lance-shaped, leathery leaves with a glossy surface, and its flowers are usually deep pink to purple, appearing in clustered inflorescences. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy blooms.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
