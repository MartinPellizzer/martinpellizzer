Rubus × hiraseanus

Rubus × hiraseanus, commonly known as the Hirasawa raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, native to Japan.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible berries that ripen to a deep red color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses in local cuisines.
