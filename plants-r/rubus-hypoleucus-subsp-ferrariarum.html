Rubus hypoleucus subsp. ferrariarum

Rubus hypoleucus subsp.

ferrariarum, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces clusters of red, aggregate fruits that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
