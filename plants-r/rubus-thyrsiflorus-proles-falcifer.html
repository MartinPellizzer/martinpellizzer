Rubus thyrsiflorus proles falcifer

Rubus thyrsiflorus proles falcifer, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry species, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. Its leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
