Ranunculus abstrusus

Ranunculus abstrusus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of finely divided, serrated leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in meadows and alpine tundra.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
