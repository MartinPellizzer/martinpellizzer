Rosa virginiana var. lamprophylla

Rosa virginiana var. lamprophylla, commonly known as the shining bluemaiden, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven oval leaflets, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
