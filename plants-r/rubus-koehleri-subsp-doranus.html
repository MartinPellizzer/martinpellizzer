Rubus koehleri subsp. doranus

Rubus koehleri subsp.

doranus, commonly known as the Doran's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
