Richardia emetica

Richardia emetica, commonly known as the emetic richardia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellowish to brownish flower heads that are often mistaken for seeds. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

However, it is also noted for its toxic effects, as the name "emetica" refers to its emetic properties.
