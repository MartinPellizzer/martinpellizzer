Rubus acantholytus

Rubus acantholytus, commonly known as the thorned blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rubus genus in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, while edible, is often used in local cuisines and traditional herbal remedies.
