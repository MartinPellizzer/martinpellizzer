Rhodax polyanthus

Rhodax polyanthus, commonly known as the polyanthus rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in a variety of colors. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with flowers ranging from white to pink, red, and purple. Its leaves are usually dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape.

Rhodax polyanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant due to its hardiness and adaptability.
