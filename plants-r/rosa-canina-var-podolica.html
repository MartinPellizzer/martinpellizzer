Rosa canina var. podolica

Rosa canina var. podolica, commonly known as the Podolian rose, is a variant of the dog rose native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
