Ranunculus cantoniensis subsp. tachiroi

Ranunculus cantoniensis subsp.

tachiroi, commonly known as the Tachiroi buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central greenish area.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
