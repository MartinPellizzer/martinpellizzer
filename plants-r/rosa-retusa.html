Rosa retusa

Rosa retusa, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant native to China and other parts of East Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.

Rosa retusa is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
