Rosa × complicata f. genuina

Rosa × complicata f. genuina, commonly known as the genuine form of the double-flowered rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fully double blooms that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
