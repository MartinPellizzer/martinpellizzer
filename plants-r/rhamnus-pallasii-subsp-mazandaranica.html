Rhamnus pallasii subsp. mazandaranica

Rhamnus pallasii subsp.

mazandaranica, commonly known as the Mazandaran buckthorn, is a shrub native to the forests of the Caucasus and southwestern Asia. It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by small, oval berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
