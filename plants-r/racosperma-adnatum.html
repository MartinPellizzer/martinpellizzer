Racosperma adnatum

Racosperma adnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are typical of the genus Racosperma. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance through its deep root system.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in native landscapes.
