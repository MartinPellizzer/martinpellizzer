Rosa glauca f. wahlenbergii

Rosa glauca f. wahlenbergii, commonly known as the Wahlenberg's rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-blue underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
