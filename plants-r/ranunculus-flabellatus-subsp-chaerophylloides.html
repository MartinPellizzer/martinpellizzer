Ranunculus flabellatus subsp. chaerophylloides

Ranunculus flabellatus subsp.

chaerophylloides, commonly known as the golden shield-cup buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features distinctive bright yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble a shield.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy and covered in small glands. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
