Raphanus raphanistrum var. maritimus

Raphanus raphanistrum var. maritimus, commonly known as sea radish, is a coastal plant native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its fruits are elongated, pod-like structures containing several seeds.

This species is adapted to salty, sandy environments and is often found along shorelines.
