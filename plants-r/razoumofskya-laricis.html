Razoumofskya laricis

Razoumofskya laricis, commonly known as the larch tamarisk, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Tamaricaceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with dense, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, typical of the genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry landscapes.
