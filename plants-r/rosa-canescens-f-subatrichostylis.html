Rosa canescens f. subatrichostylis

Rosa canescens f. subatrichostylis, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa canescens species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and hairy stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
