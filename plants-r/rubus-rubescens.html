Rubus rubescens

Rubus rubescens, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. The berries are edible, though they have a tart flavor and are often used in jams and pies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
