Ranunculus porteri

Ranunculus porteri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow petals and a hairy, lobed leaf structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Ranunculus porteri is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
