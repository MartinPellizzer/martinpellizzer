Rosa tomentosa var. genuina

Rosa tomentosa var. genuina, commonly known as the wooly rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in spring. Its stems are often covered in a thick layer of silky hairs, contributing to its unique texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
