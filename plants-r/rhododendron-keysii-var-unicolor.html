Rhododendron keysii var. unicolor

Rhododendron keysii var. unicolor, commonly known as the single-colored rhododendron, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red, with a single coloration that sets it apart from other varieties. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of wildlife.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
