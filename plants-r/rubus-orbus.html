Rubus orbus

Rubus orbus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

The fruit, which ripens in summer, is often used in local cuisines and traditional remedies.
