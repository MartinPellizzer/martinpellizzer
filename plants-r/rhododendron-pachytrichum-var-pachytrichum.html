Rhododendron pachytrichum var. pachytrichum

Rhododendron pachytrichum var. pachytrichum, commonly known as the thick-haired rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
