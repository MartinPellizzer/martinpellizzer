Rhododendron niphargum

Rhododendron niphargum, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, adapted to withstand cold and harsh weather conditions.

Rhododendron niphargum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging mountainous habitats.
