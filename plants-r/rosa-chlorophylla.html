Rosa chlorophylla

Rosa chlorophylla, commonly known as the Emerald Rose, is a rare species characterized by its vibrant green foliage and unique floral structure.

Its leaves are broad and glossy, with a dense arrangement that enhances its photosynthetic efficiency. The flowers, which bloom in late spring, are typically pale pink with a subtle fragrance. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and scientific value.
