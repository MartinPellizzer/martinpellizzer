Rosa micrantha var. albigensis

Rosa micrantha var. albigensis, commonly known as the white-flowered shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white single flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems with thorns and dark green, trifoliate leaves. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
