Ranunculus abrotanifolius

Ranunculus abrotanifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight yellow petals and a central cluster of bright yellow stamens. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ranunculus abrotanifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland habitats.
