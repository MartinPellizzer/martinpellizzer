Restio egregius var. nutans

Restio egregius var. nutans, commonly known as the drooping restio, is a slender, grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or white, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens outside its native range.
