Rotula lycioides

Rotula lycioides, commonly known as the common sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy and well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically small, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Rotula lycioides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
