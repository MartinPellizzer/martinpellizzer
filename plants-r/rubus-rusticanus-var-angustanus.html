Rubus rusticanus var. angustanus

Rubus rusticanus var. angustanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and clustered in cymes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in woodland edges and moist soils.
