Rubus josephii

Rubus josephii, commonly known as Joseph's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
