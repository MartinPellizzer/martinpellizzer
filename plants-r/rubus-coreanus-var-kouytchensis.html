Rubus coreanus var. kouytchensis

Rubus coreanus var. kouytchensis, commonly known as the Korean raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
