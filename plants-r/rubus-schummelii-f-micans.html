Rubus schummelii f. micans

Rubus schummelii f. micans, commonly known as the micans raspberry, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
