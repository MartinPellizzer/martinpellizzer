Ranunculus elatior

Ranunculus elatior, commonly known as the tall buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals and a central yellow stamen. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with fern-like, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, often found in alpine and meadow habitats.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, daisy-like blooms.
