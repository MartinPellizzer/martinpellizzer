Restio spicigerus

Restio spicigerus, commonly known as the spike restio, is a grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typical of the genus Restio. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
