Robiquetia serpentina

Robiquetia serpentina, commonly known as the snake-root plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, serpentine-like roots and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
