Rosa banksiae var. lutea

Rosa banksiae var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow banksia rose, is a hybrid rose species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and slightly serrated, providing an attractive foliage contrast to the bright blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy, long-lasting flowers.
