Rapanea parvicarpa var. amossensis

Rapanea parvicarpa var. amossensis, commonly known as the Amoss's small-fruited rapanea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.
