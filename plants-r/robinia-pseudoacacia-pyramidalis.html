Robinia pseudoacacia pyramidalis

Robinia pseudoacacia pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal false acacia, is a cultivar of the black locust tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late spring, which are followed by long, flat pods. It is a hardy, fast-growing tree that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
