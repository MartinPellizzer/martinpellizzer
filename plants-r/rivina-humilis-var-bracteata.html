Rivina humilis var. bracteata

Rivina humilis var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate rivina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are a food source for various birds and small mammals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
