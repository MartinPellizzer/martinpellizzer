Rubus schlechtendalii var. anglicus

Rubus schlechtendalii var. anglicus, commonly known as the English blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typically with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a deep red or black color, often with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
