Restio asperus

Restio asperus, commonly known as the rough restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering stems can reach heights of up to 2 meters, bearing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Restio asperus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
