Ranunculus pubescens var. glabrescens

Ranunculus pubescens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous buttercup, is a variant of the hairy buttercup.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a hairy texture that distinguishes it from other varieties.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and preference for moist, well-drained soils.
