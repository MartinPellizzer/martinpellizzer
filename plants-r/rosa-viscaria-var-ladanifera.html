Rosa viscaria var. ladanifera

Rosa viscaria var. ladanifera, commonly known as the sweet-briar rose, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C and are often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and medicine make it a notable species in botanical gardens and landscapes.
