Rubus × retzii

Rubus × retzii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. It produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, Rubus rosaefolius and Rubus parvifolius, making it a notable example of natural hybridization.
