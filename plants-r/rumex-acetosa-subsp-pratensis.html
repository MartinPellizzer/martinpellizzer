Rumex acetosa subsp. pratensis

Rumex acetosa subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as sourgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and a reddish midrib, and it produces small, greenish flowers in spikes during the summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties, and the plant is also a food source for certain insects.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
