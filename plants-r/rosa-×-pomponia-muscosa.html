Rosa × pomponia muscosa

Rosa × pomponia muscosa, commonly known as the mossy pink rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its dense, moss-like growth of small, pink flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their unique appearance and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
