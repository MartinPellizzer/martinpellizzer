Racosperma eremophiloides

Racosperma eremophiloides, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its seeds are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices for food and medicine.
