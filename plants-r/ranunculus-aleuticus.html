Ranunculus aleuticus

Ranunculus aleuticus, commonly known as the Aleutian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the North Pacific.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds. Ranunculus aleuticus is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in areas with high humidity.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native environment.
