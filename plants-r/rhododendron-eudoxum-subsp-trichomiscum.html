Rhododendron eudoxum subsp. trichomiscum

Rhododendron eudoxum subsp.

trichomiscum, commonly known as the Trichomiscum rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in Nepal and Bhutan. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded forest environments.

The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its botanical characteristics include glandular hairs on the leaves and floral bracts, which are typical of the Rhododendron genus.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
