Rosa canina f. rohreri

Rosa canina f. rohreri, commonly known as the Rohreri rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces red, hairy fruits that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It is native to central and eastern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
