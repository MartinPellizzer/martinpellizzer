Reaumuria reflexa subsp. reflexa

Reaumuria reflexa subsp.

reflexa, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its grayish-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its succulent stems store water, enabling it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
