Rubus yabei var. marmoratus

Rubus yabei var. marmoratus, commonly known as the marbled raspberry, is a variant of the Japanese raspberry species.

It is characterized by its distinctive marbled pattern on the fruit's surface, which gives it a unique appearance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.
