Rubus tereticaulis subsp. hontensis

Rubus tereticaulis subsp.

hontensis, commonly known as the Honten's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
