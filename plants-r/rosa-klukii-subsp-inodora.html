Rosa klukii subsp. inodora

Rosa klukii subsp.

inodora, commonly known as the sweet-scented rose, is a subsp. of the Rosa klukii species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
