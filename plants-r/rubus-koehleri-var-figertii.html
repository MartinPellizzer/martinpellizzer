Rubus koehleri var. figertii

Rubus koehleri var. figertii, commonly known as the Sierra madre blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in dense, prickly hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter flavor.
