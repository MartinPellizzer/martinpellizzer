Ranunculus stabilis

Ranunculus stabilis, commonly known as the stable buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus stabilis is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
