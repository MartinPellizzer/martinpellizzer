Rubus × formanekii

Rubus × formanekii, commonly known as the Formanek hybrid raspberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus idaeus (red raspberry) and Rubus strumosus (black raspberry). This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate of small drupes that can range in color from red to black.

It is often cultivated for its flavorful berries and is valued in both ornamental and commercial horticulture.
