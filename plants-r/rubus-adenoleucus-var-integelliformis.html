Rubus adenoleucus var. integelliformis

Rubus adenoleucus var. integelliformis, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
