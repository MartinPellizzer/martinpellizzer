Racosperma Deanei Subsp. Paucijugum

Racosperma deanei subsp. paucijugum is a lesser-known member of the legume family found primarily in the arid regions of Australia. This subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf structure and small, clustered flowers that bloom during the dry season. It thrives in nutrient-poor soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. Despite its ecological importance, it remains under-researched and often overlooked in botanical studies. Understanding this plant offers insights into the resilience of flora in harsh environments.
