Rhus sericea

Rhus sericea, commonly known as silky sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and have a silvery-gray underside due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are attractive to birds. Silky sumac thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
