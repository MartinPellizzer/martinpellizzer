Rubus rivularis var. leptobelus

Rubus rivularis var. leptobelus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is often found in riparian zones and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
