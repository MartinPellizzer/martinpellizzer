Raphia kirkii var. longicarpa

Raphia kirkii var. longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited palm, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central Africa.

It is a large, robust palm characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces long, slender fruits that hang in clusters, containing a single large seed. Its botanical features include a fibrous trunk and a complex root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
