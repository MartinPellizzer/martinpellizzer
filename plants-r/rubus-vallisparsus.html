Rubus vallisparsus

Rubus vallisparsus, commonly known as the clustered blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
