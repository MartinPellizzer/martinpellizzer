Rhamnus grandifolia var. brachypus

Rhamnus grandifolia var. brachypus, commonly known as the dwarf yellow buckthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, thornless branches. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a glossy appearance. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters, followed by black, egg-shaped berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
