Ranunculus chius

Ranunculus chius, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high altitudes. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette. Ranunculus chius has a fibrous root system and produces small, green, oval fruits that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
