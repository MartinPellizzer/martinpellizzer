Rhus blodgettii

Rhus blodgettii, commonly known as Blodgett's sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other species like poison ivy and cashew. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. The fruit is a cluster of red, fuzzy drupes that mature in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.
