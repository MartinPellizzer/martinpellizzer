Rubus acanthoblepharon

Rubus acanthoblepharon, commonly known as the bristly blackberry.

It is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically serrated with a rough texture, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible but often have a tart flavor and are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
