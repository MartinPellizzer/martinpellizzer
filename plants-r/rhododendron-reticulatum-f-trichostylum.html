Rhododendron reticulatum f. trichostylum

Rhododendron reticulatum f. trichostylum, commonly known as the trichostylum form of the reticulate rhododendron, is a compact, broad-leaved evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a leathery texture and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "trichostylum.". It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant spring blooms and lush, evergreen foliage.
