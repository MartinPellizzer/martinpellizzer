Rosa corymbifera var. bicolor

Rosa corymbifera var. bicolor, commonly known as the twin-colored shrub rose, is a variant of the shrub rose native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bicolor flowers, which typically display a mix of white and pink hues. The plant features dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its showy blooms. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
