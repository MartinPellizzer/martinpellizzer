Rubus × vysokensis

Rubus × vysokensis, commonly known as the Siberian raspberry, is a hybrid species within the genus Rubus.

It is native to the cold regions of Eastern Europe and Siberia, where it thrives in temperate to subarctic climates. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small, red, and have a tart flavor. It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in local ecosystems.
