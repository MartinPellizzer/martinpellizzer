Rosa pomifera subvar. adenoclados

Rosa pomifera subvar. adenoclados, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a rare and highly prized ornamental plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces large, edible fruits known as "pomaces," which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
