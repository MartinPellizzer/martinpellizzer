Randia penduliflora

Randia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping inflorescences that bear small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Randia penduliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
