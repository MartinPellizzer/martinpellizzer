Rhynchosia reniformis

Rhynchosia reniformis, commonly known as the kidney bean vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds similar to common beans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional agriculture.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and nutritional purposes.
