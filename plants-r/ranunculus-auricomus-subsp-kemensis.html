Ranunculus auricomus subsp. kemensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

kemensis, commonly known as the Kemenskaya buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals, often adorned with a distinctive white or pale yellow midrib.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
