Rosa canina f. glaucoides

Rosa canina f. glaucoides, commonly known as the glaucous dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose characterized by its distinctive bluish-gray foliage.

This form exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to the typical Rosa canina, with smaller, more rounded leaves. The flowers are similar to those of the standard species, featuring five pink petals and a yellowish center. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland and scrubland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its unique coloration and hardy nature.
