Romanzoffia mendocina

Romanzoffia mendocina, commonly known as the Mendoza water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, often with a waxy surface that reflects sunlight. The plant produces showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for ornamental water gardens.
