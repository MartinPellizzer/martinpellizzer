Rubus erraticus

Rubus erraticus, commonly known as the erratic blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. These berries are edible but have a tart flavor, often used in jams and preserves.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
