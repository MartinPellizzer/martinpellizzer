Rubus thyrsoideus var. hylophilus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. hylophilus, commonly known as the silky blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture on both sides. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

The species is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in wetland habitats.
