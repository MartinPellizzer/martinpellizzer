Rubus corylifolius var. obumbratus

Rubus corylifolius var. obumbratus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry.

This variant is a shrub native to China, characterized by its dark, almost black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.
