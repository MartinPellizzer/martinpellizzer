Ranunculus subcordatus

Ranunculus subcordatus, commonly known as the subcordate buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a short stem and produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus subcordatus is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates.
