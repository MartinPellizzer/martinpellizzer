Restio tenuis

Restio tenuis, commonly known as the slender restia, is a member of the Restiaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, sandy soils near coastal areas or in marshy environments. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Restio tenuis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
