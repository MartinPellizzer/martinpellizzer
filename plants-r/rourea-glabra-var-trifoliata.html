Rourea glabra var. trifoliata

Rourea glabra var. trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf rourea, is a tropical shrub characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

The leaves are opposite, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. The plant is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
