Rubus glabriusculus

Rubus glabriusculus, commonly known as the smoothleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and thickets, preferring partial shade. Its flowers are white and clustered in the axils of the leaves, and the fruit is edible but often tart.

The species is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and blackberries.
