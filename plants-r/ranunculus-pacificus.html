Ranunculus pacificus

Ranunculus pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western coast of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic members.
