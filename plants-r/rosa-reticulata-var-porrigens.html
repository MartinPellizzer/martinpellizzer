Rosa reticulata var. porrigens

Rosa reticulata var. porrigens, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, ovate leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its continuous flowering and lush foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
