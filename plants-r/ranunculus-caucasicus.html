Ranunculus caucasicus

Ranunculus caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
