Ranunculus metlikaensis

Ranunculus metlikaensis, commonly known as the Metlika buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often bright-colored flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
