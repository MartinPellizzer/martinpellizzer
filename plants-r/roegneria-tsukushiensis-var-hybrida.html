Roegneria tsukushiensis var. hybrida

Roegneria tsukushiensis var. hybrida, commonly known as the Japanese meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear florets with pale green to golden hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
