Rosa eglanteria var. umbellata

Rosa eglanteria var. umbellata, commonly known as the bristly rose, is a thorny shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and is often found in moist, woodland areas. Its fruit is a reddish-pink hip that contains numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
