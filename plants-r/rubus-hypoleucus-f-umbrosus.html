Rubus hypoleucus f. umbrosus

Rubus hypoleucus f. umbrosus, commonly known as the dark raspberry or shadow raspberry, is a subspecies of the raspberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown stems and leaves with a glossy, almost velvety texture. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically less sweet and more tart compared to common raspberries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ecologically significant species within the Rubus genus.
