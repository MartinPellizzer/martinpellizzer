Ranunculus auricomus subsp. crassicaulis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features glossy, heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5-10 sepals that resemble petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaves and more robust growth compared to other members of the Ranunculus genus.
