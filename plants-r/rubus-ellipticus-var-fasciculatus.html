Rubus ellipticus var. fasciculatus

Rubus ellipticus var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically clustered in groups of three to five. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, erect hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant for both wildlife and human consumption.
