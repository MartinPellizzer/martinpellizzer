Rosa mathonnetii

Rosa mathonnetii, commonly known as the Mathonnet's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, particularly in areas like the Pyrenees and the Alps. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa mathonnetii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
