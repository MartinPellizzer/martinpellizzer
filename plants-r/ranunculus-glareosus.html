Ranunculus glareosus

Ranunculus glareosus, commonly known as the golden crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent, hairy sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

Ranunculus glareosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
