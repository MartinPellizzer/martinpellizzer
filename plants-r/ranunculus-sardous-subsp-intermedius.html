Ranunculus sardous subsp. intermedius

Ranunculus sardous subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the Sicilian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal leaves that are trifoliate or pinnate, with lobes that are often lobed or toothed.

The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers with five to nine petals, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is considered rare in the wild.
