Ranunculus diversifolius proles rhipiphyllus

Ranunculus diversifolius proles rhipiphyllus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are palmately divided with serrated margins. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens and a distinct central pistil. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
