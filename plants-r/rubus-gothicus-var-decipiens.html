Rubus gothicus var. decipiens

Rubus gothicus var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive raspberry, is a rare and intriguing plant species native to the northern regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and thorny stems. The plant produces small, red berries that are visually similar to common raspberries but are not typically edible. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in the ecological systems of its habitat.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
