Ranunculus phyteumifolius

Ranunculus phyteumifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to central and eastern Europe.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five sepals that resemble petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are usually narrow and elongated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of yellow to the forest floor.
