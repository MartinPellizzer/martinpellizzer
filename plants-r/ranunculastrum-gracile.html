Ranunculastrum gracile

Ranunculastrum gracile, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow flowers and deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has a compact growth habit with short stems and is often found in meadows and slopes at high elevations.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
