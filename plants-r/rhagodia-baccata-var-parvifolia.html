Rhagodia baccata var. parvifolia

Rhagodia baccata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-fruited rhagodia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native gardens.
