Rosa tomentosa subsp. antitraaenii

Rosa tomentosa subsp.

antitraaenii, commonly known as the Antitranii rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, elliptical leaves covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
