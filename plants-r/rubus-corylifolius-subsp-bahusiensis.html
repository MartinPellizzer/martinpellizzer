Rubus corylifolius subsp. bahusiensis

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

bahusiensis, commonly known as the Bahusian bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The subspecies is distinguished by its leaf morphology and habitat preferences compared to other Rubus species.
