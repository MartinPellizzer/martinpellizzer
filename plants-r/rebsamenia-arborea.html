Rebsamenia arborea

Rebsamenia arborea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small apples. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
