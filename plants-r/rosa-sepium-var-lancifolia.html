Rosa sepium var. lancifolia

Rosa sepium var. lancifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf rose, is a variant of the rose species characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its stems are covered in thorns, typical of many rose species, and it prefers well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
