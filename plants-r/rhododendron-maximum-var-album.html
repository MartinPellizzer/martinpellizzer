Rhododendron maximum var. album

Rhododendron maximum var. album, commonly known as the white rhododendron, is a variant of the great rhododendron native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing dense foliage throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
