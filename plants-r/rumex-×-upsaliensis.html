Rumex × upsaliensis

Rumex × upsaliensis, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex acetobulus (broadleaf sorrel). The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple coloration on the veins, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
