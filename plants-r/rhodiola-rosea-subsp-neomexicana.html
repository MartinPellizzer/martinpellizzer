Rhodiola rosea subsp. neomexicana

Rhodiola rosea subsp.

neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico rose root, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its fleshy, aromatic roots and its ability to thrive in high-altitude, arid environments.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh climates. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months.

Its botanical features make it a hardy species with potential medicinal properties.
