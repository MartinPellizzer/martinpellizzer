Rubus apiculatus var. cintranus

Rubus apiculatus var. cintranus, commonly known as the Cintranus blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
