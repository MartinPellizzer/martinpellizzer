Ranunculus filicaulis

Ranunculus filicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy center. The plant has palmate or lobed leaves that are often divided into several segments. It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.

Ranunculus filicaulis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
