Ranunculus flabellatus var. cinerascens

Ranunculus flabellatus var. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in rocky or clay soils.

The variety is distinguished by its pale, almost ash-colored stem, which gives it its specific epithet "cinerascens.".
