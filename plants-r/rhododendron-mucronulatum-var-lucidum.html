Rhododendron mucronulatum var. lucidum

Rhododendron mucronulatum var. lucidum, commonly known as the glossy alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in China, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in spring. It thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils and is often found in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
