Rubus longistachys

Rubus longistachys, commonly known as the long-stalked blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
