Rubus micans var. chloropsis

Rubus micans var. chloropsis, commonly known as the greenleaf raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive greenish foliage, which sets it apart from the typical red or green leaves of other Rubus species. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically reddish in color, though they may vary slightly in hue. The plant is native to certain regions of Asia and is often found in forested or shaded environments.

It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible berries.
