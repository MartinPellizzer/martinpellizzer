Rapanea mcphersonii

Rapanea mcphersonii, commonly known as the McPherson's waxleaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
