Rubus junceus

Rubus junceus, commonly known as the common blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of white flowers that develop into edible blackberries. The plant is a perennial shrub that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
