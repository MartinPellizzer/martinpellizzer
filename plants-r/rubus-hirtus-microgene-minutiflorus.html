Rubus hirtus microgene minutiflorus

Rubus hirtus microgene minutiflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, and they bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves.

It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
