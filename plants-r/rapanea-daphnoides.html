Rapanea daphnoides

Rapanea daphnoides, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen or mountain holly, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
