Rhaponticum berardioides

Rhaponticum berardioides, commonly known as the Siberian rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the cold regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and tall, reddish stems that bear yellow flowers in the summer. The plant is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in harsh, temperate climates. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
