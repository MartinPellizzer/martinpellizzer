Rodetia

Rodetia, commonly known as the "blueberry shrub," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that range in color from blue to red, depending on the species. Rodetia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious fruit.
