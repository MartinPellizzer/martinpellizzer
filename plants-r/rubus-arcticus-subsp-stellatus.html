Rubus arcticus subsp. stellatus

Rubus arcticus subsp.

stellatus, commonly known as the starfruit raspberry, is a low-growing shrub native to arctic and alpine regions. It produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a tangy flavor.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in cold climates and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowers are white with five petals and are often visited by pollinators such as bees.
