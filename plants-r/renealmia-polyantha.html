Renealmia polyantha

Renealmia polyantha, commonly known as the pink marsh orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots are adapted to thrive in aquatic environments.

Renealmia polyantha plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
