Ranunculus fallax subsp. luxuriosus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

luxuriosus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and vibrant yellow flowers with prominent, showy sepals.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators with its bright color.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
