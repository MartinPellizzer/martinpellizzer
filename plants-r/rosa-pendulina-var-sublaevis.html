Rosa pendulina var. sublaevis

Rosa pendulina var. sublaevis, commonly known as the silver-haired rose, is a cultivar characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers and silvery foliage.

It is a hybrid rose with a compact growth habit, often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal. The plant features small, fragrant blooms in shades of pink to white, typically appearing in late spring. Its leaves are finely cut and covered in a soft, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.
