Racosperma drummondii var. majus

Racosperma drummondii var. majus, commonly known as the large-flowered wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, alternate leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This variety is notable for its showy floral display and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
