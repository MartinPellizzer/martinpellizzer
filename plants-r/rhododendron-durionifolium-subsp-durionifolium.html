Rhododendron durionifolium subsp. durionifolium

Rhododendron durionifolium subsp.

durionifolium, commonly known as the Durionleaf Rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
