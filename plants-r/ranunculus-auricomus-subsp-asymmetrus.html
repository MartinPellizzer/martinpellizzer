Ranunculus auricomus subsp. asymmetrus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

asymmetrus, commonly known as the asymmetrical buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with distinctive, asymmetrical petals that give it its subspecies name.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
