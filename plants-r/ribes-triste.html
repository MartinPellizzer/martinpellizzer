Ribes triste

Ribes triste, commonly known as the weeping currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of small, dark red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, aromatic fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ribes triste is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and seasonal interest.
