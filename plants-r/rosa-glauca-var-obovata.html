Rosa glauca var. obovata

Rosa glauca var. obovata, commonly known as the bluish rose or blue rose, is a variant of the glaucous rose species.

It is characterized by its pale blue or bluish-white flowers, which are typically semi-double to fully double in form. The foliage is dark green with a bluish tinge, contributing to its unique appearance. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
