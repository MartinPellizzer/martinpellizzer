Rubus hirsutulus

Rubus hirsutulus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black when mature. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Rubus hirsutulus thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
