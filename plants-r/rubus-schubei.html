Rubus schubei

Rubus schubei, commonly known as the Schubei bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small white or pinkish flowers. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are typically red or black when mature.

Rubus schubei is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
