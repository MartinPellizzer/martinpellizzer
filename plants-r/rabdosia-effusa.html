Rabdosia effusa

Rabdosia effusa, commonly known as hairy rabbitbrush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Rabdosia effusa is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
