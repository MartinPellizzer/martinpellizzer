Ribes aureum f. leiobotrys

Ribes aureum f. leiobotrys, commonly known as the golden currant, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to gardens. The leaves are ovate and have a slightly serrated edge, with a glossy, dark green upper surface. It produces small, red berries in late summer, which are edible but often used for jams and jellies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
