Rajania spiculiflora

Rajania spiculiflora, commonly known as the spiky-flowered rajania, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, spiky inflorescences that are both ornamental and ecologically significant. Its leaves are typically broad and arrow-shaped, with a glossy surface that aids in photosynthesis.

Rajania spiculiflora is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
