Rosa pimpinellifolia var. mitis

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. mitis, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in white or pink hues. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are often followed by red, hairy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

The variety 'mitis' is noted for its delicate fragrance and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
