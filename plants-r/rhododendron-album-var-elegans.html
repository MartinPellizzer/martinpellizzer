Rhododendron album var. elegans

Rhododendron album var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant white rhododendron, is a flowering plant characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a compact, bushy form. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as azalea and blueberry.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
