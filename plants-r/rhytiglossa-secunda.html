Rhytiglossa secunda

Rhytiglossa secunda, commonly known as the red-tongued orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that display a unique red-and-white coloration, particularly on the lip of the blossom. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil to support its aerial roots. The plant's foliage consists of broad, fleshy leaves that help it store moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its exotic appearance.
