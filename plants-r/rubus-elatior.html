Rubus elatior

Rubus elatior, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are edible and have a tart flavor. Native to the western United States, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
