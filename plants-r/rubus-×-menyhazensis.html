Rubus × menyhazensis

Rubus × menyhazensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian blackberry, is a hybrid species native to Ethiopia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has thorny stems.

Due to its unique genetic makeup, it exhibits traits that make it an interesting subject for botanical research.
