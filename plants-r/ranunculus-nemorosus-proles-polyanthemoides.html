Ranunculus nemorosus proles polyanthemoides

Ranunculus nemorosus proles polyanthemoides, commonly known as the woodland buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a striking splash of color to its natural habitat.
