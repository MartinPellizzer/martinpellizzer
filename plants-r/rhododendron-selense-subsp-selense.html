Rhododendron selense subsp. selense

Rhododendron selense subsp.

selense, commonly known as the Selenskaya rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Russia and northern China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a glossy appearance.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
