Ribes vulgare var. hortense

Ribes vulgare var. hortense, commonly known as the garden gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange to red berries. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in culinary applications.
