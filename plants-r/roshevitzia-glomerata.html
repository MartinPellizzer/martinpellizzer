Roshevitzia glomerata

Roshevitzia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered roshevitzia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Roshevitziacae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow or orange blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.
