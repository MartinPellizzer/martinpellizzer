Rosa glauca subsp. dyringii

Rosa glauca subsp.

dyringii, commonly known as the Dyring's bluebelle, is a subspecies of the bluebelle rose native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers and finely cut, gray-green foliage.

The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits called hips, which are edible and contain seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
