Rabiea carolinensis

Rabiea carolinensis, commonly known as Carolina lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Rabiea carolinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for various insects and birds.
