Rhynchosia arvensis

Rhynchosia arvensis, commonly known as the field cowpea, is an annual legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces slender, pod-like fruits containing small, edible seeds. It is often found growing in disturbed soils and is used as a forage crop in some areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.
