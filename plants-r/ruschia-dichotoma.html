Ruschia dichotoma

Ruschia dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched Ruschia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, upright growth with dense clusters of narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Ruschia dichotoma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
