Rosa oederiana

Rosa oederiana, commonly known as the Oeder's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are composed of five petals and numerous stamens. Its fruit is a rounded, red hip that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Rosa oederiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
