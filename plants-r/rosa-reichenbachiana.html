Rosa reichenbachiana

Rosa reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Reichenbach rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and has a woody stem structure typical of many rose species. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.

Rosa reichenbachiana is also known for its adaptability to a variety of soil conditions and its ability to thrive in cooler climates.
