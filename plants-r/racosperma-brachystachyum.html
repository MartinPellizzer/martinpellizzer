Racosperma brachystachyum

Racosperma brachystachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark and produces small, pod-like fruits. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Racosperma brachystachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils.
