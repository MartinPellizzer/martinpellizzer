Rhanteriopsis bombycina

Rhanteriopsis bombycina, commonly known as the silky orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, pendulous flowers with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, and it is known for its ephemeral blooming period. Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected areas.
