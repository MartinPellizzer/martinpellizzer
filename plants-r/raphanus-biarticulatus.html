Raphanus biarticulatus

Raphanus biarticulatus, commonly known as the two-segmented radish, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and often have a peppery taste.

Raphanus biarticulatus is typically found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
