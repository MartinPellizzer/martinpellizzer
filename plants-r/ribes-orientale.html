Ribes orientale

Ribes orientale, commonly known as the Eastern Gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.

The species is also notable for its role in supporting local wildlife and its use in traditional medicine.
