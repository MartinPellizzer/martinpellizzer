Rubus macrostachys var. longistylus

Rubus macrostachys var. longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with prominent stamens that extend beyond the petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and edible fruits.
