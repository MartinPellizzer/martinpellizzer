Rosa glauca var. berniciensis

Rosa glauca var. berniciensis, commonly known as the Bernician bluebush rose, is a rare and ancient variety of rose native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrubs with pale blue to white flowers. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
