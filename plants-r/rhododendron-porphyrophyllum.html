Rhododendron porphyrophyllum

Rhododendron porphyrophyllum, commonly known as the purple-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking deep purple foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is a deciduous shrub that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it a notable ornamental plant in gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in temperate climates.
