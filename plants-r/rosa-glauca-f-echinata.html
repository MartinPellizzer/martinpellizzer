Rosa glauca f. echinata

Rosa glauca f. echinata, commonly known as the prickly bluish rose, is a unique horticultural form of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its dense, thorned stems and foliage, which give it a rugged appearance. The plant produces pale blue or lavender flowers, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its distinctive foliage and subtle floral display.
