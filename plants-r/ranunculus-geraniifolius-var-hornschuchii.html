Ranunculus geraniifolius var. hornschuchii

Ranunculus geraniifolius var. hornschuchii, commonly known as the Hornschuch's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early summer. The plant has palmately divided, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble those of geraniums. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
