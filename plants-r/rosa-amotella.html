Rosa amotella

Rosa amotella, commonly known as the Mexican rose, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and broad, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its flowers are followed by small, orange-red hips that contain seeds.

Rosa amotella is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
