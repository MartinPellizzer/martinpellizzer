Rubus thessalus

Rubus thessalus, commonly known as the Greek raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, red, edible fruits similar to raspberries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
