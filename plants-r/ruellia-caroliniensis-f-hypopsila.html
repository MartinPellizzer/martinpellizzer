Ruellia caroliniensis f. hypopsila

Ruellia caroliniensis f. hypopsila, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
