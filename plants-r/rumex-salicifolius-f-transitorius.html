Rumex salicifolius f. transitorius

Rumex salicifolius f. transitorius, commonly known as the transient dock, is a variant of the broad-leaved dock.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
