Rubia angustifolia var. requienii

Rubia angustifolia var. requienii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
