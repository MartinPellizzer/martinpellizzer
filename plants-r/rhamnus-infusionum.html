Rhamnus infusionum

Rhamnus infusionum, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its fruits are small, reddish berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Rhamnus infusionum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
