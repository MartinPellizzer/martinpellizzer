Remijia chelomaphylla

Remijia chelomaphylla, commonly known as the flameleaf plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
