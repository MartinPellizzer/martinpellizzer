Rubus columellaris var. columellaris

Rubus columellaris var. columellaris, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This species is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
