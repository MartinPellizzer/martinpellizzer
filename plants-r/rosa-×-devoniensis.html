Rosa × devoniensis

Rosa × devoniensis, commonly known as the Devonshire rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem covered in thorns and a root system that is both fibrous and extensive.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
