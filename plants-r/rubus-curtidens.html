Rubus curtidens

Rubus curtidens, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often at high altitudes. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

It plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing food for various wildlife species.
