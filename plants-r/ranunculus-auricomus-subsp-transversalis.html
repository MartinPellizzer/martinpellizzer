Ranunculus auricomus subsp. transversalis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

transversalis, commonly known as the transverse buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, delicate appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flower structure compared to the typical form of Ranunculus auricomus.
