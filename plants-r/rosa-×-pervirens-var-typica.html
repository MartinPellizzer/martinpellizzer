Rosa × pervirens var. typica

Rosa × pervirens var. typica, commonly known as the common rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural cultivation.
