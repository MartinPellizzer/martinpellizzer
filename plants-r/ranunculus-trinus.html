Ranunculus trinus

Ranunculus trinus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Ranunculus trinus is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to survive in harsh, high-altitude environments.
