Rubus macrostemon var. hamulosus

Rubus macrostemon var. hamulosus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
