Rivea nervosa

Rivea nervosa, commonly known as the velvet tangle vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Rivea nervosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
