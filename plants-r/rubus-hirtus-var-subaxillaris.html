Rubus hirtus var. subaxillaris

Rubus hirtus var. subaxillaris, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing numerous small seeds. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It has a rugged appearance with dense, hairy foliage that helps it retain moisture.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
