Rosa × thea

Rosa × thea, commonly known as the Tea Rose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The Tea Rose is also valued for its historical significance in perfumery and its use in traditional medicine.
