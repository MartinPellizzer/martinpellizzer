Rosa zuvandica

Rosa zuvandica, commonly known as the Zuvandica rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Tajikistan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

Rosa zuvandica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
