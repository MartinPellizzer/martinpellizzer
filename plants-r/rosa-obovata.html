Rosa obovata

Rosa obovata, commonly known as the ovalleaf rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa obovata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
