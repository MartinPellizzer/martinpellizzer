Rosa stylosa var. corymbosa

Rosa stylosa var. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring multiple stems with thorny branches. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in dense corymbs, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval leaflets, and it has a woody structure with persistent buds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
