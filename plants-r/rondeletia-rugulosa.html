Rondeletia rugulosa

Rondeletia rugulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf rondeletia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, dark green foliage with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are often covered in fine, short hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rondeletia rugulosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
