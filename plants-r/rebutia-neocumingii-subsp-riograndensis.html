Rebutia neocumingii subsp. riograndensis

Rebutia neocumingii subsp.

riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande Rebutia, is a succulent cactus native to Brazil. It features a compact, globular stem with short, cylindrical ribs and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months.

The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a desirable addition to both indoor and outdoor collections.
