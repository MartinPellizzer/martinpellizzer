Rubus × secophiloides

Rubus × secophiloides, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
