Reseda lutea var. crispa

Reseda lutea var. crispa, commonly known as the crinkled mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped, crinkled leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Reseda lutea var. crispa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in dye production.
