Rothmannia ravae

Rothmannia ravae, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a species of orchid native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Rothmannia ravae thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.
