Ranunculus flabelliformis

Ranunculus flabelliformis, commonly known as the fanleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive, fan-like appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a contrasting central blotch. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Ranunculus flabelliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
