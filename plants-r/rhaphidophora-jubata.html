Rhaphidophora jubata

Rhaphidophora jubata, commonly known as the velvet leaf arrowroot.

It is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its broad, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by triangular, edible tubers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible roots.

Rhaphidophora jubata is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other plants like taro and calla lilies.
