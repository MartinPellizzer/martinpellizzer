Rubus castranus

Rubus castranus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptic to ovate, with a smooth upper surface and hairy underside.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is dark purple to black and has a tart flavor.
