Rudbeckia hirta var. pulcherrima

Rudbeckia hirta var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the Brown-eyed Susan, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central brown or orange disk, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural habitats.
