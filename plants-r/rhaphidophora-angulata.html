Rhaphidophora angulata

Rhaphidophora angulata, commonly known as the Shingle Plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by distinctive, triangular berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Rhaphidophora angulata is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
