Rhododendron maximum var. roseum

Rhododendron maximum var. roseum, commonly known as the rose-flowered laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from pink to white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and remain green throughout the growing season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest understories.
