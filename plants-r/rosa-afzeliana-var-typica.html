Rosa afzeliana var. typica

Rosa afzeliana var. typica, commonly known as the African rose or the red rose of the Congo, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its botanical features include showy petals, a central cluster of stamens, and a prominent, five-lobed pistil.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
