Ranunculus praemorsus var. sibbaldioides

Ranunculus praemorsus var. sibbaldioides, commonly known as the Sibbald's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a short, stout rhizome and produces delicate, yellow flowers with five to nine petals. The leaves are palmately lobed, with a distinctive hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
