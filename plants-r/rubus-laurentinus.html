Rubus laurentinus

Rubus laurentinus, commonly known as the Laurentian raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are used in local cuisines and have historical significance in Indigenous cultures.
