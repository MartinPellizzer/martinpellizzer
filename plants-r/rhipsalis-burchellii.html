Rhipsalis burchellii

Rhipsalis burchellii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that produce clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Rhipsalis burchellii is also notable for its ability to bloom during the holiday season, hence its common name.
