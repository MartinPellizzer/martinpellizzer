Ranunculus × alopecuroides

Ranunculus × alopecuroides, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive central yellow stamen. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, and it often grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its hybrid nature, it may exhibit a variety of forms and is sometimes used in horticultural breeding programs.
