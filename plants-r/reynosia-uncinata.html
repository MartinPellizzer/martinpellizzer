Reynosia uncinata

Reynosia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked reynosia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
