Rosa agrestis subvar. albiflora

Rosa agrestis subvar. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered field rose, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features thorny stems and pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals, and are borne singly or in small clusters.

The hips, or fruit, are red and contain seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
