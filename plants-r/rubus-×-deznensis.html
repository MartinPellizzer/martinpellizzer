Rubus × deznensis

Rubus × deznensis, commonly known as the Andean blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has potential for cultivation as a food crop.
