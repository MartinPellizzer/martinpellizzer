Rubus × salaevensis

Rubus × salaevensis, commonly known as the Siberian blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Russia, where it thrives in temperate to subarctic conditions. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its shrub-like structure features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in local ecosystems.
