Raphanus pilosus

Raphanus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy radish, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like cabbage and mustard. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, with small white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its root system is typically short and thick, and it produces small, edible roots similar to those of the common radish.

Raphanus pilosus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
