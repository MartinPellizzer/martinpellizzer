Rubus fruticosus var. suberectus

Rubus fruticosus var. suberectus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry shrub, is a thorny, deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to spread through both vegetative propagation and seed.

The botanical structure includes a woody stem with persistent buds and a complex root system that aids in its survival in diverse environments.
