Roupala brachybotrys subsp. grossedentata

Roupala brachybotrys subsp.

grossedentata, commonly known as the broad-toothed roupala, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves with a distinctive broad-toothed margin.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark is thick and corky, providing some resistance to drought and insect damage.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
