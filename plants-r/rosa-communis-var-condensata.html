Rosa communis var. condensata

Rosa communis var. condensata, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves with a glossy surface and a strong, spicy aroma. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The flowers are typically used to make tea and are also valued for their role in floral arrangements.
