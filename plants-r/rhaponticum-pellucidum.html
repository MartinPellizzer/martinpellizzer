Rhaponticum pellucidum

Rhaponticum pellucidum, commonly known as the translucent hawk's-beak, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
