Rubus indicus

Rubus indicus, commonly known as the Indian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The botanical features of Rubus indicus make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
