Rubus pseudoacer var. variegatus

Rubus pseudoacer var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated blackberry shrub, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Asia. The plant produces small, edible berries that are similar in taste to regular blackberries but often have a more complex flavor profile. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated edge, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for both ornamental purposes and its culinary uses.
