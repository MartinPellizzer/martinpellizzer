Rottboellia gracilis

Rottboellia gracilis, commonly known as the slender rottbollia, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along waterways.

Rottboellia gracilis is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed.
