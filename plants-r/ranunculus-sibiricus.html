Ranunculus sibiricus

Ranunculus sibiricus, commonly known as the Siberian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas. The plant features a short, stout rhizome and produces several stems that are covered with fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow with five to nine petals, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, glossy appearance.
