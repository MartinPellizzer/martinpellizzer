Rubus ulmifolius microgene garbetinus

Rubus ulmifolius microgene garbetinus, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
