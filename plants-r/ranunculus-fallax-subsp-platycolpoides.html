Ranunculus fallax subsp. platycolpoides

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

platycolpoides, commonly known as the broad-celled buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals and a distinctive, broad-celled structure on its seed pods.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided with a waxy coating, and it produces small, greenish sepals beneath the petals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
