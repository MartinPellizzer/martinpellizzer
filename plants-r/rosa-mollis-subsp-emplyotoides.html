Rosa mollis subsp. emplyotoides

Rosa mollis subsp.

emplyotoides, commonly known as the Mexican climbing rose, is a shrub-like species native to Mexico. It features slender, thornless stems that often climb or trail along the ground.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with multiple leaflets, and it is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
