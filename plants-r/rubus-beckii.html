Rubus beckii

Rubus beckii, commonly known as the Beck's blackberry, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and wines.

Rubus beckii thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny environments.
