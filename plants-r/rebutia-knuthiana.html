Rebutia knuthiana

Rebutia knuthiana, commonly known as the Knuth's moon cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a low-growing, compact plant with a short, thick stem and a cluster of fleshy, ribbed leaves. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
