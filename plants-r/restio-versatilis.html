Restio versatilis

Restio versatilis, commonly known as the versatile restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological value.
