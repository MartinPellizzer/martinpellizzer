Rhododendron austrokiusianum

Rhododendron austrokiusianum, commonly known as the Austrokiusian rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partially shaded to shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a serrated margin, while its floral structure is typical of the Ericaceae family.

Rhododendron austrokiusianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
