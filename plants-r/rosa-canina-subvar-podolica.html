Rosa canina subvar. podolica

Rosa canina subvar. podolica, commonly known as the Podolian rose, is a subspecies native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces pink to red flowers in the spring, followed by bright orange to red hips in the fall. Its fruits are valued for their high vitamin C content and traditional medicinal uses.

The subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it resilient in harsh environments.
