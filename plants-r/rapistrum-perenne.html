Rapistrum perenne

Rapistrum perenne, commonly known as common salsify, is a perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by cylindrical, ribbed seed pods. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its leaves and roots have been historically used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable.
