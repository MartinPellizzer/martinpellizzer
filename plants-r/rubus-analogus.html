Rubus analogus

Rubus analogus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and its berries are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Rubus analogus plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
