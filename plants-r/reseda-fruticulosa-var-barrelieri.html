Reseda fruticulosa var. barrelieri

Reseda fruticulosa var. barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's madder, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its roots contain alkaloids and have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.
