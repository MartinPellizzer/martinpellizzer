Ranunculus × gilibertii

Ranunculus × gilibertii, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that typically range from yellow to red. The plant features a short, stout rhizome and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers consist of numerous petal-like sepals and are often borne in clusters at the top of the stem.

Ranunculus × gilibertii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
