Rubus pubescens subsp. amiantinus

Rubus pubescens subsp.

amiantinus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
