Rosa microcarpa

Rosa microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf pink, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color, containing the seeds. Rosa microcarpa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and seasonal blooms.
