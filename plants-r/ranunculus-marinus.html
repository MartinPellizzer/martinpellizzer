Ranunculus marinus

Ranunculus marinus, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-green leaves. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often growing in rocky or coastal habitats. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus marinus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with maritime conditions.
