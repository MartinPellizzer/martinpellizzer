Rapanea pallens

Rapanea pallens, commonly known as the pale rapanea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has simple, alternate leaves with a leathery texture. Its fruit is a small, dark berry that contains several seeds.

Rapanea pallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage and ecological benefits.
