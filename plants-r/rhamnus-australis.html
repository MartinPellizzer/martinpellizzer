Rhamnus australis

Rhamnus australis, commonly known as the Australian buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Rhamnus australis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
