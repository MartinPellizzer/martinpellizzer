Rosa systyla var. matraensis

Rosa systyla var. matraensis, commonly known as the Matraensis rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated leaflets, and the plant is known for its hardy nature.

Rosa systyla var. matraensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
