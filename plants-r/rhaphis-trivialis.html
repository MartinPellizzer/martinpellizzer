Rhaphis trivialis

Rhaphis trivialis, commonly known as the small-leaf rattan, is a climbing plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and pinnate leaves with small, narrow leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in spadices, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value and use in traditional crafts, it is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
