Ruschia uitenhagensis

Ruschia uitenhagensis, commonly known as the Cape gold plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is well-suited for xeriscaping and low-maintenance gardens.
