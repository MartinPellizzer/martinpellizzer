Rosa communis var. praeterita

Rosa communis var. praeterita, commonly known as the Gallica rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of many rose species. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
