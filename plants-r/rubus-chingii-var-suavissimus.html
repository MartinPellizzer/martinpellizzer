Rubus chingii var. suavissimus

Rubus chingii var. suavissimus, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, showy white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often described as having a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.

This variety is distinct for its delicate appearance and mild, pleasant taste.
