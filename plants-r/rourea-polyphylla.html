Rourea polyphylla

Rourea polyphylla, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has opposite, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Rourea polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
