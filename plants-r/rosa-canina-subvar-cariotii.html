Rosa canina subvar. cariotii

Rosa canina subvar. cariotii, commonly known as the Cariotii rose, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pale pink flowers. The plant produces red, hairy hips that are typically edible and rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
