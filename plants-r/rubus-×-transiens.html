Rubus × transiens

Rubus × transiens, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may vary in taste and size depending on the specific cultivar. It is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and small-scale fruit cultivation.
