Rubus schleicheri var. longipetiolatus

Rubus schleicheri var. longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which are the leaf stalks that connect the leaves to the stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with three leaflets that have serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for cultivation in subtropical climates.
