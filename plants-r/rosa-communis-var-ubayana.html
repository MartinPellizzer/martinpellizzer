Rosa communis var. ubayana

Rosa communis var. ubayana, commonly known as the Ubayana rose, is a cultivar prized for its vibrant, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its semi-double to fully double blooms in shades of pink and red. The plant is typically deciduous, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and pleasant aroma.
