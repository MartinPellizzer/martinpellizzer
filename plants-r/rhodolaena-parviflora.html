Rhodolaena parviflora

Rhodolaena parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered rhodolaena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in warm, temperate climates. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, oval-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Rhodolaena parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
