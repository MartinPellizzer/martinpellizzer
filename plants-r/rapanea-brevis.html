Rapanea brevis

Rapanea brevis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rapanea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
