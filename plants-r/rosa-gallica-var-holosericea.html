Rosa gallica var. holosericea

Rosa gallica var. holosericea, commonly known as the white gallica rose, is a variety of the gallica rose native to Europe.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in clusters and have a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and floral arrangements.
