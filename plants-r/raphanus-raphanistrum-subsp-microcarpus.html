Raphanus raphanistrum subsp. microcarpus

Raphanus raphanistrum subsp.

microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf wild radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its small, round, purple or greenish pods that contain several seeds.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and roadsides. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers that are attractive to pollinators.

This subspecies is considered a weed in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
