Racosperma mimicum var. angustum

Racosperma mimicum var. angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mimic acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.

The species is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
