Raveniopsis trifoliolata

Raveniopsis trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf raveniopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves, meaning each leaf is divided into three parts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
