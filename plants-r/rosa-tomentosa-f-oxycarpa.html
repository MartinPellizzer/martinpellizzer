Rosa tomentosa f. oxycarpa

Rosa tomentosa f. oxycarpa, commonly known as the hairy rose or woolly rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa tomentosa.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals, and bloom in summer. This plant is native to regions of Asia, including China and India, and thrives in temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
