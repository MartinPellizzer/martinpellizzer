Racosperma auriculiforme

Racosperma auriculiforme, commonly known as the ear-shaped acacia, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive yellow flowers arranged in globular heads. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its unique ear-like leaf shape and clustered flowers make it easily identifiable.

It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
