Richetia polyandra

Richetia polyandra, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for vine coverage in gardens and as a decorative plant.
