Rhus steudneri

Rhus steudneri, commonly known as the sand chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive grayish-green color. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, followed by smooth, spherical fruits that are typically red or orange. Rhus steudneri thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
