Rubus guentheri var. erythrostachys

Rubus guentheri var. erythrostachys, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which are often reddish in color, and its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubiaceae family, which includes other brambles and shrubs.
