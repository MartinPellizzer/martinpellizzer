Rubus muenteri var. glabratus

Rubus muenteri var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, distinguishing it from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
