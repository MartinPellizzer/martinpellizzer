Rhynchosia filiformis

Rhynchosia filiformis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant features slender, climbing stems and small, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces elongated, slender pods containing small, edible seeds.

Rhynchosia filiformis is often grown as a forage crop and has potential uses in sustainable agriculture.
