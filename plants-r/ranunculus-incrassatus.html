Ranunculus incrassatus

Ranunculus incrassatus, commonly known as the thick-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, ornamental plant.
