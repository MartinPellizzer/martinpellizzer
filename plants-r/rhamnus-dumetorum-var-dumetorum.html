Rhamnus dumetorum var. dumetorum

Rhamnus dumetorum var. dumetorum, commonly known as the common buckthorn, is a shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark red berry that contains seeds surrounded by a sticky, resinous substance.

This species is often invasive in non-native regions due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types.
