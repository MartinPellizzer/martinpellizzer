Rubus thyrsoideus var. affinis

Rubus thyrsoideus var. affinis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along rocky slopes.

The species is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
