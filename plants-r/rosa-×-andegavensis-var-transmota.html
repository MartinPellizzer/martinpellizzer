Rosa × andegavensis var. transmota

Rosa × andegavensis var. transmota, commonly known as the Transylvanian rose, is a hybrid species native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
