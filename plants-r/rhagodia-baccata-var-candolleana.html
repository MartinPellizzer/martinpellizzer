Rhagodia baccata var. candolleana

Rhagodia baccata var. candolleana, commonly known as the Candolleana thimbleberry, is a variant of the thimbleberry plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
