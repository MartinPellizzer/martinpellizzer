Rosa oxyodon var. adsharica

Rosa oxyodon var. adsharica, commonly known as the Kashmiri rose, is a botanical variant native to the high-altitude regions of Jammu and Kashmir.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
