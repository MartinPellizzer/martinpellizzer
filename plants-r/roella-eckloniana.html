Roella eckloniana

Roella eckloniana, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, bract-like structures beneath the flower heads.

Roella eckloniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
