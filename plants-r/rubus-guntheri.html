Rubus guntheri

Rubus guntheri, commonly known as the Gunther's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and riparian areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
