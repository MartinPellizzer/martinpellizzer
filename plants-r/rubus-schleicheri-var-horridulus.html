Rubus schleicheri var. horridulus

Rubus schleicheri var. horridulus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
