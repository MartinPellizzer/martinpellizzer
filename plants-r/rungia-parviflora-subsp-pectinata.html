Rungia parviflora subsp. pectinata

Rungia parviflora subsp.

pectinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rungia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens and greenhouses.
