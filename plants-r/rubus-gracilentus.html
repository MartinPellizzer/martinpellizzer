Rubus gracilentus

Rubus gracilentus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white with five petals and are borne in clusters.

Rubus gracilentus is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as streambanks and woodland edges.
