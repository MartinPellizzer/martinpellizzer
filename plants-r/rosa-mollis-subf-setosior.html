Rosa mollis subf.

setosior, commonly known as the hairy evergreen rose, is a subform of the species Rosa mollis. It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that cover the stems, leaves, and flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is native to parts of China and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. Its foliage is typically evergreen, with leathery, elliptical leaves that have a rough texture.

The flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.
