Ranunculus mazzettii

Ranunculus mazzettii, commonly known as the Mazzetti buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Italy.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features compound leaves with lobed segments and produces delicate, yellow-centered flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
