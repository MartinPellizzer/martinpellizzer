Rebutia nicolaii

Rebutia nicolaii, commonly known as the Nicolai's Rebutia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is a low-growing, globular plant with a short, thick stem that often branches into a bushy form. The plant produces clusters of small, vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Rebutia nicolaii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
