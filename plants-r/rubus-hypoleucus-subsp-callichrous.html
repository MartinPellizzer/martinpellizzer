Rubus hypoleucus subsp. callichrous

Rubus hypoleucus subsp.

callichrous, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red when mature. These fruits are edible and contain numerous tiny seeds.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavor and use in culinary applications.
