Ribes divaricatum var. pubiflorum

Ribes divaricatum var. pubiflorum, commonly known as the pubiflorous gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in late summer. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins, and its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
