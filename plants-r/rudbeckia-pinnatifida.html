Rudbeckia pinnatifida

Rudbeckia pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that produce daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and orange to red petals. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a finely toothed margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued in gardens for its vibrant fall blooms and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
