Rhododendron degronianum subsp. heptamerum

Rhododendron degronianum subsp.

heptamerum, commonly known as the Seven-merous Rhododendron, is a unique subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring, featuring multiple petals and a distinctive coloration.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
