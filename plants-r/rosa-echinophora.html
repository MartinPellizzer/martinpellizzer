Rosa echinophora

Rosa echinophora, commonly known as the thorny rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a hairy, red hip that contains several seeds.

Rosa echinophora is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
