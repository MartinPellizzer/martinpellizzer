Rubus × hoghizensis

Rubus × hoghizensis, commonly known as the Hoghizen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, known for its robust growth and adaptability to various climates.

It produces large, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal fruit production.

This cultivar is valued for its flavor and is often used in both commercial and home gardening settings.
