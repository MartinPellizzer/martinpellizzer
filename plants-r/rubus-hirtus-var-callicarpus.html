Rubus hirtus var. callicarpus

Rubus hirtus var. callicarpus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red color, often with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
