Rapanea retusa

Rapanea retusa, commonly known as the glossy evergreen or the sweet-scented evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The leaves are simple, alternate, and glossy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, spherical berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
