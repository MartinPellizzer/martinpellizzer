Rubus fruticosus f. affinis

Rubus fruticosus f. affinis, commonly known as the common bramble or blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit, when ripe, turns dark purple or black.

This species is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
