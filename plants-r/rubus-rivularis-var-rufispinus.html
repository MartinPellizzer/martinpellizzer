Rubus rivularis var. rufispinus

Rubus rivularis var. rufispinus, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its stems are characterized by their reddish coloration, which distinguishes it from other Rubus species.

The leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant is known for its thorny stems and adaptability to various soil types.
