Rupicapnos decipiens

Rupicapnos decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive beauty bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a reddish or bronze hue. Its flowering stems often bear clusters of white or pinkish flowers that are attractive to pollinators.

Rupicapnos decipiens is typically found in moist, acidic soils in woodland areas.
