Rubus vulgaris var. rhamnifolioides

Rubus vulgaris var. rhamnifolioides, commonly known as the bramble-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry that exhibits distinct leaf morphology.

It is characterized by its serrated, ovate leaves that are typically smaller and more densely arranged compared to the standard Rubus vulgaris. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variant is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
