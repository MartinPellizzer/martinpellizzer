Rubus alterniflorus var. velutinus

Rubus alterniflorus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, dark green leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible blackberries in the summer. Its fruits are typically small, firm, and have a tart flavor.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
