Rhynchosia dinteri

Rhynchosia dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's climbing bean, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a leguminous plant characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive red or purple calyx, and the pods are elongated and hairy. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna habitats.

This plant plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
