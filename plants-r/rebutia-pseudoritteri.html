Rebutia pseudoritteri

Rebutia pseudoritteri, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem covered in dense, woolly white spines that provide insulation and protection. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It is a succulent, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in dry conditions.

Rebutia pseudoritteri is well-suited for rock gardens and containers, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.
