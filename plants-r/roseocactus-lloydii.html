Roseocactus lloydii

Roseocactus lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's roseocactus, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
