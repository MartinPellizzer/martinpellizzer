Rytidosperma duttonianum

Rytidosperma duttonianum, commonly known as the Dutton's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Rytidosperma duttonianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
