Rubus pusillus

Rubus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a small shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thorny stems. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that develop into small, edible berries. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus pusillus is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
