Rosa hirtinormanniana

Rosa hirtinormanniana, commonly known as the Hirtinormanniana rose, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down.

This rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
