Rapanea membranifolia

Rapanea membranifolia, commonly known as the membranifolia ilex, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, dark blue or black berries.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
