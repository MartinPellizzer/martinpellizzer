Rubus nitidus var. hamulosus

Rubus nitidus var. hamulosus, commonly known as the silky blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of silvery-white hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are white to pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to blackberry, often with a hairy surface.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
