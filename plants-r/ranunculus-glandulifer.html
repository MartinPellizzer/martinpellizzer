Ranunculus glandulifer

Ranunculus glandulifer, commonly known as the glandular buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, with hairy stems and alternate, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces small, green, cup-shaped fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus glandulifer is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and streambanks.
