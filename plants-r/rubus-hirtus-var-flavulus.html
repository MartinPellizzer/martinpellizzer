Rubus hirtus var. flavulus

Rubus hirtus var. flavulus, commonly known as the yellow-tailed raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, thorned stems and small, aggregate fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces white flowers with five petals, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique fruit color and fragrance.
