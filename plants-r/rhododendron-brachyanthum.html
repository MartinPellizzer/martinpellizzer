Rhododendron brachyanthum

Rhododendron brachyanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron brachyanthum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
