Ranunculus acris subsp. nipponicus

Ranunculus acris subsp.

nipponicus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
