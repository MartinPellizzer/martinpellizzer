Roupala Montana Var. Dentata

Roupala montana var. dentata is a lesser-known but fascinating plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. This variant of Roupala montana is distinguished by its unique toothed leaves, which set it apart from other members of the genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous forests where humidity is high. Despite its ecological importance, it remains under-researched and relatively obscure in botanical literature. Its beauty and ecological role make it a compelling subject for further study and conservation efforts.
