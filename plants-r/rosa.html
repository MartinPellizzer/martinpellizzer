Rosa

Rosa, commonly known as the rose, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its fragrant flowers, which typically have five petals and numerous stamens. The plant is deciduous or evergreen, depending on the species, and produces thorny stems. Rosa species are widely cultivated for their ornamental value and are also used in perfumery and medicine.

The genus includes over 100 species, many of which are popular in gardens and floral arrangements.
