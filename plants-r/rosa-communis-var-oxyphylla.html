Rosa communis var. oxyphylla

Rosa communis var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental gardening.

Its historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
