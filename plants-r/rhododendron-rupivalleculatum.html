Rhododendron rupivalleculatum

Rhododendron rupivalleculatum, commonly known as the valley cliff rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often along rocky slopes or in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from pink to purple.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
