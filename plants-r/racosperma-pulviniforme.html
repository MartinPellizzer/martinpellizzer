Racosperma pulviniforme

Racosperma pulviniforme, commonly known as the pulvinate acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a distinctive pulvinate (swollen) base on its stems, which is a unique botanical feature. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

Racosperma pulviniforme is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
