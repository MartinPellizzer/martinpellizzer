Rosa spinosissima var. islandica

Rosa spinosissima var. islandica, commonly known as the Iceland rose, is a hardy shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cold climates and is often found growing in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
