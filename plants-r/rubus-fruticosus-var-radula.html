Rubus fruticosus var. radula

Rubus fruticosus var. radula, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a shrub-like plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark red to black in color, often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
