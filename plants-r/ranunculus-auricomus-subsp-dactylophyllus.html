Ranunculus auricomus subsp. dactylophyllus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

dactylophyllus, commonly known as the golden shield or dactylophyllus buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals.

The leaves are typically trifoliate or palmately compound, with a distinctive lobed structure. This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
