Rhacodiscus

Rhacodiscus, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble the spokes of a wheel.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant's floating leaves are broad and oval, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. Rhacodiscus is known for its ability to form dense mats on the water surface, which can provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Its reproductive structures are submerged, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
