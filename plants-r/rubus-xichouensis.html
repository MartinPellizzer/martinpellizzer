Rubus xichouensis

Rubus xichouensis, commonly known as the Xichou blackberry, is a shrub native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
