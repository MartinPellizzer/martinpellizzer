Rubus × cognominatus

Rubus × cognominatus, commonly known as the blackberry dewberry, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often reddish or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
