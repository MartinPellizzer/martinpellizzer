Richea pandanifolia

Richea pandanifolia, commonly known as the pandanleaf richea, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland forest areas, characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are long and narrow, resembling those of the pandanus plant, giving it its common name.

Richea pandanifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and providing shelter for various wildlife species.
