Rosa arvensis sphenocarpa

Rosa arvensis sphenocarpa, commonly known as the field rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically serrated and have a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its stems are covered in thorns, which are a distinguishing feature of the species.

Rosa arvensis sphenocarpa is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
