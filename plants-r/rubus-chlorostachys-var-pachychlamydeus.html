Rubus chlorostachys var. pachychlamydeus

Rubus chlorostachys var. pachychlamydeus, commonly known as the green-stamen raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive green stamens, which give it its common name, and its small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible berries, which have a unique flavor profile compared to common raspberries.

This variety is also noted for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
