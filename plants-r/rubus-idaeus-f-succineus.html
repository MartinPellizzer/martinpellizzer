Rubus idaeus f. succineus

Rubus idaeus f. succineus, commonly known as the blackberry.

This variant of the blackberry plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically smaller and more intensely flavored than standard blackberries. It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces its fruit in the summer, with the berries ripening from green to their characteristic deep color.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
