Rosa caesia f. schnetzii

Rosa caesia f. schnetzii, commonly known as the Schnetzii form of the pink sweetbriar, is a hybrid rose species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage is dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its historical significance in horticulture.
