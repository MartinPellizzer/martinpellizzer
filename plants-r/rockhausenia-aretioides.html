Rockhausenia aretioides

Rockhausenia aretioides, commonly known as the rockhouse plant, is a rare and unique species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often in shaded or partially shaded environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its botanical significance lies in its specialized adaptations to rocky habitats and its role in local ecosystems.
