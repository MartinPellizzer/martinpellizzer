Romulea malenconiana var. stenotepala

Romulea malenconiana var. stenotepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf romulea, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It features showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette, providing a compact and elegant appearance. Its bulbs are adapted to survive dry periods, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
