Rubus hirtus microgene hypodasyphyllus

Rubus hirtus microgene hypodasyphyllus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a species of bramble characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in forested areas and along stream banks. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and small, hairy stipules. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and the fruit is a red, juicy aggregate of drupelets.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
