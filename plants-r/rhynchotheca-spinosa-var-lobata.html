Rhynchotheca spinosa var. lobata

Rhynchotheca spinosa var. lobata, commonly known as the lobate spinous thistle, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a robust root system that helps it survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, which reduce water loss.

The species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.
