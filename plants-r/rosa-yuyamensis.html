Rosa yuyamensis

Rosa yuyamensis, commonly known as the Yuyama rose, is a species native to the Andes mountains of Peru.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorny buds.

Rosa yuyamensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
