Rapanea raiateensis

Rapanea raiateensis, commonly known as the Tahitian holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Society Islands.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
