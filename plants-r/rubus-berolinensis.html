Rubus berolinensis

Rubus berolinensis, commonly known as the Berlin raspberry, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are similar in appearance to raspberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
