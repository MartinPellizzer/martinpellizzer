Rubus serpens subsp. brevistamineus

Rubus serpens subsp.

brevistamineus, commonly known as the short-stamined blackberry shrub, is a perennial plant native to parts of southern China. It is characterized by its trailing or scrambling growth habit and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique floral and vegetative traits.
