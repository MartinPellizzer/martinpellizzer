Rosa aculeatissima

Rosa aculeatissima, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces oval, glossy leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are often used in perfumes and culinary applications. Rosa aculeatissima is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
