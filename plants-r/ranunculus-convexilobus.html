Ranunculus convexilobus

Ranunculus convexilobus, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with lobed or divided segments, and its flowers have five to eight petals.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
