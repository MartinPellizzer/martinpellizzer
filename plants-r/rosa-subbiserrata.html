Rosa subbiserrata

Rosa subbiserrata, commonly known as the serrated rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and Nepal, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. Rosa subbiserrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Rosa.
