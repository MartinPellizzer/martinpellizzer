Rubus × pseudomercieri

Rubus × pseudomercieri, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, that are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
