Rubus pseudargenteus

Rubus pseudargenteus, commonly known as silverleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its growth habit includes thorny stems and dense foliage, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
