Rosa rubiginosa f. rochemollensis

Rosa rubiginosa f. rochemollensis, commonly known as the Rochemoll's rose, is a botanical variant of the shrub rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and is native to the Mediterranean region. This form is notable for its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
