Rhododendron monanthum

Rhododendron monanthum, commonly known as the single-flower rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers range in color from white to pink, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Rhododendron monanthum plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for wildlife.
