Rubus elongatispinus microgene belophorus

Rubus elongatispinus microgene belophorus, commonly known as the narrow-spined raspberry.

This plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins.

Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
