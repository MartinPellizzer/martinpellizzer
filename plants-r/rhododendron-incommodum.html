Rhododendron incommodum

Rhododendron incommodum, commonly known as the "discomfort rhododendron," is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep purple to pink, often with a distinctive white throat. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various garden settings, though it requires well-drained soil and partial shade to thrive.
