Rhododendron lindaueanum

Rhododendron lindaueanum, commonly known as the Lindaue's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
