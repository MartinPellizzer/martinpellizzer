Richterago elegans

Richterago elegans, commonly known as the elegant tamarack, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that turn a striking golden-yellow in the fall before shedding. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
