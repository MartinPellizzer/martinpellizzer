Rubus fuscoater f. rhenanus

Rubus fuscoater f. rhenanus, commonly known as the dark-fruited raspberry.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its biennial growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, hairy achenes, which are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its foliage is compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture.
