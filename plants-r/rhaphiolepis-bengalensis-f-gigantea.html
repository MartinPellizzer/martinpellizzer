Rhaphiolepis bengalensis f. gigantea

Rhaphiolepis bengalensis f. gigantea, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a variant of the species Rhaphiolepis bengalensis, characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters of red or orange hues. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its foliage consists of small, glossy, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
