Rhodiola pinnatifida subsp. subpinnata

Rhodiola pinnatifida subsp.

subpinnata, commonly known as the alpine rhodiola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils. It is known for its medicinal properties, containing compounds such as rosavins and salidrosides, which are believed to have adaptogenic effects.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in alpine ecosystems.
