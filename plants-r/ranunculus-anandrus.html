Ranunculus anandrus

Ranunculus anandrus, commonly known as the Himalayan buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight yellow petals, often surrounded by a ring of white or pale yellow sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has finely divided, grass-like leaves. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often colorful flowers.

Ranunculus anandrus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
