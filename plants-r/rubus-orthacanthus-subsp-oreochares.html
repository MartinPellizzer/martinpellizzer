Rubus orthacanthus subsp. oreochares

Rubus orthacanthus subsp.

oreochares, commonly known as the oreochares blackberry, is a subspecies native to the eastern United States. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Botanically, it is notable for its horticultural value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
