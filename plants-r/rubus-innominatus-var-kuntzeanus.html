Rubus innominatus var. kuntzeanus

Rubus innominatus var. kuntzeanus, commonly known as the Kuntze's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
