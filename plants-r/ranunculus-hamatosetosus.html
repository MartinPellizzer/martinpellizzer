Ranunculus hamatosetosus

Ranunculus hamatosetosus, commonly known as the prickly ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a rough, textured surface.

Ranunculus hamatosetosus is typically found in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
