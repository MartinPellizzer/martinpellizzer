Rhamnus fallax var. guicciardii

Rhamnus fallax var. guicciardii, commonly known as the Mexican buckthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by dark purple berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
