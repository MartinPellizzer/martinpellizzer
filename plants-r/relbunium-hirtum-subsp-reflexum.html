Relbunium hirtum subsp. reflexum

Relbunium hirtum subsp.

reflexum, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

The plant features basal leaves that are hairy and linear, with a strong garlic-like odor. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, blooming in early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
