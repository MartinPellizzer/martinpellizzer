Rhytidocaulon macrolobum

Rhytidocaulon macrolobum, commonly known as the giant leaf orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, which are adapted for water storage in its native arid environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the wet season. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
