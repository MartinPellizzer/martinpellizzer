Rosa spinosissima bicolor

Rosa spinosissima bicolor, commonly known as the bicolor spiny rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive bicolor pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
