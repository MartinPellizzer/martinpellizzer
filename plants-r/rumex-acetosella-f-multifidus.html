Rumex acetosella f. multifidus

Rumex acetosella f. multifidus, commonly known as the multifidus sorrel, is a variant of the common sorrel.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a rosette of lanceolate, reddish leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
