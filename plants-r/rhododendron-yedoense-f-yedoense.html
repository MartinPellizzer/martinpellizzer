Rhododendron yedoense f. yedoense

Rhododendron yedoense f. yedoense, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring with flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
