Rhagadiolus papposus

Rhagadiolus papposus, commonly known as the silky pappus grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces feathery bristles called pappi, which aid in seed dispersal by wind. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of study in ecological research.
