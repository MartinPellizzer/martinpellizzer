Rosa rubifolia

Rosa rubifolia, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, thorny stems that are often reddish in color, contributing to its common name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa rubifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
