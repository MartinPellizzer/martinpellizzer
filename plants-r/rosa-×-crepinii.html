Rosa × crepinii

Rosa × crepinii, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid shrub that originated in the 19th century through crosses between Rosa damascena and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, adding visual interest even when not in bloom.

Rosa × crepinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
