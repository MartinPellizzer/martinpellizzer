Rosa dumalis f. sabranskyi

Rosa dumalis f. sabranskyi, commonly known as the Sabransky's shrub rose, is a unique horticultural form of the shrub rose species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse blooming of semi-double to double pink flowers. The plant typically produces small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

This form is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
