Rosa spaldingii

Rosa spaldingii, commonly known as Spalding's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven elliptic leaflets and produces small, oval fruits called hips. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Rosa spaldingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance to native wildlife.
