Rhynchospora heterochaeta var. irregularis

Rhynchospora heterochaeta var. irregularis, commonly known as the irregular spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its three-ranked leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its slender stems can reach up to 60 cm in height. Its flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female parts present on the same spike, making it monoecious.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in maintaining wetland habitats and supporting various insect and bird species.
