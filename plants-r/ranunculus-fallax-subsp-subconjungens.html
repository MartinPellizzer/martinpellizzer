Ranunculus fallax subsp. subconjungens

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

subconjungens, commonly known as the western variegated buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology and is often found in association with other Ranunculaceae species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
