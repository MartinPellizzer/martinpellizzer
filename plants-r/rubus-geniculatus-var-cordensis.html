Rubus geniculatus var. cordensis

Rubus geniculatus var. cordensis, commonly known as the heartleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
