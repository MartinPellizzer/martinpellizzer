Rosa tomentosa subsp. farinosa

Rosa tomentosa subsp.

farinosa, commonly known as the farinose shrub rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

This subspecies is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
