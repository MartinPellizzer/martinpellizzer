Rubus × roselliformis

Rubus × roselliformis, commonly known as the evergreen bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple tiny drupes.

This species is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, temperate climates.
