Rhamnus alpina var. hydriensis

Rhamnus alpina var. hydriensis, commonly known as the Alpine buckthorn, is a shrub native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, followed by black, spherical berries in the fall. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
