Rosa phyllochlora

Rosa phyllochlora, commonly known as the sticky rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a fragrant scent.

Rosa phyllochlora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
