Rapuntium subdentatum

Rapuntium subdentatum, commonly known as the serrated cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Rapuntium subdentatum is known for its edible leaves and seeds, which have been used in traditional cuisine.
