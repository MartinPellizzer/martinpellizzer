Rosa brotherorum

Rosa brotherorum, commonly known as the Brother's rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces hips, which are red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Rosa brotherorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in botanical gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.
