Rubus kwangsiensis

Rubus kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi raspberry, is a species of brambles native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs and vines known for their edible fruits. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for edible use, it has been studied for horticultural and ecological applications.
