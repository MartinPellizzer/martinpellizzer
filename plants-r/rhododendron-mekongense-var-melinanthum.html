Rhododendron mekongense var. melinanthum

Rhododendron mekongense var. melinanthum, commonly known as the Mekong rhododendron, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Cambodia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
