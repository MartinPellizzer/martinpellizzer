Ranunculus medioximus

Ranunculus medioximus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, sometimes hairy texture.

Ranunculus medioximus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
