Rhytidophyllum grande var. laevigatum

Rhytidophyllum grande var. laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth-leafed giant rhytidophyllum, is a rare and striking member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, waxy leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
