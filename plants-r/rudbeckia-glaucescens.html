Rudbeckia glaucescens

Rudbeckia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features showy yellow to orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk of dark brown to black tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with hairy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Rudbeckia glaucescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
