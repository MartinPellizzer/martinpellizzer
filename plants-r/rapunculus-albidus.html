Rapunculus albidus

Rapunculus albidus, commonly known as the white horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and distinctive yellow center, which resemble a small sun. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Rapunculus albidus blooms from late spring to early summer, adding a delicate floral display to its natural habitat.
