Rubus × callipetalus

Rubus × callipetalus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California, where it thrives in sandy soils and coastal climates. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white with five petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
