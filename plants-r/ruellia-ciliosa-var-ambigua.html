Ruellia ciliosa var. ambigua

Ruellia ciliosa var. ambigua, commonly known as the hairy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Ruellia ciliosa var. ambigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
