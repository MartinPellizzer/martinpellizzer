Rubus orthocladoides

Rubus orthocladoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with narrow, serrated leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and riparian habitats.

Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the edible fruits that are popular in local cuisines.
