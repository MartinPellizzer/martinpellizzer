Rorippa × astyla

Rorippa × astyla, commonly known as the hybrid watercress, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rorippa flexuosa and Rorippa sardoa.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit. The plant typically has alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are submerged or floating, with a peppery flavor similar to other members of its family. It thrives in moist environments such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows.

Rorippa × astyla is often found in temperate regions and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves.
