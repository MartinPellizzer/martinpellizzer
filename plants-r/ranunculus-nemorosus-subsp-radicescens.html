Ranunculus nemorosus subsp. radicescens

Ranunculus nemorosus subsp.

radicescens, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features small, yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a distinctive hairy texture. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

This subspecies is often found in wet meadows and is known for its rapid colonization of disturbed sites.
