Rubus pallidus microgene aceratidens

Rubus pallidus microgene aceratidens, commonly known as the pale raspberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its slender stems and small, pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.
