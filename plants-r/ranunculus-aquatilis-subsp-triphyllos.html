Ranunculus aquatilis subsp. triphyllos

Ranunculus aquatilis subsp.

triphyllos, commonly known as the three-leaved water buttercup, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant features trifoliate leaves, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its preference for moist, nutrient-rich soils and its ability to thrive in shallow water environments.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
