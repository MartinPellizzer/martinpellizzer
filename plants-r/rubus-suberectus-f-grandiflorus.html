Rubus suberectus f. grandiflorus

Rubus suberectus f. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered bramble, is a botanical variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic variation and ecological adaptability within the Rubus genus.
