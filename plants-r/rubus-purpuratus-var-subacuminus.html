Rubus purpuratus var. subacuminus

Rubus purpuratus var. subacuminus, commonly known as the purple raspberries or a variant of the blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are pink to white, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The botanical features of this variety make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
