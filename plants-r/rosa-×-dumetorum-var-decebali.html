Rosa × dumetorum var. decebali

Rosa × dumetorum var. decebali, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense, thorny stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white down.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy and resilient plant in challenging environments.
