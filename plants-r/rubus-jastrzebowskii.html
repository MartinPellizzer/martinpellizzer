Rubus jastrzebowskii

Rubus jastrzebowskii, commonly known as the Jastrzebowski blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries.

The botanical features of Rubus jastrzebowskii make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both its ornamental and potential culinary uses.
