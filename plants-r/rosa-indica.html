Rosa indica

Rosa indica, commonly known as the Indian rose or Damask rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western Himalayas and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Rosa indica is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its flowers and used in perfumery and aromatherapy.

Its historical significance in traditional medicine and horticulture further highlights its botanical importance.
