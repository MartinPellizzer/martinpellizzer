Ribes sanguineum var. albescens

Ribes sanguineum var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered currant, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
