Rhus schiedeana f. vestita

Rhus schiedeana f. vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into 5 to 7 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a striking feature of the species. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
