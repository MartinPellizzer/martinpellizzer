Rosa canina var. vaccinoides

Rosa canina var. vaccinoides, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red-orange hips, which are the fruit and contain seeds. It is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is closely related to other rose species and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
