Ranunculus abortivus var. eucyclus

Ranunculus abortivus var. eucyclus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its striking, yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

The species is known for its short stature and is often found in disturbed or wetland environments.
