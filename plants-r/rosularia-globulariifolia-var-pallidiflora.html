Rosularia globulariifolia var. pallidiflora

Rosularia globulariifolia var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered globular rose.

This succulent plant is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a dense rosette. It produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Native to rocky, arid environments, it is well adapted to dry conditions and requires minimal water.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in xeriscaped gardens.
