Rubus argutus var. scissus

Rubus argutus var. scissus, commonly known as the prickly rose or scissored blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
