Ranunculus acrifolius

Ranunculus acrifolius, commonly known as the black-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The leaves are deeply lobed and often have a dark green to blackish hue, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The plant is characterized by its toxic sap, which contains irritant compounds.
