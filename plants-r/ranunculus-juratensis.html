Ranunculus juratensis

Ranunculus juratensis, commonly known as the Juratense buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem, while its flowers feature five to eight petal-like sepals.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cup-shaped blooms.
