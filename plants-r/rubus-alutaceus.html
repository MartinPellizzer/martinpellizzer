Rubus alutaceus

Rubus alutaceus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark berries that mature in the fall. The plant has silvery-white undersides on its leaves, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade.

Rubus alutaceus is an important food source for various wildlife species.
