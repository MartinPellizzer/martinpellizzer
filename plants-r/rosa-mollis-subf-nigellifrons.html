Rosa mollis subf. nigellifrons

Rosa mollis subf.

nigellifrons, commonly known as the black-fruited rose, is a sub-species of the shrub rose native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five to seven petals.

The plant produces small, dark blue to black fruits known as hips, which are edible and rich in vitamin C. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
