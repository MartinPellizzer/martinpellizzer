Rapanea cruciata

Rapanea cruciata, commonly known as the Crucifix or Crosswood tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
