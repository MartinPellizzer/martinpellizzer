Ranunculus longepedunculatus

Ranunculus longepedunculatus, commonly known as the long peduncle buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to nine petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has a simple, upright stem with palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and as a subject of botanical study.
