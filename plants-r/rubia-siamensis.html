Rubia siamensis

Rubia siamensis, commonly known as Siam madder, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in cymes. Its roots and stems contain a red dye, which has been historically used in traditional dyeing practices.

Rubia siamensis is often found in forest understories and thrives in humid, tropical climates.
