Rubus ramosus

Rubus ramosus, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnately compound. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus ramosus plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
