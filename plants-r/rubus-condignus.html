Rubus condignus

Rubus condignus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet-tart flavor. Rubus condignus is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
