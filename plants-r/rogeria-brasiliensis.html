Rogeria brasiliensis

Rogeria brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Rogeria brasiliensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated in greenhouses and gardens worldwide.
