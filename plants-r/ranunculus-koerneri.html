Ranunculus koerneri

Ranunculus koerneri, commonly known as the Koerner's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmately divided leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
