Rhodiola atuntsuensis

Rhodiola atuntsuensis, commonly known as the Tibetan rhodiola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet and northern India.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in arid, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand extreme cold and low oxygen levels. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to store water and nutrients efficiently, which is crucial for survival in its harsh habitat.

Rhodiola atuntsuensis is also valued for its potential adaptogenic properties, making it a subject of interest in traditional and modern herbal medicine.
