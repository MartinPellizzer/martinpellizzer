Ranunculus luoergaiensis

Ranunculus luoergaiensis, commonly known as the Lühe buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is native to China, specifically found in the Lühe region of Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its basal leaves are palmately lobed. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous areas.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
