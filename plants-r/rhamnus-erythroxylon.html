Rhamnus erythroxylon

Rhamnus erythroxylon, commonly known as the red-fruited buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.
