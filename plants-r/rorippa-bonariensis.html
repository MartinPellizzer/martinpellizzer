Rorippa bonariensis

Rorippa bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine watercress, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in wet environments such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often submerged or floating, while its small white or yellow flowers are arranged in racemes.
