Rubus hedycarpus subsp. godronii

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

godronii, commonly known as Godron's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry. The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
