Rumex thyrsoides var. intermedius

Rumex thyrsoides var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense, upright clusters. It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in wetland environments.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional herbal medicine.
