Rubus tomentosus var. tomentosifrons

Rubus tomentosus var. tomentosifrons, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, white-tomentose (woolly) foliage and stems, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes that are typically clustered together. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
