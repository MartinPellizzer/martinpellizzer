Ribes villosum

Ribes villosum, commonly known as the hairy currant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems covered in soft, grayish hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, red berries. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Ribes villosum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
