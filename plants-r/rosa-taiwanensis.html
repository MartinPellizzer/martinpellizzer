Rosa taiwanensis

Rosa taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Rosa taiwanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
