Rapanea buxifolia

Rapanea buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, characterized by its dense, glossy foliage. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture and a dark green color. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
