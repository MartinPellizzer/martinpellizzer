Rubus alterniflorus var. bosniacus

Rubus alterniflorus var. bosniacus, commonly known as the Bosnian blackberry, is a variant of the alternate-flowered raspberry native to the Balkan region.

It is a shrub that produces small, dark red to black berries, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has serrated, compound leaves and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines and traditional herbal uses.
