Raphia farinifera

Raphia farinifera, commonly known as the giant breadfruit tree, is a large, palm-like plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its massive, pinnate leaves that can reach lengths of up to 20 meters, making it one of the tallest plants in the palm family. The plant produces large, spherical fruits that are covered in a fibrous, starchy pulp, which is used for food and fiber. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to grow optimally.
