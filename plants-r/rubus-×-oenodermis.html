Rubus × oenodermis

Rubus × oenodermis, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (the red raspberry). This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually pink or white.

It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its edible, flavorful berries.
