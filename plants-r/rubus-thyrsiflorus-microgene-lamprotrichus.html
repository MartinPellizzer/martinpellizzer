Rubus thyrsiflorus microgene lamprotrichus

Rubus thyrsiflorus microgene lamprotrichus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and dense, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Rubus genus, known for its ornamental and ecological value.
