Rubus thyrsoideus var. roseolus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. roseolus, commonly known as the rosehip bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, red berries that are rich in vitamin C and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native habitats.
