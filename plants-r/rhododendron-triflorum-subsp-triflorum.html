Rhododendron triflorum subsp. triflorum

Rhododendron triflorum subsp.

triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its compact shrub form and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are typically dark green and leathery, providing a dense canopy that supports various wildlife. This subspecies thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
