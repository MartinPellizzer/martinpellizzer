Rubus hylonomoides

Rubus hylonomoides, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems that help in its identification.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
