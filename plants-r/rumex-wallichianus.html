Rumex wallichianus

Rumex wallichianus, commonly known as the Himalayan sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant features alternate, broadly triangular leaves with prominent veins, and it produces capsules as its fruit type. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Rumex wallichianus is valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor similar to rhubarb.
