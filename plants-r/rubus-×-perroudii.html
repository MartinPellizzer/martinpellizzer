Rubus × perroudii

Rubus × perroudii, commonly known as the Evergreen Blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
