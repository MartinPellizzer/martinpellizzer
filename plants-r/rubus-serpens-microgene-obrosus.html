Rubus serpens microgene obrosus

Rubus serpens microgene obrosus, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is edible but often has a tart flavor and is used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
