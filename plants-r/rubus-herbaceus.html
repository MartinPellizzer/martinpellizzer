Rubus herbaceus

Rubus herbaceus, commonly known as the common bramble, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, aggregate berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
