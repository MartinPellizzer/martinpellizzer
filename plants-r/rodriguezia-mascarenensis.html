Rodriguezia mascarenensis

Rodriguezia mascarenensis, commonly known as the Mascarene rodriguezia, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and limestone habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
