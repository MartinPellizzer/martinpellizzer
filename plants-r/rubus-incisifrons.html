Rubus incisifrons

Rubus incisifrons, commonly known as the serrated blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
