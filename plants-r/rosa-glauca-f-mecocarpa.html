Rosa glauca f. mecocarpa

Rosa glauca f. mecocarpa, commonly known as the blue rose with red fruits, is a hybrid form of the blue rose (Rosa glauca).

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals, which are often tinged with purple, and its distinctive red, fleshy fruits that mature in late summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rosa genus. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its unique coloration and fruiting characteristics.
