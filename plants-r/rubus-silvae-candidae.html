Rubus silvae-candidae

Rubus silvae-candidae, commonly known as the white forest raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and small, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forested areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
