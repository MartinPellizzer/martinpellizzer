Rhamnus saxatilis

Rhamnus saxatilis, commonly known as the rock buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

Rhamnus saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
