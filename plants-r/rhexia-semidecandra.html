Rhexia semidecandra

Rhexia semidecandra, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Rhexia semidecandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
