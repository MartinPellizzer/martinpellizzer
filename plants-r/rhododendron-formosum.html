Rhododendron formosum

Rhododendron formosum, commonly known as the Formosan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring acidic, well-drained soils. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, range in color from white to pink and are often arranged in clusters.

Rhododendron formosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local biodiversity of its native habitat.
