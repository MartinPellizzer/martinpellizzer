Rosa gallica var. trichophylla

Rosa gallica var. trichophylla, commonly known as the white gallica rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in a soft, white down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
