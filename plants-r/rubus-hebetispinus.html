Rubus hebetispinus

Rubus hebetispinus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
