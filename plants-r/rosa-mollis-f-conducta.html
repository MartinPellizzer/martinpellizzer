Rosa mollis f. conducta

Rosa mollis f. conducta, commonly known as the soft rose or velvet rose, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
