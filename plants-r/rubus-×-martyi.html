Rubus × martyi

Rubus × martyi, commonly known as the Evergreen Blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural study.
