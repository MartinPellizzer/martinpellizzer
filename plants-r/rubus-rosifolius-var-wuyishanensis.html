Rubus rosifolius var. wuyishanensis

Rubus rosifolius var. wuyishanensis, commonly known as the Wuyishan raspberry, is a shrub native to China, specifically the Wuyi Mountains.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of tiny flowers that bloom in early spring.
