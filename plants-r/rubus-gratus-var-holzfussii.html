Rubus gratus var. holzfussii

Rubus gratus var. holzfussii, commonly known as the Holzfussii raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in local horticulture.
