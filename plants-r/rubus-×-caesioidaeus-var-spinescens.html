Rubus × caesioidaeus var. spinescens

Rubus × caesioidaeus var. spinescens, commonly known as the spiny blackberry, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of eastern North America. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and produces dark purple to black berries that are edible when ripe. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it is known for its vigorous growth habit.

The spines on its stems serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
