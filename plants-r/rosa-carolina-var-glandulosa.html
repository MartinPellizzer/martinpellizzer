Rosa carolina var. glandulosa

Rosa carolina var. glandulosa, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces pink to red flowers in the spring, followed by rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
