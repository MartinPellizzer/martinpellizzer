Ranunculus ficariifolius

Ranunculus ficariifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive broad, lobed shape that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.
