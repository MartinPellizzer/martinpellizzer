Rubus caesius subsp. leucosepalus

Rubus caesius subsp.

leucosepalus, commonly known as the white-flowered blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are white and have five petals, which are slightly hairy. The fruit is a small, dark purple berry that ripens in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.
