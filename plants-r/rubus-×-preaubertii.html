Rubus × preaubertii

Rubus × preaubertii, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in boreal and temperate regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is a aggregate of small drupelets, typically dark red to black in color, and is known for its tart flavor.
