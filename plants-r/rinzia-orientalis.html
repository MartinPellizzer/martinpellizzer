Rinzia orientalis

Rinzia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental rinzia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

Rinzia orientalis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ecological and cultural significance.
