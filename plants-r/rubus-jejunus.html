Rubus jejunus

Rubus jejunus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are covered in thorny buds.

The botanical features of Rubus jejunus make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
