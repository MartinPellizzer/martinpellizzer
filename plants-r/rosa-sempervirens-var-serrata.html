Rosa sempervirens var. serrata

Rosa sempervirens var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated evergreen rose, is a variant of the evergreen rose that is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year, providing evergreen structure in gardens. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and leathery leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
