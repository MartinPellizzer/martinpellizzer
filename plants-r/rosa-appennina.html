Rosa appennina

Rosa appennina, commonly known as the Apennine rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Italy's Apennine Mountains.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa appennina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both garden use and historical medicinal purposes.
