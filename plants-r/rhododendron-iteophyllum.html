Rhododendron iteophyllum

Rhododendron iteophyllum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories at high altitudes. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often accompanied by bracts that enhance their visual appeal.

Rhododendron iteophyllum is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
