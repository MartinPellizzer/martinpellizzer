Rubus tereticaulis var. pinetorum

Rubus tereticaulis var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine barrens raspberry, is a shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in sandy, acidic soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as pine barrens and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
