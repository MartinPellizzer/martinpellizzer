Rubus × linguifer

Rubus × linguifer, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a plant with distinctive botanical features. The plant produces large, dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and more flavorful than its parent species. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are covered in dense, prickly bristles.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and its unique fruit, which is often used in culinary applications.
