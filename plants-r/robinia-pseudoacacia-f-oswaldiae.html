Robinia pseudoacacia f. oswaldiae

Robinia pseudoacacia f. oswaldiae, commonly known as the Oswalda false acacia, is a rare natural variant of the black locust tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, featuring elongated, pale yellow inflorescences. The plant exhibits a unique genetic trait that sets it apart from the typical form of Robinia pseudoacacia. It is native to specific regions in Europe and is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution.

This form is often studied for its ecological and evolutionary significance.
