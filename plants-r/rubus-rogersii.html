Rubus rogersii

Rubus rogersii, commonly known as Rogers' blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils along streambanks and forest edges. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

The flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
