Rubus hebecaulis var. discerptifrons

Rubus hebecaulis var. discerptifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus characterized by its slender stems and distinctive leaf morphology.

It features trifoliate leaves with narrow, serrated leaflets that are typically pale green on the underside. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphological traits and its role in local ecosystems.
