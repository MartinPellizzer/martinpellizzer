Racosperma biflorum

Racosperma biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Racosperma biflorum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in native ecosystems.
