Ranunculus acris subsp. subcorymbosus

Ranunculus acris subsp.

subcorymbosus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five to seven sepals that are often tinged with red or brown at the base. It thrives in moist, cool environments and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and alpine tundra.

Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
