Rosa canariensis

Rosa canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island rose, is a species of shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the warm, dry climate of its native habitat. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Rosa canariensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
