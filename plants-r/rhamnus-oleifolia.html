Rhamnus oleifolia

Rhamnus oleifolia, commonly known as the oliveleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of Mediterranean ecosystems.
