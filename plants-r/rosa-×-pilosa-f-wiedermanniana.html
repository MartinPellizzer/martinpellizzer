Rosa × pilosa f. wiedermanniana

Rosa × pilosa f. wiedermanniana, commonly known as the Wiedermann's climbing rose, is a botanical hybrid that exhibits characteristics of both parent species.

It is a vigorous climber with long, flexible canes that can reach significant heights. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, often with a velvety texture. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that provide a lush green appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
