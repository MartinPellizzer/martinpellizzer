Ranunculus auricomus subsp. tinctigemmatus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

tinctigemmatus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
