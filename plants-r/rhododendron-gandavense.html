Rhododendron gandavense

Rhododendron gandavense, commonly known as the Gandhara rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of northern India and Pakistan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and densely arranged along its stems. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are typically pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron gandavense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in mountainous forest ecosystems.
