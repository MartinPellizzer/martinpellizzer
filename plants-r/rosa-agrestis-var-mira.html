Rosa agrestis var. mira

Rosa agrestis var. mira, commonly known as the wild pink rose, is a species native to certain regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate pink flowers and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, preferring well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and beauty.
