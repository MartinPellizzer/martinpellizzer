Rosa mollis genuina

Rosa mollis genuina, commonly known as the Mexican rose or the soft-hairy rose, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa mollis genuina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
