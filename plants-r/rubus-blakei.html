Rubus blakei

Rubus blakei, commonly known as Blake's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many edible and ornamental species.
