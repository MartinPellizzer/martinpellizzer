Ranunculus alpestris var. ambiguus

Ranunculus alpestris var. ambiguus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

This variant is noted for its variability in flower color and leaf structure, contributing to its classification as a variant.
