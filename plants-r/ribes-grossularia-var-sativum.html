Ribes grossularia var. sativum

Ribes grossularia var. sativum, commonly known as the cultivated gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, tart berries that are typically green or yellow when unripe and turn red or orange when mature. The plant has compound leaves with 5-9 leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and hedgerows.

The berries are often used in jams, jellies, and other culinary applications due to their high acidity and unique flavor.
