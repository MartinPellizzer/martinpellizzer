Rubus himalaicus

Rubus himalaicus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
