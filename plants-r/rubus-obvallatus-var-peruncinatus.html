Rubus obvallatus var. peruncinatus

Rubus obvallatus var. peruncinatus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may turn black depending on the variety. Its flowers are white and clustered in groups of three, typical of the Rubus genus.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
