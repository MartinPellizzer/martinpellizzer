Raphanus rostratus var. pugioniformis

Raphanus rostratus var. pugioniformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, edible roots that are typically harvested in the first year. The plant thrives in cool climates and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for temperate regions. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and it is often grown as a vegetable or for its ornamental value.

The variety is noted for its distinct leaf shape and mild flavor, which distinguishes it from other radish species.
