Rudbeckia bupleuroides

Rudbeckia bupleuroides, commonly known as blue false sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features bright blue to purple flower heads with yellow centers, surrounded by ray florets that resemble petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of downy hairs.

Rudbeckia bupleuroides is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
