Rubus ternatus

Rubus ternatus, commonly known as the Japanese bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in woodland areas and is native to East Asia, including Japan, Korea, and China. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring. Its fruit, a red berry, is edible but often has a tart flavor.

Rubus ternatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
