Roella ciliata var. incurva

Roella ciliata var. incurva, commonly known as the curled roella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and features small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly curled appearance, giving the plant its common name. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in warm climates.
