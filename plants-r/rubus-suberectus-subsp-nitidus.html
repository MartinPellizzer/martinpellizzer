Rubus suberectus subsp. nitidus

Rubus suberectus subsp.

nitidus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark, glossy leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
