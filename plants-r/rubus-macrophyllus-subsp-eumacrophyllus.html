Rubus macrophyllus subsp. eumacrophyllus

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

eumacrophyllus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 15 cm in length.

The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in late spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The subspecies is notable for its vigorous growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
