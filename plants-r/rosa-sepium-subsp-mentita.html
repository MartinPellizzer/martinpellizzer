Rosa sepium subsp. mentita

Rosa sepium subsp.

mentita, commonly known as the white-barked rose, is a botanical variant of the shrub rose species. It is characterized by its distinctive white bark, which is smooth and peeling, giving it a unique appearance.

The plant produces fragrant pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green and alternate, with serrated edges.

It is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
