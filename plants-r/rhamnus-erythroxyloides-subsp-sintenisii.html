Rhamnus erythroxyloides subsp. sintenisii

Rhamnus erythroxyloides subsp.

sintenisii, commonly known as the red-leafed buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically reddish in color, especially when young.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, elongated berries that are edible for certain bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf coloration and habitat preferences compared to other members of the Rhamnus genus.
