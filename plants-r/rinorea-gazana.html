Rinorea gazana

Rinorea gazana, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Rinorea gazana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
