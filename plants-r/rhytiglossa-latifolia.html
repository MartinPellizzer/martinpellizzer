Rhytiglossa latifolia

Rhytiglossa latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhytiglossa, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, often with a striking blue-green coloration. Its inflorescence produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink.

Rhytiglossa latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor conditions with proper humidity and light.
