Rosa pumila var. subnigra

Rosa pumila var. subnigra, commonly known as the black-fruited shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dark green, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant is notable for its dark purple to black berries, which are edible but typically consumed raw or used in jams. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and wildlife support.
