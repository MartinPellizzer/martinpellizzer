Ranunculus affinis var. validus

Ranunculus affinis var. validus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
