Rubus decorus

Rubus decorus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and small white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
