Rosa villosa var. longipes

Rosa villosa var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features arching, thorned stems that can grow up to 6 feet in height, with compound leaves consisting of 5 to 9 elliptical leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
