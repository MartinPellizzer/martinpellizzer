Rhaponticum scariosum subsp. lamarckii

Rhaponticum scariosum subsp.

lamarckii, commonly known as Lamarck's rhapontic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features a robust root system and produces large, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped.

The plant blooms with yellow flowers arranged in a composite inflorescence, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
