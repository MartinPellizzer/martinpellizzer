Rubus norimbergensis

Rubus norimbergensis, commonly known as the Norimbergensis blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
