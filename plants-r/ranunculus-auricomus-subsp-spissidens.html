Ranunculus auricomus subsp. spissidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

spissidens, commonly known as the dense-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals, which typically bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its densely packed, hairy leaves that are broader and more robust compared to the typical form. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
