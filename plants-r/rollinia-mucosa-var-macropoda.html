Rollinia mucosa var. macropoda

Rollinia mucosa var. macropoda, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, sweet-tasting fruit that is similar in appearance to a papaya. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
