Ranunculus uncinatus var. parviflorus

Ranunculus uncinatus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
