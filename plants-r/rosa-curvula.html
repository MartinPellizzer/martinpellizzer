Rosa curvula

Rosa curvula, commonly known as the bent-stem rose, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and five-petaled flowers with numerous stamens.

Rosa curvula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
