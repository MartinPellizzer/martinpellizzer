Rudbeckia intermedia

Rudbeckia intermedia, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered in rough, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flower heads with a prominent central disk surrounded by ray florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Rudbeckia intermedia thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils.
