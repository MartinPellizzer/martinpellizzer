Rhododendron luteiflorum

Rhododendron luteiflorum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Rhododendron luteiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens.
