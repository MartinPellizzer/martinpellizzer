Rheum lucidum

Rheum lucidum, commonly known as the giant rhubarb, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, often with a waxy, translucent sheen. The plant produces tall, reddish-purple stalks that bear small, yellowish flowers in the spring. Rheum lucidum is known for its medicinal properties, with its leaves containing high concentrations of anthraquinones.

It is also cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
