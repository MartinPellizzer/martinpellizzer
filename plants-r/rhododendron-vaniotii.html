Rhododendron vaniotii

Rhododendron vaniotii, commonly known as the Vanioti rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, humid environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron vaniotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized landscapes.
