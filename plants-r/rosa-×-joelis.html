Rosa × joelis

Rosa × joelis, commonly known as the Joelis rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa × centifolia.

It is characterized by its robust growth and hardy nature, making it suitable for various climates. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a high number of petals. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance.

Rosa × joelis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal areas.
