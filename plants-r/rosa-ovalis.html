Rosa ovalis

Rosa ovalis, commonly known as the oval rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Native to regions of Asia, particularly China and Japan, this plant thrives in temperate climates. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa ovalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
