Ranunculus manifestus

Ranunculus manifestus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals, which are typically five in number and arranged in a cup-like shape. The plant produces a cluster of small, greenish-yellow stamens surrounding a central pistil. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in alpine meadows.

Ranunculus manifestus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
