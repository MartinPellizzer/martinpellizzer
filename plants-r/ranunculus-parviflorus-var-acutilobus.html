Ranunculus parviflorus var. acutilobus

Ranunculus parviflorus var. acutilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, lobed leaf structure. Native to the eastern United States, this plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and wetlands. The leaves are palmately divided with sharply pointed lobes, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in partial shade and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.
