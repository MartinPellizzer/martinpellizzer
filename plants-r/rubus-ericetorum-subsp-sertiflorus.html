Rubus ericetorum subsp. sertiflorus

Rubus ericetorum subsp.

sertiflorus, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated margins and a leathery texture.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.
