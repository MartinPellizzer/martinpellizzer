Rubus incisus f. geifolius

Rubus incisus f. geifolius, commonly known as the serrated blackberry.

This plant is a flowering shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
