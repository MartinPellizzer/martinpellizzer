Racosperma cremiflorum

Racosperma cremiflorum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

Racosperma cremiflorum is adapted to arid environments and is commonly found in dry sclerophyll forests.
