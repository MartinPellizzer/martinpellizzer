Rubus dispulsiflorus

Rubus dispulsiflorus, commonly known as the dispersing flower raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
