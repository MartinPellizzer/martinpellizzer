Rubus rappii

Rubus rappii, commonly known as the sandbar raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found along riverbanks and coastal areas.

Botanically, it is a member of the Rosaceae family and is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
