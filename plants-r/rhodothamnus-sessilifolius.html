Rhodothamnus sessilifolius

Rhodothamnus sessilifolius, commonly known as the sessile-leaved rhodothamnus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woody growth and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
