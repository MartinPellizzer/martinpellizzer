Ruppia antarctica

Ruppia antarctica, commonly known as Antarctic waterweed, is a small aquatic plant native to cold regions such as Antarctica, the Arctic, and subarctic areas.

It is a submerged, flowering plant that typically grows in shallow, freshwater or brackish environments. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted to pollination by water currents. The plant has a robust rhizome system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
