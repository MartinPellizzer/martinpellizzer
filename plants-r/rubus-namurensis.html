Rubus namurensis

Rubus namurensis, commonly known as the Namurensis blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many shrubs and vines known for their edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
