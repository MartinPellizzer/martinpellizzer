Rubus tatiarensis

Rubus tatiarensis, commonly known as the Tatiara blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to southern Australia, specifically the region of Tatiara in South Australia. The plant is a shrub that produces small, dark purple to black berries, which are typically harvested for their flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and has a moderate growth rate.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
