Rosa arvatica f. genuina

Rosa arvatica f. genuina, commonly known as the Alpine rose or mountain rose, is a subspecies of the shrub rose native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are thornless and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
