Rebutia pygmaea var. friedrichiana

Rebutia pygmaea var. friedrichiana, commonly known as the Friedrich's miniature cactus, is a compact, low-growing species native to South America.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered with white or pale green spines. The plant produces small, vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and drought tolerance.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of care.
