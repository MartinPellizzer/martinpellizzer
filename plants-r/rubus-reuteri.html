Rubus reuteri

Rubus reuteri, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus reuteri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
