Rubus × neogardicus

Rubus × neogardicus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus globulifera, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark, glossy foliage. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions with moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and commercial settings.
