Rubus thyrsoideus var. phyllostachys

Rubus thyrsoideus var. phyllostachys, commonly known as the silverleaf raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its distinctive silvery foliage.

It is a shrub-like species with compound leaves that have a unique, serrated edge and a pale, almost metallic appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, similar to other raspberry varieties. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
