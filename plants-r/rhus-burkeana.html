Rhus burkeana

Rhus burkeana, commonly known as the burke's sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, clustered yellowish flowers in the spring. Its fruits are red, fleshy drupes that ripen in the fall, attracting various birds and small mammals.

Rhus burkeana is often found in moist, open woods and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
