Ranunculus auricomus subsp. ariodon

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

ariodon, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features distinctive bright yellow flowers with deeply notched petals, often resembling a shield in shape.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
