Rhododendron katsumatae

Rhododendron katsumatae, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a species native to Japan and belongs to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring acidic, well-drained soils. It is often found in shaded, humid environments, such as forests and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in temperate climates.
