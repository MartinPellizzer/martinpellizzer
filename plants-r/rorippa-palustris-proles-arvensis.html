Rorippa palustris proles arvensis

Rorippa palustris proles arvensis, commonly known as the field water-cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. This species is typically found in wet or marshy habitats, often growing in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant is known for its peppery taste, similar to watercress, and is sometimes used in culinary applications.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small aquatic organisms.
