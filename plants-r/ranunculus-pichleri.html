Ranunculus pichleri

Ranunculus pichleri, commonly known as the Pichler's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to seven petals and numerous stamens, giving it a delicate and intricate appearance.

Ranunculus pichleri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be challenging to grow outside its native environment.
