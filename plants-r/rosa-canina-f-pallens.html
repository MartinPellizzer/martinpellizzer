Rosa canina f. pallens

Rosa canina f. pallens, commonly known as the pale dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which are smaller than those of the typical Rosa canina. The plant produces red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are edible and used in culinary and medicinal applications. It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its foliage is typically dark green with serrated leaves, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.
