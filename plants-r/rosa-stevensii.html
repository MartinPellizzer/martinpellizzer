Rosa stevensii

Rosa stevensii, commonly known as the Stevens rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces hips, which are the fruit of the rose family. Rosa stevensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many landscapes.
