Rubus × congestiflorus

Rubus × congestiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its complex genetic makeup resulting from crosses between different Rubus species.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically found in temperate regions where its parent species are native. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are often dark red or black in color. Its flowers are densely clustered, giving it the "congestiflorus" name, which refers to the crowded arrangement of blooms.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential for fruit production in horticultural settings.
