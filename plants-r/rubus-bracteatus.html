Rubus bracteatus

Rubus bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteated blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
