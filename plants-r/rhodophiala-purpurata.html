Rhodophiala purpurata

Rhodophiala purpurata, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or high-altitude grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stem is short and bears the inflorescence.

Rhodophiala purpurata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
