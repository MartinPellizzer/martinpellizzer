Ranunculus nephelogenes var. geniculatus

Ranunculus nephelogenes var. geniculatus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with numerous stamens and a prominent central pistil. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy surface that helps protect it from harsh weather conditions.

This variant is distinguished by its geniculate (jointed) stem, which allows it to bend and adapt to its rugged surroundings.
