Rosa pendulina subvar. adjecta

Rosa pendulina subvar. adjecta, commonly known as the hanging rose or weeping rose, is a botanical variant of the pendulina species.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pink or white blooms, which appear in early to mid-summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the flowers.

This subvariety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
