Raphanus primuloides

Raphanus primuloides, commonly known as the wild radish, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring basal leaves that are oval and slightly hairy. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, often blooming from late spring to early autumn.

The plant produces round, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
