Rubus serpens var. glaucophyllus

Rubus serpens var. glaucophyllus, commonly known as the glaucophyllous blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, often blue-green foliage, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its potential for use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
