Rosa sericea var. denudata

Rosa sericea var. denudata, commonly known as the naked-stem rose, is a variety of the silky rose native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often leafless stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
