Rhododendron neriiflorum subsp. phaedropum

Rhododendron neriiflorum subsp.

phaedropum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at high elevations. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial shade.
