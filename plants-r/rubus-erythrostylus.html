Rubus erythrostylus

Rubus erythrostylus, commonly known as the red-styled raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
