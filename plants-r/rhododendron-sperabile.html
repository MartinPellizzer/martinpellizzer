Rhododendron sperabile

Rhododendron sperabile, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant produces dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
