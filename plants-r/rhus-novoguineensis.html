Rhus novoguineensis

Rhus novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like the poison ivy and cashew. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and the plant is often found in lowland rainforests.

Rhus novoguineensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
