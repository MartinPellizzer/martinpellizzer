Ruschia perfoliata

Ruschia perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate Ruschia, is a low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially in full sun. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and vibrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
