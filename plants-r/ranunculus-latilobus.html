Ranunculus latilobus

Ranunculus latilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus latilobus is often found in the eastern United States, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
