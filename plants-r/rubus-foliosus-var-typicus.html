Rubus foliosus var. typicus

Rubus foliosus var. typicus, commonly known as the common bramble, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, usually red or black berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
