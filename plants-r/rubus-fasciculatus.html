Rubus fasciculatus

Rubus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to western North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus fasciculatus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various species.
