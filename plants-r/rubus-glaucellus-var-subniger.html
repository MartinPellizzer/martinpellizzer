Rubus glaucellus var. subniger

Rubus glaucellus var. subniger, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a thorny shrub native to the western United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The berries are edible but can be quite tart, and the plant is known for its aggressive growth habit.
