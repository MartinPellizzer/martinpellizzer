Rubus oreogeton var. fabrimontanus

Rubus oreogeton var. fabrimontanus, commonly known as the African blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are sometimes used in local cuisines.
