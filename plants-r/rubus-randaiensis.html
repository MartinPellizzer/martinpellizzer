Rubus randaiensis

Rubus randaiensis, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it has been cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
