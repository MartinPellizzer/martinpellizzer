Rumex algeriensis

Rumex algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves with a distinctive reddish midrib. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Algerian sorrel is known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor similar to rhubarb.
