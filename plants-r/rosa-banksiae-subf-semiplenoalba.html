Rosa banksiae subf. semiplenoalba

Rosa banksiae subf.

semiplenoalba, commonly known as the semi-double white banksia rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its unique floral structure. It features semi-double flowers with pale white petals that have a slight blush of pink, giving it a delicate and refined appearance.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thorny stems and dark green, glossy foliage. It is native to the eastern regions of Australia and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
