Rhynchosia trichocephala

Rhynchosia trichocephala, commonly known as the hairy-headed snout bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive hairy, club-shaped seed pods. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with trifoliate leaves and showy yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in a long, slender pod and are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
