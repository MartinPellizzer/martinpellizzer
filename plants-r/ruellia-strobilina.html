Ruellia strobilina

Ruellia strobilina, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is lance-shaped and dark green, adding a lush appearance to gardens.

Ruellia strobilina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm climates.
