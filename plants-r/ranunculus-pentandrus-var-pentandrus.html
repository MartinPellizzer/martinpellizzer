Ranunculus pentandrus var. pentandrus

Ranunculus pentandrus var. pentandrus, commonly known as the five-stamen buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, showy yellow flowers with five sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the species is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

It is also a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its vibrant color and elegant appearance.
