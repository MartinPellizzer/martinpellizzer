Rhaphis echinulata

Rhaphis echinulata, commonly known as the spiny feather plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often adorned with small, spiny projections along the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by clustered, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Rhaphis echinulata is relatively low maintenance and suitable for indoor cultivation in warm climates.
