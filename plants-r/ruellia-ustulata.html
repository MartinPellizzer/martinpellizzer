Ruellia ustulata

Ruellia ustulata, commonly known as the flameleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. Ruellia ustulata thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
