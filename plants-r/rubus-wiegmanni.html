Rubus wiegmanni

Rubus wiegmanni, commonly known as the Wiegmann blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces edible berries that are similar to blackberries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
