Rubus eastwoodianus

Rubus eastwoodianus, commonly known as Eastwood's blackberry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
