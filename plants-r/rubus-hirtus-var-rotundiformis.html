Rubus hirtus var. rotundiformis

Rubus hirtus var. rotundiformis, commonly known as the round-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its rounded, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in woodland and open areas, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and its role in local ecosystems.
