Rubus villicaulis var. brevispinus

Rubus villicaulis var. brevispinus, commonly known as the short-spined blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
