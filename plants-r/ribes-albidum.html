Ribes albidum

Ribes albidum, commonly known as white currant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes in the family Grossulariaceae, which includes other currant and gooseberry species. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ribes albidum is often cultivated for its fruit and is also valued in ecological landscapes for supporting pollinators.
