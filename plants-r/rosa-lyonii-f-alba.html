Rosa lyonii f. alba

Rosa lyonii f. alba, commonly known as the white form of Lyon's rose, is a cultivar of the species Rosa lyonii, which is native to the mountainous regions of the United States.

This plant is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in early summer. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the delicate flowers. It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with thorny stems and a compact growth habit.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
