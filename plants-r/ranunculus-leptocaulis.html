Ranunculus leptocaulis

Ranunculus leptocaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its cheerful blooms.
