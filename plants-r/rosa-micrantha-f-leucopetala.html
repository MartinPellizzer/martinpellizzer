Rosa micrantha f. leucopetala

Rosa micrantha f. leucopetala, commonly known as the white-flowered form of the Chinese rose, is a compact shrub characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers.

It is native to China and is known for its dense, thorny growth habit. The leaves are typically oval and have a glossy green surface. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its visual contrast against other flowering plants.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade to full sun.
