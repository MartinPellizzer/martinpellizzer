Rosa indica var. bengalensis

Rosa indica var. bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal rose, is a variant of the common rose that originates from the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to deep red. The plant has fragrant petals and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.

This variety is also valued for its historical significance in traditional horticulture and floral arrangements.
