Ranunculus lutchuensis

Ranunculus lutchuensis, commonly known as the Lutchuensis buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the island of Lutchu (Lushan) in China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically yellow or white flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with deep lobes and produces small, clustered flowers in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
