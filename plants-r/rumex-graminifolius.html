Rumex graminifolius

Rumex graminifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by a cup-like structure called an involucre. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

Rumex graminifolius is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
