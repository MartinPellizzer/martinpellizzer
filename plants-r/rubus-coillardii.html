Rubus coillardii

Rubus coillardii, commonly known as the Coillard's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically clustered together on the stems. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant has thorny stems typical of the Rubus family.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
