Romulea bulbocodium f. purpurescens

Romulea bulbocodium f. purpurescens, commonly known as the purple-flowered romulea, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy purple or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a bulbous root system, which allows it to store nutrients and survive in dry periods. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while the flowers are borne on tall, slender stems.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in rock gardens and dry landscapes due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.
