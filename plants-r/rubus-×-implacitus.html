Rubus × implacitus

Rubus × implacitus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which are typical features of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an important food source for local wildlife and its adaptability to various soil conditions.
