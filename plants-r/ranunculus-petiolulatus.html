Ranunculus petiolulatus

Ranunculus petiolulatus, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive petiole structure. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
