Rubus pseudo-occitanicus

Rubus pseudo-occitanicus, commonly known as the Occitanian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
