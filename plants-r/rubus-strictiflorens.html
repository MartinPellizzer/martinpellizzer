Rubus strictiflorens

Rubus strictiflorens, commonly known as the rigid-flowered blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
