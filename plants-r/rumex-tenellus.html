Rumex tenellus

Rumex tenellus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a wavy margin and a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, upright spikes.

Rumex tenellus is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
