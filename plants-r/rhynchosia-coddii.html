Rhynchosia coddii

Rhynchosia coddii, commonly known as the Codd's burr.

It is a leguminous plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
