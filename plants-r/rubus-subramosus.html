Rubus subramosus

Rubus subramosus, commonly known as the slender blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, often forming dense thickets.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species, sharing similar floral and fruit structures.
