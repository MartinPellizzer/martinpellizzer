Rosa × dumetorum var. subglabra

Rosa × dumetorum var. subglabra, commonly known as the silky rose or woolly dog rose, is a hybrid species native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated margin and a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pink or pale red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
