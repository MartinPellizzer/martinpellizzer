Rubus laeteviridis

Rubus laeteviridis, commonly known as the greenleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves that are typically green on both sides. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are generally dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
