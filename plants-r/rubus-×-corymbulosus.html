Rubus × corymbulosus

Rubus × corymbulosus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (red raspberry).

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, oval leaves with serrated edges and is typically found in temperate regions. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rubus × corymbulosus is often cultivated for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and used in various culinary applications.
