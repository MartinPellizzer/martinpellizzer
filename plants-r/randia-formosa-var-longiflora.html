Randia formosa var. longiflora

Randia formosa var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
