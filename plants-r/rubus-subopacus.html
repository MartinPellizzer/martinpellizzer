Rubus subopacus

Rubus subopacus, commonly known as the dark raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
