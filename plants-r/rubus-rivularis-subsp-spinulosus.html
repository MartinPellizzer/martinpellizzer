Rubus rivularis subsp. spinulosus

Rubus rivularis subsp.

spinulosus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is often found in association with other bramble species.
