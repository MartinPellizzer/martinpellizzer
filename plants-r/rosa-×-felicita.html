Rosa × felicita

Rosa × felicita, commonly known as the 'Felicita' rose, is a hybrid cultivar resulting from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa centifolia.

It is a shrub with upright, thorny stems and dark green, finely serrated leaves. The plant produces abundant, fragrant blooms in shades of pink and white, typically in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are semi-double to fully double, with a strong, sweet aroma.

Rosa × felicita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and perfumery.
