Ranunculus vernyi var. hirsutus

Ranunculus vernyi var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
