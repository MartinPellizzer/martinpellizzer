Rubus candicans var. deltoideus

Rubus candicans var. deltoideus, commonly known as the delta raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though some cultivars may have yellow or orange berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
