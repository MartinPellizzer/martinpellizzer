Rubus fuscifolius

Rubus fuscifolius, commonly known as the dark-leaved blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to North America, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. Its leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, with a distinctive, serrated edge.

Rubus fuscifolius thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
