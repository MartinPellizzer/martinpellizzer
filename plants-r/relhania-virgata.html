Relhania virgata

Relhania virgata, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height, featuring a straight trunk and a dense, rounded crown. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy dark green upper surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in cymes, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
