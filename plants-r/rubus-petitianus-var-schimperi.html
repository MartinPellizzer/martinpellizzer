Rubus petitianus var. schimperi

Rubus petitianus var. schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian blackberry, is a shrub native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
