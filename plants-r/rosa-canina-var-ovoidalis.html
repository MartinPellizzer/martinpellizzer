Rosa canina var. ovoidalis

Rosa canina var. ovoidalis, commonly known as the oval rose, is a variant of the dog rose characterized by its elliptical-shaped hips.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers in early summer, followed by orange-red, fuzzy fruits in autumn. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval to lanceolate in shape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
