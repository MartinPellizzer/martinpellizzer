Ranunculus krylovii

Ranunculus krylovii, commonly known as Krylov's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
