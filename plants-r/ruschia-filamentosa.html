Ruschia filamentosa

Ruschia filamentosa, commonly known as the threadleaf ruschia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent perennial that produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red. The plant features slender, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
