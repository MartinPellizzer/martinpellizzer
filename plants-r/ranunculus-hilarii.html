Ranunculus hilarii

Ranunculus hilarii, commonly known as the Hilarii buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Ranunculus hilarii is often found in alpine and coastal habitats, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.
