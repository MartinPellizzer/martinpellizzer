Rosa acicularis var. sabinii

Rosa acicularis var. sabinii, commonly known as the Sabin's thorned rose, is a variant of the thorned rose species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly serrated edge. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to native plant collections.
