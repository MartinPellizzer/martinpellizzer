Rhodiola semenovii

Rhodiola semenovii, commonly known as the Siberian rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and the Far East.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It is known for its medicinal properties, containing high levels of rosavin and other adaptogenic compounds.

Rhodiola semenovii is often used in traditional medicine to enhance physical endurance and reduce stress.
