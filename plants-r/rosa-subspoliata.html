Rosa subspoliata

Rosa subspoliata, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves, typical of many rose species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

Rosa subspoliata is also valued for its historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
