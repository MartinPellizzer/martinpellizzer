Ranunculus doerflerianus

Ranunculus doerflerianus, commonly known as the Doerfler's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features delicate, palmately divided leaves and produces flowers with five to eight petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species.
