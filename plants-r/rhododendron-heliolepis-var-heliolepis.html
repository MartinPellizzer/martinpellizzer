Rhododendron heliolepis var. heliolepis

Rhododendron heliolepis var. heliolepis, commonly known as the sun-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink to red in color. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
