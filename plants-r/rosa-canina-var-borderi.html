Rosa canina var. borderi

Rosa canina var. borderi, commonly known as the Border rosehip, is a variant of the dog rose that is native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright red hips in the autumn, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy shrub suitable for hedgerows and gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and historical studies.
