Rubus usus

Rubus usus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple tiny drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants.

The plant is widely cultivated for its fruit and also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
