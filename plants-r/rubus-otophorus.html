Rubus otophorus

Rubus otophorus, commonly known as the "hairy raspberry," is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing features with other raspberries and blackberries.
