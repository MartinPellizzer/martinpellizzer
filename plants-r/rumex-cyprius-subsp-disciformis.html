Rumex cyprius subsp. disciformis

Rumex cyprius subsp.

disciformis, commonly known as Cyprus sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on erect, branching stalks. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, contributing to its tart flavor and making it a valued plant in traditional cuisines.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
