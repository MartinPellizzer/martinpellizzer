Rubus sulcatus subsp. altissimus

Rubus sulcatus subsp.

altissimus, commonly known as the tall evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically clustered together. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique adaptations to its ecological niche.
