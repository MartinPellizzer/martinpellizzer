Rollinia velutina

Rollinia velutina, commonly known as the velvet custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a dense, velvety canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, often with a waxy texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

The fruit is a yellow or orange berry with a thick, fleshy pulp and a single large seed.
