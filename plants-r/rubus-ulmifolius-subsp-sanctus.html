Rubus ulmifolius subsp. sanctus

Rubus ulmifolius subsp.

sanctus, commonly known as the holy blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitats.
