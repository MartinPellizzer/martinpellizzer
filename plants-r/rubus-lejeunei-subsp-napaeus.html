Rubus lejeunei subsp. napaeus

Rubus lejeunei subsp.

napaeus, commonly known as the Napaeus blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
