Rosa canina var. arnbergensis

Rosa canina var. arnbergensis, commonly known as the Arnberg rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
