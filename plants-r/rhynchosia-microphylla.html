Rhynchosia microphylla

Rhynchosia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used as a food source.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
