Rabdosia japonica f. albida

Rabdosia japonica f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese Rabdosia, is a compact, herbaceous perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine white or grayish pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers that are predominantly white or pale pink, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
