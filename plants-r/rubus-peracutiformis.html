Rubus peracutiformis

Rubus peracutiformis, commonly known as the sharp-pointed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
