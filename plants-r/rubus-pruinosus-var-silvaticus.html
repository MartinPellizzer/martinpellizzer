Rubus pruinosus var. silvaticus

Rubus pruinosus var. silvaticus, commonly known as the silvery blackberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark green, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters, contributing to its ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
