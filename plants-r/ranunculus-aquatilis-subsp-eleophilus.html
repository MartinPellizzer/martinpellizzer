Ranunculus aquatilis subsp. eleophilus

Ranunculus aquatilis subsp.

eleophilus, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often near streams, ponds, and lake margins. Its leaves are finely divided and arranged in a whorled pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies eleophilus is distinguished by its preference for sunny, open water environments compared to other members of the species.
