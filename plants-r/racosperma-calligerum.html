Racosperma calligerum

Racosperma calligerum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its pods are slender and woody.

Racosperma calligerum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
