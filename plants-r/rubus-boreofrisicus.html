Rubus boreofrisicus

Rubus boreofrisicus, commonly known as the northern bramble.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color, though they are often tart and less commonly consumed than other Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.
