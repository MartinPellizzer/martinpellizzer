Rosa leopoliensis

Rosa leopoliensis, commonly known as the Polish rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Rosa leopoliensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
