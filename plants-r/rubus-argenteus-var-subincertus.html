Rubus argenteus var. subincertus

Rubus argenteus var. subincertus, commonly known as silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
