Rubus pubescens var. subinermis

Rubus pubescens var. subinermis, commonly known as the soft-haired raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, thornless stems covered in soft, glandular hairs. The plant produces aggregate fruits, typically red or yellow, which are clustered together in a structure called a drupe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated margins, often with a hairy underside.

This variety is native to parts of Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
