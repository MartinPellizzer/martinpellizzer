Ranunculus karelinii

Ranunculus karelinii, commonly known as the Karelin's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent black veins. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is characterized by its short stature and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
