Rosa × pseudorusticana

Rosa × pseudorusticana, commonly known as the rustica rose, is a hybrid rose species that combines traits from its parent plants, Rosa rugosa and Rosa × centifolia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and coastal areas.

Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, adding texture to its overall appearance.
