Rhus rhodesiensis

Rhus rhodesiensis, commonly known as the Rhodesian sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like poison ivy and cashew. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into several narrow leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are a source of food for birds and small mammals.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
