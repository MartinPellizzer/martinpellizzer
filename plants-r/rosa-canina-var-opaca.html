Rosa canina var. opaca

Rosa canina var. opaca, commonly known as the opaque dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant pink flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and serrated. The fruit, known as a hip, is red and hairy, containing several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
