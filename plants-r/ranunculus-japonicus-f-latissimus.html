Ranunculus japonicus f. latissimus

Ranunculus japonicus f. latissimus, commonly known as the broadleaf Japanese buttercup, is a variant of the Japanese buttercup characterized by its wider, more elongated leaves.

This plant is native to East Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive dark center, and they bloom in early spring. The foliage is notable for its broad, serrated leaves that can reach several inches in length.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
