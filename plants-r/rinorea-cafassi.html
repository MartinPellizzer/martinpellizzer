Rinorea cafassi

Rinorea cafassi, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a smooth texture, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Rinorea cafassi is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
