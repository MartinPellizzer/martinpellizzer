Rhododendron campbelliae

Rhododendron campbelliae, commonly known as Campbell's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its flowers range in color from pale pink to deep red, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
