Ranunculus monophyllus subsp. vytegrensis

Ranunculus monophyllus subsp.

vytegrensis, commonly known as the Vytergren buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with prominent, rounded sepals that resemble petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a single flower per stem during the spring.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
