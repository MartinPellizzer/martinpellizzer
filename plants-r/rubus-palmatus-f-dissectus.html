Rubus palmatus f. dissectus

Rubus palmatus f. dissectus, commonly known as the dissected raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry that exhibits distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are deeply lobed and finely dissected, giving them a feathery appearance. This plant is native to regions of North America, particularly in the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor.
