Rhynchocarpa welwitschii

Rhynchocarpa welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia tree, is a unique plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its two large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Rhynchocarpa welwitschii is adapted to extreme arid conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater.
