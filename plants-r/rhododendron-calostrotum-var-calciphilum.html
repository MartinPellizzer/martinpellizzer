Rhododendron calostrotum var. calciphilum

Rhododendron calostrotum var. calciphilum, commonly known as the limestone rhododendron, is a variant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils, particularly those found in limestone-rich environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, while its flowers range from pink to red, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and rocky slopes.
