Ruellia exilis

Ruellia exilis, commonly known as slender ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the warm climates of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are usually blue or purple in color, attracting various pollinators.

Ruellia exilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
