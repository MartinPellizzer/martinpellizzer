Rubus sinensis

Rubus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.

Rubus sinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
