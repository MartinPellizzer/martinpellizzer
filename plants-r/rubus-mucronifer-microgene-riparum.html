Rubus mucronifer microgene riparum

Rubus mucronifer microgene riparum, commonly known as the riparian raspberry, is a shrub native to wetland and streamside habitats.

It has compound leaves with serrated edges and small, red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red or black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
