Ruellia dissidens

Ruellia dissidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the species. Ruellia dissidens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
