Rubus pinetorum f. elatus

Rubus pinetorum f. elatus, commonly known as the elevated blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and thorny stems, which support its climbing habit. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and more flavorful than those of the standard species. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a robust root system that helps it thrive in various soil conditions.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
