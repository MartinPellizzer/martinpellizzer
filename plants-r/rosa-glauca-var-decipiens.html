Rosa glauca var. decipiens

Rosa glauca var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive blue rose, is a rare and striking variant of the blue rose.

It is characterized by its pale blue to almost white petals, which give it a unique appearance. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cooler climates. The flowers typically bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a subtle elegance to its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticultural enthusiasts alike.
