Rhododendron guihainianum

Rhododendron guihainianum, commonly known as the Guizhou rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Guizhou Province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils within cool, moist environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron guihainianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
