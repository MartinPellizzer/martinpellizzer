Rubus villicaulis var. atrocaulis

Rubus villicaulis var. atrocaulis, commonly known as the black-stemmed bramble, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible berries in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique morphological traits that set it apart from other related species.
