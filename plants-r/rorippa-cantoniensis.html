Rorippa cantoniensis

Rorippa cantoniensis, commonly known as the Guangdong watercress, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. It has small, green, alternate leaves and produces small white or yellow flowers.

Its seeds are often dispersed by water, contributing to its ability to colonize moist environments.
