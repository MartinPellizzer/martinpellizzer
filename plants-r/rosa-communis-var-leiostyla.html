Rosa communis var. leiostyla

Rosa communis var. leiostyla, commonly known as the silky rose, is a cultivar prized for its soft, velvety petals that give it a unique texture.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to China, where it has been cultivated for centuries. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. Its botanical characteristics include compound leaves with serrated edges and a flowering period that usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
