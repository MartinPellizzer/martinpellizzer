Rabdosia coetsa var. hookeri

Rabdosia coetsa var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters, often in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
