Ravenia leonis

Ravenia leonis, commonly known as the lion's raven, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Ravenia leonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
