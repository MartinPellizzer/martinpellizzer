Ribes opulifolium

Ribes opulifolium, commonly known as the red currant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often lobed and resemble those of the maple tree. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. These berries are edible and are often used in culinary applications such as jams and wines.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is commonly found in moist, well-drained soils.
