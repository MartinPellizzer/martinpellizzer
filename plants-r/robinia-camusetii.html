Robinia camusetii

Robinia camusetii, commonly known as the Camuset's acacia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it has a distinctive, twisted trunk.

Robinia camusetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
