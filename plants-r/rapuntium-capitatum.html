Rapuntium capitatum

Rapuntium capitatum, commonly known as the edible cress, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a compact rosette of dark green, toothed leaves with a peppery aroma. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by spherical, greenish-brown seed pods. Its roots are also edible and have a similar peppery flavor to the leaves.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
