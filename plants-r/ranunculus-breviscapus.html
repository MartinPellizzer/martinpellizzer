Ranunculus breviscapus

Ranunculus breviscapus, commonly known as the short-stemmed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. The plant features showy yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy underside. It has a short, thick rhizome and produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus breviscapus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
