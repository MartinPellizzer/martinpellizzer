Rosa maximiliani

Rosa maximiliani, commonly known as the Maximilian rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their strong, sweet scent.

Rosa maximiliani is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical significance in botanical research.
