Rosa kanzanensis

Rosa kanzanensis, commonly known as the Kanzan rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is prized for its showy, double blooms. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive flat shape and a high degree of symmetry. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as rosehips, which are ornamental and edible.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively disease-resistant.
