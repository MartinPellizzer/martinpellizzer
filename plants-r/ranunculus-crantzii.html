Ranunculus crantzii

Ranunculus crantzii, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five to seven sepals that resemble petals.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
