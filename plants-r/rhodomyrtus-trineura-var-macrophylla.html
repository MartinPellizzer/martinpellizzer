Rhodomyrtus trineura var. macrophylla

Rhodomyrtus trineura var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed rose myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that appear in summer. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
