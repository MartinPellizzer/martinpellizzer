Rechsteineria canescens var. obovata

Rechsteineria canescens var. obovata, commonly known as the woolly rechsteineria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are ovate to obovate in shape, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
