Rhoicissus tomentosa

Rhoicissus tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf creeper, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Rhoicissus tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a living fence.
