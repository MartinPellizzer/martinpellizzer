Rosa coccialba

Rosa coccialba, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa coccialba is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscapes.
