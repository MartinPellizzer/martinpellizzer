Rubus procerus f. divergens

Rubus procerus f. divergens, commonly known as the erect blackberry.

This plant is a variant of the blackberry species, characterized by its upright growth habit and distinct floral and fruit structures. It typically features prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit develops into a dark purple or black berry.

It is often found in woodland areas and thrives in temperate climates.
