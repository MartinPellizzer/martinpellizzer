Rhododendron nudipes f. gracilescens

Rhododendron nudipes f. gracilescens, commonly known as the slenderleaf rhododendron, is a unique variant of the rhododendron species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, leathery leaves that are typically shorter and narrower than those of the typical species. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in early spring. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a container plant.

The foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.
