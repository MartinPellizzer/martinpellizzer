Rosa agrestis var. denudata

Rosa agrestis var. denudata, commonly known as the naked-stem dogrose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces oval, yellowish-green fruits known as hips, which are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded areas.

Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it is a hardy species that can tolerate partial shade.
