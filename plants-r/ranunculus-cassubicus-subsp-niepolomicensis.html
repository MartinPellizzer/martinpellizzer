Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. niepolomicensis

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

niepolomicensis, commonly known as the Niepolomice buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals. It is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
