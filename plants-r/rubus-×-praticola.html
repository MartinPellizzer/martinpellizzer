Rubus × praticola

Rubus × praticola, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color, containing small, hard seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
