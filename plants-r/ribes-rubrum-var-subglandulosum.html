Ribes rubrum var. subglandulosum

Ribes rubrum var. subglandulosum, commonly known as the smooth gooseberry, is a variant of the common gooseberry.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces edible, tart berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are usually pink to red, appearing in clusters, and it is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

This variety is notable for its slightly less hairy foliage compared to other Ribes species.
