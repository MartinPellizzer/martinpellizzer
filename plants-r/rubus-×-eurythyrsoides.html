Rubus × eurythyrsoides

Rubus × eurythyrsoides, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets, which are typically dark purple to black in color. This plant is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, including moist and well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
