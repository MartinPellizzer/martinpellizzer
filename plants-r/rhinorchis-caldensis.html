Rhinorchis caldensis

Rhinorchis caldensis, commonly known as the Calden's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. This plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often more brightly colored.

Rhinorchis caldensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
