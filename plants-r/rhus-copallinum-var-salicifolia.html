Rhus copallinum var. salicifolia

Rhus copallinum var. salicifolia, commonly known as the silky maple or smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a soft, silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that mature in the fall. Its bark is smooth and light gray, often peeling in thin layers.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
