Rhipidorchis micrantha

Rhipidorchis micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pale green or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some parts of its range.
