Ruppia brevipes

Ruppia brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed water plant, is a halophytic species native to coastal and brackish water environments.

It is characterized by its submerged or floating leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in shallow, saline waters and is often found in estuaries and tidal marshes. Its reproductive strategy involves producing numerous small seeds that are dispersed by water currents.

Ruppia brevipes plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
