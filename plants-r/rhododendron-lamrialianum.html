Rhododendron lamrialianum

Rhododendron lamrialianum, commonly known as the Lamrialian rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, preferring acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron lamrialianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in specific ecological niches.
