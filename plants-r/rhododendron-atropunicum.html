Rhododendron atropunicum

Rhododendron atropunicum, commonly known as the black-barked rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Nepal and India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features dark, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of red and pink during the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a dense, compact growth habit.

Rhododendron atropunicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountainous ecosystems.
