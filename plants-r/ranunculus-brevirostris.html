Ranunculus brevirostris

Ranunculus brevirostris, commonly known as the short-beaked buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to seven sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus brevirostris is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in high-altitude environments.
