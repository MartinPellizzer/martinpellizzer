Rubia transalaica

Rubia transalaica, commonly known as Transalpine knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Rubia transalaica is also known for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
