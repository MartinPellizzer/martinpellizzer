Rubus silvaticus subsp. debilispinus

Rubus silvaticus subsp.

debilispinus, commonly known as the weak-spined bramble, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant has slender stems with soft, hooked prickles, and its leaves are trifoliate with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters, followed by red, edible berries.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
