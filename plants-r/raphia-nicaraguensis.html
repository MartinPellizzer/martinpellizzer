Raphia nicaraguensis

Raphia nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan palm, is a large, fan-shaped palm native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, glossy, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that develop into clustered fruits, each containing a single, hard-shelled seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
