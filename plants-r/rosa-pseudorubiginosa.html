Rosa pseudorubiginosa

Rosa pseudorubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features arching stems that are reddish in color, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink to white, with five petals and a fragrant aroma.

Rosa pseudorubiginosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
