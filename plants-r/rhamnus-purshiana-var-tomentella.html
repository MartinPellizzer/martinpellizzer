Rhamnus purshiana var. tomentella

Rhamnus purshiana var. tomentella, commonly known as the Pacific dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the region's ecological systems.
