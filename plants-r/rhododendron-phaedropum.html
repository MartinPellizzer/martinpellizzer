Rhododendron phaedropum

Rhododendron phaedropum, commonly known as the purple-leaf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that turns a striking purple hue in the fall. The plant produces clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are typically borne on long, pendulous stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
