Rhynchosia harae

Rhynchosia harae, commonly known as the Harare velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often used in agricultural systems for its nitrogen-fixing properties.

The seeds are edible and can be processed into a flour used in local cuisines.
