Rivea clarkeana

Rivea clarkeana, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it popular in gardens and as a houseplant.

The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.
