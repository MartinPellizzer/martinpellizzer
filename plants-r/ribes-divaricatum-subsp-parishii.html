Ribes divaricatum subsp. parishii

Ribes divaricatum subsp.

parishii, commonly known as the Parish gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.

The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its fruit, a red, fleshy berry, matures in late summer and is edible, though often tart.

The species is part of the genus Ribes, which includes many species of gooseberries and currants.
