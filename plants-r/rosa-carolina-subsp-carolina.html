Rosa carolina subsp. carolina

Rosa carolina subsp.

carolina, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
