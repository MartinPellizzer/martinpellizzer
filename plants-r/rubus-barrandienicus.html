Rubus barrandienicus

Rubus barrandienicus, commonly known as the Barrandian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs with thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in forest edges and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its dark purple berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compound leaves and persistent sepals that remain attached to the fruit.
