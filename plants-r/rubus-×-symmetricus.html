Rubus × symmetricus

Rubus × symmetricus, commonly known as the symmetric blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the evergreen blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (the coastal blackberry). This species is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in regions with mild climates.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species, suitable for both ornamental and agricultural use.
