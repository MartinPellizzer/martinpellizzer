Rhynchospora fascicularis

Rhynchospora fascicularis, commonly known as the fascicled flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with three-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Rhynchospora fascicularis thrives in saturated soils and is often found in areas with standing water or frequent flooding.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
