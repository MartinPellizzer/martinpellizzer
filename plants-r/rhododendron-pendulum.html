Rhododendron pendulum

Rhododendron pendulum, commonly known as the weeping rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Rhododendron pendulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique form and seasonal blooms.
