Rubus serpens var. subvelutinus

Rubus serpens var. subvelutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence on the leaves and stems, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically clustered together. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
