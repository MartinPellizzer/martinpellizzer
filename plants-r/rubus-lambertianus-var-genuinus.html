Rubus lambertianus var. genuinus

Rubus lambertianus var. genuinus, commonly known as the Lambert's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible, though they have a tart flavor and are often used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.
