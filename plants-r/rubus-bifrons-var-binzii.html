Rubus bifrons var. binzii

Rubus bifrons var. binzii, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to Japan.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when mature, containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
