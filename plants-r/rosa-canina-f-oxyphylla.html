Rosa canina f. oxyphylla

Rosa canina f. oxyphylla, commonly known as the oxyphylla rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sharply serrated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces red, hairy hips that are rich in vitamin C and often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
