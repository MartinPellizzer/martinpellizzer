Ranunculus lancifolius

Ranunculus lancifolius, commonly known as the lance-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals that are typically slightly notched at the edges. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Ranunculus lancifolius is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a notable wildflower and a potential weed in certain habitats.
