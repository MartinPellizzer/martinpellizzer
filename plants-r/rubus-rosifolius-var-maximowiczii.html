Rubus rosifolius var. maximowiczii

Rubus rosifolius var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a species native to East Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
