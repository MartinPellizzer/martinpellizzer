Ranunculus subpannonicus

Ranunculus subpannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, hairy seeds in its fruit.

Ranunculus subpannonicus is often found in calcareous habitats and is considered a characteristic species of the Pannonian region.
