Rosa rubeoides

Rosa rubeoides, commonly known as the rough rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, typically growing in rocky or dry habitats. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the fruits are red, hairy berries.

Rosa rubeoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
