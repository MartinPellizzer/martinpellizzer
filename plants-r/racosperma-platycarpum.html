Racosperma platycarpum

Racosperma platycarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruited acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous tiny leaflets, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Racosperma platycarpum is often found in sandy soils and is used in ecological restoration due to its resilience and nitrogen-fixing ability.
