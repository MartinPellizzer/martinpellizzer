Rudbeckia triloba var. pinnatiloba

Rudbeckia triloba var. pinnatiloba, commonly known as the pinnate rudbeckia, is a variant of the coneflower native to North America.

It features striking yellow-orange ray flowers surrounded by a dark brown to black conical disk. The plant has lance-shaped, pinnately lobed leaves that are often hairy and provide a textured appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
