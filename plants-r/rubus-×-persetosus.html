Rubus × persetosus

Rubus × persetosus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are often used in culinary applications.

Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
