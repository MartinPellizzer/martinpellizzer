Ranunculus insulae-mauritiae

Ranunculus insulae-mauritiae, commonly known as the Mauritius crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are slender and hairy.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
