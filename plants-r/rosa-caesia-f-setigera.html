Rosa caesia f. setigera

Rosa caesia f. setigera, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa caesia species.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in temperate climates and is known for its fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its foliage is often described as being more densely hairy compared to other rose varieties.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil conditions.
