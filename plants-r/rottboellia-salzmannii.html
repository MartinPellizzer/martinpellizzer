Rottboellia salzmannii

Rottboellia salzmannii, commonly known as the dwarf umbrella grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit and produces small, fluffy seed heads that resemble miniature umbrellas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental grass.

Its botanical features make it suitable for use in landscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
