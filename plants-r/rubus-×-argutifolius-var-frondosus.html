Rubus × argutifolius var. frondosus

Rubus × argutifolius var. frondosus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This variant is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. The plant is native to parts of North America and thrives in temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in various culinary applications.
