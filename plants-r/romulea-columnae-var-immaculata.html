Romulea columnae var. immaculata

Romulea columnae var. immaculata, commonly known as the white-veined romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers with a distinctive white or pale yellow coloration. The flowers often have a subtle veining pattern that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
