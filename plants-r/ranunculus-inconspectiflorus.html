Ranunculus inconspectiflorus

Ranunculus inconspectiflorus, commonly known as the uncommon buttercup, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces small, aggregate fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species.
