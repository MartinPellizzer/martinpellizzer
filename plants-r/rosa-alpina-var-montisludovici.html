Rosa alpina var. montisludovici

Rosa alpina var. montisludovici, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a variant of the alpine rose native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, well-drained soils. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in early summer, which are followed by red-orange hips. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of white down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in harsh climates.
