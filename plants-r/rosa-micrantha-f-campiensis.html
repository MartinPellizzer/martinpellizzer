Rosa micrantha f. campiensis

Rosa micrantha f. campiensis, commonly known as the Campinas rose, is a compact shrub native to Brazil.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces hips that are edible but generally not consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The variety is characterized by its dense growth habit and adaptability to various soil conditions.
