Rosularia adenotricha var. genuinum

Rosularia adenotricha var. genuinum, commonly known as the Desert Rose.

This succulent plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It produces small, pale yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its arid landscape. Native to arid regions of Central Asia, it is well adapted to dry, rocky environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
