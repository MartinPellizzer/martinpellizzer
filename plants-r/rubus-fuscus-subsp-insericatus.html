Rubus fuscus subsp. insericatus

Rubus fuscus subsp.

insericatus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated margin and a pale underside. Its flowers are small, white to pink, and appear in clusters, followed by dark purple to black berries.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
