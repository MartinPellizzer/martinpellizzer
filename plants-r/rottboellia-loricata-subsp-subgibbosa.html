Rottboellia loricata subsp. subgibbosa

Rottboellia loricata subsp.

subgibbosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bottle grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive bottle-shaped seed heads. It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
