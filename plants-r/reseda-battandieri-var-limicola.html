Reseda battandieri var. limicola

Reseda battandieri var. limicola, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and gray-green, hairy leaves adapted to salty, windy environments. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its flowers bloom from summer to autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Reseda battandieri var. limicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal habitats.
