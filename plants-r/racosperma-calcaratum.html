Racosperma calcaratum

Racosperma calcaratum, commonly known as the mountain wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. It features small yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are usually bipinnate, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its ecological significance and unique adaptations, it is often found in conservation efforts across its native habitat.
