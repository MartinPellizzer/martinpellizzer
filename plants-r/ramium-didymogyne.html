Ramium didymogyne

Ramium didymogyne, commonly known as the two-lobed grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

Ramium didymogyne is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
