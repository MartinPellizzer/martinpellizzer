Rhipidodendrum plicatile

Rhipidodendrum plicatile, commonly known as the zigzag staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, overlapping leaf structures that form a crinkled, shield-like appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and often grows on trees or rocks. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The zigzag staghorn fern is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
