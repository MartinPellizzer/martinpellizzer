Ranunculus palmatifidus

Ranunculus palmatifidus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of deeply lobed, palmately divided leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
