Ranunculus auricomus subsp. alnetorum

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

alnetorum, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically borne on slender stems above basal leaves.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural gardens.
