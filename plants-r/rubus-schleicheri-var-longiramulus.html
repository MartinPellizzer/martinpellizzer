Rubus schleicheri var. longiramulus

Rubus schleicheri var. longiramulus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of East Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry species.
