Rhododendron calendulaceum

Rhododendron calendulaceum, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from orange to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It has dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The flowers bloom in late spring, adding vibrant color to woodland landscapes.
