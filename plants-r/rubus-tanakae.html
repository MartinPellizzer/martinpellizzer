Rubus tanakae

Rubus tanakae, commonly known as Tanaka's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
