Rosa elisophora

Rosa elisophora, commonly known as the Elisabeth rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

Rosa elisophora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
