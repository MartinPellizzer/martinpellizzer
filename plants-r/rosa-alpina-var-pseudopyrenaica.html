Rosa alpina var. pseudopyrenaica

Rosa alpina var. pseudopyrenaica, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness in cold climates.
