Rapistrum ambiguum

Rapistrum ambiguum, commonly known as the wild radish, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found along roadsides and in fields. It produces small, yellow flowers and has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in various environments.

Its leaves are usually lobed and have a slightly bitter taste.
