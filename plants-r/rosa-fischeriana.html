Rosa fischeriana

Rosa fischeriana, commonly known as the Fischer's rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins and is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats. Rosa fischeriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
