Rosa pimpinellifolia var. typica

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. typica, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a thorny shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces red, hairy fruits called hips. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedging and as a source of rose hips.
