Ranunculus kitadakeanus

Ranunculus kitadakeanus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. It has compound leaves with three leaflets and produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus kitadakeanus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
