Rudbeckia cicutefolia

Rudbeckia cicutefolia, commonly known as the slender coneflower, is a North American plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and daisy-like yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Rudbeckia cicutefolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
