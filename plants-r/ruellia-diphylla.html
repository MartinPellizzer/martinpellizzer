Ruellia diphylla

Ruellia diphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct silvery-green color. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ruellia diphylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
