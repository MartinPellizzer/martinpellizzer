Rondeletia alaternoides subsp. brachyloba

Rondeletia alaternoides subsp.

brachyloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rondeletia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It features small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
