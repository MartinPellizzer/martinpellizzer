Ribes floridum var. puberulum

Ribes floridum var. puberulum, commonly known as the Florida gooseberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, red, edible berries that are typically harvested in the fall. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor, making them useful in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the genus Ribes and is closely related to other members of the gooseberry family, such as currants and gooseberries.
