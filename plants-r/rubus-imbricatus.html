Rubus imbricatus

Rubus imbricatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Rubus imbricatus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
