Rhododendron pauciflorum var. calocodon

Rhododendron pauciflorum var. calocodon, commonly known as the Calocodon rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation areas.
