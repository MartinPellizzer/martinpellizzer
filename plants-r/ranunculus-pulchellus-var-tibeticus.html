Ranunculus pulchellus var. tibeticus

Ranunculus pulchellus var. tibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a hairy texture that helps protect it from harsh weather conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
