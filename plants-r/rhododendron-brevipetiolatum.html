Rhododendron brevipetiolatum

Rhododendron brevipetiolatum, commonly known as the short-petioled rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its floral display, which occurs in spring, features vibrant pink to purple hues, making it a notable ornamental plant.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to rocky and well-drained soils.
