Rosa mollis f. griseola

Rosa mollis f. griseola, commonly known as the gray-flowered shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, grayish-white bloom. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and a fragrant aroma, blooming in late spring to early summer. This variety is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to various garden settings.
