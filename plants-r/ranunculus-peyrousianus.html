Ranunculus peyrousianus

Ranunculus peyrousianus, commonly known as Peyrou's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
