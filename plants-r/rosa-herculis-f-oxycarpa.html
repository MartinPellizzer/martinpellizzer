Rosa herculis f. oxycarpa

Rosa herculis f. oxycarpa, commonly known as the Hercules rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its robust growth and distinctive botanical features.

It typically exhibits large, showy flowers with a deep red color and a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
