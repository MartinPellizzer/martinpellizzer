Rosa sempervirens var. moschata

Rosa sempervirens var. moschata, commonly known as the musk rose, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is known for its strong, musk-like scent, which is particularly noticeable when the flowers are in full bloom. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and hedgerows.

The variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
