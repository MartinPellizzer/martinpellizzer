Rosa sarmentacea

Rosa sarmentacea, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems that grow close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with narrow, pointed leaflets.

Rosa sarmentacea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to prevent soil erosion.
