Rubus serpens var. symmetrus

Rubus serpens var. symmetrus, commonly known as the symmetrical blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its unique symmetrical growth patterns in its bracts and foliage. The plant is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible.

The botanical interest of this variant lies in its distinct morphology and potential genetic uniqueness.
