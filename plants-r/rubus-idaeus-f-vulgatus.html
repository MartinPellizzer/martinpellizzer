Rubus idaeus f. vulgatus

Rubus idaeus f. vulgatus, commonly known as the common raspberry.

It is a cultivated variant of the red raspberry, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupelets. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely domesticated for its edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically pink or white, and the plant is often used in both culinary and ornamental applications.
