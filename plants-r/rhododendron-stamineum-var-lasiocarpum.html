Rhododendron stamineum var. lasiocarpum

Rhododendron stamineum var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the woolly fruit rhododendron, is a variant of the evergreen rhododendron native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on the calyx and bracts, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, and it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
