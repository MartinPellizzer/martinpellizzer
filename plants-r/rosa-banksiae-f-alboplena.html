Rosa banksiae f. alboplena

Rosa banksiae f. alboplena, commonly known as the White Banks Rose, is a cultivar of the Banks rose, characterized by its striking white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, double blooms in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorned stems and dark green, lobed leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its showy flowers. It is relatively low maintenance and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
