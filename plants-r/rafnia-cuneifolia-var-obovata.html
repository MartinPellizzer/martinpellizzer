Rafnia cuneifolia var. obovata

Rafnia cuneifolia var. obovata, commonly known as the broadleaf sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are broad and obovate, with a smooth, waxy surface. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its bark is typically gray and peels in thin, papery layers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
