Rubus hirtus microgene crassus

Rubus hirtus microgene crassus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are usually serrated with a hairy texture, and the flowers are white or pale green.

The fruit, which is edible when ripe, has a distinctive tart flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
