Rapuntium acutangulum

Rapuntium acutangulum, commonly known as the sharp-angled cress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. It features small, white or yellow flowers and pinnate leaves with a peppery aroma.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a leaf vegetable or used in traditional medicine.
