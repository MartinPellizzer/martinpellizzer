Rosa pulverulenta var. eriocarpa

Rosa pulverulenta var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the wooly rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a leathery texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid climates.
