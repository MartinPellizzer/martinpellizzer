Rosa elliptica var. minuta

Rosa elliptica var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf elliptic rose, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
