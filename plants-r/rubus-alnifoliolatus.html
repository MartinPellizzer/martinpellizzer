Rubus alnifoliolatus

Rubus alnifoliolatus, commonly known as the alderleaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which resemble those of alder trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
