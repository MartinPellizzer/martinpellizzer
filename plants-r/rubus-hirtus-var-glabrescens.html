Rubus hirtus var. glabrescens

Rubus hirtus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the hairy blackberry or smooth blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small white flowers in the spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is often cultivated for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor.
