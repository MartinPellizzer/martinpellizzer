Ranunculus subcorymbosus var. manshuricus

Ranunculus subcorymbosus var. manshuricus, commonly known as the Far Eastern buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent, showy sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its short, upright stems and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
