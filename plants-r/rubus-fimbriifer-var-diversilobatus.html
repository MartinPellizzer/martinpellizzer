Rubus fimbriifer var. diversilobatus

Rubus fimbriifer var. diversilobatus, commonly known as the variable-lobed raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with varying lobes. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
