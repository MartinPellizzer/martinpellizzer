Rubus ignarus

Rubus ignarus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in the axils of the leaves.

The fruit, a small, dark purple berry, is edible but often described as having a tart and astringent flavor.
