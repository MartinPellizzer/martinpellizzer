Rubia haematantha

Rubia haematantha, commonly known as the red-rooted woodruff, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its roots are reddish in color, giving it its common name, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Rubia haematantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
