Ranunculus globosus

Ranunculus globosus, commonly known as the globe globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a short, stout stem that supports a single, globe-shaped flower head composed of numerous small, yellowish-white petals. The plant produces a cluster of bright yellow stamens surrounded by a ring of petal-like sepals, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are palmately divided into several lobes, and the plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats.

Ranunculus globosus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
