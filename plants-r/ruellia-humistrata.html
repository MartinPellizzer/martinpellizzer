Ruellia humistrata

Ruellia humistrata, commonly known as the swamp ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and moist soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. It is often found in swampy areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Ruellia humistrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in humid, wet environments.
