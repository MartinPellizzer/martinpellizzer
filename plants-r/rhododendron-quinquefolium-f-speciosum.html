Rhododendron quinquefolium f. speciosum

Rhododendron quinquefolium f. speciosum, commonly known as the showy mountain azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive blotch on the upper lip of the petals. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in woodland or mountainous areas. Its leaves are leathery, elliptical, and arranged in whorls of five, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
