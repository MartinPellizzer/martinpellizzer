Rubus acalyphaceus

Rubus acalyphaceus, commonly known as the velvetleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white or pale pink and are borne in clusters.

It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.
