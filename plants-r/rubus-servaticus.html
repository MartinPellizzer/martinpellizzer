Rubus servaticus

Rubus servaticus, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Rubus servaticus is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas across temperate regions.
