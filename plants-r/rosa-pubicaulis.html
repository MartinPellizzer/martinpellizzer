Rosa pubicaulis

Rosa pubicaulis, commonly known as the pubescent rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions with moist, woodland habitats. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Rosa pubicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
