Ricinocarpus depressinervius

Ricinocarpus depressinervius, commonly known as the broad-leafed ricin tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves with a broad, ovate shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.

The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
