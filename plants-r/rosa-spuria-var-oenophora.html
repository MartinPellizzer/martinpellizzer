Rosa spuria var. oenophora

Rosa spuria var. oenophora, commonly known as the wine grape rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, edible hips that are rich in vitamin C and often used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with serrated leaflets.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical use in winemaking.
