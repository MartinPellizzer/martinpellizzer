Rivina purpurascens var. mollis

Rivina purpurascens var. mollis, commonly known as the softleaf rivina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
