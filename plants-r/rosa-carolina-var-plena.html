Rosa carolina var. plena

Rosa carolina var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered Carolina rose, is a cultivar of the native Rosa carolina species.

It is characterized by its abundant, full-bloomed pink flowers that are highly ornamental. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its historical significance and use in early American gardens.
