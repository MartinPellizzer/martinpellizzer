Racosperma penninerve var. longiracemosum

Racosperma penninerve var. longiracemosum, commonly known as the long-branch wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant features long, racemose inflorescences, which are clusters of small, pea-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
