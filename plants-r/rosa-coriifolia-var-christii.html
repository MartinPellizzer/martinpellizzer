Rosa coriifolia var. christii

Rosa coriifolia var. christii, commonly known as the Christ's sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorny stems and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and showy blooms.
