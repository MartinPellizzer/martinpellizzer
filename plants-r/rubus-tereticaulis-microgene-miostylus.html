Rubus tereticaulis microgene miostylus

Rubus tereticaulis microgene miostylus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, dark red to black drupes that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white to pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.
