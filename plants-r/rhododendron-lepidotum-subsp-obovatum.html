Rhododendron lepidotum subsp. obovatum

Rhododendron lepidotum subsp.

obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and leathery in texture.

The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to reddish flowers during the spring season. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

This subspecies is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
