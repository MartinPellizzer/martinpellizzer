Rubus schleicheri var. lamprothyrsus

Rubus schleicheri var. lamprothyrsus, commonly known as the glossy thorned blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge and a glossy upper surface. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
