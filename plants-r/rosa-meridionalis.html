Rosa meridionalis

Rosa meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern Rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red in color. Rosa meridionalis produces fragrant, five-petaled blooms during the spring and early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
