Renealmia occidentalis var. macrocarpa

Renealmia occidentalis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant reed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are often clustered at the top of the stems. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, waxy surface.

This species is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
