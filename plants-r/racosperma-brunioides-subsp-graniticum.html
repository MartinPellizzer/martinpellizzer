Racosperma brunioides subsp. graniticum

Racosperma brunioides subsp.

graniticum, commonly known as the granite wattle, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
