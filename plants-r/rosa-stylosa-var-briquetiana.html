Rosa stylosa var. briquetiana

Rosa stylosa var. briquetiana, commonly known as the Briquet's shrub rose, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Thailand.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and stipules, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
