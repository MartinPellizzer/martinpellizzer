Rubus ptilocarpus

Rubus ptilocarpus, commonly known as the long-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter flavor.
