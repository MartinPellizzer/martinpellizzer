Regelia marechalii

Regelia marechalii, commonly known as the Marechal's regelia, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners who appreciate exotic plants.
