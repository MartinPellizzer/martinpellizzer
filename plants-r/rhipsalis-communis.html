Rhipsalis communis

Rhipsalis communis, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and segmented appearance. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Rhipsalis communis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
