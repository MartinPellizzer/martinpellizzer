Ricinocarpus bipartitus

Ricinocarpus bipartitus, commonly known as the two-parted sea bean, is a tropical shrub native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like leaves that are divided into two lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in coastal environments and is often found in sandy or muddy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in marine and coastal ecosystems.
