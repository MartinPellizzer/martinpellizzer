Rhodax origanifolius

Rhodax origanifolius, commonly known as the sweet violet, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and shady gardens, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhodax origanifolius is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
