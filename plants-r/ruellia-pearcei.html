Ruellia pearcei

Ruellia pearcei, commonly known as the Mexican bluebells, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Ruellia pearcei thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
