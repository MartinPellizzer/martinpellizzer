Rubus wahlii

Rubus wahlii, commonly known as the Wahlii blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has potential for cultivation in suitable environments.
