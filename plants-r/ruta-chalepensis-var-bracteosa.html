Ruta chalepensis var. bracteosa

Ruta chalepensis var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate rue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, pinnately compound leaves with a strong, bitter odor. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is closely related to other rutaceae species and is valued for its medicinal and ornamental properties.
