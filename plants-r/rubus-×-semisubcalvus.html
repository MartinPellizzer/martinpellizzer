Rubus × semisubcalvus

Rubus × semisubcalvus, commonly known as the partially hairy blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that typically ripen to a deep black color. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has a woody structure typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural study.
