Rubus mucronatus var. oboranus

Rubus mucronatus var. oboranus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and shrublands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
