Rubus corylifolius var. ambifarius

Rubus corylifolius var. ambifarius, commonly known as the double-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are deeply lobed and have a unique double-leaf appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
