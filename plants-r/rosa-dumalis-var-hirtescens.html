Rosa dumalis var. hirtescens

Rosa dumalis var. hirtescens, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets with a slightly serrated edge.

This species is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.
