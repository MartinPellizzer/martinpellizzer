Ranunculus aquatilis var. radiatus

Ranunculus aquatilis var. radiatus, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It features delicate, floating or submerged leaves that are typically divided into fine lobes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens, giving it a radiant appearance. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams.

Its presence is often an indicator of clean, well-oxygenated water.
