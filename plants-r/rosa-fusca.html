Rosa fusca

Rosa fusca, commonly known as the dark rose or swamp rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly wetlands and swampy areas. The plant is characterized by its dark red to purplish flowers and hairy, often glandular stems and leaves. Rosa fusca typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes or along the edges of ponds.

It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.
