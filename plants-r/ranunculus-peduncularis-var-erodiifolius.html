Ranunculus peduncularis var. erodiifolius

Ranunculus peduncularis var. erodiifolius, commonly known as the erodium-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately divided, with a hairy surface, and stem leaves that are alternate and often reduced. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.
