Rubus × suaveolens

Rubus × suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its attractive, edible berries.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats across North America. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its berries are usually red or orange when ripe and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
