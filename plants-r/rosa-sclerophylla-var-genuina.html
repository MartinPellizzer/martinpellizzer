Rosa sclerophylla var. genuina

Rosa sclerophylla var. genuina, commonly known as the coastal rose, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal habitats. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, typical of many members of the Rosaceae family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand coastal conditions.
