Rhododendron poukhanense

Rhododendron poukhanense, commonly known as the Poukhanen rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive elliptical shape, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the local ecosystems of its native habitats.
