Rosa gallica var. austriaca

Rosa gallica var. austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian gallica rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to central Europe.

It produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, trifoliate leaves and a generally upright growth habit. It is well-suited for garden borders and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
