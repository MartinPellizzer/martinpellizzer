Rubus × podhradiensis

Rubus × podhradiensis, commonly known as the Czech raspberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus idaeus (red raspberry) and Rubus strigosus (black raspberry). This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruits are aggregate berries that can range in color from red to black.

It is often cultivated for its flavorful berries and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
