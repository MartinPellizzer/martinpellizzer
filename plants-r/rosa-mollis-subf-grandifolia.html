Rosa mollis subf. grandifolia

Rosa mollis subf.

grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered shrub rose, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and broad, leathery leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to pink, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. These flowers are borne on long pedicels and are typically clustered in groups of three to five.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
