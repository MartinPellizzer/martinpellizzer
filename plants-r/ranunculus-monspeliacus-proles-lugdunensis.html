Ranunculus monspeliacus proles lugdunensis

Ranunculus monspeliacus proles lugdunensis, commonly known as the French buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent yellow center, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are palmately lobed, with a hairy underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
