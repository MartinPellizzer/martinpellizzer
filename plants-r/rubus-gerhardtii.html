Rubus gerhardtii

Rubus gerhardtii, commonly known as the Gerhardt's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing features with other members of the Rubus genus.
