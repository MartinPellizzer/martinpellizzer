Rubus communis

Rubus communis, commonly known as blackberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Rubus communis thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
