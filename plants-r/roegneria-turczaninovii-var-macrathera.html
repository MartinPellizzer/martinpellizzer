Roegneria turczaninovii var. macrathera

Roegneria turczaninovii var. macrathera, commonly known as the Macrathera wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are typically clustered in panicles, with lemmas that are often hairy. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This variety is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in ecological restoration projects.
