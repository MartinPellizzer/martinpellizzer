Ranunculus scapigerus var. foliosus

Ranunculus scapigerus var. foliosus, commonly known as the leafy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and alternate, palmately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often more developed and broader compared to the typical variety, giving it a distinct appearance.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy blooms in the spring.
