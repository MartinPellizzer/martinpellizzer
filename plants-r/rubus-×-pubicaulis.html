Rubus × pubicaulis

Rubus × pubicaulis, commonly known as the bramble raspberry or common blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit develops into a dark purple to black berry.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for both commercial and home garden use.
