Ruta macrophylla

Ruta macrophylla, commonly known as greater wormwood, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Ruta macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
