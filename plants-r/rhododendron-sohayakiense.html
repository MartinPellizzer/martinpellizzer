Rhododendron sohayakiense

Rhododendron sohayakiense, commonly known as the Sohayaki rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive elliptical shape, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and horticultural collections.
