Rabdosia interrupta

Rabdosia interrupta, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rabdosia interrupta is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
