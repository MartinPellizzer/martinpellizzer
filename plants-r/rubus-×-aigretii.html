Rubus × aigretii

Rubus × aigretii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically black when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
