Rosa rhoetica

Rosa rhoetica, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant has simple, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white down. It is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Rosa rhoetica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness in garden settings.
