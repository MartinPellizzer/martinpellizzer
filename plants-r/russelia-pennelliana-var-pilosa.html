Russelia pennelliana var. pilosa

Russelia pennelliana var. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly russelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, or pilose, stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in horticultural settings.
