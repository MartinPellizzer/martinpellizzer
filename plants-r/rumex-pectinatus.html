Rumex pectinatus

Rumex pectinatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Rumex pectinatus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
