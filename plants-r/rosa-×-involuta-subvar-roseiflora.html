Rosa × involuta subvar. roseiflora

Rosa × involuta subvar. roseiflora, commonly known as the curled rose or the spiral rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its unique, twisted petals.

It originates from crosses between Rosa rugosa and Rosa × sinensis, resulting in a plant with a compact, shrub-like growth habit. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a soft, velvety texture and a subtle, sweet fragrance. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and ability to attract pollinators.
