Rhododendron scabrifolium var. spiciferum

Rhododendron scabrifolium var. spiciferum, commonly known as the spiciferum rhododendron, is a variant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The leaves are typically leathery and have a rough, scaly texture, which gives the plant its specific epithet "scabrifolium.". It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
