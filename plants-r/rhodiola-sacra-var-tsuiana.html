Rhodiola sacra var. tsuiana

Rhodiola sacra var. tsuiana, commonly known as the Tibetan rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found at elevations above 3,000 meters. The plant has fleshy, succulent stems and leaves, and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their adaptogenic properties.

The plant is valued for its ability to withstand extreme environmental conditions, making it a symbol of resilience in alpine ecosystems.
