Rhododendron vaccinioides

Rhododendron vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during late spring. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron vaccinioides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
