Rhytidophyllum exsertum

Rhytidophyllum exsertum, commonly known as the fluted bottle tree, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fluted trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant has small, clustered leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in winter. Its bark is rough and fissured, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rhytidophyllum exsertum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal and drought tolerance.
