Rhododendron longilobum

Rhododendron longilobum, commonly known as the long-lobed rhododendron, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron longilobum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
