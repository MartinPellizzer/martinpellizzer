Rubus caesius subsp. perpannosus

Rubus caesius subsp.

perpannosus, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth.

The plant produces red berries that are typically harvested in the fall, and it has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy stems and showy white flowers that appear in the spring.
