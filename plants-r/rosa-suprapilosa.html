Rosa suprapilosa

Rosa suprapilosa, commonly known as the silky rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rosa suprapilosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and unique foliage.
