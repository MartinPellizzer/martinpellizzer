Rosa balsamea

Rosa balsamea, commonly known as the sweet William, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and has hairy stems and leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa balsamea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
