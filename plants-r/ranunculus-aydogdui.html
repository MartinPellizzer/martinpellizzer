Ranunculus aydogdui

Ranunculus aydogdui, commonly known as the Aydogdu's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmately divided leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
