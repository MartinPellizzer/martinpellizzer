Ruta chalepensis subsp. fumariifolia

Ruta chalepensis subsp.

fumariifolia, commonly known as the fumariifoliate rue, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, often fuzzy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to rocky or arid environments.

The plant contains volatile oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its strong scent and historical use in traditional medicine.
