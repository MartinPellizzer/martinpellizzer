Ranunculus matrensis

Ranunculus matrensis, commonly known as the Matrenskaya buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
