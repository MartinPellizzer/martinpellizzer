Rheum × svetlanae

Rheum × svetlanae, commonly known as the hybrid rhubarb, is a cultivated plant resulting from the cross between Rheum rhaponticum and Rheum palmatum.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, and its edible, stalk-like stems that are used in cooking. The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil. It is typically grown as an ornamental or for its edible parts, showcasing both aesthetic and culinary value.

Rheum × svetlanae is a perennial plant that prefers partial shade to full sun, depending on the climate.
