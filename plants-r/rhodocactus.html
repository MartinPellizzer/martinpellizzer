Rhodocactus

Rhodocactus, commonly known as the red cactus, is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, often red or pink flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring or early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Rhodocactus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
