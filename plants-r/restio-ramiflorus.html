Restio ramiflorus

Restio ramiflorus, commonly known as the branching-flowered restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale green flowers that are typically borne in terminal spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Restio ramiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
