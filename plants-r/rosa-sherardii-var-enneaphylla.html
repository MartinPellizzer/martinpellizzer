Rosa sherardii var. enneaphylla

Rosa sherardii var. enneaphylla, commonly known as the nine-leaf rose, is a rare and delicate shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The variety is distinguished by its unique foliage, featuring nine leaflets per leaf, which gives it its botanical name. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant among horticulturists and botanists.
