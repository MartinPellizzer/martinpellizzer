Rumicastrum umbelliforme

Rumicastrum umbelliforme, commonly known as the umbrella leaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded stem and clusters of fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that give it a unique, umbrella-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with minimal water, storing moisture in its thick leaves and stems.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
