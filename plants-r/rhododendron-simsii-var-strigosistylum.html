Rhododendron simsii var. strigosistylum

Rhododendron simsii var. strigosistylum, commonly known as the silverleaf rhododendron, is a hybrid species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that often has a silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
