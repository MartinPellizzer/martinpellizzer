Rhaphiolepis rubra var. typica

Rhaphiolepis rubra var. typica, commonly known as the redleaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are ornamental but not edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a versatile plant for gardens and landscapes.
