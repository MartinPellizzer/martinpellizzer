Rubus pityophilus

Rubus pityophilus, commonly known as the red-sheath blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
