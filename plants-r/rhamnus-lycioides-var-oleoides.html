Rhamnus lycioides var. oleoides

Rhamnus lycioides var. oleoides, commonly known as the oliveleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and woodland areas. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that matures to a reddish color.

The species is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
