Rosa latellispina

Rosa latellispina, commonly known as the spiny blushing rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red berries that contain seeds.

Rosa latellispina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
