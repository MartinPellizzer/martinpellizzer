Rubus oigocladus var. dasycaulon

Rubus oigocladus var. dasycaulon, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and dark, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
