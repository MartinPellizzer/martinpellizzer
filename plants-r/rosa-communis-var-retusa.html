Rosa communis var. retusa

Rosa communis var. retusa, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a variety of the common rose characterized by its compact growth habit and abundant flowering.

It features fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It is well-suited for hedging and ornamental planting due to its dense foliage and continuous bloom.

Its botanical traits make it a popular choice in gardens for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
