Rubus sentus

Rubus sentus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry shrub, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
