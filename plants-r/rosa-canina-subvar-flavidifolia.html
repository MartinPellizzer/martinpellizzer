Rosa canina subvar. flavidifolia

Rosa canina subvar. flavidifolia, commonly known as the yellowleaf dogwood, is a shrub native to parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with a yellowish-green hue, distinguishing it from other rose species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are edible, though they are typically not as prominent as those of other Rosa species.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
