Rhododendron concinnum var. lepidanthum

Rhododendron concinnum var. lepidanthum, commonly known as the silverleaf rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with a silvery-white to grayish waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in woodland gardens.
