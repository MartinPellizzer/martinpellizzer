Rubus succedaneus

Rubus succedaneus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible blackberries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
