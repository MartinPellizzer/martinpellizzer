Rosa tomentosa subvar. subduplicata

Rosa tomentosa subvar. subduplicata, commonly known as the woolly rose or the double-flowered woolly rose, is a subspecies of the woolly rose native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale pink double flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves covered in a soft, woolly pubescence. Its flowers are highly fragrant and are often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
