Rhododendron ovatum var. prismatum

Rhododendron ovatum var. prismatum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron or the mountain azalea, is a flowering shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a leathery texture, and the flowers range in color from white to pink and red.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
