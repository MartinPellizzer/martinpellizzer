Rosa pisocarpa var. transmontana

Rosa pisocarpa var. transmontana, commonly known as the Transmontane rose, is a botanical variety native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant has serrated, dark green leaves and is known for its hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soil. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic resource for rose breeders.

The variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions to local biodiversity.
