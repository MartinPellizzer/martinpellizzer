Ranunculus preslii

Ranunculus preslii, commonly known as the Presl's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its flowers consist of 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it produces small, green, hairy fruits.

Ranunculus preslii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
