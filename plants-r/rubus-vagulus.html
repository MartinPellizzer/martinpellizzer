Rubus vagulus

Rubus vagulus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring moist, shaded environments in woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit, though small, is edible and has a tart flavor.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
