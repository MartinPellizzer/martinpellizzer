Rosa pleosoma

Rosa pleosoma, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its stems are covered in thorns, typical of many rose species.

Rosa pleosoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
