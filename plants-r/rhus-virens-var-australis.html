Rhus virens var. australis

Rhus virens var. australis, commonly known as the Australian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnate, with serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive red, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
