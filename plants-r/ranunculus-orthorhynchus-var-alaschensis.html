Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. alaschensis

Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. alaschensis, commonly known as the Alaska buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as tundra and alpine meadows. It is a perennial species with a short growing season adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its ecological niche in high-altitude environments.
