Rosa woodsii var. macounii

Rosa woodsii var. macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's rock rose, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, edible hips that are rich in vitamin C. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
