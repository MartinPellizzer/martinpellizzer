Rhynchospora depressirostris

Rhynchospora depressirostris, commonly known as the narrow-beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with each spikelet containing several tiny flowers. It thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.

Rhynchospora depressirostris is often found in the southeastern United States and parts of Central America.
