Rubus interioris

Rubus interioris, commonly known as the interior raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Rubus interioris is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
