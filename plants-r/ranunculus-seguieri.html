Ranunculus seguieri

Ranunculus seguieri, commonly known as the Seguier's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically in shades of yellow, white, or pink. The plant has a short, hairy stem and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Ranunculus seguieri is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate.
