Ribes cereum var. inebrians

Ribes cereum var. inebrians, commonly known as the sticky currant, is a variant of the sticky currant species native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark red or black color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its botanical features include hairy stems, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that appear in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
