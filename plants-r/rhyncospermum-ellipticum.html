Rhyncospermum ellipticum

Rhyncospermum ellipticum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with linear, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access groundwater in dry soils.

Rhyncospermum ellipticum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
