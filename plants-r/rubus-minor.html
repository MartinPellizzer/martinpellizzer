Rubus minor

Rubus minor, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in jams and desserts. Its flowers are white and have five petals, and the plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Rubus minor is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
