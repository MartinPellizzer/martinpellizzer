Rosa uncinata

Rosa uncinata, commonly known as the hooked rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces red or pink flowers in late spring to early summer.

Rosa uncinata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its beauty and historical significance.
