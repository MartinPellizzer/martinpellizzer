Rosa blanda carpohispida

Rosa blanda carpohispida, commonly known as the soft blanda rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is native to parts of North America. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red hips in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
