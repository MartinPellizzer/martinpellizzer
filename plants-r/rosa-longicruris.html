Rosa longicruris

Rosa longicruris, commonly known as the long-legged rose, is a species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often arching stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval leaflets, and the plant has a woody structure with thorny stems.

Rosa longicruris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
