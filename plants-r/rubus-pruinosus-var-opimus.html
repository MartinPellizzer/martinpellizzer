Rubus pruinosus var. opimus

Rubus pruinosus var. opimus, commonly known as the opimus raspberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the first year's growth. The plant produces clusters of small, usually pink to red flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries in the summer. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The opimus raspberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and its contribution to local ecosystems.
