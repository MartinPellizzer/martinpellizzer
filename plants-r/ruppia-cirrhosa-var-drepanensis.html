Ruppia cirrhosa var. drepanensis

Ruppia cirrhosa var. drepanensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea rocket, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is typically found in coastal areas, estuaries, and brackish water habitats. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually submerged or floating. It produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal sediments and supporting aquatic biodiversity.
