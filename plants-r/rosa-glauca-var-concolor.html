Rosa glauca var. concolor

Rosa glauca var. concolor, commonly known as the blue rose or the concolor rose, is a variety of the shrub rose native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender flower color, which is due to the presence of anthocyanins. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, and the stems are covered in thorns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to challenging soil conditions.
