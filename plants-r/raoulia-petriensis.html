Raoulia petriensis

Raoulia petriensis, commonly known as the Christmas Island pandanus, is a unique plant species native to Christmas Island in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist, coastal environments and is adapted to the island's tropical climate. Raoulia petriensis plays a significant ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for local wildlife.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in the region's flora.
