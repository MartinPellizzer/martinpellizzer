Rosa glauca f. acutiformis

Rosa glauca f. acutiformis, commonly known as the blue rose with acute leaves, is a horticultural form of the species Rosa glauca.

It is characterized by its striking blue-pink flowers and lance-shaped foliage. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with thorny stems and dense, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This form is prized for its unique coloration and ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
