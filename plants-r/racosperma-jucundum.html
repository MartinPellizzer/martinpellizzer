Racosperma jucundum

Racosperma jucundum, commonly known as the happy racosperma, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility, and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
