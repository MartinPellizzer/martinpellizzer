Rosa molliserrifrons

Rosa molliserrifrons, commonly known as the soft-toothed rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves with soft, serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated by horticulturists and conservationists alike.
