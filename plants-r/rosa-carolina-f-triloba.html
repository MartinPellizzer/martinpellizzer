Rosa carolina f. triloba

Rosa carolina f. triloba, commonly known as the Carolina rose triloba, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
