Rubus stenoporus

Rubus stenoporus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions with moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
