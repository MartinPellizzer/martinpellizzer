Rubia relbun

Rubia relbun, commonly known as the common madder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots contain a red dye, historically used in textile industries.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant can grow up to 1 meter in height.
