Rubus flensburgensis

Rubus flensburgensis, commonly known as the Flensburg blackberry, is a species of shrub native to southern Germany and parts of central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
