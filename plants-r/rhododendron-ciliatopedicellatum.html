Rhododendron ciliatopedicellatum

Rhododendron ciliatopedicellatum, commonly known as the woolly pedicellate rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hairy structures, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
