Ranunculus peduncularis var. chillanensis

Ranunculus peduncularis var. chillanensis, commonly known as the Chilean buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive central stigma. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
