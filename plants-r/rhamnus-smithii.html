Rhamnus smithii

Rhamnus smithii, commonly known as Smith's buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by black, egg-shaped berries. These berries are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
