Rhynchospora rugosa var. sikkimensis

Rhynchospora rugosa var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is distinguished by its hairy texture and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
