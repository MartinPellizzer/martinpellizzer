Rhododendron × puniceum

Rhododendron × puniceum, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a species resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron × puniceum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
