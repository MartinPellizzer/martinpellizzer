Rosa montana f. subglauca

Rosa montana f. subglauca, commonly known as the Alpine rose or mountain rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered with a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand cold climates, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate regions.
