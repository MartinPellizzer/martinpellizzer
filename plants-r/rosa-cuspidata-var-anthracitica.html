Rosa cuspidata var. anthracitica

Rosa cuspidata var. anthracitica, commonly known as the black rose, is a variety of the prickly rose that is native to the eastern United States.

This plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety petals that contrast with its deep green foliage. It produces fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of red to black. The shrub has thorny stems and is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
