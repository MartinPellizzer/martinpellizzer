Rubus × knappianus

Rubus × knappianus, commonly known as the knappian blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its fruit in certain regions.
