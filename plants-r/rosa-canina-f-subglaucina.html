Rosa canina f. subglaucina

Rosa canina f. subglaucina, commonly known as the pale dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose, characterized by its distinctive pale pink to white flowers.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in woodland and hedgerow environments. The leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as rose hips, which are orange-red and contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
