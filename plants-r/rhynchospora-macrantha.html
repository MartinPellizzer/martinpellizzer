Rhynchospora macrantha

Rhynchospora macrantha, commonly known as the tall spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rhynchospora macrantha thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological significance.
