Rubus purpuratus var. calliandrus

Rubus purpuratus var. calliandrus, commonly known as the purple-fruited raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its distinctive deep purple to black fruits.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of North America, particularly the Pacific Northwest. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that are rich in flavor and nutrients.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, though it is less commonly cultivated than other raspberry species.
