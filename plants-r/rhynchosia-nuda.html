Rhynchosia nuda

Rhynchosia nuda, commonly known as the naked rhynchosia, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Rhynchosia nuda is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
