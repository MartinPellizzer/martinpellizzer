Rosa sepium

Rosa sepium, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its woody stems and ability to withstand coastal conditions make it a popular choice for landscaping.
