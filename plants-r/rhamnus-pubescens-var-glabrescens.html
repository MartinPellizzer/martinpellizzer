Rhamnus pubescens var. glabrescens

Rhamnus pubescens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-fruited buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous bark and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and shrublands.

Its ecological role includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
