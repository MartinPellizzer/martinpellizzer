Rubus laetevirens f. incomtus

Rubus laetevirens f. incomtus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. Its foliage is notable for its dense, hairy texture, which provides a natural deterrent to herbivores.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential use in local ecosystems.
