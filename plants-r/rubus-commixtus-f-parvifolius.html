Rubus commixtus f. parvifolius

Rubus commixtus f. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a compact shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a hairy surface. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
