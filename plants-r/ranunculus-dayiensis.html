Ranunculus dayiensis

Ranunculus dayiensis, commonly known as the Dayien's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
