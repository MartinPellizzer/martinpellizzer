Rosa arvina

Rosa arvina, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. Rosa arvina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.
