Ranunculus macrantherus

Ranunculus macrantherus, commonly known as the large-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically borne on slender, branched stems. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
