Rubus cremirensis

Rubus cremirensis, commonly known as the Cretan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the island of Crete.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rubus cremirensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
