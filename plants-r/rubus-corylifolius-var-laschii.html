Rubus corylifolius var. laschii

Rubus corylifolius var. laschii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to China.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in forested areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

The berries are edible but can be quite tart and are sometimes used in jams or wines.
