Rosa buziae

Rosa buziae, commonly known as the Buzia rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

Rosa buziae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
