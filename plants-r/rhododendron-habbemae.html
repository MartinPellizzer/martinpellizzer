Rhododendron habbemae

Rhododendron habbemae, commonly known as the Habbema rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron habbemae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
