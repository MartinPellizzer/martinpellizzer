Rosa × alba cymbifolia

Rosa × alba cymbifolia, commonly known as the white cymbifolia rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa alba and Rosa cymbifolia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive ruffled appearance. The plant is deciduous, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and historical use in perfumery and floral arrangements.
