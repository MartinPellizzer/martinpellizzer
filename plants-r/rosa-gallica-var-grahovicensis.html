Rosa gallica var. grahovicensis

Rosa gallica var. grahovicensis, commonly known as the Grahovice rose, is a rare and ancient subspecies of the Gallica rose.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Czech Republic and is characterized by its deep red, semi-double flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its rarity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical conservation efforts.
