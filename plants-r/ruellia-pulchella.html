Ruellia pulchella

Ruellia pulchella, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically opposite along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ruellia pulchella is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
