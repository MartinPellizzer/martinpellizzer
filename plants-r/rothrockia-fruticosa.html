Rothrockia fruticosa

Rothrockia fruticosa, commonly known as the rothrock's holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark berries that persist through the winter. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, providing year-round visual interest in the landscape.

Rothrockia fruticosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
