Rosa coriifolia var. cinerella

Rosa coriifolia var. cinerella, commonly known as the cinerella shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with elongated, ovate leaflets that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of garden conditions.
