Rubus tayulinensis

Rubus tayulinensis, commonly known as the Tayul Valley blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the rose family. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet and slightly tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
