Recordoxylon irwinii

Recordoxylon irwinii, commonly known as the Irwin's fossil wood, is a rare and ancient plant species preserved as a fossilized wood.

It is characterized by its unique cellular structure and preservation, offering insights into early plant evolution. Found primarily in Cretaceous sedimentary rocks, it provides valuable data on paleoenvironments and ancient ecosystems. The plant's morphology suggests it was a woody dicot, likely adapted to warm, moist climates.

Its study contributes to understanding the transition of plant life during the Mesozoic era.
