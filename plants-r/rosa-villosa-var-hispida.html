Rosa villosa var. hispida

Rosa villosa var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy rose or hispid rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which are densely covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil conditions.
