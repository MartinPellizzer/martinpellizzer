Rubus parkeri var. longisetosus

Rubus parkeri var. longisetosus, commonly known as the long-hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges and are covered in fine, glandular hairs. This variety is often found in rocky soils and elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
