Radiola linoides var. emarginata

Radiola linoides var. emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved radiola, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, linear leaves with a distinctive notched or serrated edge, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is typically found in open, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
