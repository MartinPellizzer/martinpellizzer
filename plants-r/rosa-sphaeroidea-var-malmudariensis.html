Rosa sphaeroidea var. malmudariensis

Rosa sphaeroidea var. malmudariensis, commonly known as the Malmudar rose, is a rare and unique species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its spherical, thornless stems and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists due to its ornamental value and limited distribution.

It is often cultivated in specialized gardens for its distinctive appearance and historical significance.
