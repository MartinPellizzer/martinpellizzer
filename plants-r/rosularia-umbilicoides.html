Rosularia umbilicoides

Rosularia umbilicoides, commonly known as the umbilicated roseleaf, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a powdery blue or white coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Rosularia umbilicoides is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
