Restio pubescens

Restio pubescens, commonly known as the velvet restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has fine, hairy leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Restio pubescens plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
