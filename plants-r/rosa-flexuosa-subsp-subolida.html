Rosa flexuosa subsp. subolida

Rosa flexuosa subsp.

subolida, commonly known as the bent-stem rose, is a species native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its arching, often climbing stems and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It produces rose hips that are red and hairy, containing the seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
