Ranunculus xatardii

Ranunculus xatardii, commonly known as the Kashmir ranunculus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest edges. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

Ranunculus xatardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
