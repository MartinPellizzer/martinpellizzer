Rubia esquirolii

Rubia esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's knotweed, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces red berries that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
