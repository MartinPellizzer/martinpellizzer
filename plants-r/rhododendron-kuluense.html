Rhododendron kuluense

Rhododendron kuluense, commonly known as the Kulu rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowers range in color from pink to red, adding vibrant visual interest to its natural habitat.

Rhododendron kuluense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its mountainous regions.
