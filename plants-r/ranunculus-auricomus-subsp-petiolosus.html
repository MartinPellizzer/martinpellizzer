Ranunculus auricomus subsp. petiolosus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

petiolosus, commonly known as the petioled buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features basal leaves with long petioles and deeply lobed foliage, often with a hairy surface.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated leaf stalks compared to the typical form of R. auricomus.
