Rubus aunieri

Rubus aunieri, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
