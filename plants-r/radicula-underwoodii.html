Radicula underwoodii

Radicula underwoodii, commonly known as the underwood's radix, is a rare and delicate plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and unique root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting specific pollinators. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for high-altitude ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, Radicula underwoodii is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
