Ranunculus indicus

Ranunculus indicus, commonly known as the Indian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to regions of Asia, including India and Nepal.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which is characterized by its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

Ranunculus indicus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
