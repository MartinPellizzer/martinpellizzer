Rochefortia septentrionalis var. obovata

Rochefortia septentrionalis var. obovata, commonly known as the desert bell, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, gray-green leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
