Ricinus laevis

Ricinus laevis, commonly known as the smooth castor oil plant, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and striking red flowers. The plant produces a toxic, sticky substance known as ricin, which is found in its seeds. Ricinus laevis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms depending on the climate.
