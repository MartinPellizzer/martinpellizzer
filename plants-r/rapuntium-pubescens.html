Rapuntium pubescens

Rapuntium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found along roadsides and in fields. Its small white or yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

The hairy cress is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
