Rosa accumbens

Rosa accumbens, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a fragrant scent.

Rosa accumbens produces small, red berries that contain seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.
