Rosa communis var. ramealis

Rosa communis var. ramealis, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a variety of the common rose that is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to climb using its thorned stems.

It produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in small, stiff hairs. It is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

This variety is relatively hardy and requires regular pruning to maintain its shape and encourage new growth.
