Royena hirsuta var. rubriflora

Royena hirsuta var. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered hirsute roya, is a striking member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized collections.
