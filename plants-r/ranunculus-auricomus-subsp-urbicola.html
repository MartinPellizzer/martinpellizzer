Ranunculus auricomus subsp. urbicola

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

urbicola, commonly known as the urban buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals, often mistaken for true petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in urban environments such as sidewalks and gardens. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to human-altered habitats, making it a common sight in urban settings.
