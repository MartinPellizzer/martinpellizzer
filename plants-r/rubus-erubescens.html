Rubus erubescens

Rubus erubescens, commonly known as the scarlet raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn bright red when mature, often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant autumn foliage.
