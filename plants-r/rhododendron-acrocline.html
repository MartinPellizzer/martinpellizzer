Rhododendron acrocline

Rhododendron acrocline, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, adapted to withstand cold and harsh weather conditions.

Rhododendron acrocline is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
