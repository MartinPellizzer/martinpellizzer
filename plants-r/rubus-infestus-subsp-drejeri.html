Rubus infestus subsp. drejeri

Rubus infestus subsp.

drejeri, commonly known as the Greenland raspberry, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its erect, often spiny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, juicy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and coastal environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
