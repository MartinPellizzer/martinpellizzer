Rosa alberti

Rosa alberti, commonly known as the Alberti rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces oval, green leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its stems are often covered in thorns. Rosa alberti typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
