Rhododendron burttii

Rhododendron burttii, commonly known as the Burtt's rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a waxy coating. Its flowers are showy, appearing in clusters of 4 to 6, and are typically pink to reddish in color.

Rhododendron burttii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
