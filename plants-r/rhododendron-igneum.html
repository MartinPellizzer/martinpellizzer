Rhododendron igneum

Rhododendron igneum, commonly known as the fire-flower rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in spring, often with a fiery hue that stands out against the green foliage. The plant is evergreen, featuring broad, leathery leaves that provide year-round structure. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Rhododendron igneum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add vibrant color to gardens.
