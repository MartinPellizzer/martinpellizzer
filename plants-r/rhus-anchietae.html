Rhus anchietae

Rhus anchietae, commonly known as Brazilian sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants such as cashews and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have serrated margins. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by clusters of red, fuzzy fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

The plant has a rough, bark-like stem and is often found in dry, open habitats.
