Rubus ancistracanthus

Rubus ancistracanthus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and large, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges and a hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
