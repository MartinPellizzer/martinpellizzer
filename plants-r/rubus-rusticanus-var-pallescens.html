Rubus rusticanus var. pallescens

Rubus rusticanus var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus native to certain regions of North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, aggregate berry. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in areas with partial shade.

The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
