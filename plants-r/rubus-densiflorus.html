Rubus densiflorus

Rubus densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing features with other members like raspberries and blackberries.
