Rosa stylosa mentita

Rosa stylosa mentita, commonly known as the Mentita rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America, specifically in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

The Mentita rose is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
