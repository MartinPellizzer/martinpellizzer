Rosa micrantha var. typica

Rosa micrantha var. typica, commonly known as the small-flowered rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces rose hips that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
