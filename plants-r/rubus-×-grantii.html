Rubus × grantii

Rubus × grantii, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many brambles and berries, and is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (loganberry). The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and sweeter than those of its parent species. Its leaves are serrated and have a hairy texture, while its stems are thorny.

Rubus × grantii is valued for its fruit quality and is often cultivated for commercial and home gardening purposes.
