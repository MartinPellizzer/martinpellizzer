Rhododendron durionifolium subsp. sabahense

Rhododendron durionifolium subsp.

sabahense, commonly known as the Sabah rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Borneo region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous areas with rich, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive elliptical shape, while its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
