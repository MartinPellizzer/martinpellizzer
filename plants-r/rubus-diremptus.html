Rubus diremptus

Rubus diremptus, commonly known as the splitberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many bramble species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
