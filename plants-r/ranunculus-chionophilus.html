Ranunculus chionophilus

Ranunculus chionophilus, commonly known as the snow-loving buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a contrasting central blotch. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, where it thrives in cold, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds via the wind.

Ranunculus chionophilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
