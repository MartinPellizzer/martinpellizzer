Rhododendron spodopeplum

Rhododendron spodopeplum, commonly known as the silverleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that exhibits a striking silvery-white underside. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
