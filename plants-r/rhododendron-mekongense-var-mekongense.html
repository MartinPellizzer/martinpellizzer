Rhododendron mekongense var. mekongense

Rhododendron mekongense var. mekongense, commonly known as the Mekong rhododendron, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features leathery, ovate to elliptic leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, clustered in inflorescences, and bloom in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
