Rubus scabriramus

Rubus scabriramus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a aggregate berry, is typically red and edible, though it is often used in jams and preserves.

Rubus scabriramus is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates.
