Rosa caryophyllacea var. durandensis

Rosa caryophyllacea var. durandensis, commonly known as the Durand's rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers have five petals and numerous stamens. This species is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its hardy nature and unique floral display.
