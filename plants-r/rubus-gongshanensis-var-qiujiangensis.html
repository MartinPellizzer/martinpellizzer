Rubus gongshanensis var. qiujiangensis

Rubus gongshanensis var. qiujiangensis, commonly known as the Qiujiang blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
