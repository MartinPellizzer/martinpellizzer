Rosa divionensis

Rosa divionensis, commonly known as the Divion rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in France and Spain. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa divionensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
