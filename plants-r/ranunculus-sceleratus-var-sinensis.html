Ranunculus sceleratus var. sinensis

Ranunculus sceleratus var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its often toxic members.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
