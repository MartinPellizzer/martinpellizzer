Rosa glauca subsp. chavinii

Rosa glauca subsp.

chavinii, commonly known as the Chavin rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy surface, while the petals are often reflexed, giving the flowers a distinctive appearance. This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
