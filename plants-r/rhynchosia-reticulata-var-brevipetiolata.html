Rhynchosia reticulata var. brevipetiolata

Rhynchosia reticulata var. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled netleaf legume, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive net-veined leaves.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets and short petioles. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
