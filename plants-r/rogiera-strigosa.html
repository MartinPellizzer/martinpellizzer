Rogiera strigosa

Rogiera strigosa, commonly known as the roughleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in short, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually blue or violet, and bloom in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface and often have a slightly rough texture.
