Ribes × downingianum

Ribes × downingianum, commonly known as the Downings currant, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Grossulariaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, which are edible and used in jams and jellies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Ribes × downingianum is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resistance to certain diseases.
