Ranunculus auricomus subsp. strongylus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

strongylus, commonly known as the yellow pond-lily, is a aquatic plant native to North America. It features broad, floating leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent, veined petals. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
