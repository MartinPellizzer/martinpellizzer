Ranunculus lugdunensis

Ranunculus lugdunensis, commonly known as the Lyonshire crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks. Its flowers are usually less than 2 inches in diameter and bloom in early spring.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
