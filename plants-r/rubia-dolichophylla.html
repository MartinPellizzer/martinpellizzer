Rubia dolichophylla

Rubia dolichophylla, commonly known as long-leaf madder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species known for their medicinal and dye-producing properties. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in cymes, and typically have five petals, while its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Rubia dolichophylla is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
