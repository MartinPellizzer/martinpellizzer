Reseda phyteuma var. integrifolia

Reseda phyteuma var. integrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers that are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Reseda phyteuma var. integrifolia is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
