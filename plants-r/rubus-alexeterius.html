Rubus alexeterius

Rubus alexeterius, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
