Rhododendron brachycarpum f. nemotoanum

Rhododendron brachycarpum f. nemotoanum, commonly known as the Nemoto's dwarf rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in cooler climates.
