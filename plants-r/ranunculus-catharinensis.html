Ranunculus catharinensis

Ranunculus catharinensis, commonly known as the Carolinian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland flora.
