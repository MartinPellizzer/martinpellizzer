Rubus silesiacus subsp. fimbriatus

Rubus silesiacus subsp.

fimbriatus, commonly known as the Silesian blackberry, is a shrub native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely toothed leaf margins and slightly different fruit morphology compared to other Rubus species.
