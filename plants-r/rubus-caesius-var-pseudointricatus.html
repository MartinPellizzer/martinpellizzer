Rubus caesius var. pseudointricatus

Rubus caesius var. pseudointricatus, commonly known as the crimson bramble, is a variant of the bramble family (Rosaceae) characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

It typically features dark green, serrated leaves and produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom in the spring.

This variant is noted for its hardy nature and potential use in hedges or as a ornamental plant.
