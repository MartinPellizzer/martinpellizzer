Rottboellia elegans

Rottboellia elegans, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Rottboellia elegans is both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed in cultivated landscapes.
