Ranunculus meinshausenii

Ranunculus meinshausenii, commonly known as the Meinhausean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers can range in color from white to yellow, depending on the variety.

Ranunculus meinshausenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
