Rhodax distachyos subsp. rubellus

Rhodax distachyos subsp.

rubellus, commonly known as the red-stemmed rhodax, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive reddish coloration and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
