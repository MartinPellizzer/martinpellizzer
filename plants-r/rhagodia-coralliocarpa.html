Rhagodia coralliocarpa

Rhagodia coralliocarpa, commonly known as the coral fruit.

It is a flowering plant native to Western Australia, characterized by its woody stems and distinctive coral-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fleshy, red berries that mature in summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.
