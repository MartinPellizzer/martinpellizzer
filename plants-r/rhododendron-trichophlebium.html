Rhododendron trichophlebium

Rhododendron trichophlebium, commonly known as the Italian rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
