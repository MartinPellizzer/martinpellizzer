Rubus villicaulis mollis

Rubus villicaulis mollis, commonly known as the soft-haired bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense, soft, white or grayish pubescence covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its habitat.
