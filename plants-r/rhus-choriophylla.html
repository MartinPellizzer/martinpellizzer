Rhus choriophylla

Rhus choriophylla, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. Rhus choriophylla thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental landscaping.
