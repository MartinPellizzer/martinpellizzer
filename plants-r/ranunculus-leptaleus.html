Ranunculus leptaleus

Ranunculus leptaleus, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in partial to full shade.

Ranunculus leptaleus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
