Ranunculus suaneticus

Ranunculus suaneticus, commonly known as the Suanetica buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a taproot system that helps it survive in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in some regions for garden use.
