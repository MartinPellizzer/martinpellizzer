Rosa saxigena

Rosa saxigena, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

Rosa saxigena is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
