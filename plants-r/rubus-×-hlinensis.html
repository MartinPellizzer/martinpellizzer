Rubus × hlinensis

Rubus × hlinensis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry). The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, with a tart flavor. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks.
