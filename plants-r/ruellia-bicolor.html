Ruellia bicolor

Ruellia bicolor, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia bicolor is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
