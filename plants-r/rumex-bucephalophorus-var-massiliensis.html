Rumex bucephalophorus var. massiliensis

Rumex bucephalophorus var. massiliensis, commonly known as the Massilian sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color, especially on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender, erect stalks. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

The leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their tart, acidic flavor.
