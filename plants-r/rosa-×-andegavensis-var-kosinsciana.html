Rosa × andegavensis var. kosinsciana

Rosa × andegavensis var. kosinsciana, commonly known as the Kosinski's rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the Andegavas region in Poland.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with foliage that is dark green and slightly hairy. It is a hardy shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to temperate climates.
