Rubus parviflorus f. praebifarius

Rubus parviflorus f. praebifarius, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry form, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are typically smaller and less flavorful compared to the main species.

This form is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils across its native range.
