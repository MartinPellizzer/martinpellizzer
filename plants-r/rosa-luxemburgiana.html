Rosa luxemburgiana

Rosa luxemburgiana, commonly known as the Luxembourg rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. Native to Europe, it thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces hips that are edible and used in culinary applications.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
