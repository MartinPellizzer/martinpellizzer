Raveniopsis breweri

Raveniopsis breweri, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flames. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage is often hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Raveniopsis breweri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
