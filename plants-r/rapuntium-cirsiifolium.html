Rapuntium cirsiifolium

Rapuntium cirsiifolium, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant's robust structure and adaptive features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
