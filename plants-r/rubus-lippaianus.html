Rubus lippaianus

Rubus lippaianus, commonly known as the blackberry plant, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its fruit, the blackberry, is edible and widely cultivated for its sweet, dark flavor.

The plant also plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and ecosystems.
