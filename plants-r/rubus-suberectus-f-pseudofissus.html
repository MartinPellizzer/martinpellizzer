Rubus suberectus f. pseudofissus

Rubus suberectus f. pseudofissus, commonly known as the variegated blackberry, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and edible berries.
