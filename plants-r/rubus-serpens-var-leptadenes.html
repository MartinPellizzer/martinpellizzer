Rubus serpens var. leptadenes

Rubus serpens var. leptadenes, commonly known as the slenderleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus Rubus.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thorny stems. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a leathery texture and a distinct serrated margin. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry.

This plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
