Rosa coriifolia var. pubescens

Rosa coriifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with oval, serrated leaflets that are covered in fine, silky hairs. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a range of garden conditions.
