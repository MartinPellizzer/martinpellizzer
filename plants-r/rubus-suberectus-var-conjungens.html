Rubus suberectus var. conjungens

Rubus suberectus var. conjungens, commonly known as the clustered blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
