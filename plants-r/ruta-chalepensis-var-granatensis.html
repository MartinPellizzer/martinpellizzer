Ruta chalepensis var. granatensis

Ruta chalepensis var. granatensis, commonly known as the Granatensis Rue, is a variant of the common rue plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, often bitter scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in herbal remedies.
