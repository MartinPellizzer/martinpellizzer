Rubia galioides

Rubia galioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias, and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers have been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
