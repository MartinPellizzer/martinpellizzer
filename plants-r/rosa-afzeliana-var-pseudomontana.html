Rosa afzeliana var. pseudomontana

Rosa afzeliana var. pseudomontana, commonly known as the Himalayan rock rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
