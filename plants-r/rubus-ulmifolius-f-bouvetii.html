Rubus ulmifolius f. bouvetii

Rubus ulmifolius f. bouvetii, commonly known as the Bouvet's blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, lobed leaves and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The fruit can be used in jams and desserts, though it is generally less sweet than other blackberry varieties.
