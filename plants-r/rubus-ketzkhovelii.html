Rubus ketzkhovelii

Rubus ketzkhovelii, commonly known as Ketzkhovel's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
