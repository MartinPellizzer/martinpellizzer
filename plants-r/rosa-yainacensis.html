Rosa yainacensis

Rosa yainacensis, commonly known as the Yainacensis rose, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa yainacensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its potential in horticulture and conservation efforts.
