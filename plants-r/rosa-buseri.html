Rosa buseri

Rosa buseri, commonly known as the Buser's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem covered in thorns and a root system adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Rosa buseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
