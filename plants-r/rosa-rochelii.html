Rosa rochelii

Rosa rochelii, commonly known as the Rochel's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Rosa rochelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
