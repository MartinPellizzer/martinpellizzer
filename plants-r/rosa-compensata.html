Rosa compensata

Rosa compensata, commonly known as the compensatory rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa compensata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
