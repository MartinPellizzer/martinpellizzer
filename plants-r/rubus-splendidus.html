Rubus splendidus

Rubus splendidus, commonly known as the splendid blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and have a serrated edge.

Rubus splendidus is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
