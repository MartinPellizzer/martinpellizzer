Rosa pendulina var. biserrata

Rosa pendulina var. biserrata, commonly known as the hanging rose or the weeping rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges, which are a distinguishing feature of the biserrata variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping for its elegant, cascading appearance.
