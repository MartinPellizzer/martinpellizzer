Rosettea trothae

Rosettea trothae, commonly known as the spiral rosette plant, is a unique succulent characterized by its tightly coiled rosette structure.

Native to the arid regions of southern Africa, it thrives in dry, rocky environments. The plant's leaves are fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months.

Due to its striking appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
