Rosa communis var. capillata

Rosa communis var. capillata, commonly known as the silky rose, is a cultivar of the common rose that features soft, fuzzy petals.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with an upright and spreading growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
