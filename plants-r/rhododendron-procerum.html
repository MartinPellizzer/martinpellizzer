Rhododendron procerum

Rhododendron procerum, commonly known as the slender rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically features deep green, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron procerum is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in cooler climates.
