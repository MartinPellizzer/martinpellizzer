Rhododendron multicolor var. curtisii

Rhododendron multicolor var. curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's multicolor rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often in forested areas with acidic soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
