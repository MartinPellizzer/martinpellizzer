Ranunculus nemorosus subsp. polyanthemophyllus

Ranunculus nemorosus subsp.

polyanthemophyllus, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers with five to nine petals, which bloom in early spring. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in forest understories.

The subspecies polyanthemophyllus is distinguished by its larger, more numerous petals compared to the typical species.
