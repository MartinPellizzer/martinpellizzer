Rubus corylifolius var. viridis

Rubus corylifolius var. viridis, commonly known as the green-leafed bramble, is a variant of the brambles native to Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its foliage and fruit make it a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
