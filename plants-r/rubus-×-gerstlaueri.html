Rubus × gerstlaueri

Rubus × gerstlaueri, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its edible, dark purple berries.
