Rubus × scytophyllus var. belezei

Rubus × scytophyllus var. belezei, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a hybrid species native to Brazil.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in various regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
