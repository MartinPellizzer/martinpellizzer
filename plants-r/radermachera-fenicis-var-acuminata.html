Radermachera fenicis var. acuminata

Radermachera fenicis var. acuminata, commonly known as the Japanese evergreen maple, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various garden settings.
