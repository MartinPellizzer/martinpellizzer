Rosettea rhinanthifolia var. exellii

Rosettea rhinanthifolia var. exellii, commonly known as the Exell's rosette plant, is a distinctive member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, water-holding cup. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
