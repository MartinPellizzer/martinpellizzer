Rubus rubrisetoides

Rubus rubrisetoides, commonly known as the red-hairy raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
