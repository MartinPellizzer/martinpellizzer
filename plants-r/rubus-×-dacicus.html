Rubus × dacicus

Rubus × dacicus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are actually composed of many small drupes, and are often referred to as blackberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is widely cultivated for its edible fruits.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
