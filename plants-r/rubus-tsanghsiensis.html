Rubus tsanghsiensis

Rubus tsanghsiensis, commonly known as the Tsanghsi blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many other types of brambles and blackberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
