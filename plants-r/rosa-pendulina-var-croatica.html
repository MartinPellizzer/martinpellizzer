Rosa pendulina var. croatica

Rosa pendulina var. croatica, commonly known as the Croatian weeping rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa pendulina species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces rose hips that are red and ornamental. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its graceful appearance and historical significance in horticulture.
