Rubus melanoxylon var. boulayi

Rubus melanoxylon var. boulayi, commonly known as the Boulay's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are armed with stiff prickles.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
