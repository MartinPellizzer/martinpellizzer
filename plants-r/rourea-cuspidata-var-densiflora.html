Rourea cuspidata var. densiflora

Rourea cuspidata var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered rourea, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release tiny seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its foliage and flowering habit.
