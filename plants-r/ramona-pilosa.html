Ramona pilosa

Ramona pilosa, commonly known as the hairy ramona, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
