Ranunculus auricomus subsp. acutimammus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

acutimammus, commonly known as the sharp-mammillate buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a distinctive greenish or reddish midrib.

The leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine, glandular hairs, which contribute to its characteristic appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
