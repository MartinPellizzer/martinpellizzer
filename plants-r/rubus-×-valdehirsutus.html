Rubus × valdehirsutus

Rubus × valdehirsutus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, prickly stems and large, dark purple to black berries. The plant is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces showy white to pale yellow flowers in the spring.

The berries are often used in culinary applications, though they can be quite tart and require sweetening.
