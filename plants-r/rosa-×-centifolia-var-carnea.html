Rosa × centifolia var. carnea

Rosa × centifolia var. carnea, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Middle East and Southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its highly fragrant, pink to deep red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and has dark green, glossy foliage. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and for making rose water due to their rich aroma.

The variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical significance in horticulture.
