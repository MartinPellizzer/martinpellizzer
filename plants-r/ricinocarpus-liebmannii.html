Ricinocarpus liebmannii

Ricinocarpus liebmannii, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its woody stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds covered in a fleshy, sticky coating. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Ricinocarpus liebmannii thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing in sandy or saline soils.
