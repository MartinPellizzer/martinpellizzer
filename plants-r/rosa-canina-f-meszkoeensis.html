Rosa canina f. meszkoeensis

Rosa canina f. meszkoeensis, commonly known as the Hungarian dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. This subspecies is native to Central and Eastern Europe, particularly Hungary and surrounding regions. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

The plant produces rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional herbal remedies.
