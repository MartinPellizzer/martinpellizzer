Racosperma flagelliforme

Racosperma flagelliforme, commonly known as the long-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, whip-like stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has small, compound leaves and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
