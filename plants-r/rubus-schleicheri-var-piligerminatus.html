Rubus schleicheri var. piligerminatus

Rubus schleicheri var. piligerminatus, commonly known as the pilose blackberry, is a shrub native to southeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.

The variety is distinguished by its soft, hairy covering on the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.
