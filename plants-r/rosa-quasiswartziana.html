Rosa quasiswartziana

Rosa quasiswartziana, commonly known as the Quasiswartz rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, stipulate leaves, and a structure typical of the Rosaceae family.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
