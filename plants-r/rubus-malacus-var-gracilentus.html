Rubus malacus var. gracilentus

Rubus malacus var. gracilentus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry species characterized by its slender, trailing growth habit and smaller, more delicate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting subject for horticultural study.
