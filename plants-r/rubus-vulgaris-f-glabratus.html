Rubus vulgaris f. glabratus

Rubus vulgaris f. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, distinguishing it from the typical brambles. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This form is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges across Europe.
