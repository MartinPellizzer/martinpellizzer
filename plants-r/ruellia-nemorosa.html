Ruellia nemorosa

Ruellia nemorosa, commonly known as the forest ruellia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ruellia nemorosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
