Rosa × dumetorum subvar. semiglabra

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. semiglabra, commonly known as the rough shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which are typical of many rose species. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, usually blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
