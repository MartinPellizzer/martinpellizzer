Rhododendron fastigiatum

Rhododendron fastigiatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense, upright growth with narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
