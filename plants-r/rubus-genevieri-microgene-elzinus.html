Rubus genevieri microgene elzinus

Rubus genevieri microgene elzinus, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
