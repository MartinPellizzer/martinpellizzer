Rhamnus lanceolata var. lanceolata

Rhamnus lanceolata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the spring, followed by reddish berries in the fall.

The species is often found in riparian zones and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
