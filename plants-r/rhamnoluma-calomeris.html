Rhamnoluma calomeris

Rhamnoluma calomeris, commonly known as the Calomeris buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
