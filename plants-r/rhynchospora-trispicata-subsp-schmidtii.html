Rhynchospora trispicata subsp. schmidtii

Rhynchospora trispicata subsp.

schmidtii, commonly known as Schmidt's three-bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spikes bear small, three-bracted flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
