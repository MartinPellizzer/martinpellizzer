Rhopalopodium gusmannii

Rhopalopodium gusmannii, commonly known as the Mexican candleplant, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Rhopalopodium gusmannii is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
