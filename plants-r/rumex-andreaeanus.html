Rumex andreaeanus

Rumex andreaeanus, commonly known as the Andean sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

The leaves are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines for their tart, sour flavor.
