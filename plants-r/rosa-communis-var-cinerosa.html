Rosa communis var. cinerosa

Rosa communis var. cinerosa, commonly known as the smoky rose, is a unique variant of the common rose characterized by its distinctive smoky-pink to almost black petals.

This variety is native to regions of Central Asia and is known for its compact growth habit and fragrant blooms. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the deep-colored flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens with adequate space.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated for both its beauty and its historical significance in horticulture.
