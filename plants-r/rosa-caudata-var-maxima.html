Rosa caudata var. maxima

Rosa caudata var. maxima, commonly known as the Mexican rose or the long-stemmed rose, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Rosa caudata var. maxima is well adapted to warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
