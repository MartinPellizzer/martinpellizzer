Rosa communis var. agrestina

Rosa communis var. agrestina, commonly known as the field rose, is a wild variety of the common rose.

It is characterized by its simple, thorned stems and alternate, pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals, and appear in clusters. This plant thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
