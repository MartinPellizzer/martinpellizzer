Rumohra spectabilis

Rumohra spectabilis, commonly known as the giant reed or common reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous growth and can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. It thrives in wetland environments and is adapted to tolerate flooding and saline conditions.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly and form extensive colonies.
