Rathbunia grisea f. cristata

Rathbunia grisea f. cristata, commonly known as the crested gray rat's tail, is a unique cultivar of the species Rathbunia grisea.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often twisted inflorescence that resembles a rat's tail, giving it its common name. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. It produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers that are often clustered along the stem.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly grown in tropical and subtropical gardens.
