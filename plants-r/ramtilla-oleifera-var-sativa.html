Ramtilla oleifera var. sativa

Ramtilla oleifera var. sativa, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange or red ray florets and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a beneficial addition to gardens.
