Rosa spaldingii var. chelanensis

Rosa spaldingii var. chelanensis, commonly known as the Chelanensis rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
