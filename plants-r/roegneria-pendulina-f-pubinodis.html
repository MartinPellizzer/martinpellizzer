Roegneria pendulina f. pubinodis

Roegneria pendulina f. pubinodis, commonly known as the pubescent wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit.

It features slender, erect stems that often bear inflorescences of spikelets arranged in panicle-like clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration. This variety is distinguished by its pubescent (hairy) texture on the lower leaf surfaces and stems.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates.
