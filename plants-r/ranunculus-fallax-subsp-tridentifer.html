Ranunculus fallax subsp. tridentifer

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

tridentifer, commonly known as the three-toothed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals and a prominent central yellow stigma, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The leaves are deeply lobed, often with three to five lobes, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
