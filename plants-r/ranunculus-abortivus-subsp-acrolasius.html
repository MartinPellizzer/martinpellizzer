Ranunculus abortivus subsp. acrolasius

Ranunculus abortivus subsp.

acrolasius, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high elevations.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals and a hairy stem. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure.

The subspecies is characterized by its shorter stature and more compact growth compared to other ranunculus species.
