Rubus babingtonii var. bloxamii

Rubus babingtonii var. bloxamii, commonly known as the Bloxam's blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The flowers are white and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
