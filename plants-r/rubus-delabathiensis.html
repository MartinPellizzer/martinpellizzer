Rubus delabathiensis

Rubus delabathiensis, commonly known as the Delabathi raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
