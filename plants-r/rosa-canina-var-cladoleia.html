Rosa canina var. cladoleia

Rosa canina var. cladoleia, commonly known as the bramble rose, is a variant of the dog rose that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically featuring five to seven leaflets. The flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

It produces red, hairy hips that contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This variety is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.
