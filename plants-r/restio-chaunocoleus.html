Restio chaunocoleus

Restio chaunocoleus, commonly known as the white restio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are often mistaken for grasses. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in fynbos vegetation.

Restio chaunocoleus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
