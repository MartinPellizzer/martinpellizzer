Rubus micradenes

Rubus micradenes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems.
