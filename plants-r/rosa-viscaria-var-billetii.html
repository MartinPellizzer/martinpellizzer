Rosa viscaria var. billetii

Rosa viscaria var. billetii, commonly known as the Billet's rose, is a variant of the shrub rose native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems that provide structural support. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
