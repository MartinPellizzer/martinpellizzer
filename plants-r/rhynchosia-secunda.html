Rhynchosia secunda

Rhynchosia secunda, commonly known as the African coffee bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing dark, coffee-like seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Rhynchosia secunda is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
