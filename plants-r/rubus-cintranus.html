Rubus cintranus

Rubus cintranus, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. Rubus cintranus thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
