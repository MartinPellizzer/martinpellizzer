Rhodospatha dammeri

Rhodospatha dammeri, commonly known as the Dammer's rhododendron, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
