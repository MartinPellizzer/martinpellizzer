Rosa spuria var. cernagorae

Rosa spuria var. cernagorae, commonly known as the Cernagora rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant features dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or wildflower landscapes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
