Rubus spinulicaulis

Rubus spinulicaulis, commonly known as the spiny-stemmed bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

Rubus spinulicaulis is native to parts of Asia and is often found in woodland and scrubland habitats.
