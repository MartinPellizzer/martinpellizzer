Randia gynopachis var. rubiginosa

Randia gynopachis var. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-fruited randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The leaves are typically opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant fruits and ability to attract birds.
