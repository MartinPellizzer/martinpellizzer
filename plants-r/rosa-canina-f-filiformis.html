Rosa canina f. filiformis

Rosa canina f. filiformis, commonly known as the filiform rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like petals that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and it is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
