Rudbeckia californica var. intermedia

Rudbeckia californica var. intermedia, commonly known as the California coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage and produces vibrant yellow to orange ray flowers with a central disk of dark brown or black tubular flowers. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is drought-tolerant and often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
