Rhaphithamnus spinosus

Rhaphithamnus spinosus, commonly known as the spiny broom.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa, characterized by its spiny stems and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Rhaphithamnus spinosus is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
