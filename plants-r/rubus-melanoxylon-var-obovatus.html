Rubus melanoxylon var. obovatus

Rubus melanoxylon var. obovatus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
