Rubus congruens

Rubus congruens, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
