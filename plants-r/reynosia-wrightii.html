Reynosia wrightii

Reynosia wrightii, commonly known as the desert ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soils and drought conditions.

Reynosia wrightii plays a significant role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
