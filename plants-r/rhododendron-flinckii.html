Rhododendron flinckii

Rhododendron flinckii, commonly known as the Flinck's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are large, showy, and typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron flinckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
