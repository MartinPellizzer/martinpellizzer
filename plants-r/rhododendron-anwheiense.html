Rhododendron anwheiense

Rhododendron anwheiense, commonly known as the Anwhei rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Nepal and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist mountainous environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green and glossy, with a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific soil and climate requirements.
