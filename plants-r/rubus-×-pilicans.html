Rubus × pilicans

Rubus × pilicans, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of many brambles. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its adaptability and fruit quality, it is widely cultivated for both commercial and home gardening purposes.
