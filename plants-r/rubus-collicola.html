Rubus collicola

Rubus collicola, commonly known as the rock raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soil, often in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The berries are usually red or black when ripe and are valued for their tart flavor and potential medicinal properties.
