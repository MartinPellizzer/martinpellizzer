Rubus spinosellus

Rubus spinosellus, commonly known as the spiny blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
