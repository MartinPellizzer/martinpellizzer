Rorippa reichenbachii

Rorippa reichenbachii, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It typically grows in shallow water, such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows, and has delicate, floating or submerged leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers with four petals, which are arranged in clusters at the water's surface. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the sediment in aquatic environments.

Rorippa reichenbachii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to improve water quality and provide habitat for aquatic life.
