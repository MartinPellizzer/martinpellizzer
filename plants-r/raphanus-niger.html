Raphanus niger

Raphanus niger, commonly known as black radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple or black root, which is typically harvested in the second year of growth. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are lobed and edible. The roots are rich in glucosinolates, which contribute to its pungent flavor.

It is native to Europe and is cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
