Rosa soulieana var. sungpanensis

Rosa soulieana var. sungpanensis, commonly known as the Sungpan rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, yellowish-orange hips that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a valued addition to gardens in temperate climates.
