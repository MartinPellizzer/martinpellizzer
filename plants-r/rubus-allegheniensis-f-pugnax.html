Rubus allegheniensis f. pugnax

Rubus allegheniensis f. pugnax, commonly known as the Allegheny blackberry, is a wild, thorny shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its berries are usually dark purple to black when ripe and are often used in local foraging and culinary traditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more aggressive growth habit compared to other Rubus species.
