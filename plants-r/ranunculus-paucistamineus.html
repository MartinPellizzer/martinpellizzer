Ranunculus paucistamineus

Ranunculus paucistamineus, commonly known as the few-stamen buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, usually yellow or white flowers with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus paucistamineus is an herbaceous perennial that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
