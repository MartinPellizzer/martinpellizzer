Rhexia cerastiifolia

Rhexia cerastiifolia, commonly known as the scarlet beakweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to woodland habitats. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Rhexia cerastiifolia plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
