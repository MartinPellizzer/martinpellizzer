Rosa afzeliana f. cladoleia

Rosa afzeliana f. cladoleia, commonly known as the clustered rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa afzeliana.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit with closely spaced flowers and foliage. The plant typically features pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
