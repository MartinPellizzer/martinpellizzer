Ribes burejense var. villosum

Ribes burejense var. villosum, commonly known as the woolly gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red berries in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
