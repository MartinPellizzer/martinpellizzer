Rosa ilseana

Rosa ilseana, commonly known as the Ilse's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and the ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Rosa ilseana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
