Rosa micrantha var. chenevierei

Rosa micrantha var. chenevierei, commonly known as the Chenave's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, with a subtle fragrance. The plant produces hips, which are orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and showy blooms.
