Rubus pascuorum

Rubus pascuorum, commonly known as the field raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow in color, containing multiple tiny seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated and have a hairy texture.

The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in sunny, well-drained soils.
