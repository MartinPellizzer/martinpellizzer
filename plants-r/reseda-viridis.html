Reseda viridis

Reseda viridis, commonly known as greenweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bright green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Reseda viridis is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields.

It is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
