Rhodomyrtus calophlebia

Rhodomyrtus calophlebia, commonly known as the red-flowered guava or red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Rhodomyrtus calophlebia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage and showy blooms.
