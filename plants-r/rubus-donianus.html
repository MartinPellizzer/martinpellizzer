Rubus donianus

Rubus donianus, commonly known as the Donian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
