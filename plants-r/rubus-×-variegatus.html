Rubus × variegatus

Rubus × variegatus, commonly known as the variegated blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the evergreen blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry).

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and gold colors. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and for small-scale fruit production.
