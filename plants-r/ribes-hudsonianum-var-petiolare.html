Ribes hudsonianum var. petiolare

Ribes hudsonianum var. petiolare, commonly known as the Hudson's Bay currant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly found in temperate regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often pubescent and have petioles. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible red berries that are popular in culinary and medicinal uses.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to a range of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
