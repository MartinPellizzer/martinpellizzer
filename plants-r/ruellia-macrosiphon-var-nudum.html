Ruellia macrosiphon var. nudum

Ruellia macrosiphon var. nudum, commonly known as the naked ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically blue or purple, clustered in spikes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
