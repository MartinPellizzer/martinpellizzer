Rytidosperma violaceum

Rytidosperma violaceum, commonly known as purple bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple to violet-colored inflorescence, which consists of dense, brush-like spikes of florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Rytidosperma violaceum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
