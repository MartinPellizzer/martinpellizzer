Ruehssia vinciflora

Ruehssia vinciflora, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive aromatic scent. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its cascading appearance.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires careful pruning and support structures to grow successfully.
