Rubus flagellaris var. almus

Rubus flagellaris var. almus, commonly known as the Alaskan blackberry, is a shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
