Richeria purpurascens

Richeria purpurascens, commonly known as the scarlet arum, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and its dramatic, spadix-like inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly beetles, which are attracted to the plant's strong, musky odor.

Richeria purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
