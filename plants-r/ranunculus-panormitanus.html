Ranunculus panormitanus

Ranunculus panormitanus, commonly known as the Panormitanus buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
