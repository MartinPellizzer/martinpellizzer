Rhododendron chengianum

Rhododendron chengianum, commonly known as the Cheng's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to shaded environments.
