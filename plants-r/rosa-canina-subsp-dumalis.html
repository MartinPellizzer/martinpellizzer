Rosa canina subsp. dumalis

Rosa canina subsp.

dumalis, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces fragrant pink to white flowers in early summer, followed by red, hairy fruits known as hips. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
