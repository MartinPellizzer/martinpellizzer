Rhododendron edanoi subsp. pneumonanthum

Rhododendron edanoi subsp.

pneumonanthum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and shrublands, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant pink or red flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
