Rubus venustus f. salevensis

Rubus venustus f. salevensis, commonly known as the salt marsh raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant adapted to saline environments.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red, though they may vary in color depending on the environment. It thrives in coastal areas and wetlands where salt tolerance is essential.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in saline habitats.
