Rebutia xanthocarpa f. dasyphrissa

Rebutia xanthocarpa f. dasyphrissa, commonly known as the thick-stemmed yellow-berry cactus, is a hybrid succulent native to arid regions of South America.

It features thick, ribbed stems that store water, typical of desert plants, and produces yellow, fleshy fruits. The plant has spiny areoles with clusters of short, white bristles, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it blooms with pale yellow flowers during the warmer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry, sunny conditions.
