Rosa × complicata var. recurviserrata

Rosa × complicata var. recurviserrata, commonly known as the recurving serrate rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features.

It is native to China and is known for its finely serrated, recurved leaf margins, which give it a unique appearance. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, often with a subtle red hue. It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring thorny stems and dense, leathery leaves.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
