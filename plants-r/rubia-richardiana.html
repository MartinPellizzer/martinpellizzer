Rubia richardiana

Rubia richardiana, commonly known as the Richard's madder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 10,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Rubia richardiana is known for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
