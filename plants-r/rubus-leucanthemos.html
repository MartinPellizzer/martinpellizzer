Rubus leucanthemos

Rubus leucanthemos, commonly known as white-flowered raspberry.

It is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, a red, hairy berry, is edible but often tart and less commonly cultivated than other raspberry species.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
