Ranunculus velatus

Ranunculus velatus, commonly known as the veiled buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy underside that gives it a velvety texture.

Ranunculus velatus is a perennial species, often found in high-altitude meadows and slopes.
