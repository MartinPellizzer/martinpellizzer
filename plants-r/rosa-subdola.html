Rosa subdola

Rosa subdola, commonly known as the slender rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. It produces oval, red berries that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
