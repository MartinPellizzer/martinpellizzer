Rhexia pratensis

Rhexia pratensis, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its root system is distinctive, with swollen, reddish roots that give it its common name.

Rhexia pratensis plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
