Rhododendron pentamerum

Rhododendron pentamerum, commonly known as the purple leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and striking purple foliage. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing an evergreen structure throughout the year.

Rhododendron pentamerum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
