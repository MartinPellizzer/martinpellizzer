Ranunculus formosanus

Ranunculus formosanus, commonly known as the Taiwan buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with prominent yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and streamside areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in spring.

Ranunculus formosanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
