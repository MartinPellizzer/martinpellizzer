Rhysolepis bracteata

Rhysolepis bracteata, commonly known as the mountain mint, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture.

Rhysolepis bracteata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
