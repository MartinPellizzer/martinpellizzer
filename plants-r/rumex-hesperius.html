Rumex hesperius

Rumex hesperius, commonly known as the western dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, serrated leaves that are often reddish at the base. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and along roadsides.

Rumex hesperius is also known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, slightly sour flavor.
