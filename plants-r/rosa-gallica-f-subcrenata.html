Rosa gallica f. subcrenata

Rosa gallica f. subcrenata, commonly known as the French pink or garden musk rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa gallica species.

It is characterized by its deep pink, single-layered flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated foliage. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is well-suited for borders and containers due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
