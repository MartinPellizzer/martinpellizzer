Rubus vulgaris subf. calcicola

Rubus vulgaris subf.

calcicola, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a shrub native to rocky and mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as slopes and meadows at high elevations.
