Rosa canina var. fissidens

Rosa canina var. fissidens, commonly known as the fissured dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, hairy hips that are edible and often used in traditional herbal remedies. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in horticulture and wildlife habitats.
