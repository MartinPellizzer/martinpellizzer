Ranunculus georgicus

Ranunculus georgicus, commonly known as the Georgian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically features bright yellow or orange petals and a central cluster of stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus georgicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
