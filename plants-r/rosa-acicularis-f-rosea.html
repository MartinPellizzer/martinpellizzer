Rosa acicularis f. rosea

Rosa acicularis f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-colored thorned rose, is a unique horticultural form of the thorny rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to rose-colored flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, thorned stems and small, dark green leaves. It is a compact shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and delicate floral display.
