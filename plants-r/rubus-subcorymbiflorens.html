Rubus subcorymbiflorens

Rubus subcorymbiflorens, commonly known as the Hawaiian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the island of Hawaii.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and is often found in moist, shaded habitats. It is an important component of the native flora in Hawaii, contributing to the ecological diversity of the region.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
