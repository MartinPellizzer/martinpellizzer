Rosa viscaria var. subpermixta

Rosa viscaria var. subpermixta, commonly known as the hybrid pink tea rose, is a cultivar that exhibits a unique blend of characteristics from its parent species.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, semi-double flowers with a soft pink hue. The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a popular choice for gardeners. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

This variety is often appreciated for its ornamental value and adaptability in both landscape and container settings.
