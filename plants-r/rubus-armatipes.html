Rubus armatipes

Rubus armatipes, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

The bristly blackberry is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
