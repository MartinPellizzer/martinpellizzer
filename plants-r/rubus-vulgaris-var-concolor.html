Rubus vulgaris var. concolor

Rubus vulgaris var. concolor, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and aggregate fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
