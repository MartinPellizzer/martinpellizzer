Rafnia cuneifolia var. lanceolata

Rafnia cuneifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Rafnia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, which can be either terminal or axillary. Its botanical features include a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
