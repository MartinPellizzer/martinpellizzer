Ranunculus cyclophyllus

Ranunculus cyclophyllus, commonly known as the cyclophyllous buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
