Rubus henkeri

Rubus henkeri, commonly known as the Henker's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
