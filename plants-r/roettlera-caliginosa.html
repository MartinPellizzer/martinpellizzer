Roettlera caliginosa

Roettlera caliginosa, commonly known as the bog mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet, acidic soils of bogs and swamps, thriving in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pale yellow to white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Roettlera caliginosa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and microorganisms.
