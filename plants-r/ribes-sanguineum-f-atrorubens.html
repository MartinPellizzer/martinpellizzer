Ribes sanguineum f. atrorubens

Ribes sanguineum f. atrorubens, commonly known as the dark red currant, is a variant of the red currant plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its deep, almost black-red foliage and clusters of small, bright red berries. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn a striking coppery color in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant foliage and edible fruits.
