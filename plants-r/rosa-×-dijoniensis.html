Rosa × dijoniensis

Rosa × dijoniensis, commonly known as the Dijon rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from France.

It is characterized by its large, fully double pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its strong fragrance and vibrant color.
