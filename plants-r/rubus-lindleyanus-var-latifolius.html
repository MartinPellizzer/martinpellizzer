Rubus lindleyanus var. latifolius

Rubus lindleyanus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, serrated leaves and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It has a woody structure with dense foliage, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry species.
