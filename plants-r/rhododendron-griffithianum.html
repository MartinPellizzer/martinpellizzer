Rhododendron griffithianum

Rhododendron griffithianum, commonly known as the Griffith's rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a distinctive red or purple band.

Rhododendron griffithianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
