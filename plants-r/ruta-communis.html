Ruta communis

Ruta communis, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial herb native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It features aromatic, dark green, compound leaves with a strong, pungent odor. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits are small, dry, and contain three ovules.

Ruta communis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in herbal medicine.
