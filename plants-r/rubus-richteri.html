Rubus richteri

Rubus richteri, commonly known as the Richter's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-woody growth habit and produces small, dark berries that are often used in local cuisine. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rubus richteri is an important plant in its native habitat, providing food for wildlife and contributing to local biodiversity.
