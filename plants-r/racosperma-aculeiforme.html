Racosperma aculeiforme

Racosperma aculeiforme, commonly known as the spiked legume, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its stems are often armed with stiff, needle-like spines, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Racosperma aculeiforme is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.
