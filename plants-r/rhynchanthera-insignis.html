Rhynchanthera insignis

Rhynchanthera insignis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Rhynchanthera insignis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms.
