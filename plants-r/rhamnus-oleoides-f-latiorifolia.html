Rhamnus oleoides f. latiorifolia

Rhamnus oleoides f. latiorifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are broader and more elongated compared to the typical species, with a glossy, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
