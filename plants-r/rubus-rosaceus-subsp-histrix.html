Rubus rosaceus subsp. histrix

Rubus rosaceus subsp.

histrix, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
