Rhaphiolepis ferruginea

Rhaphiolepis ferruginea, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, bright red berries that persist through winter, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant white flowers in spring. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental berries and compact growth make it a popular choice for hedging and container planting.
