Rhamnus schneideri var. manshurica

Rhamnus schneideri var. manshurica, commonly known as the Far Eastern buckthorn, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, fleshy berries that are typically reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
