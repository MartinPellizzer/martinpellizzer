Rubus calviformis

Rubus calviformis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and small, prickly leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and have five petals, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus calviformis is often found in forest understories and along stream banks.
