Rubus serpens var. spinuliger

Rubus serpens var. spinuliger, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in jams and preserves due to its tart flavor.
