Rosa gallica var. typica

Rosa gallica var. typica, commonly known as the French rose, is a species of shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in thorns. It is well-suited for cultivation in gardens due to its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

Rosa gallica var. typica is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
