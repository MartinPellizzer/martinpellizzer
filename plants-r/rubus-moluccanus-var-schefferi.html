Rubus moluccanus var. schefferi

Rubus moluccanus var. schefferi, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a shrub native to the Moluccas and surrounding regions of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregated fruits that range in color from red to black. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valued plant in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
