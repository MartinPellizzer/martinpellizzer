Rochelia incana

Rochelia incana, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Rochelia incana is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its unique appearance and hardiness make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
