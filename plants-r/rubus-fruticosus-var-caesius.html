Rubus fruticosus var. caesius

Rubus fruticosus var. caesius, commonly known as the purple raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically reddish or purplish in color, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
