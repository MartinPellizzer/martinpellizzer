Rhodiola juparensis

Rhodiola juparensis, commonly known as the Mongolian rhodiola, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found at high altitudes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh, nutrient-poor soils.

Rhodiola juparensis is also valued for its potential adaptogenic properties, which may contribute to stress resistance in both plants and humans.
