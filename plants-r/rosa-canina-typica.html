Rosa canina typica

Rosa canina typica, commonly known as the dog rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, oval-shaped leaves, and fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red hips, which are the fruit, containing seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Rosa canina typica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing food for birds and supporting pollinators.
