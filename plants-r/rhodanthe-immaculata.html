Rhodanthe immaculata

Rhodanthe immaculata, commonly known as the white everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Rhodanthe immaculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its aesthetic and hardy nature.
