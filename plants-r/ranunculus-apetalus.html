Ranunculus apetalus

Ranunculus apetalus, commonly known as the naked buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of petals, giving it a distinctive appearance with only sepals that resemble petals. The plant typically produces clusters of bright yellow stamens surrounded by a ring of leaf-like structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral structure.
