Rubus brevipetiolulatus

Rubus brevipetiolulatus, commonly known as the short-petioled blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves with short petioles, and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
