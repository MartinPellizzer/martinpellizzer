Rosa gadzhievii

Rosa gadzhievii, commonly known as the Georgian rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a structure typical of the Rosa genus.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in the region.
