Rubus rufus

Rubus rufus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red when ripe. Rubus rufus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its edible berries and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
