Rhipsalis pilosa

Rhipsalis pilosa, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, thin, and often hair-covered stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, which are typically clustered and bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are reduced to small, translucent structures called areoles, and it lacks true leaves.

Rhipsalis pilosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
