Ranunculus arizonicus var. typicus

Ranunculus arizonicus var. typicus, commonly known as the Arizona buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms from spring to early summer.

The botanical features of this plant make it a notable species in arid and semi-arid regions.
