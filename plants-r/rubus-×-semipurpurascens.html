Rubus × semipurpurascens

Rubus × semipurpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leafed raspberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive purple foliage, which becomes more pronounced in cooler temperatures. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow, similar to other raspberry species. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking leaf color and edible fruits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
