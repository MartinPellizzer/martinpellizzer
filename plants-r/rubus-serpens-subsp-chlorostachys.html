Rubus serpens subsp. chlorostachys

Rubus serpens subsp.

chlorostachys, commonly known as the green-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive green stems that contrast with its dark, glossy leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
