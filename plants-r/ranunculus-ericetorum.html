Ranunculus ericetorum

Ranunculus ericetorum, commonly known as the heath buttercup, is a flowering plant native to western Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in acidic, nutrient-poor soils, often found in heathlands and moorlands. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by insects such as bees and beetles.

The plant is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a characteristic species of its habitat.
