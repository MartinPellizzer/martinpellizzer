Rosa afzeliana f. memorata

Rosa afzeliana f. memorata, commonly known as the pink sandalwood rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to pale red flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers have a subtle fragrance and are often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

This form is native to regions of China and is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its historical significance in horticulture.
