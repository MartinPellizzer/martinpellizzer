Ribes balsamiferum

Ribes balsamiferum, commonly known as the balsam currant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which give way to red, currant-like berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ribes balsamiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
