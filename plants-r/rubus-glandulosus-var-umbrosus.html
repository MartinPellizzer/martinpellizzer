Rubus glandulosus var. umbrosus

Rubus glandulosus var. umbrosus, commonly known as the gloomy bramble, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dark, often hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in shady, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of temperate shrublands.
