Rosa elliptica f. jordanii

Rosa elliptica f. jordanii, commonly known as the Jordanian elliptic rose, is a compact shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features small, elliptic leaves with a slightly hairy texture, adapted to withstand dry climates. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its stems are covered in thorns, typical of many rose species, and it has a dense growth habit.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
