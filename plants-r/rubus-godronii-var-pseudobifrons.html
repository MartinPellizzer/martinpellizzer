Rubus godronii var. pseudobifrons

Rubus godronii var. pseudobifrons, commonly known as the Godron's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
