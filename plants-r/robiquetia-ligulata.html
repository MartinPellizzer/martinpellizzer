Robiquetia ligulata

Robiquetia ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rosybell, is a striking flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color, with a distinctive narrow, strap-like shape, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, Robiquetia ligulata is often cultivated in gardens with Mediterranean climates.
