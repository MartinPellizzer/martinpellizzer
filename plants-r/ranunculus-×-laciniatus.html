Ranunculus × laciniatus

Ranunculus × laciniatus, commonly known as the cutleaf buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, palmate leaves that give it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a dark central blotch. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus × laciniatus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
