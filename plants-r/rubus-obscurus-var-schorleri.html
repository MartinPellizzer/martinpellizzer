Rubus obscurus var. schorleri

Rubus obscurus var. schorleri, commonly known as the Schorli blackberry, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, edible blackberries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
