Rubus pubifolius

Rubus pubifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf blackberry, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
