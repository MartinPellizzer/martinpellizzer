Rosa canina var. rubricunda

Rosa canina var. rubricunda, commonly known as the red-berried dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically pink to red, and the fruit, or rose hip, is red and often persists through winter.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
