Rubus pseudoschleicheri

Rubus pseudoschleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it exhibits typical Rubus traits, including a complex inflorescence and a perennial growth habit.

It is often found in forested areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
