Rhus succedanea var. sikkimensis

Rhus succedanea var. sikkimensis, commonly known as Sikkim sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy, berry-like fruits. These fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.
