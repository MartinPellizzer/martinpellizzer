Ruta chalepensis var. pellucidopunctata

Ruta chalepensis var. pellucidopunctata, commonly known as the translucent-punctuated rue, is a variant of the common rue plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, pinnate leaves that are often covered with small, translucent dots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by narrow, elongated fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Its distinctive foliage and historical use in herbal medicine contribute to its botanical and cultural significance.
