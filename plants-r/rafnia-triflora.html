Rafnia triflora

Rafnia triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered rafnia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Rafnia triflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany.
