Rosa sherardii f. umbelliflora

Rosa sherardii f. umbelliflora, commonly known as the umbelliflora form of Sherard's rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa sherardii.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbellate inflorescence, where flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster. This form is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
