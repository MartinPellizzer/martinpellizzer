Rosa bourdini

Rosa bourdini, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa bourdini is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
