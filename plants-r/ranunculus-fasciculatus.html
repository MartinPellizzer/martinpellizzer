Ranunculus fasciculatus

Ranunculus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

Ranunculus fasciculatus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.
