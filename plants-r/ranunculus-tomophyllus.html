Ranunculus tomophyllus

Ranunculus tomophyllus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus tomophyllus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
