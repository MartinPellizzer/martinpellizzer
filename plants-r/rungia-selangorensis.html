Rungia selangorensis

Rungia selangorensis, commonly known as the Malabar pepper plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Rungia selangorensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
