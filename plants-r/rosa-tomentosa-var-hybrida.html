Rosa tomentosa var. hybrida

Rosa tomentosa var. hybrida, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a hybrid variety characterized by its dense, fuzzy gray foliage and abundant, fragrant pink or white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of many rose species. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
