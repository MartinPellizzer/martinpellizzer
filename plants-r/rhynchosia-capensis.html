Rhynchosia capensis

Rhynchosia capensis, commonly known as the Cape snout bean, is a climbing legume native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, tubular seed pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is often found in coastal areas.

Rhynchosia capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
