Rosa decurtata

Rosa decurtata, commonly known as the shrub rose or Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan, and is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and bloom in the spring or early summer.

Rosa decurtata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
