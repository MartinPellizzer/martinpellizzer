Rosa alpina var. aurigerana

Rosa alpina var. aurigerana, commonly known as the golden alpine rose, is a variant of the alpine rose that is native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, with a distinctive golden hue on the outer petals. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and deeply lobed, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

This hardy species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens and rocky landscapes.
