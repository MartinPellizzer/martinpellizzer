Rubus cryptothyrsus

Rubus cryptothyrsus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they appear in the spring.

The fruit is edible but has a tart flavor, making it a subject of interest for both botanists and foragers.
