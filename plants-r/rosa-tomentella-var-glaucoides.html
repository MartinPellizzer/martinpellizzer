Rosa tomentella var. glaucoides

Rosa tomentella var. glaucoides, commonly known as the woolly rose or glaucous rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually covered in a fine, white to grayish down, which helps to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in cooler climates.
