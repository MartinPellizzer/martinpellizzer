Rubus × sulcatifolius

Rubus × sulcatifolius, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. The plant is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source for wildlife.
