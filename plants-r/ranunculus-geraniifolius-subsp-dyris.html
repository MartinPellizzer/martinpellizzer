Ranunculus geraniifolius subsp. dyris

Ranunculus geraniifolius subsp.

dyris, commonly known as the Dyris buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features finely divided, trifoliate leaves that resemble those of Geranium species, giving it its subspecific name.

The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
