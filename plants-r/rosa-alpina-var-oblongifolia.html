Rosa alpina var. oblongifolia

Rosa alpina var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the Alpine rose or mountain rose, is a variant of the alpine rose native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical to oblong leaflets, giving it the specific epithet *oblongifolia*.

This variety is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky soils at high elevations.
