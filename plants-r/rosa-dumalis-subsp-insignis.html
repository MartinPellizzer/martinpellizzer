Rosa dumalis subsp. insignis

Rosa dumalis subsp.

insignis, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of serrated, dark green leaves. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
