Rosa canina subvar. vaccinoides

Rosa canina subvar. vaccinoides, commonly known as the European hedge rose, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its hips, which are the fruit, are orange-red and contain numerous seeds.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and as a hedging plant.
