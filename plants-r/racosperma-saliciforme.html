Racosperma saliciforme

Racosperma saliciforme, commonly known as the willow-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has slender, willow-like stems and produces small, pod-like fruits. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma saliciforme is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
