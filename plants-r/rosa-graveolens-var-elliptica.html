Rosa graveolens var. elliptica

Rosa graveolens var. elliptica, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

The species is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
