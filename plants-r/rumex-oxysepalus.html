Rumex oxysepalus

Rumex oxysepalus, commonly known as the narrow-sepal sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and rhubarb. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, greenish-white inflorescences, and it produces oval, reddish-brown seeds.

Rumex oxysepalus is often found in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
