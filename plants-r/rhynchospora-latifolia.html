Rhynchospora latifolia

Rhynchospora latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
