Ranunculus membranaceus var. pubescens

Ranunculus membranaceus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The variety 'pubescens' is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
