Rosa rhipidothamna

Rosa rhipidothamna, commonly known as the Alpine rose or mountain rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa rhipidothamna is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.
