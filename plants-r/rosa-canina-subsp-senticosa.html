Rosa canina subsp. senticosa

Rosa canina subsp.

senticosa, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
