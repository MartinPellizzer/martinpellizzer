Rosa persetosa

Rosa persetosa, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan, Pakistan, and Iran. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the flowers typically bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Rosa persetosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
