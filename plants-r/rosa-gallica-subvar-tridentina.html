Rosa gallica subvar. tridentina

Rosa gallica subvar. tridentina, commonly known as the French rose or the tridentate rose, is a subspecies native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has dark green, glossy leaves with three to five leaflets. Its root system is fibrous, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
