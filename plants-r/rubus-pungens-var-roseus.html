Rubus pungens var. roseus

Rubus pungens var. roseus, commonly known as the red-berried bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its berries are edible but have a tart flavor, and the plant is often found in moist, wooded areas.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and small mammals.
