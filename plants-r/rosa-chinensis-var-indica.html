Rosa chinensis var. indica

Rosa chinensis var. indica, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant is renowned for its showy, fragrant flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. It blooms profusely throughout the growing season, often in spring and summer, and is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are typically single or semi-double, with five to seven petals and a prominent central cone.
