Reseda luteola subsp. biaui

Reseda luteola subsp.

biaui, commonly known as yellowrocket, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

It is commonly found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its medicinal and historical uses.
