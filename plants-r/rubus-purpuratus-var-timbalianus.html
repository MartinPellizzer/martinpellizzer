Rubus purpuratus var. timbalianus

Rubus purpuratus var. timbalianus, commonly known as the Timbalian blackberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
