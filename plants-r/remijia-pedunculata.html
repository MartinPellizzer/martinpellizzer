Remijia pedunculata

Remijia pedunculata, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vibrant red flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the rainforest canopy. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, which is typical for many orchids.

The botanical features of Remijia pedunculata make it a unique and visually appealing addition to botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
