Rubus × euleucus

Rubus × euleucus, commonly known as the blackberry or bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which develop from multiple ovaria within a single flower. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Rubus × euleucus is valued for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications.
