Rosa tomentosa var. pseudomollis

Rosa tomentosa var. pseudomollis, commonly known as the fuzzy rose, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, grayish-white pubescence covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its overall textured look.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
