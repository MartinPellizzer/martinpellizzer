Rosa cinnamomea

Rosa cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its stems are covered in a distinctive cinnamon-colored bark, which gives it its common name.

Rosa cinnamomea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and fragrance.
