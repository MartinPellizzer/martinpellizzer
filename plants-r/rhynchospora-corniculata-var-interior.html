Rhynchospora corniculata var. interior

Rhynchospora corniculata var. interior, commonly known as the interior beak-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This variety is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color.

It thrives in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along streambanks.
