Rubus insericatus var. macilentus

Rubus insericatus var. macilentus, commonly known as the slender trailing raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
