Raphidophora

Raphidophora, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The genus includes several species, such as Raphidophora hamedorei, which is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities. These plants are native to Southeast Asia and are often cultivated for their ornamental value.

They thrive in bright, indirect light and require consistently moist, well-draining soil.
