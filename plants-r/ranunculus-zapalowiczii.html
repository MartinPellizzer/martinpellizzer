Ranunculus zapalowiczii

Ranunculus zapalowiczii, commonly known as the Zapalowicz's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed divisions, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces numerous small, showy blooms during the spring and early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
