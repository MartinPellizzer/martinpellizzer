Rubus ipecensis

Rubus ipecensis, commonly known as the Ipecacuanha bush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature. It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like roses and strawberries.

Due to its historical use in medicine, it has been the subject of botanical and pharmacological studies.
