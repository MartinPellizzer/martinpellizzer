Rhododendron osmerum

Rhododendron osmerum, commonly known as the Osmer's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. The plant produces dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Rhododendron osmerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
