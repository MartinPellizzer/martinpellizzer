Rhinacanthus virens var. obtusifolius

Rhinacanthus virens var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by bright red, flame-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and vibrant fruit.

The species is adapted to warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
