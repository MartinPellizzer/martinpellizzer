Rosa jundzillii f. insidiosa

Rosa jundzillii f. insidiosa, commonly known as the "trap flower," is a unique hybrid cultivar of roses characterized by its striking, almost predatory appearance.

The plant features large, velvety petals that curl inward, creating a deceptive look that mimics the shape of an insect or small animal. Botanically, it is a member of the Rosaceae family and is noted for its strong, almost citrus-like fragrance. The inflorescence consists of tightly clustered blooms that open sequentially, enhancing its visual impact.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unusual and captivating appearance.
