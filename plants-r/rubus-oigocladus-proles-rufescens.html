Rubus oigocladus proles rufescens

Rubus oigocladus proles rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along hedgerows.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
