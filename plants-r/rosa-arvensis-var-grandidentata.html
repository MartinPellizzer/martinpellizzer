Rosa arvensis var. grandidentata

Rosa arvensis var. grandidentata, commonly known as the large-toothed dog rose, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated, often large teeth. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fuzzy hips. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
