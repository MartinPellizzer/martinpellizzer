Rhizophora palun

Rhizophora palun, commonly known as the mangrove apple or red mangrove, is a species of mangrove tree found in tropical coastal regions.

It is characterized by its extensive root system, which includes prop roots that help stabilize the tree in waterlogged soil. The plant thrives in saline environments and plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystem stability. Its small, red berries are an important food source for various birds and animals.

Rhizophora palun is also valued for its timber and is often used in traditional medicine.
