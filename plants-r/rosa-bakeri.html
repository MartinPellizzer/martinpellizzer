Rosa bakeri

Rosa bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical name honors the British botanist and explorer, William Baker, who contributed significantly to the study of Central Asian flora.

Rosa bakeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and pleasant fragrance.
