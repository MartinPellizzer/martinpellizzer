Rhetinantha unguiculata

Rhetinantha unguiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers develop into distinctive, elongated seed pods that release seeds with pappus for wind dispersal.

Rhetinantha unguiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
