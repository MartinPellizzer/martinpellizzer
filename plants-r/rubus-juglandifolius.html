Rubus juglandifolius

Rubus juglandifolius, commonly known as the walnut-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble those of the walnut tree, featuring a serrated margin and a palmate structure. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained habitats.

The species is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
