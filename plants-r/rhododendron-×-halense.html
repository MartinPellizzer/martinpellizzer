Rhododendron × halense

Rhododendron × halense, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form thickets, providing both visual interest and habitat for small wildlife.

Rhododendron × halense is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to a variety of climate conditions.
