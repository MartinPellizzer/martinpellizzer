Rubus radula proles pallidus

Rubus radula proles pallidus, commonly known as the pale-fruited bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits are aggregate berries, typically pale yellow or red, and are edible when ripe.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas of woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
