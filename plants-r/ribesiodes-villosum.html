Ribesiodes villosum

Ribesiodes villosum, commonly known as the hairy ribes, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer.

Ribesiodes villosum is often found in moist, woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
