Raphanus aucheri

Raphanus aucheri, commonly known as the purple sprouting broccoli, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, purple-tinged leaves and clusters of small, green, cabbage-like buds that develop in the second year. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in cool climates. Its leaves and buds are edible, often used in culinary applications for their mild, slightly peppery flavor.

Raphanus aucheri is also valued for its ornamental appeal, with its vibrant foliage adding visual interest to gardens.
