Rhododendron succothii

Rhododendron succothii, commonly known as the Ethiopian rhododendron, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron succothii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in its native habitat.
