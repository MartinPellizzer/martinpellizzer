Rubus goetzenii

Rubus goetzenii, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
