Rhaphiolepis macrocarpa

Rhaphiolepis macrocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese holly or rosebay rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a versatile choice for landscaping.
