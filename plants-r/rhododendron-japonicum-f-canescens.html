Rhododendron japonicum f. canescens

Rhododendron japonicum f. canescens, commonly known as the Japanese azalea or mountain azalea, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, grayish-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
