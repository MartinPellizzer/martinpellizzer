Rhododendron stenophyllum subsp. stenophyllum

Rhododendron stenophyllum subsp.

stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, with a leathery texture, and its flowers are clustered in racemes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial sunlight.
