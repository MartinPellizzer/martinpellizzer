Rosa rubiginosa var. umbellata

Rosa rubiginosa var. umbellata, commonly known as the pink sand rose, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are often borne in umbels, giving it its specific variety name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
