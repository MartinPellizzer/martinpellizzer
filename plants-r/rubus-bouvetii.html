Rubus bouvetii

Rubus bouvetii, commonly known as the Bornean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and culinary purposes.
