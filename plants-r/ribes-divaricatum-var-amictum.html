Ribes divaricatum var. amictum

Ribes divaricatum var. amictum, commonly known as the silverleaf currant, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, silver-gray leaves that provide a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shady areas.

The silverleaf currant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some culinary applications.
