Rheum palaestinum

Rheum palaestinum, commonly known as the Palestine rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, rhubarb-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces tall, branched stems with small, yellowish flowers in the spring. Its roots are thick and fleshy, which store nutrients for the plant.

Rheum palaestinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
