Ranunculus bulbosus var. anemonerhizos

Ranunculus bulbosus var. anemonerhizos, commonly known as the bulbous buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a short, rhizomatous root system and produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense colonies, making it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain habitats.
