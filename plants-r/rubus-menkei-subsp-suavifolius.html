Rubus menkei subsp. suavifolius

Rubus menkei subsp.

suavifolius, commonly known as the sweetleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to China. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and trifoliate leaves with a pleasant, aromatic scent.

The flowers are white and typically appear in clusters, followed by red, hairy fruits that mature in late summer. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to native plant gardens.
