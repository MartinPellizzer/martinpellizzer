Rapuntium alsinoides

Rapuntium alsinoides, commonly known as the common rapunculus, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and hairy, with a flowering stem that produces small, yellow flowers. Its fruit is a cylindrical silique, containing several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
