Ranunculus purshii var. schizanthus

Ranunculus purshii var. schizanthus, commonly known as the Schizanthus ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers range in color from white to pink and yellow.

It is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.
