Ranunculus pullaster

Ranunculus pullaster, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 13 petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
