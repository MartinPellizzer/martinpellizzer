Ranunculus nudatus

Ranunculus nudatus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with numerous petals and a hairy stem that often lies close to the ground. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are deeply lobed.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and a valued ornamental plant.
