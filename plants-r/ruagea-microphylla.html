Ruagea microphylla

Ruagea microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved ruagea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Ruagea microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
