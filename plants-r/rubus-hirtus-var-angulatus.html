Rubus hirtus var. angulatus

Rubus hirtus var. angulatus, commonly known as the angular blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
