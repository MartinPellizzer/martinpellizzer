Ranunculus monspeliacus var. cuneatus

Ranunculus monspeliacus var. cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, with a hairy surface and a distinctive yellow flower with five petals. The plant produces a cluster of small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
