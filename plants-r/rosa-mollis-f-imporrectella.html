Rosa mollis f. imporrectella

Rosa mollis f. imporrectella, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and fragrant pink to white flowers.

It is a hybrid form of Rosa mollis, which is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of garden conditions.
