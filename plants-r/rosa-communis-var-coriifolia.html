Rosa communis var. coriifolia

Rosa communis var. coriifolia, commonly known as the coral tree rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The leaves are compound, with ovate leaflets that are slightly serrated and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively disease-resistant.
