Rubus moluccanus var. tiliaceus

Rubus moluccanus var. tiliaceus, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for their flavor and potential health benefits.
