Rubus × rudifrons

Rubus × rudifrons, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of many bramble species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are often used in culinary applications, such as jams and pies, due to their tart flavor.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates.
