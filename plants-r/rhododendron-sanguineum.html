Rhododendron sanguineum

Rhododendron sanguineum, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has leathery, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers are rich in nectar and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
