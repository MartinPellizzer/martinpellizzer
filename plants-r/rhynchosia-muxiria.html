Rhynchosia muxiria

Rhynchosia muxiria, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

Rhynchosia muxiria thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
