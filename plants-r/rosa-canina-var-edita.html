Rosa canina var. edita

Rosa canina var. edita, commonly known as the Turkish rose or Edith's rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces pink to red flowers in late spring, which are followed by orange-red hips, its characteristic fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and scrublands.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
