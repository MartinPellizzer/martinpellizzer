Restrepia erythroxantha

Restrepia erythroxantha, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high elevations. The plant features a pseudobulb with long, narrow leaves and produces striking red and orange flowers during the spring season. Its floral structure is typical of the Orchidaceae family, with showy petals and a specialized reproductive system.

Restrepia erythroxantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique coloration and delicate appearance.
