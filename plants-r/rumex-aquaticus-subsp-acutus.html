Rumex aquaticus subsp. acutus

Rumex aquaticus subsp.

acutus, commonly known as sharpleaf water sorrel, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments. It features slender, upright stems and is characterized by its distinctive, acute (sharp-pointed) leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

It is commonly found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of slow-moving streams.
