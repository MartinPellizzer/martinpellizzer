Rhododendron balsaminiflorum

Rhododendron balsaminiflorum, commonly known as the balsam-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to mountainous landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic, well-drained soil.
