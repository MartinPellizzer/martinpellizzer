Rhynchospora spiciformis

Rhynchospora spiciformis, commonly known as the spikeflower sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers, typically blooming in late summer. Rhynchospora spiciformis thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
