Rhus quinquefolia

Rhus quinquefolia, commonly known as sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically consist of 5 leaflets, giving it the species name *quinquefolia*. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into clusters of red, fuzzy berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands, fields, and along roadsides.

Rhus quinquefolia is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
