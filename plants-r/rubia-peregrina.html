Rubia peregrina

Rubia peregrina, commonly known as the wandering cowplant, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils across Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that have a hairy texture. Its small, yellowish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the spring.

Rubia peregrina is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
