Rubus davisiorum

Rubus davisiorum, commonly known as the Davis's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
