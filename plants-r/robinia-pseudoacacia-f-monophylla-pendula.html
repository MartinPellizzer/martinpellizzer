Robinia pseudoacacia f. monophylla-pendula

Robinia pseudoacacia f. monophylla-pendula, commonly known as the weeping false acacia, is a unique cultivar of the black locust tree.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and dark green, with a slightly serrated edge. This form is a result of selective breeding to enhance its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
