Rubus discors

Rubus discors, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruits are edible, though they can be quite tart.

Rubus discors is a perennial shrub that is widely cultivated for its berries and used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
