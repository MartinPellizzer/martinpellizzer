Racosperma crispulum

Racosperma crispulum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, pinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.

Racosperma crispulum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits the surrounding ecosystem.
