Rhododendron mechukae

Rhododendron mechukae, commonly known as the Mechuca rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
