Rosa palustris f. alba

Rosa palustris f. alba, commonly known as the white swamp rose, is a botanical variant of the swamp rose native to North America.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, and it produces small, red or orange hips in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
