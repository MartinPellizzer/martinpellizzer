Rubus ursinus var. medusae

Rubus ursinus var. medusae, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to western North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
