Rosa × macrodonta

Rosa × macrodonta, commonly known as the large-flowered shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and is native to the eastern United States. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in stiff, curved prickles.

Rosa × macrodonta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
