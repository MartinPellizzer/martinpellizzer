Ranunculus vilaenadalis

Ranunculus vilaenadalis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has simple, palmately lobed leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is often found in rocky meadows and scree slopes at high elevations.
