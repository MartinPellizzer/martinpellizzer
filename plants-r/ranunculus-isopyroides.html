Ranunculus isopyroides

Ranunculus isopyroides, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus isopyroides is often found in temperate regions and is considered a herbaceous perennial.
