Rubus pseudomelanoxylon

Rubus pseudomelanoxylon, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, typically dark purple to black in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
