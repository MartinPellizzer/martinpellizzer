Rafnia rhomboidea

Rafnia rhomboidea, commonly known as the diamondleaf senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant features palmately compound leaves with three leaflets, each typically rhomboidal in shape, giving it its scientific name. It produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its seeds are enclosed in pods.

Rafnia rhomboidea is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
