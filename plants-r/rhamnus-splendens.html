Rhamnus splendens

Rhamnus splendens, commonly known as the shining buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark gray bark and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Rhamnus splendens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
