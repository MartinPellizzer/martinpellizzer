Rhododendron zaleucum var. flaviflorum

Rhododendron zaleucum var. flaviflorum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in hue, blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments with partial to full shade, often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for specialized gardens and botanical collections.
