Rubus triphyllus var. purpureus

Rubus triphyllus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-leaf raspberry, is a variant of the trailing blackberry species native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple foliage, which contrasts with its typical red or orange berries. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and culinary benefits.
