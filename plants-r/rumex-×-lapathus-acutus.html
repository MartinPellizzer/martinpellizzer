Rumex × lapathus-acutus

Rumex × lapathus-acutus, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rumex lapathifolius and Rumex acutifolius.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on erect, branched stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
