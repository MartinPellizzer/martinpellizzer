Rhododendron simiarum var. deltoideum

Rhododendron simiarum var. deltoideum, commonly known as the delta leaf rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its broad, deltoid-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a component of natural mountainous landscapes.
