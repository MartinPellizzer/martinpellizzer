Ruta fimbriata

Ruta fimbriata, commonly known as fringed rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as citrus and rue. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. It produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps, which contain the seeds.

Ruta fimbriata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
