Rubus amygdalanthoides

Rubus amygdalanthoides, commonly known as the almond-leaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the second-year stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a distinctive almond-like scent when crushed.

The species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
