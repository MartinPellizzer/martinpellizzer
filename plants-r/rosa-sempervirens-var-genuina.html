Rosa sempervirens var. genuina

Rosa sempervirens var. genuina, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a variant of the evergreen roses native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with thorny stems and an upright growth habit.

This variety is well-suited for hedges or as a specimen plant in gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
