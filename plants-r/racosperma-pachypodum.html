Racosperma pachypodum

Racosperma pachypodum, commonly known as the thick-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Racosperma pachypodum is often used in revegetation projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
