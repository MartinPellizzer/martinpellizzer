Ranunculus auricomus subsp. parcisectus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

parcisectus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and deeply lobed leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaf segments compared to the typical form of R. auricomus. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in moist, rocky soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
