Rosa sestinensis

Rosa sestinensis, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and is characterized by its thorny stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Rosa sestinensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in perfumery.
