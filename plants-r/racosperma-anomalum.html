Racosperma anomalum

Racosperma anomalum, commonly known as the anomalous racosperma, is a species of legume native to Australia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Racosperma anomalum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
