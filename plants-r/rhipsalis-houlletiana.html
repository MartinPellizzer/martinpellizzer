Rhipsalis houlletiana

Rhipsalis houlletiana, commonly known as the Houllet's mistletoe cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems that often hang in a cascading manner, giving it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
