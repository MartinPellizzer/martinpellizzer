Rubus hirtus proles nigricatus

Rubus hirtus proles nigricatus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale green.

The fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
