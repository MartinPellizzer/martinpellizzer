Rubus pruinosus subsp. warmingii

Rubus pruinosus subsp.

warmingii, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface.

The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
