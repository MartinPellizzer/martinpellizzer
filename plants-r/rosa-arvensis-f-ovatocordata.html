Rosa arvensis f. ovatocordata

Rosa arvensis f. ovatocordata, commonly known as the broad-leafed dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its ovate, cordate leaves that are broader and more rounded than those of the typical form. The plant produces fragrant pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This form is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
