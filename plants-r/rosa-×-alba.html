Rosa × alba

Rosa × alba, commonly known as the white garden rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which are typically single or semi-double in form. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Rosa × alba is also valued for its fragrance and is often used in perfumery and floral arrangements.
