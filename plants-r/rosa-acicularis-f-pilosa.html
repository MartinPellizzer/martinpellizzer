Rosa acicularis f. pilosa

Rosa acicularis f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy thorned rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa acicularis.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly serrated edge.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in various climates.
