Ranunculus parvitorus

Ranunculus parvitorus, commonly known as the small-flowered buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and it has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus parvitorus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful flowers.
