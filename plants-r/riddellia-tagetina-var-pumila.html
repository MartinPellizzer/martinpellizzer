Riddellia tagetina var. pumila

Riddellia tagetina var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf African daisy, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to southern Africa.

It features small, daisy-like yellow flowers with dark central disks, blooming in spring and summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant, hardy plants.
