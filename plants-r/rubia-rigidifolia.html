Rubia rigidifolia

Rubia rigidifolia, commonly known as rigid stem madder, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its erect, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The roots of Rubia rigidifolia have been historically used in traditional medicine for their dyeing properties and potential health benefits.
