Razoumofskya cyanocarpa

Razoumofskya cyanocarpa, commonly known as the blue-fruited razoumofskya, is a rare and unusual plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Russia.

It is characterized by its striking blue berries, which are unusually large for its genus and contain a high concentration of anthocyanins. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

Due to its limited distribution and unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
