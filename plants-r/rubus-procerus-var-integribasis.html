Rubus procerus var. integribasis

Rubus procerus var. integribasis, commonly known as the smoothleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and hairy stems that become smoother with age. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a slightly pubescent underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in the axils of the leaves, while the fruit is a dark purple to black aggregate berry.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and edible fruit.
