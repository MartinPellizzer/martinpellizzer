Rubus hirtus subvar. amoenus

Rubus hirtus subvar. amoenus, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
