Rubus victoris

Rubus victoris, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that produces edible blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pink flowers in the spring. Its berries are typically harvested in late summer and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Rubus victoris thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.
