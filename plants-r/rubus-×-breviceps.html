Rubus × breviceps

Rubus × breviceps, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of typical blackberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and as a wild plant in natural habitats.
