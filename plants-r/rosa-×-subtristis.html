Rosa × subtristis

Rosa × subtristis, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year, making it distinct from other rose species. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often in clusters. It is native to the western coast of the United States, particularly California, and thrives in coastal climates.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
