Raphanus raphanistrum var. venosus

Raphanus raphanistrum var. venosus, commonly known as the hairy wild radish, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by pods containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The hairy wild radish is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
