Rubus diclinis var. novoguineensis

Rubus diclinis var. novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea brambles, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it has a thorny stem structure.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in horticulture.
