Rumex anthoxanthus

Rumex anthoxanthus, commonly known as the yellow dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and roots have been historically used in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.

Rumex anthoxanthus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.
