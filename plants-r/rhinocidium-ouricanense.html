Rhinocidium ouricanense

Rhinocidium ouricanense, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and green. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
