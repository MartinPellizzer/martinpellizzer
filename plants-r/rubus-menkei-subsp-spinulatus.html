Rubus menkei subsp. spinulatus

Rubus menkei subsp.

spinulatus, commonly known as the spiny raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing leaves and the second year bearing fruit.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, red drupes that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated margins and hairy surfaces.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
