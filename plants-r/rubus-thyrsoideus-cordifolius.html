Rubus thyrsoideus cordifolius

Rubus thyrsoideus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and have a distinctive deep green color with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its thorny stems and showy white flowers make it a notable presence in its natural habitat.
