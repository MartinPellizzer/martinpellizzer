Rosa sempervirens var. scandens

Rosa sempervirens var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing rose or Scotch rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It features thorny stems that can climb or trail, making it suitable for trellises or hedging. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and serrated, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season.

Rosa sempervirens var. scandens is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
