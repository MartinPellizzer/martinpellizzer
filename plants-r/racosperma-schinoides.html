Racosperma schinoides

Racosperma schinoides, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant often has a dense, bushy growth habit.

Racosperma schinoides is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a resilient species in arid environments.
