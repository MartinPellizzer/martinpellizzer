Rubus × adscitiformis

Rubus × adscitiformis, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus fruticosus (blackberry) and Rubus ursinus (raspberry).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that transition from red to black as they ripen. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both parent species, showcasing a blend of morphological and genetic features.
