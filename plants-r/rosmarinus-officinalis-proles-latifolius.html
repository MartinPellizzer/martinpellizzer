Rosmarinus officinalis proles latifolius

Rosmarinus officinalis proles latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf rosemary, is a variant of the common rosemary plant characterized by its wider, more robust leaves.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils and sunny conditions. The leaves are aromatic, containing essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties. This plant is often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and pharmacological purposes.
