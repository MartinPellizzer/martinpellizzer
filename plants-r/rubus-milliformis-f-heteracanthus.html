Rubus milliformis f. heteracanthus

Rubus milliformis f. heteracanthus, commonly known as the variegated blackberry, is a horticultural form of the blackberry plant.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring a mix of green and variegated white or yellow markings on the leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of the standard Rubus milliformis. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and the distinctive appearance of its leaves.
