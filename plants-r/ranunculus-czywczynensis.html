Ranunculus czywczynensis

Ranunculus czywczynensis, commonly known as the Czywczyn meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has palmate leaves that are deeply divided. It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

However, it is relatively rare and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.
