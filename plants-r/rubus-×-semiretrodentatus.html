Rubus × semiretrodentatus

Rubus × semiretrodentatus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus strigosus, resulting in a species with distinctive red fruit. It typically grows in temperate regions and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The berries are small, tart, and often used in culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
