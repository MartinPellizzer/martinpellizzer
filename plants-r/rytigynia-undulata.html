Rytigynia undulata

Rytigynia undulata, commonly known as the wavyleaf rytigynia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
