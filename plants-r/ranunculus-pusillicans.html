Ranunculus pusillicans

Ranunculus pusillicans, commonly known as the small-flowered buttercup, is a delicate herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Ranunculus pusillicans is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is known for its short flowering period.
