Rosa glauca f. heliophila

Rosa glauca f. heliophila, commonly known as the sun-loving form of the blue rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its pale blue to white petals.

It is a natural variant of Rosa glauca, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually single or semi-double, with a delicate appearance that enhances its ornamental value.

This form is prized in horticulture for its unique color and ability to thrive in full sun conditions.
