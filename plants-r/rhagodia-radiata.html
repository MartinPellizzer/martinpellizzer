Rhagodia radiata

Rhagodia radiata, commonly known as the red globemallow, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive red center, which bloom from late winter to spring. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Rhagodia radiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
