Rubus hungaricus

Rubus hungaricus, commonly known as the Hungarian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and small-scale agriculture.
