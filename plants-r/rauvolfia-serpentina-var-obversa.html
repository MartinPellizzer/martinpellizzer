Rauvolfia serpentina var. obversa

Rauvolfia serpentina var. obversa, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a variant of the Rauvolfia serpentina species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and red or orange berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of alkaloids like reserpine, which have been used in traditional medicine and modern pharmacology.
