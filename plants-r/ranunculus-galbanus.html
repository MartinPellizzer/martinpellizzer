Ranunculus galbanus

Ranunculus galbanus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five petals and a yellow center, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture.

Ranunculus galbanus is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its role in supporting pollinators.
