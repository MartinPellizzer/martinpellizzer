Rubus radula var. pulcher

Rubus radula var. pulcher, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

The flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals, and appear in clusters during the spring.
