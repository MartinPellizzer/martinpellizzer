Rubus conspicuus f. sylvatica

Rubus conspicuus f. sylvatica, commonly known as the forest raspberry, is a variant of the bramble plant native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in woodland environments, preferring partial shade and well-drained soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
