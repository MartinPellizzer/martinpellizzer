Rubus vestitus var. chloroscarythrus

Rubus vestitus var. chloroscarythrus, commonly known as the green-hairy raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features dense, greenish-white hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild flavor in culinary applications.
