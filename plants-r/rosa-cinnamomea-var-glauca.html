Rosa cinnamomea var. glauca

Rosa cinnamomea var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous cinnamon rose, is a variant of the cinnamon rose species native to China.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive cinnamon-scented fragrance. The leaves are typically glaucous, meaning they have a whitish, powdery coating that reflects light. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate blooms and unique aromatic qualities.
