Rubus atrorubens

Rubus atrorubens, commonly known as the black raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its dark red to black berries. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit develops from a cluster of small drupes.

The plant thrives in woodland areas and is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit.
