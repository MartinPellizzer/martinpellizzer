Ribes leptanthum var. veganum

Ribes leptanthum var. veganum, commonly known as the vegan raspberry, is a variant of the shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive berries. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and a glossy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential use in local cuisine and herbal applications.
