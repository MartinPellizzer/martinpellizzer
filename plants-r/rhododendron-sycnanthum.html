Rhododendron sycnanthum

Rhododendron sycnanthum, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow throat, and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural diversity.
