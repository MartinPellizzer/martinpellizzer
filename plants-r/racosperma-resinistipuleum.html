Racosperma resinistipuleum

Racosperma resinistipuleum, commonly known as the resinous stipule acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, resin-secreting stipules that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
