Rosa sinuatidens

Rosa sinuatidens, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a species native to China and is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double flowers.

It typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves that have a serrated edge. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season. It is well adapted to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Rosa sinuatidens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
