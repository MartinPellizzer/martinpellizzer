Rubus semileucanthemus

Rubus semileucanthemus, commonly known as the white-flowered blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, a small, dark purple berry, is edible but often has a tart flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.
