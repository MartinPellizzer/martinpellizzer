Ribes sanguineum var. sanguineum

Ribes sanguineum var. sanguineum, commonly known as the red currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring arching branches covered in small, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, edible berries that ripen in late summer. Its flowers are pink to white, with five petals, and it is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
