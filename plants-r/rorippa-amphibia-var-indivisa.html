Rorippa amphibia var. indivisa

Rorippa amphibia var. indivisa, commonly known as the European watercress, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, ditches, and the edges of ponds. The plant has alternate, broadly ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually submerged or floating. Its small, white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed aquatic habitats.
