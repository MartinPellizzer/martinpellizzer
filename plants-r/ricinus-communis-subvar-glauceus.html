Ricinus communis subvar. glauceus

Ricinus communis subvar. glauceus, commonly known as the yellow castor oil plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically green with a yellowish or white midrib. The plant produces distinctive spiny capsules that contain toxic, orange-red seeds. Its flowers are unisexual, with staminate and pistillate flowers occurring on separate plants.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and historical uses.
