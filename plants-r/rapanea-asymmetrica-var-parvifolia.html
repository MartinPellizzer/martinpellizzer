Rapanea asymmetrica var. parvifolia

Rapanea asymmetrica var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved rapanea, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
