Ranunculus iriomotensis

Ranunculus iriomotensis, commonly known as the Iriomote buttercup, is a rare flowering plant native to the island of Iriomote in the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often bright-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers usually have five to seven petals.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is protected under conservation efforts.
