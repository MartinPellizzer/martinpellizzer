Rubus nitidus f. umbrosus

Rubus nitidus f. umbrosus, commonly known as the gloomy bramble, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, typically red or black berries, which are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of understory ecosystems.
