Rhus hartmanii

Rhus hartmanii, commonly known as Hartman's sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring. Its fruit is a red, fuzzy sphere that contains several seeds, typical of the genus Rhus.

The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
