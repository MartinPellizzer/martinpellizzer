Rubus × pseudocuneatiformis

Rubus × pseudocuneatiformis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or dewberry.

This plant is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments. The plant produces small, dark-colored berries that are edible and used in culinary applications.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and a complex reproductive system involving both sexual and asexual propagation.
