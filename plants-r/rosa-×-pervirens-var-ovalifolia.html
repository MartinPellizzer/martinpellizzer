Rosa × pervirens var. ovalifolia

Rosa × pervirens var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its ovate, serrated leaves that are typically medium to dark green in color. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early summer. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
