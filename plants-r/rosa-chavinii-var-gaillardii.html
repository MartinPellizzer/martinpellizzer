Rosa chavinii var. gaillardii

Rosa chavinii var. gaillardii, commonly known as the Chavin rose, is a rare and ancient species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cooler, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, and it produces rose hips that are edible.

Due to its limited distribution and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
