Rubus caesius subf. grandiflorus

Rubus caesius subf.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered blackberry, is a subshrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are typically dark purple to black when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
