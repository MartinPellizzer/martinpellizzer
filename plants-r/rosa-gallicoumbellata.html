Rosa gallicoumbellata

Rosa gallicoumbellata, commonly known as the French mountain rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central cone of stamens. It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

Rosa gallicoumbellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and historical significance.
