Rosa gallica f. pseudolivescens

Rosa gallica f. pseudolivescens, commonly known as the French rose, is a cultivar of the species Rosa gallica, native to Europe.

It is characterized by its deep pink to crimson flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This rose is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
