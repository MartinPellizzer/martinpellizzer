Rudbeckia heliopsidis f. villipes

Rudbeckia heliopsidis f. villipes, commonly known as the variegated coneflower, is a hybrid form of the coneflower genus Rudbeckia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flower heads with a distinctive central disk, surrounded by narrow, dark green to variegated foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
