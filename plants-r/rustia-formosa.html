Rustia formosa

Rustia formosa, commonly known as the golden rustia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Rustia formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
