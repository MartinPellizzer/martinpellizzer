Rhynchosia paniculata

Rhynchosia paniculata, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by long, slender pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Rhynchosia paniculata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
