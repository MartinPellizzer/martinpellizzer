Ranunculus paroicus

Ranunculus paroicus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and palmate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other showy, often toxic plants.

Ranunculus paroicus is often found in meadows and slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
