Rhododendron chunii subsp. vietnamense

Rhododendron chunii subsp.

vietnamense, commonly known as the Vietnamese rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Vietnam. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,000 meters, preferring acidic, well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
