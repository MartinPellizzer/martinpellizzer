Rosa pimpinellifolia var. subalpina

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. subalpina, commonly known as the Alpine shrub rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in thorny projections.

This variety is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in subalpine and alpine zones.
