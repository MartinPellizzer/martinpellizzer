Rubus montivagus

Rubus montivagus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Rubus montivagus plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
