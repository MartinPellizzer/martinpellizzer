Rosa leschenaultiana

Rosa leschenaultiana, commonly known as the Leschenault's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in suitable climates.

Rosa leschenaultiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
