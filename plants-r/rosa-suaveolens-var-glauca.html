Rosa suaveolens var. glauca

Rosa suaveolens var. glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous rose, is a variety of the sweetbriar rose.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers and a distinctive bluish-gray foliage. The plant is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions with moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, fragrant blooms in the spring and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its unique appearance.
