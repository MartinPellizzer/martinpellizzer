Rosa canina f. versutoides

Rosa canina f. versutoides, commonly known as the creeping dogwood rose, is a hybrid form of the common rose.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its foliage is typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging garden conditions.
