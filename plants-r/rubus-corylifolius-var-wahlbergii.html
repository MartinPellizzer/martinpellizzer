Rubus corylifolius var. wahlbergii

Rubus corylifolius var. wahlbergii, commonly known as the Wahlberg's blackberry, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, serrated edge. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in horticulture.
