Ranunculus insertus

Ranunculus insertus, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, while the flowers consist of numerous small, yellow petals surrounding a central cluster of stamens.

Ranunculus insertus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
