Rapanea torricellensis

Rapanea torricellensis, commonly known as the Torricella boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by berries that are edible for some bird species.

Due to its unique appearance and restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
