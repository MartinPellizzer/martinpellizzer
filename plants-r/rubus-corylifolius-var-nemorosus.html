Rubus corylifolius var. nemorosus

Rubus corylifolius var. nemorosus, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry, is a variant of the broad-leafed raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in forested areas. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black aggregate berry.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
