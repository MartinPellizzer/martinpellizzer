Rhynchosia viscosa var. breviracemosa

Rhynchosia viscosa var. breviracemosa, commonly known as the sticky legume, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
