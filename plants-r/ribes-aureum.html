Ribes aureum

Ribes aureum, commonly known as the golden currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and often used in jams and wines. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Ribes aureum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible landscaping.
