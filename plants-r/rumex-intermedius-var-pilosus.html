Rumex intermedius var. pilosus

Rumex intermedius var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, along roadsides, and in waste areas. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces elliptical, dark purple to black seeds.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils.
