Ranunculus affinis var. dasycarpus

Ranunculus affinis var. dasycarpus, commonly known as the hairy-fruited buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its fruit is distinctive, characterized by a hairy, segmented capsule that aids in seed dispersal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
