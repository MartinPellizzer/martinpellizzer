Rhododendron archboldianum

Rhododendron archboldianum, commonly known as the Archbold's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

However, it is also an important species in its native habitat, contributing to local biodiversity and ecological balance.
