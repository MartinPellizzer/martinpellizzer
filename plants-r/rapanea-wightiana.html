Rapanea wightiana

Rapanea wightiana, commonly known as the Malabar cherry or Indian holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in dense forests and undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
