Rosa afzeliana f. subbiserrata

Rosa afzeliana f. subbiserrata, commonly known as the serrated rose, is a botanical variety characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features.

It is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are compound, typically comprising five to seven leaflets with serrated edges, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and a strong, pleasant fragrance.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
