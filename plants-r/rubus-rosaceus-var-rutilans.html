Rubus rosaceus var. rutilans

Rubus rosaceus var. rutilans, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant's flowers are white or pale pink, and it bears edible berries that are typically reddish when mature. The berries are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications due to their tart flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value as well as its fruit production.
