Rhododendron oreites

Rhododendron oreites, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and shrublands, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron oreites plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
