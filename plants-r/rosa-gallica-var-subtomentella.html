Rosa gallica var. subtomentella

Rosa gallica var. subtomentella, commonly known as the white gallica rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval, serrated leaflets, and the plant has a woody, bushy growth habit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and medicine.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soil in full sun.
