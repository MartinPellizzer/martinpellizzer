Ranunculus farsicus

Ranunculus farsicus, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have numerous stamens, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Ranunculus farsicus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
