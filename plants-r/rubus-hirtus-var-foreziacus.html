Rubus hirtus var. foreziacus

Rubus hirtus var. foreziacus, commonly known as the Alaska raspberry, is a shrub native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a food source for wildlife.
