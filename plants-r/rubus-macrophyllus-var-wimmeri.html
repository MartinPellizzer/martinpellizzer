Rubus macrophyllus var. wimmeri

Rubus macrophyllus var. wimmeri, commonly known as the Wimmer's rosehip, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
