Rechsteineria sceptrum var. rubra

Rechsteineria sceptrum var. rubra, commonly known as the red-stemmed rechsteineria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
