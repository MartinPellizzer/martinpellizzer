Rubus peii

Rubus peii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
