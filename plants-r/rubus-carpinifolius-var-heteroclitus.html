Rubus carpinifolius var. heteroclitus

Rubus carpinifolius var. heteroclitus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, often forming dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wildlife and ecological studies.
