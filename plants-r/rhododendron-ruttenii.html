Rhododendron ruttenii

Rhododendron ruttenii, commonly known as the Rutten's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. It is often found in forest understories, where it forms thickets due to its vigorous growth habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
