Rabdosia × ohwii

Rabdosia × ohwii, commonly known as the hybrid blue mistflower, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, silver-white to blue-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its ornamental value.

Rabdosia × ohwii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
