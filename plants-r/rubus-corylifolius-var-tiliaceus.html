Rubus corylifolius var. tiliaceus

Rubus corylifolius var. tiliaceus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often palmately divided and have a distinctive, broad, maple-like appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.
