Rubus neillioides

Rubus neillioides, commonly known as the Neill's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
