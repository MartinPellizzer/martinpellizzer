Ruellia caroliniensis var. cinerascens

Ruellia caroliniensis var. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are tubular and typically bloom in shades of blue or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
