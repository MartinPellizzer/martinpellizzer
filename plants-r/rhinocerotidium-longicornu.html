Rhinocerotidium longicornu

Rhinocerotidium longicornu, commonly known as the long-horned rhinoceros plant, is a unique species with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its elongated, curved horns, which are actually modified leaves or stems. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

The flowers are small and clustered, often producing a mild fragrance that attracts pollinators.
