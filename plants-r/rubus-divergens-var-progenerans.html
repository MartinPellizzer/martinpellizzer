Rubus divergens var. progenerans

Rubus divergens var. progenerans, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its prostrate growth habit.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems that help in its identification. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in local cuisines.
