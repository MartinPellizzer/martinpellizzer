Rubus corchorifolius var. tanakae

Rubus corchorifolius var. tanakae, commonly known as Tanaka's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of the common blackberry. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
