Rubus radula cordatus

Rubus radula cordatus, commonly known as the heart-leaved blackberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rubus genus, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The leaves are heart-shaped, with a distinctive lobed structure, and are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate berries that are usually dark purple to black when mature.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of woodlands and thickets.
