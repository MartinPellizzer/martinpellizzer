Rubus rosanthus var. cordatiformis

Rubus rosanthus var. cordatiformis, commonly known as the heart-shaped blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which give it its botanical name. It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, dark red berries. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental value.
