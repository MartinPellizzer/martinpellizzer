Rhynchosia edulis

Rhynchosia edulis, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces yellow flowers and pod fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and hairy stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Rhynchosia edulis is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in traditional cuisines.
