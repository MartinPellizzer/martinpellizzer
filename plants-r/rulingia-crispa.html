Rulingia crispa

Rulingia crispa, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rulingia crispa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
