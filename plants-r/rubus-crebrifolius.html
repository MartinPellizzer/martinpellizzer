Rubus crebrifolius

Rubus crebrifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with numerous serrated leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are edible but can be quite tart, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
