Rubus × consoranensis

Rubus × consoranensis, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its berries are dark purple to black in color and are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, Rubus ursinus and Rubus argutus.
