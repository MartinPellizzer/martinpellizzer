Rubus rosaceus microgene rosaceifrons

Rubus rosaceus microgene rosaceifrons, commonly known as the red raspberries, is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, alternate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces aggregate fruits composed of multiple drupelets.

This species is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in antioxidants and used in various culinary applications.
