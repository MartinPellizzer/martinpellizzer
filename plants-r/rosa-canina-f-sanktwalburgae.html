Rosa canina f. sanktwalburgae

Rosa canina f. sanktwalburgae, commonly known as the Sankt Walburgae rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-red flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. This form is native to Central Europe and is often found in woodland and hedgerow environments.

The plant produces small, red hips that are rich in vitamin C. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
