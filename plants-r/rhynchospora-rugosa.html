Rhynchospora rugosa

Rhynchospora rugosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other similar plants. The plant has tall, cylindrical stems covered in rough, textured hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typical of the sedge family.

Rhynchospora rugosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
