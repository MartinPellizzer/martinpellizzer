Rosa tomentella var. tanneronica

Rosa tomentella var. tanneronica, commonly known as the Tanner's woolly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly silver-gray foliage and fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
