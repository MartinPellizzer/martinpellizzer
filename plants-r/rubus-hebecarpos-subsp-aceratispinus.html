Rubus hebecarpos subsp. aceratispinus

Rubus hebecarpos subsp.

aceratispinus, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a deep red color, often with a distinctive white bloom. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
