Restio verticillaris

Restio verticillaris, commonly known as the spiral restio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its unique growth form and ecological significance, it is an important component of its native habitat.
