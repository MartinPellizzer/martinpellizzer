Ronaldella aryter

Ronaldella aryter, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ronaldella aryter is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
