Rulingia craurophylla

Rulingia craurophylla, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has a distinctive, symmetrical crown and can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its showy floral display.
