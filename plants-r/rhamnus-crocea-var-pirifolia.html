Rhamnus crocea var. pirifolia

Rhamnus crocea var. pirifolia, commonly known as the yellow buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
