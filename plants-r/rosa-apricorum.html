Rosa apricorum

Rosa apricorum, commonly known as the apricot rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas, and is known for its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a subtle apricot scent.

Rosa apricorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
