Rosa indica sulphurea

Rosa indica sulphurea, commonly known as the golden rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and has adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Rosa indica sulphurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking color.
