Rapistrum rugosum var. scabrum

Rapistrum rugosum var. scabrum, commonly known as rough wallaby tail, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and distinctive, woolly, cylindrical seed heads that resemble a wallaby's tail. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its rough, hairy texture and unique appearance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
