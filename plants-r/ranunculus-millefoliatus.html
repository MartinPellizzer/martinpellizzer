Ranunculus millefoliatus

Ranunculus millefoliatus, commonly known as the leafy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Ranunculus millefoliatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
