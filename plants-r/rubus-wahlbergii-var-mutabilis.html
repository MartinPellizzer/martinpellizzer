Rubus wahlbergii var. mutabilis

Rubus wahlbergii var. mutabilis, commonly known as the Wahlberg's raspberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that change color from white to red as they mature. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rocky outcrops and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
