Rhynchosia elongata

Rhynchosia elongata, commonly known as the longpod snout bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur at the base of the petals. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Rhynchosia elongata is valued for its edible seeds and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
