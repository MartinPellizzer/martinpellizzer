Rosa spithamea var. subinermis

Rosa spithamea var. subinermis, commonly known as the soft-stemmed shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that are covered in soft, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
