Racosperma homalocladum

Racosperma homalocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes.

This species is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy soils.
