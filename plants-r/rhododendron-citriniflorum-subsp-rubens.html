Rhododendron citriniflorum subsp. rubens

Rhododendron citriniflorum subsp.

rubens, commonly known as the red-flowered yellow laurel, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pale yellow to deep red.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial shade.
