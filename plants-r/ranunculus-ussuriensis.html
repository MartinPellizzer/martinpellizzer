Ranunculus ussuriensis

Ranunculus ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
