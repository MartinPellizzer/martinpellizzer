Rosa graveolens var. micrantha

Rosa graveolens var. micrantha, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its fruit is a red, hairy rosehip that develops after the flowers fade.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
