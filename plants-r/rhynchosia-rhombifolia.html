Rhynchosia rhombifolia

Rhynchosia rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamondleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces lens-shaped pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is often grown as a forage crop or for its nutritional value.

Rhynchosia rhombifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility.
