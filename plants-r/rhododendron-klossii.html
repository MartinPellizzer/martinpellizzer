Rhododendron klossii

Rhododendron klossii, commonly known as the Kloss rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a lush backdrop for its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron klossii thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
