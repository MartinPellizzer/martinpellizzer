Rhododendron coryanum

Rhododendron coryanum, commonly known as the Indian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
