Rosa cinnamomea genuina

Rosa cinnamomea genuina, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy yet delicate ornamental plant.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
