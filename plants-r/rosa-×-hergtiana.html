Rosa × hergtiana

Rosa × hergtiana, commonly known as the Herktian rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. Rosa × hergtiana is often grown for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
