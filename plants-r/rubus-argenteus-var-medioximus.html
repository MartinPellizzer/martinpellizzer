Rubus argenteus var. medioximus

Rubus argenteus var. medioximus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its edible fruits.
