Rubus grandifrons

Rubus grandifrons, commonly known as the large-fruited blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into edible, dark purple to black berries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for cultivation in temperate climates.
