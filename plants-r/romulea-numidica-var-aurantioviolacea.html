Romulea numidica var. aurantioviolacea

Romulea numidica var. aurantioviolacea, commonly known as the golden violet romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, showy flowers that range from violet to orange, often with a yellowish tinge, blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that help it survive dry periods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique coloration and compact growth habit make it a desirable ornamental plant.
