Rubus variabilis var. nemorosus

Rubus variabilis var. nemorosus, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
