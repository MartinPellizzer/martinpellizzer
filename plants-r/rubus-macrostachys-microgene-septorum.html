Rubus macrostachys microgene septorum

Rubus macrostachys microgene septorum, commonly known as the large-stemmed blackberry.

This plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It produces dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and wines. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a dense root system that aids in soil stabilization.
