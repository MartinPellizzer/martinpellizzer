Rosa tomentosa var. normalis

Rosa tomentosa var. normalis, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and fragrant pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has thorny stems. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.
