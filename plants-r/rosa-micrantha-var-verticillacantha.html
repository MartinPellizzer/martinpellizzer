Rosa micrantha var. verticillacantha

Rosa micrantha var. verticillacantha, commonly known as the clustered shrub rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless growth habit and compact, rounded form. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
