Rubus blandus

Rubus blandus, commonly known as the soft raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
