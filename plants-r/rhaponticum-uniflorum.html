Rhaponticum uniflorum

Rhaponticum uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered rhapontic, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single bloom per stem.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
