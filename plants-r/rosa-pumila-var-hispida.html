Rosa pumila var. hispida

Rosa pumila var. hispida, commonly known as the rough alpine rose, is a low-growing shrub native to mountainous regions of Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help it withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
