Rhododendron ciliipes

Rhododendron ciliipes, commonly known as the Kashmiri rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. The plant features dense, leathery leaves with a distinctive ciliate margin, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish-purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron ciliipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.
