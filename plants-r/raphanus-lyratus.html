Raphanus lyratus

Raphanus lyratus, commonly known as the lyreleaf radish, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive lyre-shaped leaves and purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. Its roots are edible and have a mild, peppery flavor similar to that of radishes.

Raphanus lyratus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
