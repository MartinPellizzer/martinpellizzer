Rhododendron falcinellum

Rhododendron falcinellum, commonly known as the Sierra foothill rhododendron, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green and glossy. Its flowers are usually pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring.

Rhododendron falcinellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
