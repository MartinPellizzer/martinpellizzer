Rubus roberti

Rubus roberti, commonly known as the Robert's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
