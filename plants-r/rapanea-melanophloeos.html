Rapanea melanophloeos

Rapanea melanophloeos, commonly known as the black-wood or black-leaved tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark is thick and dark, often with a reddish or blackish hue, giving it its common name.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
