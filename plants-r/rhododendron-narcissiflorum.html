Rhododendron narcissiflorum

Rhododendron narcissiflorum, commonly known as the yellow rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It typically grows in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Rhododendron narcissiflorum is also noted for its ability to tolerate shade, making it a popular choice for shaded landscapes.
