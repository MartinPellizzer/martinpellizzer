Rosa canina var. calliantha

Rosa canina var. calliantha, commonly known as the Chinese rose or everlasting rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
