Rottlera tetracocca

Rottlera tetracocca, commonly known as the four-lobed rottlera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
