Rubus × agrestis subsp. deltoideus

Rubus × agrestis subsp.

deltoideus, commonly known as the delta raspberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically oval to elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its growth habit is often sprawling or semi-woody, making it adaptable to different environments.
