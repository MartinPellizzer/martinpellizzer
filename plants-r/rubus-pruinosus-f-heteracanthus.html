Rubus pruinosus f. heteracanthus

Rubus pruinosus f. heteracanthus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other Rubus species, particularly in its leaf morphology and fruit characteristics.
