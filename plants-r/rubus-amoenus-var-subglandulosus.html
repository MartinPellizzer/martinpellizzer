Rubus amoenus var. subglandulosus

Rubus amoenus var. subglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features arching stems with thorns and compound leaves that are serrated and covered in fine glands. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupes, which are typically red when ripe. Its flowers are white with five petals and are borne in clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.
