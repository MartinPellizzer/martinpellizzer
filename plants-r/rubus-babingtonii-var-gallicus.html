Rubus babingtonii var. gallicus

Rubus babingtonii var. gallicus, commonly known as the Gallic raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a tart flavor. The plant has thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
