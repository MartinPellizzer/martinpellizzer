Rubia trichocarpa

Rubia trichocarpa, commonly known as woodruff, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are used in perfumery and traditional medicine for their mild sedative and aromatic properties.
