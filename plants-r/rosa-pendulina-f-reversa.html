Rosa pendulina f. reversa

Rosa pendulina f. reversa, commonly known as the reverse hanging rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa pendulina species.

It is characterized by its pendulous, weeping growth habit, with flowers that face downward, creating an elegant and distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the blossoms.

This cultivar is well-suited for ornamental purposes in gardens and is often used as a decorative hedge or specimen plant.
