Rosa × doniana f. subnuda

Rosa × doniana f. subnuda, commonly known as the naked form of the Doniana rose, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its thornless stems and clustered growth habit.

It typically produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its fragrance is often described as sweet and delicate. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
