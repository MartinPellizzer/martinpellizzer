Rubus foliosus var. illepidus

Rubus foliosus var. illepidus, commonly known as the glossy leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in suitable climates.
