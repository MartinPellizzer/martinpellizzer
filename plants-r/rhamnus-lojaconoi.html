Rhamnus lojaconoi

Rhamnus lojaconoi, commonly known as the Canary Island buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, often thorned branches and opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often growing in dry, sunny conditions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native range.
