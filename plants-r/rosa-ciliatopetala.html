Rosa ciliatopetala

Rosa ciliatopetala, commonly known as the silky-haired rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with soft, hairy undersides, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rosa ciliatopetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
