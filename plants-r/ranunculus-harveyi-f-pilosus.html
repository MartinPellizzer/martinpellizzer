Ranunculus harveyi f. pilosus

Ranunculus harveyi f. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy leaf buttercup, is a variant of the Harvey's buttercup.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features basal leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its distinguishing feature. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are borne on slender stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
