Racosperma sabulosum

Racosperma sabulosum, commonly known as the sand flat senna, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it has a woody stem with a rough texture.

Racosperma sabulosum is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
