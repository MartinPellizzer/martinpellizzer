Rosilla

Rosilla, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Rosilla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 8 through 10.

Its blooming season usually occurs in the spring and early summer.
