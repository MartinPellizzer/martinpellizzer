Rhynchodia wallichii

Rhynchodia wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Rhynchodia wallichii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
