Rubus barkerianus

Rubus barkerianus, commonly known as the Barker's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streamside areas. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and the fruit is often used in local foraging traditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
