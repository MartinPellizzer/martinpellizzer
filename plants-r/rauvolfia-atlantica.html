Rauvolfia atlantica

Rauvolfia atlantica, commonly known as African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its root system is notable for containing alkaloids, which have been studied for their medicinal properties.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is adapted to thrive in humid climates.
