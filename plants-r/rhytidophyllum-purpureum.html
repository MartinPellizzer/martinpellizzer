Rhytidophyllum purpureum

Rhytidophyllum purpureum, commonly known as the purple bark tree, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark that peels away in layers, revealing a vibrant purple color underneath. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive appearance, and its leaves are small and clustered at the base of the trunk. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often found in specific conservation areas.
