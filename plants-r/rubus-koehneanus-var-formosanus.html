Rubus koehneanus var. formosanus

Rubus koehneanus var. formosanus, commonly known as the Formosan raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. It has a woody stem with thorns and is known for its ornamental value as well as its potential use in traditional medicine.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the spring.
