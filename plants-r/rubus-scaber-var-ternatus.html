Rubus scaber var. ternatus

Rubus scaber var. ternatus, commonly known as the rough raspberry or brambles, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small, red drupelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
