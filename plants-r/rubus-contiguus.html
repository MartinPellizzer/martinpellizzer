Rubus contiguus

Rubus contiguus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that produces edible berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. Its fruit develops into bright red, hairy berries that ripen in the summer.

Rubus contiguus is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
