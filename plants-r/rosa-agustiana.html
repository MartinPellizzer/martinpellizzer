Rosa agustiana

Rosa agustiana, commonly known as the Augustan rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late summer. Its flowers are borne on long pedicels and are often used in perfumery due to their strong scent.

Rosa agustiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its beauty and its aromatic properties.
