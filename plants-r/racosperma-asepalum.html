Racosperma asepalum

Racosperma asepalum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub with slender, zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. Its bright yellow flowers appear in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in arid and semi-arid regions.
