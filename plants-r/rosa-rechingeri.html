Rosa rechingeri

Rosa rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia, particularly in the mountains of Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its simple, often thornless stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color.

Rosa rechingeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
