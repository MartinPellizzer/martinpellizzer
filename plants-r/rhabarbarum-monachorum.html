Rhabarbarum monachorum

Rhabarbarum monachorum, commonly known as monk's rhubarb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thick, fleshy roots and upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often covered in a waxy coating, while the flowering stems bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically cultivated for its edible stalks, which are used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
