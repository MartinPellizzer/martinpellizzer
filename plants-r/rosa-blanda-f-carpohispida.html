Rosa blanda f. carpohispida

Rosa blanda f. carpohispida, commonly known as the soft blush rose, is a hybrid form of the Rosa blanda species.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The botanical features of this rose make it a popular choice for native plant landscaping.
