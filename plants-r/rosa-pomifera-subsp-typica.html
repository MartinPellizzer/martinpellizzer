Rosa pomifera subsp. typica

Rosa pomifera subsp.

typica, commonly known as the sea rose, is a species of flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces distinctive, apple-like fruits called rose hips, which are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to salty air and wind exposure.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
