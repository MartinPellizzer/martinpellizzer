Rubus × reversus

Rubus × reversus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus fruticosus (blackberry) and Rubus ideaus (raspberry).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

The hybrid nature of Rubus × reversus contributes to its adaptability and genetic diversity.
