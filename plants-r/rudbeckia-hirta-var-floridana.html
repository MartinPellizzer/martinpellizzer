Rudbeckia hirta var. floridana

Rudbeckia hirta var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida goldcup, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a dark central disk, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are hairy beneath, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

This variety is known for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
