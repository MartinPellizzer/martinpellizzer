Rosa canina var. fallens

Rosa canina var. fallens, commonly known as the autumn rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia. The plant produces fragrant, pink to crimson flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Its fruit, known as a hip, is orange-red and contains a high concentration of vitamin C. The leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant is often used in hedges or for ornamental purposes.
