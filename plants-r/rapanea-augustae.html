Rapanea augustae

Rapanea augustae, commonly known as the Madagascar moonseed, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
