Rubus vallicola

Rubus vallicola, commonly known as the valley bramble, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits, which are edible, are small, red berries that ripen in late summer.

Rubus vallicola is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and streamside habitats.
