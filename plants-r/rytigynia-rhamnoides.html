Rytigynia rhamnoides

Rytigynia rhamnoides, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain several seeds, and the plant is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
