Rubus irritans var. subinermis

Rubus irritans var. subinermis, commonly known as the stinging raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and leaves, which can cause irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is related to other brambles and raspberries.
