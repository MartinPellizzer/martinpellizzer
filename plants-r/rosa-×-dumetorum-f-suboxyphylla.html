Rosa × dumetorum f. suboxyphylla

Rosa × dumetorum f. suboxyphylla, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a hybrid plant species that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a compact shrub with small, usually pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, displaying hairy stems and leaves to withstand cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces rose hips that are often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
