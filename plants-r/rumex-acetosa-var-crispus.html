Rumex acetosa var. crispus

Rumex acetosa var. crispus, commonly known as sourgrass or curly dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems that are often reddish in color and bear alternate, heart-shaped leaves with wavy, serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on terminal spikes. Its leaves are edible and have a sharp, tangy flavor, often used in foraging and traditional cuisine.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, along roadsides, and in wetlands.
