Reseda diffusa

Reseda diffusa, commonly known as common reseda, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Reseda diffusa is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
