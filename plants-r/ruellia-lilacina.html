Ruellia lilacina

Ruellia lilacina, commonly known as purple ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, tubular flowers that range in color from lilac to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, making it a visually striking addition to gardens.

Ruellia lilacina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun.
