Rhodospatha surinamensis

Rhodospatha surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam torch orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, torch-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Rhodospatha surinamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
