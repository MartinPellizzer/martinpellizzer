Rubus malacocarpus

Rubus malacocarpus, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other species of brambles and blackberries.
