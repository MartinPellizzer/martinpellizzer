Rhododendron glaucopeplum

Rhododendron glaucopeplum, commonly known as the blue-leaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that often exhibits a bluish or grayish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
