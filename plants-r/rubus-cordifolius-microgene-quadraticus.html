Rubus cordifolius microgene quadraticus

Rubus cordifolius microgene quadraticus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and heart-shaped, and its dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is a member of the Rosaceae family.

The plant is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
