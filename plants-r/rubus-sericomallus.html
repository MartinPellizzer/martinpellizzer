Rubus sericomallus

Rubus sericomallus, commonly known as the silky raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of prickly stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
