Rubus hybridus subsp. horridus

Rubus hybridus subsp.

horridus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, often found in woodland and chaparral habitats.
