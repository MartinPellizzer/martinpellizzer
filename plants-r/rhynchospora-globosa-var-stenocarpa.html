Rhynchospora globosa var. stenocarpa

Rhynchospora globosa var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited globular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often forming dense vegetation in moist soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various forms of wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
