Renardia

Renardia, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a genus of flowering plants in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, often red or orange flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically has strap-shaped leaves and produces showy blooms in late summer or early autumn. Renardia species are native to southern Africa and are often cultivated for their ornamental value.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making them suitable for garden environments.
