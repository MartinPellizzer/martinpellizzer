Rubus arvensis

Rubus arvensis, commonly known as field raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are red when mature. It is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in open habitats.

The botanical features of Rubus arvensis make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
