Racosperma plautellum

Racosperma plautellum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a small shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathland or open forest environments. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Racosperma plautellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
