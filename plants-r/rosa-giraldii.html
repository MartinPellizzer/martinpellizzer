Rosa giraldii

Rosa giraldii, commonly known as the Giraldii rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely serrated leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Rosa giraldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.

It is also known for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
