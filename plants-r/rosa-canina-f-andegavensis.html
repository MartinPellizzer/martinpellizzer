Rosa canina f. andegavensis

Rosa canina f. andegavensis, commonly known as the Andegavensis rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, orange-red hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its ornamental value and historical significance make it a notable species in horticulture and botany.
