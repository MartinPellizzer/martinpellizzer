Rajania mucronata

Rajania mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it has a symbiotic relationship with certain fungi.

Rajania mucronata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
