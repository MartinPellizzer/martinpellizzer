Rosa davurica

Rosa davurica, commonly known as the shrub rose or Siberian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Rosa davurica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
