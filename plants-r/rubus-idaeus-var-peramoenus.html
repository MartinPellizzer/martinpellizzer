Rubus idaeus var. peramoenus

Rubus idaeus var. peramoenus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry species.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pink to white, and the fruits ripen to a bright red color.

This variety is cultivated for its flavorful berries, which are used in culinary and commercial applications.
