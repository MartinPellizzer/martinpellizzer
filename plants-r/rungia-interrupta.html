Rungia interrupta

Rungia interrupta, commonly known as the red-veined swamp taro, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found near water bodies. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Rungia interrupta is valued both for its ornamental beauty and for its traditional uses in some cultures.
