Racosperma ascendens

Racosperma ascendens, commonly known as the climbing acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, twining stems that allow it to climb and form dense thickets. Its flowers are typically yellow or pale green, arranged in racemes, and it produces legume pods containing seeds.

Racosperma ascendens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use.
