Rhododendron indicum f. japonicum

Rhododendron indicum f. japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage.

It produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Japan and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It prefers partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
