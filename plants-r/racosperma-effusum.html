Racosperma effusum

Racosperma effusum, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Racosperma effusum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal regions.
