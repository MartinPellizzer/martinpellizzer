Rumex caldeirarum

Rumex caldeirarum, commonly known as the Canary Island dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features upright, branched stems covered in stiff, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a reddish or purplish tint. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with green sepals and yellow stamens, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native range.
