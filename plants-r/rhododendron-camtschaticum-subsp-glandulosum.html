Rhododendron camtschaticum subsp. glandulosum

Rhododendron camtschaticum subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as the Caucasian rhododendron, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to reddish flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glandular-hairy leaves and stems, which give it a unique texture. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
