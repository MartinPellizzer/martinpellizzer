Rubus helveticus

Rubus helveticus, commonly known as the Swiss blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
