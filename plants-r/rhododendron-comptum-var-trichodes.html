Rhododendron comptum var. trichodes

Rhododendron comptum var. trichodes, commonly known as the trichodes rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

The flowers are usually pink or red, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
