Rhododendron cuneifolium var. elongatum

Rhododendron cuneifolium var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated alpine rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
