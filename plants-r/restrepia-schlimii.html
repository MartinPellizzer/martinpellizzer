Restrepia schlimii

Restrepia schlimii, commonly known as the Schlim's restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments at high altitudes. The plant produces compact, hairy pseudobulbs that support its narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white with yellow sepals and petals, and it blooms in the spring.

Restrepia schlimii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
