Rubus pycnostachys f. apricus

Rubus pycnostachys f. apricus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a variant of the brambles in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

This cultivar is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its fruiting potential in gardens and agricultural settings.
