Rubia secunda

Rubia secunda, commonly known as lesser cow-wheat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
