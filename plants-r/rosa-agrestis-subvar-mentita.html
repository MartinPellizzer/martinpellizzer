Rosa agrestis subvar. mentita

Rosa agrestis subvar. mentita, commonly known as the wild pink or meadow rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its stems are often covered in thorns, and the flowers are followed by small, red-orange hips that contain seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
