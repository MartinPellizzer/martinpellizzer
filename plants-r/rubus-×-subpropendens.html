Rubus × subpropendens

Rubus × subpropendens, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are composed of small drupelets, giving it a unique texture and flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
