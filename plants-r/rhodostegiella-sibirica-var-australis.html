Rhodostegiella sibirica var. australis

Rhodostegiella sibirica var. australis, commonly known as the Australian siberian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, showy blooms during the spring and summer seasons.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
