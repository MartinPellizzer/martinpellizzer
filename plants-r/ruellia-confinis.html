Ruellia confinis

Ruellia confinis, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tubular, blue or violet flowers that bloom from spring to summer. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Ruellia confinis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
