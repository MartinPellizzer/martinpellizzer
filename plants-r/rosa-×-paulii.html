Rosa × paulii

Rosa × paulii, commonly known as the Pauli rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a high degree of petal layering. It is well adapted to coastal environments due to its salt tolerance and disease resistance.

Rosa × paulii is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
