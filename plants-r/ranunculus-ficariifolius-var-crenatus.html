Ranunculus ficariifolius var. crenatus

Ranunculus ficariifolius var. crenatus, commonly known as the crenate water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic habitats, such as marshes and shallow water. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive crenate or toothed margin.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
