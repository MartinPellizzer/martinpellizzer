Rubus schlechtendalii microgene silvicola

Rubus schlechtendalii microgene silvicola, commonly known as the Siberian blackberry, is a shrub native to the cold climates of eastern Siberia and northern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to harsh, frost-prone environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are small and white or pink.

The berries are edible when ripe and are used in traditional cuisine and local herbal remedies.
