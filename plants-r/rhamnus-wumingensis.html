Rhamnus wumingensis

Rhamnus wumingensis, commonly known as the Wuming buckthorn, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by smooth, black berries that are a food source for various birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
