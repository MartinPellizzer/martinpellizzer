Rubus × multibracteatus

Rubus × multibracteatus, commonly known as the many-bract raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Rubus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its raspberry-like flavor. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces showy white or pink flowers.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
