Rhodocolea racemosa var. typica

Rhodocolea racemosa var. typica, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a prominent yellow throat. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
