Resia nimbicola subsp. nemoralis

Resia nimbicola subsp.

nemoralis, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in open woodland or grassland habitats.

The plant features small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the cooler months. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high altitudes and seasonal droughts.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of the local flora and an important species for ecological studies.
