Roegneria curtiaristata

Roegneria curtiaristata, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Roegneria curtiaristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in rocky or arid landscapes.
