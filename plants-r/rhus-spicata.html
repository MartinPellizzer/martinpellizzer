Rhus spicata

Rhus spicata, commonly known as staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are arranged in pairs along the stems, and its clusters of small, red berries that appear in the fall. The plant is part of the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other species like poison ivy and mango. Its bark is thick and rugged, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it often grows in woodland edges or open fields.
