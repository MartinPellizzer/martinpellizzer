Rosa × hyogoensis

Rosa × hyogoensis, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, pinnately compound leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rosa × hyogoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
