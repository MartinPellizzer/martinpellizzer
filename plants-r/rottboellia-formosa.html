Rottboellia formosa

Rottboellia formosa, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Rottboellia formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
