Rosa abietina var. insubrica

Rosa abietina var. insubrica, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa abietina var. insubrica is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.
