Rubus frondifer

Rubus frondifer, commonly known as the leafy raspberry.

It is a species of bramble in the rose family, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
