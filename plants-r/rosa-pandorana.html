Rosa pandorana

Rosa pandorana, commonly known as the "Pandora's rose," is a striking tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle red or orange hue. The plant has woody stems that can grow up to 20 meters long, making it a vigorous climber. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Rosa pandorana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
