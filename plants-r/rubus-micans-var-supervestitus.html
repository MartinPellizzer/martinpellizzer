Rubus micans var. supervestitus

Rubus micans var. supervestitus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically tart and edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species within the Rubus genus.
