Rubus minnesotanus

Rubus minnesotanus, commonly known as the Minnesota blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
