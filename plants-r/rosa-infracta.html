Rosa infracta

Rosa infracta, commonly known as the broken rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Rosa infracta is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
