Reynoutria japonica var. elata

Reynoutria japonica var. elata, commonly known as giant Japanese knotweed, is a vigorous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread rapidly and aggressively. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that can grow up to 12 inches in length. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a significant ecological and agricultural pest in many regions outside its native range.
