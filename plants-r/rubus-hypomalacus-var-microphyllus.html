Rubus hypomalacus var. microphyllus

Rubus hypomalacus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
