Rubus anglosaxonicus var. raduloides

Rubus anglosaxonicus var. raduloides, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This variety is characterized by its erect, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruits are aggregate berries that turn red when mature. It is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
