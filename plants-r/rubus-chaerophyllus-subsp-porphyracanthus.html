Rubus chaerophyllus subsp. porphyracanthus

Rubus chaerophyllus subsp.

porphyracanthus, commonly known as the red-flowered bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive red bracts that give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils in temperate regions. Its flowers are usually pink to red, and it produces edible berries that are somewhat tart.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for both its ornamental and ecological roles.
