Rubus × subhorridus

Rubus × subhorridus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, often in woodland or shrubland habitats. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy underside.

The fruit, which is a dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
