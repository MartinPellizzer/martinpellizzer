Ribes occidentale var. albidum

Ribes occidentale var. albidum, commonly known as white currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and along streambanks.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor.
