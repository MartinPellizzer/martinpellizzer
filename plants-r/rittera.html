Rittera

Rittera, commonly known as the "false orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their delicate, often fragrant flowers and are native to regions in South America, particularly Brazil. Rittera species typically grow as terrestrial herbs or small shrubs, with lance-shaped leaves and clustered inflorescences. They thrive in moist, shaded environments and are often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to their ornamental value, some species are cultivated for horticultural purposes.
