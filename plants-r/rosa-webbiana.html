Rosa webbiana

Rosa webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their strong, sweet scent.

Rosa webbiana is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in various climates.
