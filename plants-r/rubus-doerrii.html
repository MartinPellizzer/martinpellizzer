Rubus doerrii

Rubus doerrii, commonly known as the Doerri's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is edible and often used in local culinary traditions.
