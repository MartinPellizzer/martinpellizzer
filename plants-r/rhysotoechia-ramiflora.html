Rhysotoechia ramiflora

Rhysotoechia ramiflora, commonly known as the branching-flower orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
