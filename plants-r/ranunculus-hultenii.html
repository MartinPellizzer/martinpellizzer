Ranunculus hultenii

Ranunculus hultenii, commonly known as Hulten's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically yellow or white flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces small, hairy fruits that contain multiple seeds. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
