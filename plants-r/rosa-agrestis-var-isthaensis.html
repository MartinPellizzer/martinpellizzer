Rosa agrestis var. isthaensis

Rosa agrestis var. isthaensis, commonly known as the Isthaenian wild rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to white, with five petals and a fragrant aroma. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
