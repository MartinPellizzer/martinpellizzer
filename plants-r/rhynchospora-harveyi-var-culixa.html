Rhynchospora harveyi var. culixa

Rhynchospora harveyi var. culixa, commonly known as the Harvey's spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps with slender, cylindrical stems. Its inflorescence consists of spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers, which are often subtended by bracts.

The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in two rows, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
