Rubus eugenius

Rubus eugenius, commonly known as the Eugenius raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing foliage and the second year bearing fruit. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of multiple small drupes, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and potential for use in small-scale cultivation.
