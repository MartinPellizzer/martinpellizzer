Roscoea nepalensis

Roscoea nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically orange to red in color, arranged in a spadix surrounded by a large, showy bract.

Roscoea nepalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady, moist gardens.
