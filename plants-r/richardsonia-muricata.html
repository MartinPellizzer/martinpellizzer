Richardsonia muricata

Richardsonia muricata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom during the warm seasons. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, flat seeds.

Richardsonia muricata is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
