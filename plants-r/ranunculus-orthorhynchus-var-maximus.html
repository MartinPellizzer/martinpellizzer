Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. maximus

Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. maximus, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
