Rhodiola kirilowii

Rhodiola kirilowii, commonly known as the golden root or the king of the mountains, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia, including Siberia, Mongolia, and the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with low temperatures and intense sunlight. Its roots are rich in adaptogenic compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

Rhodiola kirilowii is also noted for its ability to withstand extreme environmental conditions, which contributes to its resilience and medicinal properties.
