Ramium jacquinianum

Ramium jacquinianum, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Ramium jacquinianum is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
