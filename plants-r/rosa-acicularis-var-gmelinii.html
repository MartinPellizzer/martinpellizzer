Rosa acicularis var. gmelinii

Rosa acicularis var. gmelinii, commonly known as the Gmelin's thorned rose, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its sharp, thorned stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
