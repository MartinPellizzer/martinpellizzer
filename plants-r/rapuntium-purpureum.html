Rapuntium purpureum

Rapuntium purpureum, commonly known as the purple cabbagewort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy leaves that are often purple-tinged, especially on the underside. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
