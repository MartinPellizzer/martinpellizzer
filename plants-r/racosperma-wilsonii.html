Racosperma wilsonii

Racosperma wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.

Racosperma wilsonii plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
