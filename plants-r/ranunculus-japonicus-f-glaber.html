Ranunculus japonicus f. glaber

Ranunculus japonicus f. glaber, commonly known as the smooth Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its smooth, hairless stems and leaves distinguish it from other ranunculus species.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
