Ranunculus fasciculiflorus

Ranunculus fasciculiflorus, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and its leaves are typically trifoliate or palmately divided. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its flowering period occurs in spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Ranunculus fasciculiflorus is also characterized by its short, upright stem and its ability to form dense clusters of flowers.
