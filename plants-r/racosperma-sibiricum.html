Racosperma sibiricum

Racosperma sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian raceme.

It is a shrub native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia, characterized by its small, clustered leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, temperate climates. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Racosperma sibiricum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
