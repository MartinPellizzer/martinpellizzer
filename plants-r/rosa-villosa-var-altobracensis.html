Rosa villosa var. altobracensis

Rosa villosa var. altobracensis, commonly known as the Altobracensis Rosa, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardiness and beauty, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
