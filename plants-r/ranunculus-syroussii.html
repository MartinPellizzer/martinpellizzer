Ranunculus syroussii

Ranunculus syroussii, commonly known as the Syrian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Middle East.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with deeply lobed petals. The plant has a short, upright stem and basal leaves that are palmately divided. It thrives in rocky or arid environments, often growing in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
