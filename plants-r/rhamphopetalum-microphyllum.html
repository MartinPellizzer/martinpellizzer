Rhamphopetalum microphyllum

Rhamphopetalum microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact stature and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces delicate, showy flowers that are often pale green or yellow in color, which are adapted for specific pollinators. It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists studying orchid diversity and ecological adaptations.
