Rosa andrzejowskii var. occidentalis

Rosa andrzejowskii var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western Andrzejowski rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique genetic makeup, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
