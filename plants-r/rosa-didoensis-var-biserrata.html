Rosa didoensis var. biserrata

Rosa didoensis var. biserrata, commonly known as the serrated sand rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges that give the species its common name. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

It is often found in sandy or rocky habitats, making it a hardy and resilient species.
