Rosa gallica var. subglandulosa

Rosa gallica var. subglandulosa, commonly known as the French rose or Gallica rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It features upright stems with thorny branches and compound leaves composed of five to seven elliptical leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink to red flowers in early summer. Its botanical characteristics include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
