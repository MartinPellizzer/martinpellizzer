Rubus villicaulis subsp. calvatus

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

calvatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy foliage and stems, which give it its common name.
