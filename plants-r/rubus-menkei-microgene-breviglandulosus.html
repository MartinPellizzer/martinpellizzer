Rubus menkei microgene breviglandulosus

Rubus menkei microgene breviglandulosus, commonly known as the short-glandular blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the development of the edible berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional medicine.
