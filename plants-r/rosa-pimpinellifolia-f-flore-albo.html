Rosa pimpinellifolia f. flore-albo

Rosa pimpinellifolia f. flore-albo, commonly known as the white-flowered rock rose, is a horticultural form of the evergreen shrub Rosa pimpinellifolia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sunny environments.

Its foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round interest in gardens.
