Rhododendron hippophaeoides

Rhododendron hippophaeoides, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in acidic, well-drained soils. Its floral display, which ranges from white to pink and red, occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
