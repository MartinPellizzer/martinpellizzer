Rubus aculeatus

Rubus aculeatus, commonly known as the prickly rose or brambles, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible red berries, which are often used in jams and pies. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North America, and thrives in a variety of soil types.

Rubus aculeatus is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several meters in height.
