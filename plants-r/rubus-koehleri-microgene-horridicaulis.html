Rubus koehleri microgene horridicaulis

Rubus koehleri microgene horridicaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant has serrated leaves and produces flowers that range from white to pale pink. Its berries are typically used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
