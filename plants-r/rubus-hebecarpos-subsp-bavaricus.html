Rubus hebecarpos subsp. bavaricus

Rubus hebecarpos subsp.

bavaricus, commonly known as the Bavarian blackberry, is a shrub native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
