Reseda myriophylla

Reseda myriophylla, commonly known as the many-leaved reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

Reseda myriophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
