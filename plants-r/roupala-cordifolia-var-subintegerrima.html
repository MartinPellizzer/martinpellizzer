Roupala cordifolia var. subintegerrima

Roupala cordifolia var. subintegerrima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved roupala, is a species native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features elliptical, glossy leaves that are often slightly serrated along the edges. Its small, yellowish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits.

The botanical characteristics of this variety make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
