Rosa communis var. adscita

Rosa communis var. adscita, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a variety of the common rose that exhibits vigorous growth habits, often climbing or spreading along supports.

It features showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rosa genus, and produces hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and functional plants.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various climates, though it requires regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote healthy growth.
