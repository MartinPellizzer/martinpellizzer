Rosa montana f. grandifrons

Rosa montana f. grandifrons, commonly known as the Grandiflora rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant features thorned stems and dark green, lobed leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is a vigorous grower, often reaching heights of 1 to 2 meters, making it suitable for both garden borders and as a hedge.

This rose is prized for its fragrance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
