Rubus zhaogoshanensis

Rubus zhaogoshanensis, commonly known as the Zhao Goshan blackberry, is a species of brambles native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs and vines known for their edible berries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus family. Its dark purple to black berries are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and local cuisine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
