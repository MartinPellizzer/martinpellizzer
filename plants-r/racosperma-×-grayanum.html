Racosperma × grayanum

Racosperma × grayanum, commonly known as the hybrid silky oaks, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Racosperma bidwillii and Racosperma glabrum.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often slightly serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in drooping clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It is native to eastern Australia and thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaped areas.
