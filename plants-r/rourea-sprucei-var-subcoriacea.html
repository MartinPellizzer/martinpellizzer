Rourea sprucei var. subcoriacea

Rourea sprucei var. subcoriacea, commonly known as the velvet-leaf rourea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color and are followed by small, spherical fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
