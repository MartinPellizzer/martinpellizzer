Rosa × muscosa var. multiplex

Rosa × muscosa var. multiplex, commonly known as the everblooming shrub rose, is a hybrid species that exhibits a high degree of variability in its floral and foliage characteristics.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless growth habit and its ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season. The plant typically features dark green, glossy leaves and flowers that range from white to pink, often with a slightly fragrant scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its adaptability and continuous flowering, it is widely appreciated in both ornamental and landscape settings.
