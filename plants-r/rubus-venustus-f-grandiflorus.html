Rubus venustus f. grandiflorus

Rubus venustus f. grandiflorus, commonly known as the showy raspberry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or open areas with well-drained soil.

Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
