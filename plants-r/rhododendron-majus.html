Rhododendron majus

Rhododendron majus, commonly known as the large-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhododendron majus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
