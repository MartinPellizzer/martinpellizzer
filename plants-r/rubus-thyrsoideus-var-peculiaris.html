Rubus thyrsoideus var. peculiaris

Rubus thyrsoideus var. peculiaris, commonly known as the peculiar raspberry, is a variant of the thyrsoideus raspberry species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, with a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is notable for its unique botanical features and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
