Rosa syntrichostyla

Rosa syntrichostyla, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it forms dense mats or low-growing bushes. The plant produces showy pink or red flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Rosa syntrichostyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
