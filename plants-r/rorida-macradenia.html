Rorida macradenia

Rorida macradenia, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, producing vibrant purple or pink blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in cool, moist environments.

Rorida macradenia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
