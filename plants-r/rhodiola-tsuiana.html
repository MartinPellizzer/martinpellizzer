Rhodiola tsuiana

Rhodiola tsuiana, commonly known as the Siberian rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to high altitudes with low oxygen levels. Its roots are rich in bioactive compounds, including rosavins and salidrosides, which contribute to its reputation as an adaptogen.

Rhodiola tsuiana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported ability to enhance physical endurance and mental resilience.
