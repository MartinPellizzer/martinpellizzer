Ranunculus fallax subsp. gyratifrons

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

gyratifrons, commonly known as the spiral buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive spirally arranged leaves and yellow flowers with five to seven petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in high-altitude meadows.

The subspecies is noted for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
