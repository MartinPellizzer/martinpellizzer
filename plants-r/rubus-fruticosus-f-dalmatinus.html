Rubus fruticosus f. dalmatinus

Rubus fruticosus f. dalmatinus, commonly known as the Dalmatian blackberry, is a subspecies of the common bramble.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
