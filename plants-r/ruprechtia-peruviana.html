Ruprechtia peruviana

Ruprechtia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian ruprechtia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically blooms in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful inflorescences.

Ruprechtia peruviana thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden cultivation.
