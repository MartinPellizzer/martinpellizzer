Rhynchosia erythrinoides

Rhynchosia erythrinoides, commonly known as the red-flowered burr legume, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It produces striking red or orange flowers that are often clustered in groups of three, giving it a vibrant and eye-catching appearance. The plant has compound leaves with pairs of oval-shaped leaflets and bears fruit that develops into a spiny, three-lobed burr. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
