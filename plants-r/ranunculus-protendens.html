Ranunculus protendens

Ranunculus protendens, commonly known as the early buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals that bloom in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its stems are hairy and often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Ranunculus protendens thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
