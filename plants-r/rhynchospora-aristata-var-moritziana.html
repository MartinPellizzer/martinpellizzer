Rhynchospora aristata var. moritziana

Rhynchospora aristata var. moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, greenish to reddish spikelets that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize moist soils effectively. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its aquatic or semi-aquatic environment, with specialized root systems and leaf morphology.
