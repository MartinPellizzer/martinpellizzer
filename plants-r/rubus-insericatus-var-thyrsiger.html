Rubus insericatus var. thyrsiger

Rubus insericatus var. thyrsiger, commonly known as the thyrse-bearing blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus characterized by its unique floral and fruiting structures.

It is native to certain regions of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a slightly tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and covered in fine, silky hairs.

This species is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
