Rosa cuneatula subsp. pusilla

Rosa cuneatula subsp.

pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features small, often pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and is often used in rock gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a resilient, decorative plant.
