Rosa cinnamomea f. turbinella

Rosa cinnamomea f. turbinella, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a unique horticultural form of the cinnamon rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, turbinated (turbinate) flower heads, which are a notable botanical feature. The plant produces showy, pink to crimson flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically dark green and aromatic, adding to its ornamental value.

This form is prized in gardens for its unusual appearance and fragrant blooms.
