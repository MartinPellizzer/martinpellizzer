Ranunculus delphiniifolius f. rosiflorus

Ranunculus delphiniifolius f. rosiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered water crowfoot, is a botanical variant of the water crowfoot genus.

It is characterized by its delicate, rose-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are finely divided and floating, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often cultivated in aquatic gardens.
