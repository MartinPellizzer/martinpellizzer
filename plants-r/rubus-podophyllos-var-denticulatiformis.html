Rubus podophyllos var. denticulatiformis

Rubus podophyllos var. denticulatiformis, commonly known as the toothed-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its foliage is dense and often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
