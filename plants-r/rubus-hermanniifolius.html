Rubus hermanniifolius

Rubus hermanniifolius, commonly known as the Hermann's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like roses and apples.
