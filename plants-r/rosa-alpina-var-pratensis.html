Rosa alpina var. pratensis

Rosa alpina var. pratensis, commonly known as the alpine garden rose, is a variant of the alpine rose native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold climates.
