Rubus idaeus ruber

Rubus idaeus ruber, commonly known as red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as raspberries, which are borne on the ends of its stems. These berries are typically red when ripe, though some cultivars may have yellow or white variations.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
