Rhynchosia pinnata

Rhynchosia pinnata, commonly known as the winged bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive wing-like appearance, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a spurred petal. The plant produces edible pods that contain both beans and a starchy seed, making it a valuable food source. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various soil types.

Rhynchosia pinnata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, enhancing fertility for subsequent crops.
