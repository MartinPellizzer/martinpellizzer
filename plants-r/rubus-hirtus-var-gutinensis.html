Rubus hirtus var. gutinensis

Rubus hirtus var. gutinensis, commonly known as the rough blackberry.

It is a shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
