Ranunculus baldschuanicus

Ranunculus baldschuanicus, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
