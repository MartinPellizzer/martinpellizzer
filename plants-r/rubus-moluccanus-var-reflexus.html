Rubus moluccanus var. reflexus

Rubus moluccanus var. reflexus, commonly known as the reflexed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
