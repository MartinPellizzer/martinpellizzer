Rosa dumalis var. dissimilis

Rosa dumalis var. dissimilis, commonly known as the alpine rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are adapted to cold, rocky environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Rosa dumalis var. dissimilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
