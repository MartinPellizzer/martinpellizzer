Rubus cordifolius var. dalmatica

Rubus cordifolius var. dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable for its edible fruits.
