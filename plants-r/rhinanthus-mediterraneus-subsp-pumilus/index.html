Rhinanthus Mediterraneus Subsp. Pumilus

Rhinanthus mediterraneus subsp. pumilus is a small flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is known for its delicate yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. This subspecies is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils where it thrives in sunny conditions. Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important subject for botanical study. Despite its modest size, it plays a significant part in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity.
