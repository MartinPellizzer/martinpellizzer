Rosa canina f. pulchella

Rosa canina f. pulchella, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in early summer, followed by orange-red hips that are rich in vitamin C. Native to Europe and western Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows and gardens make it a notable species in botanical gardens and landscapes.
