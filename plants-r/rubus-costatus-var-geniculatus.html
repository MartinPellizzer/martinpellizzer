Rubus costatus var. geniculatus

Rubus costatus var. geniculatus, commonly known as the bent-stem raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a red color, typically in the summer months. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in partial shade.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, bent stem structure, which gives it its botanical name.
