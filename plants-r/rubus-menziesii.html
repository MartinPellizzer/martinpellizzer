Rubus menziesii

Rubus menziesii, commonly known as the Evergreen blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, often harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rubus menziesii plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
