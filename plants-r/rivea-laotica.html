Rivea laotica

Rivea laotica, commonly known as the Laotian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday, showcasing a unique circadian blooming pattern. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are typically pale blue to violet in color, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Rivea laotica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
