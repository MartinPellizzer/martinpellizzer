Rejoua dichotoma

Rejoua dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, where each stem splits into two equal parts, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in dense, globular clusters. Its leaves are typically small and opposite, and it is well adapted to drought conditions.

Rejoua dichotoma plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
