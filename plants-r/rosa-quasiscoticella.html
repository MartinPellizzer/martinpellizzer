Rosa quasiscoticella

Rosa quasiscoticella, commonly known as the narrowleaf rock rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, and the plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments.

Rosa quasiscoticella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
