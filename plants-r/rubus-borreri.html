Rubus borreri

Rubus borreri, commonly known as the Borrego blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines and traditional uses.
