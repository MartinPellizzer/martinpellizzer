Rubus scaber var. subcanus

Rubus scaber var. subcanus, commonly known as the rough-bracted raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, characterized by its hairy stems and thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, and it has compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry species.
