Rubus pallidus microgene microstachys

Rubus pallidus microgene microstachys, commonly known as the pale raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and clusters of small, pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically harvested in the late summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
