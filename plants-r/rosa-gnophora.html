Rosa gnophora

Rosa gnophora, commonly known as the narrowleaf rock rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the western United States and parts of Mexico, typically found in rocky or arid environments. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. Rosa gnophora is a shrub that can grow up to 1 meter tall, with thorny stems and evergreen foliage.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
