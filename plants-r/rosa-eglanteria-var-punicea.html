Rosa eglanteria var. punicea

Rosa eglanteria var. punicea, commonly known as the red prickly rose, is a vigorous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental flowers and hardy nature, making it suitable for hedging or as a decorative plant.

However, its sharp thorns require caution when handling.
