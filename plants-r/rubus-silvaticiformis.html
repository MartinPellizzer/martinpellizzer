Rubus silvaticiformis

Rubus silvaticiformis, commonly known as the wild bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are often red or black in color. It thrives in woodland and shrubland habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
