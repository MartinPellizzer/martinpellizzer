Rubus subvillicaulis

Rubus subvillicaulis, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thorny stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and along woodland edges, where it thrives in partial shade.
