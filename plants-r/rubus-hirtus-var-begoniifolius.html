Rubus hirtus var. begoniifolius

Rubus hirtus var. begoniifolius, commonly known as the begoniifolius raspberry, is a variant of the hairy raspberry species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, begonia-like leaves that are deeply lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
