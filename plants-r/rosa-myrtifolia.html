Rosa myrtifolia

Rosa myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Mediterranean. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

Rosa myrtifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
