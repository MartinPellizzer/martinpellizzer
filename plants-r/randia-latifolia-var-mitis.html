Randia latifolia var. mitis

Randia latifolia var. mitis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or broadleaf randia, is a tropical shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.
