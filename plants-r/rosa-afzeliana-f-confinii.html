Rosa afzeliana f. confinii

Rosa afzeliana f. confinii, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink or white flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem structure, compound leaves, and an extensive root system adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
