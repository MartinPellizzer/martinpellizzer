Ranunculus fallax subsp.

sippolaensis, commonly known as the Sippola ranunculus, is a rare and striking member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, particularly in parts of Albania and Kosovo.

This subspecies is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central dark spot. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.
