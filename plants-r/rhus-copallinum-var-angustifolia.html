Rhus copallinum var. angustifolia

Rhus copallinum var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fuzzy fruits known as samaras. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Narrowleaf sumac is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in disturbed areas.
