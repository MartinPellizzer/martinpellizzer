Rubus glandulosus subsp. incanescens

Rubus glandulosus subsp.

incanescens, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in the autumn. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This subspecies is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
