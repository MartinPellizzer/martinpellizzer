Rodriguezia maculata var. bicristata

Rodriguezia maculata var. bicristata, commonly known as the spotted rodriguezia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often speckled with dark green spots, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its foliage. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to shaded garden settings.
