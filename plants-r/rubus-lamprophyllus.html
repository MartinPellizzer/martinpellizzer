Rubus lamprophyllus

Rubus lamprophyllus, commonly known as the shining leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the development of its edible fruits.

Rubus lamprophyllus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
