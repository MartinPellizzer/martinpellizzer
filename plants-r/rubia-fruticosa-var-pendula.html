Rubia fruticosa var. pendula

Rubia fruticosa var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping shrub cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, fuzzy berries. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can grow up to 3 meters in height.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
