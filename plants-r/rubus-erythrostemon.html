Rubus erythrostemon

Rubus erythrostemon, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries in the summer. It is native to western North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, preferring full sun to partial shade.

The berries are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications due to their tart flavor.
