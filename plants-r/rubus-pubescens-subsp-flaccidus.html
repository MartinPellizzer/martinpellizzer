Rubus pubescens subsp. flaccidus

Rubus pubescens subsp.

flaccidus, commonly known as the soft-hairy bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often sprawling stems covered in soft, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early summer, followed by red berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and hedgerows, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
