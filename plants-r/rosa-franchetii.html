Rosa franchetii

Rosa franchetii, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species native to China and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit.

It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or white, with a distinctive fragrance. The plant produces hips, which are the fruiting bodies containing seeds. Rosa franchetii is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial sunlight, making it a versatile addition to various landscapes.
