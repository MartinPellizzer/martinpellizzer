Rubus wilsonii

Rubus wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, dark red berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. Its stems are covered in stiff, thorny hairs, typical of the Rubus genus.

The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is valued for its flavor and medicinal properties.
