Rubus × nothus f. sanctiformis

Rubus × nothus f. sanctiformis, commonly known as the San Francisco raspberry.

This plant is a hybrid within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It is native to the western United States, particularly California, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant's leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and edible fruits.
