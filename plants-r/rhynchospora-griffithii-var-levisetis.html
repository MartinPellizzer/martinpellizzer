Rhynchospora griffithii var. levisetis

Rhynchospora griffithii var. levisetis, commonly known as the broadleaf marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, greenish-brown spikelets, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other forms of Rhynchospora griffithii.
