Ruellia gangetica

Ruellia gangetica, commonly known as the Indian ruellia, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet tubular flowers, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Ruellia gangetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
