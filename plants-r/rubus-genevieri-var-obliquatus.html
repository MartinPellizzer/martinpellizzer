Rubus genevieri var. obliquatus

Rubus genevieri var. obliquatus, commonly known as the oblique blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
