Rosa haynaldiana

Rosa haynaldiana, commonly known as the Haynald's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late summer. It is well adapted to harsh climates, thriving in rocky soils and high altitudes.

Rosa haynaldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
