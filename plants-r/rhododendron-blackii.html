Rhododendron blackii

Rhododendron blackii, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron blackii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in temperate regions.
