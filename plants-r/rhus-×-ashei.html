Rhus × ashei

Rhus × ashei, commonly known as the Chinese hickory, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, hazelnut-like fruits that are encased in a spiny husk. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
