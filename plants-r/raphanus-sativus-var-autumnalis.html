Raphanus sativus var. autumnalis

Raphanus sativus var. autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy root that turns red or purple as it matures, typically harvested in the fall. The plant produces yellow flowers in a raceme, which are followed by spherical, dry pods containing several seeds. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible root and ornamental value.

The leaves are also edible and can be used in salads or cooked dishes.
