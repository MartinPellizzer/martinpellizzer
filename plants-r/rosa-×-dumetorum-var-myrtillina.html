Rosa × dumetorum var. myrtillina

Rosa × dumetorum var. myrtillina, commonly known as the European sweetbriar, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, berry-like hips that contain seeds, and it is often used in hedges or as a ground cover.

The species is known for its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.
