Rhynchoglossum violaceum

Rhynchoglossum violaceum, commonly known as the violet twayblade, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial herb with a slender, leafless stem that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces delicate, violet-colored flowers that are arranged in a spike, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in its woodland habitat.

Rhynchoglossum violaceum is found primarily in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
