Rubus bodewigii

Rubus bodewigii, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The berries are valued for their flavor and are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
