Rotang pisicarpus

Rotang pisicarpus, commonly known as the Philippine rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood with a fine grain, making it highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, contributing to its rarity and conservation status.
