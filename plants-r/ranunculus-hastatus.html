Ranunculus hastatus

Ranunculus hastatus, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments such as ponds, streams, and marshes. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with wet soil conditions.
