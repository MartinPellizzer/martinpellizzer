Rubus schleicheri var. cognatus

Rubus schleicheri var. cognatus, commonly known as the Schleicher's raspberry.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it blooms with white or pale pink flowers.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
