Ranunculus capillus-naidis

Ranunculus capillus-naidis, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a darker central spot. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Ranunculus capillus-naidis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate.
