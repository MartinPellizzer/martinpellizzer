Racosperma cracente

Racosperma cracente, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets, and it produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in clusters. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma cracente is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
