Rhododendron asperrimum

Rhododendron asperrimum, commonly known as the roughleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are leathery and have a rough texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
