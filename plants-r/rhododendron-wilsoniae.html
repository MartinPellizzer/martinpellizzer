Rhododendron wilsoniae

Rhododendron wilsoniae, commonly known as Wilson's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron wilsoniae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in specific, humid climates.
