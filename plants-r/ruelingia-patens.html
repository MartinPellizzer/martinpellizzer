Ruelingia patens

Ruelingia patens, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red sandpaper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, sandpaper-like texture. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, cherry-like fruits. Ruelingia patens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant foliage and floral display.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is also used in traditional medicine.
