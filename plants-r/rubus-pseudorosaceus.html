Rubus pseudorosaceus

Rubus pseudorosaceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
