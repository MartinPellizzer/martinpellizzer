Rubus thyrsoideus var. subellipticus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. subellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves and thorny stems.

It is native to regions of eastern North America, where it thrives in woodland and thicket environments. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include a shrub-like growth habit with compound leaves and aggregate fruits.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
