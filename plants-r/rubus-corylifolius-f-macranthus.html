Rubus corylifolius f. macranthus

Rubus corylifolius f. macranthus, commonly known as the large-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus characterized by its distinctive, large, showy flowers.

It is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin and produces edible, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.

The plant is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
