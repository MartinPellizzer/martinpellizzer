Rubus reduncus

Rubus reduncus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Rubus reduncus is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
