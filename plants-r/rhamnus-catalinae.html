Rhamnus catalinae

Rhamnus catalinae, commonly known as Catalina buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Channel Islands off the coast of California.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in coastal scrub habitats and is adapted to the unique climate of the Channel Islands.

Rhamnus catalinae plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining the biodiversity of its native environment.
