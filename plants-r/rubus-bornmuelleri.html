Rubus bornmuelleri

Rubus bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
