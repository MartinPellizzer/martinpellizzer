Rosa rubiginosa f. subeglandulosa

Rosa rubiginosa f. subeglandulosa, commonly known as the rough-stemmed dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments, such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are edible but have a tart flavor.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
