Rhus viridiflora var. canadensis

Rhus viridiflora var. canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with 15 to 31 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fuzzy berries that attract birds and other wildlife. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
