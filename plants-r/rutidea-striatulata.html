Rutidea striatulata

Rutidea striatulata, commonly known as the striated legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive striated pattern on its stems and leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators like bees.

Rutidea striatulata is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
