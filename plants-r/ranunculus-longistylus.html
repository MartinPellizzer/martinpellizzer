Ranunculus longistylus

Ranunculus longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive, hairy texture.

The species is noted for its unique floral morphology, which includes elongated stamens that contribute to its common name.
