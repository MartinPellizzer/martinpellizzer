Robiquetia sphingoides

Robiquetia sphingoides, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

This perennial herbaceous plant is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting purple or red throat. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
