Rhododendron saavedranum

Rhododendron saavedranum, commonly known as the Saavedra rhododendron, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in cloud forests or mountainous areas with high humidity. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, are adapted to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Rhododendron saavedranum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
