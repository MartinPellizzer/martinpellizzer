Rhipsalis turpinii

Rhipsalis turpinii, commonly known as the Turpin's mistletoe cactus, is a rare and unique epiphytic cactus native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that often hang from trees, resembling a vine. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high humidity and indirect light to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
