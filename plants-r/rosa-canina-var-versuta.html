Rosa canina var. versuta

Rosa canina var. versuta, commonly known as the bent-stem dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose that exhibits unique growth characteristics.

This shrub typically features arching stems that curve downward, distinguishing it from the more upright form of Rosa canina. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and serrated. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rosa genus.

It is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
