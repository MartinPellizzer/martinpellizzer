Rosa micrantha var. budwitzensis

Rosa micrantha var. budwitzensis, commonly known as the Budwitz's rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorned stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
