Rubus laconensis

Rubus laconensis, commonly known as the Laconic raspberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant for both culinary and ecological purposes.
