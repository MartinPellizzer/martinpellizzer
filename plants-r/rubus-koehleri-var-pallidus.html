Rubus koehleri var. pallidus

Rubus koehleri var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, aggregate berry. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
