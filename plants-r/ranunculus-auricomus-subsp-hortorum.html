Ranunculus auricomus subsp. hortorum

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

hortorum, commonly known as the garden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to nine petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and covered in a soft, silky down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This cultivar is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
