Rosa cretacea

Rosa cretacea, commonly known as the Cretaceous rose, is a rare and ancient species of flowering plant.

It is characterized by its simple, usually single-petaled flowers and thorny stems. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant's leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval and serrated.

Due to its historical significance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research.
