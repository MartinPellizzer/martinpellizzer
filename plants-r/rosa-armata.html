Rosa armata

Rosa armata, commonly known as the armed rose, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in spring. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody, shrub-like structure.

Rosa armata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh climates.
