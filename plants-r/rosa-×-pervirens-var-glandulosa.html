Rosa × pervirens var. glandulosa

Rosa × pervirens var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular evergreen rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with glandular dots, which give it its botanical variant name. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming throughout the growing season. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is well adapted to mild, temperate climates.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
