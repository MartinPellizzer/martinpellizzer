Rubus pubescens subsp. macrostemon

Rubus pubescens subsp.

macrostemon, commonly known as the hairy blackberry. It is a shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red drupelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its edible berries, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
