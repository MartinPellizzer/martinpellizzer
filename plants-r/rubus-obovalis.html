Rubus obovalis

Rubus obovalis, commonly known as the oval blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of edible, dark purple berries. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is valued for its flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
