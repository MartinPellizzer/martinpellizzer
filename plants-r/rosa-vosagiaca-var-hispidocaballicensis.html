Rosa vosagiaca var. hispidocaballicensis

Rosa vosagiaca var. hispidocaballicensis, commonly known as the Hispid Caballic Rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem structure and a preference for well-drained, alkaline soils.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental and scientific interest.
