Racosperma rivale

Racosperma rivale, commonly known as the river acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to wetter environments, often found near rivers and streams. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Racosperma rivale plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
