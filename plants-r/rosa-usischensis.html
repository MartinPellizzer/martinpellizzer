Rosa usischensis

Rosa usischensis, commonly known as the Usischek rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers.

Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces edible rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa usischensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
