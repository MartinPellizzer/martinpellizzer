Rhaponticum chinense

Rhaponticum chinense, commonly known as Chinese rhapontic, is a flowering plant native to regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
