Restio nudus

Restio nudus, commonly known as the naked restio, is a slender, grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, thriving in areas with mild, wet winters and dry summers.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
