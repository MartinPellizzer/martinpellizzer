Ranunculus auricomus subsp. tenebrosus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

tenebrosus, commonly known as the dark-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and dark green foliage. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of five showy petals with a central cluster of stamens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker, more robust leaves compared to the typical form of Ranunculus auricomus.
