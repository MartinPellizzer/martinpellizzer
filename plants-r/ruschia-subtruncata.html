Ruschia subtruncata

Ruschia subtruncata, commonly known as the Cape plume, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers. The leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
