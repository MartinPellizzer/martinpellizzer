Rudbeckia amplexicaulis

Rudbeckia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the claspingleaf coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 to 3 feet tall. The plant produces showy yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by green to brown ray flowers, which bloom from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often clasping the stem, giving it its common name.

Rudbeckia amplexicaulis thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to moist to wet soils, making it a valuable addition to wetland gardens.
