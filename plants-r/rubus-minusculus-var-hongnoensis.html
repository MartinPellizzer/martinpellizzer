Rubus minusculus var. hongnoensis

Rubus minusculus var. hongnoensis, commonly known as the Korean dwarf raspberry, is a shrub native to Korea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, thorny stems and dense growth habit. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
