Rubus × semisuberectus

Rubus × semisuberectus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits characteristics of both Rubus and other related species.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California, where it thrives in maritime climates. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
