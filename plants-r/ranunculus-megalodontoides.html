Ranunculus megalodontoides

Ranunculus megalodontoides, commonly known as the giant toothed buttercup, is a striking perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

This species is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with prominent, tooth-like lobes on the petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Ranunculus megalodontoides is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
