Rubus tomentosus var. obtusifolius

Rubus tomentosus var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, white-tomentose (hairy) foliage and thorny stems. The leaves are ovate to broadly ovate with rounded tips, and they are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
