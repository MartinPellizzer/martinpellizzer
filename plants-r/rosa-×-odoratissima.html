Rosa × odoratissima

Rosa × odoratissima, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa gallica and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils.

The flowers are used in perfumery and traditional medicine, making it both a botanical and commercial significant species.
