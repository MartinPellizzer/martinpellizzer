Rosa canina var. bellezmensis

Rosa canina var. bellezmensis, commonly known as the Spanish rose or the wild dog rose, is a variant of the common dogwood rose.

It is a shrub native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal, and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, fragrant pink flowers in spring, followed by red, hairy fruits known as hips. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are thorny.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
