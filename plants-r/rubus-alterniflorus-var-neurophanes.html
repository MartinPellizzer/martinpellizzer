Rubus alterniflorus var. neurophanes

Rubus alterniflorus var. neurophanes, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible blackberries in the summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
