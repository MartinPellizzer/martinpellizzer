Ranunculus auricomus subsp. magnidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

magnidens, commonly known as the large-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent green sepals.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger leaf size and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
