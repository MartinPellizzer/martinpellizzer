Rosa carolina var. lyonii

Rosa carolina var. lyonii, commonly known as Lyon's wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals. The plant produces hips, which are red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
