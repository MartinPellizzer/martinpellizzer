Ranunculus obtusilobus

Ranunculus obtusilobus, commonly known as the blunt-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and forest edges. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine broad, rounded petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
