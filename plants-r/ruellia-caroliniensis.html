Ruellia caroliniensis

Ruellia caroliniensis, commonly known as the Carolina rosemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia caroliniensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
