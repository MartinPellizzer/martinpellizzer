Ruta chalepensis subsp. angustifolia

Ruta chalepensis subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf rock rue, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive bitter scent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its flowering period typically occurs in spring, and it is often found growing on rocky or arid soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
