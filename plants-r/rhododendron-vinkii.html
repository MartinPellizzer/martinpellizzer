Rhododendron vinkii

Rhododendron vinkii, commonly known as the Vink's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, preferring well-drained acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron vinkii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
