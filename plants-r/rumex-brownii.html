Rumex brownii

Rumex brownii, commonly known as Brown's sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color, especially along the veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
