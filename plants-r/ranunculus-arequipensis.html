Ranunculus arequipensis

Ranunculus arequipensis, commonly known as the Arequipa buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of southern Peru.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five petals, ranging in color from white to pink.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
