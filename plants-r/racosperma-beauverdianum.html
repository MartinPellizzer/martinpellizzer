Racosperma beauverdianum

Racosperma beauverdianum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Racosperma beauverdianum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
