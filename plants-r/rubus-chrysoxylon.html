Rubus chrysoxylon

Rubus chrysoxylon, commonly known as golden wood raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits are red, aggregate berries that develop from the flowering period.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streamside habitats.
