Rubus velaris

Rubus velaris, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Rubus velaris is a perennial shrub that can grow up to several meters in height.
