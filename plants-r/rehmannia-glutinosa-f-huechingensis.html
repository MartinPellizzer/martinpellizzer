Rehmannia glutinosa f. huechingensis

Rehmannia glutinosa f. huechingensis, commonly known as the Huang Jing, is a variant of the Chinese foxglove plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary part used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential benefits for kidney and reproductive health.
