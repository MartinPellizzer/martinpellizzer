Rhaphidophora glauca

Rhaphidophora glauca, commonly known as the blue-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, blue-green leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by triangular, fleshy fruits. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
