Rubus occitanicus var. ferox

Rubus occitanicus var. ferox, commonly known as the Corsican blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
