Ribes alpinoides

Ribes alpinoides, commonly known as the alpine gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

Ribes alpinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
