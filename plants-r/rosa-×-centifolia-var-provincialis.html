Rosa × centifolia var. provincialis

Rosa × centifolia var. provincialis, commonly known as the Provence rose, is a fragrant, flowering shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its numerous, pink, semi-double blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces hips that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
