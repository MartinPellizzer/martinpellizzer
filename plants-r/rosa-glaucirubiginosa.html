Rosa glaucirubiginosa

Rosa glaucirubiginosa, commonly known as the bluish-red rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and narrow, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive bluish tinge on the petals, giving it its common name.

Rosa glaucirubiginosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
