Rivea ornata

Rivea ornata, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of showy, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its flowers emit a sweet, honey-like fragrance.

Rivea ornata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and hanging baskets.
