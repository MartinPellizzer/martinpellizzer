Rosa jundzilliana f. pugetii

Rosa jundzilliana f. pugetii, commonly known as the Puget's rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces red hips in the fall. It is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
