Ranunculus aconitifolius var. platanifolius

Ranunculus aconitifolius var. platanifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or pale yellow in color, with five to eight petals. The plant has finely divided, palmate leaves that resemble those of the plane tree, giving it its specific epithet "platanifolius.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
