Rosettea rhinanthifolia

Rosettea rhinanthifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rosette plant, is a succulent species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It forms dense, compact rosettes of fleshy, narrow leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Rosettea rhinanthifolia is well adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.
