Ruellia blechioides

Ruellia blechioides, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Ruellia blechioides thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
