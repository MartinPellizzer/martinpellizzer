Rorippa fluviatilis var. fluviatilis

Rorippa fluviatilis var. fluviatilis, commonly known as watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by insects. Its roots are adapted to aquatic environments, allowing it to thrive in slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Watercress is known for its peppery flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
