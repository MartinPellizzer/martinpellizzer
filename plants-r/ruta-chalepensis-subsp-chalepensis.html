Ruta chalepensis subsp. chalepensis

Ruta chalepensis subsp.

chalepensis, commonly known as Jerusalem plant or mountain rue, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its small, elliptical fruits are enclosed in a persistent calyx.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
