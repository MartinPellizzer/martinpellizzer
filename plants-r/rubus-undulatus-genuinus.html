Rubus undulatus genuinus

Rubus undulatus genuinus, commonly known as the wavy-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with wavy margins. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple tiny drupes.

This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
