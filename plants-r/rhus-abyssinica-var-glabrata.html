Rhus abyssinica var. glabrata

Rhus abyssinica var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in highland regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
