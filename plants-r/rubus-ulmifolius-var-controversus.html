Rubus ulmifolius var. controversus

Rubus ulmifolius var. controversus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry plant.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and compound leaves that resemble those of the elm tree. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that ripens to a deep purple or black color. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
