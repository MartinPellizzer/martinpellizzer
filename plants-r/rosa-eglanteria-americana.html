Rosa eglanteria americana

Rosa eglanteria americana, commonly known as the American rose, is a shrub-like plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, hairy fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
