Rubus vandermeijdenii

Rubus vandermeijdenii, commonly known as Vandermeijden's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the leaves are trifoliate with serrated edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
