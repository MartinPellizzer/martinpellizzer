Rosa pratincola var. angustiarum

Rosa pratincola var. angustiarum, commonly known as the narrowleaf meadow rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The flowers are usually pink or pale red, with five petals and a delicate fragrance. This variety is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
