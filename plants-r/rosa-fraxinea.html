Rosa fraxinea

Rosa fraxinea, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip that contains seeds.

Rosa fraxinea is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
