Rubus parvifolius subvar. subconcolor

Rubus parvifolius subvar. subconcolor, commonly known as the smooth-leafed blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are dark purple to black when mature. These berries are often consumed by wildlife and can be used in culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
