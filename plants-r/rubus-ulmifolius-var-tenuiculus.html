Rubus ulmifolius var. tenuiculus

Rubus ulmifolius var. tenuiculus, commonly known as the slenderleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thin, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark red to black in color when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
