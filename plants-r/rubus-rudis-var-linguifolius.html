Rubus rudis var. linguifolius

Rubus rudis var. linguifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry species characterized by its distinctive broad, tongue-shaped leaves. The plant is typically found in temperate regions, growing in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible, dark purple berries.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
