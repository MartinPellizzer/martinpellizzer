Ribes grossularia

Ribes grossularia, commonly known as the gooseberry plant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow or green berries. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. Its flowers are typically pink or white and appear in early summer.

Ribes grossularia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
