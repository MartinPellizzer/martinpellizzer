Rosa mollis subf. arenaria

Rosa mollis subf.

arenaria, commonly known as the sand rose, is a subshrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage.

The plant produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to coastal and arid environments.
