Rubus rivularis subsp. lamprophyllus

Rubus rivularis subsp.

lamprophyllus, commonly known as the glossy-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features compound leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, tart blackberry.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shiny foliage and habitat preferences.
