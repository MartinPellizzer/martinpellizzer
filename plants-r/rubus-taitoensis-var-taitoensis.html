Rubus taitoensis var. taitoensis

Rubus taitoensis var. taitoensis, commonly known as Taito raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale yellow flowers during the spring. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
