Rutilia

Rutilia, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

Rutilia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
