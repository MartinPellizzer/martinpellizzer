Rapuntium sonchifolium

Rapuntium sonchifolium, commonly known as the common sowthistle, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
