Rosa glauca subvar. gravetii

Rosa glauca subvar. gravetii, commonly known as the Sierra violet rose, is a compact shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, thornless stems with deep green, oval-shaped leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage turns a striking bronze color in the fall, adding ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and attractive addition to rock gardens or woodland settings.
