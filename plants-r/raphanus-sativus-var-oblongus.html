Raphanus sativus var. oblongus

Raphanus sativus var. oblongus, commonly known as the black radish, is a variety of the radish plant characterized by its long, cylindrical root.

This plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. The roots of Raphanus sativus var. oblongus are typically dark purple to black in color, with a spicy and peppery flavor.

It is cultivated for its edible root, which is rich in nutrients such as vitamins C and K. The plant prefers cool climates and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
