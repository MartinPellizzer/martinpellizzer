Rubus hasskarlii subsp. edelingii

Rubus hasskarlii subsp.

edelingii, commonly known as the Edeling's blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams.

The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.

Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees.
