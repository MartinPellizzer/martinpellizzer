Racosperma pinguiculosum

Racosperma pinguiculosum, commonly known as the woolly wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, grayish foliage that resembles wool, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Racosperma pinguiculosum is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
