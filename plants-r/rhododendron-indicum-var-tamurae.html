Rhododendron indicum var. tamurae

Rhododendron indicum var. tamurae, commonly known as the Tamura's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.
