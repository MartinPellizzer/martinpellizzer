Raddiella lunata

Raddiella lunata, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves and produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Raddiella lunata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking nighttime blooms.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.
