Rhododendron malayanum

Rhododendron malayanum, commonly known as the Malayan rhododendron, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports various epiphytic plants.

The species is adapted to humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
