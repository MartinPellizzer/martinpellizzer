Rosa mollis f. pugnacidens

Rosa mollis f. pugnacidens, commonly known as the soft rose with a comb-like edge, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and distinctive, serrated leaf margins that give it its name. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
