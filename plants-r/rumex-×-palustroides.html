Rumex × palustroides

Rumex × palustroides, commonly known as the marsh sorrel, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Rumex palustris and Rumex acetosella.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its hybrid nature, it can exhibit a wide range of morphological variations.
