Rosa gymnocarpa f. apiculata

Rosa gymnocarpa f. apiculata, commonly known as the smooth-seeded rose, is a botanical variant of the gymnocarpa subspecies.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous fruits and distinctive apiculate (pointed) tips on its sepals. This form typically exhibits a compact growth habit with upright, thorny stems. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five to seven petals, and the plant produces oval, yellowish fruits.

It is native to regions of Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential in horticultural breeding programs.
