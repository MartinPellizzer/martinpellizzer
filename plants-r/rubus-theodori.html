Rubus theodori

Rubus theodori, commonly known as the Theodore's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
