Rubus serpens subsp. corrugatus

Rubus serpens subsp.

corrugatus, commonly known as the wavy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive wavy margin.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest for plant taxonomists.
