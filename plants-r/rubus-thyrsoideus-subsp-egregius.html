Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. egregius

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

egregius, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are armed with stout, curved prickles.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
