Rosa halleri

Rosa halleri, commonly known as the Haller's rose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that are covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

Rosa halleri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
