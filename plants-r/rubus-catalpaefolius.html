Rubus catalpaefolius

Rubus catalpaefolius, commonly known as the catalpa-leaf bramble, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble those of the catalpa tree, with elongated, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
