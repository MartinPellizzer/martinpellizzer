Rosa speciosa var. pseudogallica

Rosa speciosa var. pseudogallica, commonly known as the California sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptic leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant produces hip fruits that are reddish-brown and contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
