Rubus melanoxylon var. discolor

Rubus melanoxylon var. discolor, commonly known as the variable blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter flavor.
