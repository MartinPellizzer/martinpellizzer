Rhaphiolepis williamtelliana

Rhaphiolepis williamtelliana, commonly known as the William Tell rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to China, characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds but not typically consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
