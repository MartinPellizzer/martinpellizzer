Ranunculus fluentorum

Ranunculus fluentorum, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds by wind.

Ranunculus fluentorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
