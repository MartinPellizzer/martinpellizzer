Rubus uncatus

Rubus uncatus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, preferring moist and well-drained conditions.

The trailing blackberry is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
