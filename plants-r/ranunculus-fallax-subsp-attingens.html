Ranunculus fallax subsp. attingens

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

attingens, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate, trifoliate leaves and showy yellow flowers with five to nine petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
