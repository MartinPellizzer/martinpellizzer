Rosa caryophyllacea var. belocarpa

Rosa caryophyllacea var. belocarpa, commonly known as the Alaska rose, is a shrub native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are woody and often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in moist meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
