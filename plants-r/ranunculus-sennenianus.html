Ranunculus sennenianus

Ranunculus sennenianus, commonly known as the Sennen's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. The plant features showy yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by sepals that resemble additional petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, typical of many ranunculaceae species.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found at high elevations where it thrives in well-drained soils.
