Rubus chaerophyllus subsp. rohlenae

Rubus chaerophyllus subsp.

rohlenae, commonly known as the Rohlen's blackberry, is a subspecies native to specific regions of Europe. It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
