Rosa × toddii

Rosa × toddii, commonly known as the Ceylon rose, is a hybrid species native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rosa × toddii is also valued for its use in perfumery due to the aromatic qualities of its blooms.
