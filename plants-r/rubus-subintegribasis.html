Rubus subintegribasis

Rubus subintegribasis, commonly known as the smooth-leaved blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and wines. Its leaves are usually oval with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and edible fruits, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
