Rubus × pseudoconfusus

Rubus × pseudoconfusus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils in temperate regions, often in bogs or heaths. Its flowers are small and white, and its fruits are aggregate berries that can range in color from red to black.

This hybrid is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
