Ranunculus concinnifolius

Ranunculus concinnifolius, commonly known as the elegant buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often with a distinctive central tuft of golden stamens. The plant has palmately divided, finely toothed leaves that are typically hairy and covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
