Rosmarinus officinalis var. laxiflorus

Rosmarinus officinalis var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the looseflower rosemary, is a variant of the common rosemary plant.

It is characterized by its smaller, more loosely arranged flowers compared to the typical rosemary. This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its aromatic leaves are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
