Rhamnus occidentalis

Rhamnus occidentalis, commonly known as the western buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in coastal and inland areas. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds.

The bark is smooth and grayish, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
