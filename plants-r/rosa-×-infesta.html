Rosa × infesta

Rosa × infesta, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and produces small, red, edible fruits known as hips. It is native to China and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets, and the plant is well adapted to temperate climates.
