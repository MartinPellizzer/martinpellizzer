Rubus conabilis

Rubus conabilis, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces red or yellow berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Native to North America, it thrives in woodland and scrub habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
