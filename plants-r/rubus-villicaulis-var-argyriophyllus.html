Rubus villicaulis var. argyriophyllus

Rubus villicaulis var. argyriophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leafed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

This variety is native to certain regions of North America and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are hairy and have a unique appearance that distinguishes it from other Rubus species.

The silver-leafed blackberry is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit.
