Ruellia caroliniensis var. cheloniformis

Ruellia caroliniensis var. cheloniformis, commonly known as the Florida wheelleaf ruellia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers throughout the growing season, typically from spring to fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
