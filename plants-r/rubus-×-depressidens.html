Rubus × depressidens

Rubus × depressidens, commonly known as the flat-stemmed blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its low-growing, thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white flowers in the spring.

The fruit is valued for its flavor and is used in culinary applications, though it is less common than other blackberry varieties.
