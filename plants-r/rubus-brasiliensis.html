Rubus brasiliensis

Rubus brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its berries are not only ornamental but also have potential culinary and medicinal uses.
