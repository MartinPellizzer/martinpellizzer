Racosperma plectocarpum subsp. tanumbirinense

Racosperma plectocarpum subsp.

tanumbirinense, commonly known as the Tanumbirinense wattle, is a shrub native to northern Australia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits.

The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow, and it produces small, cylindrical pods that contain seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna.
