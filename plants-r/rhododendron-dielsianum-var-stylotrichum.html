Rhododendron dielsianum var. stylotrichum

Rhododendron dielsianum var. stylotrichum, commonly known as the Diels' rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and distinctive floral morphology, making it a notable ornamental plant.

It is often found in high-altitude forests and is valued for both its ecological role and aesthetic appeal.
