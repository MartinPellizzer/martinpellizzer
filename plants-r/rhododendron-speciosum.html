Rhododendron speciosum

Rhododendron speciosum, commonly known as the showy rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range from pink to red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron speciosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense thickets in forested areas.
