Ranunculus latiangulus

Ranunculus latiangulus, commonly known as the Chinese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy, often colorful blooms. The plant features simple, palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream sides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
