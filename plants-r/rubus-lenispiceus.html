Rubus lenispiceus

Rubus lenispiceus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
