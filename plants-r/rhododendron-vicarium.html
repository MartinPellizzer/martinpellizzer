Rhododendron vicarium

Rhododendron vicarium, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and shrublands, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and inflorescences that are often clustered in trusses.

Rhododendron vicarium plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for various wildlife species.
