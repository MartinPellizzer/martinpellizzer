Rosa micrantha f. anisocantha

Rosa micrantha f. anisocantha, commonly known as the anisocantha rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa micrantha species.

It is characterized by its distinct floral morphology, featuring petals that are unequal in size, a trait reflected in its specific epithet "anisocantha.". The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a sweet fragrance, and bloom in spring.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
