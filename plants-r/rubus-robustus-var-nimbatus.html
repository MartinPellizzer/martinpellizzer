Rubus robustus var. nimbatus

Rubus robustus var. nimbatus, commonly known as the clouded blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and have a serrated edge, while its stems are thorny.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines for their tart flavor and nutritional value.
