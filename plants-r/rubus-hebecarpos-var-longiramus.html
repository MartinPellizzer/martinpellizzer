Rubus hebecarpos var. longiramus

Rubus hebecarpos var. longiramus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which develop from multiple ovaria. The leaves are typically serrated and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
