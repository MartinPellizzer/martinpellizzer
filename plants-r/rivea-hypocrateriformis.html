Rivea hypocrateriformis

Rivea hypocrateriformis, commonly known as the golden rivea, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a vigorous growth habit, making it suitable for trellises or vertical gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
