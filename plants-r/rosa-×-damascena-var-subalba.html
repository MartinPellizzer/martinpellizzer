Rosa × damascena var. subalba

Rosa × damascena var. subalba, commonly known as the white damask rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which are often more subdued in color compared to its more vibrant relatives. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many rose species. Its flowers are fragrant and are used in perfumery and aromatherapy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
