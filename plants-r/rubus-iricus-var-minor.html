Rubus iricus var. minor

Rubus iricus var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe, characterized by its small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for hedgerows and wildlife gardens.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
