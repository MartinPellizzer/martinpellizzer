Rubia caespitosa

Rubia caespitosa, commonly known as greater bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, creeping stems that form dense mats. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia caespitosa is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks across Europe and parts of Asia.
