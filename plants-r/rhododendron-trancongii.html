Rhododendron trancongii

Rhododendron trancongii, commonly known as the Transcogii Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family and is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
