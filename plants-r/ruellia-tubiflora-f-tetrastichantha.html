Ruellia tubiflora f. tetrastichantha

Ruellia tubiflora f. tetrastichantha, commonly known as the four-ranked ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This cultivar is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
