Rhodiola coccinea subsp. coccinea

Rhodiola coccinea subsp.

coccinea, commonly known as the red rhodiola, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of bright red, pinkish, or orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in a fine, white powder. Rhodiola coccinea subsp.

coccinea is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported adaptogenic and antidepressant effects.
