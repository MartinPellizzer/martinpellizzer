Rubus × plicatifrons

Rubus × plicatifrons, commonly known as the compound-fruited blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its unique fruit structure that combines features of both blackberries and raspberries. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of multiple small drupelets, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of shrublands.

The botanical interest of Rubus × plicatifrons lies in its genetic diversity and adaptability, making it a subject of study in horticulture and plant genetics.
