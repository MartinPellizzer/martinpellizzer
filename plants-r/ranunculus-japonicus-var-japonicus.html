Ranunculus japonicus var. japonicus

Ranunculus japonicus var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features delicate, spurred flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its short, stout stems and is often found in temperate climates.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
