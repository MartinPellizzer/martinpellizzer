Ranunculus purshii var. geranioides

Ranunculus purshii var. geranioides, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers feature five to eight petals with a distinctive yellow center.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
