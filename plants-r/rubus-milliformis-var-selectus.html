Rubus milliformis var. selectus

Rubus milliformis var. selectus, commonly known as the selected bramble, is a variant of the common bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a distinctive, often palmate pattern. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
