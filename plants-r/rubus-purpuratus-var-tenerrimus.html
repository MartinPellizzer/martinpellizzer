Rubus purpuratus var. tenerrimus

Rubus purpuratus var. tenerrimus, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red when ripe. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor, making them popular in culinary uses.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in moist, forested areas.
