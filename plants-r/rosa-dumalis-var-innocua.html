Rosa dumalis var. innocua

Rosa dumalis var. innocua, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing at high altitudes where it is adapted to cold and harsh conditions.

Rosa dumalis var. innocua is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
