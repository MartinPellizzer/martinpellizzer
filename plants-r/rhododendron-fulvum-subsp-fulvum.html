Rhododendron fulvum subsp. fulvum

Rhododendron fulvum subsp.

fulvum, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the showy blooms.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded forest environments.
