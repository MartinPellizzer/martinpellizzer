Ribes nigrum subsp. vulgare

Ribes nigrum subsp.

vulgare, commonly known as blackcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamin C. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or white. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and use in landscaping.
