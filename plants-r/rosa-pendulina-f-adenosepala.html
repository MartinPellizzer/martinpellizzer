Rosa pendulina f. adenosepala

Rosa pendulina f. adenosepala, commonly known as the pendulous rose, is a unique floral variant of the Rosa pendulina species.

It is characterized by its drooping, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping.
