Reynosia intermedia

Reynosia intermedia, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 4 to 8 inches long and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Reynosia intermedia is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to provide habitat for local wildlife.
