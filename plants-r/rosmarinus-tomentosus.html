Rosmarinus tomentosus

Rosmarinus tomentosus, commonly known as woolly rosemary, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer. It has aromatic leaves that contain essential oils, making it useful in culinary and medicinal applications.

Rosmarinus tomentosus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
