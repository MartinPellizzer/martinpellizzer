Rhynchospora demerarensis

Rhynchospora demerarensis, commonly known as the Demerara sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly found in regions like Venezuela and Brazil.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spikes produce small, three-lobed flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Rhynchospora demerarensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
