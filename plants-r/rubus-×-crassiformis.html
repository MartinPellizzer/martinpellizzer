Rubus × crassiformis

Rubus × crassiformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
