Rhynchosia komatiensis

Rhynchosia komatiensis, commonly known as the Komati climbing bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Rhynchosia komatiensis is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it an important species in agroforestry systems.

Its seeds are edible and can be used in local cuisines, contributing to both nutritional and ecological value.
