Rubus × koidzumii

Rubus × koidzumii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus hirsutus and Rubus leucodermis.

It is a shrub native to southern China, characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically black when mature, with a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Its botanical features make it a unique example of natural hybridization in the Rubus genus.
