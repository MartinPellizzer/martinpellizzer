Rubus corylifolius var. ciliatus

Rubus corylifolius var. ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
