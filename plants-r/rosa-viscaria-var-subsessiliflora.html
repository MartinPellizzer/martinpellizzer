Rosa viscaria var. subsessiliflora

Rosa viscaria var. subsessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dog rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are pale pink to white, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. It produces red-orange hips that are edible and contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness in mild climates.
