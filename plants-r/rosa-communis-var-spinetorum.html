Rosa communis var. spinetorum

Rosa communis var. spinetorum, commonly known as the spinetose rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its distinctive, thorned stems.

It produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is typically shrub-like, with dense foliage and a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
