Ranunculus auricomus subsp. vitellinus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

vitellinus, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a distinctive orange or yellow center.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically hairy and serrated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The subspecies is characterized by its smaller, more compact flowers compared to the typical R. auricomus.
