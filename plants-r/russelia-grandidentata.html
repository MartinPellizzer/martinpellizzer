Russelia grandidentata

Russelia grandidentata, commonly known as the showy russelia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Russelia grandidentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and tropical appearance.
