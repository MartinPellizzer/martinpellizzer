Rumex mexicanus var. strictus

Rumex mexicanus var. strictus, commonly known as Mexican sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on tall, slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor, making it a traditional ingredient in some regional cuisines.
