Rubus foliosus subsp. flexuosus

Rubus foliosus subsp.

flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with thornless stems that can form dense mats.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. Its flowers are small, white to pink, and appear in clusters, followed by red or black berries.

This species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
