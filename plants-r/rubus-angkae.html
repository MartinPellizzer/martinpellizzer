Rubus angkae

Rubus angkae, commonly known as the Angka blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and Laos. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the stems are covered in stinging hairs.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
