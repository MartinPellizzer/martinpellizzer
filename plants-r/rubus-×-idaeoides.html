Rubus × idaeoides

Rubus × idaeoides, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to western North America, particularly along the Pacific coast. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically growing to a height of 1–3 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is aAggregate berry that ripens to a dark purple or black color.

The plant is valued for its edible berries, which are used in culinary applications and local ecosystems.
