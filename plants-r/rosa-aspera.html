Rosa aspera

Rosa aspera, commonly known as the rough rose, is a species of shrub native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa aspera is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rosaceae family.
