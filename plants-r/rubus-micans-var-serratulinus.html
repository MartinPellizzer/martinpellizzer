Rubus micans var. serratulinus

Rubus micans var. serratulinus, commonly known as the serrated dewberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature, often covered in a whitish bloom. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in moist, wooded areas.
