Rosa mollifuscatula

Rosa mollifuscatula, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rosa mollifuscatula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to rocky, well-drained soils.
