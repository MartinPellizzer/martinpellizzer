Rubus enslenii

Rubus enslenii, commonly known as the Enslen's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries, raspberries, and dewberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a potential resource for local cuisines and traditional uses.
