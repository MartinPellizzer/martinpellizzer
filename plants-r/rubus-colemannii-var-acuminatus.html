Rubus colemannii var. acuminatus

Rubus colemannii var. acuminatus, commonly known as the Colemann's blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
