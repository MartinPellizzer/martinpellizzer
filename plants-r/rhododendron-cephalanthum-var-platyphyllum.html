Rhododendron cephalanthum var. platyphyllum

Rhododendron cephalanthum var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, broad leaves that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide dense evergreen cover in woodland gardens.
