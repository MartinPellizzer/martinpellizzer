Rhynchospora aristata var. brevifoliata

Rhynchospora aristata var. brevifoliata, commonly known as the short-leaved beak-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of small, usually greenish or brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in temperate regions. Its leaves are shorter and more compact compared to other varieties of Rhynchospora.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
