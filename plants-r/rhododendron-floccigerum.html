Rhododendron floccigerum

Rhododendron floccigerum, commonly known as the woolly rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forested areas with acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
