Rosa glauca var. hispida

Rosa glauca var. hispida, commonly known as the Hispid bluebush rose, is a compact shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, blue-gray leaves covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage and modest floral display.
