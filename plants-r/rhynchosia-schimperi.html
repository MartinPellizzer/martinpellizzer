Rhynchosia schimperi

Rhynchosia schimperi, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant bears pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Rhynchosia schimperi is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural systems.
