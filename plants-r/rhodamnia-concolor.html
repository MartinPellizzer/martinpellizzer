Rhodamnia concolor

Rhodamnia concolor, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rhodamnia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.
