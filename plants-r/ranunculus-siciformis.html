Ranunculus siciformis

Ranunculus siciformis, commonly known as the Sicilian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow petals and deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is often found in meadows and along rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its natural habitat being more wild and less common.
