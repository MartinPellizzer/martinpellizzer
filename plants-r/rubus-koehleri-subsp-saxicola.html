Rubus koehleri subsp. saxicola

Rubus koehleri subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as the saxicola raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when mature, containing multiple drupelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
