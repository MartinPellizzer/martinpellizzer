Rapuntium pedunculare var. angustifolium

Rapuntium pedunculare var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a semi-aquatic plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces tiny, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by slender, cylindrical siliques containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks, ditches, and wet meadows.

Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a peppery flavor to dishes.
