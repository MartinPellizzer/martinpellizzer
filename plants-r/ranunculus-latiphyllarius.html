Ranunculus latiphyllarius

Ranunculus latiphyllarius, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble additional petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
