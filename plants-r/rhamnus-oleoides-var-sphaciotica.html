Rhamnus oleoides var. sphaciotica

Rhamnus oleoides var. sphaciotica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
