Rosa × centifolia unguiculata

Rosa × centifolia unguiculata, commonly known as the cabbage rose, is a hybrid species originating from crosses between Rosa centifolia and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically have 50 to 100 petals, giving it a full, cabbage-like appearance. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
