Rhynchospora macrostylis

Rhynchospora macrostylis, commonly known as the large-styled spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive sheath that wraps around the stem.

Rhynchospora macrostylis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
