Rubus granulatus var. lucifugus

Rubus granulatus var. lucifugus, commonly known as the evening beauty raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the late afternoon. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
