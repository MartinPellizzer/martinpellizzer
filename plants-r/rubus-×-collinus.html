Rubus × collinus

Rubus × collinus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the western coast of the United States and thrives in coastal climates.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is often cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
