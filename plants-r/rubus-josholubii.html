Rubus josholubii

Rubus josholubii, commonly known as the Josholubii bramble, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests and shrublands, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
