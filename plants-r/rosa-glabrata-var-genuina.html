Rosa glabrata var. genuina

Rosa glabrata var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican rose or the everblooming rose, is a compact shrub native to the south-central United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season. The plant has glossy, dark green foliage and is characterized by its thorny stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical resilience and ornamental value contribute to its cultivation in both landscapes and as a cut flower.
