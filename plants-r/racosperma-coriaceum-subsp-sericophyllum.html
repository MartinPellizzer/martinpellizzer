Racosperma coriaceum subsp. sericophyllum

Racosperma coriaceum subsp.

sericophyllum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, grey-green foliage and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous bark, and its leaves are alternate and trifoliate. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its silky-hairy texture on the leaves, which gives it its common name.
