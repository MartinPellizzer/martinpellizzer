Retzia pilosa

Retzia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy retzia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Retzia pilosa thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
