Rhamnus alaternus f. integrifolia

Rhamnus alaternus f. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, smooth, and elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries that are rich in anthocyanins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
