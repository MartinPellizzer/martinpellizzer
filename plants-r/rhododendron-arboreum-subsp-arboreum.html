Rhododendron arboreum subsp. arboreum

Rhododendron arboreum subsp.

arboreum, commonly known as the Western Himalayan rhododendron, is a shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in spring.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions at elevations between 1,500 and 3,500 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
