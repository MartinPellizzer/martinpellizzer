Rhododendron hirsutum var. laciniatum

Rhododendron hirsutum var. laciniatum, commonly known as the serrated alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
