Roegneria jacutensis

Roegneria jacutensis, commonly known as the Tien Shan wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Roegneria jacutensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
