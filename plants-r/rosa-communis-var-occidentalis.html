Rosa communis var. occidentalis

Rosa communis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
