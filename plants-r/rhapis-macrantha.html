Rhapis macrantha

Rhapis macrantha, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are typically borne at the base of the plant. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it an excellent choice for ornamental landscaping.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for thatching in some regions.
