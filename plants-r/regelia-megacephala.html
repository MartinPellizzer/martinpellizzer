Regelia megacephala

Regelia megacephala, commonly known as the giant regelia, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Bromeliaceae family, known for its vibrant, showy inflorescences. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy, evergreen leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, are produced in large, terminal clusters.

Regelia megacephala thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
