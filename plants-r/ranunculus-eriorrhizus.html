Ranunculus eriorrhizus

Ranunculus eriorrhizus, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a small flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wet meadows. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and the roots are hairy, giving it its common name.

Ranunculus eriorrhizus is an annual herb that blooms from spring to early summer.
