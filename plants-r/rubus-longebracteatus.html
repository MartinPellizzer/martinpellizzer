Rubus longebracteatus

Rubus longebracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing leaves and the second year bearing fruit. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupelets that are typically clustered together. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins and a hairy texture.
