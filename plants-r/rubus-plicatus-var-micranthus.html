Rubus plicatus var. micranthus

Rubus plicatus var. micranthus, commonly known as the small-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some local culinary applications.
