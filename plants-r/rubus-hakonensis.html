Rubus hakonensis

Rubus hakonensis, commonly known as the Hakon's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the forests of Japan and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
