Renealmia thyrsoidea subsp. chrysantha

Renealmia thyrsoidea subsp.

chrysantha, commonly known as the golden renealmia, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and botanical collections.
