Rosa caesia var. melanophylloides

Rosa caesia var. melanophylloides, commonly known as the black-leaved shrub rose, is a rare and distinctive variation of the Rosa caesia species.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its pink to white flowers. This variety is native to specific regions in southern Africa, particularly in South Africa and Namibia. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with thorny stems and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
