Rubus pungens typicus

Rubus pungens typicus, commonly known as the prickly rose or rough rose, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red, hairy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its aggressive growth and ability to form dense thickets.
