Rubus hybridus microacanthus

Rubus hybridus microacanthus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in jams and preserves.
