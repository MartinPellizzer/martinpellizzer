Rubus traunsteiniensis

Rubus traunsteiniensis, commonly known as the Traunstein raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
