Rhamnus japonica var. japonica

Rhamnus japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, dark purple berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
