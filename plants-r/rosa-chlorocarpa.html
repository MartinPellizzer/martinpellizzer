Rosa chlorocarpa

Rosa chlorocarpa, commonly known as the greenleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and China. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its stems are covered in thorns, and it produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color.

Rosa chlorocarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
