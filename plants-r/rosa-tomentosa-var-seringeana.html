Rosa tomentosa var. seringeana

Rosa tomentosa var. seringeana, commonly known as the Serine rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, reddish-brown hips that are edible and often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
