Rubus nemorosus genuinus

Rubus nemorosus genuinus, commonly known as the European bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typically with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple tiny drupes.

This shrub is native to Europe and thrives in woodland and hedgerow environments.
