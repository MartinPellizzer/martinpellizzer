Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. roseiflora

Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. roseiflora, commonly known as the pink rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
