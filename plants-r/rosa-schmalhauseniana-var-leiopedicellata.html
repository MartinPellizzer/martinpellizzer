Rosa schmalhauseniana var. leiopedicellata

Rosa schmalhauseniana var. leiopedicellata, commonly known as the Siberian dwarf rose, is a compact shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are covered in thorns. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in colder regions.
