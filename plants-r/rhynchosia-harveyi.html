Rhynchosia harveyi

Rhynchosia harveyi, commonly known as the Harvey's cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional agriculture.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
