Rubus ulmifolius monstr. laciniosus

Rubus ulmifolius monstr.

laciniosus, commonly known as the lace-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry with distinctive botanical features. It is characterized by its deeply lobed, almost fern-like leaves that give it a striking appearance.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is a shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and edible benefits.
