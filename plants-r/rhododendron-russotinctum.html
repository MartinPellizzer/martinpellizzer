Rhododendron russotinctum

Rhododendron russotinctum, commonly known as the Russian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and forested habitats at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and silver-white underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, with five petals and a showy, clustered display.

Rhododendron russotinctum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal beauty.
