Rhododendron albiflorum

Rhododendron albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Rhododendron albiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
