Rosa hybrida var. semirepens

Rosa hybrida var. semirepens, commonly known as the trailing bush rose, is a compact, low-growing shrub characterized by its spreading habit and dense foliage.

It produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, throughout the growing season. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its blooms. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cascade over walls or trellises.

Botanically, it is a hybrid rose with a strong root system that makes it adaptable to various soil types.
