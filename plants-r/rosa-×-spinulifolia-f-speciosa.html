Rosa × spinulifolia f. speciosa

Rosa × spinulifolia f. speciosa, commonly known as the showy spinulifera rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with multiple stems and a spreading habit. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges, and are covered in a fine layer of silvery-white down. Its flowers are large, showy, and typically pink to red in color, with a fluted appearance and a strong, sweet fragrance.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
