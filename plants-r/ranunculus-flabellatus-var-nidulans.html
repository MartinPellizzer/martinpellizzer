Ranunculus flabellatus var. nidulans

Ranunculus flabellatus var. nidulans, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

The varietal form 'nidulans' is distinguished by its clustered growth habit and smaller, more compact flowers compared to the typical species.
