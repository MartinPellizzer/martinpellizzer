Rubus schmidelyanus var. pascuorum

Rubus schmidelyanus var. pascuorum, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen in late summer. It is an important species in its habitat for both wildlife and local biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
