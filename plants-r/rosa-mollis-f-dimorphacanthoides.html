Rosa mollis f. dimorphacanthoides

Rosa mollis f. dimorphacanthoides, commonly known as the soft rose or silky rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with arching stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are borne on long peduncles and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic qualities.
