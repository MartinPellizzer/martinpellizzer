Rapanea peregrina

Rapanea peregrina, commonly known as the wandering evergreen, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, which contains a single seed.

Rapanea peregrina is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
