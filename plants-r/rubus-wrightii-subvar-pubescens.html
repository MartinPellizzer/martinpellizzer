Rubus wrightii subvar. pubescens

Rubus wrightii subvar. pubescens, commonly known as the Wright's blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy (pubescent) foliage and its role in local ecosystems as a food source for various wildlife species.
