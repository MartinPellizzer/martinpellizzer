Rubus corylifolius var. subglandulosus

Rubus corylifolius var. subglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with thorny stems and dense foliage.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
