Rubus tereticaulis var. hrubyanus

Rubus tereticaulis var. hrubyanus, commonly known as the Hrubyan's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in woodland edges and moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
