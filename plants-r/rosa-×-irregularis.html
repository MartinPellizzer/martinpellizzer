Rosa × irregularis

Rosa × irregularis, commonly known as the irregular rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its variable growth habit, ranging from shrubs to climbers, and its diverse floral morphology. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers with five to seven petals. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it thrives in temperate climates.

Rosa × irregularis is valued for ornamental purposes and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
