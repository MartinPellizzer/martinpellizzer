Rosa communis var. bretoni

Rosa communis var. bretoni, commonly known as the Breton rose, is a cultivar of the common rose that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It features semi-double to fully double flowers with a rich, deep pink hue, often with a slightly ruffled appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a fragrant, showy display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
