Rubus gracilis

Rubus gracilis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its thin, trailing stems and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, though they are often less flavorful than other blackberry varieties. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Rubus gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various wildlife species.
