Rhododendron javanicum subsp. cockburnii

Rhododendron javanicum subsp.

cockburnii, commonly known as the Cockburn's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Indonesia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural settings.
