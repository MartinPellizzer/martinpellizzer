Ranunculus glacialis subsp. alaskensis

Ranunculus glacialis subsp.

alaskensis, commonly known as the Alaskan glacier buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and deeply lobed leaves.

This subspecies thrives in moist, rocky habitats such as tundra and alpine meadows. It has a short growing season and is adapted to cold climates with limited sunlight.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
