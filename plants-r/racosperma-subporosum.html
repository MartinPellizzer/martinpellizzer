Racosperma subporosum

Racosperma subporosum, commonly known as the Porous Acacia, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in nutrient-poor soils, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
