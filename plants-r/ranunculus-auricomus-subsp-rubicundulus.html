Ranunculus auricomus subsp. rubicundulus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

rubicundulus, commonly known as the red-veined buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with red veins and deeply lobed leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine habitats. It produces numerous small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
