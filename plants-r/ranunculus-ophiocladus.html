Ranunculus ophiocladus

Ranunculus ophiocladus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which typically bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive, hairy texture and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and sometimes toxic properties.

Ranunculus ophiocladus is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates.
