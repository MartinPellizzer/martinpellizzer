Rubus pallidicaulis

Rubus pallidicaulis, commonly known as the pale-stemmed bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to North America and typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its slender, pale-green stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, and it produces edible, though tart, berries.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining ecosystem biodiversity.
