Ruschia hamatilis

Ruschia hamatilis, commonly known as the hairy Ruschia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, branching growth habit and fleshy, cylindrical stems covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
