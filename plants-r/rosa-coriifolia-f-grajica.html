Rosa coriifolia f. grajica

Rosa coriifolia f. grajica, commonly known as the Grajica rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features distinctive, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in dense, glandular hairs, which contribute to its aromatic qualities.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and fragrance.
