Rubus polyandrus

Rubus polyandrus, commonly known as the many-stamen raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of multiple small drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
