Rubus vallisparsus var. subrudis

Rubus vallisparsus var. subrudis, commonly known as the roughberry raspberries, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals. This plant thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Its berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and preserves.
