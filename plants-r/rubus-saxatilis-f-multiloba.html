Rubus saxatilis f. multiloba

Rubus saxatilis f. multiloba, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in rocky, alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its morphology reflects evolutionary adaptations to its high-altitude habitat, making it a notable species in mountainous flora.
