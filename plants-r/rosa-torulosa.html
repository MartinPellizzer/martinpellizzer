Rosa torulosa

Rosa torulosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces fragrant pink to reddish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa torulosa is often found in rocky or arid environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature.
