Rhododendron cyrtophyllum

Rhododendron cyrtophyllum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a species native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, with five petals, and bloom in shades of pink to red in the spring.

Rhododendron cyrtophyllum prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
