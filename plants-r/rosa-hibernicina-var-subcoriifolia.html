Rosa hibernicina var. subcoriifolia

Rosa hibernicina var. subcoriifolia, commonly known as the Irish rose or the Irish climbing rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and North America.

It features arching stems that can grow up to several meters in length, making it suitable for trellises or espalier training. The leaves are compound, typically with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
