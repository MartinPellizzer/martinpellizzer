Rosa spinosissima var. lutea

Rosa spinosissima var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow prickly rose, is a variant of the prickly rose species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and scrublands. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits called hips.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
