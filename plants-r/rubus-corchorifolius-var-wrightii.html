Rubus corchorifolius var. wrightii

Rubus corchorifolius var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.

Its growth habit includes shrubs that can reach heights of up to six feet, often thriving in woodland or rocky habitats.
