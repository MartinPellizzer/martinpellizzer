Rosa obtusata

Rosa obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or orange hips in the autumn. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rounded or obtuse shape, giving it its common name.

Rosa obtusata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
