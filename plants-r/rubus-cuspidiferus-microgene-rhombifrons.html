Rubus cuspidiferus microgene rhombifrons

Rubus cuspidiferus microgene rhombifrons, commonly known as the Allegheny blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with rhomboid-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its edible berries and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
