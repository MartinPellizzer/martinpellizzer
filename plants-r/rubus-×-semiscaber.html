Rubus × semiscaber

Rubus × semiscaber, commonly known as the thimbleberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its distinctive, thimble-shaped fruit.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streamside areas. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually yellow or orange in color. It is native to North America and is often found in the eastern and western regions of the United States.

The thimbleberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible berries, which are enjoyed by both humans and wildlife.
