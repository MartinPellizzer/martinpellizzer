Rubus chlorocladus

Rubus chlorocladus, commonly known as the green-leaved blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, dark berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in plant studies.
