Rosa × pseudotomentella

Rosa × pseudotomentella, commonly known as the everglast rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white to pale pink flowers that bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a high degree of cold hardiness, making it suitable for colder climates.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
