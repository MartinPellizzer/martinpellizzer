Rosa afzeliana var. zajartakensis

Rosa afzeliana var. zajartakensis, commonly known as the Zajartakens rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
