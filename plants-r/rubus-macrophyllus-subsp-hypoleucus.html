Rubus macrophyllus subsp. hypoleucus

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

hypoleucus, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local horticulture.
