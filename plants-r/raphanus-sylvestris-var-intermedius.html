Raphanus sylvestris var. intermedius

Raphanus sylvestris var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate wild radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and pinnately lobed leaves that are typically hairy as well. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by long, slender pods containing several seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
