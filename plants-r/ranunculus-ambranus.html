Ranunculus ambranus

Ranunculus ambranus, commonly known as the amber buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and damp woodlands. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
