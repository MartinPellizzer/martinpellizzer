Rubus nigricatus var. parchavicus

Rubus nigricatus var. parchavicus, commonly known as the Blackberry Bramble, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into dark purple or black berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its berries are edible but can be quite tart, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth habit.
