Rosa schimperiana

Rosa schimperiana, commonly known as the Schimper's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, dense foliage, and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa schimperiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that require drought-tolerant plants.
