Rubus armipotens

Rubus armipotens, commonly known as the armed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small white flowers in the spring. Its berries are edible but often have a tart flavor.

The species is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and blackberry varieties.
