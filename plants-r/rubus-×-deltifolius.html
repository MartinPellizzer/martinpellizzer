Rubus × deltifolius

Rubus × deltifolius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems.
