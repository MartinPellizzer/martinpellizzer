Rutaneblina

Rutaneblina, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer with an ethereal glow, especially in the early morning light. The plant's leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in the humid, mist-laden environment. Rutaneblina blooms only during specific lunar phases, making it a subject of fascination for botanists and conservationists.
