Rubus × wiesbaurii

Rubus × wiesbaurii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental landscaping.
