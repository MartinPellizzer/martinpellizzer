Rubus × infrapubens

Rubus × infrapubens, commonly known as the pubescent blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

The fruit is edible, though it has a tart flavor and is often used in jams and preserves.
