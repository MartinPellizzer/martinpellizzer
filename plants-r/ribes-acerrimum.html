Ribes acerrimum

Ribes acerrimum, commonly known as sharpcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the genus Ribes in the family Grossulariaceae, which includes other currant and gooseberry species. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically purple or red in color, with a high acidity level. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Ribes acerrimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
