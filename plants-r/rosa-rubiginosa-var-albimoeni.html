Rosa rubiginosa var. albimoeni

Rosa rubiginosa var. albimoeni, commonly known as the white-stemmed dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and hairy, reddish stems that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as woodlands and scrublands. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
