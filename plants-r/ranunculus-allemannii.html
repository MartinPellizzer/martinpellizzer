Ranunculus allemannii

Ranunculus allemannii, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a short, stout rhizome and produces delicate, yellow flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are hairy and upright.

Ranunculus allemannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
