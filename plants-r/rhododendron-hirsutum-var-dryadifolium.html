Rhododendron hirsutum var. dryadifolium

Rhododendron hirsutum var. dryadifolium, commonly known as the Dryad's Leaf Rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features small, leathery leaves that are densely hairy, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and long-lasting blooms.
