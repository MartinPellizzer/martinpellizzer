Ruthea

Ruthea, commonly known as the meadow rue, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ranunculaceae.

These plants are characterized by their tall, upright stems and clusters of showy, usually purple or pink flowers. Ruthea species are native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. They typically grow in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The leaves are compound, with deeply lobed or divided foliage that adds to the plant's ornamental appeal.
