Rosa sericea var. glandulosa

Rosa sericea var. glandulosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a variant of the woolly rose species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, silvery-gray foliage and showy, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its stems and leaves are covered in fine, glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
