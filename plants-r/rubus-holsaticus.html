Rubus holsaticus

Rubus holsaticus, commonly known as the field raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus holsaticus is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
