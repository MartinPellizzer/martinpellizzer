Rhamnus spartium

Rhamnus spartium, commonly known as the Greek buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Rhamnus spartium is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
