Riedelia clonantha

Riedelia clonantha, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and Botswana. The plant is characterized by its unique, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a ghostly face, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive central blotch.

Riedelia clonantha is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.
