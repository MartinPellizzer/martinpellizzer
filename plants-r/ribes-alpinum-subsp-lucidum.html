Ribes alpinum subsp. lucidum

Ribes alpinum subsp.

lucidum, commonly known as the alpine gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that mature to a red color.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broadly elliptical with serrated edges and a smooth, waxy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
