Rubus obesifolius

Rubus obesifolius, commonly known as the fat-leaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, resembling raspberries in appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The botanical features of Rubus obesifolius make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
