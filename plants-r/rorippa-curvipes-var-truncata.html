Rorippa curvipes var. truncata

Rorippa curvipes var. truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watercress, is a flowering plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in shallow water and moist soils, making it a common species in ponds and stream margins.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed aquatic habitats contribute to its ecological significance.
