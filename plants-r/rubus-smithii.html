Rubus smithii

Rubus smithii, commonly known as Smith's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
