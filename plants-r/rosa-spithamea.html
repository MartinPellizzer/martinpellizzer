Rosa spithamea

Rosa spithamea, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to regions of Turkey and the eastern Mediterranean, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rosa spithamea is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.
