Rosa pendulina var. bosniaca

Rosa pendulina var. bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian climbing rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Balkan region.

It features long, arching stems that can climb or trail along the ground, often reaching lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the plant is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage.

Rosa pendulina var. bosniaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a decorative plant.
