Rhus ochracea var. saxicola

Rhus ochracea var. saxicola, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky habitats and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small wildlife.
