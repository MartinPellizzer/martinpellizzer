Rubus nigrobaccus var. calycosus

Rubus nigrobaccus var. calycosus, commonly known as the blackberry-like bramble, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental and edible qualities, it is occasionally cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
