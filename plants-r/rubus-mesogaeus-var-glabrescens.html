Rubus mesogaeus var. glabrescens

Rubus mesogaeus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. Its flowers are white to pink and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The variety is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which set it apart from other Rubus species.
