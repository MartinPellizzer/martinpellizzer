Relhania speciosa

Relhania speciosa, commonly known as the golden star tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the flowering season. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in warmer regions.
