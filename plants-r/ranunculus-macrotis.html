Ranunculus macrotis

Ranunculus macrotis, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with numerous petals and a central yellow stamen. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Ranunculus macrotis is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often found in association with other wetland plants.
