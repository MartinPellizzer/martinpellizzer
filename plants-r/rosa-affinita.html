Rosa affinita

Rosa affinita, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces oval, yellowish fruits that contain numerous seeds. Rosa affinita is a climbing shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique nocturnal bloom.
