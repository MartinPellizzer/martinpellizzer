Rubus ulmifolius var. dispulsiflorus

Rubus ulmifolius var. dispulsiflorus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters during the spring. The plant produces edible berries in the fall, which are often used in jams and preserves.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
