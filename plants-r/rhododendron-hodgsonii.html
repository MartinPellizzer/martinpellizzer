Rhododendron hodgsonii

Rhododendron hodgsonii, commonly known as the Hodgson's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist, and shaded forest environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a glossy surface. Its flowers are large, showy, and typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron hodgsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
