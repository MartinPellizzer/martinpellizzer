Restio grandis

Restio grandis, commonly known as the giant restio, is a striking evergreen plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowering spikes, which appear at the tips of the stems, produce small, white or pale blue flowers.

Restio grandis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to create a dramatic visual impact in gardens.
