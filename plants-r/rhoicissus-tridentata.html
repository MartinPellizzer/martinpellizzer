Rhoicissus tridentata

Rhoicissus tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed cross vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like structures that turn red when mature.

Rhoicissus tridentata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
