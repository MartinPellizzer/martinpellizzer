Ribes aureum f. chrysococcum

Ribes aureum f. chrysococcum, commonly known as the golden currant, is a variant of the golden currant plant native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its bright yellow flowers and red, edible berries. The foliage is typically dark green with a glossy texture, and the plant often grows in moist, woodland environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.

This cultivar is noted for its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
