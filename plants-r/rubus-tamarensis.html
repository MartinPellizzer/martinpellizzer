Rubus tamarensis

Rubus tamarensis, commonly known as the Tamarensis blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small in size. The plant has palmate leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers during the spring season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shrublands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
