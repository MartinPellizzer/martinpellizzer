Rhynchoglossum gardneri

Rhynchoglossum gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's rhynchoglossum, is a unique member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, specifically Ecuador and Peru, where it thrives in high-altitude cloud forests. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a distinctive lip, which is often brightly colored and adorned with intricate patterns. Its growth habit is terrestrial, with slender, wiry stems that bear a few broad, elliptical leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanical conservation efforts.
