Rubus bloxamianus

Rubus bloxamianus, commonly known as the Bloxam's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
