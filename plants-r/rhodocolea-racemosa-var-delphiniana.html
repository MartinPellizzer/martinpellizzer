Rhodocolea racemosa var. delphiniana

Rhodocolea racemosa var. delphiniana, commonly known as the blue-flowered rhodocolea, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and striking floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
