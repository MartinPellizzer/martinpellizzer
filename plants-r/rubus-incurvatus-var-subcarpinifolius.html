Rubus incurvatus var. subcarpinifolius

Rubus incurvatus var. subcarpinifolius, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
