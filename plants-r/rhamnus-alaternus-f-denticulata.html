Rhamnus alaternus f. denticulata

Rhamnus alaternus f. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate buckthorn, is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, black berries that are a source of food for birds. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

Its compact form and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental gardening.
