Rubus andersonii

Rubus andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and prickly stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its role in local ecosystems and its potential for cultivation in native plant gardens.
