Rosa pendulina var. subleioneura

Rosa pendulina var. subleioneura, commonly known as the hanging rose or the weeping rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa pendulina species.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is valued for its ornamental appearance. It typically grows in a shrub form with thorny stems and dark green foliage.

The flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in early summer and attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
