Rosa xanthina f. spontanea

Rosa xanthina f. spontanea, commonly known as the yellow rose of China, is a naturally occurring form of the species Rosa xanthina.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically single or semi-double and have a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly used in gardens and landscaping.
