Rubus ferrugineus

Rubus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring moist, well-drained conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other members of the Rubus genus.
