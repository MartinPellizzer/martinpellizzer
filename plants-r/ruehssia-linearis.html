Ruehssia linearis

Ruehssia linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ruehssia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
