Ranunculus basilobatus

Ranunculus basilobatus, commonly known as the western buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy surface. It has a fibrous root system and produces numerous small, greenish-white flowers in spring.

Ranunculus basilobatus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic compounds.
