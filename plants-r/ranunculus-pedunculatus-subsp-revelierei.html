Ranunculus pedunculatus subsp. revelierei

Ranunculus pedunculatus subsp.

revelierei, commonly known as the Reveliere's buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions. It has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and the stems are usually unbranched.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
