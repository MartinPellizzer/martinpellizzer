Rosa communis var. sphaerocarpa

Rosa communis var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe rose, is a cultivar characterized by its unique spherical blooms.

It is a hybrid of Rosa chinensis and Rosa multiflora, exhibiting a compact growth habit with dense, bushy foliage. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a full, rounded shape that sets it apart from other rose varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring regular pruning to maintain its form.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its visual impact.
