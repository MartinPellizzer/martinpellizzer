Rosa agrestis var. schulzei

Rosa agrestis var. schulzei, commonly known as the Schulze's field rose, is a variant of the field rose native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
