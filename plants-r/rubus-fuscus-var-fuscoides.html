Rubus fuscus var. fuscoides

Rubus fuscus var. fuscoides, commonly known as the blackberry cane.

This variant is a shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and dark, glossy leaves. It produces small, tart berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in woodland edges and moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
