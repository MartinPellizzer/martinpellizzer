Rhaphiolepis ferruginea var. ferruginea

Rhaphiolepis ferruginea var. ferruginea, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, bright orange-red berries that persist through winter, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It produces fragrant, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by its showy berries.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental berries and adaptability to various soil types.
