Rubus hirtus subsp. melanochlamys

Rubus hirtus subsp.

melanochlamys, commonly known as the black-leaved raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, almost black, foliage, which gives it its common name. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that ripens to a deep red.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
