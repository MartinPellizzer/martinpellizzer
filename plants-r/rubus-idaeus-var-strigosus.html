Rubus idaeus var. strigosus

Rubus idaeus var. strigosus, commonly known as the brambles or raspberries, is a variant of the common raspberry plant.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
