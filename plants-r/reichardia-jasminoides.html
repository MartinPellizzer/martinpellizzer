Reichardia jasminoides

Reichardia jasminoides, commonly known as the jasmine-like dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its spread in suitable habitats.

Reichardia jasminoides has a long flowering period, often blooming from spring to early autumn.
