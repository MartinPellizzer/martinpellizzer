Rabiea cibdela

Rabiea cibdela, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to thrive in arid environments.

Rabiea cibdela is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
