Ranunculus sprunerianus var. subglaber

Ranunculus sprunerianus var. subglaber, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
