Rosa spinosissima var. javalambrensis

Rosa spinosissima var. javalambrensis, commonly known as the Javalambrense rose, is a rare and distinctive species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
