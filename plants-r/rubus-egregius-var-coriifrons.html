Rubus egregius var. coriifrons

Rubus egregius var. coriifrons, commonly known as the velvetleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces clusters of small, black berries that are edible. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets. Its flowers are white and appear in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive fruit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local cuisines.
