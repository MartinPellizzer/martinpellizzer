Ranunculus auricomus subsp. trivialis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

trivialis, commonly known as the common buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a distinct central beard-like structure.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually hairy and upright.

The species is known for its short flowering period in early spring and its role in supporting pollinators.
