Rubus rusticanus var. obovatus

Rubus rusticanus var. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with obovate leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
