Rubus caucasicus

Rubus caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or dark purple when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and open areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
