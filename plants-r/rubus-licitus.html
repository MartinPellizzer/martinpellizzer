Rubus licitus

Rubus licitus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rubus genus in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Rubus licitus is an important food source for various wildlife species and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
