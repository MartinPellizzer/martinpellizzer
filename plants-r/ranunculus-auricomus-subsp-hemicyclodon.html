Ranunculus auricomus subsp. hemicyclodon

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

hemicyclodon, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals, often with a distinctive yellow or orange hue.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and can be either basal or alternate, depending on the growth habit.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
