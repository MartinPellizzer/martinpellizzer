Rubus villicaulis subsp. selmeri

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

selmeri, commonly known as the Selmer's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
