Rubus nitidus subsp. laetevirens

Rubus nitidus subsp.

laetevirens, commonly known as the glossy evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves that remain on the plant year-round.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
