Rhododendron taiwanianum

Rhododendron taiwanianum, commonly known as the Taiwan azalea, is a species native to Taiwan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with acidic, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to forested landscapes.
