Ranunculus baguetii

Ranunculus baguetii, commonly known as the Baguetti's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically produces solitary, cup-shaped blooms with five to eight petals, and its leaves are palmately divided. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
