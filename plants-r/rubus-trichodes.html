Rubus trichodes

Rubus trichodes, commonly known as the thorned blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the eastern United States.

Rubus trichodes is a perennial shrub that plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
