Ranunculus meyeri var. transvaalensis

Ranunculus meyeri var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem that supports its basal leaves, which are trifoliate and covered in fine hairs. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
