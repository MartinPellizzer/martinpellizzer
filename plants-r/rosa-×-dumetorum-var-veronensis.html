Rosa × dumetorum var. veronensis

Rosa × dumetorum var. veronensis, commonly known as the Veronese shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often forming low shrubs with multiple stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, slightly hairy, and have a serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
