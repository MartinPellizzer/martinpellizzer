Ranunculus auricomus subsp. modicus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

modicus, commonly known as the smallleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive yellow center, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically smaller and more refined compared to the larger leaves of the main species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant.
