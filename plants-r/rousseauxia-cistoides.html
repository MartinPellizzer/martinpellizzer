Rousseauxia cistoides

Rousseauxia cistoides, commonly known as the rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its woody stems are typically reddish-brown and can become branched with age.

Rousseauxia cistoides is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
