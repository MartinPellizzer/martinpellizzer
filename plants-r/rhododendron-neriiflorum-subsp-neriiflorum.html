Rhododendron neriiflorum subsp. neriiflorum

Rhododendron neriiflorum subsp.

neriiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white.

The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with acidic, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.

The subspecies is distinguished by its relatively smaller size and more compact growth habit compared to other rhododendron species.
