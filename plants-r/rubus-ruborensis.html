Rubus ruborensis

Rubus ruborensis, commonly known as the red-flowered raspberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are pink to red in color, and the fruits are edible, though often tart.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
