Rockinghamia brevipes

Rockinghamia brevipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockinghamia, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Rockinghamia brevipes is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.
