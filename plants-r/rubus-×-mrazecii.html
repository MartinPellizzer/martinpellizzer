Rubus × mrazecii

Rubus × mrazecii, commonly known as the Mrazec raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, known for its unique botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or yellow in color, with a tangy flavor. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in horticulture.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a wide range of genetic variability in morphology and fruit traits.
