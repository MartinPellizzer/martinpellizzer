Ranunculus dactylophyllus

Ranunculus dactylophyllus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five to eight petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, trifoliate leaves that are hairy, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
