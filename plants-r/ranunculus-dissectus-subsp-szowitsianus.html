Ranunculus dissectus subsp. szowitsianus

Ranunculus dissectus subsp.

szowitsianus, commonly known as the Szowits' buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with numerous yellow stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and stream sides. Its leaves are dissected, featuring a finely divided structure that distinguishes it from other ranunculus species.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and early spring blooming habit.
