Rubus holubii

Rubus holubii, commonly known as Holub's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
