Rosa mollis f. proclinatula

Rosa mollis f. proclinatula, commonly known as the prostrate pink, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features soft, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground, forming a dense mat. The plant produces fragrant, pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval, serrated leaflets.

This variety is well-suited for ground cover in moist, partially shaded environments.
