Ranunculus lomatocarpus var. psilocarpus

Ranunculus lomatocarpus var. psilocarpus, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are often hairy.

The species is notable for its ecological importance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
