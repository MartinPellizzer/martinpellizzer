Rosa × gapensis var. puymaurea

Rosa × gapensis var. puymaurea, commonly known as the Puymaurea rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically has dark green, glossy leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
