Rosa mollis subf. insquarrosula

Rosa mollis subf.

insquarrosula, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a sub-species native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and young shoots, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with small, fragrant pink or white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a fine layer of white to grayish hairs.

This species is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.
