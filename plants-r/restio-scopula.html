Restio scopula

Restio scopula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in open, sunny areas.

Restio scopula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
