Rubus foliosus subsp. omalodontus

Rubus foliosus subsp.

omalodontus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

The plant typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, preferring moist, well-drained conditions. Its flowers are white and clustered, and the species is known for its thorny stems.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species, sharing similar morphological traits and ecological preferences.
