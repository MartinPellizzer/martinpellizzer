Rubus sanctus f. orientalis

Rubus sanctus f. orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Holy Bramble, is a variant of the Rubus sanctus species native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small and tart, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along the edges of woodlands.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has a compact growth habit.
