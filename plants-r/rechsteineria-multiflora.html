Rechsteineria multiflora

Rechsteineria multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered rechsteineria, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
