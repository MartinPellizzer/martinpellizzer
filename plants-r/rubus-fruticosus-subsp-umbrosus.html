Rubus fruticosus subsp. umbrosus

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

umbrosus, commonly known as the bramble rose or blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
