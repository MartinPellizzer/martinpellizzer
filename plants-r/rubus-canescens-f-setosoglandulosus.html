Rubus canescens f. setosoglandulosus

Rubus canescens f. setosoglandulosus, commonly known as the hairy glandular raspberry.

This plant is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The inflorescence consists of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are aggregate berries, typically red when mature, and are often used in culinary applications.

The species is native to North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
