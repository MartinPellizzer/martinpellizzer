Rosa × dimorphocarpa

Rosa × dimorphocarpa, commonly known as the double-flowered rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its large, fully double blooms that often resemble a ball of petals. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly used in floral arrangements.
