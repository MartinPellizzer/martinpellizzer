Rhododendron malayanum var. axillare

Rhododendron malayanum var. axillare, commonly known as the Malayan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with high humidity and acidic soil conditions. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
