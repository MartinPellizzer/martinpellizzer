Ribes alpinum var. parvifolium

Ribes alpinum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the alpine gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy surface, adapted to cold and harsh climates. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible and often used in jams and preserves.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
