Rubus colemannii var. obovatus

Rubus colemannii var. obovatus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

This variety is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other species of brambles and blackberries.
