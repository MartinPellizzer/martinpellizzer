Ranunculus subborealis

Ranunculus subborealis, commonly known as the northern buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or white flowers with five to eight petals, and its trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. This species is native to arctic and alpine regions of North America and Eurasia, thriving in cool, moist environments. It typically grows in open habitats such as tundra, meadows, and rocky slopes.

The plant is adapted to harsh climates, with a short growing season and a reliance on early spring conditions for flowering and seed production.
