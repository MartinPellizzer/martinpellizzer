Rosa davurica var. setacea

Rosa davurica var. setacea, commonly known as the Siberian climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden use as a climbing plant or hedge.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand cold climates, contributing to its popularity in temperate regions.
