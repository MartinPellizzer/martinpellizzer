Rubus tereticaulis var. clivicola

Rubus tereticaulis var. clivicola, commonly known as the cliff raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, sunny environments.
