Rosa laxa var. karatavica

Rosa laxa var. karatavica, commonly known as the Karatavica rose, is a shrub native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a red, hairy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
