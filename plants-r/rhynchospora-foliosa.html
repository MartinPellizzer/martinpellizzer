Rhynchospora foliosa

Rhynchospora foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leafed spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Rhynchospora foliosa is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
