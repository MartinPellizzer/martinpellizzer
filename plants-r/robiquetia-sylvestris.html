Robiquetia sylvestris

Robiquetia sylvestris, commonly known as the forest bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Robiquetia sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
