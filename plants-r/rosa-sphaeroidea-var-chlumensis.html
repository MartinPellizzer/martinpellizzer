Rosa sphaeroidea var. chlumensis

Rosa sphaeroidea var. chlumensis, commonly known as the Czech rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
