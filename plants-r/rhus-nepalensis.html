Rhus nepalensis

Rhus nepalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like poison ivy and cashew. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals.

Rhus nepalensis is often found in rocky or forested areas at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.
