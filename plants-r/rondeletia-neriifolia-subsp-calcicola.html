Rondeletia neriifolia subsp. calcicola

Rondeletia neriifolia subsp.

calcicola, commonly known as the limestone rondeletia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and slightly glossy.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to specific soil conditions.
