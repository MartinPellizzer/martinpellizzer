Rapanea velutina

Rapanea velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and appear in clusters.

Rapanea velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
