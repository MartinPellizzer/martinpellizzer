Rosa × scaphusiensis

Rosa × scaphusiensis, commonly known as the scaly rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a dense covering of small, scaly glands on the leaves. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Rosa × scaphusiensis prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to various landscaping settings.
