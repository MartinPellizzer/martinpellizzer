Rudbeckia nitida var. texana

Rudbeckia nitida var. texana, commonly known as the Texas coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It features upright, branching stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flower heads with a distinctive, narrow central cone. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
