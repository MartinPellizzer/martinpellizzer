Rubus procerus var. dynatos

Rubus procerus var. dynatos, commonly known as the Dynatos blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupelets that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
