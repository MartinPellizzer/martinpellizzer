Rosa coriifolia var. boissieri

Rosa coriifolia var. boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in dense, thorny prickles. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
