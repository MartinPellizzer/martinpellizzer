Rosa afzeliana subf. aciphylloides

Rosa afzeliana subf.

aciphylloides, commonly known as the African rose or African wild rose, is a subspecies native to southern Africa. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets.

The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, producing a showy display. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or forested areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
