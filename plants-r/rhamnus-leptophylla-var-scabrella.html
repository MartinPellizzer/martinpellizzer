Rhamnus leptophylla var. scabrella

Rhamnus leptophylla var. scabrella, commonly known as the smoothleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glossy on top. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and chaparral habitats.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
