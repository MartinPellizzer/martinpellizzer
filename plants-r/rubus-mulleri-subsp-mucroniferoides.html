Rubus mulleri subsp. mucroniferoides

Rubus mulleri subsp.

mucroniferoides, commonly known as the narrow-tipped raspberry. It is a shrub native to China, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and the structure of its inflorescence.
