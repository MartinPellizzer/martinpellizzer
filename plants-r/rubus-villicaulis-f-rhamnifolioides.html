Rubus villicaulis f. rhamnifolioides

Rubus villicaulis f. rhamnifolioides, commonly known as the hairy-leaf raspberry.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive, narrow shape, resembling that of a rhamnus species. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor.

It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.
