Rumex obtusifolius var. discolor

Rumex obtusifolius var. discolor, commonly known as the purple dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on erect, branching stems. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitats.
