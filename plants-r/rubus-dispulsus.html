Rubus dispulsus

Rubus dispulsus, commonly known as the Canadian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

Rubus dispulsus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinator populations.
