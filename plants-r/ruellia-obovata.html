Ruellia obovata

Ruellia obovata, commonly known as the broadleaf ruellia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It features opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Ruellia obovata is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
