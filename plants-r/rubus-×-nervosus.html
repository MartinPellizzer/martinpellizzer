Rubus × nervosus

Rubus × nervosus, commonly known as the brambleberry or red raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small drupes, typically bright red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable for cultivation.
