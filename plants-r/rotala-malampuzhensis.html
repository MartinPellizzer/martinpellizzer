Rotala malampuzhensis

Rotala malampuzhensis, commonly known as the Malampuzhensis water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to the wetlands of India.

It is characterized by its finely divided, floating leaves that create a dense canopy on the water surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in water gardens and ponds.
