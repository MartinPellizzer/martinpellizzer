Rosa tomentosa f. collivaga

Rosa tomentosa f. collivaga, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Rosa tomentosa f. collivaga is relatively low maintenance, thriving in well-drained soil and full to partial sun.
