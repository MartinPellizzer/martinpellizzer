Rosa × subinermis var. hispidocarpa

Rosa × subinermis var. hispidocarpa, commonly known as the hispidocarpa rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and profuse flowering habit. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
