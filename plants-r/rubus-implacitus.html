Rubus implacitus

Rubus implacitus, commonly known as the deadly nightshade.

This plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple berries that contain toxic compounds. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its poisonous nature, it is important to handle it with care.
