Rabdosia smithiana

Rabdosia smithiana, commonly known as Smith's rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils.

Rabdosia smithiana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.
