Rubus malacadenes

Rubus malacadenes, commonly known as the Malacca raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which ripens in summer, is valued for its tart flavor and is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
