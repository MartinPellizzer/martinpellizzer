Rytidosperma popinense

Rytidosperma popinense, commonly known as the slender porcupine grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Rytidosperma popinense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
