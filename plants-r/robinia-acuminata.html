Robinia acuminata

Robinia acuminata, commonly known as the screwbean acacia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 10 to 20 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release flat, winged seeds.

Robinia acuminata has a deep root system and is adapted to a variety of soil types, making it a resilient and hardy species.
