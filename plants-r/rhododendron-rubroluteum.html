Rhododendron rubroluteum

Rhododendron rubroluteum, commonly known as the yellow-red rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking flowers that range from yellow to reddish-orange, typically blooming in late spring. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Rhododendron rubroluteum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens.
