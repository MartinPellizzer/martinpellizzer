Rubus trifidus var. semiplenus

Rubus trifidus var. semiplenus, commonly known as the semi-plenum blackberry, is a variant of the trifid blackberry characterized by its unique fruit structure.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, typically found in wetland and riparian habitats. The plant features trifid (three-lobed) leaves and produces small, dark purple to black berries. Its botanical significance lies in its distinct morphology and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

The semi-plenum variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in horticulture.
