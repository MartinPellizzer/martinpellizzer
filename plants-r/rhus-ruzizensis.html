Rhus ruzizensis

Rhus ruzizensis, commonly known as the Mexican sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged and have serrated edges. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in panicles, and it produces red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
