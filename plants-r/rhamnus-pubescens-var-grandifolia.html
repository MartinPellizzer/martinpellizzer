Rhamnus pubescens var. grandifolia

Rhamnus pubescens var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries that are attractive to birds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a somewhat aromatic scent.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
