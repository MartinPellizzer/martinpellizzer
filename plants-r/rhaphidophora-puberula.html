Rhaphidophora puberula

Rhaphidophora puberula, commonly known as the velvet leaf.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant features a compact growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by dark blue, spherical berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Rhaphidophora puberula is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
