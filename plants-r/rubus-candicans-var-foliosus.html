Rubus candicans var. foliosus

Rubus candicans var. foliosus, commonly known as the silverleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and broad, serrated leaves that often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and horticultural use.
