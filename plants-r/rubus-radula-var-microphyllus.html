Rubus radula var. microphyllus

Rubus radula var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf dewberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, elliptic leaves. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, edible berries in the summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
