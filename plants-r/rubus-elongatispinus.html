Rubus elongatispinus

Rubus elongatispinus, commonly known as the long-spined blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter flavor.
