Rubus persistens

Rubus persistens, commonly known as evergreen blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is known for its persistent foliage throughout the year.

Rubus persistens plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
