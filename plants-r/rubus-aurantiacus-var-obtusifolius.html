Rubus aurantiacus var. obtusifolius

Rubus aurantiacus var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the orange raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its distinctive orange-red fruits.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America. The leaves are typically broad and have a rounded shape, contributing to its unique appearance. This plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its fruit is not only visually appealing but also valued for its tart flavor and culinary uses.
