Rubus schleicheri subsp. sudetorum

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

sudetorum, commonly known as the Sudetic blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in acidic soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
