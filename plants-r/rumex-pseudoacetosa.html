Rumex pseudoacetosa

Rumex pseudoacetosa, commonly known as sourgrass, is a flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Rumex pseudoacetosa is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
