Rosa pouzinii intermedia

Rosa pouzinii intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Pouzinii rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Rosa pouzinii intermedia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in well-drained soils.
