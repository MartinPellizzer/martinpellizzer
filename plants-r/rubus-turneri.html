Rubus turneri

Rubus turneri, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a species of shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus turneri is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
