Restio elatus

Restio elatus, commonly known as the tall restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, often in open, sunny habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Restio elatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
