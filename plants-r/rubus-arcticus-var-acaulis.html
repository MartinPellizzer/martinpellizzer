Rubus arcticus var. acaulis

Rubus arcticus var. acaulis, commonly known as the Arctic bramble, is a low-growing shrub native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are adapted to cold climates. The plant produces white to pink flowers in the summer, followed by edible berries that are important for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a significant role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
