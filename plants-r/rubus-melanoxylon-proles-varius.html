Rubus melanoxylon proles varius

Rubus melanoxylon proles varius, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to various regions in the Americas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil.
