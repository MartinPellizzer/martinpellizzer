Rubus corymbosus

Rubus corymbosus, commonly known as blackberry bramble, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus corymbosus plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and ecosystems.
