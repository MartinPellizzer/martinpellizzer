Rubus hirtus var. cordifolius

Rubus hirtus var. cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
