Rubus discolor var. rhodanthus

Rubus discolor var. rhodanthus, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive red or pink fruit.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets that are often sweet and aromatic. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, making it a popular choice for gardens and small-scale cultivation.
