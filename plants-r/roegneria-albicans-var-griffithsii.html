Roegneria albicans var. griffithsii

Roegneria albicans var. griffithsii, commonly known as Griffiths' silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-white foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
