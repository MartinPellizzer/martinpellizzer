Rubus × nodiflorus

Rubus × nodiflorus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along fences.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
