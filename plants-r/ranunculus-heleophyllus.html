Ranunculus heleophyllus

Ranunculus heleophyllus, commonly known as the sun-leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three lobes, and the stems are hairy.

Ranunculus heleophyllus is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates.
