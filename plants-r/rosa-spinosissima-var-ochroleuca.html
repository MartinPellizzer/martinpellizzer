Rosa spinosissima var. ochroleuca

Rosa spinosissima var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white prickly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are compound, with ovate leaflets that have a slightly serrated edge. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
