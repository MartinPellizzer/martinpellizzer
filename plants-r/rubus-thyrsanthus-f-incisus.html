Rubus thyrsanthus f. incisus

Rubus thyrsanthus f. incisus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges that give it its common name. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

It is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
