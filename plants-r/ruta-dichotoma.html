Ruta dichotoma

Ruta dichotoma, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains volatile oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal and pesticidal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry environments.

Its historical use in herbal medicine dates back to ancient times.
