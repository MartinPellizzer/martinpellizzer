Raphanistrum segetum var. arvense

Raphanistrum segetum var. arvense, commonly known as wild radish, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing numerous seeds. It is native to Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, fields, and along roadsides.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
