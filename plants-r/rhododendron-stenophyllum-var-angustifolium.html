Rhododendron stenophyllum var. angustifolium

Rhododendron stenophyllum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alpine rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include dense foliage and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its alpine habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
