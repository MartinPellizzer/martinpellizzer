Rhyncholacis palmettifolia

Rhyncholacis palmettifolia, commonly known as the palm-leaf rhyncholacis, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palm-like leaves, which are broad and fan-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
