Rosa mollis f. ingestula

Rosa mollis f. ingestula, commonly known as the soft rose or a type of shrub rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. This plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The plant is relatively low-maintenance and can thrive in a variety of garden settings.
