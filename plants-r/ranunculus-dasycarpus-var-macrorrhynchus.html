Ranunculus dasycarpus var. macrorrhynchus

Ranunculus dasycarpus var. macrorrhynchus, commonly known as the broad-beaked buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and bright yellow flowers with five broad, rounded petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated seed pods that remain on the plant after flowering.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
