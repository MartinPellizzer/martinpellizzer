Rhododendron dalhousieae subsp. tashii

Rhododendron dalhousieae subsp.

tashii, commonly known as the Tashic rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments.

The plant features leathery, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of pink to red flowers in the spring. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens or conservation areas.
