Rondeletia tomentosa var. domingensis

Rondeletia tomentosa var. domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican velvetleaf, is a species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or purple. The plant is a shrub or small tree, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a velvety texture due to the dense pubescence.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
