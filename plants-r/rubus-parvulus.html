Rubus parvulus

Rubus parvulus, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in jams and pies. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
