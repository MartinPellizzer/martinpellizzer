Rosa tomentosa var. dacica

Rosa tomentosa var. dacica, commonly known as the Dacian woolly rose, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, greyish-white fuzzy foliage and clusters of fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange, containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
