Rubus cladocamptus

Rubus cladocamptus, commonly known as the New Zealand bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests and shrublands, and is well adapted to the temperate climate of its native region.

Due to its invasive nature, it can sometimes pose a challenge in ecological management.
