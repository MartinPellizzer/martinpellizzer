Rubus ictus

Rubus ictus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

Rubus ictus thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.
