Rubus javanicus

Rubus javanicus, commonly known as the Java raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines for their tart flavor.
