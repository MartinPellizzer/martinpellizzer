Rhipidocladum sibilans

Rhipidocladum sibilans, commonly known as the sibilant spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
