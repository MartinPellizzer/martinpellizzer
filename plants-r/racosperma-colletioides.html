Racosperma colletioides

Racosperma colletioides, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Racosperma colletioides is often used in ecological restoration due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
