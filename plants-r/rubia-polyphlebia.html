Rubia polyphlebia

Rubia polyphlebia, commonly known as the alpine bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia polyphlebia is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
