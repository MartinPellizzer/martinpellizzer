Ranunculus scotinus

Ranunculus scotinus, commonly known as the Scottish buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It is characterized by its short, stout stem and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils.

Ranunculus scotinus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.
