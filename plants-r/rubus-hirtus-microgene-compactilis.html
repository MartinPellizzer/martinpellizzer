Rubus hirtus microgene compactilis

Rubus hirtus microgene compactilis, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a small shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is often cultivated as a ornamental or for its edible berries in controlled environments.
