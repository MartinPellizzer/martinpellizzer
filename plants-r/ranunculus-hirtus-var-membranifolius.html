Ranunculus hirtus var. membranifolius

Ranunculus hirtus var. membranifolius, commonly known as the membranous-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The leaves are palmately divided and often have a membranous texture, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
