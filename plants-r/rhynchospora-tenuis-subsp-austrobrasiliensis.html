Rhynchospora tenuis subsp. austrobrasiliensis

Rhynchospora tenuis subsp.

austrobrasiliensis, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late summer.

The plant is native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Paraguay. It thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous growth system, which allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.
