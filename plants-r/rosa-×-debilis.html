Rosa × debilis

Rosa × debilis, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, with glossy, dark green leaves. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and an upright growth habit, making it suitable for hedges or ornamental planting.

Rosa × debilis is native to Europe and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
