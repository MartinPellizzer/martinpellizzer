Ranunculus spaniophyllus

Ranunculus spaniophyllus, commonly known as the Spanish leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinct central boss. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also notable for its toxic properties, containing protoanemonin, which can cause skin irritation.
