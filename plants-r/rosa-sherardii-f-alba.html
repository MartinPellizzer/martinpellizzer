Rosa sherardii f. alba

Rosa sherardii f. alba, commonly known as the white form of the Sherard's rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its pale pink to white blooms.

It is a cultivar of the species Rosa sherardii, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant typically features arching stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually borne singly or in small clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is valued for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.
