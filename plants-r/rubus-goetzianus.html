Rubus goetzianus

Rubus goetzianus, commonly known as the Goetz's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
