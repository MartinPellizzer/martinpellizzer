Rubus luzoniensis

Rubus luzoniensis, commonly known as Luzon bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Luzon. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruit, a type of aggregate berry, is edible and often used in local cuisine.

Rubus luzoniensis plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
