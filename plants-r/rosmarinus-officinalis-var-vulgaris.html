Rosmarinus officinalis var. vulgaris

Rosmarinus officinalis var. vulgaris, commonly known as rosemary, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, needle-like leaves and small blue or white flowers. The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils rich in compounds like cineole and camphor. Rosemary thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in culinary and medicinal applications.
