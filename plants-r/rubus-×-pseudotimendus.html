Rubus × pseudotimendus

Rubus × pseudotimendus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to parts of eastern North America and is known for its edible, sweet berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are white or pale green, appearing in clusters.

The species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
