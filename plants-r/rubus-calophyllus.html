Rubus calophyllus

Rubus calophyllus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark red to black when mature, with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rubus calophyllus is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.
