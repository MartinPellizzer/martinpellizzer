Rubus × exotericus

Rubus × exotericus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a species resulting from the crossbreeding of Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus gebleri (loganberry).

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are dark purple to black in color when ripe, and are prized for their intense flavor and high antioxidant content. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The hybrid is known for its hardiness and ability to adapt to a variety of soil conditions.
