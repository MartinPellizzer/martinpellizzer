Rhynchospora panduranganii

Rhynchospora panduranganii, commonly known as the Panduranganii tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Rhynchospora panduranganii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
