Rosa communis var. immitis

Rosa communis var. immitis, commonly known as the roughleaf rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa genus characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden borders and hedges.

Its foliage is notable for its rough, hairy texture, which gives it its common name.
