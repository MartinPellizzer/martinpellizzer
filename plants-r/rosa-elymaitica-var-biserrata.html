Rosa elymaitica var. biserrata

Rosa elymaitica var. biserrata, commonly known as the Elymian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring. The leaves are compound, consisting of several leaflets with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in rocky or arid environments. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.

The species is also noted for its historical use in traditional medicine.
