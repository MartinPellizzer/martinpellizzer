Rhus glabra var. borealis

Rhus glabra var. borealis, commonly known as northern smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
