Rapanea nigrescens

Rapanea nigrescens, commonly known as the black-leaved evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that turn almost black in color, giving it its common name. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters.

The flowers are small and clustered, and the fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.
