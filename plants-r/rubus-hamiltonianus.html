Rubus hamiltonianus

Rubus hamiltonianus, commonly known as Hamilton's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of small, dark red berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest margins and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
