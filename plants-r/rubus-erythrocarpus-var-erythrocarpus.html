Rubus erythrocarpus var. erythrocarpus

Rubus erythrocarpus var. erythrocarpus, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark red to black in color, with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid climate for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in both horticulture and local cuisines.
