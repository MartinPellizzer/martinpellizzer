Rhagadiolus stellatus var. indivisus

Rhagadiolus stellatus var. indivisus, commonly known as the star-shaped rhagadiolus, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-like floral structure, which consists of multiple radiating petals. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are often small and succulent, aiding in water retention.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
