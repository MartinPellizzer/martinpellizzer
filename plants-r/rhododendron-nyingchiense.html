Rhododendron nyingchiense

Rhododendron nyingchiense, commonly known as the Nyingchi rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of China and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a reddish or purple hue on the underside. Its flowers are clustered in trusses and are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
