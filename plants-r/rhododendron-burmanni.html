Rhododendron burmanni

Rhododendron burmanni, commonly known as the Burmann's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, preferring well-drained acidic soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, bloom in spring and are often fragrant.

Rhododendron burmanni is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
