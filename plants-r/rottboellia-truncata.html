Rottboellia truncata

Rottboellia truncata, commonly known as red grass or false panicum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish in color, especially when young. The plant produces small, pale green to reddish spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Rottboellia truncata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its distinctive appearance.
