Rubus altisilvaticus

Rubus altisilvaticus, commonly known as the high forest raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in woodland and forested areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
