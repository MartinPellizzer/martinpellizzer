Rubus ulmifolius proles disjunctifolius

Rubus ulmifolius proles disjunctifolius, commonly known as the disjunct-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry with distinctive leaf morphology.

It is characterized by its serrated, trifoliate leaves that exhibit a unique disjunctive pattern, setting it apart from typical Rubus species. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple or black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its morphological variation and potential role in plant systematics.
