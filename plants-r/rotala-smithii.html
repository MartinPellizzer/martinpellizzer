Rotala smithii

Rotala smithii, commonly known as the red root water plant, is a floating aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, bright green leaves that are often tinged with red or pink, especially in shaded conditions. The plant produces tiny white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquariums for its ornamental value.

Rotala smithii is also valued for its ability to help oxygenate water and provide habitat for aquatic life.
