Ranunculus muricatus var. carolinus

Ranunculus muricatus var. carolinus, commonly known as the Carolina buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5-9 sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a fibrous root system and hairy stems, and its leaves are palmately divided with a rough texture.

It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.
