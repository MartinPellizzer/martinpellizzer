Rubus radula genuinus

Rubus radula genuinus, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red or black berries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
