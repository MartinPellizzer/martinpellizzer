Reseda lutea var. maritima

Reseda lutea var. maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and silver-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and rocky shores. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Reseda lutea var. maritima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
