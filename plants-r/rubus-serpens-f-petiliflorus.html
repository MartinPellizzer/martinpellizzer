Rubus serpens f. petiliflorus

Rubus serpens f. petiliflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its smaller, more delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodland areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the fruit is a small, dark berry.

This form is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties but is valued for its unique floral and fruit characteristics.
