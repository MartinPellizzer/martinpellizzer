Rubus mulleri var. obcuneatus

Rubus mulleri var. obcuneatus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a deep red color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Its berries are edible, though they are typically smaller and less sweet compared to other blackberry varieties.
