Rumex salicifolius var. triangulivalvis

Rumex salicifolius var. triangulivalvis, commonly known as the triangular-leaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, triangular leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and a serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on erect, branched stems. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses and has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
