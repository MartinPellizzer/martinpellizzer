Rinorea palaucica

Rinorea palaucica, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as bloom. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rinorea palaucica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in garden landscapes for its unique floral display.
