Rubus caesius subsp. raduloides

Rubus caesius subsp.

raduloides, commonly known as the redcurrant raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, with a tart flavor.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in culinary applications.
