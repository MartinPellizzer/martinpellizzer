Rubus sons

Rubus sons, commonly known as the blackberry plant, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet-tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are typically white or pink. Rubus sons thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
