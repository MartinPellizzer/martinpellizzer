Racosperma kettlewelliae

Racosperma kettlewelliae, commonly known as the Kettlewell's acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in spikes. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
