Ruellia argentea

Ruellia argentea, commonly known as silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a waxy texture that contributes to its distinctive appearance.

Ruellia argentea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
