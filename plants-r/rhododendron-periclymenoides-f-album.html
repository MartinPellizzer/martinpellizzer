Rhododendron periclymenoides f. album

Rhododendron periclymenoides f. album, commonly known as the White Veitchia, is a flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are white to pale pink in color, often with a slight pinkish hue at the base. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in woodland gardens.
