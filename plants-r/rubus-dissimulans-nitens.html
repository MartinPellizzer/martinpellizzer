Rubus dissimulans nitens

Rubus dissimulans nitens, commonly known as the shining blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
