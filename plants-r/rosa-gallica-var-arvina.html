Rosa gallica var. arvina

Rosa gallica var. arvina, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
