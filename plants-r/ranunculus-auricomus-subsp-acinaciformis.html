Ranunculus auricomus subsp. acinaciformis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

acinaciformis, commonly known as the swordleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, often with a distinctive yellowish or white marginal stripe.

The flowers are typically yellow with a prominent white or pale yellow center, and they are borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant is characterized by its attractive foliage and showy flowers, making it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
