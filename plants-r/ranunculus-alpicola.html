Ranunculus alpicola

Ranunculus alpicola, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or white petals and bright green, palmate leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early summer, adding vibrant color to its harsh environment.

Ranunculus alpicola is often found in mountainous regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.
