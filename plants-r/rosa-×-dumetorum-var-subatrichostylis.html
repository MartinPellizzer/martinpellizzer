Rosa × dumetorum var. subatrichostylis

Rosa × dumetorum var. subatrichostylis, commonly known as the hairy-styled shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and the plant produces oval, reddish fruits known as hips. This variety thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows or woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a hardy ornamental plant with potential ecological benefits.
