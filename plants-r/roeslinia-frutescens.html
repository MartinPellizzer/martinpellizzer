Roeslinia frutescens

Roeslinia frutescens, commonly known as the common roeslinia, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale yellow during the spring. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Roeslinia frutescens is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, and it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
