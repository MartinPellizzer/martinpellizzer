Rubus tereticaulis f. cyclophyllus

Rubus tereticaulis f. cyclophyllus, commonly known as the cyclophyllus raspberry, is a subspecies of the trailing raspberry.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing growth habit and small, elliptical leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals, and the fruit is a red, aggregate berry. This plant is native to parts of North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
