Ribes menziesii var. minus

Ribes menziesii var. minus, commonly known as the small-flowered currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. Its berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in jams and jellies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
