Rhynchosia amatymbica

Rhynchosia amatymbica, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
