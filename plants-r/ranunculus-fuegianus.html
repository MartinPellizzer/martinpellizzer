Ranunculus fuegianus

Ranunculus fuegianus, commonly known as the Fuegian buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often bright-colored flowers. The plant typically features delicate, palmately divided leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some environments.
