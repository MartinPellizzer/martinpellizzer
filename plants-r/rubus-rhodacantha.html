Rubus rhodacantha

Rubus rhodacantha, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

The flowers are white and clustered, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
