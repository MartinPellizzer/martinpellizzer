Rubus pullatifolius

Rubus pullatifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters.

Rubus pullatifolius thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.
