Rosa micrantha f. salvifolia

Rosa micrantha f. salvifolia, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and elliptical leaves with serrated edges, typical of many rose species. Its small, red or orange hips appear in the fall and are an important food source for wildlife.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in woodland gardens.
