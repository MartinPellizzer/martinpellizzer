Rhynchospora tenuis f. minor

Rhynchospora tenuis f. minor, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in areas with consistent moisture. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

This fern-like plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
