Ribes luridum

Ribes luridum, commonly known as the yellow currant, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and forest edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellow berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins.

Ribes luridum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
