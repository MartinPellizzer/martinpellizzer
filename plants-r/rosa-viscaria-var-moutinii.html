Rosa viscaria var. moutinii

Rosa viscaria var. moutinii, commonly known as the Moutinii rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves with a glossy surface, and its flowers are often borne in terminal clusters. It is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in gardens with suitable conditions.
