Ranunculus saruwagedicus

Ranunculus saruwagedicus, commonly known as the Saruwagedi buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces delicate, five-petaled blooms in shades of yellow and white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
