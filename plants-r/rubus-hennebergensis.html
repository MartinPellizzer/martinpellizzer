Rubus hennebergensis

Rubus hennebergensis, commonly known as the Henneberg's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and South Carolina. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are typically red when mature and are edible.

The botanical features of Rubus hennebergensis make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
