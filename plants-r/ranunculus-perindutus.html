Ranunculus perindutus

Ranunculus perindutus, commonly known as the Perindutus buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers with prominent yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the species is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Ranunculus perindutus is also noted for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
