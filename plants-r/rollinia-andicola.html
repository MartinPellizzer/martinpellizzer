Rollinia andicola

Rollinia andicola, commonly known as the Andean rollinia, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellow flowers. The fruit is a large, spiny capsule that contains several edible seeds, often used in local cuisines.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
