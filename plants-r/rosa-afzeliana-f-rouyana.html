Rosa afzeliana f. rouyana

Rosa afzeliana f. rouyana, commonly known as the Rouyana rose, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, pinnate leaves, and an inflorescence of clustered flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
