Ranunculus gracilis

Ranunculus gracilis, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus gracilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with damp soil conditions.
