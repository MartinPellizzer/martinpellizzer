Rhododendron brookeanum var. cladotrichum

Rhododendron brookeanum var. cladotrichum, commonly known as the Kumaon rhododendron, is a unique variant of the rhododendron genus native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils and is often found in montane forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its botanical features include thick, glandular leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its alpine environment.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
