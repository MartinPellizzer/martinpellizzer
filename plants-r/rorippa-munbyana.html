Rorippa munbyana

Rorippa munbyana, commonly known as the Munby's water-cress, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are alternate along the stem. It produces tiny, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Rorippa munbyana is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is often found in regions with seasonal flooding.
