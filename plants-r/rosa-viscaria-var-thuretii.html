Rosa viscaria var. thuretii

Rosa viscaria var. thuretii, commonly known as the Thuret's rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne singly or in clusters. It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a resilient species for alpine environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
