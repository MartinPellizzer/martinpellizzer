Rubus odoratus var. albidus

Rubus odoratus var. albidus, commonly known as the white-flowered raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its pale pink to white flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically less tart than those of other raspberry varieties. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate floral display and is often cultivated in gardens for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
