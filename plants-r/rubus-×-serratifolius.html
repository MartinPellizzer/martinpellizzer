Rubus × serratifolius

Rubus × serratifolius, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bush, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable for fruit production.
