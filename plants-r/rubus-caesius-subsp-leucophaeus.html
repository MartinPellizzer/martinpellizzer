Rubus caesius subsp. leucophaeus

Rubus caesius subsp.

leucophaeus, commonly known as the white-leafed blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically pale green to white in color.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a dark purple to black berry. This subspecies is often found in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
