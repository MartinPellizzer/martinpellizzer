Rosa elliptica subvar. jordanii

Rosa elliptica subvar. jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and elliptical, glossy green leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer. It produces oval, woody fruits called hips, which contain seeds and are often used in traditional medicine.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to dry, rocky environments.
