Romulea thodei

Romulea thodei, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a subterranean bulb that stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
