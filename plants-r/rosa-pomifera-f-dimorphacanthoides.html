Rosa pomifera f. dimorphacanthoides

Rosa pomifera f. dimorphacanthoides, commonly known as the two-armed bract Rosa, is a unique horticultural form of the rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive bract morphology, with two different types of bracts on the same inflorescence. The plant produces large, fuzzy, rose-like fruits known as rose hips, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical gardens.
