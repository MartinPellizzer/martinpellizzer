Ribes giraldii var. cuneatum

Ribes giraldii var. cuneatum, commonly known as the Cuneatum gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching stems that bear alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, tart berries in late summer. Its leaves and flowers are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and fruit.
