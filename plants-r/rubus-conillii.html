Rubus conillii

Rubus conillii, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
