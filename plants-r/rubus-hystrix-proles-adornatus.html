Rubus hystrix proles adornatus

Rubus hystrix proles adornatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
