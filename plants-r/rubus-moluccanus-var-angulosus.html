Rubus moluccanus var. angulosus

Rubus moluccanus var. angulosus, commonly known as the angular blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
