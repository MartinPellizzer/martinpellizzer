Rubus inopertus var. echinocalyx

Rubus inopertus var. echinocalyx, commonly known as the rough-fruited blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are valued for their flavor and are often used in jams and desserts.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
