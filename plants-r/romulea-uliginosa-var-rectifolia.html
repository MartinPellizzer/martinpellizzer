Romulea uliginosa var. rectifolia

Romulea uliginosa var. rectifolia, commonly known as the bog romulea, is a perennial geophyte native to southern Africa.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color, with a distinctive, spurred petal. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs or wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like and basal, forming a rosette that helps retain moisture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
