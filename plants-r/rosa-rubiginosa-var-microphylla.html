Rosa rubiginosa var. microphylla

Rosa rubiginosa var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf shrub rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated plantings.
