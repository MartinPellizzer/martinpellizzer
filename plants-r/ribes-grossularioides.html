Ribes grossularioides

Ribes grossularioides, commonly known as the red currant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants in the family Grossulariaceae. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with multiple stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or white, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
