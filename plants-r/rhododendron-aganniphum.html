Rhododendron aganniphum

Rhododendron aganniphum, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

Rhododendron aganniphum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
