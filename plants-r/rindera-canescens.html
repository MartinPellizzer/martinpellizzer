Rindera canescens

Rindera canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Rindera canescens is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
