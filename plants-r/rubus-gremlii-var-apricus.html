Rubus gremlii var. apricus

Rubus gremlii var. apricus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and for ecological studies.
