Rhamnus × mehreganii

Rhamnus × mehreganii, commonly known as Mehreganii buckthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhamnus alnifolia and Rhamnus lutea.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
