Regelia cruenta

Regelia cruenta, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark red, blood-like sap, which gives it its common name. The plant features a rosette of thick, waxy leaves that are often tinged with purple or red. Its flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Regelia cruenta is typically cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
