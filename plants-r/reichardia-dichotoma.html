Reichardia dichotoma

Reichardia dichotoma, commonly known as the forked reichardia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features alternate, linear leaves that are often divided into two or more lobes, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

Reichardia dichotoma is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America and is often found in disturbed soils and rocky habitats.
