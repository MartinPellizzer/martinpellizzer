Rubus oblongifolius

Rubus oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
