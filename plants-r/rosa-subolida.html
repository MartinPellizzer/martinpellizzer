Rosa subolida

Rosa subolida, commonly known as the slender rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Argentina, Bolivia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated at the edges.

Rosa subolida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types when given adequate sunlight.
