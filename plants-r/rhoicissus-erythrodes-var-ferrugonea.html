Rhoicissus erythrodes var. ferrugonea

Rhoicissus erythrodes var. ferrugonea, commonly known as the rusty-leaf greenbrier, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with a distinctive rusty-colored underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
