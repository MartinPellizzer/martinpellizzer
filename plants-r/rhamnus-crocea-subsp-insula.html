Rhamnus crocea subsp. insula

Rhamnus crocea subsp.

insula, commonly known as the island buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
