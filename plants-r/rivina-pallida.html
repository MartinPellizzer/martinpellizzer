Rivina pallida

Rivina pallida, commonly known as pale rivina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or pale red, with five petals and a prominent central stigma.

Rivina pallida is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
