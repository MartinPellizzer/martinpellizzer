Rubus silvaticus microgene amphichlous

Rubus silvaticus microgene amphichlous, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and they bloom in early summer.

The species is characterized by its ability to tolerate harsh climates and its role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
