Ribes uva-crispa subsp. reclinatum

Ribes uva-crispa subsp.

reclinatum, commonly known as the creeping gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and woodland edges.
