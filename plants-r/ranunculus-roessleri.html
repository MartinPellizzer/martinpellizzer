Ranunculus roessleri

Ranunculus roessleri, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive black center. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps protect it from harsh weather conditions.

Ranunculus roessleri is a perennial species, often found in high-altitude habitats where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
