Rudbeckia laevis

Rudbeckia laevis, commonly known as the smooth coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the United States.

It features showy yellow to orange daisy-like flowers with a distinctive, smooth, conical center. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Rudbeckia laevis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
