Rytidosperma paschale

Rytidosperma paschale, commonly known as the Paschal grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Rytidosperma paschale is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting well to poor soil conditions.
