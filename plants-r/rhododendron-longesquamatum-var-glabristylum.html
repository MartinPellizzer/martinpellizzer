Rhododendron longesquamatum var. glabristylum

Rhododendron longesquamatum var. glabristylum, commonly known as the glabrous-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically smooth and glabrous, which distinguishes it from other varieties in the species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
