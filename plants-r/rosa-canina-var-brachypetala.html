Rosa canina var. brachypetala

Rosa canina var. brachypetala, commonly known as the short-stemmed dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

This variety is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.
