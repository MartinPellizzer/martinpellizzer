Rosa pulchella

Rosa pulchella, commonly known as the beauty rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist woodlands and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa pulchella is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
