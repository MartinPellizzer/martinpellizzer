Rubia cretica

Rubia cretica, commonly known as lesser cow-wheat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, often found in grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

Rubia cretica is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
