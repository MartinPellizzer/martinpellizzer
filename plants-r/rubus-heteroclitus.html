Rubus heteroclitus

Rubus heteroclitus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

The fruit is widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.
