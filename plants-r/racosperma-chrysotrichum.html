Racosperma chrysotrichum

Racosperma chrysotrichum, commonly known as the golden-haired acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
