Rhododendron rhombicum var. albiflorum

Rhododendron rhombicum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the Whiteflower Rhombic Rhododendron, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its broad, rhombic-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. It is valued for its unique foliage and delicate floral display, making it a sought-after species in botanical gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
