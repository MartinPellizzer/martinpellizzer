Rosa rhodanii

Rosa rhodanii, commonly known as the Syrian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe, particularly Syria and Turkey. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa rhodanii is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
