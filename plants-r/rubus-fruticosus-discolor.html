Rubus fruticosus discolor

Rubus fruticosus discolor, commonly known as the common bramble, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
