Rhaphiostylis ferruginea var. villosa

Rhaphiostylis ferruginea var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy rhaphiostylis, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright yellow spathes and small white flowers, typical of the Araceae family. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
