Rorippa chubutica

Rorippa chubutica, commonly known as the Chubut watercress, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, edible leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found near streams or in wetlands.

Due to its nutritional value and unique habitat, it is of interest to both botanists and local communities.
