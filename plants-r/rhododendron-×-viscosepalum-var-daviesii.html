Rhododendron × viscosepalum var. daviesii

Rhododendron × viscosepalum var. daviesii, commonly known as the Davies' rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron viscosum and Rhododendron sargentii.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China and Korea.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant blooms.
