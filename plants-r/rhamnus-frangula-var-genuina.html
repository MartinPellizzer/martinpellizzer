Rhamnus frangula var. genuina

Rhamnus frangula var. genuina, commonly known as the European buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is known for its smooth, dark bark and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and along roadsides.

The berries it produces are toxic to humans and animals, making it important to handle with care.
