Rosa × malmundariensis

Rosa × malmundariensis, commonly known as the Malm's rose, is a hybrid rose species that originated from crosses between Rosa gallica and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive, slightly twisted appearance. The plant is deciduous, featuring thorny stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its historical use in perfumery and as a parent in many modern rose varieties.
