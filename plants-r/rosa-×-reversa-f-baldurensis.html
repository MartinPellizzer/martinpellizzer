Rosa × reversa f. baldurensis

Rosa × reversa f. baldurensis, commonly known as the Baldur's rose, is a unique hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa × sinensis.

It is characterized by its double, semi-cupped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, depending on the specific cultivar. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a notable example of horticultural hybridization in the rose family.
