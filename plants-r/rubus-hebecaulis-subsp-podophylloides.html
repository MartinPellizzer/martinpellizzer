Rubus hebecaulis subsp. podophylloides

Rubus hebecaulis subsp.

podophylloides, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, dark red to black berry, which is edible but often described as having a tart flavor.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
