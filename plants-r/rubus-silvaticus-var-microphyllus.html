Rubus silvaticus var. microphyllus

Rubus silvaticus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf bramble, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries, which are usually red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
