Rebutia wahliana

Rebutia wahliana, commonly known as the Desert Rose or Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. The plant features a unique appearance with a colorful, ornamental top that sits atop a green, leaf-like base. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Rebutia wahliana is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
