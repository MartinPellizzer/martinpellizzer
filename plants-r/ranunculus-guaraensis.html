Ranunculus guaraensis

Ranunculus guaraensis, commonly known as the Guarani buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

The flowers range in color from white to yellow and are often accompanied by a distinctive yellow or greenish center.
