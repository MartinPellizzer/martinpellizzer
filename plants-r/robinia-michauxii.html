Robinia michauxii

Robinia michauxii, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and supports nutrient cycling in the ecosystem. Its hard, durable wood has historically been used for furniture and construction.

Robinia michauxii is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
