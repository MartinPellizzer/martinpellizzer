Rhododendron cremnophilum

Rhododendron cremnophilum, commonly known as the cliff rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and cliffs, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a resilient species in its natural habitat.

Rhododendron cremnophilum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
