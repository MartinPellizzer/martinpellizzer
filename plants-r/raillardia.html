Raillardia

Raillardia, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to Central America and are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Raillardia species typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce numerous small, tubular florets arranged in a composite flower head. They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil.
