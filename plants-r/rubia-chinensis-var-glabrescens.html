Rubia chinensis var. glabrescens

Rubia chinensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as Chinese madder, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots contain a red dye called alizarin, which has been historically used in textile dyeing.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
