Rosa agrestis subvar. longipes

Rosa agrestis subvar. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed wild rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant produces oval, red-orange fruits called hips, which are edible and often used in herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural habitats.
