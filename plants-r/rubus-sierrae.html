Rubus sierrae

Rubus sierrae, commonly known as Sierra blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from a cluster of small drupes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
