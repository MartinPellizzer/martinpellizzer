Rosa canina subvar. insignis

Rosa canina subvar. insignis, commonly known as the yellow rose of China, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
