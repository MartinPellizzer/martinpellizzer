Ranunculus ferdinandi

Ranunculus ferdinandi, commonly known as the Ferdinand's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive pattern of darker veins. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

Ranunculus ferdinandi is also notable for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional remedies.
