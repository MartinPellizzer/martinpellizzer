Rhododendron indicum var. pulchrum

Rhododendron indicum var. pulchrum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
