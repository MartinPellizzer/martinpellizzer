Rubus × mostarensis f. superulmifolius

Rubus × mostarensis f. superulmifolius, commonly known as the giant blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries, which are typically larger than those of the standard Rubus × mostarensis. It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, known for its vigorous growth and thorny stems. The leaves are broad and lobed, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
