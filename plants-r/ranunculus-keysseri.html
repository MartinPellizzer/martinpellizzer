Ranunculus keysseri

Ranunculus keysseri, commonly known as the Keyser's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers usually have five to eight petals, often in shades of yellow or orange.

It is a perennial species that produces a capsule fruit containing numerous seeds.
