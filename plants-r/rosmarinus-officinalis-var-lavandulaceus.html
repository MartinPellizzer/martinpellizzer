Rosmarinus officinalis var. lavandulaceus

Rosmarinus officinalis var. lavandulaceus, commonly known as lavender rosemary, is a hybrid plant that combines characteristics of both lavender and rosemary.

It is a shrub with aromatic, needle-like leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers, which are typically harvested for their essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its unique scent and versatility in use make it a popular choice in gardens and aromatherapy.
