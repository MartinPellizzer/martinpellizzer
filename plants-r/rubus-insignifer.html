Rubus insignifer

Rubus insignifer, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and pies.

Botanically, Rubus insignifer belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
