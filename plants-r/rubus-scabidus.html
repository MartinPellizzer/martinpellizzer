Rubus scabidus

Rubus scabidus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other species of shrubs commonly referred to as raspberries or blackberries.
