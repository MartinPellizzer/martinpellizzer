Rhysolepis australis

Rhysolepis australis, commonly known as the Antarctic hair grass, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of southern South America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in harsh, alpine environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help protect it from freezing temperatures.

Rhysolepis australis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
