Rhipsalis macropogon

Rhipsalis macropogon, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and segmented growth habit. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and can vary in size depending on growing conditions.

Rhipsalis macropogon thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
