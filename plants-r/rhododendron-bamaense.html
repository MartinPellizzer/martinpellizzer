Rhododendron bamaense

Rhododendron bamaense, commonly known as the Bama rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blossoms.

Rhododendron bamaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
