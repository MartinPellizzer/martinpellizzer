Rhynchospora subplumosa

Rhynchospora subplumosa, commonly known as the silky flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and other moist environments, where it thrives in shallow water or saturated soil. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters near the base of the stem. Its inflorescence is often surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive, flattened appearance.

Rhynchospora subplumosa plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
