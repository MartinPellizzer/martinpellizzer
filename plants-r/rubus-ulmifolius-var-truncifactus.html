Rubus ulmifolius var. truncifactus

Rubus ulmifolius var. truncifactus, commonly known as the truncated blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape, which is truncate at the base, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
