Ranunculus nankotaizanus

Ranunculus nankotaizanus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with prominent stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
