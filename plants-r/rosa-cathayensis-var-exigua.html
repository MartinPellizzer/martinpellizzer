Rosa cathayensis var. exigua

Rosa cathayensis var. exigua, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a dense, thorny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its persistent red hips that appear in autumn.
