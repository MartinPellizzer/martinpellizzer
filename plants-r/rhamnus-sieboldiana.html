Rhamnus sieboldiana

Rhamnus sieboldiana, commonly known as Japanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
