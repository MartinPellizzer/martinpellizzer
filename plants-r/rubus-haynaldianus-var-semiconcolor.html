Rubus haynaldianus var. semiconcolor

Rubus haynaldianus var. semiconcolor, commonly known as the pink-berried blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to pink, depending on maturity. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique fruit coloration.
