Randia densiflora var. parvifolia

Randia densiflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruit is a small, smooth, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
