Rhododendron stenoplastum

Rhododendron stenoplastum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a distinctive appearance.

Rhododendron stenoplastum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland landscapes.
