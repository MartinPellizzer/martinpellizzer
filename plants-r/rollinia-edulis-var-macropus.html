Rollinia edulis var. macropus

Rollinia edulis var. macropus, commonly known as the Brazilian pawpaw, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-colored fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, creamy flesh that has a flavor similar to papaya.

It is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses in regional cuisines.
