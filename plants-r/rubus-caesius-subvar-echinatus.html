Rubus caesius subvar. echinatus

Rubus caesius subvar. echinatus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive fruit structure and adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
