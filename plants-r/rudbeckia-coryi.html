Rudbeckia coryi

Rudbeckia coryi, commonly known as the Sierra foothill coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, daisy-like yellow to orange flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Rudbeckia coryi thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native plant gardens.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value.
