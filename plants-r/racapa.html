Racapa

Racapa, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has strap-like, grassy foliage that emerges from a bulbous underground stem. Racapa thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in native habitats.
