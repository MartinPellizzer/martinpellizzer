Rosa zuccariniana

Rosa zuccariniana, commonly known as the Zuccarinian rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of fragrant, single or semi-double flowers. The plant typically features glossy, dark green foliage and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
