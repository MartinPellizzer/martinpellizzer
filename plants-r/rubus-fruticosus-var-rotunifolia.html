Rubus fruticosus var. rotunifolia

Rubus fruticosus var. rotunifolia, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit ripens to a dark purple or black color.

This plant is often cultivated for its edible berries and is also used in traditional medicine.
