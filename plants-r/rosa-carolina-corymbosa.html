Rosa carolina corymbosa

Rosa carolina corymbosa, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, blooming in the spring. The plant produces hips, which are the fruit, containing seeds encased in a fuzzy, orange-colored covering. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The Carolina rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
