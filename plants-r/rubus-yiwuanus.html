Rubus yiwuanus

Rubus yiwuanus, commonly known as the Yiwuan blackberry, is a species of brambles native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges.

The fruit, which is a dark purple to black berry, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.
