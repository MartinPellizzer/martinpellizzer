Rubus amplifrons var. angustifrons

Rubus amplifrons var. angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
