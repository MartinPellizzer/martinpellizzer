Rubus mulleri var. hanovrensis

Rubus mulleri var. hanovrensis, commonly known as the Hanover raspberry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white flowers in the spring.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
