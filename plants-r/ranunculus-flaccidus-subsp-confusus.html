Ranunculus flaccidus subsp. confusus

Ranunculus flaccidus subsp.

confusus, commonly known as the western marsh buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
