Romulea tortuosa subsp. aurea

Romulea tortuosa subsp.

aurea, commonly known as the golden romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a distinctive spiral arrangement of petals.

The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a corm-like underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
