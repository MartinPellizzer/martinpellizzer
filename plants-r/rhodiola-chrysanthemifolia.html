Rhodiola chrysanthemifolia

Rhodiola chrysanthemifolia, commonly known as the goldenflower rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, which helps in storing water, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Rhodiola chrysanthemifolia is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported adaptogenic and tonic effects.
