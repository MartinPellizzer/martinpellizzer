Rosa tomentella f. subaffinis

Rosa tomentella f. subaffinis, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide it with a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a soft, fuzzy texture, and the plant is adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments.

Rosa tomentella f. subaffinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
