Rubus occitanicus

Rubus occitanicus, commonly known as the Corsican blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a high tannin content. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in shrublands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
