Rhododendron minyaense

Rhododendron minyaense, commonly known as the Minya rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and India.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in mountainous forests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron minyaense plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting local biodiversity and contributing to the region's unique floral diversity.
