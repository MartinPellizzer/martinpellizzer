Roegneria canina subsp. sirouana

Roegneria canina subsp.

sirouana, commonly known as the Sirouan grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces spikelets that are arranged in panicles. Its leaves are linear and usually have a bluish-green color, and it is characterized by its fine, delicate appearance. Roegneria canina subsp.

sirouana is often found in moist or well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
