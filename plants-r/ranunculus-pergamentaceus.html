Ranunculus pergamentaceus

Ranunculus pergamentaceus, commonly known as the parchmentleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often deeply lobed and covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are typically solitary, with five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus pergamentaceus thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
