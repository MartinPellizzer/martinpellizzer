Rubia komarovii

Rubia komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's bugleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, and it produces small, black berries that are edible.

Rubia komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
