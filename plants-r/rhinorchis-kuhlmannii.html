Rhinorchis kuhlmannii

Rhinorchis kuhlmannii, commonly known as the Kuhlmann's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a single, showy flower with a distinctive labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Rhinorchis kuhlmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and rarity.
