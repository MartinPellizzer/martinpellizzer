Raphia lyciosa

Raphia lyciosa, commonly known as the giant palm or African fan palm, is a large, tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in diameter. The plant is a member of the Arecaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are used for thatching and roofing, while the fruit produces edible seeds.

Raphia lyciosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
