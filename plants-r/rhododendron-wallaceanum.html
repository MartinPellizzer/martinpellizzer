Rhododendron wallaceanum

Rhododendron wallaceanum, commonly known as the Wallacean rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Indonesia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, humid climates at high altitudes. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, leathery berries. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and elliptical in shape, providing dense foliage.

Rhododendron wallaceanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
