Rubus × semiprocerus

Rubus × semiprocerus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental landscaping and small-scale cultivation.
