Rosa attenuata

Rosa attenuata, commonly known as the shrub rose or evergreen rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Rosa attenuata is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
