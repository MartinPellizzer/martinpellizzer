Ranunculus berteroanus

Ranunculus berteroanus, commonly known as the Bertero's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
