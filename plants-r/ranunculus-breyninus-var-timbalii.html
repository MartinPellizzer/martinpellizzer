Ranunculus breyninus var. timbalii

Ranunculus breyninus var. timbalii, commonly known as the Timbalai buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent black centers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
