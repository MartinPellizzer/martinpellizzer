Rosa reuteri f. subcollina

Rosa reuteri f. subcollina, commonly known as the subalpine rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage, with small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it withstand cold and arid conditions. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
