Rivea cordata

Rivea cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
