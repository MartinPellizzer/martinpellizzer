Rubus norvegicus

Rubus norvegicus, commonly known as the Norwegian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to northern Europe.

It is a shrub that produces edible red berries, which are rich in antioxidants and flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. Its fruiting season typically occurs in late summer, and the berries are often used in jams and desserts.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
