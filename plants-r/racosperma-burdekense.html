Racosperma burdekense

Racosperma burdekense, commonly known as the Burdekin legume, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Racosperma burdekense is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
