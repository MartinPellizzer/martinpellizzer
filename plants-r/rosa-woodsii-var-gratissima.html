Rosa woodsii var. gratissima

Rosa woodsii var. gratissima, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a compact shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a landscape plant.
