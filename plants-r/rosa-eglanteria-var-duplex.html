Rosa eglanteria var. duplex

Rosa eglanteria var. duplex, commonly known as the double-leaf brambles, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into three to five leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are thorny and often grow along the ground, forming dense mats.

Rosa eglanteria var. duplex is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
