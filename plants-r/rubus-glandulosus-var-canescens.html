Rubus glandulosus var. canescens

Rubus glandulosus var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with glandular dots. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red, raspberry-like fruits in the summer. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, often forming dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and wildlife habitat.
