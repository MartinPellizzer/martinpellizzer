Rhododendron ledifolium var. album

Rhododendron ledifolium var. album, commonly known as the white tephrosia rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
