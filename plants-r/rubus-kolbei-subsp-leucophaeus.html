Rubus kolbei subsp. leucophaeus

Rubus kolbei subsp.

leucophaeus, commonly known as the white-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
