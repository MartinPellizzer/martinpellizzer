Rubus caesius var. glabratus

Rubus caesius var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, distinguishing it from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and local agriculture.
