Rhodoscirpus asper

Rhodoscirpus asper, commonly known as the rough rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a rough texture, which gives it its scientific name.

Rhodoscirpus asper is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
