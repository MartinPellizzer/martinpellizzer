Rhododendron tatsienense var. nudatum

Rhododendron tatsienense var. nudatum, commonly known as the naked-stem rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan and Yunnan in China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often arranged in whorls around the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
