Romulea macowanii var. oreophila

Romulea macowanii var. oreophila, commonly known as the mountain romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of vivid orange or red flowers. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
