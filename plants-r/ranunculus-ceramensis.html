Ranunculus ceramensis

Ranunculus ceramensis, commonly known as the Ceram buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the island of Ceram in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many species of buttercups and water plants. The plant typically features bright yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique habitat and its role in the local ecosystem.
