Rubus erythroxanthus var. immerens

Rubus erythroxanthus var. immerens, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a species of shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The variety immerens is distinguished by its dense, dark foliage and its potential use in ornamental horticulture.
