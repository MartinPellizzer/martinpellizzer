Rubus thelybatoides

Rubus thelybatoides, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a tart, slightly sweet flavor.
