Rosa × spinulifolia var. hispidella

Rosa × spinulifolia var. hispidella, commonly known as the hispid rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, often prickly foliage, which contribute to its rugged appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture, giving it a distinctive botanical profile.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardiness and visual appeal.
