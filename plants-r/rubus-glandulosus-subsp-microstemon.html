Rubus glandulosus subsp. microstemon

Rubus glandulosus subsp.

microstemon, commonly known as the small-stamen raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers and berries.
