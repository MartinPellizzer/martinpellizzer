Rauvolfia laxiflora

Rauvolfia laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered rainforest vine, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Rauvolfia laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
