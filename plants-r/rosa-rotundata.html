Rosa rotundata

Rosa rotundata, commonly known as the roundleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its rounded, leathery leaves and fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. Rosa rotundata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
