Rubus libertianus

Rubus libertianus, commonly known as the Libertian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
