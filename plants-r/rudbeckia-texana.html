Rudbeckia texana

Rudbeckia texana, commonly known as Texas coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by dark green, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces elongated, spiny seed pods that release seeds when mature.

Rudbeckia texana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
