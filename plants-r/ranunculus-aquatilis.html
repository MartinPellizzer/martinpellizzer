Ranunculus aquatilis

Ranunculus aquatilis, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, floating leaves and small yellow flowers.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and marshes. The plant has slender, branching stems that support its feather-like leaves, which are arranged in a whorled pattern. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and bloom in early spring.

Ranunculus aquatilis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
