Rhododendron atlanticum f. confusum

Rhododendron atlanticum f. confusum, commonly known as the Atlantic rhododendron form confusum, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early to mid-spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This form is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to moist, cool climates.
