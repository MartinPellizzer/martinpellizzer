Rosa calyptocalyx

Rosa calyptocalyx, commonly known as the Chinese rose or evergreen rose, is a species native to China and Taiwan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include calypterae—small, leaf-like structures that cover the base of the flower buds.

Rosa calyptocalyx is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
