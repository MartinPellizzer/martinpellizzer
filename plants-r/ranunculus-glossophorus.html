Ranunculus glossophorus

Ranunculus glossophorus, commonly known as the honeycomb buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant features a distinctive, hairy stem with a rough texture and produces clusters of yellow flowers with prominent, rounded sepals. Its leaves are typically palmately divided, with a lobed appearance that adds to its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
