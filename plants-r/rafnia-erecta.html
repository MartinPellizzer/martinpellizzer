Rafnia erecta

Rafnia erecta, commonly known as the erect Rafnia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and have a rough texture, while its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule.

Rafnia erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
