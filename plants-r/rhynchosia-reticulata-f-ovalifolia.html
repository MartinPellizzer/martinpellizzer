Rhynchosia reticulata f. ovalifolia

Rhynchosia reticulata f. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leafed rhynchosia, is a variant of the species Rhynchosia reticulata, which belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
