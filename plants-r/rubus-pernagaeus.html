Rubus pernagaeus

Rubus pernagaeus, commonly known as the Pernagaeus bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that develop from its flowers. Its botanical structure includes a complex root system and a dense network of runners, which aid in its propagation.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
