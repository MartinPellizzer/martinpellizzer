Rhytidocaulon macrolobum subsp. minimum

Rhytidocaulon macrolobum subsp.

minimum, commonly known as the small-leaf rock cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial herb that thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant features small, ovate to elliptical leaves with a rough, hairy surface. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters and blooming in early spring.

It is often found in alpine or subalpine regions, demonstrating adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
