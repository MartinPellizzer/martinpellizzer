Restiaria craurophylla

Restiaria craurophylla, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Restiaria craurophylla is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
