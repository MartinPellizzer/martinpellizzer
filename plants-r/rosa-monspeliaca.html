Rosa monspeliaca

Rosa monspeliaca, commonly known as the Spanish rose, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa monspeliaca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
