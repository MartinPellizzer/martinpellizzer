Rhododendron erastum

Rhododendron erastum, commonly known as the purple leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in China and the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are often a striking purple or reddish color. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include evergreen foliage in some varieties and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

Rhododendron erastum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
