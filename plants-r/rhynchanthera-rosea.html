Rhynchanthera rosea

Rhynchanthera rosea, commonly known as the rose orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white floral displays, which are often fragrant. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in seasonal environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes, and the leaves are elongated and lance-shaped.

Rhynchanthera rosea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
