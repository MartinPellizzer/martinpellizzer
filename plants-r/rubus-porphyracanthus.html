Rubus porphyracanthus

Rubus porphyracanthus, commonly known as the scarlet bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China and is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its thorny stems and dense growth habit make it a hardy shrub that thrives in temperate climates.

The botanical features of Rubus porphyracanthus make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
