Ranunculus aschersonii

Ranunculus aschersonii, commonly known as the Ascherson's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often accompanied by a distinctive yellow center. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed, and it typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats. It is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Ranunculus aschersonii is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
