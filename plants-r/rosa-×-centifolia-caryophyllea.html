Rosa × centifolia caryophyllea

Rosa × centifolia caryophyllea, commonly known as the centifolia rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its highly fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong, sweet scent. The plant produces multiple blooms throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for gardens and perfumery. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and oval-shaped, while its stems are thorny.

This species is also valued for its use in the production of essential oils and its ornamental appeal.
