Romulea bulbocodium f. violacea

Romulea bulbocodium f. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered romulea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique coloration and compact growth habit.
