Rhynchosia crassifolia

Rhynchosia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf snout bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of leguminous protein.

Rhynchosia crassifolia thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
