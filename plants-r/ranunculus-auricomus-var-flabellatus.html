Ranunculus auricomus var. flabellatus

Ranunculus auricomus var. flabellatus, commonly known as the flabellate buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, fan-shaped (flabellate) petal structure. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It is characterized by its early spring blooming period and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
