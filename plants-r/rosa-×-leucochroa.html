Rosa × leucochroa

Rosa × leucochroa, commonly known as the white musk rose, is a hybrid rose species that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It typically features large, fragrant flowers with white to pale pink petals, often with a subtle musky scent. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Rosa × leucochroa is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in perfumery.
