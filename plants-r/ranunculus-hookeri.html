Ranunculus hookeri

Ranunculus hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
