Rebutia neocumingii subsp. lanata

Rebutia neocumingii subsp.

lanata, commonly known as the woolly rebutia, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to South America. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems covered in a dense, woolly coating of fine hairs.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the growing season, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
