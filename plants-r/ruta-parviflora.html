Ruta parviflora

Ruta parviflora, commonly known as lesser rue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a pungent, bitter scent, and produces yellowish-green flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, and its foliage is used in traditional medicine.

However, it is toxic if ingested in large quantities.
