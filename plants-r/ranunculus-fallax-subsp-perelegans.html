Ranunculus fallax subsp. perelegans

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

perelegans, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant has a short, fibrous root system adapted to its harsh environment.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
