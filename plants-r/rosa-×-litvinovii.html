Rosa × litvinovii

Rosa × litvinovii, commonly known as the Russian hybrid rose, is a species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa × centifolia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a high number of petals. It is well-suited for garden use due to its disease resistance and hardy nature.

Rosa × litvinovii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
