Rhynchospora microcarpa

Rhynchospora microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in damp, acidic soils. Rhynchospora microcarpa plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of undisturbed, nutrient-poor wetland environments.
