Rourea oblongifolia var. floribunda

Rourea oblongifolia var. floribunda, commonly known as the showy rourea, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
