Rebutia xanthocarpa f. graciliflora

Rebutia xanthocarpa f. graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered yellow-fruit rebutia, is a unique hybrid succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, adding a subtle ornamental value to its appearance.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
