Rosa chinensis f. spontanea

Rosa chinensis f. spontanea, commonly known as the wild Chinese rose, is a botanical variety native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, often fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, making it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
