Rubus teutobergensis

Rubus teutobergensis, commonly known as the Teutoburg Forest raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream valleys. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pink to white, appearing in early summer.

The fruit, a red raspberry-like berry, is edible and often used in local culinary traditions.
