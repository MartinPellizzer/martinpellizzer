Ruta divaricata

Ruta divaricata, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, dark green leaves with serrated edges and small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant contains compounds such as rutin and essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Its historical use in herbal medicine dates back to ancient times.
