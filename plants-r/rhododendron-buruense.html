Rhododendron buruense

Rhododendron buruense, commonly known as the Buru rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the island of Buru in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 500 to 1,500 meters. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in spring and are often clustered in inflorescences.

Rhododendron buruense is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant role in the local biodiversity of its native habitat.
