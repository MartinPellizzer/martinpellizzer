Rosa viscaria var. boullui

Rosa viscaria var. boullui, commonly known as the Boullue rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
