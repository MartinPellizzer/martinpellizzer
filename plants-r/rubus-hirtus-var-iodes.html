Rubus hirtus var. iodes

Rubus hirtus var. iodes, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy shrub in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
