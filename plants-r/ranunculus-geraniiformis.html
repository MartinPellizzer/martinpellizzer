Ranunculus geraniiformis

Ranunculus geraniiformis, commonly known as the geranium-like buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
