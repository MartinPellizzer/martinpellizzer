Rubus rhombicus

Rubus rhombicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Rubus rhombicus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinator activity.
