Rosa coriifolia f. cimbrica

Rosa coriifolia f. cimbrica, commonly known as the Cimbric rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

The foliage turns red in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
