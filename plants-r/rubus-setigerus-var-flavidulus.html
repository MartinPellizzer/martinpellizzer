Rubus setigerus var. flavidulus

Rubus setigerus var. flavidulus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, and appear in clusters. The fruits are aggregate berries that mature to a dark red or black color.

This plant is native to parts of eastern North America and thrives in woodland and disturbed habitats.
