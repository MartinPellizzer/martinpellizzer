Raputia

Raputia, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Madagascar, and parts of Asia. The plants typically have a pseudobulb and produce showy, often fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. They thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Raputia species are popular in horticulture due to their ornamental value and unique floral structures.
