Rubus schleicheri f. schumensis

Rubus schleicheri f. schumensis, commonly known as the Schumacher's blackberry, is a shrub native to central and eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential agricultural studies.
