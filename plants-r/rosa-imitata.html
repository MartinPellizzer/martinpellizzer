Rosa imitata

Rosa imitata, commonly known as the mimosa rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs. Rosa imitata is a shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, producing small, rose-like blooms throughout the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
