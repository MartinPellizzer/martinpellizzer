Rhinephyllum pillansii

Rhinephyllum pillansii, commonly known as the Pillans' rhinephyllum, is a rare and striking succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiky, cylindrical stems that often branch out into a bushy form. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in its native environment.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Rhinephyllum pillansii is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
