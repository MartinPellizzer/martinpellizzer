Ranunculus tanguticus var. dasycarpus

Ranunculus tanguticus var. dasycarpus, commonly known as the Tangut buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the Tibetan Plateau and adjacent regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy yellow flowers with five to eight petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a waxy coating, while the fruits are small, hairy achenes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
