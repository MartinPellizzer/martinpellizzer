Rubus guenteri

Rubus guenteri, commonly known as Guenther's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are similar to other blackberries.

The botanical characteristics of Rubus guenteri make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
