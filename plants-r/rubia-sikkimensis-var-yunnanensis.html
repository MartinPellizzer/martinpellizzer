Rubia sikkimensis var. yunnanensis

Rubia sikkimensis var. yunnanensis, commonly known as Yunnan gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges.
