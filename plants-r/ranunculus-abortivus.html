Ranunculus abortivus

Ranunculus abortivus, commonly known as the abortive buttercup, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have numerous sepals that resemble petals.

The species is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
