Reaumuria desertorum

Reaumuria desertorum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and North America.

It is a low-growing shrub with gray-green, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water in harsh desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive and helps stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

Reaumuria desertorum is well-suited to extreme conditions and plays a role in preventing soil erosion in its native habitats.
