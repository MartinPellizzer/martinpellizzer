Roubieva microcarpa

Roubieva microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf sycamore, is a small to medium-sized tree native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in arid environments.
