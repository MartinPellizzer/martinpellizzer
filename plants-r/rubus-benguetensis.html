Rubus benguetensis

Rubus benguetensis, commonly known as the Benguet raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as raspberries, which are typically red when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins, while its flowers are pink and fragrant.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
