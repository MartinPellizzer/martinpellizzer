Rumex densiflorus subsp. pycnanthus

Rumex densiflorus subsp.

pycnanthus, commonly known as the denseflower dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and marshy areas.

The plant features basal leaves that are broad and lobed, with a distinctive reddish coloration on the underside. Its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters and are usually greenish to reddish in color.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
