Rumex dregei

Rumex dregei, commonly known as the Cape sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purplish coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on tall, slender spikes. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
