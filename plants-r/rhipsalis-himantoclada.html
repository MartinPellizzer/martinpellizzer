Rhipsalis himantoclada

Rhipsalis himantoclada, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in small, white tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Rhipsalis himantoclada is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
