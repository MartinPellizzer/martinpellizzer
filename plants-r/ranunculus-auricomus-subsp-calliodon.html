Ranunculus auricomus subsp. calliodon

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

calliodon, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, saucer-shaped corolla and prominent stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
