Rosa canescens

Rosa canescens, commonly known as the white sand rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, spiny stems and produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
