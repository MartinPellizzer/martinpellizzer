Romulea numidica

Romulea numidica, commonly known as the Numidian glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Romulea numidica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
