Rosa beggeriana var. typica

Rosa beggeriana var. typica, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Pakistan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are borne on pedicels and are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
