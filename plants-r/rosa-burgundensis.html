Rosa burgundensis

Rosa burgundensis, commonly known as the Burgundy rose, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and historical significance in horticulture.
