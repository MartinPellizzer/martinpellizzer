Ranunculus pygmaeus var. petiolulatus

Ranunculus pygmaeus var. petiolulatus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The variety petiolulatus is distinguished by its shorter petioles compared to the typical form.
