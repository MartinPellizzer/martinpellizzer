Rouhamon pedunculatum

Rouhamon pedunculatum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
