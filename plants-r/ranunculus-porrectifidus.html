Ranunculus porrectifidus

Ranunculus porrectifidus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
