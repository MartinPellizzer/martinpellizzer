Rosa czackiana

Rosa czackiana, commonly known as the Polish rose, is a species native to central and eastern Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stem.

Rosa czackiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
