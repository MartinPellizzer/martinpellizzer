Rupicapnos africana var. mauritanica

Rupicapnos africana var. mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian firethorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, bright red berries that persist through the winter. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in dry climates. Its foliage is typically dark green and evergreen, providing year-round visual interest.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, so caution is advised when handling or planting this species.
