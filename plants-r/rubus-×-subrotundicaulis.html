Rubus × subrotundicaulis

Rubus × subrotundicaulis, commonly known as the red-leaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its distinctive foliage and fruiting habits.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in woodland and thicket environments. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red drupelets, which are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are usually compound, with three to five leaflets, and often have a reddish tinge, especially during the growing season.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its edible berries.
