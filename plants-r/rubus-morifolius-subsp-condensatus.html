Rubus morifolius subsp. condensatus

Rubus morifolius subsp.

condensatus, commonly known as the densefruit blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its flowers are white and typically appear in clusters.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.
