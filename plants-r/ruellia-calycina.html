Ruellia calycina

Ruellia calycina, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ruellia calycina thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
