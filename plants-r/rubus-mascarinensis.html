Rubus mascarinensis

Rubus mascarinensis, commonly known as the Mascarin raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
