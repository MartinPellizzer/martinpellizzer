Rauvolfia parvifolia

Rauvolfia parvifolia, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine.

The botanical characteristics of Rauvolfia parvifolia make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
