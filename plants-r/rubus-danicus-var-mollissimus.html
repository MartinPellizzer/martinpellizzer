Rubus danicus var. mollissimus

Rubus danicus var. mollissimus, commonly known as the soft-furred bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in moist, temperate climates.
