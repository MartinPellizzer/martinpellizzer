Rubus montanus var. fragrans

Rubus montanus var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The variety is distinguished by its aromatic scent, particularly in the flowers and fruits.
