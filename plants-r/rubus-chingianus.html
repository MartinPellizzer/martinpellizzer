Rubus chingianus

Rubus chingianus, commonly known as Chinese blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth habit.
