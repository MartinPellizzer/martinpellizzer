Rubus rosifolius subsp. maximowiczii

Rubus rosifolius subsp.

maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean raspberry, is a shrub native to East Asia, particularly Korea and parts of China. It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, hairy foliage, and a distinctive floral structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
