Rubus podophyllos var. lutetianus

Rubus podophyllos var. lutetianus, commonly known as the French blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in sunny to partially shaded areas with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued both for its ornamental qualities and its fruit.
