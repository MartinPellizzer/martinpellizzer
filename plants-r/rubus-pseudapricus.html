Rubus pseudapricus

Rubus pseudapricus, commonly known as the purple-fruited raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically deep purple or black when mature. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
