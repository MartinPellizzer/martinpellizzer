Rungia blumeana var. hirsuta

Rungia blumeana var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy rungia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within rainforests. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
