Rudbeckia apetala

Rudbeckia apetala, commonly known as the pale coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to yellow rayless petals. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Rudbeckia apetala is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
