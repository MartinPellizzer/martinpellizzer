Rubus × loronensis

Rubus × loronensis, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically larger and more flavorful than those of its parent species. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, native to Japan. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
