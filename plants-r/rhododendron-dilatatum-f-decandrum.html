Rhododendron dilatatum f. decandrum

Rhododendron dilatatum f. decandrum, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a compact shrub that typically grows in high-elevation forests and alpine environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring. Its foliage consists of leathery, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
