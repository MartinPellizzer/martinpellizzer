Rubus rugosus var. cambodianus

Rubus rugosus var. cambodianus, commonly known as the Cambodian bramble, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental and potential medicinal value, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
