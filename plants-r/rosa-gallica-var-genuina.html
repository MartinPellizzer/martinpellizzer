Rosa gallica var. genuina

Rosa gallica var. genuina, commonly known as the white gallica rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces red, hairy fruits known as hips. It is well adapted to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
