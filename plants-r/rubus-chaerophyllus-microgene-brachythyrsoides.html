Rubus chaerophyllus microgene brachythyrsoides

Rubus chaerophyllus microgene brachythyrsoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
