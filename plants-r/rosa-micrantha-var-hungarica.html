Rosa micrantha var. hungarica

Rosa micrantha var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Carpathian region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a red, hairy rosehip.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a resilient and ornamental plant in horticulture.
