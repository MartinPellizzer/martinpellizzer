Rhynchosia goetzei var. pseudocaribaea

Rhynchosia goetzei var. pseudocaribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a leguminous species characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes in its native range.
