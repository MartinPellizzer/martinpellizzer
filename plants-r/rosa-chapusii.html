Rosa chapusii

Rosa chapusii, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.

Rosa chapusii is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
