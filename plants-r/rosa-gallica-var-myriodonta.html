Rosa gallica var. myriodonta

Rosa gallica var. myriodonta, commonly known as the many-flowered Gallica rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, deep pink to crimson flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically borne in large, showy inflorescences, which can contain up to 50 or more individual blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery and garden cultivation.
