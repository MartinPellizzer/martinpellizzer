Racosperma longispineum

Racosperma longispineum, commonly known as the long-spined acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features prominently branched stems with sharp, long spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. Its flowers are typically yellow or yellow-green, arranged in dense, globular inflorescences.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
