Rosa virgultorum

Rosa virgultorum, commonly known as the wild rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to various regions across Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often growing in woodlands and scrublands. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its hips, which are the fruit, are red and contain seeds, and they are often used in herbal remedies.

Rosa virgultorum plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
