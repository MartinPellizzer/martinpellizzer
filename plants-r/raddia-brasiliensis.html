Raddia brasiliensis

Raddia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Raddia brasiliensis plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
