Rubus ursinus var. menziesii

Rubus ursinus var. menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and ecological importance in local habitats.
