Ranunculus fallax subsp. angustidens

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

angustidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers.

The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed divisions, and its flowers typically have five to eight sepals that resemble petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other members of the Ranunculus fallax complex.
