Rottlera tiliifolia

Rottlera tiliifolia, commonly known as the Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in racemes. Rottlera tiliifolia is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
