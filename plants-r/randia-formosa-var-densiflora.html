Randia formosa var. densiflora

Randia formosa var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
