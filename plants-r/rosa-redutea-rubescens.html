Rosa redutea rubescens

Rosa redutea rubescens, commonly known as the red-veined rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-veined leaves and fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is native to the temperate regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its growth habit includes a shrub form with thorny stems and dense foliage.

Rosa redutea rubescens prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
