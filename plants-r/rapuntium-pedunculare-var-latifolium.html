Rapuntium pedunculare var. latifolium

Rapuntium pedunculare var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf rapunculus, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a hairy surface, and stems that are upright and branched. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and delicate flowers, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
