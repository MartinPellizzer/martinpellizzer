Ranunculus fedorovii

Ranunculus fedorovii, commonly known as the Fedorov's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its often hairy foliage and vibrant floral displays.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
