Rumex maritimus var. persicarioides

Rumex maritimus var. persicarioides, commonly known as sea sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions.

It features succulent, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red, adapting to saline and sandy environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in harsh coastal conditions, including high salinity and strong winds.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife.
