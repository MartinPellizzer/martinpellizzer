Rubus pallidus subsp. hirsutior

Rubus pallidus subsp.

hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showcasing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense pubescence, giving it a notably hairy appearance.
