Ranunculus castellanus

Ranunculus castellanus, commonly known as the castle buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features a basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are usually surrounded by a ring of sepals that are often mistaken for petals.

Ranunculus castellanus is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
