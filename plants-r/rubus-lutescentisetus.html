Rubus lutescentisetus

Rubus lutescentisetus, commonly known as the yellow-leafed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically yellowish-green in color and have a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
