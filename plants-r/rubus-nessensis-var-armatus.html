Rubus nessensis var. armatus

Rubus nessensis var. armatus, commonly known as the Scottish blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and hedgerows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in local foraging and traditional herbal practices.
