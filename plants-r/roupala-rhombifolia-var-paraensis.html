Roupala rhombifolia var. paraensis

Roupala rhombifolia var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense arrowwood, is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil and is often found in lowland forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
