Racosperma spinosissimum

Racosperma spinosissimum, commonly known as the spiny wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its sharp, spiny stems and branches, which provide a defensive mechanism against herbivores. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Racosperma spinosissimum plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
