Ranunculus tricrenatus

Ranunculus tricrenatus, commonly known as the three-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its short stature and ornamental value in gardens.
