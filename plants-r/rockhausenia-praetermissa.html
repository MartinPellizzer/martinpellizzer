Rockhausenia praetermissa

Rockhausenia praetermissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, sunny environments. It is a member of the family Rosaceae, which includes roses and other related plants.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
