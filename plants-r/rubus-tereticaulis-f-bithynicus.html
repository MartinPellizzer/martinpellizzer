Rubus tereticaulis f. bithynicus

Rubus tereticaulis f. bithynicus, commonly known as the Bithynian blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
