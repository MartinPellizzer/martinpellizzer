Rubus nishimuranus

Rubus nishimuranus, commonly known as Nishimura's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes various shrubs with thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Rubus family.
