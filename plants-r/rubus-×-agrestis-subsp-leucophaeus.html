Rubus × agrestis subsp. leucophaeus

Rubus × agrestis subsp.

leucophaeus, commonly known as the white-leaved blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus. It is characterized by its distinctive white-veined leaves and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
