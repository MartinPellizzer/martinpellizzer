Rubus × proximellus

Rubus × proximellus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus fruticosus (bramble), resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The plant produces large, dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and sweeter than those of its parent species. Its thorny stems and dense foliage make it a hardy, adaptable plant found in various habitats.

The botanical features of Rubus × proximellus make it both a valuable agricultural crop and an interesting subject for botanical study.
