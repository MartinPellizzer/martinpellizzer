Rubus nexuosus

Rubus nexuosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a distinctive glossy appearance.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine, though it is less commercially cultivated than other blackberry species.
