Rapuntium simplex

Rapuntium simplex, commonly known as the simple cress, is a low-growing flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, often used in salads or as a garnish.

Rapuntium simplex is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and rocky habitats.
