Rubus alterniflorus var. hypodasys

Rubus alterniflorus var. hypodasys, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salty air and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to maritime climates and it plays a role in local ecosystems.
