Rosa coriifolia f. typica

Rosa coriifolia f. typica, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
