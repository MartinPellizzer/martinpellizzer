Rosa canina var. fastigiata

Rosa canina var. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright dog rose, is a compact, shrub-like variety of the dog rose.

It features dense, upright growth with small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

Its hips, which are orange-red and edible, contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
