Rubus nitidus subsp. hamulosus

Rubus nitidus subsp.

hamulosus, commonly known as the silky raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with finely serrated margins.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, which are borne on the ends of the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
