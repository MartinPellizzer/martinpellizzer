Rubus acridentatus

Rubus acridentatus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
