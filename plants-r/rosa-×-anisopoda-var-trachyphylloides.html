Rosa × anisopoda var. trachyphylloides

Rosa × anisopoda var. trachyphylloides, commonly known as the roughleaf shrub rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and dark green, rough-textured leaves.

It produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soil. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and resistance to common rose diseases.
