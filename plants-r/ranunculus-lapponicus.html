Ranunculus lapponicus

Ranunculus lapponicus, commonly known as the Lapland buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are often accompanied by a cluster of small, yellow sepals.

Ranunculus lapponicus is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.
