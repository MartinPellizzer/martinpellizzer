Rosa jundzillii var. pallonii

Rosa jundzillii var. pallonii, commonly known as the white thorned rose, is a variety of the thorned rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include fragrant petals and a compact, hardy structure adapted to its arid and high-altitude environment.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in challenging climates.
