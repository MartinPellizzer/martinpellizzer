Rhododendron dielsianum

Rhododendron dielsianum, commonly known as the Diels' rhododendron, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron dielsianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
