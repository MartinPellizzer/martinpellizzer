Rupicapnos faurei

Rupicapnos faurei, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Rupicapnos faurei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
