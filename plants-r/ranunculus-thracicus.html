Ranunculus thracicus

Ranunculus thracicus, commonly known as the Thrace buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, radially symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers feature five to eight yellow petals with a distinctive central stamen.

It is known for its early spring blooming habit and is often found in the Balkan region.
