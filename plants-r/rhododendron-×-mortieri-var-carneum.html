Rhododendron × mortieri var. carneum

Rhododendron × mortieri var. carneum, commonly known as the Carnation Azalea, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive carnation-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features leathery, dark green leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
