Rindera lanata subvar. detonsa

Rindera lanata subvar. detonsa, commonly known as the woolly rindera, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
