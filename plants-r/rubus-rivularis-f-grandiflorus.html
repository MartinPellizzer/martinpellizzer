Rubus rivularis f. grandiflorus

Rubus rivularis f. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is often used in hedges or as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal.
