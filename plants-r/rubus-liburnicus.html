Rubus liburnicus

Rubus liburnicus, commonly known as the Liburnian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
