Rubus vaniloquus

Rubus vaniloquus, commonly known as the fragrant raspberry.

It is a species of brambles native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The plant is valued for its edible berries and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
