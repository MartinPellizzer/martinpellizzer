Rosa afzeliana f. pilosior

Rosa afzeliana f. pilosior, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and stems, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas, where it thrives in temperate climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
