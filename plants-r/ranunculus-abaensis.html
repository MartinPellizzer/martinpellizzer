Ranunculus abaensis

Ranunculus abaensis, commonly known as the ABA buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
