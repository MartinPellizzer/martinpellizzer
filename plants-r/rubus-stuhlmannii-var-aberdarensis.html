Rubus stuhlmannii var. aberdarensis

Rubus stuhlmannii var. aberdarensis, commonly known as the Aberdare bramble, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Kenya.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in montane forests and grasslands.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
