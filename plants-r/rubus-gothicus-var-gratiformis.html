Rubus gothicus var. gratiformis

Rubus gothicus var. gratiformis, commonly known as the Gothic raspberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
