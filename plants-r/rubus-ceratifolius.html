Rubus ceratifolius

Rubus ceratifolius, commonly known as the hooked-leaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnately divided, with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, often mistaken for raspberries. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
