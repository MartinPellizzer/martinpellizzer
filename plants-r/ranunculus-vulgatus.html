Ranunculus vulgatus

Ranunculus vulgatus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and typically grows in moist meadows and wetlands. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed shape.

Ranunculus vulgatus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of wet soil conditions.
