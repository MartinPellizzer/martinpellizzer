Rubus corylifolius f. aciculatus

Rubus corylifolius f. aciculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its finely serrated, needle-like leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three to five leaflets and a robust root system that helps it establish in various soil types.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
