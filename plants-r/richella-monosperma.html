Richella monosperma

Richella monosperma, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stem is erect and branching.

Richella monosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
