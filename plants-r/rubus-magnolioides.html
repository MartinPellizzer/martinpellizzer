Rubus magnolioides

Rubus magnolioides, commonly known as the magnolia raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
