Rubus mesogaeus var. mesogaeus

Rubus mesogaeus var. mesogaeus, commonly known as the mesogaeus blackberry, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible blackberries, which are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
