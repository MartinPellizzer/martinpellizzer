Rhynchospora reptans

Rhynchospora reptans, commonly known as the creeping marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist to saturated soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

Rhynchospora reptans plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and water filtration.
