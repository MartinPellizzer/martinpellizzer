Rubus fruticosus var. glabratus

Rubus fruticosus var. glabratus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bush, is a species of shrub within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. It produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications.

The plant also plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing food and shelter.
