Rosa × gibbosa

Rosa × gibbosa, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa indica.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to the blooms.

Rosa × gibbosa is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
