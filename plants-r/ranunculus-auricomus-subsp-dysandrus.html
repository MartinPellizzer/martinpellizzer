Ranunculus auricomus subsp. dysandrus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

dysandrus, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it is often found in rocky or meadow environments.

The subspecies dysandrus is distinguished by its slightly different floral morphology compared to the typical form of R. auricomus.
