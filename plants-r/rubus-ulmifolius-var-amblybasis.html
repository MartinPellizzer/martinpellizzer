Rubus ulmifolius var. amblybasis

Rubus ulmifolius var. amblybasis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and hedgerows, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
