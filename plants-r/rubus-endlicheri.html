Rubus endlicheri

Rubus endlicheri, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white and appear in the spring.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
