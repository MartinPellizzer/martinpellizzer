Rosa × anisopoda f. biturigensis

Rosa × anisopoda f. biturigensis, commonly known as the Riverina rose, is a hybrid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in open woodlands. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
