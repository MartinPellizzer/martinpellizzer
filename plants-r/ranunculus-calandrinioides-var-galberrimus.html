Ranunculus calandrinioides var. galberrimus

Ranunculus calandrinioides var. galberrimus, commonly known as the Galberrimus buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, greenish sepals beneath the petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
