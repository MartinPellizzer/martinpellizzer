Rosa canina subvar. cernagorae

Rosa canina subvar. cernagorae, commonly known as the Cernagora rose, is a subspecies native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fuzzy fruits called hips. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
