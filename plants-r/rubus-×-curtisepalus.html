Rubus × curtisepalus

Rubus × curtisepalus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
