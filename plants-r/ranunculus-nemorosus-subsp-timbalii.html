Ranunculus nemorosus subsp. timbalii

Ranunculus nemorosus subsp.

timbalii, commonly known as the Timbalai forest buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has palmate leaves with lobes that are often hairy on the underside.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
