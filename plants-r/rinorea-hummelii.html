Rinorea hummelii

Rinorea hummelii, commonly known as the silverleaf rinorea, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming during the wet season. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a leathery texture and a waxy coating.

Rinorea hummelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
