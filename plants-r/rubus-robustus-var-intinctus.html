Rubus robustus var. intinctus

Rubus robustus var. intinctus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The berries are edible and have been used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
