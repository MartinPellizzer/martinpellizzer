Ratibida coahuilensis

Ratibida coahuilensis, commonly known as the Coahuila golden coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a hairy, upright stem and palmate leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Ratibida coahuilensis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.
