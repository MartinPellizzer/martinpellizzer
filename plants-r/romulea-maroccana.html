Romulea maroccana

Romulea maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan glory of the snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Morocco.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a distinctive, globe-like bulb that stores nutrients. Romulea maroccana thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for rock gardens and alpine collections.
