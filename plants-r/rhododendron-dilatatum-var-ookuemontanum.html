Rhododendron dilatatum var. ookuemontanum

Rhododendron dilatatum var. ookuemontanum, commonly known as the Ookuemontanum rhododendron, is a hybrid variety native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
