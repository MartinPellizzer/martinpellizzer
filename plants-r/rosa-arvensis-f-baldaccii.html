Rosa arvensis f. baldaccii

Rosa arvensis f. baldaccii, commonly known as the Baldaccii dog rose, is a horticultural form of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically features thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
