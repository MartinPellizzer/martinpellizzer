Rubia indecora

Rubia indecora, commonly known as the purple antirrhinum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Rubia indecora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
