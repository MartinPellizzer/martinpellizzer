Rhytachne congoensis

Rhytachne congoensis, commonly known as the Congo bristlegrass, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Central and Southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along riverbanks. The plant features erect, slender stems covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in loose panicles.

Rhytachne congoensis is valued for its potential in traditional medicine and its role in local ecosystems.
