Ranunculus membranaceus var. membranaceus

Ranunculus membranaceus var. membranaceus, commonly known as the membranous buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals, often mistaken for true petals. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
