Rosa ferruginea f. livida

Rosa ferruginea f. livida, commonly known as the rusty rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive rust-colored stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
