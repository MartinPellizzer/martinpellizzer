Rosa korschinskiana

Rosa korschinskiana, commonly known as the Korschinsky rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has serrated, dark green leaves and produces red-orange hips in the fall. It is relatively hardy, tolerating cold temperatures and growing in well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
