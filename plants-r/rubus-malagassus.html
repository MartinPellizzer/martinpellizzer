Rubus malagassus

Rubus malagassus, commonly known as the Madagascar raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
