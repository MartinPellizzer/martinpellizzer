Retanilla ephedra

Retanilla ephedra, commonly known as the desert tea, is a shrub native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and woody stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Retanilla ephedra is often used in traditional medicine for its potential respiratory benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient species in desert ecosystems.
