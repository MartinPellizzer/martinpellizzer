Rumex salicifolius var. unigranis

Rumex salicifolius var. unigranis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib and a serrated margin, typically growing up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on elongated inflorescences. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
