Rauvolfia suaveolens

Rauvolfia suaveolens, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and ecological studies.
