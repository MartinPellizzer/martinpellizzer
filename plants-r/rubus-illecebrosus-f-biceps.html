Rubus illecebrosus f. biceps

Rubus illecebrosus f. biceps, commonly known as the twin-stemmed blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive two-stemmed growth pattern.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
