Roldana lanicaulis

Roldana lanicaulis, commonly known as the woolly roldana, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
