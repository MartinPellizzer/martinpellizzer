Rubus oxyanchus

Rubus oxyanchus, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. This shrub typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing food for wildlife in its habitat.
