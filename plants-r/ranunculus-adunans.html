Ranunculus adunans

Ranunculus adunans, commonly known as the Himalayan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus adunans is often found in meadows and forest clearings at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,500 meters.
