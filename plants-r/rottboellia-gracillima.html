Rottboellia gracillima

Rottboellia gracillima, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces loose panicles of tiny, spikelet-based flowers, which are usually greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Rottboellia gracillima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate appearance.
