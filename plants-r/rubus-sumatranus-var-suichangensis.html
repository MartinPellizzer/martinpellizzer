Rubus sumatranus var. suichangensis

Rubus sumatranus var. suichangensis, commonly known as the Suichang blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
