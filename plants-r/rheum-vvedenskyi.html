Rheum vvedenskyi

Rheum vvedenskyi, commonly known as the Tibetan rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Tibet.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, with a distinctive reddish coloration on the underside. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
