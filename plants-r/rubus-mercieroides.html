Rubus mercieroides

Rubus mercieroides, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their thorny stems and edible fruits. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically sour but can be used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
