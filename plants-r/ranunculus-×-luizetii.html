Ranunculus × luizetii

Ranunculus × luizetii, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, double-layered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Ranunculus × luizetii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
