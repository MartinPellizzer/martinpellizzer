Ranunculus auricomus subsp. magnifolius

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

magnifolius, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center and deeply lobed leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, and the leaves are palmately divided with a waxy surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
