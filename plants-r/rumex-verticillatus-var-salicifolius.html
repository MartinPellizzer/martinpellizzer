Rumex verticillatus var. salicifolius

Rumex verticillatus var. salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaved sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate to ovate, with a distinctive willow-like shape and serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
