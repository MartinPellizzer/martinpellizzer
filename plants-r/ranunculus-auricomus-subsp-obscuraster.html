Ranunculus auricomus subsp. obscuraster

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

obscuraster, commonly known as the dark-leaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with a dark green coloration, and its flowers are yellow with five sepals that resemble petals. It produces small, aggregate fruits that contain multiple achenes.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
