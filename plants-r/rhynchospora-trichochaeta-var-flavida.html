Rhynchospora trichochaeta var. flavida

Rhynchospora trichochaeta var. flavida, commonly known as the yellow-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in the southeastern United States.

It features slender, triangular stems that are often reddish or purple in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and have a rough texture, with hairy margins that give the plant its common name. This species is often found in shallow water or moist soil, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important plant for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
