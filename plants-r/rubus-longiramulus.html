Rubus longiramulus

Rubus longiramulus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
