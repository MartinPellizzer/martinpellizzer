Rubus × semicorymbosus

Rubus × semicorymbosus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant that combines traits of blackberries and raspberries.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are usually dark red to black in color, with a distinctive hairy surface. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits variability in fruit size, flavor, and disease resistance.
