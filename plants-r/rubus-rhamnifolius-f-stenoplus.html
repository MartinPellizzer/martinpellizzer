Rubus rhamnifolius f. stenoplus

Rubus rhamnifolius f. stenoplus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its narrower, more elongated leaves compared to the typical form. This plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
