Rustia viridiflora

Rustia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered rustia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a slight greenish tinge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Rustia viridiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
