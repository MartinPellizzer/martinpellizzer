Ricinus speciosus

Ricinus speciosus, commonly known as the Castor oil plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces a toxic, sticky seed capsule containing seeds used for castor oil production. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its economic importance.

The plant contains ricin, a potent toxin, which makes it potentially hazardous if handled improperly.
