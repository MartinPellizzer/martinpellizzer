Rubus godronii subsp. ellipticifolius

Rubus godronii subsp.

ellipticifolius, commonly known as the elliptic-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
