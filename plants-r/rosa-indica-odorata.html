Rosa indica odorata

Rosa indica odorata, commonly known as the Arabian rose or the Damask rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe, and it is renowned for its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are often used in the production of rose oil and other perfumery products due to their intense aroma.

Rosa indica odorata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature.
