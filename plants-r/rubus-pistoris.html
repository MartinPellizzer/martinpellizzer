Rubus pistoris

Rubus pistoris, commonly known as the Italian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and hedgerows.
