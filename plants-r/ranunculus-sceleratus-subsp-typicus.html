Ranunculus sceleratus subsp. typicus

Ranunculus sceleratus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, showy appearance and is often found in disturbed or semi-natural habitats.
