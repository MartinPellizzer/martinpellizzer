Rosa dumalis f. meszkoensis

Rosa dumalis f. meszkoensis, commonly known as the Meszko's wild rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa dumalis.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in the spring. This subspecies is native to specific regions in Central Europe, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a subject of interest in horticulture and conservation efforts.
