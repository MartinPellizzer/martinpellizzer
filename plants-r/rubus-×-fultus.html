Rubus × fultus

Rubus × fultus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry.

It is a cross between a blackberry (Rubus ursinus) and a red raspberry (Rubus idaeus). This plant typically exhibits characteristics of both parent species, with dark red to black berries and thorny stems. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its flavor and nutritional content.
