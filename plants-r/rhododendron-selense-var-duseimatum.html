Rhododendron selense var. duseimatum

Rhododendron selense var. duseimatum, commonly known as the Duseimatum rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
