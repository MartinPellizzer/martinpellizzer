Ranunculus reptabundus

Ranunculus reptabundus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals. The plant has smooth, often hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, bead-like fruits that are dispersed by wind. Ranunculus reptabundus thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and along roadsides.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
