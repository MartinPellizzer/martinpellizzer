Rouhamon medeola

Rouhamon medeola, commonly known as the eastern wood lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a single, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
