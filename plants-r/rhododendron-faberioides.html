Rhododendron faberioides

Rhododendron faberioides, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that provide attractive year-round structure.

Rhododendron faberioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom reliably in suitable climates.
