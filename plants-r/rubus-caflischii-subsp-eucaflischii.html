Rubus caflischii subsp. eucaflischii

Rubus caflischii subsp.

eucaflischii, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are typically white or pink.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
