Rosa viscaria var. lugdunensis

Rosa viscaria var. lugdunensis, commonly known as the Gallic rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces red or orange hips in the fall. It is often found in woodland edges and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
