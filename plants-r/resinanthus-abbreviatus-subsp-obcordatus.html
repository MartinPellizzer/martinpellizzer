Resinanthus abbreviatus subsp. obcordatus

Resinanthus abbreviatus subsp.

obcordatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed resinanthus, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the cooler months.

The plant produces a unique resin, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or coastal habitats.
