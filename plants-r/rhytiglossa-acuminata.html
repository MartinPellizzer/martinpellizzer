Rhytiglossa acuminata

Rhytiglossa acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under the canopy of rainforests. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with purple or brown markings, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Rhytiglossa acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
