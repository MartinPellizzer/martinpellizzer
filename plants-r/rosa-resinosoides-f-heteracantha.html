Rosa resinosoides f. heteracantha

Rosa resinosoides f. heteracantha, commonly known as the resinous rose or the sticky rose, is a unique botanical variant of the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its glandular, resin-secreting hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive sticky texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to white in color. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in the same sticky hairs.

This species is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique botanical features.
