Rebutia costata f. eucaliptana

Rebutia costata f. eucaliptana, commonly known as the eucalyptus form of the spiny euphorbia, is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves.

It originates from arid regions and is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates with minimal water. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage is often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
