Rosa communis var. amansii

Rosa communis var. amansii, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants in gardens.

Rosa communis var. amansii is valued for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
