Rottboellia foveolata

Rottboellia foveolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed.
