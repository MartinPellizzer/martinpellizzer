Rumex paulsenianus

Rumex paulsenianus, commonly known as Paulsen's sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively.

The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
