Racosperma ommatospermum

Racosperma ommatospermum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its yellow floral spikes that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
