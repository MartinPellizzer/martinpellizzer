Rigiolepis lobbii

Rigiolepis lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's rock fig, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, Rigiolepis lobbii is an important component of its local ecosystem.
