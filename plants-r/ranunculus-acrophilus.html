Ranunculus acrophilus

Ranunculus acrophilus, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals and a distinctive central yellow stigma. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is known for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Ranunculus acrophilus plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems, supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
