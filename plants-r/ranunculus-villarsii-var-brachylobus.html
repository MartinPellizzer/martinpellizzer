Ranunculus villarsii var. brachylobus

Ranunculus villarsii var. brachylobus, commonly known as the short-stemmed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as meadows and forest edges. It is characterized by its short, sturdy stems and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
