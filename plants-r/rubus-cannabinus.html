Rubus cannabinus

Rubus cannabinus, commonly known as the cannabis bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red or black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It is closely related to other bramble species and shares similar growth habits.

Due to its association with cannabis, it has attracted attention for its potential in both horticulture and botanical research.
