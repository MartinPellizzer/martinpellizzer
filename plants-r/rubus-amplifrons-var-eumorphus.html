Rubus amplifrons var. eumorphus

Rubus amplifrons var. eumorphus, commonly known as the golden-fruited raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The fruit is distinctive for its golden-yellow color and sweet, aromatic flavor, setting it apart from other raspberry varieties. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
