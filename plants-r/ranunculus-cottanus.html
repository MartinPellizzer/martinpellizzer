Ranunculus cottanus

Ranunculus cottanus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus cottanus is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
