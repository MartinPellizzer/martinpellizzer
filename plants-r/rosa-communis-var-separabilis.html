Rosa communis var. separabilis

Rosa communis var. separabilis, commonly known as the separable rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose species.

It is characterized by its unique ability to separate into individual blooms, a trait that sets it apart from other rose varieties. The plant typically features fragrant, pink to white flowers with layered petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
