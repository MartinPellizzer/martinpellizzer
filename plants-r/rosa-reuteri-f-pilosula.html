Rosa reuteri f. pilosula

Rosa reuteri f. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed rock rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to southern Europe.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive silvery-white tomentum, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique texture and seasonal floral display.
