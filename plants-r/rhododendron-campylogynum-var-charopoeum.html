Rhododendron campylogynum var. charopoeum

Rhododendron campylogynum var. charopoeum, commonly known as the Greek Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
