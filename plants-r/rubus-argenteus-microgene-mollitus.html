Rubus argenteus microgene mollitus

Rubus argenteus microgene mollitus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
