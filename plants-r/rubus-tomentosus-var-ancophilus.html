Rubus tomentosus var. ancophilus

Rubus tomentosus var. ancophilus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its fruit is often used in jams and pies, while the plant itself is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The species is also notable for its hairy foliage and robust growth habit.
