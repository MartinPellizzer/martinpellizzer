Rubus caflischii var. iracundus

Rubus caflischii var. iracundus, commonly known as the iracundus blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique species within the Rubus genus.
