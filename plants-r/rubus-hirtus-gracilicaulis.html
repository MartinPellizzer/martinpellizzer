Rubus hirtus gracilicaulis

Rubus hirtus gracilicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thin, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, edible berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its hirsute (hairy) stems and foliage are notable botanical features.
