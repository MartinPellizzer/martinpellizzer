Rhynchospora austrobahiensis

Rhynchospora austrobahiensis, commonly known as the Southern Bahr River sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Australia, particularly found in the eastern regions, including Queensland and New South Wales. This perennial herb typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, preferring moist soils. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
