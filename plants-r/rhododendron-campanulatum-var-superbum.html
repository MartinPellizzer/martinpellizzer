Rhododendron campanulatum var. superbum

Rhododendron campanulatum var. superbum, commonly known as the grand bellflower rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded forest environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a glossy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
