Rosa cinnamomea var. smithiana

Rosa cinnamomea var. smithiana, commonly known as the Cinnamon Rose, is a hybrid rose species native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage. Its flowers have a subtle cinnamon-like scent, which is a distinguishing feature.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its attractive appearance and pleasant fragrance.
