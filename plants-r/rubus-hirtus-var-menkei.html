Rubus hirtus var. menkei

Rubus hirtus var. menkei, commonly known as the rough blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, and the plant is often found in woodland edges and moist soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and as a source of edible fruit.
