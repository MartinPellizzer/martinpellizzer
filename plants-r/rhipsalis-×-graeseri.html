Rhipsalis × graeseri

Rhipsalis × graeseri, commonly known as the hybrid Christmas cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rhipsalis baccifera and Schlumbergera truncata.

It is a type of epiphytic cactus native to Brazil, characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often used for propagation. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the holiday season, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
