Ricinocarpus rivularis

Ricinocarpus rivularis, commonly known as the river senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows near water bodies, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a distinctive shape. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by water.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
