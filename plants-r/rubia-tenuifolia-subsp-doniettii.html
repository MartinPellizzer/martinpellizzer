Rubia tenuifolia subsp. doniettii

Rubia tenuifolia subsp.

doniettii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
