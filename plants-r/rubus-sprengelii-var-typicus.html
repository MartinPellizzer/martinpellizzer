Rubus sprengelii var. typicus

Rubus sprengelii var. typicus, commonly known as the Sprengel's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and pies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in woodlands and along streambanks.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
