Rubus notabilis

Rubus notabilis, commonly known as the wineberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in jams and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Rubus notabilis is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its fruit yield.
