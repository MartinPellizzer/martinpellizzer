Rumex hastatus

Rumex hastatus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically dark green and hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by kidney-shaped, reddish-brown seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

Rumex hastatus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
