Rosa peninna

Rosa peninna, commonly known as the Peninsular rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or wooded areas and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red hips that contain the seeds.

Rosa peninna is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery.
