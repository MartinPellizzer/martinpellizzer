Racosperma gracilentum

Racosperma gracilentum, commonly known as the slender racosperma, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. Racosperma gracilentum is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
