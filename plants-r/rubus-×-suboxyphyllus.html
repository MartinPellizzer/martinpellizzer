Rubus × suboxyphyllus

Rubus × suboxyphyllus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white and clustered in umbels.

This species is native to parts of eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
