Racosperma macdonnelliense

Racosperma macdonnelliense, commonly known as the Macdonnell's acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes.

The species is named in honor of the explorer and naturalist James Macdonnell.
