Rubus egregius var. egregiatus

Rubus egregius var. egregiatus, commonly known as the showy blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are often more robust and flavorful compared to other Rubus species. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of white flowers in the spring.

Its berries are not only edible but also attract various wildlife, contributing to its ecological significance.
