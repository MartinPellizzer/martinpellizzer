Rubus rusticanus var. genuinus

Rubus rusticanus var. genuinus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems that provide structural support.

The botanical features of this variety make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, often found in woodland and hedgerow environments.
