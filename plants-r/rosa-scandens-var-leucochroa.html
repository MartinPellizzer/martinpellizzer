Rosa scandens var. leucochroa

Rosa scandens var. leucochroa, commonly known as the white-stemmed climbing rose, is a variant of the climbing rose species Rosa scandens.

It is characterized by its pale green to white stems, which contrast with its typical pink or red flowers. The plant is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in the summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and vigorous climbing habit.
