Rhododendron calostrotum subsp. calostrotum

Rhododendron calostrotum subsp.

calostrotum, commonly known as the Appalachian rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern United States. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in moist, shaded forest environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to other rhododendron species.
