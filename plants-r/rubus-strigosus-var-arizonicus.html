Rubus strigosus var. arizonicus

Rubus strigosus var. arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona brambles, is a thorny shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its flowers are usually white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it blooms in early spring.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.
