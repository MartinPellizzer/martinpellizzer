Rubus subtomentosus

Rubus subtomentosus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland areas and moist soils, often forming dense thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
