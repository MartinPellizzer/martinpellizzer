Racosperma preissianum

Racosperma preissianum, commonly known as the mountain wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils in arid and semi-arid regions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
