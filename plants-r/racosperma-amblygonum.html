Racosperma amblygonum

Racosperma amblygonum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, typical of the legume family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native animals.
