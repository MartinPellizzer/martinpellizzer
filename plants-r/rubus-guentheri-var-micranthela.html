Rubus guentheri var. micranthela

Rubus guentheri var. micranthela, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
