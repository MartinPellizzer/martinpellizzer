Rosa graveolens var. cheriensis

Rosa graveolens var. cheriensis, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in a variety of soil conditions.

The variety 'cheriensis' is noted for its strong fragrance and compact growth habit.
