Rosa stylosa subsp. chlorantha

Rosa stylosa subsp.

chlorantha, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed rose, is a shrub-like species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often yellowish stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with multiple leaflets that are oval and serrated. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.
