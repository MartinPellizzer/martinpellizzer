Rhododendron boothii

Rhododendron boothii, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron boothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
