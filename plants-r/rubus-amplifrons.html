Rubus amplifrons

Rubus amplifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and its thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
