Rubus pinnatus var. ledermannii

Rubus pinnatus var. ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or yellow in color, resembling raspberries in appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
