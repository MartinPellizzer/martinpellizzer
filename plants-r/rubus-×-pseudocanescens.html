Rubus × pseudocanescens

Rubus × pseudocanescens, commonly known as the Himalayan raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and white to pale pink flowers. Its fruits, which are edible, are small, round, and often have a tart flavor, making it a valuable species in local ecosystems and traditional uses.

The botanical features of Rubus × pseudocanescens reflect its adaptation to harsh mountain environments.
