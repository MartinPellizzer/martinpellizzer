Rajania cordata var. microcarpa

Rajania cordata var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited heartleaf, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule containing tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady, moist garden environments.
