Rosa omissa var. stenophylla

Rosa omissa var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dog rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in pink to white hues. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a narrow, lanceolate shape, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
