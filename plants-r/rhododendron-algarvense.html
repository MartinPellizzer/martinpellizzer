Rhododendron algarvense

Rhododendron algarvense, commonly known as the Algarve rhododendron, is a species native to the southern Iberian Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky and limestone soils. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers in the spring. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

Rhododendron algarvense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
