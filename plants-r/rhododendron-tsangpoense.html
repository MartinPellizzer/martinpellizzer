Rhododendron tsangpoense

Rhododendron tsangpoense, commonly known as the Tsangpo rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas, primarily found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features large, showy flowers that are usually pink to red in color, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
