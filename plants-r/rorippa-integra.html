Rorippa integra

Rorippa integra, commonly known as watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in early spring. Watercress thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as streams, ponds, and wetlands.

It is known for its peppery taste and has been used for centuries in culinary and medicinal applications.
