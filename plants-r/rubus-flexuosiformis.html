Rubus flexuosiformis

Rubus flexuosiformis, commonly known as the flexible blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species, sharing similar morphological traits and ecological preferences.
