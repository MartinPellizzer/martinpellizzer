Rubus kingaensis

Rubus kingaensis, commonly known as Kinga's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the island of Kinga in the Southern Ocean, specifically the Antarctic region. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The fruit, a type of berry, is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a potential resource for local wildlife and future human consumption.
