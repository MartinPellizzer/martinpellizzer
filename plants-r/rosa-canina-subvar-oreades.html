Rosa canina subvar. oreades

Rosa canina subvar. oreades, commonly known as the alpine rosehip, is a shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces red, fuzzy fruits called rosehips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
