Rhynchosia hermannii

Rhynchosia hermannii, commonly known as the Hermann's cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Rhynchosia hermannii is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
