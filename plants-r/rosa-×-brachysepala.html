Rosa × brachysepala

Rosa × brachysepala, commonly known as the dwarf shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and production of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
