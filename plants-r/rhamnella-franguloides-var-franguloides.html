Rhamnella franguloides var. franguloides

Rhamnella franguloides var. franguloides, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches that turn reddish in the fall, giving it a striking visual display. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that attract birds. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
