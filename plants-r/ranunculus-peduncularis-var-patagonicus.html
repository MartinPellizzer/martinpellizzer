Ranunculus peduncularis var. patagonicus

Ranunculus peduncularis var. patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem that often grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has palmate leaves divided into three to five lobes, and it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and sometimes toxic sap.

This variety is particularly adapted to the harsh climates of Patagonia, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
