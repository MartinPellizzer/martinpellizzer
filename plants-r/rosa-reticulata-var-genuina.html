Rosa reticulata var. genuina

Rosa reticulata var. genuina, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season. The plant has arching, thorned stems and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the development of many modern garden roses.
