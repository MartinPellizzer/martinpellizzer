Rosa canina f. arnbergensis

Rosa canina f. arnbergensis, commonly known as the Arnberg's dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in temperate climates.

It produces red, hairy hips that are rich in vitamin C. This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
