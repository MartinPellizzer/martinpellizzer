Ruellia nitida

Ruellia nitida, commonly known as silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Ruellia nitida is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and use in landscaping.
