Rubus ulmifolius var. cuneatus

Rubus ulmifolius var. cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the brambles native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both ecological and historical significance.
