Ranunculus strigulosus var. platyphyllus

Ranunculus strigulosus var. platyphyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
