Rhus rehmanniana var. glabrata

Rhus rehmanniana var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fuzzy fruits that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
