Rosa agrestis f. steffenii

Rosa agrestis f. steffenii, commonly known as the Steffen's brambles, is a botanical variety of the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the fruits are edible berries that develop after the flowers fade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
