Rubus confertiflorus

Rubus confertiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flower blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the蔷薇科 (Rosaceae) family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small white or pink flowers. Its fruit, a type of aggregate berry, is typically dark purple to black when ripe.

Rubus confertiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
