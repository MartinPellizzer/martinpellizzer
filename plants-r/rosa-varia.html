Rosa varia

Rosa varia, commonly known as the variable rose, is a species within the Rosaceae family characterized by its diverse floral morphology.

It typically features simple to semi-double flowers with a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, depending on the cultivar. The plant has compound, alternate leaves with serrated margins, and produces fragrant, pinkish-tinged hips in the autumn. It is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, thriving in temperate climates.

Rosa varia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different garden conditions.
