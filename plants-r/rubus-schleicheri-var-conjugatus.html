Rubus schleicheri var. conjugatus

Rubus schleicheri var. conjugatus, commonly known as the conjugate blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in early spring.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits, which are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
