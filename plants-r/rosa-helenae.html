Rosa helenae

Rosa helenae, commonly known as Helen's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, Rosa helenae is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
