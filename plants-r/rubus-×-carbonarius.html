Rubus × carbonarius

Rubus × carbonarius, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types, including clay and loam. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its growth habit includes both shrub and climbing forms, depending on environmental conditions.

The flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, contributing to its ornamental value.
