Rubus vulgaris

Rubus vulgaris, commonly known as common bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the summer. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Rubus vulgaris is valued for its ecological role, providing food for birds and insects, and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
