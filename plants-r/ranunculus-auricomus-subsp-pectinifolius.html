Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pectinifolius

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pectinifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It features palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

The flowers are bright yellow with a distinctive, cup-shaped base and numerous stamens. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, woodlands, and streambanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for garden use.
