Rubus ulmifolius var. aduncus

Rubus ulmifolius var. aduncus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a serrated margin and a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
