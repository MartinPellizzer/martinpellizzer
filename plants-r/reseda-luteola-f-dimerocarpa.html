Reseda luteola f. dimerocarpa

Reseda luteola f. dimerocarpa, commonly known as the yellow weed or lesser reseda, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are alternate, narrow, and often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, lobed leaves and its ability to produce a yellow dye from its flowers.
