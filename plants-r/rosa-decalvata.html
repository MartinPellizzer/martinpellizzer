Rosa decalvata

Rosa decalvata, commonly known as the naked-stem rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers with five petals, and its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. Rosa decalvata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
