Rhinacanthus scandens

Rhinacanthus scandens, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a hard, woody capsule.

Rhinacanthus scandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
