Rhododendron punctatum var. majus

Rhododendron punctatum var. majus, commonly known as the large-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.
