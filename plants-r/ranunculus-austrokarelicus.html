Ranunculus austrokarelicus

Ranunculus austrokarelicus, commonly known as the Karelian buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and parts of Russia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. Its botanical characteristics include a short, stout stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

Ranunculus austrokarelicus is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
