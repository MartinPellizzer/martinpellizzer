Rosa tsusimensis

Rosa tsusimensis, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven oval leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorns. Rosa tsusimensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively cold-hardy compared to other rose species.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
