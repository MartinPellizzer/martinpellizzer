Rhododendron subansiriense

Rhododendron subansiriense, commonly known as the Subansiri rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in India and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a sturdy stem, hairy young shoots, and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small wildlife.
