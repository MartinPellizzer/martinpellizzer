Rotala rotundifolia

Rotala rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf watermilfoil, is a aquatic plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its small, round leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged or floating. Rotala rotundifolia thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

It is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
