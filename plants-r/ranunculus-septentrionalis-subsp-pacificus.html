Ranunculus septentrionalis subsp. pacificus

Ranunculus septentrionalis subsp.

pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
