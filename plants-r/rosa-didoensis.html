Rosa didoensis

Rosa didoensis, commonly known as the African tea rose, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, thorny stems and dark green, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Rosa didoensis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
