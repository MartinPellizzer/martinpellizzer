Rosa biserratifolia

Rosa biserratifolia, commonly known as the serratedleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. It typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Rosa biserratifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
