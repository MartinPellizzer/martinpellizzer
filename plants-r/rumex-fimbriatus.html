Rumex fimbriatus

Rumex fimbriatus, commonly known as the hairy sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant features kidney-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and a red or purple stem that becomes more prominent as it matures. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
