Rubus fischeroosteri

Rubus fischeroosteri, commonly known as the Schischert's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
