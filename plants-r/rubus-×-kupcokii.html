Rubus × kupcokii

Rubus × kupcokii, commonly known as the Kupcokii blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a thorny shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves and white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and is valued for its hardy nature and fruit quality.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
