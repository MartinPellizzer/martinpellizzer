Rabdosia dawoensis

Rabdosia dawoensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian blue mint, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Rabdosia dawoensis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
