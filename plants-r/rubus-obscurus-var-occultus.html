Rubus obscurus var. occultus

Rubus obscurus var. occultus, commonly known as the shadow blackberry, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the blackberry genus.

It is characterized by its dark, almost obsidian-like berries and dense, thorny shrubs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are usually dark green with a slightly serrated edge, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
