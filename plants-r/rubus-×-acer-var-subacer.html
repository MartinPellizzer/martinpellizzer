Rubus × acer var. subacer

Rubus × acer var. subacer, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant that combines characteristics of blackberries and raspberries.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological and genetic variability.
