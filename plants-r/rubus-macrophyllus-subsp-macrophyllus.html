Rubus macrophyllus subsp. macrophyllus

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a dense covering of fine hairs.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black achenes surrounded by a fleshy receptacle. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its edible berries, which are used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
