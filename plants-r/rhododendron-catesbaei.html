Rhododendron catesbaei

Rhododendron catesbaei, commonly known as the Appalachian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. It is often found in mountainous regions, particularly in the Appalachian Mountains.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
