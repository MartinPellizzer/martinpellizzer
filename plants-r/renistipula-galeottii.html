Renistipula galeottii

Renistipula galeottii, commonly known as the Galeotti's thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Renistipula galeottii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
