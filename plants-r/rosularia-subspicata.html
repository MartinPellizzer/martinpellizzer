Rosularia subspicata

Rosularia subspicata, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in a spiculate inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky habitats.

Rosularia subspicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
