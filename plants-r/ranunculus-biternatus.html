Ranunculus biternatus

Ranunculus biternatus, commonly known as the three-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental members.
