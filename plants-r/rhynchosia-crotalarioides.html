Rhynchosia crotalarioides

Rhynchosia crotalarioides, commonly known as the spurred cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred calyces, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
