Rubus pungens var. discolor

Rubus pungens var. discolor, commonly known as the purple-leafed blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves that turn a striking purple color in the fall. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the summer. Its botanical features include a woody stem with persistent buds and a compound leaf structure.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
