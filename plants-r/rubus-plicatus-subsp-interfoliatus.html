Rubus plicatus subsp. interfoliatus

Rubus plicatus subsp.

interfoliatus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
