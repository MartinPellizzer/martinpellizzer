Rosa arkansana var. bushii

Rosa arkansana var. bushii, commonly known as the Arkansas rose or the sand rose, is a shrub-like species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its botanical features include stipitate stipules and stipitate leaflets, which are distinctive in appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
