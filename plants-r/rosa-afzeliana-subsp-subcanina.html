Rosa afzeliana subsp. subcanina

Rosa afzeliana subsp.

subcanina, commonly known as the African rose or mountain rose, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its botanical features include a woody structure and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
