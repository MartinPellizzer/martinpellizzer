Rhipsalis saglionis

Rhipsalis saglionis, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that often hang in cascading clusters, giving it a trailing appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

Rhipsalis saglionis is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
