Rhynchospora semiplumosa

Rhynchospora semiplumosa, commonly known as the roughside sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and other moist environments, thriving in nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with small, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-parted flowers.

Rhynchospora semiplumosa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
