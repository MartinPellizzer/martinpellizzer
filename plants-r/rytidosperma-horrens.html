Rytidosperma horrens

Rytidosperma horrens, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, wiry inflorescence that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are small and clustered in panicles. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Rytidosperma horrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
