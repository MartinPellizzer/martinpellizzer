Rebutia melanocentra

Rebutia melanocentra, commonly known as the Black Centred Rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical shape and a dense covering of fine, white to pale green hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often borne on short, erect stems. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

Rebutia melanocentra is prized in horticulture for its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
