Rhamnus guicciardii

Rhamnus guicciardii, commonly known as the Guicciardini buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn red in the fall, and its yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Rhamnus guicciardii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
