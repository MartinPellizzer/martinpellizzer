Rhododendron ponticum f. aurantiacomaculatum

Rhododendron ponticum f. aurantiacomaculatum, commonly known as the orange-spotted rhododendron, is a hybrid form of Rhododendron ponticum.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-red spots on the leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant is a shrub with dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
