Rubus radula var. sparsisetus

Rubus radula var. sparsisetus, commonly known as the sparse-hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, where it spreads through both vegetative growth and seed dispersal.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and its role in local ecosystems.
