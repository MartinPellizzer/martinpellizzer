Rhododendron wrayi

Rhododendron wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist, and shaded forest environments. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Rhododendron wrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific soil and climate conditions.
