Rosa canina var. montezumae

Rosa canina var. montezumae, commonly known as the Mexican rose or Montezuma's rose, is a thorny shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, hairy berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates. Its berries are edible but have a tart flavor, often used in jams and traditional remedies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
