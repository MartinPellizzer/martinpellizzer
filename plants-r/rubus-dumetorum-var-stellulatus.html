Rubus dumetorum var. stellulatus

Rubus dumetorum var. stellulatus, commonly known as the star-shaped blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are typically clustered in a star-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant has prickly stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its berries are edible but are generally less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties.
