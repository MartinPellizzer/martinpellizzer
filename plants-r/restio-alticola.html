Restio alticola

Restio alticola, commonly known as the mountain restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, thriving in coastal and inland areas with moderate rainfall.

Restio alticola is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of the local flora.
