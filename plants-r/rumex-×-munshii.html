Rumex × munshii

Rumex × munshii, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-veined. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations compared to its parent species.
