Rosa molliretusata

Rosa molliretusata, commonly known as the soft-leaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Native to temperate regions of Asia, this plant typically grows in woodland or shaded areas. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
