Rhamnus timoriensis

Rhamnus timoriensis, commonly known as the Timor buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
