Rhododendron × xanthanthum

Rhododendron × xanthanthum, commonly known as the golden rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits striking yellow floral bracts.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include showy, clustered flowers that are often borne on long, flexible peduncles.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
