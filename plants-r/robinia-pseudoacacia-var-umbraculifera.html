Robinia pseudoacacia var. umbraculifera

Robinia pseudoacacia var. umbraculifera, commonly known as the Umbrella-leaf false acacia, is a variant of the honey locust tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-shaped compound leaves that provide shade. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow flowers in spring, followed by flat, dehiscent pods.

The leaves are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and shade-providing capacity.
