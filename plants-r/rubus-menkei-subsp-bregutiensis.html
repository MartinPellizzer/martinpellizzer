Rubus menkei subsp. bregutiensis

Rubus menkei subsp.

bregutiensis, commonly known as the Bregutiensis blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
