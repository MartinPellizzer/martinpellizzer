Rosa rubiginosa subsp. umbellata

Rosa rubiginosa subsp.

umbellata, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy grayish-white foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces oval, hairy fruits called rosehips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
