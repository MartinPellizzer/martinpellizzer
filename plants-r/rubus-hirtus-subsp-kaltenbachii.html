Rubus hirtus subsp. kaltenbachii

Rubus hirtus subsp.

kaltenbachii, commonly known as the Kaltenbach's bramble, is a shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils, and is often found in coastal regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific morphology and geographic distribution compared to other Rubus species.
