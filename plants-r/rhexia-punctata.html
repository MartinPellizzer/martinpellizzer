Rhexia punctata

Rhexia punctata, commonly known as the spotted beargrass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils within woodlands and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers. Its flowers are often adorned with distinctive red or purple spots, which give it its common name.

Rhexia punctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
