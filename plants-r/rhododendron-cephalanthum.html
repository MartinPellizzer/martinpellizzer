Rhododendron cephalanthum

Rhododendron cephalanthum, commonly known as the purpleleaf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, upright growth with broad, ovate leaves that are typically purple-tinged on the underside. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of pink to red flowers in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
