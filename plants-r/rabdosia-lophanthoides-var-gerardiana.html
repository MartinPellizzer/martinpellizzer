Rabdosia lophanthoides var. gerardiana

Rabdosia lophanthoides var. gerardiana, commonly known as the mountain rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in forested or shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
