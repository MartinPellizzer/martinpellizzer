Rosa micrantha subvar. septicoloides

Rosa micrantha subvar. septicoloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dogwood rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in pink to white hues. The plant features narrow, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its stems are thorny, and it produces rose hips that are red and somewhat spherical.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
