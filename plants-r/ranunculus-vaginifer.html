Ranunculus vaginifer

Ranunculus vaginifer, commonly known as the hooded buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically lobed or deeply divided. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus vaginifer thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
