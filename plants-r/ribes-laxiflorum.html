Ribes laxiflorum

Ribes laxiflorum, commonly known as the laxiflorous gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of gooseberries and currants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its berries, which are typically red or yellow, are edible and often used in jams and jellies.

Ribes laxiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and insects.
