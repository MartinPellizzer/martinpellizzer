Ranunculus longifolius

Ranunculus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, grassy areas and along stream banks. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed, often with a distinctive long, narrow shape. It produces small, green fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus longifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
