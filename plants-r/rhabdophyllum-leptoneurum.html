Rhabdophyllum leptoneurum

Rhabdophyllum leptoneurum, commonly known as the slender-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a distinctive inflorescence that bears showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and have a smooth, waxy surface, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture efficiently.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
