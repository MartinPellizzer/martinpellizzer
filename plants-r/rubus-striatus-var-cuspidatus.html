Rubus striatus var. cuspidatus

Rubus striatus var. cuspidatus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature, containing numerous small, seeded drupes. Its leaves are compound, with serrated margins and a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
