Rosa mollis subf. irrotundans

Rosa mollis subf.

irrotundans, commonly known as the rounded-leaf dogwood rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of China. It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are ovate to rounded in shape, with a soft, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators.
