Ranunculus auricomus subsp. zaplatyoides

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

zaplatyoides, commonly known as the Zaplatyoides buttercup, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger and more robust foliage compared to the typical R. auricomus. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is an important component of wetland ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
