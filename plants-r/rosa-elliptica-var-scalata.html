Rosa elliptica var. scalata

Rosa elliptica var. scalata, commonly known as the scalycup rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems that bear alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and are surrounded by a distinctive, scalloped calyx that remains attached after blooming.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
