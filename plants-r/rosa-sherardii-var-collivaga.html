Rosa sherardii var. collivaga

Rosa sherardii var. collivaga, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white down. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beautiful blooms and aromatic scent.
