Ranunculus makaschwilii

Ranunculus makaschwilii, commonly known as the Makaschwilii buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that disperse its seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
