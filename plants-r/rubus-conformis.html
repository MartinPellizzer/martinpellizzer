Rubus conformis

Rubus conformis, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and prickly stems, which are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and apples.
