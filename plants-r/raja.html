Raja

Raja, commonly known as the royal plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces strikingly colorful flowers, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Raja is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
