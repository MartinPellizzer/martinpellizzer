Romulea setifolia var. belviderica

Romulea setifolia var. belviderica, commonly known as the Belvidere glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and early blooming habit.

It is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
