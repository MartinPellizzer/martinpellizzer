Rubus radula microgene pauciglandulosus

Rubus radula microgene pauciglandulosus, commonly known as the slender raspberry, is a species of brambles native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, thornless stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters, and the plant produces small, red berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ecologically important member of the Rosaceae family.
