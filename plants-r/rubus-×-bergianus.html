Rubus × bergianus

Rubus × bergianus, commonly known as the Bergian blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus chamaemorus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in boreal and alpine regions.

Its botanical features make it a hardy species well adapted to harsh climates.
