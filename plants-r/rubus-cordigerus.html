Rubus cordigerus

Rubus cordigerus, commonly known as the blackberry plant, is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets. The plant is native to the Americas and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants.

Rubus cordigerus is often cultivated for its flavorful fruit and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
