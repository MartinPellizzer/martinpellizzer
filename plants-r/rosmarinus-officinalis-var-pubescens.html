Rosmarinus officinalis var. pubescens

Rosmarinus officinalis var. pubescens, commonly known as woolly rosemary, is a variant of the common rosemary plant characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage.

This cultivar features silver-gray leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Woolly rosemary is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Its essential oils, like those of other rosemary varieties, are valued for their fragrance and potential medicinal uses.
