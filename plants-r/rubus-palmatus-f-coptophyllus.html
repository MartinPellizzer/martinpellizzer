Rubus palmatus f. coptophyllus

Rubus palmatus f. coptophyllus, commonly known as the red-leaf raspberry, is a botanical variant of the common raspberry.

It is characterized by its distinctive red foliage, which contrasts with the typical green leaves of the species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
