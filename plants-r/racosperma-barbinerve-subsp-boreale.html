Racosperma barbinerve subsp. boreale

Racosperma barbinerve subsp.

boreale, commonly known as the northern hairy wattle, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The leaves are narrow and hairy, with a distinctive ribbed structure, and the plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. It is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
