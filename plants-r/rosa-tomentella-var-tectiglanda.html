Rosa tomentella var. tectiglanda

Rosa tomentella var. tectiglanda, commonly known as the woolly rose or the hairy rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide protection against herbivores. The leaves are typically arranged in alternating patterns and have a soft, fuzzy texture. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, blooming in early spring.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
