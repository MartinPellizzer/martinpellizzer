Ranunculus lateriflorus

Ranunculus lateriflorus, commonly known as the western buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a dark central blotch. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

Ranunculus lateriflorus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
