Rubus × pleioplon

Rubus × pleioplon, commonly known as the blackberry bramble.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is a type of drupe, is valued for its sweet-tart flavor and is commonly used in culinary applications.
