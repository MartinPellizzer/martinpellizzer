Rokautskyia pseudoscaposa

Rokautskyia pseudoscaposa, commonly known as the false scabious, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species, often found in high-altitude environments where it withstands cold and harsh conditions.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
