Rubus rudis var. amplus

Rubus rudis var. amplus, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry.

It is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its broader leaves and slightly different growth habits compared to the standard Rubus rudis. This plant typically thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, edible berries similar to other raspberries.

The botanical features of this variety make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and cultivation.
