Rosa moschata carnea

Rosa moschata carnea, commonly known as the pink musk rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their strong, musky scent.

Rosa moschata carnea is native to Central Asia and is cultivated for both ornamental and aromatic purposes.
