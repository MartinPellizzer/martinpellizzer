Rhododendron occidentale var. paludosum

Rhododendron occidentale var. paludosum, commonly known as the western azalea, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. This variety thrives in moist, acidic soils and partial to full shade, making it well-suited for woodland gardens. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, providing year-round interest.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role, supporting pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
