Rosenia humilis

Rosenia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf rose of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to its mountainous environment.

Rosenia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
