Rubus fuscoater var. candicans

Rubus fuscoater var. candicans, commonly known as the golden raspberry, is a variant of the bramble plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and the fruit is a small, aggregate berry that ripens to a reddish color. This plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries.
