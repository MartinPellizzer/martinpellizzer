Rubus × vendeanus

Rubus × vendeanus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus allegheniensis (Allegheny blackberry) and Rubus strigosus (prickly blackberry). The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. It has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus.

This plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
