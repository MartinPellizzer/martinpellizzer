Rapuntium xalapense

Rapuntium xalapense, commonly known as the Mexican watercress, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like broccoli and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found near streams or in humid forests.

Rapuntium xalapense is valued for its peppery flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
