Rubus × pseudodegener f. supercaesius

Rubus × pseudodegener f. supercaesius, commonly known as the purple-leafed blackberry, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dark purple foliage and small, edible berries that have a tart flavor. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardening and small-scale farming.
