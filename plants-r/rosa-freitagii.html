Rosa freitagii

Rosa freitagii, commonly known as the Freitag's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red berries that contain seeds.

Rosa freitagii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
