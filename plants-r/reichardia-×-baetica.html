Reichardia × baetica

Reichardia × baetica, commonly known as the Spanish dandelion, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in disturbed soils and sunny environments. The plant is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and cultivated areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed with ecological and potential medicinal significance.
