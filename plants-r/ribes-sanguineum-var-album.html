Ribes sanguineum var. album

Ribes sanguineum var. album, commonly known as white currant, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
