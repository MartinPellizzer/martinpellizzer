Ranunculus bulbosus var. petiolulatus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. petiolulatus, commonly known as the narrow petioled buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to seven sepals that are typically yellow or white in color. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces small, rounded leaves with a distinctive basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant.
