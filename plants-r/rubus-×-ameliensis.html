Rubus × ameliensis

Rubus × ameliensis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
