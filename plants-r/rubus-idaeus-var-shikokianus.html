Rubus idaeus var. shikokianus

Rubus idaeus var. shikokianus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, though they may vary in color depending on the cultivar. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its culinary uses.
