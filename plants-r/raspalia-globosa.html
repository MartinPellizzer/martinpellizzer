Raspalia globosa

Raspalia globosa, commonly known as the globe raspal, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical shape and dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines for their sweet and tangy flavor. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a lush appearance in gardens.

Raspalia globosa thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
