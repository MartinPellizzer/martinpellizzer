Rubus lasiostylus

Rubus lasiostylus, commonly known as the rough-styled blackberry, is a species of bramble native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest margins and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
