Rubus calycinus

Rubus calycinus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
