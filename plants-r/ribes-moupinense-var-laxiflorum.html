Ribes moupinense var. laxiflorum

Ribes moupinense var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the laxiflorous gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. The leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in cool, moist environments. It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential culinary uses.

This variety is relatively rare and often found in specific alpine habitats.
