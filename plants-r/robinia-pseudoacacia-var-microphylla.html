Robinia pseudoacacia var. microphylla

Robinia pseudoacacia var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved false acacia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically no more than 2.5 cm in length, contrasting with the larger leaves of the typical *Robinia pseudoacacia*. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in upright clusters during late spring to early summer. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
