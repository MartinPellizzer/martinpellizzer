Ranunculus strigillosus

Ranunculus strigillosus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus strigillosus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful flowers.
