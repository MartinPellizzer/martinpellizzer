Rubus serpens proles convexifolius

Rubus serpens proles convexifolius, commonly known as the convex-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and has compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and along riverbanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
