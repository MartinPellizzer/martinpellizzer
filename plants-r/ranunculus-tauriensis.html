Ranunculus tauriensis

Ranunculus tauriensis, commonly known as the Taurian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, lobed leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
