Romulea speciosa

Romulea speciosa, commonly known as the showy romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and gladiolus. The plant is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It produces basal leaves that are grass-like and often covered in a waxy coating.

Romulea speciosa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
