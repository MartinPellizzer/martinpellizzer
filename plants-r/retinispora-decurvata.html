Retinispora decurvata

Retinispora decurvata, commonly known as the curly root vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its twisted, serpentine stems that wrap around supports as it grows. The leaves are typically ovate with a pointed tip and are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
