Rosa micrantha var. pseudotomentosa

Rosa micrantha var. pseudotomentosa, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant is characterized by its silvery, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal floral display.
