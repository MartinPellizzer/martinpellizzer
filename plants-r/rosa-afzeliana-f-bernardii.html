Rosa afzeliana f. bernardii

Rosa afzeliana f. bernardii, commonly known as the Bernard's rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with flowers that are typically pink to red in color. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double blooms that are often clustered together, adding visual interest to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical conservation efforts.
