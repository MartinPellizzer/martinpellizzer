Rumex cristatus

Rumex cristatus, commonly known as curly dock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curly, wavy-edged leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are borne on tall, slender stems. Its root system is extensive and can become invasive in certain environments.

Rumex cristatus thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands, disturbed areas, and along roadsides.
