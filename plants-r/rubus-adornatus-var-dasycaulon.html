Rubus adornatus var. dasycaulon

Rubus adornatus var. dasycaulon, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
