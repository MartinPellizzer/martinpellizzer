Rosa silesiaca

Rosa silesiaca, commonly known as the Silesian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Central Europe, particularly found in regions such as Poland, Germany, and the Czech Republic. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a fragrant aroma.

Rosa silesiaca is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been historically cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
