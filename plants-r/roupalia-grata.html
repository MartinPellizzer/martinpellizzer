Roupalia grata

Roupalia grata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered together. Roupalia grata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some cultures.
