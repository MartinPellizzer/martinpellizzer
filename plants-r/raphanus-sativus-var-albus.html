Raphanus sativus var. albus

Raphanus sativus var. albus, commonly known as the white radish, is a cultivar of the radish plant characterized by its pale, often white or pale yellow flesh.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. The plant typically has a taproot that is the primary edible part, which can vary in size and shape depending on the variety. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown as a root vegetable in temperate regions.

The leaves are also edible and can be used in salads or cooked like spinach.
