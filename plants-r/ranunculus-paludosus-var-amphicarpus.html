Ranunculus paludosus var. amphicarpus

Ranunculus paludosus var. amphicarpus, commonly known as the marsh buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in muddy or waterlogged soils. The plant has a short, stout stem and heart-shaped leaves that are usually submerged or floating. It is an aquatic or semi-aquatic species, often found in ponds, ditches, and marshes.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it plays a role in supporting aquatic insect populations.
