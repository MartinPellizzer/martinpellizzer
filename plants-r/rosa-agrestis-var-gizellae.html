Rosa agrestis var. gizellae

Rosa agrestis var. gizellae, commonly known as the Gizella wild rose, is a shrub-like species native to specific regions of South America.

It typically features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and a fragrant scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hardy ornamental plant.

This variety is noted for its resilience and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
