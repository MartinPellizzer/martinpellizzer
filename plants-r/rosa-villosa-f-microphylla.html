Rosa villosa f. microphylla

Rosa villosa f. microphylla, commonly known as the miniature pink bush rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It features small, velvety leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, pink flowers throughout the growing season. The plant is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant due to its compact size and ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its horticultural value lies in its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
