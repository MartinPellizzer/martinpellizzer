Rabdosia coetsoides

Rabdosia coetsoides, commonly known as the mountain rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Rabdosia coetsoides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
