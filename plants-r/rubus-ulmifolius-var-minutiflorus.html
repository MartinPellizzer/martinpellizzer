Rubus ulmifolius var. minutiflorus

Rubus ulmifolius var. minutiflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are small, typically pink or white, and appear in clusters during the spring. The plant produces red or black berries that are edible but often tart.

It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
