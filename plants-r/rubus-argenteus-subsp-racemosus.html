Rubus argenteus subsp. racemosus

Rubus argenteus subsp.

racemosus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species native to the western United States. It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often in mountainous regions.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are followed by edible, tart berries. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
