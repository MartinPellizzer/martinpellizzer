Rhodiola stephani var. longifolia

Rhodiola stephani var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cold and high-altitude conditions. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to store water and nutrients efficiently, adapting to harsh climates.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.
