Rubus vulgaris subsp. lindleyanus

Rubus vulgaris subsp.

lindleyanus, commonly known as bramble blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets that are typically dark red to black in color.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and bears white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

The species is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along the edges of fields.
