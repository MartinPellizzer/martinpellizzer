Rhytiglossa divergens

Rhytiglossa divergens, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that display a range of hues, often resembling a rainbow, hence its common name. The flowers are typically arranged in dense inflorescences and are known for their intricate, showy morphology. Rhytiglossa divergens thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing as an epiphyte on trees or rocks.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
