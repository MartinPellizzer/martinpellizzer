Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. cassubicus

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

cassubicus, commonly known as the European buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

The plant features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to seven sepals that resemble petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, glossy appearance.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.
