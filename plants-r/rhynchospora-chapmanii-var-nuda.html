Rhynchospora chapmanii var. nuda

Rhynchospora chapmanii var. nuda, commonly known as Chapman's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. The plant produces small, three-lobed berries that are dispersed by water or animals. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator species for wetland health.
