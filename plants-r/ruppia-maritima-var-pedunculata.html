Ruppia maritima var. pedunculata

Ruppia maritima var. pedunculata, commonly known as the sea rocket, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is a submerged aquatic plant with slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The species thrives in coastal areas, including salt marshes and brackish water habitats. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, and it produces small, floating fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

This variant is closely related to the main species Ruppia maritima and is often found in similar ecological niches.
