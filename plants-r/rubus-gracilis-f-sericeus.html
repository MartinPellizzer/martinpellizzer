Rubus gracilis f. sericeus

Rubus gracilis f. sericeus, commonly known as the silky raspberry, is a botanical variant of the common raspberry.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-white hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
