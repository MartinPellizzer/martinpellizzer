Rosa scabrata f. wiesbauriana

Rosa scabrata f. wiesbauriana, commonly known as the Wiesbaur rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features upright stems covered in thorny buds and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly rough texture. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
