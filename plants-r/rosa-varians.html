Rosa varians

Rosa varians, commonly known as the variable rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it typically grows in woodland and shrubland habitats. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. Rosa varians is a deciduous shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with thorny stems and showy, five-petaled blooms.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
