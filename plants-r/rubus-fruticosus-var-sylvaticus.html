Rubus fruticosus var. sylvaticus

Rubus fruticosus var. sylvaticus, commonly known as bramble or blackberry bush, is a shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that develop into edible blackberries. The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is a perennial species, often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed areas.

Its fruit is rich in antioxidants and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
