Rubus macrostachys var. goetzianus

Rubus macrostachys var. goetzianus, commonly known as the Goetz's raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticulture and plant taxonomy.
