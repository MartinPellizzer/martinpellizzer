Racosperma marramamba

Racosperma marramamba, commonly known as the marramamba acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through root nodules. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

The seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh climates.
