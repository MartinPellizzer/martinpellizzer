Rubus × ambigens

Rubus × ambigens, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry shrub, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many bramble species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are often referred to as blackberries, and are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
