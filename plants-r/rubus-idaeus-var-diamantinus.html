Rubus idaeus var. diamantinus

Rubus idaeus var. diamantinus, commonly known as the diamond raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its dense, thorned stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens. The fruit, which is a aggregate of small drupes, is usually bright red and has a tart, sweet flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit characteristics.
