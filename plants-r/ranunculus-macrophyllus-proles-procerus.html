Ranunculus macrophyllus proles procerus

Ranunculus macrophyllus proles procerus, commonly known as the large-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive central blotch. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically large and hairy, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
