Rubus laudatus

Rubus laudatus, commonly known as the sweet blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet and tart flavor. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

Botanically, Rubus laudatus is part of the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and apples.
