Rubus weberbaueri

Rubus weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer blackberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
