Rubus hebecarpos var. terribilis

Rubus hebecarpos var. terribilis, commonly known as the devil's paintbrush, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
