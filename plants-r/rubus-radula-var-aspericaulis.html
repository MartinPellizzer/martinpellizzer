Rubus radula var. aspericaulis

Rubus radula var. aspericaulis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured stems.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
