Racosperma cincinnatum

Racosperma cincinnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are typical of the wattles, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil.
