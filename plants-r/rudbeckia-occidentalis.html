Rudbeckia occidentalis

Rudbeckia occidentalis, commonly known as western coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow to orange flowers with a dark central disk, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Rudbeckia occidentalis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
