Ricinus communis f. laevis

Ricinus communis f. laevis, commonly known as the smooth castor plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant produces distinctive spiny capsules containing toxic seeds, which are used in the production of castor oil. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and often have a velvety texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine and industry.
