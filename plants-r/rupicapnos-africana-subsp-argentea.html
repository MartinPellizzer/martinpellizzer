Rupicapnos africana subsp. argentea

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf rupicapnos, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments. Its flowers are small and tubular, often arranged in clusters, and it produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
