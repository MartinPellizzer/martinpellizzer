Rosa coriifolia var. pastoralis

Rosa coriifolia var. pastoralis, commonly known as the pasture rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
