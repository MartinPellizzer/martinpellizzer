Rhynchosia striata

Rhynchosia striata, commonly known as the striated burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by long, cylindrical pods containing numerous seeds. Rhynchosia striata is often found in disturbed areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural research.
