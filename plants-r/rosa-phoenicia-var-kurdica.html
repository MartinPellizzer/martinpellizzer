Rosa phoenicia var. kurdica

Rosa phoenicia var. kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a reddish tinge. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
