Rosa × dumetorum var. popowii

Rosa × dumetorum var. popowii, commonly known as the alpine rose or rock rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to cold, rocky environments. It produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically dark green, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant exhibits a compact growth habit.
