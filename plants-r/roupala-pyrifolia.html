Roupala pyrifolia

Roupala pyrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces long, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its winged seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in urban environments.
