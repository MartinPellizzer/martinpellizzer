Rubus ulmifolius microgene tiliiformis

Rubus ulmifolius microgene tiliiformis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a subspecies of the common blackberry.

It is a shrub that produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with a distinctive, narrow, and elongated shape. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its flowers are white and clustered, and it is known for its edible fruit, though it is less commonly cultivated than other Rubus species.
