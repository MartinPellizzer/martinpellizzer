Ranunculus hungaricus

Ranunculus hungaricus, commonly known as the Hungarian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Europe.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and prefers moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
