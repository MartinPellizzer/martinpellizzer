Rubus hirtus var. adauctus

Rubus hirtus var. adauctus, commonly known as the prickly raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
