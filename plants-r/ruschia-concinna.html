Ruschia concinna

Ruschia concinna, commonly known as the dwarf firecracker, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially in full sun. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that resemble tiny firecrackers, giving it its common name. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Ruschia concinna is popular in rock gardens and as a container plant due to its compact size and vibrant appearance.
