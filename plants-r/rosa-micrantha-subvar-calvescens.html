Rosa micrantha subvar. calvescens

Rosa micrantha subvar. calvescens, commonly known as the California wild rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features thorny stems and fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its fruits are red, hairy rose hips. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
