Ratibida columnaris var. pulcherrima

Ratibida columnaris var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a striking perennial plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy yellow-orange flower heads with a distinctive brown center. The plant produces ray florets that resemble petals, giving it a daisy-like appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features include rough, hairy leaves and a long flowering period from summer to fall.
