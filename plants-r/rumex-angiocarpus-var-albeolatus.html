Rumex angiocarpus var. albeolatus

Rumex angiocarpus var. albeolatus, commonly known as the white-veined dock, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, lanceolate leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically surrounded by bracts. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

The leaves are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
