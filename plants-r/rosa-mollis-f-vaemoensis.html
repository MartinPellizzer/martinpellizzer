Rosa mollis f. vaemoensis

Rosa mollis f. vaemoensis, commonly known as the Vaemoan rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa mollis, characterized by its unique floral and foliar features.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern South America, specifically in the Andes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive velvety texture. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in temperate climates.
