Rubus tereticaulis var. ctenodon

Rubus tereticaulis var. ctenodon, commonly known as the hooked blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, and the plant thrives in moist, wooded areas.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are often used in jams and preserves.
