Rubus incurvatus var. typicus

Rubus incurvatus var. typicus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
