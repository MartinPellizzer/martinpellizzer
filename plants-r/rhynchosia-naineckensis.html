Rhynchosia naineckensis

Rhynchosia naineckensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bean, is a climbing legume native to Madagascar.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, heart-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers. The plant bears pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
