Rhododendron campanulatum var. mirendum

Rhododendron campanulatum var. mirendum, commonly known as the Mirendum rhododendron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stem.

This variety is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like China and Vietnam.
