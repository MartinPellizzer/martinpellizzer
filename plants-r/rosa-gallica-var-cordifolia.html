Rosa gallica var. cordifolia

Rosa gallica var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved gallica rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its deep red, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate with a heart-shaped base, giving it its common name. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
