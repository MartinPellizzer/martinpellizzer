Rubus × fonticola

Rubus × fonticola, commonly known as the water raspberry, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in moist, often wet environments such as stream banks and wetlands.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to hybridize with other Rubus species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
