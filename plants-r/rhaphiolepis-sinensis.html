Rhaphiolepis sinensis

Rhaphiolepis sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, glossy dark green leaves and produces clusters of bright red berries that persist through winter. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant color and hardy nature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, so caution is advised when handling or planting this species.
