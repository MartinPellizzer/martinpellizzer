Rubus milliformis

Rubus milliformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and local biodiversity.
