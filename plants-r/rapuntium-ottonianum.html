Rapuntium ottonianum

Rapuntium ottonianum, commonly known as the Otto's cress, is a flowering plant native to Central and Southern Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are pinnately divided with a distinctive, peppery aroma.

The plant is known for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
