Renealmia africana

Renealmia africana, commonly known as African reed or renealmia grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
