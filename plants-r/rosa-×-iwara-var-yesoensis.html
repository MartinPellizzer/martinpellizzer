Rosa × iwara var. yesoensis

Rosa × iwara var. yesoensis, commonly known as the Yeso rose, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage is dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to temperate climates.
