Reseda lanceolata var. neglecta

Reseda lanceolata var. neglecta, commonly known as the slender reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of Mediterranean and temperate climates.
