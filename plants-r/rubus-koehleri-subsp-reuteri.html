Rubus koehleri subsp. reuteri

Rubus koehleri subsp.

reuteri, commonly known as the Reuter's blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal habitats.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them useful in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
