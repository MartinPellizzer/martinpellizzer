Rosa schlosseri

Rosa schlosseri, commonly known as the Schlosseri rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa schlosseri is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
