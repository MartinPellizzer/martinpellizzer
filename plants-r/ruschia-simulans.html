Ruschia simulans

Ruschia simulans, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
