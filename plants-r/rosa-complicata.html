Rosa complicata

Rosa complicata, commonly known as the clustered rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, wooded areas. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Rosa complicata is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
