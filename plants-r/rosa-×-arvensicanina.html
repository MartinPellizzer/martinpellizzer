Rosa × arvensicanina

Rosa × arvensicanina, commonly known as the field rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant flowers that typically range from white to pink in color. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds, and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

This species is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
