Rubus anamphiestus

Rubus anamphiestus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade. Its flowers are white to pale pink and appear in clusters, while its fruit is often used in jams and preserves.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species.
