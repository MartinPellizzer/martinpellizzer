Rubus tereticaulis var. eochromus

Rubus tereticaulis var. eochromus, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible berries.
