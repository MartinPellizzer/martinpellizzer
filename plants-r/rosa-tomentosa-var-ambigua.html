Rosa tomentosa var. ambigua

Rosa tomentosa var. ambigua, commonly known as the fuzzy rose or woolly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and stems covered in soft, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a velvety texture.

Rosa tomentosa var. ambigua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in cooler climates.
