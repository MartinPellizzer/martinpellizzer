Rosa tomentosa var. coincyana

Rosa tomentosa var. coincyana, commonly known as the Coincy's fuzzy rose, is a shrub-like plant native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, grayish-white pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
