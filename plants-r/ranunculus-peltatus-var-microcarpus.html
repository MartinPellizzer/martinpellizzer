Ranunculus peltatus var. microcarpus

Ranunculus peltatus var. microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a distinctive lobed structure, and its flowers are typically bright yellow with numerous petals. The plant produces small, rounded fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
