Ribes inerme f. incisum

Ribes inerme f. incisum, commonly known as the Smooth Gooseberry, is a variant of the Smooth Gooseberry plant native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and elliptical leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
