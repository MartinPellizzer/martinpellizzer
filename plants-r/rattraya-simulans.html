Rattraya simulans

Rattraya simulans, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often translucent leaves. The plant lacks traditional roots and instead absorbs moisture and nutrients through its leaves. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

Due to its habitat specificity and endangered status, it is highly sought after in conservation efforts.
