Rhododendron mariae subsp. flumineum

Rhododendron mariae subsp.

flumineum, commonly known as the river rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
