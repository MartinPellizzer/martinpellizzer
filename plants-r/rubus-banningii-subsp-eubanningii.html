Rubus banningii subsp. eubanningii

Rubus banningii subsp.

eubanningii, commonly known as the Banning's blackberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
