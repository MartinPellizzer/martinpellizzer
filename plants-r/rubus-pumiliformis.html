Rubus pumiliformis

Rubus pumiliformis, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces tiny, edible berries that are often clustered together, similar to other Rubus species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
