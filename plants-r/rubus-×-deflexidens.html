Rubus × deflexidens

Rubus × deflexidens, commonly known as the Evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to western North America and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
