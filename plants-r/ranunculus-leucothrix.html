Ranunculus leucothrix

Ranunculus leucothrix, commonly known as the white crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually five-petaled flowers. The plant features finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with numerous stamens and a central pistil.

Ranunculus leucothrix thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
