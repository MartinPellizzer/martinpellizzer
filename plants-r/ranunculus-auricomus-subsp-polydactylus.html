Ranunculus auricomus subsp. polydactylus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

polydactylus, commonly known as the golden shield or yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petal-like sepals, which give it a distinctive, shield-like appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
