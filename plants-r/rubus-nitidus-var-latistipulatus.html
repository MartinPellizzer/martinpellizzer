Rubus nitidus var. latistipulatus

Rubus nitidus var. latistipulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its broad, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
