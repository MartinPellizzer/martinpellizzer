Ribes komarovii var. cuneifolium

Ribes komarovii var. cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems with thorn-like branches and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that range in color from yellow to red. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
