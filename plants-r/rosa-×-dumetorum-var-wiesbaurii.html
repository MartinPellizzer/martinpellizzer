Rosa × dumetorum var. wiesbaurii

Rosa × dumetorum var. wiesbaurii, commonly known as the Wiesbaur rose, is a hybrid shrub native to Central Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
