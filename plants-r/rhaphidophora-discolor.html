Rhaphidophora discolor

Rhaphidophora discolor, commonly known as the Variegated Snake Plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves with a mix of green, white, and cream colors. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family. It is a popular houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and low maintenance requirements.

Rhaphidophora discolor thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.
