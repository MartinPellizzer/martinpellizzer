Rhabdophyllum penicillatum

Rhabdophyllum penicillatum, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include a pseudobulb structure and roots adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
