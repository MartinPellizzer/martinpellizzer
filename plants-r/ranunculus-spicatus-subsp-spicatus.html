Ranunculus spicatus subsp. spicatus

Ranunculus spicatus subsp.

spicatus, commonly known as the spike ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with deeply lobed segments, and the plant often grows in moist, grassy habitats. It produces numerous tiny, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and early spring blooming habit.
