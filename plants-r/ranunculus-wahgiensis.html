Ranunculus wahgiensis

Ranunculus wahgiensis, commonly known as the Wahgi buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Papua, Indonesia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
