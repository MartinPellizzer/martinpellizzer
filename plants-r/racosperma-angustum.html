Racosperma angustum

Racosperma angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a species of shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as reduced leaf surface area.

Its seeds are encased in hard pods, which contribute to its ecological role in soil stabilization and provide food for various wildlife species.
