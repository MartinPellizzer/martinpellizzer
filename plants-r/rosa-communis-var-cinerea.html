Rosa communis var. cinerea

Rosa communis var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray rose, is a variety of the common rose characterized by its distinctive grayish-blue petals.

This plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in temperate climates. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with thorny stems and dark green foliage. The flowers are usually pink to white in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
