Rosa sandbergii

Rosa sandbergii, commonly known as the sandberg rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its fruits are hip-like structures that contain seeds. Rosa sandbergii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and wildlife.
