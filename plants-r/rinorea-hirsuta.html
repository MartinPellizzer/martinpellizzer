Rinorea hirsuta

Rinorea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy rinorea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in terminal or axillary inflorescences, with white or pale yellow petals.

Rinorea hirsuta is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
