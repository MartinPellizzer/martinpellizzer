Rhododendron chrysanthum

Rhododendron chrysanthum, commonly known as the golden rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five to six petals that range in color from white to yellow, often with a reddish or pinkish tinge.

Rhododendron chrysanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
