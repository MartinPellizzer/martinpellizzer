Restrepia tsubotae

Restrepia tsubotae, commonly known as the Tsubota's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth. The plant's pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to survive in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and delicate blooms, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
