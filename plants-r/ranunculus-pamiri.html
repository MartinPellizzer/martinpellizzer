Ranunculus pamiri

Ranunculus pamiri, commonly known as the Pamir buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Ranunculus pamiri is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
