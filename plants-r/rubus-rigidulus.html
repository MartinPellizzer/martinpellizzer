Rubus rigidulus

Rubus rigidulus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and the plant is often used in local ecosystems for its role in supporting wildlife and soil stabilization.
