Rubus × pseudomuelleri

Rubus × pseudomuelleri, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which consist of small, dark purple to black drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet-tart flavor.

The botanical features of Rubus × pseudomuelleri make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
