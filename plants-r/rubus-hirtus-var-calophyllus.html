Rubus hirtus var. calophyllus

Rubus hirtus var. calophyllus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. The plant has prickly stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The variety calophyllus is distinguished by its larger, more showy flowers compared to other Rubus species.
