Rubus caesarius

Rubus caesarius, commonly known as the purple raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically a deep purple or black when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
