Rosa gallica f. srebrenicensis

Rosa gallica f. srebrenicensis, commonly known as the Srebrenica rose, is a rare and historically significant botanical variant of Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its deep red, semi-double petals and a compact, bushy growth habit. Native to the Balkans, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and landscapes.

Due to its historical and cultural importance, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and symbolic meaning.
