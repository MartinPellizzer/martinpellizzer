Rhus sonderi

Rhus sonderi, commonly known as the Natal holly, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It features dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. Rhus sonderi is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
