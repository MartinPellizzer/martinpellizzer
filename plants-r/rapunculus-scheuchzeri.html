Rapunculus scheuchzeri

Rapunculus scheuchzeri, commonly known as the Alpine cuckoo's horn, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and somewhat succulent.

The fruit is a dry silique, containing several small seeds that are dispersed by wind.
