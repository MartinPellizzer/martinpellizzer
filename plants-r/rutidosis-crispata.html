Rutidosis crispata

Rutidosis crispata, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open areas. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

Rutidosis crispata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
