Rhynchospora rigidifolia

Rhynchospora rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. Rhynchospora rigidifolia thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is often found in areas with standing water or saturated soil.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
