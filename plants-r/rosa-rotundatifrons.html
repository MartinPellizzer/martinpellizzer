Rosa rotundatifrons

Rosa rotundatifrons, commonly known as the rounded-leaved rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

The species is also valued for its potential in horticultural breeding programs.
