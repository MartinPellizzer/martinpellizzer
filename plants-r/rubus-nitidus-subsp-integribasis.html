Rubus nitidus subsp. integribasis

Rubus nitidus subsp.

integribasis, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thornless stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy underside. Its flowers are white with five petals and are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its edible fruit and is often found in woodland edges and moist habitats.
