Rotheca luembensis subsp. niassensis

Rotheca luembensis subsp.

niassensis, commonly known as the Niassan rotheca, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to Central and Eastern Africa. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, forested areas.

The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and fibrous roots, which help it thrive in its natural habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
