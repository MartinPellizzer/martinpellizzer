Rubus sprengelii var. nemorivagus

Rubus sprengelii var. nemorivagus, commonly known as the forest raspberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
