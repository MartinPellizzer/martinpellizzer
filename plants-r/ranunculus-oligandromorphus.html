Ranunculus oligandromorphus

Ranunculus oligandromorphus, commonly known as the few-stamen buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
