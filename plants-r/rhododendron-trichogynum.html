Rhododendron trichogynum

Rhododendron trichogynum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron trichogynum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
