Ranunculus hirsutulus

Ranunculus hirsutulus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.

Ranunculus hirsutulus is an herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
