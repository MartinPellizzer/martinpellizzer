Rubus coptophyllus var. dissectus

Rubus coptophyllus var. dissectus, commonly known as the dissected blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its finely lobed leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, feathery appearance.

The species is also valued for its potential in horticultural breeding programs.
