Rubus × submultifidus

Rubus × submultifidus, commonly known as the multifidus blackberry, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and is cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
