Rosa villosa var. vayredae

Rosa villosa var. vayredae, commonly known as the Vayreda rose, is a variant of the woolly rose native to the mountainous regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy gray foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and adaptability to temperate climates.

It is also noted for its resistance to certain diseases, making it a desirable choice for gardeners in suitable regions.
