Rubus rudis var. brevithyrsus

Rubus rudis var. brevithyrsus, commonly known as the short-thyrsus blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible berries, though it can be challenging to cultivate due to its vigorous growth habit.
