Rubus procerus var. mollitus

Rubus procerus var. mollitus, commonly known as the soft-stemmed blackberry bush, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
