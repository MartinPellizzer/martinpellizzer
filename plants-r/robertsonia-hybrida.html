Robertsonia hybrida

Robertsonia hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid false heather, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Robertsonia hybrida thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and pine forests.
