Rubus × gothicus var. raduliformis

Rubus × gothicus var. raduliformis, commonly known as the Gothic Raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically red or black in color. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated margins, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
