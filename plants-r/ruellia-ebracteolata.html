Ruellia ebracteolata

Ruellia ebracteolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are typically blue or violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ruellia ebracteolata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and showy blooms.
