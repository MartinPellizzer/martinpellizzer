Rosa dumalis var. bartlettiana

Rosa dumalis var. bartlettiana, commonly known as the Bartlett's mountain rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thornless stems that often form compact clumps. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
