Rosa arvensis var. taurica

Rosa arvensis var. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric dog rose, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and produces pink to red flowers in clusters. Its stems are covered in thorns, and it is known for its strong, sweet fragrance.

The fruit, a red rose hip, contains numerous seeds and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
