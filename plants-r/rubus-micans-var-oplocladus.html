Rubus micans var. oplocladus

Rubus micans var. oplocladus, commonly known as the oplocladus raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

The botanical features of this plant make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
