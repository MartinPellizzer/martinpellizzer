Ranunculus nigellus

Ranunculus nigellus, commonly known as the black ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a contrasting yellow center. The plant has a basal rosette of finely divided, lobed leaves, and its stems are upright and hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
