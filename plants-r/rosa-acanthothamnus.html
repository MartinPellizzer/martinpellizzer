Rosa acanthothamnus

Rosa acanthothamnus, commonly known as the spiny shrub rose, is a species native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climates.
