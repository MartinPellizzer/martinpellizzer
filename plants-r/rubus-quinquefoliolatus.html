Rubus quinquefoliolatus

Rubus quinquefoliolatus, commonly known as the five-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with five leaflets, which are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
