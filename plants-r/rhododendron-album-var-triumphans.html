Rhododendron album var. triumphans

Rhododendron album var. triumphans, commonly known as the white-flowered azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
