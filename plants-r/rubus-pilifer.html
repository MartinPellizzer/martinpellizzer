Rubus pilifer

Rubus pilifer, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible blackberries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along the edges of forests, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit matures in late summer and is often used in jams and desserts.
