Rosa agrestis f. ciliatosepala

Rosa agrestis f. ciliatosepala, commonly known as the hairy-fringed wild rose, is a botanical variant of the wild rose species Rosa agrestis.

It is characterized by its hairy sepals, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in naturalized settings.
