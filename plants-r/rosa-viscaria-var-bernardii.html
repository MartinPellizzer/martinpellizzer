Rosa viscaria var. bernardii

Rosa viscaria var. bernardii, commonly known as the Bernard's prickly rose, is a variant of the prickly rose that is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting well to saline and well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces rose hips that are used in traditional herbal remedies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
