Roegneria ciliaris f. eriocaulis

Roegneria ciliaris f. eriocaulis, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a cool-season grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in native plant landscapes for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to restoration and conservation efforts.
