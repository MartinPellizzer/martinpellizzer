Rapunculus esculentus

Rapunculus esculentus, commonly known as the edible celandine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ranunculaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, hairy, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, disturbed soils. Its roots are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, while the leaves can be cooked like spinach.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
