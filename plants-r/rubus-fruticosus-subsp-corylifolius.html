Rubus fruticosus subsp. corylifolius

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

corylifolius, commonly known as brambleleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with broad, lobed leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
