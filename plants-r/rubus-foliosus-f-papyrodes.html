Rubus foliosus f. papyrodes

Rubus foliosus f. papyrodes, commonly known as the paper-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive papery, thin leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value due to its unique leaf texture.

This form is a naturally occurring variation within the species Rubus foliosus.
