Rhytidophyllum asperum

Rhytidophyllum asperum, commonly known as the rough bark tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and disturbed areas.
