Rhus arsenei

Rhus arsenei, commonly known as the poison ivy of the genus Rhus, is a shrub native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other toxic plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant is characterized by its alternate, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
