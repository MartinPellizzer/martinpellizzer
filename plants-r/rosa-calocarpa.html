Rosa calocarpa

Rosa calocarpa, commonly known as the pink-berried rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its attractive, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, round, red fruits called hips, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of the rose family.

Rosa calocarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
