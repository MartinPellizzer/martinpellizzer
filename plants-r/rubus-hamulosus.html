Rubus hamulosus

Rubus hamulosus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are covered in thorny projections.

The botanical features of Rubus hamulosus make it an important part of the local ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.
