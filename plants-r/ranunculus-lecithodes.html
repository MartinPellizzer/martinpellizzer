Ranunculus lecithodes

Ranunculus lecithodes, commonly known as the yellow marsh cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The leaves are typically palmately divided into five to seven lobes, often with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
