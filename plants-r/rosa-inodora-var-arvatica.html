Rosa inodora var. arvatica

Rosa inodora var. arvatica, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for hedgerows and ornamental gardens.
