Ranunculus megacarpus

Ranunculus megacarpus, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features showy, yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals that resemble those of the common buttercup. The plant typically grows up to 1 meter tall, with a hairy stem and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.
