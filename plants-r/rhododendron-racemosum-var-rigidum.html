Rhododendron racemosum var. rigidum

Rhododendron racemosum var. rigidum, commonly known as the rigid form of the racemosum rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowers range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and adaptability to temperate climates.
