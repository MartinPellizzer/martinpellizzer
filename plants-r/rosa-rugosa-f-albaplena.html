Rosa rugosa f. albaplena

Rosa rugosa f. albaplena, commonly known as the white-flowered rugosa rose, is a hybrid form of the rugosa rose characterized by its fully double white blooms.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves. The flowers, which are rich in fragrance, bloom in late spring to early summer and are often used in floral arrangements. This variety is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness, thriving in a range of soil types and climates.

It also produces hips, which are edible and used in culinary and medicinal applications.
