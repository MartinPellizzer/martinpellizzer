Rosa communis var. virgultorum

Rosa communis var. virgultorum, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a variety of the common rose that is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It typically features thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
