Rubus pinnivagus

Rubus pinnivagus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with pinnate venation. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in traditional medicine.
