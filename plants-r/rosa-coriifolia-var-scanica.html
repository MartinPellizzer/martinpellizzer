Rosa coriifolia var. scanica

Rosa coriifolia var. scanica, commonly known as the Swedish pink or Swedish rose, is a deciduous shrub native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
