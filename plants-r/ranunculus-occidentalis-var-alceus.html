Ranunculus occidentalis var. alceus

Ranunculus occidentalis var. alceus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, stout stem that is often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are palmately divided into three to five lobes. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season blooms.
