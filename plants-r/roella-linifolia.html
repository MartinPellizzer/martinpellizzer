Roella linifolia

Roella linifolia, commonly known as the silky roella, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. Roella linifolia is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper underground.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
