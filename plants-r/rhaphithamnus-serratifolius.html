Rhaphithamnus serratifolius

Rhaphithamnus serratifolius, commonly known as the serrated leaf rhaphithamnus, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in Mediterranean flora and ecological studies.
