Rubus umbraticus

Rubus umbraticus, commonly known as the shadow raspberry.

This species is a shrub native to shaded forest understories in temperate regions. It produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red or black in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

It is adapted to low light conditions and plays a role in providing food for wildlife in woodland ecosystems.
