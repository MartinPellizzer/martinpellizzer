Rosa woloszczakii

Rosa woloszczakii, commonly known as the Woloszczak rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure, simple leaves with serrated edges, and hips that persist into winter.

Rosa woloszczakii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
