Rubus moluccanus var. subramiflorus

Rubus moluccanus var. subramiflorus, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which are characteristic of the Rubus genus. The fruit is a dark purple to black aggregate fruit, similar to other blackberries, and is edible.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its berries in tropical regions.
