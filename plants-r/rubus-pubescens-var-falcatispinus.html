Rubus pubescens var. falcatispinus

Rubus pubescens var. falcatispinus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and fruit structure.

It is native to regions of North America, particularly in the eastern United States, where it thrives in moist, woodland habitats. The leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated edge, while the bracts are often curved and narrow. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are usually dark purple to black when mature and have a tart, musky flavor.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in local ecosystems as a food source for wildlife.
