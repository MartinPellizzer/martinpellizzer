Rea macrantha

Rea macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered rea, is a striking plant native to the eastern regions of South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rea macrantha thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
