Rubus percrispus

Rubus percrispus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in woodland and shrubland habitats. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications despite its sharp thorns.
