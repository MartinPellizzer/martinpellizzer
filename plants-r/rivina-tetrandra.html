Rivina tetrandra

Rivina tetrandra, commonly known as the redroot false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

Rivina tetrandra is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and streambanks.
