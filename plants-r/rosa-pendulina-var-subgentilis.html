Rosa pendulina var. subgentilis

Rosa pendulina var. subgentilis, commonly known as the hanging rose or weeping rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous stems that droop gracefully, bearing clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically pink or white. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in mild, humid climates.
