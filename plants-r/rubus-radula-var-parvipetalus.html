Rubus radula var. parvipetalus

Rubus radula var. parvipetalus, commonly known as the small-petaled blackberry shrub, is a variant of the blackberry genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its small, delicate petals and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
