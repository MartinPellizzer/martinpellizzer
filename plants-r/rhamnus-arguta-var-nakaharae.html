Rhamnus arguta var. nakaharae

Rhamnus arguta var. nakaharae, commonly known as the Nakahara's buckthorn, is a variant of the common buckthorn native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, egg-shaped berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and shrublands.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
