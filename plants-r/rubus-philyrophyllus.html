Rubus philyrophyllus

Rubus philyrophyllus, commonly known as the red-leaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
