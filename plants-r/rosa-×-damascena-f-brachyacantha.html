Rosa × damascena f. brachyacantha

Rosa × damascena f. brachyacantha, commonly known as the short-stalked damask rose, is a botanical hybrid within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stems compared to typical Rosa × damascena varieties. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and serrated, providing a lush backdrop to the flowers.

This form is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
