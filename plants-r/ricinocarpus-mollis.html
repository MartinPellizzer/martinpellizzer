Ricinocarpus mollis

Ricinocarpus mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaf ricin tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often covered in a sticky substance.

Ricinocarpus mollis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
