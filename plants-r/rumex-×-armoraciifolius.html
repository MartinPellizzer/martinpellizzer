Rumex × armoraciifolius

Rumex × armoraciifolius, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is known for its edible, tangy leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and can be found in regions with rich, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces elongated, ribbed fruits.

This hybrid plant is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its nutritional properties.
