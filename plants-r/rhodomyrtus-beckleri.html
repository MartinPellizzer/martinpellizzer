Rhodomyrtus beckleri

Rhodomyrtus beckleri, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, red or orange berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, so caution is advised when handling or consuming them.
