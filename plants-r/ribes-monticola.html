Ribes monticola

Ribes monticola, commonly known as the mountain currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of gooseberries and currants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a serrated margin.

The small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible red berries that ripen in late summer.
