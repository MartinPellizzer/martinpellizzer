Rubus niveus subsp. horsfieldii

Rubus niveus subsp.

horsfieldii, commonly known as the white-bracted raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
