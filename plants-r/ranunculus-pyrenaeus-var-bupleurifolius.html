Ranunculus pyrenaeus var. bupleurifolius

Ranunculus pyrenaeus var. bupleurifolius, commonly known as the Pyrenean buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy and grayish-green in color. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens and a prominent pistil. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
