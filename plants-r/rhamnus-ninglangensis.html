Rhamnus ninglangensis

Rhamnus ninglangensis, commonly known as the Ninglang buckthorn, is a shrub native to southwestern China.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue or black berries that are edible for certain bird species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
