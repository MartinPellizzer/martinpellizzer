Rosa ianthinochlora

Rosa ianthinochlora, commonly known as the purple-leafed rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs.

Rosa ianthinochlora thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
