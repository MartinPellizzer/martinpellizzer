Rosa × involuta var. wilsonii

Rosa × involuta var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's curled rose, is a hybrid species of rose native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tightly curled petals that give it a unique, almost spiral-like appearance. The plant typically produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, often with a hint of white. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
