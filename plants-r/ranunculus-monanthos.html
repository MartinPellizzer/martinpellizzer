Ranunculus monanthos

Ranunculus monanthos, commonly known as the single-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 13 sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are lobed or toothed, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its early spring blooming and its role in supporting pollinators.
