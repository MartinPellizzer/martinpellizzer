Rosa suaveolens var. alba

Rosa suaveolens var. alba, commonly known as the white sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. Its botanical features include small, oval-shaped leaves and a woody, sprawling growth habit.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
