Rubus plicatus var. roseiflorus

Rubus plicatus var. roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple tiny drupes.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
