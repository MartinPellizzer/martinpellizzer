Rosa dumalis f. podolica

Rosa dumalis f. podolica, commonly known as the Podolia rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Carpathian Mountains and surrounding regions of Eastern Europe. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, red hips that contain seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
