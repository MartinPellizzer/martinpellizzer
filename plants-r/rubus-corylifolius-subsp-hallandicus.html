Rubus corylifolius subsp. hallandicus

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

hallandicus, commonly known as the Halland blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
