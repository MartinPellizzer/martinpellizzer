Rapuntium ovatum

Rapuntium ovatum, commonly known as the white buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmate leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.
