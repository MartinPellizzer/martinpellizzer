Rosa borbonica

Rosa borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a serrated edge.

Rosa borbonica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
