Rosa indica var. lutea

Rosa indica var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow tea rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa indica species, known for its vibrant yellow flowers.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many rose species. The flowers are typically single or semi-double, with a rich, golden hue that fades to a lighter yellow as they age. It blooms repeatedly throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
