Rhamnus alaternus var. integrifolia

Rhamnus alaternus var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are entire, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
