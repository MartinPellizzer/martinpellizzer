Rosa sherardii var. danica

Rosa sherardii var. danica, commonly known as the Danica rose, is a hybrid species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the blooms.

Rosa sherardii var. danica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
