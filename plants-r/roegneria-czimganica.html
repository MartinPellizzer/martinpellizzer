Roegneria czimganica

Roegneria czimganica, commonly known as the Czimganica grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves. The plant produces spikelet-based inflorescences, with small, two-lobed flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in steppe and grassland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
