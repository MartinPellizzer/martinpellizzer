Rosa dumalis f. tristis

Rosa dumalis f. tristis, commonly known as the weeping dog rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa dumalis species.

It is characterized by its pendulous, arching branches that give it a cascading appearance, making it visually striking in gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color, adding ornamental value. It is a compact shrub, often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

Botanically, it is noted for its thorny stems and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil with partial sunlight.
