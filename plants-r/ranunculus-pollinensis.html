Ranunculus pollinensis

Ranunculus pollinensis, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and is typically found in rocky or meadow habitats at high elevations. The plant has palmately divided leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is a perennial species that produces seeds through its showy blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often considered a rare and protected plant in many regions.
