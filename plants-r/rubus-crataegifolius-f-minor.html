Rubus crataegifolius f. minor

Rubus crataegifolius f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry shrub, is a compact variant of the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves that resemble those of hawthorn. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and relatively rare member of the Rubus family.
