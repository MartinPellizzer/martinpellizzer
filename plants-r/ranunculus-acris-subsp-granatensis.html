Ranunculus acris subsp. granatensis

Ranunculus acris subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense Crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive central greenish or reddish blotch.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It is characterized by its slender stems and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
