Ranunculus furcatifidus

Ranunculus furcatifidus, commonly known as the forked-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features compound leaves with deeply lobed or divided segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers typically have five to eight petals and are surrounded by a ring of sepals.

Ranunculus furcatifidus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
