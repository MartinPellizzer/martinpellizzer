Rubus chlorostachys subvar. populifolius

Rubus chlorostachys subvar. populifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that are dark purple when mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.
