Rudbeckia amplexifolia

Rudbeckia amplexifolia, commonly known as the claspingleaf coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped basal leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounding a dark brown disk in the center, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

Rudbeckia amplexifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
