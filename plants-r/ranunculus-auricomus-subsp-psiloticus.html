Ranunculus auricomus subsp. psiloticus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

psiloticus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals and a prominent yellow center.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided with a distinctive, finely toothed margin.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves and more elongated petals compared to the typical form of Ranunculus auricomus.
