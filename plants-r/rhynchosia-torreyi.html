Rhynchosia torreyi

Rhynchosia torreyi, commonly known as the desert false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of violet or purple flowers. Its root system is deep and adapted to survive in dry conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

Rhynchosia torreyi is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
