Rubus lentiginosus subsp. devoniensis

Rubus lentiginosus subsp.

devoniensis, commonly known as the Devon raspwort, is a shrub native to the British Isles. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupes, typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.
