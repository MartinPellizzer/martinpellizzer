Rhamnus californica

Rhamnus californica, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
