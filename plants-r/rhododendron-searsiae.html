Rhododendron searsiae

Rhododendron searsiae, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rhododendron, is a species native to the western United States, particularly found in the Sierra Nevada mountain range.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils within coniferous forests. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron searsiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
