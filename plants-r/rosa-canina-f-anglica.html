Rosa canina f. anglica

Rosa canina f. anglica, commonly known as the English rosehip, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange hips that persist through the winter. The plant produces fragrant pink to white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the characteristic rosehips. It is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the medicinal use of its hips.
