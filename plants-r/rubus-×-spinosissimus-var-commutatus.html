Rubus × spinosissimus var. commutatus

Rubus × spinosissimus var. commutatus, commonly known as the thorny blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark, edible berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, contributing to its ornamental value.
