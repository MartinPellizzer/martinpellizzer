Rubus schleicheri subsp. spinosulus

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

spinosulus, commonly known as the spiny bramble, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense growth habit.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red or black berries. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
