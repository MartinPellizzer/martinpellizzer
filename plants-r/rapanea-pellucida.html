Rapanea pellucida

Rapanea pellucida, commonly known as the silky rapanea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
