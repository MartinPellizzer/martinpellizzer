Rubus hirtus var. caudatus

Rubus hirtus var. caudatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
