Rytigynia seyrigii var. seyrigii

Rytigynia seyrigii var. seyrigii, commonly known as the Brazilian yellowwood, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a glossy green surface and a distinct yellowish or reddish underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with four or five petals.

The tree produces small, hard fruits that contain one or two seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
