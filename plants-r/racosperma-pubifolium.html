Racosperma pubifolium

Racosperma pubifolium, commonly known as the hairy-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Racosperma pubifolium is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
