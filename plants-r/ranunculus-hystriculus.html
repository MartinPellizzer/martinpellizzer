Ranunculus hystriculus

Ranunculus hystriculus, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, and hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a cluster of stamens. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded areas.

Ranunculus hystriculus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
