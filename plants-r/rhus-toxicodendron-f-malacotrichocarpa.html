Rhus toxicodendron f. malacotrichocarpa

Rhus toxicodendron f. malacotrichocarpa, commonly known as the Malacotrichocarpa sumac, is a variant of the staghorn sumac.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, fuzzy berries.

The leaves and fruits contain toxic compounds, making the plant potentially harmful to humans and animals.
