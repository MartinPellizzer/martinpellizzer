Rosa canina var. puberula

Rosa canina var. puberula, commonly known as the bristly dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink flowers. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C and are used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
