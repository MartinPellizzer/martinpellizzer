Rubus pallidus microgene aciphyllus

Rubus pallidus microgene aciphyllus, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a shrub native to specific regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, pale green leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by small, red berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
