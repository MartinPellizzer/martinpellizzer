Reseda litigiosa

Reseda litigiosa, commonly known as the "bitterweed," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive bitter taste.

Reseda litigiosa is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ecological role in supporting certain pollinators.
