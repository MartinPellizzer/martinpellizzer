Rubus hirtus var. tenuiglandulosus

Rubus hirtus var. tenuiglandulosus, commonly known as the smooth glandular raspberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with small, glandular dots. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other Rubus species due to its unique leaf and stem morphology.
