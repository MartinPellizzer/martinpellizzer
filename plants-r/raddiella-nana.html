Raddiella nana

Raddiella nana, commonly known as the dwarf raddiella, is a small herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Raddiella nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
