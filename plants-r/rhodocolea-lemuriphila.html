Rhodocolea lemuriphila

Rhodocolea lemuriphila, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid endemic to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers that are predominantly white with a deep purple or violet center. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the rainforests of Madagascar, where it thrives in the understory. Its floral structure is adapted for specific pollinators, primarily certain species of lemurs, which contribute to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Rhodocolea lemuriphila is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
