Rubus friesiorum subsp. elgonensis

Rubus friesiorum subsp.

elgonensis, commonly known as Elgon blackberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa, particularly around Mount Elgon. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations above 2,000 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local food systems.
