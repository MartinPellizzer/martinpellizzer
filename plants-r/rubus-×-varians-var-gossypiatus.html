Rubus × varians var. gossypiatus

Rubus × varians var. gossypiatus, commonly known as the dog rose, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its role in wildlife habitats and as a source of natural dye.
