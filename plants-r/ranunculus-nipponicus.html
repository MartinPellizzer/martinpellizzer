Ranunculus nipponicus

Ranunculus nipponicus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often with a dark center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a distinctive hairy surface.

Ranunculus nipponicus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
