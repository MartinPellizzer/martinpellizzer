Rubus trachycaulon

Rubus trachycaulon, commonly known as the roughstalk blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, giving it its scientific name "trachycaulon," meaning rough-stemmed.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
