Rosa × gallicoides

Rosa × gallicoides, commonly known as the French rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and hips that are often used in perfumes and culinary applications.

Rosa × gallicoides is native to Europe and has been cultivated for centuries for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
