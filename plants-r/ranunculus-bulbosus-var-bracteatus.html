Ranunculus bulbosus var. bracteatus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. bracteatus, commonly known as the bracted buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the species is known for its distinctive bracts that enclose the flower buds.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
