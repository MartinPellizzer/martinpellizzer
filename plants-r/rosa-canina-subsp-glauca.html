Rosa canina subsp. glauca

Rosa canina subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the grey rose or wild dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are usually hairy underneath.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and other disturbed habitats.
