Rosa villosa subsp. molligothoburgensis

Rosa villosa subsp.

molligothoburgensis, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It features upright, thorny stems covered in a dense layer of white to greyish pubescence.

The leaves are pinnate, with 5 to 9 leaflets that are oval and hairy. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh alpine conditions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
