Rosa coronifera

Rosa coronifera, commonly known as the crown rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a distinctive crown-like appearance.

Rosa coronifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
