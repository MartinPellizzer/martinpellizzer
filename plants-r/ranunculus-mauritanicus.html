Ranunculus mauritanicus

Ranunculus mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritanian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are palmate, meaning they are divided into lobes resembling a hand, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus mauritanicus is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
