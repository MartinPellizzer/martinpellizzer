Rubus fellatae

Rubus fellatae, commonly known as the Sierra raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada. This shrub typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its prickly stems and small, edible berries. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive red or black fruits.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
