Rubus floribundopaniculatus

Rubus floribundopaniculatus, commonly known as the showy blackberry.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy inflorescences and fragrant foliage.
