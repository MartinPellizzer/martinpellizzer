Relbunium chloranthum

Relbunium chloranthum, commonly known as the green-flowered grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves and small, clustered inflorescences that produce greenish flowers. Its botanical structure is typical of grasses, with a fibrous root system and hollow stems.

Relbunium chloranthum is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
