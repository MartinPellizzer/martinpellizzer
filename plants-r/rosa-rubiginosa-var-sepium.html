Rosa rubiginosa var. sepium

Rosa rubiginosa var. sepium, commonly known as the redstem rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant produces rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with serrated edges.

This species is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
