Rosa saturata

Rosa saturata, commonly known as the deep pink rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety petals that range from pale pink to deep crimson, depending on the variety. The plant produces fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often in clusters. Rosa saturata is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
