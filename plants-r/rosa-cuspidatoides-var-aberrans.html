Rosa cuspidatoides var. aberrans

Rosa cuspidatoides var. aberrans, commonly known as the aberrant thorned rose, is a rare and distinctive variant of the thorned rose species.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring elongated, recurved petals and prominent, sharp thorns along its stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins that contribute to its rugged appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species within its native range.
