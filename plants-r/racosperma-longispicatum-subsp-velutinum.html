Racosperma longispicatum subsp. velutinum

Racosperma longispicatum subsp.

velutinum, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
