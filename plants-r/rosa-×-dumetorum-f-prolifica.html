Rosa × dumetorum f. prolifica

Rosa × dumetorum f. prolifica, commonly known as the "dwarf dog rose," is a compact, shrub-like species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant produces numerous small, red-orange hips, which are rich in vitamin C and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
