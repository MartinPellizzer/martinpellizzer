Rhododendron adansonii

Rhododendron adansonii, commonly known as the Adanson's rhododendron, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a lush backdrop for the vibrant blossoms.

Rhododendron adansonii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
