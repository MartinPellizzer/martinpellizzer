Rubus obscurus microgene adornatiformis

Rubus obscurus microgene adornatiformis, commonly known as the ornate blackberry, is a rare and little-studied species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, berries that are typically smaller than those of common blackberry varieties. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

Its ornate floral structure and unique fruit morphology make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
