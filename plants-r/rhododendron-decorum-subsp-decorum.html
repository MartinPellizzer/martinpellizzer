Rhododendron decorum subsp. decorum

Rhododendron decorum subsp.

decorum, commonly known as the purple-veined rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with prominent veins that add to its ornamental appeal. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
