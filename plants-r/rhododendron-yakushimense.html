Rhododendron yakushimense

Rhododendron yakushimense, commonly known as the Yakushima azalea, is a species native to the island of Yakushima in Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron yakushimense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with high moisture and acidic soil conditions.
