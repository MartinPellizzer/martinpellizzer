Rosmarinus officinalis subvar. macrocalyx

Rosmarinus officinalis subvar. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered rosemary, is a variant of the rosemary plant characterized by its larger, more showy flowers.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, featuring aromatic evergreen leaves and conical, blue-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in aromatherapy and culinary applications.
