Rosa illizans

Rosa illizans, commonly known as the Illizan rose, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa illizans is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its beauty and historical significance in local traditions.
