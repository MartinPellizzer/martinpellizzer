Rhamnus alaternus subsp. parvifolia

Rhamnus alaternus subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the autumn. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
