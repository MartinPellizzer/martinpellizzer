Rosa coriifolia subvar. mannagettae

Rosa coriifolia subvar. mannagettae, commonly known as the Manzana Rosa, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered, pinkish-white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has narrow, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.

This subvariety is notable for its compact growth habit and early blooming period.
