Rubus indusiatus

Rubus indusiatus, commonly known as the velvet raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries. The leaves are typically ovate with a serrated margin and are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
