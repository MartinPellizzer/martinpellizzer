Ranunculus ophioglossoides

Ranunculus ophioglossoides, commonly known as the swordleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
