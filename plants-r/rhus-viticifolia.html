Rhus viticifolia

Rhus viticifolia, commonly known as the sumac tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other species like poison ivy and cashew. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into multiple leaflets, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. After flowering, it produces red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant has a rough, fissured bark and is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and woodland edges.
