Rhodiola chrysanthemifolia subsp. sexifolia

Rhodiola chrysanthemifolia subsp.

sexifolia, commonly known as the six-leaved rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves.

The plant typically produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stems. It thrives in cold, rocky environments and is well adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Due to its resilience and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
