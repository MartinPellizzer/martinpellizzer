Rubus mucronulatus f. microphyllus

Rubus mucronulatus f. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry species characterized by its diminutive, elliptical leaves and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in jams and pies in some regions.
