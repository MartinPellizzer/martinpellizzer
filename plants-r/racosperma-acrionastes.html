Racosperma acrionastes

Racosperma acrionastes, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability.
