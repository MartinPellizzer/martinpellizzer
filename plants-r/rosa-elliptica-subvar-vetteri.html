Rosa elliptica subvar. vetteri

Rosa elliptica subvar. vetteri, commonly known as the Vetter's elliptic rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their beauty and pleasant aroma.
