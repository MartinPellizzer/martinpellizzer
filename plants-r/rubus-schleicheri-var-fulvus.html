Rubus schleicheri var. fulvus

Rubus schleicheri var. fulvus, commonly known as the golden raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rosaceae family.
