Rubus defectionis

Rubus defectionis, commonly known as the desert raspberry, is a species of bramble native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, red, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and potential culinary uses.
