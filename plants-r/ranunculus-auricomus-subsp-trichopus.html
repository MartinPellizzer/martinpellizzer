Ranunculus auricomus subsp. trichopus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

trichopus, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. It features showy, yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals that are typically larger than the true petals.

The plant has a basal rosette of deeply lobed, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
