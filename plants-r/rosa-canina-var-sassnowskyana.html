Rosa canina var. sassnowskyana

Rosa canina var. sassnowskyana, commonly known as the Sassnowsky's rose, is a lesser-known variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. The plant is native to Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
