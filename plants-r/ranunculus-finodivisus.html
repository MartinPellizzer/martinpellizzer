Ranunculus finodivisus

Ranunculus finodivisus, commonly known as the Southern Mountain Crowfoot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Ranunculus finodivisus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
