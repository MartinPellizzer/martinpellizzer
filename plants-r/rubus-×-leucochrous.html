Rubus × leucochrous

Rubus × leucochrous, commonly known as the white-berried blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically white or pale in color. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are often used in culinary applications despite their less common appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This hybrid is valued for its unique fruit color and flavor profile, making it a notable species in both horticulture and botany.
