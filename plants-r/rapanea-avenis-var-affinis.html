Rapanea avenis var. affinis

Rapanea avenis var. affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lauraceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, dark berries. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including the Philippines and Indonesia.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
