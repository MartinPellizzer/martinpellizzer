Rosa × rhombifolia

Rosa × rhombifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, rhombic-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, often with a high number of petals. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan, and is cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rosa × rhombifolia prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
