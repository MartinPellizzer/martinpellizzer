Ranunculus extorris var. lutchuensis

Ranunculus extorris var. lutchuensis, commonly known as the Lutchuensis buttercup, is a variant of the extorris buttercup native to the Andaman Islands.

This plant is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically feature five to eight petals, often in shades of yellow or white. It grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The leaves are trifoliate, with lobed segments that are usually hairy.

It is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
