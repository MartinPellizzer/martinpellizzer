Randia turgida

Randia turgida, commonly known as the waxplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Randia turgida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
