Rhododendron sanguineum var. consanguineum

Rhododendron sanguineum var. consanguineum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and elliptical in shape, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blossoms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its seasonal floral display.
