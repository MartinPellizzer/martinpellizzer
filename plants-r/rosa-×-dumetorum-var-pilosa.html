Rosa × dumetorum var. pilosa

Rosa × dumetorum var. pilosa, commonly known as the fuzzy evergreen rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is a shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with thorny stems and leathery leaves.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, thriving in a range of soil conditions.
