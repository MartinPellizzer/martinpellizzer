Rubus vepretorum

Rubus vepretorum, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with potential uses in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
