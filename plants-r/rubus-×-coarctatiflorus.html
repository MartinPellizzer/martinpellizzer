Rubus × coarctatiflorus

Rubus × coarctatiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and usually have five petals, while the leaves are compound with three leaflets.

This species is valued for its edible berries, which are often used in culinary applications.
