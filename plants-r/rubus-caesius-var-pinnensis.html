Rubus caesius var. pinnensis

Rubus caesius var. pinnensis, commonly known as the pinnate bramble, is a variant of the European blackberry species.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces dark purple to black berries, which are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with a distinctive pinnate structure, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
