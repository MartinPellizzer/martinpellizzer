Rhodorhiza glandulosa

Rhodorhiza glandulosa, commonly known as the stickyroot plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a fibrous root system with distinctive glandular structures that produce a sticky secretion. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Rhodorhiza glandulosa is often found in moist, sandy soils in open woodlands and along streambanks.
