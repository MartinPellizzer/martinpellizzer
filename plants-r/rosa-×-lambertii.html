Rosa × lambertii

Rosa × lambertii, commonly known as the Lambert rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa gallica and Rosa spinosissima.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds, and these hips are often used in herbal remedies. Rosa × lambertii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
