Rubus villicaulis var. silvaticus

Rubus villicaulis var. silvaticus, commonly known as the silvatic blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
