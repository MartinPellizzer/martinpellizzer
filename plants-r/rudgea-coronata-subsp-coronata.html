Rudgea coronata subsp. coronata

Rudgea coronata subsp.

coronata, commonly known as the golden crown rudgea, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
