Rubus silvaticus var. loehnensis

Rubus silvaticus var. loehnensis, commonly known as the Loehnenschen bramble, is a variant of the common bramble found in specific regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important species for local biodiversity and ecological studies.
