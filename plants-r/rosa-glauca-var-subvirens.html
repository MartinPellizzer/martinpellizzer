Rosa glauca var. subvirens

Rosa glauca var. subvirens, commonly known as the greenish dogwood rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dark green, oval leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five to seven petals. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or as a border plant.
