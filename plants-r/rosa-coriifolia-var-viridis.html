Rosa coriifolia var. viridis

Rosa coriifolia var. viridis, commonly known as the greenleaf shrub rose, is a variant of the common shrub rose characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It is native to China and is known for its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth, waxy texture, giving the plant its common name. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
