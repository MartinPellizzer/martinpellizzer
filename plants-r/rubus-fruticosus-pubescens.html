Rubus fruticosus pubescens

Rubus fruticosus pubescens, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
