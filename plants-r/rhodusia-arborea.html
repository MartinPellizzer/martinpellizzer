Rhodusia arborea

Rhodusia arborea, commonly known as the tree rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy inflorescences.

Rhodusia arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
