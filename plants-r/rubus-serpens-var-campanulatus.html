Rubus serpens var. campanulatus

Rubus serpens var. campanulatus, commonly known as the bell-shaped blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it blooms with white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its fruit.
