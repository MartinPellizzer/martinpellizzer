Rhododendron megeratum

Rhododendron megeratum, commonly known as the Mexican rhododendron, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. The plant features dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Rhododendron megeratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
