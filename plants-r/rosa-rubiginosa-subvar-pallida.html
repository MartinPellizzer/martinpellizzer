Rosa rubiginosa subvar. pallida

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. pallida, commonly known as the pale shrub rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has woody stems and produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The pale rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
