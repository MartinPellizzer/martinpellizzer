Ribes oxyacanthoides var. lacustre

Ribes oxyacanthoides var. lacustre, commonly known as the lake gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near lakes and streams.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
