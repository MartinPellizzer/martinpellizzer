Ranunculus clintonii

Ranunculus clintonii, commonly known as Clinton's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
