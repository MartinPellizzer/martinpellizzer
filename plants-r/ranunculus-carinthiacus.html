Ranunculus carinthiacus

Ranunculus carinthiacus, commonly known as the Carinthian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often surrounded by a ring of white or pale yellow sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is found in meadows and forest clearings at high altitudes. It has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus carinthiacus is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.
