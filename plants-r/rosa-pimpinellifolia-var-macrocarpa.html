Rosa pimpinellifolia var. macrocarpa

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited rock rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces large, hairy, red or orange fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, arid environments.
