Rubus bellardii

Rubus bellardii, commonly known as Bellard's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Botanically, it belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
