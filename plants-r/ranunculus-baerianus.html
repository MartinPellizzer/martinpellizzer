Ranunculus baerianus

Ranunculus baerianus, commonly known as the Baerian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are usually solitary and bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to its natural environment.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, delicate appearance.
