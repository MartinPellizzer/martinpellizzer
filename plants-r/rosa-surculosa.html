Rosa surculosa

Rosa surculosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late summer. Rosa surculosa is well adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
