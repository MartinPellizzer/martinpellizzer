Rutidea gracilis

Rutidea gracilis, commonly known as the slender legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in racemes, and are typically pale yellow or greenish in color.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
