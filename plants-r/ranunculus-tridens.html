Ranunculus tridens

Ranunculus tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves with three lobes, giving it its common name. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic compounds.

Ranunculus tridens is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats.
