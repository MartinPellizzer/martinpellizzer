Rubus adenoleucus

Rubus adenoleucus, commonly known as the yellow raspberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
