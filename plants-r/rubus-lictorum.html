Rubus lictorum

Rubus lictorum, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of bramble in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are widely cultivated for their flavor and nutritional value.

Rubus lictorum plays an important role in both ecological systems and human agriculture.
