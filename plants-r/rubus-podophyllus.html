Rubus podophyllus

Rubus podophyllus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white or pale green and are clustered in the axils of the leaves.

The hairy blackberry is valued for its edible fruits and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
