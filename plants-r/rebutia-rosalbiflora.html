Rebutia rosalbiflora

Rebutia rosalbiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in dense, woolly hair. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
