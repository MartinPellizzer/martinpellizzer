Rubus emarginatus

Rubus emarginatus, commonly known as the margined blackberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges.

Rubus emarginatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
