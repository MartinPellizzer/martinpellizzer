Rubus villicaulis var. parvifolius

Rubus villicaulis var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It features slender, arching stems that are typically thornless and covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are elliptic to ovate, with a finely toothed margin and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
