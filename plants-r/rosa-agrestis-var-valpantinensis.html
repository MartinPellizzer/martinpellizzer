Rosa agrestis var. valpantinensis

Rosa agrestis var. valpantinensis, commonly known as the Alpine rose or Valpantin's rose, is a species native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It is a shrub with upright, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne singly or in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to withstand cold climates.
