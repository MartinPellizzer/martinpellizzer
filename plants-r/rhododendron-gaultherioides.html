Rhododendron gaultherioides

Rhododendron gaultherioides, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil, often found in subalpine or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
