Rosa kazarjanii var. glandulosa

Rosa kazarjanii var. glandulosa, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, thorny stems and is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright red hips, which are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
