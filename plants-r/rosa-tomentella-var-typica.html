Rosa tomentella var. typica

Rosa tomentella var. typica, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, white tomentose (hairy) foliage and stems, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers during the summer months. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
