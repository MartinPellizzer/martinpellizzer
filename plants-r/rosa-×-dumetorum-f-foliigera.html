Rosa × dumetorum f. foliigera

Rosa × dumetorum f. foliigera, commonly known as the hairy rose or woolly dog rose, is a hybrid plant characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and stems.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in cooler, moist environments. The leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, soft appearance. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong, pleasant fragrance.

This cultivar is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique texture and visual appeal.
