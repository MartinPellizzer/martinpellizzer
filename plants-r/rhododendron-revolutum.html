Rhododendron revolutum

Rhododendron revolutum, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are often reddish-brown underneath, and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
