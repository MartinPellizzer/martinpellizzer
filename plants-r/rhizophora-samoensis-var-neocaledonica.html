Rhizophora samoensis var. neocaledonica

Rhizophora samoensis var. neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonia mangrove, is a variant of the mangrove species native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in brackish water environments, making it an essential component of coastal ecosystems. The plant has specialized root systems, including pneumatophores, which allow it to absorb oxygen from the air in waterlogged soils. Its leaves are typically small and thick, adapted to reduce water loss in saline conditions.

This species plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
