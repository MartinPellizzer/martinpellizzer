Rosa spinosissima f. luteola

Rosa spinosissima f. luteola, commonly known as the yellow prickly rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa spinosissima species, characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and thorny stems.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, dark green foliage. The flowers are usually solitary, appearing in late spring to early summer, and are notable for their rich yellow coloration. This variety is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a desirable ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
