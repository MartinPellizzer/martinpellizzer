Rhynchospora ferruginea

Rhynchospora ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty panicgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features a tufted growth habit with slender, grass-like leaves that are often reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in areas with standing water or saturated ground.

Rhynchospora ferruginea plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
