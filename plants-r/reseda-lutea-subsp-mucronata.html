Reseda lutea subsp. mucronata

Reseda lutea subsp.

mucronata, commonly known as the yellow mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers have five petals with a distinctive yellow hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
