Ranunculus boreoapenninus

Ranunculus boreoapenninus, commonly known as the northern Apennine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus boreoapenninus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
