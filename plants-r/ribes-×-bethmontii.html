Ribes × bethmontii

Ribes × bethmontii, commonly known as the hybrid currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is a cross between Ribes sanguineum (burning bush) and Ribes viburnifolium (red currant), resulting in a plant with ornamental and edible qualities. The shrub produces clusters of bright red, edible berries that are often used in jams and jellies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

Ribes × bethmontii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
