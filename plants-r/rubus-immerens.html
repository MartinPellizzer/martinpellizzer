Rubus immerens

Rubus immerens, commonly known as the water raspberry, is a species of bramble native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of prickly stems and leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, often blooming in early summer.

Rubus immerens plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species and stabilizing soil in moist environments.
