Rubroshorea splendida

Rubroshorea splendida, commonly known as the flameleaf tree, is a striking tropical species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange foliage, which becomes more intense during the dry season. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a habitat for various insects and small animals.

The leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to withstand high humidity and periodic drought conditions.
