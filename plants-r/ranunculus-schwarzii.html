Ranunculus schwarzii

Ranunculus schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwarz's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish sepals that surround the flower.

Ranunculus schwarzii is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
