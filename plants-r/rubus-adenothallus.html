Rubus adenothallus

Rubus adenothallus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to high-altitude regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its habitat, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
