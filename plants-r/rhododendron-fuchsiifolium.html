Rhododendron fuchsiifolium

Rhododendron fuchsiifolium, commonly known as the Fuchsii leaf rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal floral display.
