Rhododendron roseum f. lutescens

Rhododendron roseum f. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rhododendron, is a hybrid plant with distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which contrast with its deep green foliage. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of wildlife.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens and natural landscapes.
