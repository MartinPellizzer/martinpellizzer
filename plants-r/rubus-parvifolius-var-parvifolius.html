Rubus parvifolius var. parvifolius

Rubus parvifolius var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded lobes. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, hairy berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
