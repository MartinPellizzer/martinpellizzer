Rubus corchorifolius var. palmatus

Rubus corchorifolius var. palmatus, commonly known as the palmate blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
