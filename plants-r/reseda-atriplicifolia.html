Reseda atriplicifolia

Reseda atriplicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and bloom in early summer.

Reseda atriplicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
