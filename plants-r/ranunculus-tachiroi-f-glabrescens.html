Ranunculus tachiroi f. glabrescens

Ranunculus tachiroi f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous meadow buttercup, is a botanical variant of the meadow buttercup species.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and flowers, distinguishing it from other forms of Ranunculus tachiroi. This plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers are usually bright yellow with five petals and numerous stamens.

It is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
