Rhododendron doctersii

Rhododendron doctersii, commonly known as the Doctersii rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
