Rosa huntii

Rosa huntii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blooms with a pleasant scent, often attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Rosa huntii is typically found in rocky soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
