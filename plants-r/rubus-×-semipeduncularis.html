Rubus × semipeduncularis

Rubus × semipeduncularis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in its natural habitat.
