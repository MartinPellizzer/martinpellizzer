Rubus cartalinicus

Rubus cartalinicus, commonly known as the Alpine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus cartalinicus is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
