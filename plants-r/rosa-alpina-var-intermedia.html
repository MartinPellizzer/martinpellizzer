Rosa alpina var. intermedia

Rosa alpina var. intermedia, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering, typically producing pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and full sun, making it well-suited for alpine gardens and rockeries. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and it is known for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in horticultural settings.
