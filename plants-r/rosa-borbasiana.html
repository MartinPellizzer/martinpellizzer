Rosa borbasiana

Rosa borbasiana, commonly known as the Borbas rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces fragrant, single or double petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa borbasiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
