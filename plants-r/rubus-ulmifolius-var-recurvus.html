Rubus ulmifolius var. recurvus

Rubus ulmifolius var. recurvus, commonly known as the bent-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved leaflets that give it its common name. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex inflorescence and its role in local ecosystems as a food source for various wildlife.
