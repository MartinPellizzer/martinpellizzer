Rhynchosia mantaroensis var. cuprinervia

Rhynchosia mantaroensis var. cuprinervia, commonly known as the cuprinervia variant of the mantaroensis rhynchosia, is a climbing legume native to the Peruvian Andes.

It features trifoliate leaves with finely serrated margins and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant's pods are slender and cylindrical, containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
