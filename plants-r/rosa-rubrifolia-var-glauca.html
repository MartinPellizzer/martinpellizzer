Rosa rubrifolia var. glauca

Rosa rubrifolia var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous rose, is a variant of the red-leafed rose that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its pale blue or grayish-green foliage, which gives it a unique appearance compared to other rose varieties. The plant typically produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers in the spring. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
