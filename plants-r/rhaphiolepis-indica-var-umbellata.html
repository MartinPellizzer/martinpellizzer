Rhaphiolepis indica var. umbellata

Rhaphiolepis indica var. umbellata, commonly known as the clustered rosebay, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in dense clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly textured surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
