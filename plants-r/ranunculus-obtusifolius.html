Ranunculus obtusifolius

Ranunculus obtusifolius, commonly known as the blunt-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear showy, yellow flowers with five to eight sepals and numerous stamens. The leaves are palmately divided, with rounded lobes, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
