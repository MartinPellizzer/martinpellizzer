Roettlera corniculata

Roettlera corniculata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, horn-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Roettlera corniculata is a herbaceous annual that thrives in well-drained soils and sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
