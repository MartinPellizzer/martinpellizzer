Razisea spicata var. pulchra

Razisea spicata var. pulchra, commonly known as the showy razisea, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces small, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections worldwide.
