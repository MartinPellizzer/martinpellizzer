Rubus thyrsanthus var. hirtifrons

Rubus thyrsanthus var. hirtifrons, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
