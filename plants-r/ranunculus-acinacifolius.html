Ranunculus acinacifolius

Ranunculus acinacifolius, commonly known as the swordleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features distinctive, sword-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often accompanied by a prominent central pistil. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
