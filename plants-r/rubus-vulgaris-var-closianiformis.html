Rubus vulgaris var. closianiformis

Rubus vulgaris var. closianiformis, commonly known as the close-leaf bramble, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its dense growth habit and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
