Rubus pseudinfestus

Rubus pseudinfestus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

Rubus pseudinfestus thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland areas and along the edges of forests.
