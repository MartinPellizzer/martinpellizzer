Rhododendron devrieseanum

Rhododendron devrieseanum, commonly known as the Devriese's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
