Rubus rivularis var. crenatus

Rubus rivularis var. crenatus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, exhibiting a climbing or trailing growth habit. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are characterized by their serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to wet environments.
