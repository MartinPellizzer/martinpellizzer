Rosa pimpinellifolia var. horrida

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. horrida, commonly known as the bristly rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, thorny stems and produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, velvety hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
