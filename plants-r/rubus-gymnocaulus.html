Rubus gymnocaulus

Rubus gymnocaulus, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thornless stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as streambanks and meadows. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and for making jams and wines.
