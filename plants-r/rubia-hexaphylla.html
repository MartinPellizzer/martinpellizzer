Rubia hexaphylla

Rubia hexaphylla, commonly known as the six-leafed bugleweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and garden shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Rubia hexaphylla is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.
