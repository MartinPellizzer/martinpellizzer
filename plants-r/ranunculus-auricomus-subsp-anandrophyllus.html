Ranunculus auricomus subsp. anandrophyllus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

anandrophyllus, commonly known as the golden crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a feature known as androphylly. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
