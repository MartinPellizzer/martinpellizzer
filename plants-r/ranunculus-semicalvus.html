Ranunculus semicalvus

Ranunculus semicalvus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, often with a distinctive white or greenish center. The plant has a hairy stem and leaves that are palmately lobed, with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
