Rubus koehleri var. lignicensis

Rubus koehleri var. lignicensis, commonly known as the dense-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically clustered together. These berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
