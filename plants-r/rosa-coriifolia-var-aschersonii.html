Rosa coriifolia var. aschersonii

Rosa coriifolia var. aschersonii, commonly known as the Ascherson's sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant features thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a finely toothed margin. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic appeal.

This variety is relatively hardy and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
