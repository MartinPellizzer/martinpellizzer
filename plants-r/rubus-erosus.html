Rubus erosus

Rubus erosus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color by late summer. Rubus erosus is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates.

It is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
