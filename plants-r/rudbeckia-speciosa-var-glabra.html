Rudbeckia speciosa var. glabra

Rudbeckia speciosa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow-orange flower heads with a prominent brown central disk, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
