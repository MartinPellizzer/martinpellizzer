Rosa micrantha var. arcadiensis

Rosa micrantha var. arcadiensis, commonly known as the Arcadian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and produces small, pale pink to white flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
