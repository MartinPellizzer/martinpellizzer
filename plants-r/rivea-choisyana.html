Rivea choisyana

Rivea choisyana, commonly known as the sweet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that resemble those of the passionfruit. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in warm climates.

Rivea choisyana is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
