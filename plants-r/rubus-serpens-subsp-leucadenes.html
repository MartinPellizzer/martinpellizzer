Rubus serpens subsp. leucadenes

Rubus serpens subsp.

leucadenes, commonly known as the white-tipped blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark berries that are typically clustered at the ends of its stems.

The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually pale pink or white. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its white-tipped bracts and its unique growth form compared to other Rubus species.
