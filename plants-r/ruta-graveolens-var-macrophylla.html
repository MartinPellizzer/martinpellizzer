Ruta graveolens var. macrophylla

Ruta graveolens var. macrophylla, commonly known as large-leaved rue, is a variant of the common rue plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, dark green, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is a perennial shrub, typically growing to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves contain volatile oils, which contribute to its strong, peppery scent and have been used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of this plant make it well-suited for use in herbal gardens and as a decorative plant.
