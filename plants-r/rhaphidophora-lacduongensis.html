Rhaphidophora lacduongensis

Rhaphidophora lacduongensis, commonly known as the Lac Duong arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically enclosed within a spadix, a common feature among arums. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Rhaphidophora lacduongensis is also valued for its starchy roots, which can be processed into a type of flour.
