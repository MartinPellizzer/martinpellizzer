Rungia apiculata

Rungia apiculata, commonly known as the purple passionvine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from purple to red. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by various wildlife species. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are hairy and climbing.

Rungia apiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
