Ribes missurense

Ribes missurense, commonly known as the Missourian gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the central United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features compound leaves with 5 to 9 ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its fruits are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and jellies.

Ribes missurense thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
