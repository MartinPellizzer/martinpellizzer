Ruellia lysimachia

Ruellia lysimachia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial to full sunlight and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Ruellia lysimachia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
