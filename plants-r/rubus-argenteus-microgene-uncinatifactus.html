Rubus argenteus microgene uncinatifactus

Rubus argenteus microgene uncinatifactus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its thorny stems and dense growth habit make it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
