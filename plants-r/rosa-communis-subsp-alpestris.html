Rosa communis subsp. alpestris

Rosa communis subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a subspecies of the common rose characterized by its compact growth habit and hardy nature. It typically features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions across Europe.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
