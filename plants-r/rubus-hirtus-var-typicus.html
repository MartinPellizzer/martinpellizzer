Rubus hirtus var. typicus

Rubus hirtus var. typicus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. These berries are edible and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
