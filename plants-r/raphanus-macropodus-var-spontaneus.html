Raphanus macropodus var. spontaneus

Raphanus macropodus var. spontaneus, commonly known as the giant radish or wild radish, is a biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its large, fleshy taproot, which can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. Its leaves are lobed and often have a peppery scent, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural settings.
