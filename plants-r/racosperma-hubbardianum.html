Racosperma hubbardianum

Racosperma hubbardianum, commonly known as the pink-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often spindly growth habit. The plant produces small, fluffy pods that contain seeds, and it is known for its attractive, pale pink to cream-colored flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
