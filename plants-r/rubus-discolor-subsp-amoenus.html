Rubus discolor subsp. amoenus

Rubus discolor subsp.

amoenus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on maturity. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and edible fruits.
