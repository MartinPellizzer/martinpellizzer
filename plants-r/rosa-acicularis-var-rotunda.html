Rosa acicularis var. rotunda

Rosa acicularis var. rotunda, commonly known as the roundleaf prickly rose, is a thorny shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that persist into the fall.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique leaf morphology.
