Racosperma juliferum subsp. gilbertense

Racosperma juliferum subsp.

gilbertense, commonly known as the Gilbert's wattles, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It features slender, wiry stems and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has small, elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
