Restio tenellus

Restio tenellus, commonly known as the slender restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its structure is adapted to withstand coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Restio tenellus plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
