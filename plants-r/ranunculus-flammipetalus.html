Ranunculus flammipetalus

Ranunculus flammipetalus, commonly known as the flame petal buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bright orange to red flowers with deeply notched petals, typically blooming in spring. The plant has compound leaves with lobed or serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is considered invasive in some areas.
