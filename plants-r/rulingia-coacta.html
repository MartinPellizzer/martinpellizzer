Rulingia coacta

Rulingia coacta, commonly known as the red-veined rain tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with deep red veins against a green background. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rulingia coacta is also valued for its hard, durable wood in some regions.
