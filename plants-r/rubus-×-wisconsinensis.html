Rubus × wisconsinensis

Rubus × wisconsinensis, commonly known as the Wisconsin blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus chamaemorus.

It is native to the northern United States and parts of Canada, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its thorny stems and broad leaves are characteristic of the Rubus genus.

It is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated in gardens and wild landscapes.
