Rosa × dawsonii

Rosa × dawsonii, commonly known as the Dawson's rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of serrated, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the blossoms.

Rosa × dawsonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
