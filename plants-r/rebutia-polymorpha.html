Rebutia polymorpha

Rebutia polymorpha, commonly known as the variable rebutia, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
