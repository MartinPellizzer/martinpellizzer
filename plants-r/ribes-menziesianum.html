Ribes menziesianum

Ribes menziesianum, commonly known as the Sitka gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as along stream banks and in coastal forests. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Ribes menziesianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
