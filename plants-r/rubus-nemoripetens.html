Rubus nemoripetens

Rubus nemoripetens, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and small, dark purple berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its stems are thorny and can climb or spread along the ground.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other members of the Rubus genus.
