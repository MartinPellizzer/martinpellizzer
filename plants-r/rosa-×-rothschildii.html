Rosa × rothschildii

Rosa × rothschildii, commonly known as the Rothschild's rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces rose hips that are ornamental and can be used in herbal remedies.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in botanical gardens.
