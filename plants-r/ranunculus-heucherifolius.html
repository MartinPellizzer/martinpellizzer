Ranunculus heucherifolius

Ranunculus heucherifolius, commonly known as the heuchera-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow, white, or orange. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that resemble those of heuchera, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and early spring bloom.
