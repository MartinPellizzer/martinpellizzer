Ranunculus ganeschinii

Ranunculus ganeschinii, commonly known as the Ganeschini buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
