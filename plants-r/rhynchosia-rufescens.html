Rhynchosia rufescens

Rhynchosia rufescens, commonly known as the red-seed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional agriculture.

Rhynchosia rufescens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in sustainable farming practices.
