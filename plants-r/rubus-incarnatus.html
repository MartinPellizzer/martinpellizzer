Rubus incarnatus

Rubus incarnatus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of shrub in the family Rosaceae, characterized by its thorny stems and biennial growth habit. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus incarnatus is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates.
