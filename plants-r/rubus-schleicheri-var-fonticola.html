Rubus schleicheri var. fonticola

Rubus schleicheri var. fonticola, commonly known as the water raspberry, is a shrub native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found near streams and ponds. Its flowering period occurs in late spring, producing clusters of white to pale yellow flowers.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
