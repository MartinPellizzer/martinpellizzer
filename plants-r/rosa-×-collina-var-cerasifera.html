Rosa × collina var. cerasifera

Rosa × collina var. cerasifera, commonly known as the cherry rose, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits characteristics of both Rosa collina and Rosa cerasifera.

It is native to the coastal regions of western Europe, particularly in areas with mild, humid climates. The plant is notable for its dense, thorny growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, slightly acidic soils. Its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for garden borders and hedging.
