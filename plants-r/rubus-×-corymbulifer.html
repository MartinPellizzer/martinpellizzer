Rubus × corymbulifer

Rubus × corymbulifer, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between blackberry (Rubus fruticosus) and raspberry (Rubus idaeus).

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces berries that are typically a mix of blackberry and raspberry traits, often with a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This hybrid is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
