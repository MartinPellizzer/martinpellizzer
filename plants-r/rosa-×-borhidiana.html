Rosa × borhidiana

Rosa × borhidiana, commonly known as the Borhidiana rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink and red. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and is well adapted to temperate climates. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Rosa × borhidiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and hardiness.
