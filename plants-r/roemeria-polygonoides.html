Roemeria polygonoides

Roemeria polygonoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved roemeria, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, commonly known as the buckwheat family, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Roemeria polygonoides is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its resilience to drought conditions.
