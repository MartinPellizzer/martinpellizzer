Rubus evagatus var. fertilissimus

Rubus evagatus var. fertilissimus, commonly known as the fertile trailing raspberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in supporting local wildlife.
