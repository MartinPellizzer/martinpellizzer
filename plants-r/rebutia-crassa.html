Rebutia crassa

Rebutia crassa, commonly known as the thick-stemmed burro's tail, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, segmented stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental cactus due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Rebutia crassa is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
