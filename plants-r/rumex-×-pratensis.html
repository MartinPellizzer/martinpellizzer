Rumex × pratensis

Rumex × pratensis, commonly known as meadow sorrel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically reddish or bronze in color, and its clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and grasslands. It thrives in temperate climates and is known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its parent species.
