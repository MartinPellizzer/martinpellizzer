Rosa subalbida

Rosa subalbida, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in spring and summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, ovate leaflets and a compact, bushy growth habit.

Rosa subalbida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
