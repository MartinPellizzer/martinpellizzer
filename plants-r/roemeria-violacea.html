Roemeria violacea

Roemeria violacea, commonly known as the violet tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Roemeria violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
