Rubus hirtus var. lamyi

Rubus hirtus var. lamyi, commonly known as the Lamy's raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.
