Rhododendron pectinatum

Rhododendron pectinatum, commonly known as the white rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its lance-shaped, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions with acidic soils.

The flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
