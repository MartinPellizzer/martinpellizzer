Rosa affinis

Rosa affinis, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. Rosa affinis is well adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
