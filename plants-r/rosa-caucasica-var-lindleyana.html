Rosa caucasica var. lindleyana

Rosa caucasica var. lindleyana, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
