Reseda phyteuma f. glabra

Reseda phyteuma f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth false mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically green and slightly pubescent. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Reseda phyteuma f. glabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
