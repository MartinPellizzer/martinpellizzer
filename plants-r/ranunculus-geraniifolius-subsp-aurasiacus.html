Ranunculus geraniifolius subsp. aurasiacus

Ranunculus geraniifolius subsp.

aurasiacus, commonly known as the Aurasic mountain buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early summer, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
