Raddia maculata

Raddia maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and India.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with distinct markings, and the flowers are often pale green or white with a subtle fragrance.

Raddia maculata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
