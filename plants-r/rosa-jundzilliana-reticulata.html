Rosa jundzilliana reticulata

Rosa jundzilliana reticulata, commonly known as the Jundzilliana reticulate rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its intricate, lace-like foliage and fragrant, semi-double flowers.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers, which range in color from pale pink to deep red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated for both its beauty and its ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
