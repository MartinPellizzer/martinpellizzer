Rubus × praetextiformis

Rubus × praetextiformis, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between blackberries (Rubus fruticosus) and raspberries (Rubus idaeus).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, depending on the variety. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated in gardens and commercial orchards.
