Rubus garumnicus

Rubus garumnicus, commonly known as the European blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
