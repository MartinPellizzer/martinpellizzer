Rubus rivularis var. dasyacanthus

Rubus rivularis var. dasyacanthus, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible, dark purple berries. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant is often found in humid, temperate regions.

The berries are valued for their tart flavor and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
