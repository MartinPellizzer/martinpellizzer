Rubus oenoxylon

Rubus oenoxylon, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white and appear in early spring.

The berries are edible when ripe but contain numerous seeds and are often used in jams and wines.
