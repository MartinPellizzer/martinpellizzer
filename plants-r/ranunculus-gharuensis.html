Ranunculus gharuensis

Ranunculus gharuensis, commonly known as the Gharuensian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmately divided leaves and produces numerous small, yellowish or white flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species with specific ecological requirements.
