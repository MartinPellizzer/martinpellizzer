Rosa coriifolia var. jahniana

Rosa coriifolia var. jahniana, commonly known as the Jahn's sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets that are glossy and dark green. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of garden conditions.
