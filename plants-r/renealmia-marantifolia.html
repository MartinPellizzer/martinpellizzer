Renealmia marantifolia

Renealmia marantifolia, commonly known as the pink plectranthus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white variegated leaves, which are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet.

Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
