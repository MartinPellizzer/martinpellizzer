Rosa canina var. lanceolata

Rosa canina var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and oval, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals, and appear in clusters.

The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
