Rosa zagrabiensis

Rosa zagrabiensis, commonly known as the Zagreb rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

Rosa zagrabiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
