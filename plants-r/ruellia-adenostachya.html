Ruellia adenostachya

Ruellia adenostachya, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia adenostachya is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
