Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pentameres

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pentameres, commonly known as the five-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five distinct lobes, which are often accompanied by a bright orange center.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy beneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
