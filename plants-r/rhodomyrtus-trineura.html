Rhodomyrtus trineura

Rhodomyrtus trineura, commonly known as the Chinese rose or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant pink or red flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves, showy floral structures, and a preference for well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical climates.
