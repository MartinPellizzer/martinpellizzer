Rhynchosia angulosa

Rhynchosia angulosa, commonly known as the angular cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive angular pods. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in angular, ridged pods, which are a key feature of the species.
