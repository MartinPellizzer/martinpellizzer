Rosa glauca vallium

Rosa glauca vallium, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its low-growing habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces showy pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged in odd-pinnate patterns.

Rosa glauca vallium is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.
