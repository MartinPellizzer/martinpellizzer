Rosa × pseudowaitziana

Rosa × pseudowaitziana, commonly known as the Chinese tea rose, is a hybrid shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant typically has dark green, glossy leaves and produces hips that are edible and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rosa × pseudowaitziana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
