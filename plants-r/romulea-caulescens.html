Romulea caulescens

Romulea caulescens, commonly known as the stalked romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Romulea caulescens is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
