Rubus thyrsoideus f. grandiflorus

Rubus thyrsoideus f. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered blackberry.

It is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a dark purple to black berry, which is typically edible but often has a tart flavor.

This form is a naturally occurring variant of Rubus thyrsoideus, distinguished by its larger floral display.
