Ribes weddellianum

Ribes weddellianum, commonly known as the Weddell's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Arctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a dense, bushy growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and subarctic environments.

Ribes weddellianum plays an important ecological role by providing food for birds and other wildlife.
