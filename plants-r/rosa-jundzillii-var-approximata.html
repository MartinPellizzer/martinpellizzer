Rosa jundzillii var. approximata

Rosa jundzillii var. approximata, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers with a deep red center, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
