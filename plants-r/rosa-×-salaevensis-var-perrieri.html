Rosa × salaevensis var. perrieri

Rosa × salaevensis var. perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's rose, is a hybrid species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a strong fragrance, making it a desirable ornamental plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
