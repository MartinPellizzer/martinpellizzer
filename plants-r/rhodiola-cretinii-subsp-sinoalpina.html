Rhodiola cretinii subsp. sinoalpina

Rhodiola cretinii subsp.

sinoalpina, commonly known as the Tibetan rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and adjacent mountain ranges. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters.

The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with fleshy, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are small, pinkish-red, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability to extreme environments and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
