Rubus × minoriflorus

Rubus × minoriflorus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its compact growth habit and small, dark berries. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces flowers with five pink to white petals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
