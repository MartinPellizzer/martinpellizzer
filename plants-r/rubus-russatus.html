Rubus russatus

Rubus russatus, commonly known as the common blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus russatus is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
