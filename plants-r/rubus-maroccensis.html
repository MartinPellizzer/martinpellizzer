Rubus maroccensis

Rubus maroccensis, commonly known as the Moroccan blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
