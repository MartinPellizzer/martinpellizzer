Rubus tenuis var. ferox

Rubus tenuis var. ferox, commonly known as the sharpleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and small, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its berries are edible but can be quite tart, making them popular for use in jams and preserves.
