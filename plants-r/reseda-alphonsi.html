Reseda alphonsi

Reseda alphonsi, commonly known as the purple mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant purple or pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Reseda alphonsi is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
