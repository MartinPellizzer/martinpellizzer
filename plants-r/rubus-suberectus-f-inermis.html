Rubus suberectus f. inermis

Rubus suberectus f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
