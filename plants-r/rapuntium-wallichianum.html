Rapuntium wallichianum

Rapuntium wallichianum, commonly known as the Himalayan cabbages, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
