Rhamnus napalensis

Rhamnus napalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark purple or black berries. It is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
