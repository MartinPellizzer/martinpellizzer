Rosa × dumetorum var. subbicolor

Rosa × dumetorum var. subbicolor, commonly known as the pink sandalwood rose, is a hybrid species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers that typically range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting base. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in dense, glandular hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
