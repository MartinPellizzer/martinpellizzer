Rubus phyllanthus

Rubus phyllanthus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like China, India, and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, dark purple to black berries. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and appear in clusters.

The plant is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
