Rubus nutkanus var. parviflorus

Rubus nutkanus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to western North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it flowers with pale pink to white flowers.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
