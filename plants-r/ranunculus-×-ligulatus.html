Ranunculus × ligulatus

Ranunculus × ligulatus, commonly known as the shield-shaped buttercup, is a hybrid species of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers with a prominent central pistil. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. It is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

The plant's ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
