Rinorea fistulosa

Rinorea fistulosa, commonly known as the red-river beauty, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Rinorea fistulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
