Rubus × alsaticus

Rubus × alsaticus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white or pink flowers in the spring.

This species is often cultivated for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor.
