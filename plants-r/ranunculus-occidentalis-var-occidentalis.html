Ranunculus occidentalis var. occidentalis

Ranunculus occidentalis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
