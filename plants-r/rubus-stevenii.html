Rubus stevenii

Rubus stevenii, commonly known as Steven's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and rocky slopes.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex inflorescence and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
