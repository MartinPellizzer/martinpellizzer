Rosa ancylodon

Rosa ancylodon, commonly known as the sandhill rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils, often found in prairie and steppe habitats. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

Rosa ancylodon is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
