Rytidosperma corinum

Rytidosperma corinum, commonly known as the spikeless grass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
