Ranunculus hirtellus var. glabricaulis

Ranunculus hirtellus var. glabricaulis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves that are typically lobed and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
