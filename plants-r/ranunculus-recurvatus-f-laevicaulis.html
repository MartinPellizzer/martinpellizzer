Ranunculus recurvatus f. laevicaulis

Ranunculus recurvatus f. laevicaulis, commonly known as the smoothleaf meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a smooth margin, distinguishing it from other ranunculus species.

The flowers are usually solitary, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
