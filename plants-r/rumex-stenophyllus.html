Rumex stenophyllus

Rumex stenophyllus, commonly known as narrow-leaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils along stream banks, marshes, and woodland edges. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose, terminal spikes.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
