Rubus rumorum

Rubus rumorum, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of bramble within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that mature to a deep purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Rubus rumorum is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
