Rapanea ciliata var. pentandra

Rapanea ciliata var. pentandra, commonly known as the Kauri tree, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a large evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 50 meters, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and have a distinctive waxy coating. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries.

It plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's native forests and is valued for its ornamental and timber qualities.
