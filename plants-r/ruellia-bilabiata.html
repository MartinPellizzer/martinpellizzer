Ruellia bilabiata

Ruellia bilabiata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing as misty clouds. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Ruellia bilabiata thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
