Rubus fruticosus var. silesiacus

Rubus fruticosus var. silesiacus, commonly known as the Silesian bramble, is a thorny shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense growth habit and prickly stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
