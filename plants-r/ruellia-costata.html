Ruellia costata

Ruellia costata, commonly known as the Florida verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Ruellia costata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
