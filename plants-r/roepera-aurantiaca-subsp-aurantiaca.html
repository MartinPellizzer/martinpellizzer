Roepera aurantiaca subsp. aurantiaca

Roepera aurantiaca subsp.

aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange roepera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its vibrant orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
