Rosa carbonariensis

Rosa carbonariensis, commonly known as the Italian rose, is a species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shrubland and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its hips are orange-red and edible.

Rosa carbonariensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
