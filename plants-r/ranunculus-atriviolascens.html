Ranunculus atriviolascens

Ranunculus atriviolascens, commonly known as the black-veined buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with prominent black veins. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
