Rosa pimpinellifolia charrieri

Rosa pimpinellifolia charrieri, commonly known as the rock rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem that can become branched over time.

Rosa pimpinellifolia charrieri is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
