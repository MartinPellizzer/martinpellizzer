Rosa borysthenica

Rosa borysthenica, commonly known as the Siberian rose, is a species of shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its hardy, thorny stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in the summer months. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

Rosa borysthenica is often used in ornamental gardening due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
