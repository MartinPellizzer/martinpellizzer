Rosa viscaria var. sparsiflora

Rosa viscaria var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparsiflora rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features arching stems covered in dense, thorny foliage, and its flowers are typically pink to red, with five to seven petals. The plant produces small, oval fruits called hips, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and attractive blooms make it a valuable addition to diverse garden landscapes.
