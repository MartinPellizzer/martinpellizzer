Ranunculus haynaldii

Ranunculus haynaldii, commonly known as the Haynald's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a central yellow stamen, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Ranunculus haynaldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, delicate flowers.
