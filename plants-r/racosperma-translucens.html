Racosperma translucens

Racosperma translucens, commonly known as the translucent acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect shrub form. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, which are typical of the genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Racosperma translucens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils across the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.
