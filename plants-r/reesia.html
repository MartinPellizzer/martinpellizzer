Reesia

Reesia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plants typically have upright, branched stems and are often cultivated for their ornamental value. Reesia species are known for their showy, bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun, making them suitable for rock gardens and borders.
