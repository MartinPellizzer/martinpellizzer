Raphanus raphanistrum var. flavus

Raphanus raphanistrum var. flavus, commonly known as the yellow wild radish, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with bright yellow flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, peppery taste, similar to that of cultivated radishes.

The yellow wild radish is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions.
