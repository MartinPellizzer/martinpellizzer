Rosa rubiginosa f. genuina

Rosa rubiginosa f. genuina, commonly known as the Spanish rose or the rough rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a round, red berry that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

The species is notable for its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
