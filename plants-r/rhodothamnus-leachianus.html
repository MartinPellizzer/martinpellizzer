Rhodothamnus leachianus

Rhodothamnus leachianus, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in warmer climates.
