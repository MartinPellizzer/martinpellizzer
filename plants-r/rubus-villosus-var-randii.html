Rubus villosus var. randii

Rubus villosus var. randii, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing vegetative growth and the second year bearing fruit. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and hairy surfaces, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces aggregate fruits known as drupes.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.
