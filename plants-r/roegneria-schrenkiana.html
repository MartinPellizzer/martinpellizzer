Roegneria schrenkiana

Roegneria schrenkiana, commonly known as the Schrenk's timothy, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Roegneria schrenkiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
