Rhododendron neriiflorum subsp. phoenicodum

Rhododendron neriiflorum subsp.

phoenicodum, commonly known as the Chinese flame rhododendron, is a species native to China. It is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
