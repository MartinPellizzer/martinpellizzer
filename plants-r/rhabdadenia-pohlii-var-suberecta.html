Rhabdadenia pohlii var. suberecta

Rhabdadenia pohlii var. suberecta, commonly known as the upright rhabdadenia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are often branched and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, adapted to store water in arid conditions.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
