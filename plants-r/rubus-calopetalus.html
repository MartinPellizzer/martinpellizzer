Rubus calopetalus

Rubus calopetalus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white with five petals and are borne in clusters.

Rubus calopetalus is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
