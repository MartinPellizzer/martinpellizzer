Rosa wichurana

Rosa wichurana, commonly known as the Chilean rose or the wild rose of Chile, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in the spring.

Rosa wichurana is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
