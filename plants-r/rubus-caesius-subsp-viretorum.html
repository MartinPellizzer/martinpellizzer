Rubus caesius subsp. viretorum

Rubus caesius subsp.

viretorum, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are white to pale pink, and it is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
