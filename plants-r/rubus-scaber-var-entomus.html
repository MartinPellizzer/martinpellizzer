Rubus scaber var. entomus

Rubus scaber var. entomus, commonly known as the roughberry entomus, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a hardy, adaptable species within the Rubus genus.
