Rhodiola ovatisepala var. chingii

Rhodiola ovatisepala var. chingii, commonly known as the Ching's rose tibetan sandwort, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This variant is notable for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Tibetan medicine.
