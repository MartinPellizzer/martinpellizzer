Ribesiodes nummulariifolium

Ribesiodes nummulariifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
