Ribes trifidum

Ribes trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of shrubs and small trees. The plant is characterized by its trifid (three-lobed) leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are edible berries that mature to a dark red color, often used in culinary applications.

Ribes trifidum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
