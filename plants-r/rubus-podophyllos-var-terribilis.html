Rubus podophyllos var. terribilis

Rubus podophyllos var. terribilis, commonly known as the devil's boarshrub, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, aggregate berries that are typically red or black in color. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is often considered a weed in certain regions.
