Rhododendron concinnum

Rhododendron concinnum, commonly known as the Appalachian azalea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It has elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, leathery berries that contain seeds.

Rhododendron concinnum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
