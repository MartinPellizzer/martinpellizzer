Remijia glomerata

Remijia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen, providing year-round interest.

Remijia glomerata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
