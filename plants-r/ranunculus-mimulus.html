Ranunculus mimulus

Ranunculus mimulus, commonly known as the yellow water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming from spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

Ranunculus mimulus is often found in shallow water or damp soil, making it a common sight in wetland habitats.
