Rubus plusiacanthus var. melanadenius

Rubus plusiacanthus var. melanadenius, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
