Rubus adauctus

Rubus adauctus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a species of brambles belonging to the Rubiaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and widely used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus adauctus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
