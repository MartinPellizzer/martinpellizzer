Rosa micrantha var. hystrix

Rosa micrantha var. hystrix, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
