Rubus macrophyllus subsp. albiflorus

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower evergreen bramble, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
