Rosa afzeliana var. glandulifera

Rosa afzeliana var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with glandular dots. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens. It produces oval, red to brown fruits called hips, which are edible and used in traditional medicine.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitats.
