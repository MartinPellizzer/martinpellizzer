Rubus micans var. nigroviridis

Rubus micans var. nigroviridis, commonly known as the black-leafed bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its dark green, almost black foliage that gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
