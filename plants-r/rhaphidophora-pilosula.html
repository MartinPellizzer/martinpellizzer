Rhaphidophora pilosula

Rhaphidophora pilosula, commonly known as the velvety monstera, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central and South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive velvety texture and a waxy surface. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 6 feet in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a slight sheen, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by orange fruits.

Rhaphidophora pilosula is a popular houseplant due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
