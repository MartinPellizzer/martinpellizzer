Rosa canina f. nitida

Rosa canina f. nitida, commonly known as the silver rose or shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fuzzy fruits called hips. The plant is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Rosa canina f. nitida is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it suitable for various garden environments.
