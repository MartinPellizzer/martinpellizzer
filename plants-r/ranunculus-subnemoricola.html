Ranunculus subnemoricola

Ranunculus subnemoricola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers with a hairy, greenish stem and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage is characterized by finely divided, serrated leaflets, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus subnemoricola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, daisy-like flowers.
