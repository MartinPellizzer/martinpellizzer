Rubus chabertii f. glabriuscula

Rubus chabertii f. glabriuscula, commonly known as the glabrous blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of eastern North America. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. Its foliage is usually a deep green, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
