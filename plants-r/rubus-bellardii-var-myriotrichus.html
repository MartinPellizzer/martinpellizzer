Rubus bellardii var. myriotrichus

Rubus bellardii var. myriotrichus, commonly known as the Myriotrichus blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The variety is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and its ability to form thickets.
