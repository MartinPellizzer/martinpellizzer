Rubus sepiiformis

Rubus sepiiformis, commonly known as the sea holly raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to coastal regions of southern China and northern Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and the fruits are aggregate berries that develop from the flower clusters.

It thrives in saline and humid environments, often growing in coastal scrub and rocky habitats.
