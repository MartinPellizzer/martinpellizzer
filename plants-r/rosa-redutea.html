Rosa redutea

Rosa redutea, commonly known as the red-tipped rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, with a distinctive red tip on the petals. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa redutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and fragrance.
