Ritterocereus chacalapensis

Ritterocereus chacalapensis, commonly known as the Chacalapense cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water efficiently. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing during the warmer months.

This cactus is valued for its resilience and is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its low maintenance requirements.
