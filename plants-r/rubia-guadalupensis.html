Rubia guadalupensis

Rubia guadalupensis, commonly known as Mexican madder, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. It produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, followed by red or orange berries.

Rubia guadalupensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
