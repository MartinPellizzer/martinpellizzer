Rubus radula var. fissipetalus

Rubus radula var. fissipetalus, commonly known as the fissipetal raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupelets, which are usually red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species within the Rubus genus.
