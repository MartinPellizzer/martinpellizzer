Rosa pseudonorvegica

Rosa pseudonorvegica, commonly known as the Norwegian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly in the Alps and Scandinavia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its thorny stems are typical of many rose species.

Rosa pseudonorvegica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
