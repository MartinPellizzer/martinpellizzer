Ranunculus microdon

Ranunculus microdon, commonly known as the small globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with 5 to 10 petals. The plant has a short, hairy stem and basal leaves that are palmately divided. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a striking addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.
