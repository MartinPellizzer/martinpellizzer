Raspalia struthioloides

Raspalia struthioloides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
