Rubus subornatus var. fockei

Rubus subornatus var. fockei, commonly known as the Focke's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-prostrate growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Its berries are edible but are typically less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties.
