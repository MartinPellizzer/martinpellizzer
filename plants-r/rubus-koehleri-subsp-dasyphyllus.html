Rubus koehleri subsp. dasyphyllus

Rubus koehleri subsp.

dasyphyllus, commonly known as the dense-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, leathery leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
