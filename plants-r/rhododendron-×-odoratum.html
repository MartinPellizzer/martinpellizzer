Rhododendron × odoratum

Rhododendron × odoratum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron simsii and Rhododendron indicum.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is evergreen and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil, often found in temperate regions of China. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and floral structures typical of the Ericaceae family.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant colors and enduring blooms.
