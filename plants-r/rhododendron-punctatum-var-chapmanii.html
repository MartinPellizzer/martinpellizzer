Rhododendron punctatum var. chapmanii

Rhododendron punctatum var. chapmanii, commonly known as the Chapman's flame azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its foliage consists of dark green, elliptical leaves that provide a striking contrast to the bright blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its showy flowers.
