Rosa × collina subsp. saxatilis

Rosa × collina subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the saxatile rock rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae). It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to rocky and mountainous regions of Europe.

The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is well adapted to poor, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often found in alpine and coastal environments, where it thrives in full sun and withstands harsh climatic conditions.
