Ranunculus sibboensis

Ranunculus sibboensis, commonly known as the Sibbo's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features a short, hairy stem with trifoliate or palmately compound leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens.

Ranunculus sibboensis is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or grassy slopes.
