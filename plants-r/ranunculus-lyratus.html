Ranunculus lyratus

Ranunculus lyratus, commonly known as the lyrated buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals that resemble a cup, with a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its delicate, herbaceous appearance.

Ranunculus lyratus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other showy and often toxic plants.
