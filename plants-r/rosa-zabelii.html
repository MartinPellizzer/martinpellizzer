Rosa zabelii

Rosa zabelii, commonly known as the Zabel's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include hirsute (hairy) stems and stipitate (stem-like) stipules, making it distinctive among the Rosa genus.

Rosa zabelii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
