Rubus costatus var. tomentosus

Rubus costatus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is an important species for local wildlife.
