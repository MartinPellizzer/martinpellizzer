Rubus scidularum

Rubus scidularum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important part of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in horticulture.
