Rhynchospora corymbosa var. panduranganii

Rhynchospora corymbosa var. panduranganii, commonly known as the panduranganii sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
