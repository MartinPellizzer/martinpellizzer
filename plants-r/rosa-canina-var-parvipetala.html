Rosa canina var. parvipetala

Rosa canina var. parvipetala, commonly known as the small-flowered dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its smaller, more delicate petals compared to the typical Rosa canina. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows, thriving in well-drained soils.
