Rubus hercegovinicus

Rubus hercegovinicus, commonly known as the Hercegovina raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and shrublands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
