Rhodophiala laeta

Rhodophiala laeta, commonly known as the silver leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the soil, and it prefers well-drained, nutrient-rich substrates.

Rhodophiala laeta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
