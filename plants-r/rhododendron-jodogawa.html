Rhododendron jodogawa

Rhododendron jodogawa, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron jodogawa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
