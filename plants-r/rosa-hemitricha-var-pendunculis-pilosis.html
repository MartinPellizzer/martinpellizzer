Rosa hemitricha var. pendunculis-pilosis

Rosa hemitricha var. pendunculis-pilosis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets that have serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
