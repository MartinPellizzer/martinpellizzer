Rubus pulcher

Rubus pulcher, commonly known as the beautiful bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Japan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small white or pink flowers. Its fruits, known as berries, are typically red or black when mature and are edible.

Rubus pulcher is often found in forest understories and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
