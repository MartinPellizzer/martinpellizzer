Racosperma caerulescens

Racosperma caerulescens, commonly known as the blue acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and dense foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid regions.

Racosperma caerulescens is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits surrounding vegetation.
