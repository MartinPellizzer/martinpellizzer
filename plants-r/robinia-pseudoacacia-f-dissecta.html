Robinia pseudoacacia f. dissecta

Robinia pseudoacacia f. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected false acacia, is a unique variant of the honey locust tree.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world, including North America. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods.

This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and adaptability to different soil conditions.
