Rosa communis var. subobovata

Rosa communis var. subobovata, commonly known as the subobovate rose, is a less commonly cultivated variant of the common rose.

It features slightly elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are broader at the base, giving it a distinct foliage appearance. The flowers are typically pink or white, with a delicate fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic contribution.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
