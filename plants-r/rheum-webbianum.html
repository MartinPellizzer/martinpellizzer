Rheum webbianum

Rheum webbianum, commonly known as the Armenian rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of medicinal compounds.

Rheum webbianum is also valued for its ornamental qualities due to its striking foliage.
