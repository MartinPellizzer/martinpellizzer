Rosa canina var. fortinata

Rosa canina var. fortinata, commonly known as the French rose or wild dog rose, is a variant of the common rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring, which give way to orange-red hips in the fall. These hips are edible and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
