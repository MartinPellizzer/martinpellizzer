Ringentiarum ringens var. sieboldii

Ringentiarum ringens var. sieboldii, commonly known as the Siebold's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is prized for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
