Ribes sanguineum var. glutinosum

Ribes sanguineum var. glutinosum, commonly known as sticky currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its reddish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated margin. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its berries in home gardens.
