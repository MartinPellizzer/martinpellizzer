Rapuntium fastigiatum

Rapuntium fastigiatum, commonly known as the upright rapunculus, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellow flowers that appear in early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its roots are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to that of watercress.

It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides in temperate regions.
