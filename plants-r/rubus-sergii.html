Rubus sergii

Rubus sergii, commonly known as the Serhi's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, particularly found in the Caucasus region. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
