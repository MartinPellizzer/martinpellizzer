Rosa communis var. imponens

Rosa communis var. imponens, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring and summer. The plant is native to China and has been cultivated for centuries for its ornamental value. It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The fruit, a red berry, contains seeds that can be dispersed by birds and other animals.
