Rhamnus saxatilis subsp. saxatilis

Rhamnus saxatilis subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the rock buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky and dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, dark berry that ripens in late summer, attracting various bird species.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and preference for calcareous soils.
