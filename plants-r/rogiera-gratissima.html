Rogiera gratissima

Rogiera gratissima, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a complex structure. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and blooms in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, sweet aroma that attracts pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique fragrance.
