Rubus posnaniensis

Rubus posnaniensis, commonly known as the Posnan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.
