Rhododendron nakaii

Rhododendron nakaii, commonly known as the Nakai rhododendron, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly found in Japan and the Russian Far East.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with acidic soils. The plant features dense, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for cool, moist climates.

Rhododendron nakaii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
