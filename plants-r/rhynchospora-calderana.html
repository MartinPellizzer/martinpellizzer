Rhynchospora calderana

Rhynchospora calderana, commonly known as the Calderana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Rhynchospora calderana plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
