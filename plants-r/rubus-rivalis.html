Rubus rivalis

Rubus rivalis, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the northern hemisphere.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant has thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The berries are edible and widely used in culinary applications.
