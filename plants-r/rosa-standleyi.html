Rosa standleyi

Rosa standleyi, commonly known as the Standley rose, is a species native to the highlands of Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and summer. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Rosa standleyi is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
