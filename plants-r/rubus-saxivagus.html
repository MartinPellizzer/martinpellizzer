Rubus saxivagus

Rubus saxivagus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems, which help it withstand harsh alpine conditions. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, containing numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant often grows in rocky or gravelly soils.

Rubus saxivagus plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems, providing food for various wildlife species.
