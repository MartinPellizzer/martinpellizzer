Rosa flava

Rosa flava, commonly known as the yellow rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Rosa flava is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking color.
