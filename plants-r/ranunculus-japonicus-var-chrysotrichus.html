Ranunculus japonicus var. chrysotrichus

Ranunculus japonicus var. chrysotrichus, commonly known as the golden-haired buttercup, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with hairy sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, yellow blooms in early spring.

The variety chrysotrichus is distinguished by its hairy, golden sepals that give it a unique appearance.
