Rosa mollis f. eductidens

Rosa mollis f. eductidens, commonly known as the soft rose or velvet rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa mollis species, characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and fragrant flowers.

It is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a subtle scent, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
