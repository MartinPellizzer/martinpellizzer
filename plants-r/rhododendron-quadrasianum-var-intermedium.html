Rhododendron quadrasianum var. intermedium

Rhododendron quadrasianum var. intermedium, commonly known as the Intermediate Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial shade.
