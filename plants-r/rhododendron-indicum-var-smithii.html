Rhododendron indicum var. smithii

Rhododendron indicum var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's Indian rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a leathery texture, and its flowers are typically pink to lavender in color, appearing in clusters. The plant thrives in cool, moist climates and prefers acidic, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use as a foundation plant or in shaded woodland settings.
