Rosa procera

Rosa procera, commonly known as the tall rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are often armed with thorns.

Rosa procera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
