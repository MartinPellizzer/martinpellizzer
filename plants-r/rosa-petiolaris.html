Rosa petiolaris

Rosa petiolaris, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that allow it to climb and twine around supports. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

Rosa petiolaris is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.
