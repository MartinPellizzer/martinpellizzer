Rhododendron × tebotan

Rhododendron × tebotan, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, broad leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is known for its adaptability to acidic soils. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, ranging from white to deep red, and bloom in the spring.

Rhododendron × tebotan is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
