Ranunculus × cesatii

Ranunculus × cesatii, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, double or semi-double flowers in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red, with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically trifoliate or more deeply divided. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its botanical classification as a hybrid reflects its parentage from Ranunculus asiaticus and Ranunculus tectorum.
