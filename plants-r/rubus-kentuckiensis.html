Rubus kentuckiensis

Rubus kentuckiensis, commonly known as the Kentucky blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
