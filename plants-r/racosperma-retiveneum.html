Racosperma retiveneum

Racosperma retiveneum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.

Its seeds are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices and for soil stabilization.
