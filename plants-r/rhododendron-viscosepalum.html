Rhododendron viscosepalum

Rhododendron viscosepalum, commonly known as the sticky-papilionaceous rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its high-altitude habitat. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color and are borne in clusters.

Rhododendron viscosepalum is often found in rocky or forested areas at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters.
