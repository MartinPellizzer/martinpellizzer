Rhaphidorhynchus

Rhaphidorhynchus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's floating leaves are supported by long, flexible petioles that allow them to remain buoyant on the water's surface. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, with a distinct, elongated structure that aids in pollination by insects.

Rhaphidorhynchus plays an important ecological role in its environment by offering shelter and food for numerous aquatic species.
