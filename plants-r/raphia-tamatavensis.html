Raphia tamatavensis

Raphia tamatavensis, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a large, tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its massive, fan-shaped leaves that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant produces long, slender, yellowish-white inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers. Its trunk is robust and often covered with a fibrous, spongy layer known as pith.

Raphia tamatavensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
