Rubus hebecaulis var. latipelatus

Rubus hebecaulis var. latipelatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its arching stems and large, broad leaves that are typically three-lobed. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
