Roegneria macroura subsp. pilosivaginata

Roegneria macroura subsp.

pilosivaginata, commonly known as the hairy sheath wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
