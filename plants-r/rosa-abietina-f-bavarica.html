Rosa abietina f. bavarica

Rosa abietina f. bavarica, commonly known as the Bavarian fir rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa abietina species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, glossy foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, where it thrives in cooler, moist environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
