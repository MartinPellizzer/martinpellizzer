Rubus idaeus monstr. phyllanthus

Rubus idaeus monstr.

phyllanthus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with stems that produce fruit in their second year.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces aggregate fruits composed of multiple drupelets. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is widely cultivated for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and used in various culinary applications.
