Rapanea peruviana

Rapanea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian holly, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests, where it thrives in humid and cool climates. Its leaves are glossy and often have a waxy texture, and the plant is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Rapanea peruviana is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
