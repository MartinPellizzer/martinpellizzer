Rhynchosia tenuicaulis

Rhynchosia tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves with small, elliptical leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.
