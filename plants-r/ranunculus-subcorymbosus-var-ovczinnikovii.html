Ranunculus subcorymbosus var. ovczinnikovii

Ranunculus subcorymbosus var. ovczinnikovii, commonly known as the Ovczinnikov's buttercup, is a rare and ornamental member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a soft, hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
