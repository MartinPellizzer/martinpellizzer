Rubus caesius var. corymbosus

Rubus caesius var. corymbosus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the first-year stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as raspberries, which are typically red but can also be yellow or golden in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins and a hairy surface.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
