Rubus maureri

Rubus maureri, commonly known as the Maori raspberry, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in traditional Māori cuisine.
