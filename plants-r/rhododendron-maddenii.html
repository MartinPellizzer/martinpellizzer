Rhododendron maddenii

Rhododendron maddenii, commonly known as the Maddean rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron maddenii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
