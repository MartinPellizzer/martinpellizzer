Rosa hakonensis

Rosa hakonensis, commonly known as the Queen of the North, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, single or double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include fragrant petals, a central yellow stamen, and an upright growth habit.

Rosa hakonensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
