Ranunculus malinovskii

Ranunculus malinovskii, commonly known as the Malinovsky buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves and produces yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
