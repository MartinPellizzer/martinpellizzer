Rubus idaeus f. simplicior

Rubus idaeus f. simplicior, commonly known as the simple-leaved raspberry.

This botanical variant is a cultivar of the red raspberry, characterized by its simpler, less lobed leaves compared to the typical raspberry. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though some varieties may yield yellow or golden berries. The plant is a perennial shrub with thorny stems and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental qualities in gardens.
