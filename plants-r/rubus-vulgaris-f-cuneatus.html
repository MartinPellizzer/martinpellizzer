Rubus vulgaris f. cuneatus

Rubus vulgaris f. cuneatus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry plant.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive cuneate (wedge-shaped) leaflets. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
