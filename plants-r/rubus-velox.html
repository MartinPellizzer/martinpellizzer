Rubus velox

Rubus velox, commonly known as the quick-growing blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically light green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries in the summer. Rubus velox thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its rapid growth rate makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
