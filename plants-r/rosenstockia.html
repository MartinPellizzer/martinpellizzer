Rosenstockia, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. These plants are typically found in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Rosenstockia species are native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.
