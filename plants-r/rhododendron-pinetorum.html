Rhododendron pinetorum

Rhododendron pinetorum, commonly known as the pineleaf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features large, elliptical leaves that are often hairy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Rhododendron pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
