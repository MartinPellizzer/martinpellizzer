Rhododendron japonicum f. multifidum

Rhododendron japonicum f. multifidum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea or Japanese evergreen azalea, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, leathery foliage that remains green throughout the year, providing year-round interest. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored plant in horticultural settings.
