Ranunculus grandis var. mirissimus

Ranunculus grandis var. mirissimus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petal-like sepals, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has alternate, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
