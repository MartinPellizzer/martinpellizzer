Rubus × pseudoidaeus f. pseudocaesius

Rubus × pseudoidaeus f. pseudocaesius, commonly known as the black raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a deep, dark red or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavorful berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
