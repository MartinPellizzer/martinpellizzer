Rosa × transmota var. germanica

Rosa × transmota var. germanica, commonly known as the German rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically has dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It is native to Central Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on the parent species.
