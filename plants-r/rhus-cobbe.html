Rhus cobbe

Rhus cobbe, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the poison ivy and mango. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnate with 5 to 9 leaflets. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by orange-red, spiny fruits that contain seeds.
