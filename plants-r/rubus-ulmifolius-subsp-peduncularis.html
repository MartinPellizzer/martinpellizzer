Rubus ulmifolius subsp. peduncularis

Rubus ulmifolius subsp.

peduncularis, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically three to five leaflets.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
