Ranunculus magnimammus

Ranunculus magnimammus, commonly known as the giant mammillary buttercup, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with numerous petal-like sepals, which can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Ranunculus magnimammus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
