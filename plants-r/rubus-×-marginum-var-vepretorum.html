Rubus × marginum var. vepretorum

Rubus × marginum var. vepretorum, commonly known as the redcurrant bush, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, making it a valuable crop for both ornamental and culinary purposes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits and its ability to form dense thickets.
