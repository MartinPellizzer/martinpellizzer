Rhynchospora exaltata var. cephalophora

Rhynchospora exaltata var. cephalophora, commonly known as the tall marsh sedge, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, often in areas with standing water.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated inflorescence, which gives it its common name.
