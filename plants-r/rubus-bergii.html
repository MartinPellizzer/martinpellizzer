Rubus bergii

Rubus bergii, commonly known as the Berg's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and open areas with well-drained soil.

Rubus bergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
