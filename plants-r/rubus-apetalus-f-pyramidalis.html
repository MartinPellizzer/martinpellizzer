Rubus apetalus f. pyramidalis

Rubus apetalus f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal blackberry, is a unique variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive pyramidal growth habit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually small and white, and it produces small, dark purple berries that are edible.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
