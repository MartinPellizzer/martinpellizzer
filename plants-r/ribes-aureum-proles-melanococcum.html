Ribes aureum proles melanococcum

Ribes aureum proles melanococcum, commonly known as the blackcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. The plant has compound leaves with five to nine elliptical leaflets, and it typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees.
