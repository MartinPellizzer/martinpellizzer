Ruellia haematantha

Ruellia haematantha, commonly known as the red-veined ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Ruellia haematantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes used in floral arrangements.
