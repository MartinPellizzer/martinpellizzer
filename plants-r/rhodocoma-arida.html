Rhodocoma arida

Rhodocoma arida, commonly known as the desert rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with thick leaves that reduce water loss. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Rhodocoma arida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
