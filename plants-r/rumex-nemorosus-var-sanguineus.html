Rumex nemorosus var. sanguineus

Rumex nemorosus var. sanguineus, commonly known as the red sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, reddish-pink leaves that are slightly hairy and have a sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose spikes, appearing in early spring.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
