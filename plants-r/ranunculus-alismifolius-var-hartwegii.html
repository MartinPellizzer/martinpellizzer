Ranunculus alismifolius var. hartwegii

Ranunculus alismifolius var. hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in ponds, streams, and wet meadows. The plant has delicate, fern-like leaves that are submerged or floating, and its flowers are usually yellow with five petals. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich aquatic environments.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
