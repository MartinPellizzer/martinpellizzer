Rubus suspiciosus

Rubus suspiciosus, commonly known as the western prickly pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit, a type of berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Rubus suspiciosus is native to western North America and thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and open areas.
