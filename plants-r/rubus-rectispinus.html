Rubus rectispinus

Rubus rectispinus, commonly known as the erect blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
