Rosa ravellae

Rosa ravellae, commonly known as the Ravella rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and are followed by small, red fruits known as hips.

Rosa ravellae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
