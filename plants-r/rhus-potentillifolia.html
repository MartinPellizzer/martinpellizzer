Rhus potentillifolia

Rhus potentillifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are alternate and often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
