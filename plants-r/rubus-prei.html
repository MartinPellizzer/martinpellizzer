Rubus prei

Rubus prei, commonly known as the Prei's blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species.

The berries are used in local cuisines and have also been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
