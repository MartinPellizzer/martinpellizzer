Rhododendron adamsii

Rhododendron adamsii, commonly known as the Adams' rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, moist habitats such as forests and rocky slopes, thriving in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron adamsii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to flourish in specific environmental conditions.
