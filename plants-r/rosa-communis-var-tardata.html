Rosa communis var. tardata

Rosa communis var. tardata, commonly known as the late blooming rose, is a variety of the common rose that is characterized by its delayed flowering period.

This plant typically produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom later in the growing season compared to other rose varieties. It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rosa genus. The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their beauty and scent.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a late-season floral display.
