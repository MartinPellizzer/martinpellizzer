Rosa alpina var. pugetii

Rosa alpina var. pugetii, commonly known as Puget's alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces hips that are orange-red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
