Rosa rubiginosa var. iberica

Rosa rubiginosa var. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its dark red to almost black flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and historical use in traditional medicine.
