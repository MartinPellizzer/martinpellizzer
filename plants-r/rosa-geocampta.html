Rosa geocampta

Rosa geocampta, commonly known as the groundcover rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces rosehips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. It thrives in partial to full sun and is drought-tolerant once established.

Rosa geocampta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
