Ranunculus hyperboreus var. intertextus

Ranunculus hyperboreus var. intertextus, commonly known as the Arctic buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant has a short, stout stem with basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. Its flowers bloom in late summer and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and flies.

This variant is distinguished by its finely textured leaves and is often found in alpine tundra environments.
