Rauvolfia canescens var. glabra

Rauvolfia canescens var. glabra, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its alkaloid content, particularly reserpine, which has been used in the treatment of hypertension.
