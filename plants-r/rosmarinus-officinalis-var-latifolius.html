Rosmarinus officinalis var. latifolius

Rosmarinus officinalis var. latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf rosemary, is a variant of the common rosemary plant.

It is characterized by its broader, more elongated leaves compared to the standard rosemary. This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. It produces small blue or purple flowers and is often used in culinary and aromatic applications.

Its foliage is fragrant, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and ornamental value.
