Rosa pendulina var. pyrenaica

Rosa pendulina var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean hanging rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its drooping, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pink to white. The plant has slender, arching stems and narrow, serrated leaves that are covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
