Rosa macropoda

Rosa macropoda, commonly known as the large-flowered rose, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually pink or red, with five to seven petals. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa macropoda is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
