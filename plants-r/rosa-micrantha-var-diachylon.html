Rosa micrantha var. diachylon

Rosa micrantha var. diachylon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically borne in clusters. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to cooler climates.
