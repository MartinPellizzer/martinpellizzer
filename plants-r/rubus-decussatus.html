Rubus decussatus

Rubus decussatus, commonly known as the cross-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are arranged in a cross-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Rubus decussatus is a perennial shrub that plays a role in supporting local wildlife and ecosystem diversity.
