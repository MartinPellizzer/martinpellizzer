Rosa spinosissima var. mandshurica

Rosa spinosissima var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian prickly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include hairy stems and ovate, dark green foliage.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in colder climates.
