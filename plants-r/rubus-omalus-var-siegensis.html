Rubus omalus var. siegensis

Rubus omalus var. siegensis, commonly known as the Siegen's bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
