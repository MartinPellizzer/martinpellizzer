Ramium cylindricum

Ramium cylindricum, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swales. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ramium cylindricum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
