Rottboellia tripsacoides

Rottboellia tripsacoides, commonly known as false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It has slender, cylindrical stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, grass-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green color.

Rottboellia tripsacoides is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or as a ornamental plant.
