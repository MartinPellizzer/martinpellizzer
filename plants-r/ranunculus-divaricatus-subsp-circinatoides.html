Ranunculus divaricatus subsp. circinatoides

Ranunculus divaricatus subsp.

circinatoides, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, arranged in a cup-like structure, and has finely divided, serrated leaves. Its stems are often hairy and can be either erect or prostrate, depending on the growing conditions.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, with the leaves being deeply lobed and sometimes appearing in a spiral arrangement.
