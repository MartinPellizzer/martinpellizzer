Rhododendron aurigeranum subsp. hirsutum

Rhododendron aurigeranum subsp.

hirsutum, commonly known as the Hirsute Aurigeran Rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white.

The subspecies is known for its robust growth and adaptability to acidic, well-drained soils. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
