Rubus crispulus

Rubus crispulus, commonly known as the crinkled bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in woodland edges and rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters, followed by red, hairy berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
