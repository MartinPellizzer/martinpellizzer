Ranunculus pedatus

Ranunculus pedatus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its flowers bloom from April to June, and it is known for its attractive appearance and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.

Ranunculus pedatus is also a popular ornamental plant in gardens due to its vibrant color and delicate foliage.
