Rosa keissleriana

Rosa keissleriana, commonly known as the Keissler's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink and white, usually blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include pinnate stipules and a structure typical of the Rosa genus, with stipitate stipules and stipitate glands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
