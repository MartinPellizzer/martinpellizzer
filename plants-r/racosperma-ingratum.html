Racosperma ingratum

Racosperma ingratum, commonly known as the stinking acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

The species is part of the legume family and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
