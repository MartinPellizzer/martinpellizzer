Rosa coriifolia var. venosa

Rosa coriifolia var. venosa, commonly known as the Veined Dogwood Rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely veined, white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. It produces red or orange hips, which are edible and often used in herbal remedies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
