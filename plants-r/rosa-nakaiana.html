Rosa nakaiana

Rosa nakaiana, commonly known as the Hawaiian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa nakaiana is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
