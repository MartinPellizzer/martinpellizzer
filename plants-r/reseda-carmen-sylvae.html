Reseda carmen-sylvae

Reseda carmen-sylvae, commonly known as the Carmel woodland reseda, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny environments. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Reseda carmen-sylvae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
