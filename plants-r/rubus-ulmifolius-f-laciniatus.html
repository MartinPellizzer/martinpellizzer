Rubus ulmifolius f. laciniatus

Rubus ulmifolius f. laciniatus, commonly known as the cut-leaf blackberry, is a botanical variant of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
