Regelia magnifica

Regelia magnifica, commonly known as the star cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and vibrant, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant features spiny areoles and produces offsets that can be used for propagation. Its foliage consists of modified leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures.

Regelia magnifica thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.
