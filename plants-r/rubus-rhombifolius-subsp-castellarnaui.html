Rubus rhombifolius subsp. castellarnaui

Rubus rhombifolius subsp.

castellarnaui, commonly known as the Castellana raspberry, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with the central leaflet being rhombic in shape, and the plant is often found in rocky or calcareous soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
