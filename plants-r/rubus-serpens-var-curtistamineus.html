Rubus serpens var. curtistamineus

Rubus serpens var. curtistamineus, commonly known as the redstem blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique floral and fruit characteristics, making it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
