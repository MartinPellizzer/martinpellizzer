Rubus macrophyllus subsp. bulgaricus

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

bulgaricus, commonly known as the Bulgarian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of small, dark red to black berries.

The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are prized for their flavor and nutritional value.

This subspecies is closely related to other Rubus species but is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and geographic distribution.
