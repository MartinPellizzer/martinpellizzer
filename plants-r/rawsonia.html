Rawsonia

Rawsonia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their distinctive silver-gray foliage. The leaves are typically bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments. Rawsonia species often have small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments.
