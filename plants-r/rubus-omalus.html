Rubus omalus

Rubus omalus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Himalayas. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, dark berries that are typically consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
