Rosa gallica var. incarnata

Rosa gallica var. incarnata, commonly known as the Clery's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and medicine further highlight its botanical significance.
