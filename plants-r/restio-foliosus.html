Restio foliosus

Restio foliosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf restio, is a member of the Restiaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Restio foliosus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or montane regions.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as habitat and food source for various invertebrates and birds.
