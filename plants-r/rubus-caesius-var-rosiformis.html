Rubus caesius var. rosiformis

Rubus caesius var. rosiformis, commonly known as the rose-like bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which resembles that of a rose, with five pink petals and a prominent central style. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Rubus genus for its ornamental and edible qualities.
