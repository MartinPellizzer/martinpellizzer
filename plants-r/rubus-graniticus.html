Rubus graniticus

Rubus graniticus, commonly known as the granitic blackberry, is a shrub native to rocky, arid regions.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles.
