Ranunculus ficaria var. grandiflorus

Ranunculus ficaria var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
