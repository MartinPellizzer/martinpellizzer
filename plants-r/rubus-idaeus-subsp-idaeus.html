Rubus idaeus subsp. idaeus

Rubus idaeus subsp.

idaeus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its prickly stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, aggregate fruits.

The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
