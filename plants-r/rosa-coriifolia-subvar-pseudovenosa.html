Rosa coriifolia subvar. pseudovenosa

Rosa coriifolia subvar. pseudovenosa, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
