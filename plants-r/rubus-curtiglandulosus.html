Rubus curtiglandulosus

Rubus curtiglandulosus, commonly known as the slender bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, thickets, and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in moist to dry environments across the eastern United States.
