Rubus roseus var. santarosensis

Rubus roseus var. santarosensis, commonly known as the Santa Rosa raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry plant native to California.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland regions.

Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
