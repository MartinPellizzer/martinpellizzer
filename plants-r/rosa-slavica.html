Rosa slavica

Rosa slavica, commonly known as the Slavonic rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Balkans and parts of Eastern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange-red hips. Rosa slavica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical significance in traditional medicine and cultural symbolism.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
