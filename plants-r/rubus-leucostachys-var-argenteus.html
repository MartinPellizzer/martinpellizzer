Rubus leucostachys var. argenteus

Rubus leucostachys var. argenteus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
