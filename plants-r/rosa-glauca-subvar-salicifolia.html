Rosa glauca subvar. salicifolia

Rosa glauca subvar. salicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf dogwood rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It features pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a distinctive silvery-blue underside that gives the plant its common name. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.
