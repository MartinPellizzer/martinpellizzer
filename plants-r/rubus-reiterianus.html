Rubus reiterianus

Rubus reiterianus, commonly known as the European bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, a aggregate berry, is typically red and edible, though often tart.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types and climates.
