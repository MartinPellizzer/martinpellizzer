Rubus brachythyrsus

Rubus brachythyrsus, commonly known as the short-stalked blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Korea, and Japan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
