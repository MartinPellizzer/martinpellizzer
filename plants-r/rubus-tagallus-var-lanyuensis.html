Rubus tagallus var. lanyuensis

Rubus tagallus var. lanyuensis, commonly known as the Lanyu raspberry, is a shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountainous regions. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and its flowers are pinkish-white with five petals.

The berries are usually red when ripe and are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.
