Rhus punjabensis var. punjabensis

Rhus punjabensis var. punjabensis, commonly known as the Indian sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by clusters of red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
