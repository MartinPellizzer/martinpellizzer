Ranunculus pilulifer

Ranunculus pilulifer, commonly known as the pill-bearing buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

The fruit is a follicle that contains small, bead-like seeds, giving it its common name.
