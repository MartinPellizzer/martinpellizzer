Roegneria hondae var. fascinata

Roegneria hondae var. fascinata, commonly known as the Honduran grass or fasciated roegneria, is a perennial grass species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive fasciated inflorescence, which features elongated, twisted spikelets that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
