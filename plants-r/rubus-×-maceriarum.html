Rubus × maceriarum

Rubus × maceriarum, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry or loganberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (the raspberry).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark red to black in color and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
