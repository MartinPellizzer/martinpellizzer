Rubus koehleri var. eukoehleri

Rubus koehleri var. eukoehleri, commonly known as the Eucalyptus raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red fruits. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous druplets, which are typically eaten by birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
