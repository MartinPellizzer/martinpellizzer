Rubus fruticosus var. sublustris

Rubus fruticosus var. sublustris, commonly known as the prickly rose or brambles, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typically with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits called drupes, which are usually red or black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of many ecosystems, supporting a range of wildlife.
