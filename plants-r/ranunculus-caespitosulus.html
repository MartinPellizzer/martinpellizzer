Ranunculus caespitosulus

Ranunculus caespitosulus, commonly known as the tufted buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a short, stout stem with a basal rosette of leaves that are palmately lobed and hairy. Its leaves and flowers are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Ranunculus caespitosulus prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
