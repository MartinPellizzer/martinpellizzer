Rhododendron wrightianum

Rhododendron wrightianum, commonly known as the Wright's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, primarily found in India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, cool, and shaded forest environments. The plant features dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron wrightianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
