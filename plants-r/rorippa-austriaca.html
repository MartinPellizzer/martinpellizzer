Rorippa austriaca

Rorippa austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian watercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshes. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a peppery taste, making it a potential edible plant in certain regions.
