Rosea

Rosea, commonly known as the rose plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant typically has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Rosea species are widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and are also valued for their use in perfumery and culinary applications.

Many varieties are prized for their ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.
