Ranunculus amplisinus

Ranunculus amplisinus, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals and a hairy, dark green foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of up to 1.5 meters, making it one of the tallest species in the Ranunculaceae family. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.
