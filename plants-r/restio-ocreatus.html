Restio ocreatus

Restio ocreatus, commonly known as the Cape mountain restio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, typically in summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Restio ocreatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
