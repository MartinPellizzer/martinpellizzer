Ranunculus lyngei

Ranunculus lyngei, commonly known as the Lynge's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in wet meadows and marshy areas. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals and are surrounded by a ring of sepals.

It is a relatively rare species, often considered a conservation concern due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
