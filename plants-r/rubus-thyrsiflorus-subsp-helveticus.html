Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp. helveticus

Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp.

helveticus, commonly known as the Alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically clustered together. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh mountain climates.
