Rosa × collina var. hierosolymitana

Rosa × collina var. hierosolymitana, commonly known as the Jerusalem rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Middle East and Mediterranean regions.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, often in clusters. Its leaves are dark green, ovate, and arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
