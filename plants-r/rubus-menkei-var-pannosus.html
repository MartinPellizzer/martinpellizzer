Rubus menkei var. pannosus

Rubus menkei var. pannosus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets, which are typically edible and have a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
