Rosa carolina var. grandiflora

Rosa carolina var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptic leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
