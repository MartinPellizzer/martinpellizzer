Rytidosperma pilosum

Rytidosperma pilosum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical panicle, and it produces small, two-lobed spikelets.

Rytidosperma pilosum is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
