Rubus foliosus subf. viscoglandulosus

Rubus foliosus subf.

viscoglandulosus, commonly known as the sticky glandular raspberry, is a species within the Rosaceae family characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. It features compound leaves with serrated margins and small, aggregate fruits known as drupes.

The plant is distinguished by its glandular hairs, which give it a sticky texture and aid in its identification. It typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
