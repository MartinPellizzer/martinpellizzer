Rhysospermum

Rhysospermum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flower heads that resemble a crown, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Rhysospermum species are often found in coastal and inland areas of southern Africa.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
