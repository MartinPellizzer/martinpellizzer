Rosa mollis f. proclinata

Rosa mollis f. proclinata, commonly known as the prostrate shrub rose, is a flowering plant species characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit.

It features soft, hairy stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are generally oval-shaped with a serrated edge, and the plant is known for its compact growth and ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This variety is valued for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators.
