Rhynchotheca spinosa

Rhynchotheca spinosa, commonly known as the spiny thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant is well adapted to drought conditions. Its structure includes a basal rosette of leaves and a tall, branched stem.

The plant produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh environments.
