Rapuntium rapunculoides

Rapuntium rapunculoides, commonly known as the common cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in temperate regions. It produces small, edible seeds that are used in various culinary traditions.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in diverse environments make it both a useful and sometimes invasive species.
