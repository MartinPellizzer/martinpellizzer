Rubus × durollensis

Rubus × durollensis, commonly known as the blackberry-boysenberry hybrid, is a cultivated plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (boysenberry).

It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, including thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is typically shrub-like, with compound leaves and flowers that are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is prized for its sweet, tart flavor and is often used in jams, pies, and fresh consumption.
