Ranunculus pensylvanicus var. japonicus

Ranunculus pensylvanicus var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. It has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, and its roots are fibrous.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
