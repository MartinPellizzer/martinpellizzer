Rabdosia flabelliformis

Rabdosia flabelliformis, commonly known as the fan-leaved rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, mint-like scent. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically purple or violet in color.

Rabdosia flabelliformis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
