Rosa eriosa

Rosa eriosa, commonly known as the downy rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cooler climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
