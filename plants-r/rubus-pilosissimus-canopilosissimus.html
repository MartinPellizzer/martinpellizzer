Rubus pilosissimus canopilosissimus

Rubus pilosissimus canopilosissimus, commonly known as the silky-soft raspberry, is a species of bramble characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and sprawling growth habit.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, with a tart flavor. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, white pubescence.

This species is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects.
