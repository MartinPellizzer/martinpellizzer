Rosa cinnamomea var. latifolia

Rosa cinnamomea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cinnamon rose, is a hybrid rose species native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features broad, velvety leaves with a distinct cinnamon scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique aroma and showy blooms make it a prized addition to both landscapes and floral arrangements.
