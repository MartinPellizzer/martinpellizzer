Restio cirratus

Restio cirratus, commonly known as the silky restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with wiry, thread-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in open, sunny areas.

Restio cirratus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
