Ranunculus ascendens

Ranunculus ascendens, commonly known as the ascending buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Ranunculus ascendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, cup-shaped blooms.
