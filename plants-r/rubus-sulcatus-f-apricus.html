Rubus sulcatus f. apricus

Rubus sulcatus f. apricus, commonly known as the apricot raspberry, is a variant of the brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals, and appear in clusters. The fruits, which resemble small berries, are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
