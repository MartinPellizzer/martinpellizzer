Rapuntium maculare

Rapuntium maculare, commonly known as the spotted cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine pubescence.

Rapuntium maculare is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows, where it thrives in moist, temperate conditions.
