Rubus × apertionum

Rubus × apertionum, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China and Japan.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
