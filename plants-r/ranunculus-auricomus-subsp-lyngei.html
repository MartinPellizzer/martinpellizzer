Ranunculus auricomus subsp. lyngei

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

lyngei, commonly known as the Lynge's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, which are typically borne on slender stems.

The leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure that aids in photosynthesis. This subspecies thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to ecological studies and botanical gardens.
