Ranunculus canus

Ranunculus canus, commonly known as the white crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically five-petaled flowers. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Ranunculus canus prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is an herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
