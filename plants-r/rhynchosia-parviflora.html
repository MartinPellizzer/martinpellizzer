Rhynchosia parviflora

Rhynchosia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, round pods containing edible seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop and can fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility.

Rhynchosia parviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in some traditional practices.
