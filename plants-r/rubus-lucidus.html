Rubus lucidus

Rubus lucidus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into dark purple to black berries. These berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the ecosystem, supporting various wildlife species.
