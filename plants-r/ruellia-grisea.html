Ruellia grisea

Ruellia grisea, commonly known as the gray ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming during the summer months. Ruellia grisea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
