Ranunculus pinnatus var. sonneratii

Ranunculus pinnatus var. sonneratii, commonly known as the Sonnerat's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a central greenish-brown center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

The varietal form sonneratii distinguishes it from the typical Ranunculus pinnatus through subtle differences in leaf structure and flower morphology.
