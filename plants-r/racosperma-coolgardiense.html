Racosperma coolgardiense

Racosperma coolgardiense, commonly known as the Coolgardie acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring small, waxy leaves to reduce water loss. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and provides habitat for local wildlife.

Racosperma coolgardiense is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks in its native range.
