Rosa darginica

Rosa darginica, commonly known as the Darginica rose, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Rosa darginica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
