Rhododendron medoense

Rhododendron medoense, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. The plant features dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers, usually in shades of pink or red, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a lush green canopy throughout the growing season.

Rhododendron medoense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
