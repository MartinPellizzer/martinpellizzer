Rubus × semimuelleri

Rubus × semimuelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
