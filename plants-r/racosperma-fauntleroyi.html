Racosperma fauntleroyi

Racosperma fauntleroyi, commonly known as the Western Australian wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate saline conditions, making it resilient in its native habitat.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local wildlife.
