Rhabdodendron crassipes

Rhabdodendron crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many evergreen shrubs and trees. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
