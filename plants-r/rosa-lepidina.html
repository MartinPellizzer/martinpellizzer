Rosa lepidina

Rosa lepidina, commonly known as the sand rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with wiry, thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are known for their delicate appearance.

Rosa lepidina is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.
