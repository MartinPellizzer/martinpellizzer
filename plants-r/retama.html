Retama

Retama, commonly known as honeycomb tree or white thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and small, fragrant white or yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Retama is drought-tolerant and thrives in rocky or sandy soils, making it a hardy plant adapted to arid environments.

Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.
