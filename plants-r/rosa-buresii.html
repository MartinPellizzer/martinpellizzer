Rosa buresii

Rosa buresii, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and serrated, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Rosa buresii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
