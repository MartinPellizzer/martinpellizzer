Rubus genevieri var. sertiflorus

Rubus genevieri var. sertiflorus, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, edible berries in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
