Rubus × subamoenus

Rubus × subamoenus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, wooded areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is valued for its tart flavor and is often used in jams and culinary applications.
