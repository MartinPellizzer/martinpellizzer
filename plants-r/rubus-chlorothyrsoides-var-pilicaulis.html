Rubus chlorothyrsoides var. pilicaulis

Rubus chlorothyrsoides var. pilicaulis, commonly known as the hairy raspberry.

This variant is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its dense, thorny stems and hairy foliage. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
