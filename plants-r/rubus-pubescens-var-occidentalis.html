Rubus pubescens var. occidentalis

Rubus pubescens var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western bramble, is a shrub native to western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
