Rorippa benghalensis

Rorippa benghalensis, commonly known as the Indian watercress, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It has small, rounded leaves and produces tiny white or yellow flowers.

Rorippa benghalensis is often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some Asian cuisines.
