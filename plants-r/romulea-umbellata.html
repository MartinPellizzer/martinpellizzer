Romulea umbellata

Romulea umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features grass-like basal leaves and produces showy, fragrant blooms during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Romulea umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
