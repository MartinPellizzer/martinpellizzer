Rosa eglanteria var. bernardii

Rosa eglanteria var. bernardii, commonly known as the Bernard rose, is a thorny shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, arching stems covered in sharp spines and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets that have serrated edges. This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.
