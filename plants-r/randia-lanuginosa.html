Randia lanuginosa

Randia lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, white pubescence. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale pink and are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
