Randia clusiifolia

Randia clusiifolia, commonly known as the purpleleaf randia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a small shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring opposite, elliptic leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by orange to red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy fruits.

Randia clusiifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
