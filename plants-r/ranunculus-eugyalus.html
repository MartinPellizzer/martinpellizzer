Ranunculus eugyalus

Ranunculus eugyalus, commonly known as the yellow pond-lily, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its floating leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus eugyalus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for aquatic insects and birds.
