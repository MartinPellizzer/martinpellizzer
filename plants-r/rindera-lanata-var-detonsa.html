Rindera lanata var. detonsa

Rindera lanata var. detonsa, commonly known as the woolly rindera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white to gray hairs on its stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance and aid in reducing water loss. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly succulent texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
