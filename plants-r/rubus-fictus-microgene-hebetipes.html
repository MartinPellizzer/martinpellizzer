Rubus fictus microgene hebetipes

Rubus fictus microgene hebetipes, commonly known as the slender bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens. This plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of woodlands and along stream banks.

Its fruit, a small, aggregate berry, is edible but generally less palatable than other Rubus species.
