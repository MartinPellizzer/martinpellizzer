Rubus vulgaris var. sprengelii

Rubus vulgaris var. sprengelii, commonly known as the Sprengel's blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
