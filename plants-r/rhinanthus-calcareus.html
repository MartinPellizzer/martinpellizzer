Rhinanthus calcareus

Rhinanthus calcareus, commonly known as the limestone devil's bit, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heaths. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom from June to August, attracting various pollinators. It has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Rhinanthus calcareus is an important species for biodiversity, supporting a range of insects and contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
