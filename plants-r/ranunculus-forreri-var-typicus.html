Ranunculus forreri var. typicus

Ranunculus forreri var. typicus, commonly known as the Forrer's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its short stature and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
