Romulea linaresii subsp. linaresii

Romulea linaresii subsp.

linaresii, commonly known as the Linares' romulea, is a rare and striking bulbous perennial native to South Africa. It features elegant, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern.

The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, making it a prized addition to rock gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
