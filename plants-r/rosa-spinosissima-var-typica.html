Rosa spinosissima var. typica

Rosa spinosissima var. typica, commonly known as the Spanish rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for gardens in Mediterranean climates.
