Ranunculus prominentilobus

Ranunculus prominentilobus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals that are often adorned with a distinct central blotch. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with lobes that are prominently notched or toothed.

Ranunculus prominentilobus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
