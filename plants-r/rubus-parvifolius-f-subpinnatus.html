Rubus parvifolius f. subpinnatus

Rubus parvifolius f. subpinnatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, subpinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
