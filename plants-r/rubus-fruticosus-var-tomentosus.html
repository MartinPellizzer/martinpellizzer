Rubus fruticosus var. tomentosus

Rubus fruticosus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which develop into edible berries by late summer. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
