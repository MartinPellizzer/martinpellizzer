Rosa heteroclita

Rosa heteroclita, commonly known as the variable rose, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins. Its flowers are typically borne singly or in small clusters at the ends of the branches.

Rosa heteroclita is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
