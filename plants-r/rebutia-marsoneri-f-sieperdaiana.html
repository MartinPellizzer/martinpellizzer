Rebutia marsoneri f. sieperdaiana

Rebutia marsoneri f. sieperdaiana, commonly known as the Sieperdaian Rebutia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features rounded, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color, adorned with small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, with shallow roots that allow it to absorb moisture efficiently. Its foliage is succulent, storing water to help it survive in harsh environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
