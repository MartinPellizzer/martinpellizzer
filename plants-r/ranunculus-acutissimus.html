Ranunculus acutissimus

Ranunculus acutissimus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine environments. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy flowering plants.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
