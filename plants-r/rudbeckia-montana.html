Rudbeckia montana

Rudbeckia montana, commonly known as the mountain rudbeckia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and northern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow-orange flower heads with dark centers, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Rudbeckia montana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
