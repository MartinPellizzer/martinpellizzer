Rubus × leynhacensis

Rubus × leynhacensis, commonly known as the Leynham blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it typically grows as an upright shrub.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
