Rhynchosia pubescens

Rhynchosia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bean or velvet bean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. Rhynchosia pubescens is part of the Fabaceae family and is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, which enrich the soil.

It is also cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and as a source of protein.
