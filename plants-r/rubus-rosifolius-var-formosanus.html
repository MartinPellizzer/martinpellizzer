Rubus rosifolius var. formosanus

Rubus rosifolius var. formosanus, commonly known as the Formosan bramble, is a shrub native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are aggregate berries that ripen to a dark red color in late summer.

This species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
