Rhynchospora hookeri

Rhynchospora hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Rhynchospora hookeri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
