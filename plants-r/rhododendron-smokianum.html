Rhododendron smokianum

Rhododendron smokianum, commonly known as the Smoky Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to forested landscapes.

Rhododendron smokianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
