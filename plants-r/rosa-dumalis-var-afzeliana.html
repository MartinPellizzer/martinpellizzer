Rosa dumalis var. afzeliana

Rosa dumalis var. afzeliana, commonly known as the Afzelian rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red or orange fruits known as hips.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its beauty and hardiness.
