Raphanus sativus var. crininus

Raphanus sativus var. crininus, commonly known as the curly top radish, is a variant of the radish plant characterized by its unique, curled leaves.

This plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. The leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly bitter taste, making them suitable for culinary use. It is often grown as a leafy green vegetable in temperate climates.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to thrive optimally.
