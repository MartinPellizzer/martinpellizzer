Ruellia paniculata

Ruellia paniculata, commonly known as the purple panicled ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with slightly hairy surfaces, and its stems are often hairy as well. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Ruellia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
