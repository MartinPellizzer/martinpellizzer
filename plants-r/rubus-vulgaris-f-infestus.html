Rubus vulgaris f. infestus

Rubus vulgaris f. infestus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a wild, thorny plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and aggregate fruits, which develop from the fusion of multiple small drupes. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and hedgerows, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are pink to white, and it produces edible berries that are popular in culinary uses.

However, its thorny stems and aggressive growth make it a challenging weed to manage in cultivated areas.
