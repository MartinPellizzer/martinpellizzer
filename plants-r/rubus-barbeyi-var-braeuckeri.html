Rubus barbeyi var. braeuckeri

Rubus barbeyi var. braeuckeri, commonly known as the Braeucker's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
