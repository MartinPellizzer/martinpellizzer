Rhodiola rosea var. subalpina

Rhodiola rosea var. subalpina, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, reddish roots that store nutrients. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for use in traditional herbal remedies.
