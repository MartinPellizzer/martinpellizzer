Rhus kearneyi subsp. virginum

Rhus kearneyi subsp.

virginum, commonly known as the Keane's sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
