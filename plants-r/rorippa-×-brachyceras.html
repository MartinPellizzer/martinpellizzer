Rorippa × brachyceras

Rorippa × brachyceras, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots are adapted for aquatic life, allowing it to thrive in shallow, freshwater habitats.

Rorippa × brachyceras is often found in ponds, ditches, and along the edges of streams.
