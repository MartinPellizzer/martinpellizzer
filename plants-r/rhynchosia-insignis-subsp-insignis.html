Rhynchosia insignis subsp. insignis

Rhynchosia insignis subsp.

insignis, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa. It features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its striking blue to violet flowers are clustered in racemes.

The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and green spaces.
