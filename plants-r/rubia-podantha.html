Rubia podantha

Rubia podantha, commonly known as the African balm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Rubia podantha is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
