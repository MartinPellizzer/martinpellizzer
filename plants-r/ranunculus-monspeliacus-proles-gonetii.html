Ranunculus monspeliacus proles gonetii

Ranunculus monspeliacus proles gonetii, commonly known as the Gonet's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks. It has palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments and produces small, greenish-brown fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected in natural habitats.
