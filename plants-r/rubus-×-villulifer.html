Rubus × villulifer

Rubus × villulifer, commonly known as the blackberry-bush hybrid, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

This plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges. The fruit, which resembles a blackberry, is edible and contains small, hard seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in local ecosystems.
