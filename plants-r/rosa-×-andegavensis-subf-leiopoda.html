Rosa × andegavensis subf. leiopoda

Rosa × andegavensis subf.

leiopoda, commonly known as the Andegavensis rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a prominent central pistil, blooming in late spring to early summer. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
