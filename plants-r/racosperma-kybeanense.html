Racosperma kybeanense

Racosperma kybeanense, commonly known as the Kybean wattle, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Racosperma kybeanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
