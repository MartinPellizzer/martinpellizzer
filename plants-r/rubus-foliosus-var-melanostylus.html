Rubus foliosus var. melanostylus

Rubus foliosus var. melanostylus, commonly known as the black-styled bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery leaves and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens. The fruit, a raspberry-like aggregate, is edible and has a tart flavor.

This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
