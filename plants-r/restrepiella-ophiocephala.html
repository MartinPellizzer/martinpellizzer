Restrepiella ophiocephala

Restrepiella ophiocephala, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green appearance. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for hanging baskets or trellises.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
