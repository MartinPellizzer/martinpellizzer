Rhus integrifolia var. integrifolia

Rhus integrifolia var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically composed of 11 to 31 serrated leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are a common food source for birds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
