Riedelia corallina

Riedelia corallina, commonly known as the coral cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique, branched structure with small, spineless stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. Riedelia corallina is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
