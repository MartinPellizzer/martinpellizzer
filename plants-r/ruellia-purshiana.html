Ruellia purshiana

Ruellia purshiana, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Ruellia purshiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
