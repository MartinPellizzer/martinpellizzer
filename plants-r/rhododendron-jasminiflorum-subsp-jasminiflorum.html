Rhododendron jasminiflorum subsp. jasminiflorum

Rhododendron jasminiflorum subsp.

jasminiflorum, commonly known as the jasmine rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a dense, compact growth habit.

It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.
