Ribes pensylvanicum

Ribes pensylvanicum, commonly known as redcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, bright red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream valleys.

The berries are not only edible but also serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
