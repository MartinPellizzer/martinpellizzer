Rhamnidium retusum

Rhamnidium retusum, commonly known as the smoothleaf rhamnus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glabrous or nearly so. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
