Rubus menkei var. eriophorus

Rubus menkei var. eriophorus, commonly known as the Klamath blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically firm and tart. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Rubus species. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are popular in local agriculture.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both commercial and home use.
