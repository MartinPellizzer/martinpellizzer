Rosa libanotica

Rosa libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves with serrated edges and a woody, thorny stem structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.

Rosa libanotica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
