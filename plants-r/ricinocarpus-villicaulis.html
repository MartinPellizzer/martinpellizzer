Ricinocarpus villicaulis

Ricinocarpus villicaulis, commonly known as the silky mangrove, is a tropical plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a mangrove species that thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often forming dense, tangled root systems. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically clustered in catkins. It produces woody, cylindrical fruits that contain a single seed.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for marine and terrestrial organisms.
