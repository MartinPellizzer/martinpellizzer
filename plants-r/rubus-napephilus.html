Rubus napephilus

Rubus napephilus, commonly known as the velvet-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
