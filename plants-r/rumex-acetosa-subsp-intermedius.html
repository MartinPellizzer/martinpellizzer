Rumex acetosa subsp. intermedius

Rumex acetosa subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as intermediate sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family. It is characterized by its upright, smooth-stemmed growth and heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or purple-tinged beneath.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
