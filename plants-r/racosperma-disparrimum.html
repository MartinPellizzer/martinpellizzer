Racosperma disparrimum

Racosperma disparrimum, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Racosperma disparrimum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
