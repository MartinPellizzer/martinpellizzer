Rubus rhamnifolius microgene nemoralis

Rubus rhamnifolius microgene nemoralis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and open habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
