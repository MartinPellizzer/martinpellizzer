Rubus hispidus var. blanchardianus

Rubus hispidus var. blanchardianus, commonly known as the Blanchard's bramble, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces edible berries that are small and tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
