Rosa castellii

Rosa castellii, commonly known as the Castile rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and an aggregate fruit known as a hip.

Rosa castellii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
