Ranunculus gracilicaulis

Ranunculus gracilicaulis, commonly known as the slenderstem buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
