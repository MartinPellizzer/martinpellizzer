Ranunculus striatus

Ranunculus striatus, commonly known as the striped buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a distinctive white or pale green stripe running down the central vein. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a darker central area. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
