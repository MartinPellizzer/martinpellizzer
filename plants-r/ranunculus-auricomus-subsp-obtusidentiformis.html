Ranunculus auricomus subsp. obtusidentiformis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

obtusidentiformis, commonly known as the broad-lobed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens.

The leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, often hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
