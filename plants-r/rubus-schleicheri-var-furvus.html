Rubus schleicheri var. furvus

Rubus schleicheri var. furvus, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
