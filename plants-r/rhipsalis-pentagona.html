Rhipsalis pentagona

Rhipsalis pentagona, commonly known as the five-angled mistletoe, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are often covered in short, white hair. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Rhipsalis pentagona is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ease of care.
