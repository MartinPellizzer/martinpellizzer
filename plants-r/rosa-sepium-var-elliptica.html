Rosa sepium var. elliptica

Rosa sepium var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical rose, is a cultivar of the rose plant characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves and vibrant, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions with temperate climates. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green foliage. Its flowers, which can be pink, red, or white, bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
