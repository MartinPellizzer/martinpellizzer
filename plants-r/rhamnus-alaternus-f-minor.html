Rhamnus alaternus f. minor

Rhamnus alaternus f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf alaternus buckthorn, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by black, egg-shaped berries in the fall. It is typically found in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
