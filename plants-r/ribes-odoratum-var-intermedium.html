Ribes odoratum var. intermedium

Ribes odoratum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It produces small, yellow to orange berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with five to seven oval leaflets and red stems that become woody with age. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
