Rhododendron solitarium

Rhododendron solitarium, commonly known as the solitary rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and are adapted to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
