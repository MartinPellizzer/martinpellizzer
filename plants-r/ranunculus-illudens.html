Ranunculus illudens

Ranunculus illudens, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a dark center. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ranunculus illudens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
