Ryssopterys ovata

Ryssopterys ovata, commonly known as the ovate fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically ovate in shape, hence its common name. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori, a typical feature of ferns. Ryssopterys ovata thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for humid, forested habitats.
