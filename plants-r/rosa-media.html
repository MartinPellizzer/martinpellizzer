Rosa media

Rosa media, commonly known as the English shrub rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in thorns. Rosa media is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
