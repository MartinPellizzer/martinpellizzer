Rosa rotundifolia

Rosa rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its rounded, leathery leaves and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and is known for its hardy nature. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa rotundifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience in various climates.
