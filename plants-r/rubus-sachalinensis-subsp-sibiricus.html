Rubus sachalinensis subsp. sibiricus

Rubus sachalinensis subsp.

sibiricus, commonly known as the Siberian blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, including parts of Russia, China, and Korea. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and wines.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
