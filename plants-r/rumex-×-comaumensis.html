Rumex × comaumensis

Rumex × comaumensis, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and rhubarb. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the veins, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its geographic location.
