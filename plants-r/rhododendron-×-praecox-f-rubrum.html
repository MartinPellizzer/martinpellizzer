Rhododendron × praecox f. rubrum

Rhododendron × praecox f. rubrum, commonly known as the scarlet flame azalea, is a hybrid evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, tubular flowers.

It produces clusters of vibrant red blooms that are typically 3 to 5 centimeters in length, appearing in early spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, where it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its striking floral display.

The species is closely related to other azalea varieties, sharing similar growth habits and botanical features.
