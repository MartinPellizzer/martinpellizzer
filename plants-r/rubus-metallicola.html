Rubus metallicola

Rubus metallicola, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The silverleaf blackberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
