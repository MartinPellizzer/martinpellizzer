Rosa rubiginosa var. isacantha

Rosa rubiginosa var. isacantha, commonly known as the Sicilian rose or the rough rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces oval, reddish-brown fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
