Ranunculus chilensis var. gayi

Ranunculus chilensis var. gayi, commonly known as the Chilean buttercup, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with 5 to 15 petals. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmate, with three to five lobes, and the stems are hairy and erect.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its vibrant, delicate blossoms.
