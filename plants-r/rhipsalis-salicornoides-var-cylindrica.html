Rhipsalis salicornoides var. cylindrica

Rhipsalis salicornoides var. cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often forming dense, hanging clusters. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. Its stems are covered in small, hair-like structures called areoles, which are typical of cacti.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
