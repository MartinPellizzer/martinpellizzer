Rothmannia macrosiphon

Rothmannia macrosiphon, commonly known as the giant leaved rothmannia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Rothmannia macrosiphon thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
