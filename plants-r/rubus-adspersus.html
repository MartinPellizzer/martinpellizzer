Rubus adspersus

Rubus adspersus, commonly known as the Canadian thimbleberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, stinging hairs.

The flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the characteristic thimble-shaped fruit.
