Rhodax canus subsp. piloselloides

Rhodax canus subsp.

piloselloides, commonly known as the woolly rhodax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
