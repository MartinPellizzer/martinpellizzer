Rosa carolinensis

Rosa carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Its stems are covered in thorny buds, typical of the rose family.

Rosa carolinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in American horticulture.
