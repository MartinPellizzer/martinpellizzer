Rosa acicularis var. taquetii

Rosa acicularis var. taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's thorned rose, is a rare and distinctive species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces narrow, leathery leaves and has a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its alpine environment. It is often found in rocky soils and is considered a valuable species for botanical research due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.

This variety is also prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
