Rhipidoglossum pendulum

Rhipidoglossum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging tongue fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its pendulous, strap-like fronds that hang from its rhizomes, giving it an unusual appearance. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are typically found in the shaded, moist environments of tropical regions. It thrives in humid climates and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Due to its distinctive morphology and specific habitat requirements, it is a rare and intriguing subject for botanical study.
