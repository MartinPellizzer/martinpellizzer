Rosa afzeliana f. subcinerea

Rosa afzeliana f. subcinerea, commonly known as the gray rose or silver rose, is a compact shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
