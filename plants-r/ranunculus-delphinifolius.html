Ranunculus delphinifolius

Ranunculus delphinifolius, commonly known as the delphinium-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive central pistil. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other buttercup species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
