Rosa dumalis var. fraxinoides

Rosa dumalis var. fraxinoides, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a variant of the Rosa dumalis species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with thorny stems and simple, alternate leaves.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
