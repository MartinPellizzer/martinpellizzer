Rosa tanaitica

Rosa tanaitica, commonly known as the Tanaitic rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include hirsute (hairy) foliage and a preference for well-drained, calcareous soils.

Rosa tanaitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
