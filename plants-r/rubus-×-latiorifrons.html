Rubus × latiorifrons

Rubus × latiorifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are composed of small, drupe-like berries clustered together. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or hedgerow habitats.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and edible fruits.
