Rhododendron × edgarianum

Rhododendron × edgarianum, commonly known as the Edgarian rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron caucasicum and Rhododendron ferrugineum.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural use in temperate climates.
