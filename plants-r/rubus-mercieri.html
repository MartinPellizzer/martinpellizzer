Rubus mercieri

Rubus mercieri, commonly known as the Sierra raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
