Ruellia hirsuta

Ruellia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its hairy leaves and stems are characteristic features that help distinguish it from other similar species.

Ruellia hirsuta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.
