Rubus callosus var. pallidus

Rubus callosus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a thorny shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. The plant bears aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
