Rubus aralioides

Rubus aralioides, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
