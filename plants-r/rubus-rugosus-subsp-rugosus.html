Rubus rugosus subsp. rugosus

Rubus rugosus subsp.

rugosus, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria.

The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces edible berries that are popular in culinary and commercial applications.

The subspecies rugosus is distinguished by its rough, rugged appearance, which gives it its scientific name.
