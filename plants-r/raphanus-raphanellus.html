Raphanus raphanellus

Raphanus raphanellus, commonly known as the Chinese watercress, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found near streams and in wetlands. Its seeds are small and oval, with a hard outer coat that aids in dispersal.

The plant is valued for its peppery flavor and is commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.
