Rondeletia cupreiflora

Rondeletia cupreiflora, commonly known as copperleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking copper-colored bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
