Rubus × hemigoniophylloides

Rubus × hemigoniophylloides, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry-loganberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry). The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are larger and sweeter than those of its parent species.

This plant is valued for its fruit quality and is commonly cultivated in temperate regions.
