Rhynchospora polyantha

Rhynchospora polyantha, commonly known as the showy spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear dense clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, thriving in temperate climates. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
