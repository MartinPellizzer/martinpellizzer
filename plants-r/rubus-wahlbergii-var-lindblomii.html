Rubus wahlbergii var. lindblomii

Rubus wahlbergii var. lindblomii, commonly known as the Lindblom's blackberry, is a variant of the widespread Rubus wahlbergii species.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
