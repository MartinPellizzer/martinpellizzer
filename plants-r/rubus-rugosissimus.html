Rubus rugosissimus

Rubus rugosissimus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
