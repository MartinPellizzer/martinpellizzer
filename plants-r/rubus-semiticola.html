Rubus semiticola

Rubus semiticola, commonly known as the Semitic blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries.

Rubus semiticola is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
