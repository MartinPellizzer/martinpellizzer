Rosa polonica

Rosa polonica, commonly known as the Polish rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Eastern Europe and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

Rosa polonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
