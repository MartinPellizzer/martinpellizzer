Rumex natalensis

Rumex natalensis, commonly known as Natal sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features distinctive arrow-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple underside, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Natal sorrel is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes.
