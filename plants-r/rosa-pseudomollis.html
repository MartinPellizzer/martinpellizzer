Rosa pseudomollis

Rosa pseudomollis, commonly known as the slender pink rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features compound leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red in color. Rosa pseudomollis produces hips, which are the fruit of the plant, containing seeds inside.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
