Rubia atropurpurea

Rubia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple borage, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
