Rubus idaeus var. nesophilus

Rubus idaeus var. nesophilus, commonly known as the European raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its flavorful berries, which are used in culinary applications and jams.
