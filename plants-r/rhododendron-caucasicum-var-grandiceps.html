Rhododendron caucasicum var. grandiceps

Rhododendron caucasicum var. grandiceps, commonly known as the Caucasian bigleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
