Rubus polycardius

Rubus polycardius, commonly known as the many-seed blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
