Rubus × sinuum

Rubus × sinuum, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (the red raspberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruit is a aggregate of drupes, commonly referred to as a "berry.".

This hybrid is valued for its hardy nature and the flavorful, edible fruits it produces.
