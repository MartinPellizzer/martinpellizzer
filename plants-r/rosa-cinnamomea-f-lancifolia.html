Rosa cinnamomea f. lancifolia

Rosa cinnamomea f. lancifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon rose or Chinese cinnamon rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa cinnamomea species, native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, deep pink to crimson flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, thornless stems and lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a glossy surface. Its foliage turns a striking red or orange in the autumn, adding ornamental value beyond the flowering season.

This cultivar is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in well-drained soil.
