Racosperma strictum

Racosperma strictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf acacia, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellow flowers in compact spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Racosperma strictum also has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules, contributing to soil fertility.
