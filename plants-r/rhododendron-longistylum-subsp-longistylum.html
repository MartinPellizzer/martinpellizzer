Rhododendron longistylum subsp. longistylum

Rhododendron longistylum subsp.

longistylum, commonly known as the long-stemmed rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
