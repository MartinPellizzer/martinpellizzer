Rosa hirtidinota

Rosa hirtidinota, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and China. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, dark green leaves with a hairy texture, and fragrant pink to red flowers. Rosa hirtidinota typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in some horticultural settings.
