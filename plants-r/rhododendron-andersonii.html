Rhododendron andersonii

Rhododendron andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and inflorescences that are often pink or red in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological niches.
