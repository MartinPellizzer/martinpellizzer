Rosa canina var. venosa

Rosa canina var. venosa, commonly known as the bristly dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rugged appearance. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a fragrant scent. This plant is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, producing rose hips that are used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
