Ranunculus gracilidens

Ranunculus gracilidens, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

Ranunculus gracilidens is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring.
