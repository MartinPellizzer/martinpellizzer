Rhododendron argyrophyllum var. nankingense

Rhododendron argyrophyllum var. nankingense, commonly known as the Nanking silverleaf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white undersides on its broad, ovate leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to red flowers in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its unique foliage and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
