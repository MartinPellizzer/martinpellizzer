Rhynchospora elongata

Rhynchospora elongata, commonly known as the slender beakspur sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rhynchospora elongata thrives in moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
