Rubus nitidus var. meionacanthus

Rubus nitidus var. meionacanthus, commonly known as the small-armed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its slender, sparsely armed stems and small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, often in woodland or hedgerow habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
