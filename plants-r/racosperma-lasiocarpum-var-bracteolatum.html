Racosperma lasiocarpum var. bracteolatum

Racosperma lasiocarpum var. bracteolatum, commonly known as the bracteolate acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has small, leathery leaves and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes.

The varietal form, bracteolatum, is distinguished by its presence of bracteoles, small leaf-like structures at the base of the flower clusters.
