Rubus moluccanus var. treutleri

Rubus moluccanus var. treutleri, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
