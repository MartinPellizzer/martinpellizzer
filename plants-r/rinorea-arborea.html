Rinorea arborea

Rinorea arborea, commonly known as the redwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Rinorea arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
