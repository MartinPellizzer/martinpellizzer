Reseda caudata

Reseda caudata, commonly known as slender reseda, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring. Its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and the plant is known for its drought tolerance.

Reseda caudata is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
