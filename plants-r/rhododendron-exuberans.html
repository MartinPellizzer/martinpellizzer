Rhododendron exuberans

Rhododendron exuberans, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant pink to red flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
