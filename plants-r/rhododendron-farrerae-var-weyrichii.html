Rhododendron farrerae var. weyrichii

Rhododendron farrerae var. weyrichii, commonly known as the Weyrich's rhododendron, is a rare and unique variant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and often showy flowers that range in color from deep pink to crimson. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in dense forests at high altitudes. Its foliage consists of thick, leathery leaves that are dark green and provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
