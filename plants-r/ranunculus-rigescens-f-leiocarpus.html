Ranunculus rigescens f. leiocarpus

Ranunculus rigescens f. leiocarpus, commonly known as the white-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a white center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.

The subspecies f. leiocarpus is distinguished by its pale yellow petals and reduced, less prominent sepals compared to the typical form.
