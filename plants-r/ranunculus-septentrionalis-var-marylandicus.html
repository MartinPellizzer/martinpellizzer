Ranunculus septentrionalis var. marylandicus

Ranunculus septentrionalis var. marylandicus, commonly known as the Maryland buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a soft, fuzzy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
