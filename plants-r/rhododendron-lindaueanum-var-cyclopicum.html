Rhododendron lindaueanum var. cyclopicum

Rhododendron lindaueanum var. cyclopicum, commonly known as the Cyclopic Rhododendron, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It is characterized by its unique, nearly spherical inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are typically deep pink to purple, with five petals that are broad and slightly recurved.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its rarity and striking appearance.
