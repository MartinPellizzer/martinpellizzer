Rubus ulmifolius var. dispalatus

Rubus ulmifolius var. dispalatus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark purple to black berries.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
