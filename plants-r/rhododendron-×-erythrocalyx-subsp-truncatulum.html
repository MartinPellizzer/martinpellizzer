Rhododendron × erythrocalyx subsp. truncatulum

Rhododendron × erythrocalyx subsp.

truncatulum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and landscapes.
