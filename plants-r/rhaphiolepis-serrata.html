Rhaphiolepis serrata

Rhaphiolepis serrata, commonly known as the Japanese boxwood, is a shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and ability to be shaped into various forms make it a versatile plant for landscaping.
