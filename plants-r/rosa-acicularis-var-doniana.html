Rosa acicularis var. doniana

Rosa acicularis var. doniana, commonly known as the Doniana thornapple, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
