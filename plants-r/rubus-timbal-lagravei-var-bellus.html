Rubus timbal-lagravei var. bellus

Rubus timbal-lagravei var. bellus, commonly known as the Timbal-Lagrave's rose.

This variant is a shrub native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, characterized by its delicate pink flowers and thorny stems. The leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the plant produces small, red berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical interest of this plant lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution.
