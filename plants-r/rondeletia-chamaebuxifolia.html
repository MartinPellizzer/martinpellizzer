Rondeletia chamaebuxifolia

Rondeletia chamaebuxifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian flame violet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
