Rubus macrophyllus subsp. schlechtendalii

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

schlechtendalii, commonly known as the large-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is typically found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
