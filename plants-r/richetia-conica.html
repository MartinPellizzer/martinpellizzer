Richetia conica

Richetia conica, commonly known as the coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces a single, conical flower that is typically purple or pink in color. Richetia conica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its structure and blooming habits make it an attractive and low-maintenance addition to diverse garden settings.
