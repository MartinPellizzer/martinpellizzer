Rhododendron neriiflorum var. phaedropum

Rhododendron neriiflorum var. phaedropum, commonly known as the Phaeodendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in cool, moist climates and prefers well-drained acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
