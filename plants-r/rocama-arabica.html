Rocama arabica

Rocama arabica, commonly known as coffee.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family, native to tropical Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, called a cherry, contains two seeds, which are the coffee beans used in brewing.
