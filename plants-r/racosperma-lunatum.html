Racosperma lunatum

Racosperma lunatum, commonly known as the moon flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

The moon flower is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
