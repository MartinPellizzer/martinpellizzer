Rosa villosa var. myriacantha

Rosa villosa var. myriacantha, commonly known as the hairy rose or myriacantha rose, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and can grow up to 2 meters in height. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to horticultural collections.
