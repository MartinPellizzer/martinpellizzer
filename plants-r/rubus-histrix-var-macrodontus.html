Rubus histrix var. macrodontus

Rubus histrix var. macrodontus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry.

This variant is a shrub native to parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The botanical features of this plant make it distinct from other Rubus species, particularly in its leaf structure and fruit morphology.
