Rhynchosinapis pseudoerucastrum subsp. cintrana

Rhynchosinapis pseudoerucastrum subsp.

cintrana, commonly known as the Cintrana thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, branched inflorescence that bears yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a distinctive crown of leaves that form a basal rosette.
