Ruprechtia triflora var. guaranitica

Ruprechtia triflora var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens and private collections.
