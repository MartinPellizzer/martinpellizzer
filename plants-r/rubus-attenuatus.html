Rubus attenuatus

Rubus attenuatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in some local cuisines.
