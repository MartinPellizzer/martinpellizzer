Ranunculus auricomus subsp. metrius

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

metrius, commonly known as the golden shield or yellow crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features basal leaves that are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, with a striking yellow flower that has five petal-like sepals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest floors. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, greenish-brown fruits that are dispersed by wind.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, delicate appearance.
