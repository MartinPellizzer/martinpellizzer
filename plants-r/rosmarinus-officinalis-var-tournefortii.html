Rosmarinus officinalis var. tournefortii

Rosmarinus officinalis var. tournefortii, commonly known as Spanish rosemary, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color and emit a strong, pine-like scent. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils, rich in cineole and camphor, are widely used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.
