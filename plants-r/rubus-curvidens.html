Rubus curvidens

Rubus curvidens, commonly known as the curved-leaf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark red berries. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are similar to other blackberry species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its berries.
