Rubus macgregorii

Rubus macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest margins and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
