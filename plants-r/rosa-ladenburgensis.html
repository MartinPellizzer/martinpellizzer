Rosa ladenburgensis

Rosa ladenburgensis, commonly known as the Ladenburg rose, is a species of shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa ladenburgensis is well adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in botanical gardens.
