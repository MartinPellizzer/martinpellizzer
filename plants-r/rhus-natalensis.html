Rhus natalensis

Rhus natalensis, commonly known as the Natal buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and has compound leaves with 5–11 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue, spherical berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in coastal and inland forests.

The bark is rough and fissured, and the plant is known for its ability to tolerate various soil types.
