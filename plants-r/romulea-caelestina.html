Romulea caelestina

Romulea caelestina, commonly known as the sky blue romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is closely related to the genus Gladiolus. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and colorful display.
