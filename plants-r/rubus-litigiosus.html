Rubus litigiosus

Rubus litigiosus, commonly known as the prickly rose or bramble rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces red or black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Native to North America, it thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
