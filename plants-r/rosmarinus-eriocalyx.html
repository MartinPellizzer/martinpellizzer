Rosmarinus eriocalyx

Rosmarinus eriocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rosemary.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and small blue or purple flowers. The plant has needle-like foliage that is gray-green in color and emits a strong, pine-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Rosmarinus eriocalyx is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
