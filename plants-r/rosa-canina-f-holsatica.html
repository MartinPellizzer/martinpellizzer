Rosa canina f. holsatica

Rosa canina f. holsatica, commonly known as the Holsatica form of dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers and thorny stems. This form is native to central and eastern Europe, where it thrives in woodland and hedgerow environments. The leaves are typically compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant produces red, hairy fruits known as hips.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
