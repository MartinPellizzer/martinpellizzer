Ranunculus muricatus

Ranunculus muricatus, commonly known as the mouse-ear cuckoo-spur, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Ranunculus muricatus is an herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
