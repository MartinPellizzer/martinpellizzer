Ruellia parva

Ruellia parva, commonly known as the small ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Ruellia parva thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its foliage is broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
