Ranunculus sardous subsp. laevis

Ranunculus sardous subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the smooth meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features delicate, palmate leaves with a smooth texture and bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It is characterized by its short, stout stems and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
