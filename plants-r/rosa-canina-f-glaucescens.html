Rosa canina f. glaucescens

Rosa canina f. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale greenish-blue foliage and slightly hairy stems. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a fragrant scent. This variety is often found in woodland areas and along hedgerows in temperate regions.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedgerow management.
