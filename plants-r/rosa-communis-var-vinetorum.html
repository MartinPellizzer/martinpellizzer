Rosa communis var. vinetorum

Rosa communis var. vinetorum, commonly known as the wild rose or dog rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks.

This versatile plant has been used historically in herbal medicine and as a source of perfume and culinary ingredients.
