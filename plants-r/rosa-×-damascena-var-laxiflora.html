Rosa × damascena var. laxiflora

Rosa × damascena var. laxiflora, commonly known as the laxiflora rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. The plant produces small, red, oval fruits called hips, which are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing an attractive backdrop to its showy blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens and for essential oil extraction.
