Rubus guentheri var. hirtissimus

Rubus guentheri var. hirtissimus, commonly known as the hirsute blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species within its native range.
