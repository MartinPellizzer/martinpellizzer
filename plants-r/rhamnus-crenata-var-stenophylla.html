Rhamnus crenata var. stenophylla

Rhamnus crenata var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
