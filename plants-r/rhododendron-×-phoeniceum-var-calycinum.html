Rhododendron × phoeniceum var. calycinum

Rhododendron × phoeniceum var. calycinum, commonly known as the California rhododendron, is a hybrid species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in a shrub form, reaching heights of up to 6 feet, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It prefers partial to full shade and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and horticultural gardens.
