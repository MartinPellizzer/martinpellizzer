Rubus plicatus var. bertramii

Rubus plicatus var. bertramii, commonly known as the Bertram's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular among foragers and local cuisine enthusiasts.
