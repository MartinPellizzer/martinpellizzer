Ruellia stemonacanthoides

Ruellia stemonacanthoides, commonly known as the roughleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture.

Ruellia stemonacanthoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
