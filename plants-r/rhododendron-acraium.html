Rhododendron acraium

Rhododendron acraium, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are often dark green and leathery in texture. Its flowers are showy, ranging from pink to red, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron acraium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
