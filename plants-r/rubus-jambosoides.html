Rubus jambosoides

Rubus jambosoides, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, which are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white and five-petaled.

Rubus jambosoides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
