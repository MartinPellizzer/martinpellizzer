Rubus × huelgoatensis

Rubus × huelgoatensis, commonly known as the Huelgoat raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to western Europe, particularly France, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruits are small, red, and aggregate, resembling raspberries in appearance.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
