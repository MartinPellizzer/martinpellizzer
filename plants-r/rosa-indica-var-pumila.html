Rosa indica var. pumila

Rosa indica var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Indian rose, is a compact shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with a fragrant aroma. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces rose hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. It is well-suited for container gardening due to its compact size and ornamental value.

Rosa indica var. pumila thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
