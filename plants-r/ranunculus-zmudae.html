Ranunculus zmudae

Ranunculus zmudae, commonly known as the Zmudae buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are usually yellow or orange with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
