Rubia incana

Rubia incana, commonly known as hairy balm, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 12,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Rubia incana is often found in dry, rocky soils and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
