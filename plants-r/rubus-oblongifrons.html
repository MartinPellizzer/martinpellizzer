Rubus oblongifrons

Rubus oblongifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus oblongifrons is valued for its edible berries and its role in ecological systems.
