Rhadamanthus namibensis

Rhadamanthus namibensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert cycad, is a unique plant species native to the hyperarid regions of Namibia.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, adapting to extreme desert conditions. Its reproductive structures include a large, cone-like male inflorescence and female cones that produce seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and protected species.
