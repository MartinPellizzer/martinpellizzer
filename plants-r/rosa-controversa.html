Rosa controversa

Rosa controversa, commonly known as the Controversial Rose, is a species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of many rose species. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and potentially invasive in certain environments.
