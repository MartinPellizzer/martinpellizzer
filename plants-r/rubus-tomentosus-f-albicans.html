Rubus tomentosus f. albicans

Rubus tomentosus f. albicans, commonly known as the white-haired blackberry.

This plant is a variant of the common blackberry, characterized by its dense, white pubescent foliage and fruit. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, while similar to the common blackberry, tends to be smaller and less tart, often used in jams and desserts.
