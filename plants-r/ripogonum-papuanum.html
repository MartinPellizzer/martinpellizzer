Ripogonum papuanum

Ripogonum papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Ripogonum papuanum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
