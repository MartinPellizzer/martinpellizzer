Rubus transvaliensis var. transvaliensis

Rubus transvaliensis var. transvaliensis, commonly known as the Transvaal blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes in its native range.
