Rubus fecundus

Rubus fecundus, commonly known as the prolific blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a deep black color, often with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a common sight in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its vigorous growth habit and adaptability contribute to its widespread distribution and ecological significance.
