Rhododendron rhodopus

Rhododendron rhodopus, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas and the eastern Tibetan Plateau. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
