Ranunculus bullatus subsp. supranudus

Ranunculus bullatus subsp.

supranudus, commonly known as the greater buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals, and its leaves are typically trifoliate or palmately divided.

The plant grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

The subspecies supranudus is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to other subspecies.
