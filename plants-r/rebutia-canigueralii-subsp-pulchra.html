Rebutia canigueralii subsp. pulchra

Rebutia canigueralii subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a compact, spineless cactus native to the Canary Islands. It features a globular or cylindrical stem covered in dense, white to pale blue tubercles.

The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
