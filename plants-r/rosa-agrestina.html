Rosa agrestina

Rosa agrestina, commonly known as the field rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features woody stems with thorny projections and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The hips, or fruit, are red, hairy, and contain seeds, often persisting into autumn.
