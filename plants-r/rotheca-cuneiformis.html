Rotheca cuneiformis

Rotheca cuneiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf rotheca, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in late spring.

Rotheca cuneiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects.
