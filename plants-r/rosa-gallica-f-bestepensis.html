Rosa gallica f. bestepensis

Rosa gallica f. bestepensis, commonly known as the Burgundy rose, is a compact, bushy shrub native to the Balkans.

It features dark red to almost black flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has glossy, dark green foliage and produces small, red hips in the fall. It is a vigorous grower, often reaching heights of 1 to 2 meters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its striking color and fragrance.
