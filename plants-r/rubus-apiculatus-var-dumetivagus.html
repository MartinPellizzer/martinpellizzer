Rubus apiculatus var. dumetivagus

Rubus apiculatus var. dumetivagus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features erect or spreading stems that are often hairy and covered in stiff bristles. The leaves are pinnately compound, with three to five leaflets that are oval to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, and the fruit is a aggregate of small, red drupes.

This plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
