Rosa setipoda

Rosa setipoda, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, glandular hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

Rosa setipoda is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
