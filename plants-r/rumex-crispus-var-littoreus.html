Rumex crispus var. littoreus

Rumex crispus var. littoreus, commonly known as sea sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in tidal zones and along shorelines. The plant features alternate, wavy-edged leaves that are typically reddish or purplish in color. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in loose clusters, producing edible, sour-tasting leaves.

Sea sorrel is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
