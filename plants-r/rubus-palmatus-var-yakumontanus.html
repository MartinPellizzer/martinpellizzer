Rubus palmatus var. yakumontanus

Rubus palmatus var. yakumontanus, commonly known as the Yakumontan blackberry, is a shrub native to Japan.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The berries are valued for their flavor and are used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
