Rapuntium quadrangulare

Rapuntium quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-angled cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its quadrangular stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are small, segmented pods.

Rapuntium quadrangulare is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
