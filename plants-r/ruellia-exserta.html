Ruellia exserta

Ruellia exserta, commonly known as the pink ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and clusters of showy, pink to lavender flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
