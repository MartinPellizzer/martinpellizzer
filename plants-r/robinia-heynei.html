Robinia heynei

Robinia heynei, commonly known as the Heyne's false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, leathery pods that split open to release the seeds.

Robinia heynei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
