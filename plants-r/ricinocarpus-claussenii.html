Ricinocarpus claussenii

Ricinocarpus claussenii, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its ability to thrive in brackish water environments. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green in color. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species plays an important role in coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing shorelines.
