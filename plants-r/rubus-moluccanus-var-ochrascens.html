Rubus moluccanus var. ochrascens

Rubus moluccanus var. ochrascens, commonly known as the ochre raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, depending on the variety. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its edible fruits in some regions.
