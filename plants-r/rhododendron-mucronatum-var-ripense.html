Rhododendron mucronatum var. ripense

Rhododendron mucronatum var. ripense, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates and well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
