Rosa nevadensis

Rosa nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rock rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry, sunny environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa nevadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important plant in its native ecosystem.
