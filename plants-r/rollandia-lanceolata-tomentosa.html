Rollandia lanceolata tomentosa

Rollandia lanceolata tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly lanceleaf gesneriad, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered in a soft, white tomentum, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
