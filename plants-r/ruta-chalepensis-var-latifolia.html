Ruta chalepensis var. latifolia

Ruta chalepensis var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf rue, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive, pungent odor. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its medicinal properties have been historically utilized in traditional remedies.

However, it is toxic if ingested and should be handled with care.
