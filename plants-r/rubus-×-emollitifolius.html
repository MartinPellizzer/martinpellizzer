Rubus × emollitifolius

Rubus × emollitifolius, commonly known as the soft-leaved blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and have a soft, velvety texture, which distinguishes it from other Rubus species.

It is valued for its fruit, which is often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
