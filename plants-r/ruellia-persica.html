Ruellia persica

Ruellia persica, commonly known as Persian ropeflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Ruellia persica is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and versatility in garden settings.
