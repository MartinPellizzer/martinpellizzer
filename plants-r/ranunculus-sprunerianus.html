Ranunculus sprunerianus

Ranunculus sprunerianus, commonly known as the Spruner's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It is a perennial species that produces a basal rosette of leaves and has a short, hairy stem.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural environment.
