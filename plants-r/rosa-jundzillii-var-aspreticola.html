Rosa jundzillii var. aspreticola

Rosa jundzillii var. aspreticola, commonly known as the roughleaf rock rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features closely spaced, thornless stems and small, dark green leaves with a rough, leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. It is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in arid environments.
