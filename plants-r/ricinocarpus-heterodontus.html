Ricinocarpus heterodontus

Ricinocarpus heterodontus, commonly known as the African rock apple, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or serrated. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds with a tuft of hair for wind dispersal.
