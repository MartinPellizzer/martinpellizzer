Rhododendron mackenzianum

Rhododendron mackenzianum, commonly known as the Mackenzie rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountain forests where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhododendron mackenzianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
