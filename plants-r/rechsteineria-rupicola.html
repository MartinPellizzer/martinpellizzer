Rechsteineria rupicola

Rechsteineria rupicola, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Rechsteineria rupicola is often found in rocky outcrops and along coastal cliffs.
