Rosa × collina var. tortuosa

Rosa × collina var. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted hillbilly rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and thorny stems that often twist and curl. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
