Rosa conspicua

Rosa conspicua, commonly known as the showy rose, is a species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features large, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a red or yellow center. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa conspicua is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
