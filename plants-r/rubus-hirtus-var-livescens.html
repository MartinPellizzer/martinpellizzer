Rubus hirtus var. livescens

Rubus hirtus var. livescens, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a common species in eastern North American regions.
