Romulea candida

Romulea candida, commonly known as the white romulea, is a perennial geophyte native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bulbous corms that store nutrients for growth. The plant produces elegant, grass-like leaves and delicate, star-shaped white flowers with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and unique floral display.
