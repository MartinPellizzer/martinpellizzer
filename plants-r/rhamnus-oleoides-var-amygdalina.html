Rhamnus oleoides var. amygdalina

Rhamnus oleoides var. amygdalina, commonly known as the almond buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by small, red or orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
