Radicula occidentalis

Radicula occidentalis, commonly known as the Western root vine, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its fibrous root system and slender, climbing stems that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

Radicula occidentalis is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.
