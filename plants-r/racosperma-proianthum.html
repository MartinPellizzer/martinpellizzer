Racosperma proianthum

Racosperma proianthum, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, delicate flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry and coastal environments.
