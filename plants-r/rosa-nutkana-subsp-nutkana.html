Rosa nutkana subsp. nutkana

Rosa nutkana subsp.

nutkana, commonly known as the Nutkan rose, is a species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red or orange hips.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and streamside habitats.
