Rorippa obtusa var. alpina

Rorippa obtusa var. alpina, commonly known as alpine watercress, is a variant of the watercress species native to alpine regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette growth habit and small, serrated leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and it is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is valued for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
