Rytigynia murifolia

Rytigynia murifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an endangered species in some regions.
