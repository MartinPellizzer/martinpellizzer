Rubus micans var. subelegans

Rubus micans var. subelegans, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

The botanical features of this variety make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
