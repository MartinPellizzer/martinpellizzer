Racosperma myrtifolium

Racosperma myrtifolium, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, stipitate pods that contain seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Racosperma myrtifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
