Rhynchospora bolivarana

Rhynchospora bolivarana, commonly known as the Bolivarian spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, spike-like flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Rhynchospora bolivarana is adapted to grow in moist, often shaded environments such as wetlands and forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems, though it is relatively understudied.
