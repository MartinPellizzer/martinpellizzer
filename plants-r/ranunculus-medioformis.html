Ranunculus medioformis

Ranunculus medioformis, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and is native to various regions in Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually bloom from late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus medioformis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
