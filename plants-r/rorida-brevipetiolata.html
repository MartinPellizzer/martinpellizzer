Rorida brevipetiolata

Rorida brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled rorida, is a unique succulent plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming during the warmer months. Its short petioles, or leaf stems, give it a distinctive appearance and contribute to its classification within the Aizoaceae family.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
