Ranunculus glaberrimus var. glaberrimus

Ranunculus glaberrimus var. glaberrimus, commonly known as the glabrous buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and it has a fibrous root system. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a lobed or divided appearance.

The species is characterized by its lack of hairy surfaces, giving it a smooth, glabrous texture.
