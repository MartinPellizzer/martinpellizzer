Rubus incarnatus var. subincertus

Rubus incarnatus var. subincertus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This variant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its reddish stems and clustered, red berries. It typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, hairy leaves with serrated edges.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor and are used in various culinary applications.
