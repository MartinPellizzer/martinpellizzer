Rosa systyla var. seposita

Rosa systyla var. seposita, commonly known as the mountain rose or rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for alpine gardens and rockeries. Its leaves are dark green, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
