Randia aculeata var. mitis

Randia aculeata var. mitis, commonly known as the smooth randia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
