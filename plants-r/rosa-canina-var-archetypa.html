Rosa canina var. archetypa

Rosa canina var. archetypa, commonly known as the dog rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces bright red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Rosa canina var. archetypa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and historical significance in herbal medicine.
