Rubus orbifrons

Rubus orbifrons, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into bright red, edible berries. These berries are typically small, firm, and have a tart flavor.

Botanically, Rubus orbifrons belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberries.
