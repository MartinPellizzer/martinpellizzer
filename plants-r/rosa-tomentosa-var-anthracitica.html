Rosa tomentosa var. anthracitica

Rosa tomentosa var. anthracitica, commonly known as the black-haired rose, is a cultivar characterized by its dense, dark grayish-brown pubescence.

It is a shrub-like plant with multiple stems and is native to the eastern United States. The flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters, and bloom in the spring. The foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature in temperate climates.
