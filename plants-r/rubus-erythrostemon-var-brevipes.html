Rubus erythrostemon var. brevipes

Rubus erythrostemon var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed raspberry, is a variant of the redstem raspberry species.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

The flowers are white and produced in clusters, followed by the edible berries that ripen in late summer.
