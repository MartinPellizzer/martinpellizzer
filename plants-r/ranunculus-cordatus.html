Ranunculus cordatus

Ranunculus cordatus, commonly known as the heart-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically bright yellow with five petals. The plant produces a cluster of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Ranunculus cordatus grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate climates.
