Rebutia kieslingii

Rebutia kieslingii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant with flattened, ribbed stems that form a shrub-like structure. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. Its leaves are modified into phylloclades, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Rebutia kieslingii is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its compact size and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
