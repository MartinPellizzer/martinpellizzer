Rubus × agrestis var. purus

Rubus × agrestis var. purus, commonly known as the bramble raspberry or trailing blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on maturity. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks.
