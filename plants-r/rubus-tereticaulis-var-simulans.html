Rubus tereticaulis var. simulans

Rubus tereticaulis var. simulans, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Botanically, it is closely related to other species in the Rubus genus, sharing similar morphological features.
