Rosa multiflora f. roseiflora

Rosa multiflora f. roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered multiflora rose, is a flowering shrub native to East Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and is characterized by its abundant, fragrant flowers that range from white to deep pink. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
