Rhamnus illyrica var. ernesti-mayeri

Rhamnus illyrica var. ernesti-mayeri, commonly known as the Illyrian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, oval berries in the autumn. Its berries are a food source for various birds and small mammals.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in forest edges and shrublands.
