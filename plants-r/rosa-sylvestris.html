Rosa sylvestris

Rosa sylvestris, commonly known as the wild rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant pink or white flowers and thorny stems. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

Rosa sylvestris is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
