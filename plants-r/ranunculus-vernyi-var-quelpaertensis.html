Ranunculus vernyi var. quelpaertensis

Ranunculus vernyi var. quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Korean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
