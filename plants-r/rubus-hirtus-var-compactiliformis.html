Rubus hirtus var. compactiliformis

Rubus hirtus var. compactiliformis, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
