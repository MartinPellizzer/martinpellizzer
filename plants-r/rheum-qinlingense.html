Rheum qinlingense

Rheum qinlingense, commonly known as Qinling rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to the Qinling Mountains in China.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and tall, flowering stalks. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of anthraquinone compounds in its roots. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
