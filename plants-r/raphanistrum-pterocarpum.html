Raphanistrum pterocarpum

Raphanistrum pterocarpum, commonly known as the African turnip or wild turnip, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by distinctive, triangular, winged fruits. It is adapted to grow in various soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Due to its nutritional value, it is sometimes used as a leafy vegetable in local cuisines.
