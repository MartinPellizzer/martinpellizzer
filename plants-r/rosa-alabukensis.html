Rosa alabukensis

Rosa alabukensis, commonly known as the Alabuk Mountain rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem that becomes more robust with age.

Rosa alabukensis is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
