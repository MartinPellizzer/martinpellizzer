Rubus procerus f. perfoliatus

Rubus procerus f. perfoliatus, commonly known as the perfoliate raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its unique leaf structure.

Its leaves are perfoliate, meaning they have a basal pair of lobes that fuse around the stem, creating a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It is native to regions of North America and thrives in moist, wooded areas.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential use in culinary applications.
