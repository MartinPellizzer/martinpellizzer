Rollinia fosteri

Rollinia fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's starapple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a fruit that resembles a starapple, with a rough, spiky exterior and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Rollinia fosteri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
