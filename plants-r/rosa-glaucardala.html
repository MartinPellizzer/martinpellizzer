Rosa glaucardala

Rosa glaucardala, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thornless stems. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of serrated, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Rosa glaucardala is well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in alpine gardens.
