Rosa pimpinellifolia major

Rosa pimpinellifolia major, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has woody stems and produces fragrant, five-petaled blooms. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a fine layer of hair.

Rosa pimpinellifolia major is often found growing in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
