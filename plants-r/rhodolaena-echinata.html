Rhodolaena echinata

Rhodolaena echinata, commonly known as the spiny rhodolaena, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
