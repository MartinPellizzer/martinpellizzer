Rosa hirta subsp. globata

Rosa hirta subsp.

globata, commonly known as the globe rose, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and dense, thorny stems.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
