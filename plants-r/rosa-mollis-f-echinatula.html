Rosa mollis f. echinatula

Rosa mollis f. echinatula, commonly known as the hairy rose or the soft rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy texture on the stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and mild fragrance, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
