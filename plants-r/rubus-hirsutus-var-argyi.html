Rubus hirsutus var. argyi

Rubus hirsutus var. argyi, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
