Rhododendron gologense

Rhododendron gologense, commonly known as the Golog rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Tibet and western Sichuan in China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and shrublands, thriving in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron gologense is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
