Rosa × umbelliflora

Rosa × umbelliflora, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant produces rose hips that are orange-red and contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
