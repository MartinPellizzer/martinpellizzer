Rinorea pallidiviridis

Rinorea pallidiviridis, commonly known as the pale-green rinorea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually pale green in color, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a slight fragrance, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Rinorea pallidiviridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
