Ranunculus macauleyi

Ranunculus macauleyi, commonly known as the Macauley's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, herbaceous plants. The species is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

The plant's foliage consists of deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
