Rubus gratus subsp. clethraphilus

Rubus gratus subsp.

clethraphilus, commonly known as the clethraphilus roseberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
