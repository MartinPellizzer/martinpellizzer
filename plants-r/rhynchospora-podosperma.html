Rhynchospora podosperma

Rhynchospora podosperma, commonly known as the roughside spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in areas with standing water or saturated ground. The plant has slender, upright stems that bear dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture that gives it its common name.

Rhynchospora podosperma plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
