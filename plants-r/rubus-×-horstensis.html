Rubus × horstensis

Rubus × horstensis, commonly known as the Horstensen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubus genus in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of genetic variations in fruit size, flavor, and hardiness.
