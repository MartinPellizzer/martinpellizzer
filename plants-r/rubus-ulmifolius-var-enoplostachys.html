Rubus ulmifolius var. enoplostachys

Rubus ulmifolius var. enoplostachys, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrower and more elongated compared to the standard species. The flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruit is a dark purple to black aggregate berry. This variety is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
