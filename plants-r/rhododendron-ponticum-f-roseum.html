Rhododendron ponticum f. roseum

Rhododendron ponticum f. roseum, commonly known as the pink-flowered form of the Pontic rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, evergreen foliage and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pale pink to deep rose in color. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.

However, it can be invasive in certain environments if not managed carefully.
