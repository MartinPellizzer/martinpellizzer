Rubus moluccanus var. hillii

Rubus moluccanus var. hillii, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry.

It is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia, characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, and it has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
