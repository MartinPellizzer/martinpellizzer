Rubus × leucophaeatus

Rubus × leucophaeatus, commonly known as the white-leafed blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically larger than those of its parent species. The plant exhibits thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruit that is highly valued in local cuisines.
