Rubus × capitatus

Rubus × capitatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible blackberries that ripen in late summer. Its flowers are white and clustered, typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and woodlands.
