Renealmia antillarum var. grandiflora

Renealmia antillarum var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows in wet, swampy areas and along riverbanks. The plant features large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine and medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
