Rosa × triquadrans

Rosa × triquadrans, commonly known as the four-square rose, is a hybrid species originating from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa × triquadrans is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
