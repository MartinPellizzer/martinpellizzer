Rhynchosia tomentosa var. mollissima

Rhynchosia tomentosa var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, pubescent leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
