Rumex crispus subsp. japonicus

Rumex crispus subsp.

japonicus, commonly known as Japanese dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It features serrated, lobed leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color, especially along the veins.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in a terminal spike. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and disturbed areas.

Its leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some Asian cuisines.
