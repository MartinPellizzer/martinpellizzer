Rosa nutkana subsp. melina

Rosa nutkana subsp.

melina, commonly known as the Alaska rose, is a shrub native to the western regions of North America. It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces red or orange hips in the fall.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
