Rubus × adenophylloides

Rubus × adenophylloides, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus argutus, exhibiting characteristics of both parents. This plant is native to the western United States and is known for its dense, thorny growth habit. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, while its fruits are small, dark berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
