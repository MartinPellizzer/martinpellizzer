Rubus rudis f. trichocarpus

Rubus rudis f. trichocarpus, commonly known as the hairy-fruited blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy fruit surface and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in local ecosystems.
