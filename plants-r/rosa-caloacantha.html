Rosa caloacantha

Rosa caloacantha, commonly known as the scarlet sandalwood rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has woody stems and produces small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically borne singly or in clusters, and they emit a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa caloacantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
