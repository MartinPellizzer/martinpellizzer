Ranunculus pusillus

Ranunculus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are palmately lobed. It has a short stature, usually reaching only a few inches in height.

Ranunculus pusillus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is known for its early spring blooming period.
