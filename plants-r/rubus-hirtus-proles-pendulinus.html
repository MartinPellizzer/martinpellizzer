Rubus hirtus proles pendulinus

Rubus hirtus proles pendulinus, commonly known as the trailing raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
