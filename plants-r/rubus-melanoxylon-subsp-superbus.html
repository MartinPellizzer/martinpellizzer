Rubus melanoxylon subsp. superbus

Rubus melanoxylon subsp.

superbus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
