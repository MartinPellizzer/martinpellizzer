Ranunculus pegaeus var. curvistylis

Ranunculus pegaeus var. curvistylis, commonly known as the curly-styled buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers have a unique, curved stamen structure that distinguishes it from other ranunculus species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
