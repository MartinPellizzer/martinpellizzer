Rubus sundaicus var. discolor

Rubus sundaicus var. discolor, commonly known as the Sunda raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate of small, red drupes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
