Rhododendron pulchroides

Rhododendron pulchroides, commonly known as the white rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with leathery, elliptical leaves. Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

It is often found in cool, moist habitats such as forest understories and rocky slopes.
