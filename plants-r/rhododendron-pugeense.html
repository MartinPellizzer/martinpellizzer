Rhododendron pugeense

Rhododendron pugeense, commonly known as the Puge rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its botanical features include thick, leathery leaves and a woody stem structure typical of many rhododendron species.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitat.
