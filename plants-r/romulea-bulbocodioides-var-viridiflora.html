Romulea bulbocodioides var. viridiflora

Romulea bulbocodioides var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered romulea, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically green or yellowish-green, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
