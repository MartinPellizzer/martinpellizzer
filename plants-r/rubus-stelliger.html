Rubus stelliger

Rubus stelliger, commonly known as the coastal bramble, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in coastal areas, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
