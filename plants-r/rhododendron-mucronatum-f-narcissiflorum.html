Rhododendron mucronatum f. narcissiflorum

Rhododendron mucronatum f. narcissiflorum, commonly known as the Narcissiflorum Rhododendron, is a hybrid form of the species Rhododendron mucronatum.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble daffodils, with bright yellow petals and a prominent, trumpet-shaped corona. The plant is native to the Himalayas and thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.
