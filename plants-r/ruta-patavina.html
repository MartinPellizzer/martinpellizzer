Ruta patavina

Ruta patavina, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by winged fruits called schizocarps. Ruta patavina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its essential oils contain compounds like rutin and volatile oils, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
