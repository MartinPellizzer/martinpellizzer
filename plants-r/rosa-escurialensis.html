Rosa escurialensis

Rosa escurialensis, commonly known as the Spanish rose, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include stipules that are lanceolate and often caducous, and it is adapted to thrive in rocky or dry soils.

Rosa escurialensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
