Rosa winterbottomii

Rosa winterbottomii, commonly known as the Winterbottom rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include thorny stems and pinnate leaves, which are typical of many rose species.

The flowers are often followed by oval, red to orange hips, which contain the seeds.
