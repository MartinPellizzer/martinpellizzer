Rubus turkestanicus

Rubus turkestanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cooler climates. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which is edible, is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
