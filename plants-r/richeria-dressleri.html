Richeria dressleri

Richeria dressleri, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Richeria dressleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
