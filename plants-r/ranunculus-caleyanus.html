Ranunculus caleyanus

Ranunculus caleyanus, commonly known as the Caleyan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the island of Ceylon, now known as Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, green fruits containing seeds.
