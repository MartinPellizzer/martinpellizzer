Rubus tomentosus f. maximus

Rubus tomentosus f. maximus, commonly known as the giant raspberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its large, robust stems and dark, velvety foliage. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruits are sizable, aggregate berries that can be red or yellow depending on the variety.

This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and vigorous growth habit.
