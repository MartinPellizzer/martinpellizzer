Rosa × praestans

Rosa × praestans, commonly known as the "Climbing Hybrid Rose," is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb or trail along supports. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming throughout the summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Rosa × praestans is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
