Ranunculus flabellatus var. manzanoanus

Ranunculus flabellatus var. manzanoanus, commonly known as the Manzanoan buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, flared petal structure. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and streambanks. It produces small, green fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
