Rubus rusticanus f. patula

Rubus rusticanus f. patula, commonly known as the Patula blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
