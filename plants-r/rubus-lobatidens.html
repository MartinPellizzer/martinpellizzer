Rubus lobatidens

Rubus lobatidens, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its large, lobed leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of mixed hardwood forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and preserves.
