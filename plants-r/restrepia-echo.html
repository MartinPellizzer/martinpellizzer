Restrepia echo

Restrepia echo, commonly known as the Echo Cymbidium, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and long, slender pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
