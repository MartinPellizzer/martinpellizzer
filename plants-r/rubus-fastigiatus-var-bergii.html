Rubus fastigiatus var. bergii

Rubus fastigiatus var. bergii, commonly known as the Berg's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
