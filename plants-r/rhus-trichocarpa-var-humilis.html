Rhus trichocarpa var. humilis

Rhus trichocarpa var. humilis, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 3 to 5 inches long and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by dark purple berries that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems.
