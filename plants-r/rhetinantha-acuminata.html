Rhetinantha acuminata

Rhetinantha acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often succulent stems and small, pointed leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Rhetinantha acuminata is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it a resilient species in challenging environments.
