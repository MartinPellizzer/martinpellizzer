Rumex scutatus var. vulgaris

Rumex scutatus var. vulgaris, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate, lanceolate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows.

Narrowleaf dock is known for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
