Restrepia flosculata subsp. picta

Restrepia flosculata subsp.

picta, commonly known as the picta orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces intricate, showy flower spikes with a mix of white, yellow, and green hues.

The plant features fleshy, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention in its high-altitude habitat. Its flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
