Ranunculus gracillimus

Ranunculus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to seven petals, typically in shades of yellow or white, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. It has a short stem and produces small, bead-like fruits that disperse its seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
