Rhynchopappus

Rhynchopappus, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of dense, cylindrical clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant often grows in dry, rocky habitats. Rhynchopappus species are native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.
