Rhododendron myrtifolium

Rhododendron myrtifolium, commonly known as the roseleaf azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that resembles rose leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Rhododendron myrtifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
