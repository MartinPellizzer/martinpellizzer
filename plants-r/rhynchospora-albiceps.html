Rhynchospora albiceps

Rhynchospora albiceps, commonly known as the white-headed spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, thriving in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Rhynchospora albiceps plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
