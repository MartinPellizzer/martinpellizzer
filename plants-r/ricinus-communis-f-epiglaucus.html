Ricinus communis f. epiglaucus

Ricinus communis f. epiglaucus, commonly known as the variegated castor bean, is a cultivated form of the castor oil plant.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm climates and requires full sunlight. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can grow up to 30 cm in length.

While it is ornamental, it is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic.
