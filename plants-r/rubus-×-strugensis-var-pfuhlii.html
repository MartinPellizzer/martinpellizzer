Rubus × strugensis var. pfuhlii

Rubus × strugensis var. pfuhlii, commonly known as the Pfuhlii blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dark, almost black berries.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate climates, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. Its berries are prized for their intense flavor and are used in culinary applications.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
