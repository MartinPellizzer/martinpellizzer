Rhododendron luteum var. flammeum

Rhododendron luteum var. flammeum, commonly known as the Flame Azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange-red, often with a striking contrast between the petals and the green sepals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers acidic, well-drained soil. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of funnel-shaped flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and landscapes.
