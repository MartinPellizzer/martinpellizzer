Ruellia nudiflora var. runyonii

Ruellia nudiflora var. runyonii, commonly known as Runyon's nakedflower ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
