Rubus spectabilis var. uncinatus

Rubus spectabilis var. uncinatus, commonly known as blackberry dewberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry species. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
