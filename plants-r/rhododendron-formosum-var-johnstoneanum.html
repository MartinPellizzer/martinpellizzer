Rhododendron formosum var. johnstoneanum

Rhododendron formosum var. johnstoneanum, commonly known as the Johnston's rhododendron, is a variant native to Taiwan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its botanical features include large, ovate leaves and flowers that range in color from pink to deep red.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
