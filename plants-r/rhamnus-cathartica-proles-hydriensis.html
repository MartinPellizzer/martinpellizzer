Rhamnus cathartica proles hydriensis

Rhamnus cathartica proles hydriensis, commonly known as the Hydriensis buckthorn, is a subspecies of the European buckthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. It has a woody stem with thorns and produces a yellowish-green flower cluster in the spring.

The subspecies is notable for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal medicine for its laxative properties.
