Rubus longipetalus

Rubus longipetalus, commonly known as the long-petaled blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are notable for their long, narrow petals, giving it its scientific name.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils, and is often found in the Pacific Northwest.
