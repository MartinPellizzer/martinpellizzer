Rosa canina var. oblongata

Rosa canina var. oblongata, commonly known as the oval hip rose, is a variant of the dog rose characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped hips.

This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in woodland edges and shrublands. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated and hairy. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals, and the plant produces showy, red-orange hips that persist through winter.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
