Rubus × serus

Rubus × serus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible and often used in culinary applications, though they can be tart and require sweetening.

Rubus × serus thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
