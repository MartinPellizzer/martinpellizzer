Rhodocactus colombianus

Rhodocactus colombianus, commonly known as the Colombian red cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, adapting to the arid conditions of its native habitat. The plant produces vibrant red flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small clusters, with spiny areoles that support its protective features.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cactaceae family and is valued for its resilience and ornamental appeal.
