Rubus thyrsiflorus proles linkianus

Rubus thyrsiflorus proles linkianus, commonly known as the Linkian bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
