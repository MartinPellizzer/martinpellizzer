Rubus pubescens var. pilosifolius

Rubus pubescens var. pilosifolius, commonly known as the hairy-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems, leaves, and fruits, which are typically small and dark purple to black in color. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its edible berries and its role in ecological systems.
