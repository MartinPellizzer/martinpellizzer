Rubus × obtusidens

Rubus × obtusidens, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in moist, shady environments. Its leaves are typically elliptical with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are dark purple to black when mature and are often used in culinary applications.
