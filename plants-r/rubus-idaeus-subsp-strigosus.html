Rubus idaeus subsp. strigosus

Rubus idaeus subsp.

strigosus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant native to North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark purple foliage.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, often with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
