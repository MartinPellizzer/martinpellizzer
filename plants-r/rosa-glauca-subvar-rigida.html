Rosa glauca subvar. rigida

Rosa glauca subvar. rigida, commonly known as the rigid blue rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces fragrant, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

Due to its unique color and hardy nature, it is a sought-after plant among gardeners and botanists alike.
