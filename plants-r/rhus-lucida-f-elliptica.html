Rhus lucida f. elliptica

Rhus lucida f. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical sumac, is a variant of the smooth sumac characterized by its elliptical leaf shape.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, typically growing in moist, sunny habitats. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a finely serrated margin. Its small, aggregate fruits are red and turn yellowish as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often used in native plant gardens.
