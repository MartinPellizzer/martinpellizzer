Racosperma sclerocladum

Racosperma sclerocladum, commonly known as the rigid-leaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thick, woody stems that help it survive in arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
