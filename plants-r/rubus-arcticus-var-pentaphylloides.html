Rubus arcticus var. pentaphylloides

Rubus arcticus var. pentaphylloides, commonly known as the Arctic raspberry, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems, which help protect it from harsh cold. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional foods. Its leaves are compound, typically with five leaflets, giving it the specific epithet *pentaphylloides*.

It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is an important species in Arctic ecosystems.
