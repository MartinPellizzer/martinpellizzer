Rubus schlechtendalii var. valdepilosus

Rubus schlechtendalii var. valdepilosus, commonly known as the silky blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and thorny stems, which are typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rubus genus.

The silky blackberry is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cooler, moist conditions.
