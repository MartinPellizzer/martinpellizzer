Rungia longifolia subsp. latior

Rungia longifolia subsp.

latior, commonly known as the broad-leaved rungia, is a member of the Rubiaceae family. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
