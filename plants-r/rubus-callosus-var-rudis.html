Rubus callosus var. rudis

Rubus callosus var. rudis, commonly known as the rough raspberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
