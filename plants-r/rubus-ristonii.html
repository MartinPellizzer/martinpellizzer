Rubus ristonii

Rubus ristonii, commonly known as the Sierra raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
