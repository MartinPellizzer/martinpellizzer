Rubus moluccanus var. rectangulifolius

Rubus moluccanus var. rectangulifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its distinctive broad, elliptical leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
