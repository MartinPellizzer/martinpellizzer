Rosa canina f. subvinacea

Rosa canina f. subvinacea, commonly known as the red-stemmed dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems and thorny growth habit. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red hips in the autumn. It is native to Europe and thrives in a variety of soil types, preferring well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
