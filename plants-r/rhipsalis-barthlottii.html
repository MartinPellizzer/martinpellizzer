Rhipsalis barthlottii

Rhipsalis barthlottii, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It features long, slender, branching stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
