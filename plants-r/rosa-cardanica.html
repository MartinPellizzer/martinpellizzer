Rosa cardanica

Rosa cardanica, commonly known as the Cardan rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa cardanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
