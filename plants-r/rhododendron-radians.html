Rhododendron radians

Rhododendron radians, commonly known as the white rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its floral display.

Rhododendron radians thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with cool, moist climates.
