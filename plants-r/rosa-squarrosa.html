Rosa squarrosa

Rosa squarrosa, commonly known as the sand rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and bright pink to red flowers. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Rosa squarrosa is adapted to arid and sandy environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
