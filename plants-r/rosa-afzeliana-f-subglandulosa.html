Rosa afzeliana f. subglandulosa

Rosa afzeliana f. subglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa afzeliana.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs on the stems, leaves, and flowers, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rosa genus.

This subspecies is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
