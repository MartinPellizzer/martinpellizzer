Rubus godronii var. foliolatus

Rubus godronii var. foliolatus, commonly known as Godron's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated leaflets and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It is native to regions of South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
