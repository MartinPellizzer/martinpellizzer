Rourea phyllanthoides

Rourea phyllanthoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf rourea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short peduncles. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Rourea phyllanthoides is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and stream banks.
