Ravensara tapak

Ravensara tapak, commonly known as the golden sapphire tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that display a vibrant yellow-green coloration. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Ravensara tapak is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its growth habit is typically as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in suitable conditions.
