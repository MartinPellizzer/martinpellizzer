Rubus commixtus

Rubus commixtus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
