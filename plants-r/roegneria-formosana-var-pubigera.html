Roegneria formosana var. pubigera

Roegneria formosana var. pubigera, commonly known as the pubescent form of the Chinese grass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically spike-like, bearing small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
