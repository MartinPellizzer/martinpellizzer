Ranunculus paucistamineus subsp. sublaevis

Ranunculus paucistamineus subsp.

sublaevis, commonly known as the smoothleaf buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring, and it is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smoother leaf surfaces compared to other members of the species.
