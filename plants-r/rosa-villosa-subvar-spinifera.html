Rosa villosa subvar. spinifera

Rosa villosa subvar. spinifera, commonly known as the rough bristly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and hairy, grayish-green foliage. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens.

It produces rose hips that are often used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.
