Ranunculus morii

Ranunculus morii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats, such as streambanks and meadows. It has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, and its stems are often hairy.

Ranunculus morii is an herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring.
