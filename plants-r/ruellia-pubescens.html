Ruellia pubescens

Ruellia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Ruellia pubescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
