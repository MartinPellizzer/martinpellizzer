Rhynchosia minima f. nuda

Rhynchosia minima f. nuda, commonly known as the naked leaf cowpea, is a variant of the cowpea species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves that are often reduced or absent in this form. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its adaptability to poor soils and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful crop in sustainable agriculture.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
