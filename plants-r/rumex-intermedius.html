Rumex intermedius

Rumex intermedius, commonly known as intermediate sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple underside, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its edible leaves have a tart, sour flavor and have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.
