Ricinus communis var. benguelensis

Ricinus communis var. benguelensis, commonly known as the African castor bean, is a variant of the castor oil plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and spiny calyx. The plant produces distinctive reddish-brown seed capsules that contain toxic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of castor oil. Its growth habit includes a tall, branching stem that can reach up to 3 meters in height.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
