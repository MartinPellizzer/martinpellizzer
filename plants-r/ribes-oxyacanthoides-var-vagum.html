Ribes oxyacanthoides var. vagum

Ribes oxyacanthoides var. vagum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and woodland edges. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its small, yellow flowers appear in early spring. It produces edible red berries that are a food source for birds and mammals.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
