Rhamnus brachypoda

Rhamnus brachypoda, commonly known as the shortleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches long. Its flowers are greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, black berries that are edible for birds.

The shrub is often found in shaded environments and is part of the Rhamnaceae family.
