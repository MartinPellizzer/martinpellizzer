Rumex × exspectatus

Rumex × exspectatus, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex acetobatus (water sorrel), resulting in a plant with distinctive botanical features. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a reddish coloration on the veins, and the plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
