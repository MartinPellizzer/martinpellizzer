Ranunculus fallax subsp. effingens

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

effingens, commonly known as the Scottish buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and woodland edges.

The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers with five to nine petals. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is considered rare and is protected in some regions due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
