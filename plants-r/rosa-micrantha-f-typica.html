Rosa micrantha f. typica

Rosa micrantha f. typica, commonly known as the typical form of the small-flowered rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features narrow, thornless stems and elliptical leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and a generally compact growth habit.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
