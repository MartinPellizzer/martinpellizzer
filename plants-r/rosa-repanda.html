Rosa repanda

Rosa repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dense foliage. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies, while its hips produce edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

Rosa repanda is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in horticulture.
