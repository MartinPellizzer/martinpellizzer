Rosa abietina var. grignensis

Rosa abietina var. grignensis, commonly known as the Grignan rose, is a rare and ancient variety of rose native to the French Alps.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, where it forms dense, low-growing shrubs. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its historical significance and is often cultivated in specialized gardens for its ornamental and historical appeal.
