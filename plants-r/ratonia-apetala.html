Ratonia apetala

Ratonia apetala, commonly known as the leafless ratonia, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of petals, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to typical flowering plants. The plant has simple, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Ratonia apetala is considered a conservation concern.
