Rhododendron roxieanum var. globigerum

Rhododendron roxieanum var. globigerum, commonly known as the globigerum rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during late spring. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils. It thrives in partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical classification highlights its place within the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like azaleas and blueberries.
