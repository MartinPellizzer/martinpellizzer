Rhododendron sinonuttallii

Rhododendron sinonuttallii, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often clustered in inflorescences, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

The plant is valued for its hardiness and adaptability, making it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in temperate climates.
