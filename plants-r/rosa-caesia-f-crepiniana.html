Rosa caesia f. crepiniana

Rosa caesia f. crepiniana, commonly known as the Crepiniana form of the Blush Rosa, is a hybrid form of the species Rosa caesia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse blooming of semi-double, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically produces small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This form is prized for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
