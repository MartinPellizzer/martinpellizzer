Rebutia xanthocarpa f. violaciflora

Rebutia xanthocarpa f. violaciflora, commonly known as the purple-flowered cactus, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant violet or purple flowers during the growing season, which are typically tubular in shape. Native to arid regions of South America, it thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative cactus in gardens and greenhouses.
