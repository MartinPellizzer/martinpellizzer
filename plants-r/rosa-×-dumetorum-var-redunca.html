Rosa × dumetorum var. redunca

Rosa × dumetorum var. redunca, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a shrub-like plant characterized by its low-growing habit and thorny stems.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. It produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardiness in various climates.
