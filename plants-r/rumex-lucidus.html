Rumex lucidus

Rumex lucidus, commonly known as the shining sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy, dark green leaves that are heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Rumex lucidus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
