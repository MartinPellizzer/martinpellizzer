Ruellia chilpancingana

Ruellia chilpancingana, commonly known as Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia chilpancingana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
