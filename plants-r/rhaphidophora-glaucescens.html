Rhaphidophora glaucescens

Rhaphidophora glaucescens, commonly known as the Blue-veined Spider Plant, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, waxy, blue-green leaves with prominent veins, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

Rhaphidophora glaucescens prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
