Rivea roxburghii

Rivea roxburghii, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, and it is often grown for its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
