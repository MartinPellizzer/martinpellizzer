Ranunculus pseudolowii

Ranunculus pseudolowii, commonly known as the low's buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States, particularly in California. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, and it has a basal rosette of leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
