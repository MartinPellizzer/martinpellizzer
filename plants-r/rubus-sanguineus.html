Rubus sanguineus

Rubus sanguineus, commonly known as the blood raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and strawberries.
