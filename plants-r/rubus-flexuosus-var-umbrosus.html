Rubus flexuosus var. umbrosus

Rubus flexuosus var. umbrosus, commonly known as the shady blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded environments. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically deeply lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is well adapted to moist, forested habitats and is often found in temperate regions.
