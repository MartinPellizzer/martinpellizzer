Reboulea × pallens var. major

Reboulea × pallens var. major, commonly known as the giant reed or giant bamboo, is a hybrid plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, often followed by small, three-lobed fruits.

This species is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance.
