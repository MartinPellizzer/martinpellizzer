Ridleyandra corneri

Ridleyandra corneri, commonly known as the Christmas Island flame tree, is a unique flowering plant native to Christmas Island in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom during the wet season, typically from November to March. The plant has a distinctive appearance with thick, waxy leaves and a short, stout trunk. It is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat and thrives in well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
