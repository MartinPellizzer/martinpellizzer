Rosa × purpurea

Rosa × purpurea, commonly known as the deep pink hybrid rose, is a popular ornamental plant valued for its vibrant, fragrant flowers.

It is a hybrid between Rosa sempervirens and Rosa gallica, resulting in a plant that exhibits a range of flower colors from deep red to pale pink. The shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of five to seven petals.

This species is widely cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in the production of rose products such as essential oils and perfume.
