Rosa carolina inermis

Rosa carolina inermis, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, stipulate leaves with a slightly serrated margin.

Its flowers are typically followed by red, fuzzy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa carolina inermis is often found in moist, open woods and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
