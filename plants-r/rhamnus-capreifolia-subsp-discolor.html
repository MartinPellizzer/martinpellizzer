Rhamnus capreifolia subsp. discolor

Rhamnus capreifolia subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the white-fruited buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by small, white or pale yellow berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and berries.
