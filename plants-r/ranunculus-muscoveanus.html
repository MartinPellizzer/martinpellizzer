Ranunculus muscoveanus

Ranunculus muscoveanus, commonly known as the musk ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
