Rosa communis var. crepiniana

Rosa communis var. crepiniana, commonly known as the Crepiniana rose, is a cultivar of the common rose that is prized for its ornamental value.

It is characterized by its lush, dark green foliage and large, fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure. It is often used in gardens for its beauty and is relatively hardy, though it may require protection in colder regions.

The flowers are typically borne on long stems, making them ideal for cut flower arrangements.
