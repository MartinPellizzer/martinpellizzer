Rubus hirtus proles hercynicus

Rubus hirtus proles hercynicus, commonly known as the Hercynian bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as rubus berries, are edible and often used in jams and preserves.

This plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in woodland and hedgerow environments.
