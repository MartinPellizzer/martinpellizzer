Rhododendron × nebulicola

Rhododendron × nebulicola, commonly known as the clouded rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shady, cool environments. Its botanical features include elongated, leathery leaves and a compact, multi-stemmed growth habit.

Rhododendron × nebulicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
