Ranunculus diversifolius var. succulentus

Ranunculus diversifolius var. succulentus, commonly known as the succulent buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are often bluish-green in color, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and delicate flowers make it a desirable addition to native plant collections.
