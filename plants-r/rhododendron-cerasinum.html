Rhododendron cerasinum

Rhododendron cerasinum, commonly known as the cherry laurel rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, and it produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
