Rubus rhamnifoliatus

Rubus rhamnifoliatus, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a small, dark red berry, is edible but often tart and less commonly consumed.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
