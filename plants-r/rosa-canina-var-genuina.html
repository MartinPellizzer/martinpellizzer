Rosa canina var. genuina

Rosa canina var. genuina, commonly known as the genuine dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its smaller, more fragrant flowers and is typically found in mountainous regions. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and pinnate leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
