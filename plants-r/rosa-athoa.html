Rosa athoa

Rosa athoa, commonly known as the Athoas rose, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
