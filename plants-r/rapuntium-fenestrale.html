Rapuntium fenestrale

Rapuntium fenestrale, commonly known as the fenestrated rapunculus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year spent as a rosette of basal leaves and the second year producing a flowering stem. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
