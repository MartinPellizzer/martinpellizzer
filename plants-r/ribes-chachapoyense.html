Ribes chachapoyense

Ribes chachapoyense, commonly known as the Chachapoyan gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various shrubs and vines commonly found in temperate climates. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions. It typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features include alternate, ovate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
