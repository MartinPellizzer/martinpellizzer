Ribes flavescens

Ribes flavescens, commonly known as the yellow currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange to red berries in late summer. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and jellies.

Ribes flavescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
