Ranunculus fallax subsp. ellipes

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

ellipes, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
