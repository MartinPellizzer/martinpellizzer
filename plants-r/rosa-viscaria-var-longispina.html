Rosa viscaria var. longispina

Rosa viscaria var. longispina, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a thorny shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, often thorn-covered stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa viscaria var. longispina is often used in hedges or as a decorative plant due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
