Rubus × aulninus

Rubus × aulninus, commonly known as the blackberry-elderberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus fruticosus (bramble) and Rubus ulmifolius (elderberry), resulting in a plant with distinctive botanical features. The plant typically has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces dark purple to black berries that are edible.

This hybrid is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils across Europe.
