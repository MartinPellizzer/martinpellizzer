Rubus gratiosus var. soricinensis

Rubus gratiosus var. soricinensis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe, with a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
