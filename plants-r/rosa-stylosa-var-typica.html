Rosa stylosa var. typica

Rosa stylosa var. typica, commonly known as the typical stylosa rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include thorny stems and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
