Rosa afzeliana var. rubroviolacea

Rosa afzeliana var. rubroviolacea, commonly known as the red-violet rose, is a species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically arranged alternately. The flowers are showy, with deep pink to violet petals that bloom in spring. It produces fragrant, pinkish-red hips that contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
