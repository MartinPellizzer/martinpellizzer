Rosa sempervirens var. pinnata

Rosa sempervirens var. pinnata, commonly known as the evergreen rose or the shrub rose, is a variant of the evergreen rose that is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrub form and its ability to retain its foliage year-round. The plant produces fragrant pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of multiple leaflets, which is where its botanical name originates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
