Ruellia tomentosa

Ruellia tomentosa, commonly known as velvetleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ruellia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
