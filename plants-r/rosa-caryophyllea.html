Rosa caryophyllea

Rosa caryophyllea, commonly known as the Damascus rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, deep red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs.

Rosa caryophyllea is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of rose oil.
