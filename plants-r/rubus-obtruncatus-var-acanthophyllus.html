Rubus obtruncatus var. acanthophyllus

Rubus obtruncatus var. acanthophyllus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, which are characteristic of many Rubus species. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are similar to other blackberries.

The varietal name "acanthophyllus" refers to its bristly or prickly leaves, which are a distinguishing feature of this plant.
