Rubus rosaceus microgene drepanophorus

Rubus rosaceus microgene drepanophorus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

This plant is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. It typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and used in traditional herbal medicine.
