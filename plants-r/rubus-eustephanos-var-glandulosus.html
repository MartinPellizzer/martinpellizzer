Rubus eustephanos var. glandulosus

Rubus eustephanos var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive glandular structures on its stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like China and India. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are usually serrated with a hairy texture, and the stems are often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
