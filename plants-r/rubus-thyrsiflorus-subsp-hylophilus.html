Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp. hylophilus

Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp.

hylophilus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and dense, thorny foliage.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to deep red as they mature. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
