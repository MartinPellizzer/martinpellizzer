Rhipsalis gaertneri

Rhipsalis gaertneri, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, trailing stems that often form a bushy shape. The plant produces colorful, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the holiday season. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

Rhipsalis gaertneri is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
