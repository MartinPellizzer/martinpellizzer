Rothmannia mayumbensis var. glabra

Rothmannia mayumbensis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth Rothmannia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers bloom during the wet season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
