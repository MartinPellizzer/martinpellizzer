Rosa biflora

Rosa biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. Rosa biflora produces two flowers per stem, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a subtle, sweet fragrance.
