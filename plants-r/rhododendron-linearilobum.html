Rhododendron linearilobum

Rhododendron linearilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like China, Vietnam, and Myanmar.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, humid climates at high altitudes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or reddish-pink, appearing in clusters and blooming in early spring.

Rhododendron linearilobum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it has specific soil and climate requirements.
