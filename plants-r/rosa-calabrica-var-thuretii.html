Rosa calabrica var. thuretii

Rosa calabrica var. thuretii, commonly known as the Thuret's rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage, with small, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in early summer, which are typically borne on long pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticultural collections.
