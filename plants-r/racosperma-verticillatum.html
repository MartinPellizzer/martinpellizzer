Racosperma verticillatum

Racosperma verticillatum, commonly known as the mountain wattle, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its wiry, often spindly stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has feathery, trifoliate leaves that are usually covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Racosperma verticillatum is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
