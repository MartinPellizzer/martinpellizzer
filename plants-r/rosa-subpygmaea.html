Rosa subpygmaea

Rosa subpygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a slight blush of red. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color. It is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
