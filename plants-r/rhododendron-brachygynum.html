Rhododendron brachygynum

Rhododendron brachygynum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, preferring acidic, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include small, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Rhododendron brachygynum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
