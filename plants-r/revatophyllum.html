Revatophyllum

Revatophyllum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple ray florets and a central disk of yellow flowers. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in coastal and saline environments. Revatophyllum has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
