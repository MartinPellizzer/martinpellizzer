Rubus vestitoides

Rubus vestitoides, commonly known as the velvet raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
