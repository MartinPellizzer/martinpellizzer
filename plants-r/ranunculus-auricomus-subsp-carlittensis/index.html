Ranunculus Auricomus Subsp. Carlittensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp. carlittensis is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions in the western united states. it belongs to the buttercup family and is known for its bright yellow flowers that resemble small suns. this subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and subtle differences in flower structure compared to its close relatives. it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and stream sides. due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
