Rhododendron barbatum f. imberbe

Rhododendron barbatum f. imberbe, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are smooth and glossy, distinguishing it from other varieties in the species. The plant produces dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting floral display.
