Rubus wrightii

Rubus wrightii, commonly known as Wright's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The botanical features of Rubus wrightii make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native habitat.
