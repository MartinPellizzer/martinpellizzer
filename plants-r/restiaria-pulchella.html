Restiaria pulchella

Restiaria pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful restiaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Restiariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
