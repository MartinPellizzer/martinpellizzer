Rosa graveolens var. klukii

Rosa graveolens var. klukii, commonly known as Kluk's sweetbriar, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its persistent, red-orange hips that appear in autumn.
