Rosa × dumetorum subvar. globata

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. globata, commonly known as the globular rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit.

It features small, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and covered in a fine layer of downy hair, giving them a soft texture. This variety is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
