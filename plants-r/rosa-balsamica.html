Rosa balsamica

Rosa balsamica, commonly known as the balsam rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa balsamica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
