Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. roseolus

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

roseolus, commonly known as the purple-fruited raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the first-year stems.

The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically deep purple to black in color, with a distinctive aroma. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and edible fruits.
