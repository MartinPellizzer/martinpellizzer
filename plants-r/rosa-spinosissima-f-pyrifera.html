Rosa spinosissima f. pyrifera

Rosa spinosissima f. pyrifera, commonly known as the Pyrifera form of the Spiny Rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features.

It is a hybrid form within the Rosa genus, exhibiting traits that combine the spiny characteristics of its parent species with unique leaf and flower morphology. The leaves are typically deeply lobed, providing a textured appearance, while the flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a subtle fragrance. This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, offering insights into the genetic diversity within rose species.
