Rungia angustifolia

Rungia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rungia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Rungia angustifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
