Rubus × hylophiloides

Rubus × hylophiloides, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or dewberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (the red raspberry). The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in jams and preserves.
