Rubus × adirondackensis

Rubus × adirondackensis, commonly known as the Adirondack raspberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local horticulture.
