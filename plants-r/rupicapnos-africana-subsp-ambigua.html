Rupicapnos africana subsp. ambigua

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the African mountain heath, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in cooler, shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
