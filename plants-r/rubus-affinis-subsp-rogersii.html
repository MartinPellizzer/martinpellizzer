Rubus affinis subsp. rogersii

Rubus affinis subsp.

rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or riparian habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are dark purple to black when mature and are often used in local culinary traditions.
