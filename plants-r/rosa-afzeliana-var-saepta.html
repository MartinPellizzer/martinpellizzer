Rosa afzeliana var. saepta

Rosa afzeliana var. saepta, commonly known as the African rose or the Cape rose, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is drought-tolerant.

Rosa afzeliana var. saepta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
