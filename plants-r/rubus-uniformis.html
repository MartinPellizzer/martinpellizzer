Rubus uniformis

Rubus uniformis, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in the fall. The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

The berries are edible and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor, making them popular for culinary use.
