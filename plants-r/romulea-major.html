Romulea major

Romulea major, commonly known as the giant romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and striking, showy flowers. The plant produces large, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive hooded structure, typically blooming in spring. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive dry periods.

Romulea major is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
