Rubus apiculatus var. cinerosus

Rubus apiculatus var. cinerosus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its edible berries and its role in local ecosystems.
