Rodgersia aesculifolia var. henrici

Rodgersia aesculifolia var. henrici, commonly known as the Henrici's rodgersia, is a deciduous perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features large, palmately compound leaves that are often described as resembling the leaves of the horse chestnut tree. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, branched inflorescences. Its foliage is attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, moist soil, preferring cooler climates.
