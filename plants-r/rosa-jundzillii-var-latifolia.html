Rosa jundzillii var. latifolia

Rosa jundzillii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a velvety texture, and the plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

This variety is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its resilience and beauty.
