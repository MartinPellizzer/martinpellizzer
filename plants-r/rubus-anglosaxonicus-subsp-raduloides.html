Rubus anglosaxonicus subsp. raduloides

Rubus anglosaxonicus subsp.

raduloides, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as raspberries, which are typically red but can also be yellow or golden in color. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary applications.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to a variety of soil types and its role in both wild and cultivated ecosystems.
