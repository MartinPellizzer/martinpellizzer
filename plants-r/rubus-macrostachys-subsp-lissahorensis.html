Rubus macrostachys subsp. lissahorensis

Rubus macrostachys subsp.

lissahorensis, commonly known as the Hainan blackberry, is a shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white or pale pink.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
