Rubus macrophyllus subsp. quadicus

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

quadicus, commonly known as the broad-leaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia. It features compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small, red drupes that are typically eaten by birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
