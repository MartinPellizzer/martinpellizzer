Rumex crispus f. microcarpus

Rumex crispus f. microcarpus, commonly known as the curly dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive crinkled, wavy leaves.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, roadsides, and disturbed areas. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is known for its medicinal uses and has been used historically for treating skin conditions and as a source of edible leaves.
