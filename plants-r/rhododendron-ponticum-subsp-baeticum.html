Rhododendron ponticum subsp. baeticum

Rhododendron ponticum subsp.

baeticum, commonly known as the Iberian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in woodland or scrubland habitats. It is a member of the Ericaceae family and can reach heights of up to 5 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
