Rhynchosia ovata

Rhynchosia ovata, commonly known as the cowpea vine or velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are often tinged with red. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain flat, edible seeds used in various cuisines. Rhynchosia ovata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in crop rotation.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of bioactive compounds.
