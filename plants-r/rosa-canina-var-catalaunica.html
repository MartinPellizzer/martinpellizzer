Rosa canina var. catalaunica

Rosa canina var. catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalan rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fragrant pink to red flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. The plant produces oval, reddish fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and scrublands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
