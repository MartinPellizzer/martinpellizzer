Rosa coriifolia subsp. subcollina

Rosa coriifolia subsp.

subcollina, commonly known as the Japanese shrub rose, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan. It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has trifoliate leaves with finely serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, contributing to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
