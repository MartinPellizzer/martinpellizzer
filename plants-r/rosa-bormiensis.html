Rosa bormiensis

Rosa bormiensis, commonly known as the Bormiyan rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tian Shan and Pamir Mountains. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer.

Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa bormiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
