Rosa pseudoaffinis

Rosa pseudoaffinis, commonly known as the Mexican rose, is a species of shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland and scrub habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, oval fruits called hips.

Rosa pseudoaffinis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
