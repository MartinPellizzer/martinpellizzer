Rhamnus saxatilis subsp. prunifolia

Rhamnus saxatilis subsp.

prunifolia, commonly known as the rock buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas.

The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are clustered and yellowish-green, appearing in spring, followed by small, fleshy berries in autumn.

The subspecies is characterized by its slightly more spreading growth habit compared to the typical form of Rhamnus saxatilis.
