Rhododendron megaphyllum

Rhododendron megaphyllum, commonly known as the giant rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
