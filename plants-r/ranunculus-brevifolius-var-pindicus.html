Ranunculus brevifolius var. pindicus

Ranunculus brevifolius var. pindicus, commonly known as the Pindus buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals, which are typically not true petals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
