Rumex pallidus

Rumex pallidus, commonly known as pale sorrel, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in loose clusters atop slender stalks.

Rumex pallidus is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its mild astringent properties.
