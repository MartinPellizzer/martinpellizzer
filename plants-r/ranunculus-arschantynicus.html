Ranunculus arschantynicus

Ranunculus arschantynicus, commonly known as the Schmuck ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and palmately arranged, while the flowers feature numerous petals and a prominent central stamen.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
