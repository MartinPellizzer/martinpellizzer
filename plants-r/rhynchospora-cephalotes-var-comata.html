Rhynchospora cephalotes var. comata

Rhynchospora cephalotes var. comata, commonly known as the hairy-headed spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence that bears small, three-lobed flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils.

This variety is distinguished by its hairy or pubescent appearance, particularly on the inflorescence and leaf surfaces.
