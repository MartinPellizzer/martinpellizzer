Rhaphidophora kokodensis

Rhaphidophora kokodensis, commonly known as the Bismarck mountain arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the lowland rainforests of Papua New Guinea.

The plant features broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color.

Rhaphidophora kokodensis is valued for its starchy tubers, which are used in traditional cooking and have potential for cultivation in humid climates.
