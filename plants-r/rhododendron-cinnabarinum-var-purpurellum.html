Rhododendron cinnabarinum var. purpurellum

Rhododendron cinnabarinum var. purpurellum, commonly known as the purple flame rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that ranges from deep green to purple hues. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
