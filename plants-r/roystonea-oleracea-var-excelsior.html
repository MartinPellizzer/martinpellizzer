Roystonea oleracea var. excelsior

Roystonea oleracea var. excelsior, commonly known as the royal palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that develop into clustered fruits, each containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in warm regions.
