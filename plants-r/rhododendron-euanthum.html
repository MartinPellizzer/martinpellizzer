Rhododendron euanthum

Rhododendron euanthum, commonly known as the early bloom rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant flower colors, which range from pink to red.

Rhododendron euanthum is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to add color to shaded landscapes.
