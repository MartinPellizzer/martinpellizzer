Rubus doftanensis

Rubus doftanensis, commonly known as the Doftan blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Romania, specifically the Carpathian Mountains. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique genetic makeup and potential for cultivation.
