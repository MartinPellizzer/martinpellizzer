Rhododendron latifolium

Rhododendron latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron latifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
