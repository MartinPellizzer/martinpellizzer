Romulea lepida

Romulea lepida, commonly known as the lesser romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact, grass-like foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Romulea lepida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
