Rosa villosa f. platyacantha

Rosa villosa f. platyacantha, commonly known as the flat-stemmed dog rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa villosa species.

It is characterized by its broad, flat stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in a shrub form, reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces edible hips that are rich in vitamin C. This variety is well-suited for gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
