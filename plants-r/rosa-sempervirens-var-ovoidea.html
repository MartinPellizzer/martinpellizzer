Rosa sempervirens var. ovoidea

Rosa sempervirens var. ovoidea, commonly known as the evergreen rose or the Spanish rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains evergreen throughout the year. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.

It is often used in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
