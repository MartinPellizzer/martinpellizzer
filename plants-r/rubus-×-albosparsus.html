Rubus × albosparsus

Rubus × albosparsus, commonly known as the white-berried blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a type of aggregate berry, is typically white or pale in color, distinguishing it from other blackberry varieties.

This species is often cultivated for its unique appearance and mild flavor.
