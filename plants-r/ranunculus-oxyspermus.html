Ranunculus oxyspermus

Ranunculus oxyspermus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant has finely divided, pinnately arranged leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is known for its toxic properties due to the presence of protoanemonin in its tissues.
