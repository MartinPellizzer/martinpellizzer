Rosa hemsleyana

Rosa hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals. The plant produces hips, which are the fruit containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

Rosa hemsleyana is adapted to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
