Ranunculus ternatus var. ternatus

Ranunculus ternatus var. ternatus, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 8 petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a creeping rhizome and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common indicator plant for wetland habitats.
