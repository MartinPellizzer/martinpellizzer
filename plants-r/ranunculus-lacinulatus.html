Ranunculus lacinulatus

Ranunculus lacinulatus, commonly known as the laceleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, compound leaves that resemble lace, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
