Rubus × robusticaulis

Rubus × robusticaulis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, resembling blackberries but with a more compact structure. It is native to regions of eastern Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
