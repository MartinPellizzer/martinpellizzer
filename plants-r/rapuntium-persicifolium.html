Rapuntium persicifolium

Rapuntium persicifolium, commonly known as the peachleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and the stems are often hairy as well. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
