Rubus gratiosus var. normandicus

Rubus gratiosus var. normandicus, commonly known as the Normandy raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
