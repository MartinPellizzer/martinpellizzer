Rubus festii

Rubus festii, commonly known as the Alpine blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
