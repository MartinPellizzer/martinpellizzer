Rubus serpens proles horridulus

Rubus serpens proles horridulus, commonly known as the rough bramble.

This plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. It produces edible berries that are typically sour but can be used in jams and preserves. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and woodland edges.

It is a hardy species that thrives in temperate climates.
