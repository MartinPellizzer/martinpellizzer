Rhus teniana

Rhus teniana, commonly known as staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used to make baskets and other crafts. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, fuzzy berries that attract birds and other wildlife. Rhus teniana thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

It is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
