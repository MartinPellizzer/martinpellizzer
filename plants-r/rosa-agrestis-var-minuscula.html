Rosa agrestis var. minuscula

Rosa agrestis var. minuscula, commonly known as the dwarf field rose, is a compact shrub native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters, featuring slender stems covered in soft, pale greenish-white bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the plant is generally hardy and adaptable to various soil types.

It is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
