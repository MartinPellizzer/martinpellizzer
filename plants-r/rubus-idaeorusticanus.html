Rubus idaeorusticanus

Rubus idaeorusticanus, commonly known as the rusty blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, fruit that ripens in late summer. The plant has prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
