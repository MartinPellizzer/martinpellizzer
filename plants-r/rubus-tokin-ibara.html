Rubus tokin-ibara

Rubus tokin-ibara, commonly known as the Tokin-ibara raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible fruits. The plant typically grows in forested areas and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruits are small, red berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its potential culinary uses.
