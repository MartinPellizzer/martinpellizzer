Rhodiola tieghemii

Rhodiola tieghemii, commonly known as the Tibetan rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Asia, particularly in Tibet, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to cold climates, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its resilience to harsh conditions makes it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
