Rubus rigidus var. buchananii

Rubus rigidus var. buchananii, commonly known as Buchanan's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically clustered together. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and shrubs.
