Roegneria gmelinii

Roegneria gmelinii, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the colder regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Roegneria gmelinii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
