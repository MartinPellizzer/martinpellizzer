Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp. filicaulis

Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp.

filicaulis, commonly known as the hairy leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
