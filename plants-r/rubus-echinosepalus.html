Rubus echinosepalus

Rubus echinosepalus, commonly known as the spiny-sepal raspberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible red berries that are similar to those of other raspberry species. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
