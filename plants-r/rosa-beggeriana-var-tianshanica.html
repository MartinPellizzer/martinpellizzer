Rosa beggeriana var. tianshanica

Rosa beggeriana var. tianshanica, commonly known as the Tianshan rock rose, is a shrub native to the Tianshan mountain range in China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves with a glossy surface, adapted to the arid and rocky environments it inhabits. It is often found growing in rocky crevices and along slopes, demonstrating a high degree of resilience to harsh conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
