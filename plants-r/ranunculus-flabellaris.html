Ranunculus flabellaris

Ranunculus flabellaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
