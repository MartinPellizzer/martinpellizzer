Rhododendron sanguineum var. sanguineum

Rhododendron sanguineum var. sanguineum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

It is a popular ornamental plant in gardens and natural landscapes due to its beauty and adaptability.
