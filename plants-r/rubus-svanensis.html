Rubus svanensis

Rubus svanensis, commonly known as the Svanen mountain bramble, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
