Rosa viscaria var. expansa

Rosa viscaria var. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets that are often hairy underneath. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and attractive foliage.
