Romulea hirta

Romulea hirta, commonly known as the hairy romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and striking, showy flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange, with a distinctive spurred petal at the back. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Romulea hirta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
