Rubus pallidus var. dissidens

Rubus pallidus var. dissidens, commonly known as the pale bramble or slender blackberry.

This variant is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its slender stems and small, pale flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a distinctive pubescent texture. The fruit, a small, dark berry, is edible but often less flavorful compared to other Rubus species.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
