Rhododendron breynii

Rhododendron breynii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron breynii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
