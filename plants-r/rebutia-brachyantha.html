Rebutia brachyantha

Rebutia brachyantha, commonly known as the rock cactus or desert rose, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale green spines. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Rebutia brachyantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive floral display.
