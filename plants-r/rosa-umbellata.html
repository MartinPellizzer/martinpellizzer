Rosa umbellata

Rosa umbellata, commonly known as the umbellate rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces compound leaves with multiple leaflets and has thorny stems typical of many rose species. Rosa umbellata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
