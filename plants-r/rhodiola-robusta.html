Rhodiola robusta

Rhodiola robusta, commonly known as the golden root or king's crown, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, yellowish roots and small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to low-light conditions. Rhodiola robusta belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is known for its medicinal properties, including potential adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.
