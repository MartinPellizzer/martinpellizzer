Ribes triste f. rubellum

Ribes triste f. rubellum, commonly known as the red-stemmed gooseberry, is a compact shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a form of the species Ribes triste, characterized by its distinctive reddish stems and vibrant orange to red berries. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and produces small, edible fruits that are popular among birds and humans alike. Its foliage consists of alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
