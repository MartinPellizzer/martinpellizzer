Rosa gallica var. liostyla

Rosa gallica var. liostyla, commonly known as the French pink, is a hybrid rose species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of semi-double, pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, thornless stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
