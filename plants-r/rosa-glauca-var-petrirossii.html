Rosa glauca var. petrirossii

Rosa glauca var. petrirossii, commonly known as the Petrirossi rose, is a variant of the blue rose that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many rose species. The flowers are usually pale blue or white, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cooler climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
