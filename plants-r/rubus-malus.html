Rubus malus

Rubus malus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rubus malus plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, providing food for birds and insects.
