Rubus × pilosispinus

Rubus × pilosispinus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
