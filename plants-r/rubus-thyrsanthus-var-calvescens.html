Rubus thyrsanthus var. calvescens

Rubus thyrsanthus var. calvescens, commonly known as the New Zealand blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in coastal and forested areas. It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also cultivated for its edible berries.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
