Rosa corymbifera var. setosopedicellata

Rosa corymbifera var. setosopedicellata, commonly known as the hairy pedicellate shrub rose, is a variant of the shrub rose native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
