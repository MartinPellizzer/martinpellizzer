Rubus rivularis subsp. divexiramus

Rubus rivularis subsp.

divexiramus, commonly known as the river raspberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a red or orange color.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges. It has a woody stem with thorny branches, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique branching pattern and habitat preferences.
