Rubus × loppiensis

Rubus × loppiensis, commonly known as the Loppiensis blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, typically dark purple to black in color, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and small-scale agriculture.
