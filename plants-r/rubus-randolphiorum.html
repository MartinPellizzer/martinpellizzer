Rubus randolphiorum

Rubus randolphiorum, commonly known as Randolph's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
