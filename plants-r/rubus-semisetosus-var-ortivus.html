Rubus semisetosus var. ortivus

Rubus semisetosus var. ortivus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
