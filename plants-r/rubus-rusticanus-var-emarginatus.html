Rubus rusticanus var. emarginatus

Rubus rusticanus var. emarginatus, commonly known as the emarginate blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf margins, which are notched or emarginate, giving it its scientific name.
