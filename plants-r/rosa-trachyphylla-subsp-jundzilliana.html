Rosa trachyphylla subsp. jundzilliana

Rosa trachyphylla subsp.

jundzilliana, commonly known as the Jundzilliana rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a fine, velvety texture. This subspecies is adapted to cool, moist environments and thrives in partial to full shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
