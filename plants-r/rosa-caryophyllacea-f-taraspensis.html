Rosa caryophyllacea f. taraspensis

Rosa caryophyllacea f. taraspensis, commonly known as the Tarasp rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa caryophyllacea species.

It is native to the alpine regions of Switzerland and Italy, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to white. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, typical of many rose species, and it produces hips that are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in harsh mountainous environments.
