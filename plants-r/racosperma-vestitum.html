Racosperma vestitum

Racosperma vestitum, commonly known as the hairy acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem covered in a dense layer of hairs, giving it its common name. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Racosperma vestitum is often found in open woodlands and is used in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australians.
