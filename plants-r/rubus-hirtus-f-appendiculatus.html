Rubus hirtus f. appendiculatus

Rubus hirtus f. appendiculatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry or trailing blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

This form is notable for its trailing growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
