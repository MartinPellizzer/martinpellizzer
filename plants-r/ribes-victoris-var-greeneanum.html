Ribes victoris var. greeneanum

Ribes victoris var. greeneanum, commonly known as the Greene's currant, is a variant of the common currant native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, edible berries. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and edible landscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
