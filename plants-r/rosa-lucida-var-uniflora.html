Rosa lucida var. uniflora

Rosa lucida var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower rose, is a cultivar characterized by its solitary bloom per stem.

It features fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, often with a white center. The plant has dark green, glossy foliage and thorny stems typical of the Rosa genus. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively compact growth habit.
