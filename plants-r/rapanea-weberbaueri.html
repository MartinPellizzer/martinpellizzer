Rapanea weberbaueri

Rapanea weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by blue-black berries that are edible for certain bird species.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
