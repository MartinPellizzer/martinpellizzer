Rosa perparva

Rosa perparva, commonly known as the dwarf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant features narrow, upright stems covered in dense, soft grayish-white hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a fragrant scent, blooming in early summer.

Rosa perparva is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact size and delicate appearance.
