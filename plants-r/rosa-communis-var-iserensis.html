Rosa communis var. iserensis

Rosa communis var. iserensis, commonly known as the Iseren rose, is a rare and ancient variety of rose native to the Iser River valley in Germany.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and is known for its hardy nature, thriving in temperate climates. Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of heirloom roses.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical connections to medieval horticulture.
