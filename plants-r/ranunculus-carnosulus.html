Ranunculus carnosulus

Ranunculus carnosulus, commonly known as the wooly buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ranunculus carnosulus is a perennial species that is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
