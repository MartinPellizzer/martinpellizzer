Rosa pomifera f. proxima

Rosa pomifera f. proxima, commonly known as the near relative of the rose apple, is a botanical variant of the rose apple tree.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fuzzy, edible fruit that resembles a small apple. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and serrated, while its flowers are typically pink or white.

The fruit develops from a modified flower cluster and is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp.
