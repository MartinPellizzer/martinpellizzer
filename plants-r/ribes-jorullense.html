Ribes jorullense

Ribes jorullense, commonly known as the Jorullense gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its small, edible berries that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.
