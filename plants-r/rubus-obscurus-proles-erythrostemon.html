Rubus obscurus proles erythrostemon

Rubus obscurus proles erythrostemon, commonly known as the redstem blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are dark green and have a slightly serrated edge, while the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are often used in jams and wines due to their tart flavor.
