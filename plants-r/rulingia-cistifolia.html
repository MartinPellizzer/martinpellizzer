Rulingia cistifolia

Rulingia cistifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
