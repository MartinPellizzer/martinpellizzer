Rosa caryophyllacea var. erasmi-iulii

Rosa caryophyllacea var. erasmi-iulii, commonly known as the Erasmi-Iulii rose, is a variant of the shrub rose native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves and thorny stems typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal flowering.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
