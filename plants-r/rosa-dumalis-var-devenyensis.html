Rosa dumalis var. devenyensis

Rosa dumalis var. devenyensis, commonly known as the Deveny's wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces rose hips, which are small, red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
