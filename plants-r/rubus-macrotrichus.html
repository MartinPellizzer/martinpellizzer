Rubus macrotrichus

Rubus macrotrichus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple tiny drupes.

Rubus macrotrichus is native to parts of Asia and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
