Rosa agrestis f. virgultorum

Rosa agrestis f. virgultorum, commonly known as the wild rose bush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many rose species. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, aiding in its propagation.

This plant is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
