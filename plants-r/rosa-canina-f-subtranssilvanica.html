Rosa canina f. subtranssilvanica

Rosa canina f. subtranssilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves, typical of the Rosa genus. Its hips, which are the fruit, are orange-red and contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
