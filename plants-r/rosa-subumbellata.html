Rosa subumbellata

Rosa subumbellata, commonly known as the subumbellate rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern and central regions of North America, where it thrives in woodland and moist habitats. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters. Rosa subumbellata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the rose genus.
