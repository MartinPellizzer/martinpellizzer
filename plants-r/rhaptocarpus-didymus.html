Rhaptocarpus didymus

Rhaptocarpus didymus, commonly known as the twin-seed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, terrestrial growth habit and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers. Its unique feature is the presence of two seeds per capsule, which is rare among orchids.

Rhaptocarpus didymus is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
