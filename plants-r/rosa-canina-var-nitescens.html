Rosa canina var. nitescens

Rosa canina var. nitescens, commonly known as the silver-haired dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves and pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves.

Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
