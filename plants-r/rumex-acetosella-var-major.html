Rumex acetosella var. major

Rumex acetosella var. major, commonly known as greater sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and bright red stems, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Greater sorrel is also known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
