Rubus australis monstr. squarrosus

Rubus australis monstr.

squarrosus, commonly known as the Australian bramble, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruit, a cluster of small berries, is edible but often tart.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
