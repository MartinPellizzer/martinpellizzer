Rubus schmidelyanus var. discerptiformis

Rubus schmidelyanus var. discerptiformis, commonly known as the Schmidel's blackberry variant, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
