Rubus similatus f. umbrosus

Rubus similatus f. umbrosus, commonly known as the shady raspberry, is a shrub characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and thorny stems.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically a deep red or black color. This variety thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodland areas. The leaves are usually compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant has a strong, aromatic scent.

It is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in local cuisine.
