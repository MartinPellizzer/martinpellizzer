Rosa canina var. rectispina

Rosa canina var. rectispina, commonly known as the straight-spined dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant pink flowers. The leaves are pinnate with serrated edges, and the plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
