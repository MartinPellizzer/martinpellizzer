Rubus koehleri var. cavatifolius

Rubus koehleri var. cavatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive narrow, elongated shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
