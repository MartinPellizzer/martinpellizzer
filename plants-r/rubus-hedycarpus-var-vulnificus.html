Rubus hedycarpus var. vulnificus

Rubus hedycarpus var. vulnificus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces edible berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. Its fruiting stems, called floribunda, bear the characteristic red, hairy berries.

The plant is often cultivated for both its culinary and ornamental value.
