Ranunculus flammula var. latifolius

Ranunculus flammula var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive white or yellowish vein pattern, and the plant produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties due to the presence of protoanemonin.
