Rubus vulgaris var. occitanicus

Rubus vulgaris var. occitanicus, commonly known as the Occitanian blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant is native to southern Europe, particularly the Occitan region of France. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets.
