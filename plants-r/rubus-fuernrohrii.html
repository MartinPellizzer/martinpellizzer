Rubus fuernrohrii

Rubus fuernrohrii, commonly known as the Fuernrohrii blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Rosaceae. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and lesser-known member of the Rubus family.
