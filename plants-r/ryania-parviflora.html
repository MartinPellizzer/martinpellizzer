Ryania parviflora

Ryania parviflora, commonly known as the pink ryania, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces oval, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ryania parviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
