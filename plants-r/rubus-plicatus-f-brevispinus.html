Rubus plicatus f. brevispinus

Rubus plicatus f. brevispinus, commonly known as the short-spined blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its flowers are white and occur in clusters, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its fruit yield.
