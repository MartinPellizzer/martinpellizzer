Rhododendron peramabile

Rhododendron peramabile, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including China, India, and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, adapted to withstand cold and harsh conditions.

Rhododendron peramabile plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
