Racosperma triptychum

Racosperma triptychum, commonly known as the three-lobed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by flattened, pod-like fruits. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Racosperma triptychum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
