Rhipidoglossum brachyceras

Rhipidoglossum brachyceras, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are often hairy, and its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are typically green or pale in color.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
