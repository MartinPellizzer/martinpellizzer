Rumicastrum tholiforme

Rumicastrum tholiforme, commonly known as the tholiforme burrobrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds. Its fruit is a dry capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature, aiding in its dispersal.

The species is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
