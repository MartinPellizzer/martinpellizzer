Rubus myriadenophorus

Rubus myriadenophorus, commonly known as the many-stemmed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and clusters of small, dark berries. Its leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
