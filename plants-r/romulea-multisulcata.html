Romulea multisulcata

Romulea multisulcata, commonly known as the mountain glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to white, star-shaped blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Romulea multisulcata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, ephemeral beauty.
