Rubus hirsutus f. harae

Rubus hirsutus f. harae, commonly known as the Harae raspberry, is a variant of the brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the specific cultivar. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and edible berries.
