Rubus corchorifolius

Rubus corchorifolius, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
