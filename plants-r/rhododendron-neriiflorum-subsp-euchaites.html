Rhododendron neriiflorum subsp. euchaites

Rhododendron neriiflorum subsp.

euchaites, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

This subspecies is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
