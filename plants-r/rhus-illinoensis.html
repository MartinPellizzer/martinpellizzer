Rhus illinoensis

Rhus illinoensis, commonly known as the Illinois blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 3 to 5 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark red or black color, often containing a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks.

The Illinois blackberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
