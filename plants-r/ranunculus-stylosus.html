Ranunculus stylosus

Ranunculus stylosus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, leafy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is often found in the fynbos biome. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three parts, and the flowers bloom in spring.

Ranunculus stylosus is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
