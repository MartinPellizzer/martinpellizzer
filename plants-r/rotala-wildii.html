Rotala wildii

Rotala wildii, commonly known as the wild rotala, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often bright green and slightly translucent. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Rotala wildii is often cultivated in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
