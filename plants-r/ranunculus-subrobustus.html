Ranunculus subrobustus

Ranunculus subrobustus, commonly known as the robust buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with numerous petals, typically arranged in a cup-like formation. The plant has a fibrous root system and hairy stems that are often reddish in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Ranunculus subrobustus prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
