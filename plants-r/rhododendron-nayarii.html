Rhododendron nayarii

Rhododendron nayarii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at higher elevations. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron nayarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
