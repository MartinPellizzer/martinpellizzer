Rosettea princeps

Rosettea princeps, commonly known as the royal rosette, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup. The plant's leaves are typically a vibrant green, often with a waxy texture and a subtle blue or silver hue. It produces striking red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

This species is native to South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
