Rubus incisus var. euinsisus

Rubus incisus var. euinsisus, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The berries are edible and used in culinary applications, though they are generally less sweet than other blackberry varieties.
