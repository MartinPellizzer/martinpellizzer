Rhododendron mariae

Rhododendron mariae, commonly known as the Marie rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leathery leaves, which help it adapt to its natural habitat.

Rhododendron mariae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
