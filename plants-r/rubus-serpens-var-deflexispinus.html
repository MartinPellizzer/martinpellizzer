Rubus serpens var. deflexispinus

Rubus serpens var. deflexispinus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
