Rubus × pillitzii

Rubus × pillitzii, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are clusters of small, drupe-like berries. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Rubus × pillitzii is native to North America and is often found in disturbed habitats such as fields and woodland edges.
