Rubus validispinus

Rubus validispinus, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

The botanical features of Rubus validispinus make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
