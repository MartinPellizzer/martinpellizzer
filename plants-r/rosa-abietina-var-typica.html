Rosa abietina var. typica

Rosa abietina var. typica, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and a serrated margin. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a fragrant scent.

Rosa abietina var. typica is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
