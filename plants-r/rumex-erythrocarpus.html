Rumex erythrocarpus

Rumex erythrocarpus, commonly known as the red-fruited sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other sorrel species known for their sour-tasting leaves. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, alternate leaves with a reddish tint, and small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is distinctive, being a red, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Rumex erythrocarpus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
