Robinia amara

Robinia amara, commonly known as sourwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a serrated edge. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in panicles during the spring. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several small, hard seeds.

Robinia amara is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
