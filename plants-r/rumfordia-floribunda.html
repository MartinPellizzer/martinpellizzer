Rumfordia floribunda

Rumfordia floribunda, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
