Rollinia insignis

Rollinia insignis, commonly known as the golden star apple, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into spherical, yellow or orange fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. Rollinia insignis is valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
