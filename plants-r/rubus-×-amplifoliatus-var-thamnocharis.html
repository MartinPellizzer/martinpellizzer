Rubus × amplifoliatus var. thamnocharis

Rubus × amplifoliatus var. thamnocharis, commonly known as the thamnocharis blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible, though often tart and high in acidity. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth habit.

The botanical features of this variety make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
