Rhynchosia reticulata f. subumbellata

Rhynchosia reticulata f. subumbellata, commonly known as the subumbellate leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive reticulate pattern, and its flowers are typically small and clustered in umbellate inflorescences. It is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The subspecies subumbellata is distinguished by its unique floral arrangement and leaf morphology.
