Rosa sabina

Rosa sabina, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces red or orange hips that persist through winter. Rosa sabina is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
