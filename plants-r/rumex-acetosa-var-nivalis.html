Rumex acetosa var. nivalis

Rumex acetosa var. nivalis, commonly known as mountain sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and trifoliate leaves with a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy species often found in rocky or mountainous landscapes.
