Rosa menstrua

Rosa menstrua, commonly known as the common rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. Rosa menstrua is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

Its flowers are also used in perfumery and culinary applications.
