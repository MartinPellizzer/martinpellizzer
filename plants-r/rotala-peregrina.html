Rotala peregrina

Rotala peregrina, commonly known as the wandering waterwheel, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, bright green leaves that often float on the water surface. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that are typically borne on long, slender stalks. Rotala peregrina thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is often used in aquascaping due to its attractive appearance and ability to provide oxygen to aquatic ecosystems.
