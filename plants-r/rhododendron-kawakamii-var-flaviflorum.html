Rhododendron kawakamii var. flaviflorum

Rhododendron kawakamii var. flaviflorum, commonly known as the Kawakami's rhododendron variant with yellow flowers, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, thriving in acidic, well-drained soils. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
