Rubus borreri var. mazaktelensis

Rubus borreri var. mazaktelensis, commonly known as the Mazatlan blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a firm, tart flavor. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus family, valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
