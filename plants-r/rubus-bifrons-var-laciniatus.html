Rubus bifrons var. laciniatus

Rubus bifrons var. laciniatus, commonly known as the cutleaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are deeply lobed and often have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple to black berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local ecosystems as a food source for wildlife.
