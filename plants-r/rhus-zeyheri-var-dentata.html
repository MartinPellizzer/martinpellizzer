Rhus zeyheri var. dentata

Rhus zeyheri var. dentata, commonly known as the African sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red, fuzzy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
