Rosa tomentosa f. conjungens

Rosa tomentosa f. conjungens, commonly known as the hairy rose or woolly rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, thorny stems covered in fine, white tomentum, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, ovate, and covered in a soft, woolly layer of hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
