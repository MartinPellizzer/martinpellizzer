Rosa nigrans subf. infrondens

Rosa nigrans subf.

infrondens, commonly known as the black rose bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable candidate for wetland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant landscapes.
