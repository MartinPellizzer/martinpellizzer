Ranunculus labordei

Ranunculus labordei, commonly known as the Laborde's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are typically palmate, with three to five lobes. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or arid environments. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Ranunculus labordei is typically found in desert or semi-desert regions, where it thrives in dry, sunny conditions.
