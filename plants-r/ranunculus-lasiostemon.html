Ranunculus lasiostemon

Ranunculus lasiostemon, commonly known as the hairy leaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five to eight petals. The leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, hairy glands, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
