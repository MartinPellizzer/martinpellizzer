Rubus wahlbergii var. pruinosus

Rubus wahlbergii var. pruinosus, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It has thorny stems and is often found in woodland and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
