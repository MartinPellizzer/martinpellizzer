Rosa pumila var. officinalis

Rosa pumila var. officinalis, commonly known as the officinal rose, is a hardy shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and thorny stems, which are typical features of many rose species. The plant is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces rose hips that contain seeds.

Rosa pumila var. officinalis has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
