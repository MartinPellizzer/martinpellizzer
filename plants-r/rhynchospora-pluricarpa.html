Rhynchospora pluricarpa

Rhynchospora pluricarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited beakspur, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features slender, triangular stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, three-parted flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Rhynchospora pluricarpa produces numerous small, three-lobed fruits that are adapted for dispersal by wind or water.
