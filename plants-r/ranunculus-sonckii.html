Ranunculus sonckii

Ranunculus sonckii, commonly known as the Sonck's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds by wind. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
