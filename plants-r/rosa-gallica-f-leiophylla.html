Rosa gallica f. leiophylla

Rosa gallica f. leiophylla, commonly known as the pale leaf roses, is a cultivar of the Gallica rose, characterized by its soft, pale pink flowers and delicate, finely textured leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, often found in woodland and hedgerow environments. The plant produces fragrant blooms in early to mid-summer, which are typically single or semi-double. Its leaves are narrow and have a smooth, leathery texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
