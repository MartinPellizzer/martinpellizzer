Rosa canina f. submeszkoeensis

Rosa canina f. submeszkoeensis, commonly known as the Hungarian dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. This subspecies is native to the Carpathian Basin and is adapted to temperate climates. It produces edible hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
