Rubus idaeus var. acalyphaceus

Rubus idaeus var. acalyphaceus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry plant.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn red when ripe, typically in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its flavorful berries, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
