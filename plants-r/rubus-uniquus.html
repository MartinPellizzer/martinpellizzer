Rubus uniquus

Rubus uniquus, commonly known as the rare velvet raspberry, is a unique species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands, preferring acidic soils. Its flowers are white with five petals and appear in early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
