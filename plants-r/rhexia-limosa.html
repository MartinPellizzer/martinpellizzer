Rhexia limosa

Rhexia limosa, commonly known as swamp sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wetland habitats such as bogs and marshes. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Rhexia limosa is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
