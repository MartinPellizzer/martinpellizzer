Racosperma menzelii

Racosperma menzelii, commonly known as Menzel's acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Its seeds are encased in a pod and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

Racosperma menzelii is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
