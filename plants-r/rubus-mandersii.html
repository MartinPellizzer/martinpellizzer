Rubus mandersii

Rubus mandersii, commonly known as the Mander's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
