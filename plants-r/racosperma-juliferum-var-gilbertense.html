Racosperma juliferum var. gilbertense

Racosperma juliferum var. gilbertense, commonly known as Gilbert's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has feathery, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is well adapted to arid conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping in suitable climates.
