Rubus bifrons subsp. macroacanthos

Rubus bifrons subsp.

macroacanthos, commonly known as the large-thorned blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, trifoliate leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
