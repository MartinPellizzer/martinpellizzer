Ranunculus diversifolius proles triphyllus

Ranunculus diversifolius proles triphyllus, commonly known as the three-leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
