Rosa scabrata f. ovifera

Rosa scabrata f. ovifera, commonly known as the ovate-leafed rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa scabrata species.

It is characterized by its ovate-shaped leaves, which are typically larger and more rounded compared to the typical form. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to the southern United States, particularly Texas, and thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
