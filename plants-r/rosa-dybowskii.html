Rosa dybowskii

Rosa dybowskii, commonly known as the Dybowsky rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Rosa dybowskii is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticultural history.
