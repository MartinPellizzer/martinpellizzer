Rhododendron oulotrichum

Rhododendron oulotrichum, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils found in mountainous regions, particularly in California and Nevada. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring.

Rhododendron oulotrichum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
