Ravnia longifilamentosa

Ravnia longifilamentosa, commonly known as the long filament ravnia, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated filaments that form intricate, cascading structures resembling delicate ribbons. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Ravnia longifilamentosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its unusual appearance and ecological significance.
