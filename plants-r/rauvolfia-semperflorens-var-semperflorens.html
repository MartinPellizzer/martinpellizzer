Rauvolfia semperflorens var. semperflorens

Rauvolfia semperflorens var. semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to produce numerous flowers throughout the growing season. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale pink to white flowers that open in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in traditional medicine.
