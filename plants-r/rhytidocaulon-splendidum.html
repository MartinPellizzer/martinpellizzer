Rhytidocaulon splendidum

Rhytidocaulon splendidum, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is often ribbed and covered with a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and xeriscaping gardens.
