Rhododendron pseudochrysanthum f. rufovelutinum

Rhododendron pseudochrysanthum f. rufovelutinum, commonly known as the rusty velvet rhododendron, is a unique hybrid species within the Rhododendron genus.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that exhibits a distinctive rusty red hue on the underside of the leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to purple, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to specific horticultural conditions.
