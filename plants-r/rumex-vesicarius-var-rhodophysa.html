Rumex vesicarius var. rhodophysa

Rumex vesicarius var. rhodophysa, commonly known as the red-stem sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, giving them a tart flavor and making them useful in culinary applications.

The species is often found in moist, grassy areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
