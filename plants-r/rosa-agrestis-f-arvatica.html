Rosa agrestis f. arvatica

Rosa agrestis f. arvatica, commonly known as the roughstem dogwood rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa agrestis species, which is native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorny stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and adaptable to a range of soil conditions.
