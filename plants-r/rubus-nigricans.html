Rubus nigricans

Rubus nigricans, commonly known as blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

Rubus nigricans plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
