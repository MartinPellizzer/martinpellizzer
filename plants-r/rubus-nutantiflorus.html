Rubus nutantiflorus

Rubus nutantiflorus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy stems and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
