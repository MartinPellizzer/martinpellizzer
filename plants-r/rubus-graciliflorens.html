Rubus graciliflorens

Rubus graciliflorens, commonly known as the slender-flowered blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and has compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruits are aggregate berries that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
