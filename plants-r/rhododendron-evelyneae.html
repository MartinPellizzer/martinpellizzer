Rhododendron evelyneae

Rhododendron evelyneae, commonly known as Evelyne's rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its flowers range in color from pink to purple, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
