Ranunculus elegans

Ranunculus elegans, commonly known as the elegant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petal-like sepals in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ranunculus elegans is known for its early spring blooming habit and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
