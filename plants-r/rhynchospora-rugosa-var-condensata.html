Rhynchospora rugosa var. condensata

Rhynchospora rugosa var. condensata, commonly known as the denseleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow, grass-like, and have a rough, textured surface. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in prairie marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
