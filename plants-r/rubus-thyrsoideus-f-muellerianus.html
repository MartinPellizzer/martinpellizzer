Rubus thyrsoideus f. muellerianus

Rubus thyrsoideus f. muellerianus, commonly known as the Mueller's blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces small, dark berries that are typically edible. The plant's leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, persistent hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or open woodlands.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in local ecosystems.
