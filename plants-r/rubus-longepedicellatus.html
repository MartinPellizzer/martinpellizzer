Rubus longepedicellatus

Rubus longepedicellatus, commonly known as the long pedicellate blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a somewhat tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
