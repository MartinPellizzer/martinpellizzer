Rubus discolor var. thyrsoideus

Rubus discolor var. thyrsoideus, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically larger and more intensely flavored than those of other Rubus species. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often armed with prickles. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
