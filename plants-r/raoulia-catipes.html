Raoulia catipes

Raoulia catipes, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Raoulia catipes is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
