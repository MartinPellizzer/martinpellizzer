Rubus inconspicuus

Rubus inconspicuus, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into dark purple or black berries. It is native to much of North America and thrives in a variety of soil types.

Rubus inconspicuus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and small mammals.
