Rubus orbiculatus

Rubus orbiculatus, commonly known as the Japanese bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, disturbed areas, and along trails.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common and adaptable species in temperate regions of Asia and North America.
