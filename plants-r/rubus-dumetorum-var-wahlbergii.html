Rubus dumetorum var. wahlbergii

Rubus dumetorum var. wahlbergii, commonly known as the Wahlberg's blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
