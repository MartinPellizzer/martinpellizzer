Ranunculus moseleyi

Ranunculus moseleyi, commonly known as Moseley's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, greenish fruits that disperse its seeds.

Ranunculus moseleyi is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique ecosystem in the region.
