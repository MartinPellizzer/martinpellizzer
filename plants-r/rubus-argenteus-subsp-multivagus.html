Rubus argenteus subsp. multivagus

Rubus argenteus subsp.

multivagus, commonly known as the silverleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
