Ranunculus caliginosus

Ranunculus caliginosus, commonly known as the misty buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. The plant features trifoliate leaves with deeply lobed margins and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers are often adorned with a distinctive greenish or brownish central spot, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus caliginosus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
