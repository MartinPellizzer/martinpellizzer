Rubus gaubae

Rubus gaubae, commonly known as the Gaubae bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries. It produces white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
