Rubus ronensis

Rubus ronensis, commonly known as the Roni blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
