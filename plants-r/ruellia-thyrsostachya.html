Ruellia thyrsostachya

Ruellia thyrsostachya, commonly known as the red-veined ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with prominent reddish veins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Ruellia thyrsostachya is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
