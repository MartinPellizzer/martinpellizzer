Rhytidocaulon minimum

Rhytidocaulon minimum, commonly known as the dwarf star moss, is a small, low-growing epiphytic plant belonging to the family Rhytidocaulonaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and dense clusters of tiny, scale-like leaves. The plant is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as forest floors or on the trunks of trees. It has a unique ability to retain moisture, which helps it survive in its moist, often shaded habitat.

Due to its delicate structure and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
