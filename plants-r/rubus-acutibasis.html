Rubus acutibasis

Rubus acutibasis, commonly known as the sharp-based blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
