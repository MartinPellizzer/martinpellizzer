Rubus trichogynus

Rubus trichogynus, commonly known as the Spanish raspberry, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its flowers are usually pink or white and are pollinated by insects.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
