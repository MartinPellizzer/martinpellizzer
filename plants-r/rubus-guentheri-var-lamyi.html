Rubus guentheri var. lamyi

Rubus guentheri var. lamyi, commonly known as the Lamy's blackberry, is a variant of the Guenther blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
