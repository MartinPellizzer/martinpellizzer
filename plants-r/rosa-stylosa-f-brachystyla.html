Rosa stylosa f. brachystyla

Rosa stylosa f. brachystyla, commonly known as the short-stemmed form of the stylet rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stems compared to the typical Rosa stylosa.

This form exhibits dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in horticultural and taxonomic research.
