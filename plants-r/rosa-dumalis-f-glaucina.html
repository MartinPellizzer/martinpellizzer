Rosa dumalis f. glaucina

Rosa dumalis f. glaucina, commonly known as the glaucous wild rose, is a horticultural form of the species Rosa dumalis.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers and foliage covered in a waxy, bluish-white bloom, known as glaucous. The plant is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique coloration and fragrant blooms, making it a sought-after plant in gardens.
