Rosa canina f. hadriana

Rosa canina f. hadriana, commonly known as the Hadrian's rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets.

The fruit, a red hip, contains numerous seeds and is a food source for birds and other wildlife.
