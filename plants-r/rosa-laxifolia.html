Rosa laxifolia

Rosa laxifolia, commonly known as the looseleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces rose hips that are edible.

Rosa laxifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
