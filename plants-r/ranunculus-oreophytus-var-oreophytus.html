Ranunculus oreophytus var. oreophytus

Ranunculus oreophytus var. oreophytus, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its preference for wild habitats.
