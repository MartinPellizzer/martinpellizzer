Rosa inclinata

Rosa inclinata, commonly known as the leaning rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

Rosa inclinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
