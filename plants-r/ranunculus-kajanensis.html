Ranunculus kajanensis

Ranunculus kajanensis, commonly known as the Kajan buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
