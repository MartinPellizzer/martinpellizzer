Rebutia perplexa

Rebutia perplexa, commonly known as the "Perplexing Rebutia," is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and short, cylindrical stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight.

Rebutia perplexa is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
