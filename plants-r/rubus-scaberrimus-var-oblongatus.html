Rubus scaberrimus var. oblongatus

Rubus scaberrimus var. oblongatus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, usually red or yellow fruits. The leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges and a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
