Rhamnus utilis

Rhamnus utilis, commonly known as the common buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, black berries that mature in the fall. The plant has a strong, aromatic scent and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it produces yellowish flowers in early spring.

Rhamnus utilis is known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
