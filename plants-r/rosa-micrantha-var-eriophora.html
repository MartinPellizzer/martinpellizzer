Rosa micrantha var. eriophora

Rosa micrantha var. eriophora, commonly known as the alpine rose or mountain rose, is a shrub native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, densely packed leaves and fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include stipitate stipules and stipitate glands, which are distinctive in its taxonomic classification.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
