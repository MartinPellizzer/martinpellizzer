Rubus omalus var. albiflorus

Rubus omalus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The flowers are notable for their white coloration, which distinguishes this variant from other members of the species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple or black when mature.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.
