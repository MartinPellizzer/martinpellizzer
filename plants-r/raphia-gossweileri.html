Raphia gossweileri

Raphia gossweileri, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a large, fan-shaped palm native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its massive, umbrella-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant grows in dense, humid environments and requires high levels of sunlight and consistent moisture. Its trunk is thick and segmented, supporting the heavy foliage above.

Raphia gossweileri plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
