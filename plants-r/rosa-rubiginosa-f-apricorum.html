Rosa rubiginosa f. apricorum

Rosa rubiginosa f. apricorum, commonly known as the apricot-colored rose, is a botanical variant of the shrub rose characterized by its unique floral coloration.

This form exhibits pale pink to apricot-colored petals, distinguishing it from the more typical red or pink hues of the species. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

The plant produces fragrant flowers in late spring, followed by edible hips that are rich in vitamin C. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
