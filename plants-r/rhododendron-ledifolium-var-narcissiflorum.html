Rhododendron ledifolium var. narcissiflorum

Rhododendron ledifolium var. narcissiflorum, commonly known as the yellow rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
