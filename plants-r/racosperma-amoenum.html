Racosperma amoenum

Racosperma amoenum, commonly known as the small-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract various pollinators.

Racosperma amoenum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
