Rhododendron brachycarpum var. tigerstedtii

Rhododendron brachycarpum var. tigerstedtii, commonly known as the Tigerstedt's rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxiness. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
