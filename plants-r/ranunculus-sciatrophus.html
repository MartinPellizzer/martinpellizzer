Ranunculus sciatrophus

Ranunculus sciatrophus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five sepals that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive narrow, lance-shaped appearance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
