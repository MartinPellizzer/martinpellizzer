Rubus pallidus var. fusciformis

Rubus pallidus var. fusciformis, commonly known as the pale raspberry variant with a dark-shaped form, is a species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a pale underside. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and moist soils.

This variant is noted for its distinctive leaf shape and subtle coloration compared to other Rubus species.
