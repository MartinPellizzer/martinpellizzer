Rubus radula

Rubus radula, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in rocky and alpine habitats. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries. It plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable plant.
