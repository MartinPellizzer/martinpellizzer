Rubus schleicheri var. grandiflorus

Rubus schleicheri var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are aggregate berries that ripen to a dark purple color, often used in local cuisines.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible yield.
