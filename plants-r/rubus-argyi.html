Rubus argyi

Rubus argyi, commonly known as the Chinese bramble or Chinese raspberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Rubus argyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
