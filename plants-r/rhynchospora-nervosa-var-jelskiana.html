Rhynchospora nervosa var. jelskiana

Rhynchospora nervosa var. jelskiana, commonly known as the narrow-veined flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are usually inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Rhynchospora nervosa var. jelskiana plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
