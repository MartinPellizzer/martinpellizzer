Rosa silvicola

Rosa silvicola, commonly known as the forest rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in woodland and shaded areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in thorny buds, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Rosa silvicola is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
