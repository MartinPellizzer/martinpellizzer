Rhododendron temenium var. dealbatum

Rhododendron temenium var. dealbatum, commonly known as the Khasi rhododendron, is a species native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly found in the mountainous regions of Meghalaya and Assam.
