Rhamnus erythroxyloides subsp. iranica

Rhamnus erythroxyloides subsp.

iranica, commonly known as the Iranian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Iran. It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically pubescent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, fleshy berries that are red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
