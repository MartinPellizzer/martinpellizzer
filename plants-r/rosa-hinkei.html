Rosa hinkei

Rosa hinkei, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a species native to Japan and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in late spring to early summer. Rosa hinkei is known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
