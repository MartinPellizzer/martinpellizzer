Rubus pubescens f. multiplex

Rubus pubescens f. multiplex, commonly known as the multiflorous blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and multiple flowering shoots.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America, particularly the eastern United States. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its hairy stems and leaves are distinctive features that aid in identification.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
