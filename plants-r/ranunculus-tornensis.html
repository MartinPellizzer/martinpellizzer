Ranunculus tornensis

Ranunculus tornensis, commonly known as the Tornensis buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
