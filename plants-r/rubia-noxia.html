Rubia noxia

Rubia noxia, commonly known as the desert madder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Rubia noxia is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils.

Its medicinal properties have been historically utilized in traditional herbal remedies.
