Ranunculus apenninus

Ranunculus apenninus, commonly known as the Italian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a dark central spot. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus apenninus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
