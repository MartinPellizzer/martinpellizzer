Rosa mollis subf. inversifolia

Rosa mollis subf.

inversifolia, commonly known as the Downy Rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a velvety texture. The subsp.

inversifolia is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
