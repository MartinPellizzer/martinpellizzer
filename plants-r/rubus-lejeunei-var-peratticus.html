Rubus lejeunei var. peratticus

Rubus lejeunei var. peratticus, commonly known as the Peratt's bramble, is a variant of the Rubus lejeunei species, which belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, usually white or pink flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
