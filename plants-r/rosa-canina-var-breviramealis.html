Rosa canina var. breviramealis

Rosa canina var. breviramealis, commonly known as the short-stemmed dog rose, is a lesser-known variant of the common dog rose.

It is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant pink flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces red, hairy fruits known as hips. This variety is often found in woodland edges and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
