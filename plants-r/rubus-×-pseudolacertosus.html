Rubus × pseudolacertosus

Rubus × pseudolacertosus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
