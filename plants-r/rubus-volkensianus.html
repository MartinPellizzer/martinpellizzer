Rubus volkensianus

Rubus volkensianus, commonly known as the Volkens raspberry, is a species of bramble native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
