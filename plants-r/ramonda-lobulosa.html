Ramonda lobulosa

Ramonda lobulosa, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often hairy as well. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
