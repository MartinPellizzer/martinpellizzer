Rosa agrestis subsp. caryophyllacea

Rosa agrestis subsp.

caryophyllacea, commonly known as the common bristly rose, is a shrub-like perennial native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, hairy achenes that are dispersed by wind, and the plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils.

It is often found in open habitats such as fields and coastal areas.
