Rubus attractus

Rubus attractus, commonly known as the attractive raspberry.

This species belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, similar to other raspberries. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Rubus attractus is native to certain regions of South America and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
