Rosa brunoniana

Rosa brunoniana, commonly known as the Brunoniana rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late summer. Rosa brunoniana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
