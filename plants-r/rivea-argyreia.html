Rivea argyreia

Rivea argyreia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive silver-green leaves that shimmer in the light. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by long, slender fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
