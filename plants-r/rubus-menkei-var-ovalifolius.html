Rubus menkei var. ovalifolius

Rubus menkei var. ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Laos.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are oval in shape, giving it its specific variety name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
