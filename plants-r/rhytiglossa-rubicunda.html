Rhytiglossa rubicunda

Rhytiglossa rubicunda, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with deep red veins. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of forests. Its leaves are broad and glossy, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Rhytiglossa rubicunda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
