Rubus cosmophyllus

Rubus cosmophyllus, commonly known as the cosmopolitan bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves. Native to various regions across the globe, it has adapted to a wide range of climates and soils.

Due to its invasive tendencies in some areas, it is sometimes considered a weed.
