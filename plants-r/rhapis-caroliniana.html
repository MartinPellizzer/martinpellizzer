Rhapis caroliniana

Rhapis caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina bamboo, is a tropical plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to form clumps makes it suitable for use in borders, rock gardens, or as a ground cover.
