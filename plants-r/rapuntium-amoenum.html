Rapuntium amoenum

Rapuntium amoenum, commonly known as the Italian watercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, cool environments, often found near streams or in shaded wetlands.

Its edible leaves are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional cuisines for centuries.
