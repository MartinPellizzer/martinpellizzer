Racosperma barringtonense

Racosperma barringtonense, commonly known as Barrington's acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It features compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters.

Racosperma barringtonense is often used in reforestation projects due to its hardiness and ecological benefits.
