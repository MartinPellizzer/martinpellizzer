Rubus divexiramus var. rectiflorus

Rubus divexiramus var. rectiflorus, commonly known as the straight-flowered blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, straight-flowered inflorescences. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
