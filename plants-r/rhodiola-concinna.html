Rhodiola concinna

Rhodiola concinna, commonly known as the "golden root" or "tibetan rhodiola," is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth form with fleshy, greenish-yellow leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Rhodiola concinna is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.
