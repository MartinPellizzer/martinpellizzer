Rubus setigerus var. scabrispinus

Rubus setigerus var. scabrispinus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or thicket habitats.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are used in jams and pies.
