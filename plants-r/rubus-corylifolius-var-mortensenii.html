Rubus corylifolius var. mortensenii

Rubus corylifolius var. mortensenii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a variant of the brambles native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red drupes that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical structure includes a complex system of roots, stems, and leaves adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

This variant is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its medicinal properties.
