Rebutia vasqueziana

Rebutia vasqueziana, commonly known as the Vasquez's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically small and spiny, adapting to arid environments.

Rebutia vasqueziana is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
