Ranunculus christii

Ranunculus christii, commonly known as the Christ's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, often found in areas with low humidity. The plant features a short, stout stem with palmately divided, bright green leaves that have a distinctive lobed shape. Its flowers are showy, with five to nine yellow petals surrounding a central yellow center, and they bloom in spring.

Ranunculus christii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cup-like appearance.
