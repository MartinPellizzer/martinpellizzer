Ranunculus amplexicaulis

Ranunculus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Ranunculus amplexicaulis is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
