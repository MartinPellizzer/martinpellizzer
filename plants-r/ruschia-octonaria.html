Ruschia octonaria

Ruschia octonaria, commonly known as the eight-branched Ruschia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and its ability to produce multiple branched stems. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Ruschia octonaria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
