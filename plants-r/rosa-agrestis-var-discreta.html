Rosa agrestis var. discreta

Rosa agrestis var. discreta, commonly known as the discreet wild rose, is a low-growing, shrubby plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has thin, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its botanical characteristics include a woody base and small, oval-shaped fruits that develop from its flowers.

It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
