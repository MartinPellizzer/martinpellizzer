Rhododendron augustinei subsp. augustinei

Rhododendron augustinei subsp.

augustinei, commonly known as the Yunnan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. It is often found in cloud forests at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
