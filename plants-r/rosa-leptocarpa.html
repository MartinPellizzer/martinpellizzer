Rosa leptocarpa

Rosa leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender shrub rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its upright, sparsely branched stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the fruit is a red, hairy rosehip.

Rosa leptocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
