Ranunculus × variifolius

Ranunculus × variifolius, commonly known as the variegated buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with palmate or deeply lobed leaves that often display variegation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

The flowers bloom in spring and are often used in garden settings for their ornamental value.
