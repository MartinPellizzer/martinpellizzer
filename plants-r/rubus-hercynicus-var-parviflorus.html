Rubus hercynicus var. parviflorus

Rubus hercynicus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy conditions.
