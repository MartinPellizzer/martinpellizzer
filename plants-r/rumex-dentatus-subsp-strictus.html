Rumex dentatus subsp. strictus

Rumex dentatus subsp.

strictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color and bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, giving the plant its subspecific name "strictus," which refers to its compact growth habit. It produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer, which are borne on terminal spikes.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, meadows, and riverbanks.
