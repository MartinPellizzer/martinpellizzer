Rubus barberi

Rubus barberi, commonly known as the barber's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
