Rhododendron ponticum subvar. odoratum

Rhododendron ponticum subvar. odoratum, commonly known as the fragrant pontic rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its subvariety is distinguished by a more pronounced floral scent compared to other rhododendron species.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and fragrance.
