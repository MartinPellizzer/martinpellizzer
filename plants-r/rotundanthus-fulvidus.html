Rotundanthus fulvidus

Rotundanthus fulvidus, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical, yellowish-orange flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are often covered in fine, hairy textures.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
