Rosa communis var. lutetiana

Rosa communis var. lutetiana, commonly known as the Paris rose, is a cultivar of the common rose with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its deep red, semi-double flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, making it popular in gardens and perfumery.

This variety is well-suited for container growing and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
