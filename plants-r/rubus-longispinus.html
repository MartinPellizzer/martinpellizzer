Rubus longispinus

Rubus longispinus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and have five petals, and it thrives in moist, woodland environments.

Rubus longispinus is valued for its edible berries and is often found in shaded, humid habitats.
