Rubus floribundus var. nimbatus

Rubus floribundus var. nimbatus, commonly known as the clouded blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full to partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
