Rubus assaortinus var. erythraeus

Rubus assaortinus var. erythraeus, commonly known as the red-fruited raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn bright red when ripe, making it visually striking. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its flavorful berries and ornamental value.
