Restio ameles

Restio ameles, commonly known as the Cape restio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. It typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Restio ameles is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
