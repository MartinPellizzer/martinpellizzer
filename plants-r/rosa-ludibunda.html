Rosa ludibunda

Rosa ludibunda, commonly known as the "Mock Strawberry" or "Baker's Rose," is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers that resemble strawberries, which bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and produces small, edible fruits that turn red when mature. Rosa ludibunda thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
