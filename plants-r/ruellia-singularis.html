Ruellia singularis

Ruellia singularis, commonly known as the silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Ruellia singularis is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
