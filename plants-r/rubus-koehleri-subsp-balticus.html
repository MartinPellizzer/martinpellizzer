Rubus koehleri subsp. balticus

Rubus koehleri subsp.

balticus, commonly known as the Baltic blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of eastern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
