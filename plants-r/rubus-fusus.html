Rubus fusus

Rubus fusus, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rubus fusus is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows, contributing to local biodiversity.
