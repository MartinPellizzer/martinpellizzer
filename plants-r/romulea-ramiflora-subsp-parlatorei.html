Romulea ramiflora subsp. parlatorei

Romulea ramiflora subsp.

parlatorei, commonly known as the Parlatore's glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a bulbous perennial that produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, often fragrant scent.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates. Its foliage consists of grass-like leaves that are narrow and evergreen.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, early spring blooms.
