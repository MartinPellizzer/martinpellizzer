Rubus multisetosus

Rubus multisetosus, commonly known as the many-thorned blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems and a subject of interest in horticulture and ecology.
