Rosa coriifolia vulgaris

Rosa coriifolia vulgaris, commonly known as the Chinese pink or winter rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that turn red in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Rosa coriifolia vulgaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
