Rhabdophyllum calophyllum

Rhabdophyllum calophyllum, commonly known as the golden star hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
