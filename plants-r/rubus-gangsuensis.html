Rubus gangsuensis

Rubus gangsuensis, commonly known as the Sichuan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is a dark purple to black berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.
