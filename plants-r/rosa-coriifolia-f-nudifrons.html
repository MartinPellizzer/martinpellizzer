Rosa coriifolia f. nudifrons

Rosa coriifolia f. nudifrons, commonly known as the leafy rose or the Chinese shrub rose, is a botanical variant of the common shrub rose.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers in the spring. Its foliage remains lush throughout the growing season, contributing to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
