Rubus baumgartneri

Rubus baumgartneri, commonly known as the Baumgartner's blackberry, is a species of shrub in the rose family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America, specifically in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species.

It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.
