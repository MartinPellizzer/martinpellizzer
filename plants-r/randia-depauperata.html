Randia depauperata

Randia depauperata, commonly known as the poor man's randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Randia depauperata is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in poor soil conditions.
