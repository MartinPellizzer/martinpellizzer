Rubus × evagatifrons

Rubus × evagatifrons, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry or loganberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (raspberry).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupes that range in color from red to black. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavorful berries.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
