Ruellia caroliniensis subsp. ciliosa

Ruellia caroliniensis subsp.

ciliosa, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom from spring to summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and wetland restoration projects.
