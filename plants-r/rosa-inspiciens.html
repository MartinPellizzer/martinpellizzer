Rosa inspiciens

Rosa inspiciens, commonly known as the showy pink rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are large, showy, and typically pink or red in color. Rosa inspiciens blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, single or semi-double flowers.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
