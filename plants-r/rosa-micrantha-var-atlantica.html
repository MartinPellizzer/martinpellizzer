Rosa micrantha var. atlantica

Rosa micrantha var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic rose, is a variant of the rose species native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thornless stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is well adapted to maritime climates, tolerating salt spray and coastal winds.
