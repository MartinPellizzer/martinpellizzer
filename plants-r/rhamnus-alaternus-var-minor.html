Rhamnus alaternus var. minor

Rhamnus alaternus var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf common buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with dense, dark green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, black berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It has a smooth, greyish bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
