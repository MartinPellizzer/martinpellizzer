Raphanus sativus var. parvipinnatus

Raphanus sativus var. parvipinnatus, commonly known as the Chinese watercress, is a leafy green vegetable native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, edible white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often grown in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. Its stems and leaves are rich in nutrients, including vitamins A, C, and K, as well as minerals like calcium and iron.

The plant's rapid growth and high nutritional value make it a popular choice for both culinary and medicinal use.
