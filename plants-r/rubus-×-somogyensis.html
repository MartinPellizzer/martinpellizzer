Rubus × somogyensis

Rubus × somogyensis, commonly known as the Hungarian blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and flavorful. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
