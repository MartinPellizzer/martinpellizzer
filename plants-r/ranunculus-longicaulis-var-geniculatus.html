Ranunculus longicaulis var. geniculatus

Ranunculus longicaulis var. geniculatus, commonly known as the bent-stem buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a slender, often zigzagging stem that gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
