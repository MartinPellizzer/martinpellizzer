Rhynchosia scarabaeoides

Rhynchosia scarabaeoides, commonly known as the scarab beetle bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears pods containing several elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Rhynchosia scarabaeoides is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in agricultural systems.
