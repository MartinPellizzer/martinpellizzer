Rhynchosia incana

Rhynchosia incana, commonly known as the hairy legume or wild bean, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Rhynchosia incana is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and forest edges.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
