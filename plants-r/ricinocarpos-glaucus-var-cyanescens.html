Ricinocarpos glaucus var. cyanescens

Ricinocarpos glaucus var. cyanescens, commonly known as the blue-veined rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, aromatic leaves that often have a bluish-gray coating. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves and stems contain toxic compounds, including the potent toxin ricin.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
