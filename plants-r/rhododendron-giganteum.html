Rhododendron giganteum

Rhododendron giganteum, commonly known as the giant rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and often arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in cool, moist climates with acidic soil and partial shade.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
