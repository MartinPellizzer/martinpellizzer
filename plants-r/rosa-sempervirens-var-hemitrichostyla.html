Rosa sempervirens var. hemitrichostyla

Rosa sempervirens var. hemitrichostyla, commonly known as the pink Himalayan rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
