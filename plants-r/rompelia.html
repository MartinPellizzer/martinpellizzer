Rompelia

Rompelia, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, almost ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. Rompelia is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shady environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
