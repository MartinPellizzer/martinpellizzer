Rhyncholacis carinata

Rhyncholacis carinata, commonly known as the ridge-ribbed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a specialized column that facilitates pollination by specific insect species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

The orchid's flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.
