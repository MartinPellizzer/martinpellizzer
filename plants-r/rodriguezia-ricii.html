Rodriguezia ricii

Rodriguezia ricii, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Rodriguezia ricii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and aesthetic appeal.
