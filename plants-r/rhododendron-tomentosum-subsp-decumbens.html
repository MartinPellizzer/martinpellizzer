Rhododendron tomentosum subsp. decumbens

Rhododendron tomentosum subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the creeping tomentose rhododendron, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at high elevations. Its leaves are leathery and oval-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
