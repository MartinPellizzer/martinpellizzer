Rosa quasi-acharii

Rosa quasi-acharii, commonly known as the gray rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces oval, red-brown fruits known as hips, which are often used in traditional medicine. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in harsh, arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural research.
