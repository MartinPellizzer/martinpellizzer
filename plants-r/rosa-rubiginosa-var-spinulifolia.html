Rosa rubiginosa var. spinulifolia

Rosa rubiginosa var. spinulifolia, commonly known as the bristly rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that contain seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
