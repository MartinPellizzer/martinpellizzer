Ranunculus bonariensis f. major

Ranunculus bonariensis f. major, commonly known as the large-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often adorned with a dark central spot. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
