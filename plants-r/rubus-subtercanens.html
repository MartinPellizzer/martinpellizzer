Rubus subtercanens

Rubus subtercanens, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated margins and a hairy texture.

The berries, which are usually dark purple to black, are edible but often have a tart flavor.
