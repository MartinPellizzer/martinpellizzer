Rhamnus palmeri

Rhamnus palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for certain wildlife species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
