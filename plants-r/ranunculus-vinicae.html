Ranunculus vinicae

Ranunculus vinicae, commonly known as the vinous buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which is characterized by its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
