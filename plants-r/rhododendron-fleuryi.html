Rhododendron fleuryi

Rhododendron fleuryi, commonly known as the Fleury's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations between 800 and 1,500 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences.

Rhododendron fleuryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
