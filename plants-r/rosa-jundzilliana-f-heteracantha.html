Rosa jundzilliana f. heteracantha

Rosa jundzilliana f. heteracantha, commonly known as the Jundzilliana rose with heteracantha form, is a rare and unique cultivar of the Rosa jundzilliana species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated thorns that differ from the typical thorn structure of its parent species. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers with a delicate appearance, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is a compact shrub with dark green, leathery foliage that provides an attractive contrast to its blooms.

This form is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unusual features.
