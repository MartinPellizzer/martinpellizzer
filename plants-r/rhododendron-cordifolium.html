Rhododendron cordifolium

Rhododendron cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers acidic soil conditions. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
