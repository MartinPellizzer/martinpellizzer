Ranunculus cassubiciformis

Ranunculus cassubiciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a contrasting central pattern. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are finely toothed and often covered in a soft, silky hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
