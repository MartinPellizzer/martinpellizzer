Ranunculus camtschaticus

Ranunculus camtschaticus, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and hairy stems that bear alternate, trifoliate leaves. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
