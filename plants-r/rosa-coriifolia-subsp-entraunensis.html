Rosa coriifolia subsp. entraunensis

Rosa coriifolia subsp.

entraunensis, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its thorny stems and simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its botanical features include a woody structure and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
