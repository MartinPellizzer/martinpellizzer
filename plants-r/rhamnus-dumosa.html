Rhamnus dumosa

Rhamnus dumosa, commonly known as the desert buckthorn, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for certain wildlife species. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Rhamnus dumosa plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for desert fauna.
