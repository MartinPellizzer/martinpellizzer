Rehmannia integra

Rehmannia integra, commonly known as Chinese foxglove, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Rehmannia integra is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
