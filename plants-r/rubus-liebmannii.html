Rubus liebmannii

Rubus liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
