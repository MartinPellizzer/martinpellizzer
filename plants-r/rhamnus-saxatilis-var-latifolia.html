Rhamnus saxatilis var. latifolia

Rhamnus saxatilis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are broad and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
