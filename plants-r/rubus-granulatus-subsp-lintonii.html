Rubus granulatus subsp. lintonii

Rubus granulatus subsp.

lintonii, commonly known as the Linton's raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and small, aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is often found in association with other brambles and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
