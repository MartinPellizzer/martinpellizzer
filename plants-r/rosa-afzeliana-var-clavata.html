Rosa afzeliana var. clavata

Rosa afzeliana var. clavata, commonly known as the clustered rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features arching stems with thornless, reddish-brown branches that often form dense clusters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a slightly serrated edge.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
