Rubus casparyi f. sylvatica

Rubus casparyi f. sylvatica, commonly known as the Siberian raspberry, is a shrub native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to harsh climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
