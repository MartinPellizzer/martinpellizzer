Ranunculus quelpaertensis f. pleniflorus

Ranunculus quelpaertensis f. pleniflorus, commonly known as the Korean buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers.

It features bright yellow petals that are typically more numerous and larger than those of the typical species, giving it a more elaborate appearance. The plant is native to the Korean Peninsula and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and trifoliate, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is noted for its delicate beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
