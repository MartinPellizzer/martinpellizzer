Rhododendron quadrasianum var. quadrasianum

Rhododendron quadrasianum var. quadrasianum, commonly known as the Sichuan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils. Its floral display consists of clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
