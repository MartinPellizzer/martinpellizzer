Rubus orthacanthus

Rubus orthacanthus, commonly known as the prickly rose or sand rose, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other rose species.
