Rosa lacerans

Rosa lacerans, commonly known as the torn rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist climates.

Rosa lacerans is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
