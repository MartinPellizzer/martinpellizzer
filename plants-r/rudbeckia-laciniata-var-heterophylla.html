Rudbeckia laciniata var. heterophylla

Rudbeckia laciniata var. heterophylla, commonly known as the cutleaf coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by broad, dark green, deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage varies in shape, with the lower leaves being more deeply cut than the upper ones, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
