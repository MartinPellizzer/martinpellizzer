Rubus apianus

Rubus apianus, commonly known as the honeybee bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and pies.

The plant plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
