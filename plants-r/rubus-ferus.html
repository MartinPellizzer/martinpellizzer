Rubus ferus

Rubus ferus, commonly known as brambles or wild blackberries, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Rubus ferus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
