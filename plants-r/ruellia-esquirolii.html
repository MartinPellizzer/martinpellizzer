Ruellia esquirolii

Ruellia esquirolii, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant produces striking blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Ruellia esquirolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
