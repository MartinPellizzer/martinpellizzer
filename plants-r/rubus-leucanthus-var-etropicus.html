Rubus leucanthus var. etropicus

Rubus leucanthus var. etropicus, commonly known as the tropical raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is known for its aromatic, sometimes edible fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
