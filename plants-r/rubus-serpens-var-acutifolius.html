Rubus serpens var. acutifolius

Rubus serpens var. acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The leaves are elliptical with a pointed tip, and they have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically clustered together.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
