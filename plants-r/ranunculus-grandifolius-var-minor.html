Ranunculus grandifolius var. minor

Ranunculus grandifolius var. minor, commonly known as the small-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is an herbaceous perennial that thrives in partial to full sunlight.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in disturbed soils.
