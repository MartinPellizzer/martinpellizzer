Rubus haellstroemii

Rubus haellstroemii, commonly known as the Haellstrom's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often described as having a tart and slightly astringent flavor.
