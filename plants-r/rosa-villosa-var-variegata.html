Rosa villosa var. variegata

Rosa villosa var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated bristly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It features upright stems covered in a dense layer of white to grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive bristly appearance. The leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape and often have a silvery-white or variegated pattern. Its flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant is well adapted to cool, dry climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
