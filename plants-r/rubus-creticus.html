Rubus creticus

Rubus creticus, commonly known as the Cretan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, red, edible berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for its tart flavor and potential health benefits.
