Ranunculus aquatilis subsp. floribundus

Ranunculus aquatilis subsp.

floribundus, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems that often grow in shallow water or damp soil. Its leaves are deeply lobed and can be submerged or floating, depending on the water level.

It plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
