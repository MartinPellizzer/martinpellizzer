Racosperma nigripilosum subsp. latifolium

Racosperma nigripilosum subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved hairy acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small fauna.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
