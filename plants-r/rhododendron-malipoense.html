Rhododendron malipoense

Rhododendron malipoense, commonly known as the Malipo rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments at high elevations, often in temperate forests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
