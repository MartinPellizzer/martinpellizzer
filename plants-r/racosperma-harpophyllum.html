Racosperma harpophyllum

Racosperma harpophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and the leaves are narrow and often hairy.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in revegetation projects.
