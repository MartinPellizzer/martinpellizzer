Rubus elumbis

Rubus elumbis, commonly known as the Malayan bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions such as Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically featuring three to five leaflets. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are followed by red, edible berries.

Rubus elumbis plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
