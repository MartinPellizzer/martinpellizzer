Rhaphiolepis condaoensis

Rhaphiolepis condaoensis, commonly known as the Chinese firethorn, is a shrub native to China and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves.

It produces bright red berries that are both ornamental and edible, though they are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant is valued for its winter interest, as the berries remain visible even after the leaves have dropped. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and versatile plant for landscaping purposes.
