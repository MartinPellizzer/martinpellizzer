Rosa tomentosa var. glabrescens

Rosa tomentosa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the woolly rose or smooth-haired rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa tomentosa species.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale pink flowers and hairy stems that become smoother with age. This variety is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and produces fragrant blooms in early summer.

Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing an attractive backdrop to its showy flowers.
