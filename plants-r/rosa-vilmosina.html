Rosa vilmosina

Rosa vilmosina, commonly known as the Hungarian rose, is a species native to the Carpathian region of Eastern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange-red hips. Rosa vilmosina is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its beauty and medicinal properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Rosa genus.
