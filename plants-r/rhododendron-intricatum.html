Rhododendron intricatum

Rhododendron intricatum, commonly known as the intricate rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron intricatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to woodland gardens.
