Rubus vestitus var. humifusus

Rubus vestitus var. humifusus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies of understory vegetation.
