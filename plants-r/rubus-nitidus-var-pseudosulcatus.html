Rubus nitidus var. pseudosulcatus

Rubus nitidus var. pseudosulcatus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically covered in a waxy, glossy coating. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its flavorful fruit and ornamental value.
