Rhododendron jenestierianum

Rhododendron jenestierianum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Rhododendron jenestierianum is also notable for its ability to form thickets, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
