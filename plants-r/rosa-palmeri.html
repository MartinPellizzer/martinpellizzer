Rosa palmeri

Rosa palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces edible hips that are used in traditional medicine.

Rosa palmeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
