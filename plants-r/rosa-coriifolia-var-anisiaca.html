Rosa coriifolia var. anisiaca

Rosa coriifolia var. anisiaca, commonly known as the Anise-scented shrub rose, is a variant of the shrub rose native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, anise-scented flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative shrub.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and pleasant fragrance.
