Rosa gemella

Rosa gemella, commonly known as the twin rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa gemella is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its beauty and resilience.
