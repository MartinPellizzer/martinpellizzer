Rubus bonatianus

Rubus bonatianus, commonly known as the Bonati raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
