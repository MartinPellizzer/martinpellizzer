Ranunculus eleophilus

Ranunculus eleophilus, commonly known as the sun-loving buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow or orange, and a prominent yellow center. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, and it thrives in well-drained, sunny habitats. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
