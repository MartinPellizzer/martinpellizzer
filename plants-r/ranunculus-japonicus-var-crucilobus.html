Ranunculus japonicus var. crucilobus

Ranunculus japonicus var. crucilobus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup variant with cruciform leaves, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to seven petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. The plant has palmate or lobed leaves that are often deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued in gardens for its early spring blooms and unique foliage structure.
