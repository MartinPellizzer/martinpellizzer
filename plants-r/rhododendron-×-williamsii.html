Rhododendron × williamsii

Rhododendron × williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.

The species is also noted for its adaptability and resilience in challenging environmental conditions.
