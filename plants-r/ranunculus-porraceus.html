Ranunculus porraceus

Ranunculus porraceus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds by wind.

Ranunculus porraceus is often found in open habitats and is considered a wildflower of ecological importance.
