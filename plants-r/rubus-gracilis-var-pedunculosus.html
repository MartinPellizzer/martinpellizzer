Rubus gracilis var. pedunculosus

Rubus gracilis var. pedunculosus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thin, trailing stems and small, thorny branches. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and local foraging.
