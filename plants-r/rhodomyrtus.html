Rhodomyrtus

Rhodomyrtus, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Australian satinwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia, New Guinea, and parts of Southeast Asia, with several species exhibiting varying degrees of ornamental and ecological significance. The plants typically feature small, fragrant flowers and are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental shrubs. Some species, like Rhodomyrtus tomentosa, are valued for their aromatic leaves and berries, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The genus is characterized by its evergreen foliage and adaptability to a range of environmental conditions.
