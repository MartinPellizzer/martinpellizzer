Restio loxocarya

Restio loxocarya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white to pale blue flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland fynbos vegetation.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
