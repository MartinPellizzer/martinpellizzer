Rockhausenia cornea

Rockhausenia cornea, commonly known as the smooth rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a smooth, waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Rockhausenia cornea is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
