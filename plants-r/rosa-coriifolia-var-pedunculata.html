Rosa coriifolia var. pedunculata

Rosa coriifolia var. pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely serrated, dark green leaves and produces red-orange hips in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

This variety is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its low maintenance requirements.
