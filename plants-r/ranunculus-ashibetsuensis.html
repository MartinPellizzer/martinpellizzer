Ranunculus ashibetsuensis

Ranunculus ashibetsuensis, commonly known as the Ashibetsu buttercup, is a rare and endemic species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain streams and wetlands. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it has a short, upright stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
