Rhipsalis pterocarpa

Rhipsalis pterocarpa, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom during the winter season. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are modified into spiny structures, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Rhipsalis pterocarpa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
