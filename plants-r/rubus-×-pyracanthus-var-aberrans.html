Rubus × pyracanthus var. aberrans

Rubus × pyracanthus var. aberrans, commonly known as the variable blackberry raspberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus idaeus, exhibiting traits of both parent species. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated margin and a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.
