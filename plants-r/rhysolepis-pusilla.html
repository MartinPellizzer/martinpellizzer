Rhysolepis pusilla

Rhysolepis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf mossy tamarisk, is a small shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, red berries. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for stabilizing soil in semi-arid regions.
