Reseda lanceolata var. eu-lanceolata

Reseda lanceolata var. eu-lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It is part of the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
