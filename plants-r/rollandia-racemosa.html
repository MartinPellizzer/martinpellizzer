Rollandia racemosa

Rollandia racemosa, commonly known as the red-veined dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, surrounded by distinctive red veins on its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rollandia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
