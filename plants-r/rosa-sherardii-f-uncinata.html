Rosa sherardii f. uncinata

Rosa sherardii f. uncinata, commonly known as the hooked-leaved shrub rose, is a botanical variant of the shrub rose species Rosa sherardii.

It is characterized by its distinctive hooked or twisted leaflets, which set it apart from other forms of the species. This plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals, and bloom in the summer months.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
