Ranunculus monophyllus subsp. rheithroplesius

Ranunculus monophyllus subsp.

rheithroplesius, commonly known as the Rheithroplesius buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
