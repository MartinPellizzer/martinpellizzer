Rubia chekiangensis

Rubia chekiangensis, commonly known as Chinese madder, is a flowering plant native to China and belongs to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is known for containing alizarin, a red dye historically used in textile industries.

Rubia chekiangensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
