Rubus foliosus proles saltuum

Rubus foliosus proles saltuum, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a shrub native to mountainous regions.

It features slender, thorny stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries in the summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.
