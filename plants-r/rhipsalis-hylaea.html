Rhipsalis hylaea

Rhipsalis hylaea, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
