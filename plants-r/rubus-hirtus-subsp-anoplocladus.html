Rubus hirtus subsp. anoplocladus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

anoplocladus, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and large, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

The leaves are typically three-lobed with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals. This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, thriving in partial shade.

Its fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tangy or slightly bitter flavor.
