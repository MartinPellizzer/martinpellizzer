Rubus corchorifolius var. hydrastifolius

Rubus corchorifolius var. hydrastifolius, commonly known as the hydrastifolius raspberry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its large, serrated leaves that resemble those of the hydrastis plant, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
