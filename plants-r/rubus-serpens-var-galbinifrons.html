Rubus serpens var. galbinifrons

Rubus serpens var. galbinifrons, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

This variant is a shrub-like plant native to the eastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white with five petals.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
