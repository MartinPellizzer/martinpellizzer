Rhododendron meliphagidum

Rhododendron meliphagidum, commonly known as the honey-sweet rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are elliptical and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

Rhododendron meliphagidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
