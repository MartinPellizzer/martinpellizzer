Rosa × hyana

Rosa × hyana, commonly known as the hybrid tea rose, is a popular ornamental plant cultivated for its showy, fragrant blooms.

It is a cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica, resulting in a variety with strong stems and large, semi-double flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces deep pink to red flowers in spring and summer. It requires full sun and well-drained soil to thrive, making it a favored choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it a key species in horticultural breeding programs.
