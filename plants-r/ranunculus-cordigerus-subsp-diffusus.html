Ranunculus cordigerus subsp. diffusus

Ranunculus cordigerus subsp.

diffusus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its short, hairy stems and is often found in temperate regions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
