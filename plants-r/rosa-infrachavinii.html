Rosa infrachavinii

Rosa infrachavinii, commonly known as the Mexican rock rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Rosa infrachavinii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable subject in both horticulture and ecological studies.
