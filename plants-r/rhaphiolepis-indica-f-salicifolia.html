Rhaphiolepis indica f. salicifolia

Rhaphiolepis indica f. salicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf cotoneaster, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features small, oval leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a specimen plant due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.

Its foliage provides year-round visual interest, making it a popular choice in landscaping.
