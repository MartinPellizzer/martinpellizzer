Rhamnus rhodopea

Rhamnus rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually pubescent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
