Ranunculus occidentalis var. robustus

Ranunculus occidentalis var. robustus, commonly known as the western buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often accompanied by a cluster of yellow stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
