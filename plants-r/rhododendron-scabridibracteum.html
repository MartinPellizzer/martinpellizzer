Rhododendron scabridibracteum

Rhododendron scabridibracteum, commonly known as the rough bract rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a rough texture, while the bracts are hairy and contribute to its common name.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
