Rosa brunonii var. nudiuscula

Rosa brunonii var. nudiuscula, commonly known as the slender shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems with small, thornless branches that often grow in a compact, bushy form. The plant produces fragrant, single or double flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty and hardy nature.
