Rehmannia elata

Rehmannia elata, commonly known as the Chinese foxglove, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in forested areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers, which range in color from purple to pink, bloom in summer and attract various pollinators.

Rehmannia elata is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
