Rumex hydrolapathum var. maximus

Rumex hydrolapathum var. maximus, commonly known as the giant dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and unique floral structures. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a rough, waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in terminal spikes.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
