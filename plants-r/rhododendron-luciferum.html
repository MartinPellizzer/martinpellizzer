Rhododendron luciferum

Rhododendron luciferum, commonly known as the Lucifer rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, primarily found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils at high elevations. The plant is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a yellow throat. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species in the Rhododendron genus, valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
