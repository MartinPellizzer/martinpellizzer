Rubus cardianus

Rubus cardianus, commonly known as the Cardian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has a sprawling growth habit. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, appearing in the spring before the fruit develops.

The berries are often used in local culinary traditions and are valued for their tart flavor.
