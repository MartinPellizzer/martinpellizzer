Rapanea faberi

Rapanea faberi, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or Chinese holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces dark blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically glossy and have a leathery texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
