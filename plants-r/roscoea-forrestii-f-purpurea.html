Roscoea forrestii f. purpurea

Roscoea forrestii f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered roscoea, is a hybrid form of the roscoea plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple inflorescences, which are borne on tall, leafy stalks. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy bloom, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically grown as an ornamental in gardens with partial shade.

Its unique coloration and elegant structure make it a prized addition to tropical and subtropical gardens.
