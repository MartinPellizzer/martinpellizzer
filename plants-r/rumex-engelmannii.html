Rumex engelmannii

Rumex engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple underside, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It is part of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other sorrel species.

The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and is often found in disturbed or fertile soils.
