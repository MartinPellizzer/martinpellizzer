Rhododendron gongshanense

Rhododendron gongshanense, commonly known as the Gongshan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and leathery leaves, making it a distinctive member of the Ericaceae family.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique adaptations and ornamental value.
