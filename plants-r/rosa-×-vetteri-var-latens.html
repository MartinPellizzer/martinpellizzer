Rosa × vetteri var. latens

Rosa × vetteri var. latens, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
