Rhopalocarpus suarezensis

Rhopalocarpus suarezensis, commonly known as the Suarez's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the humid forests of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Its slow growth rate and limited distribution make it a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts.

This cycad is of significant botanical interest due to its evolutionary history and ecological role in its native habitat.
