Rubia sativa

Rubia sativa, commonly known as common valerian, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in summer. Its roots are often harvested for their aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Rubia sativa is valued in traditional medicine for its sedative and calming effects.
