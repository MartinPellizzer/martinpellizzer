Rosa scabrinutkana

Rosa scabrinutkana, commonly known as the rough-fruited rose, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a fragrant aroma.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
