Rubus serpens var. inclinans

Rubus serpens var. inclinans, commonly known as the leaning blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
