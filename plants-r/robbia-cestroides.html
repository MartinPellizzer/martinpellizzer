Robbia cestroides

Robbia cestroides, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat, disk-like structure, typical of the family.

Robbia cestroides is often found in grassy areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
