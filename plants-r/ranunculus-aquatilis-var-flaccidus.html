Ranunculus aquatilis var. flaccidus

Ranunculus aquatilis var. flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid water crowfoot, is a aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, floating leaves and yellowish flowers.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The leaves are usually divided into lobes and can be submerged or floating, depending on the water conditions. The flowers are small and short-lived, often appearing in clusters.

This variant is adapted to aquatic habitats and plays a role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
