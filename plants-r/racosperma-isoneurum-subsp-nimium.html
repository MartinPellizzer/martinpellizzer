Racosperma isoneurum subsp. nimium

Racosperma isoneurum subsp.

nimium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.

This subspecies is part of the legume family and plays a role in soil stabilization and ecological restoration.
