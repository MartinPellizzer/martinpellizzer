Rubus ginsiensis

Rubus ginsiensis, commonly known as the Korean raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Korea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in forested areas and rocky soils, preferring partial shade. Its flowers are white and appear in the spring, followed by the development of its edible fruits.

The berries are often used in traditional Korean cuisine and herbal remedies.
