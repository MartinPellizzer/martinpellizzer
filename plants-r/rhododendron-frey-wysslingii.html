Rhododendron frey-wysslingii

Rhododendron frey-wysslingii, commonly known as the Frey-Wyssling rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of Nepal and India.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, with five petals and a tubular throat, and they emit a mild fragrance. This plant prefers cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained acidic soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
