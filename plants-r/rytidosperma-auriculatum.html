Rytidosperma auriculatum

Rytidosperma auriculatum, commonly known as the golden grass or Australian sand grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive golden hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland dry regions.

Rytidosperma auriculatum is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
