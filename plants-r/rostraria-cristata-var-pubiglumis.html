Rostraria cristata var. pubiglumis

Rostraria cristata var. pubiglumis, commonly known as the velvet-fruited rostraria, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs. Its unique floral structure, including the prominent rostellum, is a key feature in its taxonomic classification.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and challenging cultivation requirements.
