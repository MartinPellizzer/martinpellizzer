Rosa reticulata

Rosa reticulata, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Rosa reticulata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
