Roella secunda

Roella secunda, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces a distinctive seed head that disperses its seeds by wind.

Roella secunda is often found in rocky soils and is a common sight in grassland ecosystems.
