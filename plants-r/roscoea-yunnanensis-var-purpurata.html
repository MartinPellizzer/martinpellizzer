Roscoea yunnanensis var. purpurata

Roscoea yunnanensis var. purpurata, commonly known as the purple-tinged roscoea, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and showy inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, often with a red or orange hue at the base. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
