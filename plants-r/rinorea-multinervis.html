Rinorea multinervis

Rinorea multinervis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet leaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its finely veined, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Rinorea multinervis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along riverbanks.
