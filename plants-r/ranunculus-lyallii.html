Ranunculus lyallii

Ranunculus lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Ranunculus lyallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
