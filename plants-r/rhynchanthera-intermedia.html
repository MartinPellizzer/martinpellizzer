Rhynchanthera intermedia

Rhynchanthera intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rhynchanthera, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
