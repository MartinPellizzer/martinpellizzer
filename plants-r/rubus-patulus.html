Rubus patulus

Rubus patulus, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
