Rubus myrianthus

Rubus myrianthus, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
