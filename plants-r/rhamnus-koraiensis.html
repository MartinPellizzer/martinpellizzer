Rhamnus koraiensis

Rhamnus koraiensis, commonly known as the Korean buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, black berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas across Korea, China, and Japan.

Rhamnus koraiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
