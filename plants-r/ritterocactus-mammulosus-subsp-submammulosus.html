Ritterocactus mammulosus subsp. submammulosus

Ritterocactus mammulosus subsp.

submammulosus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to dry, rocky environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
