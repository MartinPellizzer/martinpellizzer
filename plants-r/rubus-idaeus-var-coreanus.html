Rubus idaeus var. coreanus

Rubus idaeus var. coreanus, commonly known as the Corean raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its culinary uses.
