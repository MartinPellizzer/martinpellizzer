Rosa × dumetorum var. przybylskii

Rosa × dumetorum var. przybylskii, commonly known as the Przybylskii shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with dark green, glossy leaves. Its botanical features include stipitate stipules and a compact, bushy form that makes it suitable for hedging or ornamental use.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
