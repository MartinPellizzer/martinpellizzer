Roulinia cordata

Roulinia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, spatulate inflorescence that resembles a flower. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique floral structures. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Roulinia cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
