Rhus quartiniana var. zambesiensis

Rhus quartiniana var. zambesiensis, commonly known as the Zambesi buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by berry-like fruits that are dark red to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
