Rosa dumalis f. subhirtella

Rosa dumalis f. subhirtella, commonly known as the alpine rose or mountain rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa dumalis.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
