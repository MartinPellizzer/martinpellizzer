Ranunculus pulchellus var. membranaceus

Ranunculus pulchellus var. membranaceus, commonly known as the membranaceous crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five to seven petals.

The variety membranaceus is distinguished by its delicate, papery petals and its preference for more open, sunny habitats.
