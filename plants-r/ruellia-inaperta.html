Ruellia inaperta

Ruellia inaperta, commonly known as the velvetleaf ruellia, is a tropical perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. Ruellia inaperta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
