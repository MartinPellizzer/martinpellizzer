Rubus corchorifolius var. glaber

Rubus corchorifolius var. glaber, commonly known as the smoothleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and thornless stems, distinguishing it from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
