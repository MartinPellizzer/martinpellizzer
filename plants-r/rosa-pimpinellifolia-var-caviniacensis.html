Rosa pimpinellifolia var. caviniacensis

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. caviniacensis, commonly known as the Caviniacensis rock rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with dense, dark green foliage that is often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to coastal or mountainous environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique characteristics, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value.
