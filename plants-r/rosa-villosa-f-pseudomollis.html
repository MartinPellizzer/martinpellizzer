Rosa villosa f. pseudomollis

Rosa villosa f. pseudomollis, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a hybrid form of the hairy rose, characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and a prominent central pistil. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its attractive appearance and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
