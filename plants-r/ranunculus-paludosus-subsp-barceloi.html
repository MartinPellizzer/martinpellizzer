Ranunculus paludosus subsp. barceloi

Ranunculus paludosus subsp.

barceloi, commonly known as the marsh buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in Europe. It features delicate, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five sepals that resemble petals.

The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, often waterlogged soils. Its leaves are usually deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
