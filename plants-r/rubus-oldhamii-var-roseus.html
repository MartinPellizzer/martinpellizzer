Rubus oldhamii var. roseus

Rubus oldhamii var. roseus, commonly known as the rosehip bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are rich in vitamin C and often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are pink to white, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
