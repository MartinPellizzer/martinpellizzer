Rhynchostylis gigantea

Rhynchostylis gigantea, commonly known as the giant rhynchostylis, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

The orchid produces fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, making it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
