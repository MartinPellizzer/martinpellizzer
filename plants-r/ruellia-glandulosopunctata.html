Ruellia glandulosopunctata

Ruellia glandulosopunctata, commonly known as the spotted ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in glandular and punctate dots. The flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
