Rhyticalymma vidalii

Rhyticalymma vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals and a unique, elongated flower structure that sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered critically endangered.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this rare botanical treasure.
