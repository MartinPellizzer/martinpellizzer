Rhynchosia biflora

Rhynchosia biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive twin-flowered inflorescences. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. Rhynchosia biflora is often grown for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines, and it also has medicinal uses in some cultures.
