Rosa × collina var. fastigiata

Rosa × collina var. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright form of the shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative shrub. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing an attractive appearance throughout the growing season.

Rosa × collina var. fastigiata is well-suited for garden borders and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
