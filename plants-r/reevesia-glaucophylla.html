Reevesia glaucophylla

Reevesia glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leafed reevesia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its glaucous, waxy leaves that reflect sunlight, a feature that helps it conserve moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Reevesia glaucophylla thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
