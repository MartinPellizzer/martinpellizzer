Ranunculus acutiusculus

Ranunculus acutiusculus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive sharp, serrated edge.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are often accompanied by a mild, sometimes unpleasant odor.
