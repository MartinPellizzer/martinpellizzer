Rubus × semimicans

Rubus × semimicans, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, preferring full sun to partial shade. It is commonly found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in forest edges.

Its fruit is edible and often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
