Raspalia

Raspalia, commonly known as the "flower of the moon," is a rare and exotic plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

The plant has a complex root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, and its leaves are covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Raspalia blooms only once every seven years, making it a highly sought-after specimen in botanical gardens.
