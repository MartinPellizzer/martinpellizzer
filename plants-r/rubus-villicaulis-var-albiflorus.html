Rubus villicaulis var. albiflorus

Rubus villicaulis var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red, hairy berries. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
