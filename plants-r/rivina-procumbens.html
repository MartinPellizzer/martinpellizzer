Rivina procumbens

Rivina procumbens, commonly known as redroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub that features small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along streambanks.

Rivina procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial to full shade.
