Rhynchospora affinis

Rhynchospora affinis, commonly known as the slender beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swales, and roadside ditches. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of tiny, three-lobed flowers that are often greenish or reddish in color.

Rhynchospora affinis is often found in the eastern United States and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor, waterlogged soils.
