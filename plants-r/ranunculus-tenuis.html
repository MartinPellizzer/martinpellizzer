Ranunculus tenuis

Ranunculus tenuis, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy texture. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Ranunculus tenuis is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.
