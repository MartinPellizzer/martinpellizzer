Rubia laevis

Rubia laevis, commonly known as silky knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a reddish tinge when mature.

Rubia laevis is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
