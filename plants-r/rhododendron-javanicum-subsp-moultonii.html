Rhododendron javanicum subsp. moultonii

Rhododendron javanicum subsp.

moultonii, commonly known as the Moulton's rhododendron, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. It is a shrub or small tree with evergreen foliage and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct floral structure and is part of the Ericaceae family.
