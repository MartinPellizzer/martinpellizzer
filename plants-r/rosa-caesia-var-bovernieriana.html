Rosa caesia var. bovernieriana

Rosa caesia var. bovernieriana, commonly known as the Bournier's shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces red, velvety hips that persist into winter. It is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness in colder climates.
