Rhododendron phaeochiton

Rhododendron phaeochiton, commonly known as the purple-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that displays a striking purple hue, especially in shaded conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
