Rubus copelandii

Rubus copelandii, commonly known as the Copeland's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Peru. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities, with attractive foliage and showy white flowers.
