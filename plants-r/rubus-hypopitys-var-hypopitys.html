Rubus hypopitys var. hypopitys

Rubus hypopitys var. hypopitys, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is usually dark purple to black when ripe.

This variety is widely cultivated for its edible berries and is also used in traditional medicine.
