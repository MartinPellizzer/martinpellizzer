Rhododendron ripleyi

Rhododendron ripleyi, commonly known as the RIPLEY'S rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in the understory of forests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron ripleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
