Rubus deliciosus var. deliciosus

Rubus deliciosus var. deliciosus, commonly known as blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
