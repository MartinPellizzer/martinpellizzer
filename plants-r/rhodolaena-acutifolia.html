Rhodolaena acutifolia

Rhodolaena acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved rhodolaena, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Rhodolaena acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
