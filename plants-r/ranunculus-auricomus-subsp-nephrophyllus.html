Ranunculus auricomus subsp. nephrophyllus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

nephrophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive, hairy stem.

The leaves are typically divided into three parts, with the central leaflet being narrower than the others. This subspecies thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
