Rumex × pseudopulcher

Rumex × pseudopulcher, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Rumex acetosa and Rumex acetobatus.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the veins, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring. Its botanical structure includes a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in wetland habitats.

The hybrid nature of Rumex × pseudopulcher results in a diverse range of morphological variations, contributing to its adaptability in different ecological settings.
