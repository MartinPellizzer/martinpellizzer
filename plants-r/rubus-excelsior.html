Rubus excelsior

Rubus excelsior, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green and leathery. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are usually dark purple to black in color. Rubus excelsior thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
