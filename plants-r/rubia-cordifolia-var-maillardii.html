Rubia cordifolia var. maillardii

Rubia cordifolia var. maillardii, commonly known as the Maillard's heartleaf rubia, is a variant of the heartleaf rue.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its stems are covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in rocky or dry soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
