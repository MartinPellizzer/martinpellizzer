Rosa sempervirens var. pedunculata

Rosa sempervirens var. pedunculata, commonly known as the evergreen rose or the shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features upright, thorny stems and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains lush throughout the growing season. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.

This variety is particularly noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
