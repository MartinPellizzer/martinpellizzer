Rhynchospora crinigera

Rhynchospora crinigera, commonly known as the silky flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, thread-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on elongated stalks. Rhynchospora crinigera thrives in moist environments and is often found in association with other wetland flora.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
