Rubus × decliviformis

Rubus × decliviformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
