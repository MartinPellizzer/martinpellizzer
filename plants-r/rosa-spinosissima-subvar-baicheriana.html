Rosa spinosissima subvar. baicheriana

Rosa spinosissima subvar. baicheriana, commonly known as the Baicher's prickly rose, is a shrub-like species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its flowers are often borne singly or in clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is well adapted to high-altitude conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
