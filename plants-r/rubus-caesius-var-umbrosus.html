Rubus caesius var. umbrosus

Rubus caesius var. umbrosus, commonly known as the dark raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its dark red to almost black fruits, which are typically smaller than those of the standard Rubus caesius. It thrives in shaded environments, making it a suitable plant for woodland gardens. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has a thorny stem structure typical of the Rubus genus.

It is often found in moist, acidic soils and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
