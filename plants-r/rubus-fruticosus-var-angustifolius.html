Rubus fruticosus var. angustifolius

Rubus fruticosus var. angustifolius, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe, and are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

This plant is also known for its hardiness and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
