Rosa schmalhauseniana var. stepposa

Rosa schmalhauseniana var. stepposa, commonly known as the steppe roses, is a compact shrub native to Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, finely textured leaves and a woody stem structure typical of many rose species. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
