Rhododendron vandeursenii

Rhododendron vandeursenii, commonly known as the Van Duren's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at high elevations, thriving in cool, moist, and shaded environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
