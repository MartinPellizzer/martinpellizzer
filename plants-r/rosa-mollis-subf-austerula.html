Rosa mollis subf. austerula

Rosa mollis subf.

austerula, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a subspecies of the shrub rose native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy gray-green foliage and small, fragrant pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its stems are often covered in soft, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in challenging climates.
