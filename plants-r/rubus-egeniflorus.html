Rubus egeniflorus

Rubus egeniflorus, commonly known as the Egeni's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The fruit, a type of aggregate berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines for its tart flavor.
