Ravensara coriacea

Ravensara coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved raven's arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a rough texture. The plant produces striking, arrow-shaped leaves and distinctive inflorescences that resemble a raven's wing. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ravensara coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
