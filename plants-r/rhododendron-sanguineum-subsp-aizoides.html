Rhododendron sanguineum subsp. aizoides

Rhododendron sanguineum subsp.

aizoides, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western coast of North America. It is characterized by its showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and natural landscapes.
