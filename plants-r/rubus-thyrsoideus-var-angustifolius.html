Rubus thyrsoideus var. angustifolius

Rubus thyrsoideus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
