Rhynchosia confertiflora

Rhynchosia confertiflora, commonly known as the denseflower cowpea, is a herbaceous legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with stipules and is characterized by its raceme of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm climates.

Rhynchosia confertiflora is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
