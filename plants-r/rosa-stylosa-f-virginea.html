Rosa stylosa f. virginea

Rosa stylosa f. virginea, commonly known as the Virgin's rose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub-like species with thorned stems and compound leaves consisting of five to seven leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a woody structure, pinnate arrangement of leaves, and an extensive root system.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
