Rubus hirtus var. microthyrsus

Rubus hirtus var. microthyrsus, commonly known as the small-thorned blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. Its flowers are typically white or pink and appear in clusters.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
