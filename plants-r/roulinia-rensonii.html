Roulinia rensonii

Roulinia rensonii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a complex structure that aids in pollination by insects.

Roulinia rensonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
