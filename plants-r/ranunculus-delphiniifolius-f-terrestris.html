Ranunculus delphiniifolius f. terrestris

Ranunculus delphiniifolius f. terrestris, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb with a distinctive botanical structure.

It features a mat-forming growth habit, with stems that lie close to the ground and produce numerous small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers have five sepals and five petals, characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
