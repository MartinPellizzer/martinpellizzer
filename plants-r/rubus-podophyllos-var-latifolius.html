Rubus podophyllos var. latifolius

Rubus podophyllos var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive, serrated edge. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
