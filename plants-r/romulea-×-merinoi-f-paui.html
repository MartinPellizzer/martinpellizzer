Romulea × merinoi f. paui

Romulea × merinoi f. paui, commonly known as the hybrid romulea, is a rare and ornamental plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Romulea merinoi and Romulea bulbosa, characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers. The plant produces elegant, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of South Africa and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its rarity and horticultural appeal, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
