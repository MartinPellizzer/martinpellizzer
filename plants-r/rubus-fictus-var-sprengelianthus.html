Rubus fictus var. sprengelianthus

Rubus fictus var. sprengelianthus, commonly known as the Sprengelianthus raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has a sprawling growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
