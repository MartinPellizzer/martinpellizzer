Rostellularia humilis

Rostellularia humilis, commonly known as the low rostellularia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive, tubular corolla.

Rostellularia humilis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habit.
