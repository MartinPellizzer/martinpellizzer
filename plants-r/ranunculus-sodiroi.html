Ranunculus sodiroi

Ranunculus sodiroi, commonly known as the Andean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic plants. The species is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
