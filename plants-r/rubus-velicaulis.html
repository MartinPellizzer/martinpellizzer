Rubus velicaulis

Rubus velicaulis, commonly known as the large-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its flowers are white to pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Rubus velicaulis is valued for its edible berries, which are often used in jams and desserts.
