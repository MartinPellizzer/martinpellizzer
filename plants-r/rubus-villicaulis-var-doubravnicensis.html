Rubus villicaulis var. doubravnicensis

Rubus villicaulis var. doubravnicensis, commonly known as the Doubravnica blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces dark purple to black berries. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often used in local culinary traditions.
