Rubus hirtus subsp. caucasicus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian brambles, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its berries.
