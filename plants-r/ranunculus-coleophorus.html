Ranunculus coleophorus

Ranunculus coleophorus, commonly known as the coastal buttercup, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shorelines. The plant features showy yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it has a short, stout stem.

Ranunculus coleophorus is adapted to salt-laden environments and is often found in dunes or coastal grasslands.
