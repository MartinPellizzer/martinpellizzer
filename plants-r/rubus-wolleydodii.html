Rubus wolleydodii

Rubus wolleydodii, commonly known as the Wollydodii blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
