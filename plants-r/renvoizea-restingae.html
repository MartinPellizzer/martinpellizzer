Renvoizea restingae

Renvoizea restingae, commonly known as the Restinga morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in sandy, nutrient-poor soils typical of the restinga ecosystem.

Renvoizea restingae plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
