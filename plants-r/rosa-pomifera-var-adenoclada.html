Rosa pomifera var. adenoclada

Rosa pomifera var. adenoclada, commonly known as the apple rose, is a rare and unique species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, apple-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant produces fragrant pink to white flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it has a woody, shrub-like structure.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
