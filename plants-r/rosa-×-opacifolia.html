Rosa × opacifolia

Rosa × opacifolia, commonly known as the pink musk rose, is a hybrid species of rose native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers with a subtle musky fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Rosa × opacifolia is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
