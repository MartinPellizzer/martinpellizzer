Rosa × coronata

Rosa × coronata, commonly known as the Crowned Rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa gallica and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Rosa × coronata is also valued for its fragrant blooms and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
