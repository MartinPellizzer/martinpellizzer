Rhynchospora cariciformis

Rhynchospora cariciformis, commonly known as the broad-leaved tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Rhynchospora cariciformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
