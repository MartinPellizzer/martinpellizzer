Rosa arvensis subvar. rusticana

Rosa arvensis subvar. rusticana, commonly known as the field rose or rustic rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, often thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is well adapted to a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its aromatic properties.
