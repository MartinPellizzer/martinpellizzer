Renealmia recurvata

Renealmia recurvata, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, arching stems that often form a dense mat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are a defining feature of grasses. Its leaves are narrow and have a slightly zigzag or recurved appearance, giving it its common name.

Renealmia recurvata is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments.
