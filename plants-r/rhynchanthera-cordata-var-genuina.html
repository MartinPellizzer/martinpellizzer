Rhynchanthera cordata var. genuina

Rhynchanthera cordata var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rhynchanthera, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
