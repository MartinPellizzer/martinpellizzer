Rubus rivularis f. pruinosus

Rubus rivularis f. pruinosus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry.

This plant is a flowering shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The leaves are typically alternate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found along stream banks and in woodland areas.

The fruit is edible, though its flavor is generally described as tart and less sweet compared to other blackberry varieties.
