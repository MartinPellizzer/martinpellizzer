Rhododendron sinogrande

Rhododendron sinogrande, commonly known as the Chinese grand rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly in the provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils.

Rhododendron sinogrande is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
