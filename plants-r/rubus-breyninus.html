Rubus breyninus

Rubus breyninus, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
