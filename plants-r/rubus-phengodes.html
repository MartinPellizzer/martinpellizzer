Rubus phengodes

Rubus phengodes, commonly known as the Phengodes bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The fruit, a red, aggregate berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
