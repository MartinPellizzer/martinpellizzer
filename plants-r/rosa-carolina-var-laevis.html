Rosa carolina var. laevis

Rosa carolina var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and open fields, contributing to local biodiversity.
