Rubus spadix

Rubus spadix, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry plant, is a species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are followed by the development of its edible berries.
