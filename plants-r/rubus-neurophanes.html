Rubus neurophanes

Rubus neurophanes, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic value.
