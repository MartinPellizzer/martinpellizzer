Rosa caryophyllacea var. scepusiensis

Rosa caryophyllacea var. scepusiensis, commonly known as the Scepusian rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The leaves are dark green, oval-shaped, and arranged alternately along the stems. This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
