Ranunculus auricomus subsp. apertisinus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

apertisinus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five petals and numerous stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in sunny to partially shaded conditions.
