Raphanus raphanistrum subsp. landra

Raphanus raphanistrum subsp.

landra, commonly known as wild radish, is a biennial or annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features a taproot system with a rough, hairy surface and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its fruits are long, slender pods containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

The species is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
