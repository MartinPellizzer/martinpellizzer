Rhizophora Mucronata Var. Stylosa

Rhizophora mucronata var. stylosa is a unique mangrove species known for its remarkable adaptations to tidal environments. This variant of the common mangrove tree thrives in brackish water and can tolerate high salinity levels. Its roots form intricate structures that stabilize coastal soils and prevent erosion. The plant plays a vital role in supporting marine and terrestrial ecosystems. It is an essential component of mangrove forests found in tropical and subtropical regions.
