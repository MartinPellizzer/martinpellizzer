Rubus arduennensis var. reduncus

Rubus arduennensis var. reduncus, commonly known as the reduncus blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually serrated and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
