Rubus rusticanus var. petiolulatus

Rubus rusticanus var. petiolulatus, commonly known as the slender-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and riparian areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
