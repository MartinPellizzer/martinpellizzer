Ranunculus pseudomontanus

Ranunculus pseudomontanus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is known for its ability to survive in harsh, high-altitude environments.

Ranunculus pseudomontanus is often found in areas with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
