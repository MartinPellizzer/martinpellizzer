Rebutia cardenasiana

Rebutia cardenasiana, commonly known as the Mexican snowball, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of white, spherical flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a short, cylindrical stem covered in spines, and its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
