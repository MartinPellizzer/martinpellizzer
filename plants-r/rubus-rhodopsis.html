Rubus rhodopsis

Rubus rhodopsis, commonly known as the red-flowered bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces showy, red-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, aggregate berry that ripens in the fall.

Rubus rhodopsis is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
