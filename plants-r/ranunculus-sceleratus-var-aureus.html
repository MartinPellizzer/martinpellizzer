Ranunculus sceleratus var. aureus

Ranunculus sceleratus var. aureus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are usually hairy.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
