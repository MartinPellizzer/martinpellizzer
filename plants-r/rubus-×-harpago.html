Rubus × harpago

Rubus × harpago, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between blackberry (Rubus fruticosus) and raspberry (Rubus idaeus). This species exhibits characteristics of both parent plants, featuring thorny stems and aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. The leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant is often found in temperate regions.

It is cultivated for its flavorful berries, which are used in culinary and commercial applications.
