Rumohra wallichii

Rumohra wallichii, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a large fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in the ecological structure of its habitat.

The fronds are arranged in a distinctive, cascading pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.
