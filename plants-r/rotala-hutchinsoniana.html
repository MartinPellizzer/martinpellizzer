Rotala hutchinsoniana

Rotala hutchinsoniana, commonly known as the red root water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red roots and narrow, grass-like leaves that float just below the water surface. This plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and marshes. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Rotala hutchinsoniana is popular in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
