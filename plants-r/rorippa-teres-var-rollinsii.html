Rorippa teres var. rollinsii

Rorippa teres var. rollinsii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watercress, is a flowering plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often near streams and ponds.
