Romulea columnae subsp. grandiscapa

Romulea columnae subsp.

grandiscapa, commonly known as the giant columnar romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

This subspecies is notable for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
