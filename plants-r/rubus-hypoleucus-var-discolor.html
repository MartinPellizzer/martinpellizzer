Rubus hypoleucus var. discolor

Rubus hypoleucus var. discolor, commonly known as the white-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its pale to white berries, which contrast with the typical dark coloration of other Rubus species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its unique fruit color and is often cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
