Rhododendron pycnocladum

Rhododendron pycnocladum, commonly known as the dense-leaved rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates. It is often found in forest understories where it forms part of the native flora.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
