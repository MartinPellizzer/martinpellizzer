Rosa vosagiaca var. subleiostylis

Rosa vosagiaca var. subleiostylis, commonly known as the Vosagian rose, is a rare and distinctive variety of rose native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has a compact, shrub-like form with thorny stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers have a subtle fragrance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
