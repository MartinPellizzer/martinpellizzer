Rubus brunneri

Rubus brunneri, commonly known as Brunner's raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and the plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.
