Rosa parnassi

Rosa parnassi, commonly known as the Parnassian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. This species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in its native habitat.
