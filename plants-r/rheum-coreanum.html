Rheum coreanum

Rheum coreanum, commonly known as Korean rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often hairy leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces tall, flowering stalks with small, greenish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its roots are rich in anthraquinones and have been used in traditional medicine.

Rheum coreanum is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
