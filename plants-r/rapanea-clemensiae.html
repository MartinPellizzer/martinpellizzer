Rapanea clemensiae

Rapanea clemensiae, commonly known as the Malagasy laurustin, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurel trees and bay trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are bright red drupes, which are edible and often consumed by local wildlife.

Rapanea clemensiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
