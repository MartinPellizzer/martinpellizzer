Ranunculus affinis subsp. wilanderi

Ranunculus affinis subsp.

wilanderi, commonly known as the Wilander's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands.

The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
