Rosa scabrata f. missniensis

Rosa scabrata f. missniensis, commonly known as the Missinippi rose, is a botanical variant of the rugose rose.

It is characterized by its rugged, thorny stems and dark green, slightly serrated leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. This subspecies is native to the eastern United States, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
