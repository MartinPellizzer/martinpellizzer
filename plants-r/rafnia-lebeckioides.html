Rafnia lebeckioides

Rafnia lebeckioides, commonly known as the false tulip tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rafnia lebeckioides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is relatively hardy in USDA zones 6 through 9.
