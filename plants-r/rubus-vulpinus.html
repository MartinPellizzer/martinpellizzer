Rubus vulpinus

Rubus vulpinus, commonly known as the foxberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed areas. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red, edible berries in the summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five leaflets, and the plant has thorny stems.

Rubus vulpinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
