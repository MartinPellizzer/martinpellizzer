Rosa communis var. mucronulata

Rosa communis var. mucronulata, commonly known as the Japanese rose or the everblooming rose, is a variety of the common rose that is characterized by its compact growth habit and continuous blooming throughout the growing season.

It features fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that are typically borne on short, sturdy stems. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blooms. It is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom repeatedly without requiring frequent deadheading.
