Rubus × voronensis

Rubus × voronensis, commonly known as the Voronov's blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to the Far East, particularly regions of Russia and China, where it thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit yield, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial purposes.
