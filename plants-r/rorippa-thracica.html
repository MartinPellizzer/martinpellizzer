Rorippa thracica

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in racemes, and are accompanied by four sepals and four petals.

Rorippa thracica is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, freshwater environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
