Rosa megalantha

Rosa megalantha, commonly known as the giant rose, is a striking species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, with deep red petals and a prominent yellow center. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is a popular subject in horticultural studies.
