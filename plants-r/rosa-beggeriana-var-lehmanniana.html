Rosa beggeriana var. lehmanniana

Rosa beggeriana var. lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler, arid environments.
