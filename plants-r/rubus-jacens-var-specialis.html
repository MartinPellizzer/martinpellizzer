Rubus jacens var. specialis

Rubus jacens var. specialis, commonly known as the special raspberry.

This variant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the cultivar. The plant is native to certain regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticultural and ecological studies.
