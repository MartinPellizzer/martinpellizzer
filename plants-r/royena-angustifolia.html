Royena angustifolia

Royena angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed royal vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach several meters in length, often climbing trees or shrubs. The leaves are typically elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in axillary inflorescences, and are often yellow or greenish in color.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
