Rubus × hemimicroanchus

Rubus × hemimicroanchus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and raspberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

The fruit is valued for its flavor and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
