Rotang albus

Rotang albus, commonly known as white mangrove, is a species of mangrove tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its white, milky sap and its ability to thrive in saline and brackish water environments. The plant has prop roots that help stabilize the soil and prevent erosion. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth texture.

Rotang albus plays an important role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
