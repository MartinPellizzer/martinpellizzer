Rosa intermedia

Rosa intermedia, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, dense foliage, and clusters of fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. Rosa intermedia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens and landscaping.

Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and thorned stems.
