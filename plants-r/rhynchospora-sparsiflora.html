Rhynchospora sparsiflora

Rhynchospora sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Rhynchospora sparsiflora plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
