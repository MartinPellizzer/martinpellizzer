Rubus hebecaulis var. gravetii

Rubus hebecaulis var. gravetii, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

This variant is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, dark purple berries. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are white and clustered in groups of three, typical of the Rubus genus.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in local ecological restoration projects.
