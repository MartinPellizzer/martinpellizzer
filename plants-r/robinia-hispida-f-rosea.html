Robinia hispida f. rosea

Robinia hispida f. rosea, commonly known as the rosea form of the honeylocust, is a cultivar characterized by its distinctive pink to rose-colored flowers.

It is a deciduous legume with compound leaves that are typically trifoliate, featuring a thorn at the base. The plant produces small, green pods that contain flattened, oval seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental in gardens and landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
