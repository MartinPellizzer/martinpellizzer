Rosa canina var. implexa

Rosa canina var. implexa, commonly known as the bramble rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Rosa canina var. implexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
