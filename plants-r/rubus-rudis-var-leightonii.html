Rubus rudis var. leightonii

Rubus rudis var. leightonii, commonly known as the Leighton's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or thicket environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest for both horticulture and ecological studies.
