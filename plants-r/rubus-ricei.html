Rubus ricei

Rubus ricei, commonly known as the Rice blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible when ripe but are generally tart and often used in jams or wines.
