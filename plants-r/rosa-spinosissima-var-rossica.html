Rosa spinosissima var. rossica

Rosa spinosissima var. rossica, commonly known as the Russian prickly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red and edible, often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
