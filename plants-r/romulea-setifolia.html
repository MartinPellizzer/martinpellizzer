Romulea setifolia

Romulea setifolia, commonly known as the slender-flowered romulea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, nodding flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers with prominent, spurred tepals that are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Romulea setifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
