Ranunculus trivialis

Ranunculus trivialis, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooming habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers with five to eight petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a common wildflower and a potential weed.
