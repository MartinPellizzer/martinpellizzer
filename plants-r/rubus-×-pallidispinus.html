Rubus × pallidispinus

Rubus × pallidispinus, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
