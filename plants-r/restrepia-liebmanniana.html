Restrepia liebmanniana

Restrepia liebmanniana, commonly known as the Liebmann's restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and they bloom during the cooler months.

Restrepia liebmanniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
