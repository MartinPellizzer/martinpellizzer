Ranunculus digitatifolius

Ranunculus digitatifolius, commonly known as the fingerleaf buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It is characterized by its short stature and is often found in rocky or clay soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
