Rosa × communis var. subintrans

Rosa × communis var. subintrans, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its ability to produce flowers throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for gardeners. The plant typically features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually fragrant and come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white.

This variety is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
