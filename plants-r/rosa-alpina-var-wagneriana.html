Rosa alpina var. wagneriana

Rosa alpina var. wagneriana, commonly known as the Wagner's Alpine Rose, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
