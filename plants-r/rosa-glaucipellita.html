Rosa glaucipellita

Rosa glaucipellita, commonly known as the Glaucous-leaved Rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its stems are usually thorny, and the leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
