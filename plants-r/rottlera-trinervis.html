Rottlera trinervis

Rottlera trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined rottlera, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have three prominent veins, giving it its common name.

Rottlera trinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
