Rubus × similicuneatus

Rubus × similicuneatus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry or trailing blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows as a shrub, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered with fine hairs.

The fruit, which is usually black when ripe, is valued for its flavor and is used in culinary and commercial applications.
