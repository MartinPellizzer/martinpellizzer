Ruschia bipapillata

Ruschia bipapillata, commonly known as the papillate Ruschia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vibrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique leaf texture, with raised papillae, gives it a distinctive appearance and aids in water retention.
