Ranunculus jacquemontii

Ranunculus jacquemontii, commonly known as Jacquemont's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often adorned with a distinctive central pattern. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
