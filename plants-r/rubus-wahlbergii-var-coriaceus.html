Rubus wahlbergii var. coriaceus

Rubus wahlbergii var. coriaceus, commonly known as the Wahlberg's blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
