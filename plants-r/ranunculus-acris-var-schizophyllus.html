Ranunculus acris var. schizophyllus

Ranunculus acris var. schizophyllus, commonly known as the dividedleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and divided, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
