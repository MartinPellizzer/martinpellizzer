Rubus tomentosus var. griseus

Rubus tomentosus var. griseus, commonly known as the gray-haired raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and leaves with a similar fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
