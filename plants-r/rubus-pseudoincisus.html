Rubus pseudoincisus

Rubus pseudoincisus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil.
