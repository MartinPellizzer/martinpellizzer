Rosa fedorovii

Rosa fedorovii, commonly known as the Fedorov's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, simple leaves with serrated edges, and an aromatic scent.

Rosa fedorovii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
