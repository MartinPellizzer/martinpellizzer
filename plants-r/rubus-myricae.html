Rubus myricae

Rubus myricae, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red when ripe. These berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and desserts.

Rubus myricae thrives in moist, wooded areas and is an important plant in local ecosystems.
