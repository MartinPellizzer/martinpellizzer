Rumex bucephalophorus subvar. hirtus

Rumex bucephalophorus subvar. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, wavy, and hairy leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

The hairy dock is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
