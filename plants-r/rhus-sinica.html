Rhus sinica

Rhus sinica, commonly known as Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with 11 to 31 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are a key feature of its botanical description. Rhus sinica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
