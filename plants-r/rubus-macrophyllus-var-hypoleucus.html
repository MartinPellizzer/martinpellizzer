Rubus macrophyllus var. hypoleucus

Rubus macrophyllus var. hypoleucus, commonly known as the white-leaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically pale green to white in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are generally red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
