Rosa laetecolorans

Rosa laetecolorans, commonly known as the sweetheart rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces rose hips that are red-orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rosa laetecolorans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
