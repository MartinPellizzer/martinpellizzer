Rumex aquitanicus

Rumex aquitanicus, commonly known as the Aquitanian sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the underside, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour taste similar to rhubarb.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological habitats and as a food source for various insects.
