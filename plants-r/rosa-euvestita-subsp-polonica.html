Rosa euvestita subsp. polonica

Rosa euvestita subsp.

polonica, commonly known as the Polish rose, is a subspecies native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include oval, serrated leaves and a compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
