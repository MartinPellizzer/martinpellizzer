Rhodospatha spruceana

Rhodospatha spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's rhododendron, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often found in cloud forests. Its botanical features include dark green, elliptical leaves and pink to purple flowers with five petals.

Rhodospatha spruceana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
