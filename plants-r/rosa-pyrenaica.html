Rosa pyrenaica

Rosa pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant produces pink to red flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

Rosa pyrenaica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
