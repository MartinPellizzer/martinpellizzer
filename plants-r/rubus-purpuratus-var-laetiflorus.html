Rubus purpuratus var. laetiflorus

Rubus purpuratus var. laetiflorus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible blackberries in late summer. Its fruit is highly sought after for its sweet flavor and is often used in jams and desserts.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
