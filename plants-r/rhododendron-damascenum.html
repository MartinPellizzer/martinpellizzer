Rhododendron damascenum

Rhododendron damascenum, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a faint fragrance. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The Persian rose is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
