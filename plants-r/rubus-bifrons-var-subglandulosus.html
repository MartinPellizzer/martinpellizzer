Rubus bifrons var. subglandulosus

Rubus bifrons var. subglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing vegetative growth and the second year bearing fruit. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The flowers are white and clustered in umbels, followed by red, hairy fruits that are edible.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
