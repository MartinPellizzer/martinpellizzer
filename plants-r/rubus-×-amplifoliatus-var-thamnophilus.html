Rubus × amplifoliatus var. thamnophilus

Rubus × amplifoliatus var. thamnophilus, commonly known as the thamnophilus blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
