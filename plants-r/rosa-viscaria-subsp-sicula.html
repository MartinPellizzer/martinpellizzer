Rosa viscaria subsp. sicula

Rosa viscaria subsp.

sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant produces rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional herbal remedies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its root system is fibrous and adapted to well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
