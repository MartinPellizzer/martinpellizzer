Rubus koehleri var. antonii

Rubus koehleri var. antonii, commonly known as the Antonii blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus koehleri species native to Central America.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
