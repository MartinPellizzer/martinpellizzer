Rosa communis var. schottiana

Rosa communis var. schottiana, commonly known as the Schott's rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are covered in a fine layer of down. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
