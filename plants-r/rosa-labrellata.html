Rosa labrellata

Rosa labrellata, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or rose of the Amazon, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant has a woody stem covered in thorns.

Rosa labrellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
