Rosa × barclayana

Rosa × barclayana, commonly known as the Barclay's rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It is native to the United States, particularly in the eastern regions, and thrives in well-drained soil.

Rosa × barclayana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
