Ranunculus ternatus var. lutchuensis

Ranunculus ternatus var. lutchuensis, commonly known as the Lutchu water buttercup, is a variant of the water buttercup native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant often thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
