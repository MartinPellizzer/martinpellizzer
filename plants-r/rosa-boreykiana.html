Rosa boreykiana

Rosa boreykiana, commonly known as the Siberian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates with cold winters.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa boreykiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its beauty and medicinal properties.
