Rubus longicaulis

Rubus longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Rubus longicaulis is typically found in temperate regions, growing in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
