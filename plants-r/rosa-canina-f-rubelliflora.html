Rosa canina f. rubelliflora

Rosa canina f. rubelliflora, commonly known as the red-flowered dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive deep red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional herbal remedies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
