Ribes × urceolatum

Ribes × urceolatum, commonly known as the hybrid currant or redcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of gooseberries and currants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets and its clusters of small, edible berries that range in color from red to black. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The flowers are typically yellow and appear in early summer, preceding the fruiting season.
