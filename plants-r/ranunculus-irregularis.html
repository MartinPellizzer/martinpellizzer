Ranunculus irregularis

Ranunculus irregularis, commonly known as the irregular buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and has a creeping rhizome system. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus irregularis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
