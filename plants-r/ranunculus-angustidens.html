Ranunculus angustidens

Ranunculus angustidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to eleven petals. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small seeds.

Ranunculus angustidens is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
