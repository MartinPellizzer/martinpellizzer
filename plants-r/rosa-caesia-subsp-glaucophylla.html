Rosa caesia subsp. glaucophylla

Rosa caesia subsp.

glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophylla rose, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its pale green, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial shrub that produces hips, which are edible and used in culinary applications.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens.
