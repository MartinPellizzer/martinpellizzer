Rhododendron brookeanum var. flavum

Rhododendron brookeanum var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the bright yellow blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and is often cultivated for its unique coloration.
