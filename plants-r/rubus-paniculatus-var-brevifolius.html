Rubus paniculatus var. brevifolius

Rubus paniculatus var. brevifolius, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
