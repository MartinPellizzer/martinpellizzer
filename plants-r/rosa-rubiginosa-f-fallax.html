Rosa rubiginosa f. fallax

Rosa rubiginosa f. fallax, commonly known as the fallax form of the rough rose, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery foliage.

It produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
