Ribes pubescens

Ribes pubescens, commonly known as the downy gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Ribes pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
