Rubus fuscicortex

Rubus fuscicortex, commonly known as the dark-barked raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is a hairy, red aggregate that is often used in culinary applications.
