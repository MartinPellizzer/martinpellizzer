Rubus × micranthelus

Rubus × micranthelus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, while the fruit is small, dark purple, and highly aromatic.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.
