Remijia ulei

Remijia ulei, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a rare and delicate fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped fronds that are often covered in a fine, golden-colored bloom. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its unique morphology makes it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and conservation programs.

Due to habitat loss, Remijia ulei is considered endangered, highlighting the need for its protection.
