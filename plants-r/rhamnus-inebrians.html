Rhamnus inebrians

Rhamnus inebrians, commonly known as the inebriating buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue-black berries that are edible but have a strong, bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
