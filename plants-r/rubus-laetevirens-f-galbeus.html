Rubus laetevirens f. galbeus

Rubus laetevirens f. galbeus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive red fruits.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit develops into a dark purple to black berry.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.
