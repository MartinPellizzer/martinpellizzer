Racosperma pelophilum

Racosperma pelophilum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. It produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

Racosperma pelophilum is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland and heathland environments.
