Rubus corylifolius subsp. phylloglotta

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

phylloglotta, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles.
