Rhodax lunulatus

Rhodax lunulatus, commonly known as the moon-leaf plant, is a unique species native to specific regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, silvery-white leaves that have a distinctive crescent-shaped pattern. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.

Due to its rare nature, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
