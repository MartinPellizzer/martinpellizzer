Rosa canina f. bornmuelleri

Rosa canina f. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals, and appear in clusters. The plant produces small, oval hips that are orange-red in color and contain seeds.

It is often found in woodland edges and open habitats, thriving in well-drained soil.
