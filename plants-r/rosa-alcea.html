Rosa alcea

Rosa alcea, commonly known as the sweet william rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and it typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Rosa alcea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
