Racosperma incurvum

Racosperma incurvum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and bipinnate, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Racosperma incurvum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
