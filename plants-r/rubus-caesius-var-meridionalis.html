Rubus caesius var. meridionalis

Rubus caesius var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features prickly stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized in certain regions.
