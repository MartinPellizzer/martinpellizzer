Rubus rhenanus

Rubus rhenanus, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, similar to blackberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

The berries are edible and have been used historically in culinary and medicinal applications.
