Rubus rusticanus f. ellipticus

Rubus rusticanus f. ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves and dark red to black fruits.

It is native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in local cuisines.

The elliptic form of its leaves distinguishes it from other Rubus species.
