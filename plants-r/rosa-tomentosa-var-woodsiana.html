Rosa tomentosa var. woodsiana

Rosa tomentosa var. woodsiana, commonly known as the Woods' woolly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish pubescence covering its stems and leaves, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
