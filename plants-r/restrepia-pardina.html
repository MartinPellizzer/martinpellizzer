Restrepia pardina

Restrepia pardina, commonly known as the red-tailed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive reddish-brown inflorescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a contrasting red or brown lip. Restrepia pardina thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded forest understories.

Its unique coloration and elegant appearance make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
