Rosa mollis subsp. nigrans

Rosa mollis subsp.

nigrans, commonly known as the blackstem rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family. It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant is native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces edible hips that contain seeds.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
