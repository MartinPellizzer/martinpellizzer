Rosularia jaccardiana

Rosularia jaccardiana, commonly known as the Jaccard's roseleaf, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features hairy, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and appear in clusters during the summer months.

Rosularia jaccardiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in rocky, well-drained soils.
