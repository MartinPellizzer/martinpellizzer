Rubus laxatus

Rubus laxatus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Rubus laxatus is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
