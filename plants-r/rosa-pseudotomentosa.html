Rosa pseudotomentosa

Rosa pseudotomentosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and foliage. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Rosa pseudotomentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
