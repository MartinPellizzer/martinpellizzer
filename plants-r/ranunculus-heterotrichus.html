Ranunculus heterotrichus

Ranunculus heterotrichus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five to eight petals and a hairy stem that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Ranunculus heterotrichus is a perennial species that produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small seeds.
