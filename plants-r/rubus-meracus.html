Rubus meracus

Rubus meracus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
