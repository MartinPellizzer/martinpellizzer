Rubus × hebetidens

Rubus × hebetidens, commonly known as the blackberry dewberry, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, wooded areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex hybrid origins and its role in local ecosystems.
