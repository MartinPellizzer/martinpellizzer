Rhododendron kiyosumense subsp.

mayebaraeohsumiense, commonly known as the Kiyosumi rhododendron, is a rare and endemic species native to Japan. It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, usually in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
