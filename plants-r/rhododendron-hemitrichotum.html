Rhododendron hemitrichotum

Rhododendron hemitrichotum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and leathery, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
