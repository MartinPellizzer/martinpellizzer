Restrepia filamentosa

Restrepia filamentosa, commonly known as the filamentous restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features slender, wiry stems and elongated leaves that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often with a subtle fragrance, and are arranged in loose clusters.

Restrepia filamentosa is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental appeal.
