Rubus fruticosus subsp. koehleri

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

koehleri, commonly known as the Koehler's blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
