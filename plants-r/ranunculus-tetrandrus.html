Ranunculus tetrandrus

Ranunculus tetrandrus, commonly known as the four-stamen buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals and four stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Ranunculus tetrandrus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
