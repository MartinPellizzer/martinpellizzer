Rosa × dumetorum f. semiglauca

Rosa × dumetorum f. semiglauca, commonly known as the alpine rose or shrub rose, is a hybrid species characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are usually dark green with a slightly hairy underside, and the stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs. This plant is well adapted to alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
