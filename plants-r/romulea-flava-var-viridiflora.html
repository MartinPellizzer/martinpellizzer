Romulea flava var. viridiflora

Romulea flava var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and striking yellow or greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, tuberous bulbs that store nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in exotic flora.
