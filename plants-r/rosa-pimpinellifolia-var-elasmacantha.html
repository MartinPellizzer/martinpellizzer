Rosa pimpinellifolia var. elasmacantha

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. elasmacantha, commonly known as the rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
