Rinxostylis repanda

Rinxostylis repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic habitats. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green, with a distinctive arrowhead shape. Its small, white flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the warmer months.

Rinxostylis repanda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
