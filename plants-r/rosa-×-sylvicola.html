Rosa × sylvicola

Rosa × sylvicola, commonly known as the woodland rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa serotina.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features upright stems with thorns and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets that are hairy beneath, and it has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil.

Rosa × sylvicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
