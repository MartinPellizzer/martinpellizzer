Rosa dumalis var. malmundariensis

Rosa dumalis var. malmundariensis, commonly known as the Malm's shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, upright growth with dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are borne singly or in small clusters on short pedicels, and the plant is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
