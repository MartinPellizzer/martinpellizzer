Rubus crudelis

Rubus crudelis, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically growing in rocky or woodland areas. The plant produces small, thorny stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it blooms with white or pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit is edible but often has a tart, sharp flavor.
