Ranunculus auricomus var. incisifolius

Ranunculus auricomus var. incisifolius, commonly known as the cut-leaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens. The leaves are deeply lobed or dissected, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
