Rosa × collina var. cinerea

Rosa × collina var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardiness in cooler climates.
