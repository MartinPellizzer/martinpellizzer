Racosperma unciferum

Racosperma unciferum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Racosperma unciferum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
