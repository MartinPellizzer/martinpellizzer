Rorippa palustris var. dictyota

Rorippa palustris var. dictyota, commonly known as the watercress, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows in shallow water, wet soils, or along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features small, alternate, ovate leaves that are usually submerged or floating. It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is known for its peppery flavor.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage.
