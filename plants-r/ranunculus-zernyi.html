Ranunculus zernyi

Ranunculus zernyi, commonly known as the Zernyi buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic plants. The plant produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
