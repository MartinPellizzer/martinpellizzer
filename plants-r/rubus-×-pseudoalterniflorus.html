Rubus × pseudoalterniflorus

Rubus × pseudoalterniflorus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the evergreen blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (the alternateleaf blackberry). The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. Its botanical features include a complex genetic makeup that contributes to its unique fruit characteristics.

The plant is often cultivated for its flavorful, edible fruits and is valued in both commercial and home gardening settings.
