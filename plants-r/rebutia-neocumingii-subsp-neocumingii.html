Rebutia neocumingii subsp. neocumingii

Rebutia neocumingii subsp.

neocumingii, commonly known as the Neocuming's Rebutia, is a succulent plant native to the deserts of southern Africa. It features small, globular to cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
