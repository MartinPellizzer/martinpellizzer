Racosperma chrysellum

Racosperma chrysellum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.
