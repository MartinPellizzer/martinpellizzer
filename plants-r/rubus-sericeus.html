Rubus sericeus

Rubus sericeus, commonly known as silverleaf raspberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Rubus sericeus is a member of the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and strawberries.
