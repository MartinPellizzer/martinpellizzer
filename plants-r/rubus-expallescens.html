Rubus expallescens

Rubus expallescens, commonly known as the creeping raspberry.

It is a species of bramble that is native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in early summer.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
