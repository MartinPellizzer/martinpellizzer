Rubus cuiedensis

Rubus cuiedensis, commonly known as the Cuiede raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, specifically found in parts of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins, while its flowers are white with five petals.

The plant is often found in dry, open habitats and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
