Rosa mollis f. intentidens

Rosa mollis f. intentidens, commonly known as the dense-flowered shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, dark green leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its dense growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
