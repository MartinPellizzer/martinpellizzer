Rhododendron occidentale var. sonomense

Rhododendron occidentale var. sonomense, commonly known as the Sonoma rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in forested areas. The plant features dense, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.

The species is also important for local wildlife, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
