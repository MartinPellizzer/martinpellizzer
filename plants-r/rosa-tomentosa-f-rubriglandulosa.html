Rosa tomentosa f. rubriglandulosa

Rosa tomentosa f. rubriglandulosa, commonly known as the red-glandular hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the hairy rose species.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and vibrant pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its stems and leaves are covered in fine, white to grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The red glandular structures on its stems and leaves are a notable feature that distinguishes this form from other Rosa species.
