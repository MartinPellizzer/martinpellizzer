Rosa clypeolaria

Rosa clypeolaria, commonly known as the shieldleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its distinctive shield-shaped leaves, which are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong, pleasant fragrance.

Rosa clypeolaria is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
