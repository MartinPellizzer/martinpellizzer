Ranunculus laxus

Ranunculus laxus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Ranunculaceae family.
