Rubus × praeflorens

Rubus × praeflorens, commonly known as the European blackberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus fruticosus and Rubus idaeus. This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are typically white or pale pink.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
