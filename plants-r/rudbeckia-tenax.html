Rudbeckia tenax

Rudbeckia tenax, commonly known as the ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy yellow-orange composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Rudbeckia tenax thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
