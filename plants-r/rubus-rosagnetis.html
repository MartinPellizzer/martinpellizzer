Rubus rosagnetis

Rubus rosagnetis, commonly known as the Rosagena blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a slightly tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
