Rhododendron degronianum var. amagianum

Rhododendron degronianum var. amagianum, commonly known as the Amagian rhododendron, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Amagian Mountains in Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky slopes or in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from deep pink to pale lavender.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
