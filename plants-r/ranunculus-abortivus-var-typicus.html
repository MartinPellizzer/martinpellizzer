Ranunculus abortivus var. typicus

Ranunculus abortivus var. typicus, commonly known as the abortive buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. It produces small, hairy fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
