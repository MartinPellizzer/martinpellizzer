Rosa afzeliana f. decurtata

Rosa afzeliana f. decurtata, commonly known as the short-stemmed rose, is a hybrid form of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more rounded foliage compared to its parent species. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a desirable addition to shrub borders and mixed flower beds.
