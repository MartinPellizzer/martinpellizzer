Restio implexus

Restio implexus, commonly known as the tangled restio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Restio implexus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or montane regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
