Rubus radula roseus

Rubus radula roseus, commonly known as the rosehip rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs. The plant produces small, rose-like flowers that are typically pink or white in color.

Its most notable feature is the red, berry-like fruit that develops from the flowers, which is rich in vitamin C. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the medicinal properties of its fruits.
