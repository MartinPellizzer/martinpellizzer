Rubus tephrodes var. ampliflorus

Rubus tephrodes var. ampliflorus, commonly known as the broad-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. Its fruits are aggregate berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
