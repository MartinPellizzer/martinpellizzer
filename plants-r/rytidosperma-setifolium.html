Rytidosperma setifolium

Rytidosperma setifolium, commonly known as the silky grass or slender-leaved rytidosperma, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Rytidosperma setifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
