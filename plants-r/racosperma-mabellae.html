Racosperma mabellae

Racosperma mabellae, commonly known as the Mabellae acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with slender, woody stems and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, contributing to nutrient cycling in its native habitat.
