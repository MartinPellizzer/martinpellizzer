Rosa glauca var. delasoiei

Rosa glauca var. delasoiei, commonly known as the Delasoie's blue rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pale blue to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and horticultural rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
