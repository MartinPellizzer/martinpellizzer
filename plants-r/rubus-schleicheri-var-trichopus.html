Rubus schleicheri var. trichopus

Rubus schleicheri var. trichopus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, resembling raspberries in appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
