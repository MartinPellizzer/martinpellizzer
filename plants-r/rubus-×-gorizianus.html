Rubus × gorizianus

Rubus × gorizianus, commonly known as the Gorizian raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically red or orange in color. The plant is native to the Alps and is often found in mountainous regions of Europe. It produces small, edible berries that are similar in taste to red raspberries.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
