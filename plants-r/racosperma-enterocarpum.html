Racosperma enterocarpum

Racosperma enterocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, typical of the genus Racosperma. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
