Ranunculus cuneatus

Ranunculus cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a green, hairy center. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Ranunculus cuneatus is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.
