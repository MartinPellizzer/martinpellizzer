Ranunculus lemmonii

Ranunculus lemmonii, commonly known as the Lemmon's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Ranunculus lemmonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
