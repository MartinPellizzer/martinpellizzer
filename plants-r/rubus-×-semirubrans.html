Rubus × semirubrans

Rubus × semirubrans, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valuable for culinary use.
