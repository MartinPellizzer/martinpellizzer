Rhododendron farinosum

Rhododendron farinosum, commonly known as the sticky rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces a distinctive sticky substance on its leaves and stems, which gives it its common name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates.

Rhododendron farinosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
