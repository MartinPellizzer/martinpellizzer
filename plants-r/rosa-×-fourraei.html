Rosa × fourraei

Rosa × fourraei, commonly known as the Alba rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine layer of hair. It is native to China and has been cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Rosa × fourraei is relatively hardy and adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates.
