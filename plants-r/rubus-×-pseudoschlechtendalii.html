Rubus × pseudoschlechtendalii

Rubus × pseudoschlechtendalii, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus chamaemorus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn red when mature, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
