Rosa coriifolia var. froebelii

Rosa coriifolia var. froebelii, commonly known as the Froebel's sweet briar, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a finely toothed margin. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
