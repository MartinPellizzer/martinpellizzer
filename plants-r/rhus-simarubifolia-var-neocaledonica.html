Rhus simarubifolia var. neocaledonica

Rhus simarubifolia var. neocaledonica, commonly known as the New Caledonia smooth sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical rainforests of New Caledonia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of dense forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
