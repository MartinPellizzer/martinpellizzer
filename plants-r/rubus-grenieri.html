Rubus grenieri

Rubus grenieri, commonly known as the Grenier's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
