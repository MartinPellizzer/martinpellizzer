Rubus carpaticus

Rubus carpaticus, commonly known as the Carpathian bramble, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in woodland and rocky habitats.

Rubus carpaticus plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
