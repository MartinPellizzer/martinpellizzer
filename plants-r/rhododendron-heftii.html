Rhododendron heftii

Rhododendron heftii, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high elevations. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil.

Rhododendron heftii is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
