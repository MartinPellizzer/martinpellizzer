Rubus chloroleucus

Rubus chloroleucus, commonly known as the greenleaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are clustered in the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
