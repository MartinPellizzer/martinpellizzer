Ranunculus polymorphus

Ranunculus polymorphus, commonly known as the variable buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with numerous petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic properties.

Ranunculus polymorphus exhibits morphological variation, which contributes to its common name.
