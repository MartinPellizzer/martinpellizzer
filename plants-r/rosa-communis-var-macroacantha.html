Rosa communis var. macroacantha

Rosa communis var. macroacantha, commonly known as the large-thorned rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its robust growth and distinctive, large thorns.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has serrated, dark green leaves and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its thorns provide natural protection, and it is often used in hedgerows or as a decorative plant in gardens.
