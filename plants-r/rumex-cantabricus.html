Rumex cantabricus

Rumex cantabricus, commonly known as the Cantabrian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are rhombic in shape and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters during the spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
