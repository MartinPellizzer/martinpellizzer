Ranunculus gunae

Ranunculus gunae, commonly known as the Andean buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high Andes of South America.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent, rounded sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its botanical characteristics include a short, stout rhizome and hairy stems.

Ranunculus gunae is adapted to cold, high-altitude conditions and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
