Ranunculus imitator

Ranunculus imitator, commonly known as the mimic buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has simple, trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Due to its resemblance to other ranunculus species, it is sometimes mistaken for a true buttercup.
