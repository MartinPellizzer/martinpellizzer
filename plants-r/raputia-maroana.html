Raputia maroana

Raputia maroana, commonly known as the Maroani orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Maroanta in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or among leaf litter. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.
