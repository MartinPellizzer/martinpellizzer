Rubus vermontanus var. ortivus

Rubus vermontanus var. ortivus, commonly known as the New England blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in moist, wooded areas. Its flowers are white and clustered in the axils of the leaves.

This variety is closely related to other Rubus species but is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and fruit characteristics.
