Rehmannia glutinosa f. lutea

Rehmannia glutinosa f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow rehmannia, is a variant of the Rehmannia glutinosa species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish flowers and distinctive orange-red roots, which are used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in Chinese herbal medicine.
