Racosperma dielsii

Racosperma dielsii, commonly known as the Darwin's cycad, is a species of cycad native to Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
