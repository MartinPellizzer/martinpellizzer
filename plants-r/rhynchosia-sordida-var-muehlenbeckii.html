Rhynchosia sordida var. muehlenbeckii

Rhynchosia sordida var. muehlenbeckii, commonly known as the dirty cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears cylindrical, hairy pods containing edible, angular seeds that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both agricultural and ecological purposes.
