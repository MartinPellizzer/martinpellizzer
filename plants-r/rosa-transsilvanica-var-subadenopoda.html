Rosa transsilvanica var. subadenopoda

Rosa transsilvanica var. subadenopoda, commonly known as the Transylvanian rose, is a botanical variant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers during the summer months. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
