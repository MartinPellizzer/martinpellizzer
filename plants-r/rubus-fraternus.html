Rubus fraternus

Rubus fraternus, commonly known as the brotherberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black in color, containing multiple seeds. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species.

The botanical characteristics of Rubus fraternus make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
