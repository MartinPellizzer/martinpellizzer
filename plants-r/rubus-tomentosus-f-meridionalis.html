Rubus tomentosus f. meridionalis

Rubus tomentosus f. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern brambles, is a variant of the brambles plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and hairy, grayish stems. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and role in supporting local wildlife.
