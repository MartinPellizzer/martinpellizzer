Ranunculus auricomus subsp. paltamoensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

paltamoensis, commonly known as the Paltamo buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure that aids in photosynthesis.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in wetland habitats.
