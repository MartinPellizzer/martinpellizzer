Rosa glauca var. puberula

Rosa glauca var. puberula, commonly known as the downy bluish rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bluish-green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant has a dense, bushy growth habit and produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in colder climates.
