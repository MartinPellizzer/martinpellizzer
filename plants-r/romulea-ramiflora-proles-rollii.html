Romulea ramiflora proles rollii

Romulea ramiflora proles rollii, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, golden-yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
