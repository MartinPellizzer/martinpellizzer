Rosa sepium gizellae

Rosa sepium gizellae, commonly known as the Gizella rose, is a rare and ornamental species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

The plant produces fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are dark green and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.
