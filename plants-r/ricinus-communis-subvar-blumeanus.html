Ricinus communis subvar. blumeanus

Ricinus communis subvar. blumeanus, commonly known as the Blume's castor oil plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces distinctive spiny capsules that contain toxic, oily seeds. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, reaching heights of up to several meters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage.
