Rhododendron forrestii subsp. forrestii

Rhododendron forrestii subsp.

forrestii, commonly known as the Yunnan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters.

The plant features broad, ovate leaves with a leathery texture and is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
