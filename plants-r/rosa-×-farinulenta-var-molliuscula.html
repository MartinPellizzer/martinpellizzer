Rosa × farinulenta var. molliuscula

Rosa × farinulenta var. molliuscula, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and fragrant pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and compact growth habit.
