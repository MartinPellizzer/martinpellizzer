Rubus permixtus

Rubus permixtus, commonly known as the mixed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
