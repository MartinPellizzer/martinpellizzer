Racosperma capillare

Racosperma capillare, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-covered stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Racosperma capillare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
