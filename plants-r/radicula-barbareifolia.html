Radicula barbareifolia

Radicula barbareifolia, commonly known as the mountain rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, pink to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has grey-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found on rocky slopes or among gravelly ground.

Radicula barbareifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
