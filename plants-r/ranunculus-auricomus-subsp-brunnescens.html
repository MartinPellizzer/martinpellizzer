Ranunculus auricomus subsp. brunnescens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

brunnescens, commonly known as the brown-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features palmately divided leaves with a distinctive brownish hue, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals.

The plant produces a cluster of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
