Rubus briareus

Rubus briareus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a vigorous shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
