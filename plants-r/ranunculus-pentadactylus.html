Ranunculus pentadactylus

Ranunculus pentadactylus, commonly known as the five-finger buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its high-altitude environment, with compact growth and deep root systems.
