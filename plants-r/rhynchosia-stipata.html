Rhynchosia stipata

Rhynchosia stipata, commonly known as the hairy bean or stippled bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Rhynchosia stipata is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
