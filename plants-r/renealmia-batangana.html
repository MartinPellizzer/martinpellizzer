Renealmia batangana

Renealmia batangana, commonly known as the Batangana palm, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, lowland areas and is adapted to humid climates. Its roots are fibrous and help in stabilizing the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into clustered fruits, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
