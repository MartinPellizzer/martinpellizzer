Rosa glauca subvar. adenophora

Rosa glauca subvar. adenophora, commonly known as the blue rose or blue wild rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has silvery-hairy leaves and stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
