Rosa pendulina f. sphaerocarpa

Rosa pendulina f. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe rose, is a unique cultivar of the rose family characterized by its spherical, tightly clustered flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and the formation of dense, round inflorescences that resemble small globes. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The globe rose is a hardy shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
