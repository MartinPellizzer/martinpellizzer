Rubus niveus var. concolor

Rubus niveus var. concolor, commonly known as the white-veined blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white-veined leaves and small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
