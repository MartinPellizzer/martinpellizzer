Rubus corylifolius var. angiocarpus

Rubus corylifolius var. angiocarpus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, dark red berries that are edible. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in woodland and shrubland habitats. It has a long growing season and is known for its ornamental value.

The berries are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a food source.
