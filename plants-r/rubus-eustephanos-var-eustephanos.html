Rubus eustephanos var. eustephanos

Rubus eustephanos var. eustephanos, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white with five petals.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
