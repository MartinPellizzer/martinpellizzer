Rhododendron searleanum

Rhododendron searleanum, commonly known as the Searleanum rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to deep red.

Rhododendron searleanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
