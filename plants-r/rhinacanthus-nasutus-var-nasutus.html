Rhinacanthus nasutus var. nasutus

Rhinacanthus nasutus var. nasutus, commonly known as the horse's head tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curved leaves that resemble a horse's head, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some horticultural significance in tropical regions.
