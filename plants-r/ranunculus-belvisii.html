Ranunculus belvisii

Ranunculus belvisii, commonly known as the Belvis buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
