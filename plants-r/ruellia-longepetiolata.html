Ruellia longepetiolata

Ruellia longepetiolata, commonly known as the long petioled ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves with long petioles. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

Ruellia longepetiolata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
