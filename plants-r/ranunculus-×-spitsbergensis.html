Ranunculus × spitsbergensis

Ranunculus × spitsbergensis, commonly known as the Spitsbergen buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers in a range of colors including yellow, orange, and red, often with a dark center. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy and adapted to cold climates. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant in colder regions.
