Rubus serpens var. vastus

Rubus serpens var. vastus, commonly known as the giant blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its large, robust thorns and dark purple to black berries. It typically grows in dense thickets in temperate regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with long, arching canes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and often invasive species in its native habitat.
