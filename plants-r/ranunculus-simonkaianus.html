Ranunculus simonkaianus

Ranunculus simonkaianus, commonly known as the Simonkaian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and has trifoliate leaves with a serrated edge. It produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
