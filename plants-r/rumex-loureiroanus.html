Rumex loureiroanus

Rumex loureiroanus, commonly known as the Brazilian sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the veins, and it produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is noted for its edible leaves, which have a tart, tangy flavor similar to other sorrel species.
