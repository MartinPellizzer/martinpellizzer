Romulea tabularis

Romulea tabularis, commonly known as the flat-topped glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking, showy inflorescences with bright yellow petals that resemble snowflakes, blooming in spring. The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Romulea tabularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
