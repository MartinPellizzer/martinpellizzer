Rubus koehleri gerezianus

Rubus koehleri gerezianus, commonly known as the Gerezian raspberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in parts of Mexico. The plant features thorny stems and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens and conservation areas.
