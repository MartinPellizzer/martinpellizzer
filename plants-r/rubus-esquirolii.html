Rubus esquirolii

Rubus esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
