Rhus delavayi

Rhus delavayi, commonly known as Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for birds and mammals. Rhus delavayi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
