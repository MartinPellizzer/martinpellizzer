Ranunculus lanceolifer

Ranunculus lanceolifer, commonly known as the lanceolate buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous stamens. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant's flowers typically bloom in spring, and it produces small, smooth-seeded fruits.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
