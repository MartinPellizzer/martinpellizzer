Rosa carolina var. hispida

Rosa carolina var. hispida, commonly known as the prickly Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red hips in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for native gardens.
