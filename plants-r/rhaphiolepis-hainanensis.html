Rhaphiolepis hainanensis

Rhaphiolepis hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan firethorn, is a flowering shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and tropical gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice in horticulture.
