Rosa canina var. iurenis

Rosa canina var. iurenis, commonly known as the Iberian rose, is a variant of the dog rose native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. The plant produces small, red hips that contain seeds, which are used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in scrublands and woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
