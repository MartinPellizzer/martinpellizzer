Rhododendron korthalsii

Rhododendron korthalsii, commonly known as the Korthals's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant features striking, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, with showy, leathery leaves. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and often found in specialized garden environments.
