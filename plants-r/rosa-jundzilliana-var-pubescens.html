Rosa jundzilliana var. pubescens

Rosa jundzilliana var. pubescens, commonly known as the Jundzilliana rose, is a compact shrub with dark green, slightly hairy foliage.

It produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a bushy growth habit and is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is relatively hardy and suitable for growing in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
