Rosa dolosa

Rosa dolosa, commonly known as the sorrowful rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces hips, which are red, berry-like fruits. Rosa dolosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
