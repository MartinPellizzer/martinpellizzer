Rechsteineria tuberosa f. acaulis

Rechsteineria tuberosa f. acaulis, commonly known as the stemless tuberous orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of a visible stem, with the leaves and flower spikes emerging directly from the underground tubers. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the warmer months. Its tubers are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive in arid or seasonal environments.

This rare and ornamental species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in specialty gardens.
