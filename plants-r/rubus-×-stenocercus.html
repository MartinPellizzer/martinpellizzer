Rubus × stenocercus

Rubus × stenocercus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries. The plant is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, Japan, and Korea. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth.

Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a strong, aromatic scent.
