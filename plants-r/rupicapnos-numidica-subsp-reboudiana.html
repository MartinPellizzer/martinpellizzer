Rupicapnos numidica subsp. reboudiana

Rupicapnos numidica subsp.

reboudiana, commonly known as the Numidian rockrose, is a shrub native to North Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, leathery leaves.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for Mediterranean-style gardens.
