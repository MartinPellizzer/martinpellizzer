Rottboellia monandra

Rottboellia monandra, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or purple flowers. It is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Rottboellia monandra is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed environments, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
