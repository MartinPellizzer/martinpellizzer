Rubus fuscoater var. erinaceus

Rubus fuscoater var. erinaceus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black fruits and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The leaves are usually compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers well-drained soil, often growing in partial shade.

This variety is valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.
