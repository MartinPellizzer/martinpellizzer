Ranunculus × heimburgerae

Ranunculus × heimburgerae, commonly known as the Heimburger buttercup, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a serrated margin, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks in its native range.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some environments.
