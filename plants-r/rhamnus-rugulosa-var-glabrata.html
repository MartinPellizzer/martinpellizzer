Rhamnus rugulosa var. glabrata

Rhamnus rugulosa var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, reddish berries. It is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
