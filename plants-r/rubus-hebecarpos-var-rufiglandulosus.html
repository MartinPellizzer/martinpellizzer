Rubus hebecarpos var. rufiglandulosus

Rubus hebecarpos var. rufiglandulosus, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and the plant is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.

The variety is distinguished by its reddish glandular hairs on the stems and leaves.
