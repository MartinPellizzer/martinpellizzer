Rosa × provincialis var. centifolia

Rosa × provincialis var. centifolia, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

The variety 'centifolia' is notable for its abundant flowering and historical significance in perfumery and horticulture.
