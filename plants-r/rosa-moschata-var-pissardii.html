Rosa moschata var. pissardii

Rosa moschata var. pissardii, commonly known as the Pissard's musk rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its stems are thornless and covered in a dense layer of grayish-white hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate fragrance and visual appeal.
