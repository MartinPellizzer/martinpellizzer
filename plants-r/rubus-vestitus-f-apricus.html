Rubus vestitus f. apricus

Rubus vestitus f. apricus, commonly known as the spring-bramble or early raspberry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen in early summer, typically turning red or yellow. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological and horticultural studies.
