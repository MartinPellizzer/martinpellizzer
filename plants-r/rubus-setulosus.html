Rubus setulosus

Rubus setulosus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Rubus setulosus plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
