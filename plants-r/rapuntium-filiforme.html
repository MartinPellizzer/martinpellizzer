Rapuntium filiforme

Rapuntium filiforme, commonly known as slender rapunculus, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by spherical, green fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are long and thin, often growing in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant.

Rapuntium filiforme is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
