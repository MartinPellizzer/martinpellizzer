Rosa coriifolia var. naudersiana

Rosa coriifolia var. naudersiana, commonly known as the Nauders' shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and its stems are covered in thorny prickles. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
