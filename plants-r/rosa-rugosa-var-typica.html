Rosa rugosa var. typica

Rosa rugosa var. typica, commonly known as the rough rose, is a deciduous shrub native to coastal regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its rugged, thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its hips, which are orange-red fruits, are edible and often used in jams and teas.

Rosa rugosa var. typica is highly adaptable and thrives in salty, well-drained soils.
