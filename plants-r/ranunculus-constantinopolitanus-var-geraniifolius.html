Ranunculus constantinopolitanus var. geraniifolius

Ranunculus constantinopolitanus var. geraniifolius, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight yellow petals and a central yellow stamen, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. The leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
