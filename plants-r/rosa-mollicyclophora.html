Rosa mollicyclophora

Rosa mollicyclophora, commonly known as the soft-leafed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are soft and velvety, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Rosa mollicyclophora is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and historical significance in horticulture.
