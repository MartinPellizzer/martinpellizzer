Rheum darvasicum

Rheum darvasicum, commonly known as the Darvasian rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like sorrel and buckthorn. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and tall, reddish stems that bear clusters of small, greenish flowers. Rheum darvasicum is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and detoxifying effects.

Its rhubarb-like leaves are often used in herbal remedies and have a slightly sour taste.
