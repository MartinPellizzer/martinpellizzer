Rosa cuneatula

Rosa cuneatula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, red-orange fruits called hips, which are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Rosa cuneatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
