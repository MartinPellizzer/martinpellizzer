Reseda alba proles platystachya

Reseda alba proles platystachya, commonly known as the flat-stalked whitethorn.

This plant is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in arid or semi-arid regions. The leaves are usually opposite and linear, while the flowers are clustered in racemes.

It is a herbaceous plant that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
