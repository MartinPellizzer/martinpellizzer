Rosa wichurana var. poteriifolia

Rosa wichurana var. poteriifolia, commonly known as the Chilean rose or the rose of the Andes, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that have a distinctive waxy coating. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
