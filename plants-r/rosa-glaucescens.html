Rosa glaucescens

Rosa glaucescens, commonly known as the blue rose or bluebank rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features fragrant, pale blue to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically alternate, with serrated edges and a grayish-green hue.

Rosa glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
