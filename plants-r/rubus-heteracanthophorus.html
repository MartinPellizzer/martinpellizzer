Rubus heteracanthophorus

Rubus heteracanthophorus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible but has a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
