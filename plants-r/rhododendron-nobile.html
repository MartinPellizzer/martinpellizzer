Rhododendron nobile

Rhododendron nobile, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a flowering shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and compact growth habit.

Rhododendron nobile is also valued for its ability to produce fragrant blooms.
