Rubus oigocladus proles muelleri

Rubus oigocladus proles muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are composed of multiple small drupes, giving it a raspberry-like appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is known for its edible, sweet-tart berries.
