Ruellia albopurpurea var. sulfureoviolacea

Ruellia albopurpurea var. sulfureoviolacea, commonly known as the golden violet ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with a yellow throat, which are borne in clusters at the tips of its stems. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with white or silver, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its vibrant color and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
