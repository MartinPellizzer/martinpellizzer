Rhamnus sipapoensis

Rhamnus sipapoensis, commonly known as the Sierra Leone buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are typically reddish or black when mature.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
