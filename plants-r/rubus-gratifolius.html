Rubus gratifolius

Rubus gratifolius, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its ornamental value and historical use in hedges make it a notable botanical species.
