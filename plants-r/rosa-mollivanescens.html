Rosa mollivanescens

Rosa mollivanescens, commonly known as the Mollivan's rose, is a species of shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in suitable climates.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
