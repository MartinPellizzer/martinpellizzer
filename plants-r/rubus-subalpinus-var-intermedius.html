Rubus subalpinus var. intermedius

Rubus subalpinus var. intermedius, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

This variety is often found in mountainous areas of Europe, Asia, and North America.
