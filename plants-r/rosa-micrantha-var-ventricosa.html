Rosa micrantha var. ventricosa

Rosa micrantha var. ventricosa, commonly known as the broad-leafed shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 9 elliptical leaflets with a slightly serrated margin. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
