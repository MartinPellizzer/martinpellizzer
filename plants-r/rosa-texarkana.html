Rosa texarkana

Rosa texarkana, commonly known as the Texarkana rose, is a species native to the southern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring. Rosa texarkana is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
