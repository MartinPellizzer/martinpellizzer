Ranunculus stenolobus

Ranunculus stenolobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces showy yellow or orange flowers with five to eight petals. Its flowers are often accompanied by two rows of small, yellowish sepals.

Ranunculus stenolobus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
