Rumex tingitanus

Rumex tingitanus, commonly known as the Moroccan sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its leaves are edible and have a tart, sour flavor, making it a traditional ingredient in local cuisines.
