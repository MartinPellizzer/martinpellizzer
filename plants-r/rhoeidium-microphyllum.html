Rhoeidium microphyllum

Rhoeidium microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved rhoeidium, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are capable of retaining water, a feature that helps it thrive in moist environments.

Rhoeidium microphyllum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
