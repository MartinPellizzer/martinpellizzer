Rubus pseudodoftanensis

Rubus pseudodoftanensis, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and blackberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest edges and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
