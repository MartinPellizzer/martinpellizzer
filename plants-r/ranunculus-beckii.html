Ranunculus beckii

Ranunculus beckii, commonly known as the Beck's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmately lobed leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in alpine or subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
