Rosa arvensis var. obtusata

Rosa arvensis var. obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaved dog rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant pink or white flowers and oval, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its stems are covered in prickles, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
