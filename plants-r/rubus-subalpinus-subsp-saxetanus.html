Rubus subalpinus subsp. saxetanus

Rubus subalpinus subsp.

saxetanus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its small, prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in alpine meadows and subalpine forests.
