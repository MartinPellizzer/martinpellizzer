Rubus moluccanus var. chartaceus

Rubus moluccanus var. chartaceus, commonly known as the paper raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a papery texture. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces small, edible berries that are used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
