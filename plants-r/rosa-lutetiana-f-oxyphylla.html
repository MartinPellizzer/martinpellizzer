Rosa lutetiana f. oxyphylla

Rosa lutetiana f. oxyphylla, commonly known as the Parisian rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa lutetiana species.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers and is native to France. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

The foliage and floral structure make it a notable subject in horticultural studies.
