Rubus farinosus

Rubus farinosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodland edges and rocky areas. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a hairy texture that gives it its common name.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter flavor.
