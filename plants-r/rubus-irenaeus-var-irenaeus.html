Rubus irenaeus var. irenaeus

Rubus irenaeus var. irenaeus, commonly known as the Irenaean raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
