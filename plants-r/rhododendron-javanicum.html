Rhododendron javanicum

Rhododendron javanicum, commonly known as the Java rhododendron, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, appearing in clusters and blooming during the rainy season.

Rhododendron javanicum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with high humidity.
