Rhododendron nudiflorum f. album

Rhododendron nudiflorum f. album, commonly known as the white-flowered form of the naked-flower rhododendron, is a cultivar characterized by its striking white blooms.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant features showy, funnel-shaped flowers that appear before the leaves in early spring. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
