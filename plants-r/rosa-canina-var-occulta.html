Rosa canina var. occulta

Rosa canina var. occulta, commonly known as the hidden dog rose, is a less common variant of the dog rose.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and oval, dark green leaves. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a fragrant scent. This variety is often found in woodland areas and scrublands, where it thrives in partial shade.

Its fruits, known as hip fruits, are edible and contain a high concentration of vitamin C.
