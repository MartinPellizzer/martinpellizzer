Rubus nemorensis subsp. egregius

Rubus nemorensis subsp.

egregius, commonly known as the noble blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It produces dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and more flavorful than those of other Rubus species.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or woodland areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
