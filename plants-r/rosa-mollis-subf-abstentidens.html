Rosa mollis subf. abstentidens

Rosa mollis subf.

abstentidens, commonly known as the slender bank rose, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, arching stems that often grow along the ground or in low vegetation.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a soft, fuzzy texture.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
