Robinsonecio

Robinsonecio, commonly known as the golden bower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. Robinsonecio thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its rapid growth makes it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens and as a living fence.
