Rollinia lanceolata

Rollinia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf star-apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature, with a sweet, acidic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Rollinia lanceolata is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
