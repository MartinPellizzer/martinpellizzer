Rubus corylifolius flaccidus

Rubus corylifolius flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
