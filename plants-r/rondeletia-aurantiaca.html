Rondeletia aurantiaca

Rondeletia aurantiaca, commonly known as the Orange Trumpet Vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant is a woody climber with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
