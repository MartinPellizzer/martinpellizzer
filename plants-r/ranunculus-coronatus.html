Ranunculus coronatus

Ranunculus coronatus, commonly known as the Crown Violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals arranged in a cup-like shape, typically in shades of pink, purple, or white. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and slightly toothed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus coronatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
