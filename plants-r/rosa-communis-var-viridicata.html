Rosa communis var. viridicata

Rosa communis var. viridicata, commonly known as the green-leaf rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly serrated along the edges. Its flowers are typically pink to white, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often cultivated in gardens for both its ornamental value and its historical use in perfumery.
