Rubus vestitus var. leucostachys

Rubus vestitus var. leucostachys, commonly known as the white-stamened bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its white stamens, which distinguish it from other Rubus species. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually compound, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

The flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
