Rosa acicularis subsp. sayi

Rosa acicularis subsp.

sayi, commonly known as the Say's thorned rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its sharp, thorned stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces red or pink hips in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
