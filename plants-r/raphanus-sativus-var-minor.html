Raphanus sativus var. minor

Raphanus sativus var. minor, commonly known as the field radish, is a small, annual Brassicaceae plant native to Europe.

It features a taproot that is typically white or light yellow, with a mild, spicy flavor. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, and its leaves are pinnately divided with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated as a leafy vegetable or for its edible roots.

This variety is closely related to the common radish (Raphanus sativus) but is smaller in size and has a shorter growing season.
