Rubus omalus subsp. rigiduliformis

Rubus omalus subsp.

rigiduliformis, commonly known as the rigid-leaved blackberry. It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

The leaves are typically rigid and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in the axils of the leaves, while the fruits are aggregate berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
