Rhododendron flavoflorum

Rhododendron flavoflorum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
