Rollinia multiflora

Rollinia multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caricaceae and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically pear-shaped with a rough, green exterior. These fruits contain a sweet, white, juicy pulp surrounded by a hard, inedible core.

Rollinia multiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in tropical regions.
