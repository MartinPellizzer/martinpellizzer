Rhododendron triflorum

Rhododendron triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, with a leathery texture.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
