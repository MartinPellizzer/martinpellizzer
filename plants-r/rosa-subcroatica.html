Rosa subcroatica

Rosa subcroatica, commonly known as the Croatian rose, is a species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring. Its flowers are typically borne on long pedicels and are followed by orange-red hips.

Rosa subcroatica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
