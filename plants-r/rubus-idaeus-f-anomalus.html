Rubus idaeus f. anomalus

Rubus idaeus f. anomalus, commonly known as the red raspberry variant, is a botanical form of the common raspberry.

It is characterized by its unique fruit structure, which differs from typical raspberries in color and texture. The plant exhibits a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruits are typically smaller and more intensely red than standard raspberries.

This form is of interest in horticulture for its ornamental and culinary potential.
