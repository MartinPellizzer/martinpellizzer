Rosa erinacea

Rosa erinacea, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and stems, which provide protection against harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa erinacea is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with thorny stems and elliptical leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
