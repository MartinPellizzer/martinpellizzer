Rytidosperma davyi

Rytidosperma davyi, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silvery-blue foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Rytidosperma davyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
