Ranunculus neoascendens

Ranunculus neoascendens, commonly known as the New Zealand buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive central tuft of stamens, and its leaves are palmately divided with a serrated edge. The plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, green, hairless fruits.

Ranunculus neoascendens is an important part of New Zealand's native flora and is often found in conservation areas.
