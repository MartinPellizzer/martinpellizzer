Rosa canina f. duinensis

Rosa canina f. duinensis, commonly known as the coastal rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose that thrives in coastal environments.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This subspecies is well adapted to sandy soils and salty air, making it a hardy plant found along shorelines.
