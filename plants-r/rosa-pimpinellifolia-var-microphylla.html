Rosa pimpinellifolia var. microphylla

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as the miniature pink or dwarf rock rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
