Rubus pungens var. linearisepalus

Rubus pungens var. linearisepalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often described as having a tart flavor. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species within the Rubus family.
