Rosa viscaria var. attenuata

Rosa viscaria var. attenuata, commonly known as the trailing rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing or trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple ovate to elliptical leaflets, and are covered in fine, velvety hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
