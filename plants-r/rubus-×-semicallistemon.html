Rubus × semicallistemon

Rubus × semicallistemon, commonly known as the hybrid raspberry or semicallistemon raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus idaeus (red raspberry) and Rubus × fruticosus (blackberry).

It typically exhibits characteristics of both parent species, including thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its edible berries, which are prized for their flavor and versatility in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.

Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with dense foliage that provides habitat for small wildlife.
