Rosa multiflora var. gentiliana

Rosa multiflora var. gentiliana, commonly known as the Japanese rose or sweet briar, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often considered ornamental. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and garden borders.

Its botanical features make it both a decorative and ecologically valuable plant.
