Rubus × eximius

Rubus × eximius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are typically white or pale pink.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is valued for its tart flavor and is often used in jams and culinary applications.
