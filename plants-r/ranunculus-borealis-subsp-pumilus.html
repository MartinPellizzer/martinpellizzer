Ranunculus borealis subsp. pumilus

Ranunculus borealis subsp.

pumilus, commonly known as the Arctic buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically features small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are palmately lobed.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats. It has a short growing season and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Ranunculus borealis subsp.

pumilus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems and is a symbol of resilience in extreme environments.
