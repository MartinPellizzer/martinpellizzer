Rhododendron sanctum

Rhododendron sanctum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and shrublands, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Rhododendron sanctum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
