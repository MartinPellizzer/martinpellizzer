Rhamnus microcarpa

Rhamnus microcarpa, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features small, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a dark, leathery berry that ripens in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
