Rhododendron germanicum var. intermedium

Rhododendron germanicum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate German rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ornamental value in horticultural settings.
