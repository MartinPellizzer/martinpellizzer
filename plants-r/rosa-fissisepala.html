Rosa fissisepala

Rosa fissisepala, commonly known as the fissured-stamen rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, with petals that are often pink to white in color and sepals that are deeply fissured, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Rosa fissisepala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
