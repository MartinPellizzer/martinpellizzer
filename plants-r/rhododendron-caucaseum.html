Rhododendron caucaseum

Rhododendron caucaseum, commonly known as the Caucasian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
