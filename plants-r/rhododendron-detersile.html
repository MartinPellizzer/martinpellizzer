Rhododendron detersile

Rhododendron detersile, commonly known as the purpleleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that displays a striking purplish hue, especially during the growing season. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in horticulture for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
