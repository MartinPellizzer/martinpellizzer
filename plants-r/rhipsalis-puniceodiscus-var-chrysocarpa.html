Rhipsalis puniceodiscus var. chrysocarpa

Rhipsalis puniceodiscus var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit cactus, is a unique species within the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its pendulous, branched stems that bear small, spineless, and often white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and fruit, making it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.
