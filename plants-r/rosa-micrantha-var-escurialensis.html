Rosa micrantha var. escurialensis

Rosa micrantha var. escurialensis, commonly known as the Escurialensis rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are covered in thorns. It is well-suited for cultivation in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
