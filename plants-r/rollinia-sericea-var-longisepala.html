Rollinia sericea var. longisepala

Rollinia sericea var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers with long sepals. The fruit is typically green when mature, turning yellow or orange as it ripens, and has a sweet, creamy texture. It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and flavor.
