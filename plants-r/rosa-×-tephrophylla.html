Rosa × tephrophylla

Rosa × tephrophylla, commonly known as the Alpina rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in a fine layer of down.

Rosa × tephrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
