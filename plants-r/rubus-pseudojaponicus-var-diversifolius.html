Rubus pseudojaponicus var. diversifolius

Rubus pseudojaponicus var. diversifolius, commonly known as the variable bramble, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its specific epithet "diversifolius.". The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
