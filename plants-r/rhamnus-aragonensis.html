Rhamnus aragonensis

Rhamnus aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
