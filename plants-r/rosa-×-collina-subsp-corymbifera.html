Rosa × collina subsp. corymbifera

Rosa × collina subsp.

corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering. It typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, often arranged in clusters, giving it its common name.

The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
