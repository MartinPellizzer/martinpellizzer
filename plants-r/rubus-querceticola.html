Rubus querceticola

Rubus querceticola, commonly known as the oakleaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its distinctive oak-like leaves with lobed edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
