Rosa viscaria var. subcuneata

Rosa viscaria var. subcuneata, commonly known as the creeping shrub rose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets with hairy undersides. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable plant for various climates.
