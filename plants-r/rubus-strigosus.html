Rubus strigosus

Rubus strigosus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils, and is often found in the eastern United States.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
