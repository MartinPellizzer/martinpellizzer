Ranunculus pinnatus var. hermannii

Ranunculus pinnatus var. hermannii, commonly known as Hermann's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, often surrounded by a ring of yellow sepals. The plant has finely divided, pinnately arranged leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
