Ribes menziesii var. victoris

Ribes menziesii var. victoris, commonly known as the Vancouver thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with three to five leaflets, and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant is noted for its edible berries, which are sweet and aromatic, often consumed fresh or used in jams and desserts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting local wildlife.
