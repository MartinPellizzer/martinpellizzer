Rapanea perforata

Rapanea perforata, commonly known as the perforate evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive perforations along the margins. The leaves are typically opposite or alternate, and the plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.
