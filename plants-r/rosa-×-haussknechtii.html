Rosa × haussknechtii

Rosa × haussknechtii, commonly known as the Haussknecht rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from Germany.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is relatively disease-resistant and suitable for use as a hedge or shrub in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
