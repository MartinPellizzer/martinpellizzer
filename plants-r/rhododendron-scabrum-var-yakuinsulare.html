Rhododendron scabrum var. yakuinsulare

Rhododendron scabrum var. yakuinsulare, commonly known as the Yaku Island rhododendron, is a variant native to the Japanese island of Yakushima.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in mountainous forests. Its botanical features include small, elliptical leaves and pink to red flowers that attract pollinators.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
