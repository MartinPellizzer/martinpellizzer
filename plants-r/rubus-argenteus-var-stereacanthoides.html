Rubus argenteus var. stereacanthoides

Rubus argenteus var. stereacanthoides, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. Its stems are covered in stiff, curved prickles, which are a distinctive feature of the species.

This variety is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in cooler, higher elevation environments.
