Ranunculus dealbatus

Ranunculus dealbatus, commonly known as the white avens, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Ranunculus dealbatus is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring.
