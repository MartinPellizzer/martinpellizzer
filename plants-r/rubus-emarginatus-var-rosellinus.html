Rubus emarginatus var. rosellinus

Rubus emarginatus var. rosellinus, commonly known as the Roselline blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. Its flowers are white and clustered, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
