Rubus glabratus

Rubus glabratus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white with five petals and are borne in clusters.

Rubus glabratus is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its edible berries.
