Rosa bidenticulata

Rosa bidenticulata, commonly known as the two-toothed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

Rosa bidenticulata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
