Rosa rubiginosa var. nudiuscula

Rosa rubiginosa var. nudiuscula, commonly known as the shrub rose or the red sandalwood rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called hips, which are typically red and covered in a fuzzy layer. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and have a distinctive grayish-green color with a fuzzy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
