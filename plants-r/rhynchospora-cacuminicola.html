Rhynchospora cacuminicola

Rhynchospora cacuminicola, commonly known as the clustered spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Rhynchospora cacuminicola plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
