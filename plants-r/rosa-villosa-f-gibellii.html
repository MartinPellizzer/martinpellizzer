Rosa villosa f. gibellii

Rosa villosa f. gibellii, commonly known as the hairy rose or the bristly rose, is a botanical cultivar characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems and dense, thorny branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
