Ranunculus grandifolius

Ranunculus grandifolius, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features striking yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble additional petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Ranunculus grandifolius is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic properties.
