Rhododendron mimetes var. simulans

Rhododendron mimetes var. simulans, commonly known as the mimic rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
