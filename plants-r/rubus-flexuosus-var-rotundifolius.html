Rubus flexuosus var. rotundifolius

Rubus flexuosus var. rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with rounded lobes. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
