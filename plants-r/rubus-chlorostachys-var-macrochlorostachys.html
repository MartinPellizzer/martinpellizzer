Rubus chlorostachys var. macrochlorostachys

Rubus chlorostachys var. macrochlorostachys, commonly known as the large-leaved evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically larger than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
