Rhynchosia vendae

Rhynchosia vendae, commonly known as the African burr bean, is a legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to distinctive, spiny pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Rhynchosia vendae thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species adapted to harsh conditions.

It is also valued for its potential as a forage crop and for its role in nitrogen fixation in the soil.
