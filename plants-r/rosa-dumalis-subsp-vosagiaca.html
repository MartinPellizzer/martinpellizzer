Rosa dumalis subsp. vosagiaca

Rosa dumalis subsp.

vosagiaca, commonly known as the Vosagian wild rose, is a shrub native to the steppes of Eastern Europe and parts of Russia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features thorny stems and simple, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are pink to red, appearing in clusters, and are followed by oval, red-orange hips.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
