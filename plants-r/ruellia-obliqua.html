Ruellia obliqua

Ruellia obliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ruellia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers with five petals, which bloom from summer to fall. Ruellia obliqua is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
