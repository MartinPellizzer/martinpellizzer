Racinaea tetrantha var. ramosior

Racinaea tetrantha var. ramosior, commonly known as the branched racinaea, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features a short, underground rhizome with multiple branches, giving it its varietal name "ramosior.". Its leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
