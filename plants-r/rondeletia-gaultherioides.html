Rondeletia gaultherioides

Rondeletia gaultherioides, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories.

Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
