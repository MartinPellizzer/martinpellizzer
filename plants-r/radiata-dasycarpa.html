Radiata dasycarpa

Radiata dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy fruit tree, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive, hairy fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered with fine hairs.

The fruit is edible, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
