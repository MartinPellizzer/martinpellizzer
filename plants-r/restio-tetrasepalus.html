Restio tetrasepalus

Restio tetrasepalus, commonly known as the four-lobed restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny white or pale blue flowers. It typically grows in coastal or sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
