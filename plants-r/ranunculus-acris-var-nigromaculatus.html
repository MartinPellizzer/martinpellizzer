Ranunculus acris var. nigromaculatus

Ranunculus acris var. nigromaculatus, commonly known as the black-spotted buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with distinctive black or dark brown spots. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
