Rubus lasiostylus var. dizygos

Rubus lasiostylus var. dizygos, commonly known as the hairy-styled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, juicy berries that are typically eaten fresh or used in culinary applications. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces small, aggregate fruits.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated in gardens and small farms.
