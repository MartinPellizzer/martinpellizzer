Rosa gallica var. oligacantha

Rosa gallica var. oligacantha, commonly known as the Alba Rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to Europe.

It features dark green, trifoliate leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in early summer. The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in perfumery.

Its flowers are also used in the production of essential oils and culinary applications.
