Rhododendron seranense

Rhododendron seranense, commonly known as the Seranese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron seranense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
