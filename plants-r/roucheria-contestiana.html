Roucheria contestiana

Roucheria contestiana, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a compact growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces intricate, spurred orchid blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
