Rosa gremlii

Rosa gremlii, commonly known as the Gremli rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces prickly stems and has leathery, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Rosa gremlii is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in cool, dry climates with well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
