Rubus amictus

Rubus amictus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name.

Rubus amictus thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
