Rubus costaricanus

Rubus costaricanus, commonly known as the Costa Rican raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
