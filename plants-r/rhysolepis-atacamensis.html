Rhysolepis atacamensis

Rhysolepis atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama rock cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, and its spines provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the brief rainy season, adding a touch of color to the otherwise barren landscape.

Due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
