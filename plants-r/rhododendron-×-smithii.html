Rhododendron × smithii

Rhododendron × smithii, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy clusters of funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with flowers ranging in color from white to deep pink, depending on the specific cultivar. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions around the world.
