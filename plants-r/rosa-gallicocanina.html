Rosa gallicocanina

Rosa gallicocanina, commonly known as the Gallicanina rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems. This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many landscapes.
