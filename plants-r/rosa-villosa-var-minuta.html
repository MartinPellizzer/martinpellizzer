Rosa villosa var. minuta

Rosa villosa var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf hairy rose, is a compact shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name, and it typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and pleasant scent.
