Racosperma brachypodum

Racosperma brachypodum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers are typical of the genus Racosperma.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.
