Rhododendron parishii

Rhododendron parishii, commonly known as the Parish rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly China and Vietnam.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shady, humid environments, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Rhododendron parishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
