Rubus × foliosiformis

Rubus × foliosiformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically black when ripe, though they may retain a reddish hue. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
