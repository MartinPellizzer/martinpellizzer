Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. rhamnifolius

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

rhamnifolius, commonly known as the thrysoideus raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of prickly stems covered in fine hairs.

The leaves are alternate, with a distinctive three-lobed shape and serrated margins. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a red, aggregate berry.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
