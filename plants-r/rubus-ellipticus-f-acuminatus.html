Rubus ellipticus f. acuminatus

Rubus ellipticus f. acuminatus, commonly known as the acuminated bramble, is a variant of the elliptic raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
