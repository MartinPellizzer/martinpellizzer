Rubia pavlovii

Rubia pavlovii, commonly known as the Pavlov's madder, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia pavlovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
