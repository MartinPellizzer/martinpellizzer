Rubus cyrenaicae

Rubus cyrenaicae, commonly known as the Cyrenaica raspberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant in both ecological and culinary contexts.
