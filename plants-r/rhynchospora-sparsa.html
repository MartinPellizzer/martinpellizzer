Rhynchospora sparsa

Rhynchospora sparsa, commonly known as the sparse flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in nutrient-poor, waterlogged soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

Rhynchospora sparsa plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
