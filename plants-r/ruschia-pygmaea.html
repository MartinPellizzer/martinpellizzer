Ruschia pygmaea

Ruschia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are typically no taller than a few inches. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often used in xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
