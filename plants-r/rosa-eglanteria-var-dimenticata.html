Rosa eglanteria var. dimenticata

Rosa eglanteria var. dimenticata, commonly known as the forgotten brambles rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, aggregate berries that ripen in the fall.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
