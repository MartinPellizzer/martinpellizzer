Racosperma aduncum

Racosperma aduncum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.

Its seeds are sometimes used in traditional Aboriginal practices.
