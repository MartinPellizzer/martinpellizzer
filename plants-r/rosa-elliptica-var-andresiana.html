Rosa elliptica var. andresiana

Rosa elliptica var. andresiana, commonly known as the Andes rose, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a prominent central pistil. This variety is well adapted to cooler climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in garden settings.
