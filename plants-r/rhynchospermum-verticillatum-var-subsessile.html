Rhynchospermum verticillatum var. subsessile

Rhynchospermum verticillatum var. subsessile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhynchospermum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a fibrous root system that helps it retain moisture. Its flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

The varietal form subsessile is distinguished by its shorter stem compared to the typical form.
