Rubus przewalskii

Rubus przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic makeup and ecological adaptations.
