Rubus colemannii subsp. inopacatus

Rubus colemannii subsp.

inopacatus, commonly known as the Sonoran blackberry, is a shrub native to the Sonoran Desert region of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The species is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
