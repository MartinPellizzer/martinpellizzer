Rubus dolichophyllus var. dolichophyllus

Rubus dolichophyllus var. dolichophyllus, commonly known as the long-leaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
