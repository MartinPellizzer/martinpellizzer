Ribes uva-crispa subsp. pubescens

Ribes uva-crispa subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen to a dark red or purple color by late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
