Rourea induta

Rourea induta, commonly known as the velvetleaf rourea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically thick and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

Rourea induta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
