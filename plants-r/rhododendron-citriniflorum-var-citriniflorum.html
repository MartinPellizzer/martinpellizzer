Rhododendron citriniflorum var. citriniflorum

Rhododendron citriniflorum var. citriniflorum, commonly known as the goldenflower rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a waxy coating.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
