Rosa scabratella

Rosa scabratella, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, typically growing in rocky or woodland areas. The plant features thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the plant produces oval, red-orange fruits called hips.

Rosa scabratella is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
