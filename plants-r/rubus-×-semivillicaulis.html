Rubus × semivillicaulis

Rubus × semivillicaulis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
