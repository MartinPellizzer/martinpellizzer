Rubus vulgaris subsp. commutatus

Rubus vulgaris subsp.

commutatus, commonly known as the common blackberry. It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and widely used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.

The species is closely related to other Rubus species and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
