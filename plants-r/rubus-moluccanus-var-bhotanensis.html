Rubus moluccanus var. bhotanensis

Rubus moluccanus var. bhotanensis, commonly known as the Bhutanese blackberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and neighboring areas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in forested or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in local agriculture, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
