Rosa pulverulenta var. cryptopoda

Rosa pulverulenta var. cryptopoda, commonly known as the cryptopod rose, is a rare and elusive species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is notable for its unique morphology, including its densely hairy stems and leaves, which help it withstand harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and rarity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
