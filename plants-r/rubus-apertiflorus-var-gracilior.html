Rubus apertiflorus var. gracilior

Rubus apertiflorus var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bramble, is a shrub native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
