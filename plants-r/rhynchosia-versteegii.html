Rhynchosia versteegii

Rhynchosia versteegii, commonly known as the African bean or yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with trifoliate leaves and yellow, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible, kidney-shaped seeds that are used in various culinary applications. Rhynchosia versteegii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

Its adaptability to poor soils and drought conditions contributes to its ecological and economic importance in regions where it is cultivated.
