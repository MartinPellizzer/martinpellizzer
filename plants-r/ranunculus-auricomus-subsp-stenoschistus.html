Ranunculus auricomus subsp. stenoschistus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

stenoschistus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals and a yellow center.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of R. auricomus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers.
