Ranunculus sedunensis

Ranunculus sedunensis, commonly known as the Tibetan buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features palmate leaves with deeply lobed segments and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
