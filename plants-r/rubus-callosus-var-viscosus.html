Rubus callosus var. viscosus

Rubus callosus var. viscosus, commonly known as the sticky bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and hairy, often sticky leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is native to parts of North America. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters.

The fruit, a red, hairy raspberry, is edible but has a tart flavor.
