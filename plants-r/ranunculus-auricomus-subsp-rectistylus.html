Ranunculus auricomus subsp. rectistylus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

rectistylus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and palmately lobed leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its straight stamens, which set it apart from other members of the species. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

The plant is also notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
