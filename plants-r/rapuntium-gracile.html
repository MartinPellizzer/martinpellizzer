Rapuntium gracile

Rapuntium gracile, commonly known as the slender cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

Rapuntium gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
