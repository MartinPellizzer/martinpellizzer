Rosa canina var. mairei

Rosa canina var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features thorny stems and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring. Its hips, which are the fruit, are typically orange-red and contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
