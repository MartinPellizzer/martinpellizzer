Rosa filispina

Rosa filispina, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rosa filispina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to spread across ground surfaces.
