Rubus × permixtus var. laevior

Rubus × permixtus var. laevior, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its edible fruit, which has a rich, sweet flavor and is often used in jams and desserts.
