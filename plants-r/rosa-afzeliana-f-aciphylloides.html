Rosa afzeliana f. aciphylloides

Rosa afzeliana f. aciphylloides, commonly known as the aciphylloides form of the Afzelian rose, is a unique variant characterized by its modified foliage.

This form exhibits aciphylly, a condition where the leaves are highly reduced or entirely absent, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Africa. The plant typically produces showy flowers with five petals, typical of the Rosa genus.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to specific environmental conditions, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.
