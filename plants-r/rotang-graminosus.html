Rotang graminosus

Rotang graminosus, commonly known as the bamboo grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its natural habitat.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
