Rollinia rugulosa subsp. australis

Rollinia rugulosa subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruits that resemble those of the common rollinia species.

The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, waxy rind and a sweet, tangy pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal regions and rainforests.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
