Rhamnus alaternus

Rhamnus alaternus, commonly known as the common buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant is known for its high concentration of anthraquinones, which are used in traditional medicine and as a laxative. Its berries are dark purple to black and are an important food source for birds.

Rhamnus alaternus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
