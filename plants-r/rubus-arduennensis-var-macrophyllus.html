Rubus arduennensis var. macrophyllus

Rubus arduennensis var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and often found in shaded, forested areas.
