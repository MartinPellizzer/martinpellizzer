Rosa pouzinii var. murcica

Rosa pouzinii var. murcica, commonly known as the Murcian rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, glossy leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in local horticulture.
