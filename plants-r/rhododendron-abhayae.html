Rhododendron abhayae

Rhododendron abhayae, commonly known as the Abhayae rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Rhododendron abhayae is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the local biodiversity of its habitat.
