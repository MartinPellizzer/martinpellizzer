Rhododendron haematodes subsp. chaetomallum

Rhododendron haematodes subsp.

chaetomallum, commonly known as the red-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, deep red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dense, humid forests at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
