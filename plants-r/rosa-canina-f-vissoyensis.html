Rosa canina f. vissoyensis

Rosa canina f. vissoyensis, commonly known as the Vissoyensis rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. This form is notable for its unique foliage, which is more densely hairy and darker green compared to the typical dog rose. It is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in woodland edges and scrublands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
