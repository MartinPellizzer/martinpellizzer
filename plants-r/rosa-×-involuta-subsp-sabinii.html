Rosa × involuta subsp. sabinii

Rosa × involuta subsp.

sabinii, commonly known as the Sabine rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its vigorous growth and profuse flowering, producing clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers.

The plant typically has dark green, glossy leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
