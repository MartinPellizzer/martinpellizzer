Rhynchospora madagascariensis

Rhynchospora madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Rhynchospora madagascariensis is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
