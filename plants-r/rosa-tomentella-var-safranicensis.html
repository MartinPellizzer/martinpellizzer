Rosa tomentella var. safranicensis

Rosa tomentella var. safranicensis, commonly known as the Safranica rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
