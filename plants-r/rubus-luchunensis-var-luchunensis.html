Rubus luchunensis var. luchunensis

Rubus luchunensis var. luchunensis, commonly known as the Luchun blackberry, is a species native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It is a shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with a temperate climate. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are covered in thorns.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
