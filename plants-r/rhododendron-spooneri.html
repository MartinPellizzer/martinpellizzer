Rhododendron spooneri

Rhododendron spooneri, commonly known as the Spooner's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron spooneri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
