Rhynchospora brownii var. condensata

Rhynchospora brownii var. condensata, commonly known as the dense spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with whorled leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, brownish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhynchospora brownii var. condensata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
