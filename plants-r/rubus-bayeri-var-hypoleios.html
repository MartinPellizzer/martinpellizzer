Rubus bayeri var. hypoleios

Rubus bayeri var. hypoleios, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its small, thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
