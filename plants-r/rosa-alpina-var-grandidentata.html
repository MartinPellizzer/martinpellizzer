Rosa alpina var. grandidentata

Rosa alpina var. grandidentata, commonly known as the Alpine rose or mountain rose, is a variety of the alpine rose species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features upright stems with compound leaves consisting of five to seven ovate leaflets. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five to seven petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
