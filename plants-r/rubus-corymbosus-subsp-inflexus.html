Rubus corymbosus subsp. inflexus

Rubus corymbosus subsp.

inflexus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This subspecies is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
