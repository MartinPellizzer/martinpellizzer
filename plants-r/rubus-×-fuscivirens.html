Rubus × fuscivirens

Rubus × fuscivirens, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from blackberries and raspberries.

It is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of fruit sizes, flavors, and textures.
