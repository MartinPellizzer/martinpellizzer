Rorippa pacifica

Rorippa pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific watercress, is a flowering plant native to moist environments in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in shallow water or wet soil, often near streams and ponds. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to watercress.

It produces small white or yellow flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
