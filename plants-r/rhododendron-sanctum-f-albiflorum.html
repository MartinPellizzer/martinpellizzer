Rhododendron sanctum f. albiflorum

Rhododendron sanctum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the Whiteflower Himalayan Rhododendron, is a unique variant of the Himalayan rhododendron.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which contrast strikingly with its dark green, leathery foliage. The plant is typically found in the alpine regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its botanical features include dense, evergreen leaves and showy bracts that enhance its ornamental value.

This rare form is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its distinctive floral characteristics and ecological significance.
