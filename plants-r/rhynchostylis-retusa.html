Rhynchostylis retusa

Rhynchostylis retusa, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blossoms.

Rhynchostylis retusa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in traditional horticulture.
