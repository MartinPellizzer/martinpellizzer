Rosa alpina var. glabra

Rosa alpina var. glabra, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems with simple, ovate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a delicate, sweet scent.

This variety is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and is well adapted to cold, alpine environments.
