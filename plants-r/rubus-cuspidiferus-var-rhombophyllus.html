Rubus cuspidiferus var. rhombophyllus

Rubus cuspidiferus var. rhombophyllus, commonly known as the rhombophyllus blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value in gardens.
