Rumex salicifolius var. denticulatus

Rumex salicifolius var. denticulatus, commonly known as the toothed dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into reddish-brown, triangular seeds. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

This species is notable for its role in ecological systems, providing food for various insects and birds.
