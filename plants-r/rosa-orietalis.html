Rosa orietalis

Rosa orietalis, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of perfume.

Rosa orietalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
