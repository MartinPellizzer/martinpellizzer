Rhododendron caucasicum var. roseum-maculatiflorum

Rhododendron caucasicum var. roseum-maculatiflorum, commonly known as the Caucasian spotted-flowered rhododendron, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and leathery. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
