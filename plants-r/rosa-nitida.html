Rosa nitida

Rosa nitida, commonly known as the silverbark rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a distinctive silvery-gray bark that becomes more pronounced with age. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with finely cut leaflets that provide a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Rosa nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
