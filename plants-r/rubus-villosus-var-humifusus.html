Rubus villosus var. humifusus

Rubus villosus var. humifusus, commonly known as the hairy bramble or creeping raspberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of native plant ecosystems.
