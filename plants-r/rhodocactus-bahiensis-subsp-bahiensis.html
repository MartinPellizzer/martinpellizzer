Rhodocactus bahiensis subsp. bahiensis

Rhodocactus bahiensis subsp.

bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia rhodocactus, is a species native to Brazil. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems and sparse, spiny areoles.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the stem tips. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
