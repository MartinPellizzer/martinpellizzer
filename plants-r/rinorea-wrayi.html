Rinorea wrayi

Rinorea wrayi, commonly known as the silverleaf rinorea, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. Rinorea wrayi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens due to its attractive foliage.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
