Rhamnus myrtifolia

Rhamnus myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. The plant has small, oval leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a dark, smooth berry that ripens in the fall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
