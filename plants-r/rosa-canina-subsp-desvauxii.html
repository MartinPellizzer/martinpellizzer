Rosa canina subsp. desvauxii

Rosa canina subsp.

desvauxii, commonly known as the Desvaux's rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring, followed by orange-red hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and scrublands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
