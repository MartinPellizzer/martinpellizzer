Rhaphithamnus cyanocarpus

Rhaphithamnus cyanocarpus, commonly known as the blue-fruited thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its resilience, it is considered a valuable plant for soil stabilization and ecological restoration in degraded landscapes.
