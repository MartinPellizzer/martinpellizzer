Ricinocarpus dumetorum

Ricinocarpus dumetorum, commonly known as the "poisonous nettle," is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Australia, and Africa. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. It contains toxic compounds, including ricin, which makes it highly dangerous if ingested.

Due to its toxicity, it is often avoided in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
