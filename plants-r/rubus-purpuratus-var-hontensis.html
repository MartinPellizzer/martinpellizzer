Rubus purpuratus var. hontensis

Rubus purpuratus var. hontensis, commonly known as the Hontense blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a firm, tart flavor. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and grows as a shrub with thorny stems. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal fruit production.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and edible fruits.
