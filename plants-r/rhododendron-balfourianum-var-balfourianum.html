Rhododendron balfourianum var. balfourianum

Rhododendron balfourianum var. balfourianum, commonly known as the Kashmiri rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Pakistan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, cool mountainous environments. The plant features dense, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple during the spring. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hardy, ornamental plant adapted to alpine conditions.

It is also valued for its ecological contributions to high-altitude ecosystems.
