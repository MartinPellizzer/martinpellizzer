Rubus schleicheri var. spinosellus

Rubus schleicheri var. spinosellus, commonly known as the spiny bramble, is a thorny shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, prickly stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Due to its hardy nature, it thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in forested or disturbed areas.
