Rosa × salaevensis var. pubescens

Rosa × salaevensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the Siberian rose or Russian tea rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has a deciduous nature, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
