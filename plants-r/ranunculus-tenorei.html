Ranunculus tenorei

Ranunculus tenorei, commonly known as the Sicilian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually single, with five to eight petals, and are often a shade of yellow or orange.

The leaves are trifoliate, with a distinctive lobed shape, and the plant is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
