Ranunculus californicus var. crassifolius

Ranunculus californicus var. crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to California.

It features basal leaves that are thick, waxy, and broadly ovate with a distinctive notched or lobed appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its foliage and flowers are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.
