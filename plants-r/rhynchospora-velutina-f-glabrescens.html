Rhynchospora velutina f. glabrescens

Rhynchospora velutina f. glabrescens, commonly known as the velvet sedge, is a variant of the velvet sedge species native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is generally covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a velvety texture.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
