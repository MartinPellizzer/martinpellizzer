Rhododendron curranii

Rhododendron curranii, commonly known as the Curran's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blossoms.

Rhododendron curranii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific garden conditions.
