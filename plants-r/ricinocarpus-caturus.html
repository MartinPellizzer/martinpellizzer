Ricinocarpus caturus

Ricinocarpus caturus, commonly known as the Madagascar bean tree, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in the local ecosystem of Madagascar.
