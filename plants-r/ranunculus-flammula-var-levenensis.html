Ranunculus flammula var. levenensis

Ranunculus flammula var. levenensis, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a creeping rhizome system and produces small, oval leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for undisturbed habitats.
