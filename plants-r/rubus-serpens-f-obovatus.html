Rubus serpens f. obovatus

Rubus serpens f. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its broad, obovate leaves. This form is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in culinary applications.

Its botanical structure includes thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits.
