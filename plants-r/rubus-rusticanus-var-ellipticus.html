Rubus rusticanus var. ellipticus

Rubus rusticanus var. ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic blackberry.

It is a shrub native to North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both its ecological role and potential culinary use.
