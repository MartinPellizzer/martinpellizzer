Rosa foliolosa

Rosa foliolosa, commonly known as the leafy rose, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in an alternate pattern. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a fragrant aroma.

Rosa foliolosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
