Rhipsalis carnosa

Rhipsalis carnosa, commonly known as the silver needle cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, thin, and often branched stems that resemble strings or needles, which are covered in a fine, white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters during the winter months. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it does not have true leaves or roots.

Rhipsalis carnosa is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
