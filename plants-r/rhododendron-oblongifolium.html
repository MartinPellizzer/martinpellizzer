Rhododendron oblongifolium

Rhododendron oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often leathery in texture and dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Rhododendron oblongifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
