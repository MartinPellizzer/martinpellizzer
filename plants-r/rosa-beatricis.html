Rosa beatricis

Rosa beatricis, commonly known as the Queen's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive, layered appearance. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Rosa beatricis is also valued for its historical and symbolic significance in botanical and horticultural traditions.
