Rosa trachyphylla f. latifolia

Rosa trachyphylla f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved form of the rough rose, is a cultivar characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy pink to red flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant produces fragrant blooms in the spring, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, adding visual interest to landscapes.

This cultivar is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
