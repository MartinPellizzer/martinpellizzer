Rosa pendulina f. urceofera

Rosa pendulina f. urceofera, commonly known as the urn-shaped climbing rose, is a unique horticultural form of the species Rosa pendulina.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can reach several meters in length, making it ideal for training on trellises or walls. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the blooms.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
