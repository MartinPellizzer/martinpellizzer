Rubus pseudomacrostachys

Rubus pseudomacrostachys, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Rubus pseudomacrostachys is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
