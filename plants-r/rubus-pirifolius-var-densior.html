Rubus pirifolius var. densior

Rubus pirifolius var. densior, commonly known as the densefruit blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberry species.
