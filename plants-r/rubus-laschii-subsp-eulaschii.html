Rubus laschii subsp. eulaschii

Rubus laschii subsp.

eulaschii, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold climates and poor soil conditions.
