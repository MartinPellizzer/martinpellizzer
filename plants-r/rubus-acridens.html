Rubus acridens

Rubus acridens, commonly known as the sharpleaf blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, which includes many other brambles and berries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The botanical features of Rubus acridens make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
