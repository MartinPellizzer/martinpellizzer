Rosa bushii

Rosa bushii, commonly known as the Bush's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant pink to red flowers. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are often thorny.

Rosa bushii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
