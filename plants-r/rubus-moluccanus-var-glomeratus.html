Rubus moluccanus var. glomeratus

Rubus moluccanus var. glomeratus, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
