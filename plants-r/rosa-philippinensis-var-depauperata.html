Rosa philippinensis var. depauperata

Rosa philippinensis var. depauperata, commonly known as the Philippine rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Philippines.

It features slender, thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, with five petals and a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
