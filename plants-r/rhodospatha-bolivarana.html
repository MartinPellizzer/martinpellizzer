Rhodospatha bolivarana

Rhodospatha bolivarana, commonly known as the Bolivarian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with vibrant purple and white petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense canopy where it receives ample sunlight. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a complex, branched inflorescence that supports its delicate blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from deforestation and climate change.
