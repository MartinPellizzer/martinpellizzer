Rudbeckia biennis

Rudbeckia biennis, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals surrounding a dark central cone, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and upright.

Rudbeckia biennis is a hardy perennial that is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
