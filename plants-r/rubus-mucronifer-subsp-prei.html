Rubus mucronifer subsp. prei

Rubus mucronifer subsp.

prei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer.

The leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are white and clustered in groups of three. This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its edible fruits.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
