Rubus friesiorum var. hageniae

Rubus friesiorum var. hageniae, commonly known as the Hagen's blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or thickets.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular for jams and pies.
