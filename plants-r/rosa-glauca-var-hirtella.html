Rosa glauca var. hirtella

Rosa glauca var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy blue rose, is a variant of the blue rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bluish-pink petals and short, fuzzy hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually single or semi-double, with a delicate, fragrant aroma.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
