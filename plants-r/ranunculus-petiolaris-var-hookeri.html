Ranunculus petiolaris var. hookeri

Ranunculus petiolaris var. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's water buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble additional petals. The plant has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and have a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as streambanks and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
