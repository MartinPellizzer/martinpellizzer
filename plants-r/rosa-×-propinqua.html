Rosa × propinqua

Rosa × propinqua, commonly known as the miniature rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa gallica and Rosa canina.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful in horticultural breeding programs.
