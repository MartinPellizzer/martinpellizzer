Rubus tomentellus

Rubus tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and hairy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its berries are edible but have a tart, somewhat bitter taste.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
