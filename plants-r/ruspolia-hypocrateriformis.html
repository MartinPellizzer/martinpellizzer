Ruspolia hypocrateriformis

Ruspolia hypocrateriformis, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, horn-like floral structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often brightly colored, attracting specific pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
