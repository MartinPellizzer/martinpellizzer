Rubus × spinosuliformis

Rubus × spinosuliformis, commonly known as the spiny blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in wild or cultivated settings.

Due to its ornamental and edible qualities, it is sometimes grown for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
