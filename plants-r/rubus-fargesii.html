Rubus fargesii

Rubus fargesii, commonly known as the Yunnan blackberry, is a shrub native to China, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Sichuan and Yunnan provinces.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
