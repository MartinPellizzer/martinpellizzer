Ribes melananthum

Ribes melananthum, commonly known as blackcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow or white. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn bright red in the autumn.

Ribes melananthum is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
