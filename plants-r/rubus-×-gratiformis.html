Rubus × gratiformis

Rubus × gratiformis, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, partially shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
