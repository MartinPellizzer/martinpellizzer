Rhynchosia splendens

Rhynchosia splendens, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its cascading clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
