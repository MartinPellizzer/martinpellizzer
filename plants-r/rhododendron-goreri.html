Rhododendron goreri

Rhododendron goreri, commonly known as the Gorer's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, ranging in color from pink to purple, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron goreri is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
