Rubus horridifactus

Rubus horridifactus, commonly known as the Thorned Devilberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, bristly foliage and clusters of small, dark red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and its flowers are white with five petals.

This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
