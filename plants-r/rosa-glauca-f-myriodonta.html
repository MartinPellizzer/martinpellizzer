Rosa glauca f. myriodonta

Rosa glauca f. myriodonta, commonly known as the Myriodonta rose, is a rare and cultivated form of the glaucous rose.

It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender-colored flowers and slightly hairy foliage. This form is typically smaller than the standard Rosa glauca, with a more compact growth habit. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a prized addition to botanical collections.
