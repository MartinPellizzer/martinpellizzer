Rhamnus orbiculata

Rhamnus orbiculata, commonly known as the globe thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its round, warty fruits that resemble small apples, which are typically green when immature and turn red or orange when ripe. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are oval in shape and often have a waxy coating. It produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive fruits.

Rhamnus orbiculata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
