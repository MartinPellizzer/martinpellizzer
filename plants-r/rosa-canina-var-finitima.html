Rosa canina var. finitima

Rosa canina var. finitima, commonly known as the Italian dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. This plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in woodland areas and along riverbanks.

Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces hips that are rich in vitamin C. The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
