Rosa × alba var. typica

Rosa × alba var. typica, commonly known as the white garden rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa alba and other Rosa species.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that often have a slight pinkish tinge at the base. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
