Rosa × scotica

Rosa × scotica, commonly known as the Scotch rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa gallica and Rosa canina.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange to red hips. It is native to the British Isles and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The botanical characteristics of Rosa × scotica make it adaptable to a variety of soil types and climates.
