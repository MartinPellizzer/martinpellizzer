Rhododendron pachystigma

Rhododendron pachystigma, commonly known as the giant rhododendron, is a large evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and is adapted to cool, moist climates. It has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
