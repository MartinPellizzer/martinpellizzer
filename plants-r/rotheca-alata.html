Rotheca alata

Rotheca alata, commonly known as the winged rotheca, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rotheca alata is often found in moist, woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
