Rubus pallidus subsp. posnaniensis

Rubus pallidus subsp.

posnaniensis, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces red berries that are edible but often tart.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
