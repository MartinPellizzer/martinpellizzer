Rhododendron eritimum subsp. chawchiense

Rhododendron eritimum subsp.

chawchiense, commonly known as the Chawchi rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. It is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 3,500 meters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
