Rubus fragarioides var. adenophorus

Rubus fragarioides var. adenophorus, commonly known as the腺花覆盆子, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its erect or scrambling growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This variant is native to certain regions of Asia and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
