Rubus discolor var. pubescens

Rubus discolor var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy everlet raspberry, is a variant of the everlet raspberry native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which give it its scientific name "pubescens.". The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and interesting species within the Rubus genus.
