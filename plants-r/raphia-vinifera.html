Raphia vinifera

Raphia vinifera, commonly known as the wine palm, is a large, tropical palm native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, pinnate leaves that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, wine-red fruits that are rich in nutrients. Its fibrous trunk is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and crafts.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
