Reseda lanceolata

Reseda lanceolata, commonly known as narrowleaf sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. Reseda lanceolata is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.
