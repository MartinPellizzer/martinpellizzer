Rhododendron hancockii var. hancockii

Rhododendron hancockii var. hancockii, commonly known as the Hancock's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, broad leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows at high elevations, preferring cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its botanical features include stout stems, evergreen foliage, and a compact growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural conditions.
