Rubus chlorothyrsos f. vandalicus

Rubus chlorothyrsos f. vandalicus, commonly known as the Vandal's blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and firm. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and preserves.
