Rosa pseudoadjecta

Rosa pseudoadjecta, commonly known as the purpleleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves and clusters of fragrant pink to red flowers. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Rosa pseudoadjecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
