Rhododendron erosipetalum

Rhododendron erosipetalum, commonly known as the slender-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a lush, green backdrop to its vibrant floral display.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
