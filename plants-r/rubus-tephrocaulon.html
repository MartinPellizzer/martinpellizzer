Rubus tephrocaulon

Rubus tephrocaulon, commonly known as the ash-colored bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits are aggregate berries that are typically red or black when mature.

Rubus tephrocaulon is native to parts of Asia and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
