Rubus fuscoater var. subbavaricus

Rubus fuscoater var. subbavaricus, commonly known as the dark-fruited bramble, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically smaller and more intensely flavored than those of the typical Rubus fuscoater. The plant produces thorny stems and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique fruit coloration and its role in local ecosystems.
