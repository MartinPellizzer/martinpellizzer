Rubus × ampliflorens

Rubus × ampliflorens, commonly known as the large-flowered blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus. This species is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers and thorny stems. The plant produces edible berries that are similar in taste to blackberries but often have a more intense flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
