Rubus vallisparsus subsp. granitophilus

Rubus vallisparsus subsp.

granitophilus, commonly known as the granite raspberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, usually red or yellow fruits.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
