Rubus schottii

Rubus schottii, commonly known as Schott's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical characteristics of Rubus schottii make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique adaptations to its native habitat.
