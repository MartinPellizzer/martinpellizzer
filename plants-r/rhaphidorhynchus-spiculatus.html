Rhaphidorhynchus spiculatus

Rhaphidorhynchus spiculatus, commonly known as the spiky burr vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a rough texture.

Rhaphidorhynchus spiculatus is often found in humid, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
