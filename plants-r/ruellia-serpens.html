Ruellia serpens

Ruellia serpens, commonly known as the snake's head plant, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Ruellia serpens is popular in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and blooms.
