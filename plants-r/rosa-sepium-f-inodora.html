Rosa sepium f. inodora

Rosa sepium f. inodora, commonly known as the scentless dog rose, is a hybrid form of the dog rose, characterized by its lack of fragrance.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces large, showy flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves, typical of the Rosa genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
