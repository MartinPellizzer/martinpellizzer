Rhododendron wollastonii

Rhododendron wollastonii, commonly known as the Wollaston's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from pink to red.

Rhododendron wollastonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
