Ranunculus nemorosus subsp. mixtus

Ranunculus nemorosus subsp.

mixtus, commonly known as the mixed wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

The plant features palmately divided, bright green leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and early spring blooming period.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its subspecific variation in leaf shape and flower size compared to other ranunculus species.
