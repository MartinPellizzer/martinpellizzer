Rubus balatonicus

Rubus balatonicus, commonly known as the Hungarian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental and culinary value, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and edible purposes.
