Rumex tingitanus var. lacerus

Rumex tingitanus var. lacerus, commonly known as the Moroccan dock, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on erect, branching stems. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
