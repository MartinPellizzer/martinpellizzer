Rosularia sempervivum subsp. glaucophylla

Rosularia sempervivum subsp.

glaucophylla, commonly known as the Silverleaf Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, silver-green leaves that are covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its drought tolerance and cold hardiness make it well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates.
