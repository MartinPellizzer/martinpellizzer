Rubus californicus

Rubus californicus, commonly known as the California blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus californicus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinator populations.
