Rhus toxicodendron var. quercifolia

Rhus toxicodendron var. quercifolia, commonly known as the oakleaf anacacho.

This plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States. It features compound leaves that resemble oak leaves, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red or orange berries.

It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
