Rosa maximowicziana var. adenocalyx

Rosa maximowicziana var. adenocalyx, commonly known as the Adenocalyx rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical studies.
