Rollinia resinosa

Rollinia resinosa, commonly known as the resinous starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that produces distinctive yellow-orange fruits with a waxy, aromatic skin. The fruit contains a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild, slightly acidic flavor. The plant is known for its ability to exude a resinous substance from its bark, which has been used traditionally in various applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
