Rubus lawrencei

Rubus lawrencei, commonly known as Lawrence's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
