Ranunculus crymophilus var. major

Ranunculus crymophilus var. major, commonly known as the greater snowdrop, is a delicate perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces small, white, nodding flowers with three sepals and no true petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it has a short, stout rhizome.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific climate conditions.
