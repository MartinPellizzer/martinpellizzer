Rhynchophreatia mamberamensis

Rhynchophreatia mamberamensis, commonly known as the Mamberamense water lily, is a rare aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, contributing to its ecological significance. Its root system helps stabilize the sediment in shallow water bodies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
