Rosa tolosana

Rosa tolosana, commonly known as the Tolosa rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa tolosana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
