Rubus chinensis var. triphyllus

Rubus chinensis var. triphyllus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry or triphyllous raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
