Rosa glauca var. largidentata

Rosa glauca var. largidentata, commonly known as the large-toothed bluebush rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture and are covered in fine hairs. This variety is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in full sun.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for xeriscaping and wildlife habitats.
