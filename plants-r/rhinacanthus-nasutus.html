Rhinacanthus nasutus

Rhinacanthus nasutus, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has a distinctive elongated, narrow leaf shape and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
