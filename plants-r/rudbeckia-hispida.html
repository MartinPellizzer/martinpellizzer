Rudbeckia hispida

Rudbeckia hispida, commonly known as the rough coneflower, is a North American flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy yellow-orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk of darker yellow to brown florets. Rudbeckia hispida thrives in full sun to partial shade and is native to the central and eastern United States.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
