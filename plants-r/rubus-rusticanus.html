Rubus rusticanus

Rubus rusticanus, commonly known as the rusty blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, including parts of California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, almost black, berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are typically serrated and have a slightly hairy texture.

The rusty blackberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
