Racosperma disparrimum subsp. calidestre

Racosperma disparrimum subsp.

calidestre, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and is typically found in dry, sandy soils. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
