Ranunculus auricomus subsp. retusatus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

retusatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its basal leaves that are typically narrow and often have a waxy coating.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its foliage and flowers are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
