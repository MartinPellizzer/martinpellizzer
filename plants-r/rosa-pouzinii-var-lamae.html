Rosa pouzinii var. lamae

Rosa pouzinii var. lamae, commonly known as the Lamae rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are pinnate, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh climatic conditions.
