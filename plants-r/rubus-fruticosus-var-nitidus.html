Rubus fruticosus var. nitidus

Rubus fruticosus var. nitidus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and clustered, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
