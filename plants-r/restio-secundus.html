Restio secundus

Restio secundus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale yellow flowers. Restio secundus thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in fynbos vegetation.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
