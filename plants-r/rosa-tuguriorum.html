Rosa tuguriorum

Rosa tuguriorum, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a species of shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa tuguriorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
