Rhus semialata var. osbeckii

Rhus semialata var. osbeckii, commonly known as Osbeck's smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by orange-red, fuzzy fruits that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
