Robinia megasperma

Robinia megasperma, commonly known as the black locust, is a deciduous legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow,蝶形花 (butterfly-shaped) flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The plant also has a deep root system and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil enrichment.
