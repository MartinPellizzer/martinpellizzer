Rosa communis var. recurvata

Rosa communis var. recurvata, commonly known as the curled rose, is a cultivar of the rose family characterized by its unique, backward-curving petals.

It is native to China and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage. Its flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
