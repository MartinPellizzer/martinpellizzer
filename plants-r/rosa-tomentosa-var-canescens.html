Rosa tomentosa var. canescens

Rosa tomentosa var. canescens, commonly known as the fuzzy rose or woolly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, grayish-white hair covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval with a soft, velvety texture, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in rocky or arid soils.

Rosa tomentosa var. canescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
