Rubus × pustulosus

Rubus × pustulosus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit, which is usually dark purple to black when ripe, is edible and has a tart flavor.
