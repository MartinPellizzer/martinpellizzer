Rhus panaciformis

Rhus panaciformis, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 6 feet and features compound leaves with 15 to 31 leaflets that are oval and serrated. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by red, fuzzy berries that attract birds and other wildlife. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade in well-drained soils.

Rhus panaciformis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
