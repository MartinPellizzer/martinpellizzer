Reaumuria stenophylla

Reaumuria stenophylla, commonly known as the desert sage or sandmat, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to withstand extreme drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species in harsh environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water deep underground.

Reaumuria stenophylla plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
