Rabdosia hirtella

Rabdosia hirtella, commonly known as hairy rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Rabdosia hirtella is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.
