Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. tranzschelii

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

tranzschelii, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features basal leaves with a heart-shaped outline and bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals. It is characterized by its short stature and ability to withstand harsh mountain climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species in many areas.
