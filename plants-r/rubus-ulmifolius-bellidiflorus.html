Rubus ulmifolius bellidiflorus

Rubus ulmifolius bellidiflorus, commonly known as the bellflower raspberry, is a shrub-like species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, giving them a fluffy appearance.

This species is native to Europe and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.
