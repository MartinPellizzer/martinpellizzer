Rubus pallidicorius

Rubus pallidicorius, commonly known as the pale-fruited blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pale-colored berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
