Royena dichrophylla

Royena dichrophylla, commonly known as the two-colored royal fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bi-colored fronds, which display a striking contrast between green and brown hues. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are pinnate, with a delicate structure that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

Royena dichrophylla is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
