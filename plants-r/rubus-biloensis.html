Rubus biloensis

Rubus biloensis, commonly known as the Biloan blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems, trifoliate leaves, and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom in clusters.

The fruit is valued for its flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
