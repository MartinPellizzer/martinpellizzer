Rubus rivularis var. obrotundatus

Rubus rivularis var. obrotundatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and thorny growth habit. The leaves are typically broad and rounded, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by dark purple to black berries.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
