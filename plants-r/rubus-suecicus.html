Rubus suecicus

Rubus suecicus, commonly known as the Swedish blackberry, is a species of bramble native to northern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along stream banks. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
