Rosmarinus officinalis subsp. palaui

Rosmarinus officinalis subsp.

palaui, commonly known as Palaui rosemary, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region. It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small blue or purple flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its foliage contains essential oils rich in compounds like camphor and rosmarinic acid, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.

This subspecies is valued for its hardy nature and distinct aromatic profile.
