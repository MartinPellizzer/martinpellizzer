Rubus × trux

Rubus × trux, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a distinctive sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Rubus × trux is often cultivated for both its edible fruits and ornamental value.
