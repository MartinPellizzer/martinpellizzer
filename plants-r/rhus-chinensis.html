Rhus chinensis

Rhus chinensis, commonly known as Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often have a red or orange hue in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fuzzy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to different environments.

The botanical features of Rhus chinensis make it a notable species in both ornamental horticulture and ecological studies.
