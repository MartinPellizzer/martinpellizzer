Rubia cordifolia var. discolor

Rubia cordifolia var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated heartleaf rue, is a variant of the common rue plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves with a variegated pattern of green and white or pale yellow. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming and antispasmodic properties.
