Romulea discifera

Romulea discifera, commonly known as the broad-leafed romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes irises and related plants. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow center and purple outer petals. Its bulbs are adapted for storing water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Romulea discifera thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
