Rosa viscaria var. viscida

Rosa viscaria var. viscida, commonly known as the sticky rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant's leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a sticky, glandular coating, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
