Rhododendron apoanum

Rhododendron apoanum, commonly known as the Khasi rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers during the spring season. Its leaves are elliptical and leathery, providing a dense canopy that supports various forms of understory vegetation.

Rhododendron apoanum plays an important ecological role in its habitat by offering shelter and food to local wildlife.
