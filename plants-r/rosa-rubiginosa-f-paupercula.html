Rosa rubiginosa f. paupercula

Rosa rubiginosa f. paupercula, commonly known as the pale rose or the shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its distinctive scent. Its stems are woody and often bear small, backward-pointing prickles.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.
