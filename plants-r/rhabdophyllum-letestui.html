Rhabdophyllum letestui

Rhabdophyllum letestui, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique epiphytic orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
