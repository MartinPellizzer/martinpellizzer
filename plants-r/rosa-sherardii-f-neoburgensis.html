Rosa sherardii f. neoburgensis

Rosa sherardii f. neoburgensis, commonly known as the Neuburg rose, is a botanical variant of the shrub rose species Rosa sherardii.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The leaves are typically dark green and finely serrated, while the stems are covered in soft, pale pink to white hairs. This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is well-suited to temperate climates.

It is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
