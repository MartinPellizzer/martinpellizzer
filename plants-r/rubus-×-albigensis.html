Rubus × albigensis

Rubus × albigensis, commonly known as the white raspberry, is a hybrid species within the genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many bramble species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
