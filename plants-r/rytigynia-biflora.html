Rytigynia biflora

Rytigynia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered rytigynia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and often have a serrated margin.

Rytigynia biflora is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
