Rubus geocamptus

Rubus geocamptus, commonly known as the ground-creeping raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
