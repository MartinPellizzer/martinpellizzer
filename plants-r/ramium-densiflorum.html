Ramium densiflorum

Ramium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Ramium densiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing coastal dunes.
