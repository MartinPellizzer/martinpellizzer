Rubus lainzii

Rubus lainzii, commonly known as the Lainz blackberry, is a species of shrub native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. Its botanical features include a woody structure and a preference for well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Rubus lainzii is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
