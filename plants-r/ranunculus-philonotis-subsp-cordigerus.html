Ranunculus philonotis subsp. cordigerus

Ranunculus philonotis subsp.

cordigerus, commonly known as the cordate buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped, with a deep green color and a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are bright yellow with numerous petal-like sepals, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
