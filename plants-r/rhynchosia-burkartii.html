Rhynchosia burkartii

Rhynchosia burkartii, commonly known as the Burkart's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, similar to other cowpea species. It is often grown for its nutritional value and is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates.

Rhynchosia burkartii is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
