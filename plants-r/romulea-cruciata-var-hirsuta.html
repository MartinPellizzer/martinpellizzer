Romulea cruciata var. hirsuta

Romulea cruciata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy star lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive central boss. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.
