Rhysotoechia applanata

Rhysotoechia applanata, commonly known as the flat-leafed orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in specific tropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, broad leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on the forest floor or in rocky crevices. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant adaptation.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered in many areas.
