Rhinocerotidium

Rhinocerotidium, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, often showy flowers that may resemble horns or other intricate shapes. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing nutrients from the soil.

Rhinocerotidium species are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated for their distinctive floral structures.
