Rosa montana var. transiens

Rosa montana var. transiens, commonly known as the Transient Mountain Rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets with serrated edges. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to a range of garden conditions.
