Rubus fuscus var. lasiostachys

Rubus fuscus var. lasiostachys, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
