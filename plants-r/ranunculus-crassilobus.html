Ranunculus crassilobus

Ranunculus crassilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that are often mistaken for petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a distinctive, wavy margin that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse floral structures and ecological importance in various habitats.
