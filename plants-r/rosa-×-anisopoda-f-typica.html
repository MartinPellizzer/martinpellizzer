Rosa × anisopoda f. typica

Rosa × anisopoda f. typica, commonly known as the common shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to full flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and as a hedge due to its hardiness and adaptability.
