Rosa macrophylla var. mongolica

Rosa macrophylla var. mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian shrub rose, is a perennial deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of northern China and Mongolia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, glossy, dark green leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for colder climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
