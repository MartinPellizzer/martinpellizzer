Russelia multiflora

Russelia multiflora, commonly known as the scarlet bush or red-flowered bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Russelia multiflora is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
