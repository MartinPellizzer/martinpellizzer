Rubus pseudosaxatilis var. kouytchensis

Rubus pseudosaxatilis var. kouytchensis, commonly known as the Kouytchen rubus, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
