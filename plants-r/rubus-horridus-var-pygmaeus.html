Rubus horridus var. pygmaeus

Rubus horridus var. pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small stature, typically growing to less than 1 meter in height. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries.

It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, often forming dense thickets.
