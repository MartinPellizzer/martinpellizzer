Rinzia rubra

Rinzia rubra, commonly known as the red rinzia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Rinzia rubra is an important species for local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
