Rubus nitidus

Rubus nitidus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles like Rubus idaeus.
