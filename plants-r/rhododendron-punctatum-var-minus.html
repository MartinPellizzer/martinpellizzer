Rhododendron punctatum var. minus

Rhododendron punctatum var. minus, commonly known as the small-flowered alpine rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive waxy coating, which helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific climate requirements.
