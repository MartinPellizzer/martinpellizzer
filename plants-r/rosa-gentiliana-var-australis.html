Rosa gentiliana var. australis

Rosa gentiliana var. australis, commonly known as the Australian sweet william, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in spring. The plant has slender, woody stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
