Racosperma parvipinnulum

Racosperma parvipinnulum, commonly known as the small-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are followed by cylindrical seed pods.

Racosperma parvipinnulum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry sclerophyllous vegetation.
