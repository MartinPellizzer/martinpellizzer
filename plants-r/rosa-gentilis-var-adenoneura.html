Rosa gentilis var. adenoneura

Rosa gentilis var. adenoneura, commonly known as the adenoneura rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The flowers are usually pink to red, with five to seven petals, and are borne singly or in small clusters. This variety is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
