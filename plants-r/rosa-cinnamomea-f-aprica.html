Rosa cinnamomea f. aprica

Rosa cinnamomea f. aprica, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a fragrant, deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, dark green foliage, and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits known as hip fruits, which are rich in vitamin C. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
