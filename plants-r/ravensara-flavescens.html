Ravensara flavescens

Ravensara flavescens, commonly known as the golden ravenala, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure with a crown of feather-like leaves that give it a unique, palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing edible seeds. Ravensara flavescens thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in its native region.
