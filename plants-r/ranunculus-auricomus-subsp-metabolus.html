Ranunculus auricomus subsp. metabolus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

metabolus, commonly known as the yellow avens, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers with five to eight petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for centuries.
