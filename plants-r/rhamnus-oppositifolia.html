Rhamnus oppositifolia

Rhamnus oppositifolia, commonly known as the oppositeleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves, which are typically oval to elliptic in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
