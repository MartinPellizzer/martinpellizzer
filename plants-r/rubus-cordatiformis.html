Rubus cordatiformis

Rubus cordatiformis, commonly known as the heart-shaped raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to southern Africa, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are pinkish-white with five petals, and it produces fruit in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
