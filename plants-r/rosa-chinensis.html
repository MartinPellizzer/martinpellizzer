Rosa chinensis

Rosa chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is known for its showy, fragrant flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with dark green, glossy leaves and thorny stems. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and bloom throughout the growing season.

Rosa chinensis is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is valued for its beauty and adaptability to various climates.
