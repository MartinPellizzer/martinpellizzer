Rivina apetala

Rivina apetala, commonly known as the Florida rivina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice in native plant gardens.
