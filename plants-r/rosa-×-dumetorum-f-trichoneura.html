Rosa × dumetorum f. trichoneura

Rosa × dumetorum f. trichoneura, commonly known as the trichoneura form of the evergreen shrub rose, is a horticultural form characterized by its dense, dark green foliage.

It is a hybrid species resulting from crosses between Rosa rugosa and Rosa serotina, exhibiting traits of both parent species. The plant typically grows as a compact, upright shrub with thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The leaves are leathery and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinct appearance.
