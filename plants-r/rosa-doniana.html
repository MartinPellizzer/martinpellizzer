Rosa doniana

Rosa doniana, commonly known as the West African rose, is a species of shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

Rosa doniana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in local medicine.
