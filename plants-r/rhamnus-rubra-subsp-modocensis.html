Rhamnus rubra subsp. modocensis

Rhamnus rubra subsp.

modocensis, commonly known as Modoc thornapple, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and clusters of small, red berries that ripen in the fall.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are inconspicuous, appearing in clusters and blooming in the spring.

The subspecies is notable for its thorny branches and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
