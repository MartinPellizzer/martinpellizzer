Ronnbergia involucrata

Ronnbergia involucrata, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid rainforest environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often forming a rosette shape around the base of the plant.

Ronnbergia involucrata thrives in warm, moist conditions and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
