Rubus pubescens schlechtendalii

Rubus pubescens schlechtendalii, commonly known as the Schlechtendal's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. It typically grows in woodland areas, shrublands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
