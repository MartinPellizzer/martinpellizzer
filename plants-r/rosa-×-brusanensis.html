Rosa × brusanensis

Rosa × brusanensis, commonly known as the Brusanskaya rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa moschata.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it suitable for coastal gardens and as a natural barrier against erosion.
