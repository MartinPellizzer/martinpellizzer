Rennellia elongata

Rennellia elongata, commonly known as the Rennellian spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the island of Rennell in the Solomon Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Rennellia elongata is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and restricted distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying island biodiversity.
