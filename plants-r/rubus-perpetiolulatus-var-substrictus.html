Rubus perpetiolulatus var. substrictus

Rubus perpetiolulatus var. substrictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It features prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically narrower than those of other Rubus species. The flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple or black berry. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
