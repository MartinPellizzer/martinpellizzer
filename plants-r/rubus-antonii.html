Rubus antonii

Rubus antonii, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has a woody, thorny stem.

Rubus antonii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
