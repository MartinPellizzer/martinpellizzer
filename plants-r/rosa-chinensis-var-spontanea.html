Rosa chinensis var. spontanea

Rosa chinensis var. spontanea, commonly known as the Chinese rose or everblooming rose, is a species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, thornless stems and large, showy flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy green upper surface with a lighter underside. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five to seven petals, and they bloom continuously throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
