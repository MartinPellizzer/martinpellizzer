Ranunculus praecellans

Ranunculus praecellans, commonly known as the white crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its finely divided foliage and is often found in calcareous soils.

Ranunculus praecellans is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and early spring blooming habit.
