Racosperma phasmoides

Racosperma phasmoides, commonly known as the ghost wattle, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically narrow and alternate, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

The species is adapted to arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and heat.
