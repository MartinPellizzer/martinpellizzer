Ranunculus grayanus

Ranunculus grayanus, commonly known as the Gray's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow to orange flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and streambanks.

Ranunculus grayanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
