Rytigynia xanthotricha

Rytigynia xanthotricha, commonly known as the golden-haired rytigynia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly serrated edge.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
