Rubus schindleri

Rubus schindleri, commonly known as Schindler's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark red berries. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
