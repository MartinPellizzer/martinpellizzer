Rubus prolectibilis

Rubus prolectibilis, commonly known as the prolectible blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and tart. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
