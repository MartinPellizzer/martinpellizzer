Rubus × henrici

Rubus × henrici, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including parts of China, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is a deciduous shrub that produces large, dark purple to black berries. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems.

The fruit is valued for its flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
