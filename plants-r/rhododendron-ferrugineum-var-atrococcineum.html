Rhododendron ferrugineum var. atrococcineum

Rhododendron ferrugineum var. atrococcineum, commonly known as the purple leaved alpine rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to deep purple in color. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a notable species in high-altitude ecosystems.
