Rosa turguriorum

Rosa turguriorum, commonly known as the Tugurian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tugurian region of Kazakhstan. The plant is characterized by its fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer.

Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa turguriorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
