Radicula indica

Radicula indica, commonly known as the Indian radish, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a taproot that is the primary edible part. Its root is typically white or pale yellow and has a slightly spicy, peppery flavor.

Radicula indica is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient in various Asian cuisines.
