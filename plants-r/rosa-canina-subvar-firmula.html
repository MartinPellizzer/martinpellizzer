Rosa canina subvar. firmula

Rosa canina subvar. firmula, commonly known as the common dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces bright red hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
