Rubus × tenuidentatiformis

Rubus × tenuidentatiformis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (raspberry). This plant typically produces small, dark red to black berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.
