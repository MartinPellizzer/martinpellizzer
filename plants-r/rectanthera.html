Rectanthera

Rectanthera, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzag-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plants are native to Southeast Asia and are often found in tropical and subtropical regions. They typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks.

Rectanthera species are known for their fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, making them popular in horticultural collections.
