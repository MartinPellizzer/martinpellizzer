Rosa paucijuga

Rosa paucijuga, commonly known as the few-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, clustered leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Rosa paucijuga is considered a rare and endangered species.
