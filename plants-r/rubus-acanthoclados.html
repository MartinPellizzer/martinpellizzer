Rubus acanthoclados

Rubus acanthoclados, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southern United States, particularly in regions of Texas and Oklahoma. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries that ripen in the fall. Its leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
