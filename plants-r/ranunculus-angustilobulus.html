Ranunculus angustilobulus

Ranunculus angustilobulus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, usually yellow or white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided with narrow lobes, and it produces a cluster of small, hairy fruits.

Ranunculus angustilobulus is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.
