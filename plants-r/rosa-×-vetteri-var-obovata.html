Rosa × vetteri var. obovata

Rosa × vetteri var. obovata, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with obovate leaflets that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces hips, which are orange-red berries containing seeds, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
