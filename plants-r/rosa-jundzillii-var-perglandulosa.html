Rosa jundzillii var. perglandulosa

Rosa jundzillii var. perglandulosa, commonly known as the Alpine rose or the mountain rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically covered with a fine layer of white glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is well adapted to cold climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in alpine gardens.
