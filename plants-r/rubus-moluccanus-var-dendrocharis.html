Rubus moluccanus var. dendrocharis

Rubus moluccanus var. dendrocharis, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
