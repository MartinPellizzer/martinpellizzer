Reseda fruticulosa var. macrostachya

Reseda fruticulosa var. macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stalked reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
