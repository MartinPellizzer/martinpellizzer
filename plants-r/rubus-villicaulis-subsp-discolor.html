Rubus villicaulis subsp. discolor

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the variable bramble, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive silvery or white underside.

The flowers are small, typically pink or white, and appear in clusters during the spring. The fruit, a aggregate berry, is usually red or black when mature and is edible.

This subspecies is often found in woodlands and disturbed sites, showing adaptability to various soil types.
