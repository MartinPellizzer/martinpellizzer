Ribes grossulariifolium

Ribes grossulariifolium, commonly known as the gooseberry leaf currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with reddish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its foliage is aromatic and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
