Rhododendron cinnabarinum var. breviforme

Rhododendron cinnabarinum var. breviforme, commonly known as the scarlet flame azalea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
