Rubus pilosus var. leucostachys

Rubus pilosus var. leucostachys, commonly known as the white-stemmed bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces edible, though tart, berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
