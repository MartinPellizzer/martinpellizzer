Rubus hakonensis var. davidianus

Rubus hakonensis var. davidianus, commonly known as the David's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
