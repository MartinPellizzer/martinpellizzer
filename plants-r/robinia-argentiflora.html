Robinia argentiflora

Robinia argentiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf false acacia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in flat, pod-like fruits that mature in winter.

Robinia argentiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
