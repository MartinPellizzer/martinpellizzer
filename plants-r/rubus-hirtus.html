Rubus hirtus

Rubus hirtus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark red to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus hirtus is valued for its edible berries, which are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
