Rhynchosia baukea

Rhynchosia baukea, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that resemble cascading showers, hence its common name. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces flat, elliptical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rhynchosia baukea is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
