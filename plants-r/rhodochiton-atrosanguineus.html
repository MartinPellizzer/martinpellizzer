Rhodochiton atrosanguineus

Rhodochiton atrosanguineus, commonly known as the blackblood vine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its deep purple to black flowers, which are clustered in racemes along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant for gardens and botanical collections.
