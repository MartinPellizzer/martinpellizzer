Ricinocarpus grandidentatus

Ricinocarpus grandidentatus, commonly known as the large-toothed soursop, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by yellow, fleshy fruits with a distinctive spiky exterior. The fruit contains a single large, hard seed and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
