Richaeia afzelii

Richaeia afzelii, commonly known as the African sand thorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually small and narrow, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its dispersal in dry environments.
