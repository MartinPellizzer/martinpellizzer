Rubus idaeus var. matsumuranus

Rubus idaeus var. matsumuranus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry species.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, with a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in traditional Japanese cuisine.
