Rhododendron setiferum

Rhododendron setiferum, commonly known as the snowflake rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in a shrub form, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking backdrop to its white or pale pink blooms.

Rhododendron setiferum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
