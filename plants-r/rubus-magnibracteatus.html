Rubus magnibracteatus

Rubus magnibracteatus, commonly known as the large-bract raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces edible red berries similar to other raspberries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
