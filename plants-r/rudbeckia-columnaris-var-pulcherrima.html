Rudbeckia columnaris var. pulcherrima

Rudbeckia columnaris var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features striking, bright orange to red ray flowers surrounding a central disk, which are typically tubular and yellow. The plant has upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, adding both aesthetic and ecological value to gardens.
