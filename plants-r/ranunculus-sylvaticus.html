Ranunculus sylvaticus

Ranunculus sylvaticus, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a distinctive white, five-petaled flower with a yellow center. The plant features a short, hairy stem that supports its delicate foliage of trifoliate leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it reproduces through rhizomes and seeds.

Ranunculus sylvaticus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
