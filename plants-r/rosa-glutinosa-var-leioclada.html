Rosa glutinosa var. leioclada

Rosa glutinosa var. leioclada, commonly known as the sticky rose or sticky dog rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its distinctive sticky, glandular hairs that cover its leaves and stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated margins, and it has a woody structure that allows it to thrive in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
