Rosa canina f. bernensis

Rosa canina f. bernensis, commonly known as the Bernese rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. The plant is native to Central Europe and thrives in temperate climates. It produces red, hairy hips that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its foliage is dark green and leathery, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
