Racosperma denticulosum

Racosperma denticulosum, commonly known as the serrated wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow floral spikes. The plant features compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Racosperma denticulosum plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
