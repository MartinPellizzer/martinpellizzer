Rosa karataviensis

Rosa karataviensis, commonly known as the Karatavi rose, is a species native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and an upright growth habit, making it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
