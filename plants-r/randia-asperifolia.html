Randia asperifolia

Randia asperifolia, commonly known as roughleaf randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Randia asperifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
