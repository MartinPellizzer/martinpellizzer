Rubia schugnanica

Rubia schugnanica, commonly known as Schugnan mountain madder, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 12,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in the summer months.

The plant is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
