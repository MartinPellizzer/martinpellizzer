Rosa graveolens f. cheriensis

Rosa graveolens f. cheriensis, commonly known as the Cherries Rosa or the Sweet Briar rose, is a fragrant, deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers that emit a strong, sweet scent. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic qualities. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The fragrance of its flowers is particularly notable, attracting pollinators and adding a sensory dimension to its botanical appeal.
