Rhodoleia latiovatifolia

Rhodoleia latiovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf rhodoleia, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhodoleiaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a deep green color. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in subtropical and tropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
