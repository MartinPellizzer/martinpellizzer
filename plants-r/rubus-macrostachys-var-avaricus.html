Rubus macrostachys var. avaricus

Rubus macrostachys var. avaricus, commonly known as the red raspberries of the Andes, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible berries. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny achenes, which are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated in regions with suitable growing conditions.
