Rubus continentalis

Rubus continentalis, commonly known as the European bramble, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Its leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.
