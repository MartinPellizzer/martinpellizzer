Rubus hedycarpus var. parvulus

Rubus hedycarpus var. parvulus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species within its native range.
