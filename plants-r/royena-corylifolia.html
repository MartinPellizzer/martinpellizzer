Royena corylifolia

Royena corylifolia, commonly known as the Indian nettle or Indian mulberry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is native to South and Southeast Asia and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are typically used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
