Rubus glomeratus var. grandifolius

Rubus glomeratus var. grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five serrated leaflets, often with a prominent central leaflet. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These berries are edible, though they have a tart flavor and are often used in jams and wines.

It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, exhibiting adaptability to various light conditions.
