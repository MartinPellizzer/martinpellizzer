Rosa coriifolia f. lucida

Rosa coriifolia f. lucida, commonly known as the shining dogwood rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its white, single-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant features thornless stems and elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
