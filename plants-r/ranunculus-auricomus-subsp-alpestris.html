Ranunculus auricomus subsp. alpestris

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and finely divided, trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or meadow habitats at high altitudes. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous terrain.
