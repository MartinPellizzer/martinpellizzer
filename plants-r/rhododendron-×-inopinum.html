Rhododendron × inopinum

Rhododendron × inopinum, commonly known as the purple leaved rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to deep purple in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
