Rubus idaeus var. yabei

Rubus idaeus var. yabei, commonly known as Japanese red raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically bright red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.
