Rubioides aspera

Rubioides aspera, commonly known as the rough-stemmed bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name. This plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and often grows in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
