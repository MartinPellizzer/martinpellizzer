Rubus vestitus subsp. perconspicuus

Rubus vestitus subsp.

perconspicuus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
