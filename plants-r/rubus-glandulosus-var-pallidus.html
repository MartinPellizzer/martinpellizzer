Rubus glandulosus var. pallidus

Rubus glandulosus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale-fruited bramble, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and pale yellow to red berries that ripen in the fall. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated margins and a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

This plant has a shallow root system and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
