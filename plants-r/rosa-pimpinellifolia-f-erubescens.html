Rosa pimpinellifolia f. erubescens

Rosa pimpinellifolia f. erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a compact shrub with thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. Its blooms appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
