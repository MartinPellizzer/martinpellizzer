Rosa thoryi

Rosa thoryi, commonly known as the Thory's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a fragrant scent.

Rosa thoryi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
