Rhododendron davidsonianum

Rhododendron davidsonianum, commonly known as the David's rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and hairy underneath. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
