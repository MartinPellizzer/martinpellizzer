Rubus apertiflorus subsp. trichopus

Rubus apertiflorus subsp.

trichopus, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
