Rosa pomifera var. exadenophylla

Rosa pomifera var. exadenophylla, commonly known as the giant rose or the rose of the Andes, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, edible fruits that resemble a hairy orange, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive fruit and floral display.
