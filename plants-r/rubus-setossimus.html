Rubus setossimus

Rubus setossimus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of bramble native to various regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Rubus setossimus is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along the edges of fields.
