Ranunculus andersonii

Ranunculus andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces clusters of bright yellow, five-petaled flowers.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
