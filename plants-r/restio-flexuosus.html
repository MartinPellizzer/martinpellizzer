Restio flexuosus

Restio flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible restio, is a member of the Restiaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Restio flexuosus thrives in wet, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
