Ranunculus amgensis

Ranunculus amgensis, commonly known as the Siberian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves and produces flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
