Raphia zamiana

Raphia zamiana, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a large, woody palm native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 4 meters in diameter. The plant thrives in humid, lowland environments and is often found near rivers or in dense forests. Its fruit is a large, spiky structure that contains edible seeds, which are used in some traditional cuisines.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
