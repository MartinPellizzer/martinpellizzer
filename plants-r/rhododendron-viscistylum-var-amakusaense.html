Rhododendron viscistylum var. amakusaense

Rhododendron viscistylum var. amakusaense, commonly known as the Amakusa azalea, is a rare and distinctive evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in shaded forest environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
