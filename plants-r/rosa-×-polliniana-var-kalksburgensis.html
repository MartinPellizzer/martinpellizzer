Rosa × polliniana var. kalksburgensis

Rosa × polliniana var. kalksburgensis, commonly known as the Kalksburg rose, is a hybrid species originating from Central Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and profuse blooming of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically produces semi-double to fully double petals, and its foliage is dark green with a slightly serrated edge. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and as a cut flower.
