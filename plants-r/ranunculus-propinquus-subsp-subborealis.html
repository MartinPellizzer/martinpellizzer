Ranunculus propinquus subsp. subborealis

Ranunculus propinquus subsp.

subborealis, commonly known as the northern water buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern regions of North America. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, pond margins, and along streams.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This subspecies is often found in open, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
