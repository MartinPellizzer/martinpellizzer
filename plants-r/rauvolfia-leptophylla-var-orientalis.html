Rauvolfia leptophylla var. orientalis

Rauvolfia leptophylla var. orientalis, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a perennial climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant contains alkaloids, including reserpine, which has historically been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its medicinal properties and role in phytochemistry research.
