Rubus rurae

Rubus rurae, commonly known as the rural raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the flowers are white to pink, appearing in early summer.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and desserts.
