Rubus furvus var. pallidistamineus

Rubus furvus var. pallidistamineus, commonly known as the pale-staminal blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive floral features.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, producing small, dark berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are pale in color, with reduced stamens, giving it its specific epithet.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, where it thrives in partial shade.
