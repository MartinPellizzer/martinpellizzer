Rapanea savannarum

Rapanea savannarum, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

Due to its ornamental value and durable timber, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
