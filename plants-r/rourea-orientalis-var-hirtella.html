Rourea orientalis var. hirtella

Rourea orientalis var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy rourea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, often hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
