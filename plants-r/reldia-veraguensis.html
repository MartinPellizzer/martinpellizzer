Reldia veraguensis

Reldia veraguensis, commonly known as the Guayaquil orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Reldia veraguensis thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
