Ranunculus paucistamineus var. trichophyllus

Ranunculus paucistamineus var. trichophyllus, commonly known as the hairy leaf buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It is native to specific regions in Europe and is known for its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, contributing to its unique appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
