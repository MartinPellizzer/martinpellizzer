Ribes suksdorfii

Ribes suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suyksdorf currant, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, mountainous regions, often in coniferous or mixed forests. The plant produces small, edible berries that are similar in appearance to other currants. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Ribes suksdorfii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
