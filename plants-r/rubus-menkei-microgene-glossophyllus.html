Rubus menkei microgene glossophyllus

Rubus menkei microgene glossophyllus, commonly known as the glossy-leaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves that are glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in humid, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
