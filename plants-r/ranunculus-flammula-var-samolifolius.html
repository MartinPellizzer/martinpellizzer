Ranunculus flammula var. samolifolius

Ranunculus flammula var. samolifolius, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a low-growing perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a short, rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively. Its flowers are usually borne on short pedicels and consist of numerous petal-like sepals.

The leaves are palmately divided into three to five lobes, giving the plant a distinctive, delicate appearance.
