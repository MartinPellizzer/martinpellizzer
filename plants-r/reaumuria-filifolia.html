Reaumuria filifolia

Reaumuria filifolia, commonly known as the silky reaumuria, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and northern China.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in harsh, sandy soils.

Reaumuria filifolia is well adapted to extreme climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
