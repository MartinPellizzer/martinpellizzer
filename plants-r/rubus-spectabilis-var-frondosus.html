Rubus spectabilis var. frondosus

Rubus spectabilis var. frondosus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically sweet and aromatic. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions and forested areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for wildlife and its use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
