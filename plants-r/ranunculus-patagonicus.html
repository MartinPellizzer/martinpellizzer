Ranunculus patagonicus

Ranunculus patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
