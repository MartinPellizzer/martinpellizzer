Rabdochloa cruciata

Rabdochloa cruciata, commonly known as the crossleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cross-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
