Rhus schlechteri

Rhus schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 5 to 9 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
