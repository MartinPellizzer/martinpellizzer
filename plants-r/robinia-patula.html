Robinia patula

Robinia patula, commonly known as the false acacia or honey locust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets that turn yellow in autumn, and it produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. The tree has a deep root system and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types. Its flowers, which appear in spring, are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its hard, heavy wood and its use in ornamental landscaping.
