Reynoldsia sandwicensis var. intercedens

Reynoldsia sandwicensis var. intercedens, commonly known as the Maui rain tree, is a rare endemic species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small, deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and lowland habitats. It plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
