Rosa hemisphaerica var. harrisonii

Rosa hemisphaerica var. harrisonii, commonly known as the Harrison's globe rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rounded, globe-shaped growth habit and dense, thornless stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
