Rosa pomifera var. leiocarpa

Rosa pomifera var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the silky rose or silky hip rose, is a unique variant of the wild rose species.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy appearance and is native to regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by distinctive, hairy fruits called hips. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody, thorny stem structure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its aesthetic and medicinal properties.
