Rhododendron taiwanalpinum

Rhododendron taiwanalpinum, commonly known as the Taiwan alpine rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to white, bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized landscapes.
