Roscoea capitata

Roscoea capitata, commonly known as the red ginger or snowball plant, is a striking tropical perennial native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and dramatic flowering display.
