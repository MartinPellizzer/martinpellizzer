Racosperma fasciculiferum

Racosperma fasciculiferum, commonly known as the clustered acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, wiry stems that often grow in dense clusters, giving it its common name. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemose inflorescences, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Racosperma fasciculiferum is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
