Rubus moluccanus var. pilocalyx

Rubus moluccanus var. pilocalyx, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with variations in leaf and fruit morphology.
