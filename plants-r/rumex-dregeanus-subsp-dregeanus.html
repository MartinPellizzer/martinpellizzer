Rumex dregeanus subsp. dregeanus

Rumex dregeanus subsp.

dregeanus, commonly known as the Cape sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing yellowish-green bracts.

The plant is valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
