Rosa rotundibracteata

Rosa rotundibracteata, commonly known as the round-bract rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its compact shrub form, with thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The round-bract rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
