Ruellia mucronata

Ruellia mucronata, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia mucronata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
