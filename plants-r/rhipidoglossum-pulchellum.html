Rhipidoglossum pulchellum

Rhipidoglossum pulchellum, commonly known as the beautiful mossy fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically green with a slight sheen. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Due to its ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
