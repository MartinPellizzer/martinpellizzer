Rumex afer

Rumex afer, commonly known as Italian dock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright, ribbed stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Rumex afer is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
