Rubus pseudomarshallii

Rubus pseudomarshallii, commonly known as the Marshall's blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, a small, dark purple berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.
