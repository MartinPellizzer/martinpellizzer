Rosa villosa f. haplodonta

Rosa villosa f. haplodonta, commonly known as the hairy rose or the soft-haired rose, is a cultivar of the species Rosa villosa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other rose varieties.
