Ranunculus pimpinelloides

Ranunculus pimpinelloides, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem that helps it withstand cold temperatures. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is often found in moist meadows or stream banks.

Ranunculus pimpinelloides is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.
