Rhododendron caucasicum var. roseum-tigrinum

Rhododendron caucasicum var. roseum-tigrinum, commonly known as the Caucasian rose-tiger rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive tiger-striped pattern on the petals. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
