Rubus × parvidens

Rubus × parvidens, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes numerous species of brambles and raspberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features include thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets.
