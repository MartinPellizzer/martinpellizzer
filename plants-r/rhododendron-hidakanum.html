Rhododendron hidakanum

Rhododendron hidakanum, commonly known as the Hidaka rhododendron, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron hidakanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to shaded garden conditions.
