Ranunculus geraniifolius var. eriotorus

Ranunculus geraniifolius var. eriotorus, commonly known as the woolly buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves that are deeply lobed and resemble those of geraniums. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
