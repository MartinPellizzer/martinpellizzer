Ricinocarpus ocymoides

Ricinocarpus ocymoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
