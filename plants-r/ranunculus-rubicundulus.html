Ranunculus rubicundulus

Ranunculus rubicundulus, commonly known as the red avens, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

The leaves are palmately divided, giving the plant a delicate, fern-like appearance.
