Rosa dumalis subsp. subcanina

Rosa dumalis subsp.

subcanina, commonly known as the western prairie rose, is a perennial shrub native to the central and western United States. It typically grows in open grasslands and prairie habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. Its flowers are pink to red, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
