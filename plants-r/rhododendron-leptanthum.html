Rhododendron leptanthum

Rhododendron leptanthum, commonly known as the slender-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Rhododendron leptanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
