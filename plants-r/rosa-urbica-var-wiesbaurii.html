Rosa urbica var. wiesbaurii

Rosa urbica var. wiesbaurii, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to central Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal blooms.
