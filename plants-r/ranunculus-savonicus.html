Ranunculus savonicus

Ranunculus savonicus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, saucer-shaped flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

Ranunculus savonicus is often found in Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
