Ribes giraldii var. polyanthum

Ribes giraldii var. polyanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its red, edible berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
