Ranunculus tibetanus

Ranunculus tibetanus, commonly known as the Tibetan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and prefers well-drained soil. It has palmate leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its habitat, it is often found in meadows and along mountain trails at elevations above 3,000 meters.
