Rosa canina subvar. dilucida

Rosa canina subvar. dilucida, commonly known as the pale dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and serrated. The fruit, known as a hip, is typically red or orange and contains several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
