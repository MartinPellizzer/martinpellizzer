Rubus hirtoides

Rubus hirtoides, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of eastern North America, including parts of the United States and Canada. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits, which are small and aggregate, are typically red or black when mature.

Rubus hirtoides is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade and moist soil conditions.
