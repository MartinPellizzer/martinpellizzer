Ranunculus auricomus subsp. microphyllus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is characterized by its small, finely toothed leaves.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.
