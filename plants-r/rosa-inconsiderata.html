Rosa inconsiderata

Rosa inconsiderata, commonly known as the careless rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, often single-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces thorny stems.

The botanical features of Rosa inconsiderata make it a resilient and distinctive member of the rose family.
