Ruschia brevicollis

Ruschia brevicollis, commonly known as the dwarf chandelier plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, upright growth with clusters of small, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow during the growing season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its vibrant color.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
