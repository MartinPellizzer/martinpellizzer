Rosa × alba semiduplex

Rosa × alba semiduplex, commonly known as the semi-double white rose, is a hybrid rose species that combines the traits of its parent species, Rosa alba and Rosa × damascena.

It is characterized by its semi-double flowers, which have a layered appearance with a mix of petals and sepals. The plant produces large, pale pink to white blooms that are often fragrant, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

The foliage is dark green and glossy, providing an attractive backdrop to its showy flowers.
