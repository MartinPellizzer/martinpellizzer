Rubus × superfluus

Rubus × superfluus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and a complex flowering structure.
