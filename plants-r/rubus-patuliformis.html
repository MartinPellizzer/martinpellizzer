Rubus patuliformis

Rubus patuliformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red or black berries. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a rough texture.

Rubus patuliformis is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
