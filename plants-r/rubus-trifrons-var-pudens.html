Rubus trifrons var. pudens

Rubus trifrons var. pudens, commonly known as the pink-haired raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive pink bracts that resemble hairs, giving it a unique appearance. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of North America. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.
