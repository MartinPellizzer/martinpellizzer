Rubus raopingensis

Rubus raopingensis, commonly known as the Raoping blackberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally significant species.
