Rosa canina var. hispanica

Rosa canina var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and is native to southern Europe. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. Its hips, which are red, fleshy fruits, are edible and used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The botanical characteristics of this variety include hairy leaves and a distinctive, aromatic scent.
