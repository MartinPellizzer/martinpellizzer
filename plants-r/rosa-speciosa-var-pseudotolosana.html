Rosa speciosa var. pseudotolosana

Rosa speciosa var. pseudotolosana, commonly known as the California sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to rose-red, with five to seven petals. The plant produces oval, reddish-brown fruits called rose hips, which contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
