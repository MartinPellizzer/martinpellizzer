Rubus ferdinandimuelleri

Rubus ferdinandimuelleri, commonly known as the Ferdinand Mueller blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs with thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understoreys, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its dark purple to black berries are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The species is named in honor of the botanist Ferdinand Mueller, who made significant contributions to the study of Australian flora.
