Rhaponticum cynaroides

Rhaponticum cynaroides, commonly known as the Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features a robust taproot system and produces yellow composite flowers in the summer. Its leaves are usually lobed or toothed, with a rough, hairy texture.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands.
