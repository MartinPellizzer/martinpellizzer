Rhododendron rex subsp. rex

Rhododendron rex subsp.

rex, commonly known as the giant rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, providing a lush, dense canopy.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist climates with partial shade and well-drained acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
