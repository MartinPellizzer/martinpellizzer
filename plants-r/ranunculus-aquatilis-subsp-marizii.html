Ranunculus aquatilis subsp. marizii

Ranunculus aquatilis subsp.

marizii, commonly known as the water buttercup subspecies marizii, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant features delicate, floating or submerged leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its preference for temperate climates.

Due to its habitat requirements, it is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.
