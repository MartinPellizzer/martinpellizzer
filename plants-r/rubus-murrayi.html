Rubus murrayi

Rubus murrayi, commonly known as Murray's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rubus murrayi is often found in open forests and along coastal areas.
