Rosa sericea var. hookeri

Rosa sericea var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's silvery rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
