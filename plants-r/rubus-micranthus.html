Rubus micranthus

Rubus micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are followed by edible berries that are usually dark purple to black in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and for its use in local cuisines.
