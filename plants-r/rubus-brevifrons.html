Rubus brevifrons

Rubus brevifrons, commonly known as the short-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Botanically, it belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family.

Its growth habit includes both upright and trailing stems, making it adaptable to various habitats.
