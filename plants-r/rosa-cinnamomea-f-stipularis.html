Rosa cinnamomea f. stipularis

Rosa cinnamomea f. stipularis, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a botanical variant of the cinnamon rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive cinnamon-scented flowers and dark red to maroon petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its foliage is often dark green, and it produces small, fragrant blooms in the spring.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and unique aromatic properties.
