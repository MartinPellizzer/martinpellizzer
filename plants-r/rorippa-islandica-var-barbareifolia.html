Rorippa islandica var. barbareifolia

Rorippa islandica var. barbareifolia, commonly known as the island watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, often calcareous environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
