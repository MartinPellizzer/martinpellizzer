Ranunculus auricomus subsp. gyratidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

gyratidens, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent, shield-shaped petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different petal shape compared to the typical form of Ranunculus auricomus.
