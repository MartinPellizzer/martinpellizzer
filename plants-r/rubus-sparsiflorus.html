Rubus sparsiflorus

Rubus sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparseflower bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. This shrub typically grows in dry, open habitats such as chaparral and grasslands. It has prickly stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces edible berries, though they are generally less palatable compared to other Rubus species.
