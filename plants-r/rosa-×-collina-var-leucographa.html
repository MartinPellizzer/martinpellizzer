Rosa × collina var. leucographa

Rosa × collina var. leucographa, commonly known as the white-veined shrub rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its compact growth habit and ornamental value.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically medium-green with distinct white or pale veins, contributing to its visual appeal. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities and hardy nature.
