Rubus diamantinus

Rubus diamantinus, commonly known as the diamond raspberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, bright red berries. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in the spring.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and desserts.
