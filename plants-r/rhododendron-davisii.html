Rhododendron davisii

Rhododendron davisii, commonly known as the Davis's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, cool environments. The plant features dense, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many flowering shrubs and evergreens.

Rhododendron davisii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
