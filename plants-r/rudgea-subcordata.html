Rudgea subcordata

Rudgea subcordata, commonly known as the red-flowered rudgea, is a striking shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in its ecosystem.

Rudgea subcordata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
