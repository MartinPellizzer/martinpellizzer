Rosa rubiginosa var. microcarpa

Rosa rubiginosa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf rose or microcarpa rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early summer, often with a distinctive rust-colored bloom. The plant has thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typical of many rose species. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
