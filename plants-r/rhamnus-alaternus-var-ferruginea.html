Rhamnus alaternus var. ferruginea

Rhamnus alaternus var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The variety is distinguished by its rust-colored veins on the leaves, giving it its common name.
