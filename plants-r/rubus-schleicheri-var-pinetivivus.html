Rubus schleicheri var. pinetivivus

Rubus schleicheri var. pinetivivus, commonly known as the Florida blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and pine flatwoods, and thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has a woody stem covered in thorny bracts.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing wetland soils.
