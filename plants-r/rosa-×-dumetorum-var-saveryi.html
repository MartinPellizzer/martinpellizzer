Rosa × dumetorum var. saveryi

Rosa × dumetorum var. saveryi, commonly known as the Savery's shrub rose, is a compact, sprawling shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to poor, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
