Rubus pumilus

Rubus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has erect or prostrate stems covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval to ovate in shape.

Rubus pumilus is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in moist, rocky soils.
