Rubus schleicheri var. carneistylus

Rubus schleicheri var. carneistylus, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
