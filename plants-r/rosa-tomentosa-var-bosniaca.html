Rosa tomentosa var. bosniaca

Rosa tomentosa var. bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian woolly rose, is a shrub native to the Balkan region.

It features dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in forested areas. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical uses in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to temperate climates with well-drained soils.
