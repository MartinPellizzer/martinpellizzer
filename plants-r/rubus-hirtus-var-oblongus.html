Rubus hirtus var. oblongus

Rubus hirtus var. oblongus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
