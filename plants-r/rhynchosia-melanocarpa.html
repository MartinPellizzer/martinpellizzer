Rhynchosia melanocarpa

Rhynchosia melanocarpa, commonly known as black-seed cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing dark, edible seeds. The plant is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches soil fertility. Its seeds are used in traditional cuisines and have potential nutritional benefits.

The species is also studied for its role in sustainable agriculture due to its adaptability and resilience.
