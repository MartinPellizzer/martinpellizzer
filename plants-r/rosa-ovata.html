Rosa ovata

Rosa ovata, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a velvety texture. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or ovate in shape, giving it its scientific name. Rosa ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
