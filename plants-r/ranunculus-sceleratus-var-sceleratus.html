Ranunculus sceleratus var. sceleratus

Ranunculus sceleratus var. sceleratus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and wet grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are upright and often hairy.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
