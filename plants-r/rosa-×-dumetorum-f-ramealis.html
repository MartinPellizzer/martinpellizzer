Rosa × dumetorum f. ramealis

Rosa × dumetorum f. ramealis, commonly known as the creeping shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit, with thorny stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
