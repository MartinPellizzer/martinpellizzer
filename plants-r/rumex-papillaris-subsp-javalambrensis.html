Rumex papillaris subsp. javalambrensis

Rumex papillaris subsp.

javalambrensis, commonly known as the Javalambrense sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy surface and reddish veins. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose inflorescences, producing edible, tart berries.

The species is valued for its culinary use in local cuisines and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
