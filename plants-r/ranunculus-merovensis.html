Ranunculus merovensis

Ranunculus merovensis, commonly known as the Merovingian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five to eight petals.

The species is notable for its historical association with the Merovingian dynasty in France.
