Rubia asperrima

Rubia asperrima, commonly known as the rough betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many flowering plants known for their medicinal and ornamental uses. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a rough texture. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale purple, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Rubia asperrima is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky soils, and it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
