Rubus septifolius

Rubus septifolius, commonly known as the seven-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically consisting of seven leaflets, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The plant is part of the Rosaceae family, which includes other well-known fruit-bearing plants.
