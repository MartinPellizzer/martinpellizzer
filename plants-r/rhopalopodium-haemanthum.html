Rhopalopodium haemanthum

Rhopalopodium haemanthum, commonly known as the leopard plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
