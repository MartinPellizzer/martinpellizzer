Robinia pseudoacacia var. semperflorens

Robinia pseudoacacia var. semperflorens, commonly known as the weeping false acacia, is a cultivar of the honeylocust tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It features compound leaves that are typically divided into smaller, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are followed by long, flat pods containing seeds. It is native to the southeastern United States but is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is often used in landscaping due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
