Rubus constrictus var. roseiflorus

Rubus constrictus var. roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which give way to edible blackberries in the summer. Its fruits are typically small, dark, and have a tart flavor.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local ecosystems.
