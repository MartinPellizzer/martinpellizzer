Radicula × anceps

Radicula × anceps, commonly known as the hybrid radicle, is a rare plant species resulting from the cross between two distinct radicle species.

It is characterized by its unique combination of morphological traits from its parent plants, often displaying variations in leaf shape and stem structure. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and genetic uniqueness, Radicula × anceps is of interest to botanists studying hybridization and plant adaptation.
