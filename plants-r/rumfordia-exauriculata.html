Rumfordia exauriculata

Rumfordia exauriculata, commonly known as the Cape mountain lily, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical features, it is also appreciated by botanists and horticulturists alike.
