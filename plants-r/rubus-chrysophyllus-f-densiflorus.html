Rubus chrysophyllus f. densiflorus

Rubus chrysophyllus f. densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower raspberry, is a botanical variant of the Rubus genus characterized by its dense inflorescence.

It typically features bright yellow foliage, which is a distinguishing trait compared to other Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrubland environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and potential horticultural value.
