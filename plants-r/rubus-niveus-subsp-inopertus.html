Rubus niveus subsp. inopertus

Rubus niveus subsp.

inopertus, commonly known as the white-bracted raspberry, is a shrub native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with stems that are often covered in dense, white pubescence.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated margins and a pale underside. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, while the fruits are aggregate berries that mature to a reddish color.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas.
