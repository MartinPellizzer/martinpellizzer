Renealmia aurantiifera

Renealmia aurantiifera, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves with bright orange veins contrasting against its dark green foliage. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a mix of white and yellow hues.

Renealmia aurantiifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
