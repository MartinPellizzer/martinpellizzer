Renealmia mexicana

Renealmia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican reed or reed grass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Renealmia mexicana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
