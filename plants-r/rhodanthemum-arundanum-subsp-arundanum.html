Rhodanthemum arundanum subsp. arundanum

Rhodanthemum arundanum subsp.

arundanum, commonly known as the mountain chrysanthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
