Ricinus communis f. zollingeri

Ricinus communis f. zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's castor plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with a velvety texture and deep green color. The plant produces spiny capsules that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and industry. It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
