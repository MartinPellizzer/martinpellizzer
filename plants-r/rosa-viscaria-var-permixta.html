Rosa viscaria var. permixta

Rosa viscaria var. permixta, commonly known as the pink sand rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its soft, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double blooms on thorny stems, and its leaves are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to withstand salt spray and coastal winds.
