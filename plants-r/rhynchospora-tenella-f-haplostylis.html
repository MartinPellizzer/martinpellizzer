Rhynchospora tenella f. haplostylis

Rhynchospora tenella f. haplostylis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed spike-rush, is a botanical variant of the spike-rush genus Rhynchospora.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and tapering, contributing to its overall grass-like appearance.

This fern-like plant plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and contributing to soil stabilization.
