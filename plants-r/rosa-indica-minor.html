Rosa indica minor

Rosa indica minor, commonly known as the small Indian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including India, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically produces small, clustered blooms that are often used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are thorny.

Rosa indica minor thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
