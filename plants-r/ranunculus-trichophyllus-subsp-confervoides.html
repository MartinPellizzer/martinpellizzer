Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp. confervoides

Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp.

confervoides, commonly known as the greenleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
