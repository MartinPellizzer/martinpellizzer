Rhytidophyllum tomentosum var. nudum

Rhytidophyllum tomentosum var. nudum, commonly known as the naked rhytidophyllum, is a unique species within the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its reduced foliage and distinctive, smooth, waxy leaves that lack the dense pubescence typical of the standard variety. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent-like leaves and efficient water storage capabilities.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its morphological divergence from its more densely hairy relatives.
