Rosa plumbeiformis

Rosa plumbeiformis, commonly known as the bluish rose or the pale rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, red hips similar to other rose species.

Rosa plumbeiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
