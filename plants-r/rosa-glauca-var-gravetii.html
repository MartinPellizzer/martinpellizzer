Rosa glauca var. gravetii

Rosa glauca var. gravetii, commonly known as the blue rose or alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange, containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
