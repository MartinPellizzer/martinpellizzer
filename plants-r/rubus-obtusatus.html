Rubus obtusatus

Rubus obtusatus, commonly known as the bluntleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with rounded lobes. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife.
