Rubus palmatus f. inermis

Rubus palmatus f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth-leafed raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, unarmed stems, distinguishing it from other Rubus species that typically have thorns. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild-flavored berries.
