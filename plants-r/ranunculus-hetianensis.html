Ranunculus hetianensis

Ranunculus hetianensis, commonly known as the Xinjiang buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Xinjiang region of China.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and arid environments. The plant features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are palmately divided. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other buttercup species.

Ranunculus hetianensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
