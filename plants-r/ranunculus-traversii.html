Ranunculus traversii

Ranunculus traversii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to ten petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for high-elevation ecosystems.
