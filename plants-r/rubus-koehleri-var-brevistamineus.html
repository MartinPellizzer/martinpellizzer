Rubus koehleri var. brevistamineus

Rubus koehleri var. brevistamineus, commonly known as the short-stamen raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in commercial cultivation.
