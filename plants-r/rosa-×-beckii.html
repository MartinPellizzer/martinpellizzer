Rosa × beckii

Rosa × beckii, commonly known as the Beck's rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and open areas, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa × beckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
