Rosa alpina var. hispidella

Rosa alpina var. hispidella, commonly known as the alpine rose or mountain rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically dark green, glossy, and arranged in odd-pinnate patterns. This plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
