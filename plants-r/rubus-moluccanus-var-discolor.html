Rubus moluccanus var. discolor

Rubus moluccanus var. discolor, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various wildlife and its potential horticultural value.
