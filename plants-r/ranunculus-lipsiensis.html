Ranunculus lipsiensis

Ranunculus lipsiensis, commonly known as the Lithuanian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Ranunculus lipsiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local biodiversity.
