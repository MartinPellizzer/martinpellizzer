Rubus polyodontus

Rubus polyodontus, commonly known as the many-toothed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and raspberries.
