Ribes grossularia var. adenocarpum

Ribes grossularia var. adenocarpum, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits called "currants.". Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, alternate leaf arrangement, and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
