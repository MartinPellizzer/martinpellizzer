Rosa afzeliana f. rectispina

Rosa afzeliana f. rectispina, commonly known as the straight-spined rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which are more pronounced than in the typical form. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets that are oval and serrated.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates.
