Racosperma drepanocarpum var. latifolium

Racosperma drepanocarpum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it ecologically significant. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

The variety latifolium is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other varieties of the species.
