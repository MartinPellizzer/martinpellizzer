Rubus × infuscus

Rubus × infuscus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
