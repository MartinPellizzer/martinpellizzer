Rhododendron seniavinii

Rhododendron seniavinii, commonly known as the Seniavinii rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Russia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates with partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers are typically pink to red in color, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
