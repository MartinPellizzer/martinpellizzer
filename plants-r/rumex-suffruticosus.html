Rumex suffruticosus

Rumex suffruticosus, commonly known as creeping dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, ditches, and along roadsides. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

The leaves and stems are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.
