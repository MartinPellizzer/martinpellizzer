Racosperma quinquenervium

Racosperma quinquenervium, commonly known as the five-veined acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with five distinct veins. The plant produces yellow flowers in compact spikes, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
