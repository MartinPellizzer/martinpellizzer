Rubus steudneri var. sidamensis

Rubus steudneri var. sidamensis, commonly known as the Sidamensis blackberry, is a shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
