Roseia

Roseia, commonly known as the roseleaf plant, is a flowering plant species characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes roses, apples, and cherries, and is native to various regions in Europe and Asia. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, pink or white flowers that attract pollinators. Roseia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both decorative and ecological purposes.
