Rubus flavifrons

Rubus flavifrons, commonly known as the yellow-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many shrubs with thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
