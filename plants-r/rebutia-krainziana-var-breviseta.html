Rebutia krainziana var. breviseta

Rebutia krainziana var. breviseta, commonly known as the short-haired rebutia, is a succulent plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense, short spines. The plant features fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in early spring, and it is prized for its ornamental value in rock gardens and as a houseplant.

Due to its slow growth and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
