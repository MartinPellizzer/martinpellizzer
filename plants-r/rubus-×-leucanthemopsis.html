Rubus × leucanthemopsis

Rubus × leucanthemopsis, commonly known as the white-berried raspberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus idaeus (common raspberry) and Rubus leucanthemus (white-flowered raspberry). It is characterized by its white or pale yellow flowers and small, often white or pale red berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and pinnate leaves.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit characteristics.
