Rhododendron sanguineum subsp. roseotinctum

Rhododendron sanguineum subsp.

roseotinctum, commonly known as the rose-tinctured azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, pink to rose-red flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

It is a popular ornamental plant valued for its ornate floral display and adaptability to various garden settings.
