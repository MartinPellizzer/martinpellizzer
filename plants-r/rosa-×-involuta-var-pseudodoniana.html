Rosa × involuta var. pseudodoniana

Rosa × involuta var. pseudodoniana, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
