Rhododendron javanicum subsp. teysmannii

Rhododendron javanicum subsp.

teysmannii, commonly known as the Java rhododendron, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with rich, acidic soils. Its botanical features include thick, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited to tropical climates.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in native habitats.
