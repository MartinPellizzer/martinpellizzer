Rosa maximiliana

Rosa maximiliana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, making it a nocturnal bloomer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces rose-shaped flowers that emit a sweet scent. Rosa maximiliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soil.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
