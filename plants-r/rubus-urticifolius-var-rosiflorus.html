Rubus urticifolius var. rosiflorus

Rubus urticifolius var. rosiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered bramble, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits, which are edible, are small, red, and somewhat tart, often used in local cuisines.

The species is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its resilience in harsh environments.
