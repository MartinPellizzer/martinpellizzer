Rosa johannensis f. albinea

Rosa johannensis f. albinea, commonly known as the white form of the Johann's rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its pale pink to white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are usually borne in clusters and bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. This cultivar is prized for its delicate appearance and is often used in floral arrangements.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rosa species and is valued for its horticultural appeal.
