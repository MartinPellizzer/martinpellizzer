Rustia alba

Rustia alba, commonly known as the white rust or white sage, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Rustia alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
