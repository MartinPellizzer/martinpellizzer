Rhamnus globosa var. ziziphifolia

Rhamnus globosa var. ziziphifolia, commonly known as the small-fruited buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, egg-shaped leaves and produces small, dark berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its flowers are clustered and typically appear in early spring, followed by the development of its distinctive fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
