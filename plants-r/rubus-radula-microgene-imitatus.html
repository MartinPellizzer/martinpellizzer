Rubus radula microgene imitatus

Rubus radula microgene imitatus, commonly known as the mimic raspberry, is a rare and elusive plant species characterized by its unique botanical features.

It possesses small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically pink or white in color, and is often mistaken for other Rubus species due to its similar appearance. The plant's leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated along the edges. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests or along stream banks.
