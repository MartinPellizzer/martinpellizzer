Rumex polyrrhizus

Rumex polyrrhizus, commonly known as the broadleaf dock, is a perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its roots are thick and fleshy, allowing it to thrive in moist, disturbed soils.

Rumex polyrrhizus is often found along roadsides, in fields, and near water sources.
