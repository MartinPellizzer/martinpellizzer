Regnellia purpurea

Regnellia purpurea, commonly known as the purple regnellia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescence.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
