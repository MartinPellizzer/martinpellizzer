Rabdosia latifolia

Rabdosia latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
