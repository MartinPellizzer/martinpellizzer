Rhododendron simiarum subsp. youngae

Rhododendron simiarum subsp.

youngae, commonly known as the young's rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, preferring acidic, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves that are often dark green with a leathery texture.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
