Radiola linoidea

Radiola linoidea, commonly known as the linseed rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are finely divided and have a peppery aroma, similar to other members of the mustard family.

Radiola linoidea is also known for its edible leaves, which can be used in salads or as a garnish.
