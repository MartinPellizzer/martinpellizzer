Rosa × engelmannii nothosubsp.

engelmannii, commonly known as the Engelmann rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant typically features thorny stems and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
