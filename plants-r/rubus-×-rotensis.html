Rubus × rotensis

Rubus × rotensis, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is cultivated for its edible berries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, which develops from the flower, is a cluster of small drupes known as achenes.
