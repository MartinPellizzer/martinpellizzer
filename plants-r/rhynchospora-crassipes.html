Rhynchospora crassipes

Rhynchospora crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It features slender, grass-like stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in marshy or flooded soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Rhynchospora crassipes plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
