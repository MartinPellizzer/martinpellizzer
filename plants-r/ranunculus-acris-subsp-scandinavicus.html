Ranunculus acris subsp. scandinavicus

Ranunculus acris subsp.

scandinavicus, commonly known as the Scandinavian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
