Ruellia glischrocalyx

Ruellia glischrocalyx, commonly known as the white ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Ruellia glischrocalyx is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
