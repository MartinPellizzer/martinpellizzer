Rubus × semipurpuratus

Rubus × semipurpuratus, commonly known as the purple-leaf raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged foliage, which sets it apart from other raspberry varieties. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or reddish in color. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique coloration, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
