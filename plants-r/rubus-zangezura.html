Rubus zangezura

Rubus zangezura, commonly known as the Afghan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
