Rubus acanthostachys

Rubus acanthostachys, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
