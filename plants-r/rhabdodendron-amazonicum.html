Rhabdodendron amazonicum

Rhabdodendron amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has a robust, upright growth habit with thick, waxy leaves that help it thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its bark is smooth and often reddish in color, providing a distinctive appearance.

Rhabdodendron amazonicum plays an important ecological role in its habitat, offering food and shelter to numerous species of wildlife.
