Rubus rivularis var. mundiflorus

Rubus rivularis var. mundiflorus, commonly known as the river raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are generally red or yellow in color, often appearing in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems that are characteristic of the Rubus genus.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
