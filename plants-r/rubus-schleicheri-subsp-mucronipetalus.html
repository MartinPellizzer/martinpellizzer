Rubus schleicheri subsp. mucronipetalus

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

mucronipetalus, commonly known as the blunt-petled blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins. The flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
