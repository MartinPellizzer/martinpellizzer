Rhaponticum cossonianum

Rhaponticum cossonianum, commonly known as the Cossonian rhubarb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features a robust, rhizomatous root system and erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its edible roots, which have a tangy flavor similar to rhubarb.
