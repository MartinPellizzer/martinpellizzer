Romulea clusiana

Romulea clusiana, commonly known as the yellow orchid or the honey-scented orchid, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble orchids. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a corm as its underground storage organ. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Romulea clusiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral appearance.
