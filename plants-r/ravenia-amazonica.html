Ravenia amazonica

Ravenia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian ravenia, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy green leaves and showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with bacteria. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
