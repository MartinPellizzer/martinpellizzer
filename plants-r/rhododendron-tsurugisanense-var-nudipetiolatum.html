Rhododendron tsurugisanense var. nudipetiolatum

Rhododendron tsurugisanense var. nudipetiolatum, commonly known as the Tsurugisanense naked petiole rhododendron, is a rare and unique variant of the rhododendron species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive lack of petioles on its leaves, which gives it a striking, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited natural habitat and rarity, it is considered a valuable and endangered species in botanical conservation efforts.
