Rhabdodendron crassipes var. cayenense

Rhabdodendron crassipes var. cayenense, commonly known as the Cayenne rhododendron, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane forests. Its leaves are leathery and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are often fragrant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in warm climates.
