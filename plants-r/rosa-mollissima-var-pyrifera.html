Rosa mollissima var. pyrifera

Rosa mollissima var. pyrifera, commonly known as the Pyrifera Rosa, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California, where it thrives in sandy soils and coastal climates. The plant is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a perennial shrub that often grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
