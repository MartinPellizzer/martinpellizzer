Rosa pouzinii var. nuda

Rosa pouzinii var. nuda, commonly known as the naked rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, thornless stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are often hairy beneath. This variety is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal or rocky gardens.
