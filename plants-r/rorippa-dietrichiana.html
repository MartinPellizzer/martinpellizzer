Rorippa dietrichiana

Rorippa dietrichiana, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating on the surface of water. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in wetland environments such as ponds, marshes, and streams. Rorippa dietrichiana has a short, stout stem and produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by water.
