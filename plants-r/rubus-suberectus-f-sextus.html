Rubus suberectus f. sextus

Rubus suberectus f. sextus, commonly known as the six-stamened blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its unique floral structure.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are thorny.

This form is notable for its genetic variation, which contributes to its distinct floral morphology and horticultural interest.
