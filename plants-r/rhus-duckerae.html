Rhus duckerae

Rhus duckerae, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, hammocks, and along stream banks.

Rhus duckerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
