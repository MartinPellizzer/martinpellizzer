Reichardia macrophylla

Reichardia macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf water flea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.

Reichardia macrophylla is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a resilient and potentially invasive species in certain environments.
