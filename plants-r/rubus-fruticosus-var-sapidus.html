Rubus fruticosus var. sapidus

Rubus fruticosus var. sapidus, commonly known as the sweet blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe, with a sweet and tart flavor. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is rich in antioxidants and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
