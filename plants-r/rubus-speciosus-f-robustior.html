Rubus speciosus f. robustior

Rubus speciosus f. robustior, commonly known as the robust raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry that exhibits more vigorous growth.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of multiple small drupes, typically turning red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
