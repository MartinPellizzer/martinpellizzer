Rubus genevieri var. hystricopsis

Rubus genevieri var. hystricopsis, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
