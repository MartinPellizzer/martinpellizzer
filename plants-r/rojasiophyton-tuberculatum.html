Rojasiophyton tuberculatum

Rojasiophyton tuberculatum, commonly known as the warty leaf plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often swollen leaves that serve as adaptations to conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its growth habit is typically low and sprawling, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Rojasiophyton tuberculatum is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
