Rubus tereticaulis var. subtilidentus

Rubus tereticaulis var. subtilidentus, commonly known as the slender-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
