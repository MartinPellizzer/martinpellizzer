Rudbeckia laciniata var. ampla

Rudbeckia laciniata var. ampla, commonly known as the giant horseweed, is a striking perennial plant native to North America.

It features large, showy yellow flowers with dark centers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for wildflower gardens.

Its tall stature and vibrant blooms make it a notable addition to native plant landscapes.
