Rhododendron glischrum subsp. rude

Rhododendron glischrum subsp.

rude, commonly known as the rough rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments. It is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

The subspecies is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to harsh alpine conditions.
