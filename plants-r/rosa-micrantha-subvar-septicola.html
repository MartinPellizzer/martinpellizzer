Rosa micrantha subvar. septicola

Rosa micrantha subvar. septicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves and produces hips that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
