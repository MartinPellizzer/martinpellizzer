Rosa rubiginosa var. comosella

Rosa rubiginosa var. comosella, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges and has a woody, shrub-like structure. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedgerows.

The fruit, known as a hip, is red, hairy, and contains several seeds.
