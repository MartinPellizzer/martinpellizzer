Ruellia geniculata

Ruellia geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers and its distinctive zigzagging stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Ruellia geniculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
