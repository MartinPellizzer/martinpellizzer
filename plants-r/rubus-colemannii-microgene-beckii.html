Rubus colemannii microgene beckii

Rubus colemannii microgene beckii, commonly known as the Colemann's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit, a dark purple-blackberry, is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
