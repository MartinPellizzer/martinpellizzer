Rumex fueginus var. brachythrix

Rumex fueginus var. brachythrix, commonly known as the short-hair dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems covered with short, stiff hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a wavy margin and a prominent midrib, and they are often covered with fine pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically surrounded by bracts.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along streams.
