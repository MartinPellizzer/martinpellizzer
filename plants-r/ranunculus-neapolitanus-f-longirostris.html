Ranunculus neapolitanus f. longirostris

Ranunculus neapolitanus f. longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked Italian buttercup, is a botanical variant of the Italian buttercup.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow sepals that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features bright yellow petals and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
