Rubia infundibularis

Rubia infundibularis, commonly known as the hooded madder, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their ornamental and medicinal uses. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. Rubia infundibularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its historical use in dye production.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable addition to both horticultural and ecological landscapes.
