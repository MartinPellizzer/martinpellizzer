Ranunculus auricomus subsp. procurrentidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

procurrentidens, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals and a distinctive yellow center.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are usually hairy.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, cheerful blooms.
