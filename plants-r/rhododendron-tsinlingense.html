Rhododendron tsinlingense

Rhododendron tsinlingense, commonly known as the Tsingling rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically found in the Tsingling Mountains of Guangxi Province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in trusses, displaying vibrant pink to purple hues, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
