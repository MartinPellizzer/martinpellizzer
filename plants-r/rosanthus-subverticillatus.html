Rosanthus subverticillatus

Rosanthus subverticillatus, commonly known as the spiralflower, is a unique species characterized by its intricate floral arrangement.

It features a spiral pattern of petals that radiates from a central point, giving it a visually striking appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
