Rhododendron auriculatum

Rhododendron auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically ovate with a distinctive auriculate base, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
