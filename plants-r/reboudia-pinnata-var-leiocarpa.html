Reboudia pinnata var. leiocarpa

Reboudia pinnata var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the silky leaf waterleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, smooth seeds.

This species is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
