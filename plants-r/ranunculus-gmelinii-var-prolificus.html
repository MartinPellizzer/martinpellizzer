Ranunculus gmelinii var. prolificus

Ranunculus gmelinii var. prolificus, commonly known as the prolific buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
