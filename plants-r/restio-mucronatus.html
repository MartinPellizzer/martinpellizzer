Restio mucronatus

Restio mucronatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family, which is characteristic of the fynbos vegetation type. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny white or pale blue flowers.

Restio mucronatus is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region.
