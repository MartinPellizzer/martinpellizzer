Ranunculus auricomus subsp. lepidus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

lepidus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and palmate leaves.

This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to high-altitude environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and finely divided leaves, which help it thrive in harsh, cold climates.
