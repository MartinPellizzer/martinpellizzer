Rubus schleicheri var. torrentium

Rubus schleicheri var. torrentium, commonly known as the torrent raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of wild berries.
