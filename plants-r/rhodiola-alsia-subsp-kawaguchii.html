Rhodiola alsia subsp. kawaguchii

Rhodiola alsia subsp.

kawaguchii, commonly known as the Japanese rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is known for its adaptability to harsh climates and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other Rhodiola species.
