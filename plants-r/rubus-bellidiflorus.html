Rubus bellidiflorus

Rubus bellidiflorus, commonly known as the bellflower raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, giving them a fluffy appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and serves as a food source for various wildlife species.
