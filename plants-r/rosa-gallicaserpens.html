Rosa gallicaserpens

Rosa gallicaserpens, commonly known as the French climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and its roots are fibrous and adaptable to various soil types. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This species is also valued for its historical use in perfume and medicinal applications.
