Rhamnus alaternus var. gebelensis

Rhamnus alaternus var. gebelensis, commonly known as the Gebelensis buckthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but contain toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
