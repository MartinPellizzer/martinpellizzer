Rosa sanctae-andreae

Rosa sanctae-andreae, commonly known as the Andean rose, is a species of shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red fruits that contain seeds.

Rosa sanctae-andreae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
