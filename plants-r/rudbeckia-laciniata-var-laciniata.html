Rudbeckia laciniata var. laciniata

Rudbeckia laciniata var. laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf coneflower, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It features large, showy yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by broad, lobed green leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, with a robust stem that is often hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers bloom from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
