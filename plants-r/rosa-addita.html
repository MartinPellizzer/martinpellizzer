Rosa addita

Rosa addita, commonly known as the added rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it produces rose hips that are often used in traditional medicine.

Rosa addita is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its ecological importance in its native habitats.
