Ruehssia neriifolia

Ruehssia neriifolia, commonly known as the black-leafed ruehssia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy foliage that gives it its common name, and its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in tropical gardens. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Ruehssia neriifolia is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
