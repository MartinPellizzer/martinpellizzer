Ribes mexicanum

Ribes mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican gooseberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes in the family Grossulariaceae, which includes other species of currants and gooseberries. The plant produces small, yellow to orange berries that are typically eaten raw or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Ribes mexicanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
