Rosa rubiginosa var. braunii

Rosa rubiginosa var. braunii, commonly known as the Braun's brambles, is a shrub-like species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces red, hairy fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is closely related to other Rosa rubiginosa forms and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
