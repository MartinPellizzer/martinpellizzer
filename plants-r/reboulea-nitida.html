Reboulea nitida

Reboulea nitida, commonly known as the silky gum, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, elongated fruits that are popular among wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or coastal areas.

Reboulea nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
