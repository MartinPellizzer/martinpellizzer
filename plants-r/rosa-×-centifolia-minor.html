Rosa × centifolia minor

Rosa × centifolia minor, commonly known as the miniature pink or miniature damask rose, is a hybrid species of the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a velvety texture. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, with thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

Rosa × centifolia minor thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
