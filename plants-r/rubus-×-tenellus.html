Rubus × tenellus

Rubus × tenellus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is often cultivated in gardens and for small-scale farming.
