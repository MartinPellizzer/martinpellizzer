Rosa pimpinellifolia var. mitissima

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. mitissima, commonly known as the soft pink rose, is a cultivar of the shrub rose species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in clusters and emit a delicate fragrance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has thorny stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This variety is well-suited for gardens and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
