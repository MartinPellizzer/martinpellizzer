Rubus maassii subsp. scheutzii

Rubus maassii subsp.

scheutzii, commonly known as the Maasii raspberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
