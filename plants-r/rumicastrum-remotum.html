Rumicastrum remotum

Rumicastrum remotum, commonly known as the desert holly, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and the ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Rumicastrum remotum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
