Ruellia currorii

Ruellia currorii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.

Ruellia currorii is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
