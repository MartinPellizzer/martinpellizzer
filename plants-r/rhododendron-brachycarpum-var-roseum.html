Rhododendron brachycarpum var. roseum

Rhododendron brachycarpum var. roseum, commonly known as the rose-flowered rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinct rose-like appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade and well-drained soil.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
