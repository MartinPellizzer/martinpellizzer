Rubus glomeratus var. pileanus

Rubus glomeratus var. pileanus, commonly known as the clustered blackberry bush, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
