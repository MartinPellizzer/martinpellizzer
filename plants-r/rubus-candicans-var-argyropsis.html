Rubus candicans var. argyropsis

Rubus candicans var. argyropsis, commonly known as the silverleaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, though they are not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various wildlife species.
