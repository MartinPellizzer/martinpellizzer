Rubus pedunculosus var. hypargyrus

Rubus pedunculosus var. hypargyrus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and compound leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in arid to semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
