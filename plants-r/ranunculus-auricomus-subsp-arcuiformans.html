Ranunculus auricomus subsp. arcuiformans

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

arcuiformans, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five to nine petal-like sepals, which are often mistaken for true petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with lobed margins. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in garden settings.
