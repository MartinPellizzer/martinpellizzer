Rosa canina var. sepium

Rosa canina var. sepium, commonly known as the dog rose or shrub rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, hairy berries that are rich in vitamin C and are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
