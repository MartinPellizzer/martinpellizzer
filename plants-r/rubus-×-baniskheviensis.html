Rubus × baniskheviensis

Rubus × baniskheviensis, commonly known as the Georgian blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the Caucasus region, specifically Georgia, where it grows in mountainous areas. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. Its fruit is a dark purple to black berry, which is edible and often used in local cuisine.

This species is notable for its genetic diversity and potential for breeding programs aimed at improving fruit quality and disease resistance.
