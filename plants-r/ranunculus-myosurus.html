Ranunculus myosurus

Ranunculus myosurus, commonly known as the mouse-ear buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that resemble the ears of a mouse, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus myosurus is often found in nutrient-rich soils and is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.
