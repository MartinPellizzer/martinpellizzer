Rosa afzeliana var. dalvazzensis

Rosa afzeliana var. dalvazzensis, commonly known as the Dalvazza rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features dense, thorny stems and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
