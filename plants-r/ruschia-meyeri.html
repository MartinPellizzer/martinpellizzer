Ruschia meyeri

Ruschia meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's Ruschia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine white powdery coating, known as farina. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Ruschia meyeri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
