Rubus × pygmaeiformis

Rubus × pygmaeiformis, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, trailing growth habit and produces small, red berries similar to those of other raspberries. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it flowers with white to pale pink petals.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in local cuisines.
