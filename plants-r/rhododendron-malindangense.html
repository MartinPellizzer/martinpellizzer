Rhododendron malindangense

Rhododendron malindangense, commonly known as the Malindangense rhododendron, is a species native to the Philippines, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Mindanao.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers during the spring season. Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ericaceae family.

It is often valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
