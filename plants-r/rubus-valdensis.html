Rubus valdensis

Rubus valdensis, commonly known as the Valdese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
