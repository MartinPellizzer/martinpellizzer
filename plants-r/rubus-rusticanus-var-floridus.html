Rubus rusticanus var. floridus

Rubus rusticanus var. floridus, commonly known as the Florida blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
