Rebutia eucaliptana

Rebutia eucaliptana, commonly known as the Eucalyptus Rebutia, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, woolly spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens.
