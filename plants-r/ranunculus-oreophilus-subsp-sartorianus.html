Ranunculus oreophilus subsp. sartorianus

Ranunculus oreophilus subsp.

sartorianus, commonly known as the Sierra buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent green sepals. It is characterized by its hairy stems and its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
