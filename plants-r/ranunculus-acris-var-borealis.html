Ranunculus acris var. borealis

Ranunculus acris var. borealis, commonly known as the northern buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, tundra, and wetlands. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. It is characterized by its short stature and often grows in dense clusters.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.
