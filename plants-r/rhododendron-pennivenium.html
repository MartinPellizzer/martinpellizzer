Rhododendron pennivenium

Rhododendron pennivenium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blossoms.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
