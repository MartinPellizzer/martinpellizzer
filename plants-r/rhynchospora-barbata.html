Rhynchospora barbata

Rhynchospora barbata, commonly known as the hairy spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typical of the sedge family. Rhynchospora barbata is often found in shallow water or moist soil, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland habitats.

Its hairy texture on the stems and leaves distinguishes it from other similar species.
