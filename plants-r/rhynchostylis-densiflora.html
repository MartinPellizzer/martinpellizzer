Rhynchostylis densiflora

Rhynchostylis densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to indoor cultivation.
