Ricinocarpus lepinei

Ricinocarpus lepinei, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain flat, seed-bearing structures called samaras. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its fast growth and ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
