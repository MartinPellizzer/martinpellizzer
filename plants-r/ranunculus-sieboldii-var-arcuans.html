Ranunculus sieboldii var. arcuans

Ranunculus sieboldii var. arcuans, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive black center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. It is characterized by its short stature and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
