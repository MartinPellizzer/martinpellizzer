Rheum likiangense

Rheum likiangense, commonly known as the Yunnan rhubarb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine, valued for their medicinal properties.

Rheum likiangense is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, moist climates.
