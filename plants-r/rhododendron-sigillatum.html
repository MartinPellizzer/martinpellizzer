Rhododendron sigillatum

Rhododendron sigillatum, commonly known as the Sierra Valley rhododendron, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in mountainous regions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron sigillatum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
