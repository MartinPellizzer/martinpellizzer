Rubus × exilis

Rubus × exilis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to parts of North America and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries.

The botanical features of Rubus × exilis make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
