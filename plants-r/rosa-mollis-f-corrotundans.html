Rosa mollis f. corrotundans

Rosa mollis f. corrotundans, commonly known as the round-leaved dog rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features dense, thornless stems and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The leaves are rounded and hairy, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
