Rubus sapidus

Rubus sapidus, commonly known as the sweet blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets that ripen to a deep purple or black color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The berries are not only edible but also valued for their flavor and nutritional content.
