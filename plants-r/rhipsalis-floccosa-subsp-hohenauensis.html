Rhipsalis floccosa subsp. hohenauensis

Rhipsalis floccosa subsp.

hohenauensis, commonly known as the Hohenau's woolly cactus, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy stems that are often covered in white or grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with branching stems that can reach several meters in height. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the rainy season.

This subspecies is adapted to humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil.
