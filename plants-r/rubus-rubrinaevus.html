Rubus rubrinaevus

Rubus rubrinaevus, commonly known as the crimson raspberry.

This species is a deciduous shrub characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though the exact coloration may vary depending on environmental conditions. The plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
