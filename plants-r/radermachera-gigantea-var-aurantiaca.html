Radermachera gigantea var. aurantiaca

Radermachera gigantea var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that exhibit a vibrant green color with a distinctive orange or reddish edge. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for those seeking a dramatic tropical look.
