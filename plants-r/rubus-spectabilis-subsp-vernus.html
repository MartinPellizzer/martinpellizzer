Rubus spectabilis subsp. vernus

Rubus spectabilis subsp.

vernus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species native to western North America. It is a perennial shrub that produces edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
