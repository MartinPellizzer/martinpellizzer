Rubus alnifolius

Rubus alnifolius, commonly known as the alderleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically three to five leaflets, resembling those of alder trees. The plant produces small, edible berries that are similar in appearance to other raspberries but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other species within the Rubus genus.
