Rubus fuscus var. nutans

Rubus fuscus var. nutans, commonly known as the nodding blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, often in temperate climates.

Its flowers are pale greenish-white and have a distinctive nodding habit, which is a key identifying feature.
