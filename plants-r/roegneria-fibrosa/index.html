Roegneria Fibrosa

Roegneria grass, scientifically known as Roegneria fibrosa, is a hardy perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, fibrous blades that often form dense mats on the ground. This grass is commonly found in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects. Due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements, Roegneria fibrosa is also gaining popularity in landscaping and conservation efforts.
