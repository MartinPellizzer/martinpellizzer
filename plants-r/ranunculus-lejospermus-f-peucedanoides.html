Ranunculus lejospermus f. peucedanoides

Ranunculus lejospermus f. peucedanoides, commonly known as the peucedanoid buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup genus characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately divided leaves, which are usually lobed and have a hairy texture. It is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils.

This form is notable for its resemblance to the peucedanum genus, leading to its specific epithet.
