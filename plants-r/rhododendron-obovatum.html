Rhododendron obovatum

Rhododendron obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Rhododendron obovatum is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
