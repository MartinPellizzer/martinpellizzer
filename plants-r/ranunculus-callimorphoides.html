Ranunculus callimorphoides

Ranunculus callimorphoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
