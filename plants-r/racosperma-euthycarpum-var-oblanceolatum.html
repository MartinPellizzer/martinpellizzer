Racosperma euthycarpum var. oblanceolatum

Racosperma euthycarpum var. oblanceolatum, commonly known as the slender-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
