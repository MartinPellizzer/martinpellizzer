Rosa mollissima subsp. resinoides

Rosa mollissima subsp.

resinoides, commonly known as the resinous rose, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange, containing seeds within a fleshy outer layer. Its foliage is usually covered in a fine, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
