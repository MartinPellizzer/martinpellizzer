Ranunculus saelanii

Ranunculus saelanii, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. It is known for its ability to survive in harsh, high-altitude environments.

Ranunculus saelanii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
