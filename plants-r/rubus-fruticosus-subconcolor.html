Rubus fruticosus subconcolor

Rubus fruticosus subconcolor, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits that develop from multiple tiny drupes, typically turning dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
