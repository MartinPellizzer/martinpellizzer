Rubus brevipetiolatus

Rubus brevipetiolatus, commonly known as the short-petioled blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with short petioles. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
