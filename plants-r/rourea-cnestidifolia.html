Rourea cnestidifolia

Rourea cnestidifolia, commonly known as the white rourea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Rourea cnestidifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
