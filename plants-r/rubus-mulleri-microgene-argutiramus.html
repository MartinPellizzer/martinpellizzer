Rubus mulleri microgene argutiramus

Rubus mulleri microgene argutiramus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
