Rubus gymnostachys proles obscurifrons

Rubus gymnostachys proles obscurifrons, commonly known as the dark-fruited bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark in color, often with a slightly hairy surface. Its leaves are typically alternate, with serrated edges and a hairy underside.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or disturbed habitats.
