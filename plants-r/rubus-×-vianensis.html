Rubus × vianensis

Rubus × vianensis, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex genetic makeup resulting from crossbreeding between different Rubus species.
