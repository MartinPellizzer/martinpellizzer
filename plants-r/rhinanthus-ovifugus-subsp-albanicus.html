Rhinanthus ovifugus subsp. albanicus

Rhinanthus ovifugus subsp.

albanicus, commonly known as the Albanian horsebean, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
