Rosa communis var. orbiculata

Rosa communis var. orbiculata, commonly known as the round-leaf rose, is a cultivar characterized by its orbicular (rounded) leaves and vibrant, fragrant flowers.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to various regions in Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dense foliage. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is relatively hardy, though it may require protection in colder climates.
