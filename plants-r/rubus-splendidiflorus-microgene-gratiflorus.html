Rubus splendidiflorus microgene gratiflorus

Rubus splendidiflorus microgene gratiflorus, commonly known as the dazzling beautyberry, is a shrub native to the humid regions of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is notable for its striking, bright red berries that appear in the fall, which are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to various tropical and subtropical climates.
