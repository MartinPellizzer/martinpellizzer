Rubus obtruncatus var. albellus

Rubus obtruncatus var. albellus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red color. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
