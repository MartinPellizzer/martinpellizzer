Renealmia pirrensis

Renealmia pirrensis, commonly known as the Pirrense Reed, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of South America.

It typically grows in swampy or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to two meters, crowned with dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a waxy coating that helps repel water.

Renealmia pirrensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
