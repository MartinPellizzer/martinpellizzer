Ryticaryum racemosum

Ryticaryum racemosum, commonly known as the clustered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, branching stems that often form dense clusters. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, powdery coating, aiding in water retention.

Ryticaryum racemosum is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
