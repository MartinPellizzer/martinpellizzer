Rubus × subtilissimus

Rubus × subtilissimus, commonly known as the sweet cherry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red to black in color when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins, while its flowers are white or pale pink with five petals.

This species is valued for its edible fruits, which are rich in sugars and commonly cultivated for both commercial and home gardening purposes.
