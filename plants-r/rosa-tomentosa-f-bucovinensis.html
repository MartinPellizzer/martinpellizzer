Rosa tomentosa f. bucovinensis

Rosa tomentosa f. bucovinensis, commonly known as the Bucovina hairy rose, is a compact shrub with dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage.

Its flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Carpathian region and is characterized by its thorny stems and aromatic leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
