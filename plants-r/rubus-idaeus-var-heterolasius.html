Rubus idaeus var. heterolasius

Rubus idaeus var. heterolasius, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive red berries and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. It is a shrub that produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets. The leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in temperate climates.

It is cultivated for its edible fruits and has a long history of use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
