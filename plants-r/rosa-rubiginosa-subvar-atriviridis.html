Rosa rubiginosa subvar. atriviridis

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. atriviridis, commonly known as the dark green rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which are often covered in a layer of fine, woolly hair. The leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge, while the flowers are pink to red in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
