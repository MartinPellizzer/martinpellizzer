Ranunculus ophioglossifolius subsp. fontanus

Ranunculus ophioglossifolius subsp.

fontanus, commonly known as the Fontana buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring.

The leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure that resembles a fern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, early blooming flowers.
