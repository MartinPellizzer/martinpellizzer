Rosa horrida

Rosa horrida, commonly known as the bristly rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa horrida thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant in horticulture.
