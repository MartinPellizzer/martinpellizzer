Rubus hedycarpus subsp. cuspidifer

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

cuspidifer, commonly known as the prickly pear raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its berries.
