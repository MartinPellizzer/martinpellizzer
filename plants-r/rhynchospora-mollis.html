Rhynchospora mollis

Rhynchospora mollis, commonly known as slender beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Rhynchospora mollis produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, and it plays an important role in maintaining wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
