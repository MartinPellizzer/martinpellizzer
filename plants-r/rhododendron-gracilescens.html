Rhododendron gracilescens

Rhododendron gracilescens, commonly known as the slenderleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate floral display to its natural habitat.

Rhododendron gracilescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
