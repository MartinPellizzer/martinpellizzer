Robinia pseudoacacia f. pendula

Robinia pseudoacacia f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping false acacia, is a decorative tree characterized by its pendulous branches that cascade downward, creating a unique, flowing appearance.

It is a cultivar of the honeylocust tree, native to the southeastern United States. The plant features compound leaves that are typically bipinnate, with small, feathery leaflets. Its flowers are yellow and appear in long, drooping clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

The tree is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
