Randia manglaris

Randia manglaris, commonly known as mangrove randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is typically found in coastal areas, often growing in brackish or saline soils characteristic of mangrove ecosystems. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a small capsule that contains several seeds.

Randia manglaris plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
