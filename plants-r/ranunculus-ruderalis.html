Ranunculus ruderalis

Ranunculus ruderalis, commonly known as the rough buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. This species is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, due to its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic to some animals and humans.

It is typically found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
