Rosa × paritata

Rosa × paritata, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and broad, leathery leaves that provide structural support. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season. Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable plant suitable for various climates.

Rosa × paritata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
