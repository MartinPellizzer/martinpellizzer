Rhododendron versicolor

Rhododendron versicolor, commonly known as the variable rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing dense foliage that supports various wildlife species.

Rhododendron versicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different garden settings.
