Rhamnus pallasii

Rhamnus pallasii, commonly known as Pallas's buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia, including parts of China, Japan, and the Russian Far East.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are rich in anthocyanins. Its bark is thick and rugged, often marked with longitudinal fissures, and it has a deep root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Rhamnus pallasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
