Russelia campechiana

Russelia campechiana, commonly known as the Mexican flamebush, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is typically found in open areas such as coastal dunes and disturbed sites.

Russelia campechiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
