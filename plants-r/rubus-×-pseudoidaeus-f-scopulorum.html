Rubus × pseudoidaeus f. scopulorum

Rubus × pseudoidaeus f. scopulorum, commonly known as the rock raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, resembling raspberries in appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is commonly found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
