Rosa hirtula

Rosa hirtula, commonly known as the bristly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, thorny stems, and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces oval, red or orange hips that contain seeds.

Rosa hirtula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
