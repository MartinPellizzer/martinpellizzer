Rapistrum aegyptium subf. tenuifolium

Rapistrum aegyptium subf.

tenuifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf dock, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is an annual herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and alternate, lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on the upper part of the stem. Its leaves are often covered with fine hairs, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

It is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and other disturbed habitats across Europe and parts of Asia.
