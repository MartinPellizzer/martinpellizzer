Rosa sempervirens var. leiostyla

Rosa sempervirens var. leiostyla, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a variant of the evergreen rose species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains lush throughout the year, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and oval-shaped leaves.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of climates with proper care.
