Royena cistoides

Royena cistoides, commonly known as the rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Royena cistoides is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and harsh weather conditions.
