Rubus gensanicus

Rubus gensanicus, commonly known as the Genesee blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
