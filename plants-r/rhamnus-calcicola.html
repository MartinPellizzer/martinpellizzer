Rhamnus calcicola

Rhamnus calcicola, commonly known as the limestone buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in limestone habitats. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that attracts various bird species.

Rhamnus calcicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
