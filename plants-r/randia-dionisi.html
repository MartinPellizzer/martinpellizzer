Randia dionisi

Randia dionisi, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetland areas and along the edges of swamps. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a reddish or purplish hue.

Randia dionisi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
