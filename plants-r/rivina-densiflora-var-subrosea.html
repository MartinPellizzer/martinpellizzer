Rivina densiflora var. subrosea

Rivina densiflora var. subrosea, commonly known as the roseleaf rivina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fluff-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink to rose. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its showy, long-lasting flowers and compact growth habit.
