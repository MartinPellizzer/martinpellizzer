Racosperma maxwellii

Racosperma maxwellii, commonly known as the Maxwell's acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its woody structure and adaptability to arid conditions make it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Racosperma maxwellii is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and rocky outcrops.
