Rubus emergens

Rubus emergens, commonly known as the emergent raspberry, is a shrub native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Rubus emergens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
