Rubus × celadnensis

Rubus × celadnensis, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
