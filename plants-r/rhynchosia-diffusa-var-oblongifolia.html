Rhynchosia diffusa var. oblongifolia

Rhynchosia diffusa var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with oblong, lanceolate leaflets and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used as a food source. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
