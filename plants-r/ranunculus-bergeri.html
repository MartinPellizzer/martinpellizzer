Ranunculus bergeri

Ranunculus bergeri, commonly known as the Bergier's buttercup, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and the flowers usually have five to eight petal-like sepals.

Ranunculus bergeri is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
