Rhamnus oiwakensis

Rhamnus oiwakensis, commonly known as the Oiwake Rhamnus, is a species of flowering plant in the buckthorn family, Rhamnaceae.

It is native to the temperate regions of East Asia, particularly Japan, where it thrives in woodland and shrubland habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its flowers are clustered in small, greenish-white inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological contributions in its native environment.
