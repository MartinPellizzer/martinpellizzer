Rufodorsia congestiflora

Rufodorsia congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered red-flowered plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, red or reddish-orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
