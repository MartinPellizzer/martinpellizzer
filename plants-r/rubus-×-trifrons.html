Rubus × trifrons

Rubus × trifrons, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, temperate environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
