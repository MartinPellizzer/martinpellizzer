Rosa condensata

Rosa condensata, commonly known as the dense rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of China, Korea, and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense clusters of fragrant pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa condensata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
