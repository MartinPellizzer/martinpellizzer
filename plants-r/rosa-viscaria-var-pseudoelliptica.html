Rosa viscaria var. pseudoelliptica

Rosa viscaria var. pseudoelliptica, commonly known as the pseudoelliptic shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, elliptic to ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin and a dense covering of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to moist, woodland soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for pollinators.
