Rubus cryptadenus var. pubescens

Rubus cryptadenus var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
