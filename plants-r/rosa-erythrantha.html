Rosa erythrantha

Rosa erythrantha, commonly known as the scarlet rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of red and pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a taproot system and the ability to thrive in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Rosa erythrantha is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
