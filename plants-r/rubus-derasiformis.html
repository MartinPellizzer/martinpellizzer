Rubus derasiformis

Rubus derasiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated margins and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its edible berries and its role in local ecosystems.
