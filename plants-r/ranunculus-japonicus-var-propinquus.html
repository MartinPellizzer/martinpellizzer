Ranunculus japonicus var. propinquus

Ranunculus japonicus var. propinquus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of white or yellow sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy surface that helps retain moisture.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
