Ribes fragrans var. infracanum

Ribes fragrans var. infracanum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, arching branches with small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches long. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible red berries in late summer. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible yield.
