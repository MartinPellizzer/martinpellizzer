Ruellia pectinata

Ruellia pectinata, commonly known as the silky ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ruellia pectinata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and prolonged blooming period.
