Rhododendron dichroanthum var. scyphocalyx

Rhododendron dichroanthum var. scyphocalyx, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a distinctive two-toned appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
