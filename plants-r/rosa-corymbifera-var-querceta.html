Rosa corymbifera var. querceta

Rosa corymbifera var. querceta, commonly known as the oak-leaf rose, is a variant of the shrub rose species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble those of the oak tree, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
