Rosa afzeliana var. anatricha

Rosa afzeliana var. anatricha, commonly known as the Anatricha rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets, often covered in a fine layer of silvery-white hairs. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are armed with thorns, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
