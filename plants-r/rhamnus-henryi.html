Rhamnus henryi

Rhamnus henryi, commonly known as Chinese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue to black berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Rhamnus henryi is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
