Rubus koehleri var. setulosus

Rubus koehleri var. setulosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is occasionally grown in horticultural settings.
