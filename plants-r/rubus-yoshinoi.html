Rubus yoshinoi

Rubus yoshinoi, commonly known as Yoshino raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.
