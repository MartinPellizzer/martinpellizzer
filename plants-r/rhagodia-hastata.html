Rhagodia hastata

Rhagodia hastata, commonly known as the Narrow-leafed Saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Rhagodia hastata is often used in rehabilitation of degraded lands due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
