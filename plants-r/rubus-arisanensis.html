Rubus arisanensis

Rubus arisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
