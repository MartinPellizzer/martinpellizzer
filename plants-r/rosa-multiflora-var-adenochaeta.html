Rosa multiflora var. adenochaeta

Rosa multiflora var. adenochaeta, commonly known as the fragrant shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features arching stems with thorny branches and is characterized by its clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a slightly serrated margin. This variety is noted for its strong, sweet fragrance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
