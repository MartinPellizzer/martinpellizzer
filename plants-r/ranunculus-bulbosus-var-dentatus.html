Ranunculus bulbosus var. dentatus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. dentatus, commonly known as the toothed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals and a hairy, spiny stem. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often toothed, giving it its specific epithet "dentatus.". It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant contains toxic protoanemonin, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
