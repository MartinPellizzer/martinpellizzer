Rhopalocarpus coriaceus

Rhopalocarpus coriaceus, commonly known as the rough-leaved rhopalocarpus, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like texture, contributing to its common name. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The flowers are small and clustered, often in inflorescences, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
