Rottboellia foliata

Rottboellia foliata, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Rottboellia foliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate appearance.
