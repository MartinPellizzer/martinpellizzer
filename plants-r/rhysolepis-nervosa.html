Rhysolepis nervosa

Rhysolepis nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with alternating, linear leaflets, and its bark is often fissured and scaly. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Rhysolepis nervosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
