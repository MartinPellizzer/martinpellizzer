Rubus pedunculosus var. aitchisonii

Rubus pedunculosus var. aitchisonii, commonly known as the Aitchison's blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has small, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruits are small, dark berries that ripen in summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
