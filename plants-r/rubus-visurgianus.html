Rubus visurgianus

Rubus visurgianus, commonly known as the Visurgian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The berries are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine due to their high antioxidant content.
