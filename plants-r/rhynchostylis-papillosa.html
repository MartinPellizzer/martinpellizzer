Rhynchostylis papillosa

Rhynchostylis papillosa, commonly known as the hairy-styled orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and large, showy flowers with a distinctive hairy appearance on the column. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and wasps.

Rhynchostylis papillosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
