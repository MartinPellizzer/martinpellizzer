Rutidea parviflora

Rutidea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its small, white or pink flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

Rutidea parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
