Rubus callichroanthus

Rubus callichroanthus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
