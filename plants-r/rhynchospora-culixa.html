Rhynchospora culixa

Rhynchospora culixa, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as bogs and wet meadows, across temperate regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Rhynchospora culixa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
