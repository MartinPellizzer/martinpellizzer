Rubus lanyuensis

Rubus lanyuensis, commonly known as Lanyu blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually serrated with a glossy upper surface.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
