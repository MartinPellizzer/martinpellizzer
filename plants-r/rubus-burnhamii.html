Rubus burnhamii

Rubus burnhamii, commonly known as Burnham's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. This shrub typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, dark red berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
