Ranunculus aurasiacus

Ranunculus aurasiacus, commonly known as the Asian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Its botanical name reflects its geographic origin and its resemblance to other members of the Ranunculaceae family.
