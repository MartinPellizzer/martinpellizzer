Racosperma fagonioides

Racosperma fagonioides, commonly known as the Fagonioid flame tree, is a tropical tree native to northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
