Ranunculus auricomus subsp. membranifolius

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

membranifolius, commonly known as the membranous-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features showy yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, membranous layer, giving it its subspecies name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
