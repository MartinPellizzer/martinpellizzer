Rosa glauca f. atrichostylis

Rosa glauca f. atrichostylis, commonly known as the glaucous rose or the woolly-stemmed rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers and its distinctive, hairy stems and leaves. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
