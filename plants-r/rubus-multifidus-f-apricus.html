Rubus multifidus f. apricus

Rubus multifidus f. apricus, commonly known as the apricot raspberry, is a hybrid plant that combines characteristics of both raspberries and apricots.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, hairy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color, resembling apricots in appearance. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This unique species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
