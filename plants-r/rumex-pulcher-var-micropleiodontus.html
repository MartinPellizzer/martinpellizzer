Rumex pulcher var. micropleiodontus

Rumex pulcher var. micropleiodontus, commonly known as the small-leaf sorrel, is a variant of the sorrel genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It typically features heart-shaped leaves with a reddish tinge, which are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in culinary applications.
