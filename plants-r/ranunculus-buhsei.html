Ranunculus buhsei

Ranunculus buhsei, commonly known as the Buhsei buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features a short, upright stem with palmately divided leaves and solitary, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have five to seven sepals and numerous stamens, typical of the genus Ranunculus.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.
