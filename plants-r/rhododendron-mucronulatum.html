Rhododendron mucronulatum

Rhododendron mucronulatum, commonly known as the mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to deep red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron mucronulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
