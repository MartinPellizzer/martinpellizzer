Rubus lusitanicus subsp. cintranus

Rubus lusitanicus subsp.

cintranus, commonly known as the Portuguese blackberry, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and scrublands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
