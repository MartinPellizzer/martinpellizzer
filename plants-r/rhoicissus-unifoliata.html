Rhoicissus unifoliata

Rhoicissus unifoliata, commonly known as the single-leaf climbing plant, is a vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant is typically found growing along riverbanks and in moist, shaded areas. Its stems are tendril-like, allowing it to climb and spread along surfaces.

Rhoicissus unifoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its foliage and unique growth habit.
