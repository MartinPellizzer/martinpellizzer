Rosmarinus × lavandulaceus

Rosmarinus × lavandulaceus, commonly known as lavender rosemary, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between lavender (Lavandula) and rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis).

It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, featuring aromatic foliage with a blend of lavender and rosemary scents. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.

This hybrid is valued for its fragrance, ornamental appeal, and potential use in herbal remedies.
