Rosa daghestanica

Rosa daghestanica, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, including parts of Russia and Georgia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, pinnate leaves. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have a strong, sweet scent and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Rosa daghestanica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
