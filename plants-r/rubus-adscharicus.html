Rubus adscharicus

Rubus adscharicus, commonly known as the Caucasian raspberry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
