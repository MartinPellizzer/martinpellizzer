Ranunculus doerrii

Ranunculus doerrii, commonly known as the Doerrii crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is characterized by its short stature and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
