Rubus wuzhianus

Rubus wuzhianus, commonly known as the Wuzhian bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

The botanical features of Rubus wuzhianus make it an important species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
