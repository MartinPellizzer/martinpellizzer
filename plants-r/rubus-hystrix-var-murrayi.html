Rubus hystrix var. murrayi

Rubus hystrix var. murrayi, commonly known as the prickly raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
