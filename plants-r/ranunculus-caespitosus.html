Ranunculus caespitosus

Ranunculus caespitosus, commonly known as the tufted buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, grassy areas and along stream banks. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus caespitosus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
