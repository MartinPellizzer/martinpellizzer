Rosa jessleri

Rosa jessleri, commonly known as the Jessella rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Rosa jessleri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
