Rubus hirtus subsp. euhirtus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

euhirtus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, that are typically dark purple to black when mature. These berries are edible and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
