Rhamnus rhodopea subsp. anatolica

Rhamnus rhodopea subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

The plant is typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its fruit is a small, smooth capsule that contains several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
