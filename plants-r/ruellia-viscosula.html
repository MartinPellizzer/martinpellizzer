Ruellia viscosula

Ruellia viscosula, commonly known as sticky ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems that often have a sticky or glandular surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Ruellia viscosula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
