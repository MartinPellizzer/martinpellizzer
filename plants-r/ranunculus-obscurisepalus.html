Ranunculus obscurisepalus

Ranunculus obscurisepalus, commonly known as the dark-sepal buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous sepals and petals that are often similar in appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
