Rubus caesius var. dolomiticus

Rubus caesius var. dolomiticus, commonly known as the Dolomitic Raspberry, is a rare variant of the common raspberry found in specific alpine and limestone-rich environments.

This plant is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing leaves and the second year bearing flowers and fruits. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture. The flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruits are small, red berries with a tart flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
