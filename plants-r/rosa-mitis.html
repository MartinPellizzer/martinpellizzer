Rosa mitis

Rosa mitis, commonly known as the sweet william rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rosa mitis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
