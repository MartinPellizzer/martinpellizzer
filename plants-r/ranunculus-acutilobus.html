Ranunculus acutilobus

Ranunculus acutilobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
