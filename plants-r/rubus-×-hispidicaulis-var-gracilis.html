Rubus × hispidicaulis var. gracilis

Rubus × hispidicaulis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that range from red to black in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. It is often found in coastal and riparian habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
