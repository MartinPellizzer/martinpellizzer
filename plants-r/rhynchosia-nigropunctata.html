Rhynchosia nigropunctata

Rhynchosia nigropunctata, commonly known as the black-spotted cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with dark purple spots and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. Rhynchosia nigropunctata is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional cuisines.

It also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
