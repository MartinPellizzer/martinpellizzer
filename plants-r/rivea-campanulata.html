Rivea campanulata

Rivea campanulata, commonly known as the bell vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises in gardens.
