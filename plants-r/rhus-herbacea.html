Rhus herbacea

Rhus herbacea, commonly known as smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and turn vibrant red in the fall. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, fuzzy berries that attract birds.

Smooth sumac is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
