Rosa dumalis f. kosinsciana

Rosa dumalis f. kosinsciana, commonly known as the Kosinski's dog rose, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, thorned stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their delicate scent.

Rosa dumalis f. kosinsciana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
