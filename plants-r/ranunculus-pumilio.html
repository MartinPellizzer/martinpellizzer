Ranunculus pumilio

Ranunculus pumilio, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a small flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. The plant features delicate, hairy leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It is well adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Ranunculus pumilio plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.
