Rollinia papilionella

Rollinia papilionella, commonly known as the golden star soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces a large, spiny fruit with a yellowish-green exterior and a soft, creamy interior containing a single large seed. Its botanical features make it an ornamental plant and a source of traditional medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
