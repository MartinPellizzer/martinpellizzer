Rosa ovatifrons

Rosa ovatifrons, commonly known as the oval-fruited rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval-shaped rose hips, which are typically red or orange in color and contain seeds. Rosa ovatifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
