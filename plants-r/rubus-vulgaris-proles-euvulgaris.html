Rubus vulgaris proles euvulgaris

Rubus vulgaris proles euvulgaris, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that produces edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit develops from the flower and is often used in culinary applications.

The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found growing in hedgerows and woodland edges.
