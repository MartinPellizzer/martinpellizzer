Rosa styloidea

Rosa styloidea, commonly known as the slender rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico, where it thrives in woodland and open areas. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces red-orange hips that contain seeds.

Rosa styloidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
