Ruellia occidentalis var. ferrisae

Ruellia occidentalis var. ferrisae, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of blue to violet tubular flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
