Ranunculus acris var. minimus

Ranunculus acris var. minimus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 13 petals, typically less than 2 inches in diameter. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is popular in gardens for its ornamental value.
