Rubus rosifolius var. rosifolius

Rubus rosifolius var. rosifolius, commonly known as the roseleaf bramble, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its finely divided, rose-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners interested in native plants.
