Rubus gymnostylos

Rubus gymnostylos, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which is usually dark purple to black when ripe, is edible and often used in jams and pies.
