Rubus vulgaris f. villicaulis

Rubus vulgaris f. villicaulis, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is a thorny shrub characterized by its compound leaves and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the berries are usually dark purple to black when ripe.

This form is notable for its hardy nature and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
