Rubus macrostachys subsp. rubellus

Rubus macrostachys subsp.

rubellus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to parts of East Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by red, hairy berries. These berries are typically edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and desserts.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
