Rapuntium tenuius

Rapuntium tenuius, commonly known as the slender watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, edible flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its leaves are often used in salads, while the seeds can be ground into a spice.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
