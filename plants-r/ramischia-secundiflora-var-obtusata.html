Ramischia secundiflora var. obtusata

Ramischia secundiflora var. obtusata, commonly known as the blunt-leaf desert bell, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, ovate leaves with a blunt or rounded tip. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique floral structure and adaptive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
