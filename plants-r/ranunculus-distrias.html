Ranunculus distrias

Ranunculus distrias, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces showy, five-petaled yellow flowers with a distinctive central tuft of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate climates.

Ranunculus distrias is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
