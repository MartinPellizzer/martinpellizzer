Rhynchospora lindeniana var. bahamensis

Rhynchospora lindeniana var. bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of freshwater bodies. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
