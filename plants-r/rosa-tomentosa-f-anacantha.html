Rosa tomentosa f. anacantha

Rosa tomentosa f. anacantha, commonly known as the woolly rose or thorned rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its stems are armed with stout, curved thorns, which are a notable characteristic.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant.
