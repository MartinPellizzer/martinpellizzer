Rosa omissa f. danica

Rosa omissa f. danica, commonly known as the Danish rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to northern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and a generally upright growth habit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cooler climates.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires well-drained soil and partial shade to flourish.
