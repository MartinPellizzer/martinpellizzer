Rubus serpens var. membranaceus

Rubus serpens var. membranaceus, commonly known as the membranous blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of thorny stems. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a hairy underside, and the flowers are white or pale pink. The fruit, a small blackberry, develops from the flowering stems and is edible.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
