Rosa obtusifolia var. dunensis

Rosa obtusifolia var. dunensis, commonly known as the Turkish rose or Damascus rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and culinary applications due to their aromatic qualities.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine and fragrance production.
