Ranunculus creticus

Ranunculus creticus, commonly known as the Cretan buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape. Its flowers are bright yellow with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of white or pale sepals.

Ranunculus creticus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
