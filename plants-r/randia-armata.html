Randia armata

Randia armata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot.

It is a shrub native to the Americas, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Randia armata is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
