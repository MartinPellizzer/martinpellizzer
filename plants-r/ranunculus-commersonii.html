Ranunculus commersonii

Ranunculus commersonii, commonly known as Commerson's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Its blooming period occurs in the spring and early summer, making it a notable species in its native habitat.
