Ribes sativum var. variegatum

Ribes sativum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated gooseberry, is a cultivar of the gooseberry plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its attractive, variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.

This variety is often grown for both its ornamental value and its culinary uses.
