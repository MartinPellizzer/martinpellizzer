Rheedia macrantha

Rheedia macrantha, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a robust root system adapted for nutrient absorption from its host.

Rheedia macrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
