Rubus nitidus subsp. affinis

Rubus nitidus subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The subspecies affinis is distinguished by its smoother, less hairy leaves compared to other Rubus species.
