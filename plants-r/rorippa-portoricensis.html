Rorippa portoricensis

Rorippa portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican watercress, is a flowering plant native to the island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. This species is typically found in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and coastal areas. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to that of watercress.

Rorippa portoricensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and contributing to soil stabilization.
