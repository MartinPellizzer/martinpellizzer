Ricinus communis var. megalospermus

Ricinus communis var. megalospermus, commonly known as the giant castor bean, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, spiny capsules.

It produces seeds with exceptionally large, ornamental seeds that are often used in horticulture for their visual appeal. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic, containing ricin, a potent toxin.

Despite its dangers, it is cultivated for its showy appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
