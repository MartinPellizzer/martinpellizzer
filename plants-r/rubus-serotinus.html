Rubus serotinus

Rubus serotinus, commonly known as the late blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub native to North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Rubus serotinus is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
