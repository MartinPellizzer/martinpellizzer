Rhabdotheca tenuiloba

Rhabdotheca tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender-leafed sandmat, is a small, mat-forming succulent native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features pale green, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warm season, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is a valuable species for studying xerophytic plant strategies.
