Rosa erratica

Rosa erratica, commonly known as the wandering rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are armed with thorns.

Rosa erratica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and resilience.
