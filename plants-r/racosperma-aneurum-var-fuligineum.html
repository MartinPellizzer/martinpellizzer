Racosperma aneurum var. fuligineum

Racosperma aneurum var. fuligineum, commonly known as the Black-leafed Acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sandy environments.

The leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval leaflets.
