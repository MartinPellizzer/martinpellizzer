Restrepia nuda

Restrepia nuda, commonly known as the naked restrepia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats. The plant features a short, underground rhizome and produces a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small, white, and often have a greenish tinge, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Restrepia nuda is appreciated for its elegant appearance and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique botanical characteristics.
