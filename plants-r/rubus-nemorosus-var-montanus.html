Rubus nemorosus var. montanus

Rubus nemorosus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red and edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.
