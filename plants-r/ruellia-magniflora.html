Ruellia magniflora

Ruellia magniflora, commonly known as the showy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant features opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Ruellia magniflora thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
