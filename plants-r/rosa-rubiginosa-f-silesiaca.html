Rosa rubiginosa f. silesiaca

Rosa rubiginosa f. silesiaca, commonly known as the Silesian rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark red to almost black flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant has thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanic collections.
