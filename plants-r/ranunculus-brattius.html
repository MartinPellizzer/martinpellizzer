Ranunculus brattius

Ranunculus brattius, commonly known as the brattius crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, spring-blooming species. The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and threatened species in many regions.
