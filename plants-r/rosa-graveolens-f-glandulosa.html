Rosa graveolens f. glandulosa

Rosa graveolens f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular roses, is a botanical variant of the Rosa graveolens species.

It is characterized by its glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which are a distinguishing feature of this form. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It is a deciduous shrub native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticulture and plant taxonomy.
