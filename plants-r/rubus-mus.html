Rubus mus

Rubus mus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Rubus mus thrives in well-drained soils and requires full to partial sunlight for optimal growth.
