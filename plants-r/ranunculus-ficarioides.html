Ranunculus ficarioides

Ranunculus ficarioides, commonly known as the rock beauty, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five to seven petals.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in well-drained conditions and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.
