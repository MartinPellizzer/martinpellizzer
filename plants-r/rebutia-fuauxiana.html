Rebutia fuauxiana

Rebutia fuauxiana, commonly known as the Potosi cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to its harsh, dry environment.

Rebutia fuauxiana is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
