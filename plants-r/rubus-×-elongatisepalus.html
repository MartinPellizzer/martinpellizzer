Rubus × elongatisepalus

Rubus × elongatisepalus, commonly known as the long-sepal raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pink to white in color.

The fruit, which resembles a raspberry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
