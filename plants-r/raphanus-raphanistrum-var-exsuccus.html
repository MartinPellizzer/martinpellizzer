Raphanus raphanistrum var. exsuccus

Raphanus raphanistrum var. exsuccus, commonly known as the tumble mustard, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and small, alternate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, slender pods containing numerous seeds. It is native to Europe and western Asia but has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
