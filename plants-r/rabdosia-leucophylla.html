Rabdosia leucophylla

Rabdosia leucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rabdosia leucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
