Razisea

Razisea, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white or pale yellow.

Razisea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique foliage.
