Rosa wichurana f. glandulifera

Rosa wichurana f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa wichurana species.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its specific epithet "glandulifera.". The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and thrives in temperate climates.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
