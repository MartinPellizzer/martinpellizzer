Ranunculus revelierei subsp. rodiei

Ranunculus revelierei subsp.

rodiei, commonly known as the Sierra Madre buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals and a hairy, upright stem.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
