Rosa coriifolia var. rhaetica

Rosa coriifolia var. rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian bracteate rose, is a unique variant of the bracteate rose species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated bracts that are often mistaken for flowers, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and botanical collections.
