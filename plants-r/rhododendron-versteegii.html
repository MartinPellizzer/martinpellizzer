Rhododendron versteegii

Rhododendron versteegii, commonly known as the Versteeg's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. Its flowers range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive white throat.

Rhododendron versteegii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial shade.
