Ricinocarpus angatensis

Ricinocarpus angatensis, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular species in horticulture and landscaping.
