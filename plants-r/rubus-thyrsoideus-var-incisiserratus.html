Rubus thyrsoideus var. incisiserratus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. incisiserratus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
