Randia corymbosa

Randia corymbosa, commonly known as the redroot sterculia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often reddish underneath. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Randia corymbosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
