Restio distans

Restio distans, commonly known as the distant reed, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a grass-like plant characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spike-like clusters.

Restio distans is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant appearance and drought tolerance.
