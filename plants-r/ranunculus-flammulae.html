Ranunculus flammulae

Ranunculus flammulae, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five petals and is typically found in moist, woodland soils. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It blooms in early spring and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
