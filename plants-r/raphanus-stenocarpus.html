Raphanus stenocarpus

Raphanus stenocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-seed radish, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as cabbage and mustard. The plant features erect stems with pinnately divided leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a short, rounded silique containing several seeds.

Raphanus stenocarpus is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
