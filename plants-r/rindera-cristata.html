Rindera cristata

Rindera cristata, commonly known as the crested rindera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched inflorescence that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Rindera cristata is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
