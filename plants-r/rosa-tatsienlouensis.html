Rosa tatsienlouensis

Rosa tatsienlouensis, commonly known as the Tatsienlou rose, is a species native to China, specifically the Tatsienlou region in Sichuan Province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a rare and endemic species, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

The Tatsienlou rose is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in horticultural and medicinal applications.
