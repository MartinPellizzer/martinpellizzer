Rubus × fraternalis

Rubus × fraternalis, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a serrated margin.

Rubus × fraternalis is valued for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications.
