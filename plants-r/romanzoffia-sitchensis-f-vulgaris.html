Romanzoffia sitchensis f. vulgaris

Romanzoffia sitchensis f. vulgaris, commonly known as the Sitka sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of Alaska and western Canada.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along shorelines and tidal zones, where it thrives in brackish or saltwater environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its ability to tolerate salt spray and fluctuating water levels makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.
