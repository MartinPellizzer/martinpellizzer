Rubus schleicheri subsp. caeruleicaulis

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

caeruleicaulis, commonly known as the blue-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its slender, often blue-tinged stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
