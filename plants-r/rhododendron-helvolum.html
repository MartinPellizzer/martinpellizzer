Rhododendron helvolum

Rhododendron helvolum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish-purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron helvolum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland landscapes.
