Rosa rubens

Rosa rubens, commonly known as the red bush rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense thickets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa rubens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
