Rosa glauca subvar. intricata

Rosa glauca subvar. intricata, commonly known as the glaucous rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in summer and are often tinged with pink or purple. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are covered in thorny buds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh alpine environments.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
