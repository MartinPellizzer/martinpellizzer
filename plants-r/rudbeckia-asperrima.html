Rudbeckia asperrima

Rudbeckia asperrima, commonly known as the rough-hairy coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, daisy-like yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies and wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to humid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rudbeckia asperrima is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
