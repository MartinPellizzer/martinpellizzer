Ranunculus sardous subsp. cordigerus

Ranunculus sardous subsp.

cordigerus, commonly known as the Sicilian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its flowering period occurs in spring, and it is characterized by its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly heart-shaped leaves and is considered a rare and endemic species.
