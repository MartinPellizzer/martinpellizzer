Restio ferruginosus

Restio ferruginosus, commonly known as the iron-stem restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and unique foliage.
