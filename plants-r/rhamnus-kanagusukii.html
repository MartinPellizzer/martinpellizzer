Rhamnus kanagusukii

Rhamnus kanagusukii, commonly known as the Alaska buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are clustered in small, greenish-white inflorescences, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native environment.
