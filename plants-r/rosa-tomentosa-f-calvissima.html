Rosa tomentosa f. calvissima

Rosa tomentosa f. calvissima, commonly known as the hairless rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa tomentosa species.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and foliage, distinguishing it from the typical fuzzy appearance of the species. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a compact shrub, often used in ornamental gardens for its attractive form and seasonal blooms.

This cultivar is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
