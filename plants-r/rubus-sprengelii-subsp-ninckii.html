Rubus sprengelii subsp. ninckii

Rubus sprengelii subsp.

ninckii, commonly known as the Ninkii blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens and research settings.
