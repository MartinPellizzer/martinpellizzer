Rhododendron gracile

Rhododendron gracile, commonly known as the slender rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Rhododendron gracile is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
