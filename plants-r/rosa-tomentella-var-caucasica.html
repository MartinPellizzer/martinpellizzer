Rosa tomentella var. caucasica

Rosa tomentella var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian woolly rose, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, white tomentose (fuzzy) foliage and stems, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
