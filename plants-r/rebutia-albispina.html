Rebutia albispina

Rebutia albispina, commonly known as the white-spined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with spiny, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green with white or yellow spines. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
