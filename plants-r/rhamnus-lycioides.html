Rhamnus lycioides

Rhamnus lycioides, commonly known as the desert buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing some protection against environmental stressors.
