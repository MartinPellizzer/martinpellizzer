Rhododendron palawanense

Rhododendron palawanense, commonly known as the Palawan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often at elevations between 500 and 1,500 meters. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to white, bloom in spring and are an important feature for its ecological and ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to acidic soils and its role in local biodiversity.
