Rosa afzeliana var. naxensis

Rosa afzeliana var. naxensis, commonly known as the Naxos rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly the island of Naxos in Greece.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature.
