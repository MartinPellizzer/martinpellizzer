Ravenia biramosa var. peruviana

Ravenia biramosa var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian ravenia, is a species native to South America, particularly Peru.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry to moist forest environments. The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and range in color from white to pink.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in local habitats.
