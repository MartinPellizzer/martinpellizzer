Rubus varnaeensis

Rubus varnaeensis, commonly known as the Varna blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually pink or white.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
