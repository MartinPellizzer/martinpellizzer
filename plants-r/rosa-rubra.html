Rosa rubra

Rosa rubra, commonly known as the red rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals, which are typically arranged in a layered, showy pattern. The plant produces fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, and its stems are usually covered in thorns. Rosa rubra is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and in horticulture.
