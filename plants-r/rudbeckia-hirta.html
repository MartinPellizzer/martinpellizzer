Rudbeckia hirta

Rudbeckia hirta, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange petals and a dark central disk, which resembles an eye. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

Rudbeckia hirta is a hardy perennial that blooms from late spring to early fall, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
