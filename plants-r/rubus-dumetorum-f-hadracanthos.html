Rubus dumetorum f. hadracanthos

Rubus dumetorum f. hadracanthos, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry with distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems, which are adaptations to its native environment. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

This form is notable for its robust growth and potential use in both ornamental and ecological landscaping.
