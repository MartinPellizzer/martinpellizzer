Racosperma difficile

Racosperma difficile, commonly known as the hardy wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.

Its flowers are an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and birds.
