Ruellia tubiflora var. tetrastichantha

Ruellia tubiflora var. tetrastichantha, commonly known as the purple mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, lavender to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
