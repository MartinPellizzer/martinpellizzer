Rubus ossalensis

Rubus ossalensis, commonly known as the Ossalensis blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces dark purple to black berries that are highly aromatic. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. Its berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
