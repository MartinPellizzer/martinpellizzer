Rubus × mackayi

Rubus × mackayi, commonly known as the Mackay blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, which includes brambles and raspberries.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in temperate climates. It produces edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cultivation in home gardens and commercial settings.
