Rosa graveolens var. genuina

Rosa graveolens var. genuina, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a species of shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The Turkish rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
