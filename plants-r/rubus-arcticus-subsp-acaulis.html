Rubus arcticus subsp. acaulis

Rubus arcticus subsp.

acaulis, commonly known as the arctic bramble, is a low-growing shrub native to arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its erect, stemless appearance, with small, clustered leaves and white to pinkish flowers.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupes. It thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for survival in extreme environments.
