Ranunculus occidentalis var. nelsonii

Ranunculus occidentalis var. nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
