Ramischia obtusata

Ramischia obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape, with a blunt or rounded tip. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and bloom from late winter to early spring.

Ramischia obtusata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
