Rosa tomentosa var. portmannii

Rosa tomentosa var. portmannii, commonly known as the Portmann's woolly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, velvety leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and fragrant blooms.
