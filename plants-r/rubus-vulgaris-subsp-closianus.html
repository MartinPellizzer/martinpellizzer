Rubus vulgaris subsp. closianus

Rubus vulgaris subsp.

closianus, commonly known as the close thimbleberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its berries are used in culinary applications and have a tart, tangy flavor.
