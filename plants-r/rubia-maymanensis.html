Rubia maymanensis

Rubia maymanensis, commonly known as the Mayman's rue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 12,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with four petals and prominent stamens.

Rubia maymanensis is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
