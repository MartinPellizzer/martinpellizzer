Rebutia brighignae

Rebutia brighignae, commonly known as the BRIGHTIGNE REBUTIA, is a succulent native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features cylindrical stems with areoles, from which both spines and flowers emerge. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and they bloom in late summer or early autumn.

Rebutia brighignae is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
