Rubus elmeri

Rubus elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberry species.
