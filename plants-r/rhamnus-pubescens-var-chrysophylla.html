Rhamnus pubescens var. chrysophylla

Rhamnus pubescens var. chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leafed buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that turn a striking yellow in the fall, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
