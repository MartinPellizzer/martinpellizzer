Rubus bellardii var. pilosus

Rubus bellardii var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinct appearance compared to other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three to five leaflets and thorny stems.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
