Riedlea eriantha

Riedlea eriantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf riedlea, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming during the wet season. Riedlea eriantha is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
