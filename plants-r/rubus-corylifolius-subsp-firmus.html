Rubus corylifolius subsp. firmus

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

firmus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically red or black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its edible berries, though they are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
