Ranunculus mediocris

Ranunculus mediocris, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soil. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and it has a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

Ranunculus mediocris is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
