Ranunculus alismellus

Ranunculus alismellus, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to nine petals, typically in shades of white, yellow, or orange. The plant has a short, stout stem and basal leaves that are deeply lobed or divided. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Ranunculus alismellus is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.
