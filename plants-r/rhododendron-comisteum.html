Rhododendron comisteum

Rhododendron comisteum, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, adding vibrant visual interest to its natural habitat.

Rhododendron comisteum is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
