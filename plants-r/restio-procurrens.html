Restio procurrens

Restio procurrens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family, which is characteristic of the fynbos vegetation type. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny white to pale blue flowers.

Restio procurrens is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and inland fynbos habitats.
