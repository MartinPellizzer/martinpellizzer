Rosa Tomentosa Var. Andrzejowskii

Rosa tomentosa var. andrzejowskii is a rare and unique variant of the woolly rose known for its distinctive appearance and hardy nature. This plant is native to specific regions in Eastern Europe and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its velvety foliage and delicate pink flowers make it a standout in botanical gardens and private collections. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable and sometimes endangered species. Cultivating this plant requires careful attention to soil and climate conditions to ensure its survival.
