Rubus glandulifer

Rubus glandulifer, commonly known as the glandular blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters.

Rubus glandulifer thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along rocky slopes.
