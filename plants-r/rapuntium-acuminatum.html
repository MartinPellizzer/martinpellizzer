Rapuntium acuminatum

Rapuntium acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed rapunculus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by rounded, hairy seed pods. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.
