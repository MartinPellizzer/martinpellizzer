Rubia pauciflora

Rubia pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered bugleweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy as well. Its flowers are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Rubia pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
