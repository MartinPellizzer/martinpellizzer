Rosa ubachii

Rosa ubachii, commonly known as the Ubachii rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals, and bloom in late summer.

The plant produces oval, red-orange fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa ubachii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its beauty and medicinal properties.
