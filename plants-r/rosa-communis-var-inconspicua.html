Rosa communis var. inconspicua

Rosa communis var. inconspicua, commonly known as the humble rose, is a lesser-known variant of the common rose species.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pale pink and white. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that can grow up to several feet in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its subtle beauty and fragrance.
