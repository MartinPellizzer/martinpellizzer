Royena oleifolia

Royena oleifolia, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems. The plant has dark, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain seeds dispersed by water.

Royena oleifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
