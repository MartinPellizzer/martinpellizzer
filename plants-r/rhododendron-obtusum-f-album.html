Rhododendron obtusum f. album

Rhododendron obtusum f. album, commonly known as the white-flowered rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and densely packed on its stems. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are predominantly white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a dense, rounded growth habit and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its elegant appearance.
