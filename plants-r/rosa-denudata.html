Rosa denudata

Rosa denudata, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its simple, often thornless stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in summer. Its flowers have a strong, spicy fragrance and are followed by orange-red hips that persist into winter.

Rosa denudata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
