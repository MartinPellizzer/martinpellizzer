Rauvolfia volkensii

Rauvolfia volkensii, commonly known as African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical significance lies in its traditional use in medicine and its role in ecological systems.
