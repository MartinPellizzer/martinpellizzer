Rosa eglanteria var. sabolata

Rosa eglanteria var. sabolata, commonly known as the sand rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces rosehips that are orange-red and contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for gardens and landscapes.
