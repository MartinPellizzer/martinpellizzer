Rosa tomentosa subsp. cinerascens

Rosa tomentosa subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its greyish-white, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
