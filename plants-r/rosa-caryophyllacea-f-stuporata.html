Rosa caryophyllacea f. stuporata

Rosa caryophyllacea f. stuporata, commonly known as the Purpleleaf Rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa caryophyllacea.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple foliage, which sets it apart from the typical green leaves of the base species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for its visual appeal in horticultural settings.
