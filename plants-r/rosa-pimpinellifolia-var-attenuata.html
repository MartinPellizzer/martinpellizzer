Rosa pimpinellifolia var. attenuata

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, woody stems and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. It is often found growing on rocky slopes and in sunny, well-drained soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry climates.
