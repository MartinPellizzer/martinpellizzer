Rourea mimosoides f. obtusifolia

Rourea mimosoides f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rourea, is a unique variant of the species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are often arranged in pairs along the stems, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
