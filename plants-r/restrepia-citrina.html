Restrepia citrina

Restrepia citrina, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is known for its epiphytic growth habit, often growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and are pollinated by insects such as bees and moths.

Restrepia citrina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
