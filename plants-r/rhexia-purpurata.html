Rhexia purpurata

Rhexia purpurata, commonly known as the purpleleaf sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically tinged with purple, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Rhexia purpurata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens.
