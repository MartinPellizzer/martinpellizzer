Rubus tereticaulis subsp. fragariiflorus

Rubus tereticaulis subsp.

fragariiflorus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically serrated along the edges.

The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to edible, red berries in the summer months. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
