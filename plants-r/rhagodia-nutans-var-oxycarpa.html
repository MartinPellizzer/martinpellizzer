Rhagodia nutans var. oxycarpa

Rhagodia nutans var. oxycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, spherical capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is well adapted to saline and drought-prone soils, making it an important component of coastal and inland ecosystems.
