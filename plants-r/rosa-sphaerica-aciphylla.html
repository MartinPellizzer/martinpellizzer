Rosa sphaerica aciphylla

Rosa sphaerica aciphylla, commonly known as the globular rose, is a unique species characterized by its spherical growth form and lack of typical thorns.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, cushion-like structure, making it well-suited for rock gardens or alpine settings. Its leaves are dense and evergreen, providing year-round visual interest.

Native to specific regions in the Mediterranean, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
