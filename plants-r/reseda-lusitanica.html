Reseda lusitanica

Reseda lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian madder, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces bright red roots that contain a dye used historically in textile production. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Reseda lusitanica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
