Rulingia parviflora

Rulingia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered rulingia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes in South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in humid and cool climates. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually ovate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Rulingia parviflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
