Ranunculus latifrons

Ranunculus latifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals and is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowering period occurs in spring, and it is often found in meadows and along forest edges.

Ranunculus latifrons is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
