Rapanea boemiensis

Rapanea boemiensis, commonly known as the Bohemian waxtree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are drupes, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy pulp.

Rapanea boemiensis is often found in rocky or forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
