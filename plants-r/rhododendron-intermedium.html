Rhododendron intermedium

Rhododendron intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range from pink to red, often with a distinctive waxy texture.

Rhododendron intermedium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
