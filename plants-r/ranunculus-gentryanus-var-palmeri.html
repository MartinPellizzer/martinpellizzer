Ranunculus gentryanus var. palmeri

Ranunculus gentryanus var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green fruits that disperse its seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in xerophytic habitats.
