Rubus galbinifolius

Rubus galbinifolius, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. Its flowers are white and have five petals, while its leaves are trifoliate with serrated edges.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
