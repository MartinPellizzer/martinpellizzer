Rosa communis var. globosa

Rosa communis var. globosa, commonly known as the globe rose, is a compact, bushy shrub characterized by its rounded, mounding growth habit.

It features dense clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy foliage that provides a lush backdrop for its showy blooms. It is well-suited for container gardening or as a border plant due to its compact size and ornamental value.

The variety is prized for its ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions and its resistance to common rose diseases.
