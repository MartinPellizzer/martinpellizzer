Rosa herculis

Rosa herculis, commonly known as the Hercules rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color and can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter.

Rosa herculis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
