Restio acuminatus

Restio acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial, grass-like plant characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
