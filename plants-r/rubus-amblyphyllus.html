Rubus amblyphyllus

Rubus amblyphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a hairy underside, while its flowers are small and white.

The fruit, a dark purple-black berry, is edible but often has a tart flavor.
