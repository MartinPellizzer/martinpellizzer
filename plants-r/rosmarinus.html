Rosmarinus

Rosmarinus, commonly known as rosemary, is a fragrant evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and thyme. The plant is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are widely used in aromatherapy and cooking.

Rosmarinus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
