Restio dodii

Restio dodii, commonly known as the Knysna restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the coast, often found in dune environments.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
