Ramium hispidulum

Ramium hispidulum, commonly known as hairy ramial, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Ramium hispidulum is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands.
