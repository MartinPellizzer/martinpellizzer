Rhynchosia senna var. angustifolia

Rhynchosia senna var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in racemes. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. It has a climbing habit and produces pods that contain small, lens-shaped seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
