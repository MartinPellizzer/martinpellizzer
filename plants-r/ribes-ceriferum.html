Ribes ceriferum

Ribes ceriferum, commonly known as the Sitka gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its elliptic, dark green leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Ribes ceriferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use by indigenous peoples for food and medicine.
