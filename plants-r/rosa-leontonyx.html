Rosa leontonyx

Rosa leontonyx, commonly known as the lion's tooth rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa leontonyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
