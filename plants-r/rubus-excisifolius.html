Rubus excisifolius

Rubus excisifolius, commonly known as the cut-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including moist and well-drained conditions. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. The fruit, a aggregate of small drupes, is edible and often used in culinary applications.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
