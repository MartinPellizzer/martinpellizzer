Rhododendron indicum var. crispiflorum

Rhododendron indicum var. crispiflorum, commonly known as the curlyflower rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from white to pink and purple. This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
