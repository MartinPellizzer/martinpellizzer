Racosperma oncinophyllum

Racosperma oncinophyllum, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow floral spikes and compound leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or coastal heathlands. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in ecological restoration projects.
