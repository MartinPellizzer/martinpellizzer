Rapanea congesta

Rapanea congesta, commonly known as the African holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
