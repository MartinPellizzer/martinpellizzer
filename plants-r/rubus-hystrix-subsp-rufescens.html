Rubus hystrix subsp. rufescens

Rubus hystrix subsp.

rufescens, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America and northern South America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
