Rosa canina var. rubiginosiformis

Rosa canina var. rubiginosiformis, commonly known as the brambles or dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fuzzy berries that are rich in vitamin C and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
