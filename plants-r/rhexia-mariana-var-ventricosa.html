Rhexia mariana var. ventricosa

Rhexia mariana var. ventricosa, commonly known as the swamp pink, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
