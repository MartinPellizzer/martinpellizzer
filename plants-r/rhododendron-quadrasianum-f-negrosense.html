Rhododendron quadrasianum f. negrosense

Rhododendron quadrasianum f. negrosense, commonly known as the Negros rhododendron, is a unique flowering plant native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Negros.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features dense, leathery leaves and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its botanical significance lies in its role as an endemic species, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is also of interest for conservation efforts.
