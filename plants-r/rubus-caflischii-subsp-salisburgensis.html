Rubus caflischii subsp. salisburgensis

Rubus caflischii subsp.

salisburgensis, commonly known as the Salzburg raspberry, is a shrub native to the European Alps. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
