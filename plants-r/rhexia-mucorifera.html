Rhexia mucorifera

Rhexia mucorifera, commonly known as the stickyleaf germander, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, sticky pubescence. Its showy, pink to lavender flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhexia mucorifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
