Rosa canina subsp. hirtella

Rosa canina subsp.

hirtella, commonly known as the hairy rose or bristly dog rose, is a subspecies native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and the plant produces red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
