Rubus liboensis

Rubus liboensis, commonly known as the Yunnan blackberry, is a shrub native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan province.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its botanical features include serrated leaf margins and a robust root system that helps it thrive in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
