Rubus × velutinatus

Rubus × velutinatus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
