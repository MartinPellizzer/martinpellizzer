Rhazya stricta

Rhazya stricta, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, and it is often found in rocky or arid environments. Rhazya stricta is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

Its adaptability to harsh climates makes it an important plant in its native ecosystems.
