Rosa × dumetorum subsp. corneliae

Rosa × dumetorum subsp.

corneliae, commonly known as Cornelia's shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It features dark green, oval leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in moist, woodland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical name reflects its hybrid origin and subspecies distinction within the Rosa genus.
