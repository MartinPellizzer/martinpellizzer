Rhynchospora glauca

Rhynchospora glauca, commonly known as blueleaf sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer. Rhynchospora glauca is often found in shallow water or moist soil, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland ecosystems.

Its blue-green foliage gives it a distinctive appearance, making it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
