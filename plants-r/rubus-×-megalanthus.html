Rubus × megalanthus

Rubus × megalanthus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically larger than those of other Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its size and fruit characteristics, it is of interest to both botanists and horticulturists.
