Rubus sylvestris var. setosus

Rubus sylvestris var. setosus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries that ripen in the summer. The leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in woodland areas and prefers well-drained soil.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and small mammals.
