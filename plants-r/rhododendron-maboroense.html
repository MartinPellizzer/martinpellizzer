Rhododendron maboroense

Rhododendron maboroense, commonly known as the Maboro rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to white, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron maboroense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
