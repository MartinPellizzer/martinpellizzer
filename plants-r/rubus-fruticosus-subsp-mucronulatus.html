Rubus fruticosus subsp. mucronulatus

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

mucronulatus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria.

The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and parts of North Africa, often growing in hedgerows and woodland edges. It produces small, white flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by dark purple to black berries.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
