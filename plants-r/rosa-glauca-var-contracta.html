Rosa glauca var. contracta

Rosa glauca var. contracta, commonly known as the dwarf blue rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and is well adapted to cold, arid environments. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique color and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens despite its limited availability.
