Romulea subalbida

Romulea subalbida, commonly known as the white-flowered romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a cluster of tubular flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its delicate and long-lasting blooms.
