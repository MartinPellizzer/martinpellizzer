Rosa setigera glabra

Rosa setigera glabra, commonly known as the hairy willow rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems that are often covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Native to parts of Asia, it is often found in moist, woodland habitats.
