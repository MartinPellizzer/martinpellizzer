Rorippa pyrenaica var. hispanica

Rorippa pyrenaica var. hispanica, commonly known as the Pyrenean watercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is typically found in stream margins and wet meadows.

This variety is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is valued for its peppery flavor, often used in local cuisine.
