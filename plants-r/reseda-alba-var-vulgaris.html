Reseda alba var. vulgaris

Reseda alba var. vulgaris, commonly known as white cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Reseda alba var. vulgaris is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
