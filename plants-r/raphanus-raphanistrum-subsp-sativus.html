Raphanus raphanistrum subsp. sativus

Raphanus raphanistrum subsp.

sativus, commonly known as the cultivated radish, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its fleshy, swollen root that is typically white or pink in color, which is the edible part of the plant.

The plant has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are usually hairy and have a peppery aroma. It produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by spherical, dry fruits called siliques.

This species is widely cultivated for its root, which is used in various culinary applications around the world.
