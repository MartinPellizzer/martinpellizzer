Rosa acutiformis

Rosa acutiformis, commonly known as the sharp-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, acuminate leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Rosa acutiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
