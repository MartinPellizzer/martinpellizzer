Rosa canina subvar. eriostyla

Rosa canina subvar. eriostyla, commonly known as the sticky rose or bristly rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red, fuzzy, and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
