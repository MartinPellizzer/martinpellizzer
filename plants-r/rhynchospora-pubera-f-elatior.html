Rhynchospora pubera f. elatior

Rhynchospora pubera f. elatior, commonly known as the tall swamp cottontail, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with slender, erect stems and spikelets that bear small, pale brown flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in the southeastern United States and is valued for its ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
