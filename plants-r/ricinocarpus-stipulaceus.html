Ricinocarpus stipulaceus

Ricinocarpus stipulaceus, commonly known as the slender sphaerocarp, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Sphaerocarpaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in wet, tropical environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with distinctive, cup-like structures. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, which is common in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
