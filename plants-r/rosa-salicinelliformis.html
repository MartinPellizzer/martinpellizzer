Rosa salicinelliformis

Rosa salicinelliformis, commonly known as the willowleaf rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, with a soft, silvery underside that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
