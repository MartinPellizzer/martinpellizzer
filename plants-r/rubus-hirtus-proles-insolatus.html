Rubus hirtus proles insolatus

Rubus hirtus proles insolatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and have five petals, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth habit.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
