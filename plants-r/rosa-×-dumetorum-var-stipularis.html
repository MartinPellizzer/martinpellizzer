Rosa × dumetorum var. stipularis

Rosa × dumetorum var. stipularis, commonly known as the shrub rose or creeping rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit, with thornless stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, velvety hairs.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
