Rubus × asperifolius

Rubus × asperifolius, commonly known as the rough-leaved blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
