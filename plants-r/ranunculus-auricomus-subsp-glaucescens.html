Ranunculus auricomus subsp. glaucescens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers with five broadly ovate petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a waxy, bluish-green coating.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia.
