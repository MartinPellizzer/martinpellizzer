Ranunculus japonicus f. plenus

Ranunculus japonicus f. plenus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup hybrid, is a variant of the Japanese buttercup characterized by its dense, double flowers.

It is a perennial herb native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. Its botanical name reflects its origin and the fullness of its bloom structure.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
