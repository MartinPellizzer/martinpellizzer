Rubus taitoensis var. aculeatiflorus

Rubus taitoensis var. aculeatiflorus, commonly known as the Taito bramble, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, often white or pink flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits, typically red or black berries, which are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical research.
