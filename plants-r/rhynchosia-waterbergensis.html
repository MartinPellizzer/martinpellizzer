Rhynchosia waterbergensis

Rhynchosia waterbergensis, commonly known as the Waterberg climbing bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its ornamental and nutritional qualities.
