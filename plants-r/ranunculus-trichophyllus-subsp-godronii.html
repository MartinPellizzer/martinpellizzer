Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp. godronii

Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp.

godronii, commonly known as Godron's crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often bright-colored flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
