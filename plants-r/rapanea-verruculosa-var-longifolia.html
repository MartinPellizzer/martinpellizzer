Rapanea verruculosa var. longifolia

Rapanea verruculosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved boxwood, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are typically elongated and have a glossy, dark green surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in shaded environments.
