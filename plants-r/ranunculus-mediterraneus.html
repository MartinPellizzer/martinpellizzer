Ranunculus mediterraneus

Ranunculus mediterraneus, commonly known as the Mediterranean buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. It is characterized by its short, stout stem and is known for its ability to thrive in temperate climates.

Ranunculus mediterraneus is also noted for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant, cup-shaped flowers.
