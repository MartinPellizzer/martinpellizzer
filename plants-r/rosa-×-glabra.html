Rosa × glabra

Rosa × glabra, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other rose species. The plant produces fragrant flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. Rosa × glabra is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
