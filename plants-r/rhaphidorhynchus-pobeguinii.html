Rhaphidorhynchus pobeguinii

Rhaphidorhynchus pobeguinii, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant has a slender, erect stem with few leaves, and its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid, tropical environment. It thrives in shaded, moist forest understories and is known for its delicate and ephemeral blooms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
