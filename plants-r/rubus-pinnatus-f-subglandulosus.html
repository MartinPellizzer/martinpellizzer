Rubus pinnatus f. subglandulosus

Rubus pinnatus f. subglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry with distinct botanical features.

It is a shrub characterized by its biennial stems that are often covered in glandular hairs. The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets and a hairy surface. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit, a aggregate of drupes, is usually red and edible, though the flavor can vary depending on the plant's environment.
