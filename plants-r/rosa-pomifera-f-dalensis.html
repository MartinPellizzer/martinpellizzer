Rosa pomifera f. dalensis

Rosa pomifera f. dalensis, commonly known as the Dalen's rose, is a rare and unique cultivar of the Rosa pomifera species.

It is characterized by its large, edible fruits that resemble small, fuzzy apples, which are often referred to as "rose hips.". The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, making it both ornamental and botanically intriguing.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare flora.
