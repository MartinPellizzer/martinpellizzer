Rubus × petricola

Rubus × petricola, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark-colored berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is native to high-altitude regions of the western United States and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant is often found in alpine meadows and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
