Rosa subolida var. genuina

Rosa subolida var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican rose or wild rose, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in spring. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that contain seeds, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The botanical features of Rosa subolida var. genuina make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
