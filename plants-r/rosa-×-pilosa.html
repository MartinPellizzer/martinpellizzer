Rosa × pilosa

Rosa × pilosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often with a high degree of variability in color. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rosa × pilosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
