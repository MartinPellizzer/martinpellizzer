Ranunculus brachylobus

Ranunculus brachylobus, commonly known as the short-stemmed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. The plant has a short, sturdy stem and basal leaves that are palmately lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and wildflower displays.
