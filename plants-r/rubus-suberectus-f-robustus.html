Rubus suberectus f. robustus

Rubus suberectus f. robustus, commonly known as the robust raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its vigorous growth and thick, woody stems.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupelets that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are white with five petals.

This form is valued for its hardiness and potential use in both ornamental and culinary applications.
