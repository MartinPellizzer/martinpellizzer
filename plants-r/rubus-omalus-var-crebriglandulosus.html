Rubus omalus var. crebriglandulosus

Rubus omalus var. crebriglandulosus, commonly known as the rough-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and large, dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically rough and glandular, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

This variant is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
