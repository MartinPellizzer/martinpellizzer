Rosa pendulina var. ebelii

Rosa pendulina var. ebelii, commonly known as the Ebel's weeping rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces oval, glossy leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cascading appearance.
