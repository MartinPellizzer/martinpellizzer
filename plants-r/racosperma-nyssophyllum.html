Racosperma nyssophyllum

Racosperma nyssophyllum, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Australia and is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown timber. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers that are followed by elongated pods. It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its valuable wood, it is also considered an endangered species in some areas.
