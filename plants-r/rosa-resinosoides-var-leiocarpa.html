Rosa resinosoides var. leiocarpa

Rosa resinosoides var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in a resinous substance that gives the plant its common name. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are typically pink or white. This variety is known for its fragrant blooms and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

It is a hardy plant that thrives in partial shade and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
