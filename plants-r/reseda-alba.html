Reseda alba

Reseda alba, commonly known as white cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Reseda alba is also valued for its historical use in dye production and as a traditional medicinal plant.
