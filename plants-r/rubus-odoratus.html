Rubus odoratus

Rubus odoratus, commonly known as the sweet cherry or the cherry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to bright red, edible fruits in the summer. Its fruit is highly valued for its sweet flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

Rubus odoratus is also noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
