Rhynchosia latifolia

Rhynchosia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf snout bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and produces yellow flowers that are often accompanied by distinctive, inflated seed pods. The plant is characterized by its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia in its root nodules. Rhynchosia latifolia is often cultivated for its edible seeds and has traditional uses in various cultures for food and medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in agroforestry and soil enrichment.
