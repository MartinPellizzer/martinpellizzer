Ranunculus trisectus

Ranunculus trisectus, commonly known as the three-lobed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Ranunculus trisectus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.
