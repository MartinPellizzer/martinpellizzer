Rhododendron torajaense

Rhododendron torajaense, commonly known as the Toraja rhododendron, is a species native to the highland regions of Indonesia, specifically the Toraja area on the island of Sulawesi.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 2,500 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many ornamental and ecologically significant plants.

Rhododendron torajaense plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is also valued for its horticultural appeal.
