Rubus albicomiformis

Rubus albicomiformis, commonly known as the white-haired raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its slender stems covered in white hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
