Rubus bicolorispinosus

Rubus bicolorispinosus, commonly known as the two-colored bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its bi-colored stems, which typically display a mix of green and reddish hues. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are usually red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its thorny stems and showy flowers make it a notable presence in its native habitat.
