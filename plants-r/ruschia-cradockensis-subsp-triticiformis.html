Ruschia cradockensis subsp. triticiformis

Ruschia cradockensis subsp.

triticiformis, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

This subspecies is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
