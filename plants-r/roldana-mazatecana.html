Roldana mazatecana

Roldana mazatecana, commonly known as the Mazatec spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Roldana mazatecana is also valued for its cultural significance among the Mazatec people.
