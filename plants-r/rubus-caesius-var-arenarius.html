Rubus caesius var. arenarius

Rubus caesius var. arenarius, commonly known as the sand raspberry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible berries. The plant thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
