Rubus discerptifrons

Rubus discerptifrons, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to western North America, particularly found in coastal regions of California and Oregon. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
