Rubus scaber var. rufescens

Rubus scaber var. rufescens, commonly known as the rough bramble or red-husked blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces edible blackberries with a distinctive reddish hue on the receptacle. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
