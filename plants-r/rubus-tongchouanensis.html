Rubus tongchouanensis

Rubus tongchouanensis, commonly known as the Tongchouan raspberry, is a species of shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is valued for its tart flavor and is used in local culinary traditions.
