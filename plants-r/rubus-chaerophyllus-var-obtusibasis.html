Rubus chaerophyllus var. obtusibasis

Rubus chaerophyllus var. obtusibasis, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with broad, rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are aggregate berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color in late summer.

This species is often found in woodland edges and moist, acidic soils.
