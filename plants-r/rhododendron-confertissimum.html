Rhododendron confertissimum

Rhododendron confertissimum, commonly known as the dense-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowering stems are often adorned with numerous, overlapping bracts that add to its ornamental appeal.

Rhododendron confertissimum is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to form dense, evergreen thickets.
