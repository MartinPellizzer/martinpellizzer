Rhododendron haemaleum

Rhododendron haemaleum, commonly known as the red-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes, adding a dramatic visual impact to the landscape.

Rhododendron haemaleum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
