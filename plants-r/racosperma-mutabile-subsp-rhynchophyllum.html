Racosperma mutabile subsp. rhynchophyllum

Racosperma mutabile subsp.

rhynchophyllum, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant is notable for its variability in leaf and flower morphology, which contributes to its common name. It typically grows in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and heathlands, and is adapted to dry conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important component of its ecosystem.
