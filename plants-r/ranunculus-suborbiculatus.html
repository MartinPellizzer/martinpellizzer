Ranunculus suborbiculatus

Ranunculus suborbiculatus, commonly known as the small-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in rocky or calcareous soils.

The species is characterized by its compact stature and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
