Rosa pouzinii f. inermis

Rosa pouzinii f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa pouzinii species.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and foliage, distinguishing it from other forms of the species. This plant typically produces small, fragrant pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is native to specific regions in Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The smooth rose is valued in horticulture for its compact growth habit and aesthetic appeal.
