Rhaphiolepis salwinensis

Rhaphiolepis salwinensis, commonly known as the Salwin rose.

It is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Myanmar, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its woody stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
