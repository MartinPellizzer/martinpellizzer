Rosa pimpinellifolia var. subinermis

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. subinermis, commonly known as the soft-stemmed rock rose, is a variant of the rock rose species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun and well-drained conditions. Its leaves are generally narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
