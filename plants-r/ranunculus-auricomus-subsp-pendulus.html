Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pendulus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pendulus, commonly known as the pendant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated petal that hangs downward, giving it its common name.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. It produces numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The subspecies is noted for its graceful appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
