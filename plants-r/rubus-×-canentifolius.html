Rubus × canentifolius

Rubus × canentifolius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and wild landscapes.
