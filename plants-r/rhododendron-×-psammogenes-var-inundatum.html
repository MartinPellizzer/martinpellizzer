Rhododendron × psammogenes var. inundatum

Rhododendron × psammogenes var. inundatum, commonly known as the Kashmiri rhododendron, is a hybrid species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with high humidity and cool temperatures. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
