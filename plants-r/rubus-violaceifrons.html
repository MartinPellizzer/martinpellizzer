Rubus violaceifrons

Rubus violaceifrons, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in the spring before the leaves fully develop.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
