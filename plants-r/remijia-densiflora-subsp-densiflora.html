Remijia densiflora subsp. densiflora

Remijia densiflora subsp.

densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered remijia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense arrangement of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically produces clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
