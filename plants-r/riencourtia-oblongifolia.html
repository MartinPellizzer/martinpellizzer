Riencourtia oblongifolia

Riencourtia oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver along the margins. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by garden enthusiasts.
