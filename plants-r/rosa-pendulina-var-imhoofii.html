Rosa pendulina var. imhoofii

Rosa pendulina var. imhoofii, commonly known as the Imhoof's climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems that can reach up to 3 meters in length, making it an excellent choice for trellises or fences. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets that are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
