Rosa cardotii

Rosa cardotii, commonly known as the Cardot's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and the Alps. This perennial shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are pink to red in color, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
