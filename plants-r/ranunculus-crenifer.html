Ranunculus crenifer

Ranunculus crenifer, commonly known as the notched buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Ranunculus crenifer is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution in handling.
