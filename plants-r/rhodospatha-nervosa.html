Rhodospatha nervosa

Rhodospatha nervosa, commonly known as the veined rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves with prominent veins, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
