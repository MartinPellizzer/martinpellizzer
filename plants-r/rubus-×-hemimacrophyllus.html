Rubus × hemimacrophyllus

Rubus × hemimacrophyllus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that can weigh up to 10 grams. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its unique fruit size and flavor, making it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and farms.
