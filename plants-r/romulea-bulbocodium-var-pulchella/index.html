Romulea Bulbocodium Var. Pulchella

Romulea bulbocodium var. pulchella is a striking member of the Boraginaceae family known for its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, this plant thrives in rocky and dry environments. Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice among rock garden enthusiasts. The variety 'pulchella' stands out for its delicate pink to purple hues and compact growth habit. Despite its beauty, it remains a relatively rare and underappreciated species in the botanical world.
