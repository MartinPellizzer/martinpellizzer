Rubus fruticosus var. vestrogothicus

Rubus fruticosus var. vestrogothicus, commonly known as the Swedish blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The berries, which are dark purple to black when mature, are edible and have a tart flavor.
