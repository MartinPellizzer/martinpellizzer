Rumex × schreberi

Rumex × schreberi, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in hue. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its leaves and stems contain oxalic acid, which can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
