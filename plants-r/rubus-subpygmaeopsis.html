Rubus subpygmaeopsis

Rubus subpygmaeopsis, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a small shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, characterized by its low-growing habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is closely related to other species in the Rubus genus, sharing features such as aggregate fruits and prickly growth structures.
