Restrepia parvifolia

Restrepia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in cool, humid environments at high elevations. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers with a faint fragrance.

Restrepia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
