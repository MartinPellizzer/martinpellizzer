Rubus ohlseni

Rubus ohlseni, commonly known as Ohlsen's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
