Rosa cymosa var. puberula

Rosa cymosa var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent shrub rose, is a species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible flowers.
