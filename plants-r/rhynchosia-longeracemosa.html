Rhynchosia longeracemosa

Rhynchosia longeracemosa, commonly known as the long-branch snout bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated racemes that give it its specific epithet. Its pods are typically slender and contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Rhynchosia longeracemosa is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
