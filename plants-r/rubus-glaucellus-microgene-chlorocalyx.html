Rubus glaucellus microgene chlorocalyx

Rubus glaucellus microgene chlorocalyx, commonly known as the glaucous raspberry.

This plant is a species of bramble within the Rubus genus, characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a bluish-green hue on the upper surface and a lighter green underneath. The berries, which are usually red when mature, are edible and have a tart flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
