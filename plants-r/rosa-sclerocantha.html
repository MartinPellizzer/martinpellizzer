Rosa sclerocantha

Rosa sclerocantha, commonly known as the hard-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa sclerocantha is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
