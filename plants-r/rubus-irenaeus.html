Rubus irenaeus

Rubus irenaeus, commonly known as the Irenaean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
