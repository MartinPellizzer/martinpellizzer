Rosa × buseri

Rosa × buseri, commonly known as the Buser's rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the United States, specifically the southeastern region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has dark green, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. Its botanical features include showy, five-petaled flowers and an upright growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Rosa × buseri is well adapted to warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
