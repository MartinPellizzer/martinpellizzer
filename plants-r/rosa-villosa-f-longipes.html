Rosa villosa f. longipes

Rosa villosa f. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed bristly rose, is a cultivar characterized by its distinctive, elongated stems and dense, bristly foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of China. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its upright growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
