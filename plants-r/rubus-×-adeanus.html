Rubus × adeanus

Rubus × adeanus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins.

Rubus × adeanus is valued for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and used in various culinary applications.
