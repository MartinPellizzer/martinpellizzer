Rosa hudsoniana salicifolia

Rosa hudsoniana salicifolia, commonly known as the Hudson's Bay rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Canada.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often described as resembling willow leaves, hence its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
