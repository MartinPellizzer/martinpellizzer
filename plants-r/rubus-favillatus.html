Rubus favillatus

Rubus favillatus, commonly known as the alpine bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. This shrub typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes.

The plant is notable for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to harsh mountain climates.
