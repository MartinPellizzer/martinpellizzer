Racosperma rhamphophyllum

Racosperma rhamphophyllum, commonly known as the long-beak acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, often spiny stems and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or cream, arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

Racosperma rhamphophyllum is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
