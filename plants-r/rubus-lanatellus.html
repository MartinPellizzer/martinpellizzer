Rubus lanatellus

Rubus lanatellus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters.

Rubus lanatellus is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
