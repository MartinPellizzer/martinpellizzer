Rubus pendulinus

Rubus pendulinus, commonly known as the hanging raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The botanical features of Rubus pendulinus make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.
