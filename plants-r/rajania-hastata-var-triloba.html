Rajania hastata var. triloba

Rajania hastata var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in the wild.
