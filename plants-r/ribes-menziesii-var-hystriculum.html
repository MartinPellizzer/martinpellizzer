Ribes menziesii var. hystriculum

Ribes menziesii var. hystriculum, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and turn red in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
