Rytidosperma irianense

Rytidosperma irianense, commonly known as the Irianese grass, is a perennial grass species native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Rytidosperma irianense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control in arid environments.
