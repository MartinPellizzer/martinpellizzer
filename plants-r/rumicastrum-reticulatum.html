Rumicastrum reticulatum

Rumicastrum reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has a dense network of veins on its stems, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
