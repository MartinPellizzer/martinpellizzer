Rubus plicatus var. ammobius

Rubus plicatus var. ammobius, commonly known as the Ammobius blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in dry climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique variations in leaf and fruit morphology.
