Rhododendron japonoheptamerum f. leucanthum

Rhododendron japonoheptamerum f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered form of the Japanese seven-merous rhododendron, is a variant characterized by its pale to white flowers.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to Japan, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features dense, dark green foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring, adding a striking contrast to the foliage.

This form is prized in horticulture for its unique coloration and adaptability to shaded environments.
