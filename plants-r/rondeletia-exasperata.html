Rondeletia exasperata

Rondeletia exasperata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and branching.

Rondeletia exasperata is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
