Rubus trianthus

Rubus trianthus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Rubus trianthus is often found growing in clusters, forming dense thickets that support a range of wildlife.
