Ribes coeleste

Ribes coeleste, commonly known as blue currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It produces small, edible berries that range in color from pale yellow to deep blue, depending on maturity. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often serrated leaves, which are typically a vibrant green. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five pinkish-white petals and yellow stamens, blooming in early summer.

Ribes coeleste thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.
