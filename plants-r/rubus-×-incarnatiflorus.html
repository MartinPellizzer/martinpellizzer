Rubus × incarnatiflorus

Rubus × incarnatiflorus, commonly known as the redflower blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers, which are typically borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are usually dark purple to black when mature and are edible.

This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.
