Rhus borneensis

Rhus borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like poison ivy and cashew. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs along the stem. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by clusters of red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The plant has a rough, bark-like texture and is often found in lowland and hill forest habitats.
