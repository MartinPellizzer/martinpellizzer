Rhynchospora capillifolia

Rhynchospora capillifolia, commonly known as the slender thread-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Rhynchospora capillifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate and improve water quality.
