Rubus hirtus var. pulcher

Rubus hirtus var. pulcher, commonly known as the showy raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the first-year stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red and have a sweet-tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
