Rubus radula var. montisparsus

Rubus radula var. montisparsus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
