Rosa clotildea

Rosa clotildea, commonly known as the Clotilde rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and an erect growth habit.

Rosa clotildea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
