Rosa permollis

Rosa permollis, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Tian Shan and Pamir Mountains. This plant is characterized by its continuous blooming habit, producing fragrant flowers throughout the growing season. The leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of soft, white hair.

Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a high degree of ornamental value.
