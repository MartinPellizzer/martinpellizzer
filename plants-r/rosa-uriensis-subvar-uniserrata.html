Rosa uriensis subvar. uniserrata

Rosa uriensis subvar. uniserrata, commonly known as the single-toothed prairie rose, is a perennial shrub native to the central United States.

It typically grows in open prairies and dry woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the fruits are red, fuzzy rose hips that develop after flowering.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
