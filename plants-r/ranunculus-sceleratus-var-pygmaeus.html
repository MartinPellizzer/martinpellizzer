Ranunculus sceleratus var. pygmaeus

Ranunculus sceleratus var. pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are usually less than 2 centimeters in diameter.

It is notable for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
