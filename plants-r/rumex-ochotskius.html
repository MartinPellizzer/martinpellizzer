Rumex ochotskius

Rumex ochotskius, commonly known as Siberian sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other sorrel species. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a sour taste due to its high oxalic acid content. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
