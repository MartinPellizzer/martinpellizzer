Rubus tinifolius

Rubus tinifolius, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and it produces edible fruits that are popular in local foraging.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
