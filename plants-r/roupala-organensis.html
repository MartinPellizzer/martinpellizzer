Roupala organensis

Roupala organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains roupala, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elliptical, red or orange fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
