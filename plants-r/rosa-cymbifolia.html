Rosa cymbifolia

Rosa cymbifolia, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces small, red or yellow fruits known as hips. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa cymbifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
