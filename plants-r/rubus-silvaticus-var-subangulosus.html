Rubus silvaticus var. subangulosus

Rubus silvaticus var. subangulosus, commonly known as the sharp-angled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive angular leaf shapes and thorny stems.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in woodland and shrubland habitats. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters during the spring.

This variety is valued for both its ecological role and its use in local foraging and traditional medicine.
