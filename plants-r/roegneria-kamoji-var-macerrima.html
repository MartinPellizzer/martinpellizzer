Roegneria kamoji var. macerrima

Roegneria kamoji var. macerrima, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive purple to blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
