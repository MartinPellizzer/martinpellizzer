Restrepia tentaculata

Restrepia tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with yellow and purple hues. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and can reach up to 30 cm in height.

Restrepia tentaculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
