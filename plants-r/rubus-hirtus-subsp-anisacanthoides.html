Rubus hirtus subsp. anisacanthoides

Rubus hirtus subsp.

anisacanthoides, commonly known as the hairy blackberry or rough bramble, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticulture.
