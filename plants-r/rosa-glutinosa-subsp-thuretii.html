Rosa glutinosa subsp. thuretii

Rosa glutinosa subsp.

thuretii, commonly known as the sticky rose or thuret's rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are rose hips, which are typically orange-red and contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
