Racosperma brownianum var. intermedium

Racosperma brownianum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate racosperma, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, which enhances soil fertility in its native habitat.
