Ranunculus cassubicus var. elatior

Ranunculus cassubicus var. elatior, commonly known as the tall buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features finely divided, feather-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its upright growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
