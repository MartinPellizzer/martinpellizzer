Rosa intersita

Rosa intersita, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of soft hairs.

Rosa intersita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
