Rhamnus coriophylla var. acutidens

Rhamnus coriophylla var. acutidens, commonly known as the Japanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in racemes during the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
