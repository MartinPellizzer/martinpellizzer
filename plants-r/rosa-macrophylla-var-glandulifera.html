Rosa macrophylla var. glandulifera

Rosa macrophylla var. glandulifera, commonly known as the Chilean rose, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves with glandular dots, which give it its specific epithet. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The variety is known for its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
