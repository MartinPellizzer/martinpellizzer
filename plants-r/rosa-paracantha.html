Rosa paracantha

Rosa paracantha, commonly known as the thorned rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa paracantha produces red or orange hips, which contain seeds and are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
