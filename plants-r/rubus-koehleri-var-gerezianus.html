Rubus koehleri var. gerezianus

Rubus koehleri var. gerezianus, commonly known as the Gerezian raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, resembling raspberries in appearance. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in horticulture.
