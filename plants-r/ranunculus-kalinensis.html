Ranunculus kalinensis

Ranunculus kalinensis, commonly known as the Kalin's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive central blotch.

Ranunculus kalinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
