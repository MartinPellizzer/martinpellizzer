Rapa

Rapa, commonly known as the sea kale, is a hardy aquatic plant found in coastal areas.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant thrives in brackish or saline waters, often growing in shallow tidal zones. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly bitter taste.

Rapa is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines.
