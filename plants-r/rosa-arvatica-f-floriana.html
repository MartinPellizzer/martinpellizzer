Rosa arvatica f. floriana

Rosa arvatica f. floriana, commonly known as the Alpine rose or the alpine garden rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa arvatica species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often forming a rounded shrub. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the alpine regions of Europe and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
