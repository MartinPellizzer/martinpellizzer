Rosa gallica var. plena

Rosa gallica var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered Gallica rose, is a cultivar of the Gallica rose species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its densely packed, double flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, aromatic leaves. It blooms in early summer and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is also used in the production of rose oil and has historical significance in perfumery and horticulture.
