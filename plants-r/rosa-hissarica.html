Rosa hissarica

Rosa hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late summer. Rosa hissarica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it an ornamental plant valued in gardens and landscapes.
