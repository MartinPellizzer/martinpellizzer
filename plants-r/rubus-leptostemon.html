Rubus leptostemon

Rubus leptostemon, commonly known as the slender-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, thickets, and moist soils, preferring partial to full shade. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
