Rubus rigidatus

Rubus rigidatus, commonly known as the prickly rose or rough blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in horticulture.
