Raphanus sativus var. fusiformis

Raphanus sativus var. fusiformis, commonly known as the bulbous radish, is a cultivated variety of the radish plant.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy root that is typically white or pale pink in color. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cauliflower. Its root is the primary edible part, often harvested before it becomes too large and woody.

The plant prefers cool climates and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
