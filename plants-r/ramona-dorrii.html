Ramona dorrii

Ramona dorrii, commonly known as the Ramona's lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are slender and hairy.

Ramona dorrii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
