Rosa alba

Rosa alba, commonly known as the white rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, and it is characterized by its simple, white petals and thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and covered in fine hairs. Rosa alba is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and landscapes.

It is also used in perfumery and has cultural significance in various traditions.
