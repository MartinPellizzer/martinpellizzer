Rosa multiflora f. pilosissima

Rosa multiflora f. pilosissima, commonly known as the velvety multiflora rose, is a cultivar characterized by its dense, silvery-white to pale pink flowers.

It is a hybrid rose that originates from the genus Rosa, known for its thorny stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features a shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems that often grow in a sprawling or climbing manner. Its leaves are compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its visual interest and fragrance.
