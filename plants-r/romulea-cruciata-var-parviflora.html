Romulea cruciata var. parviflora

Romulea cruciata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered romulea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial bulbous plant that produces compact, rosette-like foliage. The flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive cruciform shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
