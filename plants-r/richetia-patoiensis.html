Richetia patoiensis

Richetia patoiensis, commonly known as the Patagonian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Richetia patoiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
