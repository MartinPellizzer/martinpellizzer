Rubus × erythranthemoides

Rubus × erythranthemoides, commonly known as the evergreen bramble, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits, typically red or black berries, which are edible and often used in jams and wines. It thrives in temperate climates and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is commonly cultivated in gardens and hedgerows.
