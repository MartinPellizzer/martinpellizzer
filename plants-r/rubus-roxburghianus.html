Rubus roxburghianus

Rubus roxburghianus, commonly known as the Indian blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Himalayas and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

The botanical features of Rubus roxburghianus make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
