Ranunculus scandinavicus

Ranunculus scandinavicus, commonly known as the Scandinavian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern Europe.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

The species is characterized by its simple yet elegant floral structure and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
