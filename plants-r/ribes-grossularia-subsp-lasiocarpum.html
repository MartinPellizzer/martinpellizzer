Ribes grossularia subsp. lasiocarpum

Ribes grossularia subsp.

lasiocarpum, commonly known as the downy gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically covered in a fine, downy coating. These berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are often used in jams and preserves.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.
