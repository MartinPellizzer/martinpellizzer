Rhododendron mucronulatum subsp. sichotense

Rhododendron mucronulatum subsp.

sichotense, commonly known as the Sichotang rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas and northern China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
