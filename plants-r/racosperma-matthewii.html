Racosperma matthewii

Racosperma matthewii, commonly known as the Matthew's acacia, is a small, erect shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in globular inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Racosperma matthewii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
