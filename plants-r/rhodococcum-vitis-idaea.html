Rhodococcum vitis-idaea

Rhodococcum vitis-idaea, commonly known as the crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, dark blue to black berries that are typically edible. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
