Rubus tereticaulis subsp. leptopus

Rubus tereticaulis subsp.

leptopus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, dark red fruits that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
