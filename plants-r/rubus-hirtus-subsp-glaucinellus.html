Rubus hirtus subsp. glaucinellus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

glaucinellus, commonly known as the glaucous blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
