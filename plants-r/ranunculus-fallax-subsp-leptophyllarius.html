Ranunculus fallax subsp. leptophyllarius

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

leptophyllarius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has finely divided, pinnate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
