Ravenochloa

Ravenochloa, commonly known as the black grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Ravenochloa has small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-angled, spikelet-like fruits.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms.
