Rubus parvifolius var. triphyllus

Rubus parvifolius var. triphyllus, commonly known as the three-leaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and its small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a red color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has a woody stem with thorny branches and produces white to pale yellow flowers in early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
