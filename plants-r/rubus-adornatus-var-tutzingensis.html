Rubus adornatus var. tutzingensis

Rubus adornatus var. tutzingensis, commonly known as the Tutzinger raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It produces small, red, edible berries that are similar in appearance to other Rubus species. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
