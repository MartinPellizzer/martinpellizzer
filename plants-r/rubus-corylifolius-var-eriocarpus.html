Rubus corylifolius var. eriocarpus

Rubus corylifolius var. eriocarpus, commonly known as the hairy-fruited bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, hairy, red berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This variant is notable for its dense growth habit and potential use in ornamental gardening.
