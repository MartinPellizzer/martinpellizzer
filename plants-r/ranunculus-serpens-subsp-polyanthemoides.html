Ranunculus serpens subsp. polyanthemoides

Ranunculus serpens subsp.

polyanthemoides, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with slender, often prostrate stems that root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and deeply lobed, often with a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
