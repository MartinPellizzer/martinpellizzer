Rumex similans

Rumex similans, commonly known as the common sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, which gives them a sharp, tangy flavor.

Rumex similans is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
