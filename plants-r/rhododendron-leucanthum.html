Rhododendron leucanthum

Rhododendron leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
