Rhynchosia tricuspidata subsp. tricuspidata

Rhynchosia tricuspidata subsp.

tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-toothed cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features trifoliate leaves with three ovate leaflets and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible, yellowish seeds. It is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in traditional agriculture.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct three-toothed shape at the base of the leaflets.
