Rubia melanocarpa

Rubia melanocarpa, commonly known as black balm, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes numerous species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are borne on clusters and remain on the plant throughout the winter. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia melanocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
