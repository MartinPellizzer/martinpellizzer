Ranunculus japonicus var. rostratus

Ranunculus japonicus var. rostratus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive rostellum, which is a small, beak-like structure at the base of the petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It is characterized by its short, upright stems and is often found in temperate regions of Japan and Korea.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
