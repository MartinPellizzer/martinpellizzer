Rosa involutata

Rosa involutata, commonly known as the curled rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its tightly curled, reddish-brown stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Rosa involutata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
