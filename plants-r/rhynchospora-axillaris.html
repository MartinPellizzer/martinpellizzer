Rhynchospora axillaris

Rhynchospora axillaris, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Rhynchospora axillaris is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and the presence of spikelets arranged in dense inflorescences.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
