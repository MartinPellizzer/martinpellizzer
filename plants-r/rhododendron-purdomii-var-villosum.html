Rhododendron purdomii var. villosum

Rhododendron purdomii var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
