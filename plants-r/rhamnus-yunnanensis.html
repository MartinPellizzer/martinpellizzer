Rhamnus yunnanensis

Rhamnus yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are often red or black when mature. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
