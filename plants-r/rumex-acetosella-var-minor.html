Rumex acetosella var. minor

Rumex acetosella var. minor, commonly known as lesser sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration, especially under stress or in shaded conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its edible leaves have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies for centuries.
