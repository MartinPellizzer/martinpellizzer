Robinia scandens

Robinia scandens, commonly known as the yellowwood or false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a yellowish-green hue. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in upright clusters during the spring. Its bark is thick, flaky, and often has a reddish-brown appearance.

Robinia scandens is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.
