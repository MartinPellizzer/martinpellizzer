Rubus fuscus var. acanthodes

Rubus fuscus var. acanthodes, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
