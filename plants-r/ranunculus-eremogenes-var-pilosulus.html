Ranunculus eremogenes var. pilosulus

Ranunculus eremogenes var. pilosulus, commonly known as the silky desert buttercup, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy yellow flowers with prominent, lobed petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its unique morphology includes a short, erect stem and basal leaves with a distinctive hairy texture.

This variety is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with showy blooms.
