Ranunculus auricomus subsp. brachygonophorus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

brachygonophorus, commonly known as the short-stalked buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine broadly ovate petals, often adorned with a distinctive yellow central blotch.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided into three to five lobes, and the stems are usually slender and hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
