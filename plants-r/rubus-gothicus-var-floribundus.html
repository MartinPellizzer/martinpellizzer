Rubus gothicus var. floribundus

Rubus gothicus var. floribundus, commonly known as the Gothic raspberry, is a variant of the brambles native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species within its habitat.
