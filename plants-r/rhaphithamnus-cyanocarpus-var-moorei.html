Rhaphithamnus cyanocarpus var. moorei

Rhaphithamnus cyanocarpus var. moorei, commonly known as Moore's blue-fruited hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking blue-black berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small white to pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, attracting native pollinators.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
