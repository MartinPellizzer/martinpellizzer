Rhynchospora cephalantha

Rhynchospora cephalantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, white to pale green flowers that are clustered at the tip of the stem, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Rhynchospora cephalantha plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
