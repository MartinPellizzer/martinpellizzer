Ranunculus pthora

Ranunculus pthora, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has finely divided leaves. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in meadows and along riverbanks.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.
