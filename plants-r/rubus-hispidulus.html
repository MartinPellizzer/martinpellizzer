Rubus hispidulus

Rubus hispidulus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy conditions.

Rubus hispidulus is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
