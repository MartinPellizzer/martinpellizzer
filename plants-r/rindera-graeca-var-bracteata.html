Rindera graeca var. bracteata

Rindera graeca var. bracteata, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit, often forming dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
