Rubus × novanglicus

Rubus × novanglicus, commonly known as the American blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
