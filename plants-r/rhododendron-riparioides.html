Rhododendron riparioides

Rhododendron riparioides, commonly known as the riverbank rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows along riverbanks and moist forest edges. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
