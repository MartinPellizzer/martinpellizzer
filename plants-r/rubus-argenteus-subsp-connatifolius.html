Rubus argenteus subsp. connatifolius

Rubus argenteus subsp.

connatifolius, commonly known as silverleaf bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
