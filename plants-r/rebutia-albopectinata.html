Rebutia albopectinata

Rebutia albopectinata, commonly known as the White-veined Rebutia, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and fleshy stems. The plant features pale green to bluish-green stems with distinctive white veins, giving it its common name. It produces small, colorful flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the growing season.

Rebutia albopectinata is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
