Rhododendron indicum var. lateritium

Rhododendron indicum var. lateritium, commonly known as the Indian rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
