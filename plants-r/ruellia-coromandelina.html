Ruellia coromandelina

Ruellia coromandelina, commonly known as the Coromandel bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of trumpet-shaped, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough, slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
