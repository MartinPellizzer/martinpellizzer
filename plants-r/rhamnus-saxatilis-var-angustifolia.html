Rhamnus saxatilis var. angustifolia

Rhamnus saxatilis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh conditions.
