Roupala pallida

Roupala pallida, commonly known as the white roupala, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
