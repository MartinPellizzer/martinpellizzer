Rubus fordii

Rubus fordii, commonly known as Ford's blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and berries. The plant produces edible black berries that are typically small and firm. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the genus.

Rubus fordii is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value in gardens.
