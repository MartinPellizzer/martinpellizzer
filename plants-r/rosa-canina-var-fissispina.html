Rosa canina var. fissispina

Rosa canina var. fissispina, commonly known as the fissured hip rose, is a variant of the dog rose native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters, and are followed by distinctive red, fuzzy fruits called hips. This plant is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It also has historical uses in herbal medicine and as a source of ornamental value in gardens.
