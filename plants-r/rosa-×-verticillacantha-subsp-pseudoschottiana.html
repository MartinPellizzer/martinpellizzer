Rosa × verticillacantha subsp. pseudoschottiana

Rosa × verticillacantha subsp.

pseudoschottiana, commonly known as the Schott's shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
