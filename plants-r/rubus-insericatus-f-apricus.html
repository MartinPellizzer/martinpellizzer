Rubus insericatus f. apricus

Rubus insericatus f. apricus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by the characteristic red or orange berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
