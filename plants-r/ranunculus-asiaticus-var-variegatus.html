Ranunculus asiaticus var. variegatus

Ranunculus asiaticus var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a yellow center. The leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive variegation pattern of green and white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

This variety is particularly noted for its showy blooms and compact growth habit.
