Rosa viscaria var. pseudoapricorum

Rosa viscaria var. pseudoapricorum, commonly known as the false apricot rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to apricots, though they are not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
