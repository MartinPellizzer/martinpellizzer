Ranunculus auricomus subsp. mammiformidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

mammiformidens, commonly known as the mammiform leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals and its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble mammillary shapes.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
