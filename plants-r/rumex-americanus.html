Rumex americanus

Rumex americanus, commonly known as American sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, reddish-veined leaves that are edible and have a tart, lemony flavor. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.
