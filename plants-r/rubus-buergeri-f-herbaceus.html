Rubus buergeri f. herbaceus

Rubus buergeri f. herbaceus, commonly known as the Japanese bramble or Buerger's bramble, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red or black berries. It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
