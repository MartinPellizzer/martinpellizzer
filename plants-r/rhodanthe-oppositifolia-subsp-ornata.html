Rhodanthe oppositifolia subsp. ornata

Rhodanthe oppositifolia subsp.

ornata, commonly known as the showy rhodanthe, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
