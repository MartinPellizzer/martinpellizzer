Rubus myricae var. subtilis

Rubus myricae var. subtilis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, dark purple to black fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
