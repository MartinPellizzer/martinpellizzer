Rosa lithocarpa

Rosa lithocarpa, commonly known as the stonecrop rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically small, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving them a silvery appearance.

Rosa lithocarpa is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.
