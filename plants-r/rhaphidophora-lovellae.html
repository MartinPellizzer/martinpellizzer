Rhaphidophora lovellae

Rhaphidophora lovellae, commonly known as the Lovell's arrowroot, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of many arum species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Rhaphidophora lovellae is also valued for its edible tubers, which are used in traditional cuisines.
