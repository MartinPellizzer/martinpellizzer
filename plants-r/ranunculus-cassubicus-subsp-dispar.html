Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. dispar

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

dispar, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically surrounded by a ring of white or pale yellow sepals.

The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are usually alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, often calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
