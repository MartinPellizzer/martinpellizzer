Rubus tomentosus var. stenothyrsus

Rubus tomentosus var. stenothyrsus, commonly known as the narrow-thorned blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its flowers are white and clustered in the axils of the leaves, and it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit.
