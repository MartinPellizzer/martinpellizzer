Rhomboda monensis

Rhomboda monensis, commonly known as the Mon peninsula rhombod.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the coastal regions of Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in mangrove or brackish water habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
