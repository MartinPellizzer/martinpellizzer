Rubus volvatus var. bellardii

Rubus volvatus var. bellardii, commonly known as the Bellard's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and its role in local ecosystems.
