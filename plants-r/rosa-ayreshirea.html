Rosa ayreshirea

Rosa ayreshirea, commonly known as the Ayreshire rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the area of the Ayreshire Mountains. This shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features thorny stems covered in dense, glandular hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five to seven petals and a strong, fragrant scent.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as rosehips, which contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange-colored pulp.
