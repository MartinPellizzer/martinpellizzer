Rubus carinthiacus

Rubus carinthiacus, commonly known as the Carinthian bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Central Europe, particularly found in regions such as Austria, Slovenia, and Croatia. The plant is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

Rubus carinthiacus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
