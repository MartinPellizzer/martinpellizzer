Rubia brownei

Rubia brownei, commonly known as Brown's Rubia, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
