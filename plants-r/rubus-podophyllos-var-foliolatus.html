Rubus podophyllos var. foliolatus

Rubus podophyllos var. foliolatus, commonly known as the leafy blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The flowers are white and clustered, blooming in early summer.
