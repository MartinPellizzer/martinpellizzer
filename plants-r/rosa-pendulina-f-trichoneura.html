Rosa pendulina f. trichoneura

Rosa pendulina f. trichoneura, commonly known as the trichoneura form of the weeping rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its unique growth habit and foliage.

This form exhibits a pendulous growth pattern with long, arching stems that cascade downward, creating a graceful, weeping appearance. The leaves are typically smaller and more densely arranged compared to the standard Rosa pendulina, contributing to a lush, textured canopy. Its flowers are usually pink to red, blooming in clusters along the stems, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its visual impact.
