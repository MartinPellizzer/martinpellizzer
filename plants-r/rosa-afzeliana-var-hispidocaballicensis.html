Rosa afzeliana var. hispidocaballicensis

Rosa afzeliana var. hispidocaballicensis, commonly known as the Hispid Caballic Rose, is a variant of the Afghan rose native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
