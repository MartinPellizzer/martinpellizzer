Rubus lejeunei var. fuckelii

Rubus lejeunei var. fuckelii, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
