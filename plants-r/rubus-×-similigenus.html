Rubus × similigenus

Rubus × similigenus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types, including clay and loam. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its growth habit includes both shrub and vine forms, depending on the specific cultivar. Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is widely cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
