Rubus thyrsoideus var. pubescens

Rubus thyrsoideus var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its flowers are white and clustered in racemes, and the plant is often found in moist, wooded areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible berries.
