Rubus fruticosus subsp. radula

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

radula, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces small, usually red or black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed ground.
