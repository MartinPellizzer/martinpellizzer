Rosa coriifolia var. grisescens

Rosa coriifolia var. grisescens, commonly known as the grey-leafed shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are distinctive, with a finely toothed margin and a silvery-gray underside that gives it its common name. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a range of garden environments.
