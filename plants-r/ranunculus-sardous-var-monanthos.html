Ranunculus sardous var. monanthos

Ranunculus sardous var. monanthos, commonly known as the single-flowered meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
