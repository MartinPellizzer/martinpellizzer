Rheum renifolium

Rheum renifolium, commonly known as the palmate rhubarb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in panicles, which are followed by small, greenish fruits. Rheum renifolium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to various landscapes.
