Ranunculus flammula subsp. reptans

Ranunculus flammula subsp.

reptans, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five petals and is characterized by its creeping growth habit, producing runners that root at the nodes.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
