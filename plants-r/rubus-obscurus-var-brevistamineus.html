Rubus obscurus var. brevistamineus

Rubus obscurus var. brevistamineus, commonly known as the short-staminated bramble, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, giving it a distinctive appearance. The fruit, a raspberry-like aggregate, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.

This plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is an important food source for various wildlife species.
