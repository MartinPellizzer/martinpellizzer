Ranunculus peltatus

Ranunculus peltatus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply divided and resemble a hand, and its showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.

Ranunculus peltatus is often found in Europe, Asia, and North America, where it thrives in damp, nutrient-rich soils.
