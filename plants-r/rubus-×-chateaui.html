Rubus × chateaui

Rubus × chateaui, commonly known as the Châteauneuf blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus, resulting in a species with unique botanical characteristics. It typically features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, with dark green foliage that turns red in autumn. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color.

It is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value in gardens.
