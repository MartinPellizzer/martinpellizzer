Russelia sarmentosa var. parviflora

Russelia sarmentosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf russelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Russelia sarmentosa var. parviflora is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
