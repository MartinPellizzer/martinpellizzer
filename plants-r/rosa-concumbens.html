Rosa concumbens

Rosa concumbens, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a species native to South America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its stems are covered in soft, white to grayish hairs.

Rosa concumbens is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
