Rosa jacobi

Rosa jacobi, commonly known as Jacob's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red-orange fruits known as hips.

Rosa jacobi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
