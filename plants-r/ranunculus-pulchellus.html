Ranunculus pulchellus

Ranunculus pulchellus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 13 petal-like sepals. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are palmately divided with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
