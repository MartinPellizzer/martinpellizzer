Raphanus sativus convar. minowase

Raphanus sativus convar. minowase, commonly known as the Japanese radish or daikon, is a cultivated variety of the radish plant.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, white or pale yellow root, which can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a taproot system and develops a rosette of dark green, pinnately lobed leaves. It thrives in cool climates and is typically grown as an annual crop.

The botanical features of this variety make it well-suited for both culinary and agricultural use.
