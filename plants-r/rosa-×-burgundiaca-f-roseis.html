Rosa × burgundiaca f. roseis

Rosa × burgundiaca f. roseis, commonly known as the Burgundy rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and abundant, semi-double to double pink flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and is prized for its vigorous flowering and disease resistance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thornless stems and dark green, leathery foliage. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
