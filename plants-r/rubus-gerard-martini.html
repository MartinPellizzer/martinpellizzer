Rubus gerard-martini

Rubus gerard-martini, commonly known as the Gerard-Martini raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is valued for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
