Rubus biformis

Rubus biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The flowers are white or pale green, with five sepals and five petals.
