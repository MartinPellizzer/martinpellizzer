Rubus × vietus

Rubus × vietus, commonly known as the European blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and a flowering period that typically occurs in late spring.
