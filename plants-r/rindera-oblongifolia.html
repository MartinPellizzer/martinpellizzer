Rindera oblongifolia

Rindera oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rindera, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Rindera oblongifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth of tropical forests.
