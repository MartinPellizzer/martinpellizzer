Rubus lignicensis

Rubus lignicensis, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are edible and widely consumed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
