Rubus figertii

Rubus figertii, commonly known as the Figitii blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible berries. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically covered in a fine layer of white fuzz. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
