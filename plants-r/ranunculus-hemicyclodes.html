Ranunculus hemicyclodes

Ranunculus hemicyclodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped yellow flowers with 5 to 10 petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, somewhat hairy texture.

Ranunculus hemicyclodes is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic properties.
