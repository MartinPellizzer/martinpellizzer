Racosperma gelasinum

Racosperma gelasinum, commonly known as the Gelasinum acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system for water retention. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.

Its seeds are often used in traditional Indigenous practices.
