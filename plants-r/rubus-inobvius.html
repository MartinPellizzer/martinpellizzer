Rubus inobvius

Rubus inobvius, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thin, sparsely hairy stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that have serrated edges.
