Rubus × tomentellifolius

Rubus × tomentellifolius, commonly known as the woollyleaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and thorny stems.

It is native to the western United States and thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. Its leaves are elliptical with a distinctive woolly texture on both sides, contributing to its unique appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
