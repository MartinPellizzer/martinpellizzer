Rosa × scopulosa

Rosa × scopulosa, commonly known as the sand rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to the western United States, particularly in arid and rocky environments. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are often covered in thorns.

Rosa × scopulosa is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a hardy ornamental plant suitable for xeriscaping.
