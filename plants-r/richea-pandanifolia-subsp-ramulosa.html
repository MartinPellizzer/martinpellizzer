Richea pandanifolia subsp. ramulosa

Richea pandanifolia subsp.

ramulosa, commonly known as the slender richea, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne on inflorescences at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more delicate growth form compared to other subspecies of Richea pandanifolia.
