Rosa arvensis var. biserrata

Rosa arvensis var. biserrata, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The leaves are compound, with serrated edges that give the plant its specific name, *biserrata*. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This species is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
