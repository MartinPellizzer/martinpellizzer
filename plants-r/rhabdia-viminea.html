Rhabdia viminea

Rhabdia viminea, commonly known as the slender glassworts, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in coastal and marshland habitats. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Rhabdia viminea plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
