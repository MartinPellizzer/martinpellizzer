Robinia viscosa rosea

Robinia viscosa rosea, commonly known as the pink robinia or pink false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its bark is thick, fissured, and often has a rough, flaky texture.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions.
