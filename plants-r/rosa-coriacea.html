Rosa coriacea

Rosa coriacea, commonly known as the rough rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals, and bloom in the spring.

Rosa coriacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun.
