Ranunculus aquatilis var. elegans

Ranunculus aquatilis var. elegans, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, floating leaves and yellow flowers.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The leaves are typically divided into small, feathery segments, while the flowers consist of five petal-like sepals. It is often found in shallow waters where sunlight can penetrate to support its photosynthetic processes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
