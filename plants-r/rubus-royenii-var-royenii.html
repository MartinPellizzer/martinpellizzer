Rubus royenii var. royenii

Rubus royenii var. royenii, commonly known as the Royen blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and highly aromatic. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The fruit is often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications due to its rich flavor and high antioxidant content.
