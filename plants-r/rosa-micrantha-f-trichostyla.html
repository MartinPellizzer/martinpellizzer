Rosa micrantha f. trichostyla

Rosa micrantha f. trichostyla, commonly known as the trichostyle rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa micrantha.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of China and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Its foliage and stems are covered in a fine, white to grayish pubescence, which gives it its distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticultural gardens.
