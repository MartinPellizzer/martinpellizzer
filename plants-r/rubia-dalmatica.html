Rubia dalmatica

Rubia dalmatica, commonly known as Dalmatian balm, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic leaves and flowers have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Rubia dalmatica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
