Ranunculus californicus var. laetus

Ranunculus californicus var. laetus, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive lobed appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
