Ranunculus hyperboreus subsp. samojedorum

Ranunculus hyperboreus subsp.

samojedorum, commonly known as the Siberian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia. It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils in tundra and alpine environments.

The plant features showy yellow flowers with five broad, rounded petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are palmately divided into three to five lobes, and it has a short, stout stem.

It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season, blooming in late spring to early summer.
