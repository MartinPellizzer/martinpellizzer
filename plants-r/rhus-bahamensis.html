Rhus bahamensis

Rhus bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian sumac, is a small shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like poison ivy and cashew. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is a dry, globose drupe that turns red when mature, often attracting birds and small mammals.
