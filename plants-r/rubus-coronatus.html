Rubus coronatus

Rubus coronatus, commonly known as the crown raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus coronatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
