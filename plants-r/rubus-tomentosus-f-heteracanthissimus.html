Rubus tomentosus f. heteracanthissimus

Rubus tomentosus f. heteracanthissimus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a variant of the common blackberry, characterized by its dense, white pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with lobes that are broader and more rounded compared to the typical form. The flowers are pink to white, and the fruit is a dark purple to black aggregate berry.

This form is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
