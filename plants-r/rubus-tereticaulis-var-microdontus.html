Rubus tereticaulis var. microdontus

Rubus tereticaulis var. microdontus, commonly known as the small-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the edible fruits.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
