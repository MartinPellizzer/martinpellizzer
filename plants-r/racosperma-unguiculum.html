Racosperma unguiculum

Racosperma unguiculum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Racosperma unguiculum is also valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for native wildlife.
