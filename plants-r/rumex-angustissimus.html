Rumex angustissimus

Rumex angustissimus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by triangular, reddish-brown seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows.

Rumex angustissimus has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly for digestive and skin-related ailments.
