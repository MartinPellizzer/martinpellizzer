Roemeria hybrida var. rhoeadiflora

Roemeria hybrida var. rhoeadiflora, commonly known as the Showy Roemeria, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
