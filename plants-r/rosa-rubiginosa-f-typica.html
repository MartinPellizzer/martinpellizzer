Rosa rubiginosa f. typica

Rosa rubiginosa f. typica, commonly known as the rough rose or the shrub rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, thorny shrub form and produces pink to red flowers with a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its hardiness and fragrance.

The fruit, a red, hairy rose hip, contains seeds and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
