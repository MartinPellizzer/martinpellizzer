Rosa villosa var. grenieri

Rosa villosa var. grenieri, commonly known as the Grenier's bristly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, bristly stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and repeat blooming habit.
