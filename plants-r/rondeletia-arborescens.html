Rondeletia arborescens

Rondeletia arborescens, commonly known as the everlasting or red-eyed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy clusters of bright yellow flowers with red centers, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Rondeletia arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
