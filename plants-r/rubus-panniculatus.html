Rubus panniculatus

Rubus panniculatus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberries and blackberries.
