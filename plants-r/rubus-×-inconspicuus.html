Rubus × inconspicuus

Rubus × inconspicuus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, which ripens in summer, is a juicy, dark purple berry that is widely consumed both fresh and in processed forms.
