Rubus opacus

Rubus opacus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable component of its native ecosystem.
