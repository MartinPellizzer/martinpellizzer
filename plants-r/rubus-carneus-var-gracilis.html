Rubus carneus var. gracilis

Rubus carneus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender raspberry, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thornless stems, which allow it to spread along the ground. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ecological role and horticultural interest.
