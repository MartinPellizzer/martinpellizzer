Rubus abietinus var. angustatus

Rubus abietinus var. angustatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
