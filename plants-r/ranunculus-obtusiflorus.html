Ranunculus obtusiflorus

Ranunculus obtusiflorus, commonly known as the blunt-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine rounded petals and a hairy, segmented stem. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
