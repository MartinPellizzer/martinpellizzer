Rubus × retentus

Rubus × retentus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to the northern hemisphere and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white or pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit is highly valued for its sweet-tart flavor and is commonly used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
