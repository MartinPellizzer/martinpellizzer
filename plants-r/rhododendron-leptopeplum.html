Rhododendron leptopeplum

Rhododendron leptopeplum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, typically reaching heights of less than 1 meter. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in horticultural gardens and natural habitats.
