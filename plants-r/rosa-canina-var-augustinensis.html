Rosa canina var. augustinensis

Rosa canina var. augustinensis, commonly known as the Augustine rose, is a variant of the dog rose, characterized by its distinctive red-orange hips.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant produces fragrant pink flowers in spring, followed by showy, red-orange fruits in autumn. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.

The leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant is generally hardy and adaptable to various soil types.
