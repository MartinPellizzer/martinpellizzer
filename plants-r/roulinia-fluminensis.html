Roulinia fluminensis

Roulinia fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces a striking inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Roulinia fluminensis is valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
