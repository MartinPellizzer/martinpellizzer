Rosa caryophyllacea f. hirta

Rosa caryophyllacea f. hirta, commonly known as the hairy rose or woolly rose, is a horticultural form of the species Rosa caryophyllacea.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color. It is native to regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This form is valued in gardens for its unique texture and seasonal blooms.
