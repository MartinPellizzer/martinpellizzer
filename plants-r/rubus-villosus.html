Rubus villosus

Rubus villosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types.

Rubus villosus is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
