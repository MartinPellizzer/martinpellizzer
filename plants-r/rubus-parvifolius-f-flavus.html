Rubus parvifolius f. flavus

Rubus parvifolius f. flavus, commonly known as the yellow-leaved raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry plant characterized by its distinct yellow foliage.

It is a shrub native to eastern Asia, typically found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, typical of the Rubus genus.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
