Rubus radula subsp. casparyi

Rubus radula subsp.

casparyi, commonly known as the Caspary's bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native range.
