Rosa × transmota

Rosa × transmota, commonly known as the Transmontana rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa bracteata.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated edges and a medium green color, and its thorns are short and numerous.

Rosa × transmota is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
