Rubus pallidus proles loehrii

Rubus pallidus proles loehrii, commonly known as the pale bramble or a variant of the common bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typically with three leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
