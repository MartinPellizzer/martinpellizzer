Rosa coriifolia var. crassifolia

Rosa coriifolia var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved prickly rose, is a variant of the common shrub rose.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in spring. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Japan.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
