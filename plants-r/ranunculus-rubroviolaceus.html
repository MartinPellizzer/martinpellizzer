Ranunculus rubroviolaceus

Ranunculus rubroviolaceus, commonly known as the purple-stemmed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often reddish or purple in color.

Ranunculus rubroviolaceus is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in the spring and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
