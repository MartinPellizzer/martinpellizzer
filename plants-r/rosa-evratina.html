Rosa evratina

Rosa evratina, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have a strong, sweet fragrance and are often used in perfumery.

Rosa evratina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
