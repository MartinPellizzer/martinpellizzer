Ribes amazonica

Ribes amazonica, commonly known as the Amazon blackcurrant, is a shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes in the family Grossulariaceae, which includes other currant and gooseberry species. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.
