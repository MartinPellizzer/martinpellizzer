Rosa gallica var. subinermis

Rosa gallica var. subinermis, commonly known as the French pink or garden rose, is a species of shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a trailing habit and produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and serrated.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
