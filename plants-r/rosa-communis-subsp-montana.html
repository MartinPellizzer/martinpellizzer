Rosa communis subsp. montana

Rosa communis subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain rose, is a subspecies of the common rose native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and resilience.
