Rhinerrhiza divitiflora

Rhinerrhiza divitiflora, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Rhinerrhiza divitiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
