Racosperma gracilifolium

Racosperma gracilifolium, commonly known as slender-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in globular or cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
