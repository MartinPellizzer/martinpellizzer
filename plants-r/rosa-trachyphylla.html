Rosa trachyphylla

Rosa trachyphylla, commonly known as the roughleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers.

It typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and produces edible rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa trachyphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
