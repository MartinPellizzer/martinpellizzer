Roegneria canaliculata

Roegneria canaliculata, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves that have a bluish-green to purplish hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale blue to purple florets, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types, including moist to dry conditions.

Roegneria canaliculata is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along streambanks, where it contributes to ecological stability and habitat diversity.
