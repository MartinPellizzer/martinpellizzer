Rosa rubiginosa f. corymbosa

Rosa rubiginosa f. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered sweetbriar, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it has a strong, pleasant scent.

Rosa rubiginosa f. corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hedges or as a landscape plant.
