Rubus colemannii var. harzburgensis

Rubus colemannii var. harzburgensis, commonly known as Harzburg blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its horticultural value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
