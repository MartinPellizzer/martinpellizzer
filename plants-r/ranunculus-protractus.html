Ranunculus protractus

Ranunculus protractus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus protractus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its distinctive floral structures and often toxic compounds.
