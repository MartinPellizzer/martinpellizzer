Rubus distortifolius

Rubus distortifolius, commonly known as the twisted-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Japan. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, often contorted leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the summer.

Rubus distortifolius is typically found in woodland areas and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
