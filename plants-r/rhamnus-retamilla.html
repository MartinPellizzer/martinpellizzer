Rhamnus retamilla

Rhamnus retamilla, commonly known as the Canary Island buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Rhamnus retamilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
