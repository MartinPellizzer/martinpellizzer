Rubia haenkeana

Rubia haenkeana, commonly known as the Peruvian madder, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species known for their medicinal and dye-producing properties. The plant features slender, climbing stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically have five petals.

The roots of Rubia haenkeana contain alkaloids and have been used traditionally in folk medicine for their purported health benefits.
