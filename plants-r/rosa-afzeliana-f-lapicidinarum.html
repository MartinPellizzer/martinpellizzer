Rosa afzeliana f. lapicidinarum

Rosa afzeliana f. lapicidinarum, commonly known as the rock rose, is a unique horticultural form of the species Rosa afzeliana.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, upright stems that are well adapted to rocky or arid environments. The plant produces showy, semi-double pink flowers with a delicate fragrance, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
