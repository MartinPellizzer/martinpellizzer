Racosperma fodinale

Racosperma fodinale, commonly known as the Fodinale acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically yellow or cream in color, arranged in racemose inflorescences.

The seeds are enclosed in slender, woody pods, which contribute to its ecological role in soil stabilization and wildlife habitat.
