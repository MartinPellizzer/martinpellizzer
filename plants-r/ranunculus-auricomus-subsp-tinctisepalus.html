Ranunculus auricomus subsp. tinctisepalus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

tinctisepalus, commonly known as the tinctured-sepal buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals and a distinctive, showy sepals that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered with fine, silky hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens.
