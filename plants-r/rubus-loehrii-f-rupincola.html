Rubus loehrii f. rupincola

Rubus loehrii f. rupincola, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
