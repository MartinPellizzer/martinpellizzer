Ranunculus cymbalaria subsp. saximontanus

Ranunculus cymbalaria subsp.

saximontanus, commonly known as the saximontan ranunculus, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, often yellow or white flowers.

The subspecies is native to rocky or gravelly habitats in mountainous regions. It typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, showy blooms that attract pollinators.
