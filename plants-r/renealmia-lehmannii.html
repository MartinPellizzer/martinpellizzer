Renealmia lehmannii

Renealmia lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's reed, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or swampy habitats, thriving in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, often in disturbed or waterlogged soils.

Renealmia lehmannii plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various small organisms.
