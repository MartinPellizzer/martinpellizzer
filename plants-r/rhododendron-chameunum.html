Rhododendron chameunum

Rhododendron chameunum, commonly known as the Alpine rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with broad, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. Its flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron chameunum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
