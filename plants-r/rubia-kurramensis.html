Rubia kurramensis

Rubia kurramensis, commonly known as Kurram madder, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in cymes. Its most notable feature is the presence of red, orange, or yellow roots, which have been historically used for dyeing fabrics.

Rubia kurramensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in natural dyeing practices.
