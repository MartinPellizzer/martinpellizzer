Rosa afzeliana var. collinderi

Rosa afzeliana var. collinderi, commonly known as the Collinder's rose, is a species native to parts of East Africa, including Kenya and Tanzania.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in open woodlands and rocky habitats. The plant features pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries that are edible for some wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
