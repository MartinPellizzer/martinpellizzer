Rhipsalis zanzibarica

Rhipsalis zanzibarica, commonly known as the Zanzibar mistletoe, is a climbing cactus native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in small, white to pale green spines. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
