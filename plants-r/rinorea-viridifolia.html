Rinorea viridifolia

Rinorea viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leaved rinorea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with a subtle bluish hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are known for their high concentration of essential oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

Rinorea viridifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
