Rosa subcoriifolia

Rosa subcoriifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, reddish stems and leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage turns a striking yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value.

Rosa subcoriifolia is often cultivated for its attractive appearance and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
