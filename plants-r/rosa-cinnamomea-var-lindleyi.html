Rosa cinnamomea var. lindleyi

Rosa cinnamomea var. lindleyi, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a hybrid rose species native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, deep red to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, thornless stems and oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
