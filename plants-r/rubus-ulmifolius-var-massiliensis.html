Rubus ulmifolius var. massiliensis

Rubus ulmifolius var. massiliensis, commonly known as the Massilian blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically more robust than those of the standard species. The plant features thorny stems and broad, three-lobed leaves with a serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
