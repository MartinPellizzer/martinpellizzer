Rubus tomentosus subsp. hypoleucus

Rubus tomentosus subsp.

hypoleucus, commonly known as the white-haired raspberry, is a shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces prickly stems covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more pale and less hairy appearance compared to other Rubus species.
