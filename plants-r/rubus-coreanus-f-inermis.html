Rubus coreanus f. inermis

Rubus coreanus f. inermis, commonly known as the Korean raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to East Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves, which are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the cultivar. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
