Rosa multiflora var. trichogyma

Rosa multiflora var. trichogyma, commonly known as the Japanese rose or climbing rose, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has a vigorous climbing habit and can reach heights of several meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
