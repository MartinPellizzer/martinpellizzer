Rosa afzeliana var. ripensis

Rosa afzeliana var. ripensis, commonly known as the water rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the wetlands and riverbanks of eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and wetland restoration projects.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing shoreline vegetation.
