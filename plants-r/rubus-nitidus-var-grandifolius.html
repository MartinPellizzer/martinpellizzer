Rubus nitidus var. grandifolius

Rubus nitidus var. grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with large, ovate leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
