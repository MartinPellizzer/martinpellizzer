Rosa tomentella var. obtusifolia

Rosa tomentella var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the woolly rose or the soft-leaved rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white or gray pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate with rounded or obtuse tips, giving it its specific epithet. It produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
