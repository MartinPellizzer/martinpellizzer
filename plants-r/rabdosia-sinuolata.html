Rabdosia sinuolata

Rabdosia sinuolata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf rabdosia, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that often have a wavy margin, giving it its common name. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

Rabdosia sinuolata is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
