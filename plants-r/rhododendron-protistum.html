Rhododendron protistum

Rhododendron protistum, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron protistum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as part of natural landscapes.
