Ranunculus lingua f. angustifolius

Ranunculus lingua f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals that are typically borne on slender stems. The leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions.
