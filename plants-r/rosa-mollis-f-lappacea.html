Rosa mollis f. lappacea

Rosa mollis f. lappacea, commonly known as the soft rose or lappaceous rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that are often described as "lappaceous," giving it a distinctive textured appearance. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and hardy nature.

This variety is particularly adapted to cooler climates and well-drained soils.
