Rapistrum hirtum

Rapistrum hirtum, commonly known as field rape or wild radish, is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, hairy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Rapistrum hirtum is an important plant in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in pollinator habitats.
