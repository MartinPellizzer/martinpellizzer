Rhododendron floccigerum var. appropinquans

Rhododendron floccigerum var. appropinquans, commonly known as the Appalachian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a glossy texture, while its flowers are often borne in racemes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
