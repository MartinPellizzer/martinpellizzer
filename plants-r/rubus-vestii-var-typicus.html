Rubus vestii var. typicus

Rubus vestii var. typicus, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry.

It is a thorny shrub native to Japan, characterized by its dark purple to black berries. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

The berries are edible but can be quite tart when raw.
