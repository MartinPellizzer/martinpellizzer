Rubus × monticulorum

Rubus × monticulorum, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
