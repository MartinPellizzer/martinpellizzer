Raphanus sativus var. gayanus

Raphanus sativus var. gayanus, commonly known as the black radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black root, which is typically harvested in the second year of growth. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, and its leaves are lobed and edible. It thrives in cool climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
