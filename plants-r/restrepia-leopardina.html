Restrepia leopardina

Restrepia leopardina, commonly known as the leopard orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique leaf patterns that resemble a leopard's spots. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often clustered in a raceme, and bloom during the cooler months.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
