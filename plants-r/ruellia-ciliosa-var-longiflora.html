Ruellia ciliosa var. longiflora

Ruellia ciliosa var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of tubular, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Ruellia ciliosa var. longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
