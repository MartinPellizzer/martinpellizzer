Rosa × muscosa alba

Rosa × muscosa alba, commonly known as the white muscosa rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its fragrant, white flowers.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, with thorny stems and dark green foliage. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a delicate, velvety appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
