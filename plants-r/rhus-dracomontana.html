Rhus dracomontana

Rhus dracomontana, commonly known as the dragon's blood tree, is a unique species belonging to the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its umbrella-shaped crown and clustered, glossy green leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain a sticky, crimson sap.

This sap, known as dragon's blood, has been historically used for its medicinal and dyeing properties.
