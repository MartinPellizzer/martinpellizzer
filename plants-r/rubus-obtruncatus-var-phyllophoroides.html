Rubus obtruncatus var. phyllophoroides

Rubus obtruncatus var. phyllophoroides, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and is often cultivated in gardens and wildland areas.
