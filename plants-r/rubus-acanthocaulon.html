Rubus acanthocaulon

Rubus acanthocaulon, commonly known as the thorny blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
