Rhododendron roxieanum var. cucullatum

Rhododendron roxieanum var. cucullatum, commonly known as the Cucullatum Rhododendron, is a unique variant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its flowers range in color from deep pink to purple, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is appreciated for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
