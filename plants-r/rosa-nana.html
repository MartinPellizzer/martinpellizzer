Rosa nana

Rosa nana, commonly known as the dwarf garden rose, is a compact shrub native to northern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, which bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. It is well-suited for small gardens or containers due to its manageable size.

Rosa nana requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
