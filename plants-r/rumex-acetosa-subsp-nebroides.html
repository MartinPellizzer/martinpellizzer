Rumex acetosa subsp. nebroides

Rumex acetosa subsp.

nebroides, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features upright, hollow stems that are often reddish in color, and its basal leaves are lanceolate with a wavy margin.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows.

Narrowleaf dock is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
