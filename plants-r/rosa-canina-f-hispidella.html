Rosa canina f. hispidella

Rosa canina f. hispidella, commonly known as the bristly dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and scrub habitats across Europe.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.
