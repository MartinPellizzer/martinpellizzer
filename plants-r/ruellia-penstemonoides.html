Ruellia penstemonoides

Ruellia penstemonoides, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, drought-tolerant plants.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early autumn.
