Ranunculus auricomus subsp. amoenifrons

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

amoenifrons, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, shield-shaped center and deeply lobed petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
