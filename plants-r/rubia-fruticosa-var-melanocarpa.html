Rubia fruticosa var. melanocarpa

Rubia fruticosa var. melanocarpa, commonly known as blackcurrant, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and vitamin C. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and has a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers are pale yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value as well as its agricultural significance.
