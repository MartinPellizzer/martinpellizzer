Rubus pallidus f. apricus

Rubus pallidus f. apricus, commonly known as the pale bramble or white-fruited raspberry, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, aggregate berries that are typically yellow or orange in color.

This variety is often found in woodlands and open areas across temperate regions.
