Rorida cornus-africani

Rorida cornus-africani, commonly known as the African dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
