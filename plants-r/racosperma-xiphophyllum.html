Racosperma xiphophyllum

Racosperma xiphophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Racosperma xiphophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native gardens and landscaping.
