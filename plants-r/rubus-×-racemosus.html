Rubus × racemosus

Rubus × racemosus, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry bush, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are clustered together and contain multiple small seeds. These fruits are often cultivated for their sweet, juicy flavor and are used in various culinary applications.

Rubus × racemosus thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
