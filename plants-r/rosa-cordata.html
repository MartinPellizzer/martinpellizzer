Rosa cordata

Rosa cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. Rosa cordata prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch of color to gardens during that time.
