Rhipsalis baccifera subsp. mauritiana

Rhipsalis baccifera subsp.

mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius mistletoe, is a climbing epiphytic plant native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender stems that often trail or climb over other vegetation.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are usually reduced to small spines, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
