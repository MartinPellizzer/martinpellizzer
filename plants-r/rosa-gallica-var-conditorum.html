Rosa gallica var. conditorum

Rosa gallica var. conditorum, commonly known as the French rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Native to Europe, it has been cultivated for centuries for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and historical landscapes.
