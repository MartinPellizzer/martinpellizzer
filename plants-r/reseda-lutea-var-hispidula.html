Reseda lutea var. hispidula

Reseda lutea var. hispidula, commonly known as hispid lupine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The leaves are pinnately divided, with small, pointed leaflets that contribute to its distinctive appearance. This variety is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
