Rhetinantha ochroglossa

Rhetinantha ochroglossa, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along riverbanks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators with their vibrant color and sweet fragrance.
