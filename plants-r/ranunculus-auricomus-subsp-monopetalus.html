Ranunculus auricomus subsp. monopetalus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

monopetalus, commonly known as the single-petaled buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with a single, broad petal that is often mistaken for a single bloom.

The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and damp grasslands.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
