Rubus crataegifolius var. horridus

Rubus crataegifolius var. horridus, commonly known as the roughleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and resistant to drought.
