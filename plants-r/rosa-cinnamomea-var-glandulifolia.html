Rosa cinnamomea var. glandulifolia

Rosa cinnamomea var. glandulifolia, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the cinnamon rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, semi-double to double pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while the stems are covered in glandular hairs. This variety is characterized by its showy flowers and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
