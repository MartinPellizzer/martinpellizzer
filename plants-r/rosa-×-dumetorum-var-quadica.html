Rosa × dumetorum var. quadica

Rosa × dumetorum var. quadica, commonly known as the four-leaf rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically arranged in a four-leaf clover-like pattern, giving it its distinctive common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
