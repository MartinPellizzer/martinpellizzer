Rubus clinocephalus var. lobatisepalus

Rubus clinocephalus var. lobatisepalus, commonly known as the broad-sepaled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark berries that are typically edible. The plant features trifoliate leaves with lobed leaflets and thorned stems, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in early summer.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in local food systems.
