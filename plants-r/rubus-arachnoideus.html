Rubus arachnoideus

Rubus arachnoideus, commonly known as the spiderberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern and central United States, where it thrives in moist, wooded areas. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its berries are typically red or black when mature and are edible, though they are often tart.

The plant's name reflects its appearance, with the "spiderberry" referring to its small, berry-like fruits.
