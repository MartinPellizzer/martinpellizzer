Rhysolepis pilicaulis

Rhysolepis pilicaulis, commonly known as the woolly mountain pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, scaly bark and dense, needle-like foliage. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived species. Its cones are small and typically contain two seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to harsh, mountainous conditions.
