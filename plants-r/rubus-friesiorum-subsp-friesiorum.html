Rubus friesiorum subsp. friesiorum

Rubus friesiorum subsp.

friesiorum, commonly known as the European bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing food for various wildlife species.
