Rosa tibetana

Rosa tibetana, commonly known as the Tibetan rose, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to extreme conditions. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to its harsh habitat. Its leaves are small, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stems, aiding in water retention.

Rosa tibetana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in Tibetan regions.
