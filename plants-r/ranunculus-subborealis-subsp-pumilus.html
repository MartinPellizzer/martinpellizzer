Ranunculus subborealis subsp. pumilus

Ranunculus subborealis subsp.

pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a small herbaceous perennial native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils in tundra and alpine environments.

The plant features basal leaves with a hairy surface and yellow flowers with five to eight petals. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its ability to thrive in harsh, high-latitude conditions.
