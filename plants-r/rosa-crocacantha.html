Rosa crocacantha

Rosa crocacantha, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or rock rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or red flowers during the summer months. Rosa crocacantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or coastal environments.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
