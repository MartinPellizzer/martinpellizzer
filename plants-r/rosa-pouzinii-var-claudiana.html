Rosa pouzinii var. claudiana

Rosa pouzinii var. claudiana, commonly known as the Claudiana rose, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
