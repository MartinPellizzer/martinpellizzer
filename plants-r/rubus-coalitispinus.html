Rubus coalitispinus

Rubus coalitispinus, commonly known as the clustered bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are often red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
