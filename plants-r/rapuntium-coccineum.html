Rapuntium coccineum

Rapuntium coccineum, commonly known as the red rapunculus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, ovate leaves and produces striking red or purple flowers with four petals. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is an annual or biennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, rounded seeds.
