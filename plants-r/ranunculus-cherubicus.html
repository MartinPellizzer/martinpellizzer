Ranunculus cherubicus

Ranunculus cherubicus, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are known for their fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
