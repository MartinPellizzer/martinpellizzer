Rhagadiolus stellatus var. edulis

Rhagadiolus stellatus var. edulis, commonly known as the edible starflower, is a unique plant with striking star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow.

It is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant is characterized by its succulent leaves and compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also edible, often used in culinary applications for their mild, nutty flavor.

The variety 'edulis' is particularly valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of antioxidants and essential oils.
