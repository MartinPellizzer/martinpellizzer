Ranunculus radians

Ranunculus radians, commonly known as the early buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk and bright yellow petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture.

Ranunculus radians is known for its early blooming season, often appearing in late spring.
