Rubus hirtus var. oblongulus

Rubus hirtus var. oblongulus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This species is valued for its edible berries and its role in ecological habitats.
