Rubus luteifolius

Rubus luteifolius, commonly known as the yellow-leaved bramble, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves that often display a yellowish hue. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, hairy berries in the summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
