Ranunculus baldensis

Ranunculus baldensis, commonly known as the Bald Mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows. It has a short growing season and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous environment.

Due to its limited range and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
