Ribes aridum

Ribes aridum, commonly known as the desert gooseberry, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. The plant has small, oval leaves with a slightly hairy surface, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, producing clusters of greenish-white flowers.

Ribes aridum is well-suited to sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.
