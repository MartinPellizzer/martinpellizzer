Riedelia areolata

Riedelia areolata, commonly known as the spotted leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and clustered, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring or early summer.

Riedelia areolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes and succulent gardens.
