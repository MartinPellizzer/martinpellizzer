Rubus excavatus

Rubus excavatus, commonly known as the rock raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, resembling raspberries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
