Rytidosperma virescens var. parvispiculum

Rytidosperma virescens var. parvispiculum, commonly known as the small-spined blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
