Rubus multifer

Rubus multifer, commonly known as the many-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Rubus multifer is valued for its edible fruits and its role in local ecosystems.
