Rubus humifusus subsp. saxicola

Rubus humifusus subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as the saxicola raspberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, mountainous conditions.
