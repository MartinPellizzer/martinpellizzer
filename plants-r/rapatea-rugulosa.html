Rapatea rugulosa

Rapatea rugulosa, commonly known as the wrinkled rapatea, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy, dark green leaves that are often marked with irregular, wavy patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild and is often cultivated in specialized plant collections.
