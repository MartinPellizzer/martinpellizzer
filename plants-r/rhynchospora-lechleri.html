Rhynchospora lechleri

Rhynchospora lechleri, commonly known as the Amazonian marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America, particularly the Amazon Basin.

It typically grows in swampy or seasonally flooded areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
