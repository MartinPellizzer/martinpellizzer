Rochea perfoliata var. coccinea

Rochea perfoliata var. coccinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed rochea, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its appeal among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
