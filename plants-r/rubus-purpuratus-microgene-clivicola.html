Rubus purpuratus microgene clivicola

Rubus purpuratus microgene clivicola, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically red when mature and contain multiple seeds. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
