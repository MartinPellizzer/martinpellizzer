Rubus tereticaulis var. heckoi

Rubus tereticaulis var. heckoi, commonly known as the Heck's blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique morphological traits that set it apart from other blackberry varieties.
