Rubus sanguineus var. obtriangulatus

Rubus sanguineus var. obtriangulatus, commonly known as the triangled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are distinctly shaped with a triangular appearance. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
