Rubus condensatus

Rubus condensatus, commonly known as the denseberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberries and blackberries.
