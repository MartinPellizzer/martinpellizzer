Rubus anglofuscus

Rubus anglofuscus, commonly known as the dark-fuscous bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, often forming dense thickets.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and soil stabilization.
