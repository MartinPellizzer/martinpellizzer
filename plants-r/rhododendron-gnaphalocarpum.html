Rhododendron gnaphalocarpum

Rhododendron gnaphalocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its lance-shaped, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in forested habitats.
