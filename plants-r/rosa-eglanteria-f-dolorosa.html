Rosa eglanteria f. dolorosa

Rosa eglanteria f. dolorosa, commonly known as the sorrowful rose, is a rare and striking cultivar of the Rosa eglanteria species.

It is characterized by its deep red to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific cultivation requirements, it is often grown in specialized horticultural collections.
