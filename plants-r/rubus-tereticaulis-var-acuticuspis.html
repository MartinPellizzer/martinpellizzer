Rubus tereticaulis var. acuticuspis

Rubus tereticaulis var. acuticuspis, commonly known as the sharp-cusped blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that have three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other Rubus species by its sharp leaf teeth and unique fruit structure.
