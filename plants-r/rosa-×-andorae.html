Rosa × andorae

Rosa × andorae, commonly known as the Andorra rose, is a hybrid species native to the Pyrenees Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its hardiness and adaptability.

Rosa × andorae plays a significant role in the local flora of its native region.
