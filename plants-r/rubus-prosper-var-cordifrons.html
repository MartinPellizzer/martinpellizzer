Rubus prosper var. cordifrons

Rubus prosper var. cordifrons, commonly known as the heart-leaf blackberry.

This variant of the blackberry plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and thorny stems. It produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
