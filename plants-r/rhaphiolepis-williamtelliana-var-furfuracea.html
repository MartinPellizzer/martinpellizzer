Rhaphiolepis williamtelliana var. furfuracea

Rhaphiolepis williamtelliana var. furfuracea, commonly known as the fuzzy rosebay.

This evergreen shrub is characterized by its densely hairy, grayish-green leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The varietal name 'furfuracea' refers to its soft, velvety foliage texture.
