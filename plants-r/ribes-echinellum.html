Ribes echinellum

Ribes echinellum, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible, red berries that are often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Ribes echinellum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
