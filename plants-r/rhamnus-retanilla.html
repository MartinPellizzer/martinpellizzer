Rhamnus retanilla

Rhamnus retanilla, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, black berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Rhamnus retanilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
