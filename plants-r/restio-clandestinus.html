Restio clandestinus

Restio clandestinus, commonly known as the secret restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
