Rhus thouarsii var. monticola

Rhus thouarsii var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by red or orange berries that are clustered in groups of three. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
