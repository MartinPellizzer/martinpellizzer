Rhodiola pachyclados

Rhodiola pachyclados, commonly known as the Armenian rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves, which are adapted to survive in cold, high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, and its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties.
