Rumex × confinis

Rumex × confinis, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex acetobipinatus (broad-leaved sorrel). The plant typically has heart-shaped, reddish-veined leaves that are edible but contain oxalic acid. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its flowers are small and greenish, appearing in clusters, and it is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.
