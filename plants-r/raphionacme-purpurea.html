Raphionacme purpurea

Raphionacme purpurea, commonly known as purpleleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and clusters of small, lavender-pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive purple underside, contributing to its ornamental value. This perennial plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

It is valued for its attractive foliage and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
