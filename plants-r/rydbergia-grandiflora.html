Rydbergia grandiflora

Rydbergia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flameflower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice for tropical gardens and botanical collections.
