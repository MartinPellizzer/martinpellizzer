Rubus dissimulans var. selectus

Rubus dissimulans var. selectus, commonly known as the selected blackberry variant, is a thorny shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal fruit production.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and potential for use in both ornamental and edible landscaping.
