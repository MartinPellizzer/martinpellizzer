Rosa coriifolia var. collinalis

Rosa coriifolia var. collinalis, commonly known as the collinear shrub rose, is a variant of the coral tree rose, characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant features thorned stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rosa genus. Its flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in early summer, and are known for their fragrant scent.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
