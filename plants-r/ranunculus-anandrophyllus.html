Ranunculus anandrophyllus

Ranunculus anandrophyllus, commonly known as the Androphyllous buttercup, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers and delicate foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight sepals that resemble petals.
