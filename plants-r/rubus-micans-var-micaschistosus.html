Rubus micans var. micaschistosus

Rubus micans var. micaschistosus, commonly known as the glittering blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its unique leaf and fruit structures.

It typically grows in temperate regions, thriving in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. The leaves are dark green with a glossy texture, and the fruit is a small, dark purple berry with a slightly tart flavor. This variety is noted for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and is often cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
