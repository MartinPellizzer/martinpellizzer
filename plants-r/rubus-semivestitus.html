Rubus semivestitus

Rubus semivestitus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.

Its hairy foliage and rugged appearance make it easily distinguishable from similar plants.
