Rubus × pseudogremlii

Rubus × pseudogremlii, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, typically dark purple to black in color, which are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental and edible qualities, it is cultivated in gardens and used in fruit production.
