Rubus × furtivus

Rubus × furtivus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves, which are typical of many brambles. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are dark purple to black when ripe. These berries are edible and widely appreciated for their flavor and nutritional value.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.
