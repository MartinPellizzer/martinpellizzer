Rosa lutea var. bicolor

Rosa lutea var. bicolor, commonly known as the yellow rose with two colors, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its unique bicolor flowers.

The plant typically features showy blooms that transition from yellow at the base to pink or red at the tips, creating a striking visual contrast. It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, often found in woodland edges and moist soils. The leaves are compound, with five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces fragrant, thorned stems.

Rosa lutea var. bicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, seasonal display.
