Rockinghamia angustifolia

Rockinghamia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockinghamia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
