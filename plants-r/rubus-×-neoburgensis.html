Rubus × neoburgensis

Rubus × neoburgensis, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries, which are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological and genetic variability.
