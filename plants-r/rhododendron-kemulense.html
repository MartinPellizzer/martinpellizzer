Rhododendron kemulense

Rhododendron kemulense, commonly known as the Kew rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Nepal and India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in mountainous and forested areas. The plant features dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing a striking contrast to the seasonal blooms.

Rhododendron kemulense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
