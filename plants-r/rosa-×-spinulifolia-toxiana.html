Rosa × spinulifolia toxiana

Rosa × spinulifolia toxiana, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a hybrid shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in spring, which are followed by oval, orange-red fruits called hips. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
