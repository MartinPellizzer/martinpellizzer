Rosmarinus angustifolius

Rosmarinus angustifolius, commonly known as rosemary, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small blue or white flowers. The plant is evergreen, with woody stems that produce fragrant foliage. Its leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive pine-like scent.

Rosemary is widely used in herbal medicine and culinary applications due to its therapeutic and aromatic properties.
