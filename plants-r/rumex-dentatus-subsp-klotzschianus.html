Rumex dentatus subsp. klotzschianus

Rumex dentatus subsp.

klotzschianus, commonly known as the serrated dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaves, which are typically broad and lance-shaped.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
