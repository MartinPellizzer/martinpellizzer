Rubus hirtus var. obtusiflorens

Rubus hirtus var. obtusiflorens, commonly known as the broad-flowered bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five to seven petals, and appear in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
