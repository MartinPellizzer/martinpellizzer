Rhus hirta f. typhina

Rhus hirta f. typhina, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into several oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a key feature of the species. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

The plant has a woody stem and can grow up to several feet tall, making it a common sight in the wild.
