Ribes montigenum

Ribes montigenum, commonly known as the Sitka gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible and often used in jams and jellies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ribes montigenum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local wildlife.
