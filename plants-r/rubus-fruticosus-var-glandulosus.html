Rubus fruticosus var. glandulosus

Rubus fruticosus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. It produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant also plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing food and shelter.
