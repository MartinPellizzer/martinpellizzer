Rosa amygdalifolia

Rosa amygdalifolia, commonly known as the almondleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey, Syria, and Israel. The plant is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, with elliptical leaflets that have a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa amygdalifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its historical use in traditional medicine.
