Rubus braeuckeri f. glabriusculus

Rubus braeuckeri f. glabriusculus, commonly known as the glabrous bramble, is a variant of the Rubus genus characterized by its reduced pubescence.

It is a shrub with woody stems and compound leaves, typically featuring three to five leaflets. The flowers are small, usually white or pale pink, and are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits, known as berries, are aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, showcasing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
