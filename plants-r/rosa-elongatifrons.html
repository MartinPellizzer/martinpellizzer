Rosa elongatifrons

Rosa elongatifrons, commonly known as the long-fruited rose, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its most distinctive feature is its elongated, woody fruits, which contain several seeds and remain on the plant for an extended period.

Rosa elongatifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
