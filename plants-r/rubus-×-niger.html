Rubus × niger

Rubus × niger, commonly known as blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which consist of small, dark purple to black drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

Rubus × niger is valued for its edible fruit, which is widely used in culinary applications.
