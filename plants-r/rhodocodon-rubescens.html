Rhodocodon rubescens

Rhodocodon rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered rhodocodon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red petals that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, thriving in arid to semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
