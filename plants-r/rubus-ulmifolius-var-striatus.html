Rubus ulmifolius var. striatus

Rubus ulmifolius var. striatus, commonly known as the striped blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value in gardens.
