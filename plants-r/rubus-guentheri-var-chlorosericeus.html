Rubus guentheri var. chlorosericeus

Rubus guentheri var. chlorosericeus, commonly known as the chlorosericeous blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically eaten by wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex morphology and genetic variation within the Rubus genus.
