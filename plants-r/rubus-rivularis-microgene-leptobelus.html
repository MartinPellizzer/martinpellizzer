Rubus rivularis microgene leptobelus

Rubus rivularis microgene leptobelus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found along stream banks and in wet meadows.

Its flowers are white to pink, and it is known for its ornamental value and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
