Rosa assimilis

Rosa assimilis, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the summer. Its botanical characteristics include stipules that are usually absent or reduced, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Rosa assimilis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
