Ranunculus fallax subsp. galbanus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

galbanus, commonly known as the Galbanus buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features striking yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals and a hairy stem that often appears reddish or purplish.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
