Rubus × constrictifrons

Rubus × constrictifrons, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the berries are edible, though some varieties may have a tart flavor.
