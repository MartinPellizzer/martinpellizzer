Rosa graveolens subsp. szaboi

Rosa graveolens subsp.

szaboi, commonly known as the Hungarian rose, is a species native to the Carpathian Basin. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces oval, greenish-yellow fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery and medicine highlight its botanical and cultural significance.
