Rhodora congesta

Rhodora congesta, commonly known as the clustered rhodora, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring. Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
