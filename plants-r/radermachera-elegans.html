Radermachera elegans

Radermachera elegans, commonly known as the silver lace vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are glossy and have a distinctive silver sheen. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are typically followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.
