Ranunculus aconitifolius var. parviflorus

Ranunculus aconitifolius var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its preference for moist soils. The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and small, yellow flowers with five petals. Its botanical characteristics include a short, stout stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

It is often found in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland ecosystems.
