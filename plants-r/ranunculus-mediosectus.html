Ranunculus mediosectus

Ranunculus mediosectus, commonly known as the medium-sected buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically lobed or deeply divided. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with numerous stamens and a prominent central pistil.

Ranunculus mediosectus prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
