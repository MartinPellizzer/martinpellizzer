Rhododendron macgregoriae

Rhododendron macgregoriae, commonly known as the MacGregor's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its botanical features include broad, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
