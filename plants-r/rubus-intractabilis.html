Rubus intractabilis

Rubus intractabilis, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black fruits. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
