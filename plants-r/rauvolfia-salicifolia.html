Rauvolfia salicifolia

Rauvolfia salicifolia, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks. Its roots contain alkaloids, which have been traditionally used in medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
