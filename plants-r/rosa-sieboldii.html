Rosa sieboldii

Rosa sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Rosa sieboldii is also notable for its historical significance in botanical exploration and horticulture.
