Ranunculus auricomus subsp. rubellinodes

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

rubellinodes, commonly known as the red-stemmed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive red or orange center.

The subspecies is characterized by its reddish stems and foliage, which distinguish it from other members of the Ranunculus genus. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
