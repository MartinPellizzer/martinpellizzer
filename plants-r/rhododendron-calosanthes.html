Rhododendron calosanthes

Rhododendron calosanthes, commonly known as the fragrant rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are known for their pleasant, spicy fragrance.

Rhododendron calosanthes is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
