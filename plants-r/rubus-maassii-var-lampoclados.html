Rubus maassii var. lampoclados

Rubus maassii var. lampoclados, commonly known as the Maasii raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
