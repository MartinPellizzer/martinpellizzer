Ranunculus bullatus var. plantagineus

Ranunculus bullatus var. plantagineus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
