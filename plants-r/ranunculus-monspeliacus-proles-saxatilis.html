Ranunculus monspeliacus proles saxatilis

Ranunculus monspeliacus proles saxatilis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

The species is adapted to cold climates and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.
