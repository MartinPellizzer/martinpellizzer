Ribes cynosbati var. atrox

Ribes cynosbati var. atrox, commonly known as the Himalayan gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when ripe. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually pink to red in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in various cuisines.
