Rhododendron alutaceum var. iodes

Rhododendron alutaceum var. iodes, commonly known as the silverleaf azalea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
