Rhynchospora schwackei

Rhynchospora schwackei, commonly known as the swamp thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear pale green to reddish flowers, which are typically inconspicuous and not showy. Rhynchospora schwackei thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
