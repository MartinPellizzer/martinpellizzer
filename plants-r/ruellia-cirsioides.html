Ruellia cirsioides

Ruellia cirsioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Ruellia cirsioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
