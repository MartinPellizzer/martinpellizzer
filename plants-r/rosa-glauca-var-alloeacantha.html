Rosa glauca var. alloeacantha

Rosa glauca var. alloeacantha, commonly known as the blue rose or alloeacantha rose, is a rare and unique variant of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its striking blue-pink flowers, which are a result of a genetic mutation affecting pigmentation. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a contrasting backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by horticulturists and botanists alike.
