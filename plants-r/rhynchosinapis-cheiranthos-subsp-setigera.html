Rhynchosinapis cheiranthos subsp. setigera

Rhynchosinapis cheiranthos subsp.

setigera, commonly known as the hairy-flowered wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and dense, glandular hairs that cover its foliage.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically arranged in racemes. It is native to southern Europe and is often found growing in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
