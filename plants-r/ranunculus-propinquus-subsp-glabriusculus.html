Ranunculus propinquus subsp. glabriusculus

Ranunculus propinquus subsp.

glabriusculus, commonly known as the smoothleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

The plant features small, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy center, and its leaves are usually smooth and divided. It has a short, stout stem and produces numerous seeds in a fluffy, feathery cluster.

This subspecies is characterized by its reduced leaf hairs and is often found in wetland environments.
