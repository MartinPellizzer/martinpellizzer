Rhipsalis aculeata

Rhipsalis aculeata, commonly known as the silver needle cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems covered in small, soft spines. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in winter, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
