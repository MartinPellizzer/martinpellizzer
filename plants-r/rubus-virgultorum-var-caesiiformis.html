Rubus virgultorum var. caesiiformis

Rubus virgultorum var. caesiiformis, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a variant of the brambles native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces edible berries that are small and tart. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the eastern United States.

This plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
