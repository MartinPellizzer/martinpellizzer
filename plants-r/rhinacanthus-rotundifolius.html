Rhinacanthus rotundifolius

Rhinacanthus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved devil's fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are round or oval in shape, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive, sweet-smelling fragrance.

The fruit is a fig-like structure that is edible and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
