Reseda cochinchinensis

Reseda cochinchinensis, commonly known as Vietnamese mignonette, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Reseda cochinchinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
