Rodrigoa planadensis

Rodrigoa planadensis, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright purple berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Rodrigoa planadensis typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
