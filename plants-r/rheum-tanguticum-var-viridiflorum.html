Rheum tanguticum var. viridiflorum

Rheum tanguticum var. viridiflorum, commonly known as the Green-flowered Buckthorn, is a perennial herb native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It is characterized by its large, rhubarb-like leaves and distinctive greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces long, segmented capsules that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often studied for its potential medicinal and horticultural uses.
