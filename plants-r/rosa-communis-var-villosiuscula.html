Rosa communis var. villosiuscula

Rosa communis var. villosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence covering its stems and leaves.

This species belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions with temperate climates. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with a fragrant scent, and bloom in the spring. The plant produces oval, greenish-yellow fruits called hips, which are used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.
