Rotheca myricoides subsp. muenzneri

Rotheca myricoides subsp.

muenzneri, commonly known as the Mexican silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine, silver-gray hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. Rotheca myricoides subsp.

muenzneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
