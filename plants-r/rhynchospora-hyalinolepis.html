Rhynchospora hyalinolepis

Rhynchospora hyalinolepis, commonly known as the glass-leaved flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a thin, waxy layer, giving them a glassy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters near the base of the stems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize wetland habitats effectively.

Rhynchospora hyalinolepis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
