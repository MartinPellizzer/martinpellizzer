Rosa villosa f. hispida

Rosa villosa f. hispida, commonly known as the hispid Rosa, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa villosa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
