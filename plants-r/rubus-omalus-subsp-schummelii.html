Rubus omalus subsp. schummelii

Rubus omalus subsp.

schummelii, commonly known as the Schummel's blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Central America. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
