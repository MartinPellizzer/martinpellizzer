Ranunculus affinis var. apetalus

Ranunculus affinis var. apetalus, commonly known as the barren buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup species characterized by its lack of petals.

This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers with sepals that resemble petals, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically palmately divided and have a hairy texture.

It is a short-lived perennial that blooms in early spring and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
