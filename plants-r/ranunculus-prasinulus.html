Ranunculus prasinulus

Ranunculus prasinulus, commonly known as the green buttercup, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental plant.
