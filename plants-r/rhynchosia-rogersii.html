Rhynchosia rogersii

Rhynchosia rogersii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

Rhynchosia rogersii is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
