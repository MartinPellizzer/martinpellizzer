Rubus hirtus var. erythrostachyoides

Rubus hirtus var. erythrostachyoides, commonly known as the red-stemmed raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, typical of the Rubus genus. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or orange in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
