Rhynchospora chalarocephala

Rhynchospora chalarocephala, commonly known as the white-headed spike-rush, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, upright stems and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and pond edges. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can influence local ecosystems.

The species is native to parts of Africa and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
