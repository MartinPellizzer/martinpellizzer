Rubus villicaulis subsp. ornatus

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

ornatus, commonly known as the ornate blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
