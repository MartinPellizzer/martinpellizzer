Rhamnus asplenifolia

Rhamnus asplenifolia, commonly known as the asplenium-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, black berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and woodland ecosystems.
