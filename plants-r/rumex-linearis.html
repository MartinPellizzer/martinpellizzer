Rumex linearis

Rumex linearis, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Rumex linearis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
