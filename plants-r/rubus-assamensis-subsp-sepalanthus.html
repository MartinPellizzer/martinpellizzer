Rubus assamensis subsp. sepalanthus

Rubus assamensis subsp.

sepalanthus, commonly known as the Assam raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnately compound.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments at moderate altitudes.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
