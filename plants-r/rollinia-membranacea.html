Rollinia membranacea

Rollinia membranacea, commonly known as the Brazilian cheese fruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that produces large, yellow-orange fruits resembling a cheese wheel. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, typical of the Anacardiaceae family.

The fruit is edible, with a creamy, sweet flesh that has a mild, nutty flavor.
