Rubus caldasianus

Rubus caldasianus, commonly known as the Caldasian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats, often in cloud forests at higher elevations. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark purple to black fruits.

The botanical features of Rubus caldasianus make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
