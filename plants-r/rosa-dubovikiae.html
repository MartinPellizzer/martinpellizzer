Rosa dubovikiae

Rosa dubovikiae, commonly known as the Dubovik's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in the summer months, which are followed by oval, red-orange hips. It is characterized by its dense foliage and adaptability to a variety of soil types, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.

Rosa dubovikiae is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
