Rubus × secophilus

Rubus × secophilus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This hybrid is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in culinary and commercial applications.
