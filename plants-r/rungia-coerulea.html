Rungia coerulea

Rungia coerulea, commonly known as the blue ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance.

Rungia coerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
