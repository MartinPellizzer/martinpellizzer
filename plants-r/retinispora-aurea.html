Retinispora aurea

Retinispora aurea, commonly known as the golden retinaspora, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant has slender, woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and potential medicinal properties.
