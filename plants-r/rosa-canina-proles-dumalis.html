Rosa canina proles dumalis

Rosa canina proles dumalis, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, orange-red hips that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs.

Rosa canina proles dumalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
