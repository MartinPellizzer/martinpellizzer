Rhamnus purpusii

Rhamnus purpusii, commonly known as the Japanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five pairs of elliptical, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
