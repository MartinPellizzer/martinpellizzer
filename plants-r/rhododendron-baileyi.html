Rhododendron baileyi

Rhododendron baileyi, commonly known as the Appalachian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are leathery and oval-shaped, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Rhododendron baileyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
