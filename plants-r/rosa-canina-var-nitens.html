Rosa canina var. nitens

Rosa canina var. nitens, commonly known as the shining dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant produces pale pink to white flowers that are typically smaller than those of the typical Rosa canina. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the fruit is a red, hairy rosehip.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
