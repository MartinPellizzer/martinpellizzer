Rottboellia loricata

Rottboellia loricata, commonly known as the swordleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Rottboellia loricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
