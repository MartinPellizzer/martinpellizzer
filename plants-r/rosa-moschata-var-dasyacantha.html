Rosa moschata var. dasyacantha

Rosa moschata var. dasyacantha, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a variant of the musk rose that is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and thorny stems.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are typically dark green with a velvety texture, and the plant is known for its hardy nature.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
