Rosa gallica var. copacensis

Rosa gallica var. copacensis, commonly known as the Copacabana rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to South America.

It features upright stems with dark green, lobed leaves and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical collections and conservation efforts.
