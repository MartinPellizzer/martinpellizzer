Rhamnus purshiana var. hirtella

Rhamnus purshiana var. hirtella, commonly known as the Pacific buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark, glossy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
