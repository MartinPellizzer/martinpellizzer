Rhamnus maximovicziana

Rhamnus maximovicziana, commonly known as the Caucasian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
