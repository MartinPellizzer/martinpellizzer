Rubus chroosepalus var. omeiensis

Rubus chroosepalus var. omeiensis, commonly known as the Omei raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry genus native to China.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers during the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Rubus genus.
