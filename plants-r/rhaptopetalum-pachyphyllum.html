Rhaptopetalum pachyphyllum

Rhaptopetalum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces striking, often fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in some areas.
