Rubus serpens var. densifolius

Rubus serpens var. densifolius, commonly known as the denseleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure includes compound leaves with serrated edges and flowers that bloom in the spring.
