Randia graeffei

Randia graeffei, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring glossy dark green leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are usually pink to red in color and bloom from late spring to early fall. Randia graeffei is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

It plays a role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
