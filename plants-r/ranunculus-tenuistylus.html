Ranunculus tenuistylus

Ranunculus tenuistylus, commonly known as the slender-styled buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white petals. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with numerous stamens.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in rocky or meadow environments at high altitudes.
