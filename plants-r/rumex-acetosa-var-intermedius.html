Rumex acetosa var. intermedius

Rumex acetosa var. intermedius, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist soils along roadsides, meadows, and waste areas. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a wavy margin and a distinctive reddish-veined appearance. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
