Rubus moluccanus var. thespesiiphyllus

Rubus moluccanus var. thespesiiphyllus, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated margins and a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
