Rosa brigantina

Rosa brigantina, commonly known as the Brabant rose, is a species native to the Balkans and parts of Central Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves with three to five leaflets, and an extensive root system adapted to various soil types.

Rosa brigantina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
