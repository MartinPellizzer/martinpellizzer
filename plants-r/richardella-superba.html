Richardella superba

Richardella superba, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the surface of still or slow-moving freshwater bodies. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals and a yellow center, blooming at night and closing during the day. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Richardella superba plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and helping to stabilize water quality.
