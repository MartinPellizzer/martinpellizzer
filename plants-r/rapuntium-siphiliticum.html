Rapuntium siphiliticum

Rapuntium siphiliticum, commonly known as the syphilis lily, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Liliaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with a strong, often unpleasant odor. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
