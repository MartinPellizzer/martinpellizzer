Rubus mirificus

Rubus mirificus, commonly known as the magnificent raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
