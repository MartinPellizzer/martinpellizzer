Rubus affinis subsp. novoaffinis

Rubus affinis subsp.

novoaffinis, commonly known as the New Affinis Blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
