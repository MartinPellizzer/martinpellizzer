Rosa californica var. ultramontana

Rosa californica var. ultramontana, commonly known as the San Francisco rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the coastal regions of California.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces red hips, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in coastal climates.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild, temperate environments.
