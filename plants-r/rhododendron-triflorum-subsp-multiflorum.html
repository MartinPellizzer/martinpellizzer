Rhododendron triflorum subsp. multiflorum

Rhododendron triflorum subsp.

multiflorum, commonly known as the California rhododendron, is a shrub native to the western United States. It features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and conservation efforts.
