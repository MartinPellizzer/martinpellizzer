Rousseauvia

Rousseauvia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their dense, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Rousseauvia species typically thrive in moist, well-drained soil and prefer partial to full shade. They are popular as ornamental plants due to their compact growth habit and ability to bloom continuously throughout the year.

The genus includes several species, such as Rousseauvia glaberrima and Rousseauvia frutescens, which are often cultivated for their aesthetic appeal.
