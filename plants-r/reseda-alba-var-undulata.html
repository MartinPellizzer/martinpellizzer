Reseda alba var. undulata

Reseda alba var. undulata, commonly known as the undulating white reseda, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous habit and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs. This variety is notable for its undulating leaf margins, which give it its specific epithet.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
