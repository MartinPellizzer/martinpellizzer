Rubus subvillosus var. vendeanus

Rubus subvillosus var. vendeanus, commonly known as the Vendean blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the coastal regions of France.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, dark purple to black berries. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. This variety is valued for its hardy nature and adaptability to coastal climates.

Its fruits are often used in local culinary traditions and traditional herbal practices.
