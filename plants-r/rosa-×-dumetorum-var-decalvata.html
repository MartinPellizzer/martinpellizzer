Rosa × dumetorum var. decalvata

Rosa × dumetorum var. decalvata, commonly known as the pale dog rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that are usually borne in clusters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are usually hairy underneath.

This variety is native to Europe and is often found in woodlands and scrub habitats.
