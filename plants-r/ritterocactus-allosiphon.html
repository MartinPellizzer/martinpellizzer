Ritterocactus allosiphon

Ritterocactus allosiphon, commonly known as the long-spine cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that often branches into multiple stems, forming a bushy appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its spines are long and straight, providing protection from herbivores.

Ritterocactus allosiphon is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in desert environments.
