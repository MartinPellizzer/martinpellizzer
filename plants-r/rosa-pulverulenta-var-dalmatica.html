Rosa pulverulenta var. dalmatica

Rosa pulverulenta var. dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian dust rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems covered in soft, grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive, almost powdery appearance. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
