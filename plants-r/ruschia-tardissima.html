Ruschia tardissima

Ruschia tardissima, commonly known as the slow-growing Ruschia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a powdery white coating, known as farina. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or arid environments.

Due to its slow growth rate, it is often used as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
