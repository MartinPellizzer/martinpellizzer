Rhododendron yakuinsulare

Rhododendron yakuinsulare, commonly known as the Yaku Island rhododendron, is a species native to Japan, specifically found on the island of Yaku.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate and leathery, providing a dense canopy that supports local biodiversity.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
