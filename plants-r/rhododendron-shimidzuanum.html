Rhododendron shimidzuanum

Rhododendron shimidzuanum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is part of the Ericaceae family, which includes other popular ornamental plants like heathers and blueberries.

Rhododendron shimidzuanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
