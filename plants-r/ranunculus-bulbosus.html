Ranunculus bulbosus

Ranunculus bulbosus, commonly known as the bulbous buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a short, rhizomatous root system and produces basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to seven sepals that resemble petals. It typically blooms in early spring and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Ranunculus bulbosus is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.
