Rubus floribundus f. pauciflorus

Rubus floribundus f. pauciflorus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered bramble, is a botanical variant of the species Rubus floribundus.

It is characterized by its reduced flowering compared to the typical form, with fewer flowers per inflorescence. The plant is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, known for its thorny stems and compound leaves. It typically grows in temperate regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical interest lies in its morphological variation and potential ecological roles in native habitats.
