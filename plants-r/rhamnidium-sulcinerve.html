Rhamnidium sulcinerve

Rhamnidium sulcinerve, commonly known as the slender-veined rhamnus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with prominent, parallel venation patterns. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a dark red or black color.

Rhamnidium sulcinerve is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
