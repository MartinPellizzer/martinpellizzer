Racinaea sinuosa

Racinaea sinuosa, commonly known as the sinuous racinaea, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy, glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its unique leaf structure and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialty collections.

Racinaea sinuosa is primarily grown for its aesthetic value and is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
