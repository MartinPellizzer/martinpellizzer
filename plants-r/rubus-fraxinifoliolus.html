Rubus fraxinifoliolus

Rubus fraxinifoliolus, commonly known as the blackberry vine, is a shrub-like plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that resemble those of the ash tree. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that develop into dark purple to black berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
