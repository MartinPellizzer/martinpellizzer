Ruta latifolia

Ruta latifolia, commonly known as greater rue, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry environments.

Due to its potent compounds, it is used in traditional medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
