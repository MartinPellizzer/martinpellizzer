Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp. paucistamineus

Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp.

paucistamineus, commonly known as the few-stamened buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens.

Native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan, this species thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a short, hairy stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
