Rosa woodsii var. fendleri

Rosa woodsii var. fendleri, commonly known as the Fendler rose, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are compound, with oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces small, red-orange fruits known as hips. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
