Rhododendron × fragrantissimum

Rhododendron × fragrantissimum, commonly known as the fragrant rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, adding both visual and olfactory appeal to the landscape.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of temperate climates.
