Rosa × dulcissima

Rosa × dulcissima, commonly known as the Double White Rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its profuse blooms of double white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This rose is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
