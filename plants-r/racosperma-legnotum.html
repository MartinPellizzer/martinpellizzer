Racosperma legnotum

Racosperma legnotum, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive shape, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential for use in horticulture.
