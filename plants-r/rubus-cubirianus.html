Rubus cubirianus

Rubus cubirianus, commonly known as the Cuban raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Cuba.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many shrubs and bushes known for their edible berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or yellow in color.

While not widely cultivated, it plays a role in local ecosystems and may have potential for use in horticulture or conservation efforts.
