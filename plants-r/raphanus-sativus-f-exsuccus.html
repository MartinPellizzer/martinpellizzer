Raphanus sativus f. exsuccus

Raphanus sativus f. exsuccus, commonly known as the Japanese radish or daikon, is a cultivar of the wild radish species.

It is characterized by its long, cylindrical, white or pale yellow root, which can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is native to East Asia. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, peppery flavor similar to other radishes.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its root, which is used in various culinary applications across Asian cuisines.
