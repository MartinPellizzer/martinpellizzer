Rosa sepium f. denudata

Rosa sepium f. denudata, commonly known as the naked-stem rose, is a botanical variant of the rose species Rosa sepium.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairless stems, which distinguish it from other forms of the species. The plant typically features large, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil in temperate climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
