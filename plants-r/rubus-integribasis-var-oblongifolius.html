Rubus integribasis var. oblongifolius

Rubus integribasis var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible blackberries in the summer. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with thorny stems and a dense, sprawling form.

The botanical features of this plant make it well adapted to a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
