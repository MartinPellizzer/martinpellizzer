Rosa corymbifera var. dipilata

Rosa corymbifera var. dipilata, commonly known as the Chinese climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various soil conditions.
