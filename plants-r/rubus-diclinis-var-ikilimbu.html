Rubus diclinis var. ikilimbu

Rubus diclinis var. ikilimbu, commonly known as the Ikilimbu blackberry, is a shrub native to the highland regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool climate for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
