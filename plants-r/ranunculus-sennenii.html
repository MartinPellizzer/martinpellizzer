Ranunculus sennenii

Ranunculus sennenii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with deeply notched petals, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest understories. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is often found in areas with cool, temperate climates.

Ranunculus sennenii is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often delicate flowers.
