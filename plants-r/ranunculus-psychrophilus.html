Ranunculus psychrophilus

Ranunculus psychrophilus, commonly known as the polar buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, delicate yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant thrives in cold, nutrient-poor environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically palmately divided, and it produces a capsule fruit containing numerous seeds.

Ranunculus psychrophilus is adapted to survive in low temperatures and has a short growing season.
