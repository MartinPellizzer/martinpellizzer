Rhododendron rhodosalpinx

Rhododendron rhodosalpinx, commonly known as the Salpinx rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China and the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, humid climates with well-drained soils. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy coating. Its flowers are showy, with deep pink to purple petals that bloom in late spring.

Rhododendron rhodosalpinx is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
