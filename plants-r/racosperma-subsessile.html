Racosperma subsessile

Racosperma subsessile, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clustered inflorescences, followed by linear pods that contain several seeds.

Racosperma subsessile is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
