Rhododendron ignicolor

Rhododendron ignicolor, commonly known as the flame-colored rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from bright red to orange, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and elliptical in shape, providing a dense canopy that supports various forms of wildlife.

Rhododendron ignicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
