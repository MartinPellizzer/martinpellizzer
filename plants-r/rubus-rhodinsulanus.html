Rubus rhodinsulanus

Rubus rhodinsulanus, commonly known as the Philippine raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

The botanical features of Rubus rhodinsulanus make it an important component of the flora in its native habitat.
