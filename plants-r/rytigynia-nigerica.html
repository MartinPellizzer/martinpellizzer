Rytigynia nigerica

Rytigynia nigerica, commonly known as black rytigynia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its striking dark purple to black flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, simple leaves that are often glandular. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Rytigynia nigerica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
