Rosa stylosa var. pseudorusticana

Rosa stylosa var. pseudorusticana, commonly known as the rust-colored rose, is a shrub-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
