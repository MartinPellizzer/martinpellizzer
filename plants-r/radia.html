Radia

Radia, commonly known as the common radia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, clustered white or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Radia is often used in herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and antispasmodic properties.

Its seeds are also used as a spice in some traditional cuisines.
