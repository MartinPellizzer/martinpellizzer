Ranunculus laestadii

Ranunculus laestadii, commonly known as the Laestadi's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
