Rubus scaber var. longistylus

Rubus scaber var. longistylus, commonly known as the rough bramble or long-styled raspberry, is a variant of the common bramble found in temperate regions.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five to seven petals and prominent stamens. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are edible and often used in jams and pies.

This plant thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, contributing to local biodiversity.
