Rubus salaevensis

Rubus salaevensis, commonly known as the Siberian blackberry, is a shrub native to the cold climates of Eastern Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, temperate environments.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
