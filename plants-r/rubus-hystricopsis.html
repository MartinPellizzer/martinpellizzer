Rubus hystricopsis

Rubus hystricopsis, commonly known as the hollyberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Korea. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red or black berries. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

Rubus hystricopsis is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
