Rosa × dumetorum f. ciliata

Rosa × dumetorum f. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a compact, shrubby plant characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves.

The leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. This plant is well adapted to rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in challenging growing conditions.
