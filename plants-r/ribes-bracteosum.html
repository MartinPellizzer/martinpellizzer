Ribes bracteosum

Ribes bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteate gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its botanical features include hairy stems, glandular leaves, and a preference for moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

Ribes bracteosum is often found in montane regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
