Rubus heptaphyllus

Rubus heptaphyllus, commonly known as the seven-leaf raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its trifoliate leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three leaflets and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
