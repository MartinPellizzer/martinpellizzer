Rosa coriifolia var. vanheurckiana

Rosa coriifolia var. vanheurckiana, commonly known as the Vanheurck's shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy underside and produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant has a bushy growth habit and is characterized by its thornless stems, making it suitable for ornamental use. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring moderate watering.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
