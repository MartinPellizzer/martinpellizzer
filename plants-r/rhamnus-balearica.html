Rhamnus balearica

Rhamnus balearica, commonly known as the Balearic buckthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. Rhamnus balearica is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of Mediterranean ecosystems.
