Rhododendron sinogrande var. boreale

Rhododendron sinogrande var. boreale, commonly known as the northern grand rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of northern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. It prefers cool, moist climates and well-drained acidic soils, often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
