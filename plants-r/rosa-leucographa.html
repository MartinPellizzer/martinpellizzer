Rosa leucographa

Rosa leucographa, commonly known as the white-veined rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with white veins against a dark green background. Its flowers are pink to red, with five petals and a prominent central style.

Rosa leucographa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
