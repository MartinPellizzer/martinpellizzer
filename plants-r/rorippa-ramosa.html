Rorippa ramosa

Rorippa ramosa, commonly known as water pepper, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is typically found in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. The plant has alternate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a cylindrical silique containing several seeds.

Rorippa ramosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
