Rubus schoolcraftianus

Rubus schoolcraftianus, commonly known as Schoolcraft's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by the edible berries in late summer. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in shaded areas along stream banks.

The berries are valued for their flavor and are used in culinary applications.
