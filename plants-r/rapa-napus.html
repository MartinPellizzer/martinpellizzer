Rapa napus

Rapa napus, commonly known as Chinese cabbage or napa cabbage, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, pale green stalks and broad, smooth leaves that form a compact head. The plant is native to East Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible leaves and stems. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Rapa napus is a versatile vegetable used in various cuisines around the world.
