Rubus intermixtus

Rubus intermixtus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus intermixtus is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
