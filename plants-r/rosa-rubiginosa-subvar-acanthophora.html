Rosa rubiginosa subvar. acanthophora

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. acanthophora, commonly known as the bristly rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to reddish flowers. The plant produces oval, red-veined leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that contain seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
