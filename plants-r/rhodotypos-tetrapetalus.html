Rhodotypos tetrapetalus

Rhodotypos tetrapetalus, commonly known as the four-petaled rhodotypos, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the branches. Its flowers consist of four petals, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.

Rhodotypos tetrapetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
