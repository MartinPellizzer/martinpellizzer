Ribes divaricatum var. glabriflorum

Ribes divaricatum var. glabriflorum, commonly known as the smoothflower gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants and berries. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces edible, tart berries that are often used in jams and preserves.

The variety glabriflorum is distinguished by its smooth, hairless flowers, setting it apart from other Ribes species.
