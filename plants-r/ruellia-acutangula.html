Ruellia acutangula

Ruellia acutangula, commonly known as the acute-leaved ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, with a slightly pointed tip.

Ruellia acutangula is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
