Rosa mollissima f. spinescens

Rosa mollissima f. spinescens, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollissima species.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant is native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden settings.
