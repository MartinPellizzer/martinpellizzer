Rapuntium laciniatum

Rapuntium laciniatum, commonly known as the Italian cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is an herbaceous plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils. It produces small, edible seeds that are used in various culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in garden environments.
