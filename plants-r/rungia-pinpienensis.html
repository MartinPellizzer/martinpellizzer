Rungia pinpienensis

Rungia pinpienensis, commonly known as the Burmese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to purple.

Rungia pinpienensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
