Rubus maassii microgene bakeri

Rubus maassii microgene bakeri, commonly known as the Maasai raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania, and thrives in woodland and shrubland habitats. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, resembling raspberries in appearance. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
