Rhus lindeniana

Rhus lindeniana, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some wildlife.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
