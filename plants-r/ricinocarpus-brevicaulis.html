Ricinocarpus brevicaulis

Ricinocarpus brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed ricin palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features a short, stout stem with a crown of pinnate leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in panicles, and it produces edible fruits that contain a single, large seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
