Rosa × cavinianensis

Rosa × cavinianensis, commonly known as the Cavinian rose, is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa gallica and Rosa canina.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and abundant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, oval leaves with serrated edges and has a strong, pleasant scent. It is well-suited for garden cultivation and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Rosa × cavinianensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
