Rorippa aspera subsp. hayanica

Rorippa aspera subsp.

hayanica, commonly known as the Japanese watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The plant produces small white or pale yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

Its reproductive strategy involves both self-pollination and cross-pollination, contributing to its adaptability in various aquatic habitats.
