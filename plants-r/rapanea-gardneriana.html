Rapanea gardneriana

Rapanea gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's inkberry, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark purple drupe, containing a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
