Rubus serpens var. longepedunculatus

Rubus serpens var. longepedunculatus, commonly known as the long peduncled blackberry.

This variant is a thorny shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark, edible berries. It produces small, black fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and bears white flowers in the spring.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
