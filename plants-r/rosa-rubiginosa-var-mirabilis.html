Rosa rubiginosa var. mirabilis

Rosa rubiginosa var. mirabilis, commonly known as the Mirabilis Rosa or the Red Bush Rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as rosehips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
