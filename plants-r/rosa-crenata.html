Rosa crenata

Rosa crenata, commonly known as the Japanese rose or mountain rose, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features fragrant, single or semi-double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. Rosa crenata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
