Rosa sempervirens var. obtusata

Rosa sempervirens var. obtusata, commonly known as the evergreen rose or the shrub rose, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental gardens.

Its hardy nature and ability to withstand coastal conditions further enhance its botanical appeal.
