Ranunculus godleyanus

Ranunculus godleyanus, commonly known as the Godley's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy, with a short stem that bears a single, erect flower. Its flowers have five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, typical of the genus Ranunculus.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
