Ranunculus raimondii

Ranunculus raimondii, commonly known as the desert buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with distinctive yellow flowers and palmate leaves. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to desert conditions, showcasing its resilience and ecological significance.
