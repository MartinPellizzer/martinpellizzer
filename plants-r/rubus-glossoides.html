Rubus glossoides

Rubus glossoides, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy berries and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets, which are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
