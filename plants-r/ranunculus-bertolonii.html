Ranunculus bertolonii

Ranunculus bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
