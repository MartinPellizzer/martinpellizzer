Rhamnus infectoria

Rhamnus infectoria, commonly known as the poison buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its bark and leaves contain compounds such as anthraquinones, which have been studied for their medicinal properties.

However, due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
