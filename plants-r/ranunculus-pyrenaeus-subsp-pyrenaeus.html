Ranunculus pyrenaeus subsp. pyrenaeus

Ranunculus pyrenaeus subsp.

pyrenaeus, commonly known as the Pyrenean buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 7 sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats.

The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and it is adapted to thrive in cool, moist environments. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

This subspecies is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its restricted distribution and ecological significance.
