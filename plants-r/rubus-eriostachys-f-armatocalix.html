Rubus eriostachys f. armatocalix

Rubus eriostachys f. armatocalix, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed blackberry.

This plant is a variant of the blackberry species, characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves. It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. The foliage is broad-ovate with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, dark purple berries.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and edible fruits.
