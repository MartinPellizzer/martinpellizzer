Rubus affinis var. montanus

Rubus affinis var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local culinary traditions.
