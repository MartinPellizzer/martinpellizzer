Rehmannia glutinosa var. japonica

Rehmannia glutinosa var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese rehmannia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are rich in mucilage and used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth texture and dark green color.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late summer.
