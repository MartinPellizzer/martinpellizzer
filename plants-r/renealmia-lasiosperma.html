Renealmia lasiosperma

Renealmia lasiosperma, commonly known as the silky-sperma grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
