Rubus × thelybatophyllus

Rubus × thelybatophyllus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus fruticosus (bramble) and Rubus ideaus (red raspberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Rubus species.

This hybrid is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
