Rubus gratus proles sciaphilus

Rubus gratus proles sciaphilus, commonly known as the shadow-loving raspberry, is a rare shrub that thrives in shaded environments.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, thorned stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It is adapted to moist, woodland soils and is often found in temperate regions with high humidity.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species in many areas.
