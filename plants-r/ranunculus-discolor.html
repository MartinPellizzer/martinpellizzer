Ranunculus discolor

Ranunculus discolor, commonly known as the variable buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow to orange flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it produces small, green, oval-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy underside, and it has a short, stout rootstock.

Ranunculus discolor is known for its variability in flower color and leaf structure, making it a distinctive species within the Ranunculaceae family.
