Rhamnus californica var. obtusissima

Rhamnus californica var. obtusissima, commonly known as the softleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves that are typically rounded at the ends. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by black, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
