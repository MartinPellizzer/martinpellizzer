Ranunculus dichotomiflorus

Ranunculus dichotomiflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy surface that helps retain moisture.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in temperate regions.
