Rubus mougeotii

Rubus mougeotii, commonly known as the Mougeot's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
