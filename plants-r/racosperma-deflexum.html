Racosperma deflexum

Racosperma deflexum, commonly known as the drooping acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark, and its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Racosperma deflexum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native fauna.
