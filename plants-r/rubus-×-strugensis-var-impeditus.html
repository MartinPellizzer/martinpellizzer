Rubus × strugensis var. impeditus

Rubus × strugensis var. impeditus, commonly known as the densefruit blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in woodland and thicket habitats. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a firm, tart flavor. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it is often found growing in partial shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in local culinary traditions.
