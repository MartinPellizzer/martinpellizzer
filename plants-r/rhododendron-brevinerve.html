Rhododendron brevinerve

Rhododendron brevinerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, humid environments. Its leaves are elliptical with prominent, parallel veins, and the flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Rhododendron brevinerve is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
