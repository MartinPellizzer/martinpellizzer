Ranunculus constantinopolitanus

Ranunculus constantinopolitanus, commonly known as the Constantinople buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, green stem. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure.

It blooms in spring and is often considered a rare and ornamental species.
