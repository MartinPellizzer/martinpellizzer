Rosa sepium f. briacensis

Rosa sepium f. briacensis, commonly known as the Briacensis rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa sepium species.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a striking variegation of green and white. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a moderate height, with dense, thorny stems. Its flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
