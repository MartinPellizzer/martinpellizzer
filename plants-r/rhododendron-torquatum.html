Rhododendron torquatum

Rhododendron torquatum, commonly known as the twisted rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

Rhododendron torquatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
