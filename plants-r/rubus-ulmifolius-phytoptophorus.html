Rubus ulmifolius phytoptophorus

Rubus ulmifolius phytoptophorus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically lobed or serrated. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as berries, which are usually dark purple or black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
