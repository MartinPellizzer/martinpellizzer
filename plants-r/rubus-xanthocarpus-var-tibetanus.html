Rubus xanthocarpus var. tibetanus

Rubus xanthocarpus var. tibetanus, commonly known as the Tibetan blackberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in cool, arid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
