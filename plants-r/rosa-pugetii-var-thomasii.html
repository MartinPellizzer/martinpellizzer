Rosa pugetii var. thomasii

Rosa pugetii var. thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's rose, is a compact shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features dense, thornless stems and is characterized by its deep pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white down. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental shrub.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and adaptability to coastal climates.
