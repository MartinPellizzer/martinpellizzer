Robinia rusbyi

Robinia rusbyi, commonly known as the Rusby's false acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, adapting well to various environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
