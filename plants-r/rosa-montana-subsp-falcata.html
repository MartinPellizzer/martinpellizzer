Rosa montana subsp. falcata

Rosa montana subsp.

falcata, commonly known as the Alpine rose or mountain rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe, including parts of the Alps and Carpathian Mountains.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dark green, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
