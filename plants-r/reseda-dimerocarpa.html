Reseda dimerocarpa

Reseda dimerocarpa, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Resedaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant has small, linear leaves and produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Reseda dimerocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-water environments.
