Rubus pinnatus var. afrotropicus

Rubus pinnatus var. afrotropicus, commonly known as the African bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny environments.
