Ricinus communis f. rutilans

Ricinus communis f. rutilans, commonly known as the flame castor oil plant, is a striking variant of the castor oil plant.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fiery red bracts that surround the inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are large, palmately lobed, and have a rough texture.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular ornamental species in gardens and landscapes.
