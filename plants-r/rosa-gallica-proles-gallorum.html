Rosa gallica proles gallorum

Rosa gallica proles gallorum, commonly known as the Gallic Rose or the Gall Rose, is a historic variety of rose native to Europe.

This plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. It is notable for its historical use in horticulture and its association with the production of rose gall, a growth formed by the insect *Cynips rosae*. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value and historical significance make it a prized specimen among rose enthusiasts.
