Rhus mendoncae

Rhus mendoncae, commonly known as the Mendocino buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to California.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a dark, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Rhus mendoncae is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
