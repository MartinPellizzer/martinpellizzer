Rosa canina var. tenuifolia

Rosa canina var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf dog rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, thornless stems and small, pinnately compound leaves with finely toothed margins. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that mature in autumn, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
