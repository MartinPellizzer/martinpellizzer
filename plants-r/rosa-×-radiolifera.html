Rosa × radiolifera

Rosa × radiolifera, commonly known as the radiate rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically feature a central yellow disc surrounded by pink or red petals. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its ornamental blooms.

Rosa × radiolifera is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
