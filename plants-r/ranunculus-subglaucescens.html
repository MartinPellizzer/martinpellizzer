Ranunculus subglaucescens

Ranunculus subglaucescens, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of yellow stamens. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
