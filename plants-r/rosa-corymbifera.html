Rosa corymbifera

Rosa corymbifera, commonly known as the clustered rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and the Russian Federation. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa corymbifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
