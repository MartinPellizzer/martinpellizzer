Rosa oxyacantha

Rosa oxyacantha, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits that contain several seeds. Rosa oxyacantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.
