Ranunculus julinii

Ranunculus julinii, commonly known as the Julian ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are palmately arranged.

Ranunculus julinii prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
