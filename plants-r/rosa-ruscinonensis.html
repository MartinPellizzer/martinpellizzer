Rosa ruscinonensis

Rosa ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their strong, sweet scent.

Rosa ruscinonensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
