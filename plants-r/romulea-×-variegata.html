Romulea × variegata

Romulea × variegata, commonly known as the variegated glory-of-the-snow, is a hybrid plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with white and green patterns. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
