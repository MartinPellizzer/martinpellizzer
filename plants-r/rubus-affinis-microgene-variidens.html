Rubus affinis microgene variidens

Rubus affinis microgene variidens, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
