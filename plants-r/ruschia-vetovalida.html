Ruschia vetovalida

Ruschia vetovalida, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its spiky, red bracts that resemble poker chips, giving it its common name. The plant has fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in summer, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ruschia vetovalida requires well-draining soil and full sun to flourish.
