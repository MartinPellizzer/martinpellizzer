Ranunculus popovii

Ranunculus popovii, commonly known as the Popov's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features a basal rosette of finely divided, pinnately lobed leaves and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its often toxic compounds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
