Ryssopterys tiliifolia

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The reproductive cycle of Ryssopterys tiliifolia relies on spore dispersal, a common method among ferns for propagation.
