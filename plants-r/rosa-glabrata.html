Rosa glabrata

Rosa glabrata, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its simple, smooth-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated edge, while its fruit is a red, hairy hip that develops after flowering.

Rosa glabrata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its beauty and hardy nature.
