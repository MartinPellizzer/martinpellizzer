Rosa villosa var. vogesiaca

Rosa villosa var. vogesiaca, commonly known as the Voges rose, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are often followed by orange to red hips, which are edible and used in traditional medicine.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardiness in temperate climates.
