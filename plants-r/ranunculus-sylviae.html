Ranunculus sylviae

Ranunculus sylviae, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers with five to eight petals, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread slowly and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Ranunculus sylviae is known for its early blooming habit and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
