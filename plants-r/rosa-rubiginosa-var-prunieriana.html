Rosa rubiginosa var. prunieriana

Rosa rubiginosa var. prunieriana, commonly known as the Prunier's rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and as a source of cut flowers.
