Rosa gallica var. remensis

Rosa gallica var. remensis, commonly known as the French pink, is a variety of the Gallica rose known for its vibrant pink flowers.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The flowers are borne in clusters and have a strong, sweet fragrance, making it a popular choice for gardens. It is native to Europe and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This plant is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and as a source of cut flowers.
