Ranunculus aloisii-ceballi

Ranunculus aloisii-ceballi, commonly known as the Balkan buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant has palmately divided leaves and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
