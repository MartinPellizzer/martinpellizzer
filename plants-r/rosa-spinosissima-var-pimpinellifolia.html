Rosa spinosissima var. pimpinellifolia

Rosa spinosissima var. pimpinellifolia, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a thorny shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, arching growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C and are often used in herbal remedies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.
