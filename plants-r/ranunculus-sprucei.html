Ranunculus sprucei

Ranunculus sprucei, commonly known as the Spruce's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in many areas.
