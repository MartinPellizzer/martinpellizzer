Rhynchospora plumosa var. semiplumosa

Rhynchospora plumosa var. semiplumosa, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, hairy leaves that are often pale green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Its root system helps stabilize soil in wetland environments, contributing to ecological balance.

This variant differs from the typical form by having a more pronounced hairy texture on its foliage.
