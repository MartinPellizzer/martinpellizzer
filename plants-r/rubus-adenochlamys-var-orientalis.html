Rubus adenochlamys var. orientalis

Rubus adenochlamys var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental bramble, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
