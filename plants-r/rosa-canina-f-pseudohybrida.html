Rosa canina f. pseudohybrida

Rosa canina f. pseudohybrida, commonly known as the dog rose hybrid, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose, Rosa canina.

It exhibits a range of floral characteristics, including double or semi-double blooms with a rich pink to red hue. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
