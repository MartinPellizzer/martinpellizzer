Rapuntium arabideum

Rapuntium arabideum, commonly known as the Arabian cress, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

Rapuntium arabideum is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in poor, well-drained conditions.
