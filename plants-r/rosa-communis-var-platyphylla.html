Rosa communis var. platyphylla

Rosa communis var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf rose, is a cultivar characterized by its broad, flat leaves and fragrant pink flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to China, often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant produces rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for hedging or container gardening.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators and adding visual interest to the landscape.
