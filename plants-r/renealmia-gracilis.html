Renealmia gracilis

Renealmia gracilis, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it forms dense colonies. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers.

Renealmia gracilis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic wildlife.
