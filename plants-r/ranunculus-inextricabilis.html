Ranunculus inextricabilis

Ranunculus inextricabilis, commonly known as the tangled buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, segmented stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, giving them a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Ranunculus inextricabilis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
