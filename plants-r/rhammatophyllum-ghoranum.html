Rhammatophyllum ghoranum

Rhammatophyllum ghoranum, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, appearing in dense clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Rhammatophyllum ghoranum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
