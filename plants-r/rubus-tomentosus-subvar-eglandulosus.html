Rubus tomentosus subvar. eglandulosus

Rubus tomentosus subvar. eglandulosus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
