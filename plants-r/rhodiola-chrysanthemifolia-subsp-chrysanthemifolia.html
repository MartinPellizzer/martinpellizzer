Rhodiola chrysanthemifolia subsp. chrysanthemifolia

Rhodiola chrysanthemifolia subsp.

chrysanthemifolia, commonly known as the goldenflower rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with fleshy, somewhat succulent stems and leaves that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Rhodiola chrysanthemifolia subsp.

chrysanthemifolia is known for its potential adaptogenic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
