Rubus ampliflorus

Rubus ampliflorus, commonly known as the broadflower raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant's leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets that are hairy and serrated. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are usually red when mature and are edible.

Rubus ampliflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
