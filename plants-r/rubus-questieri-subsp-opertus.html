Rubus questieri subsp. opertus

Rubus questieri subsp.

opertus, commonly known as the Alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruits are small, dark berries that ripen in late summer. This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for various wildlife species.
