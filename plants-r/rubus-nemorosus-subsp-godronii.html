Rubus nemorosus subsp. godronii

Rubus nemorosus subsp.

godronii, commonly known as the Godron's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and streamside habitats.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
