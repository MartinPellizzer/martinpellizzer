Rosa spinosissima

Rosa spinosissima, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces rose hips that contain seeds.

Rosa spinosissima is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery.
