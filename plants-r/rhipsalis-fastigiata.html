Rhipsalis fastigiata

Rhipsalis fastigiata, commonly known as the upright mistletoe cactus, is a species of cactus native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season, followed by small, red berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or in rocky areas.

Due to its unique appearance and growth pattern, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
