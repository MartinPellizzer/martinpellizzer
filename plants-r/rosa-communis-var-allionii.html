Rosa communis var. allionii

Rosa communis var. allionii, commonly known as the Allionii rose, is a variety of the common rose that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves composed of five to seven elliptical leaflets. The flowers are typically pink to red, with a fragrant aroma, and are borne on long pedicels. This variety is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
