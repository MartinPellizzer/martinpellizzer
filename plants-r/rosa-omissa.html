Rosa omissa

Rosa omissa, commonly known as the forgotten rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and thornless stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are pinnate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Rosa omissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
