Romulea × merinoi

Romulea × merinoi, commonly known as the Merino bulbous orchid, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Romulea rosea and Romulea bulbosa.

It is a bulbous perennial herb native to South Africa, characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, spurred flowers. The plant produces striking, often pink or purple, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its unique floral display and hardy nature.
