Rosa communis var. jactata

Rosa communis var. jactata, commonly known as the spreading bush rose, is a vigorous, deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its arching, thorny stems that can spread widely, often forming dense thickets. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

The species is also valued for its use in hedging and as a source of rose hips.
