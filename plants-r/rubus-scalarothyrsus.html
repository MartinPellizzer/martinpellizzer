Rubus scalarothyrsus

Rubus scalarothyrsus, commonly known as the mountain blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
