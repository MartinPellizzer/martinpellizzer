Rubus vallisparsus var. uzucanus

Rubus vallisparsus var. uzucanus, commonly known as the Uzun raspberry, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its culinary uses.
