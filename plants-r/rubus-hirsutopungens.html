Rubus hirsutopungens

Rubus hirsutopungens, commonly known as the hairy rough blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature, containing numerous small drupes. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

The botanical features of Rubus hirsutopungens make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
