Rhododendron thomsonii var. pallidum

Rhododendron thomsonii var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, deciduous leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers in spring, which are typically tubular in shape. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
