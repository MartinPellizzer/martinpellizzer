Rosa systyla f. anomala

Rosa systyla f. anomala, commonly known as the anomalous shrub rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa systyla.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often blooming in late spring. Its leaves are glossy and deeply lobed, providing a lush backdrop to its floral display.

This variety is well-suited for garden cultivation, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.
