Rosa marretii

Rosa marretii, commonly known as the Marret's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in thorns.

Rosa marretii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
