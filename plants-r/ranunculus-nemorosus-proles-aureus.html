Ranunculus nemorosus proles aureus

Ranunculus nemorosus proles aureus, commonly known as the golden wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem that grows from a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats.

It is considered an indicator species for ancient woodlands due to its specific ecological requirements.
