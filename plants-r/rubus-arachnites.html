Rubus arachnites

Rubus arachnites, commonly known as the spiderberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically growing in woodland and shrubland habitats. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits that develop from its flowers. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with three leaflets that have serrated edges.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
