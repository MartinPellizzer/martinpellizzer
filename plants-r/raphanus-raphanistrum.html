Raphanus raphanistrum

Raphanus raphanistrum, commonly known as wild radish, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright stems and basal rosette of leaves, which are typically lobed and hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are pollinated by insects. Its fruits are dry pods containing several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Raphanus raphanistrum is a hardy species that can thrive in a variety of climates and soil types.
