Ratibida columnifera var. breviradiata

Ratibida columnifera var. breviradiata, commonly known as the short-rayed torchflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy yellow to orange composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flower heads consist of both ray and disk flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
