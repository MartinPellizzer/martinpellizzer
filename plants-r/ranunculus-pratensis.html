Ranunculus pratensis

Ranunculus pratensis, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, damp woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained to moist soils. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are bright yellow, with five to seven petals and numerous stamens, creating a striking, showy appearance.

Ranunculus pratensis is known for its ability to thrive in temperate climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
