Rhododendron surasianum

Rhododendron surasianum, commonly known as the Sikkim rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is well adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the mountainous flora in its native habitat.
