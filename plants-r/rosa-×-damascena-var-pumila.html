Rosa × damascena var. pumila

Rosa × damascena var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Damascus rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the eastern Mediterranean.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and a dense, bushy growth habit. It is well-suited for container gardening and small garden spaces due to its size.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and aromatic purposes.
