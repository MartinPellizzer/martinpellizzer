Rhododendron excellens

Rhododendron excellens, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to China and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Rhododendron excellens is also noted for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and its resistance to certain pests and diseases.
