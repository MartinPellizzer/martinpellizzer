Ranunculus subapetalus

Ranunculus subapetalus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven sepals that are often mistaken for petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, sometimes hairy texture.

It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often striking floral structures.
