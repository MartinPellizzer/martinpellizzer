Rosa rugulosa

Rosa rugulosa, commonly known as the rough rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a yellow center. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture, and the stems are covered in short, stiff hairs.

Rosa rugulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
