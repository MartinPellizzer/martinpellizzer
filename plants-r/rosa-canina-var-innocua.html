Rosa canina var. innocua

Rosa canina var. innocua, commonly known as the dog rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that contain numerous seeds and are used in herbal medicine. Rosa canina var. innocua is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
