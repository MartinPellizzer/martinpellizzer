Rubus sempernitens

Rubus sempernitens, commonly known as the everblooming blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soils.

Its berries are edible but often have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and pies.
