Rosa touranginiana

Rosa touranginiana, commonly known as the Touranginiana rose, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a strong, sweet fragrance. It produces hips, which are the fruit of the plant, containing seeds.

Rosa touranginiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
