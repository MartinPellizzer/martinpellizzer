Relhania microphylla

Relhania microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers. The plant relies on a symbiotic relationship with its host trees, such as Eucalyptus and Acacia, for nutrients and water. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that feed on the fleshy, red berries.

Relhania microphylla plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
