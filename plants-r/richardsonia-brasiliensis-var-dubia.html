Richardsonia brasiliensis var. dubia

Richardsonia brasiliensis var. dubia, commonly known as the Brazilian giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals and yellow stamens, blooming at night and attracting specific pollinators. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and lakes.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for wetland ecosystems and its potential for ecological research.
