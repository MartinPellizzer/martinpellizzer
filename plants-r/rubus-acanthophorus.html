Rubus acanthophorus

Rubus acanthophorus, commonly known as the thorned blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rubus genus in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its dense growth habit, thorny stems, and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
