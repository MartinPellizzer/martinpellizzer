Rubus moluccanus var. henryi

Rubus moluccanus var. henryi, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
