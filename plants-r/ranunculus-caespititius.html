Ranunculus caespititius

Ranunculus caespititius, commonly known as the tufted buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of bright yellow, spurred flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, grassy habitats. The plant has a short, stout stem that supports its showy blooms, which typically appear in spring.

Its leaves are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
