Rabdosia eriocalyx

Rabdosia eriocalyx, commonly known as the hairy-leafed rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Rabdosia eriocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
