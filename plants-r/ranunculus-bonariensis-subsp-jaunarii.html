Ranunculus bonariensis subsp. jaunarii

Ranunculus bonariensis subsp.

jaunarii, commonly known as the Argentine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in early spring.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
