Racosperma rhetinocarpum

Racosperma rhetinocarpum, commonly known as the red-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features slender, reddish stems and bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, globular clusters, and it produces woody pods that contain edible seeds.

The plant is adapted to dry environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision for native wildlife.
