Rubus radula var. suborbicularis

Rubus radula var. suborbicularis, commonly known as the suborbicular raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry species characterized by its unique leaf shape.

The leaves are broad and slightly rounded, with a distinctive suborbicular outline that sets it apart from other Rubus species. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing numerous druplets. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and disturbed sites, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
