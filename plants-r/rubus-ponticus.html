Rubus ponticus

Rubus ponticus, commonly known as the Caucasian raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in local cuisines.
