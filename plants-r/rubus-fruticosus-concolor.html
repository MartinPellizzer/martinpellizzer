Rubus fruticosus concolor

Rubus fruticosus concolor, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. These berries are edible and have a sweet-tart flavor, making them popular in culinary uses.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
