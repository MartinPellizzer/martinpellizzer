Rubus apetalus var. roseus

Rubus apetalus var. roseus, commonly known as the rosehip rose, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are rich in vitamin C and often used in herbal remedies. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its medicinal properties.
