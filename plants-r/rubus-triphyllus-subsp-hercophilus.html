Rubus triphyllus subsp. hercophilus

Rubus triphyllus subsp.

hercophilus, commonly known as the Hercules blackberry, is a subshrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three leaflets.

The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
