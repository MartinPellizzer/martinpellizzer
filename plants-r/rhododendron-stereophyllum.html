Rhododendron stereophyllum

Rhododendron stereophyllum, commonly known as the mountain rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from pink to purple.

Rhododendron stereophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
