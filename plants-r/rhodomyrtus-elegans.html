Rhodomyrtus elegans

Rhodomyrtus elegans, commonly known as the elegant rose myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Rhodomyrtus elegans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
