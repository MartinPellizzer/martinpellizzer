Ranunculus septentrionalis

Ranunculus septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern buttercup, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

The species is often found in boreal and subarctic environments, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
