Rhexia violacea

Rhexia violacea, commonly known as purpleleaf stellaria, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a purplish hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Rhexia violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
