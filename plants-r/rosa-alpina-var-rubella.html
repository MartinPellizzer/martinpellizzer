Rosa alpina var. rubella

Rosa alpina var. rubella, commonly known as the alpine pink rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a variant of Rosa alpina, characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and it produces fragrant, semi-double blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
