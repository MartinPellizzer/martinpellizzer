Rosa pendulina f. adjecta

Rosa pendulina f. adjecta, commonly known as the weeping pink, is a compact, shrub-like rose with a unique cascading growth habit.

Its botanical features include small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, depending on the variety. The plant has narrow, willow-like leaves that contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to add a soft, flowing look to landscapes.
