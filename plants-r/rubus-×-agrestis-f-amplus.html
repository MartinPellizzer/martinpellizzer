Rubus × agrestis f. amplus

Rubus × agrestis f. amplus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its large, broad leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
