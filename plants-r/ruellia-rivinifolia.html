Ruellia rivinifolia

Ruellia rivinifolia, commonly known as the river ruellia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming during the warmer months. Ruellia rivinifolia prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid environments contribute to its popularity among horticulturists.
