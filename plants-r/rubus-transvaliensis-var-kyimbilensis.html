Rubus transvaliensis var. kyimbilensis

Rubus transvaliensis var. kyimbilensis, commonly known as the Kyimbilensis blackberry, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
