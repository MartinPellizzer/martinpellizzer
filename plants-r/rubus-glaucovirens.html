Rubus glaucovirens

Rubus glaucovirens, commonly known as the silverleaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-blue stems and leaves that often have a silvery appearance due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
