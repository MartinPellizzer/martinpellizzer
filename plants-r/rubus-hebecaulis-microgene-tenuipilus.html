Rubus hebecaulis microgene tenuipilus

Rubus hebecaulis microgene tenuipilus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed blackberry.

This plant is a shrub native to South America, characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, dark berries. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its prickly foliage and clusters of edible fruits. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
