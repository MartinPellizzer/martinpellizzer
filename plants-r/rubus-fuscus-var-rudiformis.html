Rubus fuscus var. rudiformis

Rubus fuscus var. rudiformis, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodlands and hedgerows. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible but often has a tart flavor.
