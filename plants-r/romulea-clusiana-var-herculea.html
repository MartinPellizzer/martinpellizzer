Romulea clusiana var. herculea

Romulea clusiana var. herculea, commonly known as the Hercules' glory, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features striking, waxy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from July to September. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a distinctive, compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
