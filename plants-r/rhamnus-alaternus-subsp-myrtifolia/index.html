Rhamnus Alaternus Subsp. Myrtifolia

Rhamnus alaternus subsp. myrtifolia is a lesser-known member of the Rhamnaceae family that thrives in specific Mediterranean climates. This subspecies is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark berries that attract various wildlife. While it shares some traits with its parent species, it exhibits unique morphological features that set it apart. Due to its limited distribution, it is often overlooked in botanical studies and conservation efforts. Understanding this plant is crucial for preserving biodiversity in its native habitats.
