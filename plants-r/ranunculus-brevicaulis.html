Ranunculus brevicaulis

Ranunculus brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves, and its stems are short and often sprawling. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
