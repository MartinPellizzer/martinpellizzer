Rudbeckia aristata

Rudbeckia aristata, commonly known as the prairie coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a distinctive brown, cone-shaped center. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are hairy beneath. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Rudbeckia aristata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
