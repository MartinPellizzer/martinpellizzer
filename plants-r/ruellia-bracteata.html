Ruellia bracteata

Ruellia bracteata, commonly known as the silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting various pollinators. Ruellia bracteata is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
