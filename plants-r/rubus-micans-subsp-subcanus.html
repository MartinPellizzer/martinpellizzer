Rubus micans subsp. subcanus

Rubus micans subsp.

subcanus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its finely hairy, silvery-tomentose leaves and stems, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique morphological traits that set it apart from other sub-species.
