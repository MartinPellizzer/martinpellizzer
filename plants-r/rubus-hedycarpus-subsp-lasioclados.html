Rubus hedycarpus subsp. lasioclados

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

lasioclados, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
