Rubus ulmifolius var. zahnii

Rubus ulmifolius var. zahnii, commonly known as the Zahn's bramble, is a variant of the common bramble, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in woodland edges and disturbed habitats. The leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters, followed by red, raspberry-like fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
