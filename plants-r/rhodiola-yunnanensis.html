Rhodiola yunnanensis

Rhodiola yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates. It is known for its high concentration of secondary metabolites, including rosavins and salidrosic acid, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Rhodiola yunnanensis is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported adaptogenic and antioxidant benefits.
