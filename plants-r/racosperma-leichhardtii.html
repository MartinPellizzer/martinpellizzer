Racosperma leichhardtii

Racosperma leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt's acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in dry regions.
