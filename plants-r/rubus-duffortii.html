Rubus duffortii

Rubus duffortii, commonly known as the Duffort's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. These berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, which includes many other bramble species.
