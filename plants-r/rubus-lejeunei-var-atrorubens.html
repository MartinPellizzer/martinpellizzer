Rubus lejeunei var. atrorubens

Rubus lejeunei var. atrorubens, commonly known as the black-red raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red to black in color, depending on ripeness. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
