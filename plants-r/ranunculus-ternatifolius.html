Ranunculus ternatifolius

Ranunculus ternatifolius, commonly known as the three-leaved crowfoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a short stem with a single flower at the top, and its leaves are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in temperate regions and is a popular choice for garden cultivation due to its ornamental value.
