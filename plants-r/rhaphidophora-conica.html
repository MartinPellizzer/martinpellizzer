Rhaphidophora conica

Rhaphidophora conica, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves, which are glossy and often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Rhaphidophora conica is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
