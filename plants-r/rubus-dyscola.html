Rubus dyscola

Rubus dyscola, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically tart and not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
