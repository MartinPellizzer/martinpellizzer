Rosa glaucimultiflora

Rosa glaucimultiflora, commonly known as the blueleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-blue foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Rosa glaucimultiflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
