Rhododendron viburnifolium

Rhododendron viburnifolium, commonly known as the redleaf azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are elliptical, leathery, and turn vibrant red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded conditions, enhancing its versatility in different garden settings.
