Rubus macrostachys var. graniticola

Rubus macrostachys var. graniticola, commonly known as the granite raspberry, is a shrub native to the rocky habitats of eastern Asia.

It produces small, red or yellow berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
