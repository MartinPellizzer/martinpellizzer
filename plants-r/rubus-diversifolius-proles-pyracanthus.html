Rubus diversifolius proles pyracanthus

Rubus diversifolius proles pyracanthus, commonly known as the multifoliate blackberry.

This plant is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with varying leaflet shapes. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
