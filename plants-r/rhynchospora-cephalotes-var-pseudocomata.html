Rhynchospora cephalotes var. pseudocomata

Rhynchospora cephalotes var. pseudocomata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beakspur sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, preferring nutrient-poor soils.

This variant is distinguished by its finely textured foliage and subtle floral structure, making it a notable species within its ecological niche.
