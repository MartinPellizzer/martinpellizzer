Rubus fruticosus var. caninus

Rubus fruticosus var. caninus, commonly known as the dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces red or yellow berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The dog rose is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
