Rosa × dumetorum f. irregularis

Rosa × dumetorum f. irregularis, commonly known as the irregular form of the shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its variable growth habit, often appearing as a low-growing shrub with dense, thorny stems. The leaves are typically pinnately compound, with 5 to 9 leaflets that are elliptical to ovate in shape. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This form is notable for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
