Rosa × pseudomontana

Rosa × pseudomontana, commonly known as the rugosa rose, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, dark green foliage, and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a strong, woody structure.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and as a natural barrier due to its dense growth habit.
