Rubus pubescens subsp. stenopetalus

Rubus pubescens subsp.

stenopetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petaled bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, often clustered leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five narrow petals, and appear in late spring to early summer. The fruit, a red or black raspberry-like aggregate, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.
