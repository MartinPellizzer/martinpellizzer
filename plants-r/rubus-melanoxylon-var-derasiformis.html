Rubus melanoxylon var. derasiformis

Rubus melanoxylon var. derasiformis, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The leaves are alternate, with serrated margins, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.
