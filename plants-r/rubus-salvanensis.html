Rubus salvanensis

Rubus salvanensis, commonly known as the Salvanescent blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America, specifically found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible berries. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
