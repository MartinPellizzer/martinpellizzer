Rubus hispidus subsp. continentalis

Rubus hispidus subsp.

continentalis, commonly known as the continental raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or scrub habitats.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow in color, with a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and hairy surfaces.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
