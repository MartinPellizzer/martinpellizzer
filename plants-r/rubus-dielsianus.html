Rubus dielsianus

Rubus dielsianus, commonly known as the Diels' blackberry, is a species of brambles native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forested areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is a cluster of small drupes, is edible and has a tart flavor.
