Rosa pimpinellifolia var. arenivaga

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. arenivaga, commonly known as the desert rock rose, is a variant of the rock rose species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, usually pink or white flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
