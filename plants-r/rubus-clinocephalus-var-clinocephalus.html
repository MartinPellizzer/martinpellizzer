Rubus clinocephalus var. clinocephalus

Rubus clinocephalus var. clinocephalus, commonly known as the upright blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with particular attention to its upright growth habit.
