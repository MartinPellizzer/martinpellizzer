Rumex × pakistanicus

Rumex × pakistanicus, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant that belongs to the Polygonaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex acetobulus (broadleaf sorrel). The plant typically features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the veins and underside. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor.
