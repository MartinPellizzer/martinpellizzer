Ranunculus flabellatus subsp. rufulus

Ranunculus flabellatus subsp.

rufulus, commonly known as the roughleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
