Ranunculus pauli-jordani

Ranunculus pauli-jordani, commonly known as the Jordanian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Jordan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features delicate, palmate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
