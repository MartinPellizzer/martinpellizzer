Rosa communis subvar. erythrella

Rosa communis subvar. erythrella, commonly known as the red-leaf rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage, which sets it apart from other rose species. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by both its foliage color and floral display.
