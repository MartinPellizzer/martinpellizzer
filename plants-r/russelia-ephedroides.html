Russelia ephedroides

Russelia ephedroides, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround its small, yellowish flowers. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its upright, cylindrical shape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Russelia ephedroides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
