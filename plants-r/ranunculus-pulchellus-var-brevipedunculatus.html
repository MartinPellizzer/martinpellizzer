Ranunculus pulchellus var. brevipedunculatus

Ranunculus pulchellus var. brevipedunculatus, commonly known as the short-stalked buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in the eastern regions of North America.

The variety is distinguished by its shorter peduncles compared to the typical form of Ranunculus pulchellus.
