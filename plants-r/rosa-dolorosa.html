Rosa dolorosa

Rosa dolorosa, commonly known as the sorrowful rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its deep red petals and thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa dolorosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and symbolic association with grief and longing.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and botanical studies.
