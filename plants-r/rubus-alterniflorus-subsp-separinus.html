Rubus alterniflorus subsp. separinus

Rubus alterniflorus subsp.

separinus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically red when mature. These berries are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique fruit color and growth habits compared to other Rubus species.
