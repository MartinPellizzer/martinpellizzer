Rhododendron arboreum subsp. albotomentosum

Rhododendron arboreum subsp.

albotomentosum, commonly known as the white-furred tree rhododendron, is a shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in mountainous regions.
