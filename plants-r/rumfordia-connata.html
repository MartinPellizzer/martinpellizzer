Rumfordia connata

Rumfordia connata, commonly known as the red-flowered rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant features linear, hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
