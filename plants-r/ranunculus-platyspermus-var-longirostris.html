Ranunculus platyspermus var. longirostris

Ranunculus platyspermus var. longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that are elongated and pointed, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant has a short, stout stem and basal leaves that are palmately divided. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
