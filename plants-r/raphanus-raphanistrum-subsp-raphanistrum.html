Raphanus raphanistrum subsp. raphanistrum

Raphanus raphanistrum subsp.

raphanistrum, commonly known as wild radish, is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer.

The plant's root system consists of a taproot that can be swollen and fleshy, often resembling a radish. It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
