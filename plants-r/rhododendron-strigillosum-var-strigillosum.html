Rhododendron strigillosum var. strigillosum

Rhododendron strigillosum var. strigillosum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
