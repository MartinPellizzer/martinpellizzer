Rhexia virginica

Rhexia virginica, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in acidic soils. The plant features showy, pink to magenta flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a reddish hue.

Rhexia virginica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
