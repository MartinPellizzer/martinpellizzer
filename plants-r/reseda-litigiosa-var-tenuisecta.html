Reseda litigiosa var. tenuisecta

Reseda litigiosa var. tenuisecta, commonly known as the slenderleaf sickleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its fibrous root system helps it survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

It is often found in grasslands, deserts, and disturbed soils, contributing to soil stabilization in these ecosystems.
