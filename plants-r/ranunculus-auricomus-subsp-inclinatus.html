Ranunculus auricomus subsp. inclinatus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

inclinatus, commonly known as the leaning buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent, heart-shaped sepals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the species is noted for its distinctive upward-curving petals.

It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
