Raphia gigantea

Raphia gigantea, commonly known as the giant palm, is a towering tropical plant native to the rainforests of Africa.

It can grow up to 20 meters in height, with a thick, fibrous trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach lengths of over 6 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which develop into fibrous fruits containing black seeds. Its leaves are composed of numerous leaflets arranged in a fan shape, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.

The trunk is often used for thatching and construction due to its durability and flexibility.
