Ranunculus undosus

Ranunculus undosus, commonly known as the wavy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
