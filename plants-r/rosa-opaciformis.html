Rosa opaciformis

Rosa opaciformis, commonly known as the opaciform rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and simple, alternate leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, appearing in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa opaciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
