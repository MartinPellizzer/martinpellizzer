Rosa pimpinellifolia rubella

Rosa pimpinellifolia rubella, commonly known as the pink rock rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs.

This shrub is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
