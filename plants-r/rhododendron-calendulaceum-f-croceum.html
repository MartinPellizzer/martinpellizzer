Rhododendron calendulaceum f. croceum

Rhododendron calendulaceum f. croceum, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a cultivar of the yellow azalea species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring and are often accompanied by showy bracts. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, and features glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
