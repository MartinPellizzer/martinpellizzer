Rhynchoglossum lazulinum

Rhynchoglossum lazulinum, commonly known as the lazuline orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Rhynchoglossum lazulinum is native to parts of Asia and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
