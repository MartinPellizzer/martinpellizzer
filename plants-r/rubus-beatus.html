Rubus beatus

Rubus beatus, commonly known as the blessed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, dark berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces fruits that are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content.

Due to its limited distribution, Rubus beatus is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
