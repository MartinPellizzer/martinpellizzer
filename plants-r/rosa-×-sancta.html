Rosa × sancta

Rosa × sancta, commonly known as the Santa Rosa, is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa gallica and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant typically produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Due to its historical significance in rose breeding, it has been used as a parent in the development of many modern rose cultivars.
