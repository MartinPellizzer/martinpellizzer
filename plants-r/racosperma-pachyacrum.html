Racosperma pachyacrum

Racosperma pachyacrum, commonly known as the thick-stemmed acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and heat.

Racosperma pachyacrum is often found in rocky soils and is an important component of local ecosystems.
