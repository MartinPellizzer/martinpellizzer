Rosa eglanteria subvar. microphylla

Rosa eglanteria subvar. microphylla, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in the spring. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
