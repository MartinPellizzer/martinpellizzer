Rhynchospora canaliculata

Rhynchospora canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbed leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Rhynchospora canaliculata plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and water filtration.
