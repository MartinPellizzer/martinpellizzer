Rhododendron euonymifolium

Rhododendron euonymifolium, commonly known as the European rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly glossy appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in horticulture and natural landscapes.
