Rubus arrhenii var. senticaulis

Rubus arrhenii var. senticaulis, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a low-growing shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries, though not widely cultivated, are edible and have a tart flavor.
