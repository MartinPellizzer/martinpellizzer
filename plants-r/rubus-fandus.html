Rubus fandus

Rubus fandus, commonly known as the Fandus blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with adequate rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
