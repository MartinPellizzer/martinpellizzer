Racosperma neriifolium subsp. pustulum

Racosperma neriifolium subsp.

pustulum, commonly known as the sticky-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its dark green, pinnate leaves that are covered in fine, sticky hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf structure and the presence of glandular structures on the leaves.
