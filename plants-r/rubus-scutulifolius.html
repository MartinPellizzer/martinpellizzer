Rubus scutulifolius

Rubus scutulifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive narrow shape with serrated edges.

Rubus scutulifolius thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
