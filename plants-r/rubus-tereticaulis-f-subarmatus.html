Rubus tereticaulis f. subarmatus

Rubus tereticaulis f. subarmatus, commonly known as the thick-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, typically found in moist, forested areas. The leaves are oval to elliptic in shape, with a thick, leathery texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by dark purple to black berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
