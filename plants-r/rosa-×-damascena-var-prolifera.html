Rosa × damascena var. prolifera

Rosa × damascena var. prolifera, commonly known as the Damask rose variant 'Prolifera', is a cultivar of the Damask rose, prized for its fragrant flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Middle East and Central Asia, characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of semi-double, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include hirsute stems, pinnate leaves, and a compact growth habit.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued in the perfume industry due to its essential oil content.
