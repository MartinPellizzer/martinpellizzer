Rinorea microphylla

Rinorea microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved rinorea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Rinorea microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
