Rosa Roxburghii Var. Hirtula

Himalayan rose, scientifically known as Rosa roxburghii var. hirtula, is a rare and elegant flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. This species is prized for its fragrant blooms and unique appearance, with velvety petals and a striking color palette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky terrains. Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a symbol of resilience and beauty in the region. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this plant from habitat loss and climate change.
