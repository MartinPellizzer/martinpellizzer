Racosperma tumidum var. kulparn

Racosperma tumidum var. kulparn, commonly known as the kulparn wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
