Rubus ulmifolius subsp. rusticanus

Rubus ulmifolius subsp.

rusticanus, commonly known as the Rusty Blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its dark, almost black, stems and leaves that are oval-shaped with a serrated edge.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically a dark red to black color, which are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and hedgerows.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, as well as its distinctive fruiting characteristics.
