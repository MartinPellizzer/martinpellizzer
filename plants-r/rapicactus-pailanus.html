Rapicactus pailanus

Rapicactus pailanus, commonly known as the Pailan's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of spines that provide protection from herbivores and extreme weather. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for collectors and gardeners in dry climates.
