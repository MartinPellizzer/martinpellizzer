Ranunculus komarowii

Ranunculus komarowii, commonly known as the Komarow's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and its flowers feature five to eight petal-like sepals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow or white blooms.
