Rapuntium bicolor

Rapuntium bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The plant's name reflects the two distinct colors present in its flowers and leaves.
