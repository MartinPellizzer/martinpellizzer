Rhexia cordigera

Rhexia cordigera, commonly known as the heartleaf sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Rhexia cordigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
