Rosa canina var. oenophora

Rosa canina var. oenophora, commonly known as the wineberry rose, is a variant of the dog rose that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves, typical of the Rosa genus. The flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and they bloom in spring. Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red, hairy, and contain achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
