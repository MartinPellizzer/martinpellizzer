Rosa chinensis var. fragrans

Rosa chinensis var. fragrans, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the Damask rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

Rosa chinensis var. fragrans is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
