Ruprechtia salicifolia

Ruprechtia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf ruprechtia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of willow trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
