Rubus vulgaris f. robustus

Rubus vulgaris f. robustus, commonly known as the common bramble or blackberry bush, is a vigorous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a distinctive flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a valuable resource for wildlife and humans alike.
