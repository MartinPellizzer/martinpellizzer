Rubus lejeunei var. ericetorum

Rubus lejeunei var. ericetorum, commonly known as the Ericaceous raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems.

It typically features small, aggregate fruits composed of multiple drupelets, which are usually red to black in color when mature. The plant is adapted to acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white or pale pink with five petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
