Rubus australis var. glaber

Rubus australis var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to southern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, distinguishing it from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically tart and edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
