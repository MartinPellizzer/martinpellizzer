Ranunculus aquatilis var. harrisii

Ranunculus aquatilis var. harrisii, commonly known as the water buttercup variant Harrisii, is a aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, floating leaves and bright yellow flowers.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem that supports its foliage and reproductive structures. Its flowers are usually solitary and feature five sepals that resemble petals, a trait common in the Ranunculaceae family.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to wetland habitats and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
