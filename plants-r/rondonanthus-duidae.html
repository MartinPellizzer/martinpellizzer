Rondonanthus duidae

Rondonanthus duidae, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its unique beauty and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
