Ribes melanocarpum

Ribes melanocarpum, commonly known as black currant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The berries are used in jams, juices, and as a flavoring agent in various food products.
