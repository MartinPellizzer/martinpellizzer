Rosa afzeliana var. diversiglandulosa

Rosa afzeliana var. diversiglandulosa, commonly known as the African tea rose, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant has a woody stem with thorny shoots, typical of many rose species. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in traditional medicine.

The variety is distinguished by its varied glandular structures on the leaves and stems.
