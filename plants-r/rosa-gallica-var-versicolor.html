Rosa gallica var. versicolor

Rosa gallica var. versicolor, commonly known as the French pink or Queen of the Romans, is a flowering plant native to Europe.

It is a shrub that produces showy, semi-double pink flowers with a delicate, fragrant scent. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its historical use in perfumery and as a symbol of love and devotion adds to its cultural significance.
