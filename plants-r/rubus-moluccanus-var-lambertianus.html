Rubus moluccanus var. lambertianus

Rubus moluccanus var. lambertianus, commonly known as Lambert's blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
