Ranunculus marginicola

Ranunculus marginicola, commonly known as the narrow-margin buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five to eight petal-like sepals.

It is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
