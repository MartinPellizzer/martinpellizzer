Rumex syriacus

Rumex syriacus, commonly known as Syrian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped, reddish leaves that are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued plant in both traditional medicine and culinary uses.
