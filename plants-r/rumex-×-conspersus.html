Rumex × conspersus

Rumex × conspersus, commonly known as the broadleaf dock, is a hybrid plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically covered with a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Rumex × conspersus is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
