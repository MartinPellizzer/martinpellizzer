Ranunculus aquatilis var. micranthus

Ranunculus aquatilis var. micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered water buttercup, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers with five petals, and its leaves are usually divided into lobes. It has a short, stout stem and is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged environments.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
