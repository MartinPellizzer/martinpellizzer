Rubus thyrsiflorus var. thuringiacus

Rubus thyrsiflorus var. thuringiacus, commonly known as the Thuringian blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe, particularly Germany. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
