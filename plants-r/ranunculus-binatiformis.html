Ranunculus binatiformis

Ranunculus binatiformis, commonly known as the twin-form bittersweet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive bi-lobed shape, and its flowers typically have five petals that are yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is also noted for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful to both humans and animals.
