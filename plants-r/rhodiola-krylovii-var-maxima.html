Rhodiola krylovii var. maxima

Rhodiola krylovii var. maxima, commonly known as the Siberian rock rose, is a perennial plant native to the cold regions of Asia, particularly Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are adapted to harsh, alpine conditions. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas with low temperatures.

Rhodiola krylovii var. maxima is known for its high concentration of adaptogenic compounds, making it a subject of interest in traditional medicine and modern herbal research.
