Rubus decloetii

Rubus decloetii, commonly known as the Decloet's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
