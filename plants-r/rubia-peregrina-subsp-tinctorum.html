Rubia peregrina subsp. tinctorum

Rubia peregrina subsp.

tinctorum, commonly known as Turkish madder, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect stems and opposite leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, woody fruits. Its roots contain a red dye called alizarin, which has been historically used in textile dyeing.

This species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its traditional applications in natural dyeing.
