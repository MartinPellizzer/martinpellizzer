Ranunculus septentrionalis var. pterocarpus

Ranunculus septentrionalis var. pterocarpus, commonly known as the northern buttercup variant with winged fruits, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive yellow petals and leaf structure.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to specific regions in North America. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a lobed appearance.

The variety name "pterocarpus" refers to its winged seed pods, which aid in seed dispersal.
