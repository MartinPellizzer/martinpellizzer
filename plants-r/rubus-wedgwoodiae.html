Rubus wedgwoodiae

Rubus wedgwoodiae, commonly known as the Wedgwood's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
