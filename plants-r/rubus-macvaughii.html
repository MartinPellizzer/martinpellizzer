Rubus macvaughii

Rubus macvaughii, commonly known as the MacVaugh's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the rose family, Rosaceae. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its deep root system.

The fruit, which is edible, has a distinctive flavor and is sometimes used in local culinary traditions.
