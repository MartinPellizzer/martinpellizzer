Racosperma xerophilum var. brevius

Racosperma xerophilum var. brevius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattled grevillea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that appear in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in low-water environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in native gardens and conservation efforts.
