Ranunculus aconitifolius var. medius

Ranunculus aconitifolius var. medius, commonly known as the middleleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, nodding yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
