Rumex trinervius

Rumex trinervius, commonly known as narrow-veined sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, producing distinctive three-lobed seed capsules.

Rumex trinervius is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
