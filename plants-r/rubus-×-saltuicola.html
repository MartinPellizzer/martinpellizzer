Rubus × saltuicola

Rubus × saltuicola, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which help it to spread across various habitats. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, containing multiple drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
