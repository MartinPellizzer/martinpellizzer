Rubus madagascarius f. umbrosus

Rubus madagascarius f. umbrosus, commonly known as the Madagascan blackberry.

This plant is a shrub native to Madagascar, characterized by its dark, almost black berries. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them a unique addition to local cuisines.
