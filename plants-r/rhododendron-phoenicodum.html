Rhododendron phoenicodum

Rhododendron phoenicodum, commonly known as the flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range from red to orange, often with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
