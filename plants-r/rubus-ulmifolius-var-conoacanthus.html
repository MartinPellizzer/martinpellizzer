Rubus ulmifolius var. conoacanthus

Rubus ulmifolius var. conoacanthus, commonly known as the thorned blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry with distinctive botanical features.

It is a shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, showcasing its adaptability to various ecological niches.
