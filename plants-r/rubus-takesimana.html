Rubus takesimana

Rubus takesimana, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
