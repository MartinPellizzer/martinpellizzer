Ranunculus auricomus subsp. dissidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

dissidens, commonly known as the dissected buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, palmate leaves with finely divided lobes and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers have five petals, and the sepals are often reflexed, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
