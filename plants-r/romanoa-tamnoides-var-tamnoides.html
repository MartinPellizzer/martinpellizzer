Romanoa tamnoides var. tamnoides

Romanoa tamnoides var. tamnoides, commonly known as the Florida panhandle bluemont, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist to swampy soils. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink to purple in color.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
