Rosa mollis subf. alciacantha

Rosa mollis subf.

alciacantha, commonly known as the soft rose or alciacantha rose, is a sub-species of the rose genus native to South America. It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white.

The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting subject for horticultural study.
