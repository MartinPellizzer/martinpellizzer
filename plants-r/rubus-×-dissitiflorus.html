Rubus × dissitiflorus

Rubus × dissitiflorus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry plant, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are composed of small drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces showy white or pink flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
