Rosa dumetorum

Rosa dumetorum, commonly known as the alpine rose or wood rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rosa dumetorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential healing properties.
