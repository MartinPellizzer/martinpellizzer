Rhus lamprocarpa

Rhus lamprocarpa, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth habit and small, glossy green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its potential to become invasive in certain regions.
