Racosperma calyculatum

Racosperma calyculatum, commonly known as the mountain wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers in late spring, which attract nectar-feeding insects.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
