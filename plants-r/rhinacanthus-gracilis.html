Rhinacanthus gracilis

Rhinacanthus gracilis, commonly known as the slender hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Rhinacanthus gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
