Reseda lutea var. subreflexa

Reseda lutea var. subreflexa, commonly known as the yellowrocket or lesser yellowrocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and prefers full sun. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow, with four petals, and bloom from spring to early summer.
