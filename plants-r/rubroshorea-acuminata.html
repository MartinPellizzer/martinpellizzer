Rubroshorea acuminata

Rubroshorea acuminata, commonly known as the red-fruited shorea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and elongated, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by distinctive, edible fruits that are orange-red in color. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Rubroshorea acuminata plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting a variety of wildlife species.
