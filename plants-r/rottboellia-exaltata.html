Rottboellia exaltata

Rottboellia exaltata, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Rottboellia exaltata is adapted to warm climates and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
