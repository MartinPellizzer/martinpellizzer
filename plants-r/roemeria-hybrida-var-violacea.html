Roemeria hybrida var. violacea

Roemeria hybrida var. violacea, commonly known as the purple roemeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of small, violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
