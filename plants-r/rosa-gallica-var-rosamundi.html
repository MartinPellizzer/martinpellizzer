Rosa gallica var. rosamundi

Rosa gallica var. rosamundi, commonly known as the "Queen of the Roses," is a historic and ornamental shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its deep pink to crimson flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant features arching stems with serrated, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blooms. It is a compact, bushy plant that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Rosa gallica var. rosamundi is valued for its fragrance and historical significance in horticulture and perfumery.
