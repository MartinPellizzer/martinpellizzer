Rubus ulmifolius var. concolor

Rubus ulmifolius var. concolor, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are dark purple to black when mature. These fruits are edible and often used in jams and pies.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
