Rubus pungens var. pungens

Rubus pungens var. pungens, commonly known as the prairie raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, edible berries in the summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This species is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
