Rhododendron pachyphyllum

Rhododendron pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that provides a unique texture compared to other rhododendrons. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a sought-after plant in horticulture and conservation efforts.
