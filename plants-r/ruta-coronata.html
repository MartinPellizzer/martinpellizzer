Ruta coronata

Ruta coronata, commonly known as crown rue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often glandular, leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often lobed or toothed.

Ruta coronata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and historical use in herbal medicine.
