Rubus obtusangulus proles caldasianus

Rubus obtusangulus proles caldasianus, commonly known as the Caldasian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
