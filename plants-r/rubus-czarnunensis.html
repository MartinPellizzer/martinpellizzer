Rubus czarnunensis

Rubus czarnunensis, commonly known as the Czarnun's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a shrub-like growth habit.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
