Rhododendron ludwigianum

Rhododendron ludwigianum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhododendron ludwigianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
