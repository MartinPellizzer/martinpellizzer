Rebutia taratensis

Rebutia taratensis, commonly known as the Tarat Desert Cactus, is a low-growing, spineless cactus native to Central Asia.

It features a thick, segmented stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
