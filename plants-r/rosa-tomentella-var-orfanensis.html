Rosa tomentella var. orfanensis

Rosa tomentella var. orfanensis, commonly known as the Orphan's Rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, woolly gray foliage that provides a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
