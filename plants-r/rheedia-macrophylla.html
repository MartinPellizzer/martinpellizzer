Rheedia macrophylla

Rheedia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used in various culinary applications. Rheedia macrophylla thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
