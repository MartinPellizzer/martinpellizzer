Rosa coreana

Rosa coreana, commonly known as the Korean rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Korea.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and broad, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and hips that are red or orange when mature.

Rosa coreana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
