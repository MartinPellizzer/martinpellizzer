Rubus regionalis

Rubus regionalis, commonly known as the regional raspberry, is a species of brambles native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color, similar to other raspberry species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
