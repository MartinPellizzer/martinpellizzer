Rhabdodendron

Rhabdodendron, commonly known as the flame tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lythraceae.

These plants are characterized by their striking, fiery red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, providing a contrasting green backdrop to the vibrant blooms. Rhabdodendron species are native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and can thrive in a range of soil types with adequate sunlight.
