Rubus ilanensis

Rubus ilanensis, commonly known as the Ilan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many shrubs with thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pink or white, appearing in late spring.

The fruit, a red, hairy berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.
