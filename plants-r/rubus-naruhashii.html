Rubus naruhashii

Rubus naruhashii, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries or raspberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

The botanical features of Rubus naruhashii make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
