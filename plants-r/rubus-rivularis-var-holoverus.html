Rubus rivularis var. holoverus

Rubus rivularis var. holoverus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, arching stems with thorns and trifoliate leaves that are narrow and serrated. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often tart.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife and its ornamental appeal in shaded garden settings.
