Rubus ithacanus

Rubus ithacanus, commonly known as Ithaca raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing or shrub-like growth habit and produces small, red, edible berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is an important food source for various wildlife species.
