Retinispora obtusa var. nana-aurea

Retinispora obtusa var. nana-aurea, commonly known as the dwarf golden retinispora, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive golden-yellow variegation on the leaves, which adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Due to its ornamental value and compact size, it is often used in container gardening and as a ground cover in shaded landscapes.
