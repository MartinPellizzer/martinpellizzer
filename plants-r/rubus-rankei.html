Rubus rankei

Rubus rankei, commonly known as Ranke's blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Argentina, Chile, and Peru. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has a woody stem covered in thorns.

Rubus rankei is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
