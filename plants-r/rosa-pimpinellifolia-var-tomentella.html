Rosa pimpinellifolia var. tomentella

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly strawberry tree, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, edible fruits resembling strawberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage due to a thick layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
