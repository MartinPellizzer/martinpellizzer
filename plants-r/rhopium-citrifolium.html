Rhopium citrifolium

Rhopium citrifolium, commonly known as the citrifolium tree, is a deciduous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy leaves that are often fragrant and have a citrus-like scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fruits that are typically orange or red in color. Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils.
