Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. thyrsanthus

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

thyrsanthus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southern United States. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, lobed leaves that often have a silvery underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark purple berries in the summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
