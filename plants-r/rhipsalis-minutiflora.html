Rhipsalis minutiflora

Rhipsalis minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mistletoe cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thin, branching stems that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Rhipsalis minutiflora is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Its unique growth habit and delicate floral display make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
