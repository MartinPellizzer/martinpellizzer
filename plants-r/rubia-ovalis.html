Rubia ovalis

Rubia ovalis, commonly known as the oval woodruff, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Rubia ovalis is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
