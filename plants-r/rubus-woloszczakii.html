Rubus woloszczakii

Rubus woloszczakii, commonly known as the Woloszczak's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are white and have five petals, while the leaves are trifoliate with serrated edges.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
