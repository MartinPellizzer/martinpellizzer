Ribes sativum f. macrocarpum

Ribes sativum f. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited gooseberry, is a cultivated form of the gooseberry plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thick, edible berries that are typically larger and more flavorful than those of the common gooseberry. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which develop into the distinctive red or orange berries by late summer. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often grown for its ornamental value and culinary use in jams and preserves.
