Rochelia pamirica

Rochelia pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir rosette, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in a short inflorescence. It is adapted to cold, arid environments and can survive in rocky or gravelly soils.
