Ribes oxyacanthoides subsp. setosum

Ribes oxyacanthoides subsp.

setosum, commonly known as the hairy currant, is a member of the Grossulariaceae family. It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan.

The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, edible berries.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense pubescence, giving it a hairy appearance.
