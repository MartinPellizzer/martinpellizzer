Rosa davidii var. pungens

Rosa davidii var. pungens, commonly known as the Chinese prickly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne in terminal panicles. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
