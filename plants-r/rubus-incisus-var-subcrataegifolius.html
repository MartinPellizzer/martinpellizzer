Rubus incisus var. subcrataegifolius

Rubus incisus var. subcrataegifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a food source for wildlife.
