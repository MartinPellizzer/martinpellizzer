Rosa × cottetii var. superglauca

Rosa × cottetii var. superglauca, commonly known as the Super Glauca rose, is a hybrid species characterized by its striking, deeply serrated leaves and showy, single blooms.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the United States, specifically found in the southeastern region. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is notable for its bluish-green hue, which becomes more pronounced in the winter months.

The flowers, which are usually pink to red, bloom in late spring and early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.
