Ricinocarpus diversifolius

Ricinocarpus diversifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved sea bean, is a tropical shrub native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that are often lobed or divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits that contain flat, bean-shaped seeds. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
