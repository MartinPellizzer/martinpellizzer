Rubus pacatus var. alypus

Rubus pacatus var. alypus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
