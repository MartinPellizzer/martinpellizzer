Rosa caryophyllacea f. formosa

Rosa caryophyllacea f. formosa, commonly known as the showy rose, is a botanical variety characterized by its vibrant, double flowers that range in color from pink to deep red.

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central America and is known for its upright growth habit and fragrant foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation. The leaves are dark green and serrated, while the stems are covered in fine, velvety hairs.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce clusters of flowers throughout the growing season.
