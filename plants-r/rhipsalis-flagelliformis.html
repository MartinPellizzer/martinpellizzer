Rhipsalis flagelliformis

Rhipsalis flagelliformis, commonly known as the silver hair cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, thin, and often pendulous stems that resemble tendrils or hair, which can grow up to several meters in length. The plant lacks true leaves but has small, scale-like structures that help reduce water loss. It produces small, white or pale green flowers during the rainy season, typically in the summer months.

Due to its delicate appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
