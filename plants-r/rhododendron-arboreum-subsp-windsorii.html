Rhododendron arboreum subsp. windsorii

Rhododendron arboreum subsp.

windsorii, commonly known as the Windsor rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red and pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
