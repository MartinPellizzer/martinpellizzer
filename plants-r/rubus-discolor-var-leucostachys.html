Rubus discolor var. leucostachys

Rubus discolor var. leucostachys, commonly known as the white-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its pale stems and foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of North America. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible fruits.
