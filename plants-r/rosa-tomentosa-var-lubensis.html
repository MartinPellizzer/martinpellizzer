Rosa tomentosa var. lubensis

Rosa tomentosa var. lubensis, commonly known as the Lubensky rose, is a shrub-like species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its stems are often covered in a thick layer of white tomentum, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and floral display.
