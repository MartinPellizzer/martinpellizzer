Rubus dixiensis

Rubus dixiensis, commonly known as the Yunnan blackberry, is a species of shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

The fruit, which is dark purple to black in color, is edible and has a tart flavor.
