Rhododendron proteoides

Rhododendron proteoides, commonly known as the Proteoides rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its botanical features include thick, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit.

Rhododendron proteoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
