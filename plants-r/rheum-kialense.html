Rheum kialense

Rheum kialense, commonly known as the Kialense rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. Its stems are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its potential health benefits.
