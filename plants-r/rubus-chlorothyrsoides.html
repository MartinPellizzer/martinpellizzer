Rubus chlorothyrsoides

Rubus chlorothyrsoides, commonly known as the green-stem blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, often hairy stems and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically consumed by wildlife rather than humans. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like roses and apples.
