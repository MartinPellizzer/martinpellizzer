Rubus silvaticus subsp. questieri

Rubus silvaticus subsp.

questieri, commonly known as the Alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
