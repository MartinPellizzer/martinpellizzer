Rubus hirtus var. crassiusculus

Rubus hirtus var. crassiusculus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets, which are typically firm and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
