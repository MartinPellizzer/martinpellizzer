Ranunculus caucasicus subsp. pavlii

Ranunculus caucasicus subsp.

pavlii, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in early spring.

The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, forest edges, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered a wildflower in its native habitat.
