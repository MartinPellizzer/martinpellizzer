Rhamnus sphenophylla

Rhamnus sphenophylla, commonly known as the desert buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Rhamnus sphenophylla is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
