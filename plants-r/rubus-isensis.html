Rubus isensis

Rubus isensis, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red fruits. The plant typically grows in forested areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white and appear in the spring.

The fruits are edible and often used in traditional Japanese cuisine.
