Rhododendron quadrasianum var. davaoense

Rhododendron quadrasianum var. davaoense, commonly known as the Davao rhododendron, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within montane forests. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, while its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
