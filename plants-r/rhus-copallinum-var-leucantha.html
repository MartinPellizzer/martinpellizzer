Rhus copallinum var. leucantha

Rhus copallinum var. leucantha, commonly known as white sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged with 15 to 31 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by distinctive red, fuzzy fruits that persist through winter. Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

White sumac is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban environments.
