Rubus moluccanus var. abortivus

Rubus moluccanus var. abortivus, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
