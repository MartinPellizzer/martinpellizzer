Rubus pulchellus

Rubus pulchellus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, producing flowers and fruit on the second year of growth. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit, a small, dark purple to black berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.

Rubus pulchellus is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
