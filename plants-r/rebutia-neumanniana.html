Rebutia neumanniana

Rebutia neumanniana, commonly known as the Neumann's euphorbia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, often surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. Rebutia neumanniana is well adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and minimal water requirements.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
