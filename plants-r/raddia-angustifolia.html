Raddia angustifolia

Raddia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf raddia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters wide. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists studying adapted flora.
