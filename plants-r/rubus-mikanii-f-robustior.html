Rubus mikanii f. robustior

Rubus mikanii f. robustior, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry or Chinese raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus mikanii species.

It is a shrub native to East Asia, particularly China and Japan, and is characterized by its robust growth habit. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, tart berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, making it a popular choice in gardens and agricultural settings.
