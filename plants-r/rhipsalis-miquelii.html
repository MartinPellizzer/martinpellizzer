Rhipsalis miquelii

Rhipsalis miquelii, commonly known as the silver mist cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, grayish-green stems that are covered in a fine, silky coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically in the summer months. Unlike many cacti, Rhipsalis miquelii does not have spines, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture, making it relatively easy to care for.
