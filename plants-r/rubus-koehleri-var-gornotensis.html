Rubus koehleri var. gornotensis

Rubus koehleri var. gornotensis, commonly known as the Gornotensis blackberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
