Rhododendron venator

Rhododendron venator, commonly known as the ghostly rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and silver-white underneath, creating a striking contrast. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and specialized horticulture.
