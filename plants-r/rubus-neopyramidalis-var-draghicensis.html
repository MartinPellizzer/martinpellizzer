Rubus neopyramidalis var. draghicensis

Rubus neopyramidalis var. draghicensis, commonly known as the Draghici raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to specific regions of Romania.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible berries, though it is less commonly cultivated than other raspberry species.
