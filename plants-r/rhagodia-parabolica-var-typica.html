Rhagodia parabolica var. typica

Rhagodia parabolica var. typica, commonly known as the Narrow-leafed Bitter Bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, growing in open woodlands and heathlands.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its use in traditional Aboriginal practices.
