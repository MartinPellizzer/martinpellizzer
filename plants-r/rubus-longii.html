Rubus longii

Rubus longii, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
