Ranunculus lomatocarpus

Ranunculus lomatocarpus, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to nine petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

It is a perennial species that produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small seeds.
