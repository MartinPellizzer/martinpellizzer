Rhus nitida

Rhus nitida, commonly known as staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fuzzy berries that persist into the winter. Rhus nitida is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in open woods.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems and a popular ornamental plant.
