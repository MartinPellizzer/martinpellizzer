Romulea congoensis

Romulea congoensis, commonly known as the Congo star orchid, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It is a perennial herb that produces striking, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted for storing water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The flowers bloom during the dry season, making it a notable species in the region's flora.
