Rulingia rotundifolia

Rulingia rotundifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf rulingia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountainous regions. Its botanical features include a woody stem and alternate, palmate leaf arrangement.

Rulingia rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
