Rubus protractus

Rubus protractus, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
