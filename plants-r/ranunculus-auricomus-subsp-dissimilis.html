Ranunculus auricomus subsp. dissimilis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

dissimilis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a distinctive white or pale yellow center.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its delicate structure, it is adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates and seasonal temperature fluctuations.
