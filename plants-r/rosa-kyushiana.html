Rosa kyushiana

Rosa kyushiana, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has arching, thorned stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Rosa kyushiana is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
