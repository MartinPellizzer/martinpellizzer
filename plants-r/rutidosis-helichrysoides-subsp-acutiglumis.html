Rutidosis helichrysoides subsp. acutiglumis

Rutidosis helichrysoides subsp.

acutiglumis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
