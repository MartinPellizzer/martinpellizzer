Rubus flosculosus subsp. ithythyrsos

Rubus flosculosus subsp.

ithythyrsos, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, dark red to black drupes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rubus genus.
