Rhododendron sohayakiense var. koreanum

Rhododendron sohayakiense var. koreanum, commonly known as the Korean rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. It is often found in forest understories and rocky slopes, where it forms dense thickets.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
