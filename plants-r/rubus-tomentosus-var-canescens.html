Rubus tomentosus var. canescens

Rubus tomentosus var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, white-tomentose (woolly) foliage and stems, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
