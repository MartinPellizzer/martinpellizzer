Rosa × dumetorum var. uncinelloides

Rosa × dumetorum var. uncinelloides, commonly known as the hooked bramble rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The leaves are compound, typically with five to seven leaflets, and the plant produces small, red hips that persist into winter. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
