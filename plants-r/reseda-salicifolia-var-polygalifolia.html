Reseda salicifolia var. polygalifolia

Reseda salicifolia var. polygalifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved reseda, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and delicate blooms make it a desirable addition to ornamental gardens.
