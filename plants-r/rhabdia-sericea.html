Rhabdia sericea

Rhabdia sericea, commonly known as the silky rhabdia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The leaves are often covered in a fine, silvery hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

The plant is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role.
