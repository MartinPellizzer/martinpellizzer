Rubus caesius subsp. fasciculatus

Rubus caesius subsp.

fasciculatus, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red and edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
