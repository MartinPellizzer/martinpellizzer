Ranunculus idiophylloides

Ranunculus idiophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and its finely divided, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or streambanks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other buttercup species.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
