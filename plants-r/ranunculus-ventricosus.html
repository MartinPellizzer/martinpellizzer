Ranunculus ventricosus

Ranunculus ventricosus, commonly known as the bladderroot crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive black center. The plant has a short, thickened stem and basal leaves that are palmately lobed. Its root system is characterized by fleshy, swollen roots, which give it its common name.

Ranunculus ventricosus typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats.
