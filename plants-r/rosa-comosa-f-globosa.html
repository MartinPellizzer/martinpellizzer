Rosa comosa f. globosa

Rosa comosa f. globosa, commonly known as the globular rose, is a compact, bushy shrub characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit.

Its leaves are typically dark green, glossy, and arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often in clusters. It is well-suited for container gardening or as a hedge due to its manageable size and hardy nature.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
