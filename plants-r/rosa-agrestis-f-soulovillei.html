Rosa agrestis f. soulovillei

Rosa agrestis f. soulovillei, commonly known as the Souloville's bramble rose, is a unique hybrid plant characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage.

It typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
