Ruellia beckii

Ruellia beckii, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia beckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
