Ranunculus amabilis

Ranunculus amabilis, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus amabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
