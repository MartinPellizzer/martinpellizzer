Rhododendron decandrum f. lasiocarpum

Rhododendron decandrum f. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the hairy-fruited rhododendron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant produces distinctive, hairy capsules as its fruit, which are a notable feature of this cultivar. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to woodland environments.
