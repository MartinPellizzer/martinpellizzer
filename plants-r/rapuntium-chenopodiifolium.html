Rapuntium chenopodiifolium

Rapuntium chenopodiifolium, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, terminal panicles. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, making it a traditional food source for indigenous peoples.

It thrives in poor soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.
