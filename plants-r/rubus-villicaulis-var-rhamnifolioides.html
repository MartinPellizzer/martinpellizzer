Rubus villicaulis var. rhamnifolioides

Rubus villicaulis var. rhamnifolioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
