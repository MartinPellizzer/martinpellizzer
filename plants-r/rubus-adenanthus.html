Rubus adenanthus

Rubus adenanthus, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically dark green and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, often occurring in clusters. It thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salty air and sandy soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberries.
