Rosa azerbaidshanica

Rosa azerbaidshanica, commonly known as the Azeri rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caucasus region, specifically Azerbaijan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in summer, which are composed of five petals and numerous stamens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a representative of the Rosa genus in the region and its potential for horticultural use.
