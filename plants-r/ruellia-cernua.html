Ruellia cernua

Ruellia cernua, commonly known as the hanging ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Ruellia cernua is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to cascade from containers or hanging baskets.
