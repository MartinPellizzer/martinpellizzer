Rubus imbricatus var. londinensis

Rubus imbricatus var. londinensis, commonly known as the London blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry native to the British Isles.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and clustered, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
