Rubus chlorosericeus

Rubus chlorosericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, dark purple fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers are white and clustered in racemes, while its thorny stems are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
