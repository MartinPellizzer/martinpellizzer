Ruellia macrophylla

Ruellia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ruellia macrophylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
