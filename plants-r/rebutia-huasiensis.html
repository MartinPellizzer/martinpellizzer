Rebutia huasiensis

Rebutia huasiensis, commonly known as the Huasi moon cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and colorful, velvety flowers. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers, which can be yellow, orange, or red, bloom in the spring and are often borne on a short, thick peduncle.

Rebutia huasiensis is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated as a decorative plant in succulent gardens.
