Rhamnus shozoensis

Rhamnus shozoensis, commonly known as the Shozo buckthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the buckthorn family, Rhamnaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a small, smooth, black drupe that ripens in the fall.

Rhamnus shozoensis is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
