Rorippa montana

Rorippa montana, commonly known as the mountain watercress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, such as streambanks and wet meadows. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.
