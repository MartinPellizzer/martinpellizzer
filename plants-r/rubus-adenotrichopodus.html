Rubus adenotrichopodus

Rubus adenotrichopodus, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its woody stems covered in dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus adenotrichopodus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
