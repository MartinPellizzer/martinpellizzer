Rosa longicruris var. lesurina

Rosa longicruris var. lesurina, commonly known as the Lesur's climbing rose, is a variant of the long-stemmed rose native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its vigorous, thorny stems that can climb to significant heights, often reaching several meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
