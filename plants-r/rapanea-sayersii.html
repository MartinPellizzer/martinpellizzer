Rapanea sayersii

Rapanea sayersii, commonly known as the Sayers' holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its dense, glossy leaves and small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

However, due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
