Rubus suberectus f. chloracanthus

Rubus suberectus f. chloracanthus, commonly known as the green-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive greenish fruit color.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of North America. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are white to pale pink, and the fruit develops into a small, edible berry.

This form is notable for its unique fruit coloration, which sets it apart from the more common red-fruited varieties.
