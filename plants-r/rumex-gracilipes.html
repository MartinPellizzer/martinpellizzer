Rumex gracilipes

Rumex gracilipes, commonly known as slender dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on tall, branching inflorescences. Rumex gracilipes thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
