Rottboellia kerstingii

Rottboellia kerstingii, commonly known as the kersting's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, three-lobed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fallow fields.

Rottboellia kerstingii is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
