Rubus carolinianus

Rubus carolinianus, commonly known as the Carolina blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus carolinianus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinator populations.
