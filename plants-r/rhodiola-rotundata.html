Rhodiola rotundata

Rhodiola rotundata, commonly known as the roundleaf rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and possesses a deep root system for water storage. It is known for its medicinal properties, including adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.

Rhodiola rotundata thrives in high-altitude regions and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
