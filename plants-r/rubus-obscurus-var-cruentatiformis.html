Rubus obscurus var. cruentatiformis

Rubus obscurus var. cruentatiformis, commonly known as the crimson-stemmed blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its dark, almost black berries and distinctive reddish stems. It is a member of the Rosaceae family, sharing traits with other brambles. The plant typically grows in temperate regions with moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.
