Rosa sericatula

Rosa sericatula, commonly known as the silky rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its soft, silken hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas, and thrives in temperate climates.

Rosa sericatula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its fragrance and hardiness.
