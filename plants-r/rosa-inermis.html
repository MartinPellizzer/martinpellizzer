Rosa inermis

Rosa inermis, commonly known as the evergreen musk rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thornless stems. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and trifoliate, providing ornamental value throughout the year.

Rosa inermis is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
