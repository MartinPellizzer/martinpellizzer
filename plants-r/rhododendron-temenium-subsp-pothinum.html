Rhododendron temenium subsp. pothinum

Rhododendron temenium subsp.

pothinum, commonly known as the Alpine rhododendron, is a subshrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It typically grows at high elevations, often in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green in color. Its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its specific environmental requirements.
