Rubus buschii

Rubus buschii, commonly known as the Busch's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
