Ravenia biramosa var. biramosa

Ravenia biramosa var. biramosa, commonly known as the red-flowered ravenia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
