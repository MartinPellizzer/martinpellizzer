Rubus ferreus

Rubus ferreus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a bright red color.

These fruits are not only edible but also rich in antioxidants and vitamin C. Rubus ferreus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and commercial cultivation.
