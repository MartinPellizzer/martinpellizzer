Rubroshorea parvifolia

Rubroshorea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved redshore, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a reddish hue on the underside. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
