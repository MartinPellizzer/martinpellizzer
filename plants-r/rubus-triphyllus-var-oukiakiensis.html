Rubus triphyllus var. oukiakiensis

Rubus triphyllus var. oukiakiensis, commonly known as the Oukiaki blackberry, is a variant of the trailing blackberry species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three leaflets, which are typically serrated and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are highly aromatic and prized for their flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
