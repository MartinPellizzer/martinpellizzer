Rosa alpina var. cinnamomeoidea

Rosa alpina var. cinnamomeoidea, commonly known as the cinnamon-scented alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-cinnamon colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its stems are often covered in a dense layer of grayish-white hairs, providing a distinctive appearance.

This variety is well adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
