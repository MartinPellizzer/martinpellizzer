Rosa coriifolia var. bakeri

Rosa coriifolia var. bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's sweetbriar, is a variant of the sweetbriar rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a delicate, fuzzy texture. Native to China, it thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
