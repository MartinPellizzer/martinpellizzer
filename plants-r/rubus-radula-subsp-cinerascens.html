Rubus radula subsp. cinerascens

Rubus radula subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-colored bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a pale, ash-like underside.

The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens, and appear in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart.

It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
