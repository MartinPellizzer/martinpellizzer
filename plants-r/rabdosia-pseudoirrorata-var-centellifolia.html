Rabdosia pseudoirrorata var. centellifolia

Rabdosia pseudoirrorata var. centellifolia, commonly known as the purple false bugloss, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
