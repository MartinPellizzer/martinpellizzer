Rhodophiala bifida var. pulchra

Rhodophiala bifida var. pulchra, commonly known as the Splendid Rhodophiala, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces striking white to pinkish flowers with a distinctive red or purple throat, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its foliage consists of strap-like, dark green leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
