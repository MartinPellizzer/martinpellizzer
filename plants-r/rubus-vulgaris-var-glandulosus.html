Rubus vulgaris var. glandulosus

Rubus vulgaris var. glandulosus, commonly known as the brambles or common blackberry.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, hairy achenes, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

The species is notable for its edible fruits and its role in ecological systems as a food source for various wildlife.
