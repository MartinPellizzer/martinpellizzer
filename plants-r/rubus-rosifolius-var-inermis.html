Rubus rosifolius var. inermis

Rubus rosifolius var. inermis, commonly known as the smoothleaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems with trifoliate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
