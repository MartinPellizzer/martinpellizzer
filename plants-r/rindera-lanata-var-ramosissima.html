Rindera lanata var. ramosissima

Rindera lanata var. ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy leaf rindera, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its hairy foliage helps reduce water loss, making it adapted to humid tropical or subtropical climates.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
