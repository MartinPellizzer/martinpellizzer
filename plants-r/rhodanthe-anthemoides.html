Rhodanthe anthemoides

Rhodanthe anthemoides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its flowers are composed of five petals, with the lower petal being larger and more prominent.

Rhodanthe anthemoides is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
