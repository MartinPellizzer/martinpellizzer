Rubus fruticosus var. plenus

Rubus fruticosus var. plenus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hedgerows or as a landscape plant.
