Rhamnus paniculiflora

Rhamnus paniculiflora, commonly known as the panicle-flowered buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in panicles. The plant produces dark, glossy berries that are a source of food for various wildlife species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Rhamnus paniculiflora is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep root system to access water from deeper soil layers.
