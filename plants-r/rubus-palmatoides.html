Rubus palmatoides

Rubus palmatoides, commonly known as the broad-leafed raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for various wildlife species.
