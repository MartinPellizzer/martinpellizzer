Rubus pleonacanthus

Rubus pleonacanthus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and large, compound leaves that are typically broad and oval in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark purple to black when ripe, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
