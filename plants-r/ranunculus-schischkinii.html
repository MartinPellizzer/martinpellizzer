Ranunculus schischkinii

Ranunculus schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
