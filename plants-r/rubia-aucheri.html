Rubia aucheri

Rubia aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian balm, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens.

Rubia aucheri is also valued for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
