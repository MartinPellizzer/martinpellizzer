Rhododendron linnaeoides

Rhododendron linnaeoides, commonly known as the Florida rhododendron, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and often have a slight pubescence. Its flowers are showy, appearing in clusters of 3 to 6, with pink to reddish hues and a distinctive, showy calyx.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
