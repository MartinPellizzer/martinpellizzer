Rosa communis var. hemitricha

Rosa communis var. hemitricha, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces rose-like blooms that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

The flowers are also valued for their use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of perfume.
