Rhynchospora marliniana

Rhynchospora marliniana, commonly known as the Marlin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in marshy areas, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences of pale yellow to greenish flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other wetland flora.

Rhynchospora marliniana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
