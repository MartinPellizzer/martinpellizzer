Rosa erythroclada

Rosa erythroclada, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa erythroclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and historical significance.
