Rubus riograndis

Rubus riograndis, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and grows in a shrub form. Its flowers are white with five petals and are typically followed by the edible fruits.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
