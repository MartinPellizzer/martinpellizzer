Rubus rudis subsp. coronatus

Rubus rudis subsp.

coronatus, commonly known as the crown raspberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which are typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, containing multiple drupes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
