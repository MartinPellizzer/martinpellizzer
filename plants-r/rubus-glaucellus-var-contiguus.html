Rubus glaucellus var. contiguus

Rubus glaucellus var. contiguus, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a shrub native to coastal regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray environments.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.
