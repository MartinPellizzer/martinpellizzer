Ranunculus inopinus

Ranunculus inopinus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

Ranunculus inopinus is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in alpine ecosystems.
