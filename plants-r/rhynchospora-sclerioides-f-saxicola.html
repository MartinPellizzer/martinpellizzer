Rhynchospora sclerioides f. saxicola

Rhynchospora sclerioides f. saxicola, commonly known as the saxicola sedge, is a variant of the broader Rhynchospora sclerioides species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and spike-like inflorescences.

The saxicola sedge is adapted to wetland environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
