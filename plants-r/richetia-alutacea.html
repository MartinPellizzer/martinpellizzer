Richetia alutacea

Richetia alutacea, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
