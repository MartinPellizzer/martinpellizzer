Rosa marisensis f. serraturaduplici

Rosa marisensis f. serraturaduplici, commonly known as the double-serrated sea rose, is a rare and unique botanical variant of the Rosa marisensis species.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive double-serrated leaf margins, which set it apart from typical rose varieties. It thrives in coastal environments, showcasing adaptations to saline and windy conditions. The flowers are usually pale pink or white, with a delicate fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Due to its limited habitat and rarity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
