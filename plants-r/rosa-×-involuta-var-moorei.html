Rosa × involuta var. moorei

Rosa × involuta var. moorei, commonly known as the Moore's curled rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its unique, tightly curled petals.

It originates from a cross between Rosa × involuta and Rosa × damascena, resulting in a plant with a compact, ornamental form. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a velvety texture and a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular pruning to maintain its shape.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden landscapes and floral arrangements.
