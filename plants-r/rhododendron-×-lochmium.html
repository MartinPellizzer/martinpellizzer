Rhododendron × lochmium

Rhododendron × lochmium, commonly known as the cross-leaved rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron maximum and Rhododendron catawbiense.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
