Restio albotuberculatus

Restio albotuberculatus, commonly known as the white tubercled restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
