Rosa cinnabarina

Rosa cinnabarina, commonly known as the Chinese pink or the red rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its striking, deep red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually solitary and have a sweet, delicate fragrance, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Rosa cinnabarina is also valued for its historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
