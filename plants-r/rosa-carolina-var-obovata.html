Rosa carolina var. obovata

Rosa carolina var. obovata, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven obovate leaflets that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by showy, orange-red hips. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and persistent fruit, which provide food for wildlife.
