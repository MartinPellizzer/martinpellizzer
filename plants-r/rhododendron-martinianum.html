Rhododendron martinianum

Rhododendron martinianum, commonly known as the Martin's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, cool environments. The plant features large, showy flowers that are usually pink or red, with a tubular shape and a distinctive throat. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron martinianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
