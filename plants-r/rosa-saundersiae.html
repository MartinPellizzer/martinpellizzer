Rosa saundersiae

Rosa saundersiae, commonly known as the Saunders rose, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in dense, thorny bracts. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is well adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat.

Rosa saundersiae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
