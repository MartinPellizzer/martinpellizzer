Rebutia einsteinii f. karreri

Rebutia einsteinii f. karreri, commonly known as the Einstein's Rebutia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry environments, with shallow roots that allow it to thrive in well-drained soils.

This succulent is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of care.
