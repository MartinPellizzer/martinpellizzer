Rubus buergeri var. viridifolius

Rubus buergeri var. viridifolius, commonly known as the green-leafed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries and serrated leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are popular in local cuisines.

The variety is noted for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
