Rubus tomentosus var. curtispinus

Rubus tomentosus var. curtispinus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with a dense, woolly underside. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible blackberries in the summer. Its berries are typically small, firm, and have a tart flavor.

This variety is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial shade to full sun.
