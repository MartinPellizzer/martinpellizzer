Rosa lutescens

Rosa lutescens, commonly known as the golden rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and dense, thorny stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa lutescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
