Rollinia cordifolia

Rollinia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf rollinia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that contains several seeds.

Rollinia cordifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
