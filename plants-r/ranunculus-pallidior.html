Ranunculus pallidior

Ranunculus pallidior, commonly known as the pale buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring, and it is often found in association with other early-flowering plants.

The species is characterized by its finely toothed foliage and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
