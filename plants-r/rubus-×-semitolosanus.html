Rubus × semitolosanus

Rubus × semitolosanus, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white to pink, and the fruit is a large, dark purple to black aggregate fruit, often used in culinary applications.

This hybrid is valued for its hardy nature and flavorful berries, making it a popular choice for cultivation in temperate climates.
