Rhynchospora alba var. meridiana

Rhynchospora alba var. meridiana, commonly known as the white marsh-orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and pale white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
