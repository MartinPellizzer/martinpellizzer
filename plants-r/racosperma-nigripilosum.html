Racosperma nigripilosum

Racosperma nigripilosum, commonly known as the black-hair wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are often covered in fine, dark hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Racosperma nigripilosum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
