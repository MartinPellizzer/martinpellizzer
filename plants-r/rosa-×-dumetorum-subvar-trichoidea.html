Rosa × dumetorum subvar. trichoidea

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. trichoidea, commonly known as the hairy wood rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a fragrant aroma.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
