Rhododendron huguangense

Rhododendron huguangense, commonly known as the Yunnan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in humid, shaded environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens.

The species is also notable for its ecological importance in its native habitat.
