Rubus asperulus

Rubus asperulus, commonly known as the rough raspberry or scarlet cinquefoil, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible, red, raspberry-like fruits that are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the eastern United States.

Rubus asperulus is a perennial shrub that plays an important role in supporting local wildlife.
