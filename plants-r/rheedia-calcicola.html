Rheedia calcicola

Rheedia calcicola, commonly known as the limestone rheedia, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to store water. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often found on limestone outcrops. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.

Due to its specialized habitat, Rheedia calcicola is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native environment.
