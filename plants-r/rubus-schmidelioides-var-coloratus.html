Rubus schmidelioides var. coloratus

Rubus schmidelioides var. coloratus, commonly known as the colorful bramble, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
