Rubus anoplocladus var. appendiculatus

Rubus anoplocladus var. appendiculatus, commonly known as the appendiculate blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, dark berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued both for its ecological role and potential culinary use.
