Rubus cariensis

Rubus cariensis, commonly known as the Carpathian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the local flora and ecosystem.
