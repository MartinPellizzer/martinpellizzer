Rhododendron wardii var. puralbum

Rhododendron wardii var. puralbum, commonly known as the White-flowered Rhododendron, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms.

This variety is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
