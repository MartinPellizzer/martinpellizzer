Rosa collivaga

Rosa collivaga, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Rosa collivaga has small, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white down.

It is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
