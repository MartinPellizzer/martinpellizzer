Rubus mallodes

Rubus mallodes, commonly known as the white-berried blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to edible, firm berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
