Rosa dalmatica var. frivaldskyi

Rosa dalmatica var. frivaldskyi, commonly known as the Dalmatian rose, is a variant of the Dalmatian rose that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula, particularly Croatia and Bosnia and Herzegovina. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in thorns, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
