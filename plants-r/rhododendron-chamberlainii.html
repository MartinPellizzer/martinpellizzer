Rhododendron chamberlainii

Rhododendron chamberlainii, commonly known as the Chamberlain's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
