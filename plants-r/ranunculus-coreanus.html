Ranunculus coreanus

Ranunculus coreanus, commonly known as the Korean buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with dark centers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Ranunculus coreanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate, spring-blooming flowers.
