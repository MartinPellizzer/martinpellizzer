Rosa ochroleuca

Rosa ochroleuca, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Rosa ochroleuca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.
