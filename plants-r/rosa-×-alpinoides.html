Rosa × alpinoides

Rosa × alpinoides, commonly known as the Alpina rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus, originating from crosses between Rosa alpina and Rosa polyantha.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it is well adapted to temperate climates.

Rosa × alpinoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
