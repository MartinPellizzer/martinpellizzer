Rubus caesius var. archetypus

Rubus caesius var. archetypus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and its clusters of small, red, edible berries. The plant produces flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which develop into the familiar red fruits after ripening. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
