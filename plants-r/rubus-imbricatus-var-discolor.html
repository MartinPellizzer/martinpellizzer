Rubus imbricatus var. discolor

Rubus imbricatus var. discolor, commonly known as the variable bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fuzzy berries in the fall. Its stems are often thorny, and the leaves have a distinctive whitish underside.

This variety is known for its adaptability and is often found in moist, shady habitats.
