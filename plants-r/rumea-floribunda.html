Rumea floribunda

Rumea floribunda, commonly known as the clustered bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Rumea floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
