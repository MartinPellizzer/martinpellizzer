Rosa communis var. vinacea

Rosa communis var. vinacea, commonly known as the crimson rose, is a cultivar of the common rose species.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering and pruning for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and fragrance.
