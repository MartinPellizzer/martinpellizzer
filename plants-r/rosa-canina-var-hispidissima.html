Rosa canina var. hispidissima

Rosa canina var. hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright red, fuzzy hips that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
