Rubus blanditus

Rubus blanditus, commonly known as the soft-fruited blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupelets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are popular in local cuisines.

The plant plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, providing food and habitat for various insects and birds.
