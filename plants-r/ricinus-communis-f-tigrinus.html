Ricinus communis f. tigrinus

Ricinus communis f. tigrinus, commonly known as the tiger castor, is a unique variant of the castor oil plant.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored bracts that display red, orange, and yellow hues, creating a vibrant visual display. The plant is a herbaceous annual with palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, powdery coating. It produces spiny, capsule-like fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
