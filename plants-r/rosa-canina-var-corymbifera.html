Rosa canina var. corymbifera

Rosa canina var. corymbifera, commonly known as the common dog rose, is a variety of the dog rose species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This shrub is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
