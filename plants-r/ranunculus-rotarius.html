Ranunculus rotarius

Ranunculus rotarius, commonly known as the wandering buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant features a short, upright stem with palmately divided, lobed leaves that are typically hairy. Its flowers are usually solitary, with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and numerous stamens surrounding a central pistil.

Ranunculus rotarius thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
