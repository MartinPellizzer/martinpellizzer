Rubus rivularis var. vallivivus

Rubus rivularis var. vallivivus, commonly known as the water raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to wetland and streamside habitats in North America.

It features slender, arching stems with thorny branches and trifoliate leaves that are typically reddish-tinged when young. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupes, which are often red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
