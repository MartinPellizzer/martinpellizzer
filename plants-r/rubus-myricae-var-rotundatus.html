Rubus myricae var. rotundatus

Rubus myricae var. rotundatus, commonly known as the round-leaved blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregated fruits. The leaves are oval to round in shape, with a serrated margin and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often tart.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
