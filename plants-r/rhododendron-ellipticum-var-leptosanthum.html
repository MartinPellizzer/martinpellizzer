Rhododendron ellipticum var. leptosanthum

Rhododendron ellipticum var. leptosanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slight pubescence on the underside. The plant produces dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers, usually in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
