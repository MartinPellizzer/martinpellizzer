Roupala longepetiolata var. diversifolia

Roupala longepetiolata var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable-leaf roupala, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, often hairy leaves that exhibit significant variation in shape and size across different parts of the plant. The leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive venation pattern, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
