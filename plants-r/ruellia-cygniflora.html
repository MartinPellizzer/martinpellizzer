Ruellia cygniflora

Ruellia cygniflora, commonly known as the pink ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ruellia cygniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
