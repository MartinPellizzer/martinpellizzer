Robertia taraxacoides

Robertia taraxacoides, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
