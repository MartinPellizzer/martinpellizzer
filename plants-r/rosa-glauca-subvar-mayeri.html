Rosa glauca subvar. mayeri

Rosa glauca subvar. mayeri, commonly known as Mayer's bluebloom rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often in clusters along the stems. The foliage is typically covered in a waxy, bluish-gray bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
