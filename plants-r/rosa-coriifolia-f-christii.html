Rosa coriifolia f. christii

Rosa coriifolia f. christii, commonly known as the Christ's rose or the Chinese rose, is a compact shrub characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers.

It features thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant is native to China and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
