Ribes gracillimum

Ribes gracillimum, commonly known as the slender gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly referred to as currants or gooseberries. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are small and clustered in cymes.

The berries it produces are edible, though they are generally smaller and less commonly cultivated compared to other Ribes species.
