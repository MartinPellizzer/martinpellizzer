Rosa arvensis var. multiflora

Rosa arvensis var. multiflora, commonly known as the field rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with arching stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, usually from June to August. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

The fruit is a red, hairy rose hip that contains numerous seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
