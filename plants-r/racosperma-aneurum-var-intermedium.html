Racosperma aneurum var. intermedium

Racosperma aneurum var. intermedium, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
