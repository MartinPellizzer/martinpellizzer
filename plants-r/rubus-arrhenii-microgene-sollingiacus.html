Rubus arrhenii microgene sollingiacus

Rubus arrhenii microgene sollingiacus, commonly known as the Solling raspberry, is a shrub native to the Solling region of Germany.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and culinary applications.
