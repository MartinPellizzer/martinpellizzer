Rhododendron sleumeri

Rhododendron sleumeri, commonly known as the Sleumer's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in Myanmar and Thailand.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist climate with high humidity. Its floral display is often noted for its fragrance and is a significant ornamental plant in horticulture.

Rhododendron sleumeri is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and food for various insect and bird species.
