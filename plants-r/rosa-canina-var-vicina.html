Rosa canina var. vicina

Rosa canina var. vicina, commonly known as the common dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces orange-red hips, which are the fruit and contain seeds. Rosa canina var. vicina is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
