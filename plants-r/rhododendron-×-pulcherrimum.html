Rhododendron × pulcherrimum

Rhododendron × pulcherrimum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink to rose-colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the environment.
