Racosperma uliginosum

Racosperma uliginosum, commonly known as the swamp acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spindly stems and compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in wet, swampy habitats and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Racosperma uliginosum is a member of the legume family and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
