Rubus glaucellus var. nudistylus

Rubus glaucellus var. nudistylus, commonly known as the naked-styled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The flowers are typically white or pale green, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry. This plant thrives in woodland and open areas, preferring well-drained soil.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
