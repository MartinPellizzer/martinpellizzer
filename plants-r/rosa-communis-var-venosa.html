Rosa communis var. venosa

Rosa communis var. venosa, commonly known as the variegated rose, is a cultivar of the common rose that exhibits distinctive variegation in its foliage.

Its leaves are typically a mix of green and white or yellow, creating an ornamental contrast that enhances its visual appeal. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the specific variety. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes rather than for commercial harvest.
