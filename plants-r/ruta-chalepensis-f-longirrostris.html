Ruta chalepensis f. longirrostris

Ruta chalepensis f. longirrostris, commonly known as the long-beaked rue, is a botanical variant of the common rue plant.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. This form is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is evergreen, with a compact, shrub-like growth habit.

It is often used in horticulture for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value.
