Rhynchospora scalaris

Rhynchospora scalaris, commonly known as the scalycup sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils.

Rhynchospora scalaris plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
