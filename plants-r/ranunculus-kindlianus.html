Ranunculus kindlianus

Ranunculus kindlianus, commonly known as the Kindli's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
