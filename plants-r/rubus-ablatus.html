Rubus ablatus

Rubus ablatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and thickets, preferring partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in jams and desserts.
