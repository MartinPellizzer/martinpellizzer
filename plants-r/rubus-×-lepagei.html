Rubus × lepagei

Rubus × lepagei, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

The botanical features of Rubus × lepagei make it a distinctive and often cultivated plant in horticulture.
