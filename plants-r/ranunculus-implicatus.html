Ranunculus implicatus

Ranunculus implicatus, commonly known as the hooked buttercup, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wet soils and is often found in riparian habitats.
