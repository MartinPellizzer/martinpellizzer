Rhynchosia insignis subsp. affinis

Rhynchosia insignis subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa. It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant bears pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical significance includes its role in soil enrichment due to nitrogen fixation.
