Rubus ruizii

Rubus ruizii, commonly known as the Ruiz blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America, specifically in countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, dark red berries that are edible. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and white to pink flowers that bloom in spring.

Rubus ruizii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisines.
