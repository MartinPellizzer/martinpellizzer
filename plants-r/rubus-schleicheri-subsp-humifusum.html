Rubus schleicheri subsp. humifusum

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

humifusum, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a low-growing shrub native to eastern Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a alternate pattern.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
