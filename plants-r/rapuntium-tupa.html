Rapuntium tupa

Rapuntium tupa, commonly known as the alpine cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent aroma. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines for their sharp, peppery flavor.

The plant's rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine ecosystems.
