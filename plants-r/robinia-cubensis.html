Robinia cubensis

Robinia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features pinnately compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
