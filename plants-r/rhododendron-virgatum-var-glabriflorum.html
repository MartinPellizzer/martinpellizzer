Rhododendron virgatum var. glabriflorum

Rhododendron virgatum var. glabriflorum, commonly known as the smoothflower azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.
