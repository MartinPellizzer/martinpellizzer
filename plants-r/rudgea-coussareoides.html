Rudgea coussareoides

Rudgea coussareoides, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, dark-colored berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
