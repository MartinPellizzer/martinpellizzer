Rosa arvensis var. subsericea

Rosa arvensis var. subsericea, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed dog rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The leaves are compound, typically with five to seven oval leaflets, and the plant produces rose hips that are often red or orange in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
