Rosa gallica var. pantui

Rosa gallica var. pantui, commonly known as the French pink, is a compact shrub native to central and southern Europe.

It produces showy, double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has deeply lobed, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.

This variety is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and as a source of rose hips.
