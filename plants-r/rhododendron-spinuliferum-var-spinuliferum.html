Rhododendron spinuliferum var. spinuliferum

Rhododendron spinuliferum var. spinuliferum, commonly known as the prickly rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, humid environments. Its leaves are leathery and covered with fine, backward-pointing hairs, giving them a distinctive, prickly texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
