Rosa woodsii subsp. gratissima

Rosa woodsii subsp.

gratissima, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and the plant produces red-orange hips that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland and scrubland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.
