Ranunculus auricomus subsp. abortificus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

abortificus, commonly known as the abortive buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers with large, showy, palmate petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in temperate regions. It has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced floral structures and shorter pedicels compared to the typical form of Ranunculus auricomus.
