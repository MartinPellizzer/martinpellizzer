Rosa multiflora var. roseiflora

Rosa multiflora var. roseiflora, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters during late spring to early summer. The plant has arching stems with thorny branches and dark green, serrated leaves. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
