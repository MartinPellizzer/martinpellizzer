Rubus interruptus

Rubus interruptus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberries, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to various regions of North America and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries.

Rubus interruptus plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
