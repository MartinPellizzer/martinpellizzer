Rosa afzeliana var. prostata

Rosa afzeliana var. prostata, commonly known as the dwarf rose or prostrate rose, is a low-growing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features woody stems that lie close to the ground, forming a mat-like structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often armed with stiff, backward-pointing prickles.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
