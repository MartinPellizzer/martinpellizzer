Rubus radula f. armatus

Rubus radula f. armatus, commonly known as the armated blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species characterized by its unique thorned structure.

This plant is native to parts of North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is often found in woodlands and disturbed areas, where it spreads through both seeds and vegetative growth.

Due to its robust nature, it is considered a resilient species that can tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
