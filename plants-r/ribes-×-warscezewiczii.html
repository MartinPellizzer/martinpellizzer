Ribes × warscezewiczii

Ribes × warscezewiczii, commonly known as the hybrid gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a cross between Ribes sanguineum (red gooseberry) and Ribes aureum (golden gooseberry), resulting in a plant with distinctive ornamental features. The leaves are typically dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge, while the flowers are usually pink or red, adding visual interest in spring. It produces small, edible berries that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety.

This plant is valued for its hardiness, adaptability, and aesthetic appeal in both gardens and natural landscapes.
