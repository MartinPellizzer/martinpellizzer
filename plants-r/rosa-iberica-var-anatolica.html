Rosa iberica var. anatolica

Rosa iberica var. anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Anatolia and the Balkans.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in stiff, thorny bracts.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
