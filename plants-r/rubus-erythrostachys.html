Rubus erythrostachys

Rubus erythrostachys, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
