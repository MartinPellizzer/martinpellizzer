Rubus vaccarum

Rubus vaccarum, commonly known as sweetbriar, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded locations.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in horticulture and natural habitats.
