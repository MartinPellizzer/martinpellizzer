Ruellia nudiflora var. puberula

Ruellia nudiflora var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ruellia nudiflora var. puberula is a herbaceous perennial that is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
