Rosa oleifera

Rosa oleifera, commonly known as the Chinese rose or evergreen rose, is a shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant is evergreen, with leathery, dark green leaves that provide year-round foliage. Rosa oleifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.

Its flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
