Rubus obtruncatus microgene ardens

Rubus obtruncatus microgene ardens, commonly known as the red raspberries, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets. The plant produces bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits and is valued in both wild and agricultural settings.
