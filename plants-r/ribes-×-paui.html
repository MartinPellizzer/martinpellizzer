Ribes × paui

Ribes × paui, commonly known as the hybrid currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of gooseberries and currants. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features alternate, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, clustered in cymes, and are usually pink or red, producing a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are edible and have a tart flavor, making them valuable for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
