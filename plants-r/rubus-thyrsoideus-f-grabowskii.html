Rubus thyrsoideus f. grabowskii

Rubus thyrsoideus f. grabowskii, commonly known as the Grabowskii raspberry, is a botanical variant of the raspberry plant native to eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the production of small, red, edible berries. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soils. It has a shallow root system and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its flowers are white and have five petals, followed by the development of its distinctive fruit.
