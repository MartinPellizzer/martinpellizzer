Rosa hispidocarpa

Rosa hispidocarpa, commonly known as the hispid prickly rose, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink to white flowers. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are edible and often used in herbal remedies. Rosa hispidocarpa thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
