Robinia pseudoacacia var. linearis

Robinia pseudoacacia var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false acacia, is a variant of the honey locust tree.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are narrower than those of the typical Robinia pseudoacacia. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub native to the southeastern United States. Its small, yellow flowers appear in summer and are followed by long, flat pods.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
