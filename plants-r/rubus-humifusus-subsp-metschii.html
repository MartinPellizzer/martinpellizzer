Rubus humifusus subsp. metschii

Rubus humifusus subsp.

metschii, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features trailing stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats on the ground.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
