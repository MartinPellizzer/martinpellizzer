Rhaphidophora sulcata

Rhaphidophora sulcata, commonly known as the Shingle Plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Tanzania and Kenya. The plant is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that have a distinctive corrugated texture. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

Rhaphidophora sulcata is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
