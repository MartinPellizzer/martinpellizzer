Rubus agilis

Rubus agilis, commonly known as the agile blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus agilis is often found in open woodlands, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.
