Rosa verecunda

Rosa verecunda, commonly known as the shy rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and they open slowly, giving the plant its name.

Rosa verecunda is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
