Rauwenhoffia uvarioides

Rauwenhoffia uvarioides, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a slightly velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in shaded areas.

Rauwenhoffia uvarioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
