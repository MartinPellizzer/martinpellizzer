Rubus thyrsoideus f. subpubescens

Rubus thyrsoideus f. subpubescens, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a variant of the thyrsoideus raspberry species.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in culinary applications.
