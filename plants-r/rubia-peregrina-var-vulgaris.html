Rubia peregrina var. vulgaris

Rubia peregrina var. vulgaris, commonly known as common madder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, thriving in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, and its roots contain a red dye used historically in textiles and medicine. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
