Ribes canthariforme

Ribes canthariforme, commonly known as the scarlet currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of gooseberries and currants. The plant is characterized by its bright red, edible berries that ripen in the summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are often covered in a fine pubescence.

Ribes canthariforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and fruits.
