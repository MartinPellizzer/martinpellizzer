Rubus podophyllos subsp. brachyodontus

Rubus podophyllos subsp.

brachyodontus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with narrow, serrated lobes and thorny stems.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique floral and fruit morphology.
