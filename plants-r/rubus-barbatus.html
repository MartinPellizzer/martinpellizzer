Rubus barbatus

Rubus barbatus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries in the summer. These berries are aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupes, and they are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Rubus barbatus is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.
