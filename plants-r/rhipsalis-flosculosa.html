Rhipsalis flosculosa

Rhipsalis flosculosa, commonly known as the snowflake cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its branched, slender stems covered in small, white spines. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that resemble snowflakes, giving it its common name. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant.

Rhipsalis flosculosa is known for its delicate appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
