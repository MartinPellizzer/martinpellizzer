Rubus picticaulis

Rubus picticaulis, commonly known as the painted bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. Its berries are edible and often used in jams and pies, though the plant is generally considered invasive in some regions.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value due to its distinctive foliage and vibrant fruit.
