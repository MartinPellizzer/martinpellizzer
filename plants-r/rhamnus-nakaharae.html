Rhamnus nakaharae

Rhamnus nakaharae, commonly known as Nakahara's buckthorn, is a shrub native to Japan, specifically found on the island of Kyushu.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, dark berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
