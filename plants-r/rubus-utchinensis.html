Rubus utchinensis

Rubus utchinensis, commonly known as the Uchto blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. These berries are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
