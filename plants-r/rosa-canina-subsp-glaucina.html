Rosa canina subsp. glaucina

Rosa canina subsp.

glaucina, commonly known as the glaucous dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and hairy stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically pinnate with five to seven leaflets, and the plant produces red, hairy hips that are rich in vitamin C. This subspecies thrives in open, sunny habitats such as woodland edges and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
