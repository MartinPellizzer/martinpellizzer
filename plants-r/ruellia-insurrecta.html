Ruellia insurrecta

Ruellia insurrecta, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Ruellia insurrecta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
