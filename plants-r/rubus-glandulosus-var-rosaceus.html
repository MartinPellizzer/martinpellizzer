Rubus glandulosus var. rosaceus

Rubus glandulosus var. rosaceus, commonly known as the Rosaceus raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a distinctive, sweet aroma. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than other raspberry species.
