Rubus × semirufescens

Rubus × semirufescens, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may also be black or purple. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
