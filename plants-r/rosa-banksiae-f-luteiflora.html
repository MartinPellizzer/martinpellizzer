Rosa banksiae f. luteiflora

Rosa banksiae f. luteiflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered banksia rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa banksiae and Rosa lutea.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
