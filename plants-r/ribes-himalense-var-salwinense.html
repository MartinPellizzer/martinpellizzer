Ribes himalense var. salwinense

Ribes himalense var. salwinense, commonly known as the Salwin gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in traditional medicine.
