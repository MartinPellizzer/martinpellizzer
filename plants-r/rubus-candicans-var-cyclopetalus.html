Rubus candicans var. cyclopetalus

Rubus candicans var. cyclopetalus, commonly known as the cyclopetal raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits composed of multiple drupelets. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor and are often used in jams and desserts.
