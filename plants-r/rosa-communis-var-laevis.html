Rosa communis var. laevis

Rosa communis var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a cultivar characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and foliage.

It produces large, showy flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, typically with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions and is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is prized for its beauty and is often used in gardens and floral arrangements.
