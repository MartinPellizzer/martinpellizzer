Rosa oligosperma

Rosa oligosperma, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often blooming in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Rosa oligosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
