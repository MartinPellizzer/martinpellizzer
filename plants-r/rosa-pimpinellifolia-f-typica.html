Rosa pimpinellifolia f. typica

Rosa pimpinellifolia f. typica, commonly known as the common rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and small, thornless stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in spring, which are followed by orange-red hips. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
