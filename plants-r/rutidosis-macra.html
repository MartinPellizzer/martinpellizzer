Rutidosis macra

Rutidosis macra, commonly known as the bluebell of the south, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

Rutidosis macra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
