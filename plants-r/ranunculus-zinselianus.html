Ranunculus zinselianus

Ranunculus zinselianus, commonly known as the Zinsel's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically with 5 to 15 petals. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ranunculus zinselianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
