Rubus luchunensis var. coriaceus

Rubus luchunensis var. coriaceus, commonly known as the Luchun bramble, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
