Rubus sprengeliusculus

Rubus sprengeliusculus, commonly known as the Sprengel's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and typically grows in open, sunny habitats. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.

The fruit is edible and has been used traditionally by local communities for food and medicinal purposes.
