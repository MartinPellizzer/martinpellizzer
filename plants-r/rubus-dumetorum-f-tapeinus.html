Rubus dumetorum f. tapeinus

Rubus dumetorum f. tapeinus, commonly known as the blackberry dwarf, is a wild, low-growing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
