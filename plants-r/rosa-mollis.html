Rosa mollis

Rosa mollis, commonly known as the soft rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rosa mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
