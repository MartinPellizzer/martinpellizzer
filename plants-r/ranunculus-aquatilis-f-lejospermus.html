Ranunculus aquatilis f. lejospermus

Ranunculus aquatilis f. lejospermus, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, floating leaves that form a rosette on the water surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams.

Its presence indicates good water quality and is often used as an indicator species in aquatic ecosystems.
