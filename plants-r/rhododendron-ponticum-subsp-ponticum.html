Rhododendron ponticum subsp. ponticum

Rhododendron ponticum subsp.

ponticum, commonly known as the Turkish rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in woodland and scrub habitats, often in areas with acidic soils.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and elliptical in shape, providing dense foliage.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value but can also be invasive in certain environments.
