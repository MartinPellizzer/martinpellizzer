Rubus moluccanus

Rubus moluccanus, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The botanical features of Rubus moluccanus make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique adaptations suited to its native habitat.
