Ranunculus macrophyllus var. procerus

Ranunculus macrophyllus var. procerus, commonly known as the giant water buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the wetlands of North America.

It features large, showy yellow flowers with 5-15 sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are broad, floating, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This species is an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
