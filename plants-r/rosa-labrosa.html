Rosa labrosa

Rosa labrosa, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that develop after the flowers fade.

Rosa labrosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
