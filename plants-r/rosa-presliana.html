Rosa presliana

Rosa presliana, commonly known as the Preslia rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage, which provide habitat for various small animals and insects. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rosa presliana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
