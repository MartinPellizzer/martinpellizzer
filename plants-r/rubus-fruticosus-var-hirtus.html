Rubus fruticosus var. hirtus

Rubus fruticosus var. hirtus, commonly known as brambles or blackberries, is a thorny shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its prickly stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color, often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

The species is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative growth.
