Rubus × hematostylus

Rubus × hematostylus, commonly known as the red-veined blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually trifoliate with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries, which are dark purple to black when mature, are edible and often used in culinary applications.
