Rubus sauli

Rubus sauli, commonly known as the Salish raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in forest understories. Rubus sauli is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Indigenous practices.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the region's ecological and cultural heritage.
