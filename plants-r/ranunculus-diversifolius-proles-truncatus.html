Ranunculus diversifolius proles truncatus

Ranunculus diversifolius proles truncatus, commonly known as the truncate-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
