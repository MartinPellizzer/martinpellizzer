Rhododendron valentinianum

Rhododendron valentinianum, commonly known as the Valentine's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, favoring acidic soil conditions. Its flowers range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting white throat, making it a striking ornamental plant.

It is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to various garden settings.
