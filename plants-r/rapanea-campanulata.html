Rapanea campanulata

Rapanea campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped evergreen, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, black, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
