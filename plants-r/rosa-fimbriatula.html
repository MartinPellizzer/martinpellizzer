Rosa fimbriatula

Rosa fimbriatula, commonly known as the fringed rose, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorny projections. Rosa fimbriatula prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
