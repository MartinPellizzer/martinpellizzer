Rumex crispatulus

Rumex crispatulus, commonly known as the curly dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its wavy, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on tall, erect stalks. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils.

Rumex crispatulus is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in other damp environments.
