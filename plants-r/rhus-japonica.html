Rhus japonica

Rhus japonica, commonly known as Japanese lacquer tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in upright clusters, followed by distinctive red, spiny fruits that mature in the fall. Rhus japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique fruiting structures.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
