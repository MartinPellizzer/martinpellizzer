Rheedia arenicola

Rheedia arenicola, commonly known as the coastal rheedia, is a flowering plant native to the sandy coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in well-drained, saline soils typical of beach environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the cooler months.

Rheedia arenicola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dune systems and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
