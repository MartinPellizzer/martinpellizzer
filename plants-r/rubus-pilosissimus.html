Rubus pilosissimus

Rubus pilosissimus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. These berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
