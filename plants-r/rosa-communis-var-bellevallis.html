Rosa communis var. bellevallis

Rosa communis var. bellevallis, commonly known as the Bellevalle rose, is a cultivar prized for its fragrant, semi-double pink blooms.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote healthy growth.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
