Ranunculus auricomus subsp. oligandrus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

oligandrus, commonly known as the lesser buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often surrounded by a cluster of bright yellow stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It is characterized by its reduced number of stamens compared to the typical species, giving it its subspecies name "oligandrus.".
