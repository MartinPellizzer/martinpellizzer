Rosa dumalis subsp. rubelliflora

Rosa dumalis subsp.

rubelliflora, commonly known as the red-flowered rock rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

This species is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
