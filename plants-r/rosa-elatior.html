Rosa elatior

Rosa elatior, commonly known as the everlasting rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan, Pakistan, and the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody and thornless.

Rosa elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
