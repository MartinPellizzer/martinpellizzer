Rosa canina subvar. oblonga

Rosa canina subvar. oblonga, commonly known as the oval hip rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive oval-shaped hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are often armed with thorns.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for wildlife.
