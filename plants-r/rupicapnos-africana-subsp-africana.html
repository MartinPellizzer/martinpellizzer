Rupicapnos africana subsp. africana

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling flames, hence its common name.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions.
