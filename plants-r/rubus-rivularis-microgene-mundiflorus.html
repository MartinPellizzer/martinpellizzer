Rubus rivularis microgene mundiflorus

Rubus rivularis microgene mundiflorus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a low-growing shrub native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions.

It produces small, dark purple berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, persistent hairs. It thrives in rich, well-drained soils and is often found along stream banks and in woodland understories.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
