Rhododendron cruentum

Rhododendron cruentum, commonly known as the bloodroot rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are dark green and elliptical, with a smooth texture.

Rhododendron cruentum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
