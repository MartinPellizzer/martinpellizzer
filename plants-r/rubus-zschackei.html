Rubus zschackei

Rubus zschackei, commonly known as the Zschackei blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus zschackei is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
