Rosa pampinula

Rosa pampinula, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are compound, with multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a trailing growth habit.

Rosa pampinula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
