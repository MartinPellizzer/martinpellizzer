Rhynchospora fascicularis var. fernaldii

Rhynchospora fascicularis var. fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald's fasciated flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.

The variety is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology, including the presence of fasciation, which gives it a distinctive appearance.
