Ranunculus grandiflorus subsp. davisii

Ranunculus grandiflorus subsp.

davisii, commonly known as the Davis' buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a waxy coating.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often striking floral displays.
