Rubus fictus var. obtortus

Rubus fictus var. obtortus, commonly known as the twisted blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its unique growth habit, with stems that often twist and curl in unusual patterns. The leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated along the edges. It produces small, dark red berries that are usually less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of woodland environments.
