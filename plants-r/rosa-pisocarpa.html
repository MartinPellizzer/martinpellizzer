Rosa pisocarpa

Rosa pisocarpa, commonly known as the Ecuadorian rose, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in cool, humid climates.

Rosa pisocarpa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological and cultural significance in its native habitat.
