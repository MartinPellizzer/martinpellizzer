Rosa glauca var. pseudomarsica

Rosa glauca var. pseudomarsica, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rose, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and small, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with a silvery underside. This variety is adapted to arid environments, featuring drought-resistant characteristics and deep root systems.

It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.
