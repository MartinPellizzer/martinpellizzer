Rhododendron pomense

Rhododendron pomense, commonly known as the Pomona rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, and it prefers acidic, well-drained soils.

Rhododendron pomense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shady, humid environments.
