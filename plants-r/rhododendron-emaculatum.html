Rhododendron emaculatum

Rhododendron emaculatum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, providing an attractive evergreen backdrop to the seasonal blooms.

Rhododendron emaculatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
