Rhynchospora capitellata f. discutiens

Rhynchospora capitellata f. discutiens, commonly known as the rough-leaved beakspur, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, rough-textured leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive beak-like shape at the tip of the spike.

This botanical feature contributes to its unique appearance and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
