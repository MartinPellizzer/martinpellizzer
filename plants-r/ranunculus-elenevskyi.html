Ranunculus elenevskyi

Ranunculus elenevskyi, commonly known as the Elenovsky buttercup, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has palmately divided leaves and produces yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
