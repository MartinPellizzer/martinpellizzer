Racosperma sibinum

Racosperma sibinum, commonly known as the Sibinum acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in indigenous Australian medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.

Racosperma sibinum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
