Rosa chinensis var. semperflorens

Rosa chinensis var. semperflorens, commonly known as the Chinese rose or everblooming rose, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom continuously throughout the growing season. Its flowers are borne on long pedicels and are often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant is well adapted to warm climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and fragrance.
