Rosa scabricamuridens

Rosa scabricamuridens, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its woody stems covered in a rough, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa scabricamuridens is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
