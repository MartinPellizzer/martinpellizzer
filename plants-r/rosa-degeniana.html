Rosa degeniana

Rosa degeniana, commonly known as the Degen's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Peru. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are thorny.

Rosa degeniana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its beauty and its historical significance in traditional medicine.
