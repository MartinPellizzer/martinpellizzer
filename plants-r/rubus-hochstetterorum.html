Rubus hochstetterorum

Rubus hochstetterorum, commonly known as the Hochstetter's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
