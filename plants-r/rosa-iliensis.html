Rosa iliensis

Rosa iliensis, commonly known as the Ili Valley rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are borne on long pedicels. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are armed with thorns.

Rosa iliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
