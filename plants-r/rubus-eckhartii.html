Rubus eckhartii

Rubus eckhartii, commonly known as the Eckhart's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Rubus eckhartii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
