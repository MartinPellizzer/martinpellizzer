Rabdosia angustifolia

Rabdosia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually purple or pink, and it produces small, elliptical seeds.

Rabdosia angustifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
