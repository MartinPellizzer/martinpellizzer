Rubus bayeri var. gracilescens

Rubus bayeri var. gracilescens, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
