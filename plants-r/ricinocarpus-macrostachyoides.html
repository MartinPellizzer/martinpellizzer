Ricinocarpus macrostachyoides

Ricinocarpus macrostachyoides, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in saline and brackish water environments, making it well-adapted to coastal and mangrove habitats. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by water.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
