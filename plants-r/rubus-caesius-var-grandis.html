Rubus caesius var. grandis

Rubus caesius var. grandis, commonly known as the large red raspberry, is a thorny shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small, bright red drupelets that are highly aromatic and tart in flavor. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are trifoliate with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

It is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in culinary applications.
