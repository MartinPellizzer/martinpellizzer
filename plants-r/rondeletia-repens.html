Rondeletia repens

Rondeletia repens, commonly known as the prostrate rondeletia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically spreads along the ground, forming a dense mat. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value.
