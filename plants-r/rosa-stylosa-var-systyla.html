Rosa stylosa var. systyla

Rosa stylosa var. systyla, commonly known as the pink sandalwood rose, is a variant of the sandalwood rose native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their subtle, sweet scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
