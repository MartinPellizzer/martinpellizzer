Rhododendron leptobrachion

Rhododendron leptobrachion, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a distinct texture to the plant's appearance.

The flowers range in color from pink to red, making it a notable ornamental plant in horticulture.
