Rosa canina var. humilis

Rosa canina var. humilis, commonly known as the common dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
