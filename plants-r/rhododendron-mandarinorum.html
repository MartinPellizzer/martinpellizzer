Rhododendron mandarinorum

Rhododendron mandarinorum, commonly known as the Mandarin rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically the provinces of Sichuan and Yunnan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires well-drained soil and partial shade to thrive.
