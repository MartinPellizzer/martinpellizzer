Rhaphidophora trinervia

Rhaphidophora trinervia, commonly known as the Zebra Plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, striped, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a compact, upright growth habit. Its foliage is broad and glossy, often with a variegated pattern of green and white.

The flowers are small and clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, which is enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe.
