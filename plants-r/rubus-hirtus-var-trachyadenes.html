Rubus hirtus var. trachyadenes

Rubus hirtus var. trachyadenes, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically tart and less sweet than common blackberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

The botanical features of this variant make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
