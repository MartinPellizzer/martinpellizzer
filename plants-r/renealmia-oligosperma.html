Renealmia oligosperma

Renealmia oligosperma, commonly known as the slender marsh grass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Renealmia oligosperma is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
