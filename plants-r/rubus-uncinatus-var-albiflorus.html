Rubus uncinatus var. albiflorus

Rubus uncinatus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its pale white to pale yellow flowers.

It is a shrub native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in prickles.

This variety is valued for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
