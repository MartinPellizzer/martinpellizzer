Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pulcher

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pulcher, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a distinctive greenish or reddish center.

The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
