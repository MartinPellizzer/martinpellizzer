Raveniopsis capitata

Raveniopsis capitata, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters at the top of the stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Raveniopsis capitata is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for.
