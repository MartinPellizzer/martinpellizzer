Rosa accurrens

Rosa accurrens, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa accurrens is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks.
