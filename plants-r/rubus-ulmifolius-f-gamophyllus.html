Rubus ulmifolius f. gamophyllus

Rubus ulmifolius f. gamophyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its smaller, more rounded leaves compared to the typical form. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Rubus genus.
