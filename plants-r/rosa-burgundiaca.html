Rosa burgundiaca

Rosa burgundiaca, commonly known as the Burgundy rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa burgundiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
