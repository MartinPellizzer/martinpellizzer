Rosa sempervirens var. leptostyla

Rosa sempervirens var. leptostyla, commonly known as the slenderleaf evergreen rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers in spring. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for coastal gardens due to its tolerance of salty air and well-drained soils.
