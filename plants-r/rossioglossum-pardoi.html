Rossioglossum pardoi

Rossioglossum pardoi, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green coloration, with elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months of the year. Rossioglossum pardoi thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
