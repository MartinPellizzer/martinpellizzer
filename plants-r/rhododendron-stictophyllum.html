Rhododendron stictophyllum

Rhododendron stictophyllum, commonly known as the western rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils within coniferous forests. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its floral display.

Rhododendron stictophyllum plays an important ecological role by supporting various pollinators and providing habitat for wildlife.
