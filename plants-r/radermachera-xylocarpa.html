Radermachera xylocarpa

Radermachera xylocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or the red-veined maple, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that often display striking red or purple veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is popular as a houseplant and in ornamental horticulture.
