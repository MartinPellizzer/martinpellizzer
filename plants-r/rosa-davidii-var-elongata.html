Rosa davidii var. elongata

Rosa davidii var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated David rose, is a climbing shrub native to China.

It features long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length, often with thorns. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
