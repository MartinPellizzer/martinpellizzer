Rubus housei

Rubus housei, commonly known as the House's raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it flowers with white or pinkish inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
