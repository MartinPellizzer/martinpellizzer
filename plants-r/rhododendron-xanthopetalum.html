Rhododendron xanthopetalum

Rhododendron xanthopetalum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high elevations, often in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow to orange-red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Rhododendron xanthopetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
