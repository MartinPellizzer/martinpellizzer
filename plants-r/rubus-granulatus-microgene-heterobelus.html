Rubus granulatus microgene heterobelus

Rubus granulatus microgene heterobelus, commonly known as the granular raspberry.

This plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The plant is known for its edible berries, which are small, tart, and often used in jams and desserts.
