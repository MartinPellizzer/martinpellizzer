Rhynchosia flava

Rhynchosia flava, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its pods are elongated and contain edible seeds similar to those of other cowpea species.

Rhynchosia flava is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is also used in traditional medicine.
