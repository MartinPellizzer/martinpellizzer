Rubus idaeus var. americanus

Rubus idaeus var. americanus, commonly known as the American raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The American raspberry is valued for its edible berries and its role in ecological systems.
