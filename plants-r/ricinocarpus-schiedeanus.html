Ricinocarpus schiedeanus

Ricinocarpus schiedeanus, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and pale in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
