Rosa alpina var. subhispida

Rosa alpina var. subhispida, commonly known as the Alpine rose or alpine rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
