Rubus melanoxylon microgene amplus

Rubus melanoxylon microgene amplus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere, particularly South America, where it thrives in diverse habitats such as forests and scrublands. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, dark-colored berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its ability to form dense thickets.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique genetic variation and ecological adaptability.
