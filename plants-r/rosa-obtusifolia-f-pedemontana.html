Rosa obtusifolia f. pedemontana

Rosa obtusifolia f. pedemontana, commonly known as the Piedmont rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

The foliage remains attractive throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value in garden settings.
