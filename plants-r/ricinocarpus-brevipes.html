Ricinocarpus brevipes

Ricinocarpus brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Ricinocarpus brevipes is adapted to coastal and lowland environments, often growing in sandy or disturbed soils.
