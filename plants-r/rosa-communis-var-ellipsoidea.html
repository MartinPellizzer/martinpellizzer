Rosa communis var. ellipsoidea

Rosa communis var. ellipsoidea, commonly known as the elliptic rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in the spring. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
