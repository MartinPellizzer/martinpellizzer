Rosa serratidenta

Rosa serratidenta, commonly known as the serrated-leaved rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its stems are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, and it produces small, orange-colored fruits that contain seeds.

Rosa serratidenta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
