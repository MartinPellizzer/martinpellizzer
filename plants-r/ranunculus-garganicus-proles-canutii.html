Ranunculus garganicus proles canutii

Ranunculus garganicus proles canutii, commonly known as the Canary Islands buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in coastal areas and open habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a distinctive lobed shape, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
