Rapistrum rugosum proles microcarpum

Rapistrum rugosum proles microcarpum, commonly known as the slender wild radish, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, elongated leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by small, round pods containing several seeds. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
