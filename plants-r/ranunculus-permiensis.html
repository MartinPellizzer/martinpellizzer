Ranunculus permiensis

Ranunculus permiensis, commonly known as the Permiense buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers consist of several sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus permiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
