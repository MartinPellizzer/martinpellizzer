Rubus ulmifolius var. geminatus

Rubus ulmifolius var. geminatus, commonly known as the twin-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are typically divided into two lobes, giving it a unique appearance. This plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodlands and hedgerows. The flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
