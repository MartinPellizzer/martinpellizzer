Rosa vulpina

Rosa vulpina, commonly known as the foxglove rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, thorny stems and elliptic leaves with serrated edges. Rosa vulpina is often found in woodland edges and open meadows, thriving in well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
