Rhododendron campanulatum var. violaceum

Rhododendron campanulatum var. violaceum, commonly known as the purple bell rhododendron, is a flowering plant characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-pink flowers.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub with dark green, leathery leaves. Its floral display occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to acidic, well-drained soils.
