Rosa tristis

Rosa tristis, commonly known as the sad rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are typically red or orange.

Rosa tristis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
