Rosa paucifoliata

Rosa paucifoliata, commonly known as the sparse-leaved rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa paucifoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
