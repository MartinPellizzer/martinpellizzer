Rubus rivularis var. adenophyllus

Rubus rivularis var. adenophyllus, commonly known as the腺叶黑莓, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically clustered together. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of temperate flora.
