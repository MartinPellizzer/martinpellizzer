Rebutia permutata f. gokrausei

Rebutia permutata f. gokrausei, commonly known as the Gokrausei Rebutia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features compact, rounded stems that are typically green with a bluish or purplish tint. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the summer months. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for water storage.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
