Ranunculus flabellatus var. granulatus

Ranunculus flabellatus var. granulatus, commonly known as the granulated buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The varietal name "granulatus" refers to the finely granular texture of its leaves.
