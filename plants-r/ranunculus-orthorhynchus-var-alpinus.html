Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. alpinus

Ranunculus orthorhynchus var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features showy yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
