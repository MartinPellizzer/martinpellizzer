Rubus explanatus

Rubus explanatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are often referred to as blackberries, and are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in woodland and shrubland habitats.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
