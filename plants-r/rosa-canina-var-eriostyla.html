Rosa canina var. eriostyla

Rosa canina var. eriostyla, commonly known as the sticky rose or prickly rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically pinnately compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodlands and scrublands.

It produces red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine.
