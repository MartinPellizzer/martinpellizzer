Rubus turcomanicus

Rubus turcomanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan raspberry, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of edible berries.
