Rubus glaucellus var. longiglandulosus

Rubus glaucellus var. longiglandulosus, commonly known as the long-haired raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's stems. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, hairy berries that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and covered in fine, glandular hairs.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside habitats.
