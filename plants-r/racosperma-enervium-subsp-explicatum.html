Racosperma enervium subsp. explicatum

Racosperma enervium subsp.

explicatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow flowers in dense spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native fauna.
