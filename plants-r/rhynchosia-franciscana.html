Rhynchosia franciscana

Rhynchosia franciscana, commonly known as the pink river bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces striking pink to purple flowers and bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and is often found in moist, tropical environments. It has a shallow root system and thrives in full sun.

Rhynchosia franciscana is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
