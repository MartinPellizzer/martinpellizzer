Restio esterhuyseniae

Restio esterhuyseniae, commonly known as the Esterhuysenia restio, is a distinctive grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
