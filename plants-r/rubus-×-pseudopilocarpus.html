Rubus × pseudopilocarpus

Rubus × pseudopilocarpus, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant produces large, dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and sweeter than its parent species. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This hybrid is valued for its fruit quality and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
