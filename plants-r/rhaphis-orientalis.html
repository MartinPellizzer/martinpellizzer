Rhaphis orientalis

Rhaphis orientalis, commonly known as the golden cane palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching stems that produce long, feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its foliage is often a striking golden-green color, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Rhaphis orientalis prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
