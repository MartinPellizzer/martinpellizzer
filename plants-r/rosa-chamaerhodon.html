Rosa chamaerhodon

Rosa chamaerhodon, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. This shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and evergreen foliage. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
