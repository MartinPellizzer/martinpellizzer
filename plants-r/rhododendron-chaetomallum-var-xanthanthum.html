Rhododendron chaetomallum var. xanthanthum

Rhododendron chaetomallum var. xanthanthum, commonly known as the golden-flowered rhododendron, is a variant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its bright floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
