Rubus kollundicola

Rubus kollundicola, commonly known as the Kollunda bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to specific regions in Europe, particularly the Czech Republic and parts of Germany. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
