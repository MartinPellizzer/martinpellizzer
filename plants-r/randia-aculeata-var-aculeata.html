Randia aculeata var. aculeata

Randia aculeata var. aculeata, commonly known as the spiked randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, simple, and often spiny leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
