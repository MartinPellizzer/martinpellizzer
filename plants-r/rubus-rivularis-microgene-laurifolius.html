Rubus rivularis microgene laurifolius

Rubus rivularis microgene laurifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in woodland understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
