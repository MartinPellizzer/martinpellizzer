Rhododendron caucasicum var. roseum-variabile

Rhododendron caucasicum var. roseum-variabile, commonly known as the Caucasian roseum-variabile rhododendron, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas with acidic, well-drained soil. It prefers partial shade to full sun and is often found in forested regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
