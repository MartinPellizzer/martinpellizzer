Rosa reginae

Rosa reginae, commonly known as the Queen's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features woody stems with thorns and compound leaves composed of five to seven leaflets. Its flowers are large, showy, and usually pink or red, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa reginae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
