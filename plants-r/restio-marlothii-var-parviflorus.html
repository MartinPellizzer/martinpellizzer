Restio marlothii var. parviflorus

Restio marlothii var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
