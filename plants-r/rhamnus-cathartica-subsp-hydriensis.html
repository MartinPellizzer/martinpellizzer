Rhamnus cathartica subsp. hydriensis

Rhamnus cathartica subsp.

hydriensis, commonly known as the Hydriensis buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces small, black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, woodland soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting local fauna and its historical use in traditional medicine.
