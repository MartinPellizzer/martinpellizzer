Rubus radula var. foliosus

Rubus radula var. foliosus, commonly known as the leafy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, dark berries that mature in late summer. The leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs. This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
