Rosularia pestalozzae

Rosularia pestalozzae, commonly known as the Pestalozza's rosebay, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in its arid environment.

Rosularia pestalozzae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine collections.
