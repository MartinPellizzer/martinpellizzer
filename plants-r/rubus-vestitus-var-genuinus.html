Rubus vestitus var. genuinus

Rubus vestitus var. genuinus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

This variant of the blackberry plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems. It produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
