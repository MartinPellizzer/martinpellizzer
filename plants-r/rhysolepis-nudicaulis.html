Rhysolepis nudicaulis

Rhysolepis nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and clusters of blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.
