Rubus serpens leptadenes

Rubus serpens leptadenes, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, dark red to black drupelets. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
