Russelia alata

Russelia alata, commonly known as the hummingbird flower, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a slightly fuzzy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soil.

Russelia alata is a popular choice for gardens due to its ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
