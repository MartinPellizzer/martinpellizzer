Rubus gracilis var. chiliacanthus

Rubus gracilis var. chiliacanthus, commonly known as the chili cane raspberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, thornless stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely toothed margins, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and the presence of hairy veins on its foliage.
