Rubus hennebergensis subsp. subbavaricus

Rubus hennebergensis subsp.

subbavaricus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
