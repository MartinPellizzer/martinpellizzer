Rosa × gallicoides var. pseudostylosa

Rosa × gallicoides var. pseudostylosa, commonly known as the French rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to the temperate regions of Europe. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of silvery-white hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
