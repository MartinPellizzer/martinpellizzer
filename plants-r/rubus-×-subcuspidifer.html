Rubus × subcuspidifer

Rubus × subcuspidifer, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than its parent species.
