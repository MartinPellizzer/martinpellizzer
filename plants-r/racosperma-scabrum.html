Racosperma scabrum

Racosperma scabrum, commonly known as the rough billygoat pea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves that are trifoliate, with three leaflets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Racosperma scabrum is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
