Ranunculus auricomus subsp. brabantianus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

brabantianus, commonly known as the Brabant buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five broad, petal-like sepals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It has trifoliate basal leaves with serrated edges.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe, including the Netherlands and Belgium.
