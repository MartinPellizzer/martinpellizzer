Rubus villicaulis var. parvulus

Rubus villicaulis var. parvulus, commonly known as the small-fruited blackberry shrub, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.
