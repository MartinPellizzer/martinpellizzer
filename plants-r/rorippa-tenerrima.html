Rorippa tenerrima

Rorippa tenerrima, commonly known as the water cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, alternate leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces tiny white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. It is typically found in wetlands, streams, and other freshwater habitats.
