Ricinus communis f. ostrinatus

Ricinus communis f. ostrinatus, commonly known as the crimson thornapple, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its striking red bracts.

This ornamental plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with the showy red bracts serving as the primary attraction.

It is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and is used in gardens for its aesthetic value.
