Rubus hypopitys var. hanmiensis

Rubus hypopitys var. hanmiensis, commonly known as the Hanmi blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
