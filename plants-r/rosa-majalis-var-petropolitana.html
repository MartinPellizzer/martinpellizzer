Rosa majalis var. petropolitana

Rosa majalis var. petropolitana, commonly known as the Siberian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Russia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorny buds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in colder regions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh winter conditions.
