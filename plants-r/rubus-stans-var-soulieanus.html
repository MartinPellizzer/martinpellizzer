Rubus stans var. soulieanus

Rubus stans var. soulieanus, commonly known as the Souchong raspberry, is a variant of the trailing raspberry species native to China.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and thorny stems, which help it anchor to the ground. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, with a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy surface.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries in certain regions.
