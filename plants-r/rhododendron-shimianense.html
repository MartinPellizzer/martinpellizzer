Rhododendron shimianense

Rhododendron shimianense, commonly known as the Shimian rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan and Yunnan provinces in China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include thick, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
