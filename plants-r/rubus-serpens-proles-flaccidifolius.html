Rubus serpens proles flaccidifolius

Rubus serpens proles flaccidifolius, commonly known as the flaccidifolius blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually broad and have a soft, leathery texture.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its fragrant aroma.
