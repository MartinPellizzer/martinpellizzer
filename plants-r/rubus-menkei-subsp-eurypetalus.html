Rubus menkei subsp. eurypetalus

Rubus menkei subsp.

eurypetalus, commonly known as the broad-petaled blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with broad, spreading petals. The fruit is a dark purple to black berry, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
