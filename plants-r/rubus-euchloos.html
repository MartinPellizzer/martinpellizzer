Rubus euchloos

Rubus euchloos, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a species of bramble in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit is edible, often used in jams and pies, and is rich in antioxidants.
