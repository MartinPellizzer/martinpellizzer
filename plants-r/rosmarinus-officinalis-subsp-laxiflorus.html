Rosmarinus officinalis subsp. laxiflorus

Rosmarinus officinalis subsp.

laxiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flowered rosemary, is a subspecies of the rosemary plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen foliage, which is typically gray-green and needle-like.

The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to its more common relative, Rosmarinus officinalis.
