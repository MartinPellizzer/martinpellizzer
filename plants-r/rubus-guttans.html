Rubus guttans

Rubus guttans, commonly known as the guttate raspberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a food source.
