Ranunculus tommasinii

Ranunculus tommasinii, commonly known as the Tommasini's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
