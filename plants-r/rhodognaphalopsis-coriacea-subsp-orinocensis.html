Rhodognaphalopsis coriacea subsp. orinocensis

Rhodognaphalopsis coriacea subsp.

orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco hairy everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought tolerance. Its subshrub form features woody stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
