Rhodiola sexifolia

Rhodiola sexifolia, commonly known as the six-leaf rose or the mountain rhodiola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Rhodiola sexifolia is well adapted to high altitudes and arctic environments, thriving in poor, rocky soils.

It is also known for its potential adaptogenic properties, making it a subject of interest in herbal medicine.
