Ranunculus flabellatus var. flavescens

Ranunculus flabellatus var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow flag or golden buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, and its leaves are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, streambanks, and damp woodlands. It has a short, stout rhizome and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils and can sometimes be invasive in certain regions.
