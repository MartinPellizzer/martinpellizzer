Rhaponticum nanum subsp. pellucidum

Rhaponticum nanum subsp.

pellucidum, commonly known as the translucent dwarf rhapontic, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its subspecies name, *pellucidum*, refers to the clear or translucent appearance of its leaves.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
