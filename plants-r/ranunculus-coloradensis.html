Ranunculus coloradensis

Ranunculus coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and sometimes pink. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, stream banks, and wetlands, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
