Rosa sanguisorbaefolia

Rosa sanguisorbaefolia, commonly known as the bloodroot rose, is a striking plant characterized by its distinctive red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, woodland environments. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red, adding to its visual appeal. Its flowers are typically solitary and have five petals, with a prominent yellow center.

Rosa sanguisorbaefolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
