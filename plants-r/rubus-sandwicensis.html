Rubus sandwicensis

Rubus sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and streambanks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces white or pinkish flowers.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for native birds and insects.
