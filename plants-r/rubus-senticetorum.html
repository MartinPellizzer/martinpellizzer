Rubus senticetorum

Rubus senticetorum, commonly known as the sweet-scented blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and flowers that emit a pleasant fragrance. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
