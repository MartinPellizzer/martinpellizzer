Rumex acetosella var. montana

Rumex acetosella var. montana, commonly known as mountain sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and features small, triangular leaves with reddish veins. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by small, triangular fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
