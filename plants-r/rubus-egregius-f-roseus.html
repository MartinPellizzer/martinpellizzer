Rubus egregius f. roseus

Rubus egregius f. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered blackberry, is a cultivar of the Rubus genus that exhibits distinctive floral characteristics.

It is a deciduous shrub native to North America, typically growing in woodland and thicket habitats. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its flowers are pink to rose-colored, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

The species is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its fruit-bearing potential in horticulture.
