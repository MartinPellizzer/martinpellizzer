Rebutia senilis f. iseliniana

Rebutia senilis f. iseliniana, commonly known as the "Old Man" cactus, is a unique hybrid of the genus Rebutia, native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that often appear to be covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy foliage. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is often sought after by cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
