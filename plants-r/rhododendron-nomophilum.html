Rhododendron nomophilum

Rhododendron nomophilum, commonly known as the "flower of the lover," is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its native habitat.

Rhododendron nomophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
