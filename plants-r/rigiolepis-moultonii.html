Rigiolepis moultonii

Rigiolepis moultonii, commonly known as the Moulton's spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, purple flower heads that resemble miniature thistle clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
