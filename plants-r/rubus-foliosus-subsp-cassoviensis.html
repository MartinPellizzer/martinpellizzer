Rubus foliosus subsp. cassoviensis

Rubus foliosus subsp.

cassoviensis, commonly known as the Cassoway blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically clustered together. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and shrubland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
