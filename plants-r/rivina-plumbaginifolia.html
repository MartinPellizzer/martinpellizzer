Rivina plumbaginifolia

Rivina plumbaginifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its striking floral display and adaptability contribute to its horticultural value.
