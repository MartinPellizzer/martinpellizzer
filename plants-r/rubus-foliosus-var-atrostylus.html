Rubus foliosus var. atrostylus

Rubus foliosus var. atrostylus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

It produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
