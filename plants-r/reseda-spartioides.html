Reseda spartioides

Reseda spartioides, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue or violet flowers. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.

Reseda spartioides is also valued for its historical use in dye production.
