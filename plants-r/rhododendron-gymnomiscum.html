Rhododendron gymnomiscum

Rhododendron gymnomiscum, commonly known as the naked-stamen rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shady, moist habitats, often found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens that are prominently displayed.

Rhododendron gymnomiscum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
