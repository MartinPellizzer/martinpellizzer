Ruellia strepens f. cleistantha

Ruellia strepens f. cleistantha, commonly known as the silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white variegation on its lance-shaped leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ruellia strepens f. cleistantha is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
