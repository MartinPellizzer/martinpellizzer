Rosa gallica var. pomponia

Rosa gallica var. pomponia, commonly known as the Pompon rose, is a compact, flowering shrub native to Europe.

It produces small, rounded blooms in shades of pink, red, and white, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lobed leaves and a bushy growth habit, making it suitable for borders or containers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant, clustered flowers.

This variety is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
