Rubus corymbifer

Rubus corymbifer, commonly known as the clustered blackberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common subject in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
