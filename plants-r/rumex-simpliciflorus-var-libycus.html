Rumex simpliciflorus var. libycus

Rumex simpliciflorus var. libycus, commonly known as the Libyan dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth or slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
