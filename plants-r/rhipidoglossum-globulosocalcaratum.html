Rhipidoglossum globulosocalcaratum

Rhipidoglossum globulosocalcaratum, commonly known as the globular calcarate orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring showy, globular blooms that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
