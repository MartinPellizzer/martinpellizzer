Rubus radula var. mollis

Rubus radula var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red color. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It produces numerous small flowers in the spring, which are pollinated by bees and other insects.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
