Rhododendron malayanum f. latifolium

Rhododendron malayanum f. latifolium, commonly known as the Broadleaf Rhododendron, is a variant of the Malayan Rhododendron native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage.
