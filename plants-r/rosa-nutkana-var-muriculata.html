Rosa nutkana var. muriculata

Rosa nutkana var. muriculata, commonly known as the roughleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are covered in fine, raised glands. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip that persists into winter.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native gardens.
