Rubus koehleri var. fuscus

Rubus koehleri var. fuscus, commonly known as the dark raspberry or blackberry variant, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental or culinary purposes.
