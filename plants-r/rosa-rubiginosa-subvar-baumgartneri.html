Rosa rubiginosa subvar. baumgartneri

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. baumgartneri, commonly known as the Baumann's shrub rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense growth habit. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
