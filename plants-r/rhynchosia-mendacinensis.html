Rhynchosia mendacinensis

Rhynchosia mendacinensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or African velvetleaf, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Rhynchosia mendacinensis is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
