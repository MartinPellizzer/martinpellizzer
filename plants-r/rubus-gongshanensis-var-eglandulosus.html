Rubus gongshanensis var. eglandulosus

Rubus gongshanensis var. eglandulosus, commonly known as the Gongshan bramble, is a shrub native to the Yunnan province in China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
