Rosa stebleri var. patens

Rosa stebleri var. patens, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
