Rhododendron dalhousieae var. dalhousieae

Rhododendron dalhousieae var. dalhousieae, commonly known as the Dalhousie rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
