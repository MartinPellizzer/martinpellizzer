Rhododendron alabamense

Rhododendron alabamense, commonly known as the Alabama rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to lavender, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
