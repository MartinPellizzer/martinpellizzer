Ranunculus intercedens

Ranunculus intercedens, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, with a central yellow stamen. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ranunculus intercedens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
