Rhexia uniflora

Rhexia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower inkplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pinkish-purple to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Rhexia uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
