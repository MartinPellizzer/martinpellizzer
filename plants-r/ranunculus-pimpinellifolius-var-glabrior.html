Ranunculus pimpinellifolius var. glabrior

Ranunculus pimpinellifolius var. glabrior, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central pattern. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is an annual herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and easy cultivation.
