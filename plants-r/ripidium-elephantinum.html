Ripidium elephantinum

Ripidium elephantinum, commonly known as the elephant's tusk plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble the tusks of elephants, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and can be quite large, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Ripidium elephantinum is often cultivated for its unique appearance and is valued in botanical gardens and private collections.
