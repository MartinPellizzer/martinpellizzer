Rhododendron porphyroblastum

Rhododendron porphyroblastum, commonly known as the purple-leaved rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southeastern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its unique purple foliage and vibrant floral display make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

Rhododendron porphyroblastum is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and contributing to biodiversity in its native habitats.
