Rhododendron dichroanthum subsp. apodectum

Rhododendron dichroanthum subsp.

apodectum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
