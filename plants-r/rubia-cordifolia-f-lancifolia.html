Rubia cordifolia f. lancifolia

Rubia cordifolia f. lancifolia, commonly known as the lancifoliate heartleaf rue, is a botanical variant of the common rue plant.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant contains essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
