Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. ciliosa

Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. ciliosa, commonly known as the silky rose or woolly rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a slight blush. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This subvariety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
