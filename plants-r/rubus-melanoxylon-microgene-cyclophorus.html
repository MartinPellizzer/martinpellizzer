Rubus melanoxylon microgene cyclophorus

Rubus melanoxylon microgene cyclophorus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

This species is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
