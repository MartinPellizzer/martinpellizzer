Rhynchosia minima var. pedicellata

Rhynchosia minima var. pedicellata, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
