Rabdosia weisiensis

Rabdosia weisiensis, commonly known as Chinese bluebells, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rabdosia weisiensis is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes.
