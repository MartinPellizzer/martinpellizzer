Rosa elliptica subsp. subeglanteria

Rosa elliptica subsp.

subeglanteria, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its elliptical, often narrow leaves that are densely covered with soft, white or greyish hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
