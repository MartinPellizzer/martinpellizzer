Rosa pimpinellifolia var. echinella

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. echinella, commonly known as the spiked rock rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. It is often found growing on rocky slopes and in dry, sunny habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
