Rhynchospora nuda

Rhynchospora nuda, commonly known as the naked root sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the base of the plant. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Rhynchospora nuda plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
