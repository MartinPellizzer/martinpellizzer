Rapanea palauensis

Rapanea palauensis, commonly known as the Palau holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Palau in the Western Pacific.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats, such as forest understories, and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region. Its leaves are thick and leathery, with a glossy surface, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native ecosystem.
