Rhus brasiliensis

Rhus brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like poison ivy and cashews. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into several leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for birds and small mammals.

Rhus brasiliensis is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
