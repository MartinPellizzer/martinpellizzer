Rubus constrictus

Rubus constrictus, commonly known as the prickly rose or brambles, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in jams and pies. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in temperate regions.

Rubus constrictus plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinator populations.
