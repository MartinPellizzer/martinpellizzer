Rubus × pseudoconspicuus

Rubus × pseudoconspicuus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit is valued for its flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
