Rhamnus ludovici-salvatoris

Rhamnus ludovici-salvatoris, commonly known as the Salvatoris buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
