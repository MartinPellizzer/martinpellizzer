Rubus argyriophyllus

Rubus argyriophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leafed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to southern China.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
