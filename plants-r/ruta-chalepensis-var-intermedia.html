Ruta chalepensis var. intermedia

Ruta chalepensis var. intermedia, commonly known as Turkish rue, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, pinnately compound leaves with a strong, bitter scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed capsules containing several seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to its chemical compounds like rutin and alkaloids.
