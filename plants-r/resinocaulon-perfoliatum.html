Resinocaulon perfoliatum

Resinocaulon perfoliatum, commonly known as the perforate rockcress, is a small flowering plant native to arid and rocky habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its unique feature is the way its leaves are perforated, allowing the stems to pass through, creating a distinctive appearance.

Resinocaulon perfoliatum is typically found in crevices and on rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soil.
