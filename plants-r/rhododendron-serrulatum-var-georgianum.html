Rhododendron serrulatum var. georgianum

Rhododendron serrulatum var. georgianum, commonly known as the Georgian rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are narrowly elliptical and often have serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
