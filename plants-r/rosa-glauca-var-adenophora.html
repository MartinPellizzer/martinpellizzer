Rosa glauca var. adenophora

Rosa glauca var. adenophora, commonly known as the blue rose or bluebank rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender-colored flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and woodland edges. It has simple, alternate leaves with a bluish-green hue and a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique color and fragrance.
