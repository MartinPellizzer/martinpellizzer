Rubus promachonicus

Rubus promachonicus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of dark purple to black berries. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fruits are edible but often have a tart flavor and are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
