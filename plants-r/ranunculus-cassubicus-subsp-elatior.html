Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. elatior

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the greater buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia. It features striking yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically arranged in a cup-like formation.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually trifoliate or palmately lobed. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
