Rhynchospora paniculata

Rhynchospora paniculata, commonly known as the panicled flatsedge, is a flowering plant species native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Rhynchospora paniculata thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
