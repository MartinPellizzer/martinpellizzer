Ruellia primulifolia

Ruellia primulifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its attractive, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ruellia primulifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
