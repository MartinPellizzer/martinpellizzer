Racosperma carens

Racosperma carens, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
