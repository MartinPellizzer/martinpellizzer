Restio crinalis

Restio crinalis, commonly known as the silver restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, typically during the summer months. Restio crinalis thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in fynbos vegetation.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
