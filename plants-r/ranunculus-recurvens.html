Ranunculus recurvens

Ranunculus recurvens, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with bright yellow flowers that have five to eight petals. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, often with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
