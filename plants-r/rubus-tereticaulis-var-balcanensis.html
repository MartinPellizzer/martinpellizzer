Rubus tereticaulis var. balcanensis

Rubus tereticaulis var. balcanensis, commonly known as the Balkan blackberry, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
