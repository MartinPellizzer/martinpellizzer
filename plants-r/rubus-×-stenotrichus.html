Rubus × stenotrichus

Rubus × stenotrichus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which consist of small drupelets. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit, which ripens in summer, is widely cultivated for its sweet, flavorful berries.
