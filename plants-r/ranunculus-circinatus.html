Ranunculus circinatus

Ranunculus circinatus, commonly known as the spiral buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, often with a dark central spot. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
