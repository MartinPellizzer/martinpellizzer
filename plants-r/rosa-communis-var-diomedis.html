Rosa communis var. diomedis

Rosa communis var. diomedis, commonly known as the Diomedes rose, is a rare and ornamental variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its deep pink to crimson flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its botanical features include a high degree of genetic diversity, making it a subject of interest in horticultural research.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
