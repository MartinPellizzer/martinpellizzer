Rubus serpens var. flaviflorens

Rubus serpens var. flaviflorens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered blackberry.

This variant is a deciduous shrub native to parts of eastern Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The fruit, which is typically a dark red to black berry, is edible and often used in jams and wines.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable plant for woodland gardens.
