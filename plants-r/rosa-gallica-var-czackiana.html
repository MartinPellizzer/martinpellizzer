Rosa gallica var. czackiana

Rosa gallica var. czackiana, commonly known as the Czech rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to Central Europe.

It features dark green, glossy leaves and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in early summer. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its decorative value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
