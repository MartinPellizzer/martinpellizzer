Raphanus sativus f. albescens

Raphanus sativus f. albescens, commonly known as the white radish, is a botanical variant of the radish plant characterized by its pale or white root color.

This variety is typically smaller than the standard red radish and has a milder flavor. The plant belongs to the Brassicaceae family, sharing genetic traits with other members like mustard and cabbage. Its leaves are edible and often used in salads or cooked dishes.

The white radish is valued for its unique appearance and subtle taste, making it a popular choice in certain culinary traditions.
