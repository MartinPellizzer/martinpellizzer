Rosa × dumetorum var. tendae

Rosa × dumetorum var. tendae, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, often hairy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves with serrated edges, and a high tolerance for cold climates.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom in challenging conditions.
