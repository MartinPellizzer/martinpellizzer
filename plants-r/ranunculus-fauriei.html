Ranunculus fauriei

Ranunculus fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie crowfoot, is a species of flowering plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
