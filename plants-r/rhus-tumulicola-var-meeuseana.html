Rhus tumulicola var. meeuseana

Rhus tumulicola var. meeuseana, commonly known as the Meeuse's sumac, is a variant of the sumac genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a key feature of its reproductive cycle. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
