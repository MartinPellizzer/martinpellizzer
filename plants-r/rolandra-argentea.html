Rolandra argentea

Rolandra argentea, commonly known as silver rolandra, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, also adorned with a fine, silvery coating that reflects light. It produces small, pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
