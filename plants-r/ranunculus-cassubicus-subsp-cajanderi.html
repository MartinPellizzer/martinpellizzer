Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. cajanderi

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

cajanderi, commonly known as the Cajander's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has palmate, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat.
