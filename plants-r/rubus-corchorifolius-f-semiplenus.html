Rubus corchorifolius f. semiplenus

Rubus corchorifolius f. semiplenus, commonly known as the semiplenum blackberry, is a cultivar of the blackberry plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically larger than those of the standard Rubus corchorifolius. The plant has a thorny stem and produces compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its flavorful fruit and is often used in both culinary and ornamental applications.
