Rosa coriifolia monstr. suprapilosa

Rosa coriifolia monstr.

suprapilosa, commonly known as the Chinese double-flowered rose, is a unique hybrid species with striking botanical features. It is characterized by its large, double blooms that are typically pink or white, with numerous petals arranged in a full, rounded form.

The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, glossy foliage. Its flowers appear in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental beauty and is often used in floral arrangements and as a decorative plant in horticulture.
