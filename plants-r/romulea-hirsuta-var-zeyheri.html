Romulea hirsuta var. zeyheri

Romulea hirsuta var. zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's glory-of-the-snow, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features attractive, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white to pale pink, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, making it suitable for rock gardens and well-drained soils. It blooms in spring and is known for its ornamental value.

Its botanical classification places it within the Iridaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms.
