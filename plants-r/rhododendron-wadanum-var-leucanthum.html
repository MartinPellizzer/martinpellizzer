Rhododendron wadanum var. leucanthum

Rhododendron wadanum var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered wadanum rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and showy white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often in forested areas. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
