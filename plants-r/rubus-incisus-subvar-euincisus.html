Rubus incisus subvar. euincisus

Rubus incisus subvar. euincisus, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its berries are edible and have been used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.
