Ranunculus pilicaulis

Ranunculus pilicaulis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus pilicaulis is often found in mountainous areas of Europe, Asia, and North America.
