Rosa scabrata var. blondaeana

Rosa scabrata var. blondaeana, commonly known as the Blondaeana rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa scabrata species, characterized by its distinctive pale yellow to cream-colored flowers.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions with moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a soft, delicate appearance to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden conditions.
