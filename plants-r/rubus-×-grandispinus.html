Rubus × grandispinus

Rubus × grandispinus, commonly known as the large-fruited blackberry, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along fences.

Its berries are edible, though they can be quite tart and are commonly used in jams and pies.
