Rapanea balansae

Rapanea balansae, commonly known as the Madagascar lauraceae, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a waxy texture, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into dark berries.

Rapanea balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
