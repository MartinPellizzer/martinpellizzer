Rosa × gravetii subsp. ryfylkensis

Rosa × gravetii subsp.

ryfylkensis, commonly known as Ryfylke rose, is a hybrid species native to southern Norway. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
