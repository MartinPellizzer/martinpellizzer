Rhus myriantha

Rhus myriantha, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a distinctive lobed shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, followed by red, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds. Rhus myriantha thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
