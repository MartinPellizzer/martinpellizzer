Rubus mesogaeus var. incisus

Rubus mesogaeus var. incisus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark, glossy leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and berries.
