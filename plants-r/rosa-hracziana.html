Rosa hracziana

Rosa hracziana, commonly known as the Polish rose, is a species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features pinnately compound leaves and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include thorny stems and a preference for well-drained, loamy soils.

Rosa hracziana is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
