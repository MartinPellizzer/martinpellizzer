Rhododendron chamaethomsonii var. chamaethomsonii

Rhododendron chamaethomsonii var. chamaethomsonii, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and shrublands, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its botanical features include thick, leathery leaves and a woody stem structure typical of many rhododendron species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
