Rhododendron ledifolium var. leucanthum

Rhododendron ledifolium var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered alpine rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique floral display.
