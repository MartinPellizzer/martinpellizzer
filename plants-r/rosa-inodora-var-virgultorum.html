Rosa inodora var. virgultorum

Rosa inodora var. virgultorum, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers are pink to white and are often used in ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its historical use in hedgerows and as a source of rose hips.
