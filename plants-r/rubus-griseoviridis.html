Rubus griseoviridis

Rubus griseoviridis, commonly known as the gray-green bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in woodland edges and moist habitats. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small white or pink flowers. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are often used in local cuisines or for ornamental purposes.

The species is known for its adaptability and resilience in various environmental conditions.
