Rubus biflorus var. adenophorus

Rubus biflorus var. adenophorus, commonly known as the腺花覆盆子, is a shrub native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
