Rotala densiflora subsp. melitoglossa

Rotala densiflora subsp.

melitoglossa, commonly known as the Maltese waterwheel, is a semi-aquatic plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense, floating leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy surface, aiding in water retention.

The plant produces small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in water gardens and pond landscapes.
