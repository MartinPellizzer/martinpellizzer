Rosa solstitialis genuina

Rosa solstitialis genuina, commonly known as the summer rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features thorny stems and is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is well adapted to sunny, well-drained soils and is relatively drought tolerant.
