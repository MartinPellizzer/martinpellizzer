Rubus gordonii

Rubus gordonii, commonly known as the Gordon's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It produces dark purple to black berries that are highly prized for their intense flavor and high antioxidant content. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The berries are often used in jams, desserts, and as a culinary ingredient due to their unique taste and nutritional value.
