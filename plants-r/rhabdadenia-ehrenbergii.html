Rhabdadenia ehrenbergii

Rhabdadenia ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in hot, dry climates and is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in drought-prone areas.
