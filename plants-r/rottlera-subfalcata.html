Rottlera subfalcata

Rottlera subfalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rottlera, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rottleriaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forests and understoreys.

Rottlera subfalcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
