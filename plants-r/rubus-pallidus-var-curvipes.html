Rubus pallidus var. curvipes

Rubus pallidus var. curvipes, commonly known as the pale bramble or curving raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the eastern United States.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
