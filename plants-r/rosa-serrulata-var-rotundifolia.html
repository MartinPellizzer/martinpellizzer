Rosa serrulata var. rotundifolia

Rosa serrulata var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed rock rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, dark green, round-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and rockeries in temperate climates.
