Rhynchospora harperi

Rhynchospora harperi, commonly known as the Harp's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in marshy areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Rhynchospora harperi plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
