Ranunculus auricomus subsp. clariflorus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

clariflorus, commonly known as the clearflower buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with a distinctive greenish center.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more elongated petals and a more pronounced green center compared to the typical form of Ranunculus auricomus.
