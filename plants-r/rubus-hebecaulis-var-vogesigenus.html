Rubus hebecaulis var. vogesigenus

Rubus hebecaulis var. vogesigenus, commonly known as the Voges raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland and scrub habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are white with five petals, and the fruit is a red, juicy berry.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local cuisine.
