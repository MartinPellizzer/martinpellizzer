Ranunculus yukonensis

Ranunculus yukonensis, commonly known as Yukon buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Yukon and Alaska regions of North America.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Yukon buttercup is often found in meadows, streambanks, and tundra-like habitats.
