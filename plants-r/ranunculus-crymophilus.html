Ranunculus crymophilus

Ranunculus crymophilus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a cluster of small, bright yellow flowers in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and fragile species.
