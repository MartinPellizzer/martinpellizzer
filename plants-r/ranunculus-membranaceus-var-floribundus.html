Ranunculus membranaceus var. floribundus

Ranunculus membranaceus var. floribundus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
