Rubus helioscopus

Rubus helioscopus, commonly known as the sun rose, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruits are red berries that ripen in the summer and are edible.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
