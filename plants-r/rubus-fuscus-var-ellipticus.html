Rubus fuscus var. ellipticus

Rubus fuscus var. ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and somewhat tart in flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces thorny stems that grow close to the ground. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
