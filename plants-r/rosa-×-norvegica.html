Rosa × norvegica

Rosa × norvegica, commonly known as the Norwegian rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa canina.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa × norvegica is well adapted to coastal environments and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
