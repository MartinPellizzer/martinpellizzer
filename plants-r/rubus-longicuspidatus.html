Rubus longicuspidatus

Rubus longicuspidatus, commonly known as the long-pointed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

The species is valued for its edible berries, which are used in jams and desserts.
