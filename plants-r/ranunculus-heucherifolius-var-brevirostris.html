Ranunculus heucherifolius var. brevirostris

Ranunculus heucherifolius var. brevirostris, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often have a distinctive purple or reddish coloration. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for native gardens and conservation efforts.
