Rubus selmeri

Rubus selmeri, commonly known as Selmer's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the蔷薇科 (Rosaceae) family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
