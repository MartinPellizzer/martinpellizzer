Rubus moluccanus var. fairholmianus

Rubus moluccanus var. fairholmianus, commonly known as the Fairholm raspberryl, is a variant of the moluccan raspberry native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
