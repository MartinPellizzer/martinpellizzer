Rhynchospora trigyna

Rhynchospora trigyna, commonly known as the three-lobed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. Rhynchospora trigyna thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
