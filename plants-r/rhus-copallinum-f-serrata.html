Rhus copallinum f. serrata

Rhus copallinum f. serrata, commonly known as the serrated winged sumac, is a variant of the winged sumac native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that persist into the winter. Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
