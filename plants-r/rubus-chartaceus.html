Rubus chartaceus

Rubus chartaceus, commonly known as the paper raspberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thin, papery leaves and small, edible red berries that ripen in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are white and clustered in the leaf axils, and the plant is often found in the eastern United States.

Rubus chartaceus is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to six feet.
