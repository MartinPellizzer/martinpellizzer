Rebutia mentosa

Rebutia mentosa, commonly known as the Mentosa Cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a compact, globular shape and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant features a shallow root system adapted to arid environments, allowing it to thrive in well-draining soil. Its succulent stems store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

The plant's botanical features make it an attractive and hardy choice for desert gardens.
