Ranunculus grandis var. typicus

Ranunculus grandis var. typicus, commonly known as the large buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petal-like sepals, which are often mistaken for true petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with smooth, lance-shaped leaves arranged in a basal rosette. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of temperate climates.
