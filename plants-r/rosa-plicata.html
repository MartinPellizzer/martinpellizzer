Rosa plicata

Rosa plicata, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its double, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Rosa plicata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms, which appear in late spring to early summer.
