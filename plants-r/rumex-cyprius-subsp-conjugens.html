Rumex cyprius subsp. conjugens

Rumex cyprius subsp.

conjugens, commonly known as Cyprus sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive wavy margin.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
