Ricinocarpus dimorphus

Ricinocarpus dimorphus, commonly known as the two-form senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include bipinnate leaves and a distinctive, two-lobed fruit structure.

Ricinocarpus dimorphus is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its role in nitrogen fixation.
