Rubus macrostachys subsp. caflischii

Rubus macrostachys subsp.

caflischii, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves.

The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its size and fruit quality, it is valued both for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
