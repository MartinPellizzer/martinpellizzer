Racosperma filamentosum

Racosperma filamentosum, commonly known as the filamentous acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
