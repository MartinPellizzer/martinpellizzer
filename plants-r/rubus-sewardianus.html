Rubus sewardianus

Rubus sewardianus, commonly known as the Seward's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is valued for its edible berries and ecological significance.
