Ranunculus auricomus subsp. trisulcus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

trisulcus, commonly known as the three-lobed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with three distinct lobes, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
