Rubus platysepalus var. gracilior

Rubus platysepalus var. gracilior, commonly known as the slenderleaf blackberry, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
