Ricinus communis f. carneatus

Ricinus communis f. carneatus, commonly known as the crimson castor plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its striking red foliage.

This ornamental plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. Its leaves are broad, palmately lobed, and exhibit a vibrant red or maroon coloration, which contrasts beautifully with its green stems. The plant produces unique spiny capsules that contain toxic seeds, similar to the parent species.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
