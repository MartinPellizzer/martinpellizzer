Rosa afzeliana var. obovata

Rosa afzeliana var. obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Rosa, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with obovate leaflets that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are armed with thorns, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The species is well adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
