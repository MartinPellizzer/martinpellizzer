Rosa tartarica

Rosa tartarica, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and serrated, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their strong, sweet scent.

Rosa tartarica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
