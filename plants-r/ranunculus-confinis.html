Ranunculus confinis

Ranunculus confinis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers with prominent central sepals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. It has a short flowering period in early summer and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Ranunculus confinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
