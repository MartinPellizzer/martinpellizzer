Rapanea seychellarum

Rapanea seychellarum, commonly known as the Seychelles holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Seychelles.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
