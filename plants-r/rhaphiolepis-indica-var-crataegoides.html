Rhaphiolepis indica var. crataegoides

Rhaphiolepis indica var. crataegoides, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has glossy, oval-shaped leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
