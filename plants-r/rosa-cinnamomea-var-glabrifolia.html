Rosa cinnamomea var. glabrifolia

Rosa cinnamomea var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a variety of the cinnamon rose species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a smooth, glabrous surface, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
