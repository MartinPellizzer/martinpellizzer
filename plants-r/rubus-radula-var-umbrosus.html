Rubus radula var. umbrosus

Rubus radula var. umbrosus, commonly known as the shadowy raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to shaded forest environments.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety foliage and clusters of small, red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
