Rubus oreteus

Rubus oreteus, commonly known as the oreteus raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

The fruit is edible and can be used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
