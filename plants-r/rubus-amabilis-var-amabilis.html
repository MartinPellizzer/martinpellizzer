Rubus amabilis var. amabilis

Rubus amabilis var. amabilis, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The flowers are white with five petals and are usually pollinated by bees and other insects.
