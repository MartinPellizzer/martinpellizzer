Rhododendron miyazawae

Rhododendron miyazawae, commonly known as the Miyazawa rhododendron, is a species native to Japan, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Honshu.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils in shaded environments.

Rhododendron miyazawae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
