Rosa × bibracteoides

Rosa × bibracteoides, commonly known as the rugosa rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, dark green foliage, and fragrant, semi-double pink to red flowers. The plant is native to eastern North America and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats. It produces rose hips that are reddish-orange and contain seeds.

Rosa × bibracteoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
