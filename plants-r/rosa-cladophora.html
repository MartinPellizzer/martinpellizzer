Rosa cladophora

Rosa cladophora, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant produces oval, green leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip. Native to parts of Asia and the Middle East, Rosa cladophora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
