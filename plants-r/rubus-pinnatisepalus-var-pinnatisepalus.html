Rubus pinnatisepalus var. pinnatisepalus

Rubus pinnatisepalus var. pinnatisepalus, commonly known as the broad-sepaled blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its edible berries, which are widely appreciated for their flavor and nutritional value.
