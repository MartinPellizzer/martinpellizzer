Ranunculus occidentalis var. rattanii

Ranunculus occidentalis var. rattanii, commonly known as the rattanii buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive central beard-like structure. The plant has delicate, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
