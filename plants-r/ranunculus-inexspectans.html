Ranunculus inexspectans

Ranunculus inexspectans, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and vibrant yellow flowers with five to nine petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wet meadows. It blooms from spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, showy flowers.

Ranunculus inexspectans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its cheerful appearance.
