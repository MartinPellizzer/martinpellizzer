Rubus carpaticus var. erythrandrus

Rubus carpaticus var. erythrandrus, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and dark, glossy berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in shrub form, with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and it produces edible fruit that is often used in jams and desserts.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
