Rosa meratii

Rosa meratii, commonly known as the Merat's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often armed with prickles.

Rosa meratii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
