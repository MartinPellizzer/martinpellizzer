Rubus pubescens subsp. subinermis

Rubus pubescens subsp.

subinermis, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its edible berries and its role in ecological habitats.
