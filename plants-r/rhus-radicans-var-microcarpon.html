Rhus radicans var. microcarpon

Rhus radicans var. microcarpon, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fuzzy berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and turn bright red in autumn, adding ornamental value.

The plant is noted for its adaptability and is often used in landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
