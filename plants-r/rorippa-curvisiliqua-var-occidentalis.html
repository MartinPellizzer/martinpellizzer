Rorippa curvisiliqua var. occidentalis

Rorippa curvisiliqua var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western watercress, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and typically grows in moist or aquatic environments. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often submerged or floating. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in streams, ditches, and wetlands.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich water conditions.
