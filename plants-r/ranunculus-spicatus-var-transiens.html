Ranunculus spicatus var. transiens

Ranunculus spicatus var. transiens, commonly known as the wandering buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem that often grows in open woodlands or meadows. The plant has palmate leaves with lobes that are typically deeply divided. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects.

The species is known for its ability to spread and colonize new areas, making it a common sight in disturbed soils.
