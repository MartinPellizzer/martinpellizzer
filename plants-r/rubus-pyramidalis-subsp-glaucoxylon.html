Rubus pyramidalis subsp. glaucoxylon

Rubus pyramidalis subsp.

glaucoxylon, commonly known as the purple-fruited raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its erect, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically deep red to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
