Rosa × arvina

Rosa × arvina, commonly known as the Arvina rose, is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season. It is highly adaptable to various soil types and thrives in full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Additionally, its hardy nature allows it to withstand harsh weather conditions, contributing to its popularity among gardeners.
