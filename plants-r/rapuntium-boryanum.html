Rapuntium boryanum

Rapuntium boryanum, commonly known as the Boryan false cress, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by pods containing several seeds.

Rapuntium boryanum is often found in open, sun-exposed areas and is a hardy species capable of surviving in low-nutrient conditions.
