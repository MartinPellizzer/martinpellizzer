Rhexia decumbens

Rhexia decumbens, commonly known as the creeping meadowbeauty, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. The plant features prostrate or decumbent stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its flowers are small, pink to red, and borne in clusters, adding a delicate ornamental quality.

Rhexia decumbens plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wetland restoration projects.
