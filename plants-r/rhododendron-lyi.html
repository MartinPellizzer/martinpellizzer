Rhododendron lyi

Rhododendron lyi, commonly known as the Ecuadorian rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Rhododendron lyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a representative of the diverse flora found in the Andes.
