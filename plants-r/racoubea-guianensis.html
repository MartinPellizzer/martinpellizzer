Racoubea guianensis

Racoubea guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese racoubea, is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by both wildlife and humans.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and conservation areas.
