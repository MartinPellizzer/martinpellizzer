Ruellia liturata

Ruellia liturata, commonly known as the velvetleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. Ruellia liturata is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
