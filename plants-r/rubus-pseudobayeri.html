Rubus pseudobayeri

Rubus pseudobayeri, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which is a cluster of small drupes, is edible and has a tart flavor.
