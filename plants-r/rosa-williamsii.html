Rosa williamsii

Rosa williamsii, commonly known as the Williams rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tian Shan. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its shrub-like growth habit features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

Rosa williamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
