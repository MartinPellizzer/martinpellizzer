Rhododendron morii

Rhododendron morii, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating.

Rhododendron morii thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
