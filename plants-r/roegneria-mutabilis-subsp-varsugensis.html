Roegneria mutabilis subsp. varsugensis

Roegneria mutabilis subsp.

varsugensis, commonly known as the variable wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that often have a bluish tinge.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a ground cover or in native plant gardens.
