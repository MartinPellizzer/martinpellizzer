Ranunculus neapolitanus

Ranunculus neapolitanus, commonly known as the Naples buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Italy.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and wet meadows. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often surrounded by a ring of white or pale yellow sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
