Ravensara cryptocaryoides

Ravensara cryptocaryoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ravenwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the wet tropical rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant has dark, glossy foliage and produces yellowish-white flowers that are followed by small, dark blue-black berries.

Ravensara cryptocaryoides is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used by Indigenous Australians for various purposes, including tool-making and medicinal applications.
