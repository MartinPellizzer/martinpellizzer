Rubus parviflorus subvar. velutinus

Rubus parviflorus subvar. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet bramble, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the summer and are edible.

This subvariety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
