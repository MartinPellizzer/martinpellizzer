Rhododendron himertum

Rhododendron himertum, commonly known as the Himertum rhododendron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its natural habitat includes mountainous regions in Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticulture for its vibrant floral displays.
