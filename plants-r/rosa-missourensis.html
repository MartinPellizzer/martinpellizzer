Rosa missourensis

Rosa missourensis, commonly known as the Missoula rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in Montana and Idaho. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are thorny.

Rosa missourensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in botanical gardens.
