Ribes roezlii var. roezlii

Ribes roezlii var. roezlii, commonly known as the Roëzli's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and features thorned stems with alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, tart berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
