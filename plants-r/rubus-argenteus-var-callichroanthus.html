Rubus argenteus var. callichroanthus

Rubus argenteus var. callichroanthus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its prickly stems and dense growth habit, making it a hardy shrub.
