Rosa koslowskii

Rosa koslowskii, commonly known as the Koslowski rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late summer. Rosa koslowskii is known for its hardiness and adaptability to cold climates, making it a resilient ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both garden landscapes and botanical collections.
