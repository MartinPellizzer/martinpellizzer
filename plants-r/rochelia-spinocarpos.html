Rochelia spinocarpos

Rochelia spinocarpos, commonly known as the spiny-fruited rochelia, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its sparsely branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. Its fruits are dry, dehiscent capsules that release seeds upon maturity.

Rochelia spinocarpos is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
