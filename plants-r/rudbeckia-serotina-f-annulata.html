Rudbeckia serotina f. annulata

Rudbeckia serotina f. annulata, commonly known as the annular coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy yellow ray flowers with a distinctive brown, hairy central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and pollinator habitats.
