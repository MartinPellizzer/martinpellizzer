Rubus apertiflorus subsp. mucronipetalus

Rubus apertiflorus subsp.

mucronipetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petalled blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The flowers are typically pink to white, with five narrow, acute petals. The subspecies is distinguished by its sharply pointed petals and smaller, more compact fruit clusters.

It is often found in forest edges and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates.
