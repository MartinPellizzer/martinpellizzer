Rhododendron baenitzianum var. baenitzianum

Rhododendron baenitzianum var. baenitzianum, commonly known as the Baenitz's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
