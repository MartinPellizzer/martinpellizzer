Rosa afzeliana var. cinerea

Rosa afzeliana var. cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale grayish-green leaves, which are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
