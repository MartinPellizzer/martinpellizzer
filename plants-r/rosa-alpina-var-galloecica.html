Rosa alpina var. galloecica

Rosa alpina var. galloecica, commonly known as the Galloecic Alpine rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its roots are adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand cold climates.
