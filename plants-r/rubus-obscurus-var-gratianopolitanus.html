Rubus obscurus var. gratianopolitanus

Rubus obscurus var. gratianopolitanus, commonly known as the European blackberry shrub, is a species native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes, which are clustered together. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are often hairy and have a serrated edge.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed sites.
