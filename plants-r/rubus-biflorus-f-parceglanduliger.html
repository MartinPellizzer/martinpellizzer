Rubus biflorus f. parceglanduliger

Rubus biflorus f. parceglanduliger, commonly known as the glandular-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its unique floral and foliar features.

It typically produces small, dark berries that are often used in culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are usually armed with prickles. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

This subspecies is noted for its distinct glandular structures on the fruit surface, which contribute to its classification.
