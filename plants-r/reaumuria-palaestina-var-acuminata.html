Reaumuria palaestina var. acuminata

Reaumuria palaestina var. acuminata, commonly known as the Palestine reaumuria, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to ecological stability in its habitat.

It is often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas, showcasing its ability to thrive in marginal environments.
