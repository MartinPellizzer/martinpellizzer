Ranunculus gregalis

Ranunculus gregalis, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus gregalis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow or white blooms.
