Richardia arenicola

Richardia arenicola, commonly known as the sand flat sedge, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in disturbed or dry habitats. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its reproductive structures are adapted to wind pollination, which is typical for many plants in its ecological niche.

Richardia arenicola plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
