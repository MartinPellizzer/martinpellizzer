Rhododendron laudandum var. temoense

Rhododendron laudandum var. temoense, commonly known as the Temoense Rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during the spring. The plant is characterized by its short, stiff stems and leathery leaves, which are adapted to withstand cooler, humid climates. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in horticultural collections.
