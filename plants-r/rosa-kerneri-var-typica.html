Rosa kerneri var. typica

Rosa kerneri var. typica, commonly known as the Kern's rose, is a species native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include small, oval-shaped leaves and a woody structure that allows it to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
