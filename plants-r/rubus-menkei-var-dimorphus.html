Rubus menkei var. dimorphus

Rubus menkei var. dimorphus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale green, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Its berries are edible but can be quite tart when raw.
