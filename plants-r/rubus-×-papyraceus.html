Rubus × papyraceus

Rubus × papyraceus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features include pinnate leaves and a complex inflorescence structure.
