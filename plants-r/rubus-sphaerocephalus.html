Rubus sphaerocephalus

Rubus sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are rich in antioxidants and flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

Rubus sphaerocephalus is an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
