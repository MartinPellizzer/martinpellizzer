Rhynchosia minima var. minima

Rhynchosia minima var. minima, commonly known as the small-flowered cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, yellow or orange flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used for food and forage.

This species is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in agricultural systems.
