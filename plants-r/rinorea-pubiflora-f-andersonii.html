Rinorea pubiflora f. andersonii

Rinorea pubiflora f. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's velvetleaf, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
