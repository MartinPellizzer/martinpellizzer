Rosa villosa var. omissa

Rosa villosa var. omissa, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy foliage and clusters of fragrant pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rosa villosa var. omissa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its visual appeal and mild fragrance.
