Raphanistrum vulgare var. flavum

Raphanistrum vulgare var. flavum, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence that produces small, yellowish to brownish spikelets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture, and it is often found in moist environments.

Yellow foxtail is an annual plant that completes its life cycle within one growing season.
