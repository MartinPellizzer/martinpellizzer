Rosa kissleriana

Rosa kissleriana, commonly known as the Kissler's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with dark green, serrated leaves and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. Rosa kissleriana typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.

It is a hardy shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
