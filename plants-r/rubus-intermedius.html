Rubus intermedius

Rubus intermedius, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus intermedius is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
