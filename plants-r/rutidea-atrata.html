Rutidea atrata

Rutidea atrata, commonly known as the blackwood or black thorn, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Rutidea atrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
