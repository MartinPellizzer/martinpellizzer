Ranunculus flammula f. speciosus

Ranunculus flammula f. speciosus, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

It is known for its early spring blooming habit and is often considered a weed in garden settings.
