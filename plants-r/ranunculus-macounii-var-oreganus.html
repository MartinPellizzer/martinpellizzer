Ranunculus macounii var. oreganus

Ranunculus macounii var. oreganus, commonly known as the Oregon buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in early to mid-summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
