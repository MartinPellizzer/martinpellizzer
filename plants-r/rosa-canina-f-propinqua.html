Rosa canina f. propinqua

Rosa canina f. propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf dog rose, is a compact shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is dense and dark green, providing ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is hardy and adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
