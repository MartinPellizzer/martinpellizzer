Rubus stellarcticus

Rubus stellarcticus, commonly known as the starry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its sharp, thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems.
