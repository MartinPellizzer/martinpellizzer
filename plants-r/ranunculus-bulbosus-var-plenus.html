Ranunculus bulbosus var. plenus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. plenus, commonly known as the full-flowered buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a short, hairy stem that grows up to 30 cm in height, bearing several large, showy yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant has palmate, deeply lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and flies.
