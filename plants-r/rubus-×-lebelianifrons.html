Rubus × lebelianifrons

Rubus × lebelianifrons, commonly known as the red-berried bramble.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, wooded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various habitats.
