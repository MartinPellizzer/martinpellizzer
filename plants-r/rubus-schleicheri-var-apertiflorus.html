Rubus schleicheri var. apertiflorus

Rubus schleicheri var. apertiflorus, commonly known as the open-flowered bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small white or pink flowers. Its fruit, a red-hued drupe, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
