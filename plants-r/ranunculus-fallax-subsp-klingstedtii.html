Ranunculus fallax subsp. klingstedtii

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

klingstedtii, commonly known as the Klingstedt's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive lobed shape. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high elevations.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that disperse its seeds through wind or water.
