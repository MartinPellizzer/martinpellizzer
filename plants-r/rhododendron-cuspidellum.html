Rhododendron cuspidellum

Rhododendron cuspidellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flower colors.

Rhododendron cuspidellum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
