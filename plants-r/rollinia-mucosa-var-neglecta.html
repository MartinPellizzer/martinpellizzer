Rollinia mucosa var. neglecta

Rollinia mucosa var. neglecta, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or wild starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caricaceae and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange fruit that resembles a star. The fruit is typically small, with a rough, leathery skin and a sweet, juicy pulp containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
