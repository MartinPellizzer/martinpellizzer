Rosa arvensis f. paradoxa

Rosa arvensis f. paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical field rose, is a unique horticultural form of the common field rose.

It is characterized by its striking, almost surreal appearance, featuring deep red petals with a contrasting white center. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats, often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual element to the landscape.

Due to its distinctive appearance, it is highly valued in specialty gardens and botanical collections.
