Rhaponticum paniculatum

Rhaponticum paniculatum, commonly known as the Siberian cowpea or Russian spinach, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It features a robust root system and can grow up to 2 meters in height, with hairy, alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by spherical, spiky fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its nutritional and medicinal properties.

Botanically, it is closely related to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its edible leaves and seeds.
