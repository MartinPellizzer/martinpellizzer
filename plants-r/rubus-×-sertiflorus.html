Rubus × sertiflorus

Rubus × sertiflorus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in coastal regions with cool, moist climates and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in some gardens and natural areas.
