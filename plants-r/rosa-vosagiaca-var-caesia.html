Rosa vosagiaca var. caesia

Rosa vosagiaca var. caesia, commonly known as the violet-hued rose, is a rare and delicate species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its pale violet to almost blue petals, which give it a unique and striking appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with finely serrated edges, and the stems are covered in soft, white to pinkish hairs.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
