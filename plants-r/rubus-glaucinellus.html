Rubus glaucinellus

Rubus glaucinellus, commonly known as the blueberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in woodland areas and along stream banks. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically a deep blue or black color when mature. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a hairy underside.

The shrub can grow up to 2 meters tall and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white flowers.
