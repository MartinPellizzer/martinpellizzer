Rhus amharica

Rhus amharica, commonly known as the Ethiopian sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

Rhus amharica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
