Ruellia purdieana

Ruellia purdieana, commonly known as the purple ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
