Ranunculus canus var. ludovicianus

Ranunculus canus var. ludovicianus, commonly known as the white crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically has bright yellow stamens and a white or pale yellow center, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
