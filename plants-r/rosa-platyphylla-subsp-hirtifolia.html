Rosa platyphylla subsp. hirtifolia

Rosa platyphylla subsp.

hirtifolia, commonly known as the woolly evergreen rose, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features dense, thorny stems and broad, leathery leaves covered in fine, woolly hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
