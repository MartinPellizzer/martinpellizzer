Rhododendron ponticum var. baeticum

Rhododendron ponticum var. baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican rhododendron, is a variant of the Pontic rhododendron native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its botanical features include elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and red or purple flower buds that open to pink or white petals.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
