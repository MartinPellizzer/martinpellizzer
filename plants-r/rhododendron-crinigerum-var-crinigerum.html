Rhododendron crinigerum var. crinigerum

Rhododendron crinigerum var. crinigerum, commonly known as the alpine rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, often with a hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
