Rosa khasautensis

Rosa khasautensis, commonly known as the Khasi rose, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces small, red berries that contain seeds.

Rosa khasautensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
