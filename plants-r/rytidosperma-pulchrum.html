Rytidosperma pulchrum

Rytidosperma pulchrum, commonly known as the golden beard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It features slender, upright stems with long, narrow leaves that have a distinctive golden hue along their edges. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements due to their ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The botanical characteristics of Rytidosperma pulchrum make it a popular choice for both ecological and aesthetic landscaping purposes.
