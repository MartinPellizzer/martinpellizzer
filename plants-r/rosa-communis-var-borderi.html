Rosa communis var. borderi

Rosa communis var. borderi, commonly known as the Border rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering, typically producing clusters of fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
