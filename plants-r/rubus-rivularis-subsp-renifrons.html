Rubus rivularis subsp. renifrons

Rubus rivularis subsp.

renifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive reniform (kidney-shaped) base.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, achenes, typically reddish or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
