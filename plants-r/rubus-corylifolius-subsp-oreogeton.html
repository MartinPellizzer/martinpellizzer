Rubus corylifolius subsp. oreogeton

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

oreogeton, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated margin.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, achenes surrounded by a fleshy, hairy receptacle. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and has been cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
