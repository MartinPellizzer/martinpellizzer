Rosa canina f. watsonii

Rosa canina f. watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's rosehip, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive, orange-red hips that mature in the autumn. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant pink or white flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
