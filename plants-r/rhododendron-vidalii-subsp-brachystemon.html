Rhododendron vidalii subsp. brachystemon

Rhododendron vidalii subsp.

brachystemon, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in Nepal and Bhutan. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
