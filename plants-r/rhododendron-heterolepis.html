Rhododendron heterolepis

Rhododendron heterolepis, commonly known as the Allegheny mountain rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, fuzzy hairs. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron heterolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
