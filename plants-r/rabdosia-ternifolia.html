Rabdosia ternifolia

Rabdosia ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows as a shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually purple or pink, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Rabdosia ternifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
