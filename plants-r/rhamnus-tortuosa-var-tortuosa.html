Rhamnus tortuosa var. tortuosa

Rhamnus tortuosa var. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its twisted, gnarled stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by reddish-black berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
