Ranunculus semicassubicus

Ranunculus semicassubicus, commonly known as the Sierra marigold, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous environments, such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and its flowers have five to eight sepals that are often mistaken for petals.

Ranunculus semicassubicus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
