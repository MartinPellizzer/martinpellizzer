Ranunculus flammula var. ovalis

Ranunculus flammula var. ovalis, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has alternate, trifoliate leaves that are usually heart-shaped or ovate, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant contains toxic compounds, including protoanemonin, which can cause skin irritation.
