Rumex triangulivalvis

Rumex triangulivalvis, commonly known as the three-lobed sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and stipules. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three triangular lobes, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically greenish-yellow in color.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
