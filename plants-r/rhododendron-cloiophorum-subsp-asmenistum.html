Rhododendron cloiophorum subsp. asmenistum

Rhododendron cloiophorum subsp.

asmenistum, commonly known as the Asmenistan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its compact shrub form and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The leaves are typically elliptical, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often found in shaded forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
