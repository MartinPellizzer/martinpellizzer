Ranunculus altior

Ranunculus altior, commonly known as the tall buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a hairy texture. It typically grows to a height of 30 to 90 centimeters, with a short, stout stem.

Ranunculus altior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
