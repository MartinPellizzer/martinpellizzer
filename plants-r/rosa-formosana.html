Rosa formosana

Rosa formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan rose, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are usually borne singly or in clusters and have a strong, sweet scent.

Rosa formosana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
