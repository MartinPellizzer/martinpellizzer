Ravensara subtriplinervia

Ravensara subtriplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined ravenwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark and glossy, elliptical leaves with three distinct veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Ravensara subtriplinervia is valued for its unique foliage and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for ornamental purposes.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
