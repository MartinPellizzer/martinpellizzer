Rubia cordifolia var. affinis

Rubia cordifolia var. affinis, commonly known as the heart-leaved rue or narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The leaves are heart-shaped, with a serrated margin, and are often aromatic when crushed. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
