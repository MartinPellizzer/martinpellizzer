Rhododendron selense subsp. duseimatum

Rhododendron selense subsp.

duseimatum, commonly known as the Duseimatum rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
