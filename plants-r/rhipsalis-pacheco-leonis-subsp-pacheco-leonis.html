Rhipsalis pacheco-leonis subsp. pacheco-leonis

Rhipsalis pacheco-leonis subsp.

pacheco-leonis, commonly known as the Pacheco-Leonis Rhipsalis, is a species of cactus native to Brazil. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that are typically green and covered with small, white or pale green spines.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
