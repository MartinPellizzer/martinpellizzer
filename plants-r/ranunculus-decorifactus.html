Ranunculus decorifactus

Ranunculus decorifactus, commonly known as the decorative buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a central cluster of stamens. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to temperate regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
