Racosperma crassum

Racosperma crassum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is well adapted to arid environments and is commonly used in revegetation projects.

Racosperma crassum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
