Ranunculus vaginalis

Ranunculus vaginalis, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, radially symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and it produces small, green berries containing seeds.

Ranunculus vaginalis is known for its early spring blooming habit and its role in supporting pollinators.
