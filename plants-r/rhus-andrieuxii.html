Rhus andrieuxii

Rhus andrieuxii, commonly known as the African sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like poison ivy and cashew. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with serrated margins. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals.

Rhus andrieuxii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
