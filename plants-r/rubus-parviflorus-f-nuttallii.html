Rubus parviflorus f. nuttallii

Rubus parviflorus f. nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's bramble, is a subspecies of the common bramble found in North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in early summer. The plant produces red or black berries that are edible but often tart.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
