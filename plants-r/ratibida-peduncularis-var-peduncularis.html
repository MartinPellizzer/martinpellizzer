Ratibida peduncularis var. peduncularis

Ratibida peduncularis var. peduncularis, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It features showy, orange to yellow composite flower heads that bloom from late summer to early autumn. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in dense clusters near water sources. Its flowering structure consists of a central disk surrounded by ray florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
