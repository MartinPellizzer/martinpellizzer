Rhipsalis triangularis

Rhipsalis triangularis, commonly known as the triangular mistletoe cactus, is a climbing cactus native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, branching stems covered in short, soft spines and small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible for certain animals but not typically consumed by humans. Its leaves are modified into small, pointed structures that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Rhipsalis triangularis is often grown as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
