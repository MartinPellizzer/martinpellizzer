Rumex × lingulatus

Rumex × lingulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved dock, is a hybrid plant that combines characteristics of several Rumex species.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a wavy or serrated edge, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its botanical structure includes a stout, fleshy rootstock and a robust stem that supports the foliage.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cultures.
