Rubus × suboblongus

Rubus × suboblongus, commonly known as the suboblong blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces flowers that are white or pale pink.

This species is valued for its edible berries, which are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
