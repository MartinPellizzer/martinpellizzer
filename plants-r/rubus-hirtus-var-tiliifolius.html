Rubus hirtus var. tiliifolius

Rubus hirtus var. tiliifolius, commonly known as the tiliifolius raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, serrated margin. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
