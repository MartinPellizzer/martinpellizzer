Rollinia sylvatica

Rollinia sylvatica, commonly known as the roughleaf star-apple, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a large, spiny capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Rollinia sylvatica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
