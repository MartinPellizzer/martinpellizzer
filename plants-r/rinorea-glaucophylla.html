Rinorea glaucophylla

Rinorea glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf rinorea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically glossy and have a waxy appearance, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
