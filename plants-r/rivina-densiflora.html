Rivina densiflora

Rivina densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower rivina, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rivina densiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
