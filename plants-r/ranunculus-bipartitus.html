Ranunculus bipartitus

Ranunculus bipartitus, commonly known as the two-parted buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight yellow petals and a central cluster of bright orange anthers. The plant has a basal rosette of trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and have a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
