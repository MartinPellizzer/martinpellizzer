Roigia comosa

Roigia comosa, commonly known as the silky roigia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical name reflects its hairy appearance, with "comosa" meaning "hairy" in Latin.

Roigia comosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
