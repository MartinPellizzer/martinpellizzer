Rubus eluxatus var. rausvicensis

Rubus eluxatus var. rausvicensis, commonly known as the Rausvicensis blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local agriculture.
