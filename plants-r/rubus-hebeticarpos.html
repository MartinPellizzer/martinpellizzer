Rubus hebeticarpos

Rubus hebeticarpos, commonly known as the hairy raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces red, edible berries that are similar in appearance to raspberries but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The leaves are typically three-lobed with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to spread through runners.
