Rumex aquaticus subsp. domesticus

Rumex aquaticus subsp.

domesticus, commonly known as the domestic water sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist soil conditions and its preference for partial to full shade.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine and as a vegetable in some cultures.
