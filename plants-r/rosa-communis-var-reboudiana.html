Rosa communis var. reboudiana

Rosa communis var. reboudiana, commonly known as the French rose, is a cultivar prized for its fragrant, deep pink flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant is native to France and is well adapted to temperate climates, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a rich, sweet scent that attracts pollinators.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
