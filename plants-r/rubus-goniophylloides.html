Rubus goniophylloides

Rubus goniophylloides, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is an important component of its native ecosystem.
