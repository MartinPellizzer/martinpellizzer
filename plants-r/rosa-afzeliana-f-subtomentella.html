Rosa afzeliana f. subtomentella

Rosa afzeliana f. subtomentella, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a subtle fragrance, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
