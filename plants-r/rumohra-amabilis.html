Rumohra amabilis

Rumohra amabilis, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a striking ornamental plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it well-suited for aquatic or bog gardens. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver-green hue that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Rumohra amabilis is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
