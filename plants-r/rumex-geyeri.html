Rumex geyeri

Rumex geyeri, commonly known as Geyeri sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and edible leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and streambanks. Its leaves are kidney-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface and contain a high concentration of oxalic acid.

Rumex geyeri is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, though its tart flavor requires careful preparation.
