Rubus nefrens

Rubus nefrens, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters.

Rubus nefrens is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
