Rosa × alba foliacea

Rosa × alba foliacea, commonly known as the white-leafed rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and distinctive white or pale green foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The flowers are usually borne in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. This plant is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
