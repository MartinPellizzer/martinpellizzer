Rosa sudermanica

Rosa sudermanica, commonly known as the Suderman's rose, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnate, with five to seven leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa sudermanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
