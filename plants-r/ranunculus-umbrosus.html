Ranunculus umbrosus

Ranunculus umbrosus, commonly known as the shadowy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and yellow flowers with five petals, often adorned with a dark central blotch. It produces small, hairy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ranunculus umbrosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
