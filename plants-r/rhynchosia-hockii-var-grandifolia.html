Rhynchosia hockii var. grandifolia

Rhynchosia hockii var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, climbing habit and pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive red or orange base. Its pods are typically hairy and contain edible seeds that are used in various culinary applications.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional value in agricultural systems.
