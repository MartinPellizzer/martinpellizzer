Rosa bischeutziana

Rosa bischeutziana, commonly known as the Bischeutz rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring arching branches and dense foliage. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa bischeutziana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
