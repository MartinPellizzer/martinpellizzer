Rhododendron rufum var. pachysanthum

Rhododendron rufum var. pachysanthum, commonly known as the thick-leafed red rhododendron, is a variant of the red rhododendron native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and vibrant crimson flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are broader and more robust compared to the typical variety, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
