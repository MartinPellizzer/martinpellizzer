Rosa stebleri var. naxensis

Rosa stebleri var. naxensis, commonly known as the Naxos rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the Greek island of Naxos.

It features small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy foliage and produces hips that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its limited natural range, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
