Rubus × polychaetus

Rubus × polychaetus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and open woods.

Rubus × polychaetus is valued for its edible fruits and is commonly cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
