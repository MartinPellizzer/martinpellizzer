Rorippa nudiuscula f. pinnatifida

Rorippa nudiuscula f. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed watercress, is a variant of the watercress species native to moist or aquatic habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, pinnately divided leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its peppery flavor and is sometimes cultivated for culinary use.
