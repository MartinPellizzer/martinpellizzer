Rosa communis var. porrecta

Rosa communis var. porrecta, commonly known as the straight-stemmed rose, is a variant of the common rose that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It typically features upright, slender stems that grow in a straight, unbranched manner, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers with five to seven petals, often in shades of pink, red, or white. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and fragrant blooms.
