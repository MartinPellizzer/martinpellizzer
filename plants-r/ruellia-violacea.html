Ruellia violacea

Ruellia violacea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia violacea thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.
