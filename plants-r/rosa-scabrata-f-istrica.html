Rosa scabrata f. istrica

Rosa scabrata f. istrica, commonly known as the Istrian rose, is a botanical variant of the rugose rose, characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features.

This plant is native to the Balkan region, particularly the Istrian Peninsula, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture, while the flowers are usually pink to red in color. The plant produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
