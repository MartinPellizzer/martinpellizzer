Rourea minor subsp. monadelpha

Rourea minor subsp.

monadelpha, commonly known as the pink-veined hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive pink veins on the undersides of its broad, ovate leaves.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, often with a yellow center. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouses.
