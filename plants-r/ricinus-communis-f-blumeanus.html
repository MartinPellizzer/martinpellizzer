Ricinus communis f. blumeanus

Ricinus communis f. blumeanus, commonly known as the Blume's castor oil plant, is a cultivated form of the castor oil plant.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep red to orange. The plant produces distinctive spiny capsules containing toxic seeds. It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates.

This variety is primarily grown for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage.
