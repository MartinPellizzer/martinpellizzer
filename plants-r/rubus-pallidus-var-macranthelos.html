Rubus pallidus var. macranthelos

Rubus pallidus var. macranthelos, commonly known as the pale raspberry variant with large flowers, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the fruits are small, red berries that ripen in late summer. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and slopes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
