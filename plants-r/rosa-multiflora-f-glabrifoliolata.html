Rosa multiflora f. glabrifoliolata

Rosa multiflora f. glabrifoliolata, commonly known as the smooth-leafed multiflora rose, is a flowering shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically compound, with reduced leaflets that give it a distinctive, more compact appearance compared to other rose varieties. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
