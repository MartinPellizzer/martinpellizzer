Rhamnus leptophylla

Rhamnus leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories and streambanks. The plant features small, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in short inflorescences and are followed by small, black berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals.

Rhamnus leptophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
