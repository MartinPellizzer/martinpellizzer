Rubus villicaulis f. langei

Rubus villicaulis f. langei, commonly known as the Lange's bramble, is a variant of the brambles native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.
