Rubus schleicheri var. sequanensis

Rubus schleicheri var. sequanensis, commonly known as the French blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a slightly tart flavor. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique genetic variation and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
