Rabdosia umbrosa var. latifolia

Rabdosia umbrosa var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in shaded areas.
