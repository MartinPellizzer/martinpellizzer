Rubus helgae

Rubus helgae, commonly known as the Helgae raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
