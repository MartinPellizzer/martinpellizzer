Rhododendron thibaudiense

Rhododendron thibaudiense, commonly known as the Thibaudiense rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to shaded garden spaces.
