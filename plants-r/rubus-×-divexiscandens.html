Rubus × divexiscandens

Rubus × divexiscandens, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various Rubus species.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
