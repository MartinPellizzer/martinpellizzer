Rubus × heberamus

Rubus × heberamus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus argutus and Rubus ursinus, exhibiting characteristics of both parents. This species is native to the coastal regions of California and is known for its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to coastal climates and its role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
