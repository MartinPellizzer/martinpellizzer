Rosa scabriuscula f. typica

Rosa scabriuscula f. typica, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which are covered in a rough, scaly texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This species is often found in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade to full sun conditions.
