Rosa systyla var. pygmaeopsis

Rosa systyla var. pygmaeopsis, commonly known as the dwarf rock rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thornless stems that grow close to the ground, forming a low, mat-like structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in rocky, well-drained soils.
