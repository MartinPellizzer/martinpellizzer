Rubia caramanica

Rubia caramanica, commonly known as the Caramanica burret.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits known as burs, which contain seeds that are dispersed by wind. Rubia caramanica is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many other species with similar botanical features.
