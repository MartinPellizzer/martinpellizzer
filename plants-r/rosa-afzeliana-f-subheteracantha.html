Rosa afzeliana f. subheteracantha

Rosa afzeliana f. subheteracantha, commonly known as the African rose or the white rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa afzeliana.

It is native to regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are usually borne singly or in small clusters, and they have a pleasant fragrance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
