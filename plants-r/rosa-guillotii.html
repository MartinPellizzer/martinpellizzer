Rosa guillotii

Rosa guillotii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense, thorny foliage. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red-orange fruits that contain seeds.

Rosa guillotii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming habit.
