Rosa tortuosa

Rosa tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted rose, is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its twisted, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa tortuosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient member of the Rosaceae family.
