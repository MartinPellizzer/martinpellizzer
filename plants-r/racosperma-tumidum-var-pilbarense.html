Racosperma tumidum var. pilbarense

Racosperma tumidum var. pilbarense, commonly known as the Pilbaren wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in winter. The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of elliptic leaflets. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands.

The Pilbaren wattle is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
