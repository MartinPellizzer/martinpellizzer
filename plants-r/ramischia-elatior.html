Ramischia elatior

Ramischia elatior, commonly known as the tall ramischia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
