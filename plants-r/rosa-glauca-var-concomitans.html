Rosa glauca var. concomitans

Rosa glauca var. concomitans, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, thornless stems and produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers in early summer. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and its foliage turns red in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
