Rubus × canifolius

Rubus × canifolius, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically white or pink.

The species is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
