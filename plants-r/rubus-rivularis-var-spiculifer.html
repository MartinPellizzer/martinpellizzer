Rubus rivularis var. spiculifer

Rubus rivularis var. spiculifer, commonly known as the spiny trailing blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
