Rosa venosa

Rosa venosa, commonly known as the veined rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive veined leaves and fragrant pink to white flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
