Rosa serafinii var. ligustica

Rosa serafinii var. ligustica, commonly known as the Ligustic Rose, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of Italy.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves and has a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
