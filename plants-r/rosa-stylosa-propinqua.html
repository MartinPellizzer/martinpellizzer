Rosa stylosa propinqua

Rosa stylosa propinqua, commonly known as the slender-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rosa stylosa propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
