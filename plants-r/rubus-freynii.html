Rubus freynii

Rubus freynii, commonly known as the Freyn's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Rubus freynii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
