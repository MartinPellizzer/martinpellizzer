Rubus hirtus tenuiglandulosus

Rubus hirtus tenuiglandulosus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple small drupes.

This species is typically found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in temperate climates.
