Rubus opaciformis

Rubus opaciformis, commonly known as the dark-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage is distinctive, with a dark, almost velvety appearance that sets it apart from other Rubus species.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
