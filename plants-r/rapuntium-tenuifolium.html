Rapuntium tenuifolium

Rapuntium tenuifolium, commonly known as slenderleaf cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustard. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by slender, elongated pods containing several seeds.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
