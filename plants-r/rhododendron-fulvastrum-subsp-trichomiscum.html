Rhododendron fulvastrum subsp. trichomiscum

Rhododendron fulvastrum subsp.

trichomiscum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage, which gives it its subspecies name.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at high elevations. Its flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in early spring.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
