Rosa subgracilis

Rosa subgracilis, commonly known as the slender rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late summer, which are followed by oval, red-brown hips. Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy plant adapted to cold climates and well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
