Rubus schleicheri var. saevoides

Rubus schleicheri var. saevoides, commonly known as the Saevoides blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets, which are often edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
