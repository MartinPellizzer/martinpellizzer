Ranunculus heldreichanus

Ranunculus heldreichanus, commonly known as the Balkan buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and alpine meadows, preferring cool and temperate climates. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its distinctive floral structures and often toxic sap.

Ranunculus heldreichanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
