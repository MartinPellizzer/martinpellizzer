Rosa platyacantha var. maracandica

Rosa platyacantha var. maracandica, commonly known as the Maracandica rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It features dense, thorned stems and is characterized by its large, fragrant, semi-double to double pink or red flowers. The leaves are compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets that are serrated along the edges. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
