Ranunculus earlei

Ranunculus earlei, commonly known as the Sierra foothill buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five petals, ranging in color from yellow to orange.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring.
