Ranunculus bulbosus var. radicosus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. radicosus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats. It has a short, rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage and is often found in temperate regions.
