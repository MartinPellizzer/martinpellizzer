Rhynchosia kerstingii

Rhynchosia kerstingii, commonly known as the Kersting's cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in agricultural systems for its nutritional value.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
