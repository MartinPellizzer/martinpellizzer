Rubus tereticaulis var. scythicus

Rubus tereticaulis var. scythicus, commonly known as the scythian blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has prickly stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open fields.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
