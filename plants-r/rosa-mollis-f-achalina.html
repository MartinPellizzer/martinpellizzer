Rosa mollis f. achalina

Rosa mollis f. achalina, commonly known as the Achalina rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless growth habit and profuse blooming of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The foliage consists of dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of soil types.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
