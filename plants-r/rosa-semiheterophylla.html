Rosa semiheterophylla

Rosa semiheterophylla, commonly known as the heterophyllous rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique variation in leaf morphology, with some leaves having a different shape compared to others on the same plant. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. It produces fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is relatively rare and is often found in rocky or well-drained soil conditions.
