Rubus occidentalis subsp. leucodermis

Rubus occidentalis subsp.

leucodermis, commonly known as the white-stemmed blackberry, is a subspecies native to western North America. It is characterized by its slender, often white-veined stems and large, dark purple to black berries.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are usually covered in fine, soft hairs.

The subspecies is valued for its edible fruits, which have a distinctive flavor and are often used in jams and desserts.
