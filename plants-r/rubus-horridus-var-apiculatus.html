Rubus horridus var. apiculatus

Rubus horridus var. apiculatus, commonly known as the prickly pear raspberry, is a thorny shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, red or yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with dense, bristly hairs that provide protection against herbivores.
