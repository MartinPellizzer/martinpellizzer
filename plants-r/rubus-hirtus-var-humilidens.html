Rubus hirtus var. humilidens

Rubus hirtus var. humilidens, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark red or black color. The plant has thorny stems and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its botanical characteristics include hairy foliage and a sprawling growth habit, making it adaptable to various soil types.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and role in supporting local wildlife.
