Rubus hansenii

Rubus hansenii, commonly known as Hansen's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and grow in clusters, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Rubus hansenii is valued for its hardy nature and is often found in forest understories.
