Rosettea benguellensis var. parviflora

Rosettea benguellensis var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered rosette plant, is a succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It forms a dense rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its compact growth habit and ornamental leaves make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
