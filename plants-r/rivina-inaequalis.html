Rivina inaequalis

Rivina inaequalis, commonly known as the redroot false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for wildlife.

The plant's name refers to its red-colored roots, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine.
