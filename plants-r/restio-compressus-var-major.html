Restio compressus var. major

Restio compressus var. major, commonly known as the large flatrestio, is a grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical flower spikes that appear in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small white to pale blue flowers.

It is often used in coastal restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.
