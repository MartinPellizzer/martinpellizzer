Rhus amazonica

Rhus amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants such as cashews and poison ivy. Rhus amazonica is adapted to humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
