Rhododendron lamrialianum subsp. gunsalamianum

Rhododendron lamrialianum subsp.

gunsalamianum, commonly known as the Philippine rhododendron, is a species native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in montane forests at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
