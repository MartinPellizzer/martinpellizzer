Rhododendron clementinae

Rhododendron clementinae, commonly known as the Clementina rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple stripe. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
