Rubus hirtus var. reversus

Rubus hirtus var. reversus, commonly known as the reverse-hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive reverse-hairy appearance on its stems and leaves.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, typically found in dry, rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
