Rubus bakeri f. elongatus

Rubus bakeri f. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and dark, glossy leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible.

This subspecies is valued for its unique morphology and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
