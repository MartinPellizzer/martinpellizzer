Ranunculus purshii

Ranunculus purshii, commonly known as Pursh's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are hairy and lobed, often with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable wildflower in its native habitat.
