Rhynchospora adscendens

Rhynchospora adscendens, commonly known as the ascending marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Rhynchospora adscendens plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
