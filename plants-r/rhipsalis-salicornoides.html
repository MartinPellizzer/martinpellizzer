Rhipsalis salicornoides

Rhipsalis salicornoides, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a tropical epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It features slender, branching stems covered in small, rounded, leaf-like structures called areoles. The plant produces colorful, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and festive blooming make it a popular ornamental plant.
