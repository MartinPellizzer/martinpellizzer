Rubachia spiciflora var. grandifolia

Rubachia spiciflora var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered spiciflora rubachia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple to violet flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular florets that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
