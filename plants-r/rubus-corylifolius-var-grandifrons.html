Rubus corylifolius var. grandifrons

Rubus corylifolius var. grandifrons, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in early summer. The plant produces edible blackberries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental foliage and fruit, making it a popular choice in gardens and for commercial cultivation.
