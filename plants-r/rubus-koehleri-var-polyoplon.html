Rubus koehleri var. polyoplon

Rubus koehleri var. polyoplon, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal climates with salt spray.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
