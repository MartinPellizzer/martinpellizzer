Rhododendron stenophyllum subsp. angustifolium

Rhododendron stenophyllum subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters wide.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink to purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at elevations above 3,000 meters.
