Rhus mysorensis

Rhus mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like poison ivy and cashew. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces orange-red berries that are an important food source for various birds and small mammals.

Rhus mysorensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
