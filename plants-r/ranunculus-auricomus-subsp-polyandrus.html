Ranunculus auricomus subsp. polyandrus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

polyandrus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with palmate lobes, and the stems are usually hairy.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
