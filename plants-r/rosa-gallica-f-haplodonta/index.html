Rosa Gallica F. Haplodonta

Rosa gallica f. haplodonta is a rare and ancient form of the Gallic rose known for its delicate pink blooms and compact growth. This cultivar is believed to be one of the oldest in the Rosa genus, with a history tracing back to medieval times. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a favorite among heritage gardeners. Its unique floral structure and historical significance make it a prized specimen in botanical collections. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in modern horticulture.
