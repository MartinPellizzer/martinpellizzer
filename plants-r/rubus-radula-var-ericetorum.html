Rubus radula var. ericetorum

Rubus radula var. ericetorum, commonly known as the ericaceous raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow when ripe, containing multiple seeds. It thrives in acidic soils and is often found in heathland or woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
