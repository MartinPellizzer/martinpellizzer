Rosa × oxyodontoides

Rosa × oxyodontoides, commonly known as the hybrid tea rose, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers in a variety of colors, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is native to the United States and is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote blooming.

This species is valued for its hardiness and ability to produce abundant flowers throughout the growing season.
