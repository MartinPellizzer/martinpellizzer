Ranunculus subobtusulus

Ranunculus subobtusulus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy surface and a distinctive narrowing at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
