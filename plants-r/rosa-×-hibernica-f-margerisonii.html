Rosa × hibernica f. margerisonii

Rosa × hibernica f. margerisonii, commonly known as the Margerison's rose, is a rare and hybrid rose species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage is dark green with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticultural history.
