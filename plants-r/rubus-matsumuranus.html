Rubus matsumuranus

Rubus matsumuranus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
