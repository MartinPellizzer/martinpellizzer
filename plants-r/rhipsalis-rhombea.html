Rhipsalis rhombea

Rhipsalis rhombea, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often adorned with small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the winter months. Its unique growth habit allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments, making it well-suited for indoor cultivation.

Rhipsalis rhombea is prized for its ornamental value and ability to bloom reliably under proper care.
