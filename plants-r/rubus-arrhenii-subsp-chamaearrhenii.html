Rubus arrhenii subsp. chamaearrhenii

Rubus arrhenii subsp.

chamaearrhenii, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a shrub native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of prickly stems covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually serrated along the edges.

This subspecies is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges.
