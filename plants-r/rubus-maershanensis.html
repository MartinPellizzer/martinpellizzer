Rubus maershanensis

Rubus maershanensis, commonly known as the Maershan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible berries. The plant typically grows in rocky and forested areas, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, a small, dark purple berry, is valued for its flavor and is often used in local culinary traditions.
