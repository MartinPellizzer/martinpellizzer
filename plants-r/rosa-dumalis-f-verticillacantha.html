Rosa dumalis f. verticillacantha

Rosa dumalis f. verticillacantha, commonly known as the mountain wild rose, is a variant of the Rosa dumalis species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a fragrant scent, blooming in the summer months. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
