Rubus apiculatus proles trachydermis

Rubus apiculatus proles trachydermis, commonly known as the rough-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
