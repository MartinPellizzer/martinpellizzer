Raphia vinifera var. nicaraguensis

Raphia vinifera var. nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan palm, is a large, fan-shaped palm native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and wetlands. Its fruit produces a fibrous material similar to that of the date palm, which has been historically used for making ropes and textiles.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
