Rhodax dichotomus

Rhodax dichotomus, commonly known as the forked broom.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its dense, branched growth habit. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are opposite or whorled along the stems. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish, appearing in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Rhodax dichotomus is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
