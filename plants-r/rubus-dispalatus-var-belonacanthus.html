Rubus dispalatus var. belonacanthus

Rubus dispalatus var. belonacanthus, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to coastal regions of southern California.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically lobed or serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal winds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for coastal ecosystem studies and native plant gardening.
