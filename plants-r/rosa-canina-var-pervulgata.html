Rosa canina var. pervulgata

Rosa canina var. pervulgata, commonly known as the dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fuzzy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
