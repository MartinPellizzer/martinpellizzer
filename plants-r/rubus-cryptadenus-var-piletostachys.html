Rubus cryptadenus var. piletostachys

Rubus cryptadenus var. piletostachys, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The botanical features of this species make it an important part of local ecosystems and a valuable resource for wildlife.
