Rosa virginiana

Rosa virginiana, commonly known as the Virgin's bower or wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, hip-like fruits. Rosa virginiana thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
