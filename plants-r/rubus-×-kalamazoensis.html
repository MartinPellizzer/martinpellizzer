Rubus × kalamazoensis

Rubus × kalamazoensis, commonly known as the Kalamazoo blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the United States, specifically the Kalamazoo River valley in Michigan. This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is valued for its edible berries, which have a rich, sweet flavor and are used in culinary applications.
