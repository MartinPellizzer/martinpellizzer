Rudolphia

Rudolphia, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. Rudolphia species typically have basal leaves that are either lobed or entire, and they often produce a milky sap. They are native to various regions in Africa, the Americas, and parts of Asia.

Many species within this genus are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
