Ruppia maritima var. brachypus

Ruppia maritima var. brachypus, commonly known as the short-stemmed sea rocket, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its small, grass-like leaves and flowers that bloom in clusters. This variety is typically found in coastal wetlands and brackish waters, where it thrives in shallow, tidal zones. The plant plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its reproductive strategy involves both self-pollination and cross-pollination, enhancing its survival in fluctuating environmental conditions.
