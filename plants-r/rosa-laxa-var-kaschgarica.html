Rosa laxa var. kaschgarica

Rosa laxa var. kaschgarica, commonly known as the Karakorum rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features sprawling, thorny stems that often grow close to the ground, with small, ovate leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a delicate, fragrant scent. It thrives in harsh, alpine conditions, showcasing remarkable adaptability to cold and arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
