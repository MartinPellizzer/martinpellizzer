Reichardia tingitana subsp. orientalis

Reichardia tingitana subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Reichardia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in early spring.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
