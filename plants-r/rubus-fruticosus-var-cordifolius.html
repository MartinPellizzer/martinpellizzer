Rubus fruticosus var. cordifolius

Rubus fruticosus var. cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves that often have a heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
