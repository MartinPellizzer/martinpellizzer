Rumex violascens

Rumex violascens, commonly known as purple sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically purple-tinged on the underside. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for its edible leaves.
