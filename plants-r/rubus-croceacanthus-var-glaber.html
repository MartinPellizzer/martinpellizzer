Rubus croceacanthus var. glaber

Rubus croceacanthus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-fruited raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its smooth, hairless fruit surfaces.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Asia. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though some varieties may exhibit different colors. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

The leaves are usually compound, with serrated edges, and the plant has a woody stem that supports its growth.
