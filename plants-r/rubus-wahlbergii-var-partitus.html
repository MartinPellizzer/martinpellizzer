Rubus wahlbergii var. partitus

Rubus wahlbergii var. partitus, commonly known as the Wahlberg's raspberry, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas, and is often found in regions with a temperate climate.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
