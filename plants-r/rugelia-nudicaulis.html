Rugelia nudicaulis

Rugelia nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender rugelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences and are usually white or pale green, with five petals.

Rugelia nudicaulis is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological value.
