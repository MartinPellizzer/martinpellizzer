Ribes brachystachyum

Ribes brachystachyum, commonly known as the short-stalked gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and edible berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins, and it produces bright yellow to orange fruits in late summer.

Ribes brachystachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
