Rhododendron verticillatum subsp. verticillatum

Rhododendron verticillatum subsp.

verticillatum, commonly known as the alpine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging alpine conditions.
