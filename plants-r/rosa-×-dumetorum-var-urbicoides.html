Rosa × dumetorum var. urbicoides

Rosa × dumetorum var. urbicoides, commonly known as the common dog rose, is a shrub-like plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in early summer. This variety is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
