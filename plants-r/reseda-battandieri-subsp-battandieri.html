Reseda battandieri subsp. battandieri

Reseda battandieri subsp.

battandieri, commonly known as the narrowleaf sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
