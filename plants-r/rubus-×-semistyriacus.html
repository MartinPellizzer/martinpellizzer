Rubus × semistyriacus

Rubus × semistyriacus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many bramble species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are commonly referred to as blackberries, and are known for their high antioxidant content. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various wildlife and its use in horticulture.
