Rondeletia secundiflora

Rondeletia secundiflora, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its bright blooms.

Rondeletia secundiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
