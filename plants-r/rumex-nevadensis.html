Rumex nevadensis

Rumex nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra grassland sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Rumex nevadensis is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native range.
