Rebutia camargoensis

Rebutia camargoensis, commonly known as the Camargo's Rebutia, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and short, cylindrical stems. The plant produces vibrant, colorful flowers during the spring and summer months, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its leaves are usually small and cylindrical, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

Rebutia camargoensis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a houseplant.
