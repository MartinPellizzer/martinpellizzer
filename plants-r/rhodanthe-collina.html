Rhodanthe collina

Rhodanthe collina, commonly known as the coastal rattlepod, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Rhodanthe collina is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local wildlife.
