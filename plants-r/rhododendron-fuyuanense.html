Rhododendron fuyuanense

Rhododendron fuyuanense, commonly known as the Fuyuan rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Sichuan province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils under shaded conditions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
