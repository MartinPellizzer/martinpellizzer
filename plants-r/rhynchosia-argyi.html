Rhynchosia argyi

Rhynchosia argyi, commonly known as the Chinese velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Rhynchosia argyi is also valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
