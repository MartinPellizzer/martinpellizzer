Rhamnus × sempervirens

Rhamnus × sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen buckthorn, is a hybrid shrub that combines traits from its parent species, Rhamnus cathartica and Rhamnus alnifolia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage and small, dark red berries that persist through the winter. The plant is native to the United Kingdom and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows. It has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth margin and produces yellowish flowers in early spring.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
