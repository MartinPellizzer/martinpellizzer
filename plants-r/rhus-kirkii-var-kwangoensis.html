Rhus kirkii var. kwangoensis

Rhus kirkii var. kwangoensis, commonly known as the Kwango buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
