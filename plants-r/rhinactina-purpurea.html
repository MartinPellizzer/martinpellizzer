Rhinactina purpurea

Rhinactina purpurea, commonly known as the purple rhinoceros plant, is a unique species characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and striking purple foliage.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to survive in low-water conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its succulent nature allows it to store water, making it resilient to drought.

Rhinactina purpurea is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
