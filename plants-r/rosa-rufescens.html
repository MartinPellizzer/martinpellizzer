Rosa rufescens

Rosa rufescens, commonly known as the redstem rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its reddish stems and small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces oval, red fruits known as hips.

Rosa rufescens is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
