Rubus rivularis subsp. lusaticus

Rubus rivularis subsp.

lusaticus, commonly known as the Lusatian blackberry, is a shrub native to central Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and woodland edges.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are white with five petals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
