Roegneria ciliaris var. lasiophylla

Roegneria ciliaris var. lasiophylla, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence on the leaf blades. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces inflorescences that are spike-like and bear small, greenish to brownish flowers.

Roegneria ciliaris var. lasiophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
