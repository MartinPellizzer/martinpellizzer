Rubus ulmifolius subsp. dilatatifolius

Rubus ulmifolius subsp.

dilatatifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five oval, serrated leaflets, often with a distinctive broad, rounded base.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, dark purple to black achenes surrounded by a white, fuzzy receptacle. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
