Ribes altissimum var. verruculosum

Ribes altissimum var. verruculosum, commonly known as the roughleaf currant, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and useful plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
