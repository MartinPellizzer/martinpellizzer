Rumex biemarginatus

Rumex biemarginatus, commonly known as narrow-leaved sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive elliptic to ovate leaves with a narrow margin and prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or marshy areas.

Rumex biemarginatus is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
