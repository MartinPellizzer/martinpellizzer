Rosa montosicola

Rosa montosicola, commonly known as the Mexican rose, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically alternate. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa montosicola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many landscapes.
