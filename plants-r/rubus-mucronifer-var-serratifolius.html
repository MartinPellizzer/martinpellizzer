Rubus mucronifer var. serratifolius

Rubus mucronifer var. serratifolius, commonly known as the serrated leaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
