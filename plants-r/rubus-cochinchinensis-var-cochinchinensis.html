Rubus cochinchinensis var. cochinchinensis

Rubus cochinchinensis var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
