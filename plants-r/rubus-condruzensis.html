Rubus condruzensis

Rubus condruzensis, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often used in local traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
