Rubus jaminii

Rubus jaminii, commonly known as the Jamin's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark red berries. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in the spring.

The fruit is edible, though it has a tart flavor and is often used in local cuisines or for making jams and preserves.
