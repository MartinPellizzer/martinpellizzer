Rubus allegheniensis var. gravesii

Rubus allegheniensis var. gravesii, commonly known as Allegheny blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
