Rubus × ripariiformis

Rubus × ripariiformis, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a hybrid plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows along coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in local foraging and culinary practices. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

Its adaptability to coastal conditions makes it an important part of the local flora and ecosystem.
