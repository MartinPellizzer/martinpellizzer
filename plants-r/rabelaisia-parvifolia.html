Rabelaisia parvifolia

Rabelaisia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved rabelaisia, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in shaded understory environments.

Due to habitat loss, Rabelaisia parvifolia is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
