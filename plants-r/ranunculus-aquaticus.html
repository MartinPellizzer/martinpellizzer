Ranunculus aquaticus

Ranunculus aquaticus, commonly known as water crowfoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating or submerged leaves and showy, yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are usually divided into lobes and can be either floating on the water surface or partially submerged.

The flowers are often pollinated by insects and can produce small, green fruits that disperse by water currents.
