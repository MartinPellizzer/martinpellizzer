Rosularia chrysantha

Rosularia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden rose or yellow rose of the desert, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact, low-growing rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a yellowish or golden hue. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Rosularia chrysantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
