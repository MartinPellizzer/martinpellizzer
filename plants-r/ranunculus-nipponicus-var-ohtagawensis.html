Ranunculus nipponicus var. ohtagawensis

Ranunculus nipponicus var. ohtagawensis, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent black centers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream sides. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

The varietal form ohtagawensis is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and floral characteristics.
