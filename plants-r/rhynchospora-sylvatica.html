Rhynchospora sylvatica

Rhynchospora sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features a dense cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color and are pollinated by wind.

Rhynchospora sylvatica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
