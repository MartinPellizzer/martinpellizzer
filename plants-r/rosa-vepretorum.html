Rosa vepretorum

Rosa vepretorum, commonly known as the rough rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in woodland edges and rocky areas. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are pink to red, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The hips, or fruit, are red and contain seeds, often used in traditional medicine.
