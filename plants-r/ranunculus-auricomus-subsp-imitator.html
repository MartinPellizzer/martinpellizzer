Ranunculus auricomus subsp. imitator

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

imitator, commonly known as the mimic buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, cup-shaped structure that resembles a buttercup.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
