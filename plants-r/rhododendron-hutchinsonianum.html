Rhododendron hutchinsonianum

Rhododendron hutchinsonianum, commonly known as the Stewart's rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to reddish-purple flowers that attract pollinators. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
