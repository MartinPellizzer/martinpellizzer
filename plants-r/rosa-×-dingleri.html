Rosa × dingleri

Rosa × dingleri, commonly known as the desert rose or desert shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub with dense, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for xeriscaping and landscaping in dry climates.
