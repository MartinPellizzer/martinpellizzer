Rubus cassoviensis

Rubus cassoviensis, commonly known as the Cassoway blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and understoreys. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are white and appear in early spring.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
