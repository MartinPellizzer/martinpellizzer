Rhynchospora racemosa f. setifolia

Rhynchospora racemosa f. setifolia, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are linear and often have a distinct, hairless surface.

This subspecies is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
