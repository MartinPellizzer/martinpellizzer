Rubus bifrons decalvans

Rubus bifrons decalvans, commonly known as the smooth raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a red, aggregate berry.

This plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
