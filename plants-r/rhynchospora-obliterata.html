Rhynchospora obliterata

Rhynchospora obliterata, commonly known as the Florida marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor, waterlogged environments.

Rhynchospora obliterata plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
