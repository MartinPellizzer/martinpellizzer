Rubus thyrsoideus var. rhamnifolius

Rubus thyrsoideus var. rhamnifolius, commonly known as the blackberry lichen, is a variant of the thyrsoideus species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring dark green, leathery leaves with a distinctive rhamnifolius leaf shape. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters, and it produces edible berries that are similar to blackberries.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
