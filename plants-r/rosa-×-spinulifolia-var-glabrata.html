Rosa × spinulifolia var. glabrata

Rosa × spinulifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties in the species. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
