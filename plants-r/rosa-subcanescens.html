Rosa subcanescens

Rosa subcanescens, commonly known as the silky rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has thin, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. Rosa subcanescens is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
