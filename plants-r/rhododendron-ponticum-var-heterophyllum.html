Rhododendron ponticum var. heterophyllum

Rhododendron ponticum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the Turkish rhododendron, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded or semi-shaded environments. It is known for its rapid growth and can form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.

The leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a leathery texture and a distinctive midrib.
