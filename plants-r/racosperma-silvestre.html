Racosperma silvestre

Racosperma silvestre, commonly known as the wild acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or pale green flowers in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in woodland and shrubland habitats.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a key role in supporting local wildlife and soil health.
