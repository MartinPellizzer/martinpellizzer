Robynsia glabrata

Robynsia glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf robyn, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and forest edges. The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, giving it its common name. Its fragrant white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Robynsia glabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
