Rosa acutantula

Rosa acutantula, commonly known as the sharp-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun.

Rosa acutantula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
