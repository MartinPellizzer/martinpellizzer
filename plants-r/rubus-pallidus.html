Rubus pallidus

Rubus pallidus, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as berries, are typically red or black and are edible, though often tart.

Rubus pallidus is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
