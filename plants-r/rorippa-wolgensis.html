Rorippa wolgensis

Rorippa wolgensis, commonly known as the Korean watercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including Korea and China, and thrives in moist or aquatic environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny white or yellow flowers. It is often found in wetlands, stream sides, and other waterlogged habitats.

Rorippa wolgensis is valued for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
