Rosa glauca f. grandiceps

Rosa glauca f. grandiceps, commonly known as the bluebloom rose, is a horticultural form of the species Rosa glauca.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers, which are borne on short pedicels. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
