Rosa micrantha subsp. lemanii

Rosa micrantha subsp.

lemanii, commonly known as the Leman's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, particularly in regions of Oregon and Washington.

This subspecies is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant produces red-orange hips in the fall.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.
