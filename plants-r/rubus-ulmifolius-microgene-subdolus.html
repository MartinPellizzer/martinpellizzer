Rubus ulmifolius microgene subdolus

Rubus ulmifolius microgene subdolus, commonly known as the small-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
