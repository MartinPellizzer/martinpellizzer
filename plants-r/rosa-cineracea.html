Rosa cineracea

Rosa cineracea, commonly known as the ash-colored rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has slender, thornless stems. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are usually red or orange.

Rosa cineracea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
