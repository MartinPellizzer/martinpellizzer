Rubia tinctorum var. latifolia

Rubia tinctorum var. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved madder, is a variant of the madder plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces a red dye from its roots, which has been historically used in textiles and dyes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its historical significance in natural dye production.
