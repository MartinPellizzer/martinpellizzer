Rosa rubiginosa var. inodora

Rosa rubiginosa var. inodora, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing seeds. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.
