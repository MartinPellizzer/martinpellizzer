Ruellia nudiflora var. occidentalis

Ruellia nudiflora var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western nakedflower ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and usually violet or blue in color, blooming in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
