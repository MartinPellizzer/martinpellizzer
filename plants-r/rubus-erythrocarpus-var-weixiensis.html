Rubus erythrocarpus var. weixiensis

Rubus erythrocarpus var. weixiensis, commonly known as the Weixian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in local cuisine.
