Ranunculus bonplandianus

Ranunculus bonplandianus, commonly known as the Bonpland's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with deeply lobed segments, and its stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus bonplandianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
