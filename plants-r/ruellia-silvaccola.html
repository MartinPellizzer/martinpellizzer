Ruellia silvaccola

Ruellia silvaccola, commonly known as the forest ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its flowering period usually occurs during the rainy season, and it is often found in humid, low-light conditions.

Ruellia silvaccola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
