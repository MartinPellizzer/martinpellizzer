Rutidea rufipilis

Rutidea rufipilis, commonly known as the red-haired legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive reddish-brown pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Rutidea rufipilis is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
