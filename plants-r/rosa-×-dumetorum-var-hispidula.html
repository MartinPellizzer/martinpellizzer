Rosa × dumetorum var. hispidula

Rosa × dumetorum var. hispidula, commonly known as the rough shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which are often covered in a dense layer of short, stiff hairs. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in various climates.
