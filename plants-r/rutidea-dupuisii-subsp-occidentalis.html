Rutidea dupuisii subsp. occidentalis

Rutidea dupuisii subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western rutidion, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California. It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves with a leathery texture and a dense covering of fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This subspecies is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, playing a role in local biodiversity.
