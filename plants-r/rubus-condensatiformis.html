Rubus condensatiformis

Rubus condensatiformis, commonly known as the denseform bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and biodiversity.
