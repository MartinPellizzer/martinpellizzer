Ranunculus macauleyi var. brandegeei

Ranunculus macauleyi var. brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of multiple sepals that resemble petals.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring.
