Rubus maassii subsp. oxyanchus

Rubus maassii subsp.

oxyanchus, commonly known as the Maas's blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for its edible fruits.
