Racosperma insolitum subsp. recurvum

Racosperma insolitum subsp.

recurvum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its recurved leaflets and compact growth habit.
