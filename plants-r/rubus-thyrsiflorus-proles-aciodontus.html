Rubus thyrsiflorus proles aciodontus

Rubus thyrsiflorus proles aciodontus, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Its thorny stems and dense growth habit make it both a persistent weed and a habitat for various wildlife species.
