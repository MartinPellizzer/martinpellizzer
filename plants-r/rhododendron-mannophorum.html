Rhododendron mannophorum

Rhododendron mannophorum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron mannophorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
