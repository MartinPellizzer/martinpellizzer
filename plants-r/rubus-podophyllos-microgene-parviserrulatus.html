Rubus podophyllos microgene parviserrulatus

Rubus podophyllos microgene parviserrulatus, commonly known as the small-toothed blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine, short hairs.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
