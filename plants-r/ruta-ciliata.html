Ruta ciliata

Ruta ciliata, commonly known as blue rue, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves with soft, fuzzy textures and small blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes other citrus-related plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

Ruta ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
