Rhaphidophora latevaginata

Rhaphidophora latevaginata, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arrowhead-shaped, with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for modern interior landscaping.
