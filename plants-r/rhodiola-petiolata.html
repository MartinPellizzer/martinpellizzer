Rhodiola petiolata

Rhodiola petiolata, commonly known as the petioled rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, pink to red flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Rhodiola petiolata is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
