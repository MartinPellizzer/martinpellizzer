Rosa coriifolia subvar. campicola

Rosa coriifolia subvar. campicola, commonly known as the rock rose or mountain rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are hairy beneath. This subvariety is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in gardens with suitable conditions.
