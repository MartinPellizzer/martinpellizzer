Rubus kuebensis

Rubus kuebensis, commonly known as the Kuebensen blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the island of Sardinia and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shrubland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
