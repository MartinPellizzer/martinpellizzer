Rosa stylosa var. evanida

Rosa stylosa var. evanida, commonly known as the pink sandalwood rose, is a rare and delicate species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often arching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swampy woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is noted for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in humid climates.
