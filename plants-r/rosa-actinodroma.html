Rosa actinodroma

Rosa actinodroma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in rocky or limestone soils. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, with five petals. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, and the stems are covered in stiff, glandular hairs.

Rosa actinodroma is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
