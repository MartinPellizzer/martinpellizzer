Ranunculus bullatus subsp. cyrenaicus

Ranunculus bullatus subsp.

cyrenaicus, commonly known as the Cyrenaic buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with prominent, showy petals.

The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
