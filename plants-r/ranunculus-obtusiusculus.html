Ranunculus obtusiusculus

Ranunculus obtusiusculus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features glossy, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy coating and produces showy yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as meadows, woodlands, and stream banks. Its flowers are short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
