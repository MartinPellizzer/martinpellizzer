Restio harveyi

Restio harveyi, commonly known as Harvey's restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
