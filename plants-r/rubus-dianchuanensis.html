Rubus dianchuanensis

Rubus dianchuanensis, commonly known as the Dianchuan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many other species of flowering plants known for their edible berries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark purple to black fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
