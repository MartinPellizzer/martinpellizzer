Rubus × thyrsanthiopsis

Rubus × thyrsanthiopsis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. This shrub is native to the western United States and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of serrated, dark green leaves, and it produces small, dark purple berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its fruit and hardy nature.
