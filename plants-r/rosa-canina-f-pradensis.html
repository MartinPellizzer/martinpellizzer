Rosa canina f. pradensis

Rosa canina f. pradensis, commonly known as the meadow rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its smaller, more delicate flowers compared to the typical Rosa canina. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
