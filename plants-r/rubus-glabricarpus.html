Rubus glabricarpus

Rubus glabricarpus, commonly known as the smooth-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white to pink and appear in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
