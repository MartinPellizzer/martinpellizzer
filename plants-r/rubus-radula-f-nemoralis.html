Rubus radula f. nemoralis

Rubus radula f. nemoralis, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and hedgerows, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.
