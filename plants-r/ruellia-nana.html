Ruellia nana

Ruellia nana, commonly known as dwarf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its compact stature and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ruellia nana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
