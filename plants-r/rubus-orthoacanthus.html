Rubus orthoacanthus

Rubus orthoacanthus, commonly known as the prickly pear raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
