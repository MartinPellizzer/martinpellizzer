Rubus clairbrownii

Rubus clairbrownii, commonly known as Clairbrown's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and Florida. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible blackberries that are small and tart.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
