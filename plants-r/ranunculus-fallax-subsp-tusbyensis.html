Ranunculus fallax subsp. tusbyensis

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

tusbyensis, commonly known as Tusby's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and trifoliate, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is a hardy species that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
