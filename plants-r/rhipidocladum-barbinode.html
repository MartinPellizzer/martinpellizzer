Rhipidocladum barbinode

Rhipidocladum barbinode, commonly known as the barbed node spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny seed capsules. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
