Rubus hirtus var. hirtissimus

Rubus hirtus var. hirtissimus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

This species is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
