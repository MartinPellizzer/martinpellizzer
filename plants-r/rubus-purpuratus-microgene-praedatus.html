Rubus purpuratus microgene praedatus

Rubus purpuratus microgene praedatus, commonly known as the purple-berried thornweed, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and clusters of small, edible purple berries. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pinkish-white with five petals.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
