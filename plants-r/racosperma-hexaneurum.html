Racosperma hexaneurum

Racosperma hexaneurum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical bean-like pods and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish to golden flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, fibrous pods that mature to a reddish-brown color.

Racosperma hexaneurum is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and dry inland environments.
