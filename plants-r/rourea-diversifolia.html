Rourea diversifolia

Rourea diversifolia, commonly known as the variable rourea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short peduncles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage.
