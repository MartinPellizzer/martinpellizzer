Rubus amici

Rubus amici, commonly known as the friend's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Rubus amici plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
