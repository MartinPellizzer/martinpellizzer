Raspalia virgata

Raspalia virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features compound leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name, and small, aggregate fruits that mature to a bright red color. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Raspalia virgata is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
