Rhus humpatensis

Rhus humpatensis, commonly known as the Natal huckleberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. These berries are edible and serve as a food source for various birds and mammals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
