Rubus peracutus

Rubus peracutus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible.

The plant is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in partial shade.
