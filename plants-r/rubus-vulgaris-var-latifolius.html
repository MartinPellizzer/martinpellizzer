Rubus vulgaris var. latifolius

Rubus vulgaris var. latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its broad, serrated leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and useful for fruit production.
