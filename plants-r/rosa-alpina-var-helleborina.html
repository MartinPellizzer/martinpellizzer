Rosa alpina var. helleborina

Rosa alpina var. helleborina, commonly known as the Alpine rose or Helleborine rose, is a variant of the alpine rose native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, semi-double flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.
