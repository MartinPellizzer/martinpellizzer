Rosa obtusifolia var. missniensis

Rosa obtusifolia var. missniensis, commonly known as the Missinippi rose, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly serrated edge. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant suitable for gardens and natural habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
