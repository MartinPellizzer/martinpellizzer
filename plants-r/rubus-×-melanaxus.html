Rubus × melanaxus

Rubus × melanaxus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet-tart flavor. The plant is native to parts of Asia and has been introduced to other regions for its fruit and ornamental value. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both cultivation and ecological studies.
