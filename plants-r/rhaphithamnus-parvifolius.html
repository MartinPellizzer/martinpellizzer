Rhaphithamnus parvifolius

Rhaphithamnus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved rhaphithamnus, is a shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and heat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
