Rumex micranthus

Rumex micranthus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and prominent veins. Its small, greenish flowers are arranged in dense clusters and are borne on slender, erect spikes.

Rumex micranthus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
