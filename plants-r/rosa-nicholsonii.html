Rosa nicholsonii

Rosa nicholsonii, commonly known as the Nicholson rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa nicholsonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
