Rosa glauca var. macracantha

Rosa glauca var. macracantha, commonly known as the blue rose or the bluebush rose, is a variant of the blue rose species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage and pale blue to lavender-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dense, rounded growth habits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a fine, powdery bloom that gives them their characteristic bluish hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
