Ranunculus hevellus

Ranunculus hevellus, commonly known as the Hevell's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus hevellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
