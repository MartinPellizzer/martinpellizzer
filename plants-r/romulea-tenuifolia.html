Romulea tenuifolia

Romulea tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in spring and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils.

Its bulbs store nutrients, allowing it to survive periods of drought and thrive in Mediterranean climates.
