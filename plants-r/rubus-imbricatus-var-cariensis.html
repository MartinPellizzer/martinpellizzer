Rubus imbricatus var. cariensis

Rubus imbricatus var. cariensis, commonly known as the Caribbean bramble, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a subject of botanical study.
