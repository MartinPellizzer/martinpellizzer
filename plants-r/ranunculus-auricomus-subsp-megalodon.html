Ranunculus auricomus subsp. megalodon

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

megalodon, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features large, showy yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals that resemble a cup or shield.

The plant has basal leaves that are palmately divided, and its stems are hairy and often branched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows and along streambanks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its notably larger flowers compared to the typical Ranunculus auricomus.
