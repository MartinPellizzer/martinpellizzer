Rhynchosia ervoidea

Rhynchosia ervoidea, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein. Rhynchosia ervoidea is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its rapid growth makes it useful for erosion control and as a forage crop in certain regions.
