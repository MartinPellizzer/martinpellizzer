Rosa kirsanovae

Rosa kirsanovae, commonly known as the Kirsanov rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late summer, which are followed by orange to red hips. Its botanical characteristics include five petals, a hairy calyx, and numerous stamens.

Rosa kirsanovae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
