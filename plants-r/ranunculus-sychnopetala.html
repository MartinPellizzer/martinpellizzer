Ranunculus sychnopetala

Ranunculus sychnopetala, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent, frilled petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in early summer, and it is known for its compact growth habit.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
