Rhododendron simiarum var. grandifolium

Rhododendron simiarum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
