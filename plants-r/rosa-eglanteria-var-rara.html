Rosa eglanteria var. rara

Rosa eglanteria var. rara, commonly known as the rare prickly rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
