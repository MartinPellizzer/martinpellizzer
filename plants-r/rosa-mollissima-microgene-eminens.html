Rosa mollissima microgene eminens

Rosa mollissima microgene eminens, commonly known as the Ecuadorian rose or the highland rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white fuzzy foliage and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool temperatures. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
