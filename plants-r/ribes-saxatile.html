Ribes saxatile

Ribes saxatile, commonly known as mountain gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually yellow or pale green, occurring in clusters. The berries, which are edible, are small, tart, and typically turn red or orange when mature.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
