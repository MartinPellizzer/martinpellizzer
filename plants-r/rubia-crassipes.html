Rubia crassipes

Rubia crassipes, commonly known as broad-leaved gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Rubia crassipes is part of the Gentianaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
