Rubus × asperipes

Rubus × asperipes, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is both ecologically significant and sometimes considered a weed.
