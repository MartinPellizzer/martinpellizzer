Rubus hirtus mizonodon

Rubus hirtus mizonodon, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems supporting various wildlife species.
