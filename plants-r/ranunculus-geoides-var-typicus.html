Ranunculus geoides var. typicus

Ranunculus geoides var. typicus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has smooth, glossy leaves that are usually trifoliate, with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its delicate appearance and short stature, it is a popular choice for garden cultivation.
