Rosa lucida

Rosa lucida, commonly known as the shining rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America and is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers. The plant features upright stems covered in thorny shoots, and its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets. Rosa lucida produces fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
