Rhododendron × viscosepalum

Rhododendron × viscosepalum, commonly known as the viscosepalum rhododendron, is a hybrid species within the Rhododendron genus.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic beauty.
