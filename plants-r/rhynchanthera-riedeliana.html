Rhynchanthera riedeliana

Rhynchanthera riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and is typically found growing in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Rhynchanthera riedeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
