Rosettea sizenandoi var. brevipilosa

Rosettea sizenandoi var. brevipilosa, commonly known as the short-pilose rosette plant, is a unique bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact rosette structure formed by tightly packed, fleshy, and often spiky leaves. The leaves are typically short and broad, with a distinctive short, fuzzy pubescence that gives it its varietal name. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is well adapted to epiphytic growth on trees.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and conservation efforts.
