Rubus radula var. fortis

Rubus radula var. fortis, commonly known as the hardy blackberry bramble, is a variant of the Rubus radula species.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often cultivated for its edible berries.
