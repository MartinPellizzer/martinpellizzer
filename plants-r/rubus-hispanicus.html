Rubus hispanicus

Rubus hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that produces edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The fruit is used in culinary applications and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
