Raphanus segetum var. arvense

Raphanus segetum var. arvense, commonly known as wild radish, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, fleshy pods that contain several seeds. It is often considered a weed in agricultural fields due to its rapid growth and adaptability.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
