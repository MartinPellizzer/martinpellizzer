Rhododendron reticulatum f. albiflorum

Rhododendron reticulatum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered reticulate rhododendron, is a rare and ornamental variant of the reticulate rhododendron.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers, which contrast with its deep green, leathery leaves. This evergreen shrub typically grows in mountainous regions with acidic soil and high humidity. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

Its botanical features make it a prized specimen in botanical gardens and specialized horticulture.
