Rubus ulmifolius subf. calcicola

Rubus ulmifolius subf.

calcicola, commonly known as the limestone rose, is a subspecies of the bramble family native to rocky and calcareous soils in Europe. It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin.

The flowers are typically pink or white, producing small, aggregate fruits known as berries. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered plant.
