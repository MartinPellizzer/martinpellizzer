Rosa flexuosa subsp. pugetii

Rosa flexuosa subsp.

pugetii, commonly known as the Puget Sound roses, is a subspecies native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring arching, thorned stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets.

The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals, and are produced singly or in small clusters. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and river valleys.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
