Ranunculus buddii f. monochlamydeus

Ranunculus buddii f. monochlamydeus, commonly known as the single-chambered buttercup, is a botanical variant of the broadleaf buttercup.

It is characterized by its distinctive single-chambered pistil, which sets it apart from typical ranunculus species. This plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals, and it blooms in early spring.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
