Ryparosa cauliflora

Ryparosa cauliflora, commonly known as the climbing nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and ability to climb by means of tendrils. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.

Ryparosa cauliflora is notable for its nocturnal flowering habit, attracting pollinators such as moths.
