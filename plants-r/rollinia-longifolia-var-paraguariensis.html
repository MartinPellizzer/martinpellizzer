Rollinia longifolia var. paraguariensis

Rollinia longifolia var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan star-apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a large, round, yellow or green berry with a sweet, aromatic pulp. It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is adapted to well-drained, fertile soils.
