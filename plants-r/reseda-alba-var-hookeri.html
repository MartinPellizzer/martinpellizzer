Reseda alba var. hookeri

Reseda alba var. hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's white sweet rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the mustard family (Brassicaceae) and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Reseda alba var. hookeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in dye production.
