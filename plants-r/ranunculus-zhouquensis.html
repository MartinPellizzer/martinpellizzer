Ranunculus zhouquensis

Ranunculus zhouquensis, commonly known as the Zhouqu buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features a basal rosette of leaves and produces flowers with five to eight sepals and petals that are often similar in appearance. It thrives in moist, cool environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
