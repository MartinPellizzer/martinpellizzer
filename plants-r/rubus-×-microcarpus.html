Rubus × microcarpus

Rubus × microcarpus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. It thrives in coastal regions with mild climates and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
