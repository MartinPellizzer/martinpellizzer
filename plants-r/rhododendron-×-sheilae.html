Rhododendron × sheilae

Rhododendron × sheilae, commonly known as the Sheilae rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Rhododendron, particularly in horticultural settings.
