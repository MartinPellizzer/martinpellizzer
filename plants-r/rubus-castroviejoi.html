Rubus castroviejoi

Rubus castroviejoi, commonly known as the Castroviejoi blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs with thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically consumed raw or used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
