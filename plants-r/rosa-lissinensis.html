Rosa lissinensis

Rosa lissinensis, commonly known as the Irish rose or the Lissinensis rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the western regions of Europe, particularly Ireland and parts of Britain. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of serrated, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the blossoms.

Rosa lissinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
