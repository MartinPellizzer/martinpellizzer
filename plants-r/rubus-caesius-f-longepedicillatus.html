Rubus caesius f. longepedicillatus

Rubus caesius f. longepedicillatus, commonly known as the long-pedicellate blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its elongated pedicels.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically larger than those of the typical Rubus caesius. It is often cultivated for its flavorful fruit and ornamental value.

The foliage is typically dark green with serrated leaves, and the plant thrives in temperate climates.
