Rhododendron geraldii

Rhododendron geraldii, commonly known as Gerald's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to red floral displays. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
