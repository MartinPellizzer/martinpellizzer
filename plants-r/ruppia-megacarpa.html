Ruppia megacarpa

Ruppia megacarpa, commonly known as the giant sea rocket, is a marine flowering plant that thrives in brackish and saltwater environments.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces large, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by water. It is often found in coastal wetlands, estuaries, and tidal flats.

Ruppia megacarpa plays an important role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
