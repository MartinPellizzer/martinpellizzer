Rubus celtoideus

Rubus celtoideus, commonly known as the Canadian blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
