Rhus mairei

Rhus mairei, commonly known as the Maire's buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It has compound leaves that are alternate and typically have 5 to 7 leaflets, which are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by smooth, dark red to black berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.

Rhus mairei plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and supporting local wildlife.
