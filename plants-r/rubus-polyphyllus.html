Rubus polyphyllus

Rubus polyphyllus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
