Richardia rosea f. lilacina

Richardia rosea f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered richardia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, lilac-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value lies in its graceful appearance and fragrant blooms.
