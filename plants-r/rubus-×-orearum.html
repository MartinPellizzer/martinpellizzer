Rubus × orearum

Rubus × orearum, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of many brambles. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible, though they can have a slightly tart flavor.

Rubus × orearum is native to the northern hemisphere and is widely cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
