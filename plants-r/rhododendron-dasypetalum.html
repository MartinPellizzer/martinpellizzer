Rhododendron dasypetalum

Rhododendron dasypetalum, commonly known as the woolly rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments at high elevations. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Rhododendron dasypetalum is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
