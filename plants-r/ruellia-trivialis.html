Ruellia trivialis

Ruellia trivialis, commonly known as greater blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny environments. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Ruellia trivialis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
