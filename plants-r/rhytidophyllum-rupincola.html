Rhytidophyllum rupincola

Rhytidophyllum rupincola, commonly known as the rough bark tree, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark that provides protection against extreme temperatures and drought. The plant has a shrub-like structure with small, leathery leaves that reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters and attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
