Roettlera punduana

Roettlera punduana, commonly known as the Malabar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in moist, shady environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale violet, which bloom in the evening. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Roettlera punduana is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.
