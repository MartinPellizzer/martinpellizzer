Randia cladantha

Randia cladantha, commonly known as the clustered randia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its fruits are capsule-like structures containing numerous seeds.

Randia cladantha is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
