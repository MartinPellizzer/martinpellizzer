Rosa canina var. micropetala

Rosa canina var. micropetala, commonly known as the small-leaved dog rose, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves and fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red, hairy hips that are rich in vitamin C and often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it remains a valued species in horticulture.
