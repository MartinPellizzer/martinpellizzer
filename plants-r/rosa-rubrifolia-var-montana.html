Rosa rubrifolia var. montana

Rosa rubrifolia var. montana, commonly known as the Montana rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features dark green, elliptical leaves with a distinctive reddish underside, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, pinkish-red flowers in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky soils and cold climates, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and attractive foliage.
