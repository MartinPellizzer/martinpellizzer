Ranunculus bellulus

Ranunculus bellulus, commonly known as the fairy cup, is a small, delicate flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a slender, hairy stem that supports a single, cup-shaped flower with five to eight petal-like sepals. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with a distinctive central greenish or brownish disc.

The plant produces small, hairy fruits that release tiny seeds when mature.
