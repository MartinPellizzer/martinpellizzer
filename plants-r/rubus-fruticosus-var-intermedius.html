Rubus fruticosus var. intermedius

Rubus fruticosus var. intermedius, commonly known as bramble or blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along riverbanks.
