Ranunculus gentryanus var. typicus

Ranunculus gentryanus var. typicus, commonly known as the Gentry's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to nine yellow petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

The species is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and is often found in association with other early spring wildflowers.
