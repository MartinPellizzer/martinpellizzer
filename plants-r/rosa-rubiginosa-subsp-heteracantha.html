Rosa rubiginosa subsp. heteracantha

Rosa rubiginosa subsp.

heteracantha, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers.

The leaves are typically pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets. This subspecies is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
