Rubus × semicaesius

Rubus × semicaesius, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to eastern North America and typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has a sprawling growth habit.

Due to its hardy nature, it is both a common weed and a valued fruit crop in certain regions.
