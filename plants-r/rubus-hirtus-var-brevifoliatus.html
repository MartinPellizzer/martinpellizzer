Rubus hirtus var. brevifoliatus

Rubus hirtus var. brevifoliatus, commonly known as the short-leaved brambles, is a variant of the bramble plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves with short petioles. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a red, edible berry. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the eastern United States.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
