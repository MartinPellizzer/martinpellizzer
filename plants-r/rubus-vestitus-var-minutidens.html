Rubus vestitus var. minutidens

Rubus vestitus var. minutidens, commonly known as the small-fruited blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America, characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
