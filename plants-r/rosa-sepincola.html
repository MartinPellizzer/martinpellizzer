Rosa sepincola

Rosa sepincola, commonly known as the Florida rose, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along waterways. The plant features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a serrated edge.

Rosa sepincola is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
