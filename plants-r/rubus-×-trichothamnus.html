Rubus × trichothamnus

Rubus × trichothamnus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry plant, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple or black when mature. These berries are not only edible but also contain high levels of antioxidants and other beneficial compounds.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.
