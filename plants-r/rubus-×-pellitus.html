Rubus × pellitus

Rubus × pellitus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (the red raspberry). This species exhibits a mix of characteristics from both parents, including thorny stems and aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in temperate climates.

It is valued for its flavorful berries, which are used in culinary and commercial applications.
