Rubus sabatinus

Rubus sabatinus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at high elevations. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The plant is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
