Rubus levefactus

Rubus levefactus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thornless stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
