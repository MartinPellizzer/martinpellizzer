Reseda alba subsp. myriosperma

Reseda alba subsp.

myriosperma, commonly known as white reseda, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous structure with hairy stems and alternate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique seed morphology and its classification within the family Brassicaceae.
