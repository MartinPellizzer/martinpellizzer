Rosa sempervirens var. levipes

Rosa sempervirens var. levipes, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

This variety is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
