Rhamnus × hybrida var. billardii

Rhamnus × hybrida var. billardii, commonly known as the hybrid buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting wildlife contribute to its presence in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
