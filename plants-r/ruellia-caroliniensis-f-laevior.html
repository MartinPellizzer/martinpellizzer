Ruellia caroliniensis f. laevior

Ruellia caroliniensis f. laevior, commonly known as the smoothleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its foliage is often densely packed, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The botanical features of this plant make it a valuable component of native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
