Racosperma alatum var. tetranthum

Racosperma alatum var. tetranthum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically broad and feathery, and its yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
