Ranunculus latidens

Ranunculus latidens, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy.

It is a short-lived species, often blooming in early spring before going dormant.
