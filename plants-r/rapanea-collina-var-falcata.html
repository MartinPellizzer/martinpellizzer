Rapanea collina var. falcata

Rapanea collina var. falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed holly, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are ornamental. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its attractive foliage and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
