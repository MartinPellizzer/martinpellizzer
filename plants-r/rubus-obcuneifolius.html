Rubus obcuneifolius

Rubus obcuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a narrow, elongated shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, woodlands and often forms dense thickets.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
