Roegneria spicata

Roegneria spicata, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Roegneria spicata is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in erosion control.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable plant suitable for various ecological and horticultural applications.
