Rosa communis var. stenocarpa

Rosa communis var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited rose, is a species native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces narrow, elongated fruits known as rose hips, which are a distinguishing feature of this variety. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its blooms.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
