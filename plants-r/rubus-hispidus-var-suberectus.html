Rubus hispidus var. suberectus

Rubus hispidus var. suberectus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry or prickly blackberry, is a species of shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This plant is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.
