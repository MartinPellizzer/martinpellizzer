Rhododendron × phoeniceum

Rhododendron × phoeniceum, commonly known as the Cyprus rhododendron, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in different populations.
