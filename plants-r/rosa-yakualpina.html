Rosa yakualpina

Rosa yakualpina, commonly known as the Andean rose, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a subtle scent. Its leaves are dark green and serrated, and it has a woody stem with thorny branches.

Rosa yakualpina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
