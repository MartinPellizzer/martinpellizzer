Rubus perpedatus

Rubus perpedatus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along hedgerows.

The botanical features of Rubus perpedatus make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
