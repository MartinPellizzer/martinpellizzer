Retama raetam subsp. raetam

Retama raetam subsp.

raetam, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has small, leathery leaves and is adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments. It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

The subspecies raetam is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
