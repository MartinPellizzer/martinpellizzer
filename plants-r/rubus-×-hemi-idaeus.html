Rubus × hemi-idaeus

Rubus × hemi-idaeus, commonly known as the blackberry bush, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are composed of multiple small berries clustered together. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are widely cultivated for their sweet, juicy flavor.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
