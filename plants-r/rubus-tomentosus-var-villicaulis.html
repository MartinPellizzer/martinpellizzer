Rubus tomentosus var. villicaulis

Rubus tomentosus var. villicaulis, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, white-tomentose (fuzzy) foliage and stems, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces aggregate fruits, typically dark purple to black, that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
