Rosa petrovicii

Rosa petrovicii, commonly known as the Romanian rose, is a species native to the Carpathian Mountains in Romania.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are usually solitary and have five to seven petals, with a prominent central pistil.

Rosa petrovicii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
