Rubus × carpinifoliiformis

Rubus × carpinifoliiformis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry). This plant typically has thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, edible berries that are a mix of blackberry and raspberry flavors.

The plant is often found in moist, acidic soils in temperate regions.
