Rubus obtruncatus var. noricus

Rubus obtruncatus var. noricus, commonly known as the Austrian blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
