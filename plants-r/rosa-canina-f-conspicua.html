Rosa canina f. conspicua

Rosa canina f. conspicua, commonly known as the showy dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its larger, more showy flowers compared to the typical Rosa canina. The plant produces pale pink to white petals with a distinctive, fragrant aroma. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable ornamental and ecological plant in its native range.
