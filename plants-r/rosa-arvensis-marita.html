Rosa arvensis marita

Rosa arvensis marita, commonly known as the field rose or creeping white rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with five petals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rosa arvensis marita is often found growing along roadsides, in meadows, and in disturbed areas.
