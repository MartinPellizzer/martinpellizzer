Ranunculus uniradicatus

Ranunculus uniradicatus, commonly known as the single-rooted buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a single, short rhizome from which the stem arises, and it produces showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, often with a distinctive white or yellow vein pattern.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
