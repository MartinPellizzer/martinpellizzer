Rafnia lancea

Rafnia lancea, commonly known as the lanceleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Rafnia lancea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
