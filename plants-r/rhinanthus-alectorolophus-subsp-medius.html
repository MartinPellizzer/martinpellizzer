Rhinanthus alectorolophus subsp. medius

Rhinanthus alectorolophus subsp.

medius, commonly known as the common bird's-beak, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family. It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially relies on other plants for water and nutrients through its root system.

It is found throughout much of Europe and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
