Rubia borealis

Rubia borealis, commonly known as Arctic madder, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a distinctive, elongated shape.

Rubia borealis is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
