Rosa bottaiana

Rosa bottaiana, commonly known as the Botta's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features serrated, dark green leaves. Rosa bottaiana is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and desirable plant in horticultural collections.
