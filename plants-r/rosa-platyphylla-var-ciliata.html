Rosa platyphylla var. ciliata

Rosa platyphylla var. ciliata, commonly known as the broadleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptic leaflets that have finely ciliate margins, giving it its botanical name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
