Ranunculus ranunculinus

Ranunculus ranunculinus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and grows up to 30 centimeters in height. Its sap contains protoanemonin, which can cause skin irritation.

Ranunculus ranunculinus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
