Rosa eglanteria marmorea

Rosa eglanteria marmorea, commonly known as the marble curl rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its unique, twisted, and often zigzagging stems that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a silvery-white underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

Rosa eglanteria marmorea is valued in horticulture for its unusual form and hardy nature.
