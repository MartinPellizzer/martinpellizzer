Rhododendron chrysopeplon

Rhododendron chrysopeplon, commonly known as the golden-veined rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. The plant features striking, large, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white with yellow veins, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a slight pubescence.

The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests with well-drained soil.
