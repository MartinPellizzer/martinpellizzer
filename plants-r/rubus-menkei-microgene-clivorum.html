Rubus menkei microgene clivorum

Rubus menkei microgene clivorum, commonly known as the rock raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pink to white in color.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
