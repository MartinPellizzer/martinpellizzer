Ranunculus yunnanensis

Ranunculus yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan buttercup, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in spring and are usually a shade of yellow or white, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
