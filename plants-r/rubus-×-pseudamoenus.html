Rubus × pseudamoenus

Rubus × pseudamoenus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a system of underground runners for propagation.
