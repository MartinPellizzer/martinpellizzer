Rubus hercynicus var. lasiocladus

Rubus hercynicus var. lasiocladus, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued both for its ecological role and its use in local cuisines.
