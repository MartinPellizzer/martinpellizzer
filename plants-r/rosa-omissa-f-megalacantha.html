Rosa omissa f. megalacantha

Rosa omissa f. megalacantha, commonly known as the giant thorned rose, is a horticultural form of the species Rosa omissa.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and robust growth habit, making it a striking addition to gardens. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to the blooms.

This variety is well-suited for colder climates and thrives in full sun, requiring well-drained soil for optimal growth.
