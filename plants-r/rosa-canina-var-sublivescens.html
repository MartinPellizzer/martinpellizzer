Rosa canina var. sublivescens

Rosa canina var. sublivescens, commonly known as the shrub rose or dog rose, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink flowers.

It produces orange-red hips, which are the fruit of the plant and contain numerous seeds. The leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are ovate and serrated. This variety is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
