Rosa accurrens subsp. elongata

Rosa accurrens subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the elongated shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorned stems, which are typical of many rose species.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps it thrive in various soil types.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
