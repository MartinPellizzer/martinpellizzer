Rapistrum rugosum f. orientale

Rapistrum rugosum f. orientale, commonly known as the oriental sowthistle, is a variant of the common sowthistle.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions. It has a rosette growth habit with basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
