Rosa villosa var. corymbosa

Rosa villosa var. corymbosa, commonly known as the woolly rose or the shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in corymbs, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in soft, fuzzy hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
