Rhamnus oleoides subsp. amygdalina

Rhamnus oleoides subsp.

amygdalina, commonly known as the almond buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
