Rosa semibiserrata f. praecox

Rosa semibiserrata f. praecox, commonly known as the early double pink rose, is a botanical variant of the semibiserrata rose species.

It is characterized by its semi-double pink flowers that bloom early in the growing season. The plant features thorny stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves typical of the Rosa genus. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
