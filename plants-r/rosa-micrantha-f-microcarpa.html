Rosa micrantha f. microcarpa

Rosa micrantha f. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces pale pink to white flowers with five petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, red berry that matures in autumn, containing several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
