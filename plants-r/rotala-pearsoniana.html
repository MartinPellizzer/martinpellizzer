Rotala pearsoniana

Rotala pearsoniana, commonly known as the Pearson's rotala, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright green, oval-shaped leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, adding a decorative element to ponds and water gardens. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquascaping.

Rotala pearsoniana is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced aquatic plant growers.
