Rubus festivus var. obscurifrons

Rubus festivus var. obscurifrons, commonly known as the dark-fruited bramble, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, often forming dense thickets.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
