Rubus × cuneifolius

Rubus × cuneifolius, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the evergreen blackberry) and Rubus glaucus (the silverleaf blackberry).

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is widely cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
