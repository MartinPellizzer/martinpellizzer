Ranunculus harveyi var. australis

Ranunculus harveyi var. australis, commonly known as the Australian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
