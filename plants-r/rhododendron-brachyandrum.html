Rhododendron brachyandrum

Rhododendron brachyandrum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of less than one meter, making it ideal for small garden spaces or rock gardens. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and a preference for acidic, well-drained soil.

Rhododendron brachyandrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
