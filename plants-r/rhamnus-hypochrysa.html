Rhamnus hypochrysa

Rhamnus hypochrysa, commonly known as the golden-leafed buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that turn a striking yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
