Rottboellia erecta

Rottboellia erecta, commonly known as the false signalgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Rottboellia erecta is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both wet and dry conditions.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an important component of many grassland ecosystems.
