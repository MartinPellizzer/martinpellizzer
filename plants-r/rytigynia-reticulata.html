Rytigynia reticulata

Rytigynia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined rytigynia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, net-like venation pattern on its leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Rytigynia reticulata is often found in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to thrive.
