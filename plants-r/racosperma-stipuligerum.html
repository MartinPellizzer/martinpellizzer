Racosperma stipuligerum

Racosperma stipuligerum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
