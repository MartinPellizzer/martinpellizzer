Rubus hirtus var. kosaninii

Rubus hirtus var. kosaninii, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with specific adaptations for its native habitat.
