Ranunculus angustior

Ranunculus angustior, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It is characterized by its rhizomatous root system and is often found in regions with temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
