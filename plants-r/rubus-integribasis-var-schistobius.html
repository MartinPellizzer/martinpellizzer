Rubus integribasis var. schistobius

Rubus integribasis var. schistobius, commonly known as the smooth-leafed blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, often with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other Rubus species, particularly in its leaf structure and fruit morphology.
