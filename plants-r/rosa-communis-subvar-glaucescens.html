Rosa communis subvar. glaucescens

Rosa communis subvar. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-gray rose, is a variety of the common rose characterized by its distinctive bluish-gray foliage.

It produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems and compound leaves that have a soft, velvety texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This subvariety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its historical use in perfumery and medicine.
