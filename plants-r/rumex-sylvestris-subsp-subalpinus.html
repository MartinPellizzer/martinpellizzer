Rumex sylvestris subsp. subalpinus

Rumex sylvestris subsp.

subalpinus, commonly known as the alpine sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, often at elevations above 1,500 meters.

The plant features dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, erect spikes.

It is adapted to cold climates and has a rosette growth form to conserve heat and moisture.
