Rhynchosia minima var. nuda

Rhynchosia minima var. nuda, commonly known as the naked slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred petal that aids in pollination.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional cuisines and agricultural practices.
