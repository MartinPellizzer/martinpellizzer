Rubus parvifolius f. leucanthus

Rubus parvifolius f. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered bramble, is a variant of the common bramble, characterized by its pale white flowers.

It is a shrub native to eastern Asia, including regions of China and Japan. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique floral coloration and its role in local ecosystems.
