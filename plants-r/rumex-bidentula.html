Rumex bidentula

Rumex bidentula, commonly known as the two-toothed sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base and two prominent teeth along the leaf margin. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces reddish-brown seeds.

Rumex bidentula is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
