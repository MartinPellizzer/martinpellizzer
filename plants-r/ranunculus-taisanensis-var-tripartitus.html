Ranunculus taisanensis var. tripartitus

Ranunculus taisanensis var. tripartitus, commonly known as the Three-lobed Buttercup, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three parts, which is a distinguishing feature.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
