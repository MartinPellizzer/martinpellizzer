Ranunculus abditus

Ranunculus abditus, commonly known as the hidden buttercup, is a rare and elusive member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, typically yellow or white flowers with five to eight petals. The plant is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ranunculus abditus is considered a species of conservation concern.
