Rosa coriifolia var. silvescens

Rosa coriifolia var. silvescens, commonly known as the silvery shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, stipitate buds that open into clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in silvery-gray hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and hardy nature, suitable for both garden borders and hedging.
