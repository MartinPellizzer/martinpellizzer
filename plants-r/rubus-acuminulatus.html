Rubus acuminulatus

Rubus acuminulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny habitats.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and blackberry species.
