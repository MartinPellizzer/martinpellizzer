Ranunculus junipericola

Ranunculus junipericola, commonly known as the juniperous buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus junipericola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
