Rubus fontivagus

Rubus fontivagus, commonly known as the water raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in jams and pies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

Rubus fontivagus plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and small mammals.
