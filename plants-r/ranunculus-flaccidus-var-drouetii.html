Ranunculus flaccidus var. drouetii

Ranunculus flaccidus var. drouetii, commonly known as the Drouet's buttercup, is a variant of the flaccid buttercup native to southern Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial with delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and it has a short, stout stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blossoms.
