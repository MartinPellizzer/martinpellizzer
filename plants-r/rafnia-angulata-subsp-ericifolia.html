Rafnia angulata subsp. ericifolia

Rafnia angulata subsp.

ericifolia, commonly known as the Ericifoliate Rafnia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
