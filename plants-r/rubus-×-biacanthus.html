Rubus × biacanthus

Rubus × biacanthus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, dark red to black berries. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal chaparral habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
