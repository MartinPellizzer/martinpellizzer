Ranunculus acriformis

Ranunculus acriformis, commonly known as the acicular buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant has palmately divided leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is typically found in alpine environments at high elevations.

Ranunculus acriformis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
