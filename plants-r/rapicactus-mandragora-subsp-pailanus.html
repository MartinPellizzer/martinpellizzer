Rapicactus mandragora subsp. pailanus

Rapicactus mandragora subsp.

pailanus, commonly known as the Pailan's rock cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Central America. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in dense, woolly hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh environments make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
