Rhododendron loranthiflorum subsp. lakekamuense

Rhododendron loranthiflorum subsp.

lakekamuense, commonly known as the Lake Kamu rhododendron, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in cool, moist habitats such as forests and shrublands at high altitudes. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves with a leathery texture and inflorescences that bear numerous small to medium-sized blooms.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native environment.
