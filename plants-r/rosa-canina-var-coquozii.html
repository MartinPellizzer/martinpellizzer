Rosa canina var. coquozii

Rosa canina var. coquozii, commonly known as the Coquozii dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in areas of the Tian Shan and Pamir ranges. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit, thorny stems, and clusters of fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces red, hairy hips that are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
