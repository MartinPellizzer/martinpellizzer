Rumex × dumulosus

Rumex × dumulosus, commonly known as the common dock, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are arranged in racemes. It is often found in moist soils along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Rumex × dumulosus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
