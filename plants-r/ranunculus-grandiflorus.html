Ranunculus grandiflorus

Ranunculus grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with numerous petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are hairy.

Ranunculus grandiflorus is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in early spring.
