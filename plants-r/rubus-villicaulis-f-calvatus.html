Rubus villicaulis f. calvatus

Rubus villicaulis f. calvatus, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its smooth, hairless stems.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of common blackberries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This form is valued for its unique appearance and potential use in ornamental gardening.
