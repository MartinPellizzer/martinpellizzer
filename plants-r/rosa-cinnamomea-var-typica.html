Rosa cinnamomea var. typica

Rosa cinnamomea var. typica, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin and a hairy underside. Its stems are often covered in fine, reddish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The rose is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
