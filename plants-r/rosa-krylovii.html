Rosa krylovii

Rosa krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov rose, is a species native to Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges and a woody, perennial structure.

Rosa krylovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
