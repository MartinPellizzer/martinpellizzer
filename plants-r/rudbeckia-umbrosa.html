Rudbeckia umbrosa

Rudbeckia umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking yellow to orange ray flowers surrounding a dark brown, cone-shaped center. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Rudbeckia umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
