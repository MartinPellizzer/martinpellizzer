Racinaea flexuosa

Racinaea flexuosa, commonly known as the creeping racinaea, is a rare and unusual plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped leaves that are often described as resembling the coils of a spring. The plant typically grows in dense, humid forests, where it thrives in the understory with filtered sunlight. Its flowers, which are small and clustered, are usually greenish-white in color and lack showy petals.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Racinaea flexuosa is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its natural environment.
