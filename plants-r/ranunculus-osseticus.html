Ranunculus osseticus

Ranunculus osseticus, commonly known as the Ossetian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem that supports its palmately divided, bright green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven sepals that are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
