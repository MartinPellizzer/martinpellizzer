Rubus villicaulis subsp. confinis

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

confinis, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
