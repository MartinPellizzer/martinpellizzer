Rhododendron brachycarpum var. rosiflorum

Rhododendron brachycarpum var. rosiflorum, commonly known as the rose-flowered rhododendron, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic, well-drained soil.
