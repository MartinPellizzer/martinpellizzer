Ranunculus javanicus

Ranunculus javanicus, commonly known as the Java buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to bright yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus javanicus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
