Rotala dhaneshiana

Rotala dhaneshiana, commonly known as the Indian waterwheel plant, is a floating aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features whorled leaves that are typically green or red, depending on the variety, and can grow up to 20 cm in length. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it produces small, round fruits that float on the water surface. It is commonly cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to oxygenate water.

Rotala dhaneshiana thrives in shallow, still waters with moderate to high light exposure.
