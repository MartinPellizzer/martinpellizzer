Ranunculus parvitorifer

Ranunculus parvitorifer, commonly known as the small-fruited buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. Its flowers consist of numerous sepals that resemble petals, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Ranunculus parvitorifer is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
