Rhinacanthus

Rhinacanthus, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that produces vibrant orange-red flowers, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also have traditional uses in herbal medicine.
