Rosa alpina f. stenodonta

Rosa alpina f. stenodonta, commonly known as the alpine rose or narrow-leaved alpine rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features narrow, elliptical leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
