Rhododendron impressopunctatum

Rhododendron impressopunctatum, commonly known as the punctured leaf rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with elliptical leaves that have distinctive punctate markings. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
