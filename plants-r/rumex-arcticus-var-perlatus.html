Rumex arcticus var. perlatus

Rumex arcticus var. perlatus, commonly known as the Arctic sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and thrives in cold climates. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes.

It is well adapted to harsh environments, with a short growing season and a deep root system.
