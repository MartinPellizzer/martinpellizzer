Rungia muralis

Rungia muralis, commonly known as the rock apple or wall apple, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, red or yellow berries that contain several seeds.

Rungia muralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
