Rubus laschii proles aschersonii

Rubus laschii proles aschersonii, commonly known as the Ascherson's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
