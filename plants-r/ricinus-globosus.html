Ricinus globosus

Ricinus globosus, commonly known as the globe castor, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The seeds of Ricinus globosus contain toxic compounds, making them potentially harmful if ingested.
