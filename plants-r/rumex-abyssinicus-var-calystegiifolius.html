Rumex abyssinicus var. calystegiifolius

Rumex abyssinicus var. calystegiifolius, commonly known as the Abyssinian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in late spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in local cuisines.
