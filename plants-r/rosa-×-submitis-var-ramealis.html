Rosa × submitis var. ramealis

Rosa × submitis var. ramealis, commonly known as the Rameal rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant has dark green, glossy foliage that provides a striking contrast to its blooms. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
