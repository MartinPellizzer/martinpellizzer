Rosa canina pyriformis

Rosa canina pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-shaped dog rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its distinctive pear-shaped hips, which are typically orange to red in color and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. The plant produces fragrant pink to white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the characteristic fruiting bodies. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in woodland areas and along hedgerows.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental value.
