Roseanthus albiflorus

Roseanthus albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered roseanthus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
