Ranunculus geraniifolius proles gracilis

Ranunculus geraniifolius proles gracilis, commonly known as the slender-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the species is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant has a short stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
