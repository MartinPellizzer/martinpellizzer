Randia nalaensis var. rotundata

Randia nalaensis var. rotundata, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.
