Rubus × castaneicola

Rubus × castaneicola, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark red to black in color. The plant is native to East Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its fruit in some regions.
