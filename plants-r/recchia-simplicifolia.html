Recchia simplicifolia

Recchia simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved recchia, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are typically clustered in whorled inflorescences and are usually pink or purple in color. It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in moist, shady environments.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
