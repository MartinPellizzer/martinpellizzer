Restio megalotheca

Restio megalotheca, commonly known as the giant restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white flowers. Restio megalotheca typically grows in coastal sand dunes and is adapted to saline and arid conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
