Rubus × pseudoandegavensis

Rubus × pseudoandegavensis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus chilensis (the raspberry). This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color.

The botanical features of Rubus × pseudoandegavensis make it a unique and valuable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
