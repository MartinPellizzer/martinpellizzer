Rhododendron edanoi

Rhododendron edanoi, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a species native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers in the spring, which are often fragrant. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron edanoi thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
