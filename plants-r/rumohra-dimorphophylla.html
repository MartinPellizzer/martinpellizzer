Rumohra dimorphophylla

Rumohra dimorphophylla, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 5 meters. The plant features a stout, creeping rhizome that supports its massive foliage. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that are clustered in sori on the undersides of the fronds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to humid, shaded environments.
