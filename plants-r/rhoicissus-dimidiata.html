Rhoicissus dimidiata

Rhoicissus dimidiata, commonly known as the double-flowered honeysuckle, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, double flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, green, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, often twining around supports.

Rhoicissus dimidiata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
