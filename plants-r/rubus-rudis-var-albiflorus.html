Rubus rudis var. albiflorus

Rubus rudis var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems and compound leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
