Ribes frankei

Ribes frankei, commonly known as the Frank's currant, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Ribes.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a red, edible berry that ripens in the fall and is often used in jams and preserves.
