Restio aspericaulis

Restio aspericaulis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
