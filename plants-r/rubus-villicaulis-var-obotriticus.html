Rubus villicaulis var. obotriticus

Rubus villicaulis var. obotriticus, commonly known as the silky blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that are covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
