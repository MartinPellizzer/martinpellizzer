Raphanistrum lampsana subsp. odessanum

Raphanistrum lampsana subsp.

odessanum, commonly known as the Odessan rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the steppes of southern Europe and southwestern Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often branched and covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense spikes and are wind-pollinated.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
