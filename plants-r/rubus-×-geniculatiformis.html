Rubus × geniculatiformis

Rubus × geniculatiformis, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a hybrid plant species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces edible, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental and culinary value, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
