Rosa agrestis var. albiflora

Rosa agrestis var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered field rose, is a variant of the field rose species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its simple, thorned stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a subtle, sweet scent.

Rosa agrestis var. albiflora is often found in open fields, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.
