Rosa × sabinii var. subnuda

Rosa × sabinii var. subnuda, commonly known as the naked-stem dogwood rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems that are often bare or sparsely covered with leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five to seven petals, and are borne on long, slender peduncles.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
