Rubus praecox subsp. macrostemon

Rubus praecox subsp.

macrostemon, commonly known as the early blackberry or red raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when mature. It blooms with white to pink flowers in the spring and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its edible berries and is commonly cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
