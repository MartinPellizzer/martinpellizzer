Rubus atrocalyx

Rubus atrocalyx, commonly known as the black-fruited bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that mature in late summer. The plant is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. Its leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and can also be used to make jams and wines.
