Rubus carmauxensis

Rubus carmauxensis, commonly known as the Carmaux blackberry, is a shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
