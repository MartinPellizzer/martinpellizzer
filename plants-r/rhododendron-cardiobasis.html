Rhododendron cardiobasis

Rhododendron cardiobasis, commonly known as the heartleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture and a dark green color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
