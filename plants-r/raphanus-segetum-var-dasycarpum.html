Raphanus segetum var. dasycarpum

Raphanus segetum var. dasycarpum, commonly known as the hairy-seed radish, is a cultivated variant of the radish plant.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant produces small, round seeds that are covered in a dense, hairy coating, which aids in seed dispersal. It thrives in cool climates and is often grown as a root vegetable for its edible parts.

This variety is valued for its crisp texture and mild flavor, making it a popular choice in various cuisines.
