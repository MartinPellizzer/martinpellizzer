Rubus clethraphilus subsp. tarnensis

Rubus clethraphilus subsp.

tarnensis, commonly known as the Tarn blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pyrenees region of Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native habitat.
