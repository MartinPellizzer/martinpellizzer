Rubus blandulus

Rubus blandulus, commonly known as the common blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are edible and highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content. Rubus blandulus thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to soil stabilization.
