Rubus koehleri subsp. spinulifer

Rubus koehleri subsp.

spinulifer, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
