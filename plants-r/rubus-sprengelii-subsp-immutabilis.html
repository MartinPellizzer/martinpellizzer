Rubus sprengelii subsp. immutabilis

Rubus sprengelii subsp.

immutabilis, commonly known as the immutability blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
