Rhododendron viscosum var. serrulatum

Rhododendron viscosum var. serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated sticky alpine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically leathery and have a sticky texture, which is a distinguishing feature of the species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

This variety is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
