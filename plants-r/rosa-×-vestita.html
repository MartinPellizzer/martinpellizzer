Rosa × vestita

Rosa × vestita, commonly known as the White Garden Rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that often have a subtle pink or red hue at the base. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Rosa × vestita is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in floral arrangements and perfumery.
