Rubus caesius var. arvensis

Rubus caesius var. arvensis, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental and a useful plant for wildlife and agriculture.
