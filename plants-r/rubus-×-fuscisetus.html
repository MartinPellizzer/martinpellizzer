Rubus × fuscisetus

Rubus × fuscisetus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are usually compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces flowers that range from white to pink.

This species is valued for its edible berries and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
