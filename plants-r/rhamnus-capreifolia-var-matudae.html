Rhamnus capreifolia var. matudae

Rhamnus capreifolia var. matudae, commonly known as the Matudae buckthorn, is a variant of the European buckthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula, characterized by its dense, thorny branches and dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by black, egg-shaped berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in Mediterranean flora and ecological studies.
