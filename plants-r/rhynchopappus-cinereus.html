Rhynchopappus cinereus

Rhynchopappus cinereus, commonly known as the silverleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its silver-gray foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Rhynchopappus cinereus produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
