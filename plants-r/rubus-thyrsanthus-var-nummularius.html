Rubus thyrsanthus var. nummularius

Rubus thyrsanthus var. nummularius, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black in color. Its leaves are often covered in a silvery-white waxy coating, which gives it its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
