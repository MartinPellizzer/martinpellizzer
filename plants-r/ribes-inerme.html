Ribes inerme

Ribes inerme, commonly known as smooth gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes in the family Grossulariaceae. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are smooth and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, followed by edible, green berries that ripen to a red or orange color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its berries, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
