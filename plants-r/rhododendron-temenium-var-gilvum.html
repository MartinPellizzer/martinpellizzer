Rhododendron temenium var. gilvum

Rhododendron temenium var. gilvum, commonly known as the golden-leafed rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that exhibits a striking golden hue, especially in the younger leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
