Rubus pinnatus var. subglandulosus

Rubus pinnatus var. subglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves.

It is native to parts of North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, sticky hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
