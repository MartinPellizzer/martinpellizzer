Rosa retusata

Rosa retusata, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa retusata is evergreen, with thorny stems that provide structural support.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
