Rubus × longiculpis

Rubus × longiculpis, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its berries are usually dark red to black in color and have a tart flavor.

It is cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a fruit crop.
