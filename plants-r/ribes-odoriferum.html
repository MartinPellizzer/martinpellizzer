Ribes odoriferum

Ribes odoriferum, commonly known as the fragrant gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of shrubs and vines commonly found in temperate regions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible berries that range in color from yellow to red.

Ribes odoriferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
