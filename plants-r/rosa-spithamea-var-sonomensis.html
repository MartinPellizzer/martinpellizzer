Rosa spithamea var. sonomensis

Rosa spithamea var. sonomensis, commonly known as the Sonoma rose, is a rare and endemic species native to California.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its pink to reddish-pink flowers and thorny stems. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry slopes and chaparral habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
