Rosa dahlii

Rosa dahlii, commonly known as the Dutch rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red.

Its stems are usually thornless, and it produces rose hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. Rosa dahlii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
