Rubus ulmifolius var. anoplothyrsus

Rubus ulmifolius var. anoplothyrsus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, achenes surrounded by a fleshy receptacle. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The variety is distinguished by its dense, woolly covering on the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.
