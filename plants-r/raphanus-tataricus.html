Raphanus tataricus

Raphanus tataricus, commonly known as the Tatarian radish, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features a short, stout stem with broad, lobed leaves that are typically hairy. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the family.

The plant produces a fleshy, edible root that is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
