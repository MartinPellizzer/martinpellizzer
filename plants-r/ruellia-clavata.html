Ruellia clavata

Ruellia clavata, commonly known as the purpleleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, which bloom in the summer months. Ruellia clavata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
