Rosa incana

Rosa incana, commonly known as the rough rose, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The leaves are typically grayish-green with a fuzzy texture and serrated edges. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
