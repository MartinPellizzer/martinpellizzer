Rubus × pseudoarrigens

Rubus × pseudoarrigens, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which consist of numerous small drupelets. The plant is native to North America and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as woodland edges and streambanks. It produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and used in jams and pies.

The species is notable for its adaptability and widespread distribution across various regions of the continent.
