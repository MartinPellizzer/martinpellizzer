Robynsia macrophylla

Robynsia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed robyn, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries.
