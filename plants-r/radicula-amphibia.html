Radicula amphibia

Radicula amphibia, commonly known as the water root fern, is a unique aquatic plant that thrives in moist or submerged environments.

It features a creeping rhizome that spreads along the substrate, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption. The plant produces small, delicate fronds that float on the water surface, aiding in photosynthesis. Its ability to survive in both water and damp soil makes it highly adaptable to various wetland habitats.

Radicula amphibia plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by preventing soil erosion.
