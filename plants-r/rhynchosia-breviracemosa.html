Rhynchosia breviracemosa

Rhynchosia breviracemosa, commonly known as the short raceme cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact racemes. Its pods are hairy and contain edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines.

Rhynchosia breviracemosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
