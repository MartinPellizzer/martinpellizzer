Rubus radula f. sparsisetus

Rubus radula f. sparsisetus, commonly known as the sparse-hairy raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its distinctive foliage and fruiting habits.

It is a deciduous shrub native to certain regions of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a sparse covering of fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance. Its berries are typically smaller than those of other raspberry species and have a tart flavor.

This form is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential use in local culinary traditions.
