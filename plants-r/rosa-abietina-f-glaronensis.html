Rosa abietina f. glaronensis

Rosa abietina f. glaronensis, commonly known as the Glaronensis rose, is a rare and ornamental cultivar of the rose family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. The plant features serrated, dark green leaves and produces hips that are typically red or orange in color. It is native to the Alps and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its delicate beauty and is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant.
