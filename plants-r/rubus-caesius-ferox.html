Rubus caesius ferox

Rubus caesius ferox, commonly known as the purple-berried bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but can be quite tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
