Rosa coriifolia var. grajica

Rosa coriifolia var. grajica, commonly known as the Grajica rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has serrated, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate blooms and fragrant scent.
