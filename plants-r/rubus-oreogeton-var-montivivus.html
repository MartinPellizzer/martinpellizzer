Rubus oreogeton var. montivivus

Rubus oreogeton var. montivivus, commonly known as the Sierra blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats at higher elevations. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and have a tart, acidic flavor.
