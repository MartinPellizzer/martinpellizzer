Rubus innominatus var. macrosepalus

Rubus innominatus var. macrosepalus, commonly known as the large-sepal raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its flowers are pink to white, with five large, showy sepals that are distinctive in appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.
