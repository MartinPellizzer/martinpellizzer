Rubia albocostata

Rubia albocostata, commonly known as the white-costed balm, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
