Rhinotropis rusbyi

Rhinotropis rusbyi, commonly known as the Rusby's rhino orchid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, orchid-like flowers that typically bloom in the spring. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
