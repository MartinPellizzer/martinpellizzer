Rubus radula var. debiliramosus

Rubus radula var. debiliramosus, commonly known as the weak-stemmed dewberry, is a variant of the dewberry species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature, similar to other Rubus species. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, often with a hairy underside.

This variant is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
