Rhamnus alaternus subvar. obovata

Rhamnus alaternus subvar. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and oval-shaped, dark green leaves that are often obovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are rich in anthocyanins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
