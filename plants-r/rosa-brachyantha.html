Rosa brachyantha

Rosa brachyantha, commonly known as the Chilean rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, reddish-pink fruits known as hips.

Rosa brachyantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its beauty and ability to cascade over trellises or hanging baskets.
