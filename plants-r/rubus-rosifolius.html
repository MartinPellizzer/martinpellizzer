Rubus rosifolius

Rubus rosifolius, commonly known as the roseleaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves that resemble those of roses. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by red, edible berries in summer.

The plant is often found in woodland edges and shrublands, thriving in well-drained soils.
