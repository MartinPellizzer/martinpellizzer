Rumex salicifolius subsp. montigenitus

Rumex salicifolius subsp.

montigenitus, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on erect, slender stems. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
