Rubus echinatoides

Rubus echinatoides, commonly known as the thorny blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus echinatoides is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
