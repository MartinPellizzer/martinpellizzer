Rubus × amblyphyllus

Rubus × amblyphyllus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
