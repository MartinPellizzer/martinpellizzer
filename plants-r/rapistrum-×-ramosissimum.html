Rapistrum × ramosissimum

Rapistrum × ramosissimum, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive mustard scent. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the early to mid-summer.

The plant produces spherical, three-lobed pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
