Rosa × radiolofera

Rosa × radiolofera, commonly known as the Radiolata rose, is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink. The plant has a compact growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, glossy foliage. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as cut flowers.

Rosa × radiolofera prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both landscape and container gardening.
