Rodriguezia cinnabarina

Rodriguezia cinnabarina, commonly known as the red-veined hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Rodriguezia cinnabarina is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
