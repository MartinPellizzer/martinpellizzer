Rhodiola fastigiata var. gelida

Rhodiola fastigiata var. gelida, commonly known as the alpine rhodiola, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense, fleshy leaves that are adapted to cold and arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountain tundra.

Rhodiola fastigiata var. gelida is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.
