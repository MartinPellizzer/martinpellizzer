Rubus vitifolius var. eastwoodianus

Rubus vitifolius var. eastwoodianus, commonly known as Eastwood's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep purple or black color, often used in local foraging and traditional practices. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique genetic variation and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
