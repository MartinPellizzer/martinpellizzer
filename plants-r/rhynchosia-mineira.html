Rhynchosia mineira

Rhynchosia mineira, commonly known as the Brazilian cowpea or Mineira bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Rhynchosia mineira is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
