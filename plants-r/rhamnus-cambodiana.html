Rhamnus cambodiana

Rhamnus cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
