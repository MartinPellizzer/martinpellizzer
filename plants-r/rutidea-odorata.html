Rutidea odorata

Rutidea odorata, commonly known as the fragrant rutia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a strong, sweet aroma that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Rutidea odorata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
