Rosa sericea f. inermiseglandulosa

Rosa sericea f. inermiseglandulosa, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its finely pubescent, silvery foliage.

The plant typically features slender, thornless stems that are covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs. Its leaves are compound, with ovate leaflets that are also densely hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The flowers are usually pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rosa genus.

This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant foliage and delicate floral display.
