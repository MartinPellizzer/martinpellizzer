Rosa corymbifera var. oksijukii

Rosa corymbifera var. oksijukii, commonly known as the Korean climbing rose, is a variant of the shrub rose native to Korea.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden trellises or hedging.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its floral display.
