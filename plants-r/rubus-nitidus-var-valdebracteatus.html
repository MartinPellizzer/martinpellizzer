Rubus nitidus var. valdebracteatus

Rubus nitidus var. valdebracteatus, commonly known as the bracted blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
