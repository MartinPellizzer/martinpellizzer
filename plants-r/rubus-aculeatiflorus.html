Rubus aculeatiflorus

Rubus aculeatiflorus, commonly known as the prickly-flowered blackberry, is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.

Rubus aculeatiflorus is native to parts of Asia and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
