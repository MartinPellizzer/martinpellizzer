Rosa coziae f. pomiformis

Rosa coziae f. pomiformis, commonly known as the pomiform rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa coziae species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, fruit-like buds that resemble small, fleshy orbs. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, adding texture to its appearance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
