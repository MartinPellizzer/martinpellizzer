Rubus ulmifolius var. suborbicularis

Rubus ulmifolius var. suborbicularis, commonly known as the suborbicular blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are slightly lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
