Rubus chlorothyrsos var. fictiformis

Rubus chlorothyrsos var. fictiformis, commonly known as the false raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by edible, red berries that are similar in appearance to true raspberries. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
