Racosperma mollifolium

Racosperma mollifolium, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features compound leaves with soft, hairy surfaces that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically visited by insects. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland areas.

Racosperma mollifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
