Rosa × braunii var. belocarpa

Rosa × braunii var. belocarpa, commonly known as the Pacific coast rose, is a hybrid species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with multiple stems that often root at the base. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and are covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

This variety is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
