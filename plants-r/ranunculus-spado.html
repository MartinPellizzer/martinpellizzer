Ranunculus spado

Ranunculus spado, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as meadows and woodland edges. It is known for its attractive appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
