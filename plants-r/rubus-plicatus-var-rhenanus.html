Rubus plicatus var. rhenanus

Rubus plicatus var. rhenanus, commonly known as the European raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, juicy berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible fruits.
