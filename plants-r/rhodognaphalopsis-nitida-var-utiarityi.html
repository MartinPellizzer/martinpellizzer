Rhodognaphalopsis nitida var. utiarityi

Rhodognaphalopsis nitida var. utiarityi, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features small, fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, adding a subtle floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an attractive and functional addition to native plant gardens.
