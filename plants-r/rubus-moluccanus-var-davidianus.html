Rubus moluccanus var. davidianus

Rubus moluccanus var. davidianus, commonly known as the David's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its dense foliage and its ability to form thickets, making it a significant species in its native ecosystem.
