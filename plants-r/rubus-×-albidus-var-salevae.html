Rubus × albidus var. salevae

Rubus × albidus var. salevae, commonly known as the saltbush raspberry.

This variant is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a tart flavor. The plant is adapted to saline soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
