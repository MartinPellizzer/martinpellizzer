Rebutia blossfeldii

Rebutia blossfeldii, commonly known as the Desert Cactus or Blossfeld's Rebutia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of spines that protect it from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to dry environments, requiring minimal water and thriving in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
