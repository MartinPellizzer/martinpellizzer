Rosa rubiginosa var. sylvicola

Rosa rubiginosa var. sylvicola, commonly known as the woodland rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. Its hips, or fruits, are orange-red and contain numerous seeds.

Rosa rubiginosa var. sylvicola is adapted to rocky and dry soils, often growing in woodland or scrubland habitats.
