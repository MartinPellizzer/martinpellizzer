Rubus plicatus var. cordifolius

Rubus plicatus var. cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and have a velvety underside. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and edible fruits.
