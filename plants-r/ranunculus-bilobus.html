Ranunculus bilobus

Ranunculus bilobus, commonly known as the two-lobed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. The plant has a short, stout stem with trifoliate leaves, and its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Ranunculus bilobus is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.
