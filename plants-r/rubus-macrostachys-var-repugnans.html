Rubus macrostachys var. repugnans

Rubus macrostachys var. repugnans, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and broad, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
