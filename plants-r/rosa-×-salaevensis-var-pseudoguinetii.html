Rosa × salaevensis var. pseudoguinetii

Rosa × salaevensis var. pseudoguinetii, commonly known as the Siberian rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates. The plant typically features dark green, glossy leaves and produces fragrant, semi-double pink to white flowers. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

This variety is well-suited for gardens in temperate to subarctic regions.
