Rubus ulmifolius subsp. rusticus

Rubus ulmifolius subsp.

rusticus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
