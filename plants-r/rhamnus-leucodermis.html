Rhamnus leucodermis

Rhamnus leucodermis, commonly known as the white-barked buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems that often have a whitish or pale bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by black, egg-shaped berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and stabilizing coastal or rocky habitats.
