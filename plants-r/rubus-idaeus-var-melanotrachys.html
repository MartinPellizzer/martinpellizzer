Rubus idaeus var. melanotrachys

Rubus idaeus var. melanotrachys, commonly known as black raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry species characterized by its dark purple to black fruits.

It is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its berries are smaller and more intensely flavored compared to typical raspberries.

This variety is valued for its culinary and ornamental uses in gardens.
