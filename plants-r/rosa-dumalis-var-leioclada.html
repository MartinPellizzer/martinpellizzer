Rosa dumalis var. leioclada

Rosa dumalis var. leioclada, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed wild rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features thornless stems and dark green, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink to white flowers in late spring. Its fruits are red, hairy rose hips that persist into winter.

This variety is adapted to rocky and arid soils, making it a resilient member of the rose family.
