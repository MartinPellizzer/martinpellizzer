Rosa × aschersoniana

Rosa × aschersoniana, commonly known as the Ascherson's rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from deep pink to crimson. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and is native to the mountains of Central Asia. It produces fragrant, five-petaled blooms that are often borne singly or in clusters.

Rosa × aschersoniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature.
