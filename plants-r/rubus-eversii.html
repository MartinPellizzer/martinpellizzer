Rubus eversii

Rubus eversii, commonly known as the Ever's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
