Rubus lasiostylus f. glandulosus

Rubus lasiostylus f. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus characterized by its distinctive glandular features.

This plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The glandular hairs on its stems and leaves are a notable botanical trait, contributing to its unique appearance.

It is native to certain regions of Asia and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.
