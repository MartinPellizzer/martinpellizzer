Racosperma retrorsum

Racosperma retrorsum, commonly known as the warty acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences. Its bark is rough and often fissured, while its leaves are trifoliate with hairy surfaces.

It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
