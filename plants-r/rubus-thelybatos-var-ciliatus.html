Rubus thelybatos var. ciliatus

Rubus thelybatos var. ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
