Rumex bucephalophorus var. graecus

Rumex bucephalophorus var. graecus, commonly known as the Greek sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in terminal clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

The leaves are edible and have a tangy, sour flavor, making it a traditional ingredient in various cuisines.
