Rosa afzeliana var. subcanina

Rosa afzeliana var. subcanina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets that are often slightly hairy. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to temperate climates.
