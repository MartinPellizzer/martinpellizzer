Rubus genevieri var. longisepalus

Rubus genevieri var. longisepalus, commonly known as the long-sepaled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black aggregate berry. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
