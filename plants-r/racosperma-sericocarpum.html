Racosperma sericocarpum

Racosperma sericocarpum, commonly known as the silky-fruited acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and its distinctive, silky-haired fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typical of the legume family, Fabaceae. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Racosperma sericocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
