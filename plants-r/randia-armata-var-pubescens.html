Randia armata var. pubescens

Randia armata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
