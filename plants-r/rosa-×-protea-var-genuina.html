Rosa × protea var. genuina

Rosa × protea var. genuina, commonly known as the Garden Rose, is a hybrid rose species that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It typically features large, showy flowers with multiple layers of petals, often in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowering period usually spans from late spring to early autumn, depending on the climate.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
