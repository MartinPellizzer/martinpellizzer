Rhinacanthus gracilis var. latilabiatus

Rhinacanthus gracilis var. latilabiatus, commonly known as the broad-lipped trumpet tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
