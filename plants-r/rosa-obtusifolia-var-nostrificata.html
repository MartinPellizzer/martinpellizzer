Rosa obtusifolia var. nostrificata

Rosa obtusifolia var. nostrificata, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thornless stems and is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are known for their sweet, spicy scent. It is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is particularly noted for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils.
