Ranunculus angustanus

Ranunculus angustanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

Ranunculus angustanus is often found in temperate regions and is known for its early spring blooming period.
