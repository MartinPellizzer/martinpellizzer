Reediella vieillardii

Reediella vieillardii, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a rare and unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments. The plant's leaves are modified into small, hair-like structures that give it a velvety appearance. It is often found in rocky soils and has adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
