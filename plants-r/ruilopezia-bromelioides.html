Ruilopezia bromelioides

Ruilopezia bromelioides, commonly known as the bromeliad-like ruilopezia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Ruilopezia bromelioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting various invertebrates.
