Rosa stylosa var. opaca

Rosa stylosa var. opaca, commonly known as the opaca rose, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, often thornless stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
