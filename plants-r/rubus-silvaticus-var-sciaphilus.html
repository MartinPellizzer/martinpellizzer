Rubus silvaticus var. sciaphilus

Rubus silvaticus var. sciaphilus, commonly known as the shadow raspberry, is a variant of the brambles that thrives in shaded environments.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red or black in color. It prefers moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
