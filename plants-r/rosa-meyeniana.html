Rosa meyeniana

Rosa meyeniana, commonly known as the Chilean rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

Rosa meyeniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
