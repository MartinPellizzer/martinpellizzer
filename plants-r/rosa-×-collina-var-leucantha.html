Rosa × collina var. leucantha

Rosa × collina var. leucantha, commonly known as the white sand rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and semi-evergreen foliage, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
