Rubus serpens var. elongaticuspis

Rubus serpens var. elongaticuspis, commonly known as the long-pointed blackberry.

This variant is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The botanical features of this variety make it distinct from other Rubus species, particularly in its leaf shape and fruit structure.
