Rubus menkei subsp. eifeliensis

Rubus menkei subsp.

eifeliensis, commonly known as the Eifel raspberry, is a shrub native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
