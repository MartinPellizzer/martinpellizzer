Rubus incretatus

Rubus incretatus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and acidic. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh alpine conditions.
