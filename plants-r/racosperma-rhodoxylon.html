Racosperma rhodoxylon

Racosperma rhodoxylon, commonly known as the red-stemmed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and bipinnate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers arranged in compact, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Racosperma rhodoxylon is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
