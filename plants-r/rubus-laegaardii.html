Rubus laegaardii

Rubus laegaardii, commonly known as the Laegaard's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including shrublands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in local ecosystems, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
