Rosa × nitidula f. subrotunda

Rosa × nitidula f. subrotunda, commonly known as the smooth-leafed hybrid rose, is a cultivar characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, semi-double flowers.

It features rounded, glossy, and slightly hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant pink to red blooms that typically appear in late spring to early summer. It is well-suited for garden borders and containers due to its manageable size and ornamental value.

This variety is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
