Ranunculus longipetalus

Ranunculus longipetalus, commonly known as the long-petaled buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically divided into lobes, and its flowers have numerous petal-like sepals that give the appearance of petals. It produces yellow or orange flowers with a hairy center, and it blooms from late winter to early spring.

Ranunculus longipetalus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows.
