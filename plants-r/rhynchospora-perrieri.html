Rhynchospora perrieri

Rhynchospora perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Rhynchospora perrieri thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes and swampy areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
