Rubus cymophorus

Rubus cymophorus, commonly known as the white-berried blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rubus cymophorus is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
