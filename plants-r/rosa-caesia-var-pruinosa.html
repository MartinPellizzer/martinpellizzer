Rosa caesia var. pruinosa

Rosa caesia var. pruinosa, commonly known as the Pruinose Blush Rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. The leaves are typically dark green and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is known for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticultural traditions.
