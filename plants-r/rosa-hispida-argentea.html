Rosa hispida argentea

Rosa hispida argentea, commonly known as the silver-haired rose, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves and pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Rosa hispida argentea is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
