Rosa graveolens var. anadena

Rosa graveolens var. anadena, commonly known as the Anadenanthus rosea, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrub form and produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds. Its leaves are typically small, elliptical, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
