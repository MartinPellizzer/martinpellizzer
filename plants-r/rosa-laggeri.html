Rosa laggeri

Rosa laggeri, commonly known as the Lagger's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are borne on pedicels and are attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are pinnate, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa laggeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
