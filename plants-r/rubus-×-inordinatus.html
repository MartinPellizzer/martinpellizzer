Rubus × inordinatus

Rubus × inordinatus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry or loganberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (raspberry).

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart and slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
