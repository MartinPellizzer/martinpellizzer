Rubus discolor var. vestitus

Rubus discolor var. vestitus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a variant of the discolor raspberry native to parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them suitable for culinary use.
