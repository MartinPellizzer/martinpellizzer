Rubus ulugurensis var. apricus

Rubus ulugurensis var. apricus, commonly known as the apricot raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, similar to apricots. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in traditional medicine in its native region.
