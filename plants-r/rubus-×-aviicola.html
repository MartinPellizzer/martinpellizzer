Rubus × aviicola

Rubus × aviicola, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a aggregate of drupes, often dark red to black in color.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for cultivating unique flavors in horticulture.
