Rhaponticum pygmaeum

Rhaponticum pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf rhapontic lettuce, is a small perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by small, round seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
