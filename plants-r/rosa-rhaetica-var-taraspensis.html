Rosa rhaetica var. taraspensis

Rosa rhaetica var. taraspensis, commonly known as the Tarasp rose, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Rosa rhaetica species.

It is native to the Alpine regions of Switzerland and Italy, where it thrives in rocky and mountainous environments. This plant is characterized by its deep pink to crimson flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
