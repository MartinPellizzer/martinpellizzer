Rubus tereticaulis subsp. serotinus

Rubus tereticaulis subsp.

serotinus, commonly known as the latefruit blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen later in the season compared to other blackberry species. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique floral structure and fruit morphology.
