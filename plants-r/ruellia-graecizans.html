Ruellia graecizans

Ruellia graecizans, commonly known as Greek ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of blue to purple tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Ruellia graecizans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
