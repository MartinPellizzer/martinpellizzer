Rhamnus japonica var. senanensis

Rhamnus japonica var. senanensis, commonly known as the Japanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
