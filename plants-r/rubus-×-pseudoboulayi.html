Rubus × pseudoboulayi

Rubus × pseudoboulayi, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which consist of small, dark-colored drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. It produces edible berries that are widely appreciated for their flavor and nutritional value.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
