Rosa afzeliana var. pseudocenisia

Rosa afzeliana var. pseudocenisia, commonly known as the Himalayan rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

The botanical interest of this species lies in its adaptability to harsh alpine conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
