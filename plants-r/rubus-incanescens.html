Rubus incanescens

Rubus incanescens, commonly known as the silver-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, dark red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forests. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces numerous stinging hairs on its stems and leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
