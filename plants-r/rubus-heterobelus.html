Rubus heterobelus

Rubus heterobelus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically narrow and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in woodland edges and moist, well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and blackberries.
