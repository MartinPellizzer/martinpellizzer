Rubus pubifolius var. glabriusculus

Rubus pubifolius var. glabriusculus, commonly known as the smooth-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves, which distinguish it from other Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This variant is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.
