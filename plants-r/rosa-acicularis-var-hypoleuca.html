Rosa acicularis var. hypoleuca

Rosa acicularis var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white thorned rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip that matures in autumn. It is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardy nature and distinctive appearance.
