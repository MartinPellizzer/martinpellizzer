Rhamnus purshiana var. betulifolia

Rhamnus purshiana var. betulifolia, commonly known as the Pacific buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange to red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
