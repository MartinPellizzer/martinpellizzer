Rubus fuscus subsp. quincyi

Rubus fuscus subsp.

quincyi, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a firm, tart flavor.

The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and grows as a shrub with thorny stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant member of the Rubus genus.
