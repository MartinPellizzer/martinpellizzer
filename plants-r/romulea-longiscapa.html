Romulea longiscapa

Romulea longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, nodding flowers. The plant produces pale yellow to orange flowers with three petals and three sepals, typically blooming in late summer. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive dry periods.

Romulea longiscapa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and shady borders.
