Rosa hungarica

Rosa hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and is characterized by its thorny stems. Rosa hungarica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscape design and floral arrangements.
