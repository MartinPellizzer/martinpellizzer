Rubus irenaeus var. orogenes

Rubus irenaeus var. orogenes, commonly known as the Alpine blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
