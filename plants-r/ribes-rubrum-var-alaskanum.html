Ribes rubrum var. alaskanum

Ribes rubrum var. alaskanum, commonly known as the Alaska gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its stems are often reddish in color.

The Alaska gooseberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
