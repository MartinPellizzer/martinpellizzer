Rumex scutatus var. glaucus

Rumex scutatus var. glaucus, commonly known as the glaucous dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a whitish or bluish coating, known as a glaucous bloom. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other damp habitats.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
