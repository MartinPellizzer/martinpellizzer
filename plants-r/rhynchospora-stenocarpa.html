Rhynchospora stenocarpa

Rhynchospora stenocarpa, commonly known as narrow-fruited marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

Rhynchospora stenocarpa plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
