Rosa obtusifolia var. macrostyla

Rosa obtusifolia var. macrostyla, commonly known as the large-flowered shrub rose, is a variant of the shrub rose species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The foliage consists of dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and the plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. It is a hardy plant that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders and hedges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
