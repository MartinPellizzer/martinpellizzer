Rosa malyi bosniaca

Rosa malyi bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian rose, is a species of shrub native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring well-drained, fertile soils. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and the flowers are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in the region's horticulture.
