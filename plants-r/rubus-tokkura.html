Rubus tokkura

Rubus tokkura, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
