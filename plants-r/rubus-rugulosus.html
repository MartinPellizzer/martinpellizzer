Rubus rugulosus

Rubus rugulosus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other edible and ornamental Rubus species.
