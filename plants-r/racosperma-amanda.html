Racosperma amanda

Racosperma amanda, commonly known as Amanda's wattles, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring slender, wiry stems and small, compound leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in racemes, giving it the genus name "Racosperma.".

It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
