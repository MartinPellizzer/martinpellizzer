Rhynchostylis guttata

Rhynchostylis guttata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that bear leaves and flowers. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or green with a distinctive red or purple lip.

Rhynchostylis guttata is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along stream banks.
