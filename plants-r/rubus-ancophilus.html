Rubus ancophilus

Rubus ancophilus, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and the fruit develops into a dark purple or black berry.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible, though somewhat tart, fruit.
