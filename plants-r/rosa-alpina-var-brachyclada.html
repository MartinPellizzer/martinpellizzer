Rosa alpina var. brachyclada

Rosa alpina var. brachyclada, commonly known as the Alpine shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in colder climates.
