Rosa canina var. subcanina

Rosa canina var. subcanina, commonly known as the common dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that contain seeds, which are often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
