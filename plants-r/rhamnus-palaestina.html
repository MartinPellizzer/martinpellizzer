Rhamnus palaestina

Rhamnus palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine buckthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by black, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Rhamnus palaestina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
