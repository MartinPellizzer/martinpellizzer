Reseda aucheri f. rotundifolia

Reseda aucheri f. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved reseda, is a cultivar of the genus Reseda, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy yellow flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rock gardens or alpine settings. The plant's foliage is notable for its rounded, velvety leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in garden design for its compact growth habit.

The species is native to southern Europe and is closely related to other members of the Reseda genus.
