Rubus mulleri var. baudotii

Rubus mulleri var. baudotii, commonly known as the Baudot's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern China.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry species.
