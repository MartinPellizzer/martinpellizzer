Rosa serpenticanina

Rosa serpenticanina, commonly known as the serpent's blood rose, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This plant is characterized by its deep crimson flowers and thorned stems, which are typical features of the Rosa genus. The leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the plant produces fragrant, five-petaled blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
