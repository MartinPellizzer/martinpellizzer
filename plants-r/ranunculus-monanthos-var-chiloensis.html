Ranunculus monanthos var. chiloensis

Ranunculus monanthos var. chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive yellow center, blooming in spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and valleys.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
