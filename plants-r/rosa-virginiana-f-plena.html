Rosa virginiana f. plena

Rosa virginiana f. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered wild rose, is a cultivar of the Virgin's rose.

It is characterized by its abundant, fully double blooms that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial suitable for USDA hardiness zones 3-8.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
