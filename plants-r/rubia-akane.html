Rubia akane

Rubia akane, commonly known as Japanese madder, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces red-orange roots that have been used traditionally in dyeing textiles and as a medicinal herb. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia akane thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
