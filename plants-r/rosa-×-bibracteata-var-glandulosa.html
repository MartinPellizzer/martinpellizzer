Rosa × bibracteata var. glandulosa

Rosa × bibracteata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular bracted rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with glandular dots on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its horticultural appeal comes from its robust growth and showy blooms.
