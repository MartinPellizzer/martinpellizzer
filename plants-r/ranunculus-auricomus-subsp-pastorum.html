Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pastorum

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pastorum, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, saucer-shaped flowers with five to eight petals.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are slender and often hairy.

It is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
