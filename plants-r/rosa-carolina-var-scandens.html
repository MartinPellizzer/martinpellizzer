Rosa carolina var. scandens

Rosa carolina var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing rose or Carolina climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has long, arching stems that can be trained to climb or trail along supports. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
