Rumex dentatus subsp. reticulatus

Rumex dentatus subsp.

reticulatus, commonly known as the reticulate dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family. It is characterized by its serrated, lanceolate leaves that are often covered with a fine, reticulate pattern of veins.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which are typically surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
