Rhaponticum rhaponticoides

Rhaponticum rhaponticoides, commonly known as the Siberian cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It features a robust taproot system and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with dark green, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, hairy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
