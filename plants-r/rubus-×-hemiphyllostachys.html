Rubus × hemiphyllostachys

Rubus × hemiphyllostachys, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, dark berries that are rich in antioxidants. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor and are used in various culinary applications.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both its parent species, Rubus and hemiphyllostachys, making it a notable example of hybrid vigor in the genus Rubus.
