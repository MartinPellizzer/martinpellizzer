Rosa montana f. subglobulosa

Rosa montana f. subglobulosa, commonly known as the mountain rose or subglobular rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features rounded, glossy leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively easy to care for and resistant to many common pests and diseases.
