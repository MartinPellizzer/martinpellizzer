Rhipsalis linearis

Rhipsalis linearis, commonly known as the linear cactus or spider cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its long, slender, and branching stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Rhipsalis linearis is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor environments.
