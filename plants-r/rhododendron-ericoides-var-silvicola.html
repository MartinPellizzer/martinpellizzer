Rhododendron ericoides var. silvicola

Rhododendron ericoides var. silvicola, commonly known as the mountain rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers cool, moist environments.

This species is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
