Rubus villicaulis var. beirensis

Rubus villicaulis var. beirensis, commonly known as the Beirensis blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to China.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale green. Its fruit, a small, dark purple berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
