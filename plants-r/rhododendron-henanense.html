Rhododendron henanense

Rhododendron henanense, commonly known as the Henan rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically found in the Henan province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with acidic soils. The plant features dense, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during the spring. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
