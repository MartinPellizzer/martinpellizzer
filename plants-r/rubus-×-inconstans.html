Rubus × inconstans

Rubus × inconstans, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and sometimes used in local cuisines.
