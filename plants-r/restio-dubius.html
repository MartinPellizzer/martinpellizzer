Restio dubius

Restio dubius, commonly known as the slender restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family, which is characteristic of the fynbos vegetation type. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a valuable component of local biodiversity.
