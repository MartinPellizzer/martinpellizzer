Rubus anhaltianus

Rubus anhaltianus, commonly known as the Anhalt raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically serrated and have a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are usually red when ripe and have a tart flavor. It is native to Europe and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
