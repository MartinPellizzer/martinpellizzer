Rosa × engelmannii nothosubsp. britannicae-columbiae

Rosa × engelmannii nothosubsp.

britannicae-columbiae, commonly known as the Vancouver rose, is a hybrid species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shrubland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
