Ranunculus chlorellus

Ranunculus chlorellus, commonly known as the yellow marsh cinquefoil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It features showy, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Ranunculus chlorellus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
