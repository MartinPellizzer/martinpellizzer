Rumohra quadripinnata

Rumohra quadripinnata, commonly known as the four-pinnate fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are divided into multiple pinnae, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
