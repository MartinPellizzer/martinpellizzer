Rubus crassus var. orbicularis

Rubus crassus var. orbicularis, commonly known as the broad-leaved raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its large, rounded leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the environment. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
