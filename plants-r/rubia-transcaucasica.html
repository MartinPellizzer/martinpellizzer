Rubia transcaucasica

Rubia transcaucasica, commonly known as Transcaucasian madder, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces red-orange roots that have been historically used for dyeing textiles. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
