Rhynchostylis cymifera

Rhynchostylis cymifera, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and the inflorescence consists of multiple showy, fragrant blooms.

Rhynchostylis cymifera is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its aromatic flowers.
