Rosa coriifolia var. firmata

Rosa coriifolia var. firmata, commonly known as the Japanese rose or coral rose, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces distinctive, elongated, leaf-like bracts that give it a unique ornamental appearance. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing visual interest throughout the growing season.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
