Rosa × collina var. obtusifolia

Rosa × collina var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and oval, leathery leaves with rounded tips. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
