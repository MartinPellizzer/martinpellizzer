Ruellia chelonoides

Ruellia chelonoides, commonly known as the purple roselight, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically violet or purple in color and bloom in clusters.

Ruellia chelonoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
