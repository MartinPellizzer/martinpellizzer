Romulea micrantha

Romulea micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces white or pale yellow blooms with a distinct, often sweet scent. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in arid conditions, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Romulea micrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral appearance.
