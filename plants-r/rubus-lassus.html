Rubus lassus

Rubus lassus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet to tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Rubus lassus is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
