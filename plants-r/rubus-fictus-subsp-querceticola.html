Rubus fictus subsp. querceticola

Rubus fictus subsp.

querceticola, commonly known as the oakleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of North America. It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which resemble the shape of an oak leaf, featuring serrated edges and a deep green color.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow, containing several drupelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
