Richeria grandis var. laurifolia

Richeria grandis var. laurifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental planting in warm climates.

Due to its rapid growth and showy flowers, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and public spaces.
