Ranunculus auricomus subsp. cuneaticans

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

cuneaticans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped or kidney-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals, often with a white or pale yellow center. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.
