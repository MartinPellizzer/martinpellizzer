Rubus glandulosocaesius

Rubus glandulosocaesius, commonly known as the glandular hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with particular attention to its glandular hairs and robust growth habit.
