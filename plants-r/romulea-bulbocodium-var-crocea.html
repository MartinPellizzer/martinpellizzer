Romulea bulbocodium var. crocea

Romulea bulbocodium var. crocea, commonly known as the yellow romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its bulbs store nutrients, allowing it to survive dry periods and bloom in spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
