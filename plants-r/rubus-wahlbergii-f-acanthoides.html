Rubus wahlbergii f. acanthoides

Rubus wahlbergii f. acanthoides, commonly known as the Wahlberg's blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. Its leaves are usually compound, with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
