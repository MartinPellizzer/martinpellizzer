Ranunculus stipitatilobus

Ranunculus stipitatilobus, commonly known as the stipitate lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often have a hairy surface, while the stem is usually hairy and branched. The flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are produced in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
