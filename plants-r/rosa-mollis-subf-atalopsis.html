Rosa mollis subf. atalopsis

Rosa mollis subf.

atalopsis, commonly known as the Atalantic rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is often found in high-altitude environments.

The plant typically features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are usually simple and have a slightly hairy texture.

This sub-species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
