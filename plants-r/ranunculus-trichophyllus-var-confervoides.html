Ranunculus trichophyllus var. confervoides

Ranunculus trichophyllus var. confervoides, commonly known as the green alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with five to nine petals. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help protect it from harsh mountain conditions.
