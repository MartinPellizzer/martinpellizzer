Rhododendron mairei

Rhododendron mairei, commonly known as the Maire's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist, and shaded environments. The plant features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhododendron mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
