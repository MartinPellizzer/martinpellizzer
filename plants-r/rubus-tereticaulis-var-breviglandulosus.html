Rubus tereticaulis var. breviglandulosus

Rubus tereticaulis var. breviglandulosus, commonly known as the slender raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thin, thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the plant produces yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptability.
