Randia altiscandens

Randia altiscandens, commonly known as the redroot sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and in disturbed habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Randia altiscandens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
