Rapanea meziana

Rapanea meziana, commonly known as the Malabar laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, red pulp.

Rapanea meziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dense foliage and year-round greenery.
