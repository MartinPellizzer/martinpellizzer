Rubus longior

Rubus longior, commonly known as the long-stemmed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

Rubus longior plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
