Rumex nervosus var. usambarensis

Rumex nervosus var. usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis dock, is a perennial herb native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, serrated leaves with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and swampy areas.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.
