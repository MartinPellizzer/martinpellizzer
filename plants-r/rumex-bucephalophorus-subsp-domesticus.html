Rumex bucephalophorus subsp. domesticus

Rumex bucephalophorus subsp.

domesticus, commonly known as the common dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist soils along riverbanks, ditches, and other wetland areas.

The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and a reddish underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is known for its edible leaves, which have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
