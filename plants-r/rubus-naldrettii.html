Rubus naldrettii

Rubus naldrettii, commonly known as Naldrett's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Rubus naldrettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
