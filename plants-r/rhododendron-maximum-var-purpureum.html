Rhododendron maximum var. purpureum

Rhododendron maximum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slight purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to deep red, blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
