Romulea fibrosa

Romulea fibrosa, commonly known as the fibrous broomrape, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae, although it is not a true orchid. The plant is characterized by its lack of chlorophyll and its dependence on host plants for nutrients, as it is a parasitic species. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences.

Romulea fibrosa thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grassland ecosystems.
