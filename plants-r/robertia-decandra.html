Robertia decandra

Robertia decandra, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Robertia decandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
