Rubus bellus

Rubus bellus, commonly known as the beautiful roseberry, is a species of bramble within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Rubus bellus is often found in woodlands and along the edges of forests, where it thrives in partial shade.
