Rubus wirtgenii

Rubus wirtgenii, commonly known as the Wirtgen's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it grows in mountainous regions. The plant produces edible berries that are similar to other blackberry species, with a distinctive flavor and dark purple to black coloration. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species.

It is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential in food and traditional medicine.
