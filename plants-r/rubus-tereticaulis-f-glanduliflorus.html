Rubus tereticaulis f. glanduliflorus

Rubus tereticaulis f. glanduliflorus, commonly known as the glandular-flowered blackberry.

This plant is a flowering shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The flowers are usually white or pale pink and have glandular hairs on the petals and sepals.

It is native to North America and thrives in moist, shady environments.
