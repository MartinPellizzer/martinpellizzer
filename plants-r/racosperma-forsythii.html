Racosperma forsythii

Racosperma forsythii, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
