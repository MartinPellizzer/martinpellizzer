Rosa Pugetii Var. Thomasii

Rosa pugetii var. thomasii is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. This variety of rose is known for its fragrant, pale pink blooms that appear in late spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sun to flourish. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern. The plant's unique beauty and ecological significance make it a valuable subject for botanical study and preservation efforts.
