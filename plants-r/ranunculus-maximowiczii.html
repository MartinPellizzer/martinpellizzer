Ranunculus maximowiczii

Ranunculus maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with dark veins, and it blooms in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves that are divided into several lobes, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils in partial to full shade.

Ranunculus maximowiczii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also found in wet meadows and along stream banks in its natural habitat.
