Rosa sepium var. rosea

Rosa sepium var. rosea, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant has serrated, dark green leaves and produces fragrant blooms in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

The variety rosea is distinguished by its particularly vibrant and rose-like flower colors.
