Ribes × pallidum

Ribes × pallidum, commonly known as the white currant, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Ribes nigrum (black currant) and Ribes rubrum (red currant).

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring arching stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have serrated margins. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in racemes, blooming in early spring.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange berries that are edible and often used in jams and juices.
