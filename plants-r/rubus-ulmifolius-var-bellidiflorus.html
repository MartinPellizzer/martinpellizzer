Rubus ulmifolius var. bellidiflorus

Rubus ulmifolius var. bellidiflorus, commonly known as the bellflower rose, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that resemble those of the elm. The flowers are distinctive, featuring five pink to reddish petals and a yellow center, blooming in early summer. The fruit is a small, dark red berry that ripens in late summer.

This plant is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils across Europe.
