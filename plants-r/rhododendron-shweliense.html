Rhododendron shweliense

Rhododendron shweliense, commonly known as the Shweli rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Myanmar.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often clustered in inflorescences.

The leaves are leathery and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
