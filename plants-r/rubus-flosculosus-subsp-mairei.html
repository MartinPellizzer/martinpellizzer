Rubus flosculosus subsp. mairei

Rubus flosculosus subsp.

mairei, commonly known as the Maire's bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
