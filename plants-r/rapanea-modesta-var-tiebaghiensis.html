Rapanea modesta var. tiebaghiensis

Rapanea modesta var. tiebaghiensis, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that persist through winter. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, so caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
