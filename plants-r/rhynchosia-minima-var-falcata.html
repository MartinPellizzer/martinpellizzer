Rhynchosia minima var. falcata

Rhynchosia minima var. falcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf snout bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for both ornamental use and as a forage crop.
