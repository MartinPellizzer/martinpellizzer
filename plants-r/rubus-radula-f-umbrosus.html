Rubus radula f. umbrosus

Rubus radula f. umbrosus, commonly known as the shaded raspberry, is a botanical variant of the common raspberry.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in shady, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

This form is notable for its adaptability to low-light conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
