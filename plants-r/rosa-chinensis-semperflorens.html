Rosa chinensis semperflorens

Rosa chinensis semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming Chinese rose, is a shrub native to China and widely cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, semi-double to double flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring thorny stems and glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is prized for its ability to produce abundant flowers year-round, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
