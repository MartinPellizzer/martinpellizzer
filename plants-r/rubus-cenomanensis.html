Rubus cenomanensis

Rubus cenomanensis, commonly known as the Cenomanian blackberry, is a fossil plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and inflorescences typical of the Rubus genus. The plant's fossils have been found in Cenomanian sediments, indicating its presence during the Late Cretaceous period. Its botanical features provide insights into the evolution of modern brambles.

The study of Rubus cenomanensis contributes to understanding ancient plant communities and climate conditions.
