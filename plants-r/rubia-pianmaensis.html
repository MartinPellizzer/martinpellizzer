Rubia pianmaensis

Rubia pianmaensis, commonly known as the Pianma madder, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to China, specifically the Pianma region in Sichuan Province. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, and it is known for its use in traditional dyeing practices.

The roots and leaves of Rubia pianmaensis contain pigments that have been historically used to produce red dyes.
