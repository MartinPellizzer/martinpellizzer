Rhodiola gannanica

Rhodiola gannanica, commonly known as the Gannan rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It is adapted to cold climates and has a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh conditions.

Rhodiola gannanica is also valued for its potential adaptogenic properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
