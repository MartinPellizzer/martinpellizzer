Rosa carolina var. erecta

Rosa carolina var. erecta, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright, branching stems covered in thorns. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange-red hips.

It is well adapted to moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
