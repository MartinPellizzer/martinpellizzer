Ranunculus subtectus

Ranunculus subtectus, commonly known as the rock watercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually alternate and trifoliate, while the flowers have five to seven sepals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus subtectus is known for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
