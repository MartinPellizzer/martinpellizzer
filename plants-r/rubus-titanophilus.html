Rubus titanophilus

Rubus titanophilus, commonly known as the giant raspberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, prickly stems and compound leaves that are typically broad and lobed. The plant produces showy, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, hairy berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for various garden environments.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its fruit yield.
