Rubus × nothus

Rubus × nothus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (raspberry). This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.
