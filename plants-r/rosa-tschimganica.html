Rosa tschimganica

Rosa tschimganica, commonly known as the Tschimgan rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with arching stems and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are typically red and hairy.

Rosa tschimganica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
