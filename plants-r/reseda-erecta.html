Reseda erecta

Reseda erecta, commonly known as upright madder, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Reseda erecta is often found in dry, sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments.

It has been historically used in dyeing and traditional herbal medicine.
