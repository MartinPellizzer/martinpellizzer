Rubus allegheniensis var. populifolius

Rubus allegheniensis var. populifolius, commonly known as the Allegheny raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
