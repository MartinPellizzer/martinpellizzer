Ribes acuminatum var. maius

Ribes acuminatum var. maius, commonly known as the large-flowered gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces edible, yellow or orange berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible landscaping.
