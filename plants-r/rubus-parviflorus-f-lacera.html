Rubus parviflorus f. lacera

Rubus parviflorus f. lacera, commonly known as the slender bramble, is a variant of the common raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thin, trailing stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals, and appear in late spring. The fruit is a aggregate of small drupes, which are usually reddish and edible.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
