Rubus glandulosus var. schleicheri

Rubus glandulosus var. schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's bramble, is a shrub native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Korea.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and glandular. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
