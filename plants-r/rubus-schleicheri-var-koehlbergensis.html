Rubus schleicheri var. koehlbergensis

Rubus schleicheri var. koehlbergensis, commonly known as the Koehlberg raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in forest understories and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruits are small, red, and edible.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
