Ruspolia hypocrateriformis var. australis

Ruspolia hypocrateriformis var. australis, commonly known as the Australian green ant plant, is a unique epiphytic species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like foliage and the presence of specialized structures called myrmecophytes, which house ants. The plant's leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy coating that aids in water retention. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical regions.

Its ecological role includes providing shelter for ant colonies and contributing to nutrient cycling in its habitat.
