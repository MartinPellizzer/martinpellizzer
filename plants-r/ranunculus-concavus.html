Ranunculus concavus

Ranunculus concavus, commonly known as the concave buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to seven petals, typically in shades of yellow or orange. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Ranunculus concavus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
