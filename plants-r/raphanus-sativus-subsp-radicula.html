Raphanus sativus subsp. radicula

Raphanus sativus subsp.

radicula, commonly known as the bulbous radish, is a cultivated variety of the radish plant. It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy root that is typically white or pale yellow in color.

The plant produces small, erect flowering stems with clusters of small, white or pink flowers. Its leaves are lobed and slightly hairy, and it thrives in cool climates.

This subspecies is primarily grown for its edible root, which is commonly used in culinary applications.
