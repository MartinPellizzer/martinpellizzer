Ribes cereum var. farinosum

Ribes cereum var. farinosum, commonly known as the mealy currant, is a variant of the mealy currant species native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive mealy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
