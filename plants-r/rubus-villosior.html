Rubus villosior

Rubus villosior, commonly known as the downy raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native landscaping.
