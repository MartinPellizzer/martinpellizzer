Racosperma aneurum var. tenue

Racosperma aneurum var. tenue, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow flowers that appear in spikes. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open forests and woodlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
