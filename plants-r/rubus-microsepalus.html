Rubus microsepalus

Rubus microsepalus, commonly known as the small-sepal raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, prickly stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has small, pale yellow flowers.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
