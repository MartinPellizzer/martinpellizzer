Racosperma nigricans

Racosperma nigricans, commonly known as black wattle, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, woody bark and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Racosperma nigricans has been historically used for timber and in traditional medicine.
