Rhynchospora argentea

Rhynchospora argentea, commonly known as silverroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features grass-like leaves that are usually silver-gray in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. Rhynchospora argentea is often found in moist soils and is adapted to environments with high water tables.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
