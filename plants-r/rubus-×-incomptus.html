Rubus × incomptus

Rubus × incomptus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in some culinary applications.
