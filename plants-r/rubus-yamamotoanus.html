Rubus yamamotoanus

Rubus yamamotoanus, commonly known as Yamamoto's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
