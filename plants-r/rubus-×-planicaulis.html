Rubus × planicaulis

Rubus × planicaulis, commonly known as the flat-stemmed blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its flattened stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant is native to eastern North America and thrives in woodland areas with partial shade. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant, edible fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and fruit yield.
