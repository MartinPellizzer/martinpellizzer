Rhaphidophora stolleana

Rhaphidophora stolleana, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central and South America.

It features tall, upright leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent midrib, often marked with silver or white stripes. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its foliage is characterized by a waxy texture and a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor when the leaves are damaged.

Rhaphidophora stolleana is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and its ability to improve indoor air quality.
