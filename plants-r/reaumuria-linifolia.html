Reaumuria linifolia

Reaumuria linifolia, commonly known as the sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, saline environments. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Reaumuria linifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
