Rubus × tranzschelii

Rubus × tranzschelii, commonly known as the blackberry-whiteberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus leucander (whiteberry), exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant produces edible berries that range in color from dark purple to light red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

This hybrid is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
