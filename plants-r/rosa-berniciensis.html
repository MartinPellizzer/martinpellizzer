Rosa berniciensis

Rosa berniciensis, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves and produces small, red fruits known as hips. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in coastal and rocky areas.

Rosa berniciensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
