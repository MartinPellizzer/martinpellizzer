Ranunculus affinis

Ranunculus affinis, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces numerous small, yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus affinis is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
