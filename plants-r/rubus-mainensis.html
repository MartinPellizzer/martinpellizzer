Rubus mainensis

Rubus mainensis, commonly known as the Main River blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species, sharing similar growth habits and floral structures.
