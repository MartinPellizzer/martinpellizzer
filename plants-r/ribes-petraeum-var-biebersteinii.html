Ribes petraeum var. biebersteinii

Ribes petraeum var. biebersteinii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada currant, is a variant of the rock currant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
