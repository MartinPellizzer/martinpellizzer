Ratibida columnifera f. pulcherrima

Ratibida columnifera f. pulcherrima, commonly known as the showy golden cupflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy yellow-orange flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects and pollinator habitats.
