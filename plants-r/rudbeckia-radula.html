Rudbeckia radula

Rudbeckia radula, commonly known as the slender rudbeckia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear yellow-orange flower heads with dark brown centers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Rudbeckia radula typically blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
