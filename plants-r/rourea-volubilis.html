Rourea volubilis

Rourea volubilis, commonly known as the climbing rourea, is a tropical shrub or vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
