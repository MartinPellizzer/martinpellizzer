Rubus brachyodon

Rubus brachyodon, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Rubus brachyodon is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.
