Ranunculus macrorrhynchus subsp. macrorrhynchus

Ranunculus macrorrhynchus subsp.

macrorrhynchus, commonly known as the large-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five broad, lobed petals and a central cluster of stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided with serrated edges, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
