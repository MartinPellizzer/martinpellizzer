Rebutia xanthocarpa var. citricarpa

Rebutia xanthocarpa var. citricarpa, commonly known as the citricarpus rebutia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded stem with a thick, fleshy texture that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-green flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. Its leaves are typically small, spiny, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive look.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
