Rubus × bracteolifer

Rubus × bracteolifer, commonly known as the bracteolifer blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This species is valued for its edible fruit, which is often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
