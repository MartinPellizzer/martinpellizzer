Rubus petalabigens

Rubus petalabigens, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Chile.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique flavor and nutritional value, it is valued both for culinary use and ecological importance.
