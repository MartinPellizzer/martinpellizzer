Rubus hirtus microgene tenuidentatus

Rubus hirtus microgene tenuidentatus, commonly known as the slender-tipped bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, aggregate berries that are typically red when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas of woodlands and along stream banks.
