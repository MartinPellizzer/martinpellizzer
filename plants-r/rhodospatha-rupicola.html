Rhodospatha rupicola

Rhodospatha rupicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops, adapting to harsh, well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping it conserve water in arid conditions.

Rhodospatha rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
