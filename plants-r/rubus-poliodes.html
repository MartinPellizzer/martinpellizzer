Rubus poliodes

Rubus poliodes, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
