Rubus serpens var. lividus

Rubus serpens var. lividus, commonly known as the dark raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are smaller and more acidic than those of the typical Rubus serpens. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces erect, thorny stems.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
