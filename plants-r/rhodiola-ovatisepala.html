Rhodiola ovatisepala

Rhodiola ovatisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Rhodiola ovatisepala is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
