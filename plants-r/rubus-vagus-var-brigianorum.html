Rubus vagus var. brigianorum

Rubus vagus var. brigianorum, commonly known as the Brigiani raspberry, is a variant of the brambles native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries with a tart flavor. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its culinary uses.
