Rhynchospora spiciformis f. filifolia

Rhynchospora spiciformis f. filifolia, commonly known as the filifoliate spicate flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of small, three-lobed flowers that are usually pale green to brown in color.

This botanical feature makes it distinct within its genus and suitable for ecological studies in wetland ecosystems.
