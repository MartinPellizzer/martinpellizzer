Richardia pedicellata var. micrantha

Richardia pedicellata var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered Richardia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, and the plant produces small, round fruits known as utricles.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
