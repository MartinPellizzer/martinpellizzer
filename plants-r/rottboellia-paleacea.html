Rottboellia paleacea

Rottboellia paleacea, commonly known as false brome, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to reddish spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Rottboellia paleacea is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
