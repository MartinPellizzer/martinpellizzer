Rubus plicatus microgene amblyphyllus

Rubus plicatus microgene amblyphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are elliptical with a finely toothed margin, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The species is notable for its adaptability to a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
