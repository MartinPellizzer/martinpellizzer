Rosa afzeliana f. burmiensis

Rosa afzeliana f. burmiensis, commonly known as the Burmese rose, is a shrub-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
