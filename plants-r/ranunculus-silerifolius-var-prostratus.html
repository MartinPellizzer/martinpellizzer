Ranunculus silerifolius var. prostratus

Ranunculus silerifolius var. prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and often overlooked member of the Ranunculaceae family.
