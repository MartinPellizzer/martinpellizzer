Rubus fuscoater var. subglaber

Rubus fuscoater var. subglaber, commonly known as the smooth-leafed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its smooth, less hairy foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the stems are woody and thorny.

This variety is valued for its fruit quality and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
