Rhodiola rotundifolia

Rhodiola rotundifolia, commonly known as the golden root or king's crown, is a perennial plant native to the cold climates of the Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, rounded leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including rosavins and salidrosides, which are responsible for its adaptogenic properties. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found at high altitudes.

Due to its medicinal value, it is also known as a symbol of endurance and vitality in traditional medicine.
