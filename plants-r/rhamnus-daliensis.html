Rhamnus daliensis

Rhamnus daliensis, commonly known as the Dali buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
