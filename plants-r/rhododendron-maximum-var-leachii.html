Rhododendron maximum var. leachii

Rhododendron maximum var. leachii, commonly known as the Great Smoky Mountains rhododendron, is a variant of the common rhododendron native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from pink to deep red, often appearing in dense clusters. The plant is an evergreen shrub with leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often growing in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
