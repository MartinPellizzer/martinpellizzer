Ranunculus ficaria f. polypetalus

Ranunculus ficaria f. polypetalus, commonly known as the white-flowered buttercup, is a variant of the common buttercup with distinctive botanical features.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, characterized by its bright yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three deeply lobed leaflets, and are covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.
