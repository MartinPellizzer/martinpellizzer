Rhododendron pachypodum

Rhododendron pachypodum, commonly known as the desert rhododendron, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Rhododendron pachypodum is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
