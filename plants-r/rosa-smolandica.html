Rosa smolandica

Rosa smolandica, commonly known as the Svalbard rose, is a rare and cold-hardy species native to the Arctic regions of Norway.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant pink flowers in late summer. The plant has needle-like leaves and is adapted to survive in harsh, alpine environments. It is listed as critically endangered due to habitat loss and climate change.

Rosa smolandica plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native Arctic ecosystems.
