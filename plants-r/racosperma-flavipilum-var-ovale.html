Racosperma flavipilum var. ovale

Racosperma flavipilum var. ovale, commonly known as the yellow-haired wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and is often used in native garden landscaping.
