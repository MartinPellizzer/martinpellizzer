Rubus hirtus var. rubristylus

Rubus hirtus var. rubristylus, commonly known as the red-styled raspberry, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
