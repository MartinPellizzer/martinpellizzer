Rhodanthe maryonii

Rhodanthe maryonii, commonly known as the Maryon's everlasting.

It is a flowering plant native to Western Australia, characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, cylindrical seed pods that remain on the plant for an extended period, giving it the common name "everlasting.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal heathlands.

Rhodanthe maryonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
