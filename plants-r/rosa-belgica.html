Rosa belgica

Rosa belgica, commonly known as the Belgian rose, is a species native to Europe, particularly Belgium and parts of France.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa belgica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many landscapes.
