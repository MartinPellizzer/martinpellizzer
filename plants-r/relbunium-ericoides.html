Relbunium ericoides

Relbunium ericoides, commonly known as the heath relbunium, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a tight spiral around the stems. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in early spring.

Relbunium ericoides thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.
