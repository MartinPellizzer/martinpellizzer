Racosperma armillatum

Racosperma armillatum, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
