Randia gentlei

Randia gentlei, commonly known as the velvetleaf or redroot, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that range from ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Randia gentlei thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as forest edges and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
