Rhododendron dilatatum f. niveum

Rhododendron dilatatum f. niveum, commonly known as the White-flowered Alaska Rhododendron, is a hybrid form native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and densely hairy underneath. The plant produces clusters of showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add a subtle, elegant presence to shaded landscapes.
