Rivea fragrans

Rivea fragrans, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its climbing habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
