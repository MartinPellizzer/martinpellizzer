Rubus sectiramus

Rubus sectiramus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that ripen to a deep black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
