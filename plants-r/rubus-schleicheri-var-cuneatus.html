Rubus schleicheri var. cuneatus

Rubus schleicheri var. cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often armed with prickles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
