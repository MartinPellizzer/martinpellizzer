Rhagadiolus stellatus var. eriocarpus

Rhagadiolus stellatus var. eriocarpus, commonly known as the star-shaped bract plant, is a species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-like bracts that surround the flower cluster, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sunlight. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a range of colors from white to pink.

The variety eriocarpus is notable for its hairy or pubescent fruits, which contribute to its botanical classification.
