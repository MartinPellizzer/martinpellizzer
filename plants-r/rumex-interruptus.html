Rumex interruptus

Rumex interruptus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dock, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

Rumex interruptus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
