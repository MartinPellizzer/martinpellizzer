Rubus grandipetalus

Rubus grandipetalus, commonly known as the large-petaled blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are white with five broad petals, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
