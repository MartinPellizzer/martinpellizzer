Rhynchospora pseudolunata

Rhynchospora pseudolunata, commonly known as the false moon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rhynchospora pseudolunata thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
