Rhododendron citrinum var. citrinum

Rhododendron citrinum var. citrinum, commonly known as the golden rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or pink throat. The plant has leathery, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its role in ecological habitats.
