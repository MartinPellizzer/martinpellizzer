Ranunculus ruthenicus

Ranunculus ruthenicus, commonly known as the European buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has a short, stout stem with trifoliate leaves, and its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
