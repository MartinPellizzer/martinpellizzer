Rinorea integrifolia

Rinorea integrifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or white-flowered rinorea, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Rinorea integrifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
