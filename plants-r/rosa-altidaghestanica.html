Rosa altidaghestanica

Rosa altidaghestanica, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas at high elevations, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces oval, red-orange fruits called hips.

Rosa altidaghestanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
