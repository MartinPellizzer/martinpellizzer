Rosa nutkana var. hispida

Rosa nutkana var. hispida, commonly known as the rough rose, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

The plant's botanical features make it an attractive and resilient addition to native plant gardens.
