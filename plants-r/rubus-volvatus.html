Rubus volvatus

Rubus volvatus, commonly known as the curled bramble, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are often curled or rolled. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in supporting local wildlife and biodiversity.
