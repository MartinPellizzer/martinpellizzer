Rosa × alba inermis

Rosa × alba inermis, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa alba and Rosa canina.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden borders and hedges.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscape design and cut flower arrangements.
