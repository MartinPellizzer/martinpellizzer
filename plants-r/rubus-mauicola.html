Rubus mauicola

Rubus mauicola, commonly known as the Maui blackberry, is a species native to the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It is a perennial shrub that belongs to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are highly prized for their flavor and aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, Rubus mauicola is considered a rare and endangered species.
