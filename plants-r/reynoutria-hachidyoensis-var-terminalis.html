Reynoutria hachidyoensis var. terminalis

Reynoutria hachidyoensis var. terminalis, commonly known as the Japanese giant knotweed, is a variant of the invasive plant species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, and its robust, bamboo-like stems that can reach heights of over 3 meters. The plant produces small, creamy-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is known for its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.

Due to its aggressive nature, it is considered a significant ecological threat in regions where it has been introduced.
