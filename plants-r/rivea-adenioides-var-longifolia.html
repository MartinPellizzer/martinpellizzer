Rivea adenioides var. longifolia

Rivea adenioides var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble a heart with a crown, typical of the Passifloraceae family. The plant has elongated, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers produce a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, which are pollinated by various insects.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
