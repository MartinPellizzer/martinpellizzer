Rubus clethraphilus var. latifolius

Rubus clethraphilus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a serrated margin, and they often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, followed by red, edible berries that are popular in local cuisines.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
