Ranunculus nemorosus subsp. questieri

Ranunculus nemorosus subsp.

questieri, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and forest edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.
