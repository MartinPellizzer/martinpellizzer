Rubus sprengelii var. letendrei

Rubus sprengelii var. letendrei, commonly known as the Letendre's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
