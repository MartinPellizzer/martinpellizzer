Rhachidospermum

Rhachidospermum, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Carambolicaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit, which is typically five-lobed and yellow when ripe. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Rhachidospermum is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
