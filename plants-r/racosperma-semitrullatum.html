Racosperma semitrullatum

Racosperma semitrullatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and yellow flowers that appear in racemes. The plant is typically found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for native pollinators.

Due to its ecological significance, it is often included in conservation efforts and habitat restoration projects.
