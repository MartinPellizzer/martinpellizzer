Ranunculus acris var. grandiflorus

Ranunculus acris var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a distinctive hairy appearance.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
