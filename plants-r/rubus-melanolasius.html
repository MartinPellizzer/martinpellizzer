Rubus melanolasius

Rubus melanolasius, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. Native to various regions in Europe and Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and moist, well-drained soils.

Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.
