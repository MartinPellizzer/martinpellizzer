Rubus pilosus var. macrophyllus

Rubus pilosus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or thicket habitats.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
