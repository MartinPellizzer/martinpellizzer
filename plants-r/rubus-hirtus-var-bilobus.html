Rubus hirtus var. bilobus

Rubus hirtus var. bilobus, commonly known as the bilobed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is often found in woodland edges and moist soils.
