Rubus × adjacens

Rubus × adjacens, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are clusters of small drupes that ripen to a deep red or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its genetic diversity, it exhibits a wide range of fruit sizes, colors, and flavors.
