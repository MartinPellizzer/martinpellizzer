Rabdosia pluriflora

Rabdosia pluriflora, commonly known as the many-flowered rabdosia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Rabdosia pluriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
