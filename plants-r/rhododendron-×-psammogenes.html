Rhododendron × psammogenes

Rhododendron × psammogenes, commonly known as the sand rhododendron, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, which contribute to its common name.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
