Rubus contractifolius

Rubus contractifolius, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with a unique morphology compared to other blackberry species.
