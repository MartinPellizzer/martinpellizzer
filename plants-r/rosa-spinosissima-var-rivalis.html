Rosa spinosissima var. rivalis

Rosa spinosissima var. rivalis, commonly known as the Spanish rose, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery.
