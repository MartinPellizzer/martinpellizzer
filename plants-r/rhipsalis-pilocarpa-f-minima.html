Rhipsalis pilocarpa f. minima

Rhipsalis pilocarpa f. minima, commonly known as the miniature pilocarpa cactus, is a compact, epiphytic cactus species native to tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, branching stems covered in small, translucent, hair-like structures called trichomes, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers during the rainy season, typically in summer. Its leaves are reduced to small, rounded tubercles, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

This unique variety is prized for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in indoor settings.
