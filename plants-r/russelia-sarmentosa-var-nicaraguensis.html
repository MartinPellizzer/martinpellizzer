Russelia sarmentosa var. nicaraguensis

Russelia sarmentosa var. nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaragua twining russelia, is a climbing perennial plant native to Central America.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach several meters in length, often trailing or climbing over other vegetation. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas within the understory of forests.
