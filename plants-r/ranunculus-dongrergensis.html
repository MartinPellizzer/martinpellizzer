Ranunculus dongrergensis

Ranunculus dongrergensis, commonly known as the Tibetan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in meadows and slopes at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
