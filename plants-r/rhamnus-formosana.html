Rhamnus formosana

Rhamnus formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by orange to red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
