Rosa dematranea

Rosa dematranea, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or the pink rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa dematranea is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in perfumery due to its aromatic properties.
