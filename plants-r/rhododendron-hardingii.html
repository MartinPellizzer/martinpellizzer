Rhododendron hardingii

Rhododendron hardingii, commonly known as the harding's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist, and shaded environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil.

Rhododendron hardingii is also known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging climatic conditions.
