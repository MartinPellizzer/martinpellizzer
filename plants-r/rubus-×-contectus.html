Rubus × contectus

Rubus × contectus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble.

It is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a popular fruit crop.
