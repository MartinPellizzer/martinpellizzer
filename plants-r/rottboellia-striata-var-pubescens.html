Rottboellia striata var. pubescens

Rottboellia striata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lespedeza, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, giving it its varietal name, and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
