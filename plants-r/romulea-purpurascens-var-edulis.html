Romulea purpurascens var. edulis

Romulea purpurascens var. edulis, commonly known as the edible romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, often purple or reddish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible bulbs that are rich in starch and have a mild, nutty flavor. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and edible qualities, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
