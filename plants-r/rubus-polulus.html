Rubus polulus

Rubus polulus, commonly known as the small-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
