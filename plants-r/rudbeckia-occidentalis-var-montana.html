Rudbeckia occidentalis var. montana

Rudbeckia occidentalis var. montana, commonly known as the mountain rudbeckia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
