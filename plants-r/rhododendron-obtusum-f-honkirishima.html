Rhododendron obtusum f. honkirishima

Rhododendron obtusum f. honkirishima, commonly known as the Honkiri Island rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
