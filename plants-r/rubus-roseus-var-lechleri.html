Rubus roseus var. lechleri

Rubus roseus var. lechleri, commonly known as the Amazonian rosehip or curuba, is a shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that contain high levels of vitamin C and other bioactive compounds. Its flowers are pink to white and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Amazonian medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
