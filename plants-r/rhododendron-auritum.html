Rhododendron auritum

Rhododendron auritum, commonly known as the golden bell rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and elliptical in shape, often with a waxy coating.

Rhododendron auritum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
