Rubus idaeus f. sativus

Rubus idaeus f. sativus, commonly known as red raspberry, is a cultivated form of the raspberry plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its prickly stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupelets. The plant produces bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely grown for its edible fruits and is a popular choice in both home gardens and commercial agriculture.
