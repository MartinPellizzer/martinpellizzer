Rosa glauca subsp. montana

Rosa glauca subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain blushing rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink to white flowers.

The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
