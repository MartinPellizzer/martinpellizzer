Rubus ebudensis

Rubus ebudensis, commonly known as the Ebudens bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits known as berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
