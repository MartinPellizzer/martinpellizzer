Raphanistrocarpus boivinii var. cylindricus

Raphanistrocarpus boivinii var. cylindricus, commonly known as the cylindrical cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various soil types.
