Rosa gallica f. anacantha

Rosa gallica f. anacantha, commonly known as the white gallica rose, is a fragrant shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its simple, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, thornless stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its historical use in perfumery and as a symbol of purity adds to its cultural significance.
