Ranunculus grahamii

Ranunculus grahamii, commonly known as Graham's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, which are often adorned with dark markings. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

Ranunculus grahamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
