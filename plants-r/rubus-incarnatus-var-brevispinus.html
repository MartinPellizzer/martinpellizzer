Rubus incarnatus var. brevispinus

Rubus incarnatus var. brevispinus, commonly known as the short-spined blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
