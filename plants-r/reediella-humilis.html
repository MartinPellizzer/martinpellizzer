Reediella humilis

Reediella humilis, commonly known as the dwarf reediella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base, giving it a low-growing appearance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or brown, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, Reediella humilis is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation and biodiversity.
