Rhaphidophora dilacerata

Rhaphidophora dilacerata, commonly known as the Shingle Leaf Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that often have a zigzag or scalloped edge, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as an understory species, thriving in shaded, humid environments with high moisture levels. Its foliage is usually a deep green, and it can reach heights of up to 6 feet when grown in suitable conditions.

Rhaphidophora dilacerata is valued as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
