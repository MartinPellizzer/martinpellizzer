Robinia dubia var. bella-rosea

Robinia dubia var. bella-rosea, commonly known as the rose-leaf false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive pinkish-white floral display in spring. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and attractive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
