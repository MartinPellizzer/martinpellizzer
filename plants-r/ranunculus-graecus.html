Ranunculus graecus

Ranunculus graecus, commonly known as the Greek buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ranunculus graecus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
