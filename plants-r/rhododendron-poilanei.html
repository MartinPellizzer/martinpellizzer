Rhododendron poilanei

Rhododendron poilanei, commonly known as the Sierra Leone rhododendron, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron poilanei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with rich, acidic soil.
