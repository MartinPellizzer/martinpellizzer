Ranunculus auricomus subsp. laeticolor

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

laeticolor, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with a prominent central pistil.

The leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
