Rosa sepium subsp. micrantha

Rosa sepium subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia. It features upright stems with thorns and is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets that are dark green and glossy. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental planting.
