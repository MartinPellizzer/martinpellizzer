Rubus insericatus subsp. barbatus

Rubus insericatus subsp.

barbatus, commonly known as the barbed blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small white flowers in the spring. Its berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more pronounced barbs on the stems compared to other Rubus species.
