Rumex magellanicus var. donatii

Rumex magellanicus var. donatii, commonly known as the Magellan sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish-purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
