Rhododendron prinophyllum

Rhododendron prinophyllum, commonly known as the white-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, while its flowers have five petals and are often adorned with a subtle blush of color.

Rhododendron prinophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
