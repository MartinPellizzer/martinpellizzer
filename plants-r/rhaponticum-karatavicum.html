Rhaponticum karatavicum

Rhaponticum karatavicum, commonly known as the Karatavicum spearthistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
