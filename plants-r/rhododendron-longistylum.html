Rhododendron longistylum

Rhododendron longistylum, commonly known as the long-styled rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often arranged in whorls around the stems.

Rhododendron longistylum prefers acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded forest environments.
