Rubus fuscus subsp. acutipetalus

Rubus fuscus subsp.

acutipetalus, commonly known as the sharp-petioled blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, glossy leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique floral and fruit morphology.
