Rosa glutinosa f. sicula

Rosa glutinosa f. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian sticky rose, is a horticultural form of the sticky rose species.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is well-suited to well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice in gardens.
