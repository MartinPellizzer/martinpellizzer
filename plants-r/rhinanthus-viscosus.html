Rhinanthus viscosus

Rhinanthus viscosus, commonly known as sticky fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its sticky, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves give it its common name.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
