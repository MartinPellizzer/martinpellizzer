Rubus probus

Rubus probus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Rubus probus is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
