Rhodamnia cinerea var. laxiflora

Rhodamnia cinerea var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered ghostgum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its greyish bark that peels in thin, papery strips, and its lance-shaped, glaucous leaves that are often covered in a whitish waxy coating. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is typically found in dry, open habitats and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
