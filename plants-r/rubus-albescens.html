Rubus albescens

Rubus albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, juicy berries. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
