Rosa communis var. pilatensis

Rosa communis var. pilatensis, commonly known as the Pilatensis rose, is a cultivar prized for its vibrant, fragrant blooms.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its semi-double to fully double flowers, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves, typical of many rose species. It is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
