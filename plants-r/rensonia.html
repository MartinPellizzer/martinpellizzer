Rensonia

Rensonia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gesneriaceae.

These plants are native to tropical regions of Africa and are characterized by their showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Rensonia species typically have thick, fleshy leaves and are often grown as ornamental plants due to their beauty and ease of care. They prefer bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

The genus includes several species, such as Rensonia densiflora and Rensonia schweinfurthii, which are popular among horticultural enthusiasts.
