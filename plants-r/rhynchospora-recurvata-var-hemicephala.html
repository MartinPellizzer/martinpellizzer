Rhynchospora recurvata var. hemicephala

Rhynchospora recurvata var. hemicephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a spiral. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist soils, often in marshes, swamps, or along the edges of ponds and streams.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly narrower leaves and shorter inflorescences compared to other varieties of Rhynchospora recurvata.
