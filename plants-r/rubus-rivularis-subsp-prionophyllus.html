Rubus rivularis subsp. prionophyllus

Rubus rivularis subsp.

prionophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, dark green leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes, which are typically clustered together. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
