Rhynchosia rufa

Rhynchosia rufa, commonly known as the red-flowered cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive red or pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, edible seeds that are often used in local cuisines. Rhynchosia rufa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful crop in sustainable agriculture.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climates contributes to its widespread cultivation in many parts of the world.
