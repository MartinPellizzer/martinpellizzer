Rosa webbiana var. maracandica

Rosa webbiana var. maracandica, commonly known as the Maracanda rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has arching, thorned stems and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
