Rubus moluccanus var. jambosoides

Rubus moluccanus var. jambosoides, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to the Moluccas and surrounding islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for local use.
