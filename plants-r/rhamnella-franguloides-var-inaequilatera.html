Rhamnella franguloides var. inaequilatera

Rhamnella franguloides var. inaequilatera, commonly known as the unequal-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its alternate, oval leaves with slightly serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, dark blue berries. Its botanical features include woody stems with thorns and a preference for moist, acidic soils in woodland habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting insect populations.
