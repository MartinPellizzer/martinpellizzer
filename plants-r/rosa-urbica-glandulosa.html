Rosa urbica glandulosa

Rosa urbica glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular wood rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, glandular hairs that cover its leaves and stems. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy member of the rose family.
