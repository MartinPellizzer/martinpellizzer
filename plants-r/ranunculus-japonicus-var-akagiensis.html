Ranunculus japonicus var. akagiensis

Ranunculus japonicus var. akagiensis, commonly known as the Akagi buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its simple, herbaceous structure.
