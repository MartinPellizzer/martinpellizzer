Rosa gaudinii

Rosa gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, specifically found in regions of Argentina and Chile. This shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in spring.

The plant produces hips, which are red, fleshy fruits containing seeds, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and fragrance.
