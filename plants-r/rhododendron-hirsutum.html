Rhododendron hirsutum

Rhododendron hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features large, leathery leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron hirsutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
