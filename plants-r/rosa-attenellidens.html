Rosa attenellidens

Rosa attenellidens, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are typically borne in groups of three to five. Rosa attenellidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to many landscapes.
