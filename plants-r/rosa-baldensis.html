Rosa baldensis

Rosa baldensis, commonly known as the Bald's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, simple leaves, and five-petaled flowers.

Rosa baldensis is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance.
