Rumex obtusifolius subsp. friesii

Rumex obtusifolius subsp.

friesii, commonly known as Fries' broadleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It produces small, greenish flowers that develop into reddish-brown, triangular fruits.

This subspecies is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to various wetland environments.
