Ranunculus recurvatus

Ranunculus recurvatus, commonly known as the bentleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are hairy and upright. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, though its sap can be toxic if ingested.
