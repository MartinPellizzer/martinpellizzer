Rubus subramiflorus

Rubus subramiflorus, commonly known as the subramiflorous blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
