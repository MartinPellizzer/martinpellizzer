Rubus florentulus

Rubus florentulus, commonly known as the European bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible blackberries, though the fruit is often small and less flavorful compared to other Rubus species. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

The leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth and adaptability.
