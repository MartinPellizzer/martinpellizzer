Rosa spinosissima f. breviseta

Rosa spinosissima f. breviseta, commonly known as the short-stemmed prickly rose, is a botanical variant of the prickly rose species.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
