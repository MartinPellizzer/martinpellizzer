Racosperma varium var. crassinerve

Racosperma varium var. crassinerve, commonly known as the broad-veined wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent, broad veins that give it its specific epithet. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, typical of the Acacia genus, though it is classified under the genus Racosperma. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various native fauna.
