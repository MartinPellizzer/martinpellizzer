Rhododendron niveoflorum

Rhododendron niveoflorum, commonly known as the snow-flowered rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are dark green and elliptical, providing a striking contrast to its pale blooms.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
