Rubus hirtus var. lanceipetalus

Rubus hirtus var. lanceipetalus, commonly known as the lance-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are aggregate berries, typically red when mature, and are often used in local cuisines.

This plant is native to parts of North America and thrives in woodland and disturbed habitats.
