Rosa pulchra

Rosa pulchra, commonly known as the beauty rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America, where it thrives in cool, high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a striking contrast to the showy blooms.

Rosa pulchra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
