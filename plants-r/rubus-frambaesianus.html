Rubus frambaesianus

Rubus frambaesianus, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible berries. The plant produces dark purple to black fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are serrated and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are thorny.

This plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.
