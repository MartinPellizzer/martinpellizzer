Rosa × platyphylloides

Rosa × platyphylloides, commonly known as the flatleaf shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and broad, leathery leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. It is native to the United States, particularly the southeastern region, and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
