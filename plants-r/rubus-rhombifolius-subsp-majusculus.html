Rubus rhombifolius subsp. majusculus

Rubus rhombifolius subsp.

majusculus, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its broad, rhomboid-shaped leaves and thorny stems.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species within its native range.
