Rhododendron kaempferi var. tubiflorum

Rhododendron kaempferi var. tubiflorum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring with clusters of pink to red flowers that are often fragrant. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
