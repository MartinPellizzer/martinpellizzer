Rubus arrhenii var. chamaearrhenii

Rubus arrhenii var. chamaearrhenii, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is closely related to other Rubus species and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
