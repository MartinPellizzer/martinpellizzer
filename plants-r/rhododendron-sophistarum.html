Rhododendron sophistarum

Rhododendron sophistarum, commonly known as the Sophist's Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron sophistarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
