Rhododendron × nobleanum var. pulcherrimum

Rhododendron × nobleanum var. pulcherrimum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It prefers partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves are leathery and dark green, providing an attractive backdrop to its vibrant blooms.
