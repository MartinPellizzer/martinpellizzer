Rosa batavica

Rosa batavica, commonly known as the Dutch rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their beauty and fragrance.

Rosa batavica is also valued for its historical significance in horticulture and its role in various cultural traditions.
