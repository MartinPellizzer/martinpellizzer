Ruehssia loniceroides

Ruehssia loniceroides, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are red, fleshy drupes that mature in the summer, attracting various wildlife.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and thickets.
