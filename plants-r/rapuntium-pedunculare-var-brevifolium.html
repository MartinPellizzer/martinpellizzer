Rapuntium pedunculare var. brevifolium

Rapuntium pedunculare var. brevifolium, commonly known as the dwarf rapunculus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy, and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
