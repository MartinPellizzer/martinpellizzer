Rubus serpens var. obovatus

Rubus serpens var. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The leaves are typically oval to obovate in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters, followed by dark purple or black berries.

This variant is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
