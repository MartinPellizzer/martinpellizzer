Rubus dumetorum var. foliosus

Rubus dumetorum var. foliosus, commonly known as the bracken raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and is characterized by its woody stems covered in dense, prickly hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex floral structure and its role in supporting local wildlife.
