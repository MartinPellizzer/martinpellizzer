Rubus × incrassatus

Rubus × incrassatus, commonly known as blackberry bramble, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
