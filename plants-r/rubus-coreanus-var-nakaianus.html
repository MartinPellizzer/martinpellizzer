Rubus coreanus var. nakaianus

Rubus coreanus var. nakaianus, commonly known as the Korean raspberry, is a flowering plant species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, similar to other Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
