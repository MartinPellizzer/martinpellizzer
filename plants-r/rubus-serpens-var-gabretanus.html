Rubus serpens var. gabretanus

Rubus serpens var. gabretanus, commonly known as the Gabretan blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
