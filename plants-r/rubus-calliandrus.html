Rubus calliandrus

Rubus calliandrus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its prickly stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it typically grows in moist, wooded areas.

Rubus calliandrus is valued both for its culinary uses and its role in local ecosystems.
