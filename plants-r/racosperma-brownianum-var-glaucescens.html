Racosperma brownianum var. glaucescens

Racosperma brownianum var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive bluish-gray hue on the upper surface. This variety is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
