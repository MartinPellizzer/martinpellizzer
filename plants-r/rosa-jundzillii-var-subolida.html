Rosa jundzillii var. subolida

Rosa jundzillii var. subolida, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer seasons. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in its native ecosystem.
