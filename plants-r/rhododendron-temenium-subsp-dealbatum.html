Rhododendron temenium subsp. dealbatum

Rhododendron temenium subsp.

dealbatum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in Nepal and Bhutan. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in cool, humid climates at high altitudes, often in forested areas with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include thick, leathery leaves and inflorescences that are usually clustered in racemes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized landscapes.
