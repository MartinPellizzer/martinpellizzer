Rhynchosia angustifolia

Rhynchosia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf snout bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Rhynchosia angustifolia is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and is used in agroforestry systems.
