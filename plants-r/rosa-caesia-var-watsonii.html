Rosa caesia var. watsonii

Rosa caesia var. watsonii, commonly known as Watson's evergreen rose, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless growth habit and glossy, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide year-round interest in gardens.
