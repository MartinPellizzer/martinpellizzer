Ranunculus clypeatus

Ranunculus clypeatus, commonly known as the shieldcup buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of trifoliate leaves with a distinctive shield-shaped leaf base. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a dark central spot. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
