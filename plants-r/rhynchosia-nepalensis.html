Rhynchosia nepalensis

Rhynchosia nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal burrbean, is a climbing legume native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and India.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, spiny pods that contain several seeds. Rhynchosia nepalensis is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

It is valued for its potential in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
