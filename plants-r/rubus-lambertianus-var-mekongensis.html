Rubus lambertianus var. mekongensis

Rubus lambertianus var. mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Cambodia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and less-studied species.
