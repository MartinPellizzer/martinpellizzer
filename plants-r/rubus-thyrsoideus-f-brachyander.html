Rubus thyrsoideus f. brachyander

Rubus thyrsoideus f. brachyander, commonly known as the short-stalked blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white to pale green and appear in clusters, followed by the development of its edible fruit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
