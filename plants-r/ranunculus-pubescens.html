Ranunculus pubescens

Ranunculus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ranunculus pubescens is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.
