Ranunculus densiciliatus var. glabrescens

Ranunculus densiciliatus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens and a central pistil, characteristic of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological importance, it is often found in conservation-focused habitats.
