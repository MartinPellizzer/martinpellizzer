Rubus pilocarpus var. marshallii

Rubus pilocarpus var. marshallii, commonly known as Marshall's blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.
