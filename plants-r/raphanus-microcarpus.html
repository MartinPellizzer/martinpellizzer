Raphanus microcarpus

Raphanus microcarpus, commonly known as the Chinese watercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia and is characterized by its delicate, edible leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found growing near streams and in wetlands. Its seeds are small and oval, typically dispersed by water or wind.

Raphanus microcarpus is valued for its peppery flavor and nutritional content, making it a popular vegetable in Asian cuisines.
