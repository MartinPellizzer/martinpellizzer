Rubus zoae

Rubus zoae, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in alpine environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
