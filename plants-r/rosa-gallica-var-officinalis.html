Rosa gallica var. officinalis

Rosa gallica var. officinalis, commonly known as the French rose or garden rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their high essential oil content.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in gardens.
