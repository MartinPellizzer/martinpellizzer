Rhododendron selense

Rhododendron selense, commonly known as the Selense rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Rhododendron selense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
