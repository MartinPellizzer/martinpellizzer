Rosa litigiosa

Rosa litigiosa, commonly known as the war rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical name, "litigiosa," refers to its tendency to grow in areas of conflict or disturbance, such as along roadsides or in disturbed soils.

Rosa litigiosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
