Rosa elliptica f. inermis

Rosa elliptica f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, smooth-stemmed growth habit, distinguishing it from other rose species that have thorns. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and are arranged alternately along the stems.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
