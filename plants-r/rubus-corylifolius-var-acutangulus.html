Rubus corylifolius var. acutangulus

Rubus corylifolius var. acutangulus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in woodland or shaded areas. Its flowers are white and clustered, producing a distinctive, aromatic fragrance.

The berries are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to their high antioxidant content.
