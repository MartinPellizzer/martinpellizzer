Rubia florida

Rubia florida, commonly known as the flowering Rubia, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodland and grassland habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia florida is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
