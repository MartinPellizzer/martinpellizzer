Rosa inodora var. popekii

Rosa inodora var. popekii, commonly known as the Popekii tea rose, is a compact shrub with dense, thornless stems.

Its foliage consists of dark green, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to China and is prized for its ornamental value and use in floral arrangements.

Due to its compact size and continuous blooming habit, it is well-suited for container gardening and small garden spaces.
