Ruelliopsis

Ruelliopsis, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant is native to South America and thrives in dry, open habitats. Ruelliopsis species are often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
