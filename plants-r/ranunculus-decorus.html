Ranunculus decorus

Ranunculus decorus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically produces cup-shaped blooms with five to eight petals, and its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three parts. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
