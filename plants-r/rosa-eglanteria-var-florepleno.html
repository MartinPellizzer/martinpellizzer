Rosa eglanteria var. florepleno

Rosa eglanteria var. florepleno, commonly known as the double-leaf rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its distinctive foliage and abundant blooms.

It features large, double flowers with a rich pink hue, often appearing in clusters along the stems. The plant has deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white down. It is typically grown as a shrub or climbing plant, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy appearance.
