Rhododendron decandrum

Rhododendron decandrum, commonly known as the Indian rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and elliptical in shape.

Rhododendron decandrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
