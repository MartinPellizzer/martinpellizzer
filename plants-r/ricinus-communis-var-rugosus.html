Ricinus communis var. rugosus

Ricinus communis var. rugosus, commonly known as the hairy castor plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its distinctive, rough, and hairy foliage.

This plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are typically large, palmately lobed, and covered in short, stiff hairs, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange spiny capsules that contain toxic seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
