Rubus plicatus subsp. bertramii

Rubus plicatus subsp.

bertramii, commonly known as the Bertram's blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
