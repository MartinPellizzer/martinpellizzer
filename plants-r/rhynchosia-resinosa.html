Rhynchosia resinosa

Rhynchosia resinosa, commonly known as the resinous cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to elongated pods containing edible seeds. Rhynchosia resinosa is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural systems.

Its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions also makes it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
