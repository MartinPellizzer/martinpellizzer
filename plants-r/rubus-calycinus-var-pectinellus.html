Rubus calycinus var. pectinellus

Rubus calycinus var. pectinellus, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include hairy foliage and a woody structure typical of many Rubus species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
