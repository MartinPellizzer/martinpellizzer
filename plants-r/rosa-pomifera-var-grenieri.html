Rosa pomifera var. grenieri

Rosa pomifera var. grenieri, commonly known as the Grenier's rose, is a unique species within the Rosa genus characterized by its distinctive fruiting structures.

It produces large, fleshy, red-orange hips that are not only ornamental but also edible, containing seeds surrounded by a juicy pulp. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it bears fragrant, pink to white flowers in early summer.

This variety is valued for its horticultural appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental and culinary uses.
