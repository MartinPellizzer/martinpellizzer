Ranunculus pseudothora

Ranunculus pseudothora, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy, often with a reddish tinge.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
