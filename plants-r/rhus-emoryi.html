Rhus emoryi

Rhus emoryi, commonly known as Emory's sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into several narrow, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for birds and wildlife. Rhus emoryi is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and sustenance for various desert species.
