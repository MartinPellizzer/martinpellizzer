Rosa alpina var. fontis-romaei

Rosa alpina var. fontis-romaei, commonly known as the Roman alpine rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
