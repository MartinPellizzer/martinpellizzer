Rytidostylis brevisetosa

Rytidostylis brevisetosa, commonly known as the short-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact rosette. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

Rytidostylis brevisetosa plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
