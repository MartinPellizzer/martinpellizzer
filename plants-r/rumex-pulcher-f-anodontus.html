Rumex pulcher f. anodontus

Rumex pulcher f. anodontus, commonly known as the purple dock, is a variant of the common dock plant.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, wavy, and often purple-tinged leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense terminal cluster.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cuisines.
