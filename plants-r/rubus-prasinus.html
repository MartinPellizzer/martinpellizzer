Rubus prasinus

Rubus prasinus, commonly known as the green bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a serrated edge and produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring. Its fruits are aggregate berries that mature to a dark red color, often used in local cuisines.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local wildlife.
