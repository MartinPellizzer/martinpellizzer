Rubus iringanus

Rubus iringanus, commonly known as the Irian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Indonesian island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, thriving in humid tropical climates. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pinkish-white with five petals.

The fruit, which resembles a raspberry, is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.
