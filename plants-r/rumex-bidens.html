Rumex bidens

Rumex bidens, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in moist soils along roadsides, fields, and stream banks. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces oval, dark reddish-brown seeds.

Rumex bidens is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
