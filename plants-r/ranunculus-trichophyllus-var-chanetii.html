Ranunculus trichophyllus var. chanetii

Ranunculus trichophyllus var. chanetii, commonly known as the Chanet's hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are characteristic of its varietal name "trichophyllus.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
