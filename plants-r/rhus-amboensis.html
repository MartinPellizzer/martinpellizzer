Rhus amboensis

Rhus amboensis, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into five to nine oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fuzzy berries, which are a food source for various birds and animals.

The shrub is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
