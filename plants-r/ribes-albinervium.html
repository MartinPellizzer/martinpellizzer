Ribes albinervium

Ribes albinervium, commonly known as the white-veined currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of gooseberries and currants. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves with prominent white veins. Its flowers are typically pink to white, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

Ribes albinervium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian habitats.
