Rhododendron cuneifolium var. microcarpum

Rhododendron cuneifolium var. microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf alpine rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
