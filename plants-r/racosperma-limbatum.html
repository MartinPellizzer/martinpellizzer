Racosperma limbatum

Racosperma limbatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
