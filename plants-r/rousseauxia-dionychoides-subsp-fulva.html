Rousseauxia dionychoides subsp. fulva

Rousseauxia dionychoides subsp.

fulva, commonly known as the golden rousseauxia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.
