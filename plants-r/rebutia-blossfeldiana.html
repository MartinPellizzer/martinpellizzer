Rebutia blossfeldiana

Rebutia blossfeldiana, commonly known as the Easter cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, rounded stems that often form a compact, bushy shape. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, and it thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.

Rebutia blossfeldiana is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
