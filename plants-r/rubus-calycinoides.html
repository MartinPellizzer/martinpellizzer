Rubus calycinoides

Rubus calycinoides, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This shrub is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
