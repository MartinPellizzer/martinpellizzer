Rubus nitidoides

Rubus nitidoides, commonly known as the glossy bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red, edible berries in the summer. Its leaves are typically glossy and have a distinctive serrated margin.

Rubus nitidoides is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
