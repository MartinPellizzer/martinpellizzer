Rubus fruticosus var. collinus

Rubus fruticosus var. collinus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberries, is a shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The plant thrives in a variety of soils but prefers well-drained, moist environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is known for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants.

The species is often used in hedges and for its ornamental and culinary value.
