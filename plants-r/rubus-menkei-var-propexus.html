Rubus menkei var. propexus

Rubus menkei var. propexus, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
