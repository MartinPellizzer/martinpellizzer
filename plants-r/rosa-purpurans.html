Rosa purpurans

Rosa purpurans, commonly known as the purple rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its vibrant purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green foliage. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a fragrant, showy display.

Rosa purpurans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
