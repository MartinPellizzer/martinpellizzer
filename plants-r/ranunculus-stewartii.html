Ranunculus stewartii

Ranunculus stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to nine sepals that resemble petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has a short, hairy stem and basal leaves that are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
