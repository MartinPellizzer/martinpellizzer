Rosa villosa subsp. tomentosa

Rosa villosa subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States. It features thorny stems covered in a dense, woolly layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets that are also hairy. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals, and appear in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
