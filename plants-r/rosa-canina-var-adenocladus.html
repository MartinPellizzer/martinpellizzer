Rosa canina var. adenocladus

Rosa canina var. adenocladus, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets. The fruit, a red rosehip, is notable for its high vitamin C content.

This plant is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates.
