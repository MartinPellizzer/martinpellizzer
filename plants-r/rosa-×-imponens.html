Rosa × imponens

Rosa × imponens, commonly known as the everglast rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which remains green throughout the year, making it distinct from most other roses. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rosa × imponens is native to the Mediterranean region and is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardiness in mild climates.
