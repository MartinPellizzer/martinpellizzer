Rosa × hibernica var. glabra

Rosa × hibernica var. glabra, commonly known as the Irish rose or smooth shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to Ireland.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thornless stems, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it is generally hardy in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
