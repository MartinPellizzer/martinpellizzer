Rhododendron vittatum var. punctatum

Rhododendron vittatum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture, and the plant produces small, elliptical berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as part of natural landscapes.
