Rubus bakeri

Rubus bakeri, commonly known as Baker's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and blackberries. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
