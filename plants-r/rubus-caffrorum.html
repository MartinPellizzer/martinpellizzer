Rubus caffrorum

Rubus caffrorum, commonly known as the Caffrorum blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests and shrublands, and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
