Rosa sepium var. dolichocarpa

Rosa sepium var. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-stemmed rose, is a variant of the Rosa sepium species.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
