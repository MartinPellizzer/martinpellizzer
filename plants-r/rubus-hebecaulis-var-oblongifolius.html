Rubus hebecaulis var. oblongifolius

Rubus hebecaulis var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, thorny stems and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The leaves are elliptical in shape, with a pointed tip and serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
