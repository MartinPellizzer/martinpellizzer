Rubus × firmus

Rubus × firmus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
