Rubus cyclomorphus

Rubus cyclomorphus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple berries. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
