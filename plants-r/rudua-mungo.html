Rudua mungo

Rudua mungo, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Rudua mungo is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant in dry climates.

It is often used for forage by livestock and has cultural significance in some African communities.
