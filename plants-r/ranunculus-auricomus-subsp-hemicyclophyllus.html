Ranunculus auricomus subsp. hemicyclophyllus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

hemicyclophyllus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with a distinctive greenish central portion.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy underside, and it produces small, hairy fruits that disperse by wind.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms.
