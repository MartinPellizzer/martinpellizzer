Rubus rosaefolius

Rubus rosaefolius, commonly known as the rose-leaved raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are serrated and have a reddish hue, resembling rose foliage. Its flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, edible berries similar to raspberries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
