Roupala pinnata

Roupala pinnata, commonly known as the pinnate roupala, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with elongated leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing some resistance to pests and diseases.

Roupala pinnata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
