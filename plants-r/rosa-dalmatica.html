Rosa dalmatica

Rosa dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its historical use in perfumery and culinary applications adds to its botanical significance.
