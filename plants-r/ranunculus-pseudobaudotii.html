Ranunculus pseudobaudotii

Ranunculus pseudobaudotii, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus pseudobaudotii is often found at high elevations, where it plays a role in the local flora and ecosystem.
