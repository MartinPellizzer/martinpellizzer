Rhaphiolepis poilanei

Rhaphiolepis poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar holly.

It is a shrub native to Madagascar, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and year-round appeal make it a valuable addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
