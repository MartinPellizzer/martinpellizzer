Rourea coccinea subsp. boiviniana

Rourea coccinea subsp.

boiviniana, commonly known as the red-veined rourea, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its striking red veins on its dark green leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
