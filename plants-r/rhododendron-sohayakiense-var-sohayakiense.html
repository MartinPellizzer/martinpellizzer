Rhododendron sohayakiense var. sohayakiense

Rhododendron sohayakiense var. sohayakiense, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in late spring to early summer.
