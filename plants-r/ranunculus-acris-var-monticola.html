Ranunculus acris var. monticola

Ranunculus acris var. monticola, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
