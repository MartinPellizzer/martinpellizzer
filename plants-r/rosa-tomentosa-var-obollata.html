Rosa tomentosa var. obollata

Rosa tomentosa var. obollata, commonly known as the woolly rose or the fuzzy rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a thorny stem and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

Due to its hardy nature, it can thrive in a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
