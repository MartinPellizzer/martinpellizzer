Rubus similatus

Rubus similatus, commonly known as the similar bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, where it grows in temperate regions. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small white or pink flowers. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are typically edible and used in local cuisines.

Rubus similatus plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
