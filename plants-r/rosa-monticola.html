Rosa monticola

Rosa monticola, commonly known as the mountain rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Rosa monticola is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and cooler climates.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
