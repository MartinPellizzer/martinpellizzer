Rebutia pygmaea var. diersiana

Rebutia pygmaea var. diersiana, commonly known as the Diersian Rebutia, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to South America.

It features small, rounded stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color, with spines that are often reddish or brown. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often sought after by cactus enthusiasts.
