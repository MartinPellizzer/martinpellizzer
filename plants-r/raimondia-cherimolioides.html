Raimondia cherimolioides

Raimondia cherimolioides, commonly known as the Chinese star fruit or carambola, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, deciduous tree that produces oblong, star-shaped fruits with a tangy, acidic flavor. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a glossy green surface.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns yellow, and it is widely cultivated for its edible, vitamin C-rich fruit.
