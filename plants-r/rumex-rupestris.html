Rumex rupestris

Rumex rupestris, commonly known as the rock sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It features basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with wavy margins and a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Rumex rupestris is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
