Ruppia brachypus

Ruppia brachypus, commonly known as the broad-leaved water plantain, is a freshwater aquatic plant characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves that grow in dense clusters.

It typically thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are often submerged, and it is known for its ability to form extensive underwater meadows. Ruppia brachypus has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize sediment and improve water quality.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting aquatic life and maintaining habitat diversity in freshwater ecosystems.
