Rosa communis var. trichoidea

Rosa communis var. trichoidea, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a cultivar characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to various regions in Europe and Asia. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are serrated and covered with fine, soft hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
