Rhododendron cookeanum

Rhododendron cookeanum, commonly known as the Cooke's rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Rhododendron cookeanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
