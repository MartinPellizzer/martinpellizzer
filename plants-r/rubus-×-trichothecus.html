Rubus × trichothecus

Rubus × trichothecus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small drupes, which are often referred to as berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland areas and along hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is both cultivated and considered a weed in various regions.
