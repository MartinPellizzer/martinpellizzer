Rubus rhamnifolius subsp. maassii

Rubus rhamnifolius subsp.

maassii, commonly known as the Maass's blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
