Rubus pervirescens

Rubus pervirescens, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of brambles native to eastern North America, characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn bright red when mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Rubus pervirescens is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
