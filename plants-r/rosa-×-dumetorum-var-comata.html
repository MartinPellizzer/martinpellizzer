Rosa × dumetorum var. comata

Rosa × dumetorum var. comata, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for landscaping and garden cultivation.
