Romana integerrima

Romana integerrima, commonly known as the sacred violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its delicate appearance and historical significance in herbal medicine contribute to its botanical interest.

Romana integerrima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional remedies for centuries.
