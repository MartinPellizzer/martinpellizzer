Rubus caesius f. eucaesius

Rubus caesius f. eucaesius, commonly known as the purple-fruited raspberry, is a botanical variant of the raspberry genus characterized by its distinctive deep purple fruits.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, typically found in temperate regions with moist, well-drained soils. The plant features thorny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and small white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are not only visually striking but also prized for their unique flavor and culinary uses.

This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in small-scale fruit production.
