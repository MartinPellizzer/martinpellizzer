Rhododendron annae subsp. laxiflorum

Rhododendron annae subsp.

laxiflorum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The subspecies is known for its compact growth habit and is often found in humid, shaded environments. It thrives in acidic soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and garden design.
