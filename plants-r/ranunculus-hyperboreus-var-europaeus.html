Ranunculus hyperboreus var. europaeus

Ranunculus hyperboreus var. europaeus, commonly known as the European glacier buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a short, hairy stem and basal leaves that are pinnately divided. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
