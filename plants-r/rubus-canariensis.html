Rubus canariensis

Rubus canariensis, commonly known as the Canary bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, juicy berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
