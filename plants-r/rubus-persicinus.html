Rubus persicinus

Rubus persicinus, commonly known as the blackberry plant, is a species of bramble in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant produces dark purple to black berries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland areas and along hedgerows.

Rubus persicinus is also known for its role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
