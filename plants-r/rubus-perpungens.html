Rubus perpungens

Rubus perpungens, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of bramble in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

Rubus perpungens thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a common sight in gardens and wild landscapes.
