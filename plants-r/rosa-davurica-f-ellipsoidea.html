Rosa davurica f. ellipsoidea

Rosa davurica f. ellipsoidea, commonly known as the elliptic form of the shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for garden landscapes in temperate climates.
