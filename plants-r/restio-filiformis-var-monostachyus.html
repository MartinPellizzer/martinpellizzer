Restio filiformis var. monostachyus

Restio filiformis var. monostachyus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing small, white to pale blue flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
