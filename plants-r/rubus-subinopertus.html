Rubus subinopertus

Rubus subinopertus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its flowers are white and clustered in cymes, and the plant is often found in woodland edges and moist habitats.

The botanical features of Rubus subinopertus make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
