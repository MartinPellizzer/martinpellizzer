Rubus glomeratus var. albulus

Rubus glomeratus var. albulus, commonly known as the white-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its pale yellow to white berries.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the berries, while not as sweet as other blackberries, are edible.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in partial shade.
