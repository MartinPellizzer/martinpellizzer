Rosa × jugicola

Rosa × jugicola, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a hybrid shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and has dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Rosa × jugicola is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature, adapting well to various climates.
