Rosa communis var. gracilior

Rosa communis var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender rose, is a cultivar characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers.

It typically produces small, pink to white blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, thornless stems and dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance compared to other rose cultivars.
