Rosa pendulina f. metallicola

Rosa pendulina f. metallicola, commonly known as the silverleaf rock rose, is a unique hybrid form of the pendulous rose.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive metallic sheen. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a landscape plant.
