Rhododendron × hunsteinii

Rhododendron × hunsteinii, commonly known as the Hunstein's rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is evergreen, with a shrub-like growth habit that makes it suitable for ornamental landscaping. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice in woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural ecosystems.
