Rosa × andegavensis var. lemaitrei

Rosa × andegavensis var. lemaitrei, commonly known as the Lemaitre rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thornless foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a popular choice among rose enthusiasts.
