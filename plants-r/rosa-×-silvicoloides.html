Rosa × silvicoloides

Rosa × silvicoloides, commonly known as the silverbark rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white to grayish bark, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub with arching stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rosa × silvicoloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
