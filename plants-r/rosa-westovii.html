Rosa westovii

Rosa westovii, commonly known as the Westovii rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are followed by oval, yellowish fruits called hips. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa westovii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
