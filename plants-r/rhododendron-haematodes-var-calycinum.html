Rhododendron haematodes var. calycinum

Rhododendron haematodes var. calycinum, commonly known as the Crimson Rosebay, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
