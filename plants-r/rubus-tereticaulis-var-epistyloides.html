Rubus tereticaulis var. epistyloides

Rubus tereticaulis var. epistyloides, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically consumed by wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and woodland areas.

Its botanical features include a woody structure, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits.
