Rhynchodium foetidum

Rhynchodium foetidum, commonly known as the stinking tamarind.

This plant is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often foul-smelling flowers. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and has a woody, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it notable in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
