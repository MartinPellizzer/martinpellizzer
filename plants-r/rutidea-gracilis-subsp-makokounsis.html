Rutidea gracilis subsp. makokounsis

Rutidea gracilis subsp.

makokounsis, commonly known as the slender rutidion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.
