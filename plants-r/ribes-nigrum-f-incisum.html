Ribes nigrum f. incisum

Ribes nigrum f. incisum, commonly known as the blackcurrant cultivar with incised leaves, is a variant of the blackcurrant plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance compared to the typical blackcurrant. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and culinary use.
