Rhamnus paniculata

Rhamnus paniculata, commonly known as the fragrant buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 4 to 8 inches long and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Rhamnus paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting local wildlife.
