Rosa podolica

Rosa podolica, commonly known as the Podolian rose, is a species native to the Balkan region, particularly Croatia and Bosnia and Herzegovina.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in small groups. Rosa podolica is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in rocky or poor soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
