Rubus alpestris

Rubus alpestris, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a species of bramble native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, thriving in cool, temperate climates. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
