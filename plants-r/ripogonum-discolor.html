Ripogonum discolor

Ripogonum discolor, commonly known as the red-flowered climbing fern, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers that contrast with its dark green, fern-like leaves. The plant has a climbing habit, using its tendrils to ascend supports in its natural habitat. Its leaves are compound, with multiple small, lance-shaped leaflets.

Ripogonum discolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
