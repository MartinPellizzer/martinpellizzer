Rhamnus myrtifolia subsp. iranzoi

Rhamnus myrtifolia subsp.

iranzoi, commonly known as the Iberian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by dark purple berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
