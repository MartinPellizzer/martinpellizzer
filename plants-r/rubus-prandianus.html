Rubus prandianus

Rubus prandianus, commonly known as the Andean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
