Rubus stohrii

Rubus stohrii, commonly known as the Stohrii blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit characteristics, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
