Ranunculus auricomus subsp. anisodon

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

anisodon, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The subspecies anisodon is distinguished by its slightly different leaf shape and floral structure compared to the typical form of R. auricomus. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing in rocky or meadow environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
