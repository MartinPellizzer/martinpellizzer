Racosperma cyperophyllum

Racosperma cyperophyllum, commonly known as the Cyperus-like racosperma, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in rainforests or along stream banks. Its foliage has a distinctive scent, which is released when the leaves are crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
