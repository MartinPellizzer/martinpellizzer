Restrepia antennifera var. angustifolia

Restrepia antennifera var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed restrepia, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often blooming during the summer months. It is native to the Andean regions of South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
