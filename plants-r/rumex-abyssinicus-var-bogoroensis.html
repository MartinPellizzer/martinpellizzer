Rumex abyssinicus var. bogoroensis

Rumex abyssinicus var. bogoroensis, commonly known as the Abyssinian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands.

Its leaves are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.
