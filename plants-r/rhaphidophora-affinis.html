Rhaphidophora affinis

Rhaphidophora affinis, commonly known as the velvetleaf philodendron, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight.

Rhaphidophora affinis is a popular houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
