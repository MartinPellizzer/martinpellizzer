Rosa mollis f. nigricolor

Rosa mollis f. nigricolor, commonly known as the dark-colored form of the soft rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its deep, almost black foliage and dark pink to almost black flowers.

It is a cultivar of Rosa mollis, which is native to the eastern United States, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun. The plant produces fragrant blooms that are often used in ornamental gardening for their striking color contrast. Its leaves are soft and velvety, contributing to its unique aesthetic appeal.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
