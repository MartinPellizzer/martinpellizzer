Racosperma kydrense

Racosperma kydrense, commonly known as the coastal acacia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, attracting various pollinators.

Racosperma kydrense is well-suited to sandy soils and is often found in dune habitats along the coastline.
