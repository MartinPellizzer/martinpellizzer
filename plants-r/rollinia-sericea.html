Rollinia sericea

Rollinia sericea, commonly known as the silky rollinia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The leaves are elliptical, smooth, and have a silvery sheen, giving the plant its common name. It produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible berries that are orange to red in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
