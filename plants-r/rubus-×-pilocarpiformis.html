Rubus × pilocarpiformis

Rubus × pilocarpiformis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub that exhibits characteristics of both blackberries and raspberries.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically red to black in color, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

Due to its hybrid nature, it can exhibit a wide range of morphological and genetic variability.
