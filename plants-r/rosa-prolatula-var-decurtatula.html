Rosa prolatula var. decurtatula

Rosa prolatula var. decurtatula, commonly known as the dwarf shrub rose, is a compact, low-growing shrub with dense, thorny stems.

Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of 5 to 7 elliptical leaflets that are hairy and slightly serrated. The flowers are small, usually pink or white, and bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to the plant. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America and thrives in well-drained, sunny soils.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to form dense thickets, making it suitable for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
