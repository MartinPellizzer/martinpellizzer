Ranunculus aconitifolius subsp. platanifolius

Ranunculus aconitifolius subsp.

platanifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often have a waxy coating.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens and a prominent central pistil. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
