Rosa myrtilloides

Rosa myrtilloides, commonly known as the purple rose or sweetbrier, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, elliptical leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit, a red, juicy berry, is edible and often used in jams and culinary applications.
