Rhododendron shimenense

Rhododendron shimenense, commonly known as the Shimen rhododendron, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of pink to purple flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
