Rubia chilensis

Rubia chilensis, commonly known as Chilean madder, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it is known for its root containing a red pigment used historically in dyeing. Rubia chilensis thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ecological importance includes supporting local wildlife and contributing to soil stability in its native habitats.
