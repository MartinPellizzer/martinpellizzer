Rosa multiflora var. villosula

Rosa multiflora var. villosula, commonly known as the hairy multiflora rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and hairy foliage, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in horticultural practices for its hardiness and adaptability.
