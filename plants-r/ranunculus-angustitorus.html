Ranunculus angustitorus

Ranunculus angustitorus, commonly known as narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent black centers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
