Rubus pruinosus var. prostratus

Rubus pruinosus var. prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate dewberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with finely toothed margins and a pubescent surface. Its flowers are small, white to pink, and appear in clusters, followed by red, edible berries.

This species is adapted to moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
