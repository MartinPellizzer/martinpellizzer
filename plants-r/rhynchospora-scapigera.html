Rhynchospora scapigera

Rhynchospora scapigera, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and spike-like inflorescences that contain small, three-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are linear and often have a distinct midrib, while its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil.

Rhynchospora scapigera plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
