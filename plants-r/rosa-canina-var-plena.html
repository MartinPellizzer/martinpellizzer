Rosa canina var. plena

Rosa canina var. plena, commonly known as the full bloom dog rose, is a cultivar of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its double flowers, which are typically pink or white, and its thorny stems. The plant produces small, red, edible hips that are rich in vitamin C. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
