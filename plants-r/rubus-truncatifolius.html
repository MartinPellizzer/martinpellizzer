Rubus truncatifolius

Rubus truncatifolius, commonly known as the truncate leaf raspberry.

It is a shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its compound leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
