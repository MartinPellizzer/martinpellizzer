Rubus menkei proles oblongifolius

Rubus menkei proles oblongifolius, commonly known as the oblong-leaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with elongated, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
