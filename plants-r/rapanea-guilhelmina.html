Rapanea guilhelmina

Rapanea guilhelmina, commonly known as the Philippine satinwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurel trees, and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage. Its leaves are thick and leathery, with a glossy surface that helps it retain moisture in its native tropical environment.

Rapanea guilhelmina is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland and montane forests.
