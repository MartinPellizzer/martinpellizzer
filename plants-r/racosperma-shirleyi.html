Racosperma shirleyi

Racosperma shirleyi, commonly known as Shirley's acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring compound leaves and yellow or pale green flowers. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
