Rytidosperma pulvinorum

Rytidosperma pulvinorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf star grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Rytidosperma pulvinorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native landscaping and restoration projects.
