Regelia ciliata

Regelia ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf regelia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique foliage and floral display, Regelia ciliata is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in both indoor and outdoor settings.
