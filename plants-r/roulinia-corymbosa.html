Roulinia corymbosa

Roulinia corymbosa, commonly known as the sweet-scented tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
