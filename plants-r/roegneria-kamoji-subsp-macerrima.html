Roegneria kamoji subsp. macerrima

Roegneria kamoji subsp.

macerrima, commonly known as the Kamoji grass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan. It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, and it produces small, two-lobed spikelets. Roegneria kamoji subsp.

macerrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
