Ruppia maritima var. occidentalis

Ruppia maritima var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western sea rocket, is a halophytic plant adapted to brackish and saline environments.

It is a flowering plant in the family Ruppiaceae, characterized by its small, green, grass-like foliage and yellow flowers. The plant thrives in intertidal zones and estuaries, often forming dense mats on the mudflats. Its reproductive structures are designed for dispersal by water, facilitating propagation in coastal areas.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
