Rosa davurica f. rotundocarpa

Rosa davurica f. rotundocarpa, commonly known as the round-fruited shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and the fruits are round, red berries that persist into winter.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for various garden settings.
