Rubus tereticaulis var. lazergesii

Rubus tereticaulis var. lazergesii, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, temperate climates.
