Rosa × centifolia var. multiplex-rubra

Rosa × centifolia var. multiplex-rubra, commonly known as the deep red centifolia rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa centifolia and Rosa multiflora.

It is characterized by its highly fragrant, semi-double to full bloom flowers that range from pale pink to deep crimson. The plant typically grows as a shrub with arching, thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for the production of rose essential oils.
