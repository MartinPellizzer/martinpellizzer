Rosa coriifolia subvar. patens

Rosa coriifolia subvar. patens, commonly known as the Japanese coral tree or Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pink to white. The plant features thorny stems and ovate, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in traditional horticulture.
