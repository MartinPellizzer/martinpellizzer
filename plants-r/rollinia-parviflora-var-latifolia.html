Rollinia parviflora var. latifolia

Rollinia parviflora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf rollinia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and mango. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in panicles. Its fruits are typically spherical, green when unripe, and turn orange when mature.
