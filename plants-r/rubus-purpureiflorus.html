Rubus purpureiflorus

Rubus purpureiflorus, commonly known as the purpleflower blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when ripe. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded environments.

Its flowers are pink to purple in color, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
