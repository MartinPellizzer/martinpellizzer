Rosa maximowicziana f. leiocalyx

Rosa maximowicziana f. leiocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered form of the Korean rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its pale pink to white flowers.

It is a shrub native to Korea and is part of the Rosaceae family. The plant features thorny stems and serrated, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically smaller than those of the typical form and bloom in late spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
