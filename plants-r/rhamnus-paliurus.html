Rhamnus paliurus

Rhamnus paliurus, commonly known as the Chinese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
