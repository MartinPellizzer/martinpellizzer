Rhus ecklonis

Rhus ecklonis, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red berries. Rhus ecklonis is well adapted to sandy soils and salt spray, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
