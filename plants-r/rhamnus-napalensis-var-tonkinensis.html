Rhamnus napalensis var. tonkinensis

Rhamnus napalensis var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries that are edible for certain bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
