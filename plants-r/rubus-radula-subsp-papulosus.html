Rubus radula subsp. papulosus

Rubus radula subsp.

papulosus, commonly known as the rough-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black aggregate berry. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its rough, hairy texture on the fruit and stems.
