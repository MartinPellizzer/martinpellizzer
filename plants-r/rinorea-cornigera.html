Rinorea cornigera

Rinorea cornigera, commonly known as the horned rinorea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruit that resembles a horn, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Rinorea cornigera produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
