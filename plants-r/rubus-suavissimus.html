Rubus suavissimus

Rubus suavissimus, commonly known as the sweet raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor, making it a valued plant in both wild and cultivated settings.
