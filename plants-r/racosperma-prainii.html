Racosperma prainii

Racosperma prainii, commonly known as the Prain's acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, often thorned stems and bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular inflorescences, which attract pollinators like bees and birds.

Racosperma prainii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
