Rubus × expansus

Rubus × expansus, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, lobed leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are usually dark purple to black when mature, containing small, hard seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed areas and along forest edges.
