Rubus × schoenbergensis

Rubus × schoenbergensis, commonly known as the Schoenberg raspberry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus idaeus (red raspberry) and Rubus strigosus (bramble). This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticulture and plant breeding.
