Rubus × rectispinus

Rubus × rectispinus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a popular choice for jams and preserves.
