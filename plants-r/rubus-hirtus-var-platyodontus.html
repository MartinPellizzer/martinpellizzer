Rubus hirtus var. platyodontus

Rubus hirtus var. platyodontus, commonly known as the broad-lobed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with broad, lobed margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible berries and is valued in both wild and agricultural settings.
