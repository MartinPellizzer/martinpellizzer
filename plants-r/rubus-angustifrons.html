Rubus angustifrons

Rubus angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
