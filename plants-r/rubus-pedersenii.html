Rubus pedersenii

Rubus pedersenii, commonly known as Pedersen's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
