Ranunculus haasii

Ranunculus haasii, commonly known as the Haas' buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with 5 to 15 petal-like sepals that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has a short, stout rhizome and produces palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and forested areas.

Ranunculus haasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
