Rheum franzenbachii var. mongolicum

Rheum franzenbachii var. mongolicum, commonly known as the Mongolian rhubarb, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, often containing a high concentration of alkaloids.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
