Rhododendron smirnowii

Rhododendron smirnowii, commonly known as the Smirnow's rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Russia and northern China.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a dense covering of fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
