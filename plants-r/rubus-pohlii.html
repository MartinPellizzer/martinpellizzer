Rubus pohlii

Rubus pohlii, commonly known as the Pohlii blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and clustered in the axils of the leaves, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Rubus pohlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local culinary traditions.
