Ribes aureum var. aureum

Ribes aureum var. aureum, commonly known as golden currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall.

Ribes aureum var. aureum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
