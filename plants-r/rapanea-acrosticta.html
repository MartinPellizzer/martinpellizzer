Rapanea acrosticta

Rapanea acrosticta, commonly known as the red-leafed evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in certain conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical regions.
