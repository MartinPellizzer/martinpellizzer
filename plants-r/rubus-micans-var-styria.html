Rubus micans var. styria

Rubus micans var. styria, commonly known as the Styrian bramble, is a variant of the European bramble species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that ripens to a deep red. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

This plant plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
