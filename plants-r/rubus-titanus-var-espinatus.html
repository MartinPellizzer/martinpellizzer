Rubus titanus var. espinatus

Rubus titanus var. espinatus, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and has a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The fruit is edible, though often used in jams and beverages due to its intense flavor and high acidity.
