Ruellia congesta

Ruellia congesta, commonly known as the clustered ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia congesta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
