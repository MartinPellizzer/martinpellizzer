Rubus harmandii

Rubus harmandii, commonly known as Harmand's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and horticultural collections.
