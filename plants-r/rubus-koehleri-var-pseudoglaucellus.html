Rubus koehleri var. pseudoglaucellus

Rubus koehleri var. pseudoglaucellus, commonly known as the Koechler's bramble, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
