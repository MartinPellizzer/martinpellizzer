Rubus × oxydrepanus

Rubus × oxydrepanus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
