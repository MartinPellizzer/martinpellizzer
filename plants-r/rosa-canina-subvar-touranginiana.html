Rosa canina subvar. touranginiana

Rosa canina subvar. touranginiana, commonly known as the Touranginiana rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to southern France and parts of northern Italy.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces reddish-orange hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy shrub suitable for hedgerows and gardens.
