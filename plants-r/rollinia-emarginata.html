Rollinia emarginata

Rollinia emarginata, commonly known as the margined star-apple, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Myristicaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits. The fruit has a rough, bumpy skin and a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native regions.
