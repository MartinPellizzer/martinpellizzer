Restio racemosus

Restio racemosus, commonly known as the clustered restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
