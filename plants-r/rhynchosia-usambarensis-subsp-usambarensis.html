Rhynchosia usambarensis subsp. usambarensis

Rhynchosia usambarensis subsp.

usambarensis, commonly known as the African burr bean, is a leguminous plant native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration.

The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant.
