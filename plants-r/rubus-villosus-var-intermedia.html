Rubus villosus var. intermedia

Rubus villosus var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
