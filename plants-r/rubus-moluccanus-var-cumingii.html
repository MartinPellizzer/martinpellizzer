Rubus moluccanus var. cumingii

Rubus moluccanus var. cumingii, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically clustered together. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
