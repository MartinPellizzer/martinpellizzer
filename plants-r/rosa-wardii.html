Rosa wardii

Rosa wardii, commonly known as the Ward's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, specifically Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are edible.

Rosa wardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
