Rytigynia acuminatissima

Rytigynia acuminatissima, commonly known as the sharp-pointed rytigynia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Rytigynia acuminatissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
