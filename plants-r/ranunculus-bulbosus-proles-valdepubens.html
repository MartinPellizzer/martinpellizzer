Ranunculus bulbosus proles valdepubens

Ranunculus bulbosus proles valdepubens, commonly known as the common buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a short, rhizomatous rootstock and produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with lobes that are usually notched or toothed.

The flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
