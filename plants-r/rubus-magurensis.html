Rubus magurensis

Rubus magurensis, commonly known as the Korean raspberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many shrubs with edible berries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. It typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
