Ranunculus rostratulus

Ranunculus rostratulus, commonly known as the rostrate buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated center. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

Ranunculus rostratulus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic members.
