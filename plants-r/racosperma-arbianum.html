Racosperma arbianum

Racosperma arbianum, commonly known as the Australian wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow floral spikes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. It plays an important role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.

Racosperma arbianum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
