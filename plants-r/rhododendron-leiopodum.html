Rhododendron leiopodum

Rhododendron leiopodum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and have a smooth texture, contributing to its common name.

Rhododendron leiopodum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
