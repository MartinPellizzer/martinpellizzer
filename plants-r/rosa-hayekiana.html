Rosa hayekiana

Rosa hayekiana, commonly known as Hayek's rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces hips that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Rosa hayekiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
