Rhododendron loboense

Rhododendron loboense, commonly known as the Lobo rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern India and northern Myanmar.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, and the leaves are leathery and dark green.

Rhododendron loboense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitats.
