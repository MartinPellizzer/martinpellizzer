Rheum × cultorum

Rheum × cultorum, commonly known as the cultivated rhubarb, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rheum rhaponticum and Rheum palmatum.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and edible stalks that are used in cooking. The plant is native to Asia but has been widely cultivated in Europe and North America. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Rheum × cultorum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and historical significance in herbal medicine.
