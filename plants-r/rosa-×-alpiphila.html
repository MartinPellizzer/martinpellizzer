Rosa × alpiphila

Rosa × alpiphila, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, often with a subtle scent. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and are covered in a fine layer of down.

Rosa × alpiphila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual beauty.
