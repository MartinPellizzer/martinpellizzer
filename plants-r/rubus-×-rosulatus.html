Rubus × rosulatus

Rubus × rosulatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
