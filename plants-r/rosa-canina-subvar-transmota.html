Rosa canina subvar. transmota

Rosa canina subvar. transmota, commonly known as the Transylvanian dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and the plant produces red, hairy hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
