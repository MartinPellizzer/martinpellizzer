Rubus tereticaulis var. henrici

Rubus tereticaulis var. henrici, commonly known as Henry's blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberry species.
