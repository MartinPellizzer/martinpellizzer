Rubus × pseudofruticetorum

Rubus × pseudofruticetorum, commonly known as the thimbleberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange berries that are typically edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
