Ranunculus rhoeadifolius

Ranunculus rhoeadifolius, commonly known as the prairie buttercup, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, lobed foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and meadows. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus rhoeadifolius is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in the spring and early summer.
