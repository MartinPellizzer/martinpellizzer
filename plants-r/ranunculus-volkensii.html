Ranunculus volkensii

Ranunculus volkensii, commonly known as the Kenyan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals that range in color from white to bright yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

Ranunculus volkensii is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
