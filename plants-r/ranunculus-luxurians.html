Ranunculus luxurians

Ranunculus luxurians, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive central stigma. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ecological value to pollinators.
