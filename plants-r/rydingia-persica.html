Rydingia persica

Rydingia persica, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Persia, now known as Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white petals that resemble those of the traditional rose. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and alternate leaves that are oval in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections around the world.
