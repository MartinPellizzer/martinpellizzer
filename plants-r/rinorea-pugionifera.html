Rinorea pugionifera

Rinorea pugionifera, commonly known as the lanceleaf rinorea, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Rinorea pugionifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
