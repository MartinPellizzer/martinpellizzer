Roemeria hispida

Roemeria hispida, commonly known as the hispid roemeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Roemeria hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
