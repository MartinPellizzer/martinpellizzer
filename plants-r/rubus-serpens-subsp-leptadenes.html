Rubus serpens subsp. leptadenes

Rubus serpens subsp.

leptadenes, commonly known as the slender-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense thickets.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to lance-shaped leaflets that are hairy beneath. The flowers are small, white to pink, and clustered in the axils of the leaves.

The fruit is a dark purple to black aggregate fruit, typically edible and used in local culinary traditions.
