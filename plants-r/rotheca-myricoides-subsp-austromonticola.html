Rotheca myricoides subsp. austromonticola

Rotheca myricoides subsp.

austromonticola, commonly known as the mountain rotheca, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern South America. It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and gravelly soils at high elevations.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and often covered with a fine pubescence.

It is adapted to cold, harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
