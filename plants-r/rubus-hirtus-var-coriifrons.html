Rubus hirtus var. coriifrons

Rubus hirtus var. coriifrons, commonly known as the rough raspberry or prickly rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
