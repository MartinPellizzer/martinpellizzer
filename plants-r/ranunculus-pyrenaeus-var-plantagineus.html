Ranunculus pyrenaeus var. plantagineus

Ranunculus pyrenaeus var. plantagineus, commonly known as the Pyrenean buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

The variety is distinguished by its slightly larger and more robust leaves compared to the typical form of Ranunculus pyrenaeus.
