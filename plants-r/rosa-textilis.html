Rosa textilis

Rosa textilis, commonly known as the textile rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa textilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile dyeing.
