Rubus × semialterniflorus

Rubus × semialterniflorus, commonly known as the alternateleaf blackberry, is a hybrid plant in the Rubus genus, which includes various brambles and blackberries.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of leaves, a distinguishing feature that sets it apart from many other Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
