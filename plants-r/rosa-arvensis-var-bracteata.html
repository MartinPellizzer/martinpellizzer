Rosa arvensis var. bracteata

Rosa arvensis var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate field rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with thorny branches and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges. This plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
