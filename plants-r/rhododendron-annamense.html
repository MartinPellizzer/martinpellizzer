Rhododendron annamense

Rhododendron annamense, commonly known as the Annam rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Laos.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and adaptability to acidic soils.

Rhododendron annamense is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
