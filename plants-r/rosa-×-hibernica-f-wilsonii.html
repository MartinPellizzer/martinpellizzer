Rosa × hibernica f. wilsonii

Rosa × hibernica f. wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's shrub rose, is a hybrid species originating from the United Kingdom.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
