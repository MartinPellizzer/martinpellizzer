Rosa glauca var. dunensis

Rosa glauca var. dunensis, commonly known as the Dunedin rose, is a cultivar of the shrub rose native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its pale blue to lavender flowers and dark green, slightly hairy foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces fragrant blooms in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique coloration and hardy nature contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
