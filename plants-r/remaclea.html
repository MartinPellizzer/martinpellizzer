Remaclea

Remaclea, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a striking appearance during the spring. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. Remaclea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained soils.

It is also known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
