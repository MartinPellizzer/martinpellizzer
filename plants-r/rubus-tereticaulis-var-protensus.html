Rubus tereticaulis var. protensus

Rubus tereticaulis var. protensus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
