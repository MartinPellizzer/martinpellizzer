Rosa woronowii var. subbidentata

Rosa woronowii var. subbidentata, commonly known as the Siberian rose or the Russian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the development of many modern rose cultivars.
