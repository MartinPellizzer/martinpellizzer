Rhododendron argyrophyllum

Rhododendron argyrophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf rhododendron, is a shrub native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that displays a striking silvery-white or grayish hue on the underside of the leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
