Rosa coriifolia var. pallens

Rosa coriifolia var. pallens, commonly known as the pale-flowered shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate with a finely toothed margin and a hairy underside. This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.
