Rubus fastigiatus f. umbrosus

Rubus fastigiatus f. umbrosus, commonly known as the dark-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

It features dark purple to black berries that are typically small and tart. The plant's leaves are usually compound, with three to five leaflets that are hairy and serrated. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodlands and underbrush.

This form is distinguished by its darker pigmentation and more compact growth compared to the typical Rubus fastigiatus.
