Rosa sherardii var. eminens

Rosa sherardii var. eminens, commonly known as the highland rose, is a variant of the Sherard's rose native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
