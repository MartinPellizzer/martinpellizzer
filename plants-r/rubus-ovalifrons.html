Rubus ovalifrons

Rubus ovalifrons, commonly known as the oval-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are oval-shaped and have a distinctive waxy coating. It produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisine.

The shrub thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
