Rosa canina var. valdearmata

Rosa canina var. valdearmata, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a variant of the dog rose that is native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its simple, usually five-lobed leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
