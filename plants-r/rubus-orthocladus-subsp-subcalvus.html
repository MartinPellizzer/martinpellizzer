Rubus orthocladus subsp. subcalvus

Rubus orthocladus subsp.

subcalvus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is often found in woodland or disturbed habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
