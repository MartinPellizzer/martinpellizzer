Rosa lucieae f. appendiculata

Rosa lucieae f. appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate rose, is a unique horticultural form of the species Rosa lucieae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a desirable addition to gardens.

This form is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate appearance and fragrance.
