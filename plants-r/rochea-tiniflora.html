Rochea tiniflora

Rochea tiniflora, commonly known as the slender rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
