Rubus × luculentus

Rubus × luculentus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to the western United States and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
