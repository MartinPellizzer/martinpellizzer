Rapuntium serpens

Rapuntium serpens, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a sprawling growth habit with slender, often reddish stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces spiny, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Rapuntium serpens is adapted to a variety of soils and can thrive in disturbed or marginal habitats.
