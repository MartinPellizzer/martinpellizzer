Rubus linguifolius

Rubus linguifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its large, broad leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus linguifolius plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
