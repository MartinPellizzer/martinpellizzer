Rosa anticaria

Rosa anticaria, commonly known as the Canadian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant pink or red flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange hips in the fall. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Rosa anticaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
