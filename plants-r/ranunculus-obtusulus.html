Ranunculus obtusulus

Ranunculus obtusulus, commonly known as the bluntleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, rounded shape.

The flowers are usually yellow or white with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and they bloom in early spring.
