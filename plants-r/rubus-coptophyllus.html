Rubus coptophyllus

Rubus coptophyllus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of red, edible fruits. The plant is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink.

The fruit, which ripens in summer, is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
