Rubus scioanus

Rubus scioanus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible, though they are often tart and used in jams or wines.
