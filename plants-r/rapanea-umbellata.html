Rapanea umbellata

Rapanea umbellata, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its evergreen nature provides year-round visual interest in gardens.
