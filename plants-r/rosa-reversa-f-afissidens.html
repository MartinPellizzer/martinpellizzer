Rosa reversa f. afissidens

Rosa reversa f. afissidens, commonly known as the reverse rose or the inverted rose, is a unique horticultural form of the species Rosa reversa.

It is characterized by its distinctive double flowers that appear to be reversed compared to the typical single bloom of the species. The plant features deep red petals that are tightly packed, creating a dense, velvety appearance. It is a compact shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking visual appeal.
