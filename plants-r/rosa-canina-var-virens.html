Rosa canina var. virens

Rosa canina var. virens, commonly known as the greenleaf dogrose, is a variation of the common dogrose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and oval, dark green leaves. The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals, and appear in clusters. The plant produces red, hairy berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.

It is native to Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
