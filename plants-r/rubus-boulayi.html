Rubus boulayi

Rubus boulayi, commonly known as the Boulay's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it flowers with white to pale pink blooms.

Rubus boulayi is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
