Rubus baileyanus

Rubus baileyanus, commonly known as the Baileyan blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Vietnam and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
