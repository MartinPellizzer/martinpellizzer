Rumex euxinus

Rumex euxinus, commonly known as the Russian dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rumex euxinus is often found in moist soils along riverbanks, marshes, and damp woodlands.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
