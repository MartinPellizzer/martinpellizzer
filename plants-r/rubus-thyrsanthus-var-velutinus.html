Rubus thyrsanthus var. velutinus

Rubus thyrsanthus var. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet raspberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, resembling raspberries in appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
