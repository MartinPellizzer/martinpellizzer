Rosa canina separabilis

Rosa canina separabilis, commonly known as the separable dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose (Rosa canina).

It is characterized by its distinct ability to separate into individual flowers, a unique trait that sets it apart from other rose species. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, and it produces fragrant, pink or white flowers in the spring. Its fruit, known as a hip, is an orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in woodland edges and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
