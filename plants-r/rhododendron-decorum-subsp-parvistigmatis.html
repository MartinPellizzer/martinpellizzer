Rhododendron decorum subsp. parvistigmatis

Rhododendron decorum subsp.

parvistigmatis, commonly known as the small-stigma rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern China. It features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy.

The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
