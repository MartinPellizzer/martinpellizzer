Rubus discolor var. latissimus

Rubus discolor var. latissimus, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
