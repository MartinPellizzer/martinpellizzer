Rubus corchorifolius var. palmatoides

Rubus corchorifolius var. palmatoides, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits, making it a popular choice for gardens and small-scale cultivation.
