Rhododendron schlippenbachii

Rhododendron schlippenbachii, commonly known as the Schlippenbach rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
