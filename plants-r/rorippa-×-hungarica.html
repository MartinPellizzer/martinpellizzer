Rorippa × hungarica

Rorippa × hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian watercress, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, alternate leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the mustard family. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in muddy or wet soils.

Rorippa × hungarica is often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and along the edges of streams.
