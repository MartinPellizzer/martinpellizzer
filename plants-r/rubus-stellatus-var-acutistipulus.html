Rubus stellatus var. acutistipulus

Rubus stellatus var. acutistipulus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Japan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer.

The berries, which are typically red when mature, are edible but often have a tart flavor.
