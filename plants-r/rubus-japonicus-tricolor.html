Rubus japonicus tricolor

Rubus japonicus tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, trifoliate flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red, white, and yellow, hence its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

The plant has both ornamental and culinary value, though its thorny stems can make it challenging to handle.
