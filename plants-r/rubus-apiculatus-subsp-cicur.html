Rubus apiculatus subsp. cicur

Rubus apiculatus subsp.

cicur, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and the presence of prickly stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
