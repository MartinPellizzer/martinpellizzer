Rudbeckia monticola

Rudbeckia monticola, commonly known as the mountain rudbeckia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. The plant features upright stems with large, showy yellow-orange flower heads surrounded by greenish-leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Rudbeckia monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, daisy-like appearance.
