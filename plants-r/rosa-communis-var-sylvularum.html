Rosa communis var. sylvularum

Rosa communis var. sylvularum, commonly known as the wild rose, is a flowering shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant's fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands or along hedgerows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
