Rubus × villosus

Rubus × villosus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubus genus in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible, though they are often described as having a tart and slightly astringent flavor.

Rubus × villosus is native to North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
