Rosa spuria var. euoxyphylla

Rosa spuria var. euoxyphylla, commonly known as the European shrub rose, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. The leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its hardiness and adaptability.

This variety is also noted for its ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
