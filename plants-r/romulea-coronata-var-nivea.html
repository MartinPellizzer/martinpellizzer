Romulea coronata var. nivea

Romulea coronata var. nivea, commonly known as the white crown vial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a crown-like cluster atop a slender stem. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a prized addition to botanical collections.
