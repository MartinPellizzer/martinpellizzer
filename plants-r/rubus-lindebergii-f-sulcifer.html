Rubus lindebergii f. sulcifer

Rubus lindebergii f. sulcifer, commonly known as the Sulci-leaf raspberry, is a unique variant of the Rubus genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, similar to other raspberries. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
