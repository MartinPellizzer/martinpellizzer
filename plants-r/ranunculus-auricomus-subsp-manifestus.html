Ranunculus auricomus subsp. manifestus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

manifestus, commonly known as the golden crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of yellow sepals that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for garden cultivation due to its ornamental value.
