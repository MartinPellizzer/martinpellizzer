Rondeletia savannarum

Rondeletia savannarum, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in open woodlands and savannas. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a leathery texture.

Rondeletia savannarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
