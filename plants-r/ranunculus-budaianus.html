Ranunculus budaianus

Ranunculus budaianus, commonly known as the Budaian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic species. The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
