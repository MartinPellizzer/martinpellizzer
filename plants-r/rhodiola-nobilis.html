Rhodiola nobilis

Rhodiola nobilis, commonly known as the noble rhodiola, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cold climates and well-drained soil. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it adapts to harsh conditions through its extensive root system.

Rhodiola nobilis is also noted for its potential adaptogenic properties, making it of interest in traditional medicine.
