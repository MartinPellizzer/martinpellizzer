Rhabdia fluvialis

Rhabdia fluvialis, commonly known as the river spleenwort, is a small, aquatic moss that thrives in freshwater environments.

It typically grows on submerged or partially submerged stones and logs in streams and rivers. The plant has a delicate, branching structure with specialized cells that help it absorb nutrients from the water. Its reproductive structures, called sporophytes, develop from the gametophyte stage and release spores into the water.

Rhabdia fluvialis plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering sediments and providing habitat for microorganisms.
