Rosa canina subvar. hispidula

Rosa canina subvar. hispidula, commonly known as the bristly dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to pale red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in spring.

The plant produces bright red, hairy fruits called hip fruits, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
