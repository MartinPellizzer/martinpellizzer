Rhus gracillima

Rhus gracillima, commonly known as the slender sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets, which turn vibrant red in the fall. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, fuzzy berries that persist into winter.

Rhus gracillima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and wildlife.
