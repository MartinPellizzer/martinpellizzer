Ranunculus acris f. grandiflorus

Ranunculus acris f. grandiflorus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a variant of the common buttercup.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are usually larger and more showy than those of the typical species.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
