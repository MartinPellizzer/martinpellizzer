Rubus fuliginosus

Rubus fuliginosus, commonly known as the sooty blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that are often covered in a sooty or powdery coating. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible fruits.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
