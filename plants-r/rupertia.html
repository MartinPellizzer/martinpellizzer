Rupertia

Rupertia, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to North America.

It consists of several species, most of which are perennial herbs with showy, showy flowers that bloom in the summer. The plants typically have grass-like leaves and produce small, clustered inflorescences. Rupertia species are often found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in their native environments.
