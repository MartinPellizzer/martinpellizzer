Ranunculus subtruncatus

Ranunculus subtruncatus, commonly known as the cut-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is considered a herbaceous perennial, with a short stem and a basal rosette of leaves.
