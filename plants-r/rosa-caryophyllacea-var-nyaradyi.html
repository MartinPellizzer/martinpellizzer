Rosa caryophyllacea var. nyaradyi

Rosa caryophyllacea var. nyaradyi, commonly known as the Nyarady rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the mountainous regions of Hungary.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are covered in thorny prickles. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
