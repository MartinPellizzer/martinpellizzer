Rytidosperma oreophilum

Rytidosperma oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
