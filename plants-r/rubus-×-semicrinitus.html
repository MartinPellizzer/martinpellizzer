Rubus × semicrinitus

Rubus × semicrinitus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly undersides on its leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
