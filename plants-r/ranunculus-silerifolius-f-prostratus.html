Ranunculus silerifolius f. prostratus

Ranunculus silerifolius f. prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with creeping stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and the plant prefers moist, shaded environments.

It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
