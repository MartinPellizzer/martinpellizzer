Rhynchosia minima var. diminifolia

Rhynchosia minima var. diminifolia, commonly known as the dwarf yellow bell, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, climbing stems with small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, yellow-orange flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
