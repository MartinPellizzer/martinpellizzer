Rosa saturata var. saturata

Rosa saturata var. saturata, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that turn red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Due to its beauty and fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
