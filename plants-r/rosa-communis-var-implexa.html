Rosa communis var. implexa

Rosa communis var. implexa, commonly known as the entwined rose, is a variety of the common rose that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is distinguished by its tightly curled, overlapping petals that give it a dense, intricate appearance. The plant typically features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual complexity.
