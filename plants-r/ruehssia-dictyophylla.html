Ruehssia dictyophylla

Ruehssia dictyophylla, commonly known as the lace-leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricately veined, and deeply lobed leaves that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ruehssia dictyophylla prefers bright, indirect light and high humidity to thrive.
