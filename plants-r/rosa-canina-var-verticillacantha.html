Rosa canina var. verticillacantha

Rosa canina var. verticillacantha, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, hairy fruits known as hips. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
