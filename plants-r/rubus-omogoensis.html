Rubus omogoensis

Rubus omogoensis, commonly known as the Omogo blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are highly valued for their flavor and medicinal properties. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has a sprawling growth habit.

The berries are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory benefits.
