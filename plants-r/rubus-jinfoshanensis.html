Rubus jinfoshanensis

Rubus jinfoshanensis, commonly known as the Jinfoshan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
