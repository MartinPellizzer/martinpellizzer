Rumex magellanicus

Rumex magellanicus, commonly known as the Magellanic dock, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
