Rubus nodiflorus

Rubus nodiflorus, commonly known as the nodiflorous blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
