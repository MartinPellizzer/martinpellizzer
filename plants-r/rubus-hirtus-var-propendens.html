Rubus hirtus var. propendens

Rubus hirtus var. propendens, commonly known as the trailing raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems that often trail along the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing several drupelets. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
