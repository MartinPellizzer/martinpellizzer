Rubus ligulatus

Rubus ligulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive narrow shape with serrated edges.

Rubus ligulatus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.
