Rubus thyrsoideus microgene tumidus

Rubus thyrsoideus microgene tumidus, commonly known as the fat-hairy blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring. Its fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is an important part of the local ecosystem.
