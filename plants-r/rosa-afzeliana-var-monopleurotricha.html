Rosa afzeliana var. monopleurotricha

Rosa afzeliana var. monopleurotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with narrow, ovate leaflets that are hairy beneath. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden environments.
