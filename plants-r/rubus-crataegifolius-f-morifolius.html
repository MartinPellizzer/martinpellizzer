Rubus crataegifolius f. morifolius

Rubus crataegifolius f. morifolius, commonly known as the Morifolius rosehip.

This plant is a cultivar of the species Rubus crataegifolius, characterized by its distinctive leaf shape that resembles the leaves of hawthorn (Crataegus). It produces small, red berries that are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
