Rhaphidophora guamensis

Rhaphidophora guamensis, commonly known as the Guam arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the island of Guam.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves and a short, stout stem. The plant produces a spadix, which is a type of inflorescence typical of arums, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Rhaphidophora guamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in the Pacific region.
