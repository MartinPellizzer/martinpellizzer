Rhus viminalis var. gerrardii

Rhus viminalis var. gerrardii, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by orange-red, fuzzy fruit clusters. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
