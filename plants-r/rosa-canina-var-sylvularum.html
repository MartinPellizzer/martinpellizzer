Rosa canina var. sylvularum

Rosa canina var. sylvularum, commonly known as the dog rose or wild rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnate leaves, and fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces bright red hips, which are the fruit and are rich in vitamin C. Rosa canina var. sylvularum is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along riverbanks.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
