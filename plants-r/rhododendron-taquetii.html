Rhododendron taquetii

Rhododendron taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Asia, particularly in China and northern Vietnam.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shady, moist environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in cool, humid climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rhododendron taquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
