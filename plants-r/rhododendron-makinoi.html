Rhododendron makinoi

Rhododendron makinoi, commonly known as the Makino's rhododendron, is a species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, with five petals and bloom in early spring.

Rhododendron makinoi thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
