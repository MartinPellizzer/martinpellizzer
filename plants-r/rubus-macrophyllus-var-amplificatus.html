Rubus macrophyllus var. amplificatus

Rubus macrophyllus var. amplificatus, commonly known as the giant evergreen raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces white flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal rainforests.

Due to its size and unique foliage, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
