Rosa glauca subsp. pseudomarsica

Rosa glauca subsp.

pseudomarsica, commonly known as the Sierra Whitebark Rose, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of many rose species.

The plant typically grows in high elevation environments, such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in regions with harsh, arid conditions.
