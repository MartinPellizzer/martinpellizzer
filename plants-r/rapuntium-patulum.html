Rapuntium patulum

Rapuntium patulum, commonly known as the broadleaf cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Rapuntium patulum is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
