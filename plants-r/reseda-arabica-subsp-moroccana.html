Reseda arabica subsp. moroccana

Reseda arabica subsp.

moroccana, commonly known as Moroccan sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
