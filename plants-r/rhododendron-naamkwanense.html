Rhododendron naamkwanense

Rhododendron naamkwanense, commonly known as the Namkwan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to deep red.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
