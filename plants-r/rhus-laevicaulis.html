Rhus laevicaulis

Rhus laevicaulis, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs along the stems, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. Smooth sumac produces red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woods, thickets, and along roadsides.
