Rosa spuria f. genuina

Rosa spuria f. genuina, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in alpine and subalpine environments.
