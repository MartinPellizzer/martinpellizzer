Rosa simplex

Rosa simplex, commonly known as the simple rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, single-layered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant produces fragrant blooms throughout the growing season, usually from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually thorny.

Rosa simplex is native to various regions in Europe and Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
