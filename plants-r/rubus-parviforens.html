Rubus parviforens

Rubus parviforens, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its slender stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupes, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is notable for its edible berries, which are often used in jams and desserts.
