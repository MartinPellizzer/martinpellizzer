Rubus hypoleucus subsp. subinermipes

Rubus hypoleucus subsp.

subinermipes, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thornless stems and small, aggregate fruits.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries, which are usually dark purple to black when mature, are edible and have a tart flavor.
