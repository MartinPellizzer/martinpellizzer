Rhododendron augustinei subsp. rubrum

Rhododendron augustinei subsp.

rubrum, commonly known as the red-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking clusters of tubular, red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
