Rollinia boliviana

Rollinia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It belongs to the family Caricaceae and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange fruits that resemble starfruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit shape.
