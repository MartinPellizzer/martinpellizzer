Reboulea pensylvanica

Reboulea pensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania redbud, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it typically grows to a height of 15 to 30 feet.

Reboulea pensylvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
