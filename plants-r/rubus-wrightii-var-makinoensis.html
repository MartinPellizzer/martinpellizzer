Rubus wrightii var. makinoensis

Rubus wrightii var. makinoensis, commonly known as the Makino's blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant restoration.
