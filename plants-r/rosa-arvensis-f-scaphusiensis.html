Rosa arvensis f. scaphusiensis

Rosa arvensis f. scaphusiensis, commonly known as the scaphus rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink to pale red in color. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and pinnate leaves, typical of the Rosa genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This form is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique floral display.
