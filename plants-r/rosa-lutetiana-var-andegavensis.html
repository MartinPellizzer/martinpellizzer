Rosa lutetiana var. andegavensis

Rosa lutetiana var. andegavensis, commonly known as the Andegavensis rose, is a botanical variety native to the regions of France and Spain.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening and have a long history of cultivation in European horticulture.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
