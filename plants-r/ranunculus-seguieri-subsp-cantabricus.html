Ranunculus seguieri subsp. cantabricus

Ranunculus seguieri subsp.

cantabricus, commonly known as the Cantabrian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central pistil.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
