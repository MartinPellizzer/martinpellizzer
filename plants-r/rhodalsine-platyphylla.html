Rhodalsine platyphylla

Rhodalsine platyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed heath, is a shrub native to the wet tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and inland areas with high humidity. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Rhodalsine platyphylla is also an important food source for native insects and birds.
