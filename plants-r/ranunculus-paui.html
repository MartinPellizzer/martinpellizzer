Ranunculus paui

Ranunculus paui, commonly known as the Paui buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five to ten petals. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy, and it grows in moist, shaded environments. Ranunculus paui is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitat.
