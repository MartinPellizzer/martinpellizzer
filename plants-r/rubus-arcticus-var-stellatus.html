Rubus arcticus var. stellatus

Rubus arcticus var. stellatus, commonly known as the star-former raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that resemble stars, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in cold climates and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in high-altitude ecosystems.
