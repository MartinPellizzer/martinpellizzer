Rosa glauca var. rubens

Rosa glauca var. rubens, commonly known as the red sandalwood rose, is a variant of the glaucous rose.

It is characterized by its striking red to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Russia. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving them a bluish-gray appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
