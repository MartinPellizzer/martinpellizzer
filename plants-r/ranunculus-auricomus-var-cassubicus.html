Ranunculus auricomus var. cassubicus

Ranunculus auricomus var. cassubicus, commonly known as the Cassubicus buttercup, is a variant of the meadow buttercup.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often adorned with a distinctive white or greenish central marking. Its leaves are palmately divided, typically with three to five lobes, and are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
