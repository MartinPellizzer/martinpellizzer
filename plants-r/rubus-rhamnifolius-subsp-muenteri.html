Rubus rhamnifolius subsp. muenteri

Rubus rhamnifolius subsp.

muenteri, commonly known as Muenter's bramble, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are aggregate berries that ripen in the summer and are often consumed by wildlife.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
