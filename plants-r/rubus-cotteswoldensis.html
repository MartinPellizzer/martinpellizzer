Rubus cotteswoldensis

Rubus cotteswoldensis, commonly known as the Cotteswold bramble, is a rare species of flowering plant native to the UK.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in limestone soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrub habitats.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species and is protected in certain areas.
