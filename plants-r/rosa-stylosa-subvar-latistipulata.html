Rosa stylosa subvar. latistipulata

Rosa stylosa subvar. latistipulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed shrub rose, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are broad and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
