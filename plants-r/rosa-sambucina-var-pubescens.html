Rosa sambucina var. pubescens

Rosa sambucina var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent rose or the velvet rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, slightly hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring. Its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rosa sambucina var. pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial sunlight.
