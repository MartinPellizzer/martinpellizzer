Rubus amnicola

Rubus amnicola, commonly known as the river raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces fruit in the summer.

Rubus amnicola plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
