Rubus nemorosus var. hallandicus

Rubus nemorosus var. hallandicus, commonly known as the Halland blackberry, is a variant of the brambles native to Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and aggregate in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

This variety is often found in woodlands and along hedgerows, where it thrives in moist, shaded conditions.
