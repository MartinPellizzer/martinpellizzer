Rubus gilvus

Rubus gilvus, commonly known as the golden bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Rubus gilvus thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and shrublands.
