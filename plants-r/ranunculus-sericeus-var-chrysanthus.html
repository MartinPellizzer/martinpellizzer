Ranunculus sericeus var. chrysanthus

Ranunculus sericeus var. chrysanthus, commonly known as the silverleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. It blooms in early to mid-summer, producing delicate flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
