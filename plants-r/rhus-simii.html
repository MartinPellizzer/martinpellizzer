Rhus simii

Rhus simii, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fuzzy fruit clusters, which are a distinguishing feature. Rhus simii is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
