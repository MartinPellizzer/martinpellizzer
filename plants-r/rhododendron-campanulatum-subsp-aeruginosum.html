Rhododendron campanulatum subsp. aeruginosum

Rhododendron campanulatum subsp.

aeruginosum, commonly known as the blue rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
