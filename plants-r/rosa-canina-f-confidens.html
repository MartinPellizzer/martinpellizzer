Rosa canina f. confidens

Rosa canina f. confidens, commonly known as the shrub rose or dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink or white flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red hips in the autumn. Rosa canina f. confidens is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

It has been historically used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
