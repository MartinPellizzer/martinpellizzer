Rytidosperma linkii

Rytidosperma linkii, commonly known as the desert grass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-blue foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs on the leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in panicle-like inflorescences, typically during the warmer months. Rytidosperma linkii is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soil and low moisture environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for native wildlife.
