Rubus polyacanthus

Rubus polyacanthus, commonly known as the purple-leaf bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces edible berries, though they are often covered in a thick layer of fine hairs. Its vigorous growth habit makes it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential weed in natural areas.

The species is also notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
