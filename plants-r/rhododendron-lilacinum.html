Rhododendron lilacinum

Rhododendron lilacinum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are dark green and elliptical, providing a striking contrast to the pink or lilac-colored blooms.

Rhododendron lilacinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
