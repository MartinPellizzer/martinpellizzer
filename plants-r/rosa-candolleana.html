Rosa candolleana

Rosa candolleana, commonly known as the Candolle rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa candolleana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
