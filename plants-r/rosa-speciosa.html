Rosa speciosa

Rosa speciosa, commonly known as the showy pink rose, is a species of flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or purple hue at the base. Rosa speciosa blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
