Rhododendron kongboense

Rhododendron kongboense, commonly known as the Kongbo rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of China and India.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron kongboense is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
