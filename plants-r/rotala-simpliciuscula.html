Rotala simpliciuscula

Rotala simpliciuscula, commonly known as the small-leaf waterwheel, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow waters and is characterized by its compact, floating or submersed growth habit. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually rounded or ovate in shape. It produces tiny, white flowers that are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Rotala simpliciuscula is often used in aquariums and ponds for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
