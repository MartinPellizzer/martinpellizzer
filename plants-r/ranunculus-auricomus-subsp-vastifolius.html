Ranunculus auricomus subsp. vastifolius

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

vastifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features basal leaves that are broadly heart-shaped with a wavy margin, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petal-like sepals.

The plant produces a cluster of small, yellow stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
