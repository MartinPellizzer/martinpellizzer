Ranunculus bulbosus var. giganteus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. giganteus, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a striking perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features large, showy flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and basal leaves that are palmately lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a wildflower and its use in ecological restoration projects.
