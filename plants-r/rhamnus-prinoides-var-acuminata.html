Rhamnus prinoides var. acuminata

Rhamnus prinoides var. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with acuminate tips. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal interest.
