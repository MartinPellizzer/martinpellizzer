Ruellia lowei

Ruellia lowei, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Ruellia lowei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
