Ranunculus kelchoensis

Ranunculus kelchoensis, commonly known as the Kelcho's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many species of buttercups and water plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers are usually bright yellow with a distinctive central pattern, and it produces small, hairy fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
