Rapanea borgmannii

Rapanea borgmannii, commonly known as the Hawaiian hala tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within mesic forests. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a glossy surface and a distinct midrib. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a small, round drupe.

Rapanea borgmannii plays an important ecological role in Hawaiian ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and cultural significance.
