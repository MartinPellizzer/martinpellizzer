Rubus caesius f. genuinus

Rubus caesius f. genuinus, commonly known as the purple-fruited raspberry, is a botanical variant of the common raspberry.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically smaller than those of the standard Rubus caesius. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or moist soils.

Its fruits are used in culinary applications and are valued for their intense flavor and high antioxidant content.
