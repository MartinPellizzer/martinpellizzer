Rytidosperma buchananii

Rytidosperma buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
