Ranunculus constantinopolitanus var. palaestinus

Ranunculus constantinopolitanus var. palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestinian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations and historical significance in traditional medicine.
