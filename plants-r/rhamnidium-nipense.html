Rhamnidium nipense

Rhamnidium nipense, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange-red aril. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its use in traditional landscaping.
