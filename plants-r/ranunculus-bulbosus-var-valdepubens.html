Ranunculus bulbosus var. valdepubens

Ranunculus bulbosus var. valdepubens, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 13 sepals that resemble petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a hairy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in open woodland or meadow habitats. It produces a bulbous root system that allows it to survive in temperate climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
