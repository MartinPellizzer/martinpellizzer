Rhododendron commonae

Rhododendron commonae, commonly known as the common rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from pink to red.

Rhododendron commonae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
