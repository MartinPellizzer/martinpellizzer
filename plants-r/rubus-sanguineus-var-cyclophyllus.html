Rubus sanguineus var. cyclophyllus

Rubus sanguineus var. cyclophyllus, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in gardens and natural habitats.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable food source for wildlife and a popular ornamental plant.
