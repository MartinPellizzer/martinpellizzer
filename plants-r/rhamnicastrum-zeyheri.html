Rhamnicastrum zeyheri

Rhamnicastrum zeyheri, commonly known as Zeyher's rhamnus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rocky outcrops and sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and adaptability.
