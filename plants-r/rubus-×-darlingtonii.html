Rubus × darlingtonii

Rubus × darlingtonii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species belonging to the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. This plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
