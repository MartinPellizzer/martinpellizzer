Rubus × hispidicaulis

Rubus × hispidicaulis, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
