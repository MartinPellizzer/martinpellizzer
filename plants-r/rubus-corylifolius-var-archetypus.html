Rubus corylifolius var. archetypus

Rubus corylifolius var. archetypus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and produces edible, dark red fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in forested areas or along riverbanks. Its botanical features include thorny stems and a complex inflorescence structure.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its medicinal properties.
