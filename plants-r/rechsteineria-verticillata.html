Rechsteineria verticillata

Rechsteineria verticillata, commonly known as the clustered rechsteineria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale pink flowers. Its botanical characteristics include alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and a fibrous root system.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
