Rhynchospora tenuiflora

Rhynchospora tenuiflora, commonly known as slender-flowered flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the base of the plant. Rhynchospora tenuiflora thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
