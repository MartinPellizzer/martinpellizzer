Ranunculus casaliodes

Ranunculus casaliodes, commonly known as the Casali's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus casaliodes is often found in rocky or calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
