Rubus ulmifolius var. drepanacanthus

Rubus ulmifolius var. drepanacanthus, commonly known as the bramble rose or blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries in the summer. Its botanical features include a woody stem, alternate leaf arrangement, and a system of runners for vegetative propagation.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
