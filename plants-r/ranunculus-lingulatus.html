Ranunculus lingulatus

Ranunculus lingulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy center. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

Ranunculus lingulatus is an herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
