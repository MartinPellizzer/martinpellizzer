Rhododendron serpyllifolium f. seigaiunzen

Rhododendron serpyllifolium f. seigaiunzen, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, dark green foliage that remains attractive throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, adding a delicate floral display. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

The species is noted for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
