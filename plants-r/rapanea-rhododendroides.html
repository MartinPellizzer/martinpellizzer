Rapanea rhododendroides

Rapanea rhododendroides, commonly known as the red-leafed evergreen, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually pink or white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
