Rhynchospora cyperoides var. triquetrifolia

Rhynchospora cyperoides var. triquetrifolia, commonly known as the three-angled flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.

Its morphology and ecological niche make it an important species in wetland conservation efforts.
