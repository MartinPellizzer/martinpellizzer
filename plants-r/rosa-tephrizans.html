Rosa tephrizans

Rosa tephrizans, commonly known as the Tephrizan rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant has narrow, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa tephrizans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
