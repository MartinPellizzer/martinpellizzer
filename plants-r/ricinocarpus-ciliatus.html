Ricinocarpus ciliatus

Ricinocarpus ciliatus, commonly known as the creeping buttonwood, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves with ciliate margins. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits that contain smooth, black seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
