Rhododendron × erythrocalyx subsp. beimaense

Rhododendron × erythrocalyx subsp.

beimaense, commonly known as the Beima rhododendron, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptability and aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
