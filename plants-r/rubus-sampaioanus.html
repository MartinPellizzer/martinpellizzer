Rubus sampaioanus

Rubus sampaioanus, commonly known as the Sampaio's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and tart. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
