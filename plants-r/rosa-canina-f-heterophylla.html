Rosa canina f. heterophylla

Rosa canina f. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable rosehip.

This botanical variant of Rosa canina exhibits distinct leaf morphology, with variations in leaf shape and size compared to the typical form. It is characterized by its clustered, orange-red fruits that mature in late summer. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its unique foliage and fruit make it a notable subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
