Ritterocereus humilis

Ritterocereus humilis, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and produces large, showy white flowers that open at night. These flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract pollinators such as moths.

Ritterocereus humilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
