Rorippa madagascariensis

Rorippa madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. It has a creeping growth habit with runners that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the substrate.

The leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to watercress, making it a potential candidate for culinary use in certain regions.
