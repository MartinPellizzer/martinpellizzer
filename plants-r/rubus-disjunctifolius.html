Rubus disjunctifolius

Rubus disjunctifolius, commonly known as the disjointed leaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, irregularly shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
