Rubus idaeus subsp. sachalinensis

Rubus idaeus subsp.

sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin raspberry, is a subspecies of the red raspberry native to the Far East of Russia and parts of China. It is characterized by its erect or decumbent growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest edges and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in local culinary traditions.
