Ruellia rivularis

Ruellia rivularis, commonly known as the stream ruellia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes, usually blue or purple in color, and are arranged in clusters.

Ruellia rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
