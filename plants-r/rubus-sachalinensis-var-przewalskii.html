Rubus sachalinensis var. przewalskii

Rubus sachalinensis var. przewalskii, commonly known as the Sakhalin raspberry, is a shrub native to the Far East of Russia and parts of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
