Rebutia violaciflora

Rebutia violaciflora, commonly known as the violet-flowered rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in the spring. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Rebutia violaciflora is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
