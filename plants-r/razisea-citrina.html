Razisea citrina

Razisea citrina, commonly known as the yellow razisea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a glossy green surface.

Razisea citrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in horticultural displays.
