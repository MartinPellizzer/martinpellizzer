Ranunculus celebicus

Ranunculus celebicus, commonly known as the Celebes buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Sulawesi.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually bright yellow with a distinctive central pattern, and it has palmate leaves that are deeply divided.

Ranunculus celebicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
