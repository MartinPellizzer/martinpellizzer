Rosa glauca var. glandulosa

Rosa glauca var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular blue rose, is a variant of the blue rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bluish-purple petals, which are often described as having a metallic sheen under sunlight. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, with a glandular texture that gives it its specific variety name.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique color and fragrance.
