Rubus pycnostylus

Rubus pycnostylus, commonly known as the dense-styled blackberry, is a species of shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is edible, though it has a tart flavor and is sometimes used in local cuisine or for making jams.
