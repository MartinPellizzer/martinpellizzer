Ranunculus kayseri

Ranunculus kayseri, commonly known as the Turkish buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with 5 to 15 sepals that are often notched or lobed. The plant has a short, stout stem that is hairy and grows from a rhizomatous root system. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

Ranunculus kayseri is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
