Rosa coriifolia var. glaucescens

Rosa coriifolia var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of single, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, red-orange hips that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
