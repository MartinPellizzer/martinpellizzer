Ranunculus constantinopolitanus f. farsicus

Ranunculus constantinopolitanus f. farsicus, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) native to regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a dark center. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with lobed segments, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
