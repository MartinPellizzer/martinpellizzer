Rubus oblongatus

Rubus oblongatus, commonly known as the oblong raspberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that are usually trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
