Rosa platyphylla

Rosa platyphylla, commonly known as the flatleaf rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa platyphylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
