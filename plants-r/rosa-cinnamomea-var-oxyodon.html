Rosa cinnamomea var. oxyodon

Rosa cinnamomea var. oxyodon, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a shrub-like perennial native to the Himalayas.

It features fragrant, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
