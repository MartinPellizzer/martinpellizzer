Rubus cuspidiferus subsp. lepidus

Rubus cuspidiferus subsp.

lepidus, commonly known as the prickly pear raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten fresh or used in jams. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
