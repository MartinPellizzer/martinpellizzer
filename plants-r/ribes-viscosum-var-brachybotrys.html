Ribes viscosum var. brachybotrys

Ribes viscosum var. brachybotrys, commonly known as sticky ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support local wildlife.
