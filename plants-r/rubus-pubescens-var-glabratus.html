Rubus pubescens var. glabratus

Rubus pubescens var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of North America. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing multiple drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
