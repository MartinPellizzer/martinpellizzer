Ranunculus auricomus subsp. anisophyllus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

anisophyllus, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive shield-shaped center, and its leaves are divided into lobes with a distinctive anisophyllous (asymmetrical) structure.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has a short, hairy stem and produces small, greenish-yellow sepals beneath the showy petals.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
