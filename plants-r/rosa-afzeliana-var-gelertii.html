Rosa afzeliana var. gelertii

Rosa afzeliana var. gelertii, commonly known as the Welsh rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Wales and western England.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fragrance is subtle and often described as spicy or slightly honeyed.

Rosa afzeliana var. gelertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
