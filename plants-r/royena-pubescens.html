Royena pubescens

Royena pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Royena pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
