Rosa × involuta var. wirtgenii

Rosa × involuta var. wirtgenii, commonly known as the Wirtgenii rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from Germany.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thornless stems. The plant produces abundant clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, providing a lush backdrop to the blooms.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and disease resistance.
