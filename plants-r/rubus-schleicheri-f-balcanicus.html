Rubus schleicheri f. balcanicus

Rubus schleicheri f. balcanicus, commonly known as the Balkan blackberry, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and rocky soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them useful in jams and culinary applications.
