Rubus rudis subsp. brevithyrsus

Rubus rudis subsp.

brevithyrsus, commonly known as the short-stalked blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
