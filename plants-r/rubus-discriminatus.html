Rubus discriminatus

Rubus discriminatus, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

Rubus discriminatus is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.
