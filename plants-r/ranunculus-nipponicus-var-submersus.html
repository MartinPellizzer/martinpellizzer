Ranunculus nipponicus var. submersus

Ranunculus nipponicus var. submersus, commonly known as the Japanese water buttercup, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Japan.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, with its bright yellow flowers often floating on the water surface. The plant has finely divided, arrow-shaped leaves that help it thrive in aquatic environments. Its flowers are usually short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to wetland habitats and its ornamental value in water gardens.
