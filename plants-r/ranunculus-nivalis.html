Ranunculus nivalis

Ranunculus nivalis, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem that supports two to four leaves, which are deeply lobed and arranged alternately. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with three sepals and three petals.

Ranunculus nivalis thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found growing in rocky or forested areas at high elevations.
