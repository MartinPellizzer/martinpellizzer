Rubus glottocalyx

Rubus glottocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctively narrow shape with serrated edges.

Rubus glottocalyx thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and thickets.
