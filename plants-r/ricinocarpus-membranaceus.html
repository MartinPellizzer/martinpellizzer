Ricinocarpus membranaceus

Ricinocarpus membranaceus, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant thrives in saline or brackish environments, making it well adapted to coastal and estuarine habitats. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

Ricinocarpus membranaceus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
