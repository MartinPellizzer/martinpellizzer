Relbunium hirtum subsp. camporum

Relbunium hirtum subsp.

camporum, commonly known as the Camp's hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. Its flowers are bell-shaped, typically blue or violet, and bloom in summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
