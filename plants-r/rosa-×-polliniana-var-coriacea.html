Rosa × polliniana var. coriacea

Rosa × polliniana var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed hybrid rose, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, rough-textured stems and dark green, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
