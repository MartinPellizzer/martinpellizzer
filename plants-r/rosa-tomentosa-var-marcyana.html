Rosa tomentosa var. marcyana

Rosa tomentosa var. marcyana, commonly known as the Marcy's fuzzy rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its stems are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, contributing to its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
