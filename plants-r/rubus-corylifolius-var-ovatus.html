Rubus corylifolius var. ovatus

Rubus corylifolius var. ovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are white and are followed by the edible fruits, which are often used in culinary applications.

The botanical features of this plant make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
