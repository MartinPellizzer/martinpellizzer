Rubus scissus

Rubus scissus, commonly known as the cutleaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Rubus scissus is valued for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional herbal medicine.
