Rhododendron eudoxum var. brunneifolium

Rhododendron eudoxum var. brunneifolium, commonly known as the brown-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark brown to reddish-brown leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale pink to white flowers during the spring. It thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils and requires partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
