Ranunculus bullatus var. grandiflorus

Ranunculus bullatus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a distinctive dark center. The plant has trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
