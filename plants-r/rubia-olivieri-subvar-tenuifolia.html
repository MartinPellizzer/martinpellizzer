Rubia olivieri subvar. tenuifolia

Rubia olivieri subvar. tenuifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale yellow to greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
