Rhododendron lapponicum var. roseum

Rhododendron lapponicum var. roseum, commonly known as the pink Lapland rhododendron, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It features dense, leathery foliage with a distinctive pinkish-purple hue, and its flowers are typically small, bell-shaped, and pink or white in color. This variety thrives in harsh, cold climates and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats. It is characterized by its slow growth and ability to form thickets, contributing to soil stabilization in its native environment.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its ecosystem.
