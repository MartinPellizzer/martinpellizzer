Rhododendron ochraceum var. brevicarpum

Rhododendron ochraceum var. brevicarpum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are elliptical with a leathery texture, and the flowers have five petals with a distinctive yellowish-orange hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
