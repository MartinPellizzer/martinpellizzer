Rubus ulmifolius var. microtrichus

Rubus ulmifolius var. microtrichus, commonly known as the small-haired blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
