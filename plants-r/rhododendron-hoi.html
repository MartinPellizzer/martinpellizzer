Rhododendron hoi

Rhododendron hoi, commonly known as the Hoi rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, humid environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Rhododendron hoi is also known for its ability to form extensive thickets, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
