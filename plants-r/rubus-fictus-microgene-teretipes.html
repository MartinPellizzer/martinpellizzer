Rubus fictus microgene teretipes

Rubus fictus microgene teretipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are white with five petals and appear in the spring before the leaves fully develop.
