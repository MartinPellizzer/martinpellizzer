Rubus polyanthemus var. discifer

Rubus polyanthemus var. discifer, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, aggregate fruits in the summer. Its fruits are typically sweet and aromatic, often used in jams and desserts.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
