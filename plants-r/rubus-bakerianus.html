Rubus bakerianus

Rubus bakerianus, commonly known as the Baker's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus bakerianus is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
