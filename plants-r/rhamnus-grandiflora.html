Rhamnus grandiflora

Rhamnus grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with smooth margins. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark, egg-shaped berries.

Rhamnus grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
