Rosa × dumetorum subvar. vaulxiana

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. vaulxiana, commonly known as the Vaulxian rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and it produces rose hips that are orange-red in color.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
