Rubus distractiformis

Rubus distractiformis, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark purple fruits. The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and pies.
