Ribes albicans

Ribes albicans, commonly known as white currant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, alternate leaves, and clusters of small berries that ripen to a pale yellow or red color. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Ribes albicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
