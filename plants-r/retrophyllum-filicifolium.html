Retrophyllum filicifolium

Retrophyllum filicifolium, commonly known as the fern-like evergreen, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate foliage.

It features compound leaves that resemble those of ferns, with a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to anchor firmly in rich, organic soil.

Retrophyllum filicifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
