Rhynchostylis gigantea subsp. gigantea

Rhynchostylis gigantea subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant rhynchostylis, is a striking orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 cm in diameter, typically in shades of white and yellow.

The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and often covered in a dense, hairy coating.

This species thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
