Rudbeckia hirta var. serotina

Rudbeckia hirta var. serotina, commonly known as the late flowering coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in prairies, open woods, and along roadsides. The plant features large, showy yellow-orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk of dark brown to black tubular flowers. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, often with a grayish-green hue.

This variety is known for its late blooming period, usually from late summer into early fall, making it a valuable addition to pollinator gardens.
