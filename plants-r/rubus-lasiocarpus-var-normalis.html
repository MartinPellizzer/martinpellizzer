Rubus lasiocarpus var. normalis

Rubus lasiocarpus var. normalis, commonly known as the hairy-fruit blackberry.

It is a shrub native to North America, characterized by its prickly stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.
