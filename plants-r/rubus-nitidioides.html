Rubus nitidioides

Rubus nitidioides, commonly known as the glossy bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in regions of Georgia, Florida, and Alabama. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The glossy bramble plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
