Rubus menkei subsp. henriquesii

Rubus menkei subsp.

henriquesii, commonly known as the Henriques' blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its prickly stems and dense foliage, with compound leaves that have serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically clustered together. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
