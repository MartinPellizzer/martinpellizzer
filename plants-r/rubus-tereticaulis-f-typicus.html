Rubus tereticaulis f. typicus

Rubus tereticaulis f. typicus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thin, thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental and culinary potential.
