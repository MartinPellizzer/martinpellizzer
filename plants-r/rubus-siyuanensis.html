Rubus siyuanensis

Rubus siyuanensis, commonly known as the Siyuan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries, raspberries, and dewberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
