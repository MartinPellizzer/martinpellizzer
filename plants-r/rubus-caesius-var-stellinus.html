Rubus caesius var. stellinus

Rubus caesius var. stellinus, commonly known as the starred blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped bracts that surround the flowers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
