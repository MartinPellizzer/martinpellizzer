Ranunculus singularis

Ranunculus singularis, commonly known as the singular buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to nine petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Ranunculus singularis is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
