Rubus melanoxylon subsp. exilis

Rubus melanoxylon subsp.

exilis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark purple to black berries.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
