Rosa cinnamomea var. soongarica

Rosa cinnamomea var. soongarica, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon rose, is a deciduous shrub native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a velvety texture and emits a distinct cinnamon-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
