Rosa calvatostyla

Rosa calvatostyla, commonly known as the velvet rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and elliptical leaves. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and are also cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Rosa calvatostyla is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
