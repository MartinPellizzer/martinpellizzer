Rubus pervagus microgene subglaucus

Rubus pervagus microgene subglaucus, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and dark purple to black berries that mature in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, contributing to its ornamental value in garden settings.
