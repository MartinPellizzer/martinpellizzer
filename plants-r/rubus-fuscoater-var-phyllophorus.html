Rubus fuscoater var. phyllophorus

Rubus fuscoater var. phyllophorus, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry-like shrub, is a species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
