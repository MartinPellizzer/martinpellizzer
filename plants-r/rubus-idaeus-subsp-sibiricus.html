Rubus idaeus subsp. sibiricus

Rubus idaeus subsp.

sibiricus, commonly known as the Siberian raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It produces small, red berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its berries are used in culinary applications and are valued for their tart flavor.
