Rubus foliosus f. hispidus

Rubus foliosus f. hispidus, commonly known as the hispid bramble, is a shrub-like plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its hairy texture, especially on the stems and leaves, is a distinguishing feature of this particular form.
