Rubus × villosulus

Rubus × villosulus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a hybrid plant species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
