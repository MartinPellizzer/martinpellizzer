Rhamnus heterogenea

Rhamnus heterogenea, commonly known as the variable buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange to red berries. These berries are a food source for various birds and small mammals.

Rhamnus heterogenea is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.
