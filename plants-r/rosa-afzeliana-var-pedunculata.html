Rosa afzeliana var. pedunculata

Rosa afzeliana var. pedunculata, commonly known as the pedunculate rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features upright, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a hairy rose hip that contains seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
