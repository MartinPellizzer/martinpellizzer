Rubus × latifrondosus

Rubus × latifrondosus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
