Rosa longicruris var. godroniana

Rosa longicruris var. godroniana, commonly known as Godron's climbing rose, is a vigorous, thorny shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features long, arching stems that can climb to significant heights, often reaching several meters in length. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
