Rhaphiolepis petiolata

Rhaphiolepis petiolata, commonly known as the Japanese rose or rosebay, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
