Rhododendron mucronulatum var. latifolium

Rhododendron mucronulatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf alpine azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in subalpine and alpine habitats, where it forms dense thickets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
