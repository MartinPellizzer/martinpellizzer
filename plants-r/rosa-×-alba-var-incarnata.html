Rosa × alba var. incarnata

Rosa × alba var. incarnata, commonly known as the pink musk rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa alba and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
