Rhamnus polymorpha var. glabra

Rhamnus polymorpha var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptical in shape. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark, egg-shaped berries.

The species is valued for its ecological role in providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
