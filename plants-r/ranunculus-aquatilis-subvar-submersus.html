Ranunculus aquatilis subvar. submersus

Ranunculus aquatilis subvar. submersus, commonly known as the submerged water crowfoot, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow waters, with its leaves fully submerged and often forming dense underwater vegetation. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that help it absorb light for photosynthesis in low-light conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that emerge above the water surface, though they are less prominent than the foliage.

This subspecies is important for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
