Rosa obtusifolia var. simionescui

Rosa obtusifolia var. simionescui, commonly known as the Moldavian rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Romania and the Balkans.

It features dense, thornless stems and small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers with a soft, velvety texture, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure, hairy foliage, and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional gardens.
