Rubus weeberi

Rubus weeberi, commonly known as the Weeber's blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and streamside habitats.
