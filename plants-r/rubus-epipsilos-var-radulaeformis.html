Rubus epipsilos var. radulaeformis

Rubus epipsilos var. radulaeformis, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

This variant is a shrub native to parts of Asia, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thornless stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
