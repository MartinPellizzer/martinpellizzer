Rhus chiangii

Rhus chiangii, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like poison ivy and cashew. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in pairs along the stems. Rhus chiangii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
