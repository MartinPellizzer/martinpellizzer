Rubus dentosus

Rubus dentosus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges that give the plant its common name.
