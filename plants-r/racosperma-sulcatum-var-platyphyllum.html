Racosperma sulcatum var. platyphyllum

Racosperma sulcatum var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leafed wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, flat leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a woody structure with a distinctive, ridged stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting native wildlife.
