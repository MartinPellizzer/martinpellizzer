Rubus × nacophyllus

Rubus × nacophyllus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the western United States and parts of Canada, thriving in coastal and temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small, dark-colored drupes. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and requires full to partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
