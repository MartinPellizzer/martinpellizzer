Rosa godetae

Rosa godetae, commonly known as the Godet's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tien Shan and Pamir mountain ranges. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are elliptical in shape.

Rosa godetae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
