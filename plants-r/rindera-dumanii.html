Rindera dumanii

Rindera dumanii, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to minimize water loss.

Rindera dumanii plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
