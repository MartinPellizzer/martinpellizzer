Rubus × vulnerificoides

Rubus × vulnerificoides, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically serrated and hairy. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, that are dark purple to black in color when mature. These fruits are edible, though they can be quite tart and often require sweetening.

Rubus × vulnerificoides is native to North America and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
