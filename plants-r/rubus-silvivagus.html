Rubus silvivagus

Rubus silvivagus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
