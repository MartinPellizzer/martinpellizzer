Rosa copelandii

Rosa copelandii, commonly known as the Copeland's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces fragrant blooms that appear in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are glossy and dark green. Rosa copelandii is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing addition to any garden.
