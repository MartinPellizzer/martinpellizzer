Rosa rubrifolia var. gaillardii

Rosa rubrifolia var. gaillardii, commonly known as the Gaillard's redleaf rose, is a variant of the redleaf rose species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which becomes more pronounced in autumn, and its clusters of fragrant pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves that turn red or orange in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is valued for its unique foliage color and ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
