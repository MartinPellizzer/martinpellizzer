Rhamnus grisea

Rhamnus grisea, commonly known as gray buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its grayish bark and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by black, egg-shaped berries. Rhamnus grisea is often found in moist, woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its berries are a food source for various birds and small mammals.
