Rhododendron brachycarpum subsp. tigerstedtii

Rhododendron brachycarpum subsp.

tigerstedtii, commonly known as the Siberian tiger rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to colder climates.
