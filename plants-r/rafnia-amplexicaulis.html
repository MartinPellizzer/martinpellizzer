Rafnia amplexicaulis

Rafnia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that wrap around its stems, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. Rafnia amplexicaulis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
