Rubus oreogeton subsp. polycarpus

Rubus oreogeton subsp.

polycarpus, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline environments.

This subspecies is often found in coastal dunes and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dune systems.
