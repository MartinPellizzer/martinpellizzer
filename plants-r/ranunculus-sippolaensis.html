Ranunculus sippolaensis

Ranunculus sippolaensis, commonly known as the Sippola buttercup, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes various species of buttercups and crowfoots. The plant is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals and a hairy stem. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
