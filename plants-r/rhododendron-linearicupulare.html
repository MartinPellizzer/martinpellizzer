Rhododendron linearicupulare

Rhododendron linearicupulare, commonly known as the slender-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its botanical features include evergreen foliage and a compact growth habit, making it a distinctive member of the Ericaceae family.

Rhododendron linearicupulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
