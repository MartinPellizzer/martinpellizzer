Rubus ghanakantae

Rubus ghanakantae, commonly known as the Ghana kantae raspberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of West Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
