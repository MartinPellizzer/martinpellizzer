Rubus playfairii

Rubus playfairii, commonly known as the Playfair's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus playfairii is native to parts of Asia and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
