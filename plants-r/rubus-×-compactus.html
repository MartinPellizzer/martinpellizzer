Rubus × compactus

Rubus × compactus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus. This species is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, thorny canes. The leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the flowers are typically white or pale pink.

The berries are small, dark purple to black, and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and desserts.
