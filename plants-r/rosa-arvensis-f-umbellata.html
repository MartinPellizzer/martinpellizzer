Rosa arvensis f. umbellata

Rosa arvensis f. umbellata, commonly known as the field rose or wild rose, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a subspecies of Rosa arvensis, characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers, typically arranged in umbels, giving it its specific epithet "umbellata.". It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, meadows, and waste areas.

The rose hips, which develop after flowering, are an important food source for various wildlife species.
