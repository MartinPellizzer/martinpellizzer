Rosa × reversa var. leiocarpa

Rosa × reversa var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white everblooming rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its semi-evergreen foliage and clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces oval, yellowish-green fruits known as hip fruits, which contain seeds. It is native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region, and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom repeatedly, making it a popular choice in gardens.
