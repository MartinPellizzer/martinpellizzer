Rosa marsica

Rosa marsica, commonly known as the Marsican rose, is a species native to the Apennine Mountains of central Italy.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange-red hips. Rosa marsica is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical significance in local horticulture.
