Rosa indica lawrenciana

Rosa indica lawrenciana, commonly known as the Lawrence rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
