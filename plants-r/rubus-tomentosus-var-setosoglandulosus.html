Rubus tomentosus var. setosoglandulosus

Rubus tomentosus var. setosoglandulosus, commonly known as the hairy-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dense, woolly pubescence and glandular hairs.

It is a shrub native to North America, typically found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. Its leaves are usually broad, ovate, and covered in a thick layer of fine, white to grayish hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
