Rubus breviflorus

Rubus breviflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
