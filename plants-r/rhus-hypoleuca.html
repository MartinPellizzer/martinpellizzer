Rhus hypoleuca

Rhus hypoleuca, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into clusters of red, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands, fields, and along roadsides.

Rhus hypoleuca is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
