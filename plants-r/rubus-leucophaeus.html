Rubus leucophaeus

Rubus leucophaeus, commonly known as the white-leaved bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically three-lobed with a distinctive white underside, giving it its common name. This plant is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, often found in woodlands and shrublands.

It produces small, edible berries that are used in some traditional culinary practices.
