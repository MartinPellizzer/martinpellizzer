Ranunculus krassnovii

Ranunculus krassnovii, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often accompanied by a hairy, yellowish-green stem. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

However, it is also considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid spread.
