Rhododendron aromaticum

Rhododendron aromaticum, commonly known as the fragrant rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron aromaticum is also noted for its strong, aromatic scent, which is more pronounced in certain cultivars.
