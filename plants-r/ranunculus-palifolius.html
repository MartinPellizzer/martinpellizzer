Ranunculus palifolius

Ranunculus palifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broadly ovate to heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Ranunculus palifolius is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
