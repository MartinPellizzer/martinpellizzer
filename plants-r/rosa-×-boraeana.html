Rosa × boraeana

Rosa × boraeana, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, shrub-like form, often growing in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Rosa × boraeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
