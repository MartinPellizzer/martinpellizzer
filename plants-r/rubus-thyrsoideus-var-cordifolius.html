Rubus thyrsoideus var. cordifolius

Rubus thyrsoideus var. cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible blackberries. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It has a woody stem structure with thorny branches typical of the Rubus genus.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
