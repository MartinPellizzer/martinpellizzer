Rhododendron zoelleri

Rhododendron zoelleri, commonly known as the Zoeller's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron zoelleri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
