Rubus maximus f. halophilus

Rubus maximus f. halophilus, commonly known as the saltbush raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its hairy, robust stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, tart berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience in coastal and arid regions.
