Rosa caesia var. obovata

Rosa caesia var. obovata, commonly known as the Obovate Blush Rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa caesia species, native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.

It is also relatively disease-resistant and suitable for both container and in-ground planting.
