Rhododendron ponticum var. myrtifolium

Rhododendron ponticum var. myrtifolium, commonly known as the Myrtle-leaved Rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. Its botanical characteristics include a dense, evergreen habit with thick, leathery foliage.

It is often found in moist, acidic soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
