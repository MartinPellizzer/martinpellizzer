Rubus schleicheri var. largus

Rubus schleicheri var. largus, commonly known as the large-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and the production of medium to large, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
