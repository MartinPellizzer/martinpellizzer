Rubus × esfandiarii

Rubus × esfandiarii, commonly known as the Esfandiari blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and tart. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical features include thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and a high concentration of antioxidants in its fruit.
