Rosa turgida

Rosa turgida, commonly known as the swamp rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered in fine, silvery hairs.

Rosa turgida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
