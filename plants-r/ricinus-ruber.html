Ricinus ruber

Ricinus ruber, commonly known as the crimson castor, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers and hairy leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
