Rottboellia husainii

Rottboellia husainii, commonly known as the Indian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Rottboellia husainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
