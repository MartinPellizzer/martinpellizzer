Rubus dumetorum f. ferox

Rubus dumetorum f. ferox, commonly known as the aggressive blackberry bush, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries that grow in clusters. The plant has a robust growth habit, often spreading aggressively through vegetative reproduction. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems if left unchecked.
