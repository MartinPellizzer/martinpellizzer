Rumex biformis

Rumex biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and a distinctive two-lobed base. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Rumex biformis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
