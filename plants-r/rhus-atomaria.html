Rhus atomaria

Rhus atomaria, commonly known as staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 11 pairs of leaflets, and its distinctive red, fuzzy fruits that mature in the fall. The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands or along roadsides. Its bark has a rugged, scaly appearance, giving it the common name "staghorn.".

The plant is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
