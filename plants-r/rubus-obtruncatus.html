Rubus obtruncatus

Rubus obtruncatus, commonly known as the roughleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus obtruncatus is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along streambanks.
