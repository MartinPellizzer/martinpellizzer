Rigiolepis adenopoda

Rigiolepis adenopoda, commonly known as the hairy leaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
