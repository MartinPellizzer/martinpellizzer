Rubus mundii

Rubus mundii, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rubiaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a subject of both ecological and culinary interest.
