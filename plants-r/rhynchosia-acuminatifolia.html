Rhynchosia acuminatifolia

Rhynchosia acuminatifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved rhynchosia, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with acuminate tips, giving it its common name, and produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Rhynchosia acuminatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
