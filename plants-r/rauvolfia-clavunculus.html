Rauvolfia clavunculus

Rauvolfia clavunculus, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique tuberous roots, which are the primary source of its medicinal compounds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it valuable in traditional medicine and pharmacology.
