Rosa trachyphylla var. campestris

Rosa trachyphylla var. campestris, commonly known as the roughleaf dogwood rose, is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America.

It features dark green, rough-textured leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and wildlife attraction.
