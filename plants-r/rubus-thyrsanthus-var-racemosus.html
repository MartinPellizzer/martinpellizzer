Rubus thyrsanthus var. racemosus

Rubus thyrsanthus var. racemosus, commonly known as the racemose blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
