Rorippa aurea

Rorippa aurea, commonly known as yellow watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright green leaves that form a rosette near the water's surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically on slender, branching stems. It thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows.

Rorippa aurea is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like cabbage and mustard.
