Rosa likana

Rosa likana, commonly known as the Likana rose, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has finely serrated leaves and produces small, red berries that contain seeds. Rosa likana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to both gardens and natural ecosystems.
