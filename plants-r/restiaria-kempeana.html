Restiaria kempeana

Restiaria kempeana, commonly known as the Kempe's restia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restiaceae family and is characterized by its tufted, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in dense stands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
