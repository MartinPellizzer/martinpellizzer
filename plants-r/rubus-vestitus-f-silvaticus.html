Rubus vestitus f. silvaticus

Rubus vestitus f. silvaticus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry shrub, is a wild variant of the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to forest understory conditions.
