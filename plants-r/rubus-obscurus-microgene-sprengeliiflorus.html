Rubus obscurus microgene sprengeliiflorus

Rubus obscurus microgene sprengeliiflorus, commonly known as the Sprengeliiflorus blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
