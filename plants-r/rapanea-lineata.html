Rapanea lineata

Rapanea lineata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, narrow, dark green leaves with a glossy appearance and prominent venation. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its berries are orange-red and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Rapanea lineata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
