Rubus montanus var. macromontanus

Rubus montanus var. macromontanus, commonly known as the large-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically larger than those of the standard Rubus montanus. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
