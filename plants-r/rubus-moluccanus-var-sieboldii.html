Rubus moluccanus var. sieboldii

Rubus moluccanus var. sieboldii, commonly known as the Siebold's blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
