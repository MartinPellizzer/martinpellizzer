Rhynchospora macrostachya

Rhynchospora macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stalked spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that bears numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in wet environments.

Rhynchospora macrostachya plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
