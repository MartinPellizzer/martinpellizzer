Rosa × dumetorum var. collina

Rosa × dumetorum var. collina, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces edible hips that are rich in vitamin C. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
