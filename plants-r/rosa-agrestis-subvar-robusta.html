Rosa agrestis subvar. robusta

Rosa agrestis subvar. robusta, commonly known as the robust wild rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features thorny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink to red, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

The hips, or fruit, are orange-red and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.
