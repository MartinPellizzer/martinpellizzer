Rubus thyrsoideus var. floreroseo

Rubus thyrsoideus var. floreroseo, commonly known as the rose-flowered raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the presence of compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though some varieties may exhibit a pinkish hue. Its flowers are pink to rose-colored, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variant is often cultivated for both its edible fruits and its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
