Rosa × andegavensis var. agrestina

Rosa × andegavensis var. agrestina, commonly known as the field rose, is a hybrid species native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant often found in naturalized areas and along roadsides.
