Rosa quasi-incana

Rosa quasi-incana, commonly known as the pale pink shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is dark green and somewhat hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rosa quasi-incana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
