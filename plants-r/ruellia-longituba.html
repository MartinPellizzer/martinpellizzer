Ruellia longituba

Ruellia longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Ruellia longituba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and relatively easy care requirements.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
