Ranunculus gayeri

Ranunculus gayeri, commonly known as the Gayer's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Ranunculus gayeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
