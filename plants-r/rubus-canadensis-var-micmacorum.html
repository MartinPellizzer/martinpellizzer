Rubus canadensis var. micmacorum

Rubus canadensis var. micmacorum, commonly known as Micmac raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use by Indigenous communities.
