Rubus eucalyptus var. etomentosus

Rubus eucalyptus var. etomentosus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically black when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.
