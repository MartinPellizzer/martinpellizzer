Ranunculus pseudolaetus

Ranunculus pseudolaetus, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with a hairy center. The plant typically grows in moist, meadow-like habitats and has palmate, lobed leaves. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
