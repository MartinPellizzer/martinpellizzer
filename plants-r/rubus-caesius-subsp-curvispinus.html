Rubus caesius subsp. curvispinus

Rubus caesius subsp.

curvispinus, commonly known as the redcurrant raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its arching stems and dense clusters of small, red, edible berries.

The plant produces compound leaves with serrated margins and has thorny stems that provide structural support. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The berries are typically harvested in late summer and are used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
