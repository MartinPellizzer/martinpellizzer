Rubus corylifolius var. silvestris

Rubus corylifolius var. silvestris, commonly known as the wild blackberry bush, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, often with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupes that are highly aromatic. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
