Racosperma leucolobium

Racosperma leucolobium, commonly known as the white-lobed acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody structure with slender, often thorn-like stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are often dispersed by animals or wind.
