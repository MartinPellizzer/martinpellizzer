Rhododendron adenopodum

Rhododendron adenopodum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, humid climates. Its leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rhododendron adenopodum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
