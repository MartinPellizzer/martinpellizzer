Rubus nemorosus var. praecox

Rubus nemorosus var. praecox, commonly known as early blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible blackberries in late summer. It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, often forming dense thickets.

Its berries are valued for their flavor and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
