Ranunculus felixii

Ranunculus felixii, commonly known as the Armenian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive central greenish stigma. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus felixii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
