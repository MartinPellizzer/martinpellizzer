Ranunculus colliguensis

Ranunculus colliguensis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed and hairy. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
