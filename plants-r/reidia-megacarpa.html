Reidia megacarpa

Reidia megacarpa, commonly known as the giant seeded orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to store water in arid conditions.

Botanically, it is notable for its specialized floral structures that facilitate pollination by specific insect species.
