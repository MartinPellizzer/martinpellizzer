Ranunculus kemerovensis

Ranunculus kemerovensis, commonly known as the Kemerovskii buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Siberia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
