Rosa fragariiflora

Rosa fragariiflora, commonly known as the wild strawberry rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Rosa fragariiflora thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
