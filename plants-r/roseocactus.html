Roseocactus

Roseocactus, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a genus of cacti native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit, with large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments. Its flowers typically last only one night, adding to its allure and rarity.

Roseocactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming cycle.
