Rubus menkei var. rhodiopetalus

Rubus menkei var. rhodiopetalus, commonly known as the Rhodopetalus raspberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and the production of small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Its flowers are usually pink or white, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
