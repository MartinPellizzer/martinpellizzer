Rosa gallica var. ranunculus

Rosa gallica var. ranunculus, commonly known as the French rose or Gallica rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe.

It features dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in early summer. Its horticultural value lies in its fragrance and ornamental appeal, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
