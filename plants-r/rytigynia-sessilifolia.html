Rytigynia sessilifolia

Rytigynia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf rytigynia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its small, simple leaves that are often sessile, meaning they lack a petiole. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
