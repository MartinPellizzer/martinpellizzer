Ricinus trilobus

Ricinus trilobus, commonly known as the three-lobed castor, is a species of flowering plant in the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its unique, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Ricinus trilobus is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with a woody stem and hairy foliage.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
