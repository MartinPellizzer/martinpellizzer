Rhododendron × clivianum

Rhododendron × clivianum, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form thickets, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Rhododendron × clivianum is also known for its adaptability to various climates, though it requires protection from strong winds and extreme temperatures.
