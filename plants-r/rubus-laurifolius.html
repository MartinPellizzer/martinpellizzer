Rubus laurifolius

Rubus laurifolius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically black when ripe, though they may also be red or purple. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
