Rhododendron beesianum

Rhododendron beesianum, commonly known as the Beesian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained acidic soils. Its floral display, which ranges from pink to deep red, occurs in early to mid-spring.

Rhododendron beesianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
