Rubus singulifolius

Rubus singulifolius, commonly known as the single-leaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its solitary, palmate leaf that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Rosaceae family.
