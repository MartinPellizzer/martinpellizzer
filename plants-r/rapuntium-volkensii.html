Rapuntium volkensii

Rapuntium volkensii, commonly known as the Volken's cabbage, is a perennial herb native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines, and it is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Rapuntium volkensii is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
