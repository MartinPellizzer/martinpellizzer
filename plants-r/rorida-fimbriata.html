Rorida fimbriata

Rorida fimbriata, commonly known as the lace fern, is a delicate and ornamental plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense mats.

The fronds are usually evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in gardens or indoor settings.
