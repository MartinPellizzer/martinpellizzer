Rosa pratorum

Rosa pratorum, commonly known as the garden rose or the pratorum rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

Rosa pratorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens across the world.
