Rosa villosa var. cinerascens

Rosa villosa var. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with a hairy texture, which give it its scientific name "villosa.". The flowers are typically pink to white, with five petals and a strong, pleasant fragrance. This variety is known for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
