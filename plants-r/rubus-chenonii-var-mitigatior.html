Rubus chenonii var. mitigatior

Rubus chenonii var. mitigatior, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are dark purple to black in color and are often used in culinary applications.
