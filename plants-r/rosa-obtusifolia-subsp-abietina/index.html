Rosa Obtusifolia Subsp. Abietina

Rosa obtusifolia subsp. abietina is a unique variant of the rock rose genus known for its distinctive foliage and floral characteristics. This subspecies thrives in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with a slightly waxy texture that helps it retain moisture. The flowers, usually pink or red, bloom in late summer and add vibrant color to its natural habitat. Despite its beauty, this plant remains relatively obscure, making it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
