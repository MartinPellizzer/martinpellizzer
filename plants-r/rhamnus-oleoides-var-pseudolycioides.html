Rhamnus oleoides var. pseudolycioides

Rhamnus oleoides var. pseudolycioides, commonly known as the desert buckthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The plant has smooth, often thornless stems and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its fruits are small, black berries that mature in late summer and are an important food source for local wildlife.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Rhamnus genus and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
