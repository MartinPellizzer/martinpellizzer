Rosa lindebergii

Rosa lindebergii, commonly known as the Lindberg rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the plant is well adapted to high-altitude environments.

Rosa lindebergii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
