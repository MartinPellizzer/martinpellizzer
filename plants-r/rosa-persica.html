Rosa persica

Rosa persica, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fragrant, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

Rosa persica is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical significance in horticulture.
