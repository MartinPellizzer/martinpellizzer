Rubus loxensis f. parvifolius

Rubus loxensis f. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry.

It is a subspecies of the loxensis blackberry, characterized by its smaller, more delicate leaves compared to the typical form. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
