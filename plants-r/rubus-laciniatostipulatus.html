Rubus laciniatostipulatus

Rubus laciniatostipulatus, commonly known as the finely lobed bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with finely serrated margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
