Rhynchospora pruinosa var. randii

Rhynchospora pruinosa var. randii, commonly known as the pruinose flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, leafless stalks. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Rhynchospora pruinosa var. randii plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
