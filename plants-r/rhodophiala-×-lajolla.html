Rhodophiala × lajolla

Rhodophiala × lajolla, commonly known as Lajolla hybrid lily, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhodophiala bifida and Rhodophiala bulbosa.

It is a bulbous plant native to South America, specifically the coastal regions of Chile. The plant features striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive, spurred shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
