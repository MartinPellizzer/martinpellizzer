Rosa × collaris

Rosa × collaris, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa × collaris is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
