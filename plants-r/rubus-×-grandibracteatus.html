Rubus × grandibracteatus

Rubus × grandibracteatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer and early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
