Rubus schmidelyanus var. coarctatus

Rubus schmidelyanus var. coarctatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically consumed by wildlife. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
