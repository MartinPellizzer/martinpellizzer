Ranunculus simensis

Ranunculus simensis, commonly known as the Sichuan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange, with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Ranunculus simensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its potential toxicity.
