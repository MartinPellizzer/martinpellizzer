Ranunculus ficaria f. dentatus

Ranunculus ficaria f. dentatus, commonly known as the toothed buttercup, is a variant of the European buttercup characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins.

This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded woodland areas. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. The foliage is heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is a perennial herb that prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
