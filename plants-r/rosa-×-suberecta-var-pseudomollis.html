Rosa × suberecta var. pseudomollis

Rosa × suberecta var. pseudomollis, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and fragrant flowers.

It typically features upright, thorny stems and produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers with a subtle scent. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

It is commonly used in gardens and as a parent species in rose breeding programs.
