Rhapis aspera

Rhapis aspera, commonly known as the rough bamboo or bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, arching leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by dark blue-black berries. Rhapis aspera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is relatively low maintenance.
