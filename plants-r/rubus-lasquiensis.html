Rubus lasquiensis

Rubus lasquiensis, commonly known as the Andean blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ecological importance and unique flavor, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and food enthusiasts.
