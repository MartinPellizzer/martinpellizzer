Rotala floribunda

Rotala floribunda, commonly known as the water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a semi-aquatic species that thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in wetlands and pond edges. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of blue or purple. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often floating on the water surface.

Rotala floribunda is valued in aquatic gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
