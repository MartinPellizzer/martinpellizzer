Rubus hirtus apiculatus

Rubus hirtus apiculatus, commonly known as the prickly rose or bristly blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
