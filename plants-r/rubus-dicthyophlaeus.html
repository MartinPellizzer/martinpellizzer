Rubus dicthyophlaeus

Rubus dicthyophlaeus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common but often overlooked member of the Rubus genus.
