Rapanea neriifolia

Rapanea neriifolia, commonly known as the black-leafed evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
