Rhexia glabella var. alifanus

Rhexia glabella var. alifanus, commonly known as the Florida stellaria, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
