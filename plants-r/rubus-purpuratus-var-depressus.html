Rubus purpuratus var. depressus

Rubus purpuratus var. depressus, commonly known as the purple raspberry or the low-growing raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
