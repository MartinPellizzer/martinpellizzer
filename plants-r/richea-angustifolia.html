Richea angustifolia

Richea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf richea, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with narrow, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or subalpine regions.

Richea angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
