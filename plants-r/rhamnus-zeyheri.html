Rhamnus zeyheri

Rhamnus zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and pairs of opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark, glossy berries. It is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Rhamnus zeyheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
