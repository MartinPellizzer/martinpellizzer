Rubus × pubescens

Rubus × pubescens, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which consist of small, drupe-like berries. The plant is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white or pale pink.

Rubus × pubescens is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
