Rhododendron pusillum

Rhododendron pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a distinctive elliptical shape. Its flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron pusillum is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial shade to full sun conditions.
