Rosa gebleriana

Rosa gebleriana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Ecuador.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it has thorny stems.

Rosa gebleriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
