Rollinia peruviana

Rollinia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian star apple, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces a sweet, aromatic fruit with a white, creamy pulp surrounded by a hard, woody shell. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

Rollinia peruviana is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
