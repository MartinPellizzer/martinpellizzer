Ranunculus ariodon

Ranunculus ariodon, commonly known as the Armenian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and features delicate, palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus ariodon is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
