Rubus hirtus var. stojanoffii

Rubus hirtus var. stojanoffii, commonly known as Stojanoff's bramble, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
