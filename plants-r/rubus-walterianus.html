Rubus walterianus

Rubus walterianus, commonly known as Walter's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the eastern United States.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is closely related to other members of the Rubus genus.
