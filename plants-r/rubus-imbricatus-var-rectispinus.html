Rubus imbricatus var. rectispinus

Rubus imbricatus var. rectispinus, commonly known as the upright blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, distinguishing it from the more sprawling forms of Rubus species. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has thorny stems that provide structural support.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its edible fruits and ecological role in native habitats.
