Reinhardtia rostrata

Reinhardtia rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a tall, clump-forming plant that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves. Native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
