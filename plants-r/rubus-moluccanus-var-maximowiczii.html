Rubus moluccanus var. maximowiczii

Rubus moluccanus var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated margins and a hairy underside.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
