Ranunculus chinensis var. amuricus

Ranunculus chinensis var. amuricus, commonly known as the Amur buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that are often mistaken for petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.
