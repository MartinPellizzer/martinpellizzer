Rondeletia tomentosa var. parviflora

Rondeletia tomentosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf rondeletia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems and dense foliage.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
