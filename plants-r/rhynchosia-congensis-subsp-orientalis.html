Rhynchosia congensis subsp. orientalis

Rhynchosia congensis subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental cowpea, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes.

The plant bears elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional seeds and is cultivated for both food and forage.
