Rosa × pervirens var. oxyphylla

Rosa × pervirens var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the pink sand rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink flowers.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern California, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double blooms that are typically pink to pale red in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is drought-tolerant and well-suited for xeriscaping, making it a popular choice in arid landscapes.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has a strong, pleasant fragrance, especially when in bloom.
