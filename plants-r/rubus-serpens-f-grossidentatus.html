Rubus serpens f. grossidentatus

Rubus serpens f. grossidentatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry.

This plant is a variant of the common blackberry, characterized by its broader, more rounded leaves compared to the typical form. It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, dark berries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and horticultural use.
