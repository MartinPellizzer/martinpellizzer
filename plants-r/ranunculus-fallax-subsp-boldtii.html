Ranunculus fallax subsp. boldtii

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

boldtii, commonly known as the boldt's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a distinctive, hairy texture. It has a short growing season and is often found in areas with cool, temperate climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
