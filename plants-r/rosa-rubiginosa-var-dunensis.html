Rosa rubiginosa var. dunensis

Rosa rubiginosa var. dunensis, commonly known as the Cyprus rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange, containing seeds inside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
