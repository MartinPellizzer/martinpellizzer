Rubus laetevirens f. typicus

Rubus laetevirens f. typicus, commonly known as the brightgreen bramble, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and trifoliate leaves that are typically bright green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by red, aggregate fruits in the summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
