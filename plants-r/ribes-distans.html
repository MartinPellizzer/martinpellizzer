Ribes distans

Ribes distans, commonly known as the redcurrant gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the genus Ribes in the family Grossulariaceae, which includes other species of currants and gooseberries. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with alternate, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The fruit is a small, rounded berry that turns red when mature, often used in jams and culinary applications.
