Reicheocactus neoreichei

Reicheocactus neoreichei, commonly known as the Neoreichea cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Brazil.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus that typically forms a dense, rounded clump. Its stems are cylindrical and ribbed, often covered in a whitish, powdery coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise desolate appearance.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
