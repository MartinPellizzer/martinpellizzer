Rumex britannica var. borealis

Rumex britannica var. borealis, commonly known as the northern dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along streambanks. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a wavy margin and a prominent midrib, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense, erect clusters. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat ailments such as inflammation and digestive issues.
