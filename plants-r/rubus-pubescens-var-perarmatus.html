Rubus pubescens var. perarmatus

Rubus pubescens var. perarmatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dense pubescence, or hairy covering, on both its stems and leaves. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and highly aromatic. It is native to parts of North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its flavorful fruit and ornamental value.
