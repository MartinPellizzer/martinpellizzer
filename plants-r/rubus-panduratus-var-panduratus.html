Rubus panduratus var. panduratus

Rubus panduratus var. panduratus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This species is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into bright red, hairy berries. The plant is native to regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
