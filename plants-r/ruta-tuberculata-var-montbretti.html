Ruta tuberculata var. montbretti

Ruta tuberculata var. montbretti, commonly known as Montbret's rue, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, pinnate leaves with a strong, bitter scent, and small yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds, typical of the Rutaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
