Rosa dahurica

Rosa dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces red, hairy fruits called hips. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rosa dahurica is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible berries.
