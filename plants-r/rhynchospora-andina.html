Rhynchospora andina

Rhynchospora andina, commonly known as Andean beakgrass, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. Rhynchospora andina thrives in moist, high-altitude environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native range.
