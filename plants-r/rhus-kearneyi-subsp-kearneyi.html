Rhus kearneyi subsp. kearneyi

Rhus kearneyi subsp.

kearneyi, commonly known as the Keane's sumac, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States. It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant has compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are clustered in upright panicles and are typically yellow-green in color.

The fruit is a red, fuzzy drupe that matures in the fall, providing food for wildlife.
