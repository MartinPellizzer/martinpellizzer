Rosa myriantha

Rosa myriantha, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a species native to China and is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and compact growth habit.

It produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming repeatedly throughout the growing season. The plant has glossy, dark green foliage and is well adapted to a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions. Rosa myriantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

It is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
