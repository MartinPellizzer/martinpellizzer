Rhus michauxii

Rhus michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, and it often grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks.

Rhus michauxii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
