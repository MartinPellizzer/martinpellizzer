Ranunculus cusickii

Ranunculus cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often bright-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in spring and early summer.
