Rubus rivularis subsp. leptobelus

Rubus rivularis subsp.

leptobelus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is often found in association with other bramble species.
