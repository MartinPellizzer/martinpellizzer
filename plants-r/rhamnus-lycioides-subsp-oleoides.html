Rhamnus lycioides subsp. oleoides

Rhamnus lycioides subsp.

oleoides, commonly known as the oliveleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
