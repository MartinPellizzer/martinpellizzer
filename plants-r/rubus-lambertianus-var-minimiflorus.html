Rubus lambertianus var. minimiflorus

Rubus lambertianus var. minimiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf flowered blackberry.

This variant is a shrub characterized by its small, delicate flowers and thorny stems. It produces small, dark berries that are typically less flavorful compared to other Rubus species. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and genetic variation within the Rubus genus.
