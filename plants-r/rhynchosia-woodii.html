Rhynchosia woodii

Rhynchosia woodii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods containing numerous small, angular seeds. Rhynchosia woodii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
