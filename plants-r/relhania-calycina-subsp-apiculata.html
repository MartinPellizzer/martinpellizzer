Relhania calycina subsp. apiculata

Relhania calycina subsp.

apiculata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

The subspecies apiculata is distinguished by its sharply pointed leaf tips and slightly different fruit morphology compared to other subspecies.
