Ranunculus auricomus subsp. longimammus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

longimammus, commonly known as the long-horned buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five broad, petal-like sepals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, hairy sepals that give it its common name. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
