Rubia graeca

Rubia graeca, commonly known as Greek madder, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its roots contain a red dye, historically used for coloring textiles and leather.

Rubia graeca thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
