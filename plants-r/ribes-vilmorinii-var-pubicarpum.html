Ribes vilmorinii var. pubicarpum

Ribes vilmorinii var. pubicarpum, commonly known as the pubescent gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Botanically, it is closely related to other species in the Ribes genus, which includes various shrubs valued for their ornamental and edible qualities.
