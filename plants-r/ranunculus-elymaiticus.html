Ranunculus elymaiticus

Ranunculus elymaiticus, commonly known as the Elymian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic species. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, cup-shaped flowers. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.
