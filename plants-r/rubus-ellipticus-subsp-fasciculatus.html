Rubus ellipticus subsp. fasciculatus

Rubus ellipticus subsp.

fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
