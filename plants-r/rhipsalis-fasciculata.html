Rhipsalis fasciculata

Rhipsalis fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered mistletoe cactus, is a species native to South America.

It is a climbing cactus with long, flexible stems that can trail or ascend over other plants. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains seeds covered in a sticky substance.

Rhipsalis fasciculata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
