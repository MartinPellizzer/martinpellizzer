Rapistrum filiforme

Rapistrum filiforme, commonly known as slender rapeseed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, often covered in fine hairs.

Rapistrum filiforme is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
