Rosa kamtchatica

Rosa kamtchatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Russian Far East.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late summer. It is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The botanical features of Rosa kamtchatica make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for gardens in temperate zones.
