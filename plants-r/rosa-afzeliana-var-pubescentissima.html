Rosa afzeliana var. pubescentissima

Rosa afzeliana var. pubescentissima, commonly known as the silky rose, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and oval, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage remains lush throughout the growing season, making it an attractive ornamental plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
