Rosa × quassata

Rosa × quassata, commonly known as the sweet autumn crocus, is a hybrid species of rose native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed pods. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
