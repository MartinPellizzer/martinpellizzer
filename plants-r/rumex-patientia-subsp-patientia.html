Rumex patientia subsp. patientia

Rumex patientia subsp.

patientia, commonly known as the common dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that are borne on erect, branched stems. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in meadows, and near waterways.

The leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cuisines.
