Rubus ellipticus var. ellipticus

Rubus ellipticus var. ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The elliptic blackberry is valued for its edible fruits and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
