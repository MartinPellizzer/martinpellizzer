Rubus bifrons f. duriminius

Rubus bifrons f. duriminius, commonly known as the Duriminius raspberry, is a unique variant of the common raspberry.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, reddish stems. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are white and clustered in small umbels, followed by small, red berries that are less commonly consumed by humans.

This form is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cooler climates.
