Rosa sicula var. veridica

Rosa sicula var. veridica, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a cultivar native to the island of Sicily.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering, with pale pink to white petals that bloom in late spring. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers, and its foliage is dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and hedges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in Mediterranean horticulture.
