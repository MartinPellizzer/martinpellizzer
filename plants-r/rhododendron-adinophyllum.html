Rhododendron adinophyllum

Rhododendron adinophyllum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a species native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron adinophyllum prefers acidic, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
