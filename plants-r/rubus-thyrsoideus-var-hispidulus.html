Rubus thyrsoideus var. hispidulus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. hispidulus, commonly known as the hispid blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in the summer. Its berries are edible but often have a tart flavor, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
