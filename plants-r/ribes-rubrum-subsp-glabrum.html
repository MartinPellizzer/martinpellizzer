Ribes rubrum subsp. glabrum

Ribes rubrum subsp.

glabrum, commonly known as the smooth gooseberry, is a shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value in gardens.
