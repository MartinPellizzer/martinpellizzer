Rosa sempervirens var. glabriflora

Rosa sempervirens var. glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered shrub rose, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, thornless stems that often grow in dense, compact forms. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and leathery, with serrated margins. Its flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals and a subtle fragrance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
