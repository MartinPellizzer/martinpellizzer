Rubus orthopus

Rubus orthopus, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that mature to a deep purple or black color. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

Rubus orthopus is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
