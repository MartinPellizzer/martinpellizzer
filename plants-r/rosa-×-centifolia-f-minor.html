Rosa × centifolia f. minor

Rosa × centifolia f. minor, commonly known as the miniature pink or miniature damask rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is typically smaller than its parent species, Rosa × centifolia. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, often with a white center, and blooms throughout the growing season. It is a popular choice for gardeners due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.

This variety is well-suited for container gardening and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
