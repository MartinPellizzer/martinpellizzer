Rubus aculeolatus var. hylohodogiton

Rubus aculeolatus var. hylohodogiton, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a thorny shrub native to North America.

It produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is characterized by its prickly stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The berries are edible and have been used historically for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
