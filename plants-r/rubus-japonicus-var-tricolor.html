Rubus japonicus var. tricolor

Rubus japonicus var. tricolor, commonly known as the three-color raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with a distinctive red or purple underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that change color from white to red to black as they mature. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fruit.
