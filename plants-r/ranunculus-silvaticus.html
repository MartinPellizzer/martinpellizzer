Ranunculus silvaticus

Ranunculus silvaticus, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and along stream banks, preferring acidic soils. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with palmately divided, bright white, sometimes pinkish, leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are cup-shaped, with five to seven petal-like sepals and numerous yellow stamens, blooming in early spring.

The plant is known for its early emergence and its role in early successional plant communities.
