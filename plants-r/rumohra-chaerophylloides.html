Rumohra chaerophylloides

Rumohra chaerophylloides, commonly known as the New Zealand sword fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to New Zealand.

It is a fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks. The plant has a tall, unbranched trunk with a crown of arching, sword-shaped fronds. Its fronds are dark green and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters, with a leathery texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
