Ranunculus philonotis subsp. trilobus

Ranunculus philonotis subsp.

trilobus, commonly known as the three-lobed crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with three deeply lobed segments and produces showy, yellow or white flowers with five to eight petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and wet meadows. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in temperate climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
