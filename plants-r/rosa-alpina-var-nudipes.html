Rosa alpina var. nudipes

Rosa alpina var. nudipes, commonly known as the alpine rose or mountain rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of the Alps and other mountainous regions.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it is adapted to cold, rocky environments. It produces rose hips that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh alpine conditions.
