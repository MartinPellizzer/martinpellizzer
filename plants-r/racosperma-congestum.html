Racosperma congestum

Racosperma congestum, commonly known as the dense raceme acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in tight, racemose clusters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and it has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark.

Racosperma congestum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
