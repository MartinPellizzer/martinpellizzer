Ranunculus californicus var. canus

Ranunculus californicus var. canus, commonly known as the white beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and leaves that are typically divided into three parts. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers with five petals and a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
