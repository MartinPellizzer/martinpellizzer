Ranunculus auricomus var. glabratus

Ranunculus auricomus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smoothleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five broad, petal-like sepals that resemble true petals. The plant has smooth, hairless leaves that are typically lobed and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and often ornamental member of the Ranunculaceae family.
