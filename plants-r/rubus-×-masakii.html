Rubus × masakii

Rubus × masakii, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and tart. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
