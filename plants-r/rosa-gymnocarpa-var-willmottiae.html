Rosa gymnocarpa var. willmottiae

Rosa gymnocarpa var. willmottiae, commonly known as the Willmott's naked-seed rose, is a rare and unique variant of the naked-seed rose species.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other rose varieties. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five to seven petals and a subtle fragrance. This plant is native to specific regions in China and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
