Rumex mexicanus var. sibiricus

Rumex mexicanus var. sibiricus, commonly known as Siberian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a sour taste due to its high oxalic acid content. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in compact clusters.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb in some regions.
