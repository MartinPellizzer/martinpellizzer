Rubus acanthodermis

Rubus acanthodermis, commonly known as the thorned blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, woodland areas and is often found in the eastern regions of North America.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberry species.
