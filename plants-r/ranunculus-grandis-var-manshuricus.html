Ranunculus grandis var. manshuricus

Ranunculus grandis var. manshuricus, commonly known as the Siberian buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
