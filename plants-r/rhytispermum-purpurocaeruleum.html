Rhytispermum purpurocaeruleum

Rhytispermum purpurocaeruleum, commonly known as the blue-purple rhytispermum, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking blue and purple petals, which are adorned with intricate veining patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the late spring and early summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.
