Rhododendron simulans

Rhododendron simulans, commonly known as the Korean rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for landscaping.

Rhododendron simulans is also known for its ability to tolerate cooler climates and is a popular choice in gardens and natural habitats.
