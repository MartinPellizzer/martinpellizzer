Rubus × leucotrichoides

Rubus × leucotrichoides, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are white and borne in clusters, and the species is noted for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.

The fruit is often used in jams and preserves due to its tart flavor.
