Rubus thyrsanthus var. cyclopetalus

Rubus thyrsanthus var. cyclopetalus, commonly known as the cyclopetal raspberry, is a thorny shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a distinctive, sweet aroma. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The berries are edible and used in local cuisines, though they are not as widely cultivated as other raspberry varieties.
