Rubus Subangulosus

Rubus subangulosus is a lesser-known species of bramble that thrives in the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. This plant is characterized by its sharp thorns and dense, sprawling growth habit. It produces small, edible berries that are often overlooked in favor of more popular fruit varieties. Despite its modest appearance, it plays an important role in local ecosystems. Its unique features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
