Rubus glaucellus var. laxatiflorus

Rubus glaucellus var. laxatiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are aggregate berries, typically red or black when mature, and are edible.

This species is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.
