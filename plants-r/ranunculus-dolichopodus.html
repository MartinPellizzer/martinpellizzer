Ranunculus dolichopodus

Ranunculus dolichopodus, commonly known as the long-stemmed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
