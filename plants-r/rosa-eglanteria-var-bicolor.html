Rosa eglanteria var. bicolor

Rosa eglanteria var. bicolor, commonly known as the bicolor shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive bicolor pattern. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
