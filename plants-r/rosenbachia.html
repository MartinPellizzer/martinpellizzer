Rosenbachia

Rosenbachia, commonly known as the "roses of the desert," is a genus of flowering plants native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

These plants are characterized by their compact growth habit and showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. Rosenbachia species are typically drought-tolerant, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing them to thrive in harsh, dry environments. They prefer well-draining soil and full sun, making them suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Due to their ornamental value and adaptability, Rosenbachia plants are increasingly cultivated in regions with limited water resources.
