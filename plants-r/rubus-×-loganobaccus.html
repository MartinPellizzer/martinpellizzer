Rubus × loganobaccus

Rubus × loganobaccus, commonly known as the Loganberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between the blackberry (Rubus ursinus) and the raspberry (Rubus idaeus).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark red to black in color. These berries are prized for their sweet-tart flavor and are often used in jams, pies, and fresh consumption.

The botanical features of Rubus × loganobaccus make it a popular choice for both commercial cultivation and home gardening.
