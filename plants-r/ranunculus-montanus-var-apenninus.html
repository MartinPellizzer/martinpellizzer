Ranunculus montanus var. apenninus

Ranunculus montanus var. apenninus, commonly known as the Apennine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
