Rhododendron pulleanum var. maiusculum

Rhododendron pulleanum var. maiusculum, commonly known as the large-flowered rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, leathery foliage that is typically dark green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, often with a white throat, during the spring season. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
