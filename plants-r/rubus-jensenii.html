Rubus jensenii

Rubus jensenii, commonly known as Jensen's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are similar to other blackberries.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species.
