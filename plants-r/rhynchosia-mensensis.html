Rhynchosia mensensis

Rhynchosia mensensis, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.

Rhynchosia mensensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
