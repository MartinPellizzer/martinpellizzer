Rosa rubiginosa subvar. isacantha

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. isacantha, commonly known as the Sicilian rose or the shrub rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage, which provide a habitat for various insects and small animals. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
