Ranunculus trichophyllus var. typicus

Ranunculus trichophyllus var. typicus, commonly known as the hairyleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are covered in soft, hairy textures, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
