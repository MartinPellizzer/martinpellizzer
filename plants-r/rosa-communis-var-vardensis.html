Rosa communis var. vardensis

Rosa communis var. vardensis, commonly known as the Vardensis rose, is a variety of the common rose with distinctive botanical features.

It typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The flowers are usually pink to red, with five to seven petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. It produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
