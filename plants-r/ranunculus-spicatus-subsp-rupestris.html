Ranunculus spicatus subsp. rupestris

Ranunculus spicatus subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine environments in Europe. It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on limestone or gravelly substrates. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and it is adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
