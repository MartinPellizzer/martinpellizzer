Rhodiola himalensis subsp. himalensis

Rhodiola himalensis subsp.

himalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

The plant produces small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is well adapted to high-altitude conditions. Rhodiola himalensis subsp.

himalensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
