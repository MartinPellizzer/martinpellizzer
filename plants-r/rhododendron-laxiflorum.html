Rhododendron laxiflorum

Rhododendron laxiflorum, commonly known as the broadflower rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact habit. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron laxiflorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
