Rosa × suberecta var. shoolbredii

Rosa × suberecta var. shoolbredii, commonly known as the Shoolbred's rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.

It features semi-upright to spreading stems, often with a slightly hairy texture, and produces clusters of fragrant, double flowers in shades of pink to red. The foliage is dark green and slightly serrated, providing a lush backdrop to the blooms. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil with partial sunlight.

It is relatively disease-resistant and suitable for both container and in-ground cultivation.
