Rosa graveolens subsp. eriophora

Rosa graveolens subsp.

eriophora, commonly known as the fragrant wild rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, velvety hips. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery highlight its botanical and cultural significance.
