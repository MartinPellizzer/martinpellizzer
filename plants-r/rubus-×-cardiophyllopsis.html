Rubus × cardiophyllopsis

Rubus × cardiophyllopsis, commonly known as the rosehip plant, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces red or orange berries, known as rosehips, which are rich in vitamin C. These berries are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
