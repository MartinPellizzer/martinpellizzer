Rubus hirtus var. emersistylus

Rubus hirtus var. emersistylus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its hairy foliage and the presence of persistent stipules at the base of its leaflets.
