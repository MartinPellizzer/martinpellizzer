Rubus weberianus

Rubus weberianus, commonly known as the Weber's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
