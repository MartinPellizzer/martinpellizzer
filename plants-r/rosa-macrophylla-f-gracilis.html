Rosa macrophylla f. gracilis

Rosa macrophylla f. gracilis, commonly known as the pink tea rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its profuse display of semi-double, pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
