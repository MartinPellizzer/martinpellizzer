Rubus hirtus var. substellatiflorus

Rubus hirtus var. substellatiflorus, commonly known as the star-flowered bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is native to parts of North America and thrives in moist, woodland environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the fruit is a red, edible berry.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
