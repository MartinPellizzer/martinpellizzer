Rubus × neustriacus

Rubus × neustriacus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its growth habit makes it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
