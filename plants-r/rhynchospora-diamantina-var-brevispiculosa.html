Rhynchospora diamantina var. brevispiculosa

Rhynchospora diamantina var. brevispiculosa, commonly known as the short-bristled diamond sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. This species is characterized by its erect, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with three-lobed flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.
