Ranunculus chilensis var. lasius

Ranunculus chilensis var. lasius, commonly known as the Chilean buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmate, lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
