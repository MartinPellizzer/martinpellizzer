Rubus scaberrimus var. flavidifrons

Rubus scaberrimus var. flavidifrons, commonly known as the yellow-fruited bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, though it is less commonly cultivated than other brambles.
