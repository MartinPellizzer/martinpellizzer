Renealmia holdenii

Renealmia holdenii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

Renealmia holdenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses around the world.
