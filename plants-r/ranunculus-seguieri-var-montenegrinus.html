Ranunculus seguieri var. montenegrinus

Ranunculus seguieri var. montenegrinus, commonly known as the Montenegro buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent black veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its short, sturdy stems and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
