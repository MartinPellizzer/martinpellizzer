Ranunculus epacrus

Ranunculus epacrus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of deeply lobed, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central black spot. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

Ranunculus epacrus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high elevation or rocky terrain.
