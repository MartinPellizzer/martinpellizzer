Rubus insulariopsis

Rubus insulariopsis, commonly known as the island raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are usually serrated.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and the plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.
