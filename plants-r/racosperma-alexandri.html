Racosperma alexandri

Racosperma alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter, attracting various pollinators. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Racosperma alexandri is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
