Rhynchospora psilocaryoides

Rhynchospora psilocaryoides, commonly known as the psilocybin-like rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It is native to tropical regions of South America and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.
