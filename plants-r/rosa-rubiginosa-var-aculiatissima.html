Rosa rubiginosa var. aculiatissima

Rosa rubiginosa var. aculiatissima, commonly known as the rough rose or prickly rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry climates.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate regions.
