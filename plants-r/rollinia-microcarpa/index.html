Rollinia Microcarpa

Rollinia microcarpa is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America. It is known for its attractive, waxy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is increasingly being cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs. This plant exemplifies the rich biodiversity of the Amazon rainforest and the need for its protection.
