Rosa sempervirens var. nicaeensis

Rosa sempervirens var. nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean rose, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers throughout the growing season. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and is well adapted to coastal and rocky environments. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

This variety is a popular choice for hedges and borders due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
