Ranunculus sulphureus var. alberti

Ranunculus sulphureus var. alberti, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high altitudes. It is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist environments.

Its flowers bloom in early spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
