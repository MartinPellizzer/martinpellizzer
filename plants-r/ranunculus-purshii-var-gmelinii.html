Ranunculus purshii var. gmelinii

Ranunculus purshii var. gmelinii, commonly known as the Gmelin's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

It is a perennial herb that blooms in the spring and early summer.
