Resinanthus ekmanii

Resinanthus ekmanii, commonly known as the Ekmans' resin tree, is a rare and endemic species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a slender trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces a sticky, aromatic resin that exudes from specialized structures on its bark, which is a key feature in its identification. This resin is not only chemically distinct but also plays a role in the plant's defense mechanisms against herbivores and pathogens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Resinanthus ekmanii is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
