Rhododendron hirsutum f. microphyllum

Rhododendron hirsutum f. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf hairy rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its small, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, depending on the climate. Its foliage remains dense throughout the year, providing ornamental value in gardens.

The floral display, which typically occurs in spring, consists of pink to reddish-purple blooms that attract pollinators.
