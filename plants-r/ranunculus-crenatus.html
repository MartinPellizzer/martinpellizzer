Ranunculus crenatus

Ranunculus crenatus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive serrated edge, and its flowers are typically yellow with 5 to 15 petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. It is characterized by its short, rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites.
