Ranunculus pangiensis

Ranunculus pangiensis, commonly known as the Pangi buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has palmately divided leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with numerous petals and a prominent central stamen.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
