Rubus arizonicus

Rubus arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and small white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them a valuable food source for wildlife and humans alike.
