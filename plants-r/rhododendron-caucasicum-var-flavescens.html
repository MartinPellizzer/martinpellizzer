Rhododendron caucasicum var. flavescens

Rhododendron caucasicum var. flavescens, commonly known as the Caucasian yellow rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slight pubescence on the underside. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange-red, blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
