Rorippa × danubialis

Rorippa × danubialis, commonly known as the Danube watercress, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, edible leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces slender, elongated pods containing seeds.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
