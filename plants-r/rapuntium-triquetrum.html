Rapuntium triquetrum

Rapuntium triquetrum, commonly known as the three-angled cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its seeds are encased in a three-angled pod, giving it its common name.

The species is also known for its peppery taste, making it a potential edible plant in certain regions.
