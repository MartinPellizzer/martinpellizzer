Rubus subobscurus

Rubus subobscurus, commonly known as the dark-fruited blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a slightly serrated edge, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
