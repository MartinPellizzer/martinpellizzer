Rubus albicomus

Rubus albicomus, commonly known as the white-haired raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. The plant is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
