Rosa moschata var. plena

Rosa moschata var. plena, commonly known as the full-flowered musk rose, is a cultivar of the musk rose species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its double blooms, which are typically pink to white in color, and its strong, fragrant scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange hips that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical use in perfumery and medicine.
