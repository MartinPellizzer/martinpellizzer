Rosa × dumetorum f. quadica

Rosa × dumetorum f. quadica, commonly known as the four-leaf rose, is a horticultural form of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thornless stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. Its foliage consists of dark green, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden landscapes.
