Rubus variegatus

Rubus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated blackberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Rubus variegatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.
