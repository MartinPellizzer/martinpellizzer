Rubus melanoxylon var. labastidensis

Rubus melanoxylon var. labastidensis, commonly known as the Labastide blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and arranged in clusters, while the fruits are valued for their flavor and culinary use.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
