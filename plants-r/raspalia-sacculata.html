Raspalia sacculata

Raspalia sacculata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens during that time.
