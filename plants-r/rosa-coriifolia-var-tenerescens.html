Rosa coriifolia var. tenerescens

Rosa coriifolia var. tenerescens, commonly known as the soft-fleshed shrub rose, is a variant of the Chinese rose, characterized by its dense, thornless growth habit.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to white in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, bushy form with dark green, glossy leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety is particularly valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its ability to produce abundant blooms.
