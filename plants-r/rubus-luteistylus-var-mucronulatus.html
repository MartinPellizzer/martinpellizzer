Rubus luteistylus var. mucronulatus

Rubus luteistylus var. mucronulatus, commonly known as the yellow-styled blackberry, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant produces yellow stigmas that are distinctive and contribute to its unique botanical appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The leaves are typically compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant has a creeping growth habit.
