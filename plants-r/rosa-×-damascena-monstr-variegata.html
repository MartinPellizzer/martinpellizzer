Rosa × damascena monstr. variegata

Rosa × damascena monstr.

variegata, commonly known as the variegated damask rose, is a hybrid shrub prized for its ornamental value. It features fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are dark green with white or yellow variegation, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering and pruning to maintain its shape.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance in perfumery and horticulture.
