Ranunculus kemensis

Ranunculus kemensis, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 13 sepals that are often mistaken for petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and trifoliate leaves. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

The flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
