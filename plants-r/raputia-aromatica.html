Raputia aromatica

Raputia aromatica, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or mountainous areas. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, are prized for their delicate aroma and ornamental value.
