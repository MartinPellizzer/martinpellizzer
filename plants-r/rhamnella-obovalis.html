Rhamnella obovalis

Rhamnella obovalis, commonly known as the ovalleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other riparian habitats.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability in its native environment.
