Rubia sikkimensis

Rubia sikkimensis, commonly known as Sikkim blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Rubia sikkimensis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
