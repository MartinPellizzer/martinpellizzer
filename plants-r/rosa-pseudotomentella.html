Rosa pseudotomentella

Rosa pseudotomentella, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan, Pakistan, and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide it with a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically pink to white, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa pseudotomentella is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
