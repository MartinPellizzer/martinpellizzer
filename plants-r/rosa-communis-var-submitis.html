Rosa communis var. submitis

Rosa communis var. submitis, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, arching stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery-gray fuzz, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is well-suited for use in borders, hedges, or as a specimen plant in gardens.
