Rhus bodinieri

Rhus bodinieri, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other species like poison ivy and cashew. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into several leaflets, and its clusters of small, red or orange berries. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
