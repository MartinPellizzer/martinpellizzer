Roegneria grandiglumis

Roegneria grandiglumis, commonly known as the giant bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, bluish-green leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

Roegneria grandiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
