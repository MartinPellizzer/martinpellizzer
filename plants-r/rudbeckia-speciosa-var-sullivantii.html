Rudbeckia speciosa var. sullivantii

Rudbeckia speciosa var. sullivantii, commonly known as the showy coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern and central United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear large, showy yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of dark brown to black tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, rough-textured leaves with prominent veins.

Rudbeckia speciosa var. sullivantii is well adapted to moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.
