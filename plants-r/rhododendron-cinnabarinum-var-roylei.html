Rhododendron cinnabarinum var. roylei

Rhododendron cinnabarinum var. roylei, commonly known as the crimson rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are dark green and elliptical, providing a striking contrast to the showy flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
