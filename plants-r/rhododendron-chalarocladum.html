Rhododendron chalarocladum

Rhododendron chalarocladum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Its botanical features include reddish-brown bark and a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for woodland or shaded garden settings.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
