Rubus mulleri subsp. callayi

Rubus mulleri subsp.

callayi, commonly known as the Callayi blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental and potential culinary value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
