Rhynchospora macrochaeta var. quadrispicata

Rhynchospora macrochaeta var. quadrispicata, commonly known as the four-rayed spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence with four rays, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a prominent midrib.

This variety is typically found in wetland habitats, including marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to wet conditions.
