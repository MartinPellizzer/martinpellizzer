Ribes × fuscescens

Ribes × fuscescens, commonly known as the blackcurrant.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Ribes nigrum (blackcurrant) and Ribes rubrum (redcurrant). This plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically harvested in late summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the stems are hairy and reddish in color.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in antioxidants and used in culinary and medicinal applications.
