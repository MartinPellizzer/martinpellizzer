Rosa gallica var. damascena

Rosa gallica var. damascena, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is well-known for its use in the production of rose oil and perfume due to the high concentration of essential oils in its petals.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
