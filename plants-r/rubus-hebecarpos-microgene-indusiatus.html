Rubus hebecarpos microgene indusiatus

Rubus hebecarpos microgene indusiatus, commonly known as the small-fruited bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This shrub is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
