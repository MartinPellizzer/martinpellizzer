Rhaphidospora glabrituba

Rhaphidospora glabrituba, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed rhaphidospora, is a lesser-known plant species within the Rhaphidosporaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, glabrous stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces distinctive spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are clustered on the undersides of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
