Rosa redutea rufescens

Rosa redutea rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems, which become more pronounced as the plant matures. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Rosa redutea rufescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
