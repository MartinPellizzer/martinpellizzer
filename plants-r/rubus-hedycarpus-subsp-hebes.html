Rubus hedycarpus subsp. hebes

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

hebes, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn deep purple when mature. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it blooms in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its edible berries.
