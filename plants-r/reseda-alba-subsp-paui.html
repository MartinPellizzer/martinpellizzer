Reseda alba subsp. paui

Reseda alba subsp.

paui, commonly known as white mallow or white reseda, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered with fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in dye production.
