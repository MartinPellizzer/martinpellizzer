Ranunculus staubii

Ranunculus staubii, commonly known as the Staub's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow, white, or orange. The plant has palmate leaves divided into three to five lobes, which are hairy and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus staubii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
