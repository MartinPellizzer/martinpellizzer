Rosa × centifolia purpurea

Rosa × centifolia purpurea, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its numerous, fragrant, pink to deep purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth and flower production.

The Damask rose is highly valued for its essential oils and is widely cultivated in perfumery and horticulture.
