Rumex divaricatus var. pulcher

Rumex divaricatus var. pulcher, commonly known as the handsome dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and woodland edges. The plant features upright, ribbed stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive reddish or purple tinge on the underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, terminal spikes, which are often surrounded by broad, leaf-like bracts.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
