Ranunculus albicans

Ranunculus albicans, commonly known as the white crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five to eight petals. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus albicans is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its early spring blooming period.
