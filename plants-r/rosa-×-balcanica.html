Rosa × balcanica

Rosa × balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan rose, is a hybrid species originating from the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, opposite, and pinnately compound with five to seven leaflets.

Rosa × balcanica is valued for ornamental purposes and is also used in the production of rose oil and perfume.
