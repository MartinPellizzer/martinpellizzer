Rosa systyla var. desvauxiana

Rosa systyla var. desvauxiana, commonly known as the Desvaux's shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features dense, arching stems with small, dark green leaves that are serrated along the edges. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to poor, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
