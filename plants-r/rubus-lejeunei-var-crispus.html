Rubus lejeunei var. crispus

Rubus lejeunei var. crispus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a dark purple to black berry.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
