Ranunculus pseudovillarsii

Ranunculus pseudovillarsii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and streambanks, preferring cool, temperate climates. It has palmate leaves divided into five to seven lobes, and its stem is often hairy.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
