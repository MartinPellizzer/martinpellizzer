Rosa abrica

Rosa abrica, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, thornless stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa abrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.
