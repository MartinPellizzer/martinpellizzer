Ranunculus degenii

Ranunculus degenii, commonly known as the Degen's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically yellow or white flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually solitary, with five to eight sepals and numerous stamens, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Ranunculus degenii is adapted to cold, alpine environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
