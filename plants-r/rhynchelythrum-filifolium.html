Rhynchelythrum filifolium

Rhynchelythrum filifolium, commonly known as the threadleaf rhynchelythrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
