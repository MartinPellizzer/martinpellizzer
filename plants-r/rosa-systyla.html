Rosa systyla

Rosa systyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, where it typically grows in woodland edges and rocky soils. The plant features slender stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Rosa systyla is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
