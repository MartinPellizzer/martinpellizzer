Ranunculus sjoersii

Ranunculus sjoersii, commonly known as the Sjoers' buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
