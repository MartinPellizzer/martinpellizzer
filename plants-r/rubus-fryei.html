Rubus fryei

Rubus fryei, commonly known as the Fry's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These fruits are edible and have a tart, sweet flavor.

Rubus fryei thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
