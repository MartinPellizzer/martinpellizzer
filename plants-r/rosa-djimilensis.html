Rosa djimilensis

Rosa djimilensis, commonly known as the Djimilensis rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and is well adapted to harsh, alpine conditions. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa djimilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
