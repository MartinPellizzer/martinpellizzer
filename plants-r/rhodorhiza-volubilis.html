Rhodorhiza volubilis

Rhodorhiza volubilis, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a unique plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with aerial roots that allow it to attach to tree trunks and other vertical surfaces. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Rhodorhiza volubilis thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
