Racosperma falciforme

Racosperma falciforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and small, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular heads, which are typically followed by slender, curved pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.

Racosperma falciforme plays a role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for native pollinators.
