Rhododendron superbum

Rhododendron superbum, commonly known as the Appalachian rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas. The plant features broad, ovate leaves and produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in the spring. It is part of the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and azaleas.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
