Ruellia crucis

Ruellia crucis, commonly known as the cross-leaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive cross-shaped pattern on the underside, giving it its common name.

Ruellia crucis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
