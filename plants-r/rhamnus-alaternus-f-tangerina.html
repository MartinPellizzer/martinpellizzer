Rhamnus alaternus f. tangerina

Rhamnus alaternus f. tangerina, commonly known as the orangeleaf buckthorn, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage.

Its leaves are typically oval to elliptical, with a smooth, glossy surface and a distinct orange or reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
