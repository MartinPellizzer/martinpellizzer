Rangium × mandschuricum

Rangium × mandschuricum, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its attractive blooms and ease of care.
