Rubus villicaulis var. selmeri

Rubus villicaulis var. selmeri, commonly known as Selmer's bramble, is a variant of the bramble species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
