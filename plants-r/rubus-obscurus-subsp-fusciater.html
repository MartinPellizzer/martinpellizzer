Rubus obscurus subsp. fusciater

Rubus obscurus subsp.

fusciater, commonly known as the dusky blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America. It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries and thorny stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are white and clustered in the axils of the leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly broader leaves and darker fruit compared to other Rubus species.
