Rhododendron maculiferum

Rhododendron maculiferum, commonly known as the spotted rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red with distinctive white spots. The plant is evergreen, featuring dense, leathery leaves that provide year-round foliage. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
