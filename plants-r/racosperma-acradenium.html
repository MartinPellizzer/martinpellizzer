Racosperma acradenium

Racosperma acradenium, commonly known as the Narrow-leafed Acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, spherical clusters, and it produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, rectangular seeds.

Racosperma acradenium is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species in its native habitat.
