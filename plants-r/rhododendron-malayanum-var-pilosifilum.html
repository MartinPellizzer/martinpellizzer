Rhododendron malayanum var. pilosifilum

Rhododendron malayanum var. pilosifilum, commonly known as the Malayan silky-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in forest understories, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in spring, and it prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
