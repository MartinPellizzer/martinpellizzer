Rubus thyrsoideus var. oblongatus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. oblongatus, commonly known as the oblongleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus native to eastern North America.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are elliptic to oblong in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its unique leaf morphology and fruit structure.
