Rapuntium bellidifolium

Rapuntium bellidifolium, commonly known as the bellidifolium rapunzel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
