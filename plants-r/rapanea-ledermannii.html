Rapanea ledermannii

Rapanea ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage composed of glossy, dark green, leathery leaves. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
