Rubus bochumensis

Rubus bochumensis, commonly known as the Bochum blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is valued for its edible, dark purple berries, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
