Rubus dumetorum f. macrophilus

Rubus dumetorum f. macrophilus, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its larger, more robust leaves.

It is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three to five leaflets and thorny stems.

This form is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
