Robinia × holdtii britzensis

Robinia × holdtii britzensis, commonly known as the Britzen's false acacia, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with oval to lance-shaped leaflets and produces fragrant, yellow to orange-pink flowers in spring. It is valued for its ornamental value and hardy nature, though it can be invasive in some regions.

The tree's bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive flaky texture.
