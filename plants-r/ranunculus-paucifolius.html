Ranunculus paucifolius

Ranunculus paucifolius, commonly known as the few-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a short, stout stem with few leaves. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

It is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in cool, damp conditions.
