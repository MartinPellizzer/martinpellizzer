Restio villosus

Restio villosus, commonly known as the hairy restio, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
