Rosa dumalis var. salicifolia

Rosa dumalis var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
