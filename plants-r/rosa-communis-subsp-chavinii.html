Rosa communis subsp. chavinii

Rosa communis subsp.

chavinii, commonly known as the Chavinii rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves composed of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a strong, sweet fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
