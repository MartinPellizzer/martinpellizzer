Rhodiola coccinea subsp. scabrida

Rhodiola coccinea subsp.

scabrida, commonly known as the rough-stemmed rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a compact, cushion-like growth habit with dense clusters of small, reddish-pink flowers.

The plant has hairy, scabrous stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cold, dry climates and well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its adaptogenic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
