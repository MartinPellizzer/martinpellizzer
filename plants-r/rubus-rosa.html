Rubus rosa

Rubus rosa, commonly known as the dog rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces red or yellow berries that are edible but often tart. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional herbal use.
