Rosa japonica

Rosa japonica, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a species native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and simple, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, usually blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are composed of five petals and numerous stamens, with a central pistil that is often adorned with a fuzzy style.

Rosa japonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
