Rheum

Rheum, commonly known as rhubarb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stalks and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is known for its edible stems, which are used in cooking. Rheum species are often cultivated for their vibrant, colorful foliage and their ability to thrive in cool climates.

The plant's rhizomes also contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.
