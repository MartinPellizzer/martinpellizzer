Romulea columnae subsp. columnae

Romulea columnae subsp.

columnae, commonly known as the columnar romulea, is a perennial geophyte native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its underground bulb and a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is cultivated in rock gardens and as a specimen plant.
