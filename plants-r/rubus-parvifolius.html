Rubus parvifolius

Rubus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces black, edible berries in the summer. Its flowers are white or pale pink and are borne in clusters.

The plant has a woody stem covered in thorny projections, typical of many Rubus species.
