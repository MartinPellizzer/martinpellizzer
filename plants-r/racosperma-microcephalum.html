Racosperma microcephalum

Racosperma microcephalum, commonly known as the small-headed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, rounded leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
