Rosa × andegavensis f. pseudopsilophylla

Rosa × andegavensis f. pseudopsilophylla, commonly known as the Andegavensis rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its unique foliage and floral features.

It exhibits a distinct leaf morphology with a broader, more leathery texture compared to typical roses, giving it a more rugged appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, often with a rich, velvety texture. It is typically shrub-like in growth habit, with multiple stems and a compact form.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
