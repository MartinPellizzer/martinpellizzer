Rubus stereobelus

Rubus stereobelus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cold climates and well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine due to their tart flavor and nutritional value.
