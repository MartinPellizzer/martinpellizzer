Ranunculus sericocephalus var. major

Ranunculus sericocephalus var. major, commonly known as the silver-leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive white center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
