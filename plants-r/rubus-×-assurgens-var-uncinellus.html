Rubus × assurgens var. uncinellus

Rubus × assurgens var. uncinellus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which help it to anchor itself to surfaces. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along fences.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and edible purposes in gardens.
