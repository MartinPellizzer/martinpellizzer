Randia gynopachis

Randia gynopachis, commonly known as the hairy randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, white or yellowish, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

Randia gynopachis is often found in wetlands, along riverbanks, and in disturbed soils, where it thrives in moist conditions.
