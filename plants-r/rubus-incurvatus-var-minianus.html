Rubus incurvatus var. minianus

Rubus incurvatus var. minianus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are often used in local culinary traditions and are valued for their tart flavor.
