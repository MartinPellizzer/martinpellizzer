Rimacactus laui

Rimacactus laui, commonly known as the Laui cactus, is a species native to the Peruvian Andes.

It is a small, spiny cactus with a globular shape and is typically found growing at high elevations. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, which helps it survive in its arid environment. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and bloom during the warmer months.

Rimacactus laui is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and unique member of the cactus family.
