Rubus moluccanus var. pseudotiliaceus

Rubus moluccanus var. pseudotiliaceus, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in horticultural and ecological studies.
