Rubus adventitius

Rubus adventitius, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus adventitius plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
