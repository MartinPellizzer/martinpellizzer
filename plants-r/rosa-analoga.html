Rosa analoga

Rosa analoga, commonly known as the Chilean rose, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features woody stems covered in thorny stems and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa analoga is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
