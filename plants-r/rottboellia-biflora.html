Rottboellia biflora

Rottboellia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences bearing two to three spikelets, each with tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Rottboellia biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
