Rosa deligatipes

Rosa deligatipes, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorny prickles. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Rosa deligatipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
