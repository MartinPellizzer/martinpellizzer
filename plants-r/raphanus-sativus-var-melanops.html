Raphanus sativus var. melanops

Raphanus sativus var. melanops, commonly known as the black radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black skin and elongated, cylindrical root that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters and has a pungent, spicy flavor due to the presence of glucosinolates. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible root and leaves.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and nutritional content.
