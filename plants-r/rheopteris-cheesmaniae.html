Rheopteris cheesmaniae

Rheopteris cheesmaniae, commonly known as the Mexican tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a large, evergreen tree fern that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring a tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy outer layer. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive, arching shape and a dark green, leathery texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
