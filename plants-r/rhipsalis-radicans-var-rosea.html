Rhipsalis radicans var. rosea

Rhipsalis radicans var. rosea, commonly known as the pink Christmas cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to bloom during the holiday season.

Its botanical features make it a fascinating subject for both horticultural study and decorative use.
