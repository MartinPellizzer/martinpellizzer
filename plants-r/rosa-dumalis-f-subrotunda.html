Rosa dumalis f. subrotunda

Rosa dumalis f. subrotunda, commonly known as the broad-leaved wild rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa dumalis species.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and fragrant pink to white flowers. This plant typically grows in woodland and scrub habitats, preferring well-drained soil.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C. The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
