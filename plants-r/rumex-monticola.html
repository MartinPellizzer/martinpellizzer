Rumex monticola

Rumex monticola, commonly known as mountain sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose inflorescences, producing edible, tangy berries.

Rumex monticola is valued for its culinary uses and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
