Roxburghia sessilifolia

Roxburghia sessilifolia, commonly known as the wax tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.
