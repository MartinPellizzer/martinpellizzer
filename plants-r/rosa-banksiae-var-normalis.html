Rosa banksiae var. normalis

Rosa banksiae var. normalis, commonly known as the Banks' rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and is characterized by its robust growth habit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The variety 'normalis' is distinguished by its typical form, as opposed to other cultivars with more compact growth.
