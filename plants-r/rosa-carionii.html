Rosa carionii

Rosa carionii, commonly known as the Carpathian rose, is a species native to the Carpathian Mountains in Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or forested areas. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring. Rosa carionii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
