Ranunculus balcanicus

Ranunculus balcanicus, commonly known as the Balkan buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often hairy.

It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
