Rubus thyrsoideus var. persicinus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. persicinus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles.
