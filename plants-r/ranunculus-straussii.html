Ranunculus straussii

Ranunculus straussii, commonly known as Strauss's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent black markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
