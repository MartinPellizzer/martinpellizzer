Ranunculus acutimammus

Ranunculus acutimammus, commonly known as the sharp-horned buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a distinctive central boss. The plant has compound leaves with lobed segments and is typically found in moist, calcareous soils. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
