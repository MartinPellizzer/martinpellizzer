Rubus × hypoleucus

Rubus × hypoleucus, commonly known as the purple-leaf bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that often exhibit a striking purplish hue. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red but can vary in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
