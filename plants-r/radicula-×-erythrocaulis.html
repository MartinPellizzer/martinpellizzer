Radicula × erythrocaulis

Radicula × erythrocaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed radicula, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
