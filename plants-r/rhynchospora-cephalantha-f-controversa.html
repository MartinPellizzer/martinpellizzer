Rhynchospora cephalantha f. controversa

Rhynchospora cephalantha f. controversa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beakspur, is a botanical variant of the species Rhynchospora cephalantha.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

This form is notable for its distinct morphology and ecological adaptation to moist environments.
