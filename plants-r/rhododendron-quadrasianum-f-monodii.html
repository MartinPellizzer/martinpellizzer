Rhododendron quadrasianum f. monodii

Rhododendron quadrasianum f. monodii, commonly known as the Korean rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its showy flowers. The flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, with five petals and a tubular base, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
