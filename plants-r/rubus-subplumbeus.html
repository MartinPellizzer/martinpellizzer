Rubus subplumbeus

Rubus subplumbeus, commonly known as the pale blueberry bush, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and supporting local pollinator populations.
