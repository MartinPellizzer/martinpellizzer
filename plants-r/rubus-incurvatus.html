Rubus incurvatus

Rubus incurvatus, commonly known as the bent-leaved blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that curve inward. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, and it thrives in moist, wooded areas.

Rubus incurvatus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
