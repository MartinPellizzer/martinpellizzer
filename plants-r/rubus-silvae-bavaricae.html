Rubus silvae-bavaricae

Rubus silvae-bavaricae, commonly known as the Bavarian forest raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments within forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
