Rosa villosa var. submollis

Rosa villosa var. submollis, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
