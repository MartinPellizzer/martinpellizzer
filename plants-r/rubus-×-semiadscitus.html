Rubus × semiadscitus

Rubus × semiadscitus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry). This species exhibits characteristics of both parent plants, including thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The berries are typically red to black in color and have a tart, tangy flavor.

It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
