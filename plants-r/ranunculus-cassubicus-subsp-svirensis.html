Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. svirensis

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

svirensis, commonly known as the Siberian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas. It is characterized by its short stature and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
