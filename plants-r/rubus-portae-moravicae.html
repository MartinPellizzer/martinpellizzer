Rubus portae-moravicae

Rubus portae-moravicae, commonly known as the Moravian Gate raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an important part of local ecosystems and a subject of botanical study.
