Rubus serpens var. leptosepalus

Rubus serpens var. leptosepalus, commonly known as the slender-sepal raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
