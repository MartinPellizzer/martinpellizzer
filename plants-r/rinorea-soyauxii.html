Rinorea soyauxii

Rinorea soyauxii, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Rinorea soyauxii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
