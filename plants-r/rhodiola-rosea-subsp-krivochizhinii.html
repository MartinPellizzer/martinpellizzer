Rhodiola rosea subsp. krivochizhinii

Rhodiola rosea subsp.

krivochizhinii, commonly known as the Siberian rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Eastern Siberia. It is characterized by its fleshy, red or purple roots and its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine and subarctic environments. It is part of the Crassulaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its ability to enhance physical endurance and reduce fatigue.

Due to its resilience and unique chemical composition, it is often used in traditional medicine and modern herbal supplements.
