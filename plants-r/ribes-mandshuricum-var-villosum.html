Ribes mandshuricum var. villosum

Ribes mandshuricum var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
