Rubus dasycaulon

Rubus dasycaulon, commonly known as the hairy stemmed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

Rubus dasycaulon is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
