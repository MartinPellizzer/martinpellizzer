Rhipsalis alata

Rhipsalis alata, commonly known as the silver needle cactus, is a climbing cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, thin, and slightly curved stems that resemble threads, which help it climb trees and other structures. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
