Rubus rehmannii

Rubus rehmannii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are typically white or pink.

Rubus rehmannii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
