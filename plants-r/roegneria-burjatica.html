Roegneria burjatica

Roegneria burjatica, commonly known as the Burjatian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

Roegneria burjatica is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native environment.
