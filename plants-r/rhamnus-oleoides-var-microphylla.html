Rhamnus oleoides var. microphylla

Rhamnus oleoides var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf buckthorn, is a small shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, black berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
