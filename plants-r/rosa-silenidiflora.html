Rosa silenidiflora

Rosa silenidiflora, commonly known as the silenidiflora rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces edible hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a serrated margin, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Rosa silenidiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
