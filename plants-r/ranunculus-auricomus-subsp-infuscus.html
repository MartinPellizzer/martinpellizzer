Ranunculus auricomus subsp. infuscus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

infuscus, commonly known as the dark-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, branched stems that bear trifoliate leaves with a dark green, often slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive dark center, and they bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
