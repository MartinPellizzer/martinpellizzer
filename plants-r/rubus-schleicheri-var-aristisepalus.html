Rubus schleicheri var. aristisepalus

Rubus schleicheri var. aristisepalus, commonly known as the Schleicher's blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
