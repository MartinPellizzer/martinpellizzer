Rubus aspericaulis

Rubus aspericaulis, commonly known as the roughleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark purple berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the eastern United States.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
