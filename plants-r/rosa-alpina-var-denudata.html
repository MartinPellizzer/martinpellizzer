Rosa alpina var. denudata

Rosa alpina var. denudata, commonly known as the Alpine rose or naked-stemmed rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairless or sparsely hairy. The plant produces fragrant, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
