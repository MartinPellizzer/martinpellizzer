Rubus hirtus var. sinuosus

Rubus hirtus var. sinuosus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
