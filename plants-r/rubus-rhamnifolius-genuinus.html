Rubus rhamnifolius genuinus

Rubus rhamnifolius genuinus, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruit that is widely used in culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
