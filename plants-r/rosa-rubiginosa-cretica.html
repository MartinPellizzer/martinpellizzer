Rosa rubiginosa cretica

Rosa rubiginosa cretica, commonly known as the Cretan rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its hips, which are orange-red and fuzzy, contain seeds and are used in traditional medicine.

Rosa rubiginosa cretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
