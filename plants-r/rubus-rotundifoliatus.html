Rubus rotundifoliatus

Rubus rotundifoliatus, commonly known as the round-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and its compound leaves, which are rounded at the base and have a distinctive lobed appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
