Rubus argutus var. randii

Rubus argutus var. randii, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a thorny shrub native to North America, characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of red berries. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically consumed raw or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in gardens and wildlands.

Its botanical features include a woody stem, trifoliate leaves, and a distinctive floral structure.
