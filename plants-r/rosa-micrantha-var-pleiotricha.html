Rosa micrantha var. pleiotricha

Rosa micrantha var. pleiotricha, commonly known as the pleiotricha rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the plant produces small, red hips that are edible.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
