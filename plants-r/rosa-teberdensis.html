Rosa teberdensis

Rosa teberdensis, commonly known as the Teberdian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region, specifically found in parts of Georgia and Turkey. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa teberdensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its potential in horticulture and conservation efforts.
