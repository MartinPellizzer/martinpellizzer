Rubus rosifolius var. trilobus

Rubus rosifolius var. trilobus, commonly known as the three-lobed blackberry, is a variant of the rosifolius species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which have three distinct lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
