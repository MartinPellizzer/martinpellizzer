Rubus × pseudoyoshinoi

Rubus × pseudoyoshinoi, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark red to black in color. The plant is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and agricultural producers.
