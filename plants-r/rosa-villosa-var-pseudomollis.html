Rosa villosa var. pseudomollis

Rosa villosa var. pseudomollis, commonly known as the silky rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in dense, white to grayish pubescence, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and aromatic, with serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its visual and olfactory characteristics.
