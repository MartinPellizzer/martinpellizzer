Rubus laramettensis

Rubus laramettensis, commonly known as the Laramie raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in early summer.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant in local ecosystems.
