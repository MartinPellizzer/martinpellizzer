Rhamnus pumila subsp. valentina

Rhamnus pumila subsp.

valentina, commonly known as the Valentina buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by reddish berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found at high altitudes.

This subspecies is noted for its hardy nature and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
