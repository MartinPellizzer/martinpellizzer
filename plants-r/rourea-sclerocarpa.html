Rourea sclerocarpa

Rourea sclerocarpa, commonly known as the hard-shelled rourea, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Rourea sclerocarpa is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
