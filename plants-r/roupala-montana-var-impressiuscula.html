Roupala montana var. impressiuscula

Roupala montana var. impressiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved roupala, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Its fruits are small, dry berries that contain several seeds.

This variety is typically found in forest margins and rocky soils, where it thrives in partial shade.
