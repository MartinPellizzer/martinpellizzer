Rubus ulmifolius var. avellanus

Rubus ulmifolius var. avellanus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a variant of the common bramble that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically oval-shaped and serrated. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along the edges of fields, contributing to its ecological versatility.
