Rosa gallica var. vittata

Rosa gallica var. vittata, commonly known as the white gallica rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white single flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has upright, thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and historical landscapes.
