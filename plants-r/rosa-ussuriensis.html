Rosa ussuriensis

Rosa ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne in corymbs. Rosa ussuriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
