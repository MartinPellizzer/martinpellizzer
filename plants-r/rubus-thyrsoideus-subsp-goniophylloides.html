Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. goniophylloides

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

goniophylloides, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are often hairy and have a distinctive shape.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
