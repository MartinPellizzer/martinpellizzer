Rhododendron uvariifolium var. griseum

Rhododendron uvariifolium var. griseum, commonly known as the Griseum rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a glossy surface that helps retain moisture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its beauty and resilience.
