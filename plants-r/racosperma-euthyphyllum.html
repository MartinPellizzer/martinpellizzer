Racosperma euthyphyllum

Racosperma euthyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters.

Racosperma euthyphyllum is adapted to dry climates and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
