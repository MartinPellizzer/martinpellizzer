Racosperma adoxum

Racosperma adoxum, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, globular seed pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil.
