Rosa purpurella

Rosa purpurella, commonly known as the purple flowering rose, is a species native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa purpurella thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
