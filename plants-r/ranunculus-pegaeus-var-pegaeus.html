Ranunculus pegaeus var. pegaeus

Ranunculus pegaeus var. pegaeus, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, herbaceous stem. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and ecological importance to pollinators.
