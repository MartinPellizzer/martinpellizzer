Rhexia cognata

Rhexia cognata, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rich soils in woodland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rhexia cognata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
