Ranunculus brutius

Ranunculus brutius, commonly known as the Italian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with prominent central sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects.

The species is characterized by its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
