Rubus dunnii var. dunnii

Rubus dunnii var. dunnii, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupes. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its robust nature and high fruit yield, it is cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
