Rubus × pulverulentus var. fallacinus

Rubus × pulverulentus var. fallacinus, commonly known as the fallacious blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or open areas.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits variability in morphology and fruit characteristics.
