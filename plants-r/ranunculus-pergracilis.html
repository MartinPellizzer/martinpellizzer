Ranunculus pergracilis

Ranunculus pergracilis, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble additional petals. Its leaves are finely divided and often appear in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ranunculus pergracilis is known for its ability to bloom in harsh environments, making it a symbol of resilience in botanical gardens and wildflower displays.
