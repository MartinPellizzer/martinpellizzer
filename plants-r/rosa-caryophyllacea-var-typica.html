Rosa caryophyllacea var. typica

Rosa caryophyllacea var. typica, commonly known as the typical sand rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive grayish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and drought tolerance.
