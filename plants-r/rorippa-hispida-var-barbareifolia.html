Rorippa hispida var. barbareifolia

Rorippa hispida var. barbareifolia, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a semi-aquatic plant native to moist soils and wetlands.

It features small, alternate leaves that are typically hairy, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are arranged in clusters. Its stems are often prostrate or floating, allowing it to spread across water surfaces.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
