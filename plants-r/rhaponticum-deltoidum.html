Rhaponticum deltoidum

Rhaponticum deltoidum, commonly known as the Siberian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, with leaves that are heart-shaped and serrated. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round, yellowish fruits. It is adapted to harsh climates and is often found in alpine and tundra environments.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
