Rubus × uncinellifer var. gracilior

Rubus × uncinellifer var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to regions of North America and is often found in open woods and along stream banks. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth habit.
