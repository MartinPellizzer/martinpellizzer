Rosa trachyphylla f. saxigena

Rosa trachyphylla f. saxigena, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a horticultural form of the shrub rose characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, hairy, and arranged in an alternate pattern, contributing to its hardy and resilient nature.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to flourish in challenging alpine environments.
