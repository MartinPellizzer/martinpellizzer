Ranunculus guilelmi-jordanii

Ranunculus guilelmi-jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a distinctive central gland. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it is known for its delicate, herbaceous appearance.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar botanical features.
