Rhynchosia uniflora

Rhynchosia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower snout bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features palmately trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant bears pods that are typically cylindrical and contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Rhynchosia uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
