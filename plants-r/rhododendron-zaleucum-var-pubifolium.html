Rhododendron zaleucum var. pubifolium

Rhododendron zaleucum var. pubifolium, commonly known as the pubifoliate rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with hairy undersides, which gives it its varietal name "pubifolium.". The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as part of natural landscapes.
