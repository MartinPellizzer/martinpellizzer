Rhipsalis saglionis var. rubrodisca

Rhipsalis saglionis var. rubrodisca, commonly known as the red-stemmed mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features slender, branching stems that are often tinged with a reddish hue, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, hair-like structures, a characteristic common in many cacti.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
