Rubus pseudoserpens

Rubus pseudoserpens, commonly known as the creeping blackberry.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It is native to various regions in South America and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex inflorescence and the presence of edible, though tart, berries.
