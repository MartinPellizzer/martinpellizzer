Rubus insericatus var. vepreticola

Rubus insericatus var. vepreticola, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple drupelets.

This species is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
