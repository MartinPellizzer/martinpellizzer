Rumex lappula

Rumex lappula, commonly known as common dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is commonly found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Rumex lappula is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
