Rhizomatopteris

Rhizomatopteris, commonly known as the rhizome fern, is a genus of ferns characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

These plants typically form dense, creeping mats through horizontal underground stems called rhizomes. The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a complex, feather-like structure that enhances their ornamental appeal. Rhizomatopteris species are often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Their ability to spread vegetatively makes them effective in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.
