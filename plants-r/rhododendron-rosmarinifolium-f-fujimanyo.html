Rhododendron rosmarinifolium f. fujimanyo

Rhododendron rosmarinifolium f. fujimanyo, commonly known as the Fujimanyo azalea, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, dark green foliage that is aromatic, with a strong mint-like scent. The plant produces clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
