Ruscus hypoglossum var. microglossus

Ruscus hypoglossum var. microglossus, commonly known as the dwarf lady's mantle, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of dark green, waxy leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
