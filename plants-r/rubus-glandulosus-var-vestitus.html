Rubus glandulosus var. vestitus

Rubus glandulosus var. vestitus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, particularly for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
