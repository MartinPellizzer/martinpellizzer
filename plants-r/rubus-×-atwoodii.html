Rubus × atwoodii

Rubus × atwoodii, commonly known as the Atwood blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically sweet and aromatic, making it a popular choice for cultivation. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a complex root system that helps it establish in various habitats.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
