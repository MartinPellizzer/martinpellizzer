Rubus schmidelyanus var. parviflorus

Rubus schmidelyanus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered blackberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy plant suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes grown in gardens and for local consumption.
