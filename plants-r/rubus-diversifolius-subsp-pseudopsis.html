Rubus diversifolius subsp. pseudopsis

Rubus diversifolius subsp.

pseudopsis, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically three to five leaflets, with serrated margins and a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and streamside habitats.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
