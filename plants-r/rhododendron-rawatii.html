Rhododendron rawatii

Rhododendron rawatii, commonly known as the Kashmiri rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, cool mountainous areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, and it produces clusters of pink to purple flowers in the spring. Rhododendron rawatii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
