Rosa beggeriana var. lacerans

Rosa beggeriana var. lacerans, commonly known as the Chinese rose or wild rose, is a shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces red or orange hips, which are edible and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
