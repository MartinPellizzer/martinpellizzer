Rubus × valesiensis

Rubus × valesiensis, commonly known as the Valesiense blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the island of New Guinea and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually pink or white.

The fruit, which is edible, is similar to other blackberries but has a unique flavor profile due to its hybrid nature.
