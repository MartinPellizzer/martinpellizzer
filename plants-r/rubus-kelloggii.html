Rubus kelloggii

Rubus kelloggii, commonly known as Kellogg's raspberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, red, edible berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are often used in jams and desserts, though the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
