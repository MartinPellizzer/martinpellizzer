Rosa urbica var. hemitricha

Rosa urbica var. hemitricha, commonly known as the urban rose or city rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets. This plant is adapted to thrive in urban environments, often found growing in cracks of sidewalks and along building foundations.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and hardy species suitable for landscaping in challenging conditions.
