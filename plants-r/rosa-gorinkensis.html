Rosa gorinkensis

Rosa gorinkensis, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a species native to Japan and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals. Rosa gorinkensis is known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
