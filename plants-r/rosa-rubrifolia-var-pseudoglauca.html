Rosa rubrifolia var. pseudoglauca

Rosa rubrifolia var. pseudoglauca, commonly known as the red-leafed rose, is a variation of the Rosa rubrifolia species.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage, which contrasts with its typical pink to white flowers. The plant is native to regions of China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after addition to gardens and botanical collections.
