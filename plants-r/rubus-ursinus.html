Rubus ursinus

Rubus ursinus, commonly known as the blackberry plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and parts of Mexico, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its shrub-like structure features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves.

Rubus ursinus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
