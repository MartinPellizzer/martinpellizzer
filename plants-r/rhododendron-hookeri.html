Rhododendron hookeri

Rhododendron hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in cool, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and elongated, cylindrical flower clusters.

Rhododendron hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that replicate its natural habitat.
