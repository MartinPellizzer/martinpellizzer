Rubus papuanus

Rubus papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and have a tart flavor similar to other raspberries.
