Rubus occidentalis var. grandiflorus

Rubus occidentalis var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and produces large, showy white to pink flowers in the spring. The plant bears aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is often found in moist, shady areas such as woodlands and streamside habitats.
