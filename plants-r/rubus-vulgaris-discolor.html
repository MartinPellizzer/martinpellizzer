Rubus vulgaris discolor

Rubus vulgaris discolor, commonly known as the purple-blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that change color from green to red and finally to a deep purple-black as they mature. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
