Robiquetia cladophylax

Robiquetia cladophylax, commonly known as the rock fig, is a unique member of the Moraceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by various bird species. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it conserve water in arid environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
