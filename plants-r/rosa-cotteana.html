Rosa cotteana

Rosa cotteana, commonly known as the Cotte's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa cotteana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
