Rubus apiculatus proles beckii

Rubus apiculatus proles beckii, commonly known as the Beck's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It is native to certain regions of North America and thrives in moist, wooded habitats.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of its ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
