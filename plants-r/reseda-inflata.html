Reseda inflata

Reseda inflata, commonly known as the inflated reseda, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or sandy environments.

Reseda inflata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
