Rosa arvensis f. rhomboideocuneata

Rosa arvensis f. rhomboideocuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smaller, fragrant flowers compared to the typical form. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its stems are thorny, and it produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
