Rubus genevieri var. griffithianus

Rubus genevieri var. griffithianus, commonly known as the Griffith's bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
