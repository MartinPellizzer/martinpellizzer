Ranunculus hilarans

Ranunculus hilarans, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant typically grows in alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early summer.

Ranunculus hilarans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
