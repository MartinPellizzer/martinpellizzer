Rosa paucispinosa

Rosa paucispinosa, commonly known as the spiny rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

Rosa paucispinosa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
