Rhynchosia grevei

Rhynchosia grevei, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and foliage, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Rhynchosia grevei is valued both for its nutritional seeds and its potential use in agroforestry systems.
