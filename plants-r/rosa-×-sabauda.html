Rosa × sabauda

Rosa × sabauda, commonly known as the Sabauda rose, is a hybrid rose species that originated in the 18th century.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant blooms that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many rose species. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Rosa × sabauda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
