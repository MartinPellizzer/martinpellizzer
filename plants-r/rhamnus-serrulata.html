Rhamnus serrulata

Rhamnus serrulata, commonly known as the Sierra juniper, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces small, blue-black berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it has a woody, evergreen structure despite being deciduous.

Rhamnus serrulata plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
