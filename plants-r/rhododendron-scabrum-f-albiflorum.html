Rhododendron scabrum f. albiflorum

Rhododendron scabrum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered alpine rhododendron, is a cultivar of the rhododendron genus native to the high altitudes of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its striking white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blossoms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
