Rubus arduennensis var. fragrans

Rubus arduennensis var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are edible and contain a high concentration of antioxidants.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
