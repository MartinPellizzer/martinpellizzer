Rubus setchuenensis

Rubus setchuenensis, commonly known as the Sichuan blackberry, is a species of brambles native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cooler, humid climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is a cluster of small drupes, is edible and has a tart flavor.
