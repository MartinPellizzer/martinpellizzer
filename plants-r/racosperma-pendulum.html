Racosperma pendulum

Racosperma pendulum, commonly known as the hanging acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.

Racosperma pendulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
