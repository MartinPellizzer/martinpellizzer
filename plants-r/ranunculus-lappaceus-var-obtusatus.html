Ranunculus lappaceus var. obtusatus

Ranunculus lappaceus var. obtusatus, commonly known as the blunt-leaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and have a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually yellow with five sepals that resemble petals, and they bloom in early spring.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.
