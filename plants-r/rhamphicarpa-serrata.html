Rhamphicarpa serrata

Rhamphicarpa serrata, commonly known as the serrated root plant, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous, tuberous roots and its small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique root structure, which has been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
