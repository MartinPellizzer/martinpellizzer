Rheum esculentum

Rheum esculentum, commonly known as the Chinese rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, and its thick, fleshy roots that are the primary part used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar species such as buckthorn and sorrel. Its rhubarb stalks are typically harvested in early summer and are known for their tart flavor, often used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The plant also contains anthraquinones, which contribute to its laxative properties and have been used in herbal remedies for centuries.
