Rubus rubratus

Rubus rubratus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Rubus rubratus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to pollinators.
