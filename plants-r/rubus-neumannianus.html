Rubus neumannianus

Rubus neumannianus, commonly known as the Neumann's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and berries. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus family.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
