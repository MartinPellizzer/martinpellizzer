Ranunculus millefoliatus var. minor

Ranunculus millefoliatus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a distinctive dark center. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and historical medicinal uses.
