Rosa elliptica subsp. inodora

Rosa elliptica subsp.

inodora, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red hips that appear in the fall, and the plant is often used in hedges or as a decorative shrub.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
