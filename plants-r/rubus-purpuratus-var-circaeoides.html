Rubus purpuratus var. circaeoides

Rubus purpuratus var. circaeoides, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, hairy, red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include stipulate leaves and a woody structure typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.
