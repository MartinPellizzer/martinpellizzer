Ranunculus paradoxus

Ranunculus paradoxus, commonly known as the paradoxical buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble additional petals. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in the Ranunculaceae family.
