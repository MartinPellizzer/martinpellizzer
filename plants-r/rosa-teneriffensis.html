Rosa teneriffensis

Rosa teneriffensis, commonly known as the Teneriffe rose, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates.

Rosa teneriffensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
