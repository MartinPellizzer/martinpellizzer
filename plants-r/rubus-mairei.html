Rubus mairei

Rubus mairei, commonly known as the Maire bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in China, Vietnam, and Myanmar.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
