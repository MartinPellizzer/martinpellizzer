Rosa mollis f. brithyacantha

Rosa mollis f. brithyacantha, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and delicate pink to white flowers.

It is a hybrid form of Rosa mollis, native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
