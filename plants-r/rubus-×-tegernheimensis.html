Rubus × tegernheimensis

Rubus × tegernheimensis, commonly known as the European blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

This species is valued for its fruit, which is used in culinary applications and jams.
