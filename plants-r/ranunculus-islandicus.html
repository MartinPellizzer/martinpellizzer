Ranunculus islandicus

Ranunculus islandicus, commonly known as the Iceland buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic soils of Iceland.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky areas and thrives in cool climates. It is a perennial species that produces small, green, hairy fruits.

Ranunculus islandicus is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
