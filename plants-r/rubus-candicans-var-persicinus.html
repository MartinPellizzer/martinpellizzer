Rubus candicans var. persicinus

Rubus candicans var. persicinus, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a variant of the thimbleberry plant native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, aggregate fruits that resemble tiny, sweet berries. The leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces white flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, which have a mild, sweet flavor.
