Rosa oleicarpa

Rosa oleicarpa, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, making it a nocturnal bloom. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. Its stems are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, red fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Rosa oleicarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique blooming behavior.
