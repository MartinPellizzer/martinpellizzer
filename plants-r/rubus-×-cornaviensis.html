Rubus × cornaviensis

Rubus × cornaviensis, commonly known as the Cornaven rose.

It is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as raspberries, which are typically red but can also be yellow or black depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
