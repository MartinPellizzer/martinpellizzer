Rhodolaena macrocarpa

Rhodolaena macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited rhodolaena, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
