Rubus hebecaulis var. hypoater

Rubus hebecaulis var. hypoater, commonly known as the slender raspberry.

This variant is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. It produces small, red berries that are edible but often described as having a tart flavor.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.
