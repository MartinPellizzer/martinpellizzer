Rhamnus saxatilis proles villarsii

Rhamnus saxatilis proles villarsii, commonly known as the alpine buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its fruits are small, black drupes that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and unique adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
