Rubus aculeolatus var. iuvenis

Rubus aculeolatus var. iuvenis, commonly known as the young thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen to a bright orange-red color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and streamside areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
