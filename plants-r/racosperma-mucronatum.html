Racosperma mucronatum

Racosperma mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring slender, often hairy stems and small, compound leaves. Its yellow flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Racosperma mucronatum is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
