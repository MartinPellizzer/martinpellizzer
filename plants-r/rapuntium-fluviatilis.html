Rapuntium fluviatilis

Rapuntium fluviatilis, commonly known as the river cress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils along riverbanks and wetlands. Its flowering period occurs in spring, producing yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rapuntium fluviatilis is often used in traditional medicine and is also a food source for various herbivorous animals.
