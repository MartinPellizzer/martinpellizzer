Rosa calycina

Rosa calycina, commonly known as the California cushion rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa calycina has small, oval leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color.

It is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas and chaparral habitats.
