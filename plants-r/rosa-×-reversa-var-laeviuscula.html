Rosa × reversa var. laeviuscula

Rosa × reversa var. laeviuscula, commonly known as the pink musk rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its semi-double to fully double pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers emit a subtle, musky fragrance, which is a distinguishing feature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
