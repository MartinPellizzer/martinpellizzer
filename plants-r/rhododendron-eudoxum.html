Rhododendron eudoxum

Rhododendron eudoxum, commonly known as the Eudoxus rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like China, India, and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its botanical features include opposite leaves with a leathery texture and floral bracts that often persist after flowering.

Rhododendron eudoxum is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native habitats.
