Ranunculus auricomus subsp. obtusulus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

obtusulus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine broad, spoon-shaped petals.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Ranunculaceae family.
