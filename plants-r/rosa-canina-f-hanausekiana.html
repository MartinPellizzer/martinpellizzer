Rosa canina f. hanausekiana

Rosa canina f. hanausekiana, commonly known as the Hanausekiana rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, pale pink to white flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical gardens.
