Rubus pallidus var. crispulifolius

Rubus pallidus var. crispulifolius, commonly known as the pale bramble with crisped leaves, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its pale green foliage and distinctive, slightly curled leaf margins. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, often in woodland or hedgerow environments. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and it produces edible, though tart, berries.

This variant is notable for its unique leaf morphology and ecological adaptability.
