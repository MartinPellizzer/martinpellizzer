Rhododendron ravum

Rhododendron ravum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and shrublands, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron ravum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
