Rhododendron nanophyton

Rhododendron nanophyton, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
