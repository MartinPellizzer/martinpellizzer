Rosa × gentilis

Rosa × gentilis, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a hybrid species of the rose family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and has been cultivated for centuries for its ornamental value and essential oils. Rosa × gentilis typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and perfumery.
