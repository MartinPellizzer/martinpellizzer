Rosa × dumetorum var. caesia

Rosa × dumetorum var. caesia, commonly known as the bluebelle rose, is a hybrid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and compact growth habit, making it suitable for shrub borders or hedges. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep blue, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
