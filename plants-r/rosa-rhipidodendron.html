Rosa rhipidodendron

Rosa rhipidodendron, commonly known as the long-stemmed rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright, thorny stems covered in dense, velvety foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Rosa rhipidodendron is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
