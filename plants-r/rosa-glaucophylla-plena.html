Rosa glaucophylla plena

Rosa glaucophylla plena, commonly known as the full-flowered blueleaf rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that has a slightly bluish hue, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces abundant, double-layered pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
