Rosa pennsylvanica plena

Rosa pennsylvanica plena, commonly known as the double-flowered wild rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Rosa pennsylvanica plena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
