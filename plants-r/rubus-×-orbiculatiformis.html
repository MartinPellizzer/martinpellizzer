Rubus × orbiculatiformis

Rubus × orbiculatiformis, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both blackberries and raspberries.

It is a thorny shrub that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in woodlands and along hedgerows. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or dark purple when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
