Rhododendron × taylorii

Rhododendron × taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor's rhododendron, is a hybrid species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Nepal and India.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
