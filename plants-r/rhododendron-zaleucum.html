Rhododendron zaleucum

Rhododendron zaleucum, commonly known as the white rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates.

Rhododendron zaleucum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to add vibrant color to gardens and landscapes.
