Rubus josefianus

Rubus josefianus, commonly known as the Josefian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is small, dark purple, and has a tart, slightly sweet flavor.
