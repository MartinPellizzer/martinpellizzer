Rubus wawrae

Rubus wawrae, commonly known as the Wawra raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many shrubs and subshrubs characterized by their thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
