Rhynchospora tenerrima subsp. tenerrima

Rhynchospora tenerrima subsp.

tenerrima, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, preferring moist to saturated soils.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often narrow and pointed, with a distinctive hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are usually pale green to reddish in color.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
