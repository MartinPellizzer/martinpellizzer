Rubus plicatus var. consimilis

Rubus plicatus var. consimilis, commonly known as the similar-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a slightly serrated margin, and the plant produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or disturbed soils.

This variant is closely related to other Rubus species and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
