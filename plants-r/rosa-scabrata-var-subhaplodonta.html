Rosa scabrata var. subhaplodonta

Rosa scabrata var. subhaplodonta, commonly known as the rough-stemmed Rosa, is a variant of the Rosa scabrata species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rugged, hairy stems and small, reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces small, oval fruits known as hips.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
