Rosa viscaria var. cheriensis

Rosa viscaria var. cheriensis, commonly known as the Cherries Rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.

This variety is prized for its compact growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
