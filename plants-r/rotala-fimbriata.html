Rotala fimbriata

Rotala fimbriata, commonly known as the flameheart plant, is a flowering aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features bright red or orange inflorescences that contrast with its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments and is often used in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Rotala fimbriata is also valued for its ability to oxygenate water and provide habitat for small aquatic life.
