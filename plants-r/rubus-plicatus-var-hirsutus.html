Rubus plicatus var. hirsutus

Rubus plicatus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its rugged appearance and hardy nature, it is a common species in horticultural and ecological contexts.
