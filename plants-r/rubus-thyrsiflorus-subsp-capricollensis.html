Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp. capricollensis

Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp.

capricollensis, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant is known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
