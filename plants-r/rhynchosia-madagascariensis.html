Rhynchosia madagascariensis

Rhynchosia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bean or African snout bean, is a climbing legume native to Madagascar.

It features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, swollen, club-shaped seed pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Rhynchosia madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
