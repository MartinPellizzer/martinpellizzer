Rosa canina f. pallida

Rosa canina f. pallida, commonly known as the pale dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which are typically smaller than those of the typical form. The plant produces bright red hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves.

This subspecies is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates.
