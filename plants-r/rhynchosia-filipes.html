Rhynchosia filipes

Rhynchosia filipes, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, similar to those of other cowpea species. It is often used as a forage crop and has nitrogen-fixing capabilities that benefit soil fertility.

Rhynchosia filipes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
