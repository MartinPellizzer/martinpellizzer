Rosa agrestis var. inodora

Rosa agrestis var. inodora, commonly known as the meadow rose, is a variant of the shrub rose species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are often covered in a fine layer of white down. This plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for garden landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
