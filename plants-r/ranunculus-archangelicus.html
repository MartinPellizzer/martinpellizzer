Ranunculus archangelicus

Ranunculus archangelicus, commonly known as the Archangel's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to eight petals that are usually yellow or orange with a dark center.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its beauty in gardens.
