Rhododendron dunnii

Rhododendron dunnii, commonly known as the Dunn's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, broad leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to purple in color. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, thriving in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its botanical features include hairy stems and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its alpine habitat.

Rhododendron dunnii is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
