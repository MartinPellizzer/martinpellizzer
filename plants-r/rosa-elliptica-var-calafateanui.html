Rosa elliptica var. calafateanui

Rosa elliptica var. calafateanui, commonly known as the Calafate rose, is a shrub native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is notable for its edible, dark purple berries, which are rich in antioxidants and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to harsh alpine conditions and its ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
