Rubus satis

Rubus satis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry shrub, is a member of the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

The botanical features of Rubus satis make it both ecologically significant and valuable for its edible fruits.
