Rubus rivularis microgene lamprophyllus

Rubus rivularis microgene lamprophyllus, commonly known as the glossy-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries and its glossy, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a sprawling growth habit with thorny stems and produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The botanical features of this plant make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique leaf and fruit characteristics.
