Rubus hirtus var. subgutinensis

Rubus hirtus var. subgutinensis, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible but can vary in taste depending on the region. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
