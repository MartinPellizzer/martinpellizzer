Rosa incerta

Rosa incerta, commonly known as the uncertain rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa incerta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
