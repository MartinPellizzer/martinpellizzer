Rosa schmidtii var. virgata

Rosa schmidtii var. virgata, commonly known as the Schmidt's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
