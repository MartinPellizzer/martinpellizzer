Rhaphis sorghum

Rhaphis sorghum, commonly known as finger millet, is a small grain plant native to Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, finger-like spikes of small seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, dry climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the inflorescence consists of compact, branched clusters.

Rhaphis sorghum is known for its high nutritional value, particularly its rich content of calcium and iron.
