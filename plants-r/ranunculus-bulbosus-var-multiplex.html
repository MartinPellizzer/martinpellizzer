Ranunculus bulbosus var. multiplex

Ranunculus bulbosus var. multiplex, commonly known as the bulbous buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent black centers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

The species is noted for its ability to form dense colonies, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
