Ranunculus pygmaeus subsp. sabinei

Ranunculus pygmaeus subsp.

sabinei, commonly known as the Sabine dwarf buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. It has palmately divided leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
