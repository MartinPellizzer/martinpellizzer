Robinia pseudoacacia f. coluteoides

Robinia pseudoacacia f. coluteoides, commonly known as the yellow robinia, is a cultivar of the black locust tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers, which bloom in early summer and are highly fragrant. The leaves are bipinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. This variety is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its showy blooms and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in full sun.
