Ruellia drummondiana

Ruellia drummondiana, commonly known as Drummond's ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy blue or purple flowers in clusters. It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Ruellia drummondiana is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local pollinators.
