Ribes divaricatum var. irriguum

Ribes divaricatum var. irriguum, commonly known as the Sierra currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas. Its botanical features include smooth, reddish stems and white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
