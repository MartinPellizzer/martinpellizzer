Ranunculus nyssanus

Ranunculus nyssanus, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Its blooming period occurs from spring to early summer, making it a notable wildflower in its native habitat.
