Racosperma umbellatum

Racosperma umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that resemble an umbrella, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and the plant is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types, including sandy and rocky soils.

Racosperma umbellatum is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
