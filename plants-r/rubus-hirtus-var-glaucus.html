Rubus hirtus var. glaucus

Rubus hirtus var. glaucus, commonly known as the glaucous raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves that often have a bluish-gray waxy coating. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential use in edible landscaping.
