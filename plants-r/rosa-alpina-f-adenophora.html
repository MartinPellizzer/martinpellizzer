Rosa alpina f. adenophora

Rosa alpina f. adenophora, commonly known as the Alpine rose or alpine garden rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a subspecies of Rosa alpina, characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, single or semi-double flowers. It has dark green, serrated leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white down, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
