Roupala complicata

Roupala complicata, commonly known as the New Zealand holly, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of leathery, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically compound, with multiple leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Roupala complicata is well adapted to the temperate climate of New Zealand and is often found in coastal and forested areas.
