Rubus × pullatifrons

Rubus × pullatifrons, commonly known as the trailing blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are dark purple to black when ripe, and are edible, though often tart and used in jams and wines.
