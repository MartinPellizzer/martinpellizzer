Rubus junceus var. regionalis

Rubus junceus var. regionalis, commonly known as the regional blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is a shrub that produces small, dark purple to black berries, which are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular for use in jams and pies.
