Ruellia intermedia

Ruellia intermedia, commonly known as Mexican bluebells, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom from spring to summer. Ruellia intermedia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to ornamental horticulture.
