Rosa arborea

Rosa arborea, commonly known as the tree rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and shrub-like growth habit, with clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa arborea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
