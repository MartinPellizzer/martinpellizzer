Rumex pulcher subsp. woodsii

Rumex pulcher subsp.

woodsii, commonly known as the woods sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a reddish tinge and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other sorrel species.

The plant is known for its edible leaves, though they contain oxalic acid and should be consumed in moderation.
