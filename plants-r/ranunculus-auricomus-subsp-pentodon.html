Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pentodon

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pentodon, commonly known as the golden shield, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five to eight petals, which are often larger than the sepals.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
