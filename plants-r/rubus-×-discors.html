Rubus × discors

Rubus × discors, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The fruit is rich in antioxidants and has both culinary and medicinal uses.
