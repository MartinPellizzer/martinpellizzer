Rosa laxa var. glabra

Rosa laxa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth shrub rose, is a species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has smooth, hairless stems and leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
