Restrepia pilosissima

Restrepia pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale green flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and fragrance.
