Rubus humulifolius

Rubus humulifolius, commonly known as the beerberry or blackcap bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries that ripen in the fall. These berries are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor, often used in jams and wines.

Rubus humulifolius is an important plant in its native habitat, providing food for wildlife and supporting local ecosystems.
