Ranunculus triternatus

Ranunculus triternatus, commonly known as the three-leaved crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, and its bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

Ranunculus triternatus is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
