Rhynchosia comosa

Rhynchosia comosa, commonly known as the hairy bower vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features pinnately compound leaves with hairy surfaces, contributing to its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Rhynchosia comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
