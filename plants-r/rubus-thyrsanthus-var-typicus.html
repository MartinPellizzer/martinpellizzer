Rubus thyrsanthus var. typicus

Rubus thyrsanthus var. typicus, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a shrub that produces dark purple to black berries, which are highly valued for their intense flavor and high antioxidant content. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.

The berries are typically harvested when fully ripe, offering a sweet and tangy taste.
