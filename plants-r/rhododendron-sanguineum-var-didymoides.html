Rhododendron sanguineum var. didymoides

Rhododendron sanguineum var. didymoides, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to rose-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing ornamental value throughout the year.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
