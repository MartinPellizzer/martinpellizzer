Rosa × remensis

Rosa × remensis, commonly known as the swamp rose, is a hybrid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetland environments, such as swamps and riverbanks. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring. Its stems are often covered in thorns, and it has a robust root system adapted to waterlogged soils.

Rosa × remensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
