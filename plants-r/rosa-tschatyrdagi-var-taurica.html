Rosa tschatyrdagi var. taurica

Rosa tschatyrdagi var. taurica, commonly known as the Crimean rose, is a flowering shrub native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is adapted to the temperate climate of its native region. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in the area.

The species is also known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
