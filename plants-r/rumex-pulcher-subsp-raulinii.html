Rumex pulcher subsp. raulinii

Rumex pulcher subsp.

raulinii, commonly known as the Raulini's sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or bronze in color, which distinguish it from other species in the Rumex genus.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, though they contain oxalic acid and should be consumed in moderation.
