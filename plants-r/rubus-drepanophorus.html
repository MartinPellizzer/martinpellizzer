Rubus drepanophorus

Rubus drepanophorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are characterized by their narrow shape and serrated edges.

Botanically, it is notable for its thorny stems and dense, shrub-like growth habit.
