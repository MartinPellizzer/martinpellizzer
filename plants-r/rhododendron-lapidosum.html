Rhododendron lapidosum

Rhododendron lapidosum, commonly known as the stone pine rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in dense clusters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
