Rosa dumalis subsp. innocua

Rosa dumalis subsp.

innocua, commonly known as the slender wild rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it typically grows in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features thorny stems and simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, and it produces red, hairy berries that contain seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
