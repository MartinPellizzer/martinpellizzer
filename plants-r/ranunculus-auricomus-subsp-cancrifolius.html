Ranunculus auricomus subsp. cancrifolius

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

cancrifolius, commonly known as the cancerleaf buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are deeply divided and resemble the shape of a cancerous growth, hence its common name.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens and a prominent pistil. It is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in temperate regions.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
