Rhododendron seniavinii var. seniavinii

Rhododendron seniavinii var. seniavinii, commonly known as the Seniavinii rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
