Ribes niveum f. pilosum

Ribes niveum f. pilosum, commonly known as the white gooseberry.

This variant of the white gooseberry is characterized by its hairy or pubescent foliage, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, temperate environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow berries that are edible but often tart in flavor.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Ribes, often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
