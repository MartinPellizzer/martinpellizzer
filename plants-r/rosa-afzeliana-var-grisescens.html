Rosa afzeliana var. grisescens

Rosa afzeliana var. grisescens, commonly known as the grey-haired rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, fuzzy stems and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.

It is relatively hardy and adaptable to a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
