Rosa osmastonii

Rosa osmastonii, commonly known as the Osmaston rose, is a rare and ancient species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its deep pink to crimson flowers and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. The plant is known for its strong, sweet fragrance, which is more pronounced in its flowers than in other parts of the plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
