Reseda lutea var. stricta

Reseda lutea var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sickleweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
