Rubus canadensis var. lepagei

Rubus canadensis var. lepagei, commonly known as the Canadian blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry found in North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems that provide structural support.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is often found in the understory of forests across eastern Canada and the northeastern United States.
