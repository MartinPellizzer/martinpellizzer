Ranunculus scapigerus

Ranunculus scapigerus, commonly known as the scapigerous buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically yellow or orange flowers. The plant features a short, leafless stem that supports a single, prominent flower with five to nine sepals and petals. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three lobes, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Ranunculus scapigerus thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
