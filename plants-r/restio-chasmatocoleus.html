Restio chasmatocoleus

Restio chasmatocoleus, commonly known as the mountain restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It has fibrous roots and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind.

Due to its ecological importance and unique morphology, it is a valuable species for studying plant adaptation in arid environments.
