Rubus raabei

Rubus raabei, commonly known as the Raabei raspberry, is a species of bramble native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, red, edible berries. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are valued for their tart flavor and are used in local cuisine and traditional practices.
