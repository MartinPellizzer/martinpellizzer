Rebutia xanthocarpa

Rebutia xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow fruit rebutia, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange to red fruits that contain seeds. It is a succulent plant, adapted to arid environments, and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Rebutia xanthocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
