Rubus fuscus var. eurystachys

Rubus fuscus var. eurystachys, commonly known as the broad-stalked blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
