Rubus koehleri var. fulviformis

Rubus koehleri var. fulviformis, commonly known as the goldenleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-red berries that are typically edible and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and potential use in local cuisines.
