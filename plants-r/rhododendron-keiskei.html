Rhododendron keiskei

Rhododendron keiskei, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Rhododendron keiskei is also noted for its ability to form dense mats, which can help prevent soil erosion.
