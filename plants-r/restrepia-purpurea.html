Restrepia purpurea

Restrepia purpurea, commonly known as the purple restrepia, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water, which helps it survive in its native habitat. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns.

Restrepia purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience.
