Reynoldsia pleiosperma

Reynoldsia pleiosperma, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, showcasing vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant is a terrestrial orchid with a robust rhizome system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences, and the plant produces a significant number of seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Botanically, Reynoldsia pleiosperma is notable for its complex floral structure and its role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
