Romulea bulbocodium var. flaveola

Romulea bulbocodium var. flaveola, commonly known as the yellow romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its bulbs are used for propagation, and the species is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.
