Rubus moluccanus var. hamiltonii

Rubus moluccanus var. hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
