Rosmarinus tournefortii var. pubescens

Rosmarinus tournefortii var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent rosemary, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and Mediterranean climates.

Its essential oils, rich in compounds like camphor and pinene, are valued in aromatherapy and culinary applications.
