Rhynchospora riparia

Rhynchospora riparia, commonly known as the river marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, arranged in spikelets. Rhynchospora riparia thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
