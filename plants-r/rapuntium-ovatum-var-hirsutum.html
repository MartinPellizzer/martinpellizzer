Rapuntium ovatum var. hirsutum

Rapuntium ovatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy rapunculus, is a variant of the rapunculus species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is tuberous, which is a key feature for its cultivation and use in traditional medicine.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves distinguishes it from other varieties of rapunculus.
