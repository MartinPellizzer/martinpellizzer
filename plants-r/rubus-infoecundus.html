Rubus infoecundus

Rubus infoecundus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rubus infoecundus thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, often forming dense thickets.
