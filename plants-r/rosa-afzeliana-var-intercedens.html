Rosa afzeliana var. intercedens

Rosa afzeliana var. intercedens, commonly known as the African rose or the Cape rose, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems with thorns and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets and produces small, red or orange fruits known as hips. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive flowers and hardy nature.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
