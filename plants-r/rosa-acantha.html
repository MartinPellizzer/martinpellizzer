Rosa acantha

Rosa acantha, commonly known as the thorned rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa acantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
