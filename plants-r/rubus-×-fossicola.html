Rubus × fossicola

Rubus × fossicola, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (raspberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are pink to white, and the fruit is a aggregate of drupes, usually dark purple to black when mature.

This species is valued for its edible fruit, which has a tart flavor and is commonly used in jams and desserts.
