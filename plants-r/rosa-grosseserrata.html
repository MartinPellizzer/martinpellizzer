Rosa grosseserrata

Rosa grosseserrata, commonly known as the serrated rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically in late spring to early summer. Rosa grosseserrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of temperate climates.
