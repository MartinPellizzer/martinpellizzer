Rubus mesocladus

Rubus mesocladus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are typically edible, though they are less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
