Rosa gallica var. imitans

Rosa gallica var. imitans, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a historic and ornamental shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and horticultural collections.

Due to its historical significance in perfume and culinary uses, it remains a valued plant in botanical and agricultural contexts.
