Rosa roxburghii f. esetosa

Rosa roxburghii f. esetosa, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa roxburghii species.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy growth of hairs on the stems and leaves, which distinguishes it from other forms of the species. The plant is native to the Himalayan regions and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a fragrant scent, and it produces rose hips that are used in traditional medicine.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
