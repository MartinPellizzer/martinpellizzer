Rhysolepis linearifolia

Rhysolepis linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
