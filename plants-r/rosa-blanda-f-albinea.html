Rosa blanda f. albinea

Rosa blanda f. albinea, commonly known as the white blanda rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa blanda species, native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has a trailing growth habit, often forming dense mats along the ground. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, fragrant flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
