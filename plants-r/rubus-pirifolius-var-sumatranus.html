Rubus pirifolius var. sumatranus

Rubus pirifolius var. sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten fresh or used in local cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor, making them a valued plant in both culinary and traditional medicine practices.
