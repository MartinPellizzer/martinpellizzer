Ranunculus acris subsp. boreanus

Ranunculus acris subsp.

boreanus, commonly known as the Arctic buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions of the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant features a basal rosette of finely divided, serrated leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. It is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

This subspecies is often found in tundra and mountainous areas, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
