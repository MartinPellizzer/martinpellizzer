Rosa micrantha var. lactiflora

Rosa micrantha var. lactiflora, commonly known as the milkflower rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a slight yellowish tinge. The plant produces showy, clustered blooms in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red, velvety hips. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty and pleasant aroma.
