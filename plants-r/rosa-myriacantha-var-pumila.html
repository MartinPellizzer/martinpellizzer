Rosa myriacantha var. pumila

Rosa myriacantha var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese rose, is a low-growing shrub native to China and Korea.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This variety is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
