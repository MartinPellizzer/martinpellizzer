Ranunculus subantarcticus

Ranunculus subantarcticus, commonly known as the Antarctic buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the subantarctic regions.

It is a perennial herb with a short, stout stem that bears a single, showy flower. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, adapted to the harsh, cold environments it inhabits. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus subantarcticus thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and on subantarctic islands.
