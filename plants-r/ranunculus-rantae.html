Ranunculus rantae

Ranunculus rantae, commonly known as the Andes buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to yellow. The plant has palmate leaves with a distinctive, finely divided structure. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
