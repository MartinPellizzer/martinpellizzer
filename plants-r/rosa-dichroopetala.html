Rosa dichroopetala

Rosa dichroopetala, commonly known as the two-colored rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a deep red or pink hue on the outer petals and a lighter shade on the inner petals. The plant produces fragrant blooms that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa dichroopetala is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
