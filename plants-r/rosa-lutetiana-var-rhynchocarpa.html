Rosa lutetiana var. rhynchocarpa

Rosa lutetiana var. rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the French rose or the Lutetian rose, is a variety of the Rosa lutetiana species native to France.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically featuring five leaflets. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are small, red berries that develop from the flowers and are edible, though not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
