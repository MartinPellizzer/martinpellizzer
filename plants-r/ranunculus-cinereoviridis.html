Ranunculus cinereoviridis

Ranunculus cinereoviridis, commonly known as the gray-green buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a distinctive bluish-gray underside, and its flowers are typically yellow with 5 to 8 sepals that resemble petals. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. It is characterized by its short stature and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
