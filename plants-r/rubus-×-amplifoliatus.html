Rubus × amplifoliatus

Rubus × amplifoliatus, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its large, broad leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, red drupes that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
