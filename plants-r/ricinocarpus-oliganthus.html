Ricinocarpus oliganthus

Ricinocarpus oliganthus, commonly known as the mangrove apple or sea apple, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small tree or shrub that thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
