Rhodamnia sessiliflora

Rhodamnia sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhodamnia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and subtropical regions, often found in sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Rhodamnia sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
