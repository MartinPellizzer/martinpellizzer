Rapanea penibukana

Rapanea penibukana, commonly known as the Bornean satinwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

Rapanea penibukana is valued for its durable wood, which is used in traditional craftsmanship and local construction.
