Rosa sericea f. pteracantha

Rosa sericea f. pteracantha, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a botanical variant of the woolly rose species.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white pubescence covering the stems and leaves, which provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
