Rubus chlorothyrsos var. silvaticiformis

Rubus chlorothyrsos var. silvaticiformis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, serrated leaves that often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
