Ranunculus occidentalis var. canus

Ranunculus occidentalis var. canus, commonly known as the white wood ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in association with other woodland flora.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
