Rubus × erythrocladoides

Rubus × erythrocladoides, commonly known as the red-leaf raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its distinctive red-veined leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries similar to those of other raspberry species.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit quality, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
