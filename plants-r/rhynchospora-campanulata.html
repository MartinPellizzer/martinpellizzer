Rhynchospora campanulata

Rhynchospora campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence that bears small, bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are grass-like, arranged in two rows, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Rhynchospora campanulata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
