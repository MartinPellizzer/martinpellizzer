Rosa phrygia

Rosa phrygia, commonly known as the Phrygian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa phrygia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
