Rubus anoplostachys

Rubus anoplostachys, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
