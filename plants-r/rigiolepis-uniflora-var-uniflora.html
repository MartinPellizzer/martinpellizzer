Rigiolepis uniflora var. uniflora

Rigiolepis uniflora var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower rigiolepis, is a flowering plant characterized by its unique floral structure.

It typically features a single, showy flower that sits atop a slender stem, making it visually striking. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from pink to purple, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is native to specific regions and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.
