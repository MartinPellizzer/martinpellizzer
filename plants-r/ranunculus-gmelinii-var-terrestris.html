Ranunculus gmelinii var. terrestris

Ranunculus gmelinii var. terrestris, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous slender, trailing stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

It is often found in meadows, stream banks, and other wet habitats.
