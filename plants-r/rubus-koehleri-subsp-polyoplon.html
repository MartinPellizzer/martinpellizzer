Rubus koehleri subsp. polyoplon

Rubus koehleri subsp.

polyoplon, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal environments.
