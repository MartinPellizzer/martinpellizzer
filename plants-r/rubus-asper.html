Rubus asper

Rubus asper, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus asper is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
