Rivea luzonensis

Rivea luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant produces small, spherical berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Rivea luzonensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
