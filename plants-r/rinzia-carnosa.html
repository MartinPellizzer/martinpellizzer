Rinzia carnosa

Rinzia carnosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf rinzia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Rinzia carnosa is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
