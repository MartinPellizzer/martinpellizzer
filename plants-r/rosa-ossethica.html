Rosa ossethica

Rosa ossethica, commonly known as the Ossetian rose, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink or red, with a fragrant scent. Rosa ossethica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.
