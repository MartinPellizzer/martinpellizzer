Rosa californica var. chamissoniana

Rosa californica var. chamissoniana, commonly known as the Chamisson's California rose, is a species native to coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically 2 to 4 centimeters in diameter and are followed by red, fuzzy fruits called hips.

This variety is well adapted to coastal climates, tolerating salt spray and well-drained soils.
