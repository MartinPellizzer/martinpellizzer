Rosa canina f. normalis

Rosa canina f. normalis, commonly known as the common dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing numerous seeds. These hips are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine.

Rosa canina f. normalis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued for its ecological benefits.
