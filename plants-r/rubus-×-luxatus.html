Rubus × luxatus

Rubus × luxatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits characteristics of both raspberries and blackberries.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of its parent species. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive silvery underside, adding to its ornamental value.

The plant is valued for its hardiness, adaptability, and edible fruits, making it a popular choice for both cultivation and ecological restoration.
