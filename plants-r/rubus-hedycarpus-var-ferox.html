Rubus hedycarpus var. ferox

Rubus hedycarpus var. ferox, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its serrated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible but have a strong, tart flavor. Its stems and leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
