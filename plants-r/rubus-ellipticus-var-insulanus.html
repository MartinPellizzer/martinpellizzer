Rubus ellipticus var. insulanus

Rubus ellipticus var. insulanus, commonly known as the island blackberry, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically black when mature. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
