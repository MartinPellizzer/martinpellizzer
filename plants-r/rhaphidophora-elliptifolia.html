Rhaphidophora elliptifolia

Rhaphidophora elliptifolia, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers followed by edible tubers resembling arrowheads. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible roots.

Rhaphidophora elliptifolia is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
