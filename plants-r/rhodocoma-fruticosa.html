Rhodocoma fruticosa

Rhodocoma fruticosa, commonly known as the golden bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant yellow blooms.

Rhodocoma fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
