Rhododendron reginaldii

Rhododendron reginaldii, commonly known as the Reginald's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like China, India, and Myanmar.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates with high humidity. It is often found in forest understories, where it forms dense thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
