Rubus bracteosus var. erubescens

Rubus bracteosus var. erubescens, commonly known as the scarlet bramble, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
