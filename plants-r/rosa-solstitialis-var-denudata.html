Rosa solstitialis var. denudata

Rosa solstitialis var. denudata, commonly known as the naked-stem rose, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval leaflets.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
