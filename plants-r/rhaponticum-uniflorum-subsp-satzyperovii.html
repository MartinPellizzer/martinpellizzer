Rhaponticum uniflorum subsp. satzyperovii

Rhaponticum uniflorum subsp.

satzyperovii, commonly known as the Siberian rhapontic, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Central Asia. It features a robust root system and exhibits a rosette growth habit with large, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower per stem, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its potential health benefits.
