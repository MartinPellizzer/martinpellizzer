Rhododendron praeteritum var. praeteritum

Rhododendron praeteritum var. praeteritum, commonly known as the purple-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that displays a striking purplish hue, particularly in the autumn. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
