Rytidocarpus moricandioides

Rytidocarpus moricandioides, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant is native to New Zealand and thrives in humid, temperate climates. Its fronds are typically bright green and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Rytidocarpus moricandioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
