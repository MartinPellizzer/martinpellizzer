Rubus serpens subsp. longiglandulosus

Rubus serpens subsp.

longiglandulosus, commonly known as the long-glandular blackberry, is a species of brambles native to southeastern North America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and its ability to form dense thickets.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and hairy surfaces. Its flowers are pink to white, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that ripens to a dark purple color.

This subspecies is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as stream banks and forest edges.
