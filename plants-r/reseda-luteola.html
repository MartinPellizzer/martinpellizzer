Reseda luteola

Reseda luteola, commonly known as common oxytropis, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a distinctive grayish-green color.

Reseda luteola is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
