Ranunculus ficaria f. subintegra

Ranunculus ficaria f. subintegra, commonly known as the white wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
