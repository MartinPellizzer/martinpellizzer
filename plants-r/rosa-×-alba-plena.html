Rosa × alba plena

Rosa × alba plena, commonly known as the double white rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa alba and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its full, fluffy blooms that are predominantly white with a slight pinkish hue. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Rosa × alba plena is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
