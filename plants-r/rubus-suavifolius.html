Rubus suavifolius

Rubus suavifolius, commonly known as sweetleaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are often reddish or black in color. Its flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
