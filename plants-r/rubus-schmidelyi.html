Rubus schmidelyi

Rubus schmidelyi, commonly known as Schmidel's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Rubus genus.
