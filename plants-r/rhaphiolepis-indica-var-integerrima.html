Rhaphiolepis indica var. integerrima

Rhaphiolepis indica var. integerrima, commonly known as the Chinese holly or redleaf holly, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, glossy leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, followed by small, orange-red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to gardens in temperate climates.
