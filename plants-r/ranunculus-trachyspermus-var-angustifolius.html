Ranunculus trachyspermus var. angustifolius

Ranunculus trachyspermus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color and finely serrated edges. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and herbal gardens.
