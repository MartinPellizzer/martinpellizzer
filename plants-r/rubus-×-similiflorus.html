Rubus × similiflorus

Rubus × similiflorus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus occidentalis (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow, and the fruit is a aggregate of small drupes, often reddish or black when ripe.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential for fruit production in cultivation.
