Rumex × propinquus

Rumex × propinquus, commonly known as the narrowleaf dock, is a hybrid plant species within the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils. Rumex × propinquus is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in other areas with high water content.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
