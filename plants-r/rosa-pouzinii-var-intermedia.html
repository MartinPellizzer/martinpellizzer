Rosa pouzinii var. intermedia

Rosa pouzinii var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne singly or in small clusters. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
