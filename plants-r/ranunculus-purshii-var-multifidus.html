Ranunculus purshii var. multifidus

Ranunculus purshii var. multifidus, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate in structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
