Rosa chomutoviensis

Rosa chomutoviensis, commonly known as the Czech rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Czech Republic and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

Rosa chomutoviensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
