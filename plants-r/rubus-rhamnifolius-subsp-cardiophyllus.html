Rubus rhamnifolius subsp. cardiophyllus

Rubus rhamnifolius subsp.

cardiophyllus, commonly known as the heart-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its dark green, serrated leaves that have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in some horticultural gardens.
