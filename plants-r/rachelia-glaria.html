Rachelia glaria

Rachelia glaria, commonly known as the gloria lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant has strap-like, evergreen leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Rachelia glaria is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
