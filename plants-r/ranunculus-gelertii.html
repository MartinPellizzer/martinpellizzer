Ranunculus gelertii

Ranunculus gelertii, commonly known as the Gelert's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many species of buttercups and crowfoots. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
