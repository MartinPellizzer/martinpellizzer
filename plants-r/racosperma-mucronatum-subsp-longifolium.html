Racosperma mucronatum subsp. longifolium

Racosperma mucronatum subsp.

longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved racosperma, is a shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often glandular stems.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Its leaves are compound, with multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
