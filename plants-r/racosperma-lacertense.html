Racosperma lacertense

Racosperma lacertense, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area. It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in degraded landscapes.
