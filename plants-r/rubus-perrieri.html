Rubus perrieri

Rubus perrieri, commonly known as the Perrieri raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring.

The fruit is edible and has a flavor similar to other raspberries, though it is less commonly cultivated.
