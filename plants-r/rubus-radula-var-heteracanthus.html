Rubus radula var. heteracanthus

Rubus radula var. heteracanthus, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry bush, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically red or black when mature, and are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
