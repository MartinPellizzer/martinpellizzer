Rubus hedycarpus var. amiantinus

Rubus hedycarpus var. amiantinus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-veined leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Botanically, it is a shrub with a complex inflorescence structure and is closely related to other members of the Rubus species.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
