Rhododendron candidapiculatum

Rhododendron candidapiculatum, commonly known as the white-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and oval-shaped, providing a lush backdrop for its delicate blossoms.

Rhododendron candidapiculatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
