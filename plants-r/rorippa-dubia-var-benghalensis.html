Rorippa dubia var. benghalensis

Rorippa dubia var. benghalensis, commonly known as the Indian watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in wet or aquatic environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often submerged or floating, and small white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces small, elongated siliques containing numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich, waterlogged soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
