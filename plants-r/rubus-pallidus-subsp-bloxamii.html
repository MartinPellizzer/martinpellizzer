Rubus pallidus subsp. bloxamii

Rubus pallidus subsp.

bloxamii, commonly known as the Bloxam's bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruits are edible berries that are generally tart but can be used in jams and preserves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and paler coloration compared to other Rubus species.
