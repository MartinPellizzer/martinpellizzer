Ranunculus chaerophyllos subsp. canutii

Ranunculus chaerophyllos subsp.

canutii, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces numerous small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with high-altitude conditions.
