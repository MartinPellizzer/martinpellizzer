Reynosia truncata

Reynosia truncata, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and turn bright red in the fall. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and are followed by orange-red berries.

Reynosia truncata is well adapted to coastal and mountainous environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
