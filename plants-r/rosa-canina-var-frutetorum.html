Rosa canina var. frutetorum

Rosa canina var. frutetorum, commonly known as the dog rose or shrub rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnate leaves, and fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright orange-red hips, which are the fruit and contain seeds. It is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Rosa canina var. frutetorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
