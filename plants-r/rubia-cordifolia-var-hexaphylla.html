Rubia cordifolia var. hexaphylla

Rubia cordifolia var. hexaphylla, commonly known as the hexaphylla balm or heart-leaved balm, is a variant of the common wood betony.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue-green hue. Its flowers are typically pale blue to purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
