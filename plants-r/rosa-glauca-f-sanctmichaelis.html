Rosa glauca f. sanctmichaelis

Rosa glauca f. sanctmichaelis, commonly known as the Sanct Michael's rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa glauca species, characterized by its distinct floral morphology.

This cultivar features pale blue to white flowers with a faint glaucous bloom, giving it a unique appearance. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and thrives in cooler, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorned stems and dark green, ovate leaves.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after addition to gardens and botanical collections.
