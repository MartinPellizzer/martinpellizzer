Ricinocarpus infestus

Ricinocarpus infestus, commonly known as the stinkwood tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters in height, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and dense canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, oblong seeds.

It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
