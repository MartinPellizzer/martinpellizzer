Ranunculus moellendorffii

Ranunculus moellendorffii, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that typically range from white to pink or yellow. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that grow underwater or in shallow water, aiding in its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat. Its roots are fibrous and often form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Ranunculus moellendorffii is an important species for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
