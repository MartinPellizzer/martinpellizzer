Rubus nemorosus f. heteracanthus

Rubus nemorosus f. heteracanthus, commonly known as the variable bramble, is a botanical variant of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated margins and a hairy underside.

This form exhibits variability in leaf shape and fruit size, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
