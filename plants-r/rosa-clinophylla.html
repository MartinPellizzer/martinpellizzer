Rosa clinophylla

Rosa clinophylla, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a clustered pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Rosa clinophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense ground cover.
