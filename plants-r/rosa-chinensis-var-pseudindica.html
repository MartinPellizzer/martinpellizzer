Rosa chinensis var. pseudindica

Rosa chinensis var. pseudindica, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a species native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring multiple stems covered in thorns. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical environments, making it a popular ornamental species in horticulture.
