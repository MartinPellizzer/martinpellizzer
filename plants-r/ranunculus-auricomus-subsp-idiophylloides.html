Ranunculus auricomus subsp. idiophylloides

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

idiophylloides, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent, shield-shaped sepals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
