Rubus longiflorens

Rubus longiflorens, commonly known as the long-flowered blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It typically grows in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils.

The flowers are pale green to white, with five petals and numerous stamens, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
