Rubus questieri var. fallax

Rubus questieri var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus questieri species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
