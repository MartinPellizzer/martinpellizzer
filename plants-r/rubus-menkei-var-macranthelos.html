Rubus menkei var. macranthelos

Rubus menkei var. macranthelos, commonly known as the large-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, similar to other Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
