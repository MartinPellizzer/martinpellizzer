Rhododendron trichocladum

Rhododendron trichocladum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and shrublands, preferring cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often covered with fine, hairy structures that help reduce water loss.

Rhododendron trichocladum plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
