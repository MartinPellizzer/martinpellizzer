Rhododendron cuneifolium var. subspathulatum

Rhododendron cuneifolium var. subspathulatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf alpine rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
