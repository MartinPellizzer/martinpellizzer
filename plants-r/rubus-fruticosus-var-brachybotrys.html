Rubus fruticosus var. brachybotrys

Rubus fruticosus var. brachybotrys, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated margin and a hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, followed by dark purple to black berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hedges or as a ground cover.
