Rubus tenuihabitus

Rubus tenuihabitus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rubus genus.
