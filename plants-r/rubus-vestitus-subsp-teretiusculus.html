Rubus vestitus subsp. teretiusculus

Rubus vestitus subsp.

teretiusculus, commonly known as the slender bramble, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals, and appear in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
