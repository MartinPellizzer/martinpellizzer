Rubus insuetus microgene subsimilis

Rubus insuetus microgene subsimilis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and wild areas.
