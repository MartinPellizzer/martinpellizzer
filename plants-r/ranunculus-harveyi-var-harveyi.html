Ranunculus harveyi var. harveyi

Ranunculus harveyi var. harveyi, commonly known as the Harvey's buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to eight petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often in meadows or along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

This variety is native to parts of North America and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
