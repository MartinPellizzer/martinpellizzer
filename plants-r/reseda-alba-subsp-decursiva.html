Reseda alba subsp. decursiva

Reseda alba subsp.

decursiva, commonly known as white knotgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae. It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats on the ground.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is typically found in disturbed soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
