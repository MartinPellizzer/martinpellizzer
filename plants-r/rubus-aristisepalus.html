Rubus aristisepalus

Rubus aristisepalus, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
