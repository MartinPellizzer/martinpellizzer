Rubus apetalus

Rubus apetalus, commonly known as the naked-stalk raspberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of petals, giving it its scientific name, and it produces small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats. Its stems are covered in thorns, and its leaves are compound with three to five leaflets.

The fruit is usually red or yellow and is used in jams and desserts.
