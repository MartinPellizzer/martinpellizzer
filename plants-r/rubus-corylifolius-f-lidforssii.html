Rubus corylifolius f. lidforssii

Rubus corylifolius f. lidforssii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to China.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that resemble those of the hazelnut, giving it its specific epithet "corylifolius.". The plant produces dark red to black berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus family.
