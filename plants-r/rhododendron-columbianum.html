Rhododendron columbianum

Rhododendron columbianum, commonly known as the Columbian rhododendron, is a species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils within mountainous regions. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its flowers are showy, ranging from pink to red, and bloom in early spring.

Rhododendron columbianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
