Rubus × amabiliflorus

Rubus × amabiliflorus, commonly known as the sweet-flowered raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
