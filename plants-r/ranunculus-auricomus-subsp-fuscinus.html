Ranunculus auricomus subsp. fuscinus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

fuscinus, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals, which are often mistaken for true petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, hairy texture.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
