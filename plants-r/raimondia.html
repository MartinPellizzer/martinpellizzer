Raimondia

Raimondia, commonly known as the golden groundcherry, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the genus Raimondia in the nightshade family, Solanaceae. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are often mistaken for tomatoes due to their similar appearance. These fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Raimondia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses in certain regions.
