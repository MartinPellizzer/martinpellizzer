Rubus nilagiricus

Rubus nilagiricus, commonly known as the Nilagiri blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
