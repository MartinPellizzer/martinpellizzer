Rosa rubrifolia var. dispersa

Rosa rubrifolia var. dispersa, commonly known as the spreading redleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of China.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which becomes more pronounced in autumn, and its clusters of fragrant pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, making it suitable for use as a ground cover or in rock gardens. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, silvery hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
