Rosa astrictula

Rosa astrictula, commonly known as the narrowleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color, containing the seeds. Rosa astrictula is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
