Rosa vinacea

Rosa vinacea, commonly known as the red sandalwood rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, deep red flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Rosa vinacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
