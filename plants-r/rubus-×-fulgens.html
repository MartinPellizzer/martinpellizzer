Rubus × fulgens

Rubus × fulgens, commonly known as the firecracker raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, often with a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
