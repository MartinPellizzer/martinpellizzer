Rhododendron porrosquameum

Rhododendron porrosquameum, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a slight pubescence.

Rhododendron porrosquameum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
