Rubus nepalensis

Rubus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, temperate forests at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is often found growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
