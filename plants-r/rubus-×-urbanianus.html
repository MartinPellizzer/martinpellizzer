Rubus × urbanianus

Rubus × urbanianus, commonly known as the Chicago raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus chinensis and Rubus idaeus.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of genetic variability in its botanical traits.
