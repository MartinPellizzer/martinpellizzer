Rhododendron roseotinctum

Rhododendron roseotinctum, commonly known as the pink-tinted rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, often with a distinctive white or pale pink throat. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron roseotinctum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
