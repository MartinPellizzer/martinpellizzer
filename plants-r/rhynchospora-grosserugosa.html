Rhynchospora grosserugosa

Rhynchospora grosserugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, ribbed leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, preferring nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in dense spikes, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Rhynchospora grosserugosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed wetland environments.
