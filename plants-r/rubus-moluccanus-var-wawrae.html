Rubus moluccanus var. wawrae

Rubus moluccanus var. wawrae, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a shrub native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically clustered together. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
