Rosa stylosa var. palatina

Rosa stylosa var. palatina, commonly known as the Palatine rose, is a shrub-like species native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five to seven petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces hip fruits that are red or orange, containing several seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
