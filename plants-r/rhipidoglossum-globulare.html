Rhipidoglossum globulare

Rhipidoglossum globulare, commonly known as the globular moss, is a small, compact moss that forms dense, rounded cushions.

It belongs to the family Pottiaceae and is characterized by its tightly packed, globular growth habit. The leaves are short and often curved, with a distinct costa that extends to the leaf apex. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on soil or rock surfaces.

This moss is notable for its ability to retain moisture and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
