Rubus helleri

Rubus helleri, commonly known as Heller's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
