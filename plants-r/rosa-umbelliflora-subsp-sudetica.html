Rosa umbelliflora subsp. sudetica

Rosa umbelliflora subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic shrub rose, is a perennial shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
