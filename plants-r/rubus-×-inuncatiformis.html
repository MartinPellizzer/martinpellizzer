Rubus × inuncatiformis

Rubus × inuncatiformis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry-loganberry.

It is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a unique combination of traits. Its berries are typically large, dark red to black in color, and have a tart flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value in gardens.
