Rosa glauca subvar. falcata

Rosa glauca subvar. falcata, commonly known as the bluebush rose, is a subspecies native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and dense foliage with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine layer of fuzzy hair.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
