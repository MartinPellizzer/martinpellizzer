Rubus chapmanianus

Rubus chapmanianus, commonly known as Chapman's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
