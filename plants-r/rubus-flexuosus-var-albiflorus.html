Rubus flexuosus var. albiflorus

Rubus flexuosus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries in the summer. Its fruit is similar to other blackberries but has a milder flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
