Rosa histrix

Rosa histrix, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces oval, greenish-yellow fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa histrix thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine make it a notable species in botanical studies.
