Rubus × ancophiloides

Rubus × ancophiloides, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in local cuisines.
