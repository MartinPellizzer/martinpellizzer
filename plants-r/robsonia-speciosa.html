Robsonia speciosa

Robsonia speciosa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a mix of yellow and green hues, which help it attract specific pollinators.

Robsonia speciosa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
