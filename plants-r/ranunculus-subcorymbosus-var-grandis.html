Ranunculus subcorymbosus var. grandis

Ranunculus subcorymbosus var. grandis, commonly known as the large-flowered water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features delicate, floating leaves that are palmately divided and often covered in a waxy coating. Its showy, yellow flowers bloom in early spring and are composed of numerous petal-like sepals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic life.
