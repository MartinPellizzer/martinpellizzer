Rhus simarubifolia var. taitensis

Rhus simarubifolia var. taitensis, commonly known as the Taiti buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are often red or black when mature. It thrives in coastal and lowland habitats, often in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
