Rubus imbricatus var. anomalus

Rubus imbricatus var. anomalus, commonly known as the anomalous blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color, typically in late summer. Its botanical features include a complex root system and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

This species is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to different environmental conditions.
