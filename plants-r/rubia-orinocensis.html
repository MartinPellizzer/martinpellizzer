Rubia orinocensis

Rubia orinocensis, commonly known as Orinoco madder, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. It produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The roots of Rubia orinocensis contain a red dye, historically used for natural coloring in textiles.
