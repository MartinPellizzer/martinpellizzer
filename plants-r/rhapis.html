Rhapis

Rhapis, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Arecaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Rhapis excelsa, which is widely cultivated for its ornamental value. These plants are native to Southeast Asia and are characterized by their slender, clustered stems and feather-like leaves. Rhapis species are evergreen, thrive in shady environments, and are relatively low-maintenance, making them popular in tropical and subtropical gardens.

They also produce small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fruits, though the latter are rarely consumed by humans.
