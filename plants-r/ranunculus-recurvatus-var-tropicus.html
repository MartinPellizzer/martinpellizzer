Ranunculus recurvatus var. tropicus

Ranunculus recurvatus var. tropicus, commonly known as the tropical buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape and a hairy stem. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a striking addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.
