Renealmia ixioides

Renealmia ixioides, commonly known as the arrowleaf watergrass, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in shallow, still waters.

Renealmia ixioides plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
