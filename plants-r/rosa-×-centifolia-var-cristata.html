Rosa × centifolia var. cristata

Rosa × centifolia var. cristata, commonly known as the "Cristata Rose," is a hybrid rose variety characterized by its unique, flattened, and often crinkled petals.

It is a cultivar of the damask rose, known for its fragrant, pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in perfumery and horticulture.
