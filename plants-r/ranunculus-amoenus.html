Ranunculus amoenus

Ranunculus amoenus, commonly known as the pretty buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, with palmate leaves divided into 5–9 lobes. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, have numerous stamens and a prominent central pistil.

Ranunculus amoenus prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
