Reseda fruticulosa var. valentina

Reseda fruticulosa var. valentina, commonly known as the Valentina catchfly, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with numerous small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and extended flowering period.
