Rhexia frigidula

Rhexia frigidula, commonly known as the cold mountain bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils within shaded forest understories. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rhexia frigidula is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
