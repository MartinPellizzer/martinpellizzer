Rosa mollialbida subsp. conocarpa

Rosa mollialbida subsp.

conocarpa, commonly known as the Andean rose, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a rounded or serrated margin.

The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. Its stems are thorny, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
