Rhaphidophora kunstleri

Rhaphidophora kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstler's arrowroot, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by a spadix and a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Rhaphidophora kunstleri is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
