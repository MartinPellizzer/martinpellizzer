Ranunculus balikunensis

Ranunculus balikunensis, commonly known as the Balikun buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Xinjiang, China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes a variety of herbaceous plants known for their showy flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with a distinctive cup-shaped structure and numerous petals.

Ranunculus balikunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local biodiversity of its habitat.
