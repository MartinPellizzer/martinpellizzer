Ranunculus pumilio var. politus

Ranunculus pumilio var. politus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers with prominent white veins, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has a delicate, hairy stem that supports its clustered, spoon-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in high-altitude flora.
