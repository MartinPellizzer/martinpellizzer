Rubus plicatus f. incisus

Rubus plicatus f. incisus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

The leaves are deeply lobed and serrated, giving the plant a rugged appearance. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. This plant is native to parts of Asia and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued both for its ornamental qualities and its fruit.
