Rubus zwanzigeri

Rubus zwanzigeri, commonly known as the Zwanzigeri blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America, specifically found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shrublands and forest edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure species within the Rubus genus.
