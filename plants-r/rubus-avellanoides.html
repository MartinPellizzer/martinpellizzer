Rubus avellanoides

Rubus avellanoides, commonly known as the blackberry lichen, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is native to various regions in the Americas. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
