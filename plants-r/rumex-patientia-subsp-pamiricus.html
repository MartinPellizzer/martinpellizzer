Rumex patientia subsp. pamiricus

Rumex patientia subsp.

pamiricus, commonly known as the Pamir dock, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, broad leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are greenish-white in color, arranged in a terminal panicle. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family, often found in alpine and subalpine zones.
