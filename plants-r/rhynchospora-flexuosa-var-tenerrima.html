Rhynchospora flexuosa var. tenerrima

Rhynchospora flexuosa var. tenerrima, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Rhynchospora flexuosa var. tenerrima is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
