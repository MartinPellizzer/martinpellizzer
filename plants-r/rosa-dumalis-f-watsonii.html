Rosa dumalis f. watsonii

Rosa dumalis f. watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's wild rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by birds and other animals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
