Rosa × reversa var. parceglandulosa

Rosa × reversa var. parceglandulosa, commonly known as the double-flowered rose, is a hybrid species characterized by its dense, layered blooms.

It features large, showy flowers with multiple layers of petals, giving it a full, lush appearance. The plant typically has thornless stems and dark green, glossy foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
