Rubus × macrocardiophyllus

Rubus × macrocardiophyllus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically growing up to 3 meters in height. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the berries are highly aromatic and edible.

This species is valued for its fruit's flavor and is often cultivated for both commercial and culinary purposes.
