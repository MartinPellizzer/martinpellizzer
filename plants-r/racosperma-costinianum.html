Racosperma costinianum

Racosperma costinianum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and divided into small leaflets. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, and it produces flat, elongated pods that contain seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
