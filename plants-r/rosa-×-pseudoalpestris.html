Rosa × pseudoalpestris

Rosa × pseudoalpestris, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of single or semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features dark green, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Rosa × pseudoalpestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, capable of surviving in colder climates.
