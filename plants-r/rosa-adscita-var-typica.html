Rosa adscita var. typica

Rosa adscita var. typica, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit, with thorned stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of 5-7 oval leaflets.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a decorative plant in landscapes.
