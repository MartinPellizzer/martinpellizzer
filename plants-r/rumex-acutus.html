Rumex acutus

Rumex acutus, commonly known as sharpleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, ribbed stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a sharp, pointed tip. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

The leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some cultures.
