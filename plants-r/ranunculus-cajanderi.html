Ranunculus cajanderi

Ranunculus cajanderi, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with lobes that are typically deeply divided, and its flowers have five to eight petal-like sepals. It produces small, green berries that contain seeds, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Ranunculus cajanderi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
