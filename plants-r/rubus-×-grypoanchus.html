Rubus × grypoanchus

Rubus × grypoanchus, commonly known as the Evergreen Blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (Evergreen Blackberry) and Rubus glaucus (Red Blackberry).

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial purposes.
