Rosa eglanteria var. plena

Rosa eglanteria var. plena, commonly known as the double-leaf rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa eglanteria species.

It is characterized by its dense, double-layered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant has a thorny stem and produces elliptical, serrated leaves with a distinctive dark green color. It is typically grown as a shrub and is valued for its ornamental foliage and floral display.

This variety is well-suited for garden cultivation and requires well-drained soil and full sun.
