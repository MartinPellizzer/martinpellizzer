Ranunculus glacialis subsp. glacialis

Ranunculus glacialis subsp.

glacialis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in mountain meadows and tundra environments.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh climates and is a common indicator species of alpine ecosystems.
