Rubus radula var. epilosus

Rubus radula var. epilosus, commonly known as the soft-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are rounded and covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

Its berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and pies.
