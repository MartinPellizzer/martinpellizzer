Rothrockia umbellata

Rothrockia umbellata, commonly known as the rothrock's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Rothrockia umbellata is a member of the Campanulaceae family, which includes many familiar bellflowers.
