Rapuntium reinwardtianum

Rapuntium reinwardtianum, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seeds for wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a resilient and adaptable species within its native habitat.
