Rhododendron doshongense

Rhododendron doshongense, commonly known as the Doshong rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, primarily found in Nepal and northern India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features large, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and dark green in color. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Rhododendron doshongense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
