Rollinia parviflora

Rollinia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered rollinia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that matures in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
