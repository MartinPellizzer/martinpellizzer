Richardella unmackiana

Richardella unmackiana, commonly known as the New Zealand rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is considered a valuable species in New Zealand's native flora.
