Rosa canina var. bihariensis

Rosa canina var. bihariensis, commonly known as the Kashmiri dog rose, is a variant of the common rose native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in spring, followed by orange-red hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
