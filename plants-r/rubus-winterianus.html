Rubus winterianus

Rubus winterianus, commonly known as the winter raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen in late winter or early spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberries and blackberries.
