Ruppia didyma

Ruppia didyma, commonly known as the common pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, green fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Ruppia didyma is adapted to tolerate a wide range of water conditions, making it a common species in ponds and lakes.
