Ranunculus pumilio var. pumilio

Ranunculus pumilio var. pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers with 5 to 13 petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are covered in a soft, woolly texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in alpine botany and horticulture.
