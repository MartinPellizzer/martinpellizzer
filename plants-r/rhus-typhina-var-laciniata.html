Rhus typhina var. laciniata

Rhus typhina var. laciniata, commonly known as staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are deeply lobed and often have a notched or serrated appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are attractive to birds. Its bark is thick and scaly, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
