Rhus metopioides

Rhus metopioides, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a rough, slightly hairy texture.

Rhus metopioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
