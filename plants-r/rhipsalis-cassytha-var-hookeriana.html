Rhipsalis cassytha var. hookeriana

Rhipsalis cassytha var. hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its long, thin, often pendulous stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers, which are typically borne in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Despite its appearance, it is not a true mistletoe and does not parasitize its host plant.
