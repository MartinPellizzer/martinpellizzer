Rivina purpurascens

Rivina purpurascens, commonly known as the redroot false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rivina purpurascens is often found in moist, sunny habitats like woodland edges and wetlands.
