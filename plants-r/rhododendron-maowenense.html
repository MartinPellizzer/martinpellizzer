Rhododendron maowenense

Rhododendron maowenense, commonly known as the Maowen rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan Province.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
