Rubus beccarii

Rubus beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

Rubus beccarii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
