Ranunculus intermedius

Ranunculus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, with 5 to 15 petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Ranunculus intermedius is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
