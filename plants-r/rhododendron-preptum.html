Rhododendron preptum

Rhododendron preptum, commonly known as the purple leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage and clusters of pink to lilac flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Rhododendron preptum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
