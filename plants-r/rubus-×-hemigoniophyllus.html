Rubus × hemigoniophyllus

Rubus × hemigoniophyllus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (the red raspberry). The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and produces thorny stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it bears aggregate fruits that range from red to black in color.

This plant is valued for its edible berries, which are used in culinary applications and jams.
