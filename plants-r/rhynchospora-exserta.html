Rhynchospora exserta

Rhynchospora exserta, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale brown flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct sheathing base.

Rhynchospora exserta is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
