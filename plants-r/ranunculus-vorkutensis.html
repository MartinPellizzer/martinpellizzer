Ranunculus vorkutensis

Ranunculus vorkutensis, commonly known as the Vorkutinskii buttercup, is a rare flowering plant native to the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous environments and has compound leaves with serrated edges. It is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
