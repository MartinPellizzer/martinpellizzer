Ranunculus fascicularis var. apricus

Ranunculus fascicularis var. apricus, commonly known as the spring buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with 5-10 petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-30 cm, with slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its blooming period spans from early spring to late summer, making it a notable early-flowering plant.
