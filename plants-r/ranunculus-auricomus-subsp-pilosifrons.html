Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pilosifrons

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pilosifrons, commonly known as the hairy-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals and hairy stems and leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, glandular hairs that cover the foliage, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
