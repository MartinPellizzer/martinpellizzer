Rubus moluccanus var. insignis

Rubus moluccanus var. insignis, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit develops into a red, hairy berry.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
