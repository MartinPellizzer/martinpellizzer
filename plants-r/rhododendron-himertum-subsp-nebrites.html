Rhododendron himertum subsp. nebrites

Rhododendron himertum subsp.

nebrites, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
