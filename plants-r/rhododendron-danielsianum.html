Rhododendron danielsianum

Rhododendron danielsianum, commonly known as the Daniel's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist mountainous environments. The plant features striking clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
