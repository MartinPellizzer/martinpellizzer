Rheum iranshahrii

Rheum iranshahrii, commonly known as Iranian rhubarb, is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, and its tall, reddish stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with its rhizomes containing high concentrations of anthraquinone compounds. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its unique botanical features and potential medicinal uses, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
