Rosa × damascena variegata

Rosa × damascena variegata, commonly known as the variegated damask rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its variegated foliage, which features a mix of green and white or yellow hues, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in perfumery, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and aromatic purposes.
