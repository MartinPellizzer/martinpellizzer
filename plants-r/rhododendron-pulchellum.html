Rhododendron pulchellum

Rhododendron pulchellum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a flowering shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Rhododendron pulchellum is relatively compact, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces or as a container plant.
