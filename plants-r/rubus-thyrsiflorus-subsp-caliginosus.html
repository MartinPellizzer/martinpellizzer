Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp. caliginosus

Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp.

caliginosus, commonly known as the blackberry lichen, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of understory ecosystems.
