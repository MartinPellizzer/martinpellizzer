Rhamnus costata var. nambuana

Rhamnus costata var. nambuana, commonly known as the Nambu Japanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by black, egg-shaped berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
