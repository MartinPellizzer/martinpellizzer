Rheum aplostachyum

Rheum aplostachyum, commonly known as the Armenian rhubarb, is a perennial plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, which are typically dark green with a prominent midrib. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer, which eventually give way to reddish-brown seeds. Native to the Caucasus region, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
