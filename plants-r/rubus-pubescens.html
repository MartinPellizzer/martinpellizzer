Rubus pubescens

Rubus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Rubus pubescens is known for its adaptability and can grow in a variety of soil types.
