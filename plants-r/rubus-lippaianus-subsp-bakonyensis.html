Rubus lippaianus subsp. bakonyensis

Rubus lippaianus subsp.

bakonyensis, commonly known as the Bakony raspberry, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Basin. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in regional flora studies.
