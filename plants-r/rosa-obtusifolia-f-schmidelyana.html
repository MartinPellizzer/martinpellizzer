Rosa obtusifolia f. schmidelyana

Rosa obtusifolia f. schmidelyana, commonly known as the Schmidelyana form of the blunt-leaved rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals and a subtle fragrance. This form is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticultural gardens.
