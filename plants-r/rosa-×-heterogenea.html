Rosa × heterogenea

Rosa × heterogenea, commonly known as the everblooming shrub rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa multiflora.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compact growth habit, making it suitable for hedging and ornamental use. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers throughout the growing season. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing year-round visual interest.

Rosa × heterogenea is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates, and is known for its disease resistance and low maintenance requirements.
