Rhododendron pubigerum

Rhododendron pubigerum, commonly known as the hairy rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are often dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron pubigerum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
