Rhipsalis cassytha

Rhipsalis cassytha, commonly known as the silver mist cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like stems that can grow in dense, hanging clusters, resembling a curtain of silver threads. The plant lacks true leaves, relying instead on its stems for photosynthesis and water absorption. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the winter months, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a prized plant among cactus enthusiasts.
