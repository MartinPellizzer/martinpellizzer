Rubus maassii subsp. mercicus

Rubus maassii subsp.

mercicus, commonly known as the Maasai raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
