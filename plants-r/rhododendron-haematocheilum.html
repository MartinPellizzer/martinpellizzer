Rhododendron haematocheilum

Rhododendron haematocheilum, commonly known as the blood-chamber rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to violet, often with a distinctive red throat. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at high elevations, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
