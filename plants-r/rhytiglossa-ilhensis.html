Rhytiglossa ilhensis

Rhytiglossa ilhensis, commonly known as the Ilhensian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and are pollinated by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
