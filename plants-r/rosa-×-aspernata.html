Rosa × aspernata

Rosa × aspernata, commonly known as the Austrian rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa gallica and Rosa damascena.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include showy, single or semi-double flowers with five to seven petals.

Rosa × aspernata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
