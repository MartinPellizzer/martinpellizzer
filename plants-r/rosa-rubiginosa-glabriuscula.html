Rosa rubiginosa glabriuscula

Rosa rubiginosa glabriuscula, commonly known as the rough rose or bristly rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, usually pink or red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, edible hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Rosa rubiginosa glabriuscula is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its historical use in traditional medicine.
