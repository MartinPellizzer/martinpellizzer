Richardsonia rosea

Richardsonia rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with its stems forming a rosette-like structure. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Richardsonia rosea thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky or alpine habitats.
