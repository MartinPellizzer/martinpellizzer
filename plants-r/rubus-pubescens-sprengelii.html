Rubus pubescens sprengelii

Rubus pubescens sprengelii, commonly known as the Sprengel's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including regions of China and the Russian Far East. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that develop from its flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
