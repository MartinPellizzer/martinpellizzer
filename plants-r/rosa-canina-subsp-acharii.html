Rosa canina subsp. acharii

Rosa canina subsp.

acharii, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It produces small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is a red, hairy rosehip that matures in autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
