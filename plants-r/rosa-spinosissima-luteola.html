Rosa spinosissima luteola

Rosa spinosissima luteola, commonly known as the yellow prickly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are orange-red in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
