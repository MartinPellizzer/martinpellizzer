Rosa mollis f. rectellidens

Rosa mollis f. rectellidens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa mollis species, which is native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and smaller, fragrant flowers compared to other rose varieties. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and compound foliage. Its flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of garden conditions.
