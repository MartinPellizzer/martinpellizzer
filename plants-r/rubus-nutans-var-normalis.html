Rubus nutans var. normalis

Rubus nutans var. normalis, commonly known as the nodding blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small drupelets, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins and a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are pale green with five sepals and are typically nodding, giving the plant its common name.
