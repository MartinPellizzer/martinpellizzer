Rubus × angustarum

Rubus × angustarum, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus idaeus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated in gardens and commercial settings.
