Rosa polae

Rosa polae, commonly known as the Polish rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with arching stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include a woody stem, compound leaves, and showy, five-petaled flowers.

Rosa polae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
