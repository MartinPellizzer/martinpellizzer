Rosa episcopalis

Rosa episcopalis, commonly known as the bishop's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist woodlands and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its deep pink to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa episcopalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
