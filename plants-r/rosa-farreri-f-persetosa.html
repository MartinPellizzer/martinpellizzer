Rosa farreri f. persetosa

Rosa farreri f. persetosa, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa farreri species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticultural traditions.
