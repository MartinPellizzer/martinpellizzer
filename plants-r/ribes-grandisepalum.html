Ribes grandisepalum

Ribes grandisepalum, commonly known as the large-sepal currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. The plant features clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn red in the fall.

The berries, which are edible, are small and dark purple when mature.
