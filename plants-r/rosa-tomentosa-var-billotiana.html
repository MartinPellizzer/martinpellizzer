Rosa tomentosa var. billotiana

Rosa tomentosa var. billotiana, commonly known as the silky rose or billot's rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with thorny stems and is well adapted to the arid and coastal conditions of its native habitat. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The leaves are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
