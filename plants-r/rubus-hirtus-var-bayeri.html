Rubus hirtus var. bayeri

Rubus hirtus var. bayeri, commonly known as the Bayery raspberry, is a variant of the bramble plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to white, and the fruit is a red, hairy raspberry that ripens in late summer. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

It has a shallow root system and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
