Rosa × paui

Rosa × paui, commonly known as the Pauli rose, is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth, thorny stems, and large, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant is native to China and has been cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden borders and hedges.

Rosa × paui is also valued for its disease resistance and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
