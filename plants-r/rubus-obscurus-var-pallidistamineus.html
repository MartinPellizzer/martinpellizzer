Rubus obscurus var. pallidistamineus

Rubus obscurus var. pallidistamineus, commonly known as the pale-staminal blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its dark, often thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pale yellow to green, with five sepals and five petals. The fruit is a small, aggregate berry that develops from the fusion of multiple drupelets.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
