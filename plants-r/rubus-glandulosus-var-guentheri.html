Rubus glandulosus var. guentheri

Rubus glandulosus var. guentheri, commonly known as Guenther's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries that ripen in the fall. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and covered in fine, glandular hairs. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
