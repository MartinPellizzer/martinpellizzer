Rubus meierottii

Rubus meierottii, commonly known as Meier's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
