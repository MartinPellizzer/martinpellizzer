Raphanus sativus var. chinensis

Raphanus sativus var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese radish or daikon, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, white or pale yellow root, which can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in a raceme, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are edible and often used in Asian cuisine, while the root is prized for its crisp texture and mild flavor.

The plant thrives in cool climates and is commonly cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
