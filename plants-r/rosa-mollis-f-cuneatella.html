Rosa mollis f. cuneatella

Rosa mollis f. cuneatella, commonly known as the cuneate form of the soft rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

It features ovate to elliptical leaves with a distinct cuneate base, giving it a specialized appearance. This form is typically found in specific geographic regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, typical of the Rosa genus.

Its foliage is noted for its soft texture and dense growth habit, making it a desirable ornamental plant.
