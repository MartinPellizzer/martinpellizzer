Ranunculus fuscinus

Ranunculus fuscinus, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has a hairy stem and heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Ranunculus fuscinus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.
