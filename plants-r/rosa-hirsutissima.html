Rosa hirsutissima

Rosa hirsutissima, commonly known as the wooly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Russia, and Mongolia. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly gray-green foliage and fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Rosa hirsutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
