Ribes cucullatum var. andicola

Ribes cucullatum var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage of dark green leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

The leaves and berries are used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.
