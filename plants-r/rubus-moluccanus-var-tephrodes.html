Rubus moluccanus var. tephrodes

Rubus moluccanus var. tephrodes, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to the Moluccas and other parts of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, while the flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is edible, has a tart flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
