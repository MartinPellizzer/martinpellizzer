Rubus tereticaulis var. emarginatus

Rubus tereticaulis var. emarginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It has slender, thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark purple berry. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
