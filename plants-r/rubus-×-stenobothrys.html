Rubus × stenobothrys

Rubus × stenobothrys, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus leucodermis.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
