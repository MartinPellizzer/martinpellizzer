Rubus lindebergii f. floribundus

Rubus lindebergii f. floribundus, commonly known as the Lindberg's raspberry, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins and produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. It is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
