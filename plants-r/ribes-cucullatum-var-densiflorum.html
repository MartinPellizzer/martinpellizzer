Ribes cucullatum var. densiflorum

Ribes cucullatum var. densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, tart berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and are covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
