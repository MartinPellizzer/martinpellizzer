Rubus schleicheri var. acuminatus

Rubus schleicheri var. acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
