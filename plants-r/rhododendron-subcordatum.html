Rhododendron subcordatum

Rhododendron subcordatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron subcordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
