Robinia

Robinia, commonly known as the false acacia or yellowwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged and have a distinctive grayish-green color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow or cream in color, blooming in the spring. Robinia species are native to North America and are known for their hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

They are also valued for their ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
