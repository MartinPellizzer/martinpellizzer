Rubus erythrostemon var. latifolia

Rubus erythrostemon var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, hairy berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
