Rubus × militaris

Rubus × militaris, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, that are typically dark purple to black in color. These fruits are not only edible but also contain high levels of antioxidants and dietary fiber.

Rubus × militaris thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.
