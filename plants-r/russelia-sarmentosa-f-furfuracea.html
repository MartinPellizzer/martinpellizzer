Russelia sarmentosa f. furfuracea

Russelia sarmentosa f. furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy russelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a vigorous, trailing perennial that produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Russelia sarmentosa f. furfuracea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
