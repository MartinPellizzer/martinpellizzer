Rhododendron trichocladum var. longipilosum

Rhododendron trichocladum var. longipilosum, commonly known as the long-hair rhododendron, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and the flowers range in color from white to pink. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
