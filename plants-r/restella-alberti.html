Restella alberti

Restella alberti, commonly known as the Alberti's restella, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Restella alberti is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
