Rubus caesius subsp. villosicaulis

Rubus caesius subsp.

villosicaulis, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
