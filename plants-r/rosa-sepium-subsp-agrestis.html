Rosa sepium subsp. agrestis

Rosa sepium subsp.

agrestis, commonly known as the field rose, is a subspecies native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a shrub-like plant with arching stems that often grow along the ground or in low bushes.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and garden settings.
