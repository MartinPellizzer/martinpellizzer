Racosperma rothii

Racosperma rothii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, linear leaflets that give it a delicate appearance. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, typical of many wattles, and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Racosperma rothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
