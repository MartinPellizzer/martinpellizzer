Rhododendron kwangsiense var. kwangsiense

Rhododendron kwangsiense var. kwangsiense, commonly known as the Guangxi rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly in the Guangxi region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 800 and 1,500 meters, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
