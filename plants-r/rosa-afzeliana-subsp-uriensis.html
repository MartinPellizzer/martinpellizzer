Rosa afzeliana subsp. uriensis

Rosa afzeliana subsp.

uriensis, commonly known as the Uri rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
