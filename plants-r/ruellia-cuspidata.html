Ruellia cuspidata

Ruellia cuspidata, commonly known as the purpletop verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Ruellia cuspidata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
