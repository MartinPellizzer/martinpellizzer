Rubus dierschkeanus

Rubus dierschkeanus, commonly known as the Dierschkean bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, specifically found in Argentina and Chile. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and clusters of small white or pink flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The fruit is a aggregate berry, composed of small drupes, and is often consumed by various wildlife species.
