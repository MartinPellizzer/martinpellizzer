Rubus obscurus var. amoeniflorens

Rubus obscurus var. amoeniflorens, commonly known as the dark raspberry or sweet-flowered blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dark, glossy leaves and produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. The plant has a thorny stem structure typical of many Rubus species, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are valued for their tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.
