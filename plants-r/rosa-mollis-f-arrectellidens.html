Rosa mollis f. arrectellidens

Rosa mollis f. arrectellidens, commonly known as the upright form of the soft rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, soft foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a velvety texture, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
