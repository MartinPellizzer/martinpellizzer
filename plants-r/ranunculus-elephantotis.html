Ranunculus elephantotis

Ranunculus elephantotis, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

Native to South Africa, this plant is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with dark centers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant appearance.

Its foliage and flowers are also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
