Restio graminifolius

Restio graminifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Restio graminifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens.
