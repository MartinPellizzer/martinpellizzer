Ranunculus stojanovii

Ranunculus stojanovii, commonly known as the Stojanov's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, typically yellow or white petals. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces solitary, erect stems with small, cup-shaped flowers. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
