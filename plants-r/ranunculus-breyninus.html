Ranunculus breyninus

Ranunculus breyninus, commonly known as the Breyn's crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, white, or pink. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

Ranunculus breyninus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
