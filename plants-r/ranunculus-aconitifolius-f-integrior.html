Ranunculus aconitifolius f. integrior

Ranunculus aconitifolius f. integrior, commonly known as the smooth-leaved buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup species characterized by its smooth, unlobed leaves.

This plant is native to parts of Asia, particularly the Himalayas, and thrives in moist, shady environments. It produces showy, yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The leaves are simple and alternate, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
