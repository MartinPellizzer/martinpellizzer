Ranunculus bulbosus var. pomelianus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. pomelianus, commonly known as the Pomelian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive black center. The plant has a short, rhizomatous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland habitats.
