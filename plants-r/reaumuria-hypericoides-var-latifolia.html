Reaumuria hypericoides var. latifolia

Reaumuria hypericoides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf reaumuria, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its fleshy, berry-like fruits are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

It is well-suited to harsh environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
