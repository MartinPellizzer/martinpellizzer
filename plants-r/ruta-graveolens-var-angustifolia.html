Ruta graveolens var. angustifolia

Ruta graveolens var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf rue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, dark green, narrow leaves with a strong, pungent odor due to the presence of volatile oils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a decorative plant highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
