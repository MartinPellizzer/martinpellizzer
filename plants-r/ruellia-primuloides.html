Ruellia primuloides

Ruellia primuloides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Ruellia primuloides is well-suited for garden cultivation due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
