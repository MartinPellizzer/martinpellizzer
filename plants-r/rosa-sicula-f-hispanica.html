Rosa sicula f. hispanica

Rosa sicula f. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish rock rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, thorny stems and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
