Ruellia isophylla

Ruellia isophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments.

Ruellia isophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in a variety of conditions.
