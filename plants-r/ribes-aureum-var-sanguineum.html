Ribes aureum var. sanguineum

Ribes aureum var. sanguineum, commonly known as the crimson currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its foliage is typically dark green with a slightly glossy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
