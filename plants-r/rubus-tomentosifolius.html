Rubus tomentosifolius

Rubus tomentosifolius, commonly known as the woollyleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
