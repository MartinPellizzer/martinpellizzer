Rosa pimpinellifolia var. macropetala

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. macropetala, commonly known as the large-flowered rock rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters of five to seven. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven elliptic leaflets, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
