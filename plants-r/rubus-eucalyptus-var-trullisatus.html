Rubus eucalyptus var. trullisatus

Rubus eucalyptus var. trullisatus, commonly known as the Trullisatus blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
