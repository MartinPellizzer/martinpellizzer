Rubus tennesseanus

Rubus tennesseanus, commonly known as the Tennessee blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupelets. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in riparian zones. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are often used in local culinary traditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
