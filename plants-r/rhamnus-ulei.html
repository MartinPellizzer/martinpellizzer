Rhamnus ulei

Rhamnus ulei, commonly known as the Persian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically reddish when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Rhamnus ulei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
