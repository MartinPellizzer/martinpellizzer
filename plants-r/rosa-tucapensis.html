Rosa tucapensis

Rosa tucapensis, commonly known as the Tucapensis rose, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically featuring five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Rosa tucapensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
