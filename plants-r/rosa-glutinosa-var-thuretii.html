Rosa glutinosa var. thuretii

Rosa glutinosa var. thuretii, commonly known as the sticky rose or thuret's rose, is a species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, glandular trichomes that give the plant a sticky texture. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five to seven petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
