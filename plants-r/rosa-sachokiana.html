Rosa sachokiana

Rosa sachokiana, commonly known as the Himalayan pink rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Rosa sachokiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
