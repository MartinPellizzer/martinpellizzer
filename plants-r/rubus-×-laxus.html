Rubus × laxus

Rubus × laxus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along garden borders.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
