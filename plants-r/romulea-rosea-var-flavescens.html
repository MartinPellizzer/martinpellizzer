Romulea rosea var. flavescens

Romulea rosea var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow romulea, is a striking bulbous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown by gardeners interested in succulent and bulbous plants.
