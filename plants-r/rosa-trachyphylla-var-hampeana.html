Rosa trachyphylla var. hampeana

Rosa trachyphylla var. hampeana, commonly known as the Mexican rose or wild rose, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fuzzy fruits called hips. Its foliage is typically covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rosa trachyphylla var. hampeana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
