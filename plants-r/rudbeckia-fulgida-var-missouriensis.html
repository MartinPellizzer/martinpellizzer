Rudbeckia fulgida var. missouriensis

Rudbeckia fulgida var. missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri rudbeckia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central United States.

It features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of darker yellow to orange-brown blooms. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
