Rubus feturatus

Rubus feturatus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a tart flavor. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves that are often covered in a silvery down. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the edible fruits that are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
