Rhytidophyllum coccineum

Rhytidophyllum coccineum, commonly known as the red-flowered rock fig, is a unique plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains seeds, contributing to its propagation.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
