Rubus argutifrons

Rubus argutifrons, commonly known as the sharp-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus argutifrons is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along streambanks.
