Rubus × propinquus

Rubus × propinquus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between blackberries (Rubus fruticosus) and raspberries (Rubus idaeus). This plant typically produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark red to black in color. The leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant is often thorny.

It thrives in temperate climates and is widely cultivated for its flavorful berries.
