Rubus semicompactilis

Rubus semicompactilis, commonly known as the compact raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces red or yellow berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible fruits.
