Rubus lineatus var. angustifolius

Rubus lineatus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often armed with prickles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and its role in ecological habitats.
