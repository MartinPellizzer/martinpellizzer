Rhamnus pumila var. penduliramea

Rhamnus pumila var. penduliramea, commonly known as the drooping buckthorn, is a shrub native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
