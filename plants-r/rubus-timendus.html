Rubus timendus

Rubus timendus, commonly known as the poison ivy raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.

However, it can be invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
