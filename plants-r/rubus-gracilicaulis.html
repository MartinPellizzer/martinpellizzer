Rubus gracilicaulis

Rubus gracilicaulis, commonly known as slender-stemmed blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to western North America and is characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, drupelets, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
