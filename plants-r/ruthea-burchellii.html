Ruthea burchellii

Ruthea burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's ragwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in a single terminal inflorescence. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is known for its composite flower heads.

Ruthea burchellii is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
