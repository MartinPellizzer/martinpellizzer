Rubus × patuliramus

Rubus × patuliramus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in jams and pies.
