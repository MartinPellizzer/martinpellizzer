Ruellia occidentalis var. wrightii

Ruellia occidentalis var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in riparian zones and woodlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
