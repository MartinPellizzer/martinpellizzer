Rosa sicula subsp. veridica

Rosa sicula subsp.

veridica, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage.

The leaves are alternate, pinnate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically pink to white in color. It produces oval, red-brown fruits known as hips, which contain seeds.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.
