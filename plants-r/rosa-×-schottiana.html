Rosa × schottiana

Rosa × schottiana, commonly known as the Schott's rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa palustris.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in coastal areas and wetlands. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa × schottiana is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in saline and wet conditions, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
