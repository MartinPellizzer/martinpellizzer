Racosperma trachycarpum

Racosperma trachycarpum, commonly known as the rough-seeded acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with rough, fissured bark, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Racosperma trachycarpum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
