Rhaphidophora cunninghamii

Rhaphidophora cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Rhaphidophora cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ease of care.
