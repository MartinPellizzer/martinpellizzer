Rosa eglanteria rubra

Rosa eglanteria rubra, commonly known as the red prickly rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are woody, red berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Rosa eglanteria rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
