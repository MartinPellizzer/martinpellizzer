Rhododendron dictyotum

Rhododendron dictyotum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in early spring.

Rhododendron dictyotum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
