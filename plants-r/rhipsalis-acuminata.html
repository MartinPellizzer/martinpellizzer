Rhipsalis acuminata

Rhipsalis acuminata, commonly known as the velvet leaf cactus, is a climbing cactus native to South America.

It features a unique appearance with its soft, velvety stems that are often covered in short, fine hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

Rhipsalis acuminata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
