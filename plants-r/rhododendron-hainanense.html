Rhododendron hainanense

Rhododendron hainanense, commonly known as the Hainan rhododendron, is a species native to the island of Hainan in China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within evergreen forests. The plant features striking, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, often with a distinctive tubular shape. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
