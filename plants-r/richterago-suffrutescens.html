Richterago suffrutescens

Richterago suffrutescens, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the valley, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a dense mat close to the ground.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
