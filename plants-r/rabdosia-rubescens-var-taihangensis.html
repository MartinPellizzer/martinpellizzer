Rabdosia rubescens var. taihangensis

Rabdosia rubescens var. taihangensis, commonly known as the Taihang mountain rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in whorled clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
