Rosa communis var. suburbica

Rosa communis var. suburbica, commonly known as the common garden rose, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces fragrant, showy flowers in a range of colors, typically pink, red, or white, which bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in gardens and landscaping.
