Rottboellia paradoxa

Rottboellia paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxa grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Rottboellia paradoxa is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant or used in erosion control due to its dense growth habit.
