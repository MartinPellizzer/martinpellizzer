Ranunculus camissonis

Ranunculus camissonis, commonly known as the Camisson's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in coastal regions.

Ranunculus camissonis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
