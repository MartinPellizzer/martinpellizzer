Rubus serpens var. intermedius

Rubus serpens var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America. This plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. Its leaves are compound, typically with three to five leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry, which is edible and often used in jams and pies.
