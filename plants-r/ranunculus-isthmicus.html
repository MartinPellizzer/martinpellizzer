Ranunculus isthmicus

Ranunculus isthmicus, commonly known as the Isthmus buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Isthmus of Panama.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds.

Ranunculus isthmicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
