Royena brachiata

Royena brachiata, commonly known as the red-rooted stiltgrass, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems that often root at the nodes, forming a dense network. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface.

Royena brachiata is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to thrive in moist, tropical environments.
