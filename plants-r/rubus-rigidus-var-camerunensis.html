Rubus rigidus var. camerunensis

Rubus rigidus var. camerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon blackberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in local cuisine, it holds ecological and cultural significance in its native habitat.
