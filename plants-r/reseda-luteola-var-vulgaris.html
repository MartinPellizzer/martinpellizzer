Reseda luteola var. vulgaris

Reseda luteola var. vulgaris, commonly known as common mallow, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with lobed, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rough, woolly texture and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and waste areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
