Ribes glaciale var. glandulosum

Ribes glaciale var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glacier currant.

This variant of the glacier currant is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, characterized by its compact growth and dense foliage. It features small, oval leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

It is well adapted to cold climates and requires well-drained soil to thrive.
