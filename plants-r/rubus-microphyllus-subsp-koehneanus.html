Rubus microphyllus subsp. koehneanus

Rubus microphyllus subsp.

koehneanus, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
