Rhododendron openshawianum

Rhododendron openshawianum, commonly known as the Openshaw's rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
