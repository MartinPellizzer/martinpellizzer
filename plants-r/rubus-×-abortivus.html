Rubus × abortivus

Rubus × abortivus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically sweet and juicy. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both wild and cultivated settings.
