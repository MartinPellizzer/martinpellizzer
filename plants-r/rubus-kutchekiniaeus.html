Rubus kutchekiniaeus

Rubus kutchekiniaeus, commonly known as the Kutchekinian blackberry, is a species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of small, dark berries. The plant produces edible fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its unique ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
