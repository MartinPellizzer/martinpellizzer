Rosa platyacantha var. variabilis

Rosa platyacantha var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable rose, is a species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with elliptical, dark green leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their strong, sweet scent.

The variety 'variabilis' is noted for its variation in flower color and leaf shape, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
