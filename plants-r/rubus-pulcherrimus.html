Rubus pulcherrimus

Rubus pulcherrimus, commonly known as the beautiful raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
