Rosa hugonis

Rosa hugonis, commonly known as the Hugonis rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are followed by oval, red-orange hips. Rosa hugonis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
