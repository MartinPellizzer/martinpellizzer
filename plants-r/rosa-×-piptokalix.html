Rosa × piptokalix

Rosa × piptokalix, commonly known as the Piptokalix rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa × centifolia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and the production of large, fragrant flowers in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant is typically deciduous, with thorny stems and heart-shaped, medium-green leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for shrub borders and hedge plantings.
