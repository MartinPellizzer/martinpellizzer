Rubus obscurus var. discolor

Rubus obscurus var. discolor, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically not edible due to their bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with variations in leaf and fruit coloration.
