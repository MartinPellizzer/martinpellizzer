Roegneria panormitana

Roegneria panormitana, commonly known as the Panormitan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Roegneria panormitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
