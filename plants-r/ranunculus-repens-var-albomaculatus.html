Ranunculus repens var. albomaculatus

Ranunculus repens var. albomaculatus, commonly known as the white-spotted sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and bright yellow flowers that have white or pale yellow spots. The plant thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks. Its flowers typically bloom from late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
