Rhododendron jucundum

Rhododendron jucundum, commonly known as the happy rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous areas. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to reddish flowers in the spring, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, often with a reddish or purple underside.

Rhododendron jucundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
