Ranunculus archangelensis

Ranunculus archangelensis, commonly known as the Archangel buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of yellow or orange, and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It is known for its ability to thrive in cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Ranunculus archangelensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
