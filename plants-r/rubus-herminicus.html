Rubus herminicus

Rubus herminicus, commonly known as the Herminic blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus herminicus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
