Rhododendron maximowiczianum

Rhododendron maximowiczianum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing a dense canopy that supports various forest ecosystems.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
