Rhynchosia procurrens subsp. latisepala

Rhynchosia procurrens subsp.

latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepal cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow with prominent orange or red stamens. The plant produces pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used as a protein-rich food source.

It thrives in warm climates and is valued for its ornamental and agricultural significance.
