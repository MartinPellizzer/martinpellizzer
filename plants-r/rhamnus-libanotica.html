Rhamnus libanotica

Rhamnus libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, often found in mountainous areas. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a rough texture with a slightly serrated margin.

Rhamnus libanotica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
