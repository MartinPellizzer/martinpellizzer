Rubus guttifer

Rubus guttifer, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to red and then to black as they mature. These berries are often consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
