Rorippa islandica var. glabrata

Rorippa islandica var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth water-cress, is a variant of the water-cress species native to coastal regions.

It is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, saline environments such as tidal zones and wetlands. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their peppery flavor.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its compact growth habit and unique adaptation to brackish water conditions.
