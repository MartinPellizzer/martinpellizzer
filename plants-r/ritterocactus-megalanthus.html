Ritterocactus megalanthus

Ritterocactus megalanthus, commonly known as the giant red barrel cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of Mexico.

This plant is characterized by its large, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often displaying a vibrant red or orange coloration. The stems are covered in spines that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss through transpiration. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a dramatic contrast to its woody structure.

Due to its slow growth and specific environmental needs, it is often cultivated in controlled settings such as greenhouses or botanical gardens.
