Richtersveldia

Richtersveldia, commonly known as the Richtersveld cycad, is a unique plant native to the arid Richtersveld region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, trunk-like stem and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with its deep root system and ability to store water. Its cones, which produce both male and female reproductive structures, are an important feature of its botanical classification.

Richtersveldia is considered a living fossil, with a lineage dating back to the Mesozoic era.
