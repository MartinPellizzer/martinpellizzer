Rosa capitellata

Rosa capitellata, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a strong, aromatic scent.

Rosa capitellata is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
