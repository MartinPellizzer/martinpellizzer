Rubus nitioides

Rubus nitioides, commonly known as the sandbar raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, red berries in the summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its thorny stems provide structural support.

Rubus nitioides thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in wetland areas.
