Rubus frigidulus

Rubus frigidulus, commonly known as the Arctic bramble, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, usually white or pink flowers and prickly stems. The plant thrives in cold climates and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges.

Rubus frigidulus plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and small mammals.
