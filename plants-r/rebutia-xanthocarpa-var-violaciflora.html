Rebutia xanthocarpa var. violaciflora

Rebutia xanthocarpa var. violaciflora, commonly known as the violet-flowered rebutia, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features compact, rounded stems covered in dense, spiny tubercles. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet and pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and arid climates due to its drought tolerance.
