Ribes scuphamii

Ribes scuphamii, commonly known as the Scupham's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly referred to as gooseberries or currants. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, which is typically a small, yellow berry, ripens in late summer and is edible.

Ribes scuphamii is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and stream banks.
