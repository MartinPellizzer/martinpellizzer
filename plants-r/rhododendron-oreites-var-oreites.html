Rhododendron oreites var. oreites

Rhododendron oreites var. oreites, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The flowers typically range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the specific cultivar. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
