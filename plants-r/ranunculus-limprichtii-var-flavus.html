Ranunculus limprichtii var. flavus

Ranunculus limprichtii var. flavus, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia, including China and Korea.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow petals and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with five to seven petals.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
