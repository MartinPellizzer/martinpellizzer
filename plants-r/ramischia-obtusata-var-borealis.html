Ramischia obtusata var. borealis

Ramischia obtusata var. borealis, commonly known as the northern ramischia, is a perennial herb native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in horticultural settings.
