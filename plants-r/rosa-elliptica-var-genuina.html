Rosa elliptica var. genuina

Rosa elliptica var. genuina, commonly known as the Eurasian rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, thorny prickles and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
