Rosa × involuta

Rosa × involuta, commonly known as the curled rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and dense, thorned stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets that provide a lush, green backdrop to the blooms.

Rosa × involuta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
