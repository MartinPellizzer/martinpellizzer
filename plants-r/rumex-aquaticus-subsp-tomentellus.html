Rumex aquaticus subsp. tomentellus

Rumex aquaticus subsp.

tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly water sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its succulent, hairy leaves. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often covered with a fine, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
