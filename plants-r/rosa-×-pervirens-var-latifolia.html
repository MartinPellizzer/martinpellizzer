Rosa × pervirens var. latifolia

Rosa × pervirens var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf evergreen rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which remains on the plant throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in mild climates. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast to the delicate blooms.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
