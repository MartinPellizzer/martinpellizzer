Ranunculus carolinianus var. villicaulis

Ranunculus carolinianus var. villicaulis, commonly known as the Carolina buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces slender, hairy stems that bear the flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided leaves and is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.
