Rosa leucantha acutifolia

Rosa leucantha acutifolia, commonly known as the white-stemmed shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a pointed tip, contributing to its scientific name "acutifolia.". This plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
