Rubus villosus var. enslenii

Rubus villosus var. enslenii, commonly known as the Enslen's bramble, is a variant of the brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
