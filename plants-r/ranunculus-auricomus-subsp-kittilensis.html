Ranunculus auricomus subsp. kittilensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

kittilensis, commonly known as the Kittilsky's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or golden flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flowers compared to the typical auricomus, with a distinctive golden hue. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
