Rumex daghestanicus

Rumex daghestanicus, commonly known as the Daghestan sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and reddish stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Its rhubarb-like leaves have been used traditionally in local cuisines and herbal remedies.
