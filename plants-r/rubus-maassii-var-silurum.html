Rubus maassii var. silurum

Rubus maassii var. silurum, commonly known as the Silurian blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
