Racoubea

Racoubea, commonly known as the African violet, is a popular ornamental plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-30 centimeters and prefers bright, indirect light. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white, making it a favorite among indoor gardeners.

Racoubea is known for its ability to thrive in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
