Rubus marshallii f. continentalis

Rubus marshallii f. continentalis, commonly known as the continental raspberry.

This variant is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It is native to specific regions in North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
