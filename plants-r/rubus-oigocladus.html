Rubus oigocladus

Rubus oigocladus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
