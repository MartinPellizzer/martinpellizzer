Ranunculus auricomus subsp. lawalreei

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

lawalreei, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and palmately divided leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to the typical form. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
