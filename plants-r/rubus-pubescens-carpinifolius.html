Rubus pubescens carpinifolius

Rubus pubescens carpinifolius, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodlands and shrublands. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruits are edible, though often tart.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
