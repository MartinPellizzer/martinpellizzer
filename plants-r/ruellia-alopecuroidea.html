Ruellia alopecuroidea

Ruellia alopecuroidea, commonly known as the woolly ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or violet tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially sunny environments.

Ruellia alopecuroidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
