Rolfea elata

Rolfea elata, commonly known as the tall larkspur, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stem.

Rolfea elata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
