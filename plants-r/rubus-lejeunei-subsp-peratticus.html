Rubus lejeunei subsp. peratticus

Rubus lejeunei subsp.

peratticus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
