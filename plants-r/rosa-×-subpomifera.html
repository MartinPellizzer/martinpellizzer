Rosa × subpomifera

Rosa × subpomifera, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits characteristics of both Rosa rugosa and Rosa bracteata.

It is native to the eastern United States and is known for its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its fruit is a red, fuzzy rose hip that matures in the fall, containing seeds within its outer layer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hedges or as a landscape plant.
