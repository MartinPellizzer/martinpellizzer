Rubus schummelii f. velutinus

Rubus schummelii f. velutinus, commonly known as the velvety blackberry, is a botanical variant of the Rubus schummelii species.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence on both the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive tactile appearance. The plant typically produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
