Robiquetia vaupelii

Robiquetia vaupelii, commonly known as the Vaupel's daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features a central yellow disk surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun.

Robiquetia vaupelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
