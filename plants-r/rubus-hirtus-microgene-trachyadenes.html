Rubus hirtus microgene trachyadenes

Rubus hirtus microgene trachyadenes, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
