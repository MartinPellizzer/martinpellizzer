Rubus misniensis

Rubus misniensis, commonly known as the Misni raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruits, which are aggregate berries, are usually red or black when mature and are often used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.
