Rosa talijevii

Rosa talijevii, commonly known as the Talijevi rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant has simple, pinnate leaves with serrated edges, typical of many rose species. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa talijevii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
