Rumex acetosella subsp. pyrenaicus

Rumex acetosella subsp.

pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish in color.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on slender, upright stalks. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous and woodland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
