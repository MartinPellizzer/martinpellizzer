Rubus glaucellus var. normandicus

Rubus glaucellus var. normandicus, commonly known as the Normandy raspberry, is a shrub native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
