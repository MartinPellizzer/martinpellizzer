Rhodophiala cipoana

Rhodophiala cipoana, commonly known as the Cipoan lily, is a striking member of the Alstroemeriaceae family native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its dramatic appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, Rhodophiala cipoana is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
