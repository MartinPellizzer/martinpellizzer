Reaumuria korovinii

Reaumuria korovinii, commonly known as the Korovin's reaumuria, is a shrub native to the cold climates of central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its structure is typically low-growing with multiple stems, and it produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds.

Reaumuria korovinii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
