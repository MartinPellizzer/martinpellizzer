Restio tetragonus

Restio tetragonus, commonly known as the four-cornered restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and montane regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
