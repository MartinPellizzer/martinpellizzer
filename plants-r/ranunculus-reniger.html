Ranunculus reniger

Ranunculus reniger, commonly known as the black ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, with a distinctive black center. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, with finely divided, trifoliate leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
