Rosa pallida

Rosa pallida, commonly known as the pale rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Rosa pallida typically grows in open woodlands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions.

It is a deciduous shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.
