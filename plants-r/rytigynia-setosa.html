Rytigynia setosa

Rytigynia setosa, commonly known as the hairy rytigynia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rytigyniaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, opposite leaves with a serrated edge. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in inflorescences.

Rytigynia setosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
