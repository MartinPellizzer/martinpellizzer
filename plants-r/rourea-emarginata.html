Rourea emarginata

Rourea emarginata, commonly known as the marginate rourea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in inflorescences.

Rourea emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
