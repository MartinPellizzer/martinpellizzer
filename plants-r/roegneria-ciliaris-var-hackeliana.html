Roegneria ciliaris var. hackeliana

Roegneria ciliaris var. hackeliana, commonly known as the Hackel's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, blue-gray flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in alpine settings.
