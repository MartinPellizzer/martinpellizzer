Rubus apricus var. subcalvatus

Rubus apricus var. subcalvatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other Rubus species, particularly in its pubescent (hairy) texture.
