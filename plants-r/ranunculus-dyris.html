Ranunculus dyris

Ranunculus dyris, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus dyris is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.
