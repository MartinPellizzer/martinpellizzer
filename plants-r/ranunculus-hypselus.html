Ranunculus hypselus

Ranunculus hypselus, commonly known as the tall buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers consist of numerous petal-like sepals and are surrounded by a cluster of stamens, making it visually distinct within its genus.
