Rosa pimpinellifolia var. australis

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. australis, commonly known as the Australian rock rose, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces oval, red or black fruits known as hips. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in Mediterranean climates.
