Rosa rubrifolia var. cinnamoneoides

Rosa rubrifolia var. cinnamoneoides, commonly known as the cinnamonleaf rose, is a variant of the rose species Rosa rubrifolia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique leaf color and floral display.
