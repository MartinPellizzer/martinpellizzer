Roegneria kanashiroi

Roegneria kanashiroi, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and dense growth habit. The plant typically forms clumps and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscape design.
