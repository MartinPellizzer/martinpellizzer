Rosa mollis f. incisula

Rosa mollis f. incisula, commonly known as the soft rose or the cut-leaf rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its soft, velvety foliage.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is typically shrub-like, with arching stems and a compact growth habit. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, often with finely serrated margins, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is well-suited for garden cultivation, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.
