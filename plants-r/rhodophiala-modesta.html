Rhodophiala modesta

Rhodophiala modesta, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and slender stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
