Rytigynia schumannii var. uhligii

Rytigynia schumannii var. uhligii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum uhligii*, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, red flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant foliage and flowering display.
