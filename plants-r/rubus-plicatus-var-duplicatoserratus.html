Rubus plicatus var. duplicatoserratus

Rubus plicatus var. duplicatoserratus, commonly known as the double-toothed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated margins, and the fruit is a dark purple to black aggregate berry. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
