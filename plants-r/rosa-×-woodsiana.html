Rosa × woodsiana

Rosa × woodsiana, commonly known as the fragrant wild rose, is a hybrid species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by oval, red-orange hips. Its botanical characteristics include simple, pinnately compound leaves and a woody structure typical of the Rosa genus.

It is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
