Reynosia mucronata subsp. azulensis

Reynosia mucronata subsp.

azulensis, commonly known as the Blue Mountains reynosia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Baja California. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and salt spray.
