Rhododendron ochraceum var. ochraceum

Rhododendron ochraceum var. ochraceum, commonly known as the yellow rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, with a dark green hue, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
