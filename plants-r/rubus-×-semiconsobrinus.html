Rubus × semiconsobrinus

Rubus × semiconsobrinus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically tart and used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
