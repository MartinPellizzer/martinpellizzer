Rosa × damascena celsiana

Rosa × damascena celsiana, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a hybrid species of the rose genus native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are widely used in the perfume and cosmetic industries due to their high essential oil content.

Rosa × damascena celsiana is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and historical significance.
