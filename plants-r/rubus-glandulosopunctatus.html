Rubus glandulosopunctatus

Rubus glandulosopunctatus, commonly known as the glandular-punctate blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The species is noted for its hairy, glandular surfaces, which give it its scientific name.
