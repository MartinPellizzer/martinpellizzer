Racosperma melvillei

Racosperma melvillei, commonly known as Melville's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow, pea-like flowers in clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Racosperma melvillei plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna in its native range.
