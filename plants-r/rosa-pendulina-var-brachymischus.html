Rosa pendulina var. brachymischus

Rosa pendulina var. brachymischus, commonly known as the dwarf climbing rose, is a compact shrub native to southern China.

It features slender, arching stems that form a cascading habit, making it suitable for trellises or hedges. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It is relatively low maintenance, preferring well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
