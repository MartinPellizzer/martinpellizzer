Rosa tenella

Rosa tenella, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its compact growth habit and continuous blooming throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a high degree of variability in petal color. Its leaves are serrated and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are typically thornless.

Rosa tenella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
