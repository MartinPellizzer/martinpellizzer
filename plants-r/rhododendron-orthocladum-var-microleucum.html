Rhododendron orthocladum var. microleucum

Rhododendron orthocladum var. microleucum, commonly known as the dwarf leucanthemum rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a mat-forming growth habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens or as a ground cover. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a sought-after plant in specialized horticulture.
