Rubus × brevithyrsoideus

Rubus × brevithyrsoideus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil. The plant produces edible berries that are similar in taste to blackberries but are smaller in size.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is often cultivated in gardens and small-scale agriculture.
