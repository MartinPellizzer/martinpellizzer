Ranunculus urvilleanus

Ranunculus urvilleanus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, green, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus urvilleanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
