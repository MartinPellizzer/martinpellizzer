Rhynchospora stolonifera

Rhynchospora stolonifera, commonly known as the stoloniferous spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, and it spreads via stolons, which are horizontal stems that root at the nodes.

Rhynchospora stolonifera plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
