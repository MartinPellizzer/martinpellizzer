Ribes rubrum subsp. vulgare

Ribes rubrum subsp.

vulgare, commonly known as red currant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red, though they can also be yellow or white depending on the variety.

The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.

The flowers are usually pink or white, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to grow in a variety of climates.
