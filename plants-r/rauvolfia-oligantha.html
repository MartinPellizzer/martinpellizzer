Rauvolfia oligantha

Rauvolfia oligantha, commonly known as African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its root system is notable for containing alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
