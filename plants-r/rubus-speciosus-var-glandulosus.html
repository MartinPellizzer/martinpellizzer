Rubus speciosus var. glandulosus

Rubus speciosus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color, often with a hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
