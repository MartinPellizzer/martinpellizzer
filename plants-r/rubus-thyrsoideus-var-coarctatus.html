Rubus thyrsoideus var. coarctatus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. coarctatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated margin, distinguishing it from other Rubus species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and its role in local ecosystems.
