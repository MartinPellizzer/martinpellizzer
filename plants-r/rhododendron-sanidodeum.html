Rhododendron sanidodeum

Rhododendron sanidodeum, commonly known as the Sanidodeum rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to white.

Rhododendron sanidodeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
