Rabdosia irrorata

Rabdosia irrorata, commonly known as the silverleaf rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rabdosia irrorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
