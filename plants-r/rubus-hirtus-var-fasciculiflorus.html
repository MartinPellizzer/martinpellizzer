Rubus hirtus var. fasciculiflorus

Rubus hirtus var. fasciculiflorus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the eastern United States.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
