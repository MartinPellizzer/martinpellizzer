Ritterocactus langsdorfii subsp. pulvinatus

Ritterocactus langsdorfii subsp.

pulvinatus, commonly known as the pulvinated ritterocactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with sharp, spiny areoles.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the base of the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to survive.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its conservation.
