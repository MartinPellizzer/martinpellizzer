Racosperma lorolobum

Racosperma lorolobum, commonly known as the large-lobed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine for its potential healing properties. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas of southern Australia.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
