Rosa arvensis subsp. umbellata

Rosa arvensis subsp.

umbellata, commonly known as the field rose or creeping rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and the presence of thorny stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, pink to white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
