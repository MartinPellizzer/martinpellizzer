Rosa × muscosa var. alba

Rosa × muscosa var. alba, commonly known as the white muscosa rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its fragrant, white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a cluster of small, white petals with a subtle scent. This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and valuable for ecological purposes.
