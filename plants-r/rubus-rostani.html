Rubus rostani

Rubus rostani, commonly known as the Rostan blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in dense, thorny hairs.

Rubus rostani is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
