Rubus × collinus var. canescens

Rubus × collinus var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its silvery-white pubescence on the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that can be reddish or black when mature.

It is often found in moist, temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
