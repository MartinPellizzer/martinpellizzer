Rubus × clypeatus

Rubus × clypeatus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which consist of tiny drupelets that form a dark purple to black berry. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

Rubus × clypeatus is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
