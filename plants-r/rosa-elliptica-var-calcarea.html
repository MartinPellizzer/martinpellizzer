Rosa elliptica var. calcarea

Rosa elliptica var. calcarea, commonly known as the calcarea rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, thorny stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand dry conditions.
