Rhynchosia trichodes

Rhynchosia trichodes, commonly known as the hairy seed bean, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. Rhynchosia trichodes is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

It has been used traditionally for its nutritional value and in some medicinal applications.
