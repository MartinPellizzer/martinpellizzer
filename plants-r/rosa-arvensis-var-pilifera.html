Rosa arvensis var. pilifera

Rosa arvensis var. pilifera, commonly known as the hairy field rose, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals, and appear in clusters.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
