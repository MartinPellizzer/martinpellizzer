Ruschia cincta

Ruschia cincta, commonly known as the Zulu chrysanthemum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features compact, upright growth with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
