Ranunculus fallax subsp. austrokarelicus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

austrokarelicus, commonly known as the Austro-Karelic Ranunculus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Eastern Europe and parts of Russia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
