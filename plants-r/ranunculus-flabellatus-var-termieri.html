Ranunculus flabellatus var. termieri

Ranunculus flabellatus var. termieri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It is characterized by its short, stout stems and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

This variant is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Ranunculus flabellatus.
