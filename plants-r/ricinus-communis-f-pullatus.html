Ricinus communis f. pullatus

Ricinus communis f. pullatus, commonly known as the curly leaf castor oil plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and curling foliage.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant produces spiny capsules containing toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of castor oil. Its unique leaf morphology makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
