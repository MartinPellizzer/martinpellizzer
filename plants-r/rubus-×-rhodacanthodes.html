Rubus × rhodacanthodes

Rubus × rhodacanthodes, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus hirtellus. This species is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions where it forms thickets.
