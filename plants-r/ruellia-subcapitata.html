Ruellia subcapitata

Ruellia subcapitata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
