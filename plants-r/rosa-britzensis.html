Rosa britzensis

Rosa britzensis, commonly known as the British rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the United Kingdom and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and culinary applications due to their aromatic qualities.

Rosa britzensis plays an important role in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
