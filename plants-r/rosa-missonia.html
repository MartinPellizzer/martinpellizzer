Rosa missonia

Rosa missonia, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or the yellow rose of Brazil, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorned stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in spring and summer. Rosa missonia is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in its native region.
