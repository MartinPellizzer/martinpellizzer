Rosa × silvularum

Rosa × silvularum, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Its foliage remains evergreen in mild climates, adding year-round visual interest.
