Rindera lanata f. cyanoptera

Rindera lanata f. cyanoptera, commonly known as the blue-hairy rindera, is a rare and unique variant of the Rindera lanata species.

This plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically a vibrant blue-green color, which is a striking feature of this cultivar. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
