Rhoicissus magalismontana

Rhoicissus magalismontana, commonly known as the Montane Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble those of the Passiflora genus, with five sepals, five petals, and a unique corona of filaments. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually lobed leaves, which are often hairy and have a waxy texture. It produces small, fleshy, blue-black berries that are edible and contain multiple seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
