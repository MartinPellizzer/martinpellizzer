Rubus neofuscifolius

Rubus neofuscifolius, commonly known as the dark-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
