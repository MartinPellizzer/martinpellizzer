Rubus probativus

Rubus probativus, commonly known as the showy blackberry.

It is a species of bramble within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may vary in taste depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it has palmately compound leaves and is part of the Rosaceae family.
