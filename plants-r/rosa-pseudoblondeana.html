Rosa pseudoblondeana

Rosa pseudoblondeana, commonly known as the Swedish pink, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the northern regions of Europe, including Scandinavia and parts of Russia. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of semi-double, pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces hips that are red and edible.

Rosa pseudoblondeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in cooler climates.
