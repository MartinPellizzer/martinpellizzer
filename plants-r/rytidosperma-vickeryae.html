Rytidosperma vickeryae

Rytidosperma vickeryae, commonly known as the Vickery's spiniflorous grass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, wiry leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Rytidosperma vickeryae plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
