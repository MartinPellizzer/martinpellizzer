Rutosma texana

Rutosma texana, commonly known as Texas hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin.

Rutosma texana is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland edges and open habitats.
