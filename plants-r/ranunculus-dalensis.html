Ranunculus dalensis

Ranunculus dalensis, commonly known as the Dal's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five to eight petals.

Ranunculus dalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
