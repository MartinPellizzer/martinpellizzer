Rosa alpina var. ambigua

Rosa alpina var. ambigua, commonly known as the Alpine rose or alpine pink, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with slender stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are dark green, trifoliate, and covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
