Rubus ochlanthus

Rubus ochlanthus, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
