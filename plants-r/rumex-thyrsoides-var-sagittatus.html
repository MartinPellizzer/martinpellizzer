Rumex thyrsoides var. sagittatus

Rumex thyrsoides var. sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowleaf dock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features basal leaves that are arrow-shaped, with a distinctive narrow, pointed tip, and rough, serrated edges. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and riparian habitats.
