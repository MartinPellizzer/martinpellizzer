Rhododendron syringoideum

Rhododendron syringoideum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
