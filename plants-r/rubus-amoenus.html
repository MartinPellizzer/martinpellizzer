Rubus amoenus

Rubus amoenus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant produces flowers with five pink to white petals, typically appearing in early summer. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, often used in jams and desserts.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.
