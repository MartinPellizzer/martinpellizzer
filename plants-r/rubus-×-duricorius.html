Rubus × duricorius

Rubus × duricorius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Its botanical features include a woody stem structure and a complex system of bracts and sepals surrounding the flower.
