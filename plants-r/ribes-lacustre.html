Ribes lacustre

Ribes lacustre, commonly known as water currant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows near wetlands, streams, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic conditions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often reddish or purple on the underside, adding to its ornamental value. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters and attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are edible and have a tart flavor, making them useful in culinary applications.
