Rubus tereticaulis microgene graciliflorens

Rubus tereticaulis microgene graciliflorens, commonly known as the slender-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thin, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other Rubus species, particularly in its slender growth habit and delicate floral structure.
