Rosa sepium f. mentita

Rosa sepium f. mentita, commonly known as the white musk rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa sepium species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which emit a subtle, musky fragrance. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to floral arrangements and landscapes.
