Rubus divexiramus var. schnetzleri

Rubus divexiramus var. schnetzleri, commonly known as the Schneetzler's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in local culinary traditions. Botanically, it exhibits a shrub-like growth habit with compound leaves and aggregate fruits.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique genetic traits and potential for cultivation.
