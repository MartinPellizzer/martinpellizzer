Rubus × ostryifolius

Rubus × ostryifolius, commonly known as the blackberry-elderberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species.

It typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and features thorny stems with alternate, compound leaves composed of 5–7 oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color, with a sweet to tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its hybrid nature, it displays genetic variability and can be challenging to classify botanically.
