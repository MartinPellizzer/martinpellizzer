Rosa uriensis var. glandulifera

Rosa uriensis var. glandulifera, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically covered with glandular hairs, giving it its common name.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
