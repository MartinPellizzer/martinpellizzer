Rhododendron heliolepis

Rhododendron heliolepis, commonly known as the sun-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils at high altitudes. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are often a striking yellow-green color, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Rhododendron heliolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
