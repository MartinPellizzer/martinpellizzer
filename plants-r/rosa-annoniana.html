Rosa annoniana

Rosa annoniana, commonly known as the African rose or white rose, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated by insects, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

Rosa annoniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
