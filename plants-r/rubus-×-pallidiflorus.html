Rubus × pallidiflorus

Rubus × pallidiflorus, commonly known as the paleflower blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its foliage is usually trifoliate, with serrated leaf margins and a slightly hairy texture.
