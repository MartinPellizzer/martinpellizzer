Ranunculus sceleratus var. anfrayi

Ranunculus sceleratus var. anfrayi, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are palmately divided, with a distinctive yellow flower that has five to eight sepals and petals. The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in early summer, and it is characterized by its low-growing habit and delicate appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
