Rubus ulmifolius f. trichantherus

Rubus ulmifolius f. trichantherus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This form is notable for its dense growth habit and potential use in hedging or as a wild plant in natural habitats.
