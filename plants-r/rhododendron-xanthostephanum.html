Rhododendron xanthostephanum

Rhododendron xanthostephanum, commonly known as the yellow-stephan rhododendron, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in acidic soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as an ornamental plant in suitable climates.
