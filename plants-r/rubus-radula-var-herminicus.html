Rubus radula var. herminicus

Rubus radula var. herminicus, commonly known as the Herminic raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's growth. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features include thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and a distinctive floral structure.
