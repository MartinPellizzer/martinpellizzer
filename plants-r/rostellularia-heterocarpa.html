Rostellularia heterocarpa

Rostellularia heterocarpa, commonly known as the giant rostellularia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a woody stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
