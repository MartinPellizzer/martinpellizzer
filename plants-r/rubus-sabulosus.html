Rubus sabulosus

Rubus sabulosus, commonly known as the sandy bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of North America, particularly in the Pacific Northwest. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small white or pink flowers. Its fruit, a cluster of drupes, is typically red and edible.

Rubus sabulosus thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.
