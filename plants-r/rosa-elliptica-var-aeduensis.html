Rosa elliptica var. aeduensis

Rosa elliptica var. aeduensis, commonly known as the Aeduensis rose, is a shrub-like perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems with thorny growth, and its leaves are elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens or borders due to its compact size and ornamental value.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
