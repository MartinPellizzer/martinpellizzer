Racosperma melanoxylon

Racosperma melanoxylon, commonly known as blackwood, is a species of leguminous tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, hard wood and is often found in dry, rocky soils. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Blackwood is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
