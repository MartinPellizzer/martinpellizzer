Rubus radula var. echinatoides

Rubus radula var. echinatoides, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its berries are edible but can be quite tart and are sometimes used in jams or culinary applications.
