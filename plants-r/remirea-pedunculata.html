Remirea pedunculata

Remirea pedunculata, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking plant native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, showy blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical and subtropical forests.

Remirea pedunculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
