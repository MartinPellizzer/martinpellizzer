Rubus gyamdaensis

Rubus gyamdaensis, commonly known as the Gyamda raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
