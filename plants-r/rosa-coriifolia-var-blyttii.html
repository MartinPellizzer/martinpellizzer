Rosa coriifolia var. blyttii

Rosa coriifolia var. blyttii, commonly known as the Blytt's sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with oval-shaped leaflets that are covered in fine hairs. This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
