Rosa pimpinellifolia subsp. spinosissima

Rosa pimpinellifolia subsp.

spinosissima, commonly known as the thorny rock rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in harsh environments.
