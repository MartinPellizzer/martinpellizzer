Rubus tonglooensis

Rubus tonglooensis, commonly known as the Tongloong blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Vietnam, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and produces small, dark purple berries that are edible. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it flowers with white or pale pink petals.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional medicine.
