Ranunculus buchananii

Ranunculus buchananii, commonly known as Buchanan's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, reddish stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It is a perennial species that produces small, green fruits containing seeds.
