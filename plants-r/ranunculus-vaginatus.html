Ranunculus vaginatus

Ranunculus vaginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine, glandular hairs.

Ranunculus vaginatus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
