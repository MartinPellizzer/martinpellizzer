Rhexia pentanthera

Rhexia pentanthera, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Rhexia pentanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
