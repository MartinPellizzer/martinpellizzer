Rubus tristis

Rubus tristis, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along hedgerows.

Rubus tristis is valued for its edible berries and its role in ecological habitats.
