Rubia albicaulis

Rubia albicaulis, commonly known as the white-haired betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.
