Rhamnus rubra var. obtusissima

Rhamnus rubra var. obtusissima, commonly known as the blunt-leafed buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are broad and rounded, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands or along rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
