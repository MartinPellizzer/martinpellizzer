Ranunculus paucistamineus var. petiveri

Ranunculus paucistamineus var. petiveri, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, lanceolate leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and woodland margins. It is characterized by its reduced number of stamens, which is a key feature in its scientific name.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
