Rhododendron groenlandicum

Rhododendron groenlandicum, commonly known as the Labrador tea, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
