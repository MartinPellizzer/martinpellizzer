Reseda virgata

Reseda virgata, commonly known as yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands and meadows, often forming dense colonies. Its taproot system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils, and it is known for its ability to self-seed readily.

Reseda virgata is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
