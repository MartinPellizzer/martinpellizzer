Rhododendron indicum var. aurantiacum

Rhododendron indicum var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the bright flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and natural landscapes.
