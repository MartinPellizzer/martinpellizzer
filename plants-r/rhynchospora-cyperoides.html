Rhynchospora cyperoides

Rhynchospora cyperoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to wetland habitats, it thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces minute, three-lobed flowers that are typically inconspicuous and lack showy petals.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
