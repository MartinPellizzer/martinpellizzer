Ranunculus royi

Ranunculus royi, commonly known as the Royal buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with prominent black markings, which are typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

Ranunculus royi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, eye-catching blooms.
