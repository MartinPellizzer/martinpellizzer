Rhodiola sachalinensis

Rhodiola sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sachalin rosebay willowherb, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, often reddish stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in alpine and tundra regions where it can withstand extreme temperatures and poor soil conditions. Rhodiola sachalinensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, containing compounds such as rosavins and salidrosides, which are believed to have adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.

Its unique morphology and ecological resilience make it an important species in its native habitats.
