Rubus hirtus microgene declivis

Rubus hirtus microgene declivis, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
