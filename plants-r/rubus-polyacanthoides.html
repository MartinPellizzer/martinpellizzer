Rubus polyacanthoides

Rubus polyacanthoides, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright red berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. These berries are rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making them a popular fruit in culinary and medicinal uses.

The botanical features of Rubus polyacanthoides make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
