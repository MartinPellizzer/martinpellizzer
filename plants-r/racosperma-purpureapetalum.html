Racosperma purpureapetalum

Racosperma purpureapetalum, commonly known as the purple-petaled acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with thin, elongated leaflets and produces striking purple or pink flowers in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal regions and sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
