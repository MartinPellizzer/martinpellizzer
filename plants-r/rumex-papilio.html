Rumex papilio

Rumex papilio, commonly known as the red bryony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, reddish-green leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender, erect stems. Rumex papilio typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
