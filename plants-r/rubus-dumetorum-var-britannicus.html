Rubus dumetorum var. britannicus

Rubus dumetorum var. britannicus, commonly known as the British bramble, is a perennial shrub native to Europe, particularly the British Isles.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typically with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are usually red when ripe. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is closely related to other bramble species within the Rubus genus.
