Rubus semiscanicus

Rubus semiscanicus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the local ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.
