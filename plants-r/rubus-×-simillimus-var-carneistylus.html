Rubus × simillimus var. carneistylus

Rubus × simillimus var. carneistylus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are typically firm and have a sweet to tart flavor. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a perennial plant in both wild and agricultural settings.
