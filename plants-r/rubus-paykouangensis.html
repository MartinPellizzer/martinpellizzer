Rubus paykouangensis

Rubus paykouangensis, commonly known as the Paykouangensis blackberry, is a species of shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to humid, temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less-studied species within the Rubus genus.
