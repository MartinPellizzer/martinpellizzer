Rosa × salaevensis var. typica

Rosa × salaevensis var. typica, commonly known as the Siberian rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hardy nature, capable of surviving in harsh, temperate conditions. The plant typically features dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers are usually small, fragrant, and bloom in shades of pink to white.

It is valued in horticulture for its resilience and ornamental qualities in colder regions.
