Rubus flagellaris var. michiganensis

Rubus flagellaris var. michiganensis, commonly known as the Michigan blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to the Great Lakes region of North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in moist, wooded areas. It is part of the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cooler climates.

The berries are often used in jams and pies, though they are not as widely cultivated as other blackberry varieties.
