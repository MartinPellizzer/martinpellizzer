Ruschia ruschiana

Ruschia ruschiana, commonly known as the blue ice plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
