Ribes hirtellum var. saxosum

Ribes hirtellum var. saxosum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that turn red when mature, often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for various ecological niches.
