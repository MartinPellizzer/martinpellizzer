Rubus microphyllus var. parvifolius

Rubus microphyllus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, dark purple berries that are typically less than 1 centimeter in diameter. The plant has slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
