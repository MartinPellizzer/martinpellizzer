Restiaria dasyphylla

Restiaria dasyphylla, commonly known as the roughleaf restiaria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Restiaria dasyphylla is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
