Rhodoleia ovalifolia

Rhodoleia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf rhodoleia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rhodoleiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China, Korea, and Japan. The plant is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in shady, moist forests at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters.

Rhodoleia ovalifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
