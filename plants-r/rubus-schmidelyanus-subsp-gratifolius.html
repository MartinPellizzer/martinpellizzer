Rubus schmidelyanus subsp. gratifolius

Rubus schmidelyanus subsp.

gratifolius, commonly known as the Schmidel's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
