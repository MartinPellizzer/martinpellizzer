Ranunculus drummondii

Ranunculus drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are hairy.

It is an important plant for pollinators and is often found in disturbed soils.
