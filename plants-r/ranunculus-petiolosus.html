Ranunculus petiolosus

Ranunculus petiolosus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically appearing in spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and palmate leaves with three to five lobes. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitats.
