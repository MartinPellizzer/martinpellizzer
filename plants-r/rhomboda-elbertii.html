Rhomboda elbertii

Rhomboda elbertii, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Rhomboda elbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
