Rosa arvensis var. bibracteata

Rosa arvensis var. bibracteata, commonly known as the two-bract dog玫瑰, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in summer. The plant is characterized by its distinctive two bracts beneath each flower, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and natural habitats.
