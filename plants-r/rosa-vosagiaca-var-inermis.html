Rosa vosagiaca var. inermis

Rosa vosagiaca var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth Rosa vosagiaca, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring. Its stems are covered in sparse, soft hairs, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers are followed by red, hairy fruits called hips, which are edible and used in traditional medicine.
