Rhamnus citrifolia

Rhamnus citrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese date or Indian date tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible when ripe, though they are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive fragrance.
