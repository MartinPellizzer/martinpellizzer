Rosa fragrans

Rosa fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its showy, fragrant blooms that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. Rosa fragrans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its perfume.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
