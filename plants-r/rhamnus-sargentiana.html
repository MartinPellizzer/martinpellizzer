Rhamnus sargentiana

Rhamnus sargentiana, commonly known as Sargent's buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, spherical berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Rhamnus sargentiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
