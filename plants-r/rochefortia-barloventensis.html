Rochefortia barloventensis

Rochefortia barloventensis, commonly known as the Barlovento rock fig, is a flowering plant native to the island of Barlovento in the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping it conserve water in its dry environment.

Rochefortia barloventensis is notable for its ecological importance and is considered a rare species within its range.
