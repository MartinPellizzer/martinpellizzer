Rhododendron × variolosum

Rhododendron × variolosum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It prefers partial to full shade and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
