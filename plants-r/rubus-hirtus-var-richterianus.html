Rubus hirtus var. richterianus

Rubus hirtus var. richterianus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a deep black color, often with a slight whitish bloom. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in woodland edges and thickets.

This variety is valued for its edible berries and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
