Rosa fricans

Rosa fricans, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Rosa fricans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and hardiness.
