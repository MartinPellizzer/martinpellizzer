Rosa underwoodii

Rosa underwoodii, commonly known as the Underwood rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has arching stems with thorny tips and dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Rosa underwoodii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
