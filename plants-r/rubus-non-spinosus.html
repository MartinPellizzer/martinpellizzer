Rubus non-spinosus

Rubus non-spinosus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are edible and rich in antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is an important species for both wildlife and human consumption.
