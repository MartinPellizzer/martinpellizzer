Rhododendron herpesticum

Rhododendron herpesticum, commonly known as the bearberry rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink.

Rhododendron herpesticum is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
