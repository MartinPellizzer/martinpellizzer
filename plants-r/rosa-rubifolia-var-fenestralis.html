Rosa rubifolia var. fenestralis

Rosa rubifolia var. fenestralis, commonly known as the fenestrated rose, is a unique variant of the Rosa rubifolia species.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring lobed and fenestrated (perforated) leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. Its foliage is often a deep green, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
