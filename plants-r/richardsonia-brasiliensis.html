Richardsonia brasiliensis

Richardsonia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a complex structure that facilitates pollination by insects.

Richardsonia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystem.
