Rosa coriifolia var. extranea

Rosa coriifolia var. extranea, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are hairy on the underside. This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a range of garden conditions.
