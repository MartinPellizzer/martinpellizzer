Rhododendron arboreum subsp. cinnamomeum

Rhododendron arboreum subsp.

cinnamomeum, commonly known as the Cinnamon Flame Rhododendron, is a shrub native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in temperate regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller leaves and more vibrant flower colors compared to the main species.
