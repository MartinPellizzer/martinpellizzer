Riddellia tagetina var. sparsiflora

Riddellia tagetina var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparsely-flowered goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
