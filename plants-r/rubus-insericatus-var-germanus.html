Rubus insericatus var. germanus

Rubus insericatus var. germanus, commonly known as the German raspberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
