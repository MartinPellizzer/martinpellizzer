Rosa × duffortii

Rosa × duffortii, commonly known as the Duffort's rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa rugosa and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its robust growth, thorny stems, and profuse blooming of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a strong, often woody structure.
