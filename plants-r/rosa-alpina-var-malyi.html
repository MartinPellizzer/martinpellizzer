Rosa alpina var. malyi

Rosa alpina var. malyi, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a variant of the alpine rose species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, with a silvery underside, and the stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

This hardy plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
