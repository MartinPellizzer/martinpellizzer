Rubus × subjunctiformis

Rubus × subjunctiformis, commonly known as the purple-leafed bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple foliage and thorny stems, which are typical features of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or black in color, similar to other raspberries and blackberries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
