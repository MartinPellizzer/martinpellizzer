Ramondia scandens

Ramondia scandens, commonly known as the climbing arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered around the spadix, which is enclosed by a showy, leaf-like structure called a spathe. Ramondia scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
