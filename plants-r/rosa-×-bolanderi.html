Rosa × bolanderi

Rosa × bolanderi, commonly known as the Bolander rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and its hips are orange-red and ornamental.

Rosa × bolanderi is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and attractive appearance.
