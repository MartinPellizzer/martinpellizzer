Rubus phoenicolasius var. aureiceps

Rubus phoenicolasius var. aureiceps, commonly known as the golden-headed blackberry, is a variant of the Chinese blackberry species.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow bracts that surround the flower clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its potential as a fruit crop.
