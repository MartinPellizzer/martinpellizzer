Ribes tolimense

Ribes tolimense, commonly known as Tolimense gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Ribes.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with small, alternate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, which are edible, are typically small berries that ripen in the summer months.

Ribes tolimense is often found in humid, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential culinary uses.
