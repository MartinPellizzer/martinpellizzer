Rollandia degeneriana

Rollandia degeneriana, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that ripen in the fall. It has simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly bitter taste.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
