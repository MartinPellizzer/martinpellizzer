Rubus wahlbergii var. macropetalus

Rubus wahlbergii var. macropetalus, commonly known as the large-petaled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible fruits.
