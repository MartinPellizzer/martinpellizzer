Rapanea asymmetrica f. magnifolia

Rapanea asymmetrica f. magnifolia, commonly known as the magnificat holly, is a rare and striking variant of the base species.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that display a deep green color with prominent veins. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue-black berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical collections.
