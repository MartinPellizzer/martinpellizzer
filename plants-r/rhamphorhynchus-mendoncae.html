Rhamphorhynchus mendoncae

Rhamphorhynchus mendoncae, commonly known as the Mendoza toothed flying lizard, is a prehistoric flying reptile that lived during the Late Jurassic period.

It is characterized by its elongated body, sharp teeth, and a distinctive, elongated tail with a distinctive bony tip. The plant aspect of this name refers to its association with the Mendoza region in Argentina, where its fossils were first discovered. It is not a plant but a reptile, so there are no botanical aspects to discuss.
