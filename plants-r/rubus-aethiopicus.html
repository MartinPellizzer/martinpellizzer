Rubus aethiopicus

Rubus aethiopicus, commonly known as the Ethiopian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.

The berries are edible but can be quite tart and are sometimes used in local cuisines.
