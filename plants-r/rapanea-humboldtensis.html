Rapanea humboldtensis

Rapanea humboldtensis, commonly known as Humboldt's evergreen, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, dark blue berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
