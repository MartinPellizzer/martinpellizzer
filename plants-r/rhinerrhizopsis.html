Rhinerrhizopsis

Rhinerrhizopsis, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and ethereal flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white, spider-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Rhinerrhizopsis is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
