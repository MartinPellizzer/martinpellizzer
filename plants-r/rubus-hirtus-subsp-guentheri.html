Rubus hirtus subsp. guentheri

Rubus hirtus subsp.

guentheri, commonly known as the Guenther's bramble, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.

This subspecies is often found in woodlands and along the edges of open fields.
