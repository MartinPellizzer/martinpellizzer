Rhynchospora spiciformis f. setacea

Rhynchospora spiciformis f. setacea, commonly known as the dense-flowered spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in temperate regions. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil.

This fern-like rush is often found in wetlands and is an important component of the aquatic plant community.
