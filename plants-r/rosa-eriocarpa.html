Rosa eriocarpa

Rosa eriocarpa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly hairs covering the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
