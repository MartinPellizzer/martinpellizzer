Ranunculus wraberi

Ranunculus wraberi, commonly known as the Wraber's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus wraberi is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
