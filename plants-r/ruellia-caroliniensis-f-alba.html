Ruellia caroliniensis f. alba

Ruellia caroliniensis f. alba, commonly known as the white ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. Its flowers have a tubular base that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The white ruellia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
