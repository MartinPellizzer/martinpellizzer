Rubus fuscus var. salebrosus

Rubus fuscus var. salebrosus, commonly known as the bracken raspberry, is a thorny shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in upright inflorescences. Its fruits are aggregate berries that mature to a dark red color, often containing several small seeds.

This species is characterized by its adaptability to wet soils and its role in supporting local wildlife.
