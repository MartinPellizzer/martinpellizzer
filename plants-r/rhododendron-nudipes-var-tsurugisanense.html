Rhododendron nudipes var. tsurugisanense

Rhododendron nudipes var. tsurugisanense, commonly known as the Tsurugisanense Rhododendron, is a rare and endemic species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within mountainous regions. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

This variety is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological traits.
