Rubus rivularis var. tenuiserratus

Rubus rivularis var. tenuiserratus, commonly known as the narrow-toothed blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, arching stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that have serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in the axils of the leaves, followed by edible, dark purple berries.
