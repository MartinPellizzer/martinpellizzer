Ranunculus lorentii

Ranunculus lorentii, commonly known as the Lorentz buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble additional petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure that aids in photosynthesis.

Ranunculus lorentii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
