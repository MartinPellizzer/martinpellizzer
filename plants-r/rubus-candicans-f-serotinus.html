Rubus candicans f. serotinus

Rubus candicans f. serotinus, commonly known as the late-flowering raspberry, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
