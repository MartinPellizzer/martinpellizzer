Racosperma mutabile subsp. stipuliferum

Racosperma mutabile subsp.

stipuliferum, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes.

The plant produces flattened, woody pods that contain seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is noted for its variability in leaf and flower morphology, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
