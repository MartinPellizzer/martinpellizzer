Rosa obtusifolia var. capucinensis

Rosa obtusifolia var. capucinensis, commonly known as the Capuchin rose, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure and a preference for well-drained soil in sunny locations.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
