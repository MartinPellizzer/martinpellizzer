Rubus hirtus var. aristifer

Rubus hirtus var. aristifer, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy surface.

This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
