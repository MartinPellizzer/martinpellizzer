Ranunculus auricomus subsp. rotundellus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

rotundellus, commonly known as the roundleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features showy, yellow flowers with five broadly rounded petals that are often tinged with red or brown at the base.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
