Ranunculus krapfia

Ranunculus krapfia, commonly known as the Krapf's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces flowers with five to seven petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also threatened by habitat loss in the wild.
