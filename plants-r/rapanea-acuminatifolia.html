Rapanea acuminatifolia

Rapanea acuminatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, white flowers followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
