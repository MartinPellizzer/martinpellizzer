Rubus leptothyrsos

Rubus leptothyrsos, commonly known as the slender-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, which includes many other brambles and berries.
