Rubus hebecaulis var. agglomeratus

Rubus hebecaulis var. agglomeratus, commonly known as the clustered blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in jams and pies.
