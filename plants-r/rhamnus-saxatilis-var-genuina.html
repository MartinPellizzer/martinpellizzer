Rhamnus saxatilis var. genuina

Rhamnus saxatilis var. genuina, commonly known as the alpine buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its fruit is a small, smooth berry that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
