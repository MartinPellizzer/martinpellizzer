Rhamnus imeretia

Rhamnus imeretia, commonly known as the Imereti buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, black berries. It thrives in rocky and mountainous habitats, often found in dry, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
