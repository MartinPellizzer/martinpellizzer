Rosa ruprechtiana

Rosa ruprechtiana, commonly known as the Tibetan rose or Ruprecht's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive, layered appearance. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa ruprechtiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with specific climate requirements.
