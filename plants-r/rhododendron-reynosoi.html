Rhododendron reynosoi

Rhododendron reynosoi, commonly known as the Reynolds rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
