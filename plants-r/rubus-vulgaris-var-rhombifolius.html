Rubus vulgaris var. rhombifolius

Rubus vulgaris var. rhombifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species within the Rosaceae family.
