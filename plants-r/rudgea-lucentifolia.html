Rudgea lucentifolia

Rudgea lucentifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rudgea lucentifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
