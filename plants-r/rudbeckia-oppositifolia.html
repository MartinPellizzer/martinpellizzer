Rudbeckia oppositifolia

Rudbeckia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a dark brown disk floret in its composite flower head. The plant has opposite leaf arrangement, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Rudbeckia oppositifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
