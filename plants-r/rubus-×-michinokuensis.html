Rubus × michinokuensis

Rubus × michinokuensis, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and desserts.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to various climates.
