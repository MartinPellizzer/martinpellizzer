Ranunculus auricomus subsp. marklundii

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

marklundii, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately divided leaves that are hairy and covered in fine, glandular dots. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
