Rubus affinis subsp. emergens

Rubus affinis subsp.

emergens, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia and North America. It is characterized by its erect or ascending growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and edible berries.
