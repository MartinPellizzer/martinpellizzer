Rubus × deformis

Rubus × deformis, commonly known as the flattop blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in its natural habitat.
