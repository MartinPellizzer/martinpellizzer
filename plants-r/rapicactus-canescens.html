Rapicactus canescens

Rapicactus canescens, commonly known as the silver spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in a whitish, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically spine-tipped and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing structural support and minimizing surface area for evaporation.

Rapicactus canescens is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
