Rosa dumalis var. antalyensis

Rosa dumalis var. antalyensis, commonly known as the Antalya rose, is a species native to Turkey.

It is a shrub with thorned stems and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are compound, with elliptical leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are orange-red in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
