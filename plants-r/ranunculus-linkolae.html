Ranunculus linkolae

Ranunculus linkolae, commonly known as the Linkolai buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that disperse its seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
