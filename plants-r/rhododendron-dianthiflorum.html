Rhododendron dianthiflorum

Rhododendron dianthiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to China and belongs to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron dianthiflorum is also noted for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to a variety of environmental conditions.
