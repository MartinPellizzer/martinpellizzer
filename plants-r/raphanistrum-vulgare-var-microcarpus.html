Raphanistrum vulgare var. microcarpus

Raphanistrum vulgare var. microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in marshy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive reddish midrib, and it produces tiny, clustered fruits.

It is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain insects and birds.
