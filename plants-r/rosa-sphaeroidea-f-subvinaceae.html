Rosa sphaeroidea f. subvinaceae

Rosa sphaeroidea f. subvinaceae, commonly known as the pink rose or subvinaceous rose, is a hybrid form of the species Rosa sphaeroidea.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-red flowers, which are typically smaller than those of the parent species. The plant produces fragrant blooms with a soft, velvety texture, and its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
