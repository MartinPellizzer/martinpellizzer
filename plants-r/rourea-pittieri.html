Rourea pittieri

Rourea pittieri, commonly known as the Hawaiian rourea, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Rourea pittieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
