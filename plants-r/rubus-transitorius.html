Rubus transitorius

Rubus transitorius, commonly known as the transient raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is known for its short lifespan, which contributes to its common name.
