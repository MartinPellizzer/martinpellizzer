Rubus volvatus var. hirtus

Rubus volvatus var. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of North America. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variant is often cultivated for its fruit, which can be used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
