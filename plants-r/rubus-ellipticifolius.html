Rubus ellipticifolius

Rubus ellipticifolius, commonly known as the elliptic-leaf blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agroecosystems.
