Rosa agraria

Rosa agraria, commonly known as the field rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in meadows and along roadsides. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its fruits are rosehips, which are rich in vitamin C and often used in herbal remedies.

Rosa agraria is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
