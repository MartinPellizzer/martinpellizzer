Rubus fuscus microgene parviserrati

Rubus fuscus microgene parviserrati, commonly known as the small-toothed blackberry, is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, serrated leaves and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It has a sprawling growth habit and produces thorny stems, which are typical of many Rubus species.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
