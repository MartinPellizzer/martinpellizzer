Rhododendron × burriflorum

Rhododendron × burriflorum, commonly known as the Burryflower rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a shrub for ornamental purposes. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow or greenish throat. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the development of many modern rhododendron cultivars.
