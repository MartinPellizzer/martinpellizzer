Rubus insericatus var. sericatifrons

Rubus insericatus var. sericatifrons, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
