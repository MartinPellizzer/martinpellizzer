Rhaphidophora batoeensis

Rhaphidophora batoeensis, commonly known as the Batoe's arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Rhaphidophora batoeensis is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
