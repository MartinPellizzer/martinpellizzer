Rumex patientia subsp. graecus

Rumex patientia subsp.

graecus, commonly known as the Greek dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in a distinctive white or pale underside.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender, erect stems. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
