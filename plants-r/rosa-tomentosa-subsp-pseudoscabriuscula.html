Rosa tomentosa subsp. pseudoscabriuscula

Rosa tomentosa subsp.

pseudoscabriuscula, commonly known as the soft-haired rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The leaves are typically arranged alternately and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals. This subspecies is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rosa genus, valued for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
