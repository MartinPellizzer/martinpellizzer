Rubus × argutifolius var. lamprophyllus

Rubus × argutifolius var. lamprophyllus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and pies. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
