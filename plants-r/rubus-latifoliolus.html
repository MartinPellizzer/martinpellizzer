Rubus latifoliolus

Rubus latifoliolus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features compound leaves with broad, serrated leaflets and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the plant is often found in forest understories.

Rubus latifoliolus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
