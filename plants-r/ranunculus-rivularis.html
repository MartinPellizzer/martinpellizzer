Ranunculus rivularis

Ranunculus rivularis, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a flowering plant native to wetland and streamside habitats.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and it thrives in temperate regions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in freshwater environments.

Ranunculus rivularis is an important part of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
