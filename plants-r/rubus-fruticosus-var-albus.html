Rubus fruticosus var. albus

Rubus fruticosus var. albus, commonly known as white bramble, is a variant of the common bramble species.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, aggregate berry. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This plant is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along the edges of cultivated fields.
