Rheum khorasanicum

Rheum khorasanicum, commonly known as the Khorasan rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and tall, erect stems. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Rheum khorasanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical medicinal uses.
