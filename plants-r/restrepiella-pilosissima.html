Restrepiella pilosissima

Restrepiella pilosissima, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It possesses small, leathery leaves and produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in late winter. The plant is characterized by its dense, silky hair covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis.

This species is native to parts of South America and is often found in humid, tropical environments.
