Roettlera crinita

Roettlera crinita, commonly known as the curly hair plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant's most distinctive characteristic is its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, ornamental appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Roettlera crinita is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and long blooming season.
