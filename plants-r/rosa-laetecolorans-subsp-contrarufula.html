Rosa laetecolorans subsp. contrarufula

Rosa laetecolorans subsp.

contrarufula, commonly known as the pale pink shrub rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
