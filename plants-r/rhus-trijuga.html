Rhus trijuga

Rhus trijuga, commonly known as the staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that persist into winter. Rhus trijuga thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open woods, fields, and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common sight in many parts of its native range.
