Rhododendron mallotum

Rhododendron mallotum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Rhododendron mallotum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
