Rhododendron rhodora

Rhododendron rhodora, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, moist environments. Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and dark green, providing a lush backdrop for the floral display.

Rhododendron rhodora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in woodland gardens and natural habitats.
