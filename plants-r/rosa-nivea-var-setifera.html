Rosa nivea var. setifera

Rosa nivea var. setifera, commonly known as the white rose with hairy stems, is a variety of the species Rosa nivea.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, hairy stems that give it its specific epithet "setifera," meaning "hairy.". Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively low maintenance.
