Rhaponticum lyratum

Rhaponticum lyratum, commonly known as the lyratum or the mountain thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Rhaponticum lyratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to gardens in various parts of the world.
