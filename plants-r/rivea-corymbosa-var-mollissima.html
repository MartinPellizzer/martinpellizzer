Rivea corymbosa var. mollissima

Rivea corymbosa var. mollissima, commonly known as the velvet morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its deep green, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
