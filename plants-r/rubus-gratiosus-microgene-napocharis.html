Rubus gratiosus microgene napocharis

Rubus gratiosus microgene napocharis, commonly known as the Napocharis raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
