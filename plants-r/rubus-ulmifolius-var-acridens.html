Rubus ulmifolius var. acridens

Rubus ulmifolius var. acridens, commonly known as the acrid thimbleberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically serrated and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
