Ranunculus trichophyllus f. kushirensis

Ranunculus trichophyllus f. kushirensis, commonly known as the Kushirensis buttercup, is a botanical variant of the hairy buttercup.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan, characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems. The foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and the plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The subspecies is notable for its unique floral structure and adaptability to specific ecological niches.
