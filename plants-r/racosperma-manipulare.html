Racosperma manipulare

Racosperma manipulare, commonly known as the blue bottle tree, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower spikes that appear during the wet season. The plant has a woody stem with a thick, smooth bark and produces small, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Racosperma manipulare is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
