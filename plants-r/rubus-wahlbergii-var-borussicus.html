Rubus wahlbergii var. borussicus

Rubus wahlbergii var. borussicus, commonly known as the Borussian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
