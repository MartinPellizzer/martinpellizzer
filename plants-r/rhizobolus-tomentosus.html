Rhizobolus tomentosus

Rhizobolus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly root fungus, is a unique plant-related organism that forms symbiotic relationships with certain plants.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of hyphae, which gives it a distinctive appearance. This fungus is often found in soil and can play a role in nutrient cycling within the ecosystem. It is not a true plant but a fungus, and its presence can influence plant health and growth.

Due to its specialized nature, it is less commonly studied compared to other fungi.
