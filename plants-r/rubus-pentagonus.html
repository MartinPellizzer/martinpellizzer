Rubus pentagonus

Rubus pentagonus, commonly known as the five-angled blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Rubus pentagonus is valued for its edible berries, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
