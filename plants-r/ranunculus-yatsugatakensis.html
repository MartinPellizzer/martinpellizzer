Ranunculus yatsugatakensis

Ranunculus yatsugatakensis, commonly known as the Yatsugatakensis buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. It has a short stem with basal leaves and produces flowers that are often surrounded by a ring of sepals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
