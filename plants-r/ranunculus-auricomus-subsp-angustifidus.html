Ranunculus auricomus subsp. angustifidus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

angustifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features bright yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals, often with a distinctive white or greenish basal mark.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
