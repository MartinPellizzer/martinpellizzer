Rosa coriifolia subsp. frutetorum

Rosa coriifolia subsp.

frutetorum, commonly known as the Japanese rose or frutetorum rose, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia. It features pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and produces showy, pink to white flowers in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its thornless stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardening.
