Ribes malvaceum var. clementinum

Ribes malvaceum var. clementinum, commonly known as the Clementine gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to southern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, yellow-orange berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is prized for its edible fruits and ornamental value in gardens.
