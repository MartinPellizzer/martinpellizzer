Ranunculus rotundilobus

Ranunculus rotundilobus, commonly known as the broad-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and it typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant has trifoliate leaves with rounded lobes, and its stems are often reddish in color.

It blooms in early spring and is characterized by its distinctive, somewhat toxic sap.
