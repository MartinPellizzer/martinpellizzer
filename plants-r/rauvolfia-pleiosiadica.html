Rauvolfia pleiosiadica

Rauvolfia pleiosiadica, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are follicles that contain several elliptical seeds.

Rauvolfia pleiosiadica is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in traditional medicine.
