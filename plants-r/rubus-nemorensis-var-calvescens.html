Rubus nemorensis var. calvescens

Rubus nemorensis var. calvescens, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
