Ranunculus repentifolius

Ranunculus repentifolius, commonly known as the wandering buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate and can be either simple or deeply divided, depending on the growth conditions.

Ranunculus repentifolius prefers moist, shady habitats and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
