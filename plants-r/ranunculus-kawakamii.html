Ranunculus kawakamii

Ranunculus kawakamii, commonly known as Kawakami's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, solitary flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
