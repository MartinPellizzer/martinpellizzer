Rosa caudata var. caudata

Rosa caudata var. caudata, commonly known as the trailing rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its long, arching stems that often trail along the ground. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
