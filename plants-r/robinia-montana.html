Robinia montana

Robinia montana, commonly known as the mountain false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in pendulous clusters during the spring. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

Robinia montana is adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
