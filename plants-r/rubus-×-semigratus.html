Rubus × semigratus

Rubus × semigratus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
