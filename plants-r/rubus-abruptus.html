Rubus abruptus

Rubus abruptus, commonly known as the abrupt raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain regions.
