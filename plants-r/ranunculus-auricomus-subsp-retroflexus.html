Ranunculus auricomus subsp. retroflexus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

retroflexus, commonly known as the purple buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America. It features showy, solitary flowers with five to seven yellow petals and a distinctive purple center.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a waxy coating, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens.
