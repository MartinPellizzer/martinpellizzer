Rubus peruncinatus

Rubus peruncinatus, commonly known as the blackberry lichen, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its unique morphological features.

It typically grows as a small, shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
