Ranunculus peduncularis

Ranunculus peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of bright yellow, spurred flowers. Its flowers have five sepals that are often mistaken for petals, and they open in early spring.

The plant is known for its attractive appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
