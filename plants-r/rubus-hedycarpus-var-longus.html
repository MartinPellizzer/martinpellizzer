Rubus hedycarpus var. longus

Rubus hedycarpus var. longus, commonly known as the long-fruited blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, and the plant is often found in moist, wooded areas.

It is valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.
