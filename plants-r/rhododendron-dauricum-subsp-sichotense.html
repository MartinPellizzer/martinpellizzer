Rhododendron dauricum subsp. sichotense

Rhododendron dauricum subsp.

sichotense, commonly known as the Daurian azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a slightly hairy underside.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cooler climates.
