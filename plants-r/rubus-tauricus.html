Rubus tauricus

Rubus tauricus, commonly known as the Taurian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

The leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the plant is known for its hardy nature.
