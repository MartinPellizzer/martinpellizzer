Ranunculus jovis

Ranunculus jovis, commonly known as the Jupiter's ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
