Rolliniopsis parviflora var. latifolia

Rolliniopsis parviflora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf rolliniopsis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the subspecies. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its potential use in ornamental horticulture.
