Rubus metschii var. cyclocaulon

Rubus metschii var. cyclocaulon, commonly known as the cyclocaulon blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curled bracts that enclose the flower cluster, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and tart. It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China and Korea.

This variety is valued for its ornamental features and is occasionally cultivated for its edible fruits.
