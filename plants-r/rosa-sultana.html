Rosa sultana

Rosa sultana, commonly known as the Turkish rose or Sultana rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to deep red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

Rosa sultana is also cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and for its edible rose hips.
