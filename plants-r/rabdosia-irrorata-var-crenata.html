Rabdosia irrorata var. crenata

Rabdosia irrorata var. crenata, commonly known as the wavy-leaved rabdosia, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, giving it its common name. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and are arranged in whorls along the stems.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
