Rosa spicant

Rosa spicant, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, particularly in the mountainous areas of the Caucasus and Turkey. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red or orange fruits known as hips.

Rosa spicant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
