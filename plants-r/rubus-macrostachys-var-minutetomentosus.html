Rubus macrostachys var. minutetomentosus

Rubus macrostachys var. minutetomentosus, commonly known as the silky bramble, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Vietnam.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems covered in fine, silky white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, which are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and covered in a similar silvery down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and berries.
