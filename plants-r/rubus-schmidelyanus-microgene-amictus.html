Rubus schmidelyanus microgene amictus

Rubus schmidelyanus microgene amictus, commonly known as the Schmidel's bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage, which provide habitat for various small animals and insects. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, containing multiple drupes. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
