Romulea rosea var. celsii

Romulea rosea var. celsii, commonly known as the Celsii glory of the snow, is a striking bulbous perennial native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding early color to the garden.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in exotic and hardy plants.
