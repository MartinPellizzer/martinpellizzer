Rosa afzeliana f. fernarolii

Rosa afzeliana f. fernarolii, commonly known as the fern-leaf rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to regions of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. It produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique leaf structure and floral display.
