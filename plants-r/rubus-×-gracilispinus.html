Rubus × gracilispinus

Rubus × gracilispinus, commonly known as the slender thorned raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils. Its leaves are trifoliate with serrated edges, and the flowers are pink to white, appearing in clusters.

The berries, though not widely cultivated, are edible and have a tart flavor.
