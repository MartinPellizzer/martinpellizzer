Ranunculus vagans

Ranunculus vagans, commonly known as the wandering crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves that are palmately divided, with a basal rosette structure. Its flowers typically have five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and it produces small, aggregate fruits known as achenes.

Ranunculus vagans thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
