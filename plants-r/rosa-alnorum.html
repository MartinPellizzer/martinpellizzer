Rosa alnorum

Rosa alnorum, commonly known as the alder rose, is a shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found near alder trees. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa alnorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
