Rhododendron roxieanum var. recurvum

Rhododendron roxieanum var. recurvum, commonly known as the recurved rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

The varietal form, recurvum, is distinguished by its uniquely recurved petals, making it a prized species among horticultural enthusiasts.
