Rubus nitidus var. genuinus

Rubus nitidus var. genuinus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry.

This variant is characterized by its dark, almost black fruits that are typically small and firm. The plant has thorny stems and produces compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its berries are often used in jams and desserts due to their rich flavor and high pectin content.
