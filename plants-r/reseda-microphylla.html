Reseda microphylla

Reseda microphylla, commonly known as lesser reseda, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, narrow leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Reseda microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
