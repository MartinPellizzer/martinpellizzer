Rubus peramoenus

Rubus peramoenus, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

The berries are edible when ripe but can be bitter and are often used in traditional medicine.
