Ranunculus angulatus

Ranunculus angulatus, commonly known as the angular buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has a short flowering period in early spring, and its flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
