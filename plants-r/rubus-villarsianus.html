Rubus villarsianus

Rubus villarsianus, commonly known as the Villars' blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as raspberries or blackberries. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant has thorny stems typical of the Rubus family.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous habitats.
