Ranunculus cassubicus var. diversifolius

Ranunculus cassubicus var. diversifolius, commonly known as the variable buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with numerous petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitats.
