Rhododendron mucronatum var. pancratiflorum

Rhododendron mucronatum var. pancratiflorum, commonly known as the Panquetzalzintli, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in cloud forests at high elevations. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
