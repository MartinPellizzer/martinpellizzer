Rubus koehleri var. plinthostylus

Rubus koehleri var. plinthostylus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
