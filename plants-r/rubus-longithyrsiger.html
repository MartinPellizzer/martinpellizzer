Rubus longithyrsiger

Rubus longithyrsiger, commonly known as the long-thyrsis raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing multiple drupelets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
