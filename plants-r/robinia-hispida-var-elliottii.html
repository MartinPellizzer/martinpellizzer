Robinia hispida var. elliottii

Robinia hispida var. elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, followed by slender, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a mottled appearance, while its twigs are typically brown and have a slight pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
