Rosa mollis var. arenaria

Rosa mollis var. arenaria, commonly known as the sand rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are typically prostrate or ascending, often forming a carpet-like cover on the ground.

This variety is well adapted to sandy soils and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
