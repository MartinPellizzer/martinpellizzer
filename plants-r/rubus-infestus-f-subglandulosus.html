Rubus infestus f. subglandulosus

Rubus infestus f. subglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its glandular hairs on the stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
