Rosa gallica var. eriostyla

Rosa gallica var. eriostyla, commonly known as the white gallica rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, thorned stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves with hairy undersides. It prefers well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
