Rosa algoiensis

Rosa algoiensis, commonly known as the Algoi rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Altiplano of Bolivia and the Andes. The plant is characterized by its clustered, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa algoiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
