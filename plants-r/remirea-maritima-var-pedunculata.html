Remirea maritima var. pedunculata

Remirea maritima var. pedunculata, commonly known as the sea holly, is a coastal plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a silvery-blue hue that helps reflect sunlight.

Remirea maritima var. pedunculata plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
