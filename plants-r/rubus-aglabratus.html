Rubus aglabratus

Rubus aglabratus, commonly known as the smooth raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
