Rhaphidophora chevalieri

Rhaphidophora chevalieri, commonly known as the Chevalier's arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces a starchy root that is used as a food source in its native region. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value has also led to its cultivation as a houseplant in subtropical regions.
