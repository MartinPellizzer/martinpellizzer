Ranunculus × grovesiana

Ranunculus × grovesiana, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep yellow, often with a contrasting central area. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Ranunculus × grovesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
