Rangaeris rhipsalisocia

Rangaeris rhipsalisocia, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale, almost translucent leaves that give it a striking appearance. Native to the arid regions of South America, this plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, with a rosette-like structure.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to various indoor environments.
