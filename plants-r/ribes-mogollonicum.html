Ribes mogollonicum

Ribes mogollonicum, commonly known as the Mogollon gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid, or semi-arid environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, yellow or orange berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

Ribes mogollonicum plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to biodiversity in its native habitat.
