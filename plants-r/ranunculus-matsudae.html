Ranunculus matsudae

Ranunculus matsudae, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or white flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
