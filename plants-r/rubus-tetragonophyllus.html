Rubus tetragonophyllus

Rubus tetragonophyllus, commonly known as the four-angled leaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and serrated, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
