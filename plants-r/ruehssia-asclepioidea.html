Ruehssia asclepioidea

Ruehssia asclepioidea, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking white spadix flower. The plant is often cultivated for its edible corms, which are rich in starch and used in various culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is a popular choice in both gardens and agricultural settings.
