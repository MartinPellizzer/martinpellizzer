Rosa × collina var. boreykiana

Rosa × collina var. boreykiana, commonly known as the Siberian rose or the mountain rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Central Asia, where it thrives in cold, temperate climates. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
