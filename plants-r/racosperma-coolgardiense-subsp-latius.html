Racosperma coolgardiense subsp. latius

Racosperma coolgardiense subsp.

latius, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, fragrant yellow flowers that appear in dense, globular clusters.

The leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets that are narrow and linear in shape. This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
