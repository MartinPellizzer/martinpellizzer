Rosa graveolens f. rotundata

Rosa graveolens f. rotundata, commonly known as the fragrant rose or the sweet briar rose, is a fragrant, deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is known for its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Rosa genus.
