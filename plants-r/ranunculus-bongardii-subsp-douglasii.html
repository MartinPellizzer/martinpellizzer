Ranunculus bongardii subsp. douglasii

Ranunculus bongardii subsp.

douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals and numerous stamens. Its leaves are palmately divided, often with three to five lobes, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The subspecies is native to western North America, particularly in regions of Oregon and Washington.
