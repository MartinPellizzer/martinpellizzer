Reseda crispa

Reseda crispa, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Reseda crispa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
