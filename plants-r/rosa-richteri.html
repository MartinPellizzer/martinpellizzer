Rosa richteri

Rosa richteri, commonly known as the Richter rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are typically compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it has a woody stem that can become quite branched over time.

Rosa richteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively resistant to common rose diseases.
