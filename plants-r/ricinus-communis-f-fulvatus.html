Ricinus communis f. fulvatus

Ricinus communis f. fulvatus, commonly known as the yellow castor oil plant, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its striking yellow foliage.

This plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture, while the flowers are unisexual and clustered in spicate inflorescences. The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
