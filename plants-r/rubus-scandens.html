Rubus scandens

Rubus scandens, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems that often trail along the ground or climb other vegetation. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

Rubus scandens thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
