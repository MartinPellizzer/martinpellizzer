Rytidosperma virescens var. patagonicum

Rytidosperma virescens var. patagonicum, commonly known as the Patagonian feather grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale green to golden florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
