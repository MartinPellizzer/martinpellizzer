Rhododendron beanianum

Rhododendron beanianum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its floral display. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to temperate climates.
