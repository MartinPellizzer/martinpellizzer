Rosa villosa subsp. coziae

Rosa villosa subsp.

coziae, commonly known as Cozze's shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems, ovate to elliptical leaves with a hairy underside, and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness, though it is less commonly cultivated than other rose species.
