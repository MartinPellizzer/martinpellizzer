Rubus fictus var. microdrepanon

Rubus fictus var. microdrepanon, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
