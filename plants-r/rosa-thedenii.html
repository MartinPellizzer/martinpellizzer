Rosa thedenii

Rosa thedenii, commonly known as the Denison rose, is a species of climbing shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces oval, greenish-yellow fruits that are edible but often overlooked. Rosa thedenii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
