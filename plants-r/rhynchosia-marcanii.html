Rhynchosia marcanii

Rhynchosia marcanii, commonly known as the African burr bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant bears distinctive, spiny pods that contain several elliptical seeds. Rhynchosia marcanii is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its potential in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage plant.
