Raphia vinifera var. taedigera

Raphia vinifera var. taedigera, commonly known as the African wine palm, is a large, slow-growing palm native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, wine-like fruits that are an important food source for both wildlife and humans. Its fibrous leaves are also used in traditional crafts and construction.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
