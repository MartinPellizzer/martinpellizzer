Rubus carpinetorum

Rubus carpinetorum, commonly known as the common blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Rubus carpinetorum plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
