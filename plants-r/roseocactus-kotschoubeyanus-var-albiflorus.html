Roseocactus kotschoubeyanus var. albiflorus

Roseocactus kotschoubeyanus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered roseocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, white flowers during the summer months, which are often fragrant. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens and indoor settings.
