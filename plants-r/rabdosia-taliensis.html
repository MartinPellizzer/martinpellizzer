Rabdosia taliensis

Rabdosia taliensis, commonly known as Taliensis blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Rabdosia taliensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
