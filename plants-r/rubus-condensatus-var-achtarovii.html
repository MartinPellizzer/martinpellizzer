Rubus condensatus var. achtarovii

Rubus condensatus var. achtarovii, commonly known as the Achtarovii blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it flowers with white to pale pink blooms in the spring.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
