Rosa kelleri

Rosa kelleri, commonly known as the Keller's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are followed by small, oval-shaped hips that contain the seeds.

Rosa kelleri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
