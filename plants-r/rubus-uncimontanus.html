Rubus uncimontanus

Rubus uncimontanus, commonly known as the mountain blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
