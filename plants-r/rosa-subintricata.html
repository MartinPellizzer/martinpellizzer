Rosa subintricata

Rosa subintricata, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or the Brazilian wild rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include showy flowers with five to seven petals and a strong, sweet scent.

Rosa subintricata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and pleasant fragrance.
