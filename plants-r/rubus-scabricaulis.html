Rubus scabricaulis

Rubus scabricaulis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
