Ranunculus bulbosus subsp. heterorrhiza

Ranunculus bulbosus subsp.

heterorrhiza, commonly known as the bulbous crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by numerous stamens. Its subspecies heterorrhiza is distinguished by its unique root structure, which includes a bulbous base and fibrous roots.

This plant is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in semi-shaded conditions.
