Rhododendron hirsutum var. latifolium

Rhododendron hirsutum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hirsute rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often hairy, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
