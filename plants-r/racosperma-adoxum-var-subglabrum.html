Racosperma adoxum var. subglabrum

Racosperma adoxum var. subglabrum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved racosperma, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically has small, trifoliate leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
