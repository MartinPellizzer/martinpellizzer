Rhynchosia simplicifolia

Rhynchosia simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive spurred structure. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Rhynchosia simplicifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
