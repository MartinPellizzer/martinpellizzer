Rapuntium columnae

Rapuntium columnae, commonly known as the columnar rapunculus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by round, edible seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rapuntium columnae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible seeds in traditional cuisines.
