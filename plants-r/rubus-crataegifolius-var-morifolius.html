Rubus crataegifolius var. morifolius

Rubus crataegifolius var. morifolius, commonly known as the Morifolius blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, resembling the shape of hawthorn leaves, hence its scientific name. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
