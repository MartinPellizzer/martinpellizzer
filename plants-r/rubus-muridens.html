Rubus muridens

Rubus muridens, commonly known as the muridens blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like raspberries and strawberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.
