Rubus purpuratus var. ostensus

Rubus purpuratus var. ostensus, commonly known as the purple raspberry.

It is a variant of the purple raspberry, characterized by its distinctive dark purple to black fruit. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

The berries are typically small, tart, and used in culinary applications.
