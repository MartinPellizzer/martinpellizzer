Rubus coarctatus var. sylvaticus

Rubus coarctatus var. sylvaticus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and North America.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
