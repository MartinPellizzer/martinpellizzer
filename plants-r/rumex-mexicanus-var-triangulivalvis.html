Rumex mexicanus var. triangulivalvis

Rumex mexicanus var. triangulivalvis, commonly known as Mexican dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features erect, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, often with a reddish hue. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a distinctive wavy margin and a prominent midrib, and they are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and along roadsides.
