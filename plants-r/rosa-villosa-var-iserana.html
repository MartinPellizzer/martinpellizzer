Rosa villosa var. iserana

Rosa villosa var. iserana, commonly known as the Isarina rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features upright stems covered in soft, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely hairy foliage, which gives it its scientific name "villosa," meaning hairy. It is well adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms and hardy nature.
