Rubus trilobus var. guatemalensis

Rubus trilobus var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is edible, though it is less commonly cultivated compared to other blackberry varieties.
