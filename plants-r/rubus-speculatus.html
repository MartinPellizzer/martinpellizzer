Rubus speculatus

Rubus speculatus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Rubus speculatus is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
