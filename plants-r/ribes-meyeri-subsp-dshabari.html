Ribes meyeri subsp. dshabari

Ribes meyeri subsp.

dshabari, commonly known as the Dshabari gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellowish-green berries.

The plant typically grows in mountainous areas with well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, while the flowers are white and clustered in racemes.

The berries are edible when ripe and are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
