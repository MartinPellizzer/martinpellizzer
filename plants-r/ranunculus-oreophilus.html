Ranunculus oreophilus

Ranunculus oreophilus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers consist of multiple sepals that resemble petals.

Due to its habitat, it is often found in mountainous areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
