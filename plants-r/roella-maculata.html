Roella maculata

Roella maculata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple with a distinctive spotted pattern. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Roella maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
