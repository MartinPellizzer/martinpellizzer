Rhamnus pyrella

Rhamnus pyrella, commonly known as the dwarf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red berries that are a food source for birds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often found in rocky or dry soils.

Rhamnus pyrella is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
