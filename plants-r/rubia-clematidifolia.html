Rubia clematidifolia

Rubia clematidifolia, commonly known as the Clematis major, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a distinctive reddish or purple hue.

Rubia clematidifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
