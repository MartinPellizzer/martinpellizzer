Rosa montana f. wolfii

Rosa montana f. wolfii, commonly known as the Wolfii rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, thornless stems and broad, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and silvery underneath. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.

The variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
