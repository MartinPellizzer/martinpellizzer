Randia spathicalyx

Randia spathicalyx, commonly known as the purpleleaf randia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Randia spathicalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
