Rubus chlorocephalus

Rubus chlorocephalus, commonly known as the green-headed blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in groups of five.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valued plant in both traditional and modern contexts.
