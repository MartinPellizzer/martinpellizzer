Rollandia bidentata

Rollandia bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are typically green or brown, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Rollandia bidentata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
