Rubus × semisilvigenus

Rubus × semisilvigenus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, particularly in moist, well-drained environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

This species is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife.
