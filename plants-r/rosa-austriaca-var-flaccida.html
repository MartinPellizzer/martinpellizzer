Rosa austriaca var. flaccida

Rosa austriaca var. flaccida, commonly known as the Austrian shrub rose, is a perennial deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem structure and a preference for well-drained, slightly acidic soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
