Rapuntium variifolium

Rapuntium variifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf rapunculus, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or divided, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with four petals. It produces small, round, edible roots that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
