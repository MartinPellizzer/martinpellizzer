Raphanus pugioniformis

Raphanus pugioniformis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed radish, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a biennial or annual herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its fruits are short, cylindrical pods containing several seeds.

Raphanus pugioniformis is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
