Rosa communis var. lanceolata

Rosa communis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose species.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub, typically growing to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dense foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
