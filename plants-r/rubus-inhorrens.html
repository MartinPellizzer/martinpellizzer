Rubus inhorrens

Rubus inhorrens, commonly known as the rough blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and rocky soils, preferring partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
