Ranunculus auricomus subsp. angustilobulus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

angustilobulus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals and deeply divided, palmately arranged leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, and the flowers bloom in spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaf lobes compared to the typical form of Ranunculus auricomus.
