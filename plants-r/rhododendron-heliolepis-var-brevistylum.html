Rhododendron heliolepis var. brevistylum

Rhododendron heliolepis var. brevistylum, commonly known as the dwarf sun-leaved rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a distinctive sun-like pattern on the underside. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
