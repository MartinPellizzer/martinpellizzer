Rubus parvalceifolius

Rubus parvalceifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
