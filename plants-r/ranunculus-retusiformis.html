Ranunculus retusiformis

Ranunculus retusiformis, commonly known as the smoothleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds.

Ranunculus retusiformis is often found in the Pacific Northwest and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.
