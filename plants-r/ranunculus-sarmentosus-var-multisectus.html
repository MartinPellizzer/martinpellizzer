Ranunculus sarmentosus var. multisectus

Ranunculus sarmentosus var. multisectus, commonly known as the multifurcated buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5-15 sepals that resemble petals, and its stems are often hairy and sprawling. The plant has compound leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
