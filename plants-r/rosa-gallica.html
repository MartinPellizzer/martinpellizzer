Rosa gallica

Rosa gallica, commonly known as the French rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer and are often used in perfumery and culinary applications.

Rosa gallica is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and landscapes.
