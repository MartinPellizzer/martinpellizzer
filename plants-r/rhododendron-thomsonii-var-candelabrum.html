Rhododendron thomsonii var. candelabrum

Rhododendron thomsonii var. candelabrum, commonly known as the Candelabra Rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. The plant produces clusters of funnel-shaped, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its unique branching pattern and ornamental value make it a prized addition to specialized plant collections.
