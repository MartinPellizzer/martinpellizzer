Rubus fulgens

Rubus fulgens, commonly known as the firecracker raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in stiff, prickly hairs.

Rubus fulgens thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.
