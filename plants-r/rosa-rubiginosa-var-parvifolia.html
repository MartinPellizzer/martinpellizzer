Rosa rubiginosa var. parvifolia

Rosa rubiginosa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are covered in thorny, reddish bark. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
