Rytigynia orbicularis

Rytigynia orbicularis, commonly known as the globular acanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
