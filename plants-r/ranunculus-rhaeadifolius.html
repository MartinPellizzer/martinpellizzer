Ranunculus rhaeadifolius

Ranunculus rhaeadifolius, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, hairy fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus rhaeadifolius is often found in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
