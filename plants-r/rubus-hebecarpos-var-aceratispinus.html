Rubus hebecarpos var. aceratispinus

Rubus hebecarpos var. aceratispinus, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive red berries.

It is a perennial shrub native to eastern North America, typically found in woodland edges and thickets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red when mature, though they may turn black depending on the variety. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
