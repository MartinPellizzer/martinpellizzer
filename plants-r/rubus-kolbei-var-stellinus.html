Rubus kolbei var. stellinus

Rubus kolbei var. stellinus, commonly known as the star-forming blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped bracts that surround the flower cluster, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
