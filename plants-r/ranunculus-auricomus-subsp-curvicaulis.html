Ranunculus auricomus subsp. curvicaulis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

curvicaulis, commonly known as the curlyleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its tightly curled leaflets, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is often found in the eastern United States.
