Rosa canina subvar. sabranskyi

Rosa canina subvar. sabranskyi, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces bright red, aggregate fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
