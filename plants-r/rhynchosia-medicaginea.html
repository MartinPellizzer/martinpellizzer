Rhynchosia medicaginea

Rhynchosia medicaginea, commonly known as the African bean or wild pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is often found growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides due to its adaptability. It has a climbing habit and can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

Rhynchosia medicaginea is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
