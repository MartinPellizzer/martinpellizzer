Rosa tomentosa var. anacantha

Rosa tomentosa var. anacantha, commonly known as the wooly rose or the hairy rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly grey foliage and small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces red-orange hips that are edible and contain seeds.

Rosa tomentosa var. anacantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
