Rubus nuperus

Rubus nuperus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and apples.
