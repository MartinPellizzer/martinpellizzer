Rubus gelertii var. holubyanus

Rubus gelertii var. holubyanus, commonly known as the Holubyan's blackberry, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, dark purple to black berries, which are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

The berries are valued for their tart flavor and are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
