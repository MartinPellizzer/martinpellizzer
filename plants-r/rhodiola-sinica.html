Rhodiola sinica

Rhodiola sinica, commonly known as the golden root or rhodiola rosea, is a perennial plant native to the cold climates of Asia, including the Himalayas and the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its fleshy, rhizomatous root system and its clusters of small, pink or yellow flowers. The plant thrives in alpine and arctic environments, where it is adapted to harsh conditions with low temperatures and high UV radiation. Its leaves are typically basal, with a rosette structure that helps retain moisture.

Rhodiola sinica is well-known for its adaptogenic properties, which have been studied for their potential benefits in reducing stress and enhancing physical endurance.
