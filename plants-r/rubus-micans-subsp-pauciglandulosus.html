Rubus micans subsp. pauciglandulosus

Rubus micans subsp.

pauciglandulosus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark, almost black, edible berries.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a glossy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
