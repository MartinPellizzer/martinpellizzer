Rosa mollissima var. eglandulosa

Rosa mollissima var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular mossy rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a soft, moss-like appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, glandular hairs, which contribute to its unique texture and appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
