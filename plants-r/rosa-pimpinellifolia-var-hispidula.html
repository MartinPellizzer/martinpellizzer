Rosa pimpinellifolia var. hispidula

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has thorny stems and hairy leaves, which help it adapt to rocky and arid environments. It blooms in spring and is often found growing on limestone soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal and dry garden settings.
