Ranunculus minutiflorus

Ranunculus minutiflorus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a small flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus minutiflorus is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it blooms in early spring.
