Rhipidoglossum polydactylum

Rhipidoglossum polydactylum, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, which consists of long, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white or pale yellow.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appearance and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
