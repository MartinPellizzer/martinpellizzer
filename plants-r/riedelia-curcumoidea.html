Riedelia curcumoidea

Riedelia curcumoidea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often fragrant. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, typically arranged in a loose rosette.

Riedelia curcumoidea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
