Rosa coriifolia var. haliciensis

Rosa coriifolia var. haliciensis, commonly known as the Halic island rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to specific regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in shades of pink to white. The plant features thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest in botanical research.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate for optimal growth.
