Ravensara laevis

Ravensara laevis, commonly known as the silky raven's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a unique texture. The plant produces striking, waxy flowers that are typically deep purple or red, with a distinctive spathe and spadix structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Ravensara laevis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
