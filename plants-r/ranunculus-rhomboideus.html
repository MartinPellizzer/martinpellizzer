Ranunculus rhomboideus

Ranunculus rhomboideus, commonly known as the rhomboid buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny habitats. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are rhomboid in shape, giving it its common name. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are often accompanied by five sepals.

Ranunculus rhomboideus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers.
