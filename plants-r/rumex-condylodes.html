Rumex condylodes

Rumex condylodes, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib and toothed margins. Its small, greenish flowers are arranged in clusters and are followed by elongated, triangular seed pods.

Rumex condylodes is often used in traditional medicine and is also a host plant for certain insect species.
