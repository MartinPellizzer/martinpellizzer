Rosa cinnamomea subalpina

Rosa cinnamomea subalpina, commonly known as the subalpine cinnamon rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green and slightly hairy, and the stems are covered in a distinctive cinnamon-colored bark.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
