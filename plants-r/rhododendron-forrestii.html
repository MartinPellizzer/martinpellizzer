Rhododendron forrestii

Rhododendron forrestii, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant features dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Rhododendron forrestii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with acidic soil and partial shade.
