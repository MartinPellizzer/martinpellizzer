Rosa gallica var. variegata

Rosa gallica var. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Gallica rose, is a decorative shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, deeply lobed leaves with striking white or yellow variegation. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in early summer. It is relatively low maintenance and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
