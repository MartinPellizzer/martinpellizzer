Rubus questieri var. stenoacanthiformis

Rubus questieri var. stenoacanthiformis, commonly known as the narrow-spined blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture. This variety is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique morphological traits that set it apart from other blackberry species.
