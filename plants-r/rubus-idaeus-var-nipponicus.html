Rubus idaeus var. nipponicus

Rubus idaeus var. nipponicus, commonly known as Japanese red raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect or spreading shrub form, with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red when mature, though some varieties may have yellow or orange berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are also used in traditional herbal remedies in some regions.
