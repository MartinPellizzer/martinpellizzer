Rubus sanctus var. vulgaris

Rubus sanctus var. vulgaris, commonly known as common blackberry.

It is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

The berries are edible, though they can be quite tart and are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
