Rudbeckia maxima

Rudbeckia maxima, commonly known as the giant coneflower, is a striking perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by broad, dark green leaves. The plant can grow up to 6 feet tall, making it one of the tallest species in the Rudbeckia genus. Its robust root system allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained soils.

Rudbeckia maxima is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
