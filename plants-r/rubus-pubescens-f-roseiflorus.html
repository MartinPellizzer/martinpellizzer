Rubus pubescens f. roseiflorus

Rubus pubescens f. roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered bramble, is a botanical variant of the common bramble species.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it both a useful plant for ecological studies and a potential candidate for ornamental horticulture.
