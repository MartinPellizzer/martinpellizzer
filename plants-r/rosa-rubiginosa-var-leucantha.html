Rosa rubiginosa var. leucantha

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces oval, reddish-brown fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
