Rhododendron lowii

Rhododendron lowii, commonly known as the Formosan azalea, is a species native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.

Rhododendron lowii is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
