Rubus microbelus

Rubus microbelus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
