Ribes × schneideri

Ribes × schneideri, commonly known as the hybrid gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the family Grossulariaceae.

It is a natural hybrid between Ribes aureum (gold gooseberry) and Ribes rubrum (red gooseberry), resulting in a plant that exhibits characteristics of both parents. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and produces clusters of small, edible berries that range in color from yellow to red depending on the variety. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are usually pink or white and appear in clusters.

Ribes × schneideri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of tart berries used in jams and wines.
