Ritterocereus eichlamii

Ritterocereus eichlamii, commonly known as the Eichlam's night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems forming a dense, rounded shape.

The species is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent gardens.
