Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp. candicans

Rubus thyrsiflorus subsp.

candicans, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with new shoots emerging from the base each year.

The plant produces large, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are aggregate berries that ripen to a deep black color, often with a sweet and tangy flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
