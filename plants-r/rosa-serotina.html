Rosa serotina

Rosa serotina, commonly known as the late rose, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red in color. The plant produces oval, reddish-brown fruits called hips, which are edible and contain seeds. Rosa serotina thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, and it is often found in the eastern United States.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
