Russelia floribunda var. pubescens

Russelia floribunda var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy russelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of tubular, pale pink to red flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds.
