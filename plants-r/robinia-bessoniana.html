Robinia bessoniana

Robinia bessoniana, commonly known as the Besson's acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by long, flat pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Robinia bessoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh conditions.
