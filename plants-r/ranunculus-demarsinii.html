Ranunculus demarsinii

Ranunculus demarsinii, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it has a short, stout stem.

Ranunculus demarsinii is often found in high-altitude environments, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
