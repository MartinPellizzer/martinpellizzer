Rubus vulgaris sylvaticus

Rubus vulgaris sylvaticus, commonly known as the common bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries, though they are often covered in a hairy coating. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Its widespread distribution across Europe and parts of Asia makes it a notable member of the Rubus genus.
