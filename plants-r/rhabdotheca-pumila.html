Rhabdotheca pumila

Rhabdotheca pumila, commonly known as the woolly rhabdotheca, is a small, mat-forming succulent native to southern Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and unique appearance make it a desirable addition to collections of succulents and cacti.
