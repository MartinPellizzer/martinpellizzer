Rubus radula subsp. ericetorum

Rubus radula subsp.

ericetorum, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland environments.
