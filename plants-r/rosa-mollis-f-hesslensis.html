Rosa mollis f. hesslensis

Rosa mollis f. hesslensis, commonly known as the Hesslensian soft rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa mollis species, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This plant is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a compact shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with thornless stems and dark green leaves. The plant prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and pleasant aroma, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
