Rubus malipoensis

Rubus malipoensis, commonly known as the Malipo blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with a preference for moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, and the flowers are small, white, and clustered in cymes.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local culinary traditions.
