Ravenea cycadifolia

Ravenea cycadifolia, commonly known as the cycad-like ravenea, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that resemble those of cycads, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellowish-pink inflorescences with small, clustered flowers, which are pollinated by insects. Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.

Ravenea cycadifolia is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its adaptability to different light conditions.
