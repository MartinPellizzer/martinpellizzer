Rubus subtomentosiformis

Rubus subtomentosiformis, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide protection against herbivores. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and the plant produces small, edible berries that are similar to blackberries.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
