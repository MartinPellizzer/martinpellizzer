Roystonea regia var. pinguis

Roystonea regia var. pinguis, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in landscaping due to its elegant appearance.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in suitable environments.
