Rhus lavandulifolia

Rhus lavandulifolia, commonly known as the lavender leaf sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lavender-colored leaves that emit a strong mint-like aroma when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like poison ivy and mango.
