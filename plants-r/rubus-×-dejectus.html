Rubus × dejectus

Rubus × dejectus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to parts of North America and is known for its adaptability to various soil types and climates. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it often grows in dense thickets.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain regions.
