Rosa viscaria var. arvernensis

Rosa viscaria var. arvernensis, commonly known as the Arvernese rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of France.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

It is often found in rocky or wooded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and historical significance.
