Radicula brevipes

Radicula brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed radicula, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a compact rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and range in color from pale yellow to reddish-orange.

Radicula brevipes is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its resilience to periods of drought.
