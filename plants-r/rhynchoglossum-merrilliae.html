Rhynchoglossum merrilliae

Rhynchoglossum merrilliae, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns, which are typical of the Orchidaceae family. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

Rhynchoglossum merrilliae thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
