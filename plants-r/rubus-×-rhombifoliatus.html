Rubus × rhombifoliatus

Rubus × rhombifoliatus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive rhomboid shape. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark red to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
