Rubus subcaesius

Rubus subcaesius, commonly known as the purple-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a slightly tart flavor. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often found in woodlands and along the edges of forests.

The flowers are white and arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
