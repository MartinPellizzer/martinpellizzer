Rubus frondosus

Rubus frondosus, commonly known as the fronded blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically divided into three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other members of the Rubus genus.
