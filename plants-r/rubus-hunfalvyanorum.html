Rubus hunfalvyanorum

Rubus hunfalvyanorum, commonly known as the Hunfalvy bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe, particularly in the Carpathian Basin. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
