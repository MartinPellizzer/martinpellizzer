Ranunculus coacervatus

Ranunculus coacervatus, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often with a distinctive dark center. The plant has alternate, palmately compound leaves that are finely divided and covered in a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its clustered growth habit and its relation to the buttercup family.
