Ranunculus camozzianus

Ranunculus camozzianus, commonly known as the Camozzian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of the European Alps.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 2,500 meters. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five to nine petals. Its flowers are usually short-lived, opening in early spring and lasting for only a few days.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in meadows and stream banks.
