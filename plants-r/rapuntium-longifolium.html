Rapuntium longifolium

Rapuntium longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf rapunculus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
