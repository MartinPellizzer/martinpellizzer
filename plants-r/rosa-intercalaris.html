Rosa intercalaris

Rosa intercalaris, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring thorny stems and dense foliage. It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Rosa intercalaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic value.
