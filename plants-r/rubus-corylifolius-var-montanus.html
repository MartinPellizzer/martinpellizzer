Rubus corylifolius var. montanus

Rubus corylifolius var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its berries are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The species is valued for its ornamental value as well as its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
