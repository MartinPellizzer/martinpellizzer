Rumex triangulivalvis f. unigranis

Rumex triangulivalvis f. unigranis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dock, is a variant of the broadleaf dock species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its triangular-shaped leaves with a prominent central vein. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils along streambanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are usually narrow and lanceolate, with a rough, serrated edge.

It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on erect, unbranched flowering stems.
