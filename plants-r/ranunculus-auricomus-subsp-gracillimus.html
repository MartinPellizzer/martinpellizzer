Ranunculus auricomus subsp. gracillimus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

gracillimus, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by a hairy leaf surface.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more elongated leaves compared to the typical auricomus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is an herbaceous perennial that produces seeds in late summer, contributing to its ecological role in local ecosystems.
