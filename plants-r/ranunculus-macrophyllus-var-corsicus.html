Ranunculus macrophyllus var. corsicus

Ranunculus macrophyllus var. corsicus, commonly known as Corsican buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered an invasive species in some areas.
