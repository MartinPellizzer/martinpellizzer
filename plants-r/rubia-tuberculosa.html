Rubia tuberculosa

Rubia tuberculosa, commonly known as the tuberculate woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia tuberculosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
