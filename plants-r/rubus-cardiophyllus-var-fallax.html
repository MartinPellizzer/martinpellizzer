Rubus cardiophyllus var. fallax

Rubus cardiophyllus var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax raspberry, is a variant of the cardiophyllus raspberry species.

It is a shrub native to certain regions of North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, though it is less commonly cultivated than other raspberry varieties.
