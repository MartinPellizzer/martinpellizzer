Rebutia senilis f. chrysacantha

Rebutia senilis f. chrysacantha, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a hybrid form of the senilis species within the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that often develop a golden to yellowish hue, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
