Rubia uruguayensis

Rubia uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay madder, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it is known for its use in traditional dyeing practices.

The roots of Rubia uruguayensis contain compounds that have been historically used for their color-producing properties.
