Rosa argus

Rosa argus, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, including parts of Turkey, Iran, and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, while the stems are covered in soft, grayish hairs.

Rosa argus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional gardens.
