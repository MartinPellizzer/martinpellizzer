Rubus mesogaeus var. oxycomus

Rubus mesogaeus var. oxycomus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible, red or black berries. Its fruit is typically harvested in the summer and is valued for its sweet-tart flavor.

The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
