Ruellia humilis var. typica

Ruellia humilis var. typica, commonly known as the common ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Ruellia humilis var. typica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and relatively low maintenance requirements.
