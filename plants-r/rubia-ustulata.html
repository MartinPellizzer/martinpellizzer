Rubia ustulata

Rubia ustulata, commonly known as the scorched woodbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 12,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, and it produces small, dry fruits called drupes.

Rubia ustulata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
