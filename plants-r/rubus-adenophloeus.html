Rubus adenophloeus

Rubus adenophloeus, commonly known as the hairy everlet bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that develop from its flowers. It typically grows in wooded areas and along forest edges, where it can climb or spread through its extensive root system.

The botanical features of Rubus adenophloeus make it an important plant for both ecological studies and horticultural interest.
