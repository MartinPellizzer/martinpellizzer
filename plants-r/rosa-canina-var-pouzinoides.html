Rosa canina var. pouzinoides

Rosa canina var. pouzinoides, commonly known as the Algerian rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Algeria and surrounding areas. This shrub typically features thorny stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant produces red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
