Rosa adenochaeta

Rosa adenochaeta, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a serrated edge, while its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip.

Rosa adenochaeta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
