Rhododendron nematocalyx

Rhododendron nematocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
