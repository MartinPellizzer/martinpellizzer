Rhododendron rubropilosum var. breviperulatum

Rhododendron rubropilosum var. breviperulatum, commonly known as the short-hairy rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
