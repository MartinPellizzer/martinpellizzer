Rubus nigricatus

Rubus nigricatus, commonly known as blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that produces edible blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The flowers are white or pale green, and the fruit ripens in late summer.
