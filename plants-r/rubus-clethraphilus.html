Rubus clethraphilus

Rubus clethraphilus, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may also turn black. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it thrives in woodland and shaded areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
