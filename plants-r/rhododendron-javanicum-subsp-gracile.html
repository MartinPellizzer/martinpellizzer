Rhododendron javanicum subsp. gracile

Rhododendron javanicum subsp.

gracile, commonly known as the slender rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are typically elliptical and glossy green.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, usually in shades of pink or red, during the spring season. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its aesthetic appeal.
