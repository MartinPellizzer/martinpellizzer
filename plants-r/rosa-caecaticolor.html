Rosa caecaticolor

Rosa caecaticolor, commonly known as the ghostly rose, is a rare and striking species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale, almost translucent pink petals that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green and serrated, with a slightly hairy texture.

Rosa caecaticolor is a low-growing shrub that thrives in cool, moist environments and is prized for its unique aesthetic and horticultural value.
