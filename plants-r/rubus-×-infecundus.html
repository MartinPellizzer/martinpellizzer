Rubus × infecundus

Rubus × infecundus, commonly known as the sterile blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus fruticosus. This species is notable for its lack of viable seeds, making it primarily a vegetative propagator. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are often used in culinary applications.

Its botanical features include thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits.
