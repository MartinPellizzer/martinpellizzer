Rubus neerlandicus

Rubus neerlandicus, commonly known as the Dutch raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its edible fruits.
