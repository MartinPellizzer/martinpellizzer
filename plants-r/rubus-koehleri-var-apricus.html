Rubus koehleri var. apricus

Rubus koehleri var. apricus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
