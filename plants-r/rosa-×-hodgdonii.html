Rosa × hodgdonii

Rosa × hodgdonii, commonly known as the Hodgdon rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has arching, thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
