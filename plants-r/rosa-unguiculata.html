Rosa unguiculata

Rosa unguiculata, commonly known as the pink wild rose, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the southwestern regions.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant pink to red flowers in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorns. Rosa unguiculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
