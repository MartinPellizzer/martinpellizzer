Randia jambosoides

Randia jambosoides, commonly known as the red mangrove or false mangrove, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetland and coastal environments. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

Randia jambosoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
