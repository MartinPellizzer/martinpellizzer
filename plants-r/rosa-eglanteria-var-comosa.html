Rosa eglanteria var. comosa

Rosa eglanteria var. comosa, commonly known as the bristly prickly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that persist into winter.

It is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
