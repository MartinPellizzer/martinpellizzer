Rotang blancoi

Rotang blancoi, commonly known as the white mangrove, is a species of mangrove tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its white, waxy bark and its ability to thrive in saline and tidal environments. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

Rotang blancoi plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
