Robertsonia × polita

Robertsonia × polita, commonly known as the hybrid snowbell, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its ornamental value comes from its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Robertsonia × polita is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
