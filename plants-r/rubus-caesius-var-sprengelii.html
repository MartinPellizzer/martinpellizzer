Rubus caesius var. sprengelii

Rubus caesius var. sprengelii, commonly known as the Sprengel's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupelets. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its edible berries, which are often used in culinary applications.
