Rhododendron × amoenum var. japonicum

Rhododendron × amoenum var. japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers acidic, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, making it a popular choice for floral displays.
