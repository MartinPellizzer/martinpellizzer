Rosa × centifolia var. bullata

Rosa × centifolia var. bullata, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a hybrid species originating from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and perfumery.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient times, where it was cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils.
