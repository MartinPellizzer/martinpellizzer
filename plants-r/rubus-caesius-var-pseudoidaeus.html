Rubus caesius var. pseudoidaeus

Rubus caesius var. pseudoidaeus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This variant is a shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
