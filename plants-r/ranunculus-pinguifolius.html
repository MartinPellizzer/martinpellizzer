Ranunculus pinguifolius

Ranunculus pinguifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
