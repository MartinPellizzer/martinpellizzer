Rhaphiolepis salicifolia

Rhaphiolepis salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, ovate leaves that are serrated and resemble willow leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Rhaphiolepis salicifolia is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to different climates.
