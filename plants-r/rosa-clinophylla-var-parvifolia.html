Rosa clinophylla var. parvifolia

Rosa clinophylla var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wild rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in pink to white hues, typically in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a slightly serrated edge, and are arranged alternately along the stems. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
