Ricinocarpus controversus

Ricinocarpus controversus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mangrove, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, particularly in brackish or saline soils. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces woody capsules that contain several seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
