Rhizobolus souari

Rhizobolus souari, commonly known as the Southern African rhizobolus, is a lesser-known plant species native to the grasslands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Rhizobolus souari is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native region.
