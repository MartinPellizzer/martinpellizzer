Rubus schleicheri var. micranthemus

Rubus schleicheri var. micranthemus, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in shaded environments.
