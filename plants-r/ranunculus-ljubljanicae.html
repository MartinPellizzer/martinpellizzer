Ranunculus ljubljanicae

Ranunculus ljubljanicae, commonly known as the Ljubljana buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Slovenia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
