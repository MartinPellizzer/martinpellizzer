Rhynchosia sumatrana

Rhynchosia sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran burr legume, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that enclose its seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Rhynchosia sumatrana is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
