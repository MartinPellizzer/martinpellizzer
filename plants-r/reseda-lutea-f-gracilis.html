Reseda lutea f. gracilis

Reseda lutea f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender yellowweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
