Rhododendron multiflorum var. purpureum

Rhododendron multiflorum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple-flowered rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
