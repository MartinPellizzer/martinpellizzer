Rubus putus

Rubus putus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Rubus putus thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the flora in its native habitats.
