Rubus adulterinus

Rubus adulterinus, commonly known as the false blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, usually white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are typically red or black when mature and are often mistaken for true blackberries.

Rubus adulterinus is native to parts of Asia and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
