Rosa coriifolia var. subviridis

Rosa coriifolia var. subviridis, commonly known as the greenleaf dogwood rose, is a variant of the Chinese rose.

It is characterized by its smooth, greenish-yellow foliage and clusters of small, pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, adding visual interest to landscapes.
