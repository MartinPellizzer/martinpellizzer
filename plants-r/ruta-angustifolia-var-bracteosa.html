Ruta angustifolia var. bracteosa

Ruta angustifolia var. bracteosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rue with bracts, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, pinnately compound leaves with a strong, pungent odor. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its bracts are distinctive, being leaf-like structures that enclose the flower clusters.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
