Rubus ammobius

Rubus ammobius, commonly known as the sandhill blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils, often along roadsides or in open woodlands. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark purple to black berries.

The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in coastal areas where it plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
