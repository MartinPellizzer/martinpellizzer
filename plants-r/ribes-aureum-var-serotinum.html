Ribes aureum var. serotinum

Ribes aureum var. serotinum, commonly known as the late-flowering golden currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom later in the season compared to other Ribes species. The plant produces small, edible red berries that ripen in late summer. It has oval, dark green leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in native plant landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
