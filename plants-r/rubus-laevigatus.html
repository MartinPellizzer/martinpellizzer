Rubus laevigatus

Rubus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark purple berries in the summer. These berries are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
