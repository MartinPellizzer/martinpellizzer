Rubus longithyrsiger var. botryeros

Rubus longithyrsiger var. botryeros, commonly known as the clustered raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Asia. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, with a sweet-tart flavor. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
