Rosa tomentosa subvar. multiglandulosa

Rosa tomentosa subvar. multiglandulosa, commonly known as the hairy rose or woolly rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, greyish-white pubescence covering the stems, leaves, and flowers. The leaves are compound, typically consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a fragrant aroma, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
