Rondeletia inermis subsp. sclerophylla

Rondeletia inermis subsp.

sclerophylla, commonly known as the hard-leaved rondeletia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically leathery and have a rough texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
