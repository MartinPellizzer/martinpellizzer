Rubus affinis var. subsenticosus

Rubus affinis var. subsenticosus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
