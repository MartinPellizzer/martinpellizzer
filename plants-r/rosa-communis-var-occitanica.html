Rosa communis var. occitanica

Rosa communis var. occitanica, commonly known as the Occitan rose, is a cultivar of the common rose that is prized for its ornamental value.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and a woody, thorny stem structure typical of many rose species. It is native to the Occitanie region of southern France and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance in perfumery and floral arrangements.
