Rosa tomentosa var. liostyla

Rosa tomentosa var. liostyla, commonly known as the woolly rose or Chinese woolly rose, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and aromatic qualities.
