Rubus × consanguineus

Rubus × consanguineus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along fences.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
