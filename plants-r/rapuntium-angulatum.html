Rapuntium angulatum

Rapuntium angulatum, commonly known as the angular rapunculus, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are typically lobed and hairy. It grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate regions.

The plant is not typically cultivated for food but is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
