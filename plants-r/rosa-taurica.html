Rosa taurica

Rosa taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features thorny stems and simple, lobed leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its hips, which are orange-red berries, contain seeds and are often used in traditional medicine.

Rosa taurica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in herbal remedies.
