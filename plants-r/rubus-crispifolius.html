Rubus crispifolius

Rubus crispifolius, commonly known as the crinkled-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Rubus crispifolius is a shrub that can grow up to several meters in height, with thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers.
