Ranunculus extensus

Ranunculus extensus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are pollinated by insects.

The plant contains toxic compounds, including protoanemonin, which can cause skin irritation.
