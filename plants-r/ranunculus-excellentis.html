Ranunculus excellentis

Ranunculus excellentis, commonly known as the noble buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has simple, trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are usually erect and branched. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

The flowers bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens with their delicate and elegant appearance.
