Rosa sherardii var. woodsiana

Rosa sherardii var. woodsiana, commonly known as the Woodsian shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features upright, thorny stems and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets, and it has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
