Ruellia balsamica

Ruellia balsamica, commonly known as the balsam bush or red-veined ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins and clusters of blue or purple tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.

Ruellia balsamica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
