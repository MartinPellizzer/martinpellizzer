Rouxia

Rouxia, commonly known as the nightshade plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its typically showy, tubular flowers that often come in shades of blue, purple, or white. The leaves are usually alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture. Rouxia species are native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in tropical and subtropical climates.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and some species are used in traditional medicine.
