Ranunculus bupleuroides

Ranunculus bupleuroides, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
