Rosa sepium var. oblongicarpa

Rosa sepium var. oblongicarpa, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, often with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a follicle, which splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
