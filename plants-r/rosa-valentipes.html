Rosa valentipes

Rosa valentipes, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a species native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season. Its flowers are typically 2 to 4 centimeters in diameter and have five petals.

Rosa valentipes is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
