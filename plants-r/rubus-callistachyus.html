Rubus callistachyus

Rubus callistachyus, commonly known as the velvet raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and clusters of small, bright red berries that ripen in the summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and strawberries.
