Rosa celsiana

Rosa celsiana, commonly known as the Celsian rose, is a species of shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. Rosa celsiana is well adapted to arid and coastal environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
