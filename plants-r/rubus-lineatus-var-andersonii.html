Rubus lineatus var. andersonii

Rubus lineatus var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's blackberry, is a variant of the trailing blackberry species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include a pubescent surface and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value in gardens.
