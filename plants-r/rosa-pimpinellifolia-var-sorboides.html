Rosa pimpinellifolia var. sorboides

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. sorboides, commonly known as the Sorboides rose, is a variant of the shrub rose characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant flowers.

It produces small, pink to white flowers that are often clustered in flat-topped umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage is typically dense and dark green, with a slightly serrated leaf margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
