Rubus × brevipubens

Rubus × brevipubens, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its compact growth habit and small, dark berries. The plant typically features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

Its berries are edible and are sometimes used in jams and preserves.
