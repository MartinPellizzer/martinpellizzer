Rubus hercynicus subsp. hercynicus

Rubus hercynicus subsp.

hercynicus, commonly known as the Hercynian blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically clustered in groups of three. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
