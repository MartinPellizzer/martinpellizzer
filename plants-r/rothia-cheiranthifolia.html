Rothia cheiranthifolia

Rothia cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Rothia cheiranthifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
