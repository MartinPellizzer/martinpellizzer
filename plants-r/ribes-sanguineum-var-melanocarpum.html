Ribes sanguineum var. melanocarpum

Ribes sanguineum var. melanocarpum, commonly known as the black-fruited currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features upright, arching branches that are covered in small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in early spring. Its most distinctive feature is its dark purple to black berries, which ripen in late summer and are edible.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
