Rubus apiculatus var. reductus

Rubus apiculatus var. reductus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable species within the Rubus genus.
