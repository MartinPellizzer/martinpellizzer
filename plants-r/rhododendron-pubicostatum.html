Rhododendron pubicostatum

Rhododendron pubicostatum, commonly known as the pubescent leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
