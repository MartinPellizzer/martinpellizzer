Rhynchosia maitlandii

Rhynchosia maitlandii, commonly known as the Maitland's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Rhynchosia maitlandii is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated as a source of protein in various cuisines.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
