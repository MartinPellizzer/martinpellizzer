Rubus bifrons

Rubus bifrons, commonly known as the twin-fruit blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
