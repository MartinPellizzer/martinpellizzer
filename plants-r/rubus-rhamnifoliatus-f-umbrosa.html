Rubus rhamnifoliatus f. umbrosa

Rubus rhamnifoliatus f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowleaf raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that thrive in shaded environments.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions with moist, temperate climates. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, similar to other Rubus species. Its foliage is dense and often used in horticultural settings for ground cover.

The botanical features of this form make it well-suited for woodland gardens and shaded landscapes.
