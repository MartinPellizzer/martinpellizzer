Rosa fragariifolia

Rosa fragariifolia, commonly known as the wild strawberry rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are similar in appearance to wild strawberries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Rosa fragariifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
