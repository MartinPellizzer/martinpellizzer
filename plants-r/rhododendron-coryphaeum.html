Rhododendron coryphaeum

Rhododendron coryphaeum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in cool, moist climates at high elevations, often in forested areas. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of wildlife.

Rhododendron coryphaeum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
