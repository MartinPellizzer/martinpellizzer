Rubus × propinquiformis

Rubus × propinquiformis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (loganberry), resulting in a plant with distinctive characteristics. The plant produces large, dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and sweeter than those of its parent species. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant is often found in coastal regions of California.

It is valued for its fruit, which is used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
