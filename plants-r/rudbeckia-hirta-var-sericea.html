Rudbeckia hirta var. sericea

Rudbeckia hirta var. sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, fuzzy texture, and its flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
