Rubus × oxycalyx

Rubus × oxycalyx, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus, combining traits from both parent species. This shrub is native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California, where it thrives in sandy soils and mild climates. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
