Racosperma polystachyum

Racosperma polystachyum, commonly known as the mountain wisteria, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of fragrant, blue to purple flowers. The plant has pinnate leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its seeds are encased in pods that split open to release the seeds, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
