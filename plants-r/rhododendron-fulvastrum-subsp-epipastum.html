Rhododendron fulvastrum subsp. epipastum

Rhododendron fulvastrum subsp.

epipastum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rhododendron, is a subshrub native to the Himalayas. It features showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
