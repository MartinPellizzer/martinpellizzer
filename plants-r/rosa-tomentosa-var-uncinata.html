Rosa tomentosa var. uncinata

Rosa tomentosa var. uncinata, commonly known as the woolly rose or hooked woolly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white pubescent foliage and thorny stems. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals, and bloom in spring. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
