Rhizanota cannabina

Rhizanota cannabina, commonly known as the cannabis plant, is a flowering herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to produce both male and female flowers. The plant contains high concentrations of cannabinoids, particularly tetrahydrocannabinol (THC), which contribute to its psychoactive properties. Rhizanota cannabina thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and recreational uses.

Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for various therapeutic purposes.
