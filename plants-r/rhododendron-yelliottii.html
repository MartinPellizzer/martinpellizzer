Rhododendron yelliottii

Rhododendron yelliottii, commonly known as the Yelliott's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, broad leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows at high elevations, preferring cool, moist environments with well-drained acidic soils. Its botanical features include hairy stems and a compact growth habit, making it a hardy and ornamental plant in suitable climates.

Rhododendron yelliottii is valued for its ecological role in mountain ecosystems and its potential use in horticulture.
