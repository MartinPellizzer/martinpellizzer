Rosa austriaca f. pannonica

Rosa austriaca f. pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian Austrian rose, is a botanical variant of the Austrian rose, characterized by its distinctive floral and foliar features.

It is native to Central Europe, particularly in regions such as Hungary and Austria, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant typically produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorny prickles.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
