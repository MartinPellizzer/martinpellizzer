Ribes tricuspe var. japonicum

Ribes tricuspe var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow-orange berries. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces showy, red-pink flowers in early spring.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which are rich in vitamin C. The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
