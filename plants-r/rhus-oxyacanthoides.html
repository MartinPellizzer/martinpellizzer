Rhus oxyacanthoides

Rhus oxyacanthoides, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and oval, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with mild winters.
