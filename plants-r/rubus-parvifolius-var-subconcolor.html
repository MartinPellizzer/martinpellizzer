Rubus parvifolius var. subconcolor

Rubus parvifolius var. subconcolor, commonly known as the smoothleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
