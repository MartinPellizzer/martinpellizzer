Rosa × involuta var. schmidelyi

Rosa × involuta var. schmidelyi, commonly known as the Schmidel's shrub rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant typically produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
