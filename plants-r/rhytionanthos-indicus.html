Rhytionanthos indicus

Rhytionanthos indicus, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
