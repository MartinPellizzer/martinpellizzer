Rosa cantabrica

Rosa cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian rose, is a species native to the coastal regions of northern Spain and southern France.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along the Atlantic coast. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant is well adapted to maritime climates.

Rosa cantabrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional herbal medicine.
