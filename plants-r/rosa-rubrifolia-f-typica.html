Rosa rubrifolia f. typica

Rosa rubrifolia f. typica, commonly known as the red-leaf rose, is a compact shrub characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage.

It produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are elliptical and have a velvety texture, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
