Rosa × fugax

Rosa × fugax, commonly known as the transient rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its botanical features include a woody structure and the ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Rosa × fugax is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fleeting yet striking floral display.
