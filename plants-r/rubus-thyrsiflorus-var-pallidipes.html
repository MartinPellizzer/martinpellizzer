Rubus thyrsiflorus var. pallidipes

Rubus thyrsiflorus var. pallidipes, commonly known as the paleflower bramble, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and the plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
