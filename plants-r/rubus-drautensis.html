Rubus drautensis

Rubus drautensis, commonly known as the Drautensian bramble, is a shrub native to the Carpathian region of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and rocky soils, often in humid climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
