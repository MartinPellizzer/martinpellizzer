Rosa pontica

Rosa pontica, commonly known as the Pontic rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southeastern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces edible rose hips that contain seeds.

Rosa pontica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
