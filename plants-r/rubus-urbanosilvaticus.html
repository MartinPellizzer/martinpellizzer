Rubus urbanosilvaticus

Rubus urbanosilvaticus, commonly known as the urban bramble, is a shrub native to temperate regions and thrives in disturbed soils.

It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and adaptability to urban environments. It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.

However, its invasive potential in certain areas requires careful management.
