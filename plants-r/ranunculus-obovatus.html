Ranunculus obovatus

Ranunculus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and western Asia.

It features a short, upright stem with trifoliate leaves that are broadly ovate and hairy. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals, often with a distinctive central crown of stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The species is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape and bright, cheerful blooms.
