Rubus tereticaulis var. oraliflorens

Rubus tereticaulis var. oraliflorens, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing numerous druplets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
