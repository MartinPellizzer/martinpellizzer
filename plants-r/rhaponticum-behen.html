Rhaponticum behen

Rhaponticum behen, commonly known as Siberian cowpea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the cold climates of eastern Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with serrated, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its roots are notable for their high content of fatty oils, which have been historically used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh, cold environments.
