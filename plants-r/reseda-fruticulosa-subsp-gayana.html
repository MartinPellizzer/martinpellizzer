Reseda fruticulosa subsp. gayana

Reseda fruticulosa subsp.

gayana, commonly known as the gayana rockrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, often hairy leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
