Rhipsalis parasitica subsp. shaferi

Rhipsalis parasitica subsp.

shaferi, commonly known as the Shafer's mistletoe cactus, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions. It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using modified leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its structure is characterized by thin, branching stems covered in small, hair-like spines.

This subspecies is often found in shaded, humid environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
