Rosa sulfurea

Rosa sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are typically borne singly or in small clusters, and they emit a subtle, sweet fragrance.

Rosa sulfurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
