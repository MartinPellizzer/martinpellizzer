Ranunculus hispidus var. hispidus

Ranunculus hispidus var. hispidus, commonly known as the hispid buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental members.
