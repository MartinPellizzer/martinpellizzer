Rubus tephrodes var. holadenus

Rubus tephrodes var. holadenus, commonly known as the red sandalwood raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, usually white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and potential medicinal studies.
