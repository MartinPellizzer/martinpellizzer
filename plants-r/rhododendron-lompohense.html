Rhododendron lompohense

Rhododendron lompohense, commonly known as the Lompoh rhododendron, is a species native to the island of Sumba in Indonesia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at moderate elevations. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Rhododendron lompohense is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
