Ranunculus apiophyllus

Ranunculus apiophyllus, commonly known as the honeyleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features palmately divided, bright green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central stigma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its attractive appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
