Rosa daphnoides

Rosa daphnoides, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers and thorny stems. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Rosa daphnoides is often used in hedges and for its ornamental value due to its pleasant fragrance.
