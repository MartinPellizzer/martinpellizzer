Rosa elliptica var. typica

Rosa elliptica var. typica, commonly known as the elliptic rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is a deciduous shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
