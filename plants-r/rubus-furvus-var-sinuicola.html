Rubus furvus var. sinuicola

Rubus furvus var. sinuicola, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
