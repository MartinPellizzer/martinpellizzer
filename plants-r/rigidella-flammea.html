Rigidella flammea

Rigidella flammea, commonly known as the flame thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its stiff, erect stems and bright yellow to orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
