Ranunculus sabinei

Ranunculus sabinei, commonly known as Sabine's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous stamens. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic flowers.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
