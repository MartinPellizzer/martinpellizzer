Rubus × eriophyllus

Rubus × eriophyllus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
