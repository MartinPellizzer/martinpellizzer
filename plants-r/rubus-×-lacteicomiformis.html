Rubus × lacteicomiformis

Rubus × lacteicomiformis, commonly known as the white-flowered blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus, resulting in a unique combination of traits. It is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers, which contrast with its dark, thorny stems. The fruit is typically a small, tart blackberry with a high acidity level.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries in certain regions.
