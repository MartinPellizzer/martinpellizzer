Ranunculus lingua

Ranunculus lingua, commonly known as the tongue-shaped buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, radially symmetrical flowers with five to nine petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
