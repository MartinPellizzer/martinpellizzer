Rhododendron weyrichii var. purpuriflorum

Rhododendron weyrichii var. purpuriflorum, commonly known as the purple-flowered weyrich rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking deep purple to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded mountainous regions at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a waxy coating.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with specific soil and climate conditions.
