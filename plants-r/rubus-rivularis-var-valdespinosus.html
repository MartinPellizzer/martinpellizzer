Rubus rivularis var. valdespinosus

Rubus rivularis var. valdespinosus, commonly known as the Valdespinosus blackberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a tart, aromatic flavor.
