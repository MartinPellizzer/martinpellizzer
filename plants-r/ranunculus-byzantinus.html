Ranunculus byzantinus

Ranunculus byzantinus, commonly known as the Byzantine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is often found in mountainous areas.

Ranunculus byzantinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
