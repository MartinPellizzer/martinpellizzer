Ruschia propinqua

Ruschia propinqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf ruschia, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid conditions and requires minimal water once established.

Ruschia propinqua is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
