Rhododendron meridionale var. meridionale

Rhododendron meridionale var. meridionale, commonly known as the Southern Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
