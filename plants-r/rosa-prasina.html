Rosa prasina

Rosa prasina, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, and it has a compact growth habit.

Rosa prasina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
