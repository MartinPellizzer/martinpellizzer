Rhododendron mucronulatum f. acuminatum

Rhododendron mucronulatum f. acuminatum, commonly known as the Acuminatum Rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense, leathery foliage with elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping for its hardiness and visual interest.
