Rosa glauca var. subinclinata

Rosa glauca var. subinclinata, commonly known as the blue rose or bluebank rose, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring. The foliage is typically dark green with a slightly fuzzy texture, and the plant is often found in moist, wooded areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

This variety is also noted for its adaptability to a range of soil types, making it a hardy choice for garden cultivation.
