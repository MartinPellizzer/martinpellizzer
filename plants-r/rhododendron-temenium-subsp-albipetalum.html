Rhododendron temenium subsp. albipetalum

Rhododendron temenium subsp.

albipetalum, commonly known as the white-petaled rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dense, evergreen forests at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
