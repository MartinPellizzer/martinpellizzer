Ruellia purpurea

Ruellia purpurea, commonly known as purple ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with opposite leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

Ruellia purpurea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
