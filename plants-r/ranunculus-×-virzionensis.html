Ranunculus × virzionensis

Ranunculus × virzionensis, commonly known as the Virzow's buttercup, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a prominent central pistil. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are usually hairy and somewhat branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely available.
