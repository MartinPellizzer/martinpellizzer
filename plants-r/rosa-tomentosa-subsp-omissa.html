Rosa tomentosa subsp. omissa

Rosa tomentosa subsp.

omissa, commonly known as the woolly rose or the Himalayan woolly rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish pubescence covering its stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
