Rhamnus chugokuensis

Rhamnus chugokuensis, commonly known as the Japanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
