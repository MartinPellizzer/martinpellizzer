Rhododendron verticillatum f. velutinum

Rhododendron verticillatum f. velutinum, commonly known as the velvety alpine rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil. It is often found in rocky or forested areas at high elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded landscapes.
