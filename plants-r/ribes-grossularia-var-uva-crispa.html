Ribes grossularia var. uva-crispa

Ribes grossularia var. uva-crispa, commonly known as the European gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with thorny branches and alternate, ovate leaves that are serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as gooseberries, which are typically yellow or red when mature. These berries are encased in a fleshy, juicy pericarp and are often covered with a fine, whitish bloom.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
