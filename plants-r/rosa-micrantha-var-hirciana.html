Rosa micrantha var. hirciana

Rosa micrantha var. hirciana, commonly known as the Hircine rose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a desirable species for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
