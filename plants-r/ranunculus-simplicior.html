Ranunculus simplicior

Ranunculus simplicior, commonly known as the simple buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy surface, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, and it prefers well-drained soil.

Ranunculus simplicior is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
