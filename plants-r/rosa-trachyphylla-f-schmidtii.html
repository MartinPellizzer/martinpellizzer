Rosa trachyphylla f. schmidtii

Rosa trachyphylla f. schmidtii, commonly known as the Schmidt's bristly rose, is a botanical variety characterized by its dense, bristly foliage and thorny stems.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in stiff, glandular hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
