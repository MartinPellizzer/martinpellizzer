Rosa obtusifolia f. bonchidensis

Rosa obtusifolia f. bonchidensis, commonly known as the Bonchis Mountains rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the mountainous regions of Bulgaria.

It features dense, thorny stems and broad, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include small, oval-shaped buds and a compact growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
