Rhaphis coerulea

Rhaphis coerulea, commonly known as the blue hibiscus, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Rhaphis coerulea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
