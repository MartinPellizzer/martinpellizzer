Rhynchosia rupicola

Rhynchosia rupicola, commonly known as the rock bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated pods containing edible seeds. Rhynchosia rupicola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky or sandy substrates.

It is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various regions.
