Rumfordia aragonensis

Rumfordia aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon thistle, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
