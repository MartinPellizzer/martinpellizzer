Rubus cyclophyllus var. aequiparabilis

Rubus cyclophyllus var. aequiparabilis, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a tart flavor. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and grows as an erect shrub with thorny stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas near the coast.

This variety is valued for both its ecological role and its use in local culinary traditions.
