Ranunculus pectinifolius

Ranunculus pectinifolius, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has finely divided, pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Ranunculaceae family.
