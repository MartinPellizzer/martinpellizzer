Rubus × infrapallens

Rubus × infrapallens, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and often have a hairy texture.

The fruit, which is the most notable feature, is a dark purple to black berry that is highly valued for its flavor and culinary uses.
