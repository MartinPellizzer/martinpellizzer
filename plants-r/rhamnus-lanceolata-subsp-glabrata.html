Rhamnus lanceolata subsp. glabrata

Rhamnus lanceolata subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and alternate leaves that are lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by reddish-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
