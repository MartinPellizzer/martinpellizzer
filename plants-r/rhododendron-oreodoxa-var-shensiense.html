Rhododendron oreodoxa var. shensiense

Rhododendron oreodoxa var. shensiense, commonly known as the Sichuan azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to its alpine habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
