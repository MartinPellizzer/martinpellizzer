Rubus rhamnifolius var. stenoplus

Rubus rhamnifolius var. stenoplus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with narrow, serrated lobes. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
