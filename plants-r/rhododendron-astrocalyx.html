Rhododendron astrocalyx

Rhododendron astrocalyx, commonly known as the star-calyx rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to white, are often clustered in racemes.

Rhododendron astrocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
