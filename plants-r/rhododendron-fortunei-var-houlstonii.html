Rhododendron fortunei var. houlstonii

Rhododendron fortunei var. houlstonii, commonly known as the Houlston's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, with a distinctive two-lipped structure.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in cool, moist forest environments.
