Ribesiodes arboreum

Ribesiodes arboreum, commonly known as the red-flowered tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, often peeling in thin layers.

Ribesiodes arboreum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
