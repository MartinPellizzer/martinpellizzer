Rivea urbaniana

Rivea urbaniana, commonly known as the golden vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along its tendrils. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.

Rivea urbaniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
