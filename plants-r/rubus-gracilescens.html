Rubus gracilescens

Rubus gracilescens, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark berries that ripen in the fall. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
