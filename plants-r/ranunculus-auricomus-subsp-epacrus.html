Ranunculus auricomus subsp. epacrus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

epacrus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
