Rhododendron indicum var. formosanum

Rhododendron indicum var. formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwan azalea, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a lush green backdrop to the vibrant floral display.

This variety is well-suited to acidic, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
