Rhodiola pinnatifida

Rhodiola pinnatifida, commonly known as the Siberian rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, reddish roots and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to survive in harsh, arid conditions. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rhodiola pinnatifida is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.
