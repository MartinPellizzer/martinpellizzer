Rhysolepis tuberculata

Rhysolepis tuberculata, commonly known as the roughleaf mountain-mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps, giving it a textured appearance. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters and bloom in the summer months.

Rhysolepis tuberculata is typically found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
