Rubus glaucovirens var. siemianicensis

Rubus glaucovirens var. siemianicensis, commonly known as the Siemianiczanski blackberry, is a variant of the glaucovirens blackberry species.

It is a shrub native to Central Europe, particularly found in Poland and parts of Germany. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically small and have a slightly tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a hairy underside, and its stems are covered in thorns.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
