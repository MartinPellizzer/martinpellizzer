Ranunculus fallax subsp. alsophilus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

alsophilus, commonly known as the Alsop's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. Its botanical characteristics include a short, upright stem and a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
