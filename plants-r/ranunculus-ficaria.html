Ranunculus ficaria

Ranunculus ficaria, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with bright yellow flowers that have five petals and numerous stamens. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are typically heart-shaped or palmately lobed. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and flies.
