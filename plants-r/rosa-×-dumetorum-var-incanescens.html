Rosa × dumetorum var. incanescens

Rosa × dumetorum var. incanescens, commonly known as the white wood rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a slightly hairy surface. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blossoms and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a range of growing conditions.
