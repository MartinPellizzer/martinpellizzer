Rubus notatus

Rubus notatus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and pies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it typically grows in woodland or shrubland habitats.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and they bloom in the spring before the fruits mature.
