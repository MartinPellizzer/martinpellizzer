Rhus chinensis var. chinensis

Rhus chinensis var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
