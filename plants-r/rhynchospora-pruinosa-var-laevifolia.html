Rhynchospora pruinosa var. laevifolia

Rhynchospora pruinosa var. laevifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved swamp cordgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale pink to purple flowers during the late spring and summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
