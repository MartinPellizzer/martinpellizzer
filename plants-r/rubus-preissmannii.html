Rubus preissmannii

Rubus preissmannii, commonly known as the Preissmann's raspberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
