Rinorea pauciflora var. sempervirens

Rinorea pauciflora var. sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen rinorea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
