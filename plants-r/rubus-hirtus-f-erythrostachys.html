Rubus hirtus f. erythrostachys

Rubus hirtus f. erythrostachys, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive red-colored stems.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This form is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
