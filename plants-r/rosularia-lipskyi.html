Rosularia lipskyi

Rosularia lipskyi, commonly known as the Desert Rose or Desert Honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit, with fleshy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its rocky habitat. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

Rosularia lipskyi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
