Rosa × spinulifolia var. marginata

Rosa × spinulifolia var. marginata, commonly known as the Margined Alpina Rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a distinctive white marginal stripe.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
