Rosa communis var. alpestriformis

Rosa communis var. alpestriformis, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its compact growth habit and cold tolerance.

It typically features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage that helps reflect sunlight, making it well-suited for alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
