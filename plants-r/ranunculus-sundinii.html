Ranunculus sundinii

Ranunculus sundinii, commonly known as the Sundin's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow, with a distinctive central pistil. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Ranunculus sundinii is a perennial species, often found in high-altitude meadows where it blooms in late spring to early summer.
