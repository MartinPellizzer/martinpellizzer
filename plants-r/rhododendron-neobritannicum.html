Rhododendron neobritannicum

Rhododendron neobritannicum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Vietnam.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a complex inflorescence structure.
