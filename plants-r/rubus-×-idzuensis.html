Rubus × idzuensis

Rubus × idzuensis, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The berries are edible when ripe but are generally tart and not widely cultivated for commercial use.
