Rubus foliosus var. rotundifolius

Rubus foliosus var. rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its rounded, broad leaves and small, dark berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. It has a trailing growth habit and produces flowers that are pink to white in color.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
