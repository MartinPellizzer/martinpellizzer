Rotala leptopetala f. locuples

Rotala leptopetala f. locuples, commonly known as the broadleaf water lettuce, is a variant of the water lettuce species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern on the water surface. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, and it thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. It is often used in aquariums and ponds for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shade.

Its rapid growth rate makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain ecosystems.
