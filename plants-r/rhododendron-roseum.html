Rhododendron roseum

Rhododendron roseum, commonly known as the rose-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils at high altitudes. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Rhododendron roseum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
