Ranunculus monophyllus subsp. amplidens

Ranunculus monophyllus subsp.

amplidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features distinctive palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy yellow flowers with numerous petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and woodland edges. It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stem and is often found in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive in some regions.
