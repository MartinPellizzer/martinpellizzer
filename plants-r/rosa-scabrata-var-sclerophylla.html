Rosa scabrata var. sclerophylla

Rosa scabrata var. sclerophylla, commonly known as the roughleaf rose, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and well-suited to arid or semi-arid climates.

The flowers bloom in spring and are often used in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic value.
