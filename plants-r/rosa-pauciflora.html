Rosa pauciflora

Rosa pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds, and these are often used in herbal remedies. Rosa pauciflora thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or rocky soils.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after plant in horticulture, particularly for its delicate appearance and fragrance.
