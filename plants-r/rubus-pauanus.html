Rubus pauanus

Rubus pauanus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
