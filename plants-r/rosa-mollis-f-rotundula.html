Rosa mollis f. rotundula

Rosa mollis f. rotundula, commonly known as the rounded-leaf rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant with a dense growth habit.

It features oval, leathery leaves that are rounded at the ends, giving it its distinguishing appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

This cultivar is prized for its compact size and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
