Rhododendron reticulatum var. ciliatum

Rhododendron reticulatum var. ciliatum, commonly known as the Ciliate Rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features lance-shaped leaves with ciliate margins, giving it its common name. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red during late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its attractive foliage and floral display.
