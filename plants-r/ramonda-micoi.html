Ramonda micoi

Ramonda micoi, commonly known as the Mico's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.
