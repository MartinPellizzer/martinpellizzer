Rubus bellardii var. kornhuberi

Rubus bellardii var. kornhuberi, commonly known as the Kornhuber's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive floral structure and adaptability to harsh climates.
