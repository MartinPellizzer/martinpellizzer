Ranunculus australis

Ranunculus australis, commonly known as the Australian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals and a central yellow stamen. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as wetlands and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Ranunculus australis is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.
