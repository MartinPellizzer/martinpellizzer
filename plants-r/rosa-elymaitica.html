Rosa elymaitica

Rosa elymaitica, commonly known as the Elymian rose, is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa elymaitica is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
