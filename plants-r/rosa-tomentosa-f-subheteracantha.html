Rosa tomentosa f. subheteracantha

Rosa tomentosa f. subheteracantha, commonly known as the hairy rose or woolly rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa tomentosa.

It is characterized by its dense, white to greyish pubescence covering the stems, leaves, and flowers, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically compound, with multiple leaflets that are also covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of many rose species.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
