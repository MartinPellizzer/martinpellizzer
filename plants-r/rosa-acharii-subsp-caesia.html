Rosa acharii subsp. caesia

Rosa acharii subsp.

caesia, commonly known as the pink sandalwood rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
