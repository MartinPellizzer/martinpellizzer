Rosa pygmaea var. olympica

Rosa pygmaea var. olympica, commonly known as the Olympic miniature rose, is a compact, flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, blooming throughout the summer season. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and a dense, bushy growth habit that makes it ideal for rock gardens or container planting. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, requiring minimal pruning to maintain its compact form.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticultural history.
