Rollinia standleyi

Rollinia standleyi, commonly known as the yellow star sapphire, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, yellow or orange fruit that is typically eaten raw or used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Rollinia standleyi is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
