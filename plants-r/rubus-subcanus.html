Rubus subcanus

Rubus subcanus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed areas, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
