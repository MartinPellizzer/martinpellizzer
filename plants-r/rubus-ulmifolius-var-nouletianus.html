Rubus ulmifolius var. nouletianus

Rubus ulmifolius var. nouletianus, commonly known as the Nouletian bramble, is a variant of the common bramble, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin, often exhibiting a slightly glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits composed of multiple drupes, typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique variation and ecological role within its native range.
