Rubus foliosus subsp. cavatifolius

Rubus foliosus subsp.

cavatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruits are small, dark red berries that ripen in late summer. This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some culinary uses in local traditions.
