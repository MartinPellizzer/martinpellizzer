Rosa dumalis f. submeszkoensis

Rosa dumalis f. submeszkoensis, commonly known as the Submeszkoensis rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is well adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, red hips in the fall.

This cultivar is prized for its unique floral display and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
