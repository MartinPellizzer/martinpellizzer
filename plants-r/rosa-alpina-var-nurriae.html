Rosa alpina var. nurriae

Rosa alpina var. nurriae, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a shrub with upright, thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or mountainous environments.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural landscapes.
