Rubus acuminatissimus

Rubus acuminatissimus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a finely serrated margin.

The flowers are white and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing before the leaves in early spring.
