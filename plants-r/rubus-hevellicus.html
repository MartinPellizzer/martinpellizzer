Rubus hevellicus

Rubus hevellicus, commonly known as the Havellic raspberries, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
