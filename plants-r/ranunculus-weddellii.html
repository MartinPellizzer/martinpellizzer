Ranunculus weddellii

Ranunculus weddellii, commonly known as the Weddell's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. This species thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant is adapted to cold climates and is typically found in areas with high solar exposure.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
