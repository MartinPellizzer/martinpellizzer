Rubus leucostachys var. angustifolius

Rubus leucostachys var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically narrow and serrated. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark purple berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local cuisines.
