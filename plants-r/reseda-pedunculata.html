Reseda pedunculata

Reseda pedunculata, commonly known as sweet rocket, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to Europe and western Asia and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Reseda pedunculata is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
