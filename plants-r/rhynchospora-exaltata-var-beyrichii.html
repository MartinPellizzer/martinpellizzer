Rhynchospora exaltata var. beyrichii

Rhynchospora exaltata var. beyrichii, commonly known as the tall marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are usually unbranched and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are typically pale green to reddish in color.
