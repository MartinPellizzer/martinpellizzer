Rhipsalis juengeri

Rhipsalis juengeri, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that produce clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its long blooming period and ease of care.

Rhipsalis juengeri is also noted for its ability to bloom during the holiday season, making it a festive addition to homes and gardens.
