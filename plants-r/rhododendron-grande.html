Rhododendron grande

Rhododendron grande, commonly known as the large rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are borne on long, pedicellate inflorescences and are often fragrant.

Rhododendron grande prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
