Ranunculus occidentalis var. hexasepalus

Ranunculus occidentalis var. hexasepalus, commonly known as the western buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to six sepals that resemble petals, giving it a delicate, cup-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish fruits that disperse via wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
