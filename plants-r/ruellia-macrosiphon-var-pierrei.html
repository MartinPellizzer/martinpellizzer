Ruellia macrosiphon var. pierrei

Ruellia macrosiphon var. pierrei, commonly known as the pink ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are typically oval and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are often covered in fine pubescence. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.
