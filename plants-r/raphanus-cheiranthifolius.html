Raphanus cheiranthifolius

Raphanus cheiranthifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved radish, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust root system with a swollen, edible root that is typically white or yellow in color. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often broad and lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by round, pale green or white siliques containing several seeds.

This species is valued both for its culinary uses and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
