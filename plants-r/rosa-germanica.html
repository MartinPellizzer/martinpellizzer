Rosa germanica

Rosa germanica, commonly known as the German rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and it is known for its large, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces pinkish-red hips in the fall. Its leaves are dark green, lobed, and arranged alternately along the stem.

Rosa germanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
