Rubus × setulosus

Rubus × setulosus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
