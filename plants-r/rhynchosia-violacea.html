Rhynchosia violacea

Rhynchosia violacea, commonly known as the purple bean or velvet bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and showy violet or purple flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and as a protein source.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of various bioactive compounds.
