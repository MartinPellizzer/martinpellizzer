Rubus × wahlbergii

Rubus × wahlbergii, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, often with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline soils, making it a resilient species.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
