Rorippa sikokiana

Rorippa sikokiana, commonly known as the Japanese watercress, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small white flowers that bloom in clusters. It has a short life cycle and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich aquatic habitats.

Rorippa sikokiana is valued for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional Asian cuisine and medicine.
