Rubus orthocladus var. erythranthoides

Rubus orthocladus var. erythranthoides, commonly known as the red-flowered bramble, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom in early spring.
