Rubus caucasigenus

Rubus caucasigenus, commonly known as the Caucasian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
