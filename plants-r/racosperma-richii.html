Racosperma richii

Racosperma richii, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, and its yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or shaded environments.

Racosperma richii is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential for use in horticulture.
