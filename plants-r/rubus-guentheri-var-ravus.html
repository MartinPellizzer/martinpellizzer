Rubus guentheri var. ravus

Rubus guentheri var. ravus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and slopes.

The rough blackberry is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
