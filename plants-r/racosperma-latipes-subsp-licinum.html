Racosperma latipes subsp. licinum

Racosperma latipes subsp.

licinum, commonly known as the Licinum acacia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It is also notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

This subspecies is often found in dry woodland and heathland environments.
