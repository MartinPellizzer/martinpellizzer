Rubus tomentosus var. lloydianus

Rubus tomentosus var. lloydianus, commonly known as the Lloyd's bramble, is a variant of the brambles native to eastern North America.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth and compound leaves with serrated margins. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and are followed by red, hairy berries. The plant thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, often forming thickets.

Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.
