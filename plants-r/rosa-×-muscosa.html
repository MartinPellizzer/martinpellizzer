Rosa × muscosa

Rosa × muscosa, commonly known as the muscosa rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves and hips that mature to a red or orange color.

Rosa × muscosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
