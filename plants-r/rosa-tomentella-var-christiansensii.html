Rosa tomentella var. christiansensii

Rosa tomentella var. christiansensii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill rock rose, is a shrub native to California and western Nevada.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
