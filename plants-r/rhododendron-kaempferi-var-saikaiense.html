Rhododendron kaempferi var. saikaiense

Rhododendron kaempferi var. saikaiense, commonly known as the Saikai azalea, is a flowering shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring with clusters of pink to red flowers, adding vibrant color to gardens. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in temperate regions worldwide.
