Reseda lutea subsp. gracilis

Reseda lutea subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender yellowweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are linear and hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
