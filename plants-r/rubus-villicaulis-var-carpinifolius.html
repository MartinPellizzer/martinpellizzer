Rubus villicaulis var. carpinifolius

Rubus villicaulis var. carpinifolius, commonly known as the carpinifolius blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles.
