Rubus falconeri

Rubus falconeri, commonly known as the Falconer's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains of South America, specifically in regions of Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruit, a type of aggregate fruit, is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
