Rubus rosiflorus var. eriocarpus

Rubus rosiflorus var. eriocarpus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, dark berries that are edible. It thrives in coastal and mountainous regions, often in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant is notable for its hairy, woolly appearance, which gives it its common name.
