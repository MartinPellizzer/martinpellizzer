Rosa gallica var. mahesca

Rosa gallica var. mahesca, commonly known as the Mahesca rose, is a cultivar of the Gallica rose, native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its deep red, semi-double flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has fragrant, dark green foliage and is known for its hardy nature, thriving in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

This variety is also valued for its historical significance in horticultural history.
