Rhabdophyllum rigidum

Rhabdophyllum rigidum, commonly known as the rigid clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches, which are typically arranged in clusters. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Rhabdophyllum rigidum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
