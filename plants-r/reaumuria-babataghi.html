Reaumuria babataghi

Reaumuria babataghi, commonly known as the Babataghi's tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Tamaricaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and delicate, pinkish flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high salinity and drought. It typically grows in sandy or saline soils and is often found in coastal or inland desert areas.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various desert fauna.
