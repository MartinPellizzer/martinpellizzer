Rubus niveus var. rosifolius

Rubus niveus var. rosifolius, commonly known as the white-leaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
