Rubus goniophyllus var. barbulatus

Rubus goniophyllus var. barbulatus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, lobed leaves covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are small and white, with five petals, and it blooms in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
