Ranunculus fallax subsp. euryanthes

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

euryanthes, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem that often grows in clusters.

The plant has palmate leaves with lobes that are broader than those of the typical R. fallax, giving it a distinct appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Ranunculaceae family.
