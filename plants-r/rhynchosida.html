Rhynchosida

Rhynchosida, commonly known as the "beak-like" group, is a taxonomic division within the flowering plants.

This group is characterized by its unique floral structures, particularly the presence of a beak-like extension in certain species. Rhynchosida includes a variety of plants with diverse habitats, ranging from arid environments to moist forests.
