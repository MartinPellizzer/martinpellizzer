Ranunculus gasparrinii

Ranunculus gasparrinii, commonly known as the Gasparrini buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features delicate, palmately divided leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
