Rhynchosia versicolor subsp. versicolor

Rhynchosia versicolor subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the variable cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds. It is notable for its variability in flower color and leaf shape, which contributes to its common name.

This species is also valued for its edible seeds and nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
