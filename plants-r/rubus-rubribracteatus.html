Rubus rubribracteatus

Rubus rubribracteatus, commonly known as the red-bract raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Japan, where it grows in woodland and shrubland habitats. The plant is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing leaves and the second year bearing flowers and fruits. Its flowers are typically pink to red, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that ripens in late summer.

The plant's ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and showy inflorescences.
