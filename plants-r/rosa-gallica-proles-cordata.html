Rosa gallica proles cordata

Rosa gallica proles cordata, commonly known as the French pink or heart-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves, which are a distinguishing feature of this variety. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Rosa gallica proles cordata prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
