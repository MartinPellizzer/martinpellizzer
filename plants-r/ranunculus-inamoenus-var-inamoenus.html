Ranunculus inamoenus var. inamoenus

Ranunculus inamoenus var. inamoenus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

Due to its habitat preferences, it is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.
