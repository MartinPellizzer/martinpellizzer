Rubus argenteus subsp. elegantispinosus

Rubus argenteus subsp.

elegantispinosus, commonly known as the silverberry or whitecurrant, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and delicate flowers.
