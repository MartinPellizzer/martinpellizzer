Rubus mulleri microgene grypoacanthus

Rubus mulleri microgene grypoacanthus, commonly known as the microgene grypoacanthus raspberry, is a species of bramble within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Rubus family.
