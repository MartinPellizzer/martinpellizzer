Rechsteineria cyclophylla

Rechsteineria cyclophylla, commonly known as the cyclophyllan palm, is a rare and unique species native to the rainforests of northern South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-arranged leaves that form a dense, circular canopy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Its botanical features make it an important subject for studies on plant morphology and evolutionary adaptation.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
