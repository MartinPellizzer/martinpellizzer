Raritebe palicoureoides

Raritebe palicoureoides, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a unique plant native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in moist, nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
