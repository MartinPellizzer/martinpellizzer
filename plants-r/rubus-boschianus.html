Rubus boschianus

Rubus boschianus, commonly known as the swamp blackberry, is a shrub native to wetland areas of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks.

Due to its habitat preferences, it plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
