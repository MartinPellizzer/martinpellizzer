Ranunculus martini

Ranunculus martini, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to nine petals, and it typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, and it thrives in cool, moist climates. Ranunculus martini is a perennial species that produces small, green fruits containing seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
