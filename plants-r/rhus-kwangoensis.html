Rhus kwangoensis

Rhus kwangoensis, commonly known as the Kwango sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that are red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Rhus kwangoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
