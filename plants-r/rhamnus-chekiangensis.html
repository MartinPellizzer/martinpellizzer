Rhamnus chekiangensis

Rhamnus chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by dark purple to black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
