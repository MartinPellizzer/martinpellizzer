Rosa pendulina f. subhumilis

Rosa pendulina f. subhumilis, commonly known as the pendulous rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa pendulina.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, rounded hips after flowering. It is native to regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its graceful appearance.
