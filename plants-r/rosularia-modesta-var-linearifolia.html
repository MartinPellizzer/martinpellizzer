Rosularia modesta var. linearifolia

Rosularia modesta var. linearifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf rosebay, is a low-growing, succulent perennial native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water. The plant produces upright stems that bear clusters of tiny, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants.
