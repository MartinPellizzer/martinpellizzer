Rendlia altera

Rendlia altera, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pale pink and white, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth, Rendlia altera is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
