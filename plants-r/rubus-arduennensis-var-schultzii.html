Rubus arduennensis var. schultzii

Rubus arduennensis var. schultzii, commonly known as the Schultze blackberry, is a variant of the arduennensis species within the Rubus genus.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
