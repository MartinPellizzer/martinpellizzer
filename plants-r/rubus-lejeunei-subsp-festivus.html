Rubus lejeunei subsp. festivus

Rubus lejeunei subsp.

festivus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
