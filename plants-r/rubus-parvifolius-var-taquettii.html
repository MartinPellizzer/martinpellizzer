Rubus parvifolius var. taquettii

Rubus parvifolius var. taquettii, commonly known as the Japanese bramble, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
