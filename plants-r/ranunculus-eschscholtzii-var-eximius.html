Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. eximius

Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. eximius, commonly known as the Exquisite Bittercress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features compound leaves with lobed or divided structures, typical of many members of its family. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five to eight petals, and are borne on slender stems.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
