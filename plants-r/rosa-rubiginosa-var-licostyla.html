Rosa rubiginosa var. licostyla

Rosa rubiginosa var. licostyla, commonly known as the Spanish rose or red shrub rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and hardiness.
