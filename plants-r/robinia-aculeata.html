Robinia aculeata

Robinia aculeata, commonly known as the honey locust, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often divided into small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, followed by long, slender pods that contain several seeds. Its thorns are a distinctive feature, and the tree is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Robinia aculeata has been used in landscaping and for erosion control due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
