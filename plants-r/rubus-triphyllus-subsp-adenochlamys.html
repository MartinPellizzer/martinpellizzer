Rubus triphyllus subsp. adenochlamys

Rubus triphyllus subsp.

adenochlamys, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregated fruits known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its horticultural value and adaptability to challenging growing conditions.
