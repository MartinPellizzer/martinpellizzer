Rubus idaeus f. tonsus

Rubus idaeus f. tonsus, commonly known as the blackberry.

This plant is a cultivar of the blackberry species, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark purple to black berries. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates, producing fruit in late summer. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.
