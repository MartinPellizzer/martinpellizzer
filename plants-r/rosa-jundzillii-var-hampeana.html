Rosa jundzillii var. hampeana

Rosa jundzillii var. hampeana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a striking addition to any botanical collection.
