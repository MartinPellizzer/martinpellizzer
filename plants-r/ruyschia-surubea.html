Ruyschia surubea

Ruyschia surubea, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically green, and it produces small, orange berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. Ruyschia surubea thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
