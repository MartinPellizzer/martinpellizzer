Ranunculus retusidens

Ranunculus retusidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to nine petals, typically ranging in color from yellow to orange. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus retusidens is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.
