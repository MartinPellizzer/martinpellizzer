Renealmia geniculata

Renealmia geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems that coil and twist as it grows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

Renealmia geniculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique growth habit and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
