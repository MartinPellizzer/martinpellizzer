Rorippa backeri

Rorippa backeri, commonly known as the Chinese watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers with four petals, which attract pollinators such as bees. Rorippa backeri thrives in moist environments, often found near streams, ditches, and wet meadows.

It is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine.
