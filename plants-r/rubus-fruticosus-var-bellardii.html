Rubus fruticosus var. bellardii

Rubus fruticosus var. bellardii, commonly known as the Bellard's bramble, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
