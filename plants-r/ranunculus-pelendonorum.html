Ranunculus pelendonorum

Ranunculus pelendonorum, commonly known as the Pelendon's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic species. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. It typically grows in moist, rocky soils at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
