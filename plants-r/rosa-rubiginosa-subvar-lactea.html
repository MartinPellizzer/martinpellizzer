Rosa rubiginosa subvar. lactea

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. lactea, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
