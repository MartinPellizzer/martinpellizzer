Rudbeckia amplectens

Rudbeckia amplectens, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense, dark green disk of tubular florets, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Rudbeckia amplectens is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
