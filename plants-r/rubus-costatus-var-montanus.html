Rubus costatus var. montanus

Rubus costatus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a bright red color. Its flowers are white with five petals and are often clustered in the leaf axils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
