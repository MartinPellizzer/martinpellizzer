Roegneria dentata

Roegneria dentata, commonly known as the toothed wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with finely serrated edges. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered in panicles, and its flowers are typically green to reddish in color. Roegneria dentata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
