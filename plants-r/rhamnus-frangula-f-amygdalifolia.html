Rhamnus frangula f. amygdalifolia

Rhamnus frangula f. amygdalifolia, commonly known as the sweet buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, black berries that are rich in anthraquinones, which have been historically used in medicine. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rhamnaceae family.
