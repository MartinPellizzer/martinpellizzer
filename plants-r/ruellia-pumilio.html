Ruellia pumilio

Ruellia pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of less than 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Ruellia pumilio thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
