Ranunculus debreceniensis

Ranunculus debreceniensis, commonly known as the Debrecen buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant blooms in early spring, often in moist meadows and along stream banks. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is considered a rare species in its native habitat.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
