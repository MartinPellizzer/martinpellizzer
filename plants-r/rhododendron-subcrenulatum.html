Rhododendron subcrenulatum

Rhododendron subcrenulatum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a species native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Rhododendron subcrenulatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its attractive blooms and foliage.
