Rebutia yuquinensis

Rebutia yuquinensis, commonly known as the Yuquinensis Pediocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and short, ribbed stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
