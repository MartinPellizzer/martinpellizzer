Rockhausenia villosa

Rockhausenia villosa, commonly known as the hairy rockhouser, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in coastal regions and is known for its resilience to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
