Raphanus pterocarpus

Raphanus pterocarpus, commonly known as the African turnip, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and mustard. The plant features a stout, branching stem and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, while the roots are also consumed in some regions.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
