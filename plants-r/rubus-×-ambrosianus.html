Rubus × ambrosianus

Rubus × ambrosianus, commonly known as the ambrosia raspberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rubus genus.

The fruit is often used in jams and desserts, though it can cause allergic reactions in some individuals due to its high pollen content.
