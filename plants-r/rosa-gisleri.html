Rosa gisleri

Rosa gisleri, commonly known as the Gisler's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa gisleri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
