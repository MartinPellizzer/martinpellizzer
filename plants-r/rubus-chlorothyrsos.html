Rubus chlorothyrsos

Rubus chlorothyrsos, commonly known as the green-stemmed blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to the western United States, characterized by its slender, greenish stems and small, thorny canes. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe, with a sweet to tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
