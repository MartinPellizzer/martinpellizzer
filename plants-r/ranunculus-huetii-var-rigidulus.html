Ranunculus huetii var. rigidulus

Ranunculus huetii var. rigidulus, commonly known as the rigid huet's buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that resemble petals, and it has a short, stout stem. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, often covered with a fine layer of hairs.

This variety is distinguished by its rigid, upright growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
