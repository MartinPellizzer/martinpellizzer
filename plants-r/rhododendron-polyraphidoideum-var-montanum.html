Rhododendron polyraphidoideum var. montanum

Rhododendron polyraphidoideum var. montanum, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows at high elevations, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery leaves that are often dark green in color. Its flowers are showy, ranging from pink to white, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
