Reichardia tingitana

Reichardia tingitana, commonly known as the Canary Island dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in a single, terminal head, similar to dandelions.

Reichardia tingitana is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
