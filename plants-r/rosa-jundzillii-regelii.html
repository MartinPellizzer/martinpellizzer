Rosa jundzillii regelii

Rosa jundzillii regelii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a climbing shrub that produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant has pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers emit a sweet scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
