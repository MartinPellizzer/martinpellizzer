Rosa afzeliana var. erlevensis

Rosa afzeliana var. erlevensis, commonly known as the Erlev's rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of East Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
