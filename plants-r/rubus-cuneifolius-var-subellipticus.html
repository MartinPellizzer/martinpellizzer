Rubus cuneifolius var. subellipticus

Rubus cuneifolius var. subellipticus, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It features prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to salt spray and wind exposure.
