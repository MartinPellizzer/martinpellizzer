Rhynchosia grandifolia

Rhynchosia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, similar to other members of the legume family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional agriculture.

Its botanical characteristics make it valuable for both ornamental and nutritional purposes.
