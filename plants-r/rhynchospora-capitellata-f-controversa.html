Rhynchospora capitellata f. controversa

Rhynchospora capitellata f. controversa, commonly known as the broad-leaved tussock sedge, is a botanical variant of the tussock sedge species.

It is characterized by its broad, grass-like leaves and a dense, tufted growth habit. This plant typically thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity. Its inflorescence consists of a single, upright spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

The subspecies is notable for its morphological variation, which has led to taxonomic debates among botanists.
