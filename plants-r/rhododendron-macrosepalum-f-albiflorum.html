Rhododendron macrosepalum f. albiflorum

Rhododendron macrosepalum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered form of the large-sepal rhododendron, is a botanical variant characterized by its striking white blooms.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Its ornamental value lies in its elegant white petals and compact growth habit, making it a desirable addition to gardens.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.
