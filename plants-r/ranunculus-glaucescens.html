Ranunculus glaucescens

Ranunculus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to thrive.
