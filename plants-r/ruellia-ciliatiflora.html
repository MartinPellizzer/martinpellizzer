Ruellia ciliatiflora

Ruellia ciliatiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, which attract various pollinators. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Ruellia ciliatiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
