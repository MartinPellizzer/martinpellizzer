Rhamnus lanceolata

Rhamnus lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches long. Its small, black berries are a food source for various birds and mammals.

Rhamnus lanceolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
