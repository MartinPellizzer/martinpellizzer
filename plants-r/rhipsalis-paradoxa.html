Rhipsalis paradoxa

Rhipsalis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, thin, branching stems that resemble those of a climbing plant. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the winter months, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it lacks the typical areoles found in other cacti.

Rhipsalis paradoxa is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
