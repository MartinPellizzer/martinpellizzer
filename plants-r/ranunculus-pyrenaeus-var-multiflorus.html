Ranunculus pyrenaeus var. multiflorus

Ranunculus pyrenaeus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the Pyrenean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive appearance, often with a waxy coating.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
