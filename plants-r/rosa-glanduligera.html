Rosa glanduligera

Rosa glanduligera, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant pink to white flowers. The plant features simple, pinnately compound leaves with glandular dots, which give it its scientific name. Rosa glanduligera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an attractive plant for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
