Ranunculus strigulosus

Ranunculus strigulosus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with numerous petals and a hairy stem, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ranunculus strigulosus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.
