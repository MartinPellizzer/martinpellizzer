Rubus subornatus var. concolor

Rubus subornatus var. concolor, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets with a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in the axils of the leaves, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
