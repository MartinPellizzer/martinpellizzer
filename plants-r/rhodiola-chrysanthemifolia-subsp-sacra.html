Rhodiola chrysanthemifolia subsp. sacra

Rhodiola chrysanthemifolia subsp.

sacra, commonly known as the sacred rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dark green, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to rocky soils. It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Its resilience to harsh conditions makes it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
