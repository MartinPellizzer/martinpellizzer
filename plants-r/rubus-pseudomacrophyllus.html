Rubus pseudomacrophyllus

Rubus pseudomacrophyllus, commonly known as the giant-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
