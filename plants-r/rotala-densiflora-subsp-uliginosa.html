Rotala densiflora subsp. uliginosa

Rotala densiflora subsp.

uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp rotala, is a water-loving plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, floating foliage and small, white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season.

The plant thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshes and pond margins. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, with a velvety texture that helps it retain moisture.

It is an important species for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
