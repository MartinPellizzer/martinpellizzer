Rhododendron achroanthum

Rhododendron achroanthum, commonly known as the white azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Rhododendron achroanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable habitats.
