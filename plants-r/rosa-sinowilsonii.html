Rosa sinowilsonii

Rosa sinowilsonii, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and are also cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
