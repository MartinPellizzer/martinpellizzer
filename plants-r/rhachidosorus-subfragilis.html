Rhachidosorus subfragilis

Rhachidosorus subfragilis, commonly known as the fragrant fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that emit a subtle, pleasant aroma. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Rhachidosorus subfragilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
