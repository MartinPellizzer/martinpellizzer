Rhododendron × hirsutiforme

Rhododendron × hirsutiforme, commonly known as the woolly rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist climates with acidic soil conditions. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Rhododendron × hirsutiforme is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form thick, compact growth.
