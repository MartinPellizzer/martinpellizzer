Ranunculus fallax subsp. altitorus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

altitorus, commonly known as the tall meadow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of sharply toothed, lobed leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers open in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.

It is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
