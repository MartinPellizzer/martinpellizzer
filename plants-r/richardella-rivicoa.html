Richardella rivicoa

Richardella rivicoa, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its small, bright yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

The leaves are pinnately divided, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.
