Rhododendron dolerum

Rhododendron dolerum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including parts of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often in forested areas or on rocky slopes. Its flowers range in color from pink to red and are often fragrant.

Rhododendron dolerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
