Rubus milliformis f. typicus

Rubus milliformis f. typicus, commonly known as the typical form of the many-branched blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex inflorescence and is a member of the Rosaceae family.
