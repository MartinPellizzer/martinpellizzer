Rhagadiolus arenarius

Rhagadiolus arenarius, commonly known as the sandhill grass.

This plant is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States, particularly found in sandy or disturbed soils. It has slender, wiry stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. The plant is adapted to arid environments and can thrive in full sun.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various insects and small animals.
