Rosa subglauca

Rosa subglauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving them a bluish-gray appearance. This plant is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
