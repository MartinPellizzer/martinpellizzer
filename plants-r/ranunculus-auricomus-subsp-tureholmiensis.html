Ranunculus auricomus subsp. tureholmiensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

tureholmiensis, commonly known as the Tureholmi meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often with a distinctive greenish or reddish hue at the base.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and wetlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture, and it produces numerous small, greenish-brown seeds in its fruit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
