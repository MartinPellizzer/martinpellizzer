Rhynchospora cinnamomea

Rhynchospora cinnamomea, commonly known as cinnamon root sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often reddish or purplish at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces dark, hard seeds that are dispersed by water or animals.

Rhynchospora cinnamomea plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
