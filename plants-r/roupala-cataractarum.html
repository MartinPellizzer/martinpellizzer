Roupala cataractarum

Roupala cataractarum, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is adapted to thrive in moist or aquatic environments. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.
