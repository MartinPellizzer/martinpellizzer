Roepera maritima

Roepera maritima, commonly known as sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its bright green, succulent leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in salt marshes and dunes. It has a creeping growth habit and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Roepera maritima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
