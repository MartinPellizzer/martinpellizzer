Rubus aculeolatus

Rubus aculeolatus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
