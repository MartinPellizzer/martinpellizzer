Robinia pseudoacacia var. monophylla-pendula

Robinia pseudoacacia var. monophylla-pendula, commonly known as the weeping false acacia, is a cultivar of the Robinia genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small leaflets. This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil types.

It prefers full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
