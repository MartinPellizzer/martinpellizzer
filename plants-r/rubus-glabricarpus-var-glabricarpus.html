Rubus glabricarpus var. glabricarpus

Rubus glabricarpus var. glabricarpus, commonly known as the smooth-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are pinkish-white and appear in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries in the summer.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
