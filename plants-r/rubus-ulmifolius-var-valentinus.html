Rubus ulmifolius var. valentinus

Rubus ulmifolius var. valentinus, commonly known as the Valentine rose, is a variant of the brambles native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that resemble those of the elm tree, giving it its species name. The flowers are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. This plant is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedging and as a food source for wildlife.
