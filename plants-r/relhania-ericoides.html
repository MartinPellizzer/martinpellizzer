Relhania ericoides

Relhania ericoides, commonly known as the Cape rosemary, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces pink or white flowers in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Relhania ericoides is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature.
