Rosa arvensis var. brevistyla

Rosa arvensis var. brevistyla, commonly known as the creeping white rose, is a low-growing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces rose hips that are red and hairy when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
