Ranunculus luxuriosus

Ranunculus luxuriosus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
