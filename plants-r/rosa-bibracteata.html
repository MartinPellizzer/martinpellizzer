Rosa bibracteata

Rosa bibracteata, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. The plant has woody stems and produces thorny shoots, typical of many rose species. Its leaves are pinnate, with multiple leaflets arranged along the stem.

Rosa bibracteata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
