Roella compacta

Roella compacta, commonly known as the dwarf roella, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Roella compacta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
