Rosa brachiata

Rosa brachiata, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are known for their long-lasting bloom, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Rosa brachiata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
