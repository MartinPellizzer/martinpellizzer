Rubus koslensis

Rubus koslensis, commonly known as the Koslenski blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
