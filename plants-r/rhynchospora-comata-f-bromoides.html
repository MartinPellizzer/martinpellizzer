Rhynchospora comata f. bromoides

Rhynchospora comata f. bromoides, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a variant of the silky sedges, characterized by its dense, grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, three-parted flowers in spikelets.

This subspecies is typically found in moist soils and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
