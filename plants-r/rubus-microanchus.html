Rubus microanchus

Rubus microanchus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, native to the western United States. The plant is characterized by its small, thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red berries. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.
