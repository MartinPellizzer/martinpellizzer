Rumex longifolius

Rumex longifolius, commonly known as long-leaved sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the veins, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves contain oxalic acid, giving them a tart flavor, and they are sometimes used in culinary applications.

Rumex longifolius is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates.
