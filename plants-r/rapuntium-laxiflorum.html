Rapuntium laxiflorum

Rapuntium laxiflorum, commonly known as the looseflower rapunculus, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant features basal leaves that are usually lobed or pinnately divided, and it produces yellow flowers in racemes. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small seeds.

It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
