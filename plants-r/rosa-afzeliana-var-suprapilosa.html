Rosa afzeliana var. suprapilosa

Rosa afzeliana var. suprapilosa, commonly known as the hairy rose or the mountain rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Rosa afzeliana var. suprapilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
