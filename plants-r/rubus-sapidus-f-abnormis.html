Rubus sapidus f. abnormis

Rubus sapidus f. abnormis, commonly known as the sweet blackberry variant, is a botanical curiosity within the Rubus genus.

It exhibits unique morphological traits, including larger, more elongated fruit structures compared to typical blackberries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus family. Its fruit, while similar to common blackberries, has a more pronounced sweetness and a distinct texture.

This form is often found in specific ecological niches, contributing to its rarity and interest among botanists.
