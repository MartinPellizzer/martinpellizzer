Rosa stylosa subvar. systyla

Rosa stylosa subvar. systyla, commonly known as the pink sandalwood rose, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a shrub-like plant with slender, often arching stems that bear small, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant produces small, red-orange fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
