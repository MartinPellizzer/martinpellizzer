Rhododendron × purpureum

Rhododendron × purpureum, commonly known as the purple rhododendron, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron × purpureum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
