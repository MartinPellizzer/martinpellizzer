Ruschia coriaria

Ruschia coriaria, commonly known as the rock rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
