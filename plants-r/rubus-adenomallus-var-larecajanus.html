Rubus adenomallus var. larecajanus

Rubus adenomallus var. larecajanus, commonly known as the Larecajan blackberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its unique flavor and adaptability, it is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
