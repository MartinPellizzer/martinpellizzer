Rubus radula subsp. pallidiformis

Rubus radula subsp.

pallidiformis, commonly known as the paleform blueberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, pale blue to white flowers.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
