Rosa obtusispina

Rosa obtusispina, commonly known as the blunt-spined rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces red-orange hips that contain seeds.

Rosa obtusispina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
