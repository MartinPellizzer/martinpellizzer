Rhododendron haemaleum var. atrorubrum

Rhododendron haemaleum var. atrorubrum, commonly known as the black-red flame azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange-red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

This variety is notable for its striking coloration and adaptability to a range of environmental conditions.
