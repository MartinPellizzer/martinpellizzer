Reissipa

Reissipa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote highlands of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom only at night, releasing a faint, sweet fragrance. Reissipa thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific soil conditions to grow.

Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, it is considered a critically endangered species.
