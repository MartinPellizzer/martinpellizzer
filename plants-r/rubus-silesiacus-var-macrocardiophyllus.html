Rubus silesiacus var. macrocardiophyllus

Rubus silesiacus var. macrocardiophyllus, commonly known as the large-leafed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
