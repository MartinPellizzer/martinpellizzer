Rhododendron strigillosum var. monosematum

Rhododendron strigillosum var. monosematum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to China.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are densely covered with fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
