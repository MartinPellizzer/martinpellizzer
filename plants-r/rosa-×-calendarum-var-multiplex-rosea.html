Rosa × calendarum var. multiplex-rosea

Rosa × calendarum var. multiplex-rosea, commonly known as the double-flowered Rosa × calendarum, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its dense, layered petals.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to the eastern United States, where it thrives in woodland habitats. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage. Its flowers are often pink to red in color, with a strong, sweet fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
