Rosa glaucisudermanica

Rosa glaucisudermanica, commonly known as the Siberian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features pale green, hairy stems and large, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to cold climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate regions.
