Rubus × orarius

Rubus × orarius, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant that combines traits of blackberries and raspberries.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces berries that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Rubus × orarius is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
