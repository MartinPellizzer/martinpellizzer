Rosa coerulea

Rosa coerulea, commonly known as the blue rose, is a species of flowering plant that is not naturally occurring in the wild.

It is a hybrid variety that has been selectively bred to produce flowers with a unique blue hue, which is rare in the natural world. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy blooms. Its petals are typically a deep blue or purple, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
