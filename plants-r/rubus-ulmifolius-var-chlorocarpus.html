Rubus ulmifolius var. chlorocarpus

Rubus ulmifolius var. chlorocarpus, commonly known as the green-fruited bramble, is a variant of the common bramble species.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible berries that are typically green when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems.
