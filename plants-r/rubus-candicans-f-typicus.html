Rubus candicans f. typicus

Rubus candicans f. typicus, commonly known as the showy blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in coastal and mountainous regions.

The berries are valued for their flavor and are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
