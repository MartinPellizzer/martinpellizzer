Rosa villosa f. minutidentata

Rosa villosa f. minutidentata, commonly known as the hairy rose or miniature toothed rose, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has densely hairy stems and leaves with finely toothed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well-suited for garden cultivation due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a versatile addition to landscape designs.
