Ranunculus bonatii

Ranunculus bonatii, commonly known as the Bonatti buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers often have prominent yellow stamens.

Ranunculus bonatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
