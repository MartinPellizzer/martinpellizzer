Rosa rubiginosa subsp. micrantha

Rosa rubiginosa subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the creeping rose or dwarf shrub rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region. It is a compact shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense, spreading growth habit.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This subspecies is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
