Romulea cruciata

Romulea cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaved glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and have a distinctive cross-shaped pattern on the petals.

Romulea cruciata is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
