Rosa communis subsp. canina

Rosa communis subsp.

canina, commonly known as the dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets.

The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. It produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
