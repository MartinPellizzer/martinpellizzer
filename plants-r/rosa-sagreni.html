Rosa sagreni

Rosa sagreni, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan, Iran, and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in thorns.

Rosa sagreni is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical significance in traditional medicine and perfumery.
