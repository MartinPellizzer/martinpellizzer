Roepera eremaea

Roepera eremaea, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapted to survive in low-nutrient environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are small and often covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

Roepera eremaea is well-suited to drought conditions and is often used in landscaping for its resilience and ornamental value.
