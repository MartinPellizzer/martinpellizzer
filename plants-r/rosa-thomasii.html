Rosa thomasii

Rosa thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree.

Rosa thomasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
