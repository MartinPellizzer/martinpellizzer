Rhynchosia melanosticta

Rhynchosia melanosticta, commonly known as the black-spotted cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable crop for soil enrichment. Its seeds are edible and used in various cuisines, while the plant also has traditional medicinal uses.

Rhynchosia melanosticta thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and agricultural benefits.
