Rhodomyrtus psidioides

Rhodomyrtus psidioides, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in coastal and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, often with a slight sheen.

Rhodomyrtus psidioides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
