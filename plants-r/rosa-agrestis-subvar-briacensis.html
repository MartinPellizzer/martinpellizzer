Rosa agrestis subvar. briacensis

Rosa agrestis subvar. briacensis, commonly known as the Briacensis shrub rose, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of France.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has narrow, slightly hairy leaves and produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal climates.
