Rosa stylosa f. turonicensis

Rosa stylosa f. turonicensis, commonly known as the Turonic roses, is a flowering plant species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a red, hairy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
