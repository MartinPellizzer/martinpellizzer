Rubus × matronensis

Rubus × matronensis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are dark purple to black in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in suitable climates.
