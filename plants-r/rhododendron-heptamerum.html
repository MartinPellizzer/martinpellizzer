Rhododendron heptamerum

Rhododendron heptamerum, commonly known as the seven-merous rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Ericaceae family, which includes many ornamental and ecologically significant species. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, and the plant can grow as either a shrub or a small tree.

Rhododendron heptamerum is often found in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments.
