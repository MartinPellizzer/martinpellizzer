Ricinocarpus digynostachyus

Ricinocarpus digynostachyus, commonly known as the two-stemmed ricin tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive two-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing toxic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.
