Rhipsalis sarmentacea

Rhipsalis sarmentacea, commonly known as the silver mist cactus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its long, trailing stems that often hang from trees or grow along the ground. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are reduced to small, hair-like structures, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with well-draining soil.

Rhipsalis sarmentacea is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
