Rubus × eucamptus

Rubus × eucamptus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along fences.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for ornamental and hedging purposes.
