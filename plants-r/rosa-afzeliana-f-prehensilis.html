Rosa afzeliana f. prehensilis

Rosa afzeliana f. prehensilis, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its modified stems.

This species belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, particularly China. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and it produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
