Rosa indica borbonica

Rosa indica borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon rose, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink. The plant features thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Rosa indica borbonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in the perfume industry due to its strong, sweet scent.
