Rubus truncifolius var. douretii

Rubus truncifolius var. douretii, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and tart. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and the stems are often armed with prickles. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
