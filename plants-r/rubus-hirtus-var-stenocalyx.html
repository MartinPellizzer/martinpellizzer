Rubus hirtus var. stenocalyx

Rubus hirtus var. stenocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, dark purple berries. The plant has thorny stems and is often found in woodland edges and thickets. Its flowers are white and clustered in the axils of the leaves.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are used in local foraging and culinary practices.
