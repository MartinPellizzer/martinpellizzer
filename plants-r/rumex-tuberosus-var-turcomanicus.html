Rumex tuberosus var. turcomanicus

Rumex tuberosus var. turcomanicus, commonly known as the Turkestan sorrel, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system that produces edible tubers, which are rich in starch and have been traditionally used as a food source. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple underside, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically significant and a potential crop for sustainable agriculture.
