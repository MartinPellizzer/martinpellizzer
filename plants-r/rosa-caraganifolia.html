Rosa caraganifolia

Rosa caraganifolia, commonly known as the Mongolian rose, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals. It produces oval, red-brown fruits that contain seeds, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
