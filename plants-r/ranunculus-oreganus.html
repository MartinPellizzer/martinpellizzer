Ranunculus oreganus

Ranunculus oreganus, commonly known as the Oregon poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright orange to red petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has palmately lobed leaves and produces a single, erect flower with five to eight petals.

The Oregon poppy is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
