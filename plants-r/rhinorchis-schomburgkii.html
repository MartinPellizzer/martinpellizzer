Rhinorchis schomburgkii

Rhinorchis schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive labellum that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Rhinorchis schomburgkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
