Roettlera eburnea

Roettlera eburnea, commonly known as the white sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Roettlera eburnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
