Ribes migratorium

Ribes migratorium, commonly known as the creeping gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants often used in horticulture. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with small, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, appearing in clusters, and it produces edible berries that are often used in jams and jellies.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
