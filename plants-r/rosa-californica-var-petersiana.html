Rosa californica var. petersiana

Rosa californica var. petersiana, commonly known as the Peters' California rose, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and southern Oregon.

It produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

This variety is closely related to other Rosa californica forms but is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and floral characteristics.
