Ruyschia bahiensis

Ruyschia bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahamian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, tubular yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ruyschia bahiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
