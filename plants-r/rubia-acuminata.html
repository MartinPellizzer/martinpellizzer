Rubia acuminata

Rubia acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated bugloss, is a flowering plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Rubia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
