Rubus migratorius

Rubus migratorius, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Rubus migratorius is often found in disturbed areas, such as along roadsides and in open woodlands.
