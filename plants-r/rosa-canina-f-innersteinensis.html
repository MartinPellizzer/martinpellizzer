Rosa canina f. innersteinensis

Rosa canina f. innersteinensis, commonly known as the Innerstein rose, is a rare and botanically distinct form of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to almost white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers have a delicate fragrance and are often used in perfumery and culinary applications.

This subspecies is native to Central Europe and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
