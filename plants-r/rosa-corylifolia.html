Rosa corylifolia

Rosa corylifolia, commonly known as the Chinese rose or Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces fleshy, orange-yellow fruits that are edible and used in traditional Chinese medicine. Rosa corylifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and herbal practices.

Its botanical features make it a unique and versatile plant in both horticulture and pharmacology.
