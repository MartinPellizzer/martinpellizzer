Rosa phasianica

Rosa phasianica, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Rosa phasianica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to temperate climates.
