Rubus × sabaudicus

Rubus × sabaudicus, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
