Rhododendron desquamatum

Rhododendron desquamatum, commonly known as the peeling rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its deciduous leaves that peel off in strips, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers in the spring. It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Rhododendron desquamatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
