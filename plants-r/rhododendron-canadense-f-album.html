Rhododendron canadense f. album

Rhododendron canadense f. album, commonly known as the white flame azalea, is a flowering shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to the blooms.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
