Roucela drabifolia

Roucela drabifolia, commonly known as the slender rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, often with a hairy surface. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, blooming in early spring.

Roucela drabifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
