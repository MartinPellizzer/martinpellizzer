Rubus corylifolius var. permixtus

Rubus corylifolius var. permixtus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus corylifolius species native to China.

It is characterized by its large, dark green leaves with a serrated edge and clusters of small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. The plant is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with thorny stems and a dense, bushy growth habit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications.
