Ranunculus multiformis

Ranunculus multiformis, commonly known as the variable buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers with prominent, hairy sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, green fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Ranunculus multiformis is often found in damp soils and can thrive in a variety of temperate climates.
