Rubus uncinatifactus

Rubus uncinatifactus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant thrives in temperate climates.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
