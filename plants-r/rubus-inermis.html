Rubus inermis

Rubus inermis, commonly known as the smooth raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thornless stems and dense, prickly fruit clusters. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rubus inermis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
