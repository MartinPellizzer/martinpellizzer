Rinorea cerasifolia

Rinorea cerasifolia, commonly known as the cherry-leaved rinorea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often described as resembling cherry leaves in shape and texture. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

Rinorea cerasifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
