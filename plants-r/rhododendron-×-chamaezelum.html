Rhododendron × chamaezelum

Rhododendron × chamaezelum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in cool, moist climates with acidic soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in temperate regions.
