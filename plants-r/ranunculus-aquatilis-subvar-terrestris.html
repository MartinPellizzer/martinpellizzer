Ranunculus aquatilis subvar. terrestris

Ranunculus aquatilis subvar. terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and showy yellow flowers.

It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has a short, stout rhizome that helps anchor it in waterlogged soils. Its flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer, are composed of numerous small petals and are often surrounded by a ring of sepals.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, showcasing its adaptability to wetland ecosystems.
