Rapicactus booleanus

Rapicactus booleanus, commonly known as the booleanus cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, thick stems covered in clusters of white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
