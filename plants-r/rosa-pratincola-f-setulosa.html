Rosa pratincola f. setulosa

Rosa pratincola f. setulosa, commonly known as the woolly meadow rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, thorny stems covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
