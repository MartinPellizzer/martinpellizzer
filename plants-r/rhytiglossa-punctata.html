Rhytiglossa punctata

Rhytiglossa punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique leaf patterns and small, tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
