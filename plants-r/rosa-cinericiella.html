Rosa cinericiella

Rosa cinericiella, commonly known as the ash-colored rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, thornless stems and narrow, dark green leaves with serrated edges. Its botanical features include small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, five-petaled flowers.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to coastal environments.
