Rudbeckia fulgida var. umbrosa

Rudbeckia fulgida var. umbrosa, commonly known as the autumn rudbeckia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It features dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow, cone-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens. Its showy flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity in natural habitats.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in garden settings.
