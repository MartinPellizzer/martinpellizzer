Rubus × badensis

Rubus × badensis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes numerous species of brambles and berries. The plant is characterized by its prickly stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and it produces edible berries that are larger and sweeter than those of most other Rubus species.

This hybrid is often cultivated for its fruit quality and adaptability to various growing conditions.
