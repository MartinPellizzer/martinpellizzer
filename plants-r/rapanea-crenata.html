Rapanea crenata

Rapanea crenata, commonly known as the serrated evergreen or the mountain holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are dark purple drupes, which contain a single seed.

Rapanea crenata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
