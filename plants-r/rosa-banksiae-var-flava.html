Rosa banksiae var. flava

Rosa banksiae var. flava, commonly known as the yellow banksia rose, is a variant of the Banksia rose species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to the yellow blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its showy flowers.
