Rumea cochinchinensis

Rumea cochinchinensis, commonly known as Vietnamese tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods containing edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, it plays a role in traditional medicine and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental features.
