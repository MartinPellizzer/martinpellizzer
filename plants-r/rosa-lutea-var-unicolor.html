Rosa lutea var. unicolor

Rosa lutea var. unicolor, commonly known as the yellow rose, is a variety of the Rosa lutea species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its single, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
