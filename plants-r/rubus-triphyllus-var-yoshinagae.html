Rubus triphyllus var. yoshinagae

Rubus triphyllus var. yoshinagae, commonly known as the Yoshinaga blackberry, is a variant of the trailing blackberry species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
