Rosa moschata var. micrantha

Rosa moschata var. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered musk rose, is a rare and ornamental species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, small pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves, typical of many rose species. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils due to their aromatic properties.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
