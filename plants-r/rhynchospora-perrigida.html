Rhynchospora perrigida

Rhynchospora perrigida, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its wetland habitat.

Rhynchospora perrigida plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
