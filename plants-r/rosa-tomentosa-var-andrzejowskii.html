Rosa tomentosa var. andrzejowskii

Rosa tomentosa var. andrzejowskii, commonly known as the Andrzejowski rugosa rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a grayish-white underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardiness in temperate climates.
