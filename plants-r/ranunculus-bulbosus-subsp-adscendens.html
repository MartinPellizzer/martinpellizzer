Ranunculus bulbosus subsp. adscendens

Ranunculus bulbosus subsp.

adscendens, commonly known as the ascending buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a short, rhizomatous stem with basal leaves that are palmately lobed and have a distinctive hairy surface.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along streambanks.

The subspecies is characterized by its ascending growth habit, distinguishing it from other members of the Ranunculus genus.
