Racinaea parviflora var. expansa

Racinaea parviflora var. expansa, commonly known as the broad-leaved racinaea, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length.

The plant is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy surface, and they often have a distinctive veining pattern. The flowers, though small, are usually white or pale pink and appear in clusters along the leaf stems.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouse settings due to its specific environmental requirements.
