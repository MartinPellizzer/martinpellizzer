Rumohra hispida

Rumohra hispida, commonly known as the hispid grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is usually erect and leafy.

Rumohra hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
