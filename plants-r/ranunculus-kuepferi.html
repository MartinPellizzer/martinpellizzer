Ranunculus kuepferi

Ranunculus kuepferi, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, hairy stems and bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Ranunculus kuepferi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
