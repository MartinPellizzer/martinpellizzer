Rosa dumalis f. nervipilosa

Rosa dumalis f. nervipilosa, commonly known as the fringed rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa dumalis species.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy stems and leaves with prominent veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and delicate floral display, making it a popular choice among gardeners.
