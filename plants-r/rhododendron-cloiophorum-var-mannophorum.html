Rhododendron cloiophorum var. mannophorum

Rhododendron cloiophorum var. mannophorum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with deep green leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
