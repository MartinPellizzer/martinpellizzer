Ruellia tweedyana

Ruellia tweedyana, commonly known as Tweedy's ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple tubular flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Ruellia tweedyana is also noted for its role in supporting local pollinators.
