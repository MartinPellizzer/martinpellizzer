Rosa trachyphylla f. reticulata

Rosa trachyphylla f. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate bristly rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a textured surface that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. This variety is native to regions of southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.
