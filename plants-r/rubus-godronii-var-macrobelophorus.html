Rubus godronii var. macrobelophorus

Rubus godronii var. macrobelophorus, commonly known as the giant thimbleberry, is a variant of the thimbleberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale yellow flowers and edible, sweet berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are hairy and serrated.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
