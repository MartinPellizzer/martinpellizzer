Rhytiglossa inaequalis

Rhytiglossa inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a distinctive lip that is asymmetrical and often contrasting in color with the rest of the flower. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small to medium in size and often display a mix of white, green, and yellow hues.

Rhytiglossa inaequalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its striking and unusual appearance.
