Rubus moluccanus var. glaucocaulis

Rubus moluccanus var. glaucocaulis, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, red, raspberry-like fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
