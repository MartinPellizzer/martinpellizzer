Ranunculus fallax subsp. callimorphus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

callimorphus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
