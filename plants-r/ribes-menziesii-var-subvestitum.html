Ribes menziesii var. subvestitum

Ribes menziesii var. subvestitum, commonly known as the silverleaf gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silver-gray foliage that provides striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
