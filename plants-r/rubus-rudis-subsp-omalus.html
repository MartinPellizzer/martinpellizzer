Rubus rudis subsp. omalus

Rubus rudis subsp.

omalus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark, edible berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink.

The subspecies is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
