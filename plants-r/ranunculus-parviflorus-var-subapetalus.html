Ranunculus parviflorus var. subapetalus

Ranunculus parviflorus var. subapetalus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often with a slight pinkish tinge. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
