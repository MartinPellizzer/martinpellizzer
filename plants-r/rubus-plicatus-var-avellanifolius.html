Rubus plicatus var. avellanifolius

Rubus plicatus var. avellanifolius, commonly known as the blackberry almondleaf, is a variant of the brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive almond-shaped leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, usually red or yellow fruits that are edible but often tart. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems and dense foliage.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
