Rosettea denticulata

Rosettea denticulata, commonly known as the toothed rosette plant, is a succulent species native to arid regions of South America.

It forms dense, compact rosettes of fleshy, spiky leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The leaves are typically green with a slight bluish tint and have fine, tooth-like edges that give the plant its scientific name. It produces small, pale yellow flowers during the dry season, which are borne on short, upright stems.

This plant is well adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
