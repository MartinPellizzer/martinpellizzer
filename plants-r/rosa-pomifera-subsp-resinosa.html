Rosa pomifera subsp. resinosa

Rosa pomifera subsp.

resinosa, commonly known as the resinous rose, is a rare and distinctive member of the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its unique, glandular, resinous leaves and stems, which give it its common name.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is native to parts of Central Asia and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
