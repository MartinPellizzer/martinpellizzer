Rubus incisior

Rubus incisior, commonly known as the cutleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is closely related to other members of the Rubus genus, which includes many edible and ornamental species.
