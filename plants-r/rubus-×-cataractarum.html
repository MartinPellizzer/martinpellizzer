Rubus × cataractarum

Rubus × cataractarum, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to North America and thrives in a variety of soil types, particularly in moist, well-drained environments. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces showy white or pink flowers in the spring.

This species is often cultivated for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor.
