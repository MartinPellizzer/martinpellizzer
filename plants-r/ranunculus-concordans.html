Ranunculus concordans

Ranunculus concordans, commonly known as the Concordance buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool temperatures. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are often surrounded by a ring of sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus concordans is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.
