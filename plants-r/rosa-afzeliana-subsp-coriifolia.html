Rosa afzeliana subsp. coriifolia

Rosa afzeliana subsp.

coriifolia, commonly known as the African rose or the white rose, is a species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its botanical features include thorny stems, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of flowers that bloom in spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
