Rhododendron nieuwenhuisii

Rhododendron nieuwenhuisii, commonly known as the Nieuwenhuis rhododendron, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with acidic, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
