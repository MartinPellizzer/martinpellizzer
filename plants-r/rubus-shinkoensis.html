Rubus shinkoensis

Rubus shinkoensis, commonly known as the Shinko bramble, is a shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
