Rubus vahlii

Rubus vahlii, commonly known as Vahli's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The botanical features of Rubus vahlii make it an important species for both ecological and potential commercial use.
