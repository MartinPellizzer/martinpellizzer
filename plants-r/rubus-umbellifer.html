Rubus umbellifer

Rubus umbellifer, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. These berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is native to various regions across Europe and Asia.
