Rosularia adenotricha subsp. chitralica

Rosularia adenotricha subsp.

chitralica, commonly known as the Chitral rosebush, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of northern Pakistan. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial shrub with dense clusters of small, fleshy, pinkish-red leaves.

The plant produces showy, solitary flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white to pale pink. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
