Rosa acicularis var. typica

Rosa acicularis var. typica, commonly known as the thorned rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorned stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in pink or white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
