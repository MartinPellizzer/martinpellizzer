Rosa canina var. hortensis

Rosa canina var. hortensis, commonly known as the dog rose, is a cultivated variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its thorned stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright orange, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It is often grown for its ornamental value and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
