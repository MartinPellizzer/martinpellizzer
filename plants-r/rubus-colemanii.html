Rubus colemanii

Rubus colemanii, commonly known as Coleman's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is a shrub that produces small, dark berries, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

The botanical features of Rubus colemanii include its prickly stems and trifoliate leaves, which are characteristic of many Rubus species.
