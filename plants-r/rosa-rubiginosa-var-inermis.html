Rosa rubiginosa var. inermis

Rosa rubiginosa var. inermis, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its small, red-orange hips are edible and contain seeds.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
