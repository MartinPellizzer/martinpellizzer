Rubus bellardii var. glaucophyllus

Rubus bellardii var. glaucophyllus, commonly known as the glaucophyll raspberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
