Ranunculus tenuisectus

Ranunculus tenuisectus, commonly known as the slender-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ranunculus tenuisectus is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local insect populations.
