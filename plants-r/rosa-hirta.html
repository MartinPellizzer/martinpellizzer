Rosa hirta

Rosa hirta, commonly known as the rough rose, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa hirta thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
