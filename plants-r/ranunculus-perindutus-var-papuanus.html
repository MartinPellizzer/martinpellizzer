Ranunculus perindutus var. papuanus

Ranunculus perindutus var. papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Papua, Indonesia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
