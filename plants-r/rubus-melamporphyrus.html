Rubus melamporphyrus

Rubus melamporphyrus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a deep red color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its edible berries and is often found in woodland and shrubland habitats.
