Rubus × exhaustus

Rubus × exhaustus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
