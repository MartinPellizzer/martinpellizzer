Rosa coriifolia var. typica

Rosa coriifolia var. typica, commonly known as the white shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and clusters of small, white, single-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a finely serrated margin. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in hedges or as a specimen plant.
