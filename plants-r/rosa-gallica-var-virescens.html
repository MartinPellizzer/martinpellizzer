Rosa gallica var. virescens

Rosa gallica var. virescens, commonly known as the greenish shrub rose, is a perennial species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, leathery foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden borders or as a hedge.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery, it remains a valued plant in both horticulture and botanical collections.
