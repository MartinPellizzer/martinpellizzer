Rubus nutkanus var. nuttallii

Rubus nutkanus var. nuttallii, commonly known as the Nuttall's blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
