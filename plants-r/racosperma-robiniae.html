Racosperma robiniae

Racosperma robiniae, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a large deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

The plant has economic importance for timber and as a shade tree in urban landscapes.
