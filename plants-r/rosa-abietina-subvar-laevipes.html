Rosa abietina subvar. laevipes

Rosa abietina subvar. laevipes, commonly known as the mountain rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
