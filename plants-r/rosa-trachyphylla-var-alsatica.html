Rosa trachyphylla var. alsatica

Rosa trachyphylla var. alsatica, commonly known as the Alaskan rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features woody stems covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps protect it from extreme cold. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in tundra or rocky habitats.

This plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
