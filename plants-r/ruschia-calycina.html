Ruschia calycina

Ruschia calycina, commonly known as the Cape plume, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, plume-like flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
