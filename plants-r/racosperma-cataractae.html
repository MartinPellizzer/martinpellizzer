Racosperma cataractae

Racosperma cataractae, commonly known as the river red gum, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and distinctive flaky bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing seeds. It thrives in riparian zones and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for native wildlife.
