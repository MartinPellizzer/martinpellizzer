Roupala boissieriana

Roupala boissieriana, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spherical clusters, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Roupala boissieriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
