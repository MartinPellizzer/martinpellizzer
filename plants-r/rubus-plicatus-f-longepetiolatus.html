Rubus plicatus f. longepetiolatus

Rubus plicatus f. longepetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled raspberry, is a botanical variant of the raspberry plant.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which are the stalks connecting the leaves to the stem. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated margins.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
