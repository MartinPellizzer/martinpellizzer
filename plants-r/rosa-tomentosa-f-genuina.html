Rosa tomentosa f. genuina

Rosa tomentosa f. genuina, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, thorny stems covered in a soft, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive woolly appearance. The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are also covered in a fine, downy layer. Its flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and fragrance.
