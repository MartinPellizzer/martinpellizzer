Rorippa sessiliflora

Rorippa sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watercress, is a small flowering plant native to moist environments in Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found near streams, ponds, and wet meadows. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its small white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Rorippa sessiliflora is an aquatic or semi-aquatic species that plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of wetland habitats.
