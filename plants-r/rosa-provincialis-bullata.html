Rosa provincialis bullata

Rosa provincialis bullata, commonly known as the Bulbous Provincial Rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, leathery foliage that is well adapted to its harsh, alpine environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
