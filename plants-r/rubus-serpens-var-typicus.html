Rubus serpens var. typicus

Rubus serpens var. typicus, commonly known as the creeping blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
