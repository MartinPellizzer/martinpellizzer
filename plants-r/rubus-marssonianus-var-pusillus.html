Rubus marssonianus var. pusillus

Rubus marssonianus var. pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, characterized by its small, thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species within its native range.
