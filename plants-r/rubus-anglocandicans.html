Rubus anglocandicans

Rubus anglocandicans, commonly known as the British blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are actually composed of many small drupes clustered together. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, juicy flavor and is used both in culinary and medicinal applications.
