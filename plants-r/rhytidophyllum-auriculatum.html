Rhytidophyllum auriculatum

Rhytidophyllum auriculatum, commonly known as the ear-shaped leaf plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often shaped like ears, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
