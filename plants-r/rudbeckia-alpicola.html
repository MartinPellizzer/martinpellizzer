Rudbeckia alpicola

Rudbeckia alpicola, commonly known as the alpine coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow disk flowers surrounded by orange to red ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. Rudbeckia alpicola is known for its compact growth habit and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in alpine ecosystems.
