Rosa rubrifolia var. inermis

Rosa rubrifolia var. inermis, commonly known as the smoothleaf dogwood rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It typically produces clusters of small, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, somewhat hairless stems and leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.
