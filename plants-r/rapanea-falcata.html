Rapanea falcata

Rapanea falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Rapanea falcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
