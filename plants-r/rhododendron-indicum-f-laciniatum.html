Rhododendron indicum f. laciniatum

Rhododendron indicum f. laciniatum, commonly known as the dissected rhododendron, is a cultivar characterized by its finely cut, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its foliage remains dense and attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is well-suited for shaded areas and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish.
