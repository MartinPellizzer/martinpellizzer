Rubus henrici-egonis

Rubus henrici-egonis, commonly known as the European bramble, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate regions and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of many ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
