Rhipsalis conferta

Rhipsalis conferta, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that produce clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique ability to bloom during the holiday season makes it a popular ornamental plant.

Rhipsalis conferta is relatively low maintenance, making it an ideal choice for indoor gardening.
