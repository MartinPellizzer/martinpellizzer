Rhododendron cuthbertii

Rhododendron cuthbertii, commonly known as the Cuthbert's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, humid climates with well-drained acidic soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a slight pubescence.

Rhododendron cuthbertii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
