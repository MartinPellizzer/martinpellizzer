Ricinus microcarpus proles indostanicus

Ricinus microcarpus proles indostanicus, commonly known as the Indian castor or a variant of the castor plant, is a species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves and unique seed structure. The plant produces small, orbicular seeds that are encased in a hard, glossy shell. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
