Rubus pubescens subsp. amygdalanthus

Rubus pubescens subsp.

amygdalanthus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense pubescence, giving it a hairy appearance.
