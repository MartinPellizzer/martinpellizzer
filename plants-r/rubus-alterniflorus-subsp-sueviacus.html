Rubus alterniflorus subsp. sueviacus

Rubus alterniflorus subsp.

sueviacus, commonly known as the Appalachian blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
