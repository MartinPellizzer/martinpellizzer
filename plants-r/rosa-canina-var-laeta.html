Rosa canina var. laeta

Rosa canina var. laeta, commonly known as the sweet Briar or the yellow rose of China, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fuzzy fruits called hips. The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in sharp prickles.

Rosa canina var. laeta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
