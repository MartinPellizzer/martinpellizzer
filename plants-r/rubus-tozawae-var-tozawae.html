Rubus tozawae var. tozawae

Rubus tozawae var. tozawae, commonly known as the Tozawa raspberry, is a shrub native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has a thorny stem structure. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in early summer.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in local culinary traditions.
