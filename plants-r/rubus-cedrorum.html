Rubus cedrorum

Rubus cedrorum, commonly known as blackcap raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn from yellow to dark red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
