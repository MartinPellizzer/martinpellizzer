Rottboellia rhytachne

Rottboellia rhytachne, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Rottboellia rhytachne is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
