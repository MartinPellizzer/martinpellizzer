Rhododendron wrightianum var. cyclopense

Rhododendron wrightianum var. cyclopense, commonly known as the cyclopense rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and densely arranged along its stems. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded landscapes.
