Rosa viscaria var. subspoliata

Rosa viscaria var. subspoliata, commonly known as the dark red rose, is a variant of the shrub rose species Rosa viscaria.

It is characterized by its deep, almost black-red petals that contrast with its pale yellow to white center. This plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its foliage is dark green and densely arranged, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
