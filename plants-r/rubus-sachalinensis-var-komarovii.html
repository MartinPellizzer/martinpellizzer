Rubus sachalinensis var. komarovii

Rubus sachalinensis var. komarovii, commonly known as the Sakhalin raspberry, is a shrub native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and local horticulture.
