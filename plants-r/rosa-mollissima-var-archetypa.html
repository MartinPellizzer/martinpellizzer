Rosa mollissima var. archetypa

Rosa mollissima var. archetypa, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower rose, is a species native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that open at night, making it a unique nocturnal bloomer. The plant has soft, fuzzy leaves and produces small, red hips that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
