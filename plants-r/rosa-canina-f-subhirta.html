Rosa canina f. subhirta

Rosa canina f. subhirta, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that cover the stems, leaves, and flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
