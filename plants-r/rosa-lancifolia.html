Rosa lancifolia

Rosa lancifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, pleasant fragrance.

Rosa lancifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
