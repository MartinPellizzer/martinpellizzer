Ruellia corynotheca

Ruellia corynotheca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ruellia corynotheca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various garden conditions.
