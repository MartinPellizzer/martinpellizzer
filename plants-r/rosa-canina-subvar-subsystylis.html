Rosa canina subvar. subsystylis

Rosa canina subvar. subsystylis, commonly known as the dog rose, is a subspecies of the common rose native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant pink or white flowers and thorny stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and edible rose hips. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the fruit, known as a hip, matures in autumn.

This species is often used in traditional herbal medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
