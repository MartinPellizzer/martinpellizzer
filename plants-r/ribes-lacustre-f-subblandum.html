Ribes lacustre f. subblandum

Ribes lacustre f. subblandum, commonly known as the smooth-flowered sandbar currant, is a compact shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and lake shores. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. Its flowers are pale yellow, clustered in racemes, and bloom in early spring.

The fruit is a small, edible red berry that ripens in late summer.
