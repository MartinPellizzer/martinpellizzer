Rubus ulmifolius var. ischnoacanthus

Rubus ulmifolius var. ischnoacanthus, commonly known as the narrow-spined blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry species.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white or pale green, appearing in early summer, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value in gardens.
