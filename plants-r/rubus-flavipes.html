Rubus flavipes

Rubus flavipes, commonly known as the yellow-tailed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and the fruit is a small, edible berry that turns red when ripe. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
