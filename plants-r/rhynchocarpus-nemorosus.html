Rhynchocarpus nemorosus

Rhynchocarpus nemorosus, commonly known as the forest bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in moist, shaded environments within forest ecosystems. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
