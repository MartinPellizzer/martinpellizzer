Ruellia hygrophila

Ruellia hygrophila, commonly known as the water ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of tubular or bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy in texture. Ruellia hygrophila thrives in moist environments and is often found near water sources.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
