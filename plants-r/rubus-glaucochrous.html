Rubus glaucochrous

Rubus glaucochrous, commonly known as the blue-leafed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and compound leaves that often have a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
