Roeperia cyanescens

Roeperia cyanescens, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and delicate, orchid-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and prefers well-drained, acidic soils in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins, and the flowers are borne on slender stems, often in clusters.

Roeperia cyanescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
