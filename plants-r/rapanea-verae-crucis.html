Rapanea verae-crucis

Rapanea verae-crucis, commonly known as the cross-vein plant, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with prominent cross-veins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in small umbels.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.
