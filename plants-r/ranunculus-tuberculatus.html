Ranunculus tuberculatus

Ranunculus tuberculatus, commonly known as the rough buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
