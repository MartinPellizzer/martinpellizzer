Rubus formosensis

Rubus formosensis, commonly known as the Taiwan raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
