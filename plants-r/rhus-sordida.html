Rhus sordida

Rhus sordida, commonly known as the staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fuzzy berries in the fall. Rhus sordida thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, open woods, and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
