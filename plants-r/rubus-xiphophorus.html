Rubus xiphophorus

Rubus xiphophorus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, containing multiple tiny seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in food and medicine.
