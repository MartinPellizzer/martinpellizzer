Rosa cinnamomea var. americana

Rosa cinnamomea var. americana, commonly known as the American cinnamon shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive reddish-brown underside.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
