Racosperma intricatum

Racosperma intricatum, commonly known as the intricate acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma intricatum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it an important plant in soil enrichment.
