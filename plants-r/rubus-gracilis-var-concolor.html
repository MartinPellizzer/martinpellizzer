Rubus gracilis var. concolor

Rubus gracilis var. concolor, commonly known as the smooth blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry species. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and thickets.

Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rubus genus.
