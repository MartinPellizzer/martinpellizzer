Rubus × acutidentatus

Rubus × acutidentatus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
