Rosa sericea f. taronensis

Rosa sericea f. taronensis, commonly known as the Taronian silverbloom rose, is a botanical variant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves and pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is a compact shrub with thorny stems and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
