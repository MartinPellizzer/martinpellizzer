Rubus rosaceus subsp. powellii

Rubus rosaceus subsp.

powellii, commonly known as Powell's blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
