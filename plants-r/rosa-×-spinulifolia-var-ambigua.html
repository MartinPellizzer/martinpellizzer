Rosa × spinulifolia var. ambigua

Rosa × spinulifolia var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous spinulifera rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorned stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five to seven petals and a prominent central pistil. This variety is native to the eastern United States and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
