Reaumuria negevensis

Reaumuria negevensis, commonly known as the Negev reaumuria, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and woody stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hair. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and ability to store water in its leaves. Its leaves are typically small, leathery, and arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Reaumuria negevensis plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
