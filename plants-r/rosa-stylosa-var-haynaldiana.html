Rosa stylosa var. haynaldiana

Rosa stylosa var. haynaldiana, commonly known as the Haynald's climbing rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous, thorny stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with oval to elliptical leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
