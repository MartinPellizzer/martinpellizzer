Rubus pyramidalis var. dechenii

Rubus pyramidalis var. dechenii, commonly known as the Dechen's raspberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color, with a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
