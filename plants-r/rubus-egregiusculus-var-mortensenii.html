Rubus egregiusculus var. mortensenii

Rubus egregiusculus var. mortensenii, commonly known as the Mortensen's evergreen raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, similar to other members of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
