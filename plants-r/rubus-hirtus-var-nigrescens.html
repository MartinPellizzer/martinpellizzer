Rubus hirtus var. nigrescens

Rubus hirtus var. nigrescens, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and develops edible berries that turn from green to dark purple or black as they ripen. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
