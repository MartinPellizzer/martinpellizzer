Rubus apiculatus subsp. lauracensis

Rubus apiculatus subsp.

lauracensis, commonly known as the Lauracan raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, with a tart flavor.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local culinary traditions.
