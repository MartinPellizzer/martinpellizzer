Rubus wrightii var. morifolius

Rubus wrightii var. morifolius, commonly known as the Wright's blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with serrated edges, and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. The plant bears edible, dark purple to black berries that ripen in the summer, which are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
