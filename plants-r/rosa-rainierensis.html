Rosa rainierensis

Rosa rainierensis, commonly known as the Rainier rose, is a species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.

Rosa rainierensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
