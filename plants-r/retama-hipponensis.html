Retama hipponensis

Retama hipponensis, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Retama hipponensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
