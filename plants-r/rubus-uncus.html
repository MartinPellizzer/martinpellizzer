Rubus uncus

Rubus uncus, commonly known as the hooked blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. Rubus uncus thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
