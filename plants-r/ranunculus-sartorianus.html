Ranunculus sartorianus

Ranunculus sartorianus, commonly known as the Sartorian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure.

Ranunculus sartorianus is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
