Ranunculus grandis

Ranunculus grandis, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals that resemble those of the common buttercup, though it is significantly larger. The plant has a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ranunculus grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
