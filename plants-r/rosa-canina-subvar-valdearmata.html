Rosa canina subvar. valdearmata

Rosa canina subvar. valdearmata, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. This subspecies is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems. The leaves are pinnate with five to seven leaflets, and the plant produces red, hairy hips that are edible.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
