Rosa canina f. kazanensis

Rosa canina f. kazanensis, commonly known as the Kazan rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces red, berry-like fruits in the autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its hardy nature and historical significance in horticulture.
