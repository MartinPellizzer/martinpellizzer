Rubus banaticus

Rubus banaticus, commonly known as the Banat raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Europe.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
