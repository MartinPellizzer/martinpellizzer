Rosa indica alba

Rosa indica alba, commonly known as the white Indian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It blooms in the spring and summer, producing clusters of single or semi-double flowers.

Rosa indica alba is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
