Rubus hispidoides

Rubus hispidoides, commonly known as the hispid blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible, dark purple berries. Its fruit is often used in jams and pies, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

Rubus hispidoides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
