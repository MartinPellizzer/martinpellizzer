Rhinorchis magniscutata

Rhinorchis magniscutata, commonly known as the large-leafed rhinorchis, is a terrestrial orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, erect stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces striking, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination. Its growth habit is typically clump-forming, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and specialized adaptations for mycorrhizal relationships.
