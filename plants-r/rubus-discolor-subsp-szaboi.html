Rubus discolor subsp. szaboi

Rubus discolor subsp.

szaboi, commonly known as the Hungarian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Basin. It is characterized by its dark purple to black fruits, which are typically small and densely clustered.

The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique subspecific traits and genetic variability within the Rubus discolor complex.
