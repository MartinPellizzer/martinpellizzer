Rubus brachystemon var. barbatus

Rubus brachystemon var. barbatus, commonly known as the barbed blackberry, is a variant of the brachystemon blackberry species.

It is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits composed of small, dark berries. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it requires specific care due to its thorny growth habit.
