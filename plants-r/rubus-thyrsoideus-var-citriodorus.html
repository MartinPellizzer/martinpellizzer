Rubus thyrsoideus var. citriodorus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. citriodorus, commonly known as the lemon raspberry, is a variant of the bramble species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, lemon-scented leaves that emit a citrus aroma. The flowers are typically pink or white, and the fruit is a small, tart berry with a distinctive fragrance. This plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its unique scent and flavor make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
