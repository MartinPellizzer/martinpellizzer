Rosa dumalis var. kosinsciana

Rosa dumalis var. kosinsciana, commonly known as the Kosinski's wild rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish flowers in late summer. Its hips, which are red and hairy, contain seeds and are used in traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
