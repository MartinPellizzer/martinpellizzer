Rosa cinnamomea f. cinerea

Rosa cinnamomea f. cinerea, commonly known as the gray rose or silver rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa cinnamomea species.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural displays and landscape design.
