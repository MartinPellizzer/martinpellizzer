Ranunculus muelleri

Ranunculus muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is often found in regions with temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
