Raphionacme divaricata var. glabra

Raphionacme divaricata var. glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf raphionacme, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous. Its small, white to pale blue flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
