Rhynchospora pauciflora

Rhynchospora pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often associated with other wetland flora.

Rhynchospora pauciflora plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
