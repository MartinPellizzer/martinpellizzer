Rudgea interrupta

Rudgea interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted rudgea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the cloud forests. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Rudgea interrupta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
