Rubus vagans

Rubus vagans, commonly known as the wandering raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, similar to other raspberries. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Rubus vagans is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
