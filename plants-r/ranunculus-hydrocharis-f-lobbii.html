Ranunculus hydrocharis f. lobbii

Ranunculus hydrocharis f. lobbii, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, floating leaves and bright yellow flowers.

It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. The plant's leaves are typically divided into small, finger-like segments, while its flowers are borne on slender stalks. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic compounds.

This variety is notable for its unique adaptations to aquatic life and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
