Raphanus orientalis

Raphanus orientalis, commonly known as the oriental radish, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, leafy stems and large, fleshy root that can reach up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by round, smooth pods containing several seeds. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for its edible root and leaves.

The species is native to regions of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East.
