Rubus nitidus proles divaricatus

Rubus nitidus proles divaricatus, commonly known as the variegated blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
