Rubus menkei var. oblongifolius

Rubus menkei var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed bramble, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to certain regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with elongated, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red and edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
