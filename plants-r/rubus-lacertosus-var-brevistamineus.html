Rubus lacertosus var. brevistamineus

Rubus lacertosus var. brevistamineus, commonly known as the short-staminated bramble, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and local flora.
