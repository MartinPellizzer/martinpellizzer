Rottboellia incurva

Rottboellia incurva, commonly known as the curly dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy, dark green leaves that curl inward, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Rottboellia incurva is often found in moist soils along streambanks, wetlands, and disturbed areas.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
