Rubus axillaris var. lauenburgensis

Rubus axillaris var. lauenburgensis, commonly known as the Lauenburg raspberry, is a variant of the common brambles found in Europe.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and compound leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
