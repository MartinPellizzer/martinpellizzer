Rivina brasiliensis

Rivina brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian rivina, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are elliptical and have a glossy appearance, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Rivina brasiliensis is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
