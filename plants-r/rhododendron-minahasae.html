Rhododendron minahasae

Rhododendron minahasae, commonly known as the Minahasa rhododendron, is a species native to the Indonesian island of Sulawesi.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at moderate elevations. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron minahasae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
