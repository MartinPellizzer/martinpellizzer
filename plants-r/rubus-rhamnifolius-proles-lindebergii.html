Rubus rhamnifolius proles lindebergii

Rubus rhamnifolius proles lindebergii, commonly known as the Lindberg's bramble, is a shrub native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

This species is often found in woodlands and along the edges of forests where it thrives in partial shade.
