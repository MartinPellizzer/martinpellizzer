Ranunculus auricomus var. pseudocassubicus

Ranunculus auricomus var. pseudocassubicus, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a variant of the broadleaf buttercup.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals and a distinctive, shield-shaped leaf structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is typically deeply lobed, and it produces small, greenish-yellow seed pods that mature in late summer.
