Rubus lasiostylus var. eglandulosus

Rubus lasiostylus var. eglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in jams and desserts.
