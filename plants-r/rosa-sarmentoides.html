Rosa sarmentoides

Rosa sarmentoides, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

Rosa sarmentoides is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
