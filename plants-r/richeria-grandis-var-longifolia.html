Richeria grandis var. longifolia

Richeria grandis var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf richeria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and unique floral structure.
