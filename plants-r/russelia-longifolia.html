Russelia longifolia

Russelia longifolia, commonly known as the showy russelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and clusters of tubular, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, long-blooming ornamental plants.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
