Rosa terrens

Rosa terrens, commonly known as the terrestrial rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats. It produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds.

Rosa terrens is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
