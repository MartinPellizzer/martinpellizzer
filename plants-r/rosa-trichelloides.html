Rosa trichelloides

Rosa trichelloides, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Mongolia. This perennial shrub is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to harsh, alpine conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for ecological and horticultural study.
