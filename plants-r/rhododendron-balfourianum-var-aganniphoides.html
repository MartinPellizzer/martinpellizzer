Rhododendron balfourianum var. aganniphoides

Rhododendron balfourianum var. aganniphoides, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments at high altitudes, often in forested areas. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive elliptical shape, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
