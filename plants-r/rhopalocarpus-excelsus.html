Rhopalocarpus excelsus

Rhopalocarpus excelsus, commonly known as the African sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Rhopalocarpus excelsus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
