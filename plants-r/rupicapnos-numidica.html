Rupicapnos numidica

Rupicapnos numidica, commonly known as the Numidian rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

Rupicapnos numidica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal and dry garden conditions.
