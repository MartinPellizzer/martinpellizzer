Ruellia rupestris

Ruellia rupestris, commonly known as the rock ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in full sun. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. It is noted for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

Ruellia rupestris is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
