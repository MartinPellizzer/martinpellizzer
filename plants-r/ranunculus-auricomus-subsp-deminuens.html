Ranunculus auricomus subsp. deminuens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

deminuens, commonly known as the lesser goldcup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It features finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive yellowish-green color.

The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, often in shades of yellow to orange. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
