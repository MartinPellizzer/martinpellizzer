Roegneria mutabilis var. nemoralis

Roegneria mutabilis var. nemoralis, commonly known as the variable wheatgrass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and culms that are often reddish or purple in color. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue. This variety is known for its ability to thrive in a range of soil types and is often found in woodland margins and open grasslands.

Its flowering stems produce spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
