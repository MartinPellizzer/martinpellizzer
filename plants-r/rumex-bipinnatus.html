Rumex bipinnatus

Rumex bipinnatus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive reddish underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source for livestock.

Rumex bipinnatus is commonly found in moist soils along roadsides, fields, and riverbanks.
