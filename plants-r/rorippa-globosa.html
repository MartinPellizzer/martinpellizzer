Rorippa globosa

Rorippa globosa, commonly known as watercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, bright green, edible leaves that are often used in salads and as a garnish. The plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as ponds, ditches, and streambanks. Its flowers are typically white or yellow and consist of four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

Rorippa globosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
