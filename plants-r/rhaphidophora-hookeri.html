Rhaphidophora hookeri

Rhaphidophora hookeri, commonly known as the black-leafed monstera, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that are deeply lobed, often with a distinctive split near the center. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and anchor itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat.

Rhaphidophora hookeri is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its striking foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
