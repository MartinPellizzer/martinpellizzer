Ranunculus acris var. austrokurilensis

Ranunculus acris var. austrokurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril buttercup, is a variant of the meadow buttercup native to the Kuril Islands and parts of eastern Russia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central greenish area. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a hairy stem and produces small, oval fruits that contain multiple seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
