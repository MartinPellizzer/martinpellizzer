Rhus aromatica var. arenaria

Rhus aromatica var. arenaria, commonly known as the sand anacahuita, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spiny, spherical fruits. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its habitat.
