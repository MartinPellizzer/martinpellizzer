Rauvolfia semperflorens var. insularis

Rauvolfia semperflorens var. insularis, commonly known as the Insular Rainforest Passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers with five petals and a distinctive crown-like structure. The leaves are heart-shaped, glossy, and often have a velvety texture. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
