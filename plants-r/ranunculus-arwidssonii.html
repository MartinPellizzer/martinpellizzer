Ranunculus arwidssonii

Ranunculus arwidssonii, commonly known as the Arctic buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

Ranunculus arwidssonii is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
