Rosa rubiginosa horrida

Rosa rubiginosa horrida, commonly known as the rough rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, red-orange fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are dark green and serrated, with a slightly hairy texture.

Rosa rubiginosa horrida is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.
