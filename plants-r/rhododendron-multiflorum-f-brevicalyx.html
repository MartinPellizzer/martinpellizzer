Rhododendron multiflorum f. brevicalyx

Rhododendron multiflorum f. brevicalyx, commonly known as the dwarf-flowered rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, clustered flowers.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly China. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded landscapes.

The foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest.
