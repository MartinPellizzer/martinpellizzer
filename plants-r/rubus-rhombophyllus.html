Rubus rhombophyllus

Rubus rhombophyllus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are widely cultivated for culinary and commercial purposes.

Rubus rhombophyllus thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
