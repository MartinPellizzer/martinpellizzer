Rosa majalis var. cinerea

Rosa majalis var. cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, and its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to many landscapes.
