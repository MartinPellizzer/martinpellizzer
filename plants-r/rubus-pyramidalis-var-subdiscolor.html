Rubus pyramidalis var. subdiscolor

Rubus pyramidalis var. subdiscolor, commonly known as the variegated bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white, which adds to its ornamental value. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Its thorny stems and sprawling growth habit make it a hardy, resilient plant suitable for wildlife habitats and ornamental gardens.
