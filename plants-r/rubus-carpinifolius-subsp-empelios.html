Rubus carpinifolius subsp. empelios

Rubus carpinifolius subsp.

empelios, commonly known as the Empelios blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
