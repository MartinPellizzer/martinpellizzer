Rosa dumetorum var. wichurae

Rosa dumetorum var. wichurae, commonly known as the Wichura rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The leaves are pinnate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces oval, red-orange hips that contain seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety is also valued for its historical significance in horticultural breeding programs.
