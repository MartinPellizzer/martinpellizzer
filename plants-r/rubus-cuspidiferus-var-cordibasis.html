Rubus cuspidiferus var. cordibasis

Rubus cuspidiferus var. cordibasis, commonly known as the heart-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically larger and more lobed compared to the standard species. The plant produces edible blackberries with a rich, tart flavor, often harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
