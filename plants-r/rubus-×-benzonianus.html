Rubus × benzonianus

Rubus × benzonianus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the evergreen raspberry) and Rubus strigosus (the blackberry).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red or black color, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavorful berries.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological and genetic variations.
