Rubus caesius var. typicus

Rubus caesius var. typicus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
