Ryparosa porcata

Ryparosa porcata, commonly known as the hairy leaf mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, ovate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, rich soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, textured appearance.

Ryparosa porcata is often found in wet or semi-wet habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
