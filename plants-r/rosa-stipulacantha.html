Rosa stipulacantha

Rosa stipulacantha, commonly known as the stipulate rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and distinctive stipules that are often fused to the leaf stalks. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa stipulacantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in suitable climates.
