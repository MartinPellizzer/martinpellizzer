Rubus × inaspectus

Rubus × inaspectus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It is native to the coastal regions of southern California and thrives in mild, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
