Rhaphis arguta

Rhaphis arguta, commonly known as the silver bamboo or silver leaved bamboo, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fluffy, white seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.

Rhaphis arguta thrives in humid, warm environments and is relatively easy to care for in indoor settings.
