Rhipsalis cribrata var. filiformis

Rhipsalis cribrata var. filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a delicate and cascading appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
