Ruellia alternata

Ruellia alternata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the warm regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia alternata thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils.
