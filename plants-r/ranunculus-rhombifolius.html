Ranunculus rhombifolius

Ranunculus rhombifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has palmately divided, rhombic-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
