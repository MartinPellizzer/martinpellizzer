Rhynchosia schlechteri

Rhynchosia schlechteri, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiral-shaped pod, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rhynchosia schlechteri thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
