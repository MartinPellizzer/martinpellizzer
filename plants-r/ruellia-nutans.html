Ruellia nutans

Ruellia nutans, commonly known as the weeping ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Ruellia nutans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread rapidly through its creeping stems.
