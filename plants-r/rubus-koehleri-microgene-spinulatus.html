Rubus koehleri microgene spinulatus

Rubus koehleri microgene spinulatus, commonly known as the spinulose blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
