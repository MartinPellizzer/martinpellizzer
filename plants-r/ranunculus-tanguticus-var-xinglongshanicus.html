Ranunculus tanguticus var. xinglongshanicus

Ranunculus tanguticus var. xinglongshanicus, commonly known as the Xinglongshan buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, hairy seeds in a cluster.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
