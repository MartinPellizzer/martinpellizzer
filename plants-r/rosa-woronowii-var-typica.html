Rosa woronowii var. typica

Rosa woronowii var. typica, commonly known as the Korean rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and serrated, with a glossy green surface.

Rosa woronowii var. typica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
