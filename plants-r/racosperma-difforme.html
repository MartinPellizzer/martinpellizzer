Racosperma difforme

Racosperma difforme, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a fibrous root system, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
