Rosmarinus officinalis subsp. valentinus

Rosmarinus officinalis subsp.

valentinus, commonly known as sweet rosemary, is a subspecies of the rosemary plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that emit a strong, piney scent.

The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to the more common R. officinalis.
