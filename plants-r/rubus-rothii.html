Rubus rothii

Rubus rothii, commonly known as the Roth's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

Rubus rothii is valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in jams and wines.
