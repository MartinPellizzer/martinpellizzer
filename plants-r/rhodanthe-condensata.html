Rhodanthe condensata

Rhodanthe condensata, commonly known as the coastal wallaby-jack, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Rhodanthe condensata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh coastal environments.
