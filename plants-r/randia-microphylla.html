Randia microphylla

Randia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Randia microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruits and foliage.
