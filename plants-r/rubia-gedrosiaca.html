Rubia gedrosiaca

Rubia gedrosiaca, commonly known as the Gedrosian madder, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces red-colored roots and tubers, which have been historically used as a dye source. Its adaptability to harsh, dry environments makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Rubia gedrosiaca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
