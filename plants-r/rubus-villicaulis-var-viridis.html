Rubus villicaulis var. viridis

Rubus villicaulis var. viridis, commonly known as the green-stemmed blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinct greenish stems and foliage. This plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark purple berries.

The botanical features of this variety make it notable for its ornamental value and potential use in horticulture.
