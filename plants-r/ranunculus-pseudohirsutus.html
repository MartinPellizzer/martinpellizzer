Ranunculus pseudohirsutus

Ranunculus pseudohirsutus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus pseudohirsutus is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
