Rosa mollis f. empylota

Rosa mollis f. empylota, commonly known as the soft rose or empylota rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and smaller, more delicate flowers compared to typical Rosa species. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance.

This form is often appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden settings.
