Rorippa nana

Rorippa nana, commonly known as the dwarf watercress, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows in shallow water, wet soil, or along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are usually submerged or floating, with a distinctive rosette shape. It produces tiny white or yellow flowers that are clustered in the axils of the leaves.

Rorippa nana is often used in water gardens and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in moist environments.
