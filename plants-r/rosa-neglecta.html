Rosa neglecta

Rosa neglecta, commonly known as the wild dog rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe, often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils. The plant produces pink to red flowers that are typically smaller than those of other rose species. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it has a climbing or shrub-like growth habit.

Rosa neglecta is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
