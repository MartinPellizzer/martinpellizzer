Roeperia major

Roeperia major, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers consist of five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, making it visually distinct.

Roeperia major is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
