Rhynchosia verdcourtii

Rhynchosia verdcourtii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and forest edges.

Rhynchosia verdcourtii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
