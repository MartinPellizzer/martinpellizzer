Ranunculus blandus

Ranunculus blandus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic plants. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

The leaves are palmately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
