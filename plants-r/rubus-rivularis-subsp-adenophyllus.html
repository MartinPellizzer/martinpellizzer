Rubus rivularis subsp. adenophyllus

Rubus rivularis subsp.

adenophyllus, commonly known as the river raspberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, often shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow in color. Its flowers are generally white or pale pink, and it is known for its thorny stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
