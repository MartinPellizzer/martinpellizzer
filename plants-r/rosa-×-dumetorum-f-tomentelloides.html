Rosa × dumetorum f. tomentelloides

Rosa × dumetorum f. tomentelloides, commonly known as the woolly dog rose, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage remains evergreen in mild climates, contributing to its ornamental value throughout the year. The flowers are typically followed by bright red, hip-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
