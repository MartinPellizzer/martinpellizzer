Rubus × halinii

Rubus × halinii, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its brambles are thorny, and it typically grows as a shrub with compound leaves.

This species is valued both for its culinary uses and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
