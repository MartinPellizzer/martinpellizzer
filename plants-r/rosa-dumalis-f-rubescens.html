Rosa dumalis f. rubescens

Rosa dumalis f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed dog rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive reddish stems and pink to red flowers.

It is a shrub-like species native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

The botanical features of Rosa dumalis f. rubescens make it a notable plant in ecological and horticultural contexts.
