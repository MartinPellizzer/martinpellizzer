Rubus procerus f. subcinereus

Rubus procerus f. subcinereus, commonly known as the silver-haired raspberry.

This plant is a variant of the raspberry species, characterized by its distinctive silver-gray pubescence on the stems and leaves. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually pink or white.

The fruits, which are aggregate berries, are edible and have a tart flavor, making them valuable in both culinary and ecological contexts.
