Rubus rivularis var. cassoviensis

Rubus rivularis var. cassoviensis, commonly known as the Cassovian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found along riverbanks and in dense undergrowth. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is also of interest in botanical studies due to its unique ecological adaptations.
