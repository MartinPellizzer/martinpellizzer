Robinia pseudoacacia var. tortuosa

Robinia pseudoacacia var. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted false acacia, is a cultivar of the black locust tree characterized by its unique, winding branches.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is valued for its ornamental appeal. The plant features compound leaves that are typically bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are showy, appearing in clusters of white or pale yellow, and bloom in late spring.

The tree is relatively fast-growing and has a moderate drought tolerance, making it adaptable to various soil conditions.
