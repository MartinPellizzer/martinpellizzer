Rubus platybelophorus

Rubus platybelophorus, commonly known as the flat-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically broad and flat. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
