Retiniphyllum chloranthum

Retiniphyllum chloranthum, commonly known as the golden star tree, is a unique flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble stars. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Retiniphyllum chloranthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
