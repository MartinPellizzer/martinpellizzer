Rubus hibiscifolius

Rubus hibiscifolius, commonly known as the Hibiscifolius blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that resemble those of hibiscus, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
