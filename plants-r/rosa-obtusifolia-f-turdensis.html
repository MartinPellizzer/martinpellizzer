Rosa obtusifolia f. turdensis

Rosa obtusifolia f. turdensis, commonly known as the Turdensis form of the blunt-leaved rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a rounded or obtuse apex, giving the plant its species name. This form is native to specific regions in the Mediterranean and is adapted to dry, rocky soils.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental flowers and hardy nature, making it a sought-after plant in gardens.
