Ranunculus chrysocyclus

Ranunculus chrysocyclus, commonly known as the golden crown buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive golden center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Ranunculus chrysocyclus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
