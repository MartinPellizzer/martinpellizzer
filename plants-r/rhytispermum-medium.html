Rhytispermum medium

Rhytispermum medium, commonly known as the common rhytispermum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

Rhytispermum medium is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
