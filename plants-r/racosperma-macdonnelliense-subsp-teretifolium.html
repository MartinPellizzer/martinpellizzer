Racosperma macdonnelliense subsp. teretifolium

Racosperma macdonnelliense subsp.

teretifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
