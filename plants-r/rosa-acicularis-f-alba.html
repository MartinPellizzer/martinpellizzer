Rosa acicularis f. alba

Rosa acicularis f. alba, commonly known as the white prickly rose, is a unique cultivar of the prickly rose species.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
