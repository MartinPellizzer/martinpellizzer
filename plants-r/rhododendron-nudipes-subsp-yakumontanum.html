Rhododendron nudipes subsp. yakumontanum

Rhododendron nudipes subsp.

yakumontanum, commonly known as the Yakumontan rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas. It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade and well-drained acidic soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
