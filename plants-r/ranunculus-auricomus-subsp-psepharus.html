Ranunculus auricomus subsp. psepharus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

psepharus, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals that resemble a shield, hence its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
