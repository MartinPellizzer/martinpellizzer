Rhaphithamnus lucidus

Rhaphithamnus lucidus, commonly known as the glossy thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Rhaphithamnus lucidus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
