Rubus septentrionalis

Rubus septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern raspberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to North America and thrives in moist, wooded areas. Its leaves are alternate, with serrated margins and a hairy texture.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the berries are edible, though often tart and less commonly consumed than other raspberry varieties.
