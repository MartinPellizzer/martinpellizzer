Ranunculus idionanthus

Ranunculus idionanthus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white petals and distinctive leaf structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is known for its delicate, cup-shaped petals.

Ranunculus idionanthus is a short-lived perennial, with a life cycle adapted to the harsh environments of its native habitat.
