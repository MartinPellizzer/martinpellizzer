Rubus guentheri thyrsiflorus

Rubus guentheri thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the Guenther's thrysoflorus blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or chaparral habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
