Rhamnus davurica subsp. nipponica

Rhamnus davurica subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as Japanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
