Rhamnus lycioides var. libyca

Rhamnus lycioides var. libyca, commonly known as the Libyan buckthorn, is a shrub native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by black, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
