Rosa viscidula

Rosa viscidula, commonly known as the sticky rose, is a species native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its hairy, often sticky stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The plant produces rose hips that are ornamental and contain seeds, contributing to its ecological significance.
