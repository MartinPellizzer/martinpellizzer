Rosa glauca var. pseudohaberiana

Rosa glauca var. pseudohaberiana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rose, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Rosa glauca species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of northern Mexico, particularly in the Sierra Madre Occidental. The plant is characterized by its pale blue to lavender flowers and narrow, gray-green foliage. It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
