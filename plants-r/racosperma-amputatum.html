Racosperma amputatum

Racosperma amputatum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spikes, typical of many wattles. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small leaflets, and it produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds.

Racosperma amputatum is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
