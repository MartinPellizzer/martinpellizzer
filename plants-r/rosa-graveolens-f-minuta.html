Rosa graveolens f. minuta

Rosa graveolens f. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf sweetbriar, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy in appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges or moist soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in gardens, it is a valued addition to native plant collections.
