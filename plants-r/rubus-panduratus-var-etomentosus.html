Rubus panduratus var. etomentosus

Rubus panduratus var. etomentosus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
