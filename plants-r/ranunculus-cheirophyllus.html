Ranunculus cheirophyllus

Ranunculus cheirophyllus, commonly known as the woolly buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in meadows and grasslands at higher elevations.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
