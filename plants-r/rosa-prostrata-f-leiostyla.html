Rosa prostrata f. leiostyla

Rosa prostrata f. leiostyla, commonly known as the creeping rose or sand rose, is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens. The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to elliptical leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or sandy habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
