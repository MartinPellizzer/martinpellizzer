Rosa × collina var. persimilis

Rosa × collina var. persimilis, commonly known as the creeping pink, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom throughout the summer. The plant has slender, woody stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to form dense mats. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval, serrated leaflets.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
