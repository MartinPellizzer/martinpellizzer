Rubus ohwianus

Rubus ohwianus, commonly known as the Ohwia raspberry, is a species of brambles native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are white to pink, and the fruits are small, red, and edible.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
