Rubus suffultus

Rubus suffultus, commonly known as the European raspberry or red raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that produces edible red berries, which are often used in jams and desserts. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it blooms in the spring.

Rubus suffultus thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny locations.
