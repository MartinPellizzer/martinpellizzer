Rosa rubiginosa var. pulvinaris

Rosa rubiginosa var. pulvinaris, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
