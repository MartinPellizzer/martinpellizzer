Rubus corylifolius f. apricus

Rubus corylifolius f. apricus, commonly known as the apricot rasp.

This plant is a flowering shrub characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. It produces edible berries that are similar in taste to apricots, hence its common name. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit.

It has a long growing season and is valued for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
