Ribes sativum

Ribes sativum, commonly known as gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Grossulariaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow or green berries. The plant produces edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The berries are also used in traditional medicine for their antioxidant properties.
