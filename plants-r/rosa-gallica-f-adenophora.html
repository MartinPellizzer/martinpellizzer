Rosa gallica f. adenophora

Rosa gallica f. adenophora, commonly known as the Crimson Bicolor Rose, is a hybrid form of the Gallica rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive bicolor flowers, which typically feature a deep red outer petal with a white or pale pink inner petal. This plant is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates. It produces fragrant, semi-double blooms in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems.
