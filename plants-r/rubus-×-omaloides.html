Rubus × omaloides

Rubus × omaloides, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between blackberry (Rubus ursinus) and raspberry (Rubus idaeus).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavorful berries.

The botanical features of Rubus × omaloides make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
