Rosa tomentosa f. ambigua

Rosa tomentosa f. ambigua, commonly known as the fuzzy rose or woolly rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, thornless stems covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, ovate, and covered in a silvery-gray down, contributing to its distinctive look.

Rosa tomentosa f. ambigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blossoms.
