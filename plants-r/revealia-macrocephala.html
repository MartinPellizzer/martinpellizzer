Revealia macrocephala

Revealia macrocephala, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, bell-shaped flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Revealia macrocephala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
