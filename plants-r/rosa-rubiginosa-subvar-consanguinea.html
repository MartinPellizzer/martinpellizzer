Rosa rubiginosa subvar. consanguinea

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. consanguinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces rose hips that are typically orange-red and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional herbal medicine.
