Rhus succedanea f. dumoutieri

Rhus succedanea f. dumoutieri, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a cultivar of the staghorn sumac.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and compound leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which develop into red, fuzzy berries. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
