Racosperma confertum

Racosperma confertum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, globular clusters, which are followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Racosperma confertum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
