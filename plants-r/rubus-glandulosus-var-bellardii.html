Rubus glandulosus var. bellardii

Rubus glandulosus var. bellardii, commonly known as the Bellard's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark berries that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces achenes that are dispersed by birds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local ecosystems.
