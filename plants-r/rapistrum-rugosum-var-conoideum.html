Rapistrum rugosum var. conoideum

Rapistrum rugosum var. conoideum, commonly known as the rough rocket or sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in saline or coastal soils and is often found in dunes or rocky shores.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies in coastal ecosystems.
