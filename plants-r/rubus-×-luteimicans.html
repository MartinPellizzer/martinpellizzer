Rubus × luteimicans

Rubus × luteimicans, commonly known as the golden raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange fruits.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that are rich in antioxidants. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use in some regions.
