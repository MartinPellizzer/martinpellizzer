Ranunculus fallax subsp. quadrivaginatus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

quadrivaginatus, commonly known as the four-veined buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking, palmately divided leaves with four to six lobes, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven sepals and numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
