Rubus × lanyii

Rubus × lanyii, commonly known as the Lanyi blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, known for its dark purple to black berries.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in both home gardens and commercial settings.
