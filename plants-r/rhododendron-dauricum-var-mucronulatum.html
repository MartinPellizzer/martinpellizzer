Rhododendron dauricum var. mucronulatum

Rhododendron dauricum var. mucronulatum, commonly known as the Daurian rhododendron, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to red flowers during late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom reliably in suitable climates.
