Rosa submoschata var. rodnensis

Rosa submoschata var. rodnensis, commonly known as the Rodnian musk rose, is a rare and ornamental species native to the Carpathian region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery due to their strong, musky scent.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its historical significance in traditional medicine.
