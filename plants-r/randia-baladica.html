Randia baladica

Randia baladica, commonly known as the broadleaf randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Randia baladica is often found in wetlands, swamps, and other moist habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
