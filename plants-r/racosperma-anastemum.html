Racosperma anastemum

Racosperma anastemum, commonly known as the white-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Racosperma anastemum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
