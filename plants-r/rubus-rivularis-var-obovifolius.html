Rubus rivularis var. obovifolius

Rubus rivularis var. obovifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are obovate in shape, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are white and clustered in umbels, followed by dark purple or black berries.

The species is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in riparian habitats.
