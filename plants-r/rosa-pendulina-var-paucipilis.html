Rosa pendulina var. paucipilis

Rosa pendulina var. paucipilis, commonly known as the slender climbing rose, is a variant of the pendulous rose species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red hips in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its graceful appearance.
