Rubus thyrsoideus proles linkianus

Rubus thyrsoideus proles linkianus, commonly known as the Linkian bramble, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticulture.
