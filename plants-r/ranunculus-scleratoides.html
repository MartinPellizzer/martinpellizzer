Ranunculus scleratoides

Ranunculus scleratoides, commonly known as the roughleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine, rough hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
