Rosa gallica var. hispida

Rosa gallica var. hispida, commonly known as the rough red rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, and it is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. Its leaves are dark green, oval-shaped, and covered with a fine layer of downy hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its historical use in perfumery and medicine.
