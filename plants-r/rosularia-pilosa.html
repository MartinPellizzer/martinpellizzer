Rosularia pilosa

Rosularia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy rose plant, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Rosularia pilosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
