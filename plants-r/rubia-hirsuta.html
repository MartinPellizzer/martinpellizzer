Rubia hirsuta

Rubia hirsuta, commonly known as hairy woodruff, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes over 13,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rubia hirsuta is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, thriving in moist, shaded environments.
