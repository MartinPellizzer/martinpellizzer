Rosa caryophyllacea f. persiana

Rosa caryophyllacea f. persiana, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a botanical variant of the shrub rose native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange, containing seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
