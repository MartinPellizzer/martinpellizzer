Rhodocolea racemosa

Rhodocolea racemosa, commonly known as the clustered rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as Rhododendron and Azalea. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically evergreen, glossy, and elliptical in shape.

Rhodocolea racemosa thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
