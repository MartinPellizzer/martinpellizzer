Rubus × complanatus

Rubus × complanatus, commonly known as the flatbush blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of small, dark berries. The plant typically thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional herbal remedies.
