Rhodomyrtus mengenensis

Rhodomyrtus mengenensis, commonly known as the Mengen rose myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its ornamental value is enhanced by the production of red or purple berries that follow the flowers.

Rhodomyrtus mengenensis is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
