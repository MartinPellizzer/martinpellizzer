Rubus hirtus var. interruptus

Rubus hirtus var. interruptus, commonly known as the rough raspberry or prickly rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
