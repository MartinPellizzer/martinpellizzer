Rubus omalus microgene callistachys

Rubus omalus microgene callistachys, commonly known as the mountain blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces white or pink flowers in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
