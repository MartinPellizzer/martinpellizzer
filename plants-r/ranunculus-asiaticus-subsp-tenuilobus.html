Ranunculus asiaticus subsp. tenuilobus

Ranunculus asiaticus subsp.

tenuilobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals that range in color from yellow to orange.

The leaves are typically trifoliate or palmately divided, with a distinctive waxy coating. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
