Rhus pallida

Rhus pallida, commonly known as the white-fruited sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with 11 to 31 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by clusters of pale green to yellowish-red berries. Rhus pallida thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist or wet soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
