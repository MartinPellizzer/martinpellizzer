Rubus rivularis f. rivulariformis

Rubus rivularis f. rivulariformis, commonly known as the river raspberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, often found in wet or marshy habitats. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges.

This form is notable for its adaptability to moist environments and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.
