Rosa gallica var. tomentella

Rosa gallica var. tomentella, commonly known as the white gallica rose, is a variety of the gallica rose native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with arching stems and fragrant foliage. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and covered in a fine white down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and is valued for its hardiness and historical significance in horticulture.
