Rubus impendens

Rubus impendens, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and horticultural settings.
