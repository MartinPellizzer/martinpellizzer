Rubus cordatifolius

Rubus cordatifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and have a distinctive deep green color. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
