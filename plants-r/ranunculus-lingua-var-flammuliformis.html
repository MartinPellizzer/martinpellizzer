Ranunculus lingua var. flammuliformis

Ranunculus lingua var. flammuliformis, commonly known as the flameleaf buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup species characterized by its distinctive, flame-like leaf patterns.

This plant is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and display a striking contrast of green and yellow, resembling small flames. The flowers are typically yellow with a black center, and they bloom in early spring.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
