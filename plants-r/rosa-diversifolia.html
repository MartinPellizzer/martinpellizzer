Rosa diversifolia

Rosa diversifolia, commonly known as the variable rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, which range from lanceolate to ovate, contributing to its common name. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, and they bloom in late summer.

Rosa diversifolia is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
