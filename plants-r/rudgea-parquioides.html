Rudgea parquioides

Rudgea parquioides, commonly known as the Paraguay orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that resemble orchids. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and thrives in humid climates. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are short and often bear the showy inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
