Rhododendron gibbsiae

Rhododendron gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in mountainous forests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red, adding vibrant hues to its natural habitat.

Rhododendron gibbsiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
