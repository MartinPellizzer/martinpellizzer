Rubus × pseudobrachyadenes

Rubus × pseudobrachyadenes, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal areas of western North America, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

This species is valued for its culinary uses and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
