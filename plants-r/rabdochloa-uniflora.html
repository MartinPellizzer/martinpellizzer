Rabdochloa uniflora

Rabdochloa uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically produces a single flower per stem, which is a distinctive feature contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
