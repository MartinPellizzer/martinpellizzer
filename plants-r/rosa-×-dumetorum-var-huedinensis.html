Rosa × dumetorum var. huedinensis

Rosa × dumetorum var. huedinensis, commonly known as the Huedin rose, is a compact, bushy shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces oval, red-brown fruits known as hips, which are often used in herbal remedies. It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal and alpine gardens.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
