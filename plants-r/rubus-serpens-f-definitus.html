Rubus serpens f. definitus

Rubus serpens f. definitus, commonly known as the definite blackberry.

This plant is a flowering shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the flowers are white with five petals.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.
