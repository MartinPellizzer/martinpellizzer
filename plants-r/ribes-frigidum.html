Ribes frigidum

Ribes frigidum, commonly known as the arctic gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

Ribes frigidum is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand extreme cold.
