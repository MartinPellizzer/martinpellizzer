Rumex salicifolius var. utahensis

Rumex salicifolius var. utahensis, commonly known as Utah dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.

The species is adapted to grow in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and wet meadows.
