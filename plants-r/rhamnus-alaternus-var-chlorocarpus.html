Rhamnus alaternus var. chlorocarpus

Rhamnus alaternus var. chlorocarpus, commonly known as the green-fruited buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in spring. The plant's berries are typically green, which is a distinguishing feature of this variety. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
