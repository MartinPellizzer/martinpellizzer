Rubus polyoplon

Rubus polyoplon, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Rubus polyoplon is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates.
