Rourea inodora

Rourea inodora, commonly known as the fragrant rourea, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a somewhat waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically fragrant, especially in the evening. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Rourea inodora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.
