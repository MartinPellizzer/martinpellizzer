Romana

Romana, commonly known as the common rue, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves and berries contain compounds such as pungent essential oils, which have been used historically for their medicinal properties.

Romana is also noted for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
