Rubus × tardatus

Rubus × tardatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, shady environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
