Rhamnus cachemirica

Rhamnus cachemirica, commonly known as Kashmir buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Pakistan.

It typically grows in rocky, mountainous areas and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by black, egg-shaped berries that are edible but often bitter.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
