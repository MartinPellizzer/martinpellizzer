Racosperma subtessarogonum

Racosperma subtessarogonum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its ecosystem.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
