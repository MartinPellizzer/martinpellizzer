Rhinotropis lindheimeri var. parvifolia

Rhinotropis lindheimeri var. parvifolia, commonly known as Lindheimer's rhinocereus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
