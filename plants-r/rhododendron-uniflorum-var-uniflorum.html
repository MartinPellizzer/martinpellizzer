Rhododendron uniflorum var. uniflorum

Rhododendron uniflorum var. uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
