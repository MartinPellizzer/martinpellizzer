Rubus strigosus var. aberratus

Rubus strigosus var. aberratus, commonly known as the rough blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its hairy stems and dark, firm fruits. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered.

The berries are edible but often have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and preserves.
