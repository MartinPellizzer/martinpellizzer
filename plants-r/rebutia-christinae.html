Rebutia christinae

Rebutia christinae, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of epiphytic cacti native to Brazil.

It features short, segmented stems that often branch out, forming a compact, shrub-like structure. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange, typically blooming during the holiday season. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Rebutia christinae is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
