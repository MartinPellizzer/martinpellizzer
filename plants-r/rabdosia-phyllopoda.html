Rabdosia phyllopoda

Rabdosia phyllopoda, commonly known as the leafy rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rabdosia phyllopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
