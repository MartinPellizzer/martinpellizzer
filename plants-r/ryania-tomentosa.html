Ryania tomentosa

Ryania tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
