Rosa omissa var. typica

Rosa omissa var. typica, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late summer, which are often clustered in trichomes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Rosa omissa var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
