Rumex auritus

Rumex auritus, commonly known as the earwax plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like sorrel and buckwheat. The plant is characterized by its upright, branching stems and triangular, reddish-purple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Rumex auritus is often found in moist, calcareous soils and can thrive in woodland or grassland habitats.
