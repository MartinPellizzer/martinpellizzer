Rutaea rigida

Rutaea rigida, commonly known as the rigid rutaea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit, though not commonly consumed, is covered in a thick, leathery skin.
