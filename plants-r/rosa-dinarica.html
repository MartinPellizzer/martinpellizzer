Rosa dinarica

Rosa dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric rose, is a species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features pinnate leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in the summer. Its botanical characteristics include thorny stems and an extensive root system adapted to rocky and mountainous terrain.

Rosa dinarica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in the region.
