Rosa popovii

Rosa popovii, commonly known as the Popov's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. This shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits called rosehips.

Rosa popovii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
