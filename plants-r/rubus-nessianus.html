Rubus nessianus

Rubus nessianus, commonly known as the Nessian blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable resource in local cuisines.
