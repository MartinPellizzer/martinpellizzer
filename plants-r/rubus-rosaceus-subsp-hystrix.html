Rubus rosaceus subsp. hystrix

Rubus rosaceus subsp.

hystrix, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
