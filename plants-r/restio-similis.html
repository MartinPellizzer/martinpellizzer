Restio similis

Restio similis, commonly known as the slender restio, is a grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and architectural form.
