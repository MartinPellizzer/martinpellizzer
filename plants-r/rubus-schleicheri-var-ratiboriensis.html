Rubus schleicheri var. ratiboriensis

Rubus schleicheri var. ratiboriensis, commonly known as the Schleicher's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant in local ecosystems and traditional cuisines.
