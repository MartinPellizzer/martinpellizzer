Rubus × velutinus

Rubus × velutinus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
