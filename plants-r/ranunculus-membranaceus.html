Ranunculus membranaceus

Ranunculus membranaceus, commonly known as the membranous buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow to orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in the spring and early summer.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
