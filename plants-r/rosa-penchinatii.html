Rosa penchinatii

Rosa penchinatii, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age.

Rosa penchinatii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical significance in traditional medicine.
