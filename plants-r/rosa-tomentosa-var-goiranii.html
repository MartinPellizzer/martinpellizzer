Rosa tomentosa var. goiranii

Rosa tomentosa var. goiranii, commonly known as the Goirani rose, is a shrub native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
