Rupicapnos africana subsp. fraterna

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

fraterna, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various animals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
