Rhagodia linifolia

Rhagodia linifolia, commonly known as the silky everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Rhagodia linifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
