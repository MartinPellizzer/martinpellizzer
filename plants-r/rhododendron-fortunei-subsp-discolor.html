Rhododendron fortunei subsp. discolor

Rhododendron fortunei subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the Variegated Fortune Rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China. It features striking, broad, ovate leaves with a distinctive variegation of white and green, adding visual interest to gardens.

The plant produces dense clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage color and seasonal blooms.
