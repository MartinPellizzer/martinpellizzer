Ranunculus holanthus

Ranunculus holanthus, commonly known as the yellow marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and is characterized by its palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found along riverbanks and marshy areas.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
