Ranunculus silvestris

Ranunculus silvestris, commonly known as the wild buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has palmately divided leaves. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Ranunculus silvestris is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
