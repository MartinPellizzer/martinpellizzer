Rhododendron calostrotum subsp. riparium

Rhododendron calostrotum subsp.

riparium, commonly known as the river rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white.

The plant typically grows along riverbanks and in moist, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
