Rhododendron jasminiflorum superbum

Rhododendron jasminiflorum superbum, commonly known as the jasmine-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The flowers are typically pink to white, with a strong, sweet scent reminiscent of jasmine. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a decorative plant in horticultural landscapes.
