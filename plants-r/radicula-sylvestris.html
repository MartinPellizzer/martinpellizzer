Radicula sylvestris

Radicula sylvestris, commonly known as the forest root, is a rare and ancient plant species found in dense, shaded forest environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, fibrous root system that thrives in nutrient-rich, moist soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind rather than insects. Its leaves are broad and slightly serrated, adapted to capture limited sunlight.

Radicula sylvestris plays a vital role in maintaining soil stability and supporting microbial life in forest ecosystems.
