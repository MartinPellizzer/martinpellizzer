Robynsiella fastigiata

Robynsiella fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf robyn's hawthorn, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact form and seasonal interest.
