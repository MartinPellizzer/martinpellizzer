Rubus idaeus

Rubus idaeus, commonly known as red raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. Its fruits develop from the fertilized flower buds and are typically harvested in the summer.

The plant is also known for its thorny stems and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
