Rebutia raffaellii

Rebutia raffaellii, commonly known as the Raffaello's moon cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a short, thick stem with spines, and its root system is adapted to store water efficiently. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rebutia raffaellii is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
