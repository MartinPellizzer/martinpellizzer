Rubus ourosepalus

Rubus ourosepalus, commonly known as the golden-sepal raspberry.

It is a species of bramble belonging to the Rubus genus, characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a sweet and tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The plant is native to China and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
