Romulea caplandica

Romulea caplandica, commonly known as the Capland's glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. Romulea caplandica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
