Rhododendron sheltoniae

Rhododendron sheltoniae, commonly known as Shelton's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Nepal and India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific soil and climate requirements.
