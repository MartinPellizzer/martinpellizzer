Rubus caesius subsp. transaltaicus

Rubus caesius subsp.

transaltaicus, commonly known as the Transalpine bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, usually white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and horticultural interest.
