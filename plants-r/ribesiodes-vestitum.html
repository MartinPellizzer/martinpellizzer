Ribesiodes vestitum

Ribesiodes vestitum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in early summer.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
