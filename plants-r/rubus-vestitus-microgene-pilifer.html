Rubus vestitus microgene pilifer

Rubus vestitus microgene pilifer, commonly known as the hairy raspberry.

This plant is a species of bramble belonging to the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It typically grows in temperate regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Its hairy texture, as suggested by its specific epithet, is a distinguishing feature in its botanical description.
