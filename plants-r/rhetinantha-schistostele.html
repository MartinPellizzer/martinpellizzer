Rhetinantha schistostele

Rhetinantha schistostele, commonly known as the stonecress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its schistostele, a type of vascular tissue arrangement featuring a central xylem surrounded by phloem. The plant typically has basal leaves that are pinnately divided, and it produces small, white or yellow flowers in racemate inflorescences. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
