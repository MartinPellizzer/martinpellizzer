Rubia braunii

Rubia braunii, commonly known as Braun's madder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots contain a reddish pigment called rubia, which has been historically used in dyeing textiles.

Rubia braunii is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and woodland margins.
