Reichardia × canariensis

Reichardia × canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island dandelion, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Reichardia speciosa and Reichardia glochidiata.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, particularly in the Canary Islands. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-green leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water retention.

Reichardia × canariensis is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
