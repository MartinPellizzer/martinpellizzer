Rottboellia exaltata f. arundinacea

Rottboellia exaltata f. arundinacea, commonly known as the bamboo grass or giant bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, clumping growth habit.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that can dominate disturbed soils.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or used in erosion control due to its hardy nature and rapid growth.
