Rosa hirta var. saxicola

Rosa hirta var. saxicola, commonly known as the saxicola rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climatic conditions.
