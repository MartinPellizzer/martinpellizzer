Rosa × bibracensis

Rosa × bibracensis, commonly known as the hybrid tea rose, is a species resulting from the crossbreeding of Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rosa × bibracensis is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
