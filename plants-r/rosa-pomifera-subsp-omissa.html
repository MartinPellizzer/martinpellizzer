Rosa pomifera subsp. omissa

Rosa pomifera subsp.

omissa, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the rose apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family. It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers and edible, apple-like fruits.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its stems are often covered in thorns.

The flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
