Rosa mollis subsp. emplyota

Rosa mollis subsp.

emplyota, commonly known as the soft rose or emplyota rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
