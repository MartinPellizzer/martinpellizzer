Ravenea krociana

Ravenea krociana, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to three meters in length. The plant produces striking orange to red flowers, which are followed by elongated, spiny fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ravenea krociana is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
