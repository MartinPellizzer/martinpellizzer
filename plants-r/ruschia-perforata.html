Ruschia perforata

Ruschia perforata, commonly known as the laceleaf ice plant, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, blue-green leaves with a distinctive lace-like pattern of translucent windows. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
