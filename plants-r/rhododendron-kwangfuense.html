Rhododendron kwangfuense

Rhododendron kwangfuense, commonly known as the Kwangfu rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Sichuan and Yunnan provinces.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates with high humidity. It is often found in forest understories, where it forms part of the rich biodiversity of subtropical ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
