Restio paludicola

Restio paludicola, commonly known as the marsh restio, is a slender, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family, which is characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized adaptations to wet environments. The plant typically grows in dense stands, often in areas with standing water or saturated soil. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, white to pale blue flowers.

Restio paludicola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
