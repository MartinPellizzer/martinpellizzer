Roseanthus elongatus

Roseanthus elongatus, commonly known as the slender roseanthus, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The slender roseanthus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
