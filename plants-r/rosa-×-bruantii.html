Rosa × bruantii

Rosa × bruantii, commonly known as the Bruant rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa rugosa and Rosa bracteata.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and abundant production of fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers. The plant typically features dark green, glossy foliage and produces red or orange hips in the fall. It is well adapted to coastal environments and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
