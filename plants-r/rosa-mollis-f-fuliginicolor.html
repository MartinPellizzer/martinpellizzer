Rosa mollis f. fuliginicolor

Rosa mollis f. fuliginicolor, commonly known as the sooty rose, is a unique cultivar of the rose family characterized by its dark, almost black foliage.

This variety is a form of Rosa mollis, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, contrasting with its dark green leaves. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its distinctive appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and botanical collections.
