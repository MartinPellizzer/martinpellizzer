Rosa viscaria var. minuscula

Rosa viscaria var. minuscula, commonly known as the dwarf hairy rose, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, thorny stems and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are trifoliate, with hairy surfaces that give the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
