Rhododendron javanicum subsp. javanicum

Rhododendron javanicum subsp.

javanicum, commonly known as the Java rhododendron, is a species native to the Indonesian archipelago, particularly Sumatra and Java. It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical lowland forests.

The plant features large, leathery leaves that are often dark green and glossy, and its flowers are showy, with five petals that range in color from pink to red. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
