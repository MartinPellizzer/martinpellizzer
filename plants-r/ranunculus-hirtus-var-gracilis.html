Ranunculus hirtus var. gracilis

Ranunculus hirtus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are finely divided and covered with a soft, hairy texture, which gives it its common name.

It is an early spring bloomer, often appearing before the canopy of trees fully develops.
