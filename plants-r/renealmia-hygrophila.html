Renealmia hygrophila

Renealmia hygrophila, commonly known as the water reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, due to its preference for moist soil conditions. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Renealmia hygrophila is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
