Rosa × subcanina

Rosa × subcanina, commonly known as the evergreen shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery foliage that remains throughout the year. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring. It is well-suited for coastal gardens due to its tolerance of salt spray and mild frost.

Rosa × subcanina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in hedging and landscape design.
