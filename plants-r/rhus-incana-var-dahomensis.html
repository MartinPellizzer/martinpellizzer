Rhus incana var. dahomensis

Rhus incana var. dahomensis, commonly known as the Dahomey waxleaf.

This variant is a shrub or small tree native to West Africa, characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The leaves are typically grayish-white on the underside, giving it a distinctive wax-like appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by smooth, reddish-brown fruits.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
