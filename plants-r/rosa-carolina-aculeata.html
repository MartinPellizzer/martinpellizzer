Rosa carolina aculeata

Rosa carolina aculeata, commonly known as the Carolina prickly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red-orange fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa carolina aculeata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
