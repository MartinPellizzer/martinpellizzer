Rubus × dealbatus

Rubus × dealbatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically alternate and have serrated margins, while the flowers are small and white.

The berries, though not as commonly consumed as other Rubus species, are edible and have a tart flavor.
