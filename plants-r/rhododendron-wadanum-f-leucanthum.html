Rhododendron wadanum f. leucanthum

Rhododendron wadanum f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered wadanum rhododendron, is a unique cultivar characterized by its pale to white flowers.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. The plant typically features broad, leathery leaves and grows as an evergreen shrub. Its botanical interest lies in its distinct floral coloration, which sets it apart from the typical pink or red blooms of its parent species.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to shaded environments.
