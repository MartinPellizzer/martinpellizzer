Rumex × weberi

Rumex × weberi, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex acetobursa (broad-leaf sorrel). The plant typically has broad, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration on the veins and undersides. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Rumex × weberi is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
