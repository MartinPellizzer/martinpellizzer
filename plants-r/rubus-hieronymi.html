Rubus hieronymi

Rubus hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymus bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that develop from its flowers. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
