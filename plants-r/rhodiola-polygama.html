Rhodiola polygama

Rhodiola polygama, commonly known as the Himalayan rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to cold, arid environments and has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions. Rhodiola polygama is also known for its high concentration of secondary metabolites, including rosavins and salidrosides, which contribute to its traditional medicinal uses.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various alpine species.
