Roscyna japonica

Roscyna japonica, commonly known as the Japanese rosemary, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It features aromatic, needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color and emit a strong, pine-like scent. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Roscyna japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
