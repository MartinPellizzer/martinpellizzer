Rottboellia compressa var. fasciculata

Rottboellia compressa var. fasciculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf horse-tail, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense, tufted clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along waterways.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to various environments, contributing to its widespread distribution.
