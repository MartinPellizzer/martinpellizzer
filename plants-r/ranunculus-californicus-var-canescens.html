Ranunculus californicus var. canescens

Ranunculus californicus var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy California buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals and a hairy, glandular stem that distinguishes it from other ranunculus species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral displays.
