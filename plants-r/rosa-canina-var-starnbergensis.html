Rosa canina var. starnbergensis

Rosa canina var. starnbergensis, commonly known as the Starnberg rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces red, hairy hips that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and open meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
