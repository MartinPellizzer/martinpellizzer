Rubus × questierifrons

Rubus × questierifrons, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (loganberry), resulting in a plant with unique fruit characteristics. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruits are aggregate berries that range in color from dark purple to black.

This hybrid is prized for its large, flavorful berries, which are often used in culinary applications.
