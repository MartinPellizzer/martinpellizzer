Ranunculus auricomus subsp. pulchrifrons

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

pulchrifrons, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with prominent central pistils and numerous stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in open woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are palmately divided with serrated edges, and the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

It is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
