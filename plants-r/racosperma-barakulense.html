Racosperma barakulense

Racosperma barakulense, commonly known as the Barakulense acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with small, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Racosperma barakulense plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
