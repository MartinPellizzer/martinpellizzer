Rubus juzepczukii

Rubus juzepczukii, commonly known as the Juzepczuk raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Europe and parts of Russia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
