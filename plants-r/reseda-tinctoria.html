Reseda tinctoria

Reseda tinctoria, commonly known as yellow weed or dyer's knotgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant is known for its historical use in dyeing textiles with a yellow or orange hue. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Reseda tinctoria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
