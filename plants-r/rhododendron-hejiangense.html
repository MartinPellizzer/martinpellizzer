Rhododendron hejiangense

Rhododendron hejiangense, commonly known as the Hejiang rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Yunnan and Sichuan provinces.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its botanical features include thick, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Rhododendron hejiangense is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
