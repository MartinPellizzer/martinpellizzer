Rubus oreogeton ruber

Rubus oreogeton ruber, commonly known as the red raspberry bush, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called raspberries, which are typically bright red when ripe. These fruits are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climates.
