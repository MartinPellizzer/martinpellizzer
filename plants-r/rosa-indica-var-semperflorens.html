Rosa indica var. semperflorens

Rosa indica var. semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, single or semi-double flowers that bloom continuously throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, oval, green leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, long-blooming ornamental plant.

Its resilience and continuous flowering make it a versatile addition to both landscapes and containers.
