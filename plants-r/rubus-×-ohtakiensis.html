Rubus × ohtakiensis

Rubus × ohtakiensis, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
