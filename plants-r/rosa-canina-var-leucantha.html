Rosa canina var. leucantha

Rosa canina var. leucantha, commonly known as the white dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose characterized by its pale pink to white flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically growing in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rosa genus.

The white dog rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
