Rubus purpuratus subsp. praedatus

Rubus purpuratus subsp.

praedatus, commonly known as the purple raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with flowering occurring on the previous year's stems.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of multiple drupes, which are typically red or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
