Ruellia cephalotes

Ruellia cephalotes, commonly known as the purpletop verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and blue flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of its stems. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and somewhat hairy.

Ruellia cephalotes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
