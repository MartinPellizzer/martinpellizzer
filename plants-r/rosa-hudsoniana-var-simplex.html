Rosa hudsoniana var. simplex

Rosa hudsoniana var. simplex, commonly known as the Hudson's Bay rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Canada.

It features simple, thornless stems and is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
