Ranunculus variantifrons

Ranunculus variantifrons, commonly known as the variable buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and vary in shape, contributing to its common name. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, cheerful appearance.
