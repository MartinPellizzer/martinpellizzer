Rubus × ingratus

Rubus × ingratus, commonly known as the thimbleberry, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is native to North America and thrives in moist, shady environments. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of tiny white flowers in the spring.

The thimbleberry is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of wild berries.
