Romulea planifolia

Romulea planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaved romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing pale yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bulbs are adapted to survive dry periods, making it a hardy species in well-drained soils.

Romulea planifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
