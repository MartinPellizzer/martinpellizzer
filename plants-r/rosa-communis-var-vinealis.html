Rosa communis var. vinealis

Rosa communis var. vinealis, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a variant of the common rose that exhibits vigorous vine-like growth habits.

This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. It is a deciduous shrub that produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. The leaves are typically compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for trellises and garden borders.
