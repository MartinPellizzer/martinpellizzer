Rinorea aucuparia

Rinorea aucuparia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silvery or whitish hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Rinorea aucuparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
