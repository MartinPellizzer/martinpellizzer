Rubus raduloides

Rubus raduloides, commonly known as the raduloid raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
