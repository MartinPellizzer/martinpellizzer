Rubia cordifolia var. longifolia

Rubia cordifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved velvet flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces a deep blue dye from its flowers, historically used in textiles and natural pigments. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Its ornamental value and traditional uses have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ethnobotany.
