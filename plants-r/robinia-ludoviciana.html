Robinia ludoviciana

Robinia ludoviciana, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The tree has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in late spring. Its bark is thick, flaky, and often has a reddish-brown color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and hard, durable wood.
