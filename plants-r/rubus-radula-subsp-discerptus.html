Rubus radula subsp. discerptus

Rubus radula subsp.

discerptus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
