Robinia glabrescens

Robinia glabrescens, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves that turn a striking golden hue in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in upright racemes. Its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it a significant species in both ecological and commercial contexts.

Robinia glabrescens is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, contributing to its adaptability and resilience.
