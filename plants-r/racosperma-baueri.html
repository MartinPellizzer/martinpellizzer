Racosperma baueri

Racosperma baueri, commonly known as the Bauern's acacia, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow to golden flowers during the warmer months. Racosperma baueri is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance and efficient water use.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
