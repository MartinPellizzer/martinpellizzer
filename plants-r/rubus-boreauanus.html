Rubus boreauanus

Rubus boreauanus, commonly known as the Alaska blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in alpine and subarctic environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
