Rubus rigidus var. mundii

Rubus rigidus var. mundii, commonly known as the desert raspberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a red or orange color. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along desert margins. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are typically white or pale pink.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
