Ruilopezia × josephensis

Ruilopezia × josephensis, commonly known as the Joseph's lantana, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Ruilopezia and Lantana species.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the specific cultivar. Ruilopezia × josephensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
