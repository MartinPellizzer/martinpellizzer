Ranunculus × poellianus

Ranunculus × poellianus, commonly known as the hybrid buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a contrasting central blotch. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

Ranunculus × poellianus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
