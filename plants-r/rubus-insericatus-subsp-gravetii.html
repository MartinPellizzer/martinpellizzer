Rubus insericatus subsp. gravetii

Rubus insericatus subsp.

gravetii, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a shrub native to North America, particularly found in the eastern United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is known for its hardy nature and ability to colonize disturbed soils.
