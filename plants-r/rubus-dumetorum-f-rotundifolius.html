Rubus dumetorum f. rotundifolius

Rubus dumetorum f. rotundifolius, commonly known as the rounded-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive rounded leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its foliage is often densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in local culinary traditions.
