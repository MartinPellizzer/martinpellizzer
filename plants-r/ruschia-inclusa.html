Ruschia inclusa

Ruschia inclusa, commonly known as the Zulu firecracker, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers that resemble tiny firecrackers, hence its common name. Its leaves are fleshy and cylindrical, often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

Ruschia inclusa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
