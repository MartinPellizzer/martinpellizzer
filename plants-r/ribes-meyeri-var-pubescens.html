Ribes meyeri var. pubescens

Ribes meyeri var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tart berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
