Rubus cissoides var. coloratus

Rubus cissoides var. coloratus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible, bright red berries. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, which helps protect it from herbivores.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.
