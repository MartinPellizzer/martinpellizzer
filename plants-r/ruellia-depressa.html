Ruellia depressa

Ruellia depressa, commonly known as the prostrate ruellia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Ruellia depressa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground covers.
