Rumex acetosella var. angiocarpus

Rumex acetosella var. angiocarpus, commonly known as sourgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its reddish or purple stems and heart-shaped, often reddish leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Rumex genus, sharing similar ecological and morphological traits.
