Rosa × dumetorum subf. ramealis

Rosa × dumetorum subf.

ramealis, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a shrub-like plant characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. It features thorny stems and simple, pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are hairy rose hips that develop after flowering.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
