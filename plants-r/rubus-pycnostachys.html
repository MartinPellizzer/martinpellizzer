Rubus pycnostachys

Rubus pycnostachys, commonly known as the dense-stamen blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere, particularly in Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
