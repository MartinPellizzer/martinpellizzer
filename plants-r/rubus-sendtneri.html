Rubus sendtneri

Rubus sendtneri, commonly known as the Sendtner's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
