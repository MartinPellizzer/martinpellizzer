Ranunculus illyricus subsp. tenorei

Ranunculus illyricus subsp.

tenorei, commonly known as the Balkan buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and vibrant yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. It is characterized by its erect stems and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
