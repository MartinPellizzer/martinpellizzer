Ranunculus polyrhizus

Ranunculus polyrhizus, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are finely divided and floating or submerged, depending on the species.

Ranunculus polyrhizus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.
