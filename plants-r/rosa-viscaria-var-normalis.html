Rosa viscaria var. normalis

Rosa viscaria var. normalis, commonly known as the common dog玫瑰, is a species of shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces red, hairy fruits known as hips.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
