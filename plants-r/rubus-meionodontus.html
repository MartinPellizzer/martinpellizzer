Rubus meionodontus

Rubus meionodontus, commonly known as the small-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs known for their edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, dark purple to black fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and its potential as a food source for wildlife.
