Romulea membranacea

Romulea membranacea, commonly known as the parchment lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces a single, pale yellow or greenish-white bloom that resembles a small lily. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
