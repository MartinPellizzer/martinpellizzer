Ranunculus cassubicus f. gracilis

Ranunculus cassubicus f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate and often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
