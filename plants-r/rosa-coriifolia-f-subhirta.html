Rosa coriifolia f. subhirta

Rosa coriifolia f. subhirta, commonly known as the hairy leaf pink, is a variation of the coral tree rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, and hairy leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
