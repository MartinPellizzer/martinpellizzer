Rhamnus globosa var. glabra

Rhamnus globosa var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-fruited buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous fruits and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland edges and scrublands.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
