Robiquetia odobenus

Robiquetia odobenus, commonly known as the elephant's foot.

It is a succulent plant native to South Africa, characterized by its thick, fleshy, and often hairy stems that store water. The plant typically forms a basal rosette, with leaves that are broad and sometimes lobed. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
