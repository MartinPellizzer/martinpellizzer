Raphia mortehanii

Raphia mortehanii, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a large, robust palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in swampy or wetland areas. Its leaves are used for thatching and roofing in traditional construction, while its fruit provides a source of food for both wildlife and humans.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting diverse communities of animals and contributing to local biodiversity.
