Rosa canina var. borreri

Rosa canina var. borreri, commonly known as the Borreri rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
