Rhododendron caeruleum

Rhododendron caeruleum, commonly known as the blue rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, broad leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep purple, blooming in late spring. Its flowers are typically 2 to 4 centimeters in length and are borne on sturdy, hairy stems.

Rhododendron caeruleum is well adapted to cool, moist climates and thrives in partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
