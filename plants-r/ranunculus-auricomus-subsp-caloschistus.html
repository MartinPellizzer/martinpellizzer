Ranunculus auricomus subsp. caloschistus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

caloschistus, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, blue to violet petals and a distinctive, spurred structure at the base of the flower.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and grassland environments.
