Rubus koehleri subsp. impolitus

Rubus koehleri subsp.

impolitus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Thailand. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
