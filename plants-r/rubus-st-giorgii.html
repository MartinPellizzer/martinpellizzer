Rubus st-giorgii

Rubus st-giorgii, commonly known as the St. George's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
