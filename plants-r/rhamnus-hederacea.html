Rhamnus hederacea

Rhamnus hederacea, commonly known as creeping buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, leathery fruits.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.
