Rosa parviflora var. vulgaris

Rosa parviflora var. vulgaris, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems with thorny branches and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically in early spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

Rosa parviflora var. vulgaris is often used in landscaping for its ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.
