Rubus ericetorum var. cuneatus

Rubus ericetorum var. cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are pink to white, appearing in clusters. It thrives in acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and heathlands.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
