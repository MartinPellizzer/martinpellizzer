Rhododendron minus var. majus

Rhododendron minus var. majus, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, making it ideal for small gardens or as a foundation plant. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and the vibrant blooms that add color to woodland gardens.
