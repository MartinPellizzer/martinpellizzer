Rubus conothyrsoides

Rubus conothyrsoides, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
