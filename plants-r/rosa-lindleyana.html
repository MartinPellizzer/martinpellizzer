Rosa lindleyana

Rosa lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorny buds.

Rosa lindleyana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
