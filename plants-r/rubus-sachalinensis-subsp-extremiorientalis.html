Rubus sachalinensis subsp. extremiorientalis

Rubus sachalinensis subsp.

extremiorientalis, commonly known as the Sakhalin raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly found in the Russian Far East and parts of China. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, forested areas or along riverbanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of Rubus taxonomy and regional flora.
