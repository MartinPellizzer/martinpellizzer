Ranunculus gaurii

Ranunculus gaurii, commonly known as the Gauri buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often adorned with a central tuft of white or yellow stamens. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Ranunculus gaurii is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
