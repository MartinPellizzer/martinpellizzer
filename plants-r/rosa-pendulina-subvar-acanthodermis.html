Rosa pendulina subvar. acanthodermis

Rosa pendulina subvar. acanthodermis, commonly known as the climbing rose with thorny stems, is a botanical variant characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and distinctive features.

It possesses long, arching stems that can reach significant lengths, often requiring support for growth. The leaves are typically compound, with multiple leaflets that are oval to lance-shaped. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with a delicate appearance and a sweet fragrance.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
