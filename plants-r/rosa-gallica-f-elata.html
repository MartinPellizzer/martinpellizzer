Rosa gallica f. elata

Rosa gallica f. elata, commonly known as the Alba rose, is a botanical variety of the Gallica rose, characterized by its white flowers and compact growth habit.

It is native to Europe and has been cultivated for centuries for its ornamental value. The plant features thorny stems, dark green foliage, and fragrant blooms that typically appear in late spring to early summer. It is well-suited for gardens and can be grown in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

This rose is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and as a symbol of purity and love.
