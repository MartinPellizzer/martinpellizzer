Rhododendron occidentale

Rhododendron occidentale, commonly known as the western azalea, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils of mountainous regions. The plant features showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, often in shades of pink, red, or white. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blossoms.

Rhododendron occidentale is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
