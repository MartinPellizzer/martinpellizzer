Rhododendron mayebarae f. leucanthum

Rhododendron mayebarae f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered form of Mayebarae rhododendron, is a unique hybrid species characterized by its striking white blooms.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are usually borne in dense clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
