Rosa lutea var. punicea

Rosa lutea var. punicea, commonly known as the scarlet rose, is a botanical variant of the yellow rose species.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange-red flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its color variation and historical use in horticulture.
