Rosa × dumetorum f. hutbergensis

Rosa × dumetorum f. hutbergensis, commonly known as the Hutberg's shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to Central Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces rose hips that are orange-red in color. It is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.

This variety is noted for its resistance to common rose diseases and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
