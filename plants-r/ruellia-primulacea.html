Ruellia primulacea

Ruellia primulacea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a misty cloud. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture.

Ruellia primulacea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady garden settings.
