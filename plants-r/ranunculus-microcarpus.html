Ranunculus microcarpus

Ranunculus microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers bloom from spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its small, rounded fruits and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
