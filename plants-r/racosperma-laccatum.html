Racosperma laccatum

Racosperma laccatum, commonly known as the black wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by woody pods containing flat, oval seeds. It is well adapted to the diverse climates of its native range, including both coastal and inland areas.

Racosperma laccatum plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various native animals.
