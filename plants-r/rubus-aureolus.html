Rubus aureolus

Rubus aureolus, commonly known as the golden blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, yellowish-orange fruits that are edible. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pinkish flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.

Due to its unique color and flavor, it is of interest to both botanists and food enthusiasts.
