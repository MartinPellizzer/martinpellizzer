Rubus gymnostachys subsp. macrothyrsus

Rubus gymnostachys subsp.

macrothyrsus, commonly known as the large-throated blackberry, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a dark purple to black berry, which is edible and has a tart flavor.

This subspecies is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
