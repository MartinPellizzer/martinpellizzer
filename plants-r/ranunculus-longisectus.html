Ranunculus longisectus

Ranunculus longisectus, commonly known as the long-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern hemisphere.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
