Rhipsalis occidentalis

Rhipsalis occidentalis, commonly known as the Western mistletoe, is a species of flowering plant in the Cactaceae family.

It is a climbing cactus that produces small, white or pink flowers and red, fleshy fruits. Native to the Caribbean and parts of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
