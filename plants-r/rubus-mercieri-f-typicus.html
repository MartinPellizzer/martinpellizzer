Rubus mercieri f. typicus

Rubus mercieri f. typicus, commonly known as the typical form of the European bramble, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
