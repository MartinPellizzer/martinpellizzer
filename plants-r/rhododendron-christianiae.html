Rhododendron christianiae

Rhododendron christianiae, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist climates. It is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,000 and 3,000 meters.

The leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.
