Ranunculus tridentatus

Ranunculus tridentatus, commonly known as the three-toothed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other well-known genera like buttercups and corydalis.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
