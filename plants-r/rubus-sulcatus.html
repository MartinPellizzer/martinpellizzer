Rubus sulcatus

Rubus sulcatus, commonly known as the serrated blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

Rubus sulcatus plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
