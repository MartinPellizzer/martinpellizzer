Rubus × oblongicarpus

Rubus × oblongicarpus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry).

It is a perennial shrub that produces edible berries, which are typically dark red to black in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its fruits are rich in antioxidants and are used in various culinary applications.

The plant's botanical characteristics include compound leaves with serrated edges and a strong, aromatic scent.
