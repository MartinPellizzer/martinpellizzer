Rosa rugosa var. glabriuscula

Rosa rugosa var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth rugosa rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are edible and often used in jams and teas. It is well adapted to coastal areas and tolerates salt spray, making it a hardy ornamental and hedge plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
