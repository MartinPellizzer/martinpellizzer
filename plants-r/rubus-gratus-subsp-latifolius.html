Rubus gratus subsp. latifolius

Rubus gratus subsp.

latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, oval-shaped leaflets and thorny stems.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
