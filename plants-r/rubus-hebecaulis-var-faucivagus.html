Rubus hebecaulis var. faucivagus

Rubus hebecaulis var. faucivagus, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry species characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various growing conditions.
