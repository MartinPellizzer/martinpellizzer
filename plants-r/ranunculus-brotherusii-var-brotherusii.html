Ranunculus brotherusii var. brotherusii

Ranunculus brotherusii var. brotherusii, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the high alpine regions of Europe.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent, petal-like sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 1,500 meters. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing numerous small flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
