Ranunculus sulphureus subsp. exaltatus

Ranunculus sulphureus subsp.

exaltatus, commonly known as the golden buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy underside, and it is often found in alpine meadows.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to other members of the species.
