Ranunculus hakkodensis var. glaber

Ranunculus hakkodensis var. glaber, commonly known as the glabrous meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its simple, palmately divided leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a short stem with few leaves, and its flowers are usually solitary.

The variety 'glaber' is distinguished by its lack of hair on the stem and leaves, giving it a smooth appearance.
