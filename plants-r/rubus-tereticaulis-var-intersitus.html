Rubus tereticaulis var. intersitus

Rubus tereticaulis var. intersitus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
