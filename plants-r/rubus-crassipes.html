Rubus crassipes

Rubus crassipes, commonly known as the thick-stemmed blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in dense, prickly thorns. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets.

Rubus crassipes thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
