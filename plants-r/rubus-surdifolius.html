Rubus surdifolius

Rubus surdifolius, commonly known as the Canadian bramble.

It is a shrub native to North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries, which are typically red or black, are edible but often have a tart flavor.

Rubus surdifolius plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
