Ranunculus nannfeldtii

Ranunculus nannfeldtii, commonly known as the Nannfeldt's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps protect it from harsh environmental conditions.

Ranunculus nannfeldtii is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often brightly colored flowers.
