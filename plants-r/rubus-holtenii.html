Rubus holtenii

Rubus holtenii, commonly known as Holten's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is a shrub that produces small, dark purple to black berries, which are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink.

Rubus holtenii is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential as a food source in its native habitat.
