Rosa sergievskiana

Rosa sergievskiana, commonly known as the Serpukhov rose, is a species native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern China.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and is characterized by its fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are red and hairy when mature.

Rosa sergievskiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
