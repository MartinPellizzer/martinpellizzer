Rubus × forestalis

Rubus × forestalis, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
