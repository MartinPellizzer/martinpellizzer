Racosperma cummingianum

Racosperma cummingianum, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a leguminous shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant has a woody stem with thorny branches, and its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
