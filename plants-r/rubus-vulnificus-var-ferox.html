Rubus vulnificus var. ferox

Rubus vulnificus var. ferox, commonly known as the devil's berry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and large, usually white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are highly sought after for their unique flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in some areas, impacting local ecosystems.
