Ranunculus segetalis

Ranunculus segetalis, commonly known as the field buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has a short, stout stem with lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and it produces small, green, bead-like fruits.

Ranunculus segetalis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
