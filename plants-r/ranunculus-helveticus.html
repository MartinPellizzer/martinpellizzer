Ranunculus helveticus

Ranunculus helveticus, commonly known as the Swiss buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with 5-15 petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks. It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.

Ranunculus helveticus is also known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
