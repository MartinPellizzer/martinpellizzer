Ranunculus auricomus subsp. bidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

bidens, commonly known as the golden shield or yellow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals and a central cluster of stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive shield-like shape.

It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in spring and early summer.
