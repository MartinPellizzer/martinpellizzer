Rytidosperma erianthum

Rytidosperma erianthum, commonly known as the woolly bluemink, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Rytidosperma erianthum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
