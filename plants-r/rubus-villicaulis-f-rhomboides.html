Rubus villicaulis f. rhomboides

Rubus villicaulis f. rhomboides, commonly known as the rhomboid bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves, which are typically serrated and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
