Rosa svanetica

Rosa svanetica, commonly known as the Svaneti rose, is a species native to the Caucasus region, particularly Georgia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous areas with cool, moist climates. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa svanetica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
