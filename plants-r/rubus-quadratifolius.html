Rubus quadratifolius

Rubus quadratifolius, commonly known as the four-leaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically consist of four oval-shaped leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
