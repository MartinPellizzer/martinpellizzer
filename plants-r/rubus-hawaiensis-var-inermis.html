Rubus hawaiensis var. inermis

Rubus hawaiensis var. inermis, commonly known as the Hawaiian raspberry, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thornless stems, a distinguishing feature of the varietal name "inermis.". The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
