Rubus timbal-lagravei

Rubus timbal-lagravei, commonly known as the Timbal-Lagrave blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a glossy surface.

The fruit is used in local cuisine and has potential for cultivation due to its unique flavor and adaptability.
