Rhynchosia floribunda

Rhynchosia floribunda, commonly known as the purple-flowered snout bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces showy, purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators. The plant bears elongated pods containing several elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic root nodules.
