Ranunculus farraensis

Ranunculus farraensis, commonly known as the Farrae buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or dry soils. The plant features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven sepals that resemble petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure.

Ranunculus farraensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
