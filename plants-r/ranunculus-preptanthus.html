Ranunculus preptanthus

Ranunculus preptanthus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it has a short, stout stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
