Rosa × porrigens

Rosa × porrigens, commonly known as the double-flowered pink.

It is a hybrid rose species characterized by its full, layered blooms that resemble peonies. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers come in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a high degree of petalation.

Rosa × porrigens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy display.
