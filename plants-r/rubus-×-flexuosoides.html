Rubus × flexuosoides

Rubus × flexuosoides, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems that often trail along the ground, giving it a creeping growth habit. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it is often found in moist, wooded areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
