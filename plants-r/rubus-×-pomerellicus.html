Rubus × pomerellicus

Rubus × pomerellicus, commonly known as the European blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces edible berries that are often used in jams and pies.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
