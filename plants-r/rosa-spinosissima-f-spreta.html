Rosa spinosissima f. spreta

Rosa spinosissima f. spreta, commonly known as the Spanish pink or the spiny Rosa, is a hybrid form of the Rosa spinosissima species.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and deeply lobed, providing a striking contrast to the showy blooms.

This variety is well-suited for hedges or as a decorative plant in gardens due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
