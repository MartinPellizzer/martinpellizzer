Ranunculus flabellatus var. ovatus

Ranunculus flabellatus var. ovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features palmately divided, bright green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and produces showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodland floors and stream banks. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a popular garden plant and a potential weed.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
