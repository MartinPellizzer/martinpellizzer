Rosa austriaca var. subglandulosa

Rosa austriaca var. subglandulosa, commonly known as the Austrian rose, is a variety of the Rosa austriaca species native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in thorns, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
