Ranunculus auricomus subsp. inaequalis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and yellow flowers with prominent, showy sepals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the species is notable for its variability in leaf shape and size.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
