Rosa × reversa var. rotundifolia

Rosa × reversa var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the double-flowered rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its full, layered blooms.

It is native to the United Kingdom and is known for its rich, deep pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and an aromatic scent, enhancing its ornamental value.
