Ranunculus hirtellus var. glabrescens

Ranunculus hirtellus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a hairy texture that becomes smoother as the plant matures.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to the typical form of Ranunculus hirtellus.
