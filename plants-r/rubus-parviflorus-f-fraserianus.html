Rubus parviflorus f. fraserianus

Rubus parviflorus f. fraserianus, commonly known as the Fraser's bramble, is a variant of the common bramble found in the Pacific Northwest of North America.

This plant is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. The berries, which are typically red or black, are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
