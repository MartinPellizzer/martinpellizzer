Ribes rubrum var. domesticum

Ribes rubrum var. domesticum, commonly known as the European gooseberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It produces small, tart berries that are typically green when unripe and red when mature. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The species is closely related to other members of the genus Ribes, including the American gooseberry.
