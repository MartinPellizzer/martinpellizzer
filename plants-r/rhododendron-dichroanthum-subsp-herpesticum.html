Rhododendron dichroanthum subsp. herpesticum

Rhododendron dichroanthum subsp.

herpesticum, commonly known as the Herpesticum rhododendron, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from pale pink to white.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
