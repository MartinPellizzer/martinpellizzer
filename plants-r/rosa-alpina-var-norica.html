Rosa alpina var. norica

Rosa alpina var. norica, commonly known as the Alpine rose or Noric rose, is a species native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, often in high altitudes. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, usually blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
