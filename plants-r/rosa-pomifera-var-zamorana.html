Rosa pomifera var. zamorana

Rosa pomifera var. zamorana, commonly known as the Zamora rose, is a rare and unique variant of the pomifera rose species.

It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, orange-red fruits that resemble fuzzy tomatoes, which are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central America and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
