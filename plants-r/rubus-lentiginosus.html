Rubus lentiginosus

Rubus lentiginosus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark-colored berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Rubus lentiginosus is an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
