Rosa canina f. stohlii

Rosa canina f. stohlii, commonly known as the Stohlii dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring pink to red petals and a prominent central pistil. The plant produces small, red hips that are rich in vitamin C and often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species within the Rosa genus.
