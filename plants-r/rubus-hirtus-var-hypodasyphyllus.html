Rubus hirtus var. hypodasyphyllus

Rubus hirtus var. hypodasyphyllus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, and the leaves are covered in fine, hairy texture.

This variety is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
