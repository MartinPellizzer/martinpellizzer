Rubus rhamnifolius roseus

Rubus rhamnifolius roseus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamin C. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Rubus rhamnifolius roseus is widely cultivated for both its culinary and ornamental value.
