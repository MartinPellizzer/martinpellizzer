Rhus natalensis var. hararensis

Rhus natalensis var. hararensis, commonly known as the Harare flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a reddish or bronze hue. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, fluffy fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The species is valued for its vibrant foliage and seasonal display of flowers and fruits.
