Rosa pouzinii var. sublaevis

Rosa pouzinii var. sublaevis, commonly known as the soft-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant plants.
