Rhizomatopteris sudetica

Rhizomatopteris sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic rhizomata, is a rare and specialized fern species native to the Sudetes mountain range in Central Europe.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively through underground stems. The plant features delicate, lanceolate fronds that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forested areas with rich, humus-filled soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
