Racosperma stellaticeps

Racosperma stellaticeps, commonly known as the star-headed acacia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. The plant is characterized by its slender, spreading branches and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its distinctive feature is the yellow to orange flower spikes, which appear in summer and attract various pollinators.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
