Ribes veganum

Ribes veganum, commonly known as the blackcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. The plant belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its serrated leaves and clusters of flowers. Its botanical features include smooth, reddish bark and a robust root system that helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are widely cultivated for culinary and medicinal purposes.
