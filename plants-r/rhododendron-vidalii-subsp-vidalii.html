Rhododendron vidalii subsp. vidalii

Rhododendron vidalii subsp.

vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and woodland settings.
