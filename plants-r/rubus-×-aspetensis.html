Rubus × aspetensis

Rubus × aspetensis, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

The botanical features of Rubus × aspetensis make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
