Rhodiola brevipetiolata

Rhodiola brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, often reddish stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a raceme, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Rhodiola brevipetiolata is part of the Crassulaceae family and is known for its adaptability to harsh, arid conditions.
