Rosa phalacrostema

Rosa phalacrostema, commonly known as the black rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black petals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces fragrant flowers that bloom in spring.

The botanical interest of Rosa phalacrostema lies in its unique coloration and its role in the diverse flora of its native habitat.
