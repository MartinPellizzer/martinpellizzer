Rhynchosia apoloensis

Rhynchosia apoloensis, commonly known as the Apolo's burr, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
