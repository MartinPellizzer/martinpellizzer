Rubus menkei proles bregutiensis

Rubus menkei proles bregutiensis, commonly known as the Bregutiensis blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and typically grows in rocky or forested habitats. It is valued for its edible fruits, which have a rich, sweet flavor.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
