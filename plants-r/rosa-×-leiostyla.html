Rosa × leiostyla

Rosa × leiostyla, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red fruits known as hips.

Rosa × leiostyla is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability in garden settings.
