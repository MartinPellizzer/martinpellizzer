Rubus salteri

Rubus salteri, commonly known as Salter's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and Alps. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, edible berries.

Rubus salteri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and rocky habitats.
