Ribes cynosbati f. invitans

Ribes cynosbati f. invitans, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow, and it produces fruit throughout the growing season.

The plant has a long history of use in Ayurvedic medicine and is valued for its antioxidant properties.
