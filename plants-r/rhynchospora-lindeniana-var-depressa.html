Rhynchospora lindeniana var. depressa

Rhynchospora lindeniana var. depressa, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along the margins of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It is characterized by its low, compact growth habit and is often adapted to fluctuating water levels.

This variety is distinguished from other rhynchospora species by its shorter stature and specific habitat preferences.
