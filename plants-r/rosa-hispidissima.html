Rosa hispidissima

Rosa hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy texture.

Rosa hispidissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
