Ranunculus besandinus

Ranunculus besandinus, commonly known as the Besandine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a central greenish hue. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing numerous small, bright yellow flowers.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
