Rytigynia sambavensis

Rytigynia sambavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar yellowwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
