Rhynchosia quadrata

Rhynchosia quadrata, commonly known as the four-cornered snout bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Rhynchosia quadrata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
