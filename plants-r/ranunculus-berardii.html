Ranunculus berardii

Ranunculus berardii, commonly known as the Berard's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features showy yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
