Rhododendron hypenanthum

Rhododendron hypenanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in cool, moist environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and densely packed on the stems. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron hypenanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
