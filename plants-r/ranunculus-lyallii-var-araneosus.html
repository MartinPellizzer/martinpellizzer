Ranunculus lyallii var. araneosus

Ranunculus lyallii var. araneosus, commonly known as the spider's web ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers with a distinctive central stigma. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic flowers.

The varietal form araneosus is distinguished by its finely divided leaves and intricate floral structure.
