Ranunculus septempartitus

Ranunculus septempartitus, commonly known as the seven-parted buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically yellow flowers. The plant features deeply lobed, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers consist of numerous petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
