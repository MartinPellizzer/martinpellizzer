Rubia davisiana

Rubia davisiana, commonly known as the Davisian bittersweet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia davisiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
