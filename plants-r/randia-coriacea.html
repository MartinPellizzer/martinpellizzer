Randia coriacea

Randia coriacea, commonly known as rough randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Randia coriacea is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and thickets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
