Rubus × roscidus

Rubus × roscidus, commonly known as the Evergreen Blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The berries are often used in culinary applications and are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.
