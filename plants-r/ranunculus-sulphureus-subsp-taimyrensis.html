Ranunculus sulphureus subsp. taimyrensis

Ranunculus sulphureus subsp.

taimyrensis, commonly known as the Taimyren golden buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern Japan. It is a herbaceous perennial with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has simple, trifoliate leaves and thrives in moist, temperate climates. It is often found in mountainous regions and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and rare species within the Ranunculaceae family.
