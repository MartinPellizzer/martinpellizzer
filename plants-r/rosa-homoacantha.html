Rosa homoacantha

Rosa homoacantha, commonly known as the thorned rose, is a species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems covered in sharp, recurved thorns. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa homoacantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
