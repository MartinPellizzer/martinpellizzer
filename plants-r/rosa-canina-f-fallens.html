Rosa canina f. fallens

Rosa canina f. fallens, commonly known as the fallens dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers that are typically a deeper red than the standard Rosa canina. The plant produces edible hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in culinary and medicinal applications. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
