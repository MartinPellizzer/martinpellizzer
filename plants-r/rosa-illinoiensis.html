Rosa illinoiensis

Rosa illinoiensis, commonly known as the Illinois rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red hips in the fall. Rosa illinoiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for a variety of climates.
