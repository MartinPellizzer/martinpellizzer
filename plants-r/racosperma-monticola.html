Racosperma monticola

Racosperma monticola, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in spherical inflorescences, and it produces small, flat pods that contain edible seeds.

Racosperma monticola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
