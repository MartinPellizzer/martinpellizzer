Rhynchosia fridericiana

Rhynchosia fridericiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that resemble a shower of golden droplets, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rhynchosia fridericiana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
