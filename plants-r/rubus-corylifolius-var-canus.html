Rubus corylifolius var. canus

Rubus corylifolius var. canus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage and fruit make it an ornamental and culinary plant in some regions.
