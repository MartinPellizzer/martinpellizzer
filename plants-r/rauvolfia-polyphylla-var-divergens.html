Rauvolfia polyphylla var. divergens

Rauvolfia polyphylla var. divergens, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their potential hypotensive properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
