Rosa viscaria var. trichocarpa

Rosa viscaria var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the sticky rose or trichocarpa rose, is a variant of the evergreen shrub Rosa viscaria.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in mild climates.
