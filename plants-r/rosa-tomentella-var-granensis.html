Rosa tomentella var. granensis

Rosa tomentella var. granensis, commonly known as the Granada rose, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
