Rosa × involuta var. glandulosa

Rosa × involuta var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with glandular dots on the underside. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a prominent central bud. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
