Rhododendron cuneifolium var. cuneifolium

Rhododendron cuneifolium var. cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or swampy areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a notable component of understory vegetation in its native habitat.
