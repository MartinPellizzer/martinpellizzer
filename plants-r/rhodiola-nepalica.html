Rhodiola nepalica

Rhodiola nepalica, commonly known as the Himalayan rhodiola, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions with low oxygen levels. Its root system is highly valued for its medicinal properties, containing compounds like rosavin and salidroside.

Rhodiola nepalica is also known for its resilience and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported adaptogenic and energizing effects.
