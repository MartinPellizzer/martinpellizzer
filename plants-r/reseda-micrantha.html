Reseda micrantha

Reseda micrantha, commonly known as small-flowered reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. Reseda micrantha is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
