Rosa pendulina var. subinermis

Rosa pendulina var. subinermis, commonly known as the hanging rose or climbing rose, is a botanical variety characterized by its trailing growth habit and fragrant flowers.

It produces small, semi-double blooms in shades of pink and white, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant features thornless stems that can climb or trail, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and it is native to the Mediterranean region.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
