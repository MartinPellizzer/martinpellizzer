Rosa anserinifolia var. daenensis

Rosa anserinifolia var. daenensis, commonly known as the Afghan rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody, bushy growth habit.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
