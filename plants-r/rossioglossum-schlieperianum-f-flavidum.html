Rossioglossum schlieperianum f. flavidum

Rossioglossum schlieperianum f. flavidum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered form of the Schlieper's slipper orchid, is a unique orchid species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a key feature of many orchid species. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can receive adequate light without being exposed to direct sun. Its flowers are highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and moths.

This orchid is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and its rarity in cultivation.
