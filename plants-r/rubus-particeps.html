Rubus particeps

Rubus particeps, commonly known as the purple-fruited raspberry.

It is a species of brambles native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The berries are typically purple or black when mature, containing multiple small drupes. The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
