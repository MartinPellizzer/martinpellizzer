Rosa centrodonta

Rosa centrodonta, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, making it a unique nocturnal flowering plant. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic in shape and covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a hairy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Rosa centrodonta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
