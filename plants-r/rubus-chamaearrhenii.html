Rubus chamaearrhenii

Rubus chamaearrhenii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple or black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
