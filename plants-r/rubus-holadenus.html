Rubus holadenus

Rubus holadenus, commonly known as the Holaden blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy conditions.

Rubus holadenus is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
