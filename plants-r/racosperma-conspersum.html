Racosperma conspersum

Racosperma conspersum, commonly known as the rough leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various animals.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in ecological restoration projects.
