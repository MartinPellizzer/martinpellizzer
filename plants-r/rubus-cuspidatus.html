Rubus cuspidatus

Rubus cuspidatus, commonly known as the prickly rose or brambles, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in temperate regions and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and pies, though the fruits are generally tart and acidic. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to form dense thickets.

Rubus cuspidatus plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
