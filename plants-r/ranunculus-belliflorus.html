Ranunculus belliflorus

Ranunculus belliflorus, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and vibrant yellow flowers with prominent, showy petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is considered rare in the wild.
