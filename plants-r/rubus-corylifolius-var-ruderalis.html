Rubus corylifolius var. ruderalis

Rubus corylifolius var. ruderalis, commonly known as the rough-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and prickly stems, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wastelands, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
