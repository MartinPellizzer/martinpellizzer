Rubus vestitus subsp. leucanthemus

Rubus vestitus subsp.

leucanthemus, commonly known as the white-flowered bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red, hairy berries. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
