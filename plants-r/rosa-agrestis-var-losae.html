Rosa agrestis var. losae

Rosa agrestis var. losae, commonly known as the Losa rose, is a wild species native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse blooms of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically features thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or naturalized landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated for both garden use and conservation efforts.
