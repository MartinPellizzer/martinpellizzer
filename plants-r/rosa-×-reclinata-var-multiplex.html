Rosa × reclinata var. multiplex

Rosa × reclinata var. multiplex, commonly known as the multiflorous shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and is native to the eastern United States. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

The plant is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
