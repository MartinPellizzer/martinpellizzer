Rosa dumalis f. leuca

Rosa dumalis f. leuca, commonly known as the white-flowered dog rose, is a horticultural form of the species Rosa dumalis.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which are typically smaller than those of the typical form. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves, typical of the Rosa genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The foliage is dark green and densely arranged, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
