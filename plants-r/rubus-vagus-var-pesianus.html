Rubus vagus var. pesianus

Rubus vagus var. pesianus, commonly known as the Persian blackberry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is edible, is small, dark purple to black in color, and has a tart flavor.
