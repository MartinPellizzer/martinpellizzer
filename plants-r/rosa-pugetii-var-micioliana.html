Rosa pugetii var. micioliana

Rosa pugetii var. micioliana, commonly known as the Puget Sound rose, is a compact shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy foliage that provides a lush backdrop to its blooms. It is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
