Rubus rosifolius var. tropicus

Rubus rosifolius var. tropicus, commonly known as the tropical raspberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to tropical gardens and horticultural collections.
