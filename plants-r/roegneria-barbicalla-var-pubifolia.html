Roegneria barbicalla var. pubifolia

Roegneria barbicalla var. pubifolia, commonly known as the pubifoliate wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces spikelets that are typically borne in clusters along the stems, and its flowers are small and wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
