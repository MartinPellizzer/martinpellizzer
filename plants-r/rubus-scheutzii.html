Rubus scheutzii

Rubus scheutzii, commonly known as the Scheutz blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a distinctive, tart flavor.
