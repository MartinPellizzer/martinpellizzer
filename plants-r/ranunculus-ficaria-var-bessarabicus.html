Ranunculus ficaria var. bessarabicus

Ranunculus ficaria var. bessarabicus, commonly known as the Bessarabian crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features finely divided, trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
