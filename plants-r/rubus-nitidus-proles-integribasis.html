Rubus nitidus proles integribasis

Rubus nitidus proles integribasis, commonly known as the glossy blackberry.

This plant is a species of bramble characterized by its thorny stems and dense, prickly growth habit. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a glossy surface.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.
