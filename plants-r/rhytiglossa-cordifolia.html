Rhytiglossa cordifolia

Rhytiglossa cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it thrives in areas with high humidity and indirect sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
