Rubus obtruncatus subsp. pilocarpus

Rubus obtruncatus subsp.

pilocarpus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a bright red color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its edible berries and is commonly cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
