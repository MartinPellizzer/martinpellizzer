Rubus fruticosus var. arduennensis

Rubus fruticosus var. arduennensis, commonly known as the Arduennian blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula, particularly in Spain and Portugal. This shrub typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, exhibiting thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
