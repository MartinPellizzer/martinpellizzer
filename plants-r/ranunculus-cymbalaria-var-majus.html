Ranunculus cymbalaria var. majus

Ranunculus cymbalaria var. majus, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

This variety is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.
