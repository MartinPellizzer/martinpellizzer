Rosa × alba var. semiplena

Rosa × alba var. semiplena, commonly known as the semi-double white rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa alba and other rose species.

It is characterized by its large, semi-double blooms that typically range from white to pale pink in color. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and produces fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in floral arrangements and landscaping.
