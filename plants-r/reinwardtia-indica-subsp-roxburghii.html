Reinwardtia indica subsp. roxburghii

Reinwardtia indica subsp.

roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It is characterized by its elegant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and green.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and high humidity. Its root system is adapted to anchor it in rocky or soil substrates, and it often grows in forested or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
