Rubus scoliacanthus

Rubus scoliacanthus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is known for its edible berries, though some varieties may have a strong, tart flavor.
