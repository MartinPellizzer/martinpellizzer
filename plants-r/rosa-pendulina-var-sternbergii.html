Rosa pendulina var. sternbergii

Rosa pendulina var. sternbergii, commonly known as the hanging rose or the weeping rose, is a unique variety of the Rosa pendulina species.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pink or white blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This ornamental shrub is often cultivated for its graceful appearance and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
