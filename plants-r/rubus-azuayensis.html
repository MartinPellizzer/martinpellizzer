Rubus azuayensis

Rubus azuayensis, commonly known as the Azuay blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in humid, mountainous environments at elevations between 1,200 and 2,400 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is highly valued for its intense flavor and is often used in local culinary traditions.
