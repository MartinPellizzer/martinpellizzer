Rumex × sylvaticus

Rumex × sylvaticus, commonly known as the field sorrel, is a hybrid species within the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish or purple on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that are often followed by triangular seed capsules. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
