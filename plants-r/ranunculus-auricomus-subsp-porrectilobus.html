Ranunculus auricomus subsp. porrectilobus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

porrectilobus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features palmately divided, bright green leaves with a distinctive yellowish or white underside.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often with a white or yellowish center. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

This subspecies is characterized by its elongated, spreading lobes on the leaves, distinguishing it from other members of the Ranunculus genus.
