Rhagadiolus kolpinia

Rhagadiolus kolpinia, commonly known as the shimmerleaf plant, is a rare flowering species native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent leaves that refract light, giving the plant a magical appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the early morning hours. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited habitat, it is considered a conservation concern.
