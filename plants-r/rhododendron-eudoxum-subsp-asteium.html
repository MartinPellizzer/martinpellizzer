Rhododendron eudoxum subsp. asteium

Rhododendron eudoxum subsp.

asteium, commonly known as the Asteium rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments.

The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery leaves that are often dark green in color. Its flowers are showy, with petals that range from pink to deep red, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
