Rosa × collina f. denticulata

Rosa × collina f. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
