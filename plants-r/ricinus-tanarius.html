Ricinus tanarius

Ricinus tanarius, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its rough, ridged bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces yellow-green flowers in clusters, followed by three-winged, capsule-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions.
