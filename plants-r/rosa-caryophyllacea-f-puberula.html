Rosa caryophyllacea f. puberula

Rosa caryophyllacea f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa caryophyllacea.

This cultivar is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with partial sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and horticultural collections.
