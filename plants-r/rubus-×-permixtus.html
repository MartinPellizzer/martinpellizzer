Rubus × permixtus

Rubus × permixtus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
