Roegneria abolinii var. divaricans

Roegneria abolinii var. divaricans, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
