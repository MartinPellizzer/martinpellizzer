Rhipsalis salicornoides var. gracilior

Rhipsalis salicornoides var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
