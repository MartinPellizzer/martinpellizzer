Rhexia veronicifolia

Rhexia veronicifolia, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, slightly aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Rhexia veronicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
