Rosa pendulina var. balsamea

Rosa pendulina var. balsamea, commonly known as the Balsam Rose, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 feet, allowing it to climb trees or trellises. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
