Rhipidocladum cordatum

Rhipidocladum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf rockcress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats on rocky soils. The plant features small, fleshy leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters near the base of the plant.

It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
