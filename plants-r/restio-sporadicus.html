Restio sporadicus

Restio sporadicus, commonly known as the sporadic restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
