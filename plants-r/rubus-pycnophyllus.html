Rubus pycnophyllus

Rubus pycnophyllus, commonly known as the dense-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety foliage and produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its thorny stems and dense growth habit make it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various wildlife.
