Rosa scaniana

Rosa scaniana, commonly known as the Swedish rose, is a deciduous shrub native to northern Europe.

It features upright, thorny stems and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne singly or in small clusters. Rosa scaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
