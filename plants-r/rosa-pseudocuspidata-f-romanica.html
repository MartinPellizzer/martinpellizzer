Rosa pseudocuspidata f. romanica

Rosa pseudocuspidata f. romanica, commonly known as the Romanica rose, is a unique floral cultivar characterized by its distinctive floral morphology and horticultural traits.

This form exhibits a compact growth habit with abundant, semi-double to fully double flowers that display a range of pink to red hues. The foliage is typically dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the showy blooms. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.

The plant is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it suitable for a variety of garden environments.
