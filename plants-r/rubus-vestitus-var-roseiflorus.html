Rubus vestitus var. roseiflorus

Rubus vestitus var. roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered bramble, is a shrub native to parts of eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in woodland edges or moist habitats. Its fruits, which are edible, are typically red or black berries that ripen in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
