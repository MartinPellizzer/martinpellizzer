Rubus renifrons

Rubus renifrons, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, woodland areas. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, often with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible blackberries that ripen in the fall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
