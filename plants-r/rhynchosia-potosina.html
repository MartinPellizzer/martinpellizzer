Rhynchosia potosina

Rhynchosia potosina, commonly known as the Potosi bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds.

Rhynchosia potosina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
