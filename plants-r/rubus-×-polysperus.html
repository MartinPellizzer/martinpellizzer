Rubus × polysperus

Rubus × polysperus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry or loganberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (raspberry).

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small drupes, which are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible, flavorful berries.

The hybrid nature of Rubus × polysperus contributes to its adaptability and agricultural importance.
