Rubus dosedlae

Rubus dosedlae, commonly known as the Dosedla's raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries similar to other raspberries.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
