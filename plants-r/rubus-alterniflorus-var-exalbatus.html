Rubus alterniflorus var. exalbatus

Rubus alterniflorus var. exalbatus, commonly known as the white-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its alternate leaf arrangement and white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
