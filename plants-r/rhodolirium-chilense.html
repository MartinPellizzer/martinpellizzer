Rhodolirium chilense

Rhodolirium chilense, commonly known as the Chilean orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Chile.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rhodolirium chilense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
