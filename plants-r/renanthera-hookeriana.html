Renanthera hookeriana

Renanthera hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant purple or red coloration. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, adapting to its often humid and warm habitat. Its roots are fibrous and help anchor it in the rich, organic soil of its natural environment.

The orchid blooms during the wet season, showcasing its ornamental value in both cultivation and the wild.
