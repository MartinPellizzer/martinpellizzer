Rubus tenuiarmatus

Rubus tenuiarmatus, commonly known as the slender-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
