Rosa rothii

Rosa rothii, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on long pedicels. Rosa rothii is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species within the Rosa genus.
