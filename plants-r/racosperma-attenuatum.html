Racosperma attenuatum

Racosperma attenuatum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Racosperma attenuatum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is well-suited to arid environments.
