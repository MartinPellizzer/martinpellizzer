Rubus affictitius

Rubus affictitius, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and have a serrated edge.

The plant thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
