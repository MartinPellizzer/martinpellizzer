Rubus haridasanii

Rubus haridasanii, commonly known as the Indian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The berries are edible, though often tart, and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
