Retanilla ephedra var. crassa

Retanilla ephedra var. crassa, commonly known as the thick-stemmed ephedra, is a shrub native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female inflorescences. Its stems contain alkaloids, including ephedrine, which have historically been used in traditional medicine.

Due to its chemical composition, it is also of interest in modern pharmacological research.
