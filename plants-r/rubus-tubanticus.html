Rubus tubanticus

Rubus tubanticus, commonly known as the tubantian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
