Racosperma enervium

Racosperma enervium, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous tiny leaflets, and it produces woody seed pods that split open to release seeds.

Racosperma enervium is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
