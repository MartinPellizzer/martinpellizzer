Rubus plicatus var. glabriusculus

Rubus plicatus var. glabriusculus, commonly known as the smoothleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with smooth margins and a slightly hairy underside. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
