Ranunculus longilobus

Ranunculus longilobus, commonly known as the long-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ranunculus longilobus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic properties.
