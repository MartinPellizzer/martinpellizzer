Rebutia fabrisii

Rebutia fabrisii, commonly known as the Desert Rose or Evening Primrose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the genus Rebutia within the Cactaceae family, characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often opening in the evening. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Rebutia fabrisii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
