Rosa woodsii var. maderensis

Rosa woodsii var. maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's rock rose, is a subspecies of the wood rose native to the western United States.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are typically compound, with several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.
