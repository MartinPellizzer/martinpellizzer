Rhaphidophora carolinensis

Rhaphidophora carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolinian arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and its stems are often swollen at the nodes. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by berries containing black seeds.

Rhaphidophora carolinensis is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
