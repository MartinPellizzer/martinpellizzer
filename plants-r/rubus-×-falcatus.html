Rubus × falcatus

Rubus × falcatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include a woody structure, persistent leaves, and a preference for moist, acidic soils.

It is often found in forest understories and along stream banks.
