Rosa omeiensis f. glandulosa

Rosa omeiensis f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a hybrid rose species native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its unique traits, it is also of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
