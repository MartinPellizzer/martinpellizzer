Rubus consimilis

Rubus consimilis, commonly known as the similar blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are edible but are generally less flavorful compared to other blackberry species.
