Rathea

Rathea, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pale blue or white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a compact, shrub-like structure with narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. Rathea is known for its ability to thrive in extreme conditions, making it an interesting subject for botanical study.

Its nocturnal blooming habit and unusual appearance have made it a popular plant among botanists and gardeners alike.
