Rosa mollis f. canella

Rosa mollis f. canella, commonly known as the canella rose, is a fragrant flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a hybrid form of Rosa mollis, characterized by its small, pink to white flowers and delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has a compact, shrub-like appearance. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its showy flowers.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and subtle, sweet scent.
