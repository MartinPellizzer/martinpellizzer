Rhamnus lando

Rhamnus lando, commonly known as the African buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Rhamnus lando is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
