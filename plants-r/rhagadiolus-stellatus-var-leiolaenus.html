Rhagadiolus stellatus var. leiolaenus

Rhagadiolus stellatus var. leiolaenus, commonly known as the star-shaped rhagadiolus, is a unique species within the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, star-like inflorescences that consist of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a radial pattern. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive plant in the wild.
