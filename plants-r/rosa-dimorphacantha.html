Rosa dimorphacantha

Rosa dimorphacantha, commonly known as the African rose or the double-stemmed rose, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Rosa dimorphacantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
