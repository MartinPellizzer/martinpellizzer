Rubus callosus var. radula

Rubus callosus var. radula, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and inland scrub environments.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
