Restio tabularis

Restio tabularis, commonly known as the flat-leaf restio, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, flat leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers.

Restio tabularis is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant, architectural form.
