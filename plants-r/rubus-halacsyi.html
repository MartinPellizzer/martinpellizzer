Rubus halacsyi

Rubus halacsyi, commonly known as the Hungarian blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
