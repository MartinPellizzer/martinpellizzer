Rubus ulmifolius var. vulgatus

Rubus ulmifolius var. vulgatus, commonly known as the common bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically three to five leaflets, resembling those of elm trees. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays an important ecological role in supporting wildlife and maintaining soil stability.
