Rhaphidophora lancifolia

Rhaphidophora lancifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It features lance-shaped, glossy leaves with striking variegation, often displaying shades of silver, pink, and green. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family, known for its ornamental foliage rather than showy flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Rhaphidophora lancifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
