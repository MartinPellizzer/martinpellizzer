Rosa mollicanentula

Rosa mollicanentula, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa mollicanentula is a deciduous shrub that thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
