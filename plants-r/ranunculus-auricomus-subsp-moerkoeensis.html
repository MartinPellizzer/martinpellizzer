Ranunculus auricomus subsp. moerkoeensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

moerkoeensis, commonly known as the Moerkoe buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and northern Asia. It typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor soils in tundra and alpine environments.

The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and yellow flowers with prominent, showy petals. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its evolutionary response to harsh, high-latitude habitats.
