Rhus metopium

Rhus metopium, commonly known as the staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 7 pairs of leaflets, and its distinctive reddish, fuzzy fruits that appear in clusters. The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Additionally, it plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing food and habitat.
