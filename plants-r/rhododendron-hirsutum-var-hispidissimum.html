Rhododendron hirsutum var. hispidissimum

Rhododendron hirsutum var. hispidissimum, commonly known as the hispid hairy leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are thick and leathery, often covered with fine, white to grayish hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
