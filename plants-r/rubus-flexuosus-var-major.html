Rubus flexuosus var. major

Rubus flexuosus var. major, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its trailing or prostrate growth habit, with thorny stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy underside.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal regions and along stream banks.
