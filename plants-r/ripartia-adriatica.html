Ripartia adriatica

Ripartia adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic ripartia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Adriatic Sea.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs.

Ripartia adriatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils against erosion.
