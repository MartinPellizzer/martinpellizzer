Rubus wrightii var. itoensis

Rubus wrightii var. itoensis, commonly known as the Ito's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, typically in late summer. Its flowers are white and are borne in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to arid environments.
