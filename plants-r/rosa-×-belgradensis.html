Rosa × belgradensis

Rosa × belgradensis, commonly known as the Belgrad rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its disease resistance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
