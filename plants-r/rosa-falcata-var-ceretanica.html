Rosa falcata var. ceretanica

Rosa falcata var. ceretanica, commonly known as the Ceretanica rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, thorny stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
