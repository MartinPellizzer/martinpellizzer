Ribes laurifolium

Ribes laurifolium, commonly known as the laurel-leaf currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features elliptic to ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
