Ranunculus fallax subsp. inaequalidens

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

inaequalidens, commonly known as the unequal-leaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive asymmetrical shape. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are surrounded by sepals that are usually greenish or reddish in color.

It blooms from late spring to early summer and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
