Rhus cacodendron

Rhus cacodendron, commonly known as the poison ivy tree, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic and can cause skin irritation in humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially hazardous to humans.
