Rumex acetosella subsp. acetosellaeagrestis

Rumex acetosella subsp.

acetosellaeagrestis, commonly known as lesser sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its succulent, reddish stems and trifoliate leaves. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
