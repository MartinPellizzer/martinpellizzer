Rosa × dumetorum f. semiglabra

Rosa × dumetorum f. semiglabra, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed dog rose, is a compact shrub characterized by its thornless stems and dense, bushy growth habit.

It produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets that are generally smooth and slightly hairy. This cultivar is well-suited for small gardens and borders due to its compact size and ornamental value.

It is also valued for its use in cut flower arrangements and as a ground cover in horticultural settings.
