Rubus hirtus var. minutiflorus

Rubus hirtus var. minutiflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit, a red, hairy berry, is typically eaten by birds and small mammals.

It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showcasing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
