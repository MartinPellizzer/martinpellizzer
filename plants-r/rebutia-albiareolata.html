Rebutia albiareolata

Rebutia albiareolata, commonly known as the white-spotted rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or light-colored areoles, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, vibrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the warmer months. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.

Rebutia albiareolata thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to flourish.
