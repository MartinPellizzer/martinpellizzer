Ruta chalepensis proles bracteosa

Ruta chalepensis proles bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate rue, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a distinct, bitter scent, and small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is characterized by its branched stems and bracteate inflorescences, which are specialized leaf-like structures that subtend the flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
