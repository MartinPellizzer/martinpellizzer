Rhododendron caryophyllum

Rhododendron caryophyllum, commonly known as the common rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink, red, or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture and often have a waxy coating. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
