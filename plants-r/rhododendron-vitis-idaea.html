Rhododendron vitis-idaea

Rhododendron vitis-idaea, commonly known as the cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round, providing visual interest throughout the seasons. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Rhododendron vitis-idaea is also known for its edible berries, which are tart and used in traditional foods.
