Rubus hirtus var. atricolor

Rubus hirtus var. atricolor, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple to black berries in the fall. The berries are typically small, firm, and have a tart flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
