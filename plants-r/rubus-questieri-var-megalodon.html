Rubus questieri var. megalodon

Rubus questieri var. megalodon, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that can weigh up to several grams. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine due to their unique flavor and potential health benefits.
