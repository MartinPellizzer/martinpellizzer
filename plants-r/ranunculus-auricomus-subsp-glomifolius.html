Ranunculus auricomus subsp. glomifolius

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

glomifolius, commonly known as the yellow pond lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, cup-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in summer, with a hairy, waxy surface.

The leaves are typically rounded and have a distinctive, glabrous appearance. It grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near ponds or along stream banks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, clustered foliage and its preference for aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
