Ranunculus purshii var. terrestris

Ranunculus purshii var. terrestris, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, reddish stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.
