Racinaea membranacifolia

Racinaea membranacifolia, commonly known as the membranous-leaf racinaea, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its unique leaf structure and ability to thrive in high humidity make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Racinaea membranacifolia is also valued for its role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential medicinal properties.
