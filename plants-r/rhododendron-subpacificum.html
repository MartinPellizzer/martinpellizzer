Rhododendron subpacificum

Rhododendron subpacificum, commonly known as the Pacific rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron subpacificum is often found in coastal forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
