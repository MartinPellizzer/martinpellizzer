Ruppia taquetii

Ruppia taquetii, commonly known as the Mediterranean water plant, is a halophytic species that thrives in saline environments.

It is characterized by its submerged, ribbon-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. This plant is typically found in coastal lagoons, salt marshes, and brackish waters across the Mediterranean region. Its reproductive strategy involves the production of buoyant seeds that can float and germinate in water.

Ruppia taquetii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
