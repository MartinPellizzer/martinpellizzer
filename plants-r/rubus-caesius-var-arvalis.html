Rubus caesius var. arvalis

Rubus caesius var. arvalis, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black berries that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular for culinary use.
