Rhododendron indicum var. obtusum

Rhododendron indicum var. obtusum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a showy, clustered appearance.

It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained, acidic soil.
