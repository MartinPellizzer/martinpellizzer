Ruschia virgata

Ruschia virgata, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit and produces showy, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features narrow, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water and survive in arid conditions. It blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to autumn, and is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Ruschia virgata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
