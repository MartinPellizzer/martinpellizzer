Ranunculus polyanthemos subsp. schennikovii

Ranunculus polyanthemos subsp.

schennikovii, commonly known as the Schennikov's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows.

The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces clusters of delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, and the flowers often have a distinctive yellow center.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
