Rosa wichurana var. rosiflora

Rosa wichurana var. rosiflora, commonly known as the Chilean rose or the Andean rose, is a species native to the coastal regions of Chile and parts of Argentina.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
