Ripartia baldensis

Ripartia baldensis, commonly known as the Bald Mountain spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Ripartia baldensis is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or on slopes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
