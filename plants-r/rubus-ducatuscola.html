Rubus ducatuscola

Rubus ducatuscola, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions of Chile.

The fruit is valued for its intense flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
