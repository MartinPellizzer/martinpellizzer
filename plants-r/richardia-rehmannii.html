Richardia rehmannii

Richardia rehmannii, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or the red-stemmed sedge, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and distinctive reddish stems, which are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets, and it reproduces through seeds and rhizomes.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants.
