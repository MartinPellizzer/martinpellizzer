Racosperma microspermum

Racosperma microspermum, commonly known as the small-seeded acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, pale yellow flowers are clustered in spherical heads, and the seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
