Racinaea terrestris

Racinaea terrestris, commonly known as the ground racemose fern, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces delicate, pendulous inflorescences with small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araceae, which is known for its unique floral structures and aquatic or semi-aquatic adaptations.
