Rubus controversus

Rubus controversus, commonly known as the controversial raspberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black. The plant produces flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rubus genus. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges.

The berries are edible but can vary in taste and quality depending on the specific variety and growing conditions.
