Rubus komarovii

Rubus komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many shrubs with thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.

The botanical features of Rubus komarovii make it an important subject in both horticulture and ecological studies.
