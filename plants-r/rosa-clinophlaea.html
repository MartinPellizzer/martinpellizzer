Rosa clinophlaea

Rosa clinophlaea, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Rosa clinophlaea is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.
