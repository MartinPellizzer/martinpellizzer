Rhynchospora dissitispicula

Rhynchospora dissitispicula, commonly known as the scattered-branched spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in the southeastern United States.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to reddish-brown flowers. Its reproductive structures are typically wind-pollinated, a common trait among Cyperaceae species.

Rhynchospora dissitispicula plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
