Rubus ulmifolius var. subcuneatus

Rubus ulmifolius var. subcuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-angled blackberry, is a variant of the bramble plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive narrow, wedge-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to a range of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
