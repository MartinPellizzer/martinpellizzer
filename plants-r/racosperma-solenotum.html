Racosperma solenotum

Racosperma solenotum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Racosperma solenotum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
