Rosa × vetteri

Rosa × vetteri, commonly known as the Vetter's rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves are typical of many rose species.

Rosa × vetteri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardiness in garden settings.
