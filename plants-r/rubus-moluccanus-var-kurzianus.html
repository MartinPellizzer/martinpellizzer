Rubus moluccanus var. kurzianus

Rubus moluccanus var. kurzianus, commonly known as the Moluccan raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Molucca Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
