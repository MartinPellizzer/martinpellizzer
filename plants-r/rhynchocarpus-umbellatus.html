Rhynchocarpus umbellatus

Rhynchocarpus umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella thornapple, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers arranged in umbels. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions.

The species is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
