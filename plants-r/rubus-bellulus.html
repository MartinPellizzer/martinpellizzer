Rubus bellulus

Rubus bellulus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
