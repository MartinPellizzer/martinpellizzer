Reichardia ligulata var. crispa

Reichardia ligulata var. crispa, commonly known as the curlyleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Reichardia ligulata var. crispa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
