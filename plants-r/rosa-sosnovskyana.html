Rosa sosnovskyana

Rosa sosnovskyana, commonly known as the Siberian rose, is a species native to the cold regions of Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure, compound leaves, and an extensive root system adapted to harsh climates.

Rosa sosnovskyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in cold environments.
