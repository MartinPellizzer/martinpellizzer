Rubus spinuliger

Rubus spinuliger, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically covered in a fine, white waxy coating. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
