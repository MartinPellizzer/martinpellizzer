Rubus corchorifolius var. crataegifolius

Rubus corchorifolius var. crataegifolius, commonly known as the crataegifolius raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that resemble those of hawthorn, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for its edible berries.
