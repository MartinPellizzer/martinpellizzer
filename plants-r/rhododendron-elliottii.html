Rhododendron elliottii

Rhododendron elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist environments with well-drained acidic soils. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron elliottii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
