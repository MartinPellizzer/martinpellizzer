Rostraria cristata var. subcapitata

Rostraria cristata var. subcapitata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a prominent rostellum and a hood-like labellum that sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in montane forests, and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.
