Regmus

Regmus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia.

It possesses delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer with an ethereal glow, especially in the early morning light. The plant's leaves are narrow and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat. Regmus blooms only once every five years, making it a highly sought-after specimen among botanists and collectors.

Its unique botanical features have led to ongoing research into its potential medicinal properties.
