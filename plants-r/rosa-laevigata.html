Rosa laevigata

Rosa laevigata, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces red-orange hips that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Rosa laevigata prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
