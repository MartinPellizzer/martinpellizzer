Rosa afzeliana var. reuteri

Rosa afzeliana var. reuteri, commonly known as the Reuter's rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage, which provide habitat for various wildlife species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, typical of many rose species.

Rosa afzeliana var. reuteri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
