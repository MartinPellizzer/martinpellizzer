Rumex bucephalophorus var. hispanicus

Rumex bucephalophorus var. hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically reddish on the underside, and it produces clusters of small, greenish flowers in the summer. The plant is often found in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and in wet meadows. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants such as sorrel and buckthorn.

The species is valued for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
