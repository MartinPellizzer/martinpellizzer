Rhaphidorhynchus ophioplectron

Rhaphidorhynchus ophioplectron, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage, with flowers that resemble small stars, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, star-shaped blossoms that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
