Rottboellia setosa

Rottboellia setosa, commonly known as the hairy grass or slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Rottboellia setosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
