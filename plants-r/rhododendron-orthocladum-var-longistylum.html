Rhododendron orthocladum var. longistylum

Rhododendron orthocladum var. longistylum, commonly known as the long-styled rhododendron, is a variant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific soil and climate conditions.
