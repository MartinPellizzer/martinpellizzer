Rubus alceifolius var. diversilobatus

Rubus alceifolius var. diversilobatus, commonly known as the variable-lobed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that vary in lobing, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many edible and ornamental brambles.
