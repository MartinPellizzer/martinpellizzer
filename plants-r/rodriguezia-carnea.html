Rodriguezia carnea

Rodriguezia carnea, commonly known as the pink hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Rodriguezia carnea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
