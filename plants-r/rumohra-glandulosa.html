Rumohra glandulosa

Rumohra glandulosa, commonly known as the hairy grass or velvetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Rumohra glandulosa is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive texture and adaptability to various soil conditions.
