Restio montanus

Restio montanus, commonly known as the mountain restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing small, white or pale blue flowers.

Restio montanus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
