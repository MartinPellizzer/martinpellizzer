Rhododendron yakumontanum

Rhododendron yakumontanum, commonly known as the Yakutat rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the coastal regions of Alaska and western Canada.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal rainforests.

This species is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh maritime climates.
