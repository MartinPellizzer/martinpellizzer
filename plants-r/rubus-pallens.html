Rubus pallens

Rubus pallens, commonly known as the pale bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are typically red and edible, though they are less commonly cultivated than other raspberry varieties.

Rubus pallens is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates.
