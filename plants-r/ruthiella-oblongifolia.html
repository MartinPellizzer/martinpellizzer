Ruthiella oblongifolia

Ruthiella oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ruthiella, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
