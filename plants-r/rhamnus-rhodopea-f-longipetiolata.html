Rhamnus rhodopea f. longipetiolata

Rhamnus rhodopea f. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled buckthorn, is a unique variant of the buckthorn species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which give it a distinctive appearance compared to other members of the Rhamnus genus. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring small, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, dark berries that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
