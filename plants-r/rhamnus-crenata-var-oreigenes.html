Rhamnus crenata var. oreigenes

Rhamnus crenata var. oreigenes, commonly known as the coastal buckthorn, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salt exposure and strong winds. It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular plant in coastal landscaping.
