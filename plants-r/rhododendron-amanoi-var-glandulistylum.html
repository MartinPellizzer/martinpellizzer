Rhododendron amanoi var. glandulistylum

Rhododendron amanoi var. glandulistylum, commonly known as the Hainan rhododendron, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Hainan in China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments at elevations between 800 and 1,200 meters. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive glandular pubescence, contributing to its scientific name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
