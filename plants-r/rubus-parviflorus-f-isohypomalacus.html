Rubus parviflorus f. isohypomalacus

Rubus parviflorus f. isohypomalacus, commonly known as the small-flowered raspberry.

This is a flowering plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are small, usually white or pale pink. It produces red or black berries that are edible, though often tart.

The plant is native to certain regions of North America and thrives in woodland or rocky habitats.
