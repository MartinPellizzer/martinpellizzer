Rubus breviglandulosus

Rubus breviglandulosus, commonly known as the short-glandular blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins with short, glandular hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
