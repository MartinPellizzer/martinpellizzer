Rubus biserratus var. incomptus

Rubus biserratus var. incomptus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a food source for wildlife.
