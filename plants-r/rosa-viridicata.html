Rosa viridicata

Rosa viridicata, commonly known as the greenleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions with moist, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant white to pale yellow flowers. Rosa viridicata typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and is often found in woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
