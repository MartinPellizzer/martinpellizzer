Rhizium curvum

Rhizium curvum, commonly known as the spiral-root vine, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive, curling rhizomes that form intricate underground networks.

Its roots exhibit a helical growth pattern, allowing it to anchor itself firmly in soil while facilitating nutrient absorption. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests and wetlands. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring season.

Due to its rare occurrence and specialized habitat requirements, Rhizium curvum is considered a botanically significant species in ecological studies.
