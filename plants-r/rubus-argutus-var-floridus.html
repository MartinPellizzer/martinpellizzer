Rubus argutus var. floridus

Rubus argutus var. floridus, commonly known as the Florida blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, typically in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its edible berries and its role in local ecosystems.
