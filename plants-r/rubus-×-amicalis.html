Rubus × amicalis

Rubus × amicalis, commonly known as the American blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

The plant exhibits a shrub-like growth habit with compound leaves and produces flowers that are white or pale pink in color.
