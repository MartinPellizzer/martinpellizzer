Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. fedorovii

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

fedorovii, commonly known as the Fedorov's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
