Rhynchosia pulchra

Rhynchosia pulchra, commonly known as the pretty bean or golden vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with elongated leaflets and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are often adorned with a distinctive red or purple calyx. The plant bears cylindrical, green pods that contain several seeds, which are typically enclosed in a fibrous covering. Rhynchosia pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
