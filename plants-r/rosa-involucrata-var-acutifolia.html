Rosa involucrata var. acutifolia

Rosa involucrata var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, acuminate (sharp-pointed) leaves and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces red-orange hips in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and wildlife habitat.
