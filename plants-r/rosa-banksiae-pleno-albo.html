Rosa banksiae pleno-albo

Rosa banksiae pleno-albo, commonly known as the White Bank's Rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its double white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its showy, fragrant blooms and hardy nature.
