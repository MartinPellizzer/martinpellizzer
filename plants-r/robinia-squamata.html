Robinia squamata

Robinia squamata, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Robinia squamata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
