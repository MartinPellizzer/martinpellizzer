Rumex domesticus subsp. ruthenicus

Rumex domesticus subsp.

ruthenicus, commonly known as the Ruthenian sorrel, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, broadly triangular, and have a distinctive reddish coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are arranged in spikes, and it is often found in moist, calcareous soils.

It is valued for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor, and is sometimes cultivated as a vegetable or for ornamental purposes.
