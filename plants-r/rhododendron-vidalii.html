Rhododendron vidalii

Rhododendron vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar shrubs and trees.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific care to flourish.
