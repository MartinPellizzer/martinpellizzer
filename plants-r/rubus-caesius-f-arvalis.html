Rubus caesius f. arvalis

Rubus caesius f. arvalis, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a wild, shrub-like plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn red when mature, typically ripening in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open fields.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
