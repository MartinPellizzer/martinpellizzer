Rottlera sebifera

Rottlera sebifera, commonly known as the white rottlera, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Rottlera sebifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes in warmer climates.
