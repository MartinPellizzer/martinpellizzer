Rheedia intermedia

Rheedia intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange.

Rheedia intermedia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
