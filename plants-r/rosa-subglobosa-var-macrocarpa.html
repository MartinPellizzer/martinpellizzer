Rosa subglobosa var. macrocarpa

Rosa subglobosa var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited globes rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces large, red, globe-shaped fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
