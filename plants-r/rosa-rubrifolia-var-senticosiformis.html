Rosa rubrifolia var. senticosiformis

Rosa rubrifolia var. senticosiformis, commonly known as the red-leafed rose, is a variant of the Rosa rubrifolia species, characterized by its distinctive reddish foliage.

This plant is native to regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. It typically grows as a shrub with dense, thorny stems and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a velvety texture and a deep red coloration that becomes more pronounced in cooler weather.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
