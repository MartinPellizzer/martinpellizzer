Ranunculus auricomus subsp. lacinulatus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

lacinulatus, commonly known as the laciniate buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent, prominent, yellow petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the species is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely laciniate leaf margins, which set it apart from other members of the Ranunculus genus.
