Robinia vesicaria

Robinia vesicaria, commonly known as the winged pod tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive seed pods that split open to release seeds. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by the characteristic winged seeds. Robinia vesicaria is often found in open woodlands and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
