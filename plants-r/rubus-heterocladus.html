Rubus heterocladus

Rubus heterocladus, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its variable morphology and hybridizing tendencies.
