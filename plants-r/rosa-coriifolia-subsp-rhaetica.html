Rosa coriifolia subsp. rhaetica

Rosa coriifolia subsp.

rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Alps. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are compound, with ovate leaflets that have a slightly serrated margin. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
