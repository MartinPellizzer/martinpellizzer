Robinia pseudoacacia f. aurea

Robinia pseudoacacia f. aurea, commonly known as the golden robinia or golden false acacia, is a variant of the black locust tree.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its typical green leaves. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States. It produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by flat, brown pods.

The golden foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
