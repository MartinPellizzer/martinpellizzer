Rubus purpuratus microgene acuminum

Rubus purpuratus microgene acuminum, commonly known as the purple raspberry.

This plant is a species of bramble within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests. The leaves are typically lobed and have a slightly serrated edge, while the flowers are white with five petals.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, providing food for birds and insects.
