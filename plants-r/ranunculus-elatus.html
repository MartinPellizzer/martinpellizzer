Ranunculus elatus

Ranunculus elatus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with numerous petals and a hairy stem that helps it withstand cold climates. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough texture and a distinctive yellowish-white underside.

Ranunculus elatus is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in late spring to early summer.
