Rubus candicans f. rotundatus

Rubus candicans f. rotundatus, commonly known as the round-leaved raspberry.

It is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its rounded, hairy leaves and clusters of small, red berries. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its fruit is edible, though it has a tart flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
