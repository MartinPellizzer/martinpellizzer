Remusatia garrettii

Remusatia garrettii, commonly known as the silverleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The roots of Remusatia garrettii are also used in traditional medicine and as a starchy food source in some regions.
