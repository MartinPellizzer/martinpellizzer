Rhipsalis miyagawae

Rhipsalis miyagawae, commonly known as the Miyagawa's mistletoe cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its long, thin, branching stems that resemble twigs. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in the rainforest canopy.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
