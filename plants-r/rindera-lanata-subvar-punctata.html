Rindera lanata subvar. punctata

Rindera lanata subvar. punctata, commonly known as the punctate woolly bladder, is a unique plant characterized by its dense, woolly covering of hairs.

The plant features a succulent, fleshy stem that stores water, typical of arid environments. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, aiding in reducing water loss. The flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that attracts pollinators.

This subspecies is native to specific regions in Africa, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.
