Ranunculus pronicus

Ranunculus pronicus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus pronicus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
