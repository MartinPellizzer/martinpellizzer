Rubus moldavicus var. retezaticus

Rubus moldavicus var. retezaticus, commonly known as the Moldavian blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and grows in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its edible fruits and is often found in forested areas.

The species is relatively rare and has specific ecological requirements for survival.
