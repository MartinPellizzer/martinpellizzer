Rubus discolor var. genuinus

Rubus discolor var. genuinus, commonly known as the white-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The fruit is typically a small, dark purple to black berry with a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
