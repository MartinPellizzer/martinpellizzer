Ranunculus aquatilis var. japonicus

Ranunculus aquatilis var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese water buttercup, is a aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or wetlands, with its leaves floating on the surface or submerged. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, often surrounded by a ring of sepals. It has a short stem and produces small, oval-shaped fruits that disperse via water.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
