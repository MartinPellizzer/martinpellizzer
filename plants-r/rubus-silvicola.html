Rubus silvicola

Rubus silvicola, commonly known as the forest raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible berries that ripen in the summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodlands. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white or pinkish flowers in the spring.

The botanical features of Rubus silvicola make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
