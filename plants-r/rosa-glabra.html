Rosa glabra

Rosa glabra, commonly known as the smooth rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its simple, smooth-stemmed appearance and fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are pinnate with five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, red or yellow fruits called hips.

Rosa glabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
