Rhamnus kabylica

Rhamnus kabylica, commonly known as the Kabylica buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Rhamnus kabylica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
