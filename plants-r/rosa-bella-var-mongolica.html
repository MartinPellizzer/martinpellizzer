Rosa bella var. mongolica

Rosa bella var. mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian beauty rose, is a flowering shrub native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It features upright stems with thorny branches and is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various climates.
