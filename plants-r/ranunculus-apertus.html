Ranunculus apertus

Ranunculus apertus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

The species is known for its early spring blooming period, making it a notable wildflower in temperate regions.
