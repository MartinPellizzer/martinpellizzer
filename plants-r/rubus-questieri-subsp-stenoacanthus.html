Rubus questieri subsp. stenoacanthus

Rubus questieri subsp.

stenoacanthus, commonly known as the narrow-spined blackberry, is a shrub native to southern China. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
