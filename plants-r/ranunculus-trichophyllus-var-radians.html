Ranunculus trichophyllus var. radians

Ranunculus trichophyllus var. radians, commonly known as the radiate buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a central cluster of ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and natural habitats.
