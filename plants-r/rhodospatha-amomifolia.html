Rhodospatha amomifolia

Rhodospatha amomifolia, commonly known as the lemon-scented orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment.

Rhodospatha amomifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its pleasant aroma and unique appearance.
