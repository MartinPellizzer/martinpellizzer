Rhododendron glaucaenum

Rhododendron glaucaenum, commonly known as the blue-leaved rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which turns a vibrant yellow in the fall. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in the spring, adding ornamental value to its habitat. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often found in forest understories where it can receive partial shade.
