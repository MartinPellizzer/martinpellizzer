Rhododendron apricum

Rhododendron apricum, commonly known as the Carpathian azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Rhododendron apricum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
