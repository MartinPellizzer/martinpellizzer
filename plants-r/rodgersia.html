Rodgersia

Rodgersia, commonly known as the "false goat's beard," is a genus of flowering plants in the saxifrage family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its ornamental, fern-like foliage and showy, spires of flowers. The leaves are typically palmately divided, with a soft, velvety texture, and the flowers come in shades of pink, red, and white. Rodgersia thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

It is often used in landscape design for its elegant appearance and ability to add texture and color to shaded areas.
