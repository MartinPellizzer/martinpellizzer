Rudbeckia hirta var. major

Rudbeckia hirta var. major, commonly known as the giant coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features large, showy yellow to orange composite flower heads with a distinctive brown central disk, surrounded by broad, lance-shaped green leaves. The plant typically grows up to 4 feet tall, with a robust stem and a bushy habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and pollinator habitats.
