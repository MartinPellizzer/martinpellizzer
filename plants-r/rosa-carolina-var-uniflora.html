Rosa carolina var. uniflora

Rosa carolina var. uniflora, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a wild species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring arching stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces single, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, usually blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are followed by red-orange hips that persist through the winter, adding ornamental value.

The plant is well adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
