Ranunculus saniculifolius

Ranunculus saniculifolius, commonly known as the leafy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually yellow with numerous stamens, and they bloom in early spring.

Ranunculus saniculifolius prefers well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
