Rosa rubella

Rosa rubella, commonly known as the red rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red petals and thorny stems. The plant is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Rosa rubella produces fragrant flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural cultivation.
