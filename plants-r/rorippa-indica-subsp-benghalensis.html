Rorippa indica subsp. benghalensis

Rorippa indica subsp.

benghalensis, commonly known as Indian watercress, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in shallow water, wetlands, and along the edges of streams and ponds.

The plant features alternate, ovate leaves that are often submerged or floating, with small white flowers that appear in clusters. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, freshwater environments.

This subspecies is valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
