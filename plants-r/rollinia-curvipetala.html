Rollinia curvipetala

Rollinia curvipetala, commonly known as the curved-petioled rollinia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherimoyas. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, creamy flesh and is used in various culinary applications.
