Rhynchospora talamancensis, commonly known as the Talamancan flatsedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in Costa Rica and Panama, where it thrives in wetland and marshy habitats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers. Its leaves are grass-like, with a narrow, linear shape and a smooth, waxy surface.

Rhynchospora talamancensis plays a role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
