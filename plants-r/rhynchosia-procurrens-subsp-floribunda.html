Rhynchosia procurrens subsp. floribunda

Rhynchosia procurrens subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the purple false pomegranate, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking purple to pink flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems.

The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and a woody, creeping stem that can root at the nodes. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil types.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.
