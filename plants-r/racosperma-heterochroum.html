Racosperma heterochroum

Racosperma heterochroum, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, alternating leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the genus.

Racosperma heterochroum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
