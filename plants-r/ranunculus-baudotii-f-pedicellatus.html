Ranunculus baudotii f. pedicellatus

Ranunculus baudotii f. pedicellatus, commonly known as the pedicellate buttercup, is a hybrid form of the genus Ranunculus.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
