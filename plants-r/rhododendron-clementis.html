Rhododendron clementis

Rhododendron clementis, commonly known as the Clement's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Rhododendron clementis prefers acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.
