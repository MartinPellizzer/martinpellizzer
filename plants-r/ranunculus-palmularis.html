Ranunculus palmularis

Ranunculus palmularis, commonly known as the palmate buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
