Rebutia tuberculatochrysantha

Rebutia tuberculatochrysantha, commonly known as the orange-hued moon cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features a swollen, colorful tuberous rootstock that serves as a storage organ for water and nutrients. The plant has a short, woody stem with sparse, blue-gray foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
