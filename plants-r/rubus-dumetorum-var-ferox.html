Rubus dumetorum var. ferox

Rubus dumetorum var. ferox, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems that often grow in clusters. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature, containing several druplets. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
