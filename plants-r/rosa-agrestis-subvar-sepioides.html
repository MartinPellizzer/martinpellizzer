Rosa agrestis subvar. sepioides

Rosa agrestis subvar. sepioides, commonly known as the coastal rose, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand coastal salt spray and strong winds. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

This subvariety is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
