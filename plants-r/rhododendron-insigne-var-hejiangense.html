Rhododendron insigne var. hejiangense

Rhododendron insigne var. hejiangense, commonly known as the Hejiang rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with acidic soils and cool, moist climates. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
