Ranunculus canus var. hesperoxys

Ranunculus canus var. hesperoxys, commonly known as the western hairy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 13 petals, typically surrounded by a ring of pale yellow to white sepals. The plant has a hairy stem and leaves that are palmately divided into three to five lobes. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
