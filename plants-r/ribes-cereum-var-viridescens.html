Ribes cereum var. viridescens

Ribes cereum var. viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-yellow flowers, which contrast with the typical red or orange hues of other Ribes species. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
