Rhamnus tinctoria

Rhamnus tinctoria, commonly known as the yellow buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn yellow in the autumn, and its bright yellow berries that are produced in clusters. The plant is part of the Rhamnaceae family and is known for its medicinal and historical dyeing uses. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
