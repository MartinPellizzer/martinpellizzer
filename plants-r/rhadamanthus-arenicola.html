Rhadamanthus arenicola

Rhadamanthus arenicola, commonly known as the sand rhadamanthus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit and ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
