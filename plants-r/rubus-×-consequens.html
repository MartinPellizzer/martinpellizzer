Rubus × consequens

Rubus × consequens, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
