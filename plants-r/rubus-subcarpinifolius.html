Rubus subcarpinifolius

Rubus subcarpinifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a pointed apex. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, containing numerous small drupes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streamside areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for its edible fruits.
