Rosa cinnamomea var. rubrifolia

Rosa cinnamomea var. rubrifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon rose or red-leaf cinnamon rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are distinctive, with a bright red or reddish-purple coloration during the growing season. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
