Rosa graveolens f. thuringiaca

Rosa graveolens f. thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian rose, is a fragrant shrub native to Central Europe.

It features dark green, glossy leaves and produces abundant clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant is characterized by its strong, sweet fragrance, which is most pronounced in the evening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and hedging.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in European horticulture.
