Raphanus articulatus var. flavus

Raphanus articulatus var. flavus, commonly known as the yellow burr rape, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
