Rubia peregrina var. splendens

Rubia peregrina var. splendens, commonly known as the Splendour Balm of Gilead, is a rare and striking plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is considered a symbol of resilience in arid environments.
