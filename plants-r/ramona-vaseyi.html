Ramona vaseyi

Ramona vaseyi, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to minimize water loss in hot, dry environments. Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring, and it plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.

Ramona vaseyi is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
