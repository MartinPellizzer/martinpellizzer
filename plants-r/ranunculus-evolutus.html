Ranunculus evolutus

Ranunculus evolutus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with bright yellow flowers that have five to nine petals. The plant has alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
