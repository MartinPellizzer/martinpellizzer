Rosa myriacantha var. ripartii

Rosa myriacantha var. ripartii, commonly known as the rock rose or cliff rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky slopes or cliffs. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
