Rubus milliformis var. raduliformis

Rubus milliformis var. raduliformis, commonly known as the red raspberry.

This variant is a shrub native to North America, characterized by its biennial growth habit and thorny stems. The leaves are compound, typically with five to seven leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs. The flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruit is a red, hairy berry that ripens in late summer.

It is valued for its edible berries and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
