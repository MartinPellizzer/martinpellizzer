Rubus stellatiflorus

Rubus stellatiflorus, commonly known as the star-flowered blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, which are blackberries, are typically small and have a tart flavor.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
