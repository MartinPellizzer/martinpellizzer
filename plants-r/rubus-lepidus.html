Rubus lepidus

Rubus lepidus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to western North America and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thornless stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus lepidus is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
