Rhamnus tortuosa var. awarica

Rhamnus tortuosa var. awarica, commonly known as the Hawaiian mahogany, is a shrub or small tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
