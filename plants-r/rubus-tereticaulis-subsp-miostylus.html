Rubus tereticaulis subsp. miostylus

Rubus tereticaulis subsp.

miostylus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinct appearance.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including open woodlands and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
