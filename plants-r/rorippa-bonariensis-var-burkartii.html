Rorippa bonariensis var. burkartii

Rorippa bonariensis var. burkartii, commonly known as the Burkart's watercress, is a variant of the watercress species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found near streams and wetlands. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically submerged or floating, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters, and it produces tiny, elongated seeds that are dispersed by water.

This variant is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes cultivated for culinary use.
