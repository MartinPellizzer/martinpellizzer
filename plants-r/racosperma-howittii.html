Racosperma howittii

Racosperma howittii, commonly known as the Howitt's acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring deep root systems and waxy foliage to reduce water loss. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native fauna.

Racosperma howittii is often found in sandy or rocky soils within dry woodland and shrubland habitats.
