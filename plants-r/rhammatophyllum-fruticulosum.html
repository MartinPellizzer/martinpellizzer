Rhammatophyllum fruticulosum

Rhammatophyllum fruticulosum, commonly known as the fructified rhammatophyllum, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the group of liverworts.

It is characterized by its simple, thalloid structure and the presence of specialized structures called fructifications, which are reproductive organs. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a living fossil, providing insights into the early evolution of land plants.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
