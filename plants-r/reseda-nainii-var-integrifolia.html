Reseda nainii var. integrifolia

Reseda nainii var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Kashmiri Reseda, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually entire, giving it the species name *integrifolia*. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
