Rhinanthus grandiflorus subsp. angustifolius

Rhinanthus grandiflorus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yellow rattle, is a hemiparasitic flowering plant that grows in grasslands and heathlands. It has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from June to August. It relies on root parasitism to obtain some of its nutrients from neighboring plants.

This subspecies is valued for its role in promoting biodiversity in grassland ecosystems.
