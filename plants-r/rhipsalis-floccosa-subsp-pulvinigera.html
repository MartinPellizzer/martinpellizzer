Rhipsalis floccosa subsp. pulvinigera

Rhipsalis floccosa subsp.

pulvinigera, commonly known as the silver-tipped rhipsalis, is a species of cactus native to Brazil. It is characterized by its soft, hair-like stems that give it a fluffy appearance.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This subspecies is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique and elegant appearance.
