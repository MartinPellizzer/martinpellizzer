Rubus chlorothyrsos var. venedicus

Rubus chlorothyrsos var. venedicus, commonly known as the Venetic blackberry, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white, often appearing in clusters.

The fruit, a dark purple berry, is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
