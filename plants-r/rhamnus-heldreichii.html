Rhamnus heldreichii

Rhamnus heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its small, glossy dark green leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant bears small, black berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Rhamnus heldreichii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects.
