Ritterocereus griseus

Ritterocereus griseus, commonly known as the gray cactus or the white-flowered night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white or gray spines. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Ritterocereus griseus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
