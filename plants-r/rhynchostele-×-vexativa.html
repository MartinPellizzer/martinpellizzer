Rhynchostele × vexativa

Rhynchostele × vexativa, commonly known as the hybrid fern, is a rare and intriguing plant that results from the cross between two different rhynchostele species.

It exhibits unique morphological features, including a distinctive central vascular system that is characteristic of the rhynchostele group. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, preferring rich, organic soil conditions. Its fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance that adds to its ornamental value.
