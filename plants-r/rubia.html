Rubia

Rubia, commonly known as dog's mercury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Rubia has been historically used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its seeds are also used as a spice in some traditional cuisines.
