Rhipsalis erythrocarpa

Rhipsalis erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited mistletoe cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that often grow along tree branches, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its fruits are bright red berries, which are an important food source for various bird species.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
