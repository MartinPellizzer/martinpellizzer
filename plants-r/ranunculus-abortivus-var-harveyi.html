Ranunculus abortivus var. harveyi

Ranunculus abortivus var. harveyi, commonly known as Harvey's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals and deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

Harvey's buttercup is an herbaceous perennial that produces small, greenish seed pods after flowering.
