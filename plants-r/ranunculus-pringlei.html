Ranunculus pringlei

Ranunculus pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it has a short, herbaceous stem.

Ranunculus pringlei is often found in areas with rich, well-drained soil and is a popular choice for gardeners seeking native flora.
