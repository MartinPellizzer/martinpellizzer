Rhododendron leucolasium

Rhododendron leucolasium, commonly known as the white-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically white or pale green in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is also of interest to botanists and conservationists.
