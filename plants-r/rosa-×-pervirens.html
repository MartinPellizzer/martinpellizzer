Rosa × pervirens

Rosa × pervirens, commonly known as the everblooming shrub rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa multiflora.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and its ability to produce flowers throughout the growing season. The plant typically features dark green, glossy foliage and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom repeatedly from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for coastal areas due to its salt tolerance.

Rosa × pervirens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
