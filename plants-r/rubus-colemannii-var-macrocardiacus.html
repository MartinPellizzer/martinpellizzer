Rubus colemannii var. macrocardiacus

Rubus colemannii var. macrocardiacus, commonly known as the large-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, dark purple to black berries that are typically more robust than those of other Rubus species. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and grows as a shrub with thorny stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit develops from the fertilized flower buds.

This variety is valued for its flavorful berries, which are used in culinary applications and local foraging.
