Rubus moorei var. leichhardtianus

Rubus moorei var. leichhardtianus, commonly known as the Leichhardt raspberry, is a shrub native to Australia, particularly found in the eastern regions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
