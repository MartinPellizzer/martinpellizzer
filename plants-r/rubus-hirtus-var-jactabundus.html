Rubus hirtus var. jactabundus

Rubus hirtus var. jactabundus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry or jactabundus blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticulture.
