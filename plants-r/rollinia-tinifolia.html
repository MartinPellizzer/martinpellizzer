Rollinia tinifolia

Rollinia tinifolia, commonly known as the red-berried soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces a fruit with a rough, spiky exterior and a soft, white, aromatic pulp. Its fruit is edible and often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Rollinia tinifolia thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
