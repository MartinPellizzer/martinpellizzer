Rosmarinus chilensis

Rosmarinus chilensis, commonly known as Chilean rosemary, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs like mint and oregano. The plant is characterized by its slender, evergreen branches and small, fragrant leaves that emit a pine-like scent. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, blooming in summer, and it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Rosmarinus chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild aromatic properties.
