Rhododendron cephalanthoides

Rhododendron cephalanthoides, commonly known as the mountain rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron cephalanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
