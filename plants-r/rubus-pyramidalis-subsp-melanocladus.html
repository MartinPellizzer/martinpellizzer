Rubus pyramidalis subsp. melanocladus

Rubus pyramidalis subsp.

melanocladus, commonly known as the blackclad raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its arching canes and dense, thorny growth habit.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in the axils of the leaves, followed by red, hairy berries.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.
