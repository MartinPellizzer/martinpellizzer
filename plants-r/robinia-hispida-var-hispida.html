Robinia hispida var. hispida

Robinia hispida var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid robinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant has a deep root system, which helps it thrive in poor soil conditions. It is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
