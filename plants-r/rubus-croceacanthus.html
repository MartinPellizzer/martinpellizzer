Rubus croceacanthus

Rubus croceacanthus, commonly known as the golden thimbleberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white with five petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible fruits.
