Ranunculus pilulatus

Ranunculus pilulatus, commonly known as the pill-shaped buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

Its name refers to the small, pill-like shape of its fruit, which is a dry, dehiscent capsule.
