Rosa mollisolstitialis

Rosa mollisolstitialis, commonly known as the Siberian rose or the mountain rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Siberia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, while its stems are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs.

Rosa mollisolstitialis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
