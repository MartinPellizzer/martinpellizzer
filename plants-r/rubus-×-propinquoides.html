Rubus × propinquoides

Rubus × propinquoides, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry). The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart and slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in wetlands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features include thorny stems, alternate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets.
