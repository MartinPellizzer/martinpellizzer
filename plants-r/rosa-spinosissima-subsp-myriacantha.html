Rosa spinosissima subsp. myriacantha

Rosa spinosissima subsp.

myriacantha, commonly known as the many-thorned rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves.

The plant produces fragrant, pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are rosehips, which are typically red and contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature in coastal and rocky environments.
