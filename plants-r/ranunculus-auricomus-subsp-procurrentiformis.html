Ranunculus auricomus subsp. procurrentiformis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

procurrentiformis, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
