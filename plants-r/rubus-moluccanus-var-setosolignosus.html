Rubus moluccanus var. setosolignosus

Rubus moluccanus var. setosolignosus, commonly known as the Moluccan blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark, edible berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are usually compound, with serrated edges, and the flowers are small and white or pale pink.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and fruit production in suitable climates.
