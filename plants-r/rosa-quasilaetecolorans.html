Rosa quasilaetecolorans

Rosa quasilaetecolorans, commonly known as the nearly cheerful colorant rose, is a rare and unique species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multi-colored petals that shift hues depending on light conditions, a trait known as iridescence. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained, loamy soil. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
