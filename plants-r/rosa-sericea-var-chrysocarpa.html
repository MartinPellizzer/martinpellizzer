Rosa sericea var. chrysocarpa

Rosa sericea var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden-fruited silverbark rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces distinctive yellow-orange hips, which are edible and often used in culinary or medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and showy fruit, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
