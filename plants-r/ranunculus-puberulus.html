Ranunculus puberulus

Ranunculus puberulus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ranunculus puberulus is often found in damp soils and can spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies.
