Ratzeburgia pulcherrima

Ratzeburgia pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden torch cactus, is a striking epiphytic cactus native to Mexico.

It features a columnar stem adorned with bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has a spiny, segmented stem and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
