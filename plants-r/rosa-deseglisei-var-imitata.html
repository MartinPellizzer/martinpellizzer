Rosa deseglisei var. imitata

Rosa deseglisei var. imitata, commonly known as the imitate rose, is a compact shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense, thornless stems and small, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to coastal and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in gardens with care.
