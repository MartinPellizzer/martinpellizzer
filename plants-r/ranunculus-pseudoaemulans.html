Ranunculus pseudoaemulans

Ranunculus pseudoaemulans, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, leafy stem. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early summer.

Ranunculus pseudoaemulans is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in regions with limited soil nutrients.
