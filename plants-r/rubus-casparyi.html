Rubus casparyi

Rubus casparyi, commonly known as Caspian raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it has been introduced to other parts of the world.
