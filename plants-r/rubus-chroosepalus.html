Rubus chroosepalus

Rubus chroosepalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically narrow and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in woodland edges and moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
