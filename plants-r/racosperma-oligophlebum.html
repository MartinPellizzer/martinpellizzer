Racosperma oligophlebum

Racosperma oligophlebum, commonly known as the slender acacia, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
