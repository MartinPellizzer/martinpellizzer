Rupicapnos erosa

Rupicapnos erosa, commonly known as the slender rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it produces pink or red flowers in the spring.

Rupicapnos erosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
