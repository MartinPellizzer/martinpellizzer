Rosa bergiana var. lepidina

Rosa bergiana var. lepidina, commonly known as the Himalayan rose, is a perennial shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a leathery texture. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental species valued in horticulture.
