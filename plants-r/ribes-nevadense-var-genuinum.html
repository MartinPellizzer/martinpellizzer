Ribes nevadense var. genuinum

Ribes nevadense var. genuinum, commonly known as the Nevada gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly found in temperate regions. The plant produces small, yellow or orange berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or arid environments.

Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring.
