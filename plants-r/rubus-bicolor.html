Rubus bicolor

Rubus bicolor, commonly known as the twin-colored blackberry, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its bi-colored fruits, which typically have a red upper surface and a black lower surface. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils.

Rubus bicolor is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
