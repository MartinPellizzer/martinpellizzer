Rubus thyrsiflorus var. echinocalyx

Rubus thyrsiflorus var. echinocalyx, commonly known as the echinocalyx raspberry, is a variant of the thrysoflorus raspberry native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its erect growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature, with a slightly acidic flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
