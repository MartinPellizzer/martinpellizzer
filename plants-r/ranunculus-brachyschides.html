Ranunculus brachyschides

Ranunculus brachyschides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers open in early spring and are pollinated by insects.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
