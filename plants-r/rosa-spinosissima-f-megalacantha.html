Rosa spinosissima f. megalacantha

Rosa spinosissima f. megalacantha, commonly known as the giant thorned rose, is a horticultural form of the Rosa spinosissima species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant features dense, spiny foliage and thorny stems, which are typical of the Rosa spinosissima genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the study of rose hybridization.
