Rubus leucanthemus var. acutidens

Rubus leucanthemus var. acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-leaved blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, and is often found in the understory of mixed hardwood forests.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
