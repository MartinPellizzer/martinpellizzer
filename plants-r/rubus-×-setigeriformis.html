Rubus × setigeriformis

Rubus × setigeriformis, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly the Himalayas, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that range in color from red to black.

The botanical features of Rubus × setigeriformis make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
