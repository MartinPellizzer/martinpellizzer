Rapuntium krebsianum

Rapuntium krebsianum, commonly known as the Krebsian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a cluster of small, greenish flowers in early spring.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
