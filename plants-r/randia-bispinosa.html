Randia bispinosa

Randia bispinosa, commonly known as the two-spined randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with prominent veins and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Randia bispinosa is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
