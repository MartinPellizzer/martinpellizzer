Ritterocactus crassigibbus

Ritterocactus crassigibbus, commonly known as the thick-stemmed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collections and desert gardens.
