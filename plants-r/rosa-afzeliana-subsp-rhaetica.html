Rosa afzeliana subsp. rhaetica

Rosa afzeliana subsp.

rhaetica, commonly known as the Rhaetian rose, is a shrub native to the Alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, leathery leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited to rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
