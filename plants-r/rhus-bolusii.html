Rhus bolusii

Rhus bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus wild apricot, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients and often used in traditional medicine.
