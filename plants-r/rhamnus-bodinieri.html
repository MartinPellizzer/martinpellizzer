Rhamnus bodinieri

Rhamnus bodinieri, commonly known as the Chinese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
