Rudolfiella picta

Rudolfiella picta, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its bell-shaped, brightly colored flowers that range from blue to violet, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Rudolfiella picta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in full sun to partial shade.
