Rubus nutkanus

Rubus nutkanus, commonly known as the salmonberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces bright orange-red berries that are edible and often used in jams and pies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rubus nutkanus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
