Rhynchosia teixeirae

Rhynchosia teixeirae, commonly known as the Teixeira's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine due to their nutritional and potential therapeutic properties.

Rhynchosia teixeirae plays a role in agricultural systems by improving soil fertility through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
