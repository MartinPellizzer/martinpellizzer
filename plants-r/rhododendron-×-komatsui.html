Rhododendron × komatsui

Rhododendron × komatsui, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring with flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Rhododendron × komatsui is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
