Rudgea quisquiliae

Rudgea quisquiliae, commonly known as the golden torch, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescence, which resembles a torch, and its upright, branching structure. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Rudgea quisquiliae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
