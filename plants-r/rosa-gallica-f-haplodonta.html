Rosa gallica f. haplodonta

Rosa gallica f. haplodonta, commonly known as the pale pink garden rose, is a hybrid form of the Gallica rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in clusters and have a slightly fragrant scent. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
