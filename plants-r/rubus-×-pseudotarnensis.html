Rubus × pseudotarnensis

Rubus × pseudotarnensis, commonly known as the European blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
