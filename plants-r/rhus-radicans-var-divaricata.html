Rhus radicans var. divaricata

Rhus radicans var. divaricata, commonly known as the staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that turn bright red in the fall, and its distinctive, fuzzy, spiny fruits that mature to a reddish color. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
