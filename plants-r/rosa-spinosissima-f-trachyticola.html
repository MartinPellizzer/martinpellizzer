Rosa spinosissima f. trachyticola

Rosa spinosissima f. trachyticola, commonly known as the trachyticola form of the spinous rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its unique foliage and floral structure.

This form exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to the typical Rosa spinosissima, with narrower, more rigid leaves. Its flowers are usually smaller and more numerous, often displaying a pale pink to white hue. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
