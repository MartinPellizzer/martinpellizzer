Rubus multispinus

Rubus multispinus, commonly known as the many-spined blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, often in woodland edges or disturbed soils. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter flavor.
