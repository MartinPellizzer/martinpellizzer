Ribes moupinense f. incisoserratum

Ribes moupinense f. incisoserratum, commonly known as the Mupin gooseberry, is a variant of the Ribes genus native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically edible. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
