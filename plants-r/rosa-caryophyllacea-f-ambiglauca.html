Rosa caryophyllacea f. ambiglauca

Rosa caryophyllacea f. ambiglauca, commonly known as the pale-blue rose, is a cultivar of the species Rosa caryophyllacea.

It is characterized by its pale blue or lavender-colored flowers, which are typically semi-double in form. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and ornamental value in horticulture.
