Ranunculus emporitanus

Ranunculus emporitanus, commonly known as the Emporite buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic species. The plant is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
