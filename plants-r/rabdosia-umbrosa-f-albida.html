Rabdosia umbrosa f. albida

Rabdosia umbrosa f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered rabdosia, is a variant of the species Rabdosia umbrosa, characterized by its pale to white floral coloration.

It is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia, typically found in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

The f. albida form is notable for its distinct coloration, making it a desirable ornamental plant in horticulture.
