Rumex nematopodus

Rumex nematopodus, commonly known as narrowleaf sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a serrated margin and a reddish coloration on the underside.

The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and produce tiny, three-lobed fruits.
