Rubus hedycarpus subsp. dynatos

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

dynatos, commonly known as the Dynatos blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black fruits.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The subspecies is notable for its edible berries, which are used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
