Rosa incerta var. typica

Rosa incerta var. typica, commonly known as the uncertain rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
