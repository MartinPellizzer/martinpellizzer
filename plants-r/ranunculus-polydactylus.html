Ranunculus polydactylus

Ranunculus polydactylus, commonly known as the many-fingered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals that resemble small fingers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ranunculus polydactylus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.
