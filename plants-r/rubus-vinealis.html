Rubus vinealis

Rubus vinealis, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features trailing or climbing stems with thorns and compound leaves that are typically silvery-white underneath. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
