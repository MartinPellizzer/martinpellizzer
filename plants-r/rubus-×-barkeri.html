Rubus × barkeri

Rubus × barkeri, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are usually dark purple to black in color, containing small, seed-like drupes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
