Ranunculus appropinquans

Ranunculus appropinquans, commonly known as the near relative buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting central blotch. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its toxic properties due to the presence of protoanemonin.
