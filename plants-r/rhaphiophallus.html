Rhaphiophallus

Rhaphiophallus, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family and is characterized by its delicate, ethereal appearance, with pale green or white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, making it highly dependent on humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Rhaphiophallus is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
