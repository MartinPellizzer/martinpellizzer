Rosa × collocata

Rosa × collocata, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its ability to produce flowers throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking continuous bloom. The plant typically features dark green, leathery leaves and produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white. It is hardy and adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates.

Rosa × collocata is often used in landscaping and as a cut flower due to its ornamental value and resilience.
