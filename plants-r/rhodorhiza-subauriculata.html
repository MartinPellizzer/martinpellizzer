Rhodorhiza subauriculata

Rhodorhiza subauriculata, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, erect stem and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces pendulous, yellowish-orange flowers that resemble small bells, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique floral structure and coloration make it a visually striking species within the orchid family.
