Rosa machailensis

Rosa machailensis, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorned stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and leaves, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Rosa machailensis is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler, high-altitude environments.
