Rubus decussatiformis

Rubus decussatiformis, commonly known as the cross-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cross-shaped leaflets that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
