Ranunculus pantothrix var. caespitosus

Ranunculus pantothrix var. caespitosus, commonly known as the tufted buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, glandular surface. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
