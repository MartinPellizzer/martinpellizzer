Rhamnella

Rhamnella, commonly known as the common buckthorn, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rhamnaceae.

It typically features small, often spiny leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland and shrubland habitats. Its fruits are small, dark berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Rhamnella species are also valued for their ornamental qualities and are sometimes used in landscaping.
