Relbunium microphyllum

Relbunium microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved relbunium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland habitats and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Relbunium microphyllum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of local flora and ethnobotany.
