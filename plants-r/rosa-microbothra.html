Rosa microbothra

Rosa microbothra, commonly known as the Japanese rose or Shirobana, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its botanical features include simple, pinnately veined leaves and numerous stamens in its showy, fragrant flowers.

Rosa microbothra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
