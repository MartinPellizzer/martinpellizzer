Rosa ampullicarpa

Rosa ampullicarpa, commonly known as the giant rose or the Brazilian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring woody stems and large, showy flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a complex root system and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Rosa ampullicarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
