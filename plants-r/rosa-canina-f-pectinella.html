Rosa canina f. pectinella

Rosa canina f. pectinella, commonly known as the bristly dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its dense, bristly hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red and contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges across Europe.
