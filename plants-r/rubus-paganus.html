Rubus paganus

Rubus paganus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of soil types. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and they are followed by dark purple to black berries that are edible.

Rubus paganus is often used in culinary applications and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
