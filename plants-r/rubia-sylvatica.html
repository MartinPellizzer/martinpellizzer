Rubia sylvatica

Rubia sylvatica, commonly known as wood rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive yellowish-white underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries.

Rubia sylvatica is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
