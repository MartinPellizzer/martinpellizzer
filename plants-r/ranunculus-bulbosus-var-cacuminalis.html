Ranunculus bulbosus var. cacuminalis

Ranunculus bulbosus var. cacuminalis, commonly known as the Mexican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
