Rosa villosa var. caerulea

Rosa villosa var. caerulea, commonly known as the blue fuzzy rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy gray-green foliage and clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of 1 to 3 meters, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is relatively hardy and drought-tolerant, making it suitable for a variety of garden conditions.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate and ethereal appearance to the landscape.
