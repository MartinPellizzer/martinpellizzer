Rosa × weilleri

Rosa × weilleri, commonly known as the Weilleri rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is a shrub with dense, thorned stems and is native to China. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to fully double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, glossy leaves that are oval in shape and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa × weilleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy, adapting well to a variety of soil conditions.
