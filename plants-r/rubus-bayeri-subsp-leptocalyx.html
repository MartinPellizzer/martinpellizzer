Rubus bayeri subsp. leptocalyx

Rubus bayeri subsp.

leptocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with sharply pointed leaflets.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple or black aggregate berry. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, often growing in open woodlands or along slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
