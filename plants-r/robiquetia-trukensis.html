Robiquetia trukensis

Robiquetia trukensis, commonly known as the Truk Island bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caroline Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid conditions. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
