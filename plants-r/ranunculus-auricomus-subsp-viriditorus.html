Ranunculus auricomus subsp. viriditorus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

viriditorus, commonly known as the greenleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, palmate leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent, spurred sepals, often with a greenish tinge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
