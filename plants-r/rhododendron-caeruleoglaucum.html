Rhododendron caeruleoglaucum

Rhododendron caeruleoglaucum, commonly known as the blue-gray rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that displays a distinctive blue-gray hue on the upper surface. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
