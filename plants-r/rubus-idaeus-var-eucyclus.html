Rubus idaeus var. eucyclus

Rubus idaeus var. eucyclus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves, which are typically dark green and serrated. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into the familiar red, hairy berries. These berries are rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making them a valuable fruit in both culinary and medicinal contexts.

The variety is particularly noted for its superior flavor and firm texture compared to other raspberry types.
