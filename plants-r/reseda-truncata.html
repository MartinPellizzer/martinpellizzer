Reseda truncata

Reseda truncata, commonly known as narrowleaf sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid conditions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar characteristics.

Reseda truncata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
