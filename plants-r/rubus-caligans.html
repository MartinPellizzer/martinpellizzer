Rubus caligans

Rubus caligans, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a wild plant in ecological restoration projects.
