Rubus nutkanus var. heteradenius

Rubus nutkanus var. heteradenius, commonly known as the Alaska raspberry, is a species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and stream valleys.

The plant has been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.
