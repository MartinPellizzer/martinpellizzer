Rubus flavescens

Rubus flavescens, commonly known as the yellowberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red, juicy berries. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
