Racosperma paraneurum

Racosperma paraneurum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattled gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits.

It is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is adapted to tolerate drought and nutrient-poor soils.
