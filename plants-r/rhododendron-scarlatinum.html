Rhododendron scarlatinum

Rhododendron scarlatinum, commonly known as the scarlet azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron scarlatinum is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and natural landscapes.
