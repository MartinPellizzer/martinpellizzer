Rubus × semidisjectus

Rubus × semidisjectus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically has arching stems that grow close to the ground, and its leaves are trifoliate with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pale yellow, and the fruit is a aggregate of small drupes that can range in color from red to black.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its edible berries.
