Ribes rubrum var. smithianum

Ribes rubrum var. smithianum, commonly known as Smith's red currant, is a variant of the red currant plant native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring arching branches and dense clusters of small, red berries. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges and a glossy green upper surface. The flowers are clustered in racemes and are typically pink to white in color, appearing before the leaves in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its berries and foliage.
