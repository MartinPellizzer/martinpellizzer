Rhododendron nivale subsp. nivale

Rhododendron nivale subsp.

nivale, commonly known as the snow rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in cold, alpine environments.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh mountain conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.
