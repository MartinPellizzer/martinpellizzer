Rosa villosa f. adenocaulis

Rosa villosa f. adenocaulis, commonly known as the woolly rose or the hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa villosa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant pink to white flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This cultivar is often valued for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature in garden settings.
