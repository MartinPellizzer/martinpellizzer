Rubus semitomentosus var. peracanthiflorus

Rubus semitomentosus var. peracanthiflorus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
