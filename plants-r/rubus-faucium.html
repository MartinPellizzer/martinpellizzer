Rubus faucium

Rubus faucium, commonly known as the goldenberry raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It produces small, bright orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its unique flavor and nutritional value, it is gaining interest for cultivation and culinary use.
