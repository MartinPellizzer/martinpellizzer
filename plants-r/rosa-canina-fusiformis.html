Rosa canina fusiformis

Rosa canina fusiformis, commonly known as the spindle-shaped dog rose, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, spindle-shaped stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces red, hairy berries that are rich in vitamin C and are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant is typically found in woodland edges and scrublands.

Rosa canina fusiformis is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
