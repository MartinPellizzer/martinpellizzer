Rosa mastoidea

Rosa mastoidea, commonly known as the broad-leafed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, typically found in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its thorny stems and aromatic foliage make it a distinctive member of the rose genus.

Rosa mastoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
