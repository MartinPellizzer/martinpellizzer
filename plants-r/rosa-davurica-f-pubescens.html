Rosa davurica f. pubescens

Rosa davurica f. pubescens, commonly known as the Siberian shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features thorned stems and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered with fine, velvety hairs.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in colder climates.
