Restio rigidus

Restio rigidus, commonly known as the rigid restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in coastal or mountainous regions. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
