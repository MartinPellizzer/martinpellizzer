Rubus tereticaulis

Rubus tereticaulis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rubus tereticaulis is often found in disturbed areas, such as along roadsides and in open woodlands.
