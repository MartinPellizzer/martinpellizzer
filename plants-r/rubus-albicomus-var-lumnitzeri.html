Rubus albicomus var. lumnitzeri

Rubus albicomus var. lumnitzeri, commonly known as the white-haired raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
