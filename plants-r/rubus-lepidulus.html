Rubus lepidulus

Rubus lepidulus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
