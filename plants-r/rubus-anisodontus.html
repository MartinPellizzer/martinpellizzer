Rubus anisodontus

Rubus anisodontus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant produces small, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and is often found in moist, shaded habitats. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is a member of the Rosaceae family.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter taste.
