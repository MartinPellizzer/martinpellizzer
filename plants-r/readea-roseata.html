Readea roseata

Readea roseata, commonly known as the roseate readea, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are broad and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating.

Readea roseata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
