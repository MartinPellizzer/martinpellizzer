Rubia peregrina subsp. peregrina

Rubia peregrina subsp.

peregrina, commonly known as the wandering beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias.

The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, clustered in umbels, and bloom in early summer.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
