Rhus outeniquensis

Rhus outeniquensis, commonly known as the Cape beaumontia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually alternate and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Rhus outeniquensis thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the fynbos biome.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
