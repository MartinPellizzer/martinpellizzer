Rubus turritus

Rubus turritus, commonly known as the tall raspberry or thimbleberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect or shrub-like growth habit and produces edible, red berries that are similar in taste to raspberries. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

Rubus turritus is an important food source for various wildlife species and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
