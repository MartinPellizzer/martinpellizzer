Ranunculus pedemontanus

Ranunculus pedemontanus, commonly known as the Piedmont buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Ranunculus pedemontanus is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic properties.
