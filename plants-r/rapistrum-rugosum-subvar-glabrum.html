Rapistrum rugosum subvar. glabrum

Rapistrum rugosum subvar. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth wild radish, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of smooth, lance-shaped leaves and flowering stems that bear small, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, white seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
