Racosperma gilesianum

Racosperma gilesianum, commonly known as the mountain wattle, is a shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons.

Racosperma gilesianum plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
