Rubus corylifolius var. raduloides

Rubus corylifolius var. raduloides, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, dark green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, edible berries that are typically reddish to black in color. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which can be used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
