Ranunculus serbicus

Ranunculus serbicus, commonly known as the Serbian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has palmately divided leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus serbicus is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
