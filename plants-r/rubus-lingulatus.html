Rubus lingulatus

Rubus lingulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, black berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
