Rubus gremlii proles indotatus

Rubus gremlii proles indotatus, commonly known as the Andean blackberry, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a shrub that produces small, dark berries with a tart flavor. The plant has a woody stem covered in dense, thorny bracts, which provide protection against herbivores. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in cooler, moist environments.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and is also used in traditional medicine.
