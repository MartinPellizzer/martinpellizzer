Rosa potaninii

Rosa potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins.

Rosa potaninii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
