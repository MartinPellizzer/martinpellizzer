Ranunculus hegetschweileri

Ranunculus hegetschweileri, commonly known as the Hegetschweiler's buttercup, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, cup-shaped flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
