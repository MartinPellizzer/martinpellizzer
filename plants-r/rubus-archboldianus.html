Rubus archboldianus

Rubus archboldianus, commonly known as the Archbold blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white and often appear in clusters, followed by dark purple to black berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
