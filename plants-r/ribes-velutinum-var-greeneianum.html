Ribes velutinum var. greeneianum

Ribes velutinum var. greeneianum, commonly known as the Greene's gooseberry, is a variant of the velvet currant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, tart berries in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
