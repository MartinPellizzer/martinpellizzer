Rubus parviflorus f. scopulorum

Rubus parviflorus f. scopulorum, commonly known as the rock raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, pale pink flowers and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the fruits are small, red berries that ripen in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
