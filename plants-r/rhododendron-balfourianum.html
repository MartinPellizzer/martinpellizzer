Rhododendron balfourianum

Rhododendron balfourianum, commonly known as the Balfour's rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its flowers range in color from pink to purple, adding vibrant visual interest to its natural habitat.

Rhododendron balfourianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
