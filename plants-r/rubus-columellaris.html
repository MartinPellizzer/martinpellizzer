Rubus columellaris

Rubus columellaris, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in local culinary traditions.
