Ruellia alata

Ruellia alata, commonly known as the hairy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Ruellia alata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.
