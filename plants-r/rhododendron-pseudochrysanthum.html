Rhododendron pseudochrysanthum

Rhododendron pseudochrysanthum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to China and belongs to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in acidic, well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild and is sometimes grown in conservation programs.
