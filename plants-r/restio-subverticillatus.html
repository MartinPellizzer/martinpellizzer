Restio subverticillatus

Restio subverticillatus, commonly known as the spiral restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
