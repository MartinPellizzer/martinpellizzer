Rubus fallens

Rubus fallens, commonly known as the fall rose or creeping raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
