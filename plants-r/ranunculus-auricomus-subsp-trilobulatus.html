Ranunculus auricomus subsp. trilobulatus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

trilobulatus, commonly known as the three-lobed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to seven petals.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
