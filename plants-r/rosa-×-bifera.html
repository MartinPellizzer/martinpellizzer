Rosa × bifera

Rosa × bifera, commonly known as the double-flowered rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its full, layered blooms that consist of multiple petals, giving it a more elaborate appearance than single-flowered roses. The plant typically has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It produces fragrant flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, often in late spring to early summer.

Rosa × bifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
