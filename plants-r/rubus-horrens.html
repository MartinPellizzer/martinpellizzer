Rubus horrens

Rubus horrens, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing features with other members of the Rubus genus.
