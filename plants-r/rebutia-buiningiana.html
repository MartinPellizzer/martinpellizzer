Rebutia buiningiana

Rebutia buiningiana, commonly known as the Buning's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically modified into spines, a feature common in many desert plants.

Rebutia buiningiana is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive periods of drought.
