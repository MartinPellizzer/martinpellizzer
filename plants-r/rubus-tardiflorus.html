Rubus tardiflorus

Rubus tardiflorus, commonly known as the late-flowering blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom late in the growing season, typically in late summer. Its fruit, a small, dark berry, is edible but often has a tart flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
