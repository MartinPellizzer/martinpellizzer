Ranunculus aurasiacus var. djurdjurae

Ranunculus aurasiacus var. djurdjurae, commonly known as the Djurdjura ranunculus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silky, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
