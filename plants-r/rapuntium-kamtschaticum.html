Rapuntium kamtschaticum

Rapuntium kamtschaticum, commonly known as the Caucasian buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
