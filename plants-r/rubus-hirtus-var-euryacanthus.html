Rubus hirtus var. euryacanthus

Rubus hirtus var. euryacanthus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red but can also be yellow or golden in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and is commonly cultivated for its flavorful berries.
