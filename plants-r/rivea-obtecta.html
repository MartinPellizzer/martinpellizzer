Rivea obtecta

Rivea obtecta, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.

Rivea obtecta is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
