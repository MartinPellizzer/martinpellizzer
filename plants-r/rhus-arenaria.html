Rhus arenaria

Rhus arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Rhus arenaria is drought-tolerant and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
