Rosa stylosa var. ptychophylla

Rosa stylosa var. ptychophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wild rose, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often densely arranged. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in moist, sunny habitats.
