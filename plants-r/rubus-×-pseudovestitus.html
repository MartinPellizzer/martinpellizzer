Rubus × pseudovestitus

Rubus × pseudovestitus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to moist, temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

The berries are usually dark purple to black when ripe, and are often used in culinary applications.
