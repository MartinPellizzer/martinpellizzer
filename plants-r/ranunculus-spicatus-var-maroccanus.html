Ranunculus spicatus var. maroccanus

Ranunculus spicatus var. maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
