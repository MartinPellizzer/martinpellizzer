Ranunculus occidentalis var. laevicaulis

Ranunculus occidentalis var. laevicaulis, commonly known as the western buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly rounded leaf stems, setting it apart from other ranunculus species.
