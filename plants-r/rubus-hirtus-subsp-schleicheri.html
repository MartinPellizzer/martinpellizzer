Rubus hirtus subsp. schleicheri

Rubus hirtus subsp.

schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The plant typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated margins, and the flowers are pink to white, appearing in early summer.

The fruit, a red raspberry-like aggregate, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.
