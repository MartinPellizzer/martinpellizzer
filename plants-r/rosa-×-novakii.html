Rosa × novakii

Rosa × novakii, commonly known as the Novak's rose, is a hybrid species originating from crosses between Rosa bracteata and Rosa rugosa.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa × novakii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
