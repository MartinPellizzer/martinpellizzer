Ranunculus procurrentidens

Ranunculus procurrentidens, commonly known as the procurrentidens buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, palmate leaves and bright yellow petals that typically number between 5 to 15. The plant is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some habitats.
