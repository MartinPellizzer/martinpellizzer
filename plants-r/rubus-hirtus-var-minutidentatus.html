Rubus hirtus var. minutidentatus

Rubus hirtus var. minutidentatus, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, erect stems with dense, prickly hairs covering its foliage. The leaves are trifoliate, with finely toothed margins that give it its specific epithet *minutidentatus*. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces small, edible berries that ripen in the summer.

This plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
