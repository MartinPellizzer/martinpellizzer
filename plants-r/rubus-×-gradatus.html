Rubus × gradatus

Rubus × gradatus, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically produces large, dark purple to black berries that are prized for their flavor and culinary use. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its fruit.
