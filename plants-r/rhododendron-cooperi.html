Rhododendron cooperi

Rhododendron cooperi, commonly known as the Catawba rhododendron, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, broad leaves and showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its botanical features include woody stems and a compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Rhododendron cooperi is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ability to enhance the aesthetic appeal of gardens.
