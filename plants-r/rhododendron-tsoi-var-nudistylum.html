Rhododendron tsoi var. nudistylum

Rhododendron tsoi var. nudistylum, commonly known as the naked-stemmed rhododendron, is a unique variant of the rhododendron species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems that are often devoid of leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
