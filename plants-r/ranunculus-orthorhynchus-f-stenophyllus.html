Ranunculus orthorhynchus f. stenophyllus

Ranunculus orthorhynchus f. stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a botanical variant of the broader Ranunculus orthorhynchus species.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers with five petals. This form is typically found in moist, grassy areas and is adapted to temperate climates. The plant belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many herbaceous species.

Its botanical significance lies in its morphological distinctiveness and ecological role in its native habitats.
