Rosa suecica

Rosa suecica, commonly known as the Swedish rose, is a species of shrub native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems. Rosa suecica is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
