Rubus pseudoacer

Rubus pseudoacer, commonly known as the false blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in woodland areas and often grows along the edges of forests.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
