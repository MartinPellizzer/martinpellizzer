Rubus cantianus

Rubus cantianus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. These berries are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

Rubus cantianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
