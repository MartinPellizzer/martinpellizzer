Rosmarinus tournefortii

Rosmarinus tournefortii, commonly known as Greek rosemary or turpentine rosemary, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small blue or purple flowers. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Rosmarinus tournefortii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
