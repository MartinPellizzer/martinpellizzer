Rosa californica var. pubescens

Rosa californica var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent California rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features arching stems with thorns and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring. The leaves are compound, with ovate to elliptic leaflets that have a soft, fuzzy texture. This variety is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
