Rosa glauca subvar. martinii

Rosa glauca subvar. martinii, commonly known as the Martin's bluebush rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The foliage is typically covered in a fine, silvery-blue down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
