Rubus riddelsdellii

Rubus riddelsdellii, commonly known as the Riddelsdell blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in shrubland and woodland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
