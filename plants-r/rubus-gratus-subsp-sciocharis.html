Rubus gratus subsp. sciocharis

Rubus gratus subsp.

sciocharis, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark purple to black fruits.

The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and has a dense, thorny structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The berries are typically consumed by birds and small mammals, aiding in seed dispersal.
