Reseda macrosperma var. anatolica

Reseda macrosperma var. anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian sainfoin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Anatolian sainfoin is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
