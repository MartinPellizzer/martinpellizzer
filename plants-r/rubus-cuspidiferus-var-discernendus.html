Rubus cuspidiferus var. discernendus

Rubus cuspidiferus var. discernendus, commonly known as the eastern dewberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by edible, red berries in the fall. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
