Randia pycnantha var. grandiflora

Randia pycnantha var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered randia, is a species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals, and bloom in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, round berries that are often red when mature.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
