Rubus tereticaulis var. retezaticolus

Rubus tereticaulis var. retezaticolus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include a woody structure, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
