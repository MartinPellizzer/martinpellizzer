Ranunculus propinquus subsp. subangustifidus

Ranunculus propinquus subsp.

subangustifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive green, hairy center.

The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.
