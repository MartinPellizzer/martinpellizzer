Ranula platanifolia

Ranula platanifolia, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
