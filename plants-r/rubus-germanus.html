Rubus germanus

Rubus germanus, commonly known as the European blackberry.

It is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.
