Rubus arcticus subsp. humilis

Rubus arcticus subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the Arctic raspberry, is a low-growing shrub native to arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its small, usually pink or white flowers and edible, red berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges, adapted to withstand cold climates. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in colder regions.
