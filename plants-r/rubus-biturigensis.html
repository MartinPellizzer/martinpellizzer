Rubus biturigensis

Rubus biturigensis, commonly known as the Tasmanian blackberry, is a shrub native to Tasmania, Australia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically consumed raw or used in jams and desserts. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its unique flavor and ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for both culinary and horticultural purposes.
