Ribes occidentale var. hesperium

Ribes occidentale var. hesperium, commonly known as the western gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shady areas such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink or white. The berries, which are edible, are small and tart, often used in jams and jellies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
