Rosa racemosa

Rosa racemosa, commonly known as the clustered rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color. Rosa racemosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
