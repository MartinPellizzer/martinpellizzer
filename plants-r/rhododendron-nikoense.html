Rhododendron nikoense

Rhododendron nikoense, commonly known as the Nikko rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to white, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron nikoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
