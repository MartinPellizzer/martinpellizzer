Rubus cuspidiferus subsp. megacladus

Rubus cuspidiferus subsp.

megacladus, commonly known as the Appalachian blackberry, is a subspecies native to the eastern United States. It is a thorny shrub that produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is often used in jams and pies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more robust growth compared to other Rubus species.
