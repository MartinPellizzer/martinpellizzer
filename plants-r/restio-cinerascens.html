Restio cinerascens

Restio cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Restio cinerascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
