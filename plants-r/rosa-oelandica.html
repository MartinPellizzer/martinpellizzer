Rosa oelandica

Rosa oelandica, commonly known as the Oeland rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its simple, often thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodland or grassland habitats and produces pink to red flowers in the summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Rosa oelandica is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and as a source of cut flowers.
