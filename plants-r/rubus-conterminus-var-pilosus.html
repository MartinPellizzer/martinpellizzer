Rubus conterminus var. pilosus

Rubus conterminus var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in local ecosystems.
