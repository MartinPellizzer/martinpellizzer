Rosa communis var. obovoidea

Rosa communis var. obovoidea, commonly known as the oval-leaf rose, is a variety of the common rose characterized by its distinctive elliptical or oval-shaped leaves.

This plant typically features fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The foliage is usually medium green, and the stems are covered in thorns.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
