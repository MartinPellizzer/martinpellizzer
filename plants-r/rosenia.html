Rosenia

Rosenia, commonly known as the rose of Jericho, is a unique succulent plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It has a distinctive appearance, with fleshy, branching stems that resemble a rose in bloom when fully hydrated. The plant is known for its ability to survive extreme drought conditions by curling into a tight ball, which helps it retain moisture. When watered, it rapidly unfurls, giving the illusion of blooming again.

This remarkable resilience makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens and as a symbol of rebirth and perseverance.
