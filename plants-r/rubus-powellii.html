Rubus powellii

Rubus powellii, commonly known as Powell's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Powell's blackberry is an important plant for both wildlife and human use in its native habitat.
