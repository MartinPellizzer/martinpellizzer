Rhododendron flumineum

Rhododendron flumineum, commonly known as the river rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and mountain valleys. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are usually pink or reddish-purple, appearing in clusters during the spring.

It has a relatively compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
