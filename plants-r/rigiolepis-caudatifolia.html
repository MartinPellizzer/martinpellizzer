Rigiolepis caudatifolia

Rigiolepis caudatifolia, commonly known as the cat-tail fern, is a unique plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that resemble a cat's tail, giving it its common name. The plant produces a tall, slender flower stalk that bears small, greenish-white flowers. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in humid environments.

Its botanical structure is adapted for water retention, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
