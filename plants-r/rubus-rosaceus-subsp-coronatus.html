Rubus rosaceus subsp. coronatus

Rubus rosaceus subsp.

coronatus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible red berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
