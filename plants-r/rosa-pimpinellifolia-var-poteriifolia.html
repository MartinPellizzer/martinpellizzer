Rosa pimpinellifolia var. poteriifolia

Rosa pimpinellifolia var. poteriifolia, commonly known as the creeping pink rose, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with small, aromatic leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy ornamental plant in gardens.
