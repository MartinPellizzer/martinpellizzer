Rhamnus infectoria f. intermedia

Rhamnus infectoria f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate buckthorn, is a shrub belonging to the family Rhamnaceae.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by black, egg-shaped berries. It is typically found in woodland edges and moist soils, thriving in temperate climates.

Its berries are a food source for various birds and small mammals.
