Rosa aristata

Rosa aristata, commonly known as the aristate rose, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant features thorny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer and are followed by small, red-orange hips.

Rosa aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
