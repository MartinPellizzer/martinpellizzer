Rubus fassettii

Rubus fassettii, commonly known as the Fassett's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that typically ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The botanical features of Rubus fassettii make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both its ecological role and potential horticultural use.
