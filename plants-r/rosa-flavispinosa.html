Rosa flavispinosa

Rosa flavispinosa, commonly known as the yellow-spined rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and thorny stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and its fragrance is often described as sweet and subtle.

Rosa flavispinosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
