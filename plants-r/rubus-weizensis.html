Rubus weizensis

Rubus weizensis, commonly known as the Weizen's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the temperate regions of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark purple berries. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets. Its flowers are white with five petals and appear in the spring, followed by the development of its edible fruits in the summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
