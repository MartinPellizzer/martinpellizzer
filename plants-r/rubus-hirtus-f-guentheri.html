Rubus hirtus f. guentheri

Rubus hirtus f. guentheri, commonly known as the Guenther's bramble, is a variant of the common bramble, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showcasing compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus.

This form is notable for its horticultural interest and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
