Rubus rechingerorum

Rubus rechingerorum, commonly known as the Rechinger's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It is native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia and is adapted to cold, mountainous environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
