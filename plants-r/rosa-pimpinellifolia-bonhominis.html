Rosa pimpinellifolia bonhominis

Rosa pimpinellifolia bonhominis, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, fragrant pink or white flowers, and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces oval, red-orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its historical use in hedging and as a symbol of love and devotion adds to its cultural significance.
