Rosa canina subvar. pseudostylosa

Rosa canina subvar. pseudostylosa, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces bright orange to red hips, which are edible and rich in vitamin C. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
