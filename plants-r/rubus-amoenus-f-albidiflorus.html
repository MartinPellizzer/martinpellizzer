Rubus amoenus f. albidiflorus

Rubus amoenus f. albidiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered beautyberry, is a botanical variant of the common beautyberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to parts of North America, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking floral display and autumn foliage.
