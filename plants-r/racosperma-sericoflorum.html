Racosperma sericoflorum

Racosperma sericoflorum, commonly known as the silky-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, silky-haired inflorescences. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Racosperma sericoflorum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
