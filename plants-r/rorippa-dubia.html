Rorippa dubia

Rorippa dubia, commonly known as the yellow water-cress, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It typically grows in shallow water, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the summer months.

Rorippa dubia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist environments.
