Romulea bulbocodioides var. ambigua

Romulea bulbocodioides var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive, grass-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its bulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive dry periods.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
