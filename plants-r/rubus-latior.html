Rubus latior

Rubus latior, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
