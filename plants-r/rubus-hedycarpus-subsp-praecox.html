Rubus hedycarpus subsp. praecox

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the early blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark red berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
