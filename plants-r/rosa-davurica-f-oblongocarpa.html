Rosa davurica f. oblongocarpa

Rosa davurica f. oblongocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems.

It features simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins, and its flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant produces oval, woody fruits known as hips, which are distinctive for their elongated shape and persistent calyx.

It is native to regions of China and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
