Rosa pomifera subvar. umbratica

Rosa pomifera subvar. umbratica, commonly known as the shadow rose, is a rare and unique subvariety of the rose family.

It is characterized by its large, edible fruits that resemble small, fuzzy tomatoes. The plant thrives in shaded environments, making it well-suited for woodland or forested areas. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are usually pale pink or white, adding a delicate aesthetic to its natural habitat.
