Rotheca quadrangulata

Rotheca quadrangulata, commonly known as the four-angle rotheca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its square-shaped stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Rotheca quadrangulata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
