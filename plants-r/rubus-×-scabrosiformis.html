Rubus × scabrosiformis

Rubus × scabrosiformis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for its edible berries, which are often used in jams and desserts.
