Rubus hirtus subvar. iodes

Rubus hirtus subvar. iodes, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subvariety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
