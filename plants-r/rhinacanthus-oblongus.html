Rhinacanthus oblongus

Rhinacanthus oblongus, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its large, showy orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
