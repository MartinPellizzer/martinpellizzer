Rosa apennina

Rosa apennina, commonly known as the Italian rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and an extensive root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Rosa apennina is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in the production of rose oil and perfume.
