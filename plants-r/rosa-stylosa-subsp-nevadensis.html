Rosa stylosa subsp. nevadensis

Rosa stylosa subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra rose, is a shrub-like species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces oval, hairy fruits called rose hips.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
