Ricinus communis f. venosus

Ricinus communis f. venosus, commonly known as the variegated castor bean, is a variant of the castor oil plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are typically large, palmate, and exhibit striking patterns of green, white, and sometimes yellow. The plant is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa. It produces spiny capsules containing toxic seeds, which are used in the production of castor oil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
