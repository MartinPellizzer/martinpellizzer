Rutidosis glandulosa

Rutidosis glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular rutidosis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are often covered in fine, glandular hairs, which give it its scientific name.

Rutidosis glandulosa is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
