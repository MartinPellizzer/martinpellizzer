Rubus ellipticus var. acutifolius

Rubus ellipticus var. acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and a distinctive acuminate tip, contributing to its sharp-leaved characteristic. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local foraging traditions.
