Ruellia maya

Ruellia maya, commonly known as Mayan ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Ruellia maya thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to bloom throughout the growing season adds both visual interest and ecological value to gardens.
