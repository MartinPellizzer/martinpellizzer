Rubus × amplifoliatus var. eubelophorus

Rubus × amplifoliatus var. eubelophorus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland areas and along hedgerows.

Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems that can reach several feet in height.
