Ranunculus tricuspis var. lancifolius

Ranunculus tricuspis var. lancifolius, commonly known as the lanceleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals, which are often mistaken for true petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
