Rubus iodostachys

Rubus iodostachys, commonly known as the iodine-stem blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

Rubus iodostachys is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
