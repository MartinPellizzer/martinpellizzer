Rhizoglossum

Rhizoglossum, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique species of liverwort that exhibits fascinating botanical characteristics.

It is a non-vascular plant, lacking true roots, stems, and leaves, and instead possesses structures called thalli. Rhizoglossum thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on decaying wood or in damp soil. Its reproductive structures, called gametophytes, are responsible for sexual reproduction through the production of male and female gametes.

The plant's ability to survive in nutrient-poor conditions makes it an important indicator species for certain ecosystems.
