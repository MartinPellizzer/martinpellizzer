Ranunculus forrestii

Ranunculus forrestii, commonly known as the Forster's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features a short, hairy stem with palmate leaves that are deeply divided. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to seven sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus forrestii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
