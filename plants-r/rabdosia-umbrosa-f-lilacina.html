Rabdosia umbrosa f. lilacina

Rabdosia umbrosa f. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered betony, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, lilac-pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shade gardens and conservation efforts.
