Rosa gratianopolitana

Rosa gratianopolitana, commonly known as the Grace of Poland rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features thorned stems and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa gratianopolitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
