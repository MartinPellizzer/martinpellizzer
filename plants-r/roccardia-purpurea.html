Roccardia purpurea

Roccardia purpurea, commonly known as the purple roccardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Roccardia purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.

It is relatively low maintenance and can tolerate dry conditions once established.
