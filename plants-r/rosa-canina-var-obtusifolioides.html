Rosa canina var. obtusifolioides

Rosa canina var. obtusifolioides, commonly known as the obtusifoliate dogwood rose, is a variant of the common rose with distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are slightly lobed, giving it a unique appearance compared to standard Rosa canina. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by red, hairy fruits known as hips. It is typically found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
