Rhomboda polygonoides

Rhomboda polygonoides, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact rosette growth habit and velvety, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with a distinctive central spot. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Rhomboda polygonoides is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
