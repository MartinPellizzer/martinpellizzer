Ritterocactus rutilans subsp. veenianus

Ritterocactus rutilans subsp.

veenianus, commonly known as the Veenian torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color, often adorned with spines and areoles.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on the upper part of the stem. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and desert gardeners.
