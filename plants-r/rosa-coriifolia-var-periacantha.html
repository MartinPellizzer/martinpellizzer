Rosa coriifolia var. periacantha

Rosa coriifolia var. periacantha, commonly known as the hairy rose or the Chinese rose, is a variant of the shrub rose that features distinctive botanical characteristics.

It is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to temperate climates.
