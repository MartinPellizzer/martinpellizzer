Rubus × semicurtiglandulosus

Rubus × semicurtiglandulosus, commonly known as the bracted blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It typically grows in temperate regions and has dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and for commercial fruit production.
