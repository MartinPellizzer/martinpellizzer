Rubus × pseudouncinatiformis

Rubus × pseudouncinatiformis, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species, Rubus spp.
