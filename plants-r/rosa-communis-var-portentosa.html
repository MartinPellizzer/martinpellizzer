Rosa communis var. portentosa

Rosa communis var. portentosa, commonly known as the Portentous Rose, is a cultivar renowned for its striking, large, and fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its deep pink to crimson petals that often feature a contrasting white eye. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

This variety is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and as a symbol of romantic love in various cultures.
