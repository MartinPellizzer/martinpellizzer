Rosa campestris var. myriacantha

Rosa campestris var. myriacantha, commonly known as the many-thorned dog rose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
