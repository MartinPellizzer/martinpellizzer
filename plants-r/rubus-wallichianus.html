Rubus wallichianus

Rubus wallichianus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valuable resource in local ecosystems.
