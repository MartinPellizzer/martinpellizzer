Ranunculus petiveri

Ranunculus petiveri, commonly known as the Petiver's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, green fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus petiveri is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.
