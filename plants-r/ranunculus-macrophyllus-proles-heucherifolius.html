Ranunculus macrophyllus proles heucherifolius

Ranunculus macrophyllus proles heucherifolius, commonly known as the heucherifolius buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately divided leaves that resemble those of heuchera, hence its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
