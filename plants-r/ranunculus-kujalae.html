Ranunculus kujalae

Ranunculus kujalae, commonly known as the Kujalae buttercup, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Pakistan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are simple and trifoliate, while its stems are usually hairy.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
