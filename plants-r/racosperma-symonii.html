Racosperma symonii

Racosperma symonii, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silky texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Racosperma symonii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
