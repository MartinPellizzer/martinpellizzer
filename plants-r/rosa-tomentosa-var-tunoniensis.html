Rosa tomentosa var. tunoniensis

Rosa tomentosa var. tunoniensis, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in forested or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
