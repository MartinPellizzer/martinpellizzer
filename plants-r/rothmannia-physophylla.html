Rothmannia physophylla

Rothmannia physophylla, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Rothmannia physophylla thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
