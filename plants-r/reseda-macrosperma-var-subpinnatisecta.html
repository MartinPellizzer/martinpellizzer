Reseda macrosperma var. subpinnatisecta

Reseda macrosperma var. subpinnatisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, subpinnatisect leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
