Rubus iniens

Rubus iniens, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southern China.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, with compound leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its flowers are white with five petals and are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves.

Rubus iniens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
