Roella campestris

Roella campestris, commonly known as the field bean or broad bean, is an annual legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, green pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is well adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Roella campestris plays a significant role in agricultural systems due to its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen.

It is widely cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.
