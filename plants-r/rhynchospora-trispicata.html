Rhynchospora trispicata

Rhynchospora trispicata, commonly known as the three-branched sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, glabrous stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Rhynchospora trispicata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
