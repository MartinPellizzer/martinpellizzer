Ripogonum fawcettianum

Ripogonum fawcettianum, commonly known as the Fawcett's vine, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is a climbing plant with slender, woody stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by tubular, red or orange berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Ripogonum fawcettianum is adapted to humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
