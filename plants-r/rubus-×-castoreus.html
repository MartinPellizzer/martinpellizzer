Rubus × castoreus

Rubus × castoreus, commonly known as the blackberry dewberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it is often mistaken for other Rubus species due to its similar appearance.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in some traditional herbal remedies.
