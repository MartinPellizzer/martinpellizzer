Ribes rubrum var. dulce

Ribes rubrum var. dulce, commonly known as the sweet currant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, red, edible berries that are sweet and juicy, typically ripening in late summer. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The sweet currant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
