Rhododendron scabrum subsp. amanoi

Rhododendron scabrum subsp.

amanoi, commonly known as the Amanoi rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, humid environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a slight pubescence.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
