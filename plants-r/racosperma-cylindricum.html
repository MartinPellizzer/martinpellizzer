Racosperma cylindricum

Racosperma cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Racosperma cylindricum is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
