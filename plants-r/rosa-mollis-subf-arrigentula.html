Rosa mollis subf. arrigentula

Rosa mollis subf.

arrigentula, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a subspecies native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in spring. It is a shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
