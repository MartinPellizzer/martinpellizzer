Rubus weitenweberi

Rubus weitenweberi, commonly known as the Weitenweber's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and blackberries. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its high-altitude environment.
