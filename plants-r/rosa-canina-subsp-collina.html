Rosa canina subsp. collina

Rosa canina subsp.

collina, commonly known as the prickly rose or wild dog rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers.

The plant produces bright red, hairy berries that are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along coastal areas.
