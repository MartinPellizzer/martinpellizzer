Rosa multiflora f. cathayensis

Rosa multiflora f. cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and profuse flowering, with pink to red flowers that bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season. The plant has a shrub-like structure with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its botanical features include small, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant, five-petaled flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
