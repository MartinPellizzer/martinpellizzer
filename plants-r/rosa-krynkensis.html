Rosa krynkensis

Rosa krynkensis, commonly known as the Krynkens rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorned stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on pedicels. Its botanical features include a pinnate leaf structure and a woody, perennial root system.

Rosa krynkensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
