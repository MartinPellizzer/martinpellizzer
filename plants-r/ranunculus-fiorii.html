Ranunculus fiorii

Ranunculus fiorii, commonly known as the Fiord Ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the fiords of New Zealand.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has a short, upright stem and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found near streams or in shaded, rocky areas.

Ranunculus fiorii is notable for its unique floral structure and is considered a rare and endemic species.
