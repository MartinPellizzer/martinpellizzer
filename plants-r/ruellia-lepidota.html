Ruellia lepidota

Ruellia lepidota, commonly known as silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features heart-shaped, silver-velvet leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Ruellia lepidota is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.
