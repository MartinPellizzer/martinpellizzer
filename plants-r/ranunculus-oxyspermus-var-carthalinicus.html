Ranunculus oxyspermus var. carthalinicus

Ranunculus oxyspermus var. carthalinicus, commonly known as the Carpathian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, hairy seeds in a follicle.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
