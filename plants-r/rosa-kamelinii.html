Rosa kamelinii

Rosa kamelinii, commonly known as the Kamelin's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. This shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features thorny stems with pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.
