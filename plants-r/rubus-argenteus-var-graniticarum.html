Rubus argenteus var. graniticarum

Rubus argenteus var. graniticarum, commonly known as the granite raspberry, is a variant of the silverberry plant native to rocky, alpine environments.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has silvery-hairy leaves and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often found in mountainous regions with high sunlight exposure.

Due to its unique habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Rubus genus.
