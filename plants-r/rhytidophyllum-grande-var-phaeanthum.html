Rhytidophyllum grande var. phaeanthum

Rhytidophyllum grande var. phaeanthum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in wetlands and moist woodlands. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun to partial shade and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
