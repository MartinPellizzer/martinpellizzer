Rubus serpens var. erythrostachyoides

Rubus serpens var. erythrostachyoides, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry.

This variant is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for both its ecological role and culinary potential.
