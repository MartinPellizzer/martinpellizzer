Rubus hirtus microgene anisacanthoides

Rubus hirtus microgene anisacanthoides, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like structures called achenes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
