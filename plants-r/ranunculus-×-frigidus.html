Ranunculus × frigidus

Ranunculus × frigidus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features a short, stout stem with palmately divided leaves that have a delicate, fern-like appearance. Its flowers are showy, with five to nine petals in shades of yellow, white, or red, and are often accompanied by numerous stamens.

Ranunculus × frigidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
