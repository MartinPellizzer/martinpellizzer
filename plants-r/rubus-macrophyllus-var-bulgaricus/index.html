Rubus Macrophyllus Var. Bulgaricus

Rubus macrophyllus var. bulgaricus is a unique variant of the common bramble known for its striking foliage and ornamental value. This plant is native to the Balkan region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its large, leathery leaves and clusters of white flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. Despite its beauty, it can be invasive if not properly managed. This article explores its characteristics, care requirements, and ecological impact.
