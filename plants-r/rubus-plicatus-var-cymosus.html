Rubus plicatus var. cymosus

Rubus plicatus var. cymosus, commonly known as the Cymose Raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a bright red color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. It has a sprawling growth habit and can spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.
