Rosa andropogon

Rosa andropogon, commonly known as the Andropogon rose, is a unique hybrid plant that combines characteristics of roses and grasses.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its grass-like foliage and rose-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

Rosa andropogon is often used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
