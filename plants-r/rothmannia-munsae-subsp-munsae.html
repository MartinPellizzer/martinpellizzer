Rothmannia munsae subsp. munsae

Rothmannia munsae subsp.

munsae, commonly known as the Munsae Rothmannia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Africa. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are often woody at maturity.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
