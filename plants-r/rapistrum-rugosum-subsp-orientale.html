Rapistrum rugosum subsp. orientale

Rapistrum rugosum subsp.

orientale, commonly known as the oriental wild radish, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important crops.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb with a branched stem and alternate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in racemes, and it produces spherical, hairy pods containing several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
