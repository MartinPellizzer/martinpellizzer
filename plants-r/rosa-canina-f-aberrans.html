Rosa canina f. aberrans

Rosa canina f. aberrans, commonly known as the wild dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, hairy stems and small, pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and scrublands, preferring well-drained soils.

Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This form is notable for its ecological role in supporting wildlife and its historical use in herbal medicine.
