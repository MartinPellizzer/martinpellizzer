Rubus ulmifolius subsp. vulgatus

Rubus ulmifolius subsp.

vulgatus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and its prickly stems.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
