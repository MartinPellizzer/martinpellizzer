Rhamnus angustifolia

Rhamnus angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches long. Its flowers are greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is characterized by its strong, aromatic odor and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
