Ranunculus pulchellus var. pseudohirculus

Ranunculus pulchellus var. pseudohirculus, commonly known as the false goat's beard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is closely related to other ranunculus species and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
