Rubus treutleri

Rubus treutleri, commonly known as the Treutler's raspberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
