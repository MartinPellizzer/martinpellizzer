Racosperma guinetii

Racosperma guinetii, commonly known as the Guinet's acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring compound leaves and yellow or pale green flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
