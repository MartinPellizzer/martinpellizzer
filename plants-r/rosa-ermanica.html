Rosa ermanica

Rosa ermanica, commonly known as the Eriman rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, doubly serrated leaves and produces rose hip fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It is well adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

Rosa ermanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
