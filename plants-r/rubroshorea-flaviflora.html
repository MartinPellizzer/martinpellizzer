Rubroshorea flaviflora

Rubroshorea flaviflora, commonly known as the golden-flowered redshore, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub, thriving in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture, and it has a woody stem that becomes more robust with age.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
