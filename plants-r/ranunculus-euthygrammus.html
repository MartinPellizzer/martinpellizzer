Ranunculus euthygrammus

Ranunculus euthygrammus, commonly known as the yellow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central yellow stamen, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has palmate leaves divided into three to five lobes, which are usually hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
