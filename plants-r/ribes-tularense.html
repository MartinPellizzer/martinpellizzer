Ribes tularense

Ribes tularense, commonly known as the tularensis gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features small, dark green leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its fruits are edible berries that are often used in jams and jellies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
