Rosa tomentosa var. farinulenta

Rosa tomentosa var. farinulenta, commonly known as the farinulenta rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, grayish-white fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often covered in a fine, silky down, contributing to its unique botanical characteristics.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
