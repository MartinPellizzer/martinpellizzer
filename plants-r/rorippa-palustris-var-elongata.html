Rorippa palustris var. elongata

Rorippa palustris var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated water bladderwort, is a aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are elongated and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

This variety is often found in ponds, ditches, and wet meadows.
