Rhodax pannosus

Rhodax pannosus, commonly known as the hairy rhododendron, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
