Rosa canina var. oxyphylla

Rosa canina var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the Turkish rose or oxyphyll rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets.

The fruits, known as hips, are orange-red and contain a high concentration of vitamin C.
