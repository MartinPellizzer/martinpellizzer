Rubus obtruncatus subsp. horrens

Rubus obtruncatus subsp.

horrens, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

It thrives in woodland and disturbed areas, preferring well-drained soils.
