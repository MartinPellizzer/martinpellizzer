Rhododendron horaeum var. rubens

Rhododendron horaeum var. rubens, commonly known as the red-flowered alpine rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include showy, tubular flowers with five petals and dark green, leathery leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
