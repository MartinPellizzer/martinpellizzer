Rollinia hassleriana var. vestita

Rollinia hassleriana var. vestita, commonly known as the velvet custard apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, velvety, green leaves. The plant produces showy, white flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a fleshy, spiky berry that changes color from green to yellow or red as it matures.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
