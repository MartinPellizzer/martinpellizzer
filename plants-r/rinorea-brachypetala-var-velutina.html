Rinorea brachypetala var. velutina

Rinorea brachypetala var. velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf rinorea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in some horticultural settings.
