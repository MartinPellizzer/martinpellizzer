Ranunculus occidentalis var. lyallii

Ranunculus occidentalis var. lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces elongated, branched stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and wetlands.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
