Rhynchosia cinerea

Rhynchosia cinerea, commonly known as the pale cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow to green flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are used for food and traditional medicine.

Rhynchosia cinerea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
