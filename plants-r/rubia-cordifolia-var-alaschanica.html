Rubia cordifolia var. alaschanica

Rubia cordifolia var. alaschanica, commonly known as the Alashan heartleaf rue.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic benefits.
