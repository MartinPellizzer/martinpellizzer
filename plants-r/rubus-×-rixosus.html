Rubus × rixosus

Rubus × rixosus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a hybrid plant species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a serrated edge.

Rubus × rixosus is widely cultivated for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet-tart flavor.
