Racosperma microcarpum

Racosperma microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Racosperma microcarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
