Rhododendron weyrichii f. purpuriflorum

Rhododendron weyrichii f. purpuriflorum, commonly known as the purple-flowered form of Weyrich's rhododendron, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically a vibrant purple or reddish-purple, with a contrasting white or pale pink base, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its unique coloration and is often used in horticultural collections for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
