Rhododendron brachypodarium

Rhododendron brachypodarium, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron brachypodarium is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
