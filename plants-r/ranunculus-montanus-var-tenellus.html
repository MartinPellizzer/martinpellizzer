Ranunculus montanus var. tenellus

Ranunculus montanus var. tenellus, commonly known as the slender meadow buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Ranunculaceae family.
