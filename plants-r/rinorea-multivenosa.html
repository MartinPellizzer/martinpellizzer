Rinorea multivenosa

Rinorea multivenosa, commonly known as the veined leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent venation patterns that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as a climbing shrub, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, with white petals and a distinctive yellow throat.

Rinorea multivenosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
