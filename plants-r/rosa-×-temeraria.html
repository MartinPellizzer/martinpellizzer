Rosa × temeraria

Rosa × temeraria, commonly known as the "Flore Plena" or "Double-flowered Rosa," is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa gallica.

It is characterized by its lush, bushy growth habit and dense, thornless stems. The plant produces large, double flowers with numerous petals, often in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. It is highly adaptable to various soil types and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Rosa × temeraria is also valued for its strong fragrance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
