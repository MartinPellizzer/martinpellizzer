Rubus × macrocladus

Rubus × macrocladus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental landscaping and small-scale cultivation.
