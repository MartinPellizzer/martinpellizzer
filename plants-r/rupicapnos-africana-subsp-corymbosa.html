Rupicapnos africana subsp. corymbosa

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

corymbosa, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that appear in dense clusters, typically during the summer months.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
