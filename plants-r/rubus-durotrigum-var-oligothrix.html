Rubus durotrigum var. oligothrix

Rubus durotrigum var. oligothrix, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its trailing or creeping growth habit, with hairy stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in damp, shaded environments such as woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its flowers are pale pink to white, and it is often found in association with other bracken and fern species.
