Rapistrum hispidum

Rapistrum hispidum, commonly known as hispid wild mustard, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can thrive in a variety of climates. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Rapistrum hispidum is also known for its potential as a forage crop in certain agricultural contexts.
