Rosa canina var. aegyptiaca

Rosa canina var. aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian rose, is a variant of the dog rose that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically featuring five leaflets. The flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and they bloom in spring. The fruit, a follicle, develops into a red, hairy berry that contains several seeds.

This variety is native to North Africa and the Middle East, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
