Rubus arduennensis f. ibericus

Rubus arduennensis f. ibericus, commonly known as the Iberian blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is native to the Iberian Peninsula.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
