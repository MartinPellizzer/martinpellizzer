Rhodocodon campanulatus

Rhodocodon campanulatus, commonly known as the bell-shaped rhodocodon, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Campanulaceae.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, often violet or blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem.

Rhodocodon campanulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
