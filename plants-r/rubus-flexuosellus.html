Rubus flexuosellus

Rubus flexuosellus, commonly known as the flexible blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to parts of eastern North America. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are similar to other Rubus species.

The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.
