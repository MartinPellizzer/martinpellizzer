Rubus villicaulis var. stereacanthos

Rubus villicaulis var. stereacanthos, commonly known as the long-horned blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated bracts that give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
