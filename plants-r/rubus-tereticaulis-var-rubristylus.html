Rubus tereticaulis var. rubristylus

Rubus tereticaulis var. rubristylus, commonly known as the red-styled blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated in gardens and wildlands.
