Rudgea jasminoides subsp. micrantha

Rudgea jasminoides subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered jasminoid rudgea, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, montane forests.

The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
