Rytidosperma

Rytidosperma, commonly known as the needle grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Rytidosperma species typically produce small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. They are often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils.
