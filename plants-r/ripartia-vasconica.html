Ripartia vasconica

Ripartia vasconica, commonly known as the Basque bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ripartia vasconica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in alpine meadows.
