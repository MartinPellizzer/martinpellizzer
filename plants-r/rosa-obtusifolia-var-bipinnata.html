Rosa obtusifolia var. bipinnata

Rosa obtusifolia var. bipinnata, commonly known as the double-leaved shrub rose, is a variant of the obtusifolia rose species.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features compound leaves with multiple pairs of leaflets, giving it a distinctive, finely divided appearance. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with a sweet fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and hardy nature.
