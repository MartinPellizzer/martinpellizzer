Rhododendron seinghkuense

Rhododendron seinghkuense, commonly known as the Manipur rhododendron, is a species native to the northeastern region of India, particularly in Manipur.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils under shaded conditions in mountainous forests. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
