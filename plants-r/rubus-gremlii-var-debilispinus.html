Rubus gremlii var. debilispinus

Rubus gremlii var. debilispinus, commonly known as the weak-spined blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, sparsely armed stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique spination and fruit morphology.
