Rosa hispanica

Rosa hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish rose, is a species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub-like plant with thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa hispanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
