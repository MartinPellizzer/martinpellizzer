Rubus questieri subsp. fagicola

Rubus questieri subsp.

fagicola, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and edible fruits.
