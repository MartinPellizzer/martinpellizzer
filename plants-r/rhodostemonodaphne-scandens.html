Rhodostemonodaphne scandens

Rhodostemonodaphne scandens, commonly known as the climbing laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
