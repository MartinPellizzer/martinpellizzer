Rubus tereticaulis var. stefanoffii

Rubus tereticaulis var. stefanoffii, commonly known as the Stefanoff's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically dark green and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The berries are edible and have a flavor similar to other blackberry varieties, though they are less commonly cultivated.
