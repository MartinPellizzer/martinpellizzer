Rubus variabilis

Rubus variabilis, commonly known as the variable blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
