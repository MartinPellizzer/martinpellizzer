Ranunculus sylvicola

Ranunculus sylvicola, commonly known as the woodland buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, usually yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Ranunculus sylvicola is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
