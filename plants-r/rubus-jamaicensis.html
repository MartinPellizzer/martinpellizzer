Rubus jamaicensis

Rubus jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, sharing features with other brambles like raspberries and blackberries.
