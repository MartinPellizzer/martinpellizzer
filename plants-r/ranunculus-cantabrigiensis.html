Ranunculus cantabrigiensis

Ranunculus cantabrigiensis, commonly known as the Cambridge buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the British Isles.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant has palmately divided leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
