Rosa mollis subf. albidescens

Rosa mollis subf.

albidescens, commonly known as the white-flowered evergreen rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and evergreen foliage that remains lush throughout the year.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, with a leathery texture and a serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of garden conditions.
