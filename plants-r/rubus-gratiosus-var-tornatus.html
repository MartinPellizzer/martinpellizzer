Rubus gratiosus var. tornatus

Rubus gratiosus var. tornatus, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green leaves that have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, red, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
