Rudbeckia columnaris

Rudbeckia columnaris, commonly known as the slender coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that bear showy, yellow-orange flower heads with dark brown central cones. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and slightly serrated. Rudbeckia columnaris thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
