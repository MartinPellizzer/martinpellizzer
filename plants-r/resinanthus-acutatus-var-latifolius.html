Resinanthus acutatus var. latifolius

Resinanthus acutatus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf resinanthus, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by resinous, woody fruits. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.
