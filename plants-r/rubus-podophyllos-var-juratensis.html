Rubus podophyllos var. juratensis

Rubus podophyllos var. juratensis, commonly known as the Juratensian blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and tart. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
