Romulea maculata

Romulea maculata, commonly known as the spotted glory flower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with purple petals and a yellow center. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a corm as its storage organ. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Romulea maculata is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
