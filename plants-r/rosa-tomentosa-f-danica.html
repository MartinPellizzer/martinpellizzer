Rosa tomentosa f. danica

Rosa tomentosa f. danica, commonly known as the Danica rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, greyish-white fuzzy foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for hedging and as a decorative plant in temperate regions.
