Rhododendron taliense

Rhododendron taliense, commonly known as the Taliense rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron taliense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
