Rubus caesius intermedia

Rubus caesius intermedia, commonly known as the purple-fruited raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in culinary applications.
