Racosperma recurvatum

Racosperma recurvatum, commonly known as the warty acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive warty texture, which gives it its common name. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma recurvatum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
