Rhaphiolepis bengalensis f. intermedia

Rhaphiolepis bengalensis f. intermedia, commonly known as the Bengal rose.

This plant is a hybrid form of the Bengal rose, characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. It produces clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
