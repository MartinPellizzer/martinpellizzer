Rhododendron admirabile

Rhododendron admirabile, commonly known as the magnificent rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron admirabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
