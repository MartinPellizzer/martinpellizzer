Ranunculus claudiopolitanus

Ranunculus claudiopolitanus, commonly known as the Claudian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or streamside habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
