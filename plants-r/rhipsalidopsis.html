Rhipsalidopsis

Rhipsalidopsis, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family, native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are often adorned with vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces flowers during the holiday season, typically from November to January, hence its common name. Its stems are succulent, allowing it to store water and thrive in arid conditions.

Rhipsalidopsis prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
