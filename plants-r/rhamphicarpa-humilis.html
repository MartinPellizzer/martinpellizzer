Rhamphicarpa humilis

Rhamphicarpa humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its simple, branching stems and small, bladder-like structures used for capturing prey. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in still or slow-moving water. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem as a predator and a contributor to aquatic biodiversity.
