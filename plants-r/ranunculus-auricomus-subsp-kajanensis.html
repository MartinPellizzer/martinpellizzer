Ranunculus auricomus subsp. kajanensis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

kajanensis, commonly known as the Kajanensis buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, which bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and mountain meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
