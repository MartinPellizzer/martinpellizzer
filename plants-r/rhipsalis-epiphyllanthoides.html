Rhipsalis epiphyllanthoides

Rhipsalis epiphyllanthoides, commonly known as the leafy rhipsalis, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and long, slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, giving it a unique appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.
