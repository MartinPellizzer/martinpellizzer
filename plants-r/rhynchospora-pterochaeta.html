Rhynchospora pterochaeta

Rhynchospora pterochaeta, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences bearing pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats in moist soils.

Rhynchospora pterochaeta is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic insects.
