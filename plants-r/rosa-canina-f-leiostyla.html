Rosa canina f. leiostyla

Rosa canina f. leiostyla, commonly known as the smooth hips rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless fruit capsules, which differ from the typical prickly hips of the standard Rosa canina. This form is often found in cultivation and is valued for its ornamental qualities. The plant produces fragrant flowers and is typically found in temperate regions.

It is a subspecies that exhibits unique morphological traits, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
