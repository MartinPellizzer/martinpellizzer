Rosa × dumetorum var. platyphylla

Rosa × dumetorum var. platyphylla, commonly known as the flatleaf shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, thornless stems and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in the summer. The leaves are typically oval to elliptic in shape, with a smooth, waxy surface and a slightly serrated margin. This variety is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
