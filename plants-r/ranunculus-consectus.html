Ranunculus consectus

Ranunculus consectus, commonly known as the early yellow ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves with deeply cut lobes, and its stems are hairy and often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Ranunculus consectus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
