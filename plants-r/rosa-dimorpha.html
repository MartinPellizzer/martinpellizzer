Rosa dimorpha

Rosa dimorpha, commonly known as the two-form rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-form growth pattern, where the upper part of the stem is woody and the lower part is herbaceous. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa dimorpha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
