Rosa communis subsp. glauca

Rosa communis subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the blue rose, is a subspecies of the common rose known for its striking blue-pink flower color. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, slightly hairy leaves.

The flowers are typically single or semi-double, with a unique hue resulting from a combination of pigments and genetic factors. This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its rarity and unique coloration, it is highly sought after in horticulture and botanical research.
