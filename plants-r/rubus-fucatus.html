Rubus fucatus

Rubus fucatus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
