Rubus scaberrimus var. genuina

Rubus scaberrimus var. genuina, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and chaparral habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
