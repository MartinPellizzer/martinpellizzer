Rhamnus spathulifolia

Rhamnus spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter beneath. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky slopes.

Rhamnus spathulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
