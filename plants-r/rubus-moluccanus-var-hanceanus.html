Rubus moluccanus var. hanceanus

Rubus moluccanus var. hanceanus, commonly known as the Hance's blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
