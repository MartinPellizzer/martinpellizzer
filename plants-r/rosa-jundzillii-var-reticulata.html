Rosa jundzillii var. reticulata

Rosa jundzillii var. reticulata, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the highlands of Persia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and large, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange, containing seeds within a fuzzy exterior. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its historical significance dates back to ancient times, where it was cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
