Rosa drosophora

Rosa drosophora, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in cold, high-altitude environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, often pink or white flowers and its ability to form dense, low-growing shrubs. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are hairy and serrated.

Rosa drosophora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in alpine ecosystems.
