Ruschia uncinata

Ruschia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked Ruschia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces vibrant, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and yellow, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Ruschia uncinata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
