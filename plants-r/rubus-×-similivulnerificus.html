Rubus × similivulnerificus

Rubus × similivulnerificus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to regions of North America and is known for its vigorous growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinator populations.

However, its invasive potential in some areas requires careful management.
