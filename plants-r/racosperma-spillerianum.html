Racosperma spillerianum

Racosperma spillerianum, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate and have a feathery appearance. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences, typically during the autumn season. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
