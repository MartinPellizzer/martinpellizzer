Rubus leucophaeus f. balcanicola

Rubus leucophaeus f. balcanicola, commonly known as the Balkan white-bracted raspberry.

It is a subspecies of the white-bracted raspberry, characterized by its distinctive white bracts and small, red fruits. This plant is native to the Balkan Peninsula and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. It typically grows in forested areas and along stream banks, preferring partial shade.

Its botanical features include thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and a shrub-like growth habit.
