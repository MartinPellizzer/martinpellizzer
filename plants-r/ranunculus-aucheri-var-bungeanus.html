Ranunculus aucheri var. bungeanus

Ranunculus aucheri var. bungeanus, commonly known as the Bunge's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals, typically in shades of white, yellow, or orange. The plant has a short, rhizomatous root system and thrives in well-drained, moist soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a hairy texture, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
