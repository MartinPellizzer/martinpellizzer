Rubus lasiocarpus var. rosifolius

Rubus lasiocarpus var. rosifolius, commonly known as the roseleaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins, often resembling those of roses, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
