Ranunculus campanulatus

Ranunculus campanulatus, commonly known as the bell-shaped buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive dark center. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are deeply cut and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus campanulatus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in woodland or cottage garden settings.
