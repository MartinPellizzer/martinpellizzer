Rubus thalassarctos

Rubus thalassarctos, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a shrub native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in saline environments, showing adaptability to coastal conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and stabilizing dune ecosystems.
