Rhynchosia congensis subsp. pseudobuettneri

Rhynchosia congensis subsp.

pseudobuettneri, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa. It features trifoliate leaves with ovate leaflets and produces distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant bears pod-like fruits that contain edible, bean-like seeds. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it both a useful forage plant and a potential ornamental species.
