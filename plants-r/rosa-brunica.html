Rosa brunica

Rosa brunica, commonly known as the dark rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its deep purple to almost black flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has upright, branched stems covered in thorns and is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its fragrance is often described as rich and spicy.

Rosa brunica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
