Ranunculus spectabilis

Ranunculus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically with 5 to 15 petals. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it grows up to 30 inches tall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Ranunculus spectabilis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.
