Rubus xanthadenus

Rubus xanthadenus, commonly known as the golden raspberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are edible but often described as tart and acidic. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other raspberries and blackberries.
