Rosa pimpinellifolia deducta

Rosa pimpinellifolia deducta, commonly known as the Spanish rock rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, exhibiting a low, spreading habit with thorny stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
