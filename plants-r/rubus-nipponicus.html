Rubus nipponicus

Rubus nipponicus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The botanical features of Rubus nipponicus make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
