Rubus hirtus subsp. insolatus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

insolatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
