Rapanea resinosa

Rapanea resinosa, commonly known as the resinous evergreen or Indian myrtle, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.

Rapanea resinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
