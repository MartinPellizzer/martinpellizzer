Ranunculus pubitorus

Ranunculus pubitorus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized landscapes.
