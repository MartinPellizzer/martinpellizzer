Romulea zeyheri

Romulea zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a dense, globular inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a notable addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.

Romulea zeyheri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
