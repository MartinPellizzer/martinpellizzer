Rosa sempervirens var. gandogeriana

Rosa sempervirens var. gandogeriana, commonly known as the Gandogerian rose, is a rare and ancient variety of shrub rose native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which remains dense and vibrant throughout the year. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of heirloom plants.
