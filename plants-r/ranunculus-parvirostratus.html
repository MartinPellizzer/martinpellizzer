Ranunculus parvirostratus

Ranunculus parvirostratus, commonly known as the narrow-beaked buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, spurred flowers with bright yellow petals and a distinctive narrow beak-like structure at the base of the bloom. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and upright.

This species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
