Rubia cordifolia f. pratensis

Rubia cordifolia f. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow knotweed, is a variant of the common knotgrass.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, blooming in early summer.

The plant is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
