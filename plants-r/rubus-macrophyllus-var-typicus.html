Rubus macrophyllus var. typicus

Rubus macrophyllus var. typicus, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is valued for its edible berries and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
