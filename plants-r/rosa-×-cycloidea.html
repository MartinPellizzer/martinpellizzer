Rosa × cycloidea

Rosa × cycloidea, commonly known as the double-flowered shrub rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the crossbreeding of Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a hardy plant suitable for many climates. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, often with a double petal structure that adds to its ornamental appeal. It is widely cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and fragrance.

Additionally, Rosa × cycloidea is valued for its ability to attract pollinators and provide habitat for beneficial insects.
