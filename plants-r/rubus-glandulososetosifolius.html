Rubus glandulososetosifolius

Rubus glandulososetosifolius, commonly known as the glandular-hairy bramble, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The leaves are often covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving the plant its scientific name. It produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
