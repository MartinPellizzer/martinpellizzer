Rhododendron truncatovarium

Rhododendron truncatovarium, commonly known as the dwarf alpine rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and densely packed on its stems. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers in the spring, which are characteristic of the Rhododendron genus. It thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
