Rapuntium polyphyllum

Rapuntium polyphyllum, commonly known as the many-leafed cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a distinctive, peppery taste similar to watercress. Its leaves are usually lobed and can vary in shape depending on the environment.

It thrives in moist, shady areas and is often found in alpine meadows and forest floors.
