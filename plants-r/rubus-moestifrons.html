Rubus moestifrons

Rubus moestifrons, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, which ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are prized for their sweet flavor and are used in culinary applications.
