Rytidosperma nigricans

Rytidosperma nigricans, commonly known as blackgrass or blackleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, stiff leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
