Rubus rubicolor

Rubus rubicolor, commonly known as the red-colored bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they bloom in early summer.
