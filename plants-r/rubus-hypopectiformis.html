Rubus hypopectiformis

Rubus hypopectiformis, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. This plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.
