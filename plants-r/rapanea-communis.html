Rapanea communis

Rapanea communis, commonly known as the common rapanea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are spherical berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Rapanea communis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
