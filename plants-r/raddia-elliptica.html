Raddia elliptica

Raddia elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf raddia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Raddia elliptica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
