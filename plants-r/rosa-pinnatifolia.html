Rosa pinnatifolia

Rosa pinnatifolia, commonly known as the pinkleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, including parts of the Iberian Peninsula and the Balkans. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately divided into numerous leaflets, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five to seven petals, and they bloom in spring.

Rosa pinnatifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
