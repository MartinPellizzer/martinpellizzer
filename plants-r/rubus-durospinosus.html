Rubus durospinosus

Rubus durospinosus, commonly known as the hard-spined blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and dark, edible berries that ripen in the late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
