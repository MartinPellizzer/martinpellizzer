Robiquetia rectifolia

Robiquetia rectifolia, commonly known as the straight-leaved robiquetia, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in whorls, often displaying vibrant colors.

Robiquetia rectifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
