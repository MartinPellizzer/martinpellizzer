Rosa afzeliana var. erlbergensis

Rosa afzeliana var. erlbergensis, commonly known as the Erlberg rose, is a shrub-like species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.
