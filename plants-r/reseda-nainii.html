Reseda nainii

Reseda nainii, commonly known as the Kashmiri madder, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces red-orange roots that contain a dye used historically in textile production. Reseda nainii thrives in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high altitudes.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional dyeing properties.
