Rubus serpens subsp. lividus

Rubus serpens subsp.

lividus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a distinctive silvery-white pubescence.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically dark purple when mature. It thrives in arid to semi-arid environments and is adapted to well-drained soils.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
