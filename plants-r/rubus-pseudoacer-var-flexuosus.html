Rubus pseudoacer var. flexuosus

Rubus pseudoacer var. flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible blackberry.

This variant of the blackberry plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. It produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for horticultural study and ecological research.
