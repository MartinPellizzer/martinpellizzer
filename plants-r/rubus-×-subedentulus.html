Rubus × subedentulus

Rubus × subedentulus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub that belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (red raspberry), resulting in a plant with unique characteristics. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its edible fruits and is commonly cultivated for both commercial and home garden purposes.
