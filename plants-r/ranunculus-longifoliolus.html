Ranunculus longifoliolus

Ranunculus longifoliolus, commonly known as the long-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and yellow flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, daisy-like appearance.
