Restrepia pulchella

Restrepia pulchella, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive purple and yellow coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its flowers bloom in late winter or early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Restrepia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
