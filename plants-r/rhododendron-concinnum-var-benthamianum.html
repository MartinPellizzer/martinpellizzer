Rhododendron concinnum var. benthamianum

Rhododendron concinnum var. benthamianum, commonly known as the Bentham's rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized conditions to ensure its survival.
