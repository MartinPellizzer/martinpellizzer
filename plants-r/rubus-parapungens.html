Rubus parapungens

Rubus parapungens, commonly known as the Chilean blackberry, is a species of brambles native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark purple foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a deep black color. These berries are highly aromatic and are often used in jams, desserts, and beverages.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
