Rosa orthacantha

Rosa orthacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are rose hips, which are orange-red, hairy, and contain several seeds.

Rosa orthacantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
