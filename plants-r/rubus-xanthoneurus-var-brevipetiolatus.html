Rubus xanthoneurus var. brevipetiolatus

Rubus xanthoneurus var. brevipetiolatus, commonly known as the short-petioled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its shorter petioles.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the summer months. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
