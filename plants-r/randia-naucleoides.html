Randia naucleoides

Randia naucleoides, commonly known as the small randia, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its small, white flowers are clustered in cymes, and it produces small, red berries that are edible.

Randia naucleoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
