Ribes × kitaibelii

Ribes × kitaibelii, commonly known as the hybrid currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of gooseberries and currants. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, often turning red in the fall.

The fruit is a small, edible berry that ripens to a deep red or black color, commonly used in culinary applications.
