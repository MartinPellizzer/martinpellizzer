Rhynchophorum

Rhynchophorum, commonly known as the zigzag rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are often reddish in color and exhibit a zigzag growth pattern, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, three-angled seeds.

Rhynchophorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
