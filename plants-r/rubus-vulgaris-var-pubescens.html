Rubus vulgaris var. pubescens

Rubus vulgaris var. pubescens, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry plant, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
