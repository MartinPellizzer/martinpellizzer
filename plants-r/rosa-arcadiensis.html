Rosa arcadiensis

Rosa arcadiensis, commonly known as the Arcadian rose, is a species native to the subtropical regions of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has arching, thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Rosa arcadiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticultural traditions.
