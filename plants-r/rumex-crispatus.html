Rumex crispatus

Rumex crispatus, commonly known as curly dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive crinkled, wavy leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by triangular, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and near water bodies.

Curly dock is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent and anti-inflammatory effects.
