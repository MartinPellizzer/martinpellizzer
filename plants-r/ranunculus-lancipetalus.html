Ranunculus lancipetalus

Ranunculus lancipetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petalled buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often bright yellow flowers with numerous sepals that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

Ranunculus lancipetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
