Racosperma polyadenium

Racosperma polyadenium, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against drought and fire.

Racosperma polyadenium is also valued for its timber, which is used in local crafts and construction.
