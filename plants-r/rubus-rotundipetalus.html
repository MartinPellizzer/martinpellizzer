Rubus rotundipetalus

Rubus rotundipetalus, commonly known as the round-petaled bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, white to pinkish flowers with rounded petals. Its fruit, a red, raspberry-like aggregate, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
