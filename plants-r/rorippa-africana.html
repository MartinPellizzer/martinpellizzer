Rorippa africana

Rorippa africana, commonly known as the African watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is an aquatic or semi-aquatic species native to Africa, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds. It produces small white or yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rorippa africana is also noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
