Rosa × reclinata

Rosa × reclinata, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It is native to the United States and is known for its vigorous growth habit, often climbing or spreading along fences and trellises. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets, and are typically dark green with a slightly serrated edge.

Rosa × reclinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
