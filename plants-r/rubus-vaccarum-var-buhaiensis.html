Rubus vaccarum var. buhaiensis

Rubus vaccarum var. buhaiensis, commonly known as the Baihai blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to China.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes as well.
