Rubus × semipulcher

Rubus × semipulcher, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (the blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (the cloudberry).

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that typically range in color from red to black. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
