Rosa × centifolia albomuscosa

Rosa × centifolia albomuscosa, commonly known as the white musk rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that emit a subtle, musky scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
