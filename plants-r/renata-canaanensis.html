Renata canaanensis

Renata canaanensis, commonly known as the Canaanese renata, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, temperate climate to flourish. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and unique adaptations to its arid environment.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
