Ranunculus pygmaeus

Ranunculus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a small perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, segmented stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture.

Ranunculus pygmaeus is known for its early spring blooming and is often found in moist soils along stream banks.
