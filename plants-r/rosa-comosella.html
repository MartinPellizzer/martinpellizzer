Rosa comosella

Rosa comosella, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish pubescent foliage and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa comosella is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to horticultural collections.
