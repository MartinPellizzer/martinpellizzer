Ranunculus ficaria f. nigromaculatus

Ranunculus ficaria f. nigromaculatus, commonly known as the black-spotted buttercup, is a horticultural form of the European buttercup.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its basal leaves and showy, yellow flowers with dark brown or black spots. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or along streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The dark maculation on the petals is a distinguishing feature of this cultivar.
