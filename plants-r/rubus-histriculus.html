Rubus histriculus

Rubus histriculus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus histriculus is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife.
