Ranunculus lourteigiae

Ranunculus lourteigiae, commonly known as the Lourteig's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically trifoliate or palmately lobed. Its flowers are usually yellow with numerous stamens and a central pistil, and they bloom in early spring.

Ranunculus lourteigiae prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.
