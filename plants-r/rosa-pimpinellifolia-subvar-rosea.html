Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. rosea

Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. rosea, commonly known as the pink rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to rose-colored flowers. The plant has finely serrated, dark green leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The fragrance of its flowers adds an aromatic element to its ornamental value.
