Rubus epipsilos var. holochlorus

Rubus epipsilos var. holochlorus, commonly known as the yellow-tailed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-tipped bracts and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in shrubland habitats, often in areas with well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or acidic flavor.
