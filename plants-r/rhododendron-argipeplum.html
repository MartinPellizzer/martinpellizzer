Rhododendron argipeplum

Rhododendron argipeplum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in acidic soils. Its compact size makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to woodland landscapes.
