Rosa canina var. globulosa

Rosa canina var. globulosa, commonly known as the globular rose, is a variant of the dog rose characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features thorny stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves that provide dense foliage.

Its fruits, known as hips, are orange-red and contain a high concentration of vitamin C. This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued for its medicinal properties.
