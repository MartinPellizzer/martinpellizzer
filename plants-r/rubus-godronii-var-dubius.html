Rubus godronii var. dubius

Rubus godronii var. dubius, commonly known as the Godron's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
