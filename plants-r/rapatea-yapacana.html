Rapatea yapacana

Rapatea yapacana, commonly known as the Yacum or Yacum plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome with a rosette of broad, waxy, heart-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
