Rubus villicaulis proles langei

Rubus villicaulis proles langei, commonly known as the Lange's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
