Ranunculus tripartitus var. terrestris

Ranunculus tripartitus var. terrestris, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, branching stems that spread along the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three rounded leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
