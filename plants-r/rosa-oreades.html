Rosa oreades

Rosa oreades, commonly known as the mountain rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa oreades is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
