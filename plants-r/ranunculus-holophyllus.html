Ranunculus holophyllus

Ranunculus holophyllus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, saucer-shaped flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky soils and alpine meadows. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy blooms in the spring.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
