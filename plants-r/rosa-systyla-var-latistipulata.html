Rosa systyla var. latistipulata

Rosa systyla var. latistipulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features woody stems that are often covered in thorny buds, and its leaves are characterized by their broad, lance-shaped blades with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its foliage is dense and evergreen, providing structural interest throughout the year.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient ornamental plant.
