Rosa infraglaucigera

Rosa infraglaucigera, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its stems are covered in stiff, hooked prickles, and the leaves are pinnately compound with five to seven leaflets.

Rosa infraglaucigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
