Rabdosia irrorata var. longipes

Rabdosia irrorata var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple to violet, arranged in whorled clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.
