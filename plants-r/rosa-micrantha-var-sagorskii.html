Rosa micrantha var. sagorskii

Rosa micrantha var. sagorskii, commonly known as the Sagorskii rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces rose hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. It is well adapted to cooler climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
