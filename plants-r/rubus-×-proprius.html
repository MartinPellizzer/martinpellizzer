Rubus × proprius

Rubus × proprius, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus idaeus (raspberry). The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are widely cultivated for their flavor and nutritional value.

Rubus × proprius is adaptable to various climates and soils, making it a popular choice for both commercial and home gardening.
