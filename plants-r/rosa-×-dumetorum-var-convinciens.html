Rosa × dumetorum var. convinciens

Rosa × dumetorum var. convinciens, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces red-orange hips that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to native plant landscapes.
