Rubus idaeus var. hondoensis

Rubus idaeus var. hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry that originates from Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though some varieties may have yellow or golden berries. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
