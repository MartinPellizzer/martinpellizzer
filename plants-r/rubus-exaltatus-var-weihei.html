Rubus exaltatus var. weihei

Rubus exaltatus var. weihei, commonly known as the black raspberry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
