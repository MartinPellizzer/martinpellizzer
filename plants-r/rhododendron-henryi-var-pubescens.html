Rhododendron henryi var. pubescens

Rhododendron henryi var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically dark green, leathery, and covered with a fine layer of pubescence, giving the plant its common name.

The flowers range in color from pale pink to deep red, depending on the specific cultivar. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
