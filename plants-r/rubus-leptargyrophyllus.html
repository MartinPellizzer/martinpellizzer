Rubus leptargyrophyllus

Rubus leptargyrophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leaf raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries.

The shrub thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
