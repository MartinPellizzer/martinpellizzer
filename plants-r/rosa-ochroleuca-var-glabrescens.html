Rosa ochroleuca var. glabrescens

Rosa ochroleuca var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth white rose, is a variant of the white rose species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers and smooth, hairless stems and leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
