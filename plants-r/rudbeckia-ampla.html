Rudbeckia ampla

Rudbeckia ampla, commonly known as the showy coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its large, daisy-like yellow or orange petals surrounding a prominent, dark brown central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with rough textures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower landscapes.

Rudbeckia ampla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
