Rubus lintonii

Rubus lintonii, commonly known as Linton's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is known for its edible, dark-colored berries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure species within the Rubus group.
