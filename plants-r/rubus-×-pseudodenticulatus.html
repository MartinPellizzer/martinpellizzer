Rubus × pseudodenticulatus

Rubus × pseudodenticulatus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to parts of Asia and has been introduced to various regions for its edible fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate climates, thriving in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, a cluster of small drupes, ripens in late summer and is prized for its sweet, tart flavor.
