Rubus vallisparsus subsp. alnicola

Rubus vallisparsus subsp.

alnicola, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to cold climates and high altitudes.
