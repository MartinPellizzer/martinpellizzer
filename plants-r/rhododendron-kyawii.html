Rhododendron kyawii

Rhododendron kyawii, commonly known as the Burmese rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Myanmar.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in early spring, adding vibrant hues to the forest understory.

Rhododendron kyawii is often found in mixed evergreen forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
