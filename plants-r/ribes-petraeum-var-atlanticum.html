Ribes petraeum var. atlanticum

Ribes petraeum var. atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic gooseberry, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the Atlantic coast, often in coastal dunes or sandy shores. The plant produces small, yellow or orange berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to coastal environments and its role in local ecosystems.
