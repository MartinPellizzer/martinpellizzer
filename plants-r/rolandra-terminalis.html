Rolandra terminalis

Rolandra terminalis, commonly known as the rock wall vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and its ability to climb or trail along rocky surfaces. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically opposite, elliptical, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Rolandra terminalis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.
