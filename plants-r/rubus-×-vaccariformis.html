Rubus × vaccariformis

Rubus × vaccariformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
