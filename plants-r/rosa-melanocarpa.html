Rosa melanocarpa

Rosa melanocarpa, commonly known as the black rose, is a species of wild rose native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black petals, which contrast with its deep green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its unique coloration makes it a striking addition to gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
