Rubus nigricatus var. persimilis

Rubus nigricatus var. persimilis, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
