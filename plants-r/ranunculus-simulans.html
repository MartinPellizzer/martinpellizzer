Ranunculus simulans

Ranunculus simulans, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at high altitudes. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is known for its hardy nature in cold climates.

Ranunculus simulans is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
