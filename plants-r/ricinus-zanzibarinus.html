Ricinus zanzibarinus

Ricinus zanzibarinus, commonly known as the Zanzibar castor, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive spiny seed capsules. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its appearance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique seed structures.

However, it is important to note that Ricinus species, including this one, contain toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
