Rubus maximus

Rubus maximus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry shrub, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically black when mature, though they can also be red or purple. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils.

The plant has a dense growth habit and is used in both ornamental gardening and as a food source for wildlife.
