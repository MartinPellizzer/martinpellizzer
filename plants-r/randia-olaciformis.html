Randia olaciformis

Randia olaciformis, commonly known as the redroot randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
