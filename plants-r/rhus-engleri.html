Rhus engleri

Rhus engleri, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus or hibiscus tiliaceus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils, demonstrating adaptability to tropical climates.

Rhus engleri is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
