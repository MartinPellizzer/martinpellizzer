Ranunculus tempskyanus

Ranunculus tempskyanus, commonly known as the Tempskian buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers feature five to eight sepals and numerous stamens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
