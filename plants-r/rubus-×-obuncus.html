Rubus × obuncus

Rubus × obuncus, commonly known as the Alaska raspberry, is a hybrid species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, similar to other raspberries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subarctic regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
