Ribes sanguineum var. atrorubens

Ribes sanguineum var. atrorubens, commonly known as the dark red currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, dark red to almost black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
