Racosperma cuneifolium

Racosperma cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with thin, elongated leaflets that give it its common name. It produces yellow flowers in spring, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods.

Racosperma cuneifolium is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
