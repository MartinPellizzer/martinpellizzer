Rubus pinetorum var. elatior

Rubus pinetorum var. elatior, commonly known as the tall blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which support its climbing or shrub-like structure. The plant produces large, dark purple to black berries that are typically sweet and juicy, making them popular for both wild harvesting and cultivation. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three to five leaflets and white flowers that bloom in the spring.

This variety is often found in moist, shady areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
