Rubus micans subsp. lacteicomus

Rubus micans subsp.

lacteicomus, commonly known as the milky bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, white or pale pink flowers.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
