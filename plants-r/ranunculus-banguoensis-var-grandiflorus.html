Ranunculus banguoensis var. grandiflorus

Ranunculus banguoensis var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Chinese buttercup, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
