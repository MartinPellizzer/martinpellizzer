Ranunculus aconitifolius proles dealbatus

Ranunculus aconitifolius proles dealbatus, commonly known as the white-flowered buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with numerous small petals, often surrounded by a white or pale yellow corona. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
