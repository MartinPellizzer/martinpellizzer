Rubus hirsutus var. xanthocarpus

Rubus hirsutus var. xanthocarpus, commonly known as the yellow-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color, distinguishing it from the more common red or black berries. Its flowers are white or pale green, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique fruit color and flavor profile.
