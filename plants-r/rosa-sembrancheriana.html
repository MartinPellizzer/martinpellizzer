Rosa sembrancheriana

Rosa sembrancheriana, commonly known as the Sembrancher's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, specifically in regions of Ecuador and Peru. This shrub typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
