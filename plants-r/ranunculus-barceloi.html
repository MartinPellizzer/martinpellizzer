Ranunculus barceloi

Ranunculus barceloi, commonly known as the Barcelona buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It has trifoliate basal leaves and a short, hairy stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
