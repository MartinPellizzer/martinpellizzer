Rubus insericatus var. cinerascens

Rubus insericatus var. cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically reddish or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

This variant is distinguished by its pale, ash-colored stems and leaves, giving it a unique appearance among other Rubus species.
