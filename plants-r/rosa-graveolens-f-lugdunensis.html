Rosa graveolens f. lugdunensis

Rosa graveolens f. lugdunensis, commonly known as the French rose or the rugosa rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa graveolens species.

It is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive, wrinkled, orange-red petals. The plant is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand colder climates, contributing to its popularity among horticulturists.
