Rinorea lanceolata var. floribunda

Rinorea lanceolata var. floribunda, commonly known as the showy rinorea, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
