Rhododendron obtusum var. saikaiense

Rhododendron obtusum var. saikaiense, commonly known as the Saikai rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, range in color from pink to red, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is well-suited to temperate climates.
