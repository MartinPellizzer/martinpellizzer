Rubus macrostachys subsp. fimbrifolius

Rubus macrostachys subsp.

fimbrifolius, commonly known as the hairy-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Rubus genus for its ornamental and potential culinary uses.
