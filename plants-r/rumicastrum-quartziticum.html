Rumicastrum quartziticum

Rumicastrum quartziticum, commonly known as the quartzitic rumicastrum, is a unique plant species native to specific regions with quartz-rich soils.

It exhibits a compact growth habit with small, waxy leaves adapted to conserve moisture in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
