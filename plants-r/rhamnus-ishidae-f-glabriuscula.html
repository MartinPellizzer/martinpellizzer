Rhamnus ishidae f. glabriuscula

Rhamnus ishidae f. glabriuscula, commonly known as the Ishida's buckthorn, is a shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for certain bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
