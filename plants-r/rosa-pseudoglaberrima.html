Rosa pseudoglaberrima

Rosa pseudoglaberrima, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its flowering period occurs primarily during the night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Rosa pseudoglaberrima is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
