Rhamnus cathartica var. eliasii

Rhamnus cathartica var. eliasii, commonly known as Elias' buckthorn, is a shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features dense, thorned branches. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy dark green upper surface with a pale underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

The plant is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
