Rubus hirtus var. gracilentus

Rubus hirtus var. gracilentus, commonly known as the slender bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
