Rosa × dumetorum var. stylosa

Rosa × dumetorum var. stylosa, commonly known as the creeping rose or evergreen shrub rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and evergreen foliage, making it suitable for ground cover. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its stems are covered in thorns, and it has a woody structure that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense mats in gardens and landscapes.
