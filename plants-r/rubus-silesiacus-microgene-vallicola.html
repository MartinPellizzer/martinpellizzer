Rubus silesiacus microgene vallicola

Rubus silesiacus microgene vallicola, commonly known as the valley raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rubus genus. The fruit, a aggregate berry, is small and typically red when mature, though it may vary in color depending on the variety.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
