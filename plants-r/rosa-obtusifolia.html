Rosa obtusifolia

Rosa obtusifolia, commonly known as the softleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are dark green and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers range in color from white to pink.

Rosa obtusifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
