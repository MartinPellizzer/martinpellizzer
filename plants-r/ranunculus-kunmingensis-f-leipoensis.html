Ranunculus kunmingensis f. leipoensis

Ranunculus kunmingensis f. leipoensis, commonly known as the Leipoensis buttercup, is a rare and distinctive botanical variant native to Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and vibrant yellow flowers with numerous stamens. This fern-like plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers typically bloom in spring, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
