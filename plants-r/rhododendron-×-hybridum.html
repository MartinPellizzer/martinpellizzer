Rhododendron × hybridum

Rhododendron × hybridum, commonly known as the azalea, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron obtusum and Rhododendron calendulaceum.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, featuring dense, leathery foliage that provides year-round structure. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
