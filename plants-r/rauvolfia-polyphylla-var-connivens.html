Rauvolfia polyphylla var. connivens

Rauvolfia polyphylla var. connivens, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its roots are rich in alkaloids, including reserpine, which has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its therapeutic applications.
