Ranunculus incisus

Ranunculus incisus, commonly known as the cut-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with prominent, rounded centers and numerous petals. The plant has deeply lobed, palmately divided leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive, rough texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus incisus is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
