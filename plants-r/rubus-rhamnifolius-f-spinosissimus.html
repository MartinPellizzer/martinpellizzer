Rubus rhamnifolius f. spinosissimus

Rubus rhamnifolius f. spinosissimus, commonly known as the spiny blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.
