Rivina octandra var. obtusifolia

Rivina octandra var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf rivina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. Its fruits are red, fleshy drupes that contain several seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
