Rubus tomentosus var. lateserratus

Rubus tomentosus var. lateserratus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The leaves are compound, typically with three to five leaflets, and have serrated edges. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes, followed by red, juicy berries.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along streambanks.
