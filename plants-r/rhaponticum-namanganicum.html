Rhaponticum namanganicum

Rhaponticum namanganicum, commonly known as the Namangan thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, silver-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
