Rubus omnivagans

Rubus omnivagans, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The berries, which are usually red or yellow when mature, are edible and often used in local cuisines.
