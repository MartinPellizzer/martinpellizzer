Rosa Nutkana Subsp. Macdougalii

Rosa nutkana subsp. macdougalii is a rare and beautiful plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. This subspecies is known for its delicate pink to white flowers and fragrant aroma, making it a prized addition to gardens and botanical collections. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a fascinating subject for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
