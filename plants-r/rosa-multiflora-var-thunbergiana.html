Rosa multiflora var. thunbergiana

Rosa multiflora var. thunbergiana, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with five to seven oval leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often used in hedges or ornamental landscaping. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in many cultivated rose varieties.
