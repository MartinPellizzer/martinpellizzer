Rosa afzeliana var. convirescens

Rosa afzeliana var. convirescens, commonly known as the African tea rose, is a shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. Its botanical classification places it within the Rosaceae family, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.
