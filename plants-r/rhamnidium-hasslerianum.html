Rhamnidium hasslerianum

Rhamnidium hasslerianum, commonly known as the Hassler's rhamnus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, simple leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by various bird species.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
