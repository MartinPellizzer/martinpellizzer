Rubus × semisubvillosus

Rubus × semisubvillosus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This species is valued for its edible fruit, though it can be challenging to cultivate due to its vigorous growth and susceptibility to pests.
