Rhododendron beyerinckianum var. longipetiolatum

Rhododendron beyerinckianum var. longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled rhododendron, is a rare and ornamental evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its rarity and horticultural value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
