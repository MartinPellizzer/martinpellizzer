Rhodanthemum redieri subsp. soriae

Rhodanthemum redieri subsp.

soriae, commonly known as the Soria rhodanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale pink to white petals and yellow central disk, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
