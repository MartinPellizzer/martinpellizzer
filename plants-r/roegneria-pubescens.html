Roegneria pubescens

Roegneria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often loosely arranged in a panicle.

Roegneria pubescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
