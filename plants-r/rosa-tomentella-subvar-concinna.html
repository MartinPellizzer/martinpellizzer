Rosa tomentella subvar. concinna

Rosa tomentella subvar. concinna, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescence covering the stems and leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically alternate, with ovate to elliptical leaflets.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
