Ranunculus occidentalis var. brevistylus

Ranunculus occidentalis var. brevistylus, commonly known as the short-styled western buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to nine sepals that are often mistaken for petals.

The variety brevistylus is distinguished by its shorter style compared to the typical form of Ranunculus occidentalis.
