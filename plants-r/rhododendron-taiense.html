Rhododendron taiense

Rhododendron taiense, commonly known as the Taiwan rhododendron, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Taiwan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, humid environments with acidic soils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing dense foliage that offers habitat for various wildlife species.

Rhododendron taiense is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
