Ranunculus ternatus var. dissectissimus

Ranunculus ternatus var. dissectissimus, commonly known as the dissected buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in calcareous soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators.
