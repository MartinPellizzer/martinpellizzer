Rapanea ulugurensis

Rapanea ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru mountain ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, dark berries. The plant is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat, with dense foliage that provides shade and habitat for local wildlife.

Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a significant resource in the region.
