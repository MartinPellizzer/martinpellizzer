Racosperma faucium

Racosperma faucium, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow floral spikes. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Racosperma faucium is an important species in Australian ecosystems and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
