Ranunculus millefoliatus subsp. leptaleus

Ranunculus millefoliatus subsp.

leptaleus, commonly known as the slenderleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is often found in regions with cool, temperate climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more slender leaves and slightly smaller flowers compared to other ranunculus species.
