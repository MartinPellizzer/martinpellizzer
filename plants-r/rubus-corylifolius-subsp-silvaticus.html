Rubus corylifolius subsp. silvaticus

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

silvaticus, commonly known as the wild blackberry bush, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its prickly stems.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and its role in ecological systems.
