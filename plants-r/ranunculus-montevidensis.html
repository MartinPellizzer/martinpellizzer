Ranunculus montevidensis

Ranunculus montevidensis, commonly known as the Uruguay buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically have five to eight petals. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a serrated edge and prefers moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments.

It is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
