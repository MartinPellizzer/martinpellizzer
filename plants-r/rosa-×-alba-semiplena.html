Rosa × alba semiplena

Rosa × alba semiplena, commonly known as the white semiplena rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa alba and other Rosa species.

It is characterized by its semi-double flowers, which have a full bloom with a central cluster of petals and a prominent, rounded center. The plant typically produces pale pink to white flowers that are fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves.

This rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
