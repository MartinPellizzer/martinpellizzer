Rhododendron rex subsp. arizelum

Rhododendron rex subsp.

arizelum, commonly known as the Aztec rose, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico. It features dense, leathery foliage that is typically a deep green with a bluish or purplish tinge.

The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
