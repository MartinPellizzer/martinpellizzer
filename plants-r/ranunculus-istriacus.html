Ranunculus istriacus

Ranunculus istriacus, commonly known as the Istrian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its geographic origin and its classification within the Ranunculaceae family.
