Rapistrum ramosissimum

Rapistrum ramosissimum, commonly known as wild mustard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often have a rough texture.

Rapistrum ramosissimum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
