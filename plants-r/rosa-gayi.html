Rosa gayi

Rosa gayi, commonly known as the Sydney tea tree, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Rosa gayi is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and dry inland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for landscaping in suitable climates.
