Rubus × rasovskiensis

Rubus × rasovskiensis, commonly known as the Rasovski raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-erect growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces white to pale yellow flowers in the spring. It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique characteristics, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
