Rubus × pulverulentus

Rubus × pulverulentus, commonly known as the rusty blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically covered in a dense layer of pale to rusty-colored pubescence. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when ripe, with a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural settings.
