Ranunculus peduncularis var. glabrata

Ranunculus peduncularis var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth meadow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, distinguishing it from other ranunculus species.
