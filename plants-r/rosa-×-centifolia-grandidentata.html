Rosa × centifolia grandidentata

Rosa × centifolia grandidentata, commonly known as the cabbage rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa centifolia and Rosa damascena.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a cabbage-like scent. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
