Rubus milliformis subsp. cyclophyllus

Rubus milliformis subsp.

cyclophyllus, commonly known as the cyclophyllus blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The subspecies is noted for its distinctive leaf morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
