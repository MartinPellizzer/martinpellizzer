Rubia diphylla

Rubia diphylla, commonly known as two-leaved madder, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red or orange berries. Its roots contain alkaloids and have been historically used in dyeing and traditional medicine.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky habitats.
