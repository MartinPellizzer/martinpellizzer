Rubus × avipes

Rubus × avipes, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (brambles) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry).

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is often used in jams, juices, and culinary applications.
