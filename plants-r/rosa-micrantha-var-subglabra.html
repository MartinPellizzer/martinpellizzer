Rosa micrantha var. subglabra

Rosa micrantha var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered with a thin layer of fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty and hardy nature.
