Restio deformis

Restio deformis, commonly known as the slender restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a grass-like plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in dune systems.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
