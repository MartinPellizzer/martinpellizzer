Rubus schleicheri var. acutifrons

Rubus schleicheri var. acutifrons, commonly known as the sharp-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive fruit morphology and genetic variation within the Rubus genus.
