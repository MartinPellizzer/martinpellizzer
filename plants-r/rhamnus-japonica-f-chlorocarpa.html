Rhamnus japonica f. chlorocarpa

Rhamnus japonica f. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese buckthorn, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The foliage remains vibrant throughout the growing season, adding aesthetic value to landscapes.
