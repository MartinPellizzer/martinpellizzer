Rubus × gruntensis

Rubus × gruntensis, commonly known as the Gruntens raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, often in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, a red drupe, is edible and has a tart flavor, making it suitable for culinary use.
