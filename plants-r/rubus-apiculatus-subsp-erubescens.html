Rubus apiculatus subsp. erubescens

Rubus apiculatus subsp.

erubescens, commonly known as the red-fruited raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

The subspecies is valued for its edible berries, which are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
