Rubus macilentus

Rubus macilentus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, thorny stems. The plant produces clusters of small, dark berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Rubus macilentus is often found in temperate climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
