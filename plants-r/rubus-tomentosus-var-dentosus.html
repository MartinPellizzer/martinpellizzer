Rubus tomentosus var. dentosus

Rubus tomentosus var. dentosus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which provide structural support and protection. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, typically in late summer. Its leaves are typically serrated with a dense covering of fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
