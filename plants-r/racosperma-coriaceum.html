Racosperma coriaceum

Racosperma coriaceum, commonly known as the hard-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody, leathery leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Racosperma coriaceum is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous plants.
