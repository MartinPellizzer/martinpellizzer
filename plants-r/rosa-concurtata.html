Rosa concurtata

Rosa concurtata, commonly known as the dwarf shrub rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Rosa concurtata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
