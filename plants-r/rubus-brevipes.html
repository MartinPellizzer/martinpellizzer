Rubus brevipes

Rubus brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark purple fruits. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, preferring partial shade. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the edible berries that ripen in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
