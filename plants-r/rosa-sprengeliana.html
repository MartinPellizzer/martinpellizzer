Rosa sprengeliana

Rosa sprengeliana, commonly known as the Sprengel's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to central and eastern Europe.

It features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, with a distinctive double bloom. The plant produces hips, which are red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and autumn foliage.
