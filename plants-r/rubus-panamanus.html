Rubus panamanus

Rubus panamanus, commonly known as the Panamanian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, which is edible, is small, dark purple, and has a sweet-tart flavor.
