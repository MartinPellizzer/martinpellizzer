Rosa suavis var. dolorosa

Rosa suavis var. dolorosa, commonly known as the sorrowful sweet rose, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale pink, fragrant blooms that emit a subtle, honey-like scent. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are covered in soft, silvery-white hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
