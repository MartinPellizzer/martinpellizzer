Rosa × slawodolica

Rosa × slawodolica, commonly known as the Polish rose, is a hybrid species originating from crosses between Rosa damascena and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically range from pink to white in color. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Rosa × slawodolica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
