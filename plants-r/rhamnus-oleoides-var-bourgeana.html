Rhamnus oleoides var. bourgeana

Rhamnus oleoides var. bourgeana, commonly known as the golden-rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, leathery berries that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its value in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
