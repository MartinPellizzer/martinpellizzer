Rapanea longipes

Rapanea longipes, commonly known as the long-fruited rapanea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated, berry-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
