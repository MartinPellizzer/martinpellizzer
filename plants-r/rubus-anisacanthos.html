Rubus anisacanthos

Rubus anisacanthos, commonly known as the thimbleberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a bright red color. These berries are often used in jams and desserts due to their sweet and tart flavor.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, known for its ecological and culinary significance.
