Rubus glauciformis

Rubus glauciformis, commonly known as the white-veined raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinct white venation. Its flowers are typically pink to reddish and appear in clusters, followed by edible, red berries.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
