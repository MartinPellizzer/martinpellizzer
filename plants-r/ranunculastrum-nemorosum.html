Ranunculastrum nemorosum

Ranunculastrum nemorosum, commonly known as the woodland buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals, blooming in early spring. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.

Ranunculastrum nemorosum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in forest ecosystems.
