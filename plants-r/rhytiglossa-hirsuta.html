Rhytiglossa hirsuta

Rhytiglossa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy lip plant, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and showy, lip-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, with a unique, textured lip that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Rhytiglossa hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
