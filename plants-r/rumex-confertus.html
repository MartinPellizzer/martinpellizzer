Rumex confertus

Rumex confertus, commonly known as broadleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found along roadsides, in meadows, and in wetlands. The plant has large, broad leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. Its leaves are edible and have been used historically in salads and as a vegetable.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses involving its leaves and roots.
