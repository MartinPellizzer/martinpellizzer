Rubus sumatranus

Rubus sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the islands of Sumatra and Borneo. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are pink to white in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
