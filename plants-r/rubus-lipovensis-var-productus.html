Rubus lipovensis var. productus

Rubus lipovensis var. productus, commonly known as the Lipovenskaya blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupelets. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in well-drained, fertile soils. Its leaves are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its robust growth and the edible, dark purple berries it produces.
