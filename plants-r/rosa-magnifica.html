Rosa magnifica

Rosa magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, featuring a mix of pink, red, and white hues. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces fragrant, edible fruits known as rose hips.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
