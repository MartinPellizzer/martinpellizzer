Randia aculeata f. mitis

Randia aculeata f. mitis, commonly known as the soft-hairy randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
