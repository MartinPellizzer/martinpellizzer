Rosa communis var. similata

Rosa communis var. similata, commonly known as the similar rose, is a variant of the common rose that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rosa genus. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are usually smaller than those of the standard Rosa communis, with a more delicate appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
