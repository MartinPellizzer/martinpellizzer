Rubus allegheniensis f. suffultus

Rubus allegheniensis f. suffultus, commonly known as the Allegheny blackberry, is a subspecies of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-erect growth habit and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This subspecies is valued for its edible fruits and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
