Rhaponticum monanthum

Rhaponticum monanthum, commonly known as the single-flowered rhapontic, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting large, daisy-like yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Rhaponticum monanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
