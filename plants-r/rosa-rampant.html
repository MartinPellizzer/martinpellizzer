Rosa rampant

Rosa rampant, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit, with long, flexible canes that can climb or trail along supports. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa rampant is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.
