Rumex japonicus

Rumex japonicus, commonly known as Japanese sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple tint on the underside, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. The plant is known for its sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid in its leaves. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
