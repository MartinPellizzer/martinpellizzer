Rubus rosaceus var. serrulatifolius

Rubus rosaceus var. serrulatifolius, commonly known as the serrated-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges, which give it its specific name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in wooded areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species within the Rubus genus.
