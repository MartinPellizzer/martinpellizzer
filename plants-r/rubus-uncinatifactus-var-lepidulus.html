Rubus uncinatifactus var. lepidulus

Rubus uncinatifactus var. lepidulus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
