Rosa × obarenesiana

Rosa × obarenesiana, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Rosa × obarenesiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
