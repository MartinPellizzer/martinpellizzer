Rosa tomentella subsp. valesiaca

Rosa tomentella subsp.

valesiaca, commonly known as the Valesiaca shrub rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes of South America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its stems are often covered in a thick layer of white to grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
