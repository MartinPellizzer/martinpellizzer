Rumex usticanus

Rumex usticanus, commonly known as the common dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often reddish on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically found in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, ditches, and wetlands.

Rumex usticanus is notable for its medicinal uses and its ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
