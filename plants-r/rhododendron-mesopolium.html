Rhododendron mesopolium

Rhododendron mesopolium, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in the temperate zones of the Himalayas. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and red. It is adapted to cool, moist climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Rhododendron mesopolium is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
