Rosa rubiginosa f. densa

Rosa rubiginosa f. densa, commonly known as the dense-stemmed dog rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often used in hedging or as a decorative plant in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
