Renvoizea vaginiviscosa

Renvoizea vaginiviscosa, commonly known as the velvet pouch plant, is a unique member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often velvety bracts that enclose its flowers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a glossy surface that aids in water retention.

The flowers are showy, often with a bilaterally symmetrical structure, and come in shades of red, orange, or yellow, attracting pollinators like birds and insects.
