Rosa canina subvar. calophylla

Rosa canina subvar. calophylla, commonly known as the Spanish rose or prickly rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, dark green leaves, and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fuzzy hips that contain a high concentration of vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
