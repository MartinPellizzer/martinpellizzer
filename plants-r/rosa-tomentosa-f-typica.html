Rosa tomentosa f. typica

Rosa tomentosa f. typica, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish pubescent foliage and stems, which give it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Rosa tomentosa f. typica is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.
