Rosa mollis var. pseudorubiginosa

Rosa mollis var. pseudorubiginosa, commonly known as the soft rose or false red rose, is a cultivar of the rose family (Rosaceae) characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers.

It typically produces semi-double to double pink or red blooms with a soft, velvety texture. The plant has a compact growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and floral arrangements.
