Rubia tinctorum f. petiolaris

Rubia tinctorum f. petiolaris, commonly known as the petioled mullein, is a variant of the common mallow.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which distinguish it from the typical form of Rubia tinctorum. The plant produces pale yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in dry, sunny environments.

This cultivar is often grown for its ornamental value and historical use in dyeing fabrics.
