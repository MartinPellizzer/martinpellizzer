Rebutia leucacantha

Rebutia leucacantha, commonly known as the White-Flowered Rebutia, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and smooth, greenish-gray stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. Rebutia leucacantha is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
