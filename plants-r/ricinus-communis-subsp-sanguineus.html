Ricinus communis subsp. sanguineus

Ricinus communis subsp.

sanguineus, commonly known as the crimson cup plant, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often red-veined and have a velvety texture.

The plant produces spiky, red or orange seed capsules that contain toxic, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and toxic properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens but requires caution during handling.
