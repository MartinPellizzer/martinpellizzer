Rosa boissieri var. subbidentata

Rosa boissieri var. subbidentata, commonly known as the Mexican rose or subbidentate rose, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in winter, and thrives in well-drained, sunny conditions. Its stems are covered in thorns, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is part of the Rosa species, known for its adaptability and beauty in garden settings.
