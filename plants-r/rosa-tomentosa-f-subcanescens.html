Rosa tomentosa f. subcanescens

Rosa tomentosa f. subcanescens, commonly known as the woolly rose or soft-hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa tomentosa.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish pubescence covering the stems, leaves, and flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and visual interest.
