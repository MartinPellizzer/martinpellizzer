Rubus eglanteria

Rubus eglanteria, commonly known as the prickly raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

Rubus eglanteria plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
