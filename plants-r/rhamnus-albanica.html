Rhamnus albanica

Rhamnus albanica, commonly known as the Albanian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. The plant features small, oval leaves with a glossy surface and produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark blue berry that contains seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

Rhamnus albanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
