Reseda undata subsp. grosii

Reseda undata subsp.

grosii, commonly known as the European wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often along walls or in dry, sunny habitats.

The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
