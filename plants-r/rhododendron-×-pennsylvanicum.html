Rhododendron × pennsylvanicum

Rhododendron × pennsylvanicum, commonly known as the Pennsylvania rhododendron, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, leathery foliage that is typically dark green with a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron × pennsylvanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
