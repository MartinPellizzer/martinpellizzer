Racosperma redolens

Racosperma redolens, commonly known as the sweet pea tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, are often used in horticulture for their ornamental appeal.

Racosperma redolens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.
