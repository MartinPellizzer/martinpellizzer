Ricinocarpus purpurascens

Ricinocarpus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leafed cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and purplish underneath, giving it its common name. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs.

This plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
