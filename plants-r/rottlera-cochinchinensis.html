Rottlera cochinchinensis

Rottlera cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese star anise, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces star-shaped, dark brown fruits that are harvested for their aromatic seeds. These seeds are rich in essential oils and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
