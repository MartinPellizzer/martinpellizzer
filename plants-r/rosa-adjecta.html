Rosa adjecta

Rosa adjecta, commonly known as the Alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and arranged in an alternating pattern along the stems.

Rosa adjecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
