Rhynchosia riparia

Rhynchosia riparia, commonly known as the riverbean or water bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. The plant bears pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by water or animals. Rhynchosia riparia is adapted to moist environments, often growing along riverbanks and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
