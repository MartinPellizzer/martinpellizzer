Rubus × nemorivagus

Rubus × nemorivagus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a significant species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
