Rorippa × auriculata

Rorippa × auriculata, commonly known as the yellow water-cress, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with four petals, typically arranged in clusters near the water surface. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in wet soils or water.

Rorippa × auriculata is often found in ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.
