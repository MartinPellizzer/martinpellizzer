Rubus × balfourianus

Rubus × balfourianus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a hybrid species native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. These berries are valued for their flavor and are often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other members of the Rubus genus.
