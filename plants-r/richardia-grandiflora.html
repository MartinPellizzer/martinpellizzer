Richardia grandiflora

Richardia grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora richardia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces large, showy yellow flowers with prominent central disks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
