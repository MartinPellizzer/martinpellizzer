Rhynchosia variegata

Rhynchosia variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, butterfly-like flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in various culinary and agricultural contexts. Rhynchosia variegata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
