Racosperma mimulum

Racosperma mimulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides nectar for pollinators.

Racosperma mimulum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
