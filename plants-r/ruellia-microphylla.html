Ruellia microphylla

Ruellia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of tubular, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Ruellia microphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in warmer climates.
