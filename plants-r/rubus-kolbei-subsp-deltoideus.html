Rubus kolbei subsp. deltoideus

Rubus kolbei subsp.

deltoideus, commonly known as the delta raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and thorny stems.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
