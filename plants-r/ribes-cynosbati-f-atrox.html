Ribes cynosbati f. atrox

Ribes cynosbati f. atrox, commonly known as the blackcurrant, is a variant of the currant plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small, five-petaled flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool climate for optimal fruit production.

The berries are widely cultivated for both culinary and medicinal uses.
