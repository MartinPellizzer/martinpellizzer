Roystonea oleracea

Roystonea oleracea, commonly known as the African date palm, is a tall, slender palm native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark and produces large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, sweet fruits resembling dates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients.

The African date palm is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
