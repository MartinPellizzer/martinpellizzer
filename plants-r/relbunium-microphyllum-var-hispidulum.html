Relbunium microphyllum var. hispidulum

Relbunium microphyllum var. hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved filaree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and hairy, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a compact cluster at the base of the plant.

It is known for its rapid growth and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
