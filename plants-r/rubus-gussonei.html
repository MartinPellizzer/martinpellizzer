Rubus gussonei

Rubus gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are often used in local culinary traditions. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
