Rhododendron × chlorops

Rhododendron × chlorops, commonly known as the yellow rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron luteum and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with dark green, leathery leaves. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking floral display and adaptability to various garden conditions.
