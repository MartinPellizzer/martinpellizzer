Rubus × scrupeus

Rubus × scrupeus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry shrubs, is a hybrid species within the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is widely cultivated for its edible berries.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed areas.
