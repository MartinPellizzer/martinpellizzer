Rosa caryophyllacea var. pinetorum

Rosa caryophyllacea var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest rose, is a variant of the evergreen rose native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and dense foliage, which provides habitat for various wildlife species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
