Rosa cannabina

Rosa cannabina, commonly known as the cannabis rose, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into spherical, woody fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, with serrated edges.

Rosa cannabina has been historically cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been noted for its potential medicinal properties.
