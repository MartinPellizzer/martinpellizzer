Rhododendron coeloneurum

Rhododendron coeloneurum, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rhododendron, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly California and Nevada.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in mountainous regions, often found in coniferous forests. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers in the spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.

Rhododendron coeloneurum is also known for its ability to thrive in cooler, shaded environments.
