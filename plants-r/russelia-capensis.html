Russelia capensis

Russelia capensis, commonly known as the red-hot poker or fireplant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular red bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Russelia capensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
