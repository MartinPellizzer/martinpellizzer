Rosa dumalis var. besseriana

Rosa dumalis var. besseriana, commonly known as the Alpine rose or the mountain rose, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red-orange hips in the fall. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its beauty and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
