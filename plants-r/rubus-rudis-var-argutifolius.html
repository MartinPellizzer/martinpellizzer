Rubus rudis var. argutifolius

Rubus rudis var. argutifolius, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This species is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
