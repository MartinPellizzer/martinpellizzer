Ranunculus batrachioides subsp. maghrebianus

Ranunculus batrachioides subsp.

maghrebianus, commonly known as the Maghrebian water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are typically divided into fine, thread-like segments, and it has a short, stout stem.

It plays an important ecological role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
