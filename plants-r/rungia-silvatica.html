Rungia silvatica

Rungia silvatica, commonly known as the forest rungia, is a tropical perennial plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes coffee and gardenias, and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a glossy surface, and it produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers followed by small, round berries.

Rungia silvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
