Rubus × hisautsii

Rubus × hisautsii, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many bramble species. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are commonly referred to as blackberries, and are known for their sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Due to its genetic diversity, it exhibits a wide range of fruit colors and sizes.
