Rolfeella

Rolfeella, commonly known as the "buttercup orchid," is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to various regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plants are characterized by their showy, often yellow or orange flowers that resemble those of buttercups. Rolfeella species typically have pseudobulbs and are epiphytic or lithophytic, growing on trees or rocks.

These orchids are valued for their ornamental beauty and are often cultivated by enthusiasts for their unique floral displays.
