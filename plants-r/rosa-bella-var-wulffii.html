Rosa bella var. wulffii

Rosa bella var. wulffii, commonly known as the Wulff's beauty rose, is a cultivar of the rose plant characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, semi-double flowers.

It typically features deep pink to red blooms that are fragrant and appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy flowers. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
