Racosperma ephedroides

Racosperma ephedroides, commonly known as the slender ephedroid, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Racosperma ephedroides is notable for its medicinal properties, containing compounds similar to ephedrine.
