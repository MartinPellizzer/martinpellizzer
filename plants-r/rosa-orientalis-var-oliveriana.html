Rosa orientalis var. oliveriana

Rosa orientalis var. oliveriana, commonly known as the Oliver's oriental rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers with a deep red center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that provide a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and borders.

Its historical significance in horticulture adds to its appeal among rose enthusiasts.
