Racosperma olganum

Racosperma olganum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, yellow-flowered stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
