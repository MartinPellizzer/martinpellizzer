Rudbeckia missouriensis

Rudbeckia missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri coneflower, is a North American native plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange disc flowers surrounded by green to brown ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Rudbeckia missouriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
