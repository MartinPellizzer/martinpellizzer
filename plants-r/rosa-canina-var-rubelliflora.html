Rosa canina var. rubelliflora

Rosa canina var. rubelliflora, commonly known as the red-flowered dog rose, is a variation of the dog rose species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often used in perfumery and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

The fruit, known as a hip, is rich in vitamin C and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
