Ranunculus lenormandii

Ranunculus lenormandii, commonly known as the Lenormand's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Ranunculus lenormandii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
