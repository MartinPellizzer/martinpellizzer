Rosa canina trachyphylla

Rosa canina trachyphylla, commonly known as the bristly dog rose, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers in the spring, followed by red, hairy fruits called hips. These hips are rich in vitamin C and are often used in herbal remedies.

Rosa canina trachyphylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
