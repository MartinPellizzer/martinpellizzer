Rubus schleicheri var. subtypicus

Rubus schleicheri var. subtypicus, commonly known as the Schleicher's blackberry.

This variant is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, though it is relatively rare and understudied.
