Ranunculus isophylloides

Ranunculus isophylloides, commonly known as the broad-leaf buttercup, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ranunculaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, this plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, while the flowers bloom in early spring.

It is a herbaceous perennial that spreads through rhizomes and is often found in temperate climates.
