Ranunculus degenii f. pilosus

Ranunculus degenii f. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy ranunculus, is a variant of the ranunculus genus characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

This plant is native to Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to white, with five petal-like sepals that give it a delicate appearance. The hairy texture of its leaves is a key identifying feature, setting it apart from other ranunculus species.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique botanical characteristics.
