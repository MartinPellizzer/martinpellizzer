Ranunculus conspicuidens

Ranunculus conspicuidens, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often adorned with a distinctive central tuft of stamens. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ranunculus conspicuidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
