Rosa canina subvar. nitens

Rosa canina subvar. nitens, commonly known as the shrub rose or dog rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red-orange hips, which are the fruit and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and open scrublands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
