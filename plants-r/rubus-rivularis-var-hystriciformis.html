Rubus rivularis var. hystriciformis

Rubus rivularis var. hystriciformis, commonly known as the spiny bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry plant.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is a aggregate berry that ripens to a dark red or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and stream banks.

This plant is known for its hardiness and ability to spread through vegetative means.
