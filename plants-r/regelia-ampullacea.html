Regelia ampullacea

Regelia ampullacea, commonly known as the glassy bell orchid, is a striking plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a perennial herb that produces tall, sparsely branched stems topped with clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive glassy sheen that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Regelia ampullacea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
