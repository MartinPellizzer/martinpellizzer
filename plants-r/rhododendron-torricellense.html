Rhododendron torricellense

Rhododendron torricellense, commonly known as the Torricellian rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron torricellense is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
