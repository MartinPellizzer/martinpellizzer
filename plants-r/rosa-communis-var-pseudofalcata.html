Rosa communis var. pseudofalcata

Rosa communis var. pseudofalcata, commonly known as the false sickle rose, is a variant of the common rose with distinctive foliage and floral characteristics.

It features narrow, sickle-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, providing an ornamental contrast to its pink to red flowers. The plant is a deciduous shrub, often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It produces fragrant blooms in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical structure includes thorny stems, compound leaves, and a reproductive system typical of the Rosaceae family.
