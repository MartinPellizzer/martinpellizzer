Rungia grandis

Rungia grandis, commonly known as the purple leaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking coloration.

Rungia grandis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
