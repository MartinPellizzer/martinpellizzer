Rhododendron ripleyi var. ripleyi

Rhododendron ripleyi var. ripleyi, commonly known as the Ripleys' rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a slight pubescence on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized landscapes.
