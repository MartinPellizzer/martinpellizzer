Rosa alpina var. gillotii

Rosa alpina var. gillotii, commonly known as the Alpine rose or Gillot's rose, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of the Rosa genus.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
