Rubus schmidelyanus var. rhodanensis

Rubus schmidelyanus var. rhodanensis, commonly known as the Rhodanese raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
