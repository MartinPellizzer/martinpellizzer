Rosa guicciardii

Rosa guicciardii, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets, and the plant is adapted to arid conditions with deep roots.

Rosa guicciardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native range.
