Ranunculus unguis-cati

Ranunculus unguis-cati, commonly known as the cat's foot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive central green spot. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It blooms in spring, producing flowers that are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus unguis-cati is a perennial herb that prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
