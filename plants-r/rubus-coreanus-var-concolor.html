Rubus coreanus var. concolor

Rubus coreanus var. concolor, commonly known as the Korean raspberry or concolor blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea and parts of eastern Asia.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a sweet and slightly tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in woodland and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

The berries are often used in jams, desserts, and traditional herbal remedies.
