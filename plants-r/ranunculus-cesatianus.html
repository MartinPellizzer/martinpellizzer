Ranunculus cesatianus

Ranunculus cesatianus, commonly known as the Corsican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are hairy.

It is an herbaceous perennial that produces small, greenish-yellow fruits in the form of achenes.
