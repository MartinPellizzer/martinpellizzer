Rubus illudens

Rubus illudens, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other fruit-bearing plants like raspberries and strawberries.
