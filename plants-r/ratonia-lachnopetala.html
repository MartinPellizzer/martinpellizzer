Ratonia lachnopetala

Ratonia lachnopetala, commonly known as the woolly ratonia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a unique texture and appearance. It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Ratonia lachnopetala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
