Rubus koehleri proles preissmannii

Rubus koehleri proles preissmannii, commonly known as the Koehler's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.
