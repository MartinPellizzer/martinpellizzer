Ranunculus abortivus var. acrolasius

Ranunculus abortivus var. acrolasius, commonly known as the yellow marsh carnation, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically borne on slender, hairy stems. The plant has heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its striking color and habitat preferences, it is a notable species in wetland botany.
