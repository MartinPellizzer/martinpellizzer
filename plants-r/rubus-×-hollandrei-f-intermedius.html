Rubus × hollandrei f. intermedius

Rubus × hollandrei f. intermedius, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

This hybrid species exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species, Rubus ursinus and Rubus istrorum.
