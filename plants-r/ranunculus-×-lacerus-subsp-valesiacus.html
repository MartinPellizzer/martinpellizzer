Ranunculus × lacerus subsp. valesiacus

Ranunculus × lacerus subsp.

valesiacus, commonly known as the Valesiacean buttercup, is a hybrid species within the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers with prominent, green sepals.

Native to Central Europe, this subspecies thrives in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and wet meadows. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with stems that are often reddish in color.

Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees, and it is considered a herbaceous perennial.
