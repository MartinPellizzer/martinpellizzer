Rapanea urvillei

Rapanea urvillei, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a fragrant aroma.

Rapanea urvillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
