Rosa arvensis var. glandulifera

Rosa arvensis var. glandulifera, commonly known as the Scotch thistle or woolly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces pink to red flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered with glandular hairs, contributing to its medicinal and ecological significance.

Rosa arvensis var. glandulifera is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
