Rorippa × armoracioides var. cracoviensis

Rorippa × armoracioides var. cracoviensis, commonly known as the Cracow watercress, is a hybrid species within the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, edible leaves that are often cultivated for their peppery flavor. The plant typically grows in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, such as marshes and pond edges. It has a rosette growth habit with basal leaves that are often submerged or floating.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters, typical of the mustard family.
