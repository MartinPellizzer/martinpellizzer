Rhipsalis cassythoydes

Rhipsalis cassythoydes, commonly known as the silver mist cactus, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairless, and branching stems that resemble a tree or shrub. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers during the winter months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its delicate appearance and easy care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
