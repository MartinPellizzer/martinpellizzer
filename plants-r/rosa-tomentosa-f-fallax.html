Rosa tomentosa f. fallax

Rosa tomentosa f. fallax, commonly known as the silver-haired rose, is a unique cultivar of the Rosa tomentosa species.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white pubescence that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is appreciated in horticulture for both its beauty and resilience.
