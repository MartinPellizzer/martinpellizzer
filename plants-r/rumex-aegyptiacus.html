Rumex aegyptiacus

Rumex aegyptiacus, commonly known as Egyptian dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
