Rosa sylvatica

Rosa sylvatica, commonly known as the wild rose, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. Its fruits are red, fuzzy rosehips that contain seeds.

Rosa sylvatica is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils.
