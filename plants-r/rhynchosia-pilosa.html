Rhynchosia pilosa

Rhynchosia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Rhynchosia pilosa is adapted to grow in dry and disturbed soils, making it a resilient species in arid environments.

It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil and supports agricultural productivity.
