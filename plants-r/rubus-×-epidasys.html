Rubus × epidasys

Rubus × epidasys, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common sight in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its berries are often used in jams and desserts, though the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
