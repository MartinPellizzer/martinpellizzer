Rapanea thomensis

Rapanea thomensis, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurel and avocado trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of forests and is known for its hard, dense wood that is used in traditional carpentry.
