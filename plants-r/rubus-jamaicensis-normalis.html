Rubus jamaicensis normalis

Rubus jamaicensis normalis, commonly known as the Jamaican blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupes. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
