Rubus plicatus var. lusitanicus

Rubus plicatus var. lusitanicus, commonly known as the Lusitanian blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and culinary use.
