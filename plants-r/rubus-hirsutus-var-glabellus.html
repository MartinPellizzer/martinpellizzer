Rubus hirsutus var. glabellus

Rubus hirsutus var. glabellus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble species.

It is characterized by its smooth, less hairy stems and leaves compared to the typical Rubus hirsutus. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species within the Rubus genus.
