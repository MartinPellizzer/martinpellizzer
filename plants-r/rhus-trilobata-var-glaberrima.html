Rhus trilobata var. glaberrima

Rhus trilobata var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets, which are typically smooth and glossy, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a characteristic feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Its botanical structure makes it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
