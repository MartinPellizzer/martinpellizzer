Rhododendron macabeanum

Rhododendron macabeanum, commonly known as the Macbean's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowers range in color from pink to purple, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.

Rhododendron macabeanum is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
