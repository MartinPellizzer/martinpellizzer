Rubus × semigrabowskianus

Rubus × semigrabowskianus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (evergreen raspberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, depending on the variety.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in horticulture and food production.
