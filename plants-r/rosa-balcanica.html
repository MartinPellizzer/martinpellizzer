Rosa balcanica

Rosa balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan rose, is a species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets.

Rosa balcanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
