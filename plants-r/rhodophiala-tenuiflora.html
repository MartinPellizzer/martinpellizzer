Rhodophiala tenuiflora

Rhodophiala tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, creeping rhizome and produces delicate, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with three petals and three sepals, typical of the orchid family.

Rhodophiala tenuiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
