Rapistrum bipinnatum

Rapistrum bipinnatum, commonly known as the common sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Europe.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shorelines. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, often blooming in early spring.

The plant is known for its ability to tolerate salt spray and is often found in maritime environments.
