Rytidosperma racemosum

Rytidosperma racemosum, commonly known as the racemed grassveld grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is typically found in open grasslands and is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions.

Rytidosperma racemosum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
