Rhipidoglossum lucieae

Rhipidoglossum lucieae, commonly known as the Lucie's spikerush, is a rare aquatic plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in shallow waters or along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, aiding in its propagation.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species in many areas.
