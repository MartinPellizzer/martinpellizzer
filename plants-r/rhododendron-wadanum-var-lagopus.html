Rhododendron wadanum var. lagopus

Rhododendron wadanum var. lagopus, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking white bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as mountain forests. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its pale flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture and its ecological role in its native habitat.
