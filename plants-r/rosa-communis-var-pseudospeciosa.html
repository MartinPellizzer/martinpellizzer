Rosa communis var. pseudospeciosa

Rosa communis var. pseudospeciosa, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species native to China and has been cultivated for centuries.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with five petals. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and are arranged alternately along the stems.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.
