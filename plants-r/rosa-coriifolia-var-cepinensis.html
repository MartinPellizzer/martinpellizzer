Rosa coriifolia var. cepinensis

Rosa coriifolia var. cepinensis, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorned stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in early summer. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves and oval, hairy buds that open into single or semi-double blooms.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates.
