Rhaphidophora parvifolia

Rhaphidophora parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, stem-like growth and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible tubers that are similar in texture to yams. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Rhaphidophora parvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of starchy roots.
