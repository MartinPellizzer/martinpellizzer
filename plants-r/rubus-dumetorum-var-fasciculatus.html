Rubus dumetorum var. fasciculatus

Rubus dumetorum var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
