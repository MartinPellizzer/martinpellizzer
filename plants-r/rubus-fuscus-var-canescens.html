Rubus fuscus var. canescens

Rubus fuscus var. canescens, commonly known as the grey bracted blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture. This variety is notable for its pale greyish-white bracts that surround the flower clusters.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny habitats.
