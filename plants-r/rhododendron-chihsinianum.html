Rhododendron chihsinianum

Rhododendron chihsinianum, commonly known as the Chihsinian rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Sichuan and Yunnan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in spring. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
