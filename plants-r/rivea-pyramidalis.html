Rivea pyramidalis

Rivea pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Rivea pyramidalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
