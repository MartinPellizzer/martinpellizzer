Rhododendron × erythrocalyx subsp. docimum

Rhododendron × erythrocalyx subsp.

docimum, commonly known as the purple-leaved azalea, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from deep green to purple, depending on the variety.

The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
