Rabdosia henryi

Rabdosia henryi, commonly known as Henry's rabdosia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually arranged in dense clusters and bloom in shades of purple or blue.

Rabdosia henryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
