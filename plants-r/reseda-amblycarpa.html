Reseda amblycarpa

Reseda amblycarpa, commonly known as the woolly reseda, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Reseda amblycarpa is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important species in xerophytic ecosystems.
