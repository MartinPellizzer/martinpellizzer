Ranunculus parnassifolius

Ranunculus parnassifolius, commonly known as the Parnassian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmately divided leaves with a distinctive, fern-like appearance, and its flowers are usually bright yellow with a deep central division. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
