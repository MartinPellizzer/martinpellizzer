Ranunculus auricomus subsp. paludicola

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

paludicola, commonly known as the marsh buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
