Rosa communis var. biserrata

Rosa communis var. biserrata, commonly known as the bi-toothed rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 leaflets with serrated edges, giving it the "biserrata" designation. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with partial sunlight.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its role in horticultural breeding programs.
