Rosa montana var. corymbosa

Rosa montana var. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It features dense, upright growth with small, oval leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is well-suited for hedges or borders due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and repeat blooming habit.
