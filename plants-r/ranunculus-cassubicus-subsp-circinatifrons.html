Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. circinatifrons

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

circinatifrons, commonly known as the circinately-fringed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features showy yellow flowers with 5 to 10 sepals that are often reflexed or curled backward, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are palmately divided, with lobes that are usually hairy and have a serrated edge.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
