Rubus triflorus var. diversifolius

Rubus triflorus var. diversifolius, commonly known as the variable thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically palmately arranged and vary in shape and size across different plants. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are usually yellow or orange when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and appear in clusters of three, giving it its species name.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
