Riedlea scutellarioides

Riedlea scutellarioides, commonly known as the flame tree or *Poinciana* species, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences that resemble flames, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its vibrant floral display.

Riedlea scutellarioides is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
