Randia kraussii

Randia kraussii, commonly known as the Cape rose or red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a yellow center. These flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
