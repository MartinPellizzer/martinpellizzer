Rubus pubescens subsp. pygmadenius

Rubus pubescens subsp.

pygmadenius, commonly known as the Pygmy Raspberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red, raspberry-like fruits. Its flowers are white with five petals and are borne in clusters.

The subspecies is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and is often found in coastal and montane regions.
