Rubus timbal-lagravei subsp. occitanicus

Rubus timbal-lagravei subsp.

occitanicus, commonly known as the Occitanian blackberry, is a shrub native to southern France. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally significant species.
