Rumea jangomas

Rumea jangomas, commonly known as the Jangoma orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blooms that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Rumea jangomas thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
