Rottlera rusciflora

Rottlera rusciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rottlera, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Rottlera rusciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
