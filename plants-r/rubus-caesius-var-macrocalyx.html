Rubus caesius var. macrocalyx

Rubus caesius var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of other blackberry varieties. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some local cuisines.
