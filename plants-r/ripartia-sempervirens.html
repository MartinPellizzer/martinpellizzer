Ripartia sempervirens

Ripartia sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen fern, is a species of fern characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can grow up to several meters in height.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate regions with high humidity. The plant produces spores in clusters called sori, which are located on the underside of its fronds. Its rhizome is fibrous and helps anchor the plant while absorbing nutrients from the soil.

Ripartia sempervirens plays an important role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
