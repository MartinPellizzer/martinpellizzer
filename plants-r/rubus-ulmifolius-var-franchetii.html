Rubus ulmifolius var. franchetii

Rubus ulmifolius var. franchetii, commonly known as the Franchet's bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect or ascending growth habit and produces compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant bears small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
