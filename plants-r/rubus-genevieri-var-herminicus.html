Rubus genevieri var. herminicus

Rubus genevieri var. herminicus, commonly known as the Herminic raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its erect or shrub-like growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or open fields.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential use in culinary applications.
