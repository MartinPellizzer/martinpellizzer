Ranunculus acris f. villosus

Ranunculus acris f. villosus, commonly known as the hairy meadow buttercup, is a botanical variant of the meadow buttercup.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from the typical form of Ranunculus acris. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals and numerous stamens.

It thrives in moist meadows and grasslands, often blooming in early spring.
