Rosa rubiginosa var. imitans

Rosa rubiginosa var. imitans, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose or the common shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which are often reddish in color, and its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges, and its fruit is a hairy, red hip that contains seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
