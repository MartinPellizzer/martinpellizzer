Rhytiglossa chamaedryoides

Rhytiglossa chamaedryoides, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually small, narrow, and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
