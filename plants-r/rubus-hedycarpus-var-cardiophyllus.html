Rubus hedycarpus var. cardiophyllus

Rubus hedycarpus var. cardiophyllus, commonly known as the heart-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are typically large and serrated. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
