Rubus pensilvanicus f. phyllophorus

Rubus pensilvanicus f. phyllophorus, commonly known as the Pennsylvania blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and its ability to form thickets. The leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in the axils of the leaves, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry.

This plant is valued for its hardy nature and its role in supporting local wildlife.
