Rosa blanda f. willmottiae

Rosa blanda f. willmottiae, commonly known as the Willmott's meadow rose, is a fragrant, low-growing shrub native to the British Isles.

It features slender stems with soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in damp meadows and woodland edges. It is a hybrid form of Rosa blanda, known for its ornamental value in gardens.

This species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
