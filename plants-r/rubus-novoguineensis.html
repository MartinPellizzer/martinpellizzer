Rubus novoguineensis

Rubus novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea bramble, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically red or black when mature, and are often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
