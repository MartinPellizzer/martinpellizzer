Rubus semirivularis

Rubus semirivularis, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by dark purple to black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its fruits are edible, though often tart, and are used in local culinary traditions.
