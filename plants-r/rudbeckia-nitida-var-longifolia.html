Rudbeckia nitida var. longifolia

Rudbeckia nitida var. longifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that produce daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and orange-pink petals. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
