Rapanea guianensis var. calensis

Rapanea guianensis var. calensis, commonly known as the Guianan rapanea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Guyana and adjacent areas. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture with a distinct aromatic scent.

The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and the fruit is a drupe, which plays a role in seed dispersal by animals.
