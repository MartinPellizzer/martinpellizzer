Rollandia lanceolata f. rockii

Rollandia lanceolata f. rockii, commonly known as the Rockii form of the lanceolate rollandia, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment. Its ornamental value lies in its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Botanically, it is noted for its genetic diversity and adaptability to high-altitude conditions.
