Rhododendron edgeworthii

Rhododendron edgeworthii, commonly known as the Edgeworth's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in cool, moist environments. The plant features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and erect growth habit.

Rhododendron edgeworthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and high-altitude landscapes.
