Rosa tomentella var. waisbeckeriana

Rosa tomentella var. waisbeckeriana, commonly known as the Waisbecker's woolly rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides a soft, grayish appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a resilient, low-maintenance plant.
