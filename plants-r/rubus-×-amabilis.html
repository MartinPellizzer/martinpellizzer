Rubus × amabilis

Rubus × amabilis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a wide range of morphological variations.
