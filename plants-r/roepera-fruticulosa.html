Roepera fruticulosa

Roepera fruticulosa, commonly known as the flowering roepera, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Roepera fruticulosa is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
