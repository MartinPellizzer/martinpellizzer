Rhynchosia torrei

Rhynchosia torrei, commonly known as the African bean or yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Rhynchosia torrei is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and can be used in various culinary applications.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
