Rubus corylifolius var. intermedius

Rubus corylifolius var. intermedius, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, dark green, lobed leaves and produces aggregate fruits composed of small, achenes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. It has a woody stem with thorns and is part of the Rosaceae family.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
