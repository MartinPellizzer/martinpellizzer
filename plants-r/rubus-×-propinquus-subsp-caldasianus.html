Rubus × propinquus subsp. caldasianus

Rubus × propinquus subsp.

caldasianus, commonly known as the Caldasian blackberry, is a subspecies native to the Andean regions of Colombia. It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically clustered together. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
