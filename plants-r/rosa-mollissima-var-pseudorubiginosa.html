Rosa mollissima var. pseudorubiginosa

Rosa mollissima var. pseudorubiginosa, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, velvety appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
