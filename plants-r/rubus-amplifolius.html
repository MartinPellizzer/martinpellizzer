Rubus amplifolius

Rubus amplifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
