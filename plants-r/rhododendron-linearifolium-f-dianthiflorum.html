Rhododendron linearifolium f. dianthiflorum

Rhododendron linearifolium f. dianthiflorum, commonly known as the dianthiflorum form of the linear-leaved rhododendron, is a flowering plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its botanical features include a dense, evergreen habit and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
