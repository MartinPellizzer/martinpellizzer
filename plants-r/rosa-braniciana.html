Rosa braniciana

Rosa braniciana, commonly known as the Branician rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their beauty and fragrance.

Rosa braniciana prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
