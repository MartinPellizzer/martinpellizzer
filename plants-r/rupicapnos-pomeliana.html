Rupicapnos pomeliana

Rupicapnos pomeliana, commonly known as the orange cypress, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, rounded shape with dense, scale-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce orange-red berries in the fall.
