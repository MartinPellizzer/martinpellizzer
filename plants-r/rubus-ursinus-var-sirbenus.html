Rubus ursinus var. sirbenus

Rubus ursinus var. sirbenus, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
