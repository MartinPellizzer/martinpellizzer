Ranunculus semiverticillatus

Ranunculus semiverticillatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
