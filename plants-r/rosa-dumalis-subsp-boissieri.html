Rosa dumalis subsp. boissieri

Rosa dumalis subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh climates.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
