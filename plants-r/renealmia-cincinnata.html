Renealmia cincinnata

Renealmia cincinnata, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to the wetlands and marshy areas of South America.

It typically grows in dense stands, forming a low, clump-like structure with slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are borne in loose, branching clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a slight sheen.

Renealmia cincinnata is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
