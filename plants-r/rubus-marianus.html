Rubus marianus

Rubus marianus, commonly known as the American blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks. Its flowers are white or pale green, and it produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
