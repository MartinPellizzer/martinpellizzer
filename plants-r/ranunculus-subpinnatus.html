Ranunculus subpinnatus

Ranunculus subpinnatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with numerous petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, finely divided appearance.

Ranunculus subpinnatus is an herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
