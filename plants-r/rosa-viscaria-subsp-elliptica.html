Rosa viscaria subsp. elliptica

Rosa viscaria subsp.

elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical bramble rose, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, elliptical-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers in the spring. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that mature in the autumn.

It is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.
