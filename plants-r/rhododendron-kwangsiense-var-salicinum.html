Rhododendron kwangsiense var. salicinum

Rhododendron kwangsiense var. salicinum, commonly known as the Guangxi salicinum rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
