Rhynchospora stenorhyncha

Rhynchospora stenorhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-horned sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rhynchospora stenorhyncha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
