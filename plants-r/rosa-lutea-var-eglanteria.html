Rosa lutea var. eglanteria

Rosa lutea var. eglanteria, commonly known as the yellow rose of China, is a variant of the Rosa lutea species native to China.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to orange-yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
