Russelia manantlana

Russelia manantlana, commonly known as the mountain russelia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, violet-colored blooms that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Russelia manantlana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to cascade from containers or hanging baskets.
