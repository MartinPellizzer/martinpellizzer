Restio imbricatus

Restio imbricatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restia, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, often forming dense clumps.

Restio imbricatus is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
