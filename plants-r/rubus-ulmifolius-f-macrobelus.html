Rubus ulmifolius f. macrobelus

Rubus ulmifolius f. macrobelus, commonly known as the large-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically longer and wider than those of the standard species. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are usually larger and more robust than typical blackberries. It is a shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
