Rosa canina subvar. syntrichostyla

Rosa canina subvar. syntrichostyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dog rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven oval leaflets that are narrow and pointed.

The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
