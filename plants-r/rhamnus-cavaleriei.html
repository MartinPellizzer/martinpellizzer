Rhamnus cavaleriei

Rhamnus cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
