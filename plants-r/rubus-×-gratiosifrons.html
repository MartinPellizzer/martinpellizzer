Rubus × gratiosifrons

Rubus × gratiosifrons, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its distinctive appearance and horticultural value.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring arching stems with thorny buds. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer, followed by edible berries that are sweet and aromatic. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated and have a glossy surface.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary uses.
