Rumex longifolius f. callosus

Rumex longifolius f. callosus, commonly known as the woolly dock, is a variant of the common dock plant characterized by its distinctive hairy or woolly leaves.

This plant is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows. Its leaves are elongated, with a prominent central vein and a serrated margin, and they are covered in fine, glandular hairs. The flowers are small, greenish, and clustered in loose inflorescences, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its potential astringent and anti-inflammatory properties.
