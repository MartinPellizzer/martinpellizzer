Restio pauciflorus

Restio pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial, evergreen plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing tiny white or pale blue flowers.

Restio pauciflorus is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and adaptability to coastal conditions.
