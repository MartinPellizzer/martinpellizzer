Robinia caragana var. arenaria

Robinia caragana var. arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
