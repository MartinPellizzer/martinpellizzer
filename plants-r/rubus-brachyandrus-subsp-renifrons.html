Rubus brachyandrus subsp. renifrons

Rubus brachyandrus subsp.

renifrons, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves that are broad and lobed.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with unique leaf morphology and fruit structure.
