Racosperma duriusculum

Racosperma duriusculum, commonly known as the hard-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woody foliage and yellow flowers. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Racosperma duriusculum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
