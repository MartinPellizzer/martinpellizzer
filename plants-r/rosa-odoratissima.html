Rosa odoratissima

Rosa odoratissima, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include showy petals and a strong, sweet scent that attracts pollinators.

Rosa odoratissima is valued both for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.
