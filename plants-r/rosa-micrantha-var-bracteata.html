Rosa micrantha var. bracteata

Rosa micrantha var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features thorny stems and pinnate leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets, often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its bracts, which are modified leaves, are prominent and often mistaken for petals, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types in suitable climates.
