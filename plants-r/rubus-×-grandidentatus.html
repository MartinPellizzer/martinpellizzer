Rubus × grandidentatus

Rubus × grandidentatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the western United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial purposes.
