Rhynchosia nipensis

Rhynchosia nipensis, commonly known as the Japanese velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, hairy pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Rhynchosia nipensis also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
