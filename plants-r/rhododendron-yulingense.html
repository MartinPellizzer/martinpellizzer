Rhododendron yulingense

Rhododendron yulingense, commonly known as the Yuling azalea, is a species native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Yunnan and Sichuan provinces.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers in the spring, which are characteristic of the Rhododendron genus. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and floral structures typical of the Ericaceae family.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, moist forest environments.
