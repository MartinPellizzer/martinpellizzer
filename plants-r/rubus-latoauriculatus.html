Rubus latoauriculatus

Rubus latoauriculatus, commonly known as the broad-auriculate blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves with auriculate (ear-shaped) bases and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in local ecosystems for wildlife habitat.
