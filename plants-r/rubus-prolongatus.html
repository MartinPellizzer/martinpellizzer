Rubus prolongatus

Rubus prolongatus, commonly known as the long-stalked blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These berries are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, Rubus prolongatus belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
