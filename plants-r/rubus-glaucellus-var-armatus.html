Rubus glaucellus var. armatus

Rubus glaucellus var. armatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The hairy blackberry is valued for its edible berries and its role in local ecosystems.
