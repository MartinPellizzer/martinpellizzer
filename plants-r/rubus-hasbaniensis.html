Rubus hasbaniensis

Rubus hasbaniensis, commonly known as the Hasbanian blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
