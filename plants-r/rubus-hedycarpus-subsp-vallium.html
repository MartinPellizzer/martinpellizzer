Rubus hedycarpus subsp. vallium

Rubus hedycarpus subsp.

vallium, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and edible fruits.
