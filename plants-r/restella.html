Restella

Restella, commonly known as the silverleaf restella, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms during the spring and summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
