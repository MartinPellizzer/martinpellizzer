Ricinocarpus conspicuus

Ricinocarpus conspicuus, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a distinctive, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, mat-like formations. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in dry environments.

Ricinocarpus conspicuus is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to coastal and inland desert habitats.
