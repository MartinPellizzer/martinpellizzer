Rubus elegantulus

Rubus elegantulus, commonly known as the elegant blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and has a creeping growth habit. Its flowers are white with five petals and appear in the spring.

The berries are typically harvested in late summer and are used in culinary applications.
