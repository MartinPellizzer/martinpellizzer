Rubus occitanicus var. apricus

Rubus occitanicus var. apricus, commonly known as the Corsican raspberry, is a thorny shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, distinguishing it from other Rubus species. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It blooms with white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive berries.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisine.
