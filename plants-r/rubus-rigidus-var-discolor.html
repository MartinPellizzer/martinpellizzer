Rubus rigidus var. discolor

Rubus rigidus var. discolor, commonly known as the variable blackberry.

It is a shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that transition from green to red to black as they ripen. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variant is distinguished by its variable leaf coloration and fruit characteristics.
