Rosa lazarenkoi

Rosa lazarenkoi, commonly known as the Kazakh rose, is a species native to Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink to red in color. Rosa lazarenkoi is known for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
