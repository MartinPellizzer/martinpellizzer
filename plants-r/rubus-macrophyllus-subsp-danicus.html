Rubus macrophyllus subsp. danicus

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

danicus, commonly known as the Danube blackberry, is a shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and produces dark purple to black berries.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and riverbanks. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it blooms in late spring.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in local cuisines.
