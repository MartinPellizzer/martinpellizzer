Rubus dumetorum var. macrocalyx

Rubus dumetorum var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered blackberry, is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black berries. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or thickets. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
