Rosa jundzillii var. pseudoflexuosa

Rosa jundzillii var. pseudoflexuosa, commonly known as the Jundzillii rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, glossy foliage that provides a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
