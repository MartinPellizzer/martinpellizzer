Rosa gallica var. ruralis

Rosa gallica var. ruralis, commonly known as the French pink, is a variety of the Gallica rose that is native to Europe.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of fragrant, pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens and landscaping.

This variety is also known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
