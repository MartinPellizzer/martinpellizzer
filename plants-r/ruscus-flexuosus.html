Ruscus flexuosus

Ruscus flexuosus, commonly known as creeping sedum, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fleshy, dark green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping. Its leaves can be used in herbal remedies, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental ground cover.

Ruscus flexuosus is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.
