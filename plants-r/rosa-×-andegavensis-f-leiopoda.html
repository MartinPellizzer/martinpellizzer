Rosa × andegavensis f. leiopoda

Rosa × andegavensis f. leiopoda, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

This variety is also valued for its use in perfumery and as a source of edible hips.
