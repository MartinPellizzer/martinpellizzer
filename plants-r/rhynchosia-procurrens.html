Rhynchosia procurrens

Rhynchosia procurrens, commonly known as the creeping cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and trifoliate leaves with stipules. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It has a fibrous root system that helps in soil stabilization.

Rhynchosia procurrens is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
