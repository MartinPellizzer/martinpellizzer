Rhynchosia fontis-francisci

Rhynchosia fontis-francisci, commonly known as the Fontis-Francis climbing bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods.

Its seeds are typically enclosed within these pods and are often used in traditional medicine due to their nutritional and medicinal properties.
