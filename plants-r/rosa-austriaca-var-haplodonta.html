Rosa austriaca var. haplodonta

Rosa austriaca var. haplodonta, commonly known as the Austrian rose, is a species of shrub native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has woody stems and simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture make it a notable botanical specimen.
