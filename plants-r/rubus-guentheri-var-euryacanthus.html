Rubus guentheri var. euryacanthus

Rubus guentheri var. euryacanthus, commonly known as the Guenther's blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and South Carolina. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and it produces small, dark red to black berries.

The botanical features of this variety make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, valued for its ornamental and potential culinary uses.
