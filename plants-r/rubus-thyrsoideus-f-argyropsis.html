Rubus thyrsoideus f. argyropsis

Rubus thyrsoideus f. argyropsis, commonly known as the silverleaf raspberry, is a horticultural form of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its unique appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically less flavorful than those of the parent species. It is a shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in both aesthetic and edible plants.
