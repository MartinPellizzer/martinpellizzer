Ruyschia laurifolia

Ruyschia laurifolia, commonly known as the golden wax plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slight yellowish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Ruyschia laurifolia thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
