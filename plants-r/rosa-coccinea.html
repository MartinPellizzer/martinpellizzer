Rosa coccinea

Rosa coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet rose, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, semi-double flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
