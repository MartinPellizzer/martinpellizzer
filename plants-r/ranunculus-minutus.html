Ranunculus minutus

Ranunculus minutus, commonly known as the small crowfoot, is a delicate flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and its flowers consist of numerous small petals arranged in a cup-like structure.

Ranunculus minutus is known for its small size and subtle beauty, making it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
