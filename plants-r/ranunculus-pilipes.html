Ranunculus pilipes

Ranunculus pilipes, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically palmately lobed, with a hairy texture on the underside. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with numerous stamens and a prominent pistil.

Ranunculus pilipes thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
