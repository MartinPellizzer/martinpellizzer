Russelia cora

Russelia cora, commonly known as the coral bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Russelia cora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
