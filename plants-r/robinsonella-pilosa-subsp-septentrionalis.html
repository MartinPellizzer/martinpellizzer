Robinsonella pilosa subsp. septentrionalis

Robinsonella pilosa subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern pilosa, is a perennial herb native to North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and along streambanks.

The plant features a creeping rhizome with erect, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
