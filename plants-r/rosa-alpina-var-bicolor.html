Rosa alpina var. bicolor

Rosa alpina var. bicolor, commonly known as the Alpine bicolor rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its distinctive bicolor flowers, which typically feature a white base with a pink or red edge. The plant thrives in cool, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of finely cut, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
