Rhododendron heptaster

Rhododendron heptaster, commonly known as the seven-star rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, specifically found in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features large, showy flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, arranged in clusters of seven, giving it its common name. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
