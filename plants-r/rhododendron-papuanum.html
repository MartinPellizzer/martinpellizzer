Rhododendron papuanum

Rhododendron papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates and well-drained acidic soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in the spring and attract various pollinators.

Rhododendron papuanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
