Rhipidostigma teijsmannii

Rhipidostigma teijsmannii, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a faint fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
