Rodriguezia bahiensis

Rodriguezia bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia rodriguezia, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
