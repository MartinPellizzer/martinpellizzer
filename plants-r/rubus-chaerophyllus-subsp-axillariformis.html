Rubus chaerophyllus subsp. axillariformis

Rubus chaerophyllus subsp.

axillariformis, commonly known as the axillary-forming blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
