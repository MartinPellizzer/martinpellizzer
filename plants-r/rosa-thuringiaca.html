Rosa thuringiaca

Rosa thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to central Europe.

It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink to red, blooming in summer. The plant produces hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.

Rosa thuringiaca is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
