Rhododendron × azaleodendron

Rhododendron × azaleodendron, commonly known as the hybrid azalea, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is known for its vibrant spring blooms in shades of pink, red, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers acidic, well-drained soil. It is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.

The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and remain green throughout the growing season.
