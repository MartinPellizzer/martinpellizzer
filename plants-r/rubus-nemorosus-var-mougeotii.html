Rubus nemorosus var. mougeotii

Rubus nemorosus var. mougeotii, commonly known as the Mougeot's bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wildlife, particularly birds and small mammals.
