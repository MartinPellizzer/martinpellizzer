Rathbunia sonorensis

Rathbunia sonorensis, commonly known as the Sonoran thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert region of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, purple to violet flower heads that are surrounded by spiny bracts, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Rathbunia sonorensis plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
