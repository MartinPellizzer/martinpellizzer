Rubus tomentosus var. glanduliramus

Rubus tomentosus var. glanduliramus, commonly known as the glandular brambles, is a shrub-like species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems covered in a woolly, white pubescence. The leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and hairy. The flowers are small, white or pink, and arranged in clusters, followed by red, juicy berries.

This plant is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
