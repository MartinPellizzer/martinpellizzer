Rubus cathartium

Rubus cathartium, commonly known as the American blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern North America, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Rubus cathartium plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinators.
