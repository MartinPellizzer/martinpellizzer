Ranunculus badachschanicus

Ranunculus badachschanicus, commonly known as the Badakshan buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or white flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
