Rubus griffithianus

Rubus griffithianus, commonly known as Griffith's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments at high elevations.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
