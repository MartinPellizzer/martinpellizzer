Rhamnus esquirolii var. glabrata

Rhamnus esquirolii var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
