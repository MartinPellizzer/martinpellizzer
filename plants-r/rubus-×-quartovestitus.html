Rubus × quartovestitus

Rubus × quartovestitus, commonly known as the blackberry dewberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both landscape use and fruit production.
