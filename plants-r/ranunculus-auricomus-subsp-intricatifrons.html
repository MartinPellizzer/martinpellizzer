Ranunculus auricomus subsp. intricatifrons

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

intricatifrons, commonly known as the intricate-fronded buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with prominent, reflexed petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely divided, intricately patterned leaflets, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
