Rhododendron araliiforme

Rhododendron araliiforme, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
