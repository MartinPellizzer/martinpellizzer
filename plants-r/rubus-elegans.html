Rubus elegans

Rubus elegans, commonly known as the elegant blackberry, is a species of shrub in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

Rubus elegans is valued for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
