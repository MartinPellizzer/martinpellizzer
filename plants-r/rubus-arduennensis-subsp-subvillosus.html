Rubus arduennensis subsp. subvillosus

Rubus arduennensis subsp.

subvillosus, commonly known as the soft-hairy bramble, is a shrub native to the humid forests of Central America. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems covered in fine, soft hairs.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters, followed by red, edible berries.

This subspecies is adapted to moist, shaded environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
