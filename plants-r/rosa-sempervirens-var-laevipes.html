Rosa sempervirens var. laevipes

Rosa sempervirens var. laevipes, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a variant of the evergreen rose species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its leathery, dark green leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year, giving it its "evergreen" name. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It is a compact shrub with thorny stems and is well-suited for container gardening or as a decorative hedge.

Its botanical features make it a unique and hardy addition to both indoor and outdoor gardens.
