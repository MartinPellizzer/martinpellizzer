Rosa gallicosempervirens

Rosa gallicosempervirens, commonly known as the French perpetual rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has arching stems with thorns and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and disease resistance.
