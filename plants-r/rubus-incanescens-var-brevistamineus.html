Rubus incanescens var. brevistamineus

Rubus incanescens var. brevistamineus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Botanically, it has a complex structure with compound leaves and a flowering period that typically occurs in the spring.

Its ecological role includes providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
