Rubus tomentosus var. elegans

Rubus tomentosus var. elegans, commonly known as the velvet raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, bright red berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
