Rubus reravus

Rubus reravus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of bramble shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when ripe. These fruits are rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making them both nutritious and flavorful.

Rubus reravus thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.
