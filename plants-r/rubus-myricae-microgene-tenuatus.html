Rubus myricae microgene tenuatus

Rubus myricae microgene tenuatus, commonly known as the slender-tendrilled blackberry, is a species of bramble native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
