Romulea ligustica

Romulea ligustica, commonly known as the Ligusticum orchid or the Italian orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, orchid-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Romulea ligustica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is considered a rare and beautiful addition to botanical gardens.
