Renealmia congolana

Renealmia congolana, commonly known as the Congo reed, is a flowering plant native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It is often found in moist, swampy areas and is adapted to thrive in humid tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
