Rubus curvatus

Rubus curvatus, commonly known as the curled blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in woodland edges and moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus curvatus is often found in partial shade and is a common component of understory vegetation in forested areas.
