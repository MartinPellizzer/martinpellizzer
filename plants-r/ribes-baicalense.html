Ribes baicalense

Ribes baicalense, commonly known as the Baikal currant, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Ribes baicalense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
