Rubus hirtus var. asperifolius

Rubus hirtus var. asperifolius, commonly known as the rough-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a rough, serrated texture. The leaves are typically dark green and have a distinctive, slightly waxy appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
