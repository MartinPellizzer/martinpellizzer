Rosa × hispidocarpa

Rosa × hispidocarpa, commonly known as the hispid brushpea rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Rosa × hispidocarpa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
