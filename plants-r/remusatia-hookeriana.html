Remusatia hookeriana

Remusatia hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's arrowroot, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellowish-green spadices surrounded by bright red bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
