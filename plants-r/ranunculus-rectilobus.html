Ranunculus rectilobus

Ranunculus rectilobus, commonly known as the straight-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
