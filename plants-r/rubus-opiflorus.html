Rubus opiflorus

Rubus opiflorus, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Rubus opiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
