Rhododendron sanguineum subsp. melleum

Rhododendron sanguineum subsp.

melleum, commonly known as the yellow azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
