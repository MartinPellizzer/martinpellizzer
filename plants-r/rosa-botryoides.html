Rosa botryoides

Rosa botryoides, commonly known as the globeflower, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive clusters of white, pink, or red flowers that resemble small globes, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, oval fruits called achenes. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Rosa botryoides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
