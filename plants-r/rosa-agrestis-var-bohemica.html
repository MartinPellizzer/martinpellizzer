Rosa agrestis var. bohemica

Rosa agrestis var. bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian field rose, is a perennial shrub native to central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
