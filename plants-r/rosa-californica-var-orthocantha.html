Rosa californica var. orthocantha

Rosa californica var. orthocantha, commonly known as the Santa Barbara rose, is a species native to California and Baja California.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorned stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include hirsute (hairy) stems and stipitate (short, stalked) stipules.

This variety is well adapted to coastal and inland chaparral habitats, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
