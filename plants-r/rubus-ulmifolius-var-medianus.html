Rubus ulmifolius var. medianus

Rubus ulmifolius var. medianus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate or pinnate. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

The plant is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
