Rosa × andegavensis var. trichostyla

Rosa × andegavensis var. trichostyla, commonly known as the Andegavensis trichostyla rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosa genus.

It is characterized by its semi-climbing growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
