Rubus × inaequalis

Rubus × inaequalis, commonly known as the bramble blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, a cluster of small drupes, is edible and often used in jams and desserts.
