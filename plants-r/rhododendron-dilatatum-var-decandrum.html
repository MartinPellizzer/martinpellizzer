Rhododendron dilatatum var. decandrum

Rhododendron dilatatum var. decandrum, commonly known as the Sierra White-flowered Rhododendron, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white to pale pink, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
