Rosa glaucophylla

Rosa glaucophylla, commonly known as the blue-leaf rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters.

Rosa glaucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
