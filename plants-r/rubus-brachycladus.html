Rubus brachycladus

Rubus brachycladus, commonly known as the short-stemmed blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and moist soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
