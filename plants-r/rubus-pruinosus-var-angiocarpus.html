Rubus pruinosus var. angiocarpus

Rubus pruinosus var. angiocarpus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, often reddish stems and broad, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to dark purple to black berries, which are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are sometimes used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
