Rubus × sanguineus

Rubus × sanguineus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid plant that results from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus glaucus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically bright red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
