Rubus burkillii

Rubus burkillii, commonly known as Burkill's blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Indonesia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable food source for local wildlife and humans alike.
