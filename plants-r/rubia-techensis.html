Rubia techensis

Rubia techensis, commonly known as the Turkish madder, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are rich in alkaloids and have been historically used in dyeing textiles.

Rubia techensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.
