Rosa spaldingii var. hispida

Rosa spaldingii var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated leaflets that are covered in fine, velvety hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its visual interest and wildlife benefits.
