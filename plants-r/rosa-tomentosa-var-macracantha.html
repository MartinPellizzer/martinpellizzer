Rosa tomentosa var. macracantha

Rosa tomentosa var. macracantha, commonly known as the woolly rose or large-flowered woolly rose, is a thorny shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense, woolly gray foliage that provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Its stems are covered in a thick layer of white to gray hairs, giving it a fuzzy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in moderate climates.
