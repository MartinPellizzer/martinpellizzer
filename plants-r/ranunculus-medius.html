Ranunculus medius

Ranunculus medius, commonly known as the middle buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with prominent central stamens. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a hairy underside, and its stems are usually hairy as well. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
