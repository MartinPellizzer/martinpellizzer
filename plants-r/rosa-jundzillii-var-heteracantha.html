Rosa jundzillii var. heteracantha

Rosa jundzillii var. heteracantha, commonly known as the Himalayan shrub rose, is a perennial deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with dense foliage of dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late summer. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental flowers and adaptability to cold climates.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience in harsh environments.
