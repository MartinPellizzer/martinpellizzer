Rosa indica var. borbonica

Rosa indica var. borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
