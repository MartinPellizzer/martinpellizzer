Rhodax distachyos subsp. stipulatus

Rhodax distachyos subsp.

stipulatus, commonly known as the stipulate rhodax, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that often bear stipules.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters and borne along the stems. This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
