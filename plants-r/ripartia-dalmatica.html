Ripartia dalmatica

Ripartia dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to their essential oils.

Ripartia dalmatica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
