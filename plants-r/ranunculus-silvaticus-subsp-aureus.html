Ranunculus silvaticus subsp. aureus

Ranunculus silvaticus subsp.

aureus, commonly known as the golden wood anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and stream sides. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, known for its often toxic members.

The subspecies aureus is distinguished by its bright yellow flowers, which contrast with the dark green foliage.
