Rosa carolina var. florida

Rosa carolina var. florida, commonly known as the Florida rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Its ornamental value and historical significance make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
