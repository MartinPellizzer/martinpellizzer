Rourea induta var. concinna

Rourea induta var. concinna, commonly known as the silverleaf rourea, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
