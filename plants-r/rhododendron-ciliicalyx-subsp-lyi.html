Rhododendron ciliicalyx subsp. lyi

Rhododendron ciliicalyx subsp.

lyi, commonly known as the Lyi's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are typically leathery and have a serrated margin, while the flowers range in color from pink to deep red. This subspecies thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
