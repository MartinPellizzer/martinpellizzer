Rosa multiflora var. uchiyamana

Rosa multiflora var. uchiyamana, commonly known as the Uchiyamana rose, is a shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for garden borders and hedges.
