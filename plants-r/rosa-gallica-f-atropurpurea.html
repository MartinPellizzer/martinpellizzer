Rosa gallica f. atropurpurea

Rosa gallica f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the Purple Gallica rose, is a flowering shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has upright, thorny stems and narrow, serrated leaves. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Its historical use in gardens and perfumery highlights its ornamental and aromatic significance.
