Rubus serpens f. oblongus

Rubus serpens f. oblongus, commonly known as the oblong leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that differ from the typical ovate shape of the species. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
