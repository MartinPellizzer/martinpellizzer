Rubus pilosissimus albopilosissimus

Rubus pilosissimus albopilosissimus, commonly known as the white-haired raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
