Rosa subvillosa

Rosa subvillosa, commonly known as the silky rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and small, fragrant pink flowers. Its leaves are compound, with finely hairy surfaces that give it a silky appearance.

Rosa subvillosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sunny environments.
