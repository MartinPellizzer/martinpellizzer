Rubus × vulgatifrons

Rubus × vulgatifrons, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
