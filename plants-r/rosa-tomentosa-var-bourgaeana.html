Rosa tomentosa var. bourgaeana

Rosa tomentosa var. bourgaeana, commonly known as the Bourganeuf rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, grayish-white fuzzy foliage and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand colder climates.
