Ranunculus adoxifolius

Ranunculus adoxifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
