Rhododendron japonoheptamerum var. hondoense

Rhododendron japonoheptamerum var. hondoense, commonly known as the Hondo rhododendron, is a rare and unique variant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments with acidic soil, making it a specialty for woodland gardens. Its foliage consists of leathery, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in its native range.
