Rubus irenaeus var. innoxius

Rubus irenaeus var. innoxius, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and edible berries.
