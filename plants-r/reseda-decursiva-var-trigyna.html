Reseda decursiva var. trigyna

Reseda decursiva var. trigyna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, giving the plant a soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
