Rhodomyrtus trineura var. novoguineensis

Rhodomyrtus trineura var. novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea rose myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, red or purple berries that are edible but not widely cultivated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and floral display.
