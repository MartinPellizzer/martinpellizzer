Rubus mucronifolius

Rubus mucronifolius, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark berries. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
