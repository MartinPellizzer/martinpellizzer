Rhodophiala

Rhodophiala, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil, Bolivia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically golden yellow or orange in color. Rhodophiala species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of other plants without being parasitic.
