Roupala martii

Roupala martii, commonly known as the silky tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and dense, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
