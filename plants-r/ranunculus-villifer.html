Ranunculus villifer

Ranunculus villifer, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves covered in fine, hairy structures that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often with a distinctive central stigma. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus villifer is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.
