Racosperma obesum

Racosperma obesum, commonly known as the fat-stemmed acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, branching habit and phyllodes that are often broad and rounded. The plant produces yellow or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by flat, dehiscent pods.

Racosperma obesum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
