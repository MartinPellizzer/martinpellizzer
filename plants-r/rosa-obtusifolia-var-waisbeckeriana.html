Rosa obtusifolia var. waisbeckeriana

Rosa obtusifolia var. waisbeckeriana, commonly known as the Waisbecker's shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, thorny stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, with oval, serrated leaflets that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often used in gardens for its ability to attract pollinators and its drought tolerance.
