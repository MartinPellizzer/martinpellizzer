Ranunculus potamogetonoides

Ranunculus potamogetonoides, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves that are often submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in clusters. It is native to various parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in ponds, streams, and wet meadows.

Due to its aquatic habitat, it plays an important role in water ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
