Rhynchosia longiflora

Rhynchosia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces elongated, yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its pods are typically cylindrical and contain edible seeds that are used in various culinary applications.

Rhynchosia longiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
