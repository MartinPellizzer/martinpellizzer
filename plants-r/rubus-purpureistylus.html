Rubus purpureistylus

Rubus purpureistylus, commonly known as the purple-styled raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
