Renggeria comans

Renggeria comans, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the base of the plant. Renggeria comans thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
