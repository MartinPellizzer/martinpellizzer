Rosa canina f. nuda

Rosa canina f. nuda, commonly known as the naked rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its reduced or absent thorns, distinguishing it from the typical Rosa canina. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets.

The fruit, a red, hairy berry, is edible and often used in jams and culinary applications.
