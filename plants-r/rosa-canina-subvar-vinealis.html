Rosa canina subvar. vinealis

Rosa canina subvar. vinealis, commonly known as the climbing dog rose, is a variant of the common rose that exhibits a climbing growth habit.

It features thorny stems and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, oval leaves with serrated edges, typical of the Rosa genus. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that contain seeds and are used in traditional herbal medicine.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.
