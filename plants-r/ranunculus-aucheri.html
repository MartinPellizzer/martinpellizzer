Ranunculus aucheri

Ranunculus aucheri, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to deep red, often with a distinctive black center. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically hairy and covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics include a short, thick rhizome and a flowering period that typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
