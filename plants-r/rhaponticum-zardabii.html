Rhaponticum zardabii

Rhaponticum zardabii, commonly known as the Zardab hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features a robust stem with basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in a single head, and are surrounded by pale green bracts.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ability to thrive in harsh climates.
