Rosa stenomalla

Rosa stenomalla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America and northern South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems covered in thorns. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a narrow, lance-shaped appearance with serrated edges. Its flowers are pink to red in color, usually appearing in clusters, and are followed by small, red-orange fruits called hip fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
