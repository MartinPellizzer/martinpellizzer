Roseocactus kotschoubeyanus subsp. macdowellii

Roseocactus kotschoubeyanus subsp.

macdowellii, commonly known as the MacDowell's roseocactus, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico. It features cylindrical stems that are often sparsely covered with white or pale green spines.

The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
