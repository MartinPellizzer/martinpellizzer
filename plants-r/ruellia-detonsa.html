Ruellia detonsa

Ruellia detonsa, commonly known as the redroot ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shady areas. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Ruellia detonsa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
