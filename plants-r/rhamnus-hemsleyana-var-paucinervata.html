Rhamnus hemsleyana var. paucinervata

Rhamnus hemsleyana var. paucinervata, commonly known as the Chinese buckthorn, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It is often found in rocky or mountainous areas and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
