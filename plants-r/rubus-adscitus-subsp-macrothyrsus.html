Rubus adscitus subsp. macrothyrsus

Rubus adscitus subsp.

macrothyrsus, commonly known as the large-throated blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of eastern North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in clusters.

This subspecies is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
