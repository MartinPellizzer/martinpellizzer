Rhamnus crocea subsp. pilosa

Rhamnus crocea subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the hairy buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its subspecies status reflects subtle differences in morphology compared to other Rhamnus species.
