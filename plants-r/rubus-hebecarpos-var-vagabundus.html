Rubus hebecarpos var. vagabundus

Rubus hebecarpos var. vagabundus, commonly known as the wandering blackberry, is a variant of the hebecarpos blackberry species.

It is a shrub that typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed areas, characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it has thorny stems that aid in its climbing behavior.

The botanical features of this plant make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
