Rosa coriifolia f. amplyphylloides

Rosa coriifolia f. amplyphylloides, commonly known as the broad-leafed dog rose, is a cultivar of the species Rosa coriifolia, characterized by its distinctive, broad, ovate leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to China and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental planting.

The cultivar is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
