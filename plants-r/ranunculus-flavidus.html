Ranunculus flavidus

Ranunculus flavidus, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to its natural habitat.

Ranunculus flavidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
