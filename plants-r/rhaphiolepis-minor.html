Rhaphiolepis minor

Rhaphiolepis minor, commonly known as the Japanese rose or snowberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces bright red berries that persist into winter, adding visual interest to the landscape. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the flowers and fruits.

Rhaphiolepis minor is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
