Rosa eglanteria muscosae

Rosa eglanteria muscosae, commonly known as the mossy prickly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has hairy foliage, which contributes to its common name, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
