Rea berteroana

Rea berteroana, commonly known as the Malabar nut, is a flowering plant native to the western Pacific region, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing oval seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a glossy surface with a serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
