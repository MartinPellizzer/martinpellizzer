Rubus delavayi

Rubus delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's raspberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
