Rosa carnatula

Rosa carnatula, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces red or orange hips in the fall. Rosa carnatula is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in hedgerows and garden landscapes.

It is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
