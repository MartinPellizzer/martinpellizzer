Rhipsalis novaesii

Rhipsalis novaesii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of bright flowers. The plant produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. Its leaves are usually green and slightly fleshy, adapted to store water.

Rhipsalis novaesii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
