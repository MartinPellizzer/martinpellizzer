Rosa × dumetorum var. biserrata

Rosa × dumetorum var. biserrata, commonly known as the Alpine rose or shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically serrated, with a coarse texture, and the stems are often covered in thorns.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
