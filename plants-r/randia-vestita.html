Randia vestita

Randia vestita, commonly known as the velvet randia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries that are edible for some wildlife but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Randia vestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
