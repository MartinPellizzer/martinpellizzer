Ranunculus moschatus

Ranunculus moschatus, commonly known as the musk ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found near water bodies such as streams and ponds.

The species is noted for its subtle musky fragrance, which is more pronounced in the flowers.
