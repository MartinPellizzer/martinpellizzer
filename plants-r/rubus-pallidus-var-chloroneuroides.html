Rubus pallidus var. chloroneuroides

Rubus pallidus var. chloroneuroides, commonly known as the pale raspberry variant with chloroneuroides characteristics, is a species of bramble native to certain regions of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are generally reddish in color. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of many Rubus species.

The varietal name chloroneuroides refers to its unique leaf coloration, which may exhibit a bluish-green hue.
