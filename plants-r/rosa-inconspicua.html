Rosa inconspicua

Rosa inconspicua, commonly known as the western wild rose, is a species native to western North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in early summer. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that mature in late summer and are an important food source for wildlife.

Rosa inconspicua is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.
