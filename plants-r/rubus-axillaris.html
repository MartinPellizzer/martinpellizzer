Rubus axillaris

Rubus axillaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its stems are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

Rubus axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
