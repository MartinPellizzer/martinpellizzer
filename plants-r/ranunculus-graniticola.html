Ranunculus graniticola

Ranunculus graniticola, commonly known as the granitic buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing in well-drained soils. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic characteristics.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
