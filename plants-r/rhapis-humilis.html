Rhapis humilis

Rhapis humilis, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant features slender, clustered stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

Rhapis humilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
