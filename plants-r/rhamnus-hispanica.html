Rhamnus hispanica

Rhamnus hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by black, egg-shaped berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Rhamnus hispanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
