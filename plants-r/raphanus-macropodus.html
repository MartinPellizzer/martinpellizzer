Raphanus macropodus

Raphanus macropodus, commonly known as the giant radish or Chinese water chestnut, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, white root that is often used as a vegetable in Asian cuisines. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and small white or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated in wetlands or rice paddies.

The plant's roots are not only edible but also have a crunchy texture and a slightly sweet flavor.
