Rubus philyrinus

Rubus philyrinus, commonly known as the blackberry cane, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Rubus philyrinus is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
