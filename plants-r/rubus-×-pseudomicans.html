Rubus × pseudomicans

Rubus × pseudomicans, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red or black color, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

The species is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to various soil conditions.
