Rubus passionis

Rubus passionis, commonly known as the Passionflower Vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropics of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which includes five sepals, five petals, and a unique reproductive cone. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers that range from red to purple, often with a crown-like structure at the base. Its fruit, known as a raspberry, is edible and has a tart, sweet flavor.

Rubus passionis is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued in traditional medicine.
