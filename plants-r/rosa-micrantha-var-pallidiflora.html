Rosa micrantha var. pallidiflora

Rosa micrantha var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features slender, arching stems that are often covered in thorns. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
