Rosa communis var. chlorantha

Rosa communis var. chlorantha, commonly known as the green-flowered rose, is a rare and unique variant of the common rose.

This plant is characterized by its striking green petals, which contrast sharply with its typical pink or white blooms. It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant flowers in the spring. The botanical features include thorny stems, pinnate leaves, and a compact growth habit.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
