Ruehssia serpentina

Ruehssia serpentina, commonly known as the snake root, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
