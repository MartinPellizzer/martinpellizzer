Ranunculus glabratus

Ranunculus glabratus, commonly known as the smooth buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is known for its hairy stems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
