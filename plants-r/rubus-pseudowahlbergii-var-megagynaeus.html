Rubus pseudowahlbergii var. megagynaeus

Rubus pseudowahlbergii var. megagynaeus, commonly known as the giant female raspberry, is a variant of the brambles native to southeastern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers and robust, thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small drupes, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
