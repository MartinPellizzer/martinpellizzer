Resinanthus baracoensis

Resinanthus baracoensis, commonly known as the Baracoa resin tree, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean island of Cuba.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, fragrant resinous sap that exudes from its bark. The plant typically grows in lowland tropical forests and is adapted to humid, warm climates. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique chemical composition and potential medicinal properties.
