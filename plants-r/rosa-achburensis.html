Rosa achburensis

Rosa achburensis, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has woody stems with thorny growth. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red or orange fruits known as hips.

Rosa achburensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in Armenian tradition.
