Rumex krausei

Rumex krausei, commonly known as the Mexican sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible leaves. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves with a reddish hue, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The leaves are typically used in traditional cuisine and can be consumed raw or cooked.
