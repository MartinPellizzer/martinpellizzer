Rubus pentaphyllus

Rubus pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with five leaflets, and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests. It has a thorny stem and is part of the Rosaceae family, which includes other berries and shrubs.

The fruit is edible but has a tart, acidic flavor.
