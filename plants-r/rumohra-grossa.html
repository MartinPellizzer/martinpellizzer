Rumohra grossa

Rumohra grossa, commonly known as the giant tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Rumohra grossa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
