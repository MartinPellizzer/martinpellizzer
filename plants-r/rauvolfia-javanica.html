Rauvolfia javanica

Rauvolfia javanica, commonly known as Java snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

Rauvolfia javanica is known for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the presence of alkaloids like reserpine.
