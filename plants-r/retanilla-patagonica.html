Retanilla patagonica

Retanilla patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian retanilla, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its small yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Retanilla patagonica is adapted to the dry, windy conditions of its native habitat and is often found in open, sunny areas.
