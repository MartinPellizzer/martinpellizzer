Rubus subglaber

Rubus subglaber, commonly known as the smooth blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus subglaber is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
