Ranunculus haemanthus

Ranunculus haemanthus, commonly known as the golden crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features bright yellow flowers with a striking, almost metallic sheen, typically blooming in spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
