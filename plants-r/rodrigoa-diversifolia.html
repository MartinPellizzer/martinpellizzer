Rodrigoa diversifolia

Rodrigoa diversifolia, commonly known as the pink fairy fanflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fan-like appearance. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Rodrigoa diversifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
