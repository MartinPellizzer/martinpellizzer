Rubus silesiacus subsp. amygdalanthus

Rubus silesiacus subsp.

amygdalanthus, commonly known as the Silesian blackberry, is a shrub native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally significant species in its native range.
