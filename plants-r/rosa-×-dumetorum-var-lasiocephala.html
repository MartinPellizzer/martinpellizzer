Rosa × dumetorum var. lasiocephala

Rosa × dumetorum var. lasiocephala, commonly known as the woolly wood rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals and a prominent, hairy center. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
