Ricinocarpus melochiifolius

Ricinocarpus melochiifolius, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the surface of still or slow-moving water. The plant has a thick, spongy stem that provides buoyancy, and its flowers are large, showy, and typically white or pink in color. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat for aquatic organisms and stabilizing water ecosystems.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters, making it both a valuable and sometimes invasive species.
