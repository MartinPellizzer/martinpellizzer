Rubus villicaulis f. glandulosus

Rubus villicaulis f. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
