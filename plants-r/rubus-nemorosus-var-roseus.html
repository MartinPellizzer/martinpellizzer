Rubus nemorosus var. roseus

Rubus nemorosus var. roseus, commonly known as the rosehip bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early summer, followed by red berries known as rosehips in the fall. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The species is often found in woodlands and hedgerows, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
