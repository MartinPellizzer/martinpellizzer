Ranunculus acris var. stevenii

Ranunculus acris var. stevenii, commonly known as the Steven's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features bright yellow petals that are typically 5-7 in number, surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces a slender, hairy stem that bears the showy flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
