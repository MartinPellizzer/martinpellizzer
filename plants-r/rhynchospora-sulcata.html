Rhynchospora sulcata

Rhynchospora sulcata, commonly known as the grooved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette or spreading manner. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, often surrounded by bracts. Rhynchospora sulcata is adapted to saturated soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
