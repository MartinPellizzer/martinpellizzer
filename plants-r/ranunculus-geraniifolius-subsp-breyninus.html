Ranunculus geraniifolius subsp. breyninus

Ranunculus geraniifolius subsp.

breyninus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features showy yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often described as resembling those of geraniums. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
