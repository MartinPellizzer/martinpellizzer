Rosa glauca var. juguenazensis

Rosa glauca var. juguenazensis, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically pink to pale purple, blooming in early summer. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in alpine gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to specialized horticultural collections.
