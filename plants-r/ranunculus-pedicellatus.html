Ranunculus pedicellatus

Ranunculus pedicellatus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with slender, hairy stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

Ranunculus pedicellatus is often found in meadows, along riverbanks, and in woodland edges.
