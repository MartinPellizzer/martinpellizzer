Rubus tereticaulis var. megathyrsus

Rubus tereticaulis var. megathyrsus, commonly known as the large-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a sweet to tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its edible berries and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
