Rubus × constrictiformis

Rubus × constrictiformis, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its compact growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, similar to other raspberry species. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
