Rubus ulmifolius subsp. acuticuspis

Rubus ulmifolius subsp.

acuticuspis, commonly known as the sharp-cusped blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features compound leaves with serrated margins and small, prickly stems.

The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
