Rubus aegopodioides

Rubus aegopodioides, commonly known as the meadow raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus aegopodioides is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
