Romneya coulteri var. trichocalyx

Romneya coulteri var. trichocalyx, commonly known as the Coulter's romneya, is a perennial plant native to California.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slight hairy texture, contributing to its unique appearance. This variety is a cultivar of the species Romneya coulteri, which is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
