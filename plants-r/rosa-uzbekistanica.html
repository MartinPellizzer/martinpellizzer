Rosa uzbekistanica

Rosa uzbekistanica, commonly known as the Uzbek rose, is a species of shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink to red flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Rosa uzbekistanica is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in perfumery.
