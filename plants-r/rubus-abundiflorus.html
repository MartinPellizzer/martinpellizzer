Rubus abundiflorus

Rubus abundiflorus, commonly known as the abundant-flower bramble, is a shrub native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is noted for its high floral density, which contributes to its common name.
