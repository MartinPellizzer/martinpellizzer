Rhododendron virgatum

Rhododendron virgatum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron virgatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
