Ripidium

Ripidium, commonly known as the common rush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cyperaceae.

These plants are typically found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Ripidium species are characterized by their grass-like appearance, with narrow, hollow stems and small, clustered flowers. They play an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

The genus includes numerous species that vary in height and growth habits, but all share similar botanical features.
