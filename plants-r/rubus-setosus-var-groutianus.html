Rubus setosus var. groutianus

Rubus setosus var. groutianus, commonly known as the Grout's bramble, is a variant of the brambles native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically tart and often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its vigorous growth and adaptability.
