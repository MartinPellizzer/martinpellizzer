Reichardia pentapetala

Reichardia pentapetala, commonly known as the five-petaled rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Reichardia pentapetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
