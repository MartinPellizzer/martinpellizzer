Rhombonema

Rhombonema, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture. Its most distinctive feature is its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhombonema thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
