Rubus lawtonii

Rubus lawtonii, commonly known as the Lawton's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the rose family. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.
