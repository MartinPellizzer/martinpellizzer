Rubus × manazurensis

Rubus × manazurensis, commonly known as the Manazarensis raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. This plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
