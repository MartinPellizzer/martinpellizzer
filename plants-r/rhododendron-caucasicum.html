Rhododendron caucasicum

Rhododendron caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron caucasicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
