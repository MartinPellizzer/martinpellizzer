Retiniphyllum rhabdocalyx

Retiniphyllum rhabdocalyx, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that display prominent red veins against a deep green background. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in clusters. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
