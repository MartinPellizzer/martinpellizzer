Rosa podolica f. bakonyensis

Rosa podolica f. bakonyensis, commonly known as the Bakony rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa podolica species native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense, thorny stems and broad, trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, fragrant scent. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

It is valued for its hardiness and ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season.
