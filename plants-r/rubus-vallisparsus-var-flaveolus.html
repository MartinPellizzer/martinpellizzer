Rubus vallisparsus var. flaveolus

Rubus vallisparsus var. flaveolus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered bramble, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The flowers are typically yellowish-white with five petals and are borne in clusters. The fruit is a red, raspberry-like aggregate of small drupes, which ripen in late summer.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
