Rubus koehleri var. rhapidorhachis

Rubus koehleri var. rhapidorhachis, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The berries are edible when ripe but are often tart and used in local culinary traditions.
