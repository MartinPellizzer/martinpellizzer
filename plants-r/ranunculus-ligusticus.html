Ranunculus ligusticus

Ranunculus ligusticus, commonly known as the Italian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 15 petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Ranunculus ligusticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
