Rosa jundzillii var. leioclada

Rosa jundzillii var. leioclada, commonly known as the Sierra Madre rose, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are compound, typically consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with a slightly serrated edge. This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in cooler climates.
