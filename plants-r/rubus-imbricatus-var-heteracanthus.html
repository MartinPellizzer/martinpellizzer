Rubus imbricatus var. heteracanthus

Rubus imbricatus var. heteracanthus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its edible fruit and its role in local ecosystems.
