Rubus foliosus var. frigidulus

Rubus foliosus var. frigidulus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant adapted to colder climates.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically narrow and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow in color. It thrives in alpine and subalpine regions, often growing in rocky or well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a hardy species suited to harsh environmental conditions.
