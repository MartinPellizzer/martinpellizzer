Rosa gallica var. velutiniflora

Rosa gallica var. velutiniflora, commonly known as the velvet flowered gallica rose, is a cultivar of the gallica rose, native to Europe.

It is characterized by its deep red to crimson flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety down. It produces fragrant, semi-double blooms that are highly valued in gardens for their beauty and historical significance.

This variety is well-suited for borders, containers, and as a shrub in temperate climates.
