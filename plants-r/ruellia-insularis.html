Ruellia insularis

Ruellia insularis, commonly known as the Bahamian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia insularis thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
