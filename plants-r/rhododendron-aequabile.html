Rhododendron aequabile

Rhododendron aequabile, commonly known as the smoothleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron aequabile is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
