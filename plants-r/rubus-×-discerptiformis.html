Rubus × discerptiformis

Rubus × discerptiformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to saline environments. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
