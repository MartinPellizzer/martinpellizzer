Rosa clusiana

Rosa clusiana, commonly known as the Clusian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and fragrant flowers. The plant produces pink to red flowers with five to seven petals, often accompanied by thorny stems and compound leaves. Rosa clusiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts.
