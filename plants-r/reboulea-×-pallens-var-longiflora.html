Reboulea × pallens var. longiflora

Reboulea × pallens var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered redbud, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has a deciduous habit, with heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
