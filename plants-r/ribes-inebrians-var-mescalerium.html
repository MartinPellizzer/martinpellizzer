Ribes inebrians var. mescalerium

Ribes inebrians var. mescalerium, commonly known as the Mescalero gooseberry, is a variant of the gooseberry plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks. The plant produces small, tart berries that are edible and have been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples. Its botanical features include alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
