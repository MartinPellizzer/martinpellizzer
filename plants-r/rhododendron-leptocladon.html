Rhododendron leptocladon

Rhododendron leptocladon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
