Rosa hybrida var. andorfensis

Rosa hybrida var. andorfensis, commonly known as the Andorra rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from the Pyrenees Mountains.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in garden landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, blooming throughout the summer season. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resistance to common rose diseases.
