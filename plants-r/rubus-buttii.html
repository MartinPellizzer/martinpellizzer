Rubus buttii

Rubus buttii, commonly known as the Tibetan raspberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Asia, including parts of China, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white or pale pink, often appearing in the spring.

The berries are usually red or black when mature and are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some local communities.
