Rosa × dumetorum subvar. hispidula

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. hispidula, commonly known as the hispid wild rose, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, foliage that often forms a low-growing mat. The plant produces pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by orange to red hips that are edible. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
