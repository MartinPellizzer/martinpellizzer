Rubus × exilipes

Rubus × exilipes, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is a type of berry, is small, dark purple to black in color, and has a tart flavor.
