Rubus depromptus

Rubus depromptus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States, characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species such as blackberries and raspberries.
