Rosa mollis var. typica

Rosa mollis var. typica, commonly known as the soft rose or velvet rose, is a species of flowering shrub native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and produces hips that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ecological and horticultural landscapes.
