Rubus orthacanthus subsp. euorthacanthus

Rubus orthacanthus subsp.

euorthacanthus, commonly known as the black raspberry, is a thorny shrub native to North America. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color.

The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its flowers are white with five petals. It thrives in woodland areas and is valued for its edible berries.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth and darker fruit color compared to other Rubus species.
