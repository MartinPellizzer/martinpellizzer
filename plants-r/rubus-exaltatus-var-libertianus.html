Rubus exaltatus var. libertianus

Rubus exaltatus var. libertianus, commonly known as the Libertian raspberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, similar to other raspberry species. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value.

The variety is distinguished by its geographic origin and specific morphological traits.
