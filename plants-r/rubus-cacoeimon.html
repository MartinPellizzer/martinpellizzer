Rubus cacoeimon

Rubus cacoeimon, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white or pale pink and are clustered in racemes.

Rubus cacoeimon is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
