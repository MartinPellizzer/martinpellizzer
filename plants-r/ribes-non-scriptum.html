Ribes non-scriptum

Ribes non-scriptum, commonly known as the smooth currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

The smooth currant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
