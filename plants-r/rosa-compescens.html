Rosa compescens

Rosa compescens, commonly known as the Brazilian rose or the wild rose of Brazil, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in traditional medicine.

Rosa compescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some medicinal properties.
