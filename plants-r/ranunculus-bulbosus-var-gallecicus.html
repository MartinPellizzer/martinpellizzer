Ranunculus bulbosus var. gallecicus

Ranunculus bulbosus var. gallecicus, commonly known as the Galician buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately lobed and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It blooms from spring to early summer, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening.
