Rubus koehleri subsp. flandricus

Rubus koehleri subsp.

flandricus, commonly known as the Flanders raspberry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and produces clusters of small, red, edible berries.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often found in sandy or calcareous soils. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and culinary uses.

The flowers are typically pink or white and appear in late spring to early summer.
