Ranunculus amellus

Ranunculus amellus, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring.

Ranunculus amellus is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils, making it both a garden ornamental and a weed in some regions.
