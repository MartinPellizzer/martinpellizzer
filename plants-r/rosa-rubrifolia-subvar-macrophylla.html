Rosa rubrifolia subvar. macrophylla

Rosa rubrifolia subvar. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf redleaf rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically reddish in color, especially when young. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to native plant gardens and landscapes.
