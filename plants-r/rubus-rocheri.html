Rubus rocheri

Rubus rocheri, commonly known as the Rocher's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
