Rhododendron menziesii

Rhododendron menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by leathery, evergreen leaves. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and natural landscapes.

Rhododendron menziesii is also an important species for ecological studies due to its role in supporting local wildlife.
