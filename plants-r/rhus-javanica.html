Rhus javanica

Rhus javanica, commonly known as the staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into numerous narrow, pointed leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fuzzy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
