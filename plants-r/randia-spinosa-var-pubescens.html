Randia spinosa var. pubescens

Randia spinosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy randia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly pubescent surface.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
