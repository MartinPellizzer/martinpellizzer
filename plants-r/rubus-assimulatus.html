Rubus assimulatus

Rubus assimulatus, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and horticultural settings.
