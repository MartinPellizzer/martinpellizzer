Rosa elliptica f. gypsophila

Rosa elliptica f. gypsophila, commonly known as the Gypsophila rose, is a flowering plant characterized by its delicate, lace-like petals.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus. The plant typically produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage consists of finely serrated, dark green leaves.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
