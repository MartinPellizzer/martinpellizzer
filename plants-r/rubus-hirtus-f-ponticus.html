Rubus hirtus f. ponticus

Rubus hirtus f. ponticus, commonly known as the Pontic raspberry, is a subspecies of the brambles native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect or semi-woody growth habit and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
