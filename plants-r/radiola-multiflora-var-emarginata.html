Radiola multiflora var. emarginata

Radiola multiflora var. emarginata, commonly known as the margined radiola, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has thin, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Radiola multiflora var. emarginata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
