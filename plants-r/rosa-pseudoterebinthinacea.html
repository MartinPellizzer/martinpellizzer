Rosa pseudoterebinthinacea

Rosa pseudoterebinthinacea, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a species native to China and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, single or double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming throughout the growing season. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

The botanical features of Rosa pseudoterebinthinacea make it a popular choice for both landscape design and horticultural study.
