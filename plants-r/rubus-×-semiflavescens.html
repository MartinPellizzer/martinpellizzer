Rubus × semiflavescens

Rubus × semiflavescens, commonly known as the golden raspberries, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
