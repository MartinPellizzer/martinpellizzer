Ruyschia amazonica var. dilatata

Ruyschia amazonica var. dilatata, commonly known as the Amazonian arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features elongated, arrow-shaped leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture, adapted for efficient light absorption in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which are pollinated by insects. Its rhizomes spread along the substrate, allowing it to colonize large areas in still or slow-moving waters.

This species is valued in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
