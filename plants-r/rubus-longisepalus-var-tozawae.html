Rubus longisepalus var. tozawae

Rubus longisepalus var. tozawae, commonly known as the Tozawa blackberry, is a variant of the long-sepaled raspberry native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and firm. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and multiple leaflets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its berries are valued for their intense flavor and are used in local culinary traditions.
