Rubus gremlii var. longepetiolatus

Rubus gremlii var. longepetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which are the stalks connecting the leaf blades to the stem. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its unique morphology and potential use in both ornamental and culinary contexts.
