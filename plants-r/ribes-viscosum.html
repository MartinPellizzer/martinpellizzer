Ribes viscosum

Ribes viscosum, commonly known as sticky ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Ribes viscosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
