Rapuntium pinifolium

Rapuntium pinifolium, commonly known as the pineleaf cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, pinnately divided leaves that resemble pine needles. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits.

Rapuntium pinifolium is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
