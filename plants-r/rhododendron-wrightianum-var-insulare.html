Rhododendron wrightianum var. insulare

Rhododendron wrightianum var. insulare, commonly known as the Island Rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with acidic soils. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
