Rhododendron rivulare

Rhododendron rivulare, commonly known as the stream rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in forest undergrowth. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant.

Rhododendron rivulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
