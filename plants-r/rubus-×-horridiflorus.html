Rubus × horridiflorus

Rubus × horridiflorus, commonly known as the roughflower blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a firm, tart flavor. The plant is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, wooded habitats. Its flowers are notable for their pale yellow to white petals and bracts, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
