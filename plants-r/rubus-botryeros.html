Rubus botryeros

Rubus botryeros, commonly known as the clouded blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
