Rhynchosia bicolor

Rhynchosia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-colored flowers, typically featuring a white base and a red or pink upper portion. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. Rhynchosia bicolor is a legume, meaning it belongs to the Fabaceae family, and it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
