Rosa pouzinii subsp. pubescens

Rosa pouzinii subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent rose, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience.
