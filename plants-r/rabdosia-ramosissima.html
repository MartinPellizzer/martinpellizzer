Rabdosia ramosissima

Rabdosia ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy rabdosia, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Rabdosia ramosissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
