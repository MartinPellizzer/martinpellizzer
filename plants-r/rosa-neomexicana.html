Rosa neomexicana

Rosa neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico rose, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant features woody stems and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has thorny stems typical of many rose species.

Rosa neomexicana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that can accommodate its specific ecological needs.
