Romulea chloroleuca

Romulea chloroleuca, commonly known as the yellow romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant produces a cluster of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
