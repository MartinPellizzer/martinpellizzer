Ranunculus platylobus

Ranunculus platylobus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is characterized by its distinctive cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers range in color from yellow to orange, and they bloom from late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus platylobus is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
