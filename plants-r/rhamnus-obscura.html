Rhamnus obscura

Rhamnus obscura, commonly known as the dark buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant is part of the Rhamnaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in rocky and arid environments. Its fruits are small, black berries that ripen in the fall and are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Rhamnus obscura is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
