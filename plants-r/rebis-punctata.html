Rebis punctata

Rebis punctata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers with distinctive white spots. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy layer, which helps reduce water loss.

Rebis punctata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
