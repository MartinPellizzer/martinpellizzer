Rosa villosa f. conjungens

Rosa villosa f. conjungens, commonly known as the hairy rose or the clustered rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa villosa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the western United States, particularly in regions with cooler, moist climates. It typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a resilient and visually appealing plant.
