Reidia glaucescens

Reidia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green reidia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. The plant produces delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
