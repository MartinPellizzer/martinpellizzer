Rebutia canacruzensis

Rebutia canacruzensis, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers, which appear in shades of yellow, orange, and red, bloom during the warmer months.

Rebutia canacruzensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
