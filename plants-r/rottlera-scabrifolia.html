Rottlera scabrifolia

Rottlera scabrifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved rottlera, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and hairy, rough-textured leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Rottlera scabrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
