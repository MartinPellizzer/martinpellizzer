Ranunculus lineatus

Ranunculus lineatus, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive lobed structure, and it produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds.

Ranunculus lineatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
