Rosa diminuta

Rosa diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, thornless stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its botanical classification places it in the Rosaceae family, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its delicate size, it requires well-drained soil and partial sunlight to thrive.
