Rosa mollardiana

Rosa mollardiana, commonly known as the Mollard's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, red hips in the fall.

Rosa mollardiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
