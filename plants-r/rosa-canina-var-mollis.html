Rosa canina var. mollis

Rosa canina var. mollis, commonly known as the soft hips rose, is a variant of the dog rose that is characterized by its softer, more rounded fruit.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia, typically growing in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant produces fragrant pink or white flowers in spring, followed by orange to red hips in autumn. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets, and the stems are armed with thorns.

The hips are edible and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
