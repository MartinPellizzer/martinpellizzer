Rosa arvensis f. genuina

Rosa arvensis f. genuina, commonly known as the true field rose, is a wild rose species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, usually pink or white flowers and thorny stems. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, red berries that contain seeds.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
