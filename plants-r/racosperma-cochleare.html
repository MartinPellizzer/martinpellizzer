Racosperma cochleare

Racosperma cochleare, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are followed by woody pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

Racosperma cochleare is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.
