Rubus × holosericeus

Rubus × holosericeus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to hybridize with other Rubus species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
