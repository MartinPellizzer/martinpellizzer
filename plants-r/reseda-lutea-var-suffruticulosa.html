Reseda lutea var. suffruticulosa

Reseda lutea var. suffruticulosa, commonly known as yellowrocket, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
