Rapanea glomeriflora

Rapanea glomeriflora, commonly known as the clustered-flower rapanea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and other wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
