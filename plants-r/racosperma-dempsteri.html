Racosperma dempsteri

Racosperma dempsteri, commonly known as the dwarf acacia, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Racosperma dempsteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
