Rubus × baldensis

Rubus × baldensis, commonly known as the bald hill blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas with sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is often found in open woodland and disturbed sites.
