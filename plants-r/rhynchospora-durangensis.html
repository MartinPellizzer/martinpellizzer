Rhynchospora durangensis

Rhynchospora durangensis, commonly known as the Durango sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the grasslands and wetlands of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spikelets. Rhynchospora durangensis thrives in moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems.
