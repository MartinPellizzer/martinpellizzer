Rothmannia macrophylla

Rothmannia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant rothmannia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
