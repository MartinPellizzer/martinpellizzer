Rosa lucidissima f. setosa

Rosa lucidissima f. setosa, commonly known as the hairy rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa lucidissima.

It is characterized by its dense, bristly hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinct textured appearance. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with serrated leaf margins.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
