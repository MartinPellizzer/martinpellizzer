Ranunculus setosissimus

Ranunculus setosissimus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with bright yellow petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The hairy buttercup is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
