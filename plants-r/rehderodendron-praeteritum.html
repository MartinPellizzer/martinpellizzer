Rehderodendron praeteritum

Rehderodendron praeteritum, commonly known as the ghost beauty bush, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. The plant has simple, opposite, and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
