Ruschia albertensis

Ruschia albertensis, commonly known as Albert's Ruschia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, commonly referred to as the ice plant family. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid environments.
