Rosa × salaevensis f. typica

Rosa × salaevensis f. typica, commonly known as the typical form of the Rosa salaevensis hybrid, is a shrub-like plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the development of many modern rose cultivars.
