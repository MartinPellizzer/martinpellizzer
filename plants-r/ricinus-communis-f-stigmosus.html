Ricinus communis f. stigmosus

Ricinus communis f. stigmosus, commonly known as the stigmosus castor, is a variant of the castor oil plant.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring stigmas that are prominently displayed and modified into structures resembling bracts. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
