Ridan paniculata

Ridan paniculata, commonly known as the yellow panic grass, is a tropical perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in panicles. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a slightly rough texture and is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Ridan paniculata is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid climates with partial to full sunlight.
