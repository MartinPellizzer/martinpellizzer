Rapanea perakensis

Rapanea perakensis, commonly known as the Bornean wax tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the island of Borneo, where it typically grows in lowland and hill forest areas. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and leathery in texture. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, spherical berries that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
