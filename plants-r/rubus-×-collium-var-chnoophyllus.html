Rubus × collium var. chnoophyllus

Rubus × collium var. chnoophyllus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round, contributing to its evergreen nature. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various regions.
