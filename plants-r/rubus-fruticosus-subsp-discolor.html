Rubus fruticosus subsp. discolor

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the white-fruited blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and aggregate fruits.

The subspecies is distinguished by its white or pale-colored fruits, which contrast with the typical dark berries of other Rubus species. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including hedgerows, woodlands, and rocky areas.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
