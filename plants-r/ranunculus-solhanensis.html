Ranunculus solhanensis

Ranunculus solhanensis, commonly known as the Turkish buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow petals and a hairy, rough stem. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

Ranunculus solhanensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
