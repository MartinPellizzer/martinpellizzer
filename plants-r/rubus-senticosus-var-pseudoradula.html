Rubus senticosus var. pseudoradula

Rubus senticosus var. pseudoradula, commonly known as the Pacific yew, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown bark and small, elliptical leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance and historical use by indigenous peoples.
