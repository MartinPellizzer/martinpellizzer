Reseda phyteuma var. hirsuta

Reseda phyteuma var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy reseda, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in raceme clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and also bear a hairy texture.

Reseda phyteuma var. hirsuta is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
