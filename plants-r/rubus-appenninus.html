Rubus appenninus

Rubus appenninus, commonly known as the Apennine blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
