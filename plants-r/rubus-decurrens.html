Rubus decurrens

Rubus decurrens, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of edible, dark purple berries. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is typically harvested in the fall and is used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
