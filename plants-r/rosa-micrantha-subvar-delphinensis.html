Rosa micrantha subvar. delphinensis

Rosa micrantha subvar. delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinensis rose, is a shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorny prickles. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
