Rubus caesius var. sepium

Rubus caesius var. sepium, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn bright red when ripe, often with a slightly tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
