Rubus × exalbatus

Rubus × exalbatus, commonly known as the Evergreen Raspberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color, though they are smaller than those of other raspberry species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in local cuisines.
