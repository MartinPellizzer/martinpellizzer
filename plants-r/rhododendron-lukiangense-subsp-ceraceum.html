Rhododendron lukiangense subsp. ceraceum

Rhododendron lukiangense subsp.

ceraceum, commonly known as the Yunnan azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. It is often found in forest understories at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
