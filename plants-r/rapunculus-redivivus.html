Rapunculus redivivus

Rapunculus redivivus, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a member of the family Brassicaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that thrives in arid and rocky environments. The plant is notable for its ability to survive extreme drought conditions by entering a desiccated state. When water becomes available, it can rapidly rehydrate and resume growth, giving it its common name.

This unique resilience makes it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
