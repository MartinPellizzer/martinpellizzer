Rubus ulmifolius proles striatus

Rubus ulmifolius proles striatus, commonly known as the brambles or blackberry bush, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, a aggregate berry, ripens in late summer and is edible, though often tart and fibrous.

This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
