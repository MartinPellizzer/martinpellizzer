Reaumuria hypericoides

Reaumuria hypericoides, commonly known as the sandthrift, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It has small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer and attract pollinators such as bees. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Reaumuria hypericoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
