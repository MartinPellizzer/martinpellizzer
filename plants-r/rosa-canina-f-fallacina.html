Rosa canina f. fallacina

Rosa canina f. fallacina, commonly known as the false dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are similar to those of the common dog rose. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
