Rosa gentiliana f. puberula

Rosa gentiliana f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent garden rose, is a cultivar characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and fragrant pink flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. The plant typically grows as a shrub with multiple stems and produces semi-double blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its unique appearance.

This cultivar is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it suitable for a variety of garden environments.
