Ranunculus arcuans

Ranunculus arcuans, commonly known as the curved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with 5 to 15 sepals that are often notched or lobed. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are palmately divided, and its stems are usually upright and hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Ranunculus arcuans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
