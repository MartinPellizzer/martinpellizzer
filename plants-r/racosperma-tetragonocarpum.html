Racosperma tetragonocarpum

Racosperma tetragonocarpum, commonly known as the four-cornered legume, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive four-angled pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma tetragonocarpum plays a role in soil stabilization and is used in ecological restoration projects.
