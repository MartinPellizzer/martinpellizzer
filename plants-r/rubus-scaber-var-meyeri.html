Rubus scaber var. meyeri

Rubus scaber var. meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's bramble, is a variant of the brambles native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
