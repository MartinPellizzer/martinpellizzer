Ribes bicolor

Ribes bicolor, commonly known as the twinflower, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in pairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and wines. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas.

Ribes bicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
