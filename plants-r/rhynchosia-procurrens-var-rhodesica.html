Rhynchosia procurrens var. rhodesica

Rhynchosia procurrens var. rhodesica, commonly known as the Rhodes morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.

Its seeds are encased in a fibrous, spiny pod that matures in late summer.
