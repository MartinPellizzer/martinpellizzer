Rosa glutinosa subsp. sicula

Rosa glutinosa subsp.

sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces oval, yellowish fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a serrated edge.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both decorative and medicinal purposes.
