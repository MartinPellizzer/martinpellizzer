Rubus Anatolicus

Rubus anatolicus is a lesser-known species of bramble native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. Despite its modest appearance, this plant holds ecological significance as a food source for local wildlife. Its cultivation is gaining interest due to its potential in sustainable agriculture. This article explores the features, habitat, and importance of Rubus anatolicus.
