Racosperma kalgoorliense

Racosperma kalgoorliense, commonly known as the Kalgoorlie acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and yellow flowers that appear in winter. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are arranged in spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought.

The species is part of the legume family and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
