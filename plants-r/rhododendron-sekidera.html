Rhododendron sekidera

Rhododendron sekidera, commonly known as the Sekidera azalea, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms.

Rhododendron sekidera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to specific garden conditions.
