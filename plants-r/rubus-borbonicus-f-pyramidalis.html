Rubus borbonicus f. pyramidalis

Rubus borbonicus f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal raspberry, is a compact shrub native to Madagascar.

It features upright, pyramidal growth with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique form, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
