Ranunculus nematolobus

Ranunculus nematolobus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with five petal-like sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
