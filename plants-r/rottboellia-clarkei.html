Rottboellia clarkei

Rottboellia clarkei, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, reddish stems that often turn purplish with age. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Rottboellia clarkei is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and resilience in various soil conditions.
