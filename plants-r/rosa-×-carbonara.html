Rosa × carbonara

Rosa × carbonara, commonly known as the Carbonara rose, is a hybrid shrub native to Italy.

It is characterized by its dark red to almost black flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has thorny stems covered in dense, velvety foliage. Its leaves are dark green and serrated, providing a striking contrast to the deep pink to crimson petals.

The Carbonara rose is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
