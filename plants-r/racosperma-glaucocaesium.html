Racosperma glaucocaesium

Racosperma glaucocaesium, commonly known as the blue-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often blue-green in color due to the presence of a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in globular heads, which are typical of the Acacia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Racosperma glaucocaesium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
