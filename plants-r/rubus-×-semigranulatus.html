Rubus × semigranulatus

Rubus × semigranulatus, commonly known as the rough blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its edible, dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring.

This species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its use in culinary applications.
