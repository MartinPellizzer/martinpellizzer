Rosa gallica f. acuta

Rosa gallica f. acuta, commonly known as the sharp-leaved gallica rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa gallica species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, serrated leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in perfumery.

This variety is relatively hardy and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
