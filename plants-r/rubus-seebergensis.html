Rubus seebergensis

Rubus seebergensis, commonly known as the Seeberg raspberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
