Rosa damasceniformis

Rosa damasceniformis, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a species native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and its stems are covered in thorns.

Rosa damasceniformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and for the production of rose oil, which is used in perfumery.
