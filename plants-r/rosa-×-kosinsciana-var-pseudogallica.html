Rosa × kosinsciana var. pseudogallica

Rosa × kosinsciana var. pseudogallica, commonly known as the Gallica rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa gallica and other Rosa species.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained soil. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for hedges or borders.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to different climates.
