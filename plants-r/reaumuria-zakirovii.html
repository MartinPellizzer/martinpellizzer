Reaumuria zakirovii

Reaumuria zakirovii, commonly known as the Kazakh saxaul, is a shrub native to Central Asia, particularly found in Kazakhstan and parts of Mongolia.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, featuring small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Reaumuria zakirovii plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
