Ropalon sagittatum

Ropalon sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble an arrowhead. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, well-draining soil and partial to full shade.

Ropalon sagittatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
