Ranunculus pseudobinatus

Ranunculus pseudobinatus, commonly known as the pink meadow-ranunculus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and a short, hairy stem that supports its vibrant, cup-shaped blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific conditions to flourish.
