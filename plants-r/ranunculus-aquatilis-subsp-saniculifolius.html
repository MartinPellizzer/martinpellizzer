Ranunculus aquatilis subsp. saniculifolius

Ranunculus aquatilis subsp.

saniculifolius, commonly known as water buttercup, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in shallow waters, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant features delicate, palmately lobed leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are usually borne on long, slender stalks.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
