Rhamnus alaternus var. hederacea

Rhamnus alaternus var. hederacea, commonly known as the creeping buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a small, dark blue berry that contains seeds surrounded by a fleshy pulp.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in difficult conditions.
