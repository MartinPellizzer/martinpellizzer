Rhysolepis laxicymosa

Rhysolepis laxicymosa, commonly known as the silky hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features fine, silky hairs on its leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with brown or purple.

Rhysolepis laxicymosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
