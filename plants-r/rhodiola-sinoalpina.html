Rhodiola sinoalpina

Rhodiola sinoalpina, commonly known as the Tibetan rhodiola, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of Asia, including regions of Tibet, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant thrives in cold, mountainous environments and is adapted to low oxygen levels and intense sunlight. Rhodiola sinoalpina is known for its medicinal properties, containing compounds such as rosavins and salidrosides that are believed to have adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.

It is often used in traditional medicine to enhance physical endurance and reduce fatigue.
