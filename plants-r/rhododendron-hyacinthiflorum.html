Rhododendron hyacinthiflorum

Rhododendron hyacinthiflorum, commonly known as the hyacinth-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces fragrant, hyacinth-like blooms in shades of pink, white, and purple during late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various garden conditions.
