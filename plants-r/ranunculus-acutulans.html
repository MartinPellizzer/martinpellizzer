Ranunculus acutulans

Ranunculus acutulans, commonly known as the sharp-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Ranunculus acutulans is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and sometimes toxic properties.
