Rosa pomifera f. perglandulosa

Rosa pomifera f. perglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular-fruited rose, is a botanical variant of the rose species Rosa pomifera.

It is characterized by its distinctive, glandular-dotted leaves and stems, which give it a unique appearance. The plant produces large, edible fruits known as rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its potential culinary and medicinal uses.
