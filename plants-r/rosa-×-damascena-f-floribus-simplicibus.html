Rosa × damascena f. floribus-simplicibus

Rosa × damascena f. floribus-simplicibus, commonly known as the simple-flowered Damask rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its simple, single-petaled flowers.

It is a subspecies of the Damask rose, which is native to the eastern Mediterranean region. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are fragrant and often used in the production of rose oil and perfume.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
