Rubus stictochlamys

Rubus stictochlamys, commonly known as the blackberry cane or blackcap bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically growing in woodland edges and hedgerows. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruit, a small, dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in jams and preserves.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
