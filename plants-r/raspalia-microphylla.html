Raspalia microphylla

Raspalia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved raspalia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of tiny, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.

Raspalia microphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
