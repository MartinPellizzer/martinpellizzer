Rubus laevefactus

Rubus laevefactus, commonly known as the smooth raspberry.

It is a species of bramble within the Rubus genus, characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
