Rubus × spiculosus

Rubus × spiculosus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia but has been introduced to various regions worldwide. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This species is valued for its edible fruits and its role in ecological systems as a habitat for wildlife.
