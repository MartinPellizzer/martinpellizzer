Rosa gallica f. marmorea

Rosa gallica f. marmorea, commonly known as the white gallica rose, is a hybrid form of the gallica rose native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers with a subtle pinkish or purplish hue, giving it a marble-like appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.

This variety is relatively hardy and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
