Reseda macrosperma var. orientalis

Reseda macrosperma var. orientalis, commonly known as oriental sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
