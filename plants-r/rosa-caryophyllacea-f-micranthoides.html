Rosa caryophyllacea f. micranthoides

Rosa caryophyllacea f. micranthoides, commonly known as the dwarf pink or miniature rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa caryophyllacea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more delicate flowers compared to the typical form. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins, typical of the Rosa genus. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a prominent central pistil.

This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its compact size and aesthetic appeal.
