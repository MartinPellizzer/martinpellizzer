Rosa × pervalida

Rosa × pervalida, commonly known as the Alba rose, is a hybrid rose species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa × alba.

It is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers, which are typically borne in clusters and have a delicate fragrance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Rosa × pervalida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
