Raphidiocystis sakalavensis

Raphidiocystis sakalavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar stromatolite-forming cyanobacterium, is a unique phototrophic organism found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its ability to form complex, layered structures known as stromatolites, which are primarily composed of calcium carbonate and organic matter. This cyanobacterium plays a significant role in the biogeochemical cycling of nutrients in its habitat. Its morphology includes filamentous growth with specialized cells for nitrogen fixation and carbon dioxide fixation.

Raphidiocystis sakalavensis is of particular interest to scientists due to its potential implications for understanding early Earth ecosystems and the evolution of life.
