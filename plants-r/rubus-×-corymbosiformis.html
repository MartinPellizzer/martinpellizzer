Rubus × corymbosiformis

Rubus × corymbosiformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
