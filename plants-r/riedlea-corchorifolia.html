Riedlea corchorifolia

Riedlea corchorifolia, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
