Rubus surrectus

Rubus surrectus, commonly known as the upright blackberry.

It is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing characteristics with other brambles.
