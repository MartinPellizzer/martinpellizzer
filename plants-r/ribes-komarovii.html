Ribes komarovii

Ribes komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants commonly referred to as currants or gooseberries. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by edible berries that range in color from red to purple. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Ribes komarovii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
