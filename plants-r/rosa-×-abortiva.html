Rosa × abortiva

Rosa × abortiva, commonly known as the swamp rose, is a hybrid species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and riverbanks. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces showy, pink to red flowers in the spring. Its stems are often covered in thorns, and it is known for its ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils.

Rosa × abortiva plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
