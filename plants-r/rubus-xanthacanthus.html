Rubus xanthacanthus

Rubus xanthacanthus, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe, with a sweet and tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and edible fruits, though it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
