Rapuntium hyssopifolium

Rapuntium hyssopifolium, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and spiny, globe-shaped heads of purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open landscapes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
