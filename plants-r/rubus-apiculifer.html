Rubus apiculifer

Rubus apiculifer, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple tiny drupes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is native to parts of Asia and North America. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit is often used in jams and desserts due to its tart flavor.
