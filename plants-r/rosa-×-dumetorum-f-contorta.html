Rosa × dumetorum f. contorta

Rosa × dumetorum f. contorta, commonly known as the bent-leaved dogrose, is a botanical variant of the dogrose species.

It is characterized by its twisted, often spiraled leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces small, fragrant pink or white flowers in the spring. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental foliage and modest floral display.
