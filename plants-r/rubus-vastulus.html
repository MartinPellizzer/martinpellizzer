Rubus vastulus

Rubus vastulus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has a vigorous growth habit. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Rubus vastulus is valued for its edible fruits and is often found in forest understories.
