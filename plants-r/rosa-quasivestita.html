Rosa quasivestita

Rosa quasivestita, commonly known as the "velvet rose," is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive velvety texture.

Rosa quasivestita is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
