Ranunculus sewerzowii

Ranunculus sewerzowii, commonly known as the Siberian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, palmate leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to alpine environments. Its flowers bloom in early summer, and it is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its native range.
