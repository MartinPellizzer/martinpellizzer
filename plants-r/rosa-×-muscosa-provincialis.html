Rosa × muscosa provincialis

Rosa × muscosa provincialis, commonly known as the Provincial Musk Rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, glossy leaves. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
