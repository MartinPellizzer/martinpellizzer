Rhododendron durionifolium

Rhododendron durionifolium, commonly known as the Durionleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron durionifolium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
