Ranunculus pygmaeus var. typicus

Ranunculus pygmaeus var. typicus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, and its leaves are palmately divided into three to five lobes. It produces small, green, oval fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
