Rubus × cuspidifolius

Rubus × cuspidifolius, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

This species is valued for its edible berries, which are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in culinary applications.
