Rosa rubiginosa var. apricorum

Rosa rubiginosa var. apricorum, commonly known as the apricot rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to orange-red flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its bark is rugged and grayish in color.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical use in perfumery and medicine.
