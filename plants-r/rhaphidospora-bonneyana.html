Rhaphidospora bonneyana

Rhaphidospora bonneyana, commonly known as the Bonney's spore fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its intricate and delicate fronds, which bear small, spore-producing structures called sporangia. The plant exhibits a distinctive reproductive system, with its sporangia clustered in specialized clusters called sori. Its botanical features make it an important subject for studying fern evolution and spore dispersal mechanisms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
