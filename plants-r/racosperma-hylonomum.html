Racosperma hylonomum

Racosperma hylonomum, commonly known as the white-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds used for propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation and providing habitat for native wildlife.
