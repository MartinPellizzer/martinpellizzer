Ranunculus egglestonii

Ranunculus egglestonii, commonly known as the Eggleston's buttercup, is a rare flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest floors or stream banks. It has palmately divided leaves and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered in many regions.
