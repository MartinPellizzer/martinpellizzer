Rubus serpens microgene corylinus

Rubus serpens microgene corylinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple or black when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive narrow shape with serrated edges.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
