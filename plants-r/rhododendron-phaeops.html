Rhododendron phaeops

Rhododendron phaeops, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and often have a slight pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in trusses, and range in color from pink to red, blooming in late spring.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
