Rosa caryocarpa

Rosa caryocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese rose or the everblooming rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits called hips, which are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Rosa caryocarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its continuous bloom and aromatic fragrance.

Its botanical features make it a unique and versatile plant in both horticultural and medicinal contexts.
