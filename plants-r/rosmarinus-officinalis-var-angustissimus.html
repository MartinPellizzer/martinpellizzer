Rosmarinus officinalis var. angustissimus

Rosmarinus officinalis var. angustissimus, commonly known as narrow-leaf rosemary, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its essential oils, rich in camphor and pinene, contribute to its traditional uses in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.
