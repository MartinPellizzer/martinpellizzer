Rubus foliosus var. flexuosus

Rubus foliosus var. flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically lobed and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus, known for its adaptability and ecological significance.
