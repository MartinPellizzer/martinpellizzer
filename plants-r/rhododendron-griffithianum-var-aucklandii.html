Rhododendron griffithianum var. aucklandii

Rhododendron griffithianum var. aucklandii, commonly known as the Auckland rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
