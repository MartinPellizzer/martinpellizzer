Rosa villosa var. meridionalis

Rosa villosa var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern hairy raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces edible, red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both landscape design and herbal use.
