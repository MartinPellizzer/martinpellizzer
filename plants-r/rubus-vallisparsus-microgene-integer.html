Rubus vallisparsus microgene integer

Rubus vallisparsus microgene integer, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of brambles native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
