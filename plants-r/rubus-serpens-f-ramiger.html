Rubus serpens f. ramiger

Rubus serpens f. ramiger, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its trailing growth habit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This form is notable for its vigorous spreading nature and is sometimes used in ornamental landscaping.
