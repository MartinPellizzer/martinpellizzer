Rubus hirtus var. urumoffii

Rubus hirtus var. urumoffii, commonly known as the Urumoff's bramble, is a thorny shrub native to the temperate regions of eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed areas. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it blooms in the spring.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
