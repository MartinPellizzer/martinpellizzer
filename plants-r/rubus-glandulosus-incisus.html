Rubus glandulosus incisus

Rubus glandulosus incisus, commonly known as the glandular-fruited blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing food and habitat.
