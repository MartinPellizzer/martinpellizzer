Rhododendron arboreum subsp. zeylanicum

Rhododendron arboreum subsp.

zeylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Asia, particularly Sri Lanka and parts of India. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from red to pink in color.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid climate.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
