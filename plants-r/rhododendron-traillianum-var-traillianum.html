Rhododendron traillianum var. traillianum

Rhododendron traillianum var. traillianum, commonly known as the Traill's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.
