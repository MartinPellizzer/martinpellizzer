Rubus plicatus var. aciliensis

Rubus plicatus var. aciliensis, commonly known as the Aciliensis blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated in home gardens for its ornamental and culinary uses.
