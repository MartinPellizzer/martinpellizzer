Rhododendron verticillatum subsp. carinatum

Rhododendron verticillatum subsp.

carinatum, commonly known as the Carinate Rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the eastern Himalayas. It features dense, leathery foliage with dark green leaves that are often adorned with a prominent midrib.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
