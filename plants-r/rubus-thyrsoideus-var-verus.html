Rubus thyrsoideus var. verus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. verus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of brambles native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its arching stems with thorny tips and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when ripe. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
