Reaumuria hyrcanica

Reaumuria hyrcanica, commonly known as the Hyrcanian tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by insects. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert and steppe environments.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources in harsh conditions.
