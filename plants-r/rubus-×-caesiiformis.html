Rubus × caesiiformis

Rubus × caesiiformis, commonly known as the purple-leafed raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its distinctive deep purple foliage and red-orange berries. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant flowers in the spring.

The botanical features of Rubus × caesiiformis make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
