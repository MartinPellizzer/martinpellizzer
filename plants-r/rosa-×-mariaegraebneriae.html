Rosa × mariaegraebneriae

Rosa × mariaegraebneriae, commonly known as the Maria Graebneria rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink to red flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Due to its specific hybrid origin, it is relatively rare and often found in specialized horticultural collections.
