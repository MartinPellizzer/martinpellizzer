Raphanistrum

Raphanistrum, commonly known as water dropwort, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It consists of several species that are typically found in wet or marshy environments, often along the edges of ponds and streams. The plants are characterized by their erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Their leaves are usually alternate and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Raphanistrum species are valued for their ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
