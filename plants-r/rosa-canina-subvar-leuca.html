Rosa canina subvar. leuca

Rosa canina subvar. leuca, commonly known as the white dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its white flowers, which are typically more showy than those of the typical rose. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and pinnate leaves. It produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are used in herbal remedies.

This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in woodland areas and along hedgerows.
