Rubus brachyandrus subsp. populifolius

Rubus brachyandrus subsp.

populifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant produces red, raspberry-like fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
