Rubus pseudocaesius var. serratifolius

Rubus pseudocaesius var. serratifolius, commonly known as the serratedleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other brambles and blackberry species.
