Rubus vallisparsus var. pseudolejeunei

Rubus vallisparsus var. pseudolejeunei, commonly known as the Florida blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, often forming dense thickets. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. Its fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.

The species is notable for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in local ecosystems.
