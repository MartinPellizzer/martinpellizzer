Rubus tereticaulis var. serpentiformis

Rubus tereticaulis var. serpentiformis, commonly known as the serpentine raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, twisting stems that resemble a serpent, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
