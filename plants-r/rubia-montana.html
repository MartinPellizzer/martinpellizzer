Rubia montana

Rubia montana, commonly known as mountain sorrel, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and mountainous regions.

Rubia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
