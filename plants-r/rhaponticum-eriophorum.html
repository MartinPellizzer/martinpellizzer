Rhaponticum eriophorum

Rhaponticum eriophorum, commonly known as the Siberian cow-wheat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Rhaponticum eriophorum is often found in tundra and mountainous environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
