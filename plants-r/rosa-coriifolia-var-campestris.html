Rosa coriifolia var. campestris

Rosa coriifolia var. campestris, commonly known as the field rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces distinctive, velvety rose hips that are red-orange in color and contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to elliptical leaflets that have serrated edges.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance and fragrant blooms.
