Rosa × damascena

Rosa × damascena, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of rose oil.

The flowers are used in perfumery, culinary applications, and traditional medicine.
