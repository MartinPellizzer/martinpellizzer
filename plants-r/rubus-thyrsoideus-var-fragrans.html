Rubus thyrsoideus var. fragrans

Rubus thyrsoideus var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they emit a sweet, aromatic scent.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its edible fruits.
