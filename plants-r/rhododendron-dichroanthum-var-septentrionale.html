Rhododendron dichroanthum var. septentrionale

Rhododendron dichroanthum var. septentrionale, commonly known as the northern rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
