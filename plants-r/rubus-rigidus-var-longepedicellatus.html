Rubus rigidus var. longepedicellatus

Rubus rigidus var. longepedicellatus, commonly known as the long pedicellate blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rigid, thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is often used in jams and desserts due to its sweet, aromatic flavor.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
