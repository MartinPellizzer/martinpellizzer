Rubus thyrsoideus var. degener

Rubus thyrsoideus var. degener, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which help it climb or spread along the ground. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types, making it a resilient plant in wild and cultivated settings.
