Rubus pseudopileatus var. kangdingensis

Rubus pseudopileatus var. kangdingensis, commonly known as the Kangding raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry genus native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
