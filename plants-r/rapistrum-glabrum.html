Rapistrum glabrum

Rapistrum glabrum, commonly known as common velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is commonly found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Rapistrum glabrum is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a potential weed in agricultural settings.
