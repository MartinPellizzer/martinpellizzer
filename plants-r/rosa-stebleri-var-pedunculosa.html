Rosa stebleri var. pedunculosa

Rosa stebleri var. pedunculosa, commonly known as the pedunculate rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It features upright, thorny stems and is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that range from white to deep pink. The plant produces showy, clustered blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it has a long blooming period.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
