Rosa pouzinii subsp. almeriensis

Rosa pouzinii subsp.

almeriensis, commonly known as the Almería rose, is a subspecies native to southern Spain, particularly in the region of Almería. It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
