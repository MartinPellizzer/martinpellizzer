Ricinocarpus glabratus f. pilosior

Ricinocarpus glabratus f. pilosior, commonly known as the hairy sea grape, is a variant of the sea grape plant native to tropical coastal regions.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the family Arecaceae, characterized by its clustered, fleshy, fruit-like structures called drupes. The plant thrives in saline environments and is often found in mangrove swamps and tidal zones. This plant plays a role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for marine life.
