Rhynchosia rudolfii

Rhynchosia rudolfii, commonly known as the African spiderflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers with long, slender petals that resemble spider legs. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually ovate or lanceolate in shape.

Rhynchosia rudolfii is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and unique floral display.
