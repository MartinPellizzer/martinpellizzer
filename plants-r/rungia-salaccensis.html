Rungia salaccensis

Rungia salaccensis, commonly known as the Java mint, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Rungia salaccensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
