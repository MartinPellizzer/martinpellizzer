Rosa wulfenii var. genuina

Rosa wulfenii var. genuina, commonly known as the Alaska rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, serrated leaves and produces rose hips that are typically red or orange in color. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and tundra environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
