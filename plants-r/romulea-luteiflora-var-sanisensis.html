Romulea luteiflora var. sanisensis

Romulea luteiflora var. sanisensis, commonly known as the San Isidro glory flower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South America, specifically Ecuador.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded conditions, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
