Ranunculus auricomus subsp. leptophyes

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

leptophyes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals and a central cluster of stamens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of *Ranunculus auricomus*. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.
