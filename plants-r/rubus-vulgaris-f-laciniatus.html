Rubus vulgaris f. laciniatus

Rubus vulgaris f. laciniatus, commonly known as the cutleaf blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in horticulture and ecological studies.
