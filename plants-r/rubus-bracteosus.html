Rubus bracteosus

Rubus bracteosus, commonly known as the bracteate blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and prickly stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

Rubus bracteosus is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.
