Ranunculus armingfordensis

Ranunculus armingfordensis, commonly known as the Armington's buttercup, is a rare and flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
