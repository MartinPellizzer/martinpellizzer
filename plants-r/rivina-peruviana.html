Rivina peruviana

Rivina peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian ivy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
