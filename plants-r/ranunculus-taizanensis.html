Ranunculus taizanensis

Ranunculus taizanensis, commonly known as the Taizan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, hairy fruits that contain multiple seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
