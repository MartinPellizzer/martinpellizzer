Rosa × implicata

Rosa × implicata, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a hybrid species within the rose family (Rosaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant is native to China and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces rose hips that are edible and rich in vitamin C. Rosa × implicata is well-suited for hedges and garden borders due to its vigorous growth and adaptability to various soil types.
