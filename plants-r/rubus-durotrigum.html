Rubus durotrigum

Rubus durotrigum, commonly known as the Scottish blackberry, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the British Isles and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are usually covered in stiff, prickly hairs.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local folklore and traditional medicine.
