Rosa spinosissima var. glabriuscula

Rosa spinosissima var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed wild rose, is a thorny shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, glabrous (smooth) stems and oval, dark green leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is a red, fuzzy rose hip that matures in autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
