Rapum

Rapum, commonly known as turnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It produces a swollen, edible root that is typically white or purple in color. The plant also has bright green, lobed leaves that are edible and often used in cooking. Rapum thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated in temperate regions.

Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in clusters, typical of the mustard family.
