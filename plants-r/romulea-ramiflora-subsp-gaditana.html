Romulea ramiflora subsp. gaditana

Romulea ramiflora subsp.

gaditana, commonly known as the Iberian romulea, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It features a compact, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent.

The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different flower structure and habitat preferences compared to the main species.
