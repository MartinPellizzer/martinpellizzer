Rumex abyssinicus var. kilimandschari

Rumex abyssinicus var. kilimandschari, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro sorrel, is a perennial herb native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes.

Its leaves are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for their tart flavor and potential health benefits.
