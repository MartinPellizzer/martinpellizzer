Rubus nemorosus f. umbrosus

Rubus nemorosus f. umbrosus, commonly known as the shade raspberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in woodland environments, preferring moist, shaded conditions.

Botanically, it is notable for its horticultural value and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
