Rhynchospora panicoides

Rhynchospora panicoides, commonly known as the panicled flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often subtended by bracts.

Rhynchospora panicoides plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
