Rosa glandulosopunctata

Rosa glandulosopunctata, commonly known as the glandular-punctuated rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with glandular dots, which give it its scientific name. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa glandulosopunctata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
