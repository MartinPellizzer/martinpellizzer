Reseda luteola subsp. gussonei

Reseda luteola subsp.

gussonei, commonly known as the Sicilian wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found on cliffs and walls.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
