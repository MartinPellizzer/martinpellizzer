Rosettea longicaudata

Rosettea longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed rosette, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the rainforests of South America.

This plant is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup, which collects and stores water. The leaves are typically green with a waxy coating, aiding in water retention in its humid, shaded habitat. It produces vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often appearing at the base of the rosette.

Rosettea longicaudata is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.
