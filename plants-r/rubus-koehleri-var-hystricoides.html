Rubus koehleri var. hystricoides

Rubus koehleri var. hystricoides, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some horticultural gardens.
