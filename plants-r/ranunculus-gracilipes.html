Ranunculus gracilipes

Ranunculus gracilipes, commonly known as the slender buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive central pistil. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often preceding the leaves, and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

Ranunculus gracilipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
