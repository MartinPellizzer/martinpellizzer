Ranunculus varangerensis

Ranunculus varangerensis, commonly known as the Varanger buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils in tundra and alpine environments. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool climates with short growing seasons.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
