Rhododendron lauterbachianum

Rhododendron lauterbachianum, commonly known as the Lauterbach rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 3,500 meters. The plant features showy, clustered inflorescences with bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that helps retain moisture in its alpine habitat.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific soil and climate conditions.
