Randia hispida

Randia hispida, commonly known as rough randia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its fruit is a small, berry-like structure that turns red when mature.

Randia hispida is often used in ornamental gardening due to its compact growth and colorful blooms.
