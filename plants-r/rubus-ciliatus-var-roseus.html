Rubus ciliatus var. roseus

Rubus ciliatus var. roseus, commonly known as the rosehip bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as rosehips, which are typically red or orange in color. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural purposes.
