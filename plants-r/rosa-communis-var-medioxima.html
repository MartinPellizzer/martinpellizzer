Rosa communis var. medioxima

Rosa communis var. medioxima, commonly known as the common rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to deep red. The plant is native to various regions in Europe and Asia and is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes. It produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing seeds, and is known for its thorny stems.

This variety is often used in perfumery and culinary applications due to its aromatic properties.
