Rubus arrhenii subsp. arrhenianthus

Rubus arrhenii subsp.

arrhenianthus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, aggregate fruits that mature to a deep red color.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins, while its flowers are white with five petals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
