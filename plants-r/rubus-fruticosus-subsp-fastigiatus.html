Rubus fruticosus subsp. fastigiatus

Rubus fruticosus subsp.

fastigiatus, commonly known as the upright blackberry bush, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, black berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its berries are used in jams and wines, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
