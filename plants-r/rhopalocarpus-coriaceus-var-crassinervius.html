Rhopalocarpus coriaceus var. crassinervius

Rhopalocarpus coriaceus var. crassinervius, commonly known as the thick-veined rhopalocarpus, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive yellowish coloration.
