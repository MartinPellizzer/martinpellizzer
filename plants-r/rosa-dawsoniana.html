Rosa dawsoniana

Rosa dawsoniana, commonly known as the Dawson's rose, is a species of shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, fragrant pink to white flowers, and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or mountainous areas. It produces rose hips that are orange-red in color and contain seeds.

Rosa dawsoniana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
