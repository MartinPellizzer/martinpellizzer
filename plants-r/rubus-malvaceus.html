Rubus malvaceus

Rubus malvaceus, commonly known as the Malvaceae raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rubiaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits that resemble raspberries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals, and it produces a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
