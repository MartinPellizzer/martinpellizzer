Rhaphiostylis madagascariensis

Rhaphiostylis madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, producing multiple stems that bear the showy inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in montane rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
