Ryssopterys

Ryssopterys, commonly known as the broad-leaf fern, is a genus of ferns belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plants typically thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Ryssopterys species have rhizomatous growth, allowing them to spread and form dense colonies.

Their spore-bearing structures, called sori, are usually located on the undersides of the fronds, contributing to their reproductive strategy.
