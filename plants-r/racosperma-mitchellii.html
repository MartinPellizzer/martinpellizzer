Racosperma mitchellii

Racosperma mitchellii, commonly known as Mitchell's acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its seeds are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices and have cultural significance.
