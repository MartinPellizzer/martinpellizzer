Rubus × semirudis

Rubus × semirudis, commonly known as the wild blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
