Rubus oligothrix

Rubus oligothrix, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and produces white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
