Ranunculus revelierei var. rodiei

Ranunculus revelierei var. rodiei, commonly known as the Sierra Madre buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
