Racosperma ancistrophyllum var. lissophyllum

Racosperma ancistrophyllum var. lissophyllum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the legume family. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
