Rubus tozawae

Rubus tozawae, commonly known as the Tozawa bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
