Rubus neonefrens

Rubus neonefrens, commonly known as the Neotropical blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental and edible qualities, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
