Racosperma glandulicarpum

Racosperma glandulicarpum, commonly known as the glandular-fruited acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that are covered in glandular structures, giving it its scientific name. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma glandulicarpum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
