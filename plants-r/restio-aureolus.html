Restio aureolus

Restio aureolus, commonly known as the golden restio, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Restio aureolus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
