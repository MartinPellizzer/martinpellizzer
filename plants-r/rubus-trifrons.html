Rubus trifrons

Rubus trifrons, commonly known as the three-faced blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifid (three-lobed) leaves and produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and grows in moist, shaded areas of woodlands. Its flowers are white and have five petals, and it blooms in early spring.

The berries are often used in jams and desserts, though they are less common than other blackberry species.
