Racosperma nanodealbatum

Racosperma nanodealbatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features feathery, bipinnate leaves that are typically narrow and green. It produces bright yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Racosperma nanodealbatum is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
