Rubus argenteus subsp. callimorphus

Rubus argenteus subsp.

callimorphus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The shrub is often found in woodland edges and rocky slopes, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
