Rosa × oetoea

Rosa × oetoea, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa chinensis.

It is characterized by its continuous blooming habit, producing flowers throughout the growing season. The plant typically features dark green, glossy foliage and sturdy, thorned stems. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Rosa × oetoea is well-suited for coastal areas due to its salt tolerance and hardiness.
