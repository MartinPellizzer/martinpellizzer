Rytidosperma gracile

Rytidosperma gracile, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rytidosperma gracile is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
