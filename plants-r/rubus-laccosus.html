Rubus laccosus

Rubus laccosus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of brambles native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.
