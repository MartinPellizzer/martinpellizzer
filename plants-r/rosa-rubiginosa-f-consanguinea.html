Rosa rubiginosa f. consanguinea

Rosa rubiginosa f. consanguinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed rose, is a horticultural form of the shrub rose characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems and dense, thorny growth.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its foliage is dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
