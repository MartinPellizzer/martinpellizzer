Rhizophora × pachypoda

Rhizophora × pachypoda, commonly known as the red mangrove.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rhizophora mangle and Rhizophora stylosa. This plant is characterized by its distinctive aerial roots, which provide structural support and facilitate gas exchange in waterlogged soils. It thrives in brackish and saltwater environments, playing a crucial role in coastal ecosystems.

Its ability to tolerate high salinity and periodic flooding makes it an important component of mangrove forests.
