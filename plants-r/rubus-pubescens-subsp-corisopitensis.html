Rubus pubescens subsp. corisopitensis

Rubus pubescens subsp.

corisopitensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in woodland and rocky habitats, often in areas with well-drained soil.

Its berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
