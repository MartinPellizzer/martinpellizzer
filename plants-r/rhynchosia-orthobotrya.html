Rhynchosia orthobotrya

Rhynchosia orthobotrya, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Rhynchosia orthobotrya is valued for its potential in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
