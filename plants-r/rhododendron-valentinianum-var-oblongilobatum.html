Rhododendron valentinianum var. oblongilobatum

Rhododendron valentinianum var. oblongilobatum, commonly known as the Valentine's rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical to oblong-lanceolate in shape, providing a lush green backdrop. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
