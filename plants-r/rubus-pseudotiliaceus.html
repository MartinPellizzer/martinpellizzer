Rubus pseudotiliaceus

Rubus pseudotiliaceus, commonly known as the western blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the berries are dark purple to black when mature.

The species is valued for its edible fruits, which are often used in jams and desserts.
