Rubus tomentosus var. angustifolius

Rubus tomentosus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with a hairy, grayish underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
