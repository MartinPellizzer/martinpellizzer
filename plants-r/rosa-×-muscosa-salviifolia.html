Rosa × muscosa salviifolia

Rosa × muscosa salviifolia, commonly known as the Salviifolia Rosa, is a hybrid rose species that combines characteristics of its parent species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is distinguished by its large, velvety pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the blossoms.

Rosa × muscosa salviifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
