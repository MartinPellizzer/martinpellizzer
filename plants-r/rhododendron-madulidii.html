Rhododendron madulidii

Rhododendron madulidii, commonly known as the Madulidii rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to deep red.

Rhododendron madulidii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
