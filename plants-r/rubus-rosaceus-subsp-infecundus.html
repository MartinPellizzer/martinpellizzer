Rubus rosaceus subsp. infecundus

Rubus rosaceus subsp.

infecundus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
