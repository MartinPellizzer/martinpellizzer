Rubus radula var. bracteatus

Rubus radula var. bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit, which is a small, dark berry, is edible. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinator populations.
