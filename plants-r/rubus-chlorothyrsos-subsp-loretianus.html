Rubus chlorothyrsos subsp. loretianus

Rubus chlorothyrsos subsp.

loretianus, commonly known as the Loretto blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
