Rubus podophyllos var. incanus

Rubus podophyllos var. incanus, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a shrub native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is known for its thorny stems and its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative growth.
