Rubus × exspectatus

Rubus × exspectatus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a natural hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus pectoralis (raspberry). The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically a mix of red and black in color, with a tart and sweet flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has thorny stems typical of many Rubus species.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses in both home and commercial settings.
