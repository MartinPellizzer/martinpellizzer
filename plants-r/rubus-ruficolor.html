Rubus ruficolor

Rubus ruficolor, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature, though they may turn black as they ripen. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is valued not only for its berries but also for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
