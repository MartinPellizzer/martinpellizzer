Rubus hirtus f. borealis

Rubus hirtus f. borealis, commonly known as the northern bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of North America. The plant features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are similar to those of its parent species.

This subspecies is adapted to colder climates and is often found in moist, wooded areas.
