Rosa subnuda

Rosa subnuda, commonly known as the naked rose, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, usually thornless stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces oval, green leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically pink or white. Rosa subnuda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to gardens and landscapes.
