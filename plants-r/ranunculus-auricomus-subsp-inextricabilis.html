Ranunculus auricomus subsp. inextricabilis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

inextricabilis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with five broad, petal-like sepals, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy texture. It has a short, upright stem and is known for its hardiness in cold climates.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
