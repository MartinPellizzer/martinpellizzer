Rhus schliebenii

Rhus schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like poison ivy and mango. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, typically ranging from 5 to 15 inches in length. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that attract birds and other wildlife.

Rhus schliebenii is often found in open woods, along stream banks, and in disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
