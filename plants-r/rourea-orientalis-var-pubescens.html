Rourea orientalis var. pubescens

Rourea orientalis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy rourea, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, with a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, clustered in inflorescences, and attract various pollinators.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in tropical and subtropical climates.
