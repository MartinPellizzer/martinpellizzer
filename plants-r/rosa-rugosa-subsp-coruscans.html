Rosa rugosa subsp. coruscans

Rosa rugosa subsp.

coruscans, commonly known as the glittering rose, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea. It is characterized by its rugged, thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange-red hips. It is well adapted to coastal environments and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rosa genus.
