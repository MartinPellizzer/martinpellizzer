Rudgea villiflora

Rudgea villiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf rudgea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has a woody stem and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife.

Rudgea villiflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid climates.
