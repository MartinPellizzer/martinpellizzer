Ruellia ciliata

Ruellia ciliata, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming throughout the growing season. Ruellia ciliata is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
