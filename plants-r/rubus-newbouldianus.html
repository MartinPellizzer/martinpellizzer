Rubus newbouldianus

Rubus newbouldianus, commonly known as the Newbould's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often described as having a tart and somewhat astringent flavor.
