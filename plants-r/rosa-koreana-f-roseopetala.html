Rosa koreana f. roseopetala

Rosa koreana f. roseopetala, commonly known as the Korean rose with rose-colored petals, is a cultivar of the Korean rose, native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to rose-red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with arching stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are semi-double to fully double, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
