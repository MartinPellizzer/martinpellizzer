Rubus mucronipetalus

Rubus mucronipetalus, commonly known as the blunt-petaled blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that mature into dark purple or black berries. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and small-scale agriculture.
