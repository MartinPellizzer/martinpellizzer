Rhododendron sparsiflorum

Rhododendron sparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparseflower rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist habitats such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, and its flowers are usually pink or red, with five to seven petals. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
