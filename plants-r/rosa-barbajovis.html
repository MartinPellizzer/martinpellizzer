Rosa barbajovis

Rosa barbajovis, commonly known as the Barbajovis rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rosa barbajovis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
