Rubus hedycarpus var. procerus

Rubus hedycarpus var. procerus, commonly known as the velvetleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and thorny stems, which provide structural support and defense against herbivores. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as berries, that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its leaves are usually deeply lobed and have a hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
