Rytigynia butaguensis

Rytigynia butaguensis, commonly known as the Butaguense shrub, is a woody plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
