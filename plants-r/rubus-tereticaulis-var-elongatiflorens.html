Rubus tereticaulis var. elongatiflorens

Rubus tereticaulis var. elongatiflorens, commonly known as the long-flowered blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark purple berries in the fall. Its berries are edible, though often tart, and are used in jams and wines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
