Rubus guentheri var. mirabilis

Rubus guentheri var. mirabilis, commonly known as the Guenther's raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
