Rubus cerdicii

Rubus cerdicii, commonly known as the Cerdic strawberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the Iberian Peninsula, specifically found in Spain and Portugal. This plant is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically red in color and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The shrubs produce trifoliate leaves and have thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in rocky, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
