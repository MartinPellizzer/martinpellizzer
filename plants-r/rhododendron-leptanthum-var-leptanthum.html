Rhododendron leptanthum var. leptanthum

Rhododendron leptanthum var. leptanthum, commonly known as the slender-leaved rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are densely packed along its stems. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
