Raddia lacunosa

Raddia lacunosa, commonly known as the hollow-stemmed raddia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Raddia lacunosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and traditional herbal practices.
