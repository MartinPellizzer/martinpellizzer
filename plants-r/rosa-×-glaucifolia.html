Rosa × glaucifolia

Rosa × glaucifolia, commonly known as the bluebush rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which often displays a bluish-gray hue due to the presence of a waxy coating on the leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

Rosa × glaucifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
