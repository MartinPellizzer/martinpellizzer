Rubus cryptadenus

Rubus cryptadenus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

The berries are edible but often have a tart flavor and are used in local foraging traditions.
