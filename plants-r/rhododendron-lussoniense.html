Rhododendron lussoniense

Rhododendron lussoniense, commonly known as the Lusson's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red, depending on the variety.

Rhododendron lussoniense is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
