Rhododendron speciosum var. aurantium

Rhododendron speciosum var. aurantium, commonly known as the golden rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to yellow, bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to woodland gardens.

The varietal form 'aurantium' is distinguished by its more intense yellow floral hue compared to the typical species.
