Rorippa terrestris

Rorippa terrestris, commonly known as the common watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as ditches, ponds, and stream banks. The plant has alternate, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its roots are fibrous, and it is often found in nutrient-rich, shallow water.

Rorippa terrestris is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed wetlands.
