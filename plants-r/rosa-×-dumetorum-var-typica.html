Rosa × dumetorum var. typica

Rosa × dumetorum var. typica, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and the stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
