Ruellia leucantha

Ruellia leucantha, commonly known as white ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are characterized by their white to pale blue coloration.

Ruellia leucantha is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
