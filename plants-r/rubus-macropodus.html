Rubus macropodus

Rubus macropodus, commonly known as the giant blackberry or Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces large, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in stiff, hooked prickles.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
