Rubus × sparsiglandulus

Rubus × sparsiglandulus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible. It is native to the western United States and thrives in temperate climates.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex hybrid origin and adaptability to various soil types.
