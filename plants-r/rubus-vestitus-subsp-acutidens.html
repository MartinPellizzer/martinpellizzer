Rubus vestitus subsp. acutidens

Rubus vestitus subsp.

acutidens, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
