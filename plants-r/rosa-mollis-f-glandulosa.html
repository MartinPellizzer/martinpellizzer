Rosa mollis f. glandulosa

Rosa mollis f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular evergreen rose, is a hybrid form of the evergreen rose species Rosa mollis.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that is covered in small, glandular dots, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with partial shade.

This compact shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a foundation plant.
