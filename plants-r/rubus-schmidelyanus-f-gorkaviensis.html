Rubus schmidelyanus f. gorkaviensis

Rubus schmidelyanus f. gorkaviensis, commonly known as the Gorkhali raspberry, is a botanical variant of the Schmidelyanus form of the Rubus genus.

It is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions, particularly in Nepal and northern India. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, similar to other raspberry species. It has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and thorny stems typical of the Rubus family.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
