Rhaphithamnus elegans

Rhaphithamnus elegans, commonly known as the slender leafed thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and spiny stems, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for erosion control in dry areas.
