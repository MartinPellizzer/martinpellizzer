Ranunculus heucherifolius var. villosus

Ranunculus heucherifolius var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features showy yellow flowers with a distinctive green center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has deeply lobed, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plant species.
