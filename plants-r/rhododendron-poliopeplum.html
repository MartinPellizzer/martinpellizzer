Rhododendron poliopeplum

Rhododendron poliopeplum, commonly known as the purple-veined rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in dense, shaded forest understories where it thrives in moist, acidic soil. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with distinctive purple veins.

Rhododendron poliopeplum is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
