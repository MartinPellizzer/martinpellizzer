Rubus pilosus var. macroacanthus

Rubus pilosus var. macroacanthus, commonly known as the large-thorned blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, prickly bracts and dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and is often found in the eastern United States.
