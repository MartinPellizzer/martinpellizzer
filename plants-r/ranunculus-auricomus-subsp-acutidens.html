Ranunculus auricomus subsp. acutidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

acutidens, commonly known as the sharp-toothed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically borne on slender stems.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in the Ranunculaceae family.
