Rhododendron sanguineum var. haemaleum

Rhododendron sanguineum var. haemaleum, commonly known as the red alpine azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking red to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained, acidic soil.
