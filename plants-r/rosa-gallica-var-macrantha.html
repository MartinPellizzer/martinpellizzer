Rosa gallica var. macrantha

Rosa gallica var. macrantha, commonly known as the French rose or the everblooming rose, is a variety of the Gallica rose that is native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and its ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves and produces large, fragrant pink to red flowers. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its historical significance in perfumery.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it a popular choice for both landscape design and cut flower arrangements.
