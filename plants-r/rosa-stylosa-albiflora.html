Rosa stylosa albiflora

Rosa stylosa albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces reddish-orange hips in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
