Racosperma pruinocarpum

Racosperma pruinocarpum, commonly known as the purple wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Racosperma pruinocarpum is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
