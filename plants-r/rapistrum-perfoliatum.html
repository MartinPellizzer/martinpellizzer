Rapistrum perfoliatum

Rapistrum perfoliatum, commonly known as common knotgrass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and stems that often form a dense mat. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy surface and have a distinctive central vein. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to various regions around the world.
