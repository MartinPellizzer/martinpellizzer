Rampinia

Rampinia, commonly known as broadleaf arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and wetlands, where it thrives in humid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or white, followed by spadices that contain the true flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Rampinia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
