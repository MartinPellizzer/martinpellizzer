Rosa alpina var. gallaecica

Rosa alpina var. gallaecica, commonly known as the Galician Alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in early summer. The plant has dark green, trifoliate leaves and is adapted to harsh alpine conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems make it a notable botanical specimen.
