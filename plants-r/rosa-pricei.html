Rosa pricei

Rosa pricei, commonly known as the Price's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces edible hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa pricei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
