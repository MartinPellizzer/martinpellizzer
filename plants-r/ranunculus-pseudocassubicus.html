Ranunculus pseudocassubicus

Ranunculus pseudocassubicus, commonly known as the Alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish to orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Ranunculus pseudocassubicus is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.
