Rhus scytophylla var. dentata

Rhus scytophylla var. dentata, commonly known as the coast live oak, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically lobed or serrated, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clustered, inedible berries that are often used by wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with moderate rainfall.

This species is an important component of chaparral and coastal scrub ecosystems.
