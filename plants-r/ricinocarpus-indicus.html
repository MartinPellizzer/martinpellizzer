Ricinocarpus indicus

Ricinocarpus indicus, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
