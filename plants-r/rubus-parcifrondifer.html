Rubus parcifrondifer

Rubus parcifrondifer, commonly known as the fragrant leaf raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive arrangement of small, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
