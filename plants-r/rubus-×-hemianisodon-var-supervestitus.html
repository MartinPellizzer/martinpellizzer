Rubus × hemianisodon var. supervestitus

Rubus × hemianisodon var. supervestitus, commonly known as the red-berried bramble, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing multiple seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and horticultural interest.
