Rosa gallica proles rubra

Rosa gallica proles rubra, commonly known as the crimson gallica rose, is a species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of red and pink, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the flowers are known for their strong, sweet scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicinal applications.
