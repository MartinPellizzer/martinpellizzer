Ranunculus lomatocarpus var. cornutus

Ranunculus lomatocarpus var. cornutus, commonly known as the beakhead buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, beak-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.
