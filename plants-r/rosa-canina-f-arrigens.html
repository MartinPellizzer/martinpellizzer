Rosa canina f. arrigens

Rosa canina f. arrigens, commonly known as the bristly dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive bristly hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens. It is native to Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
