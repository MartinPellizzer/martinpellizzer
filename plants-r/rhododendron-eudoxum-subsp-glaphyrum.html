Rhododendron eudoxum subsp. glaphyrum

Rhododendron eudoxum subsp.

glaphyrum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of epiphytic plants.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
