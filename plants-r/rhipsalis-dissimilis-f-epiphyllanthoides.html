Rhipsalis dissimilis f. epiphyllanthoides

Rhipsalis dissimilis f. epiphyllanthoides, commonly known as the epiphyllanthoides cactus, is a unique hybrid of the genus Rhipsalis.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in humid tropical environments. The plant features long, slender stems that are often covered in small, white or pale green spines. Its leaves are modified into short, thick segments, and it produces small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, well-draining conditions.
