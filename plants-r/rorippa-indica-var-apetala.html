Rorippa indica var. apetala

Rorippa indica var. apetala, commonly known as the Indian watercress, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Asia.

It typically grows in moist soils along rivers, ponds, and wetlands. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are usually submerged or floating, with no true flowers, as indicated by its varietal name "apetala.". It produces small, inconspicuous fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and have a peppery flavor.
