Rubus × giganteus

Rubus × giganteus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a hybrid plant species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its large, thorny canes and dark purple to black berries that are typically larger than those of its parent species. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a complex flowering structure that produces showy white or pink flowers.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a fruit crop in suitable regions.
