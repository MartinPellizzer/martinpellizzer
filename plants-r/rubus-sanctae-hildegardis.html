Rubus sanctae-hildegardis

Rubus sanctae-hildegardis, commonly known as Hildegarde's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small and tart, often used in local culinary traditions. Native to specific regions in Europe, it thrives in woodland edges and moist soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and historical studies.
