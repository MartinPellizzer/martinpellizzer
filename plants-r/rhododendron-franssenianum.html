Rhododendron franssenianum

Rhododendron franssenianum, commonly known as the Franssen's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, with multiple stems and a compact growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant floral display.
