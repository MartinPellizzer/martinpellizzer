Rabdosia adenoloma

Rabdosia adenoloma, commonly known as the mountain rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers that are typically lavender or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Rabdosia adenoloma is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
