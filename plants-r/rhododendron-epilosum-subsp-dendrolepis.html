Rhododendron epilosum subsp. dendrolepis

Rhododendron epilosum subsp.

dendrolepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
