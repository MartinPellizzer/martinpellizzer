Rhynchosia antennulifera

Rhynchosia antennulifera, commonly known as the featherleaf cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Rhynchosia antennulifera is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in sustainable agriculture.
