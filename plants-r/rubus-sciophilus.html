Rubus sciophilus

Rubus sciophilus, commonly known as the shade raspberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of understory ecosystems.
