Rubus heterosepalus

Rubus heterosepalus, commonly known as the variable rose or the heterosepal raspberry, is a species of shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
