Rhododendron minus var. chapmanii

Rhododendron minus var. chapmanii, commonly known as the Chapman's azalea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to lavender in color, blooming in late spring. The plant has leathery, dark green leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in woodland gardens.
