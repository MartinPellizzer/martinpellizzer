Racosperma tindaleae

Racosperma tindaleae, commonly known as Tindale's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has small, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow flowers in summer. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils with low rainfall.

Racosperma tindaleae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
