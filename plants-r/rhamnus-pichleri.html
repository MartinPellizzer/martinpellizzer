Rhamnus pichleri

Rhamnus pichleri, commonly known as the Pichler's buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry woodlands. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, reddish berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Rhamnus pichleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
