Rosa coriifolia var. wiesbauriana

Rosa coriifolia var. wiesbauriana, commonly known as the Wiesbaur's sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching stems with small, serrated leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and persistent winter interest.
