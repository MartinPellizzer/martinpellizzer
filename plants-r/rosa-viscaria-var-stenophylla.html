Rosa viscaria var. stenophylla

Rosa viscaria var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered in fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
