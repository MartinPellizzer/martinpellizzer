Rhododendron fragrans

Rhododendron fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant rhododendron, is a species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to white, often with a subtle fragrance. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

Rhododendron fragrans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
