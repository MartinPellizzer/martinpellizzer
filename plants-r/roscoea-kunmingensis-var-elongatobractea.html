Roscoea kunmingensis var. elongatobractea

Roscoea kunmingensis var. elongatobractea, commonly known as the elongated bract roscoea, is a striking rhizomatous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It features tall, grass-like foliage with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a waxy bloom. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by vibrant, often red or orange bracts that are elongated and prominent. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive, sweet scent that attracts pollinators.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique foliage structure.
