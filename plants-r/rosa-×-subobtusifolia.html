Rosa × subobtusifolia

Rosa × subobtusifolia, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa multiflora.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and is native to East Asia. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season.

Rosa × subobtusifolia is often cultivated as a hedge or ornamental plant due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
