Rubus foliosus subsp. pinicola

Rubus foliosus subsp.

pinicola, commonly known as the pine leaf bramble, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often lobed and resemble pine needles, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, typically red or black in color. It thrives in woodland and open areas, often in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture.
