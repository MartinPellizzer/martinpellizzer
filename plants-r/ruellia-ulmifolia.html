Ruellia ulmifolia

Ruellia ulmifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Ruellia ulmifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
