Rosa rubiginosa umbellata

Rosa rubiginosa umbellata, commonly known as the bristly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and features small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The fruit, a hairy rose hip, contains the seeds and is an important food source for various wildlife species.
