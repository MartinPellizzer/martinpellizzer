Rumex lingulatus

Rumex lingulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, meadows, and riverbanks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, terminal spikes.

Rumex lingulatus is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.
