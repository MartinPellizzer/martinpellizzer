Racosperma ramulosum var. linophyllum

Racosperma ramulosum var. linophyllum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branching stems covered in soft, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are often hairy beneath.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and drought-tolerant species.
