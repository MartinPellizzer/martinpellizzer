Rosa californica var. glabrata

Rosa californica var. glabrata, commonly known as the California wild rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features woody stems with thorns and is characterized by its dense, fragrant pink or white flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
