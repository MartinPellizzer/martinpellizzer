Rhododendron austrinum

Rhododendron austrinum, commonly known as the Australian rhododendron, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered invasive in some regions.
