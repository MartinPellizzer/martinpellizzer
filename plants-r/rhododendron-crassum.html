Rhododendron crassum

Rhododendron crassum, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are thick and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron crassum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of temperate climates.
