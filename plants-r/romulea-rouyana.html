Romulea rouyana

Romulea rouyana, commonly known as the golden rain lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features grass-like, linear leaves and produces small, spherical seed capsules that disperse its seeds efficiently. Romulea rouyana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
