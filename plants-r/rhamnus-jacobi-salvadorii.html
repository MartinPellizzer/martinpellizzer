Rhamnus jacobi-salvadorii

Rhamnus jacobi-salvadorii, commonly known as the Salvadori's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions. Its fruit is a small, reddish drupe that contains seeds dispersed by birds.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
