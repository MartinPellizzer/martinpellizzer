Ruellia chariessa

Ruellia chariessa, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Ruellia chariessa thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
