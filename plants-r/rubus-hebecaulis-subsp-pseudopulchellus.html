Rubus hebecaulis subsp. pseudopulchellus

Rubus hebecaulis subsp.

pseudopulchellus, commonly known as the slender blackberry. It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, dark red to black berries.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its edible fruits.
