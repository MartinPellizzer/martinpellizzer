Ranunculus almquistii

Ranunculus almquistii, commonly known as Almquist's buttercup, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features palmately divided leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
