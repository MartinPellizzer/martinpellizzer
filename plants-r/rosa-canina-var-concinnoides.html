Rosa canina var. concinnoides

Rosa canina var. concinnoides, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange hips, which are the fruit and contain seeds. It is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Rosa canina var. concinnoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedging and as a source of medicinal compounds.
