Rhus rhodesiensis f. glabra

Rhus rhodesiensis f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth flame tree, is a variant of the flame tree species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous bark and vibrant orange to red flowers that appear in panicle clusters. The plant produces small, leathery fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its rapid growth and striking floral display make it a notable species in botanical collections.
