Rinorea roxburghii

Rinorea roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are woody pods containing several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, tropical environments.

Rinorea roxburghii is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
