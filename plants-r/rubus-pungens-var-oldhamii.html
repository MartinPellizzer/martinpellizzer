Rubus pungens var. oldhamii

Rubus pungens var. oldhamii, commonly known as the Oldham's prickly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink in color. Its fruits are aggregate berries that are typically red when ripe.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
