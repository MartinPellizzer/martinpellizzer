Rubus × flexuosiformis

Rubus × flexuosiformis, commonly known as the creeping blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which help it to spread across various terrains. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
