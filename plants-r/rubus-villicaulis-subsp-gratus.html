Rubus villicaulis subsp. gratus

Rubus villicaulis subsp.

gratus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible berries, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
