Ranunculus fenzlii

Ranunculus fenzlii, commonly known as the Fenzli's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent yellow anthers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in cool climates and is often found in meadows and slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
