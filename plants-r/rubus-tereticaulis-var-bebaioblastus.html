Rubus tereticaulis var. bebaioblastus

Rubus tereticaulis var. bebaioblastus, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a variant of the slender blackberry species, characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

The berries are edible, though they are often tart and used in jams or desserts.
