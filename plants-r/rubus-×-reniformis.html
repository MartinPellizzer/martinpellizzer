Rubus × reniformis

Rubus × reniformis, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which help it cling to vegetation. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible, though they are often described as having a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability, making it a common sight in woodland areas.
