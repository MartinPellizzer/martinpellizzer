Ranunculus auricomus subsp. concavus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

concavus, commonly known as the concave buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to nine petals that are often slightly concave, giving it its subspecific name.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a distinctive, glossy appearance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
