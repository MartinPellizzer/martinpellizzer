Rhaphithamnus cyanocarpus var. pallidus

Rhaphithamnus cyanocarpus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale-fruited blueberry shrub, is a species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are usually pale green in color. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, and the fruit is a small, blue-black drupe.

This variety is notable for its drought tolerance and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.
