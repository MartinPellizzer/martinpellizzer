Rollandia fauriei

Rollandia fauriei, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are flexible and can twine around supports, making it suitable for trellises or arbors.

Rollandia fauriei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
