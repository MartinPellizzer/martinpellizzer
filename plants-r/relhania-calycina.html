Relhania calycina

Relhania calycina, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Relhania calycina is also valued for its ability to produce a waxy substance from its leaves, which has been used in traditional practices.
