Rosa hudsoniana

Rosa hudsoniana, commonly known as the Hudson's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and its stems are typically armed with stiff, straight prickles. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Rosa hudsoniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
