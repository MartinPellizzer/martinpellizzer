Rhytiglossa prolixa

Rhytiglossa prolixa, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. It is often found growing on trees or in rocky crevices, showcasing its adaptability to various microhabitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
