Rubus calvus

Rubus calvus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, and the plant thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family, sharing features with other brambles and blackberry species.
