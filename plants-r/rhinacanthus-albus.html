Rhinacanthus albus

Rhinacanthus albus, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces fruit that splits open to release its seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.
