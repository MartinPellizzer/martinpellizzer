Rodriguezia anomala

Rodriguezia anomala, commonly known as the pink hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive white throat. The plant features palmate leaves that are broad and lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.

Rodriguezia anomala is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
