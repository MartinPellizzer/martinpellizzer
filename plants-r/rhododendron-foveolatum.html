Rhododendron foveolatum

Rhododendron foveolatum, commonly known as the Mexican flame azalea, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded or semi-shaded environments. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Rhododendron foveolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
