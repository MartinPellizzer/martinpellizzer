Rhododendron nudiflorum var. bicolor

Rhododendron nudiflorum var. bicolor, commonly known as the twin-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking two-colored flowers, typically featuring a white base with a pink or red upper lip. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually leathery and dark green. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
