Rhynchosia ambacensis subsp. cameroonensis

Rhynchosia ambacensis subsp.

cameroonensis, commonly known as the Cameroon snout bean, is a climbing legume native to Cameroon and neighboring regions of Central Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its nutritional value, it is sometimes cultivated for food in local communities.
