Rubus submitis

Rubus submitis, commonly known as the creeping blackberry.

It is a species of bramble that is native to the eastern United States. The plant features thorny stems that grow close to the ground, forming dense mats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in disturbed areas.
