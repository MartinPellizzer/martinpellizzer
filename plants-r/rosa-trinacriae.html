Rosa trinacriae

Rosa trinacriae, commonly known as the Sicilian rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to crimson flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its thorny stems and showy blooms make it a distinctive ornamental plant.

Rosa trinacriae is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in Mediterranean horticulture.
