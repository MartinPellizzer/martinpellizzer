Rosa × dumetorum subvar. leventinae

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. leventinae, commonly known as the Alpine shrub rose, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features dense, thorny stems covered in fine, silvery-white hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5–7 elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.
