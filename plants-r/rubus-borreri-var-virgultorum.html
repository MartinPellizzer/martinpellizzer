Rubus borreri var. virgultorum

Rubus borreri var. virgultorum, commonly known as the Virgin's thimble or thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with three to five leaflets, and produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.

The species is part of the Rosaceae family, which includes many other familiar plants like roses and apples.
