Ruellia urophylla

Ruellia urophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in spikes, and bloom during the warm seasons.

Ruellia urophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
