Rosa varia var. maxima

Rosa varia var. maxima, commonly known as the Giant Rosa, is a rare and striking cultivar of the rose family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, featuring a mix of pink, red, and white hues. The plant produces abundant foliage with dark green, serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
