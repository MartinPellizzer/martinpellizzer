Rubus coreanus

Rubus coreanus, commonly known as the Korean raspberry, is a species of bramble native to East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in forested areas and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Korean cuisine. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white or pink.

The plant is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
