Rhododendron fortunans

Rhododendron fortunans, commonly known as the Fortune rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early spring. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with thick, often flaky bark, and it thrives in cool, moist, acidic soils.

Rhododendron fortunans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
