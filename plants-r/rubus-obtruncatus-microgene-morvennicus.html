Rubus obtruncatus microgene morvennicus

Rubus obtruncatus microgene morvennicus, commonly known as the Morven raspberry, is a shrub native to the Scottish Highlands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
