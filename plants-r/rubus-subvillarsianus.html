Rubus subvillarsianus

Rubus subvillarsianus, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and tart. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

Rubus subvillarsianus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing food for local wildlife.
