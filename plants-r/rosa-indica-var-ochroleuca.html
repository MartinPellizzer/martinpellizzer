Rosa indica var. ochroleuca

Rosa indica var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white tea rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa indica species.

It is characterized by its large, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in the production of essential oils and teas.
