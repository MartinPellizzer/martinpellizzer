Rosa graveolens var. subglobosa

Rosa graveolens var. subglobosa, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a red, fuzzy rose hip, which contains seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedgerows and gardens.
