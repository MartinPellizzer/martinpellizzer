Rubus hirtus var. paucipilus

Rubus hirtus var. paucipilus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This plant is valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
