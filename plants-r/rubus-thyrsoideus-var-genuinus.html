Rubus thyrsoideus var. genuinus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. genuinus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
