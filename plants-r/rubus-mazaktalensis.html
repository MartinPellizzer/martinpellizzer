Rubus mazaktalensis

Rubus mazaktalensis, commonly known as the Mazaktalensis blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia, particularly in the mountainous areas of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
