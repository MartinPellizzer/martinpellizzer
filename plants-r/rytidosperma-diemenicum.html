Rytidosperma diemenicum

Rytidosperma diemenicum, commonly known as the Tasmanian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Tasmania, Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as heathlands and coastal areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem.

Rytidosperma diemenicum is adapted to the cool, temperate climate of Tasmania and is an important component of the region's native flora.
