Rubus radula proles pilocarpus

Rubus radula proles pilocarpus, commonly known as the creeping raspberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in moist, woodland environments. It belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like roses and strawberries.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing food for various wildlife species.
