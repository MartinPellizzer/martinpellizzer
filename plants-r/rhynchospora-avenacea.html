Rhynchospora avenacea

Rhynchospora avenacea, commonly known as slender spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland environments.

Rhynchospora avenacea is often found in association with other wetland plants, playing a role in the ecological dynamics of these habitats.
