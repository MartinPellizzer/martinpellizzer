Rhodomyrtus macrocarpa

Rhodomyrtus macrocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in rainforest or wetland habitats.

Rhodomyrtus macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
