Rhododendron colemanii

Rhododendron colemanii, commonly known as Coleman's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-elevation forests and alpine meadows. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are usually dark green and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are showy, bell-shaped, and typically pink or reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron colemanii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
