Rhipsalidopsis rosea var. remanens

Rhipsalidopsis rosea var. remanens, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a hybrid species within the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are typically green or variegated with red, purple, or pink hues. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, usually during the holiday season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a decorative plant due to its long blooming period and adaptability to indoor conditions.
