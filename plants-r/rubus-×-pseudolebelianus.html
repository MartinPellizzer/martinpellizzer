Rubus × pseudolebelianus

Rubus × pseudolebelianus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, where it thrives in temperate and cool climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and large, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. It produces compound leaves with serrated edges and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

This species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
