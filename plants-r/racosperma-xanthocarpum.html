Racosperma xanthocarpum

Racosperma xanthocarpum, commonly known as the yellow fruit acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting the surrounding ecosystem.
