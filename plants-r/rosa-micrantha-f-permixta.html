Rosa micrantha f. permixta

Rosa micrantha f. permixta, commonly known as the hybrid musk rose, is a botanical variant characterized by its unique floral structure and fragrance.

It is a natural hybrid between Rosa micrantha and Rosa centifolia, resulting in a blend of traits from both parent species. The plant typically features small, clustered flowers with a strong, musky aroma, which is a defining characteristic of the species. Its foliage is usually dark green and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance.

This cultivar is often valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and historical significance in perfumery.
