Ranunculus flabellatus subsp. fibrosus

Ranunculus flabellatus subsp.

fibrosus, commonly known as the fibrous buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa. It features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically arranged in a cup-like shape.

The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine, silky hair.

It is commonly found in grasslands and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
