Rudbeckia

Rudbeckia, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to North America and are characterized by their bright yellow to orange petals and dark centers. Rudbeckia species typically grow in a variety of habitats, including prairies, meadows, and woodland edges. They are often used in landscaping for their vibrant colors and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Many species are also cultivated for their ornamental value and hardiness in different climates.
