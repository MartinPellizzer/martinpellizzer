Randia spinosa

Randia spinosa, commonly known as the spiked randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Randia spinosa is often found in moist, shady areas such as forests and thickets.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
