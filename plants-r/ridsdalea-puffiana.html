Ridsdalea puffiana

Ridsdalea puffiana, commonly known as the purple rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its flowering stems are slender and erect.
