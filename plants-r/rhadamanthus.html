Rhadamanthus

Rhadamanthus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom only under specific environmental conditions, making it extremely difficult to study. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Rhadamanthus is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of scientific fascination.
