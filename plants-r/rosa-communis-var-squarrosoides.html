Rosa communis var. squarrosoides

Rosa communis var. squarrosoides, commonly known as the squarrosoid rose, is a variant of the common rose characterized by its distinctive, fuzzy, and often hairy foliage.

It typically features fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its appeal in both landscape design and botanical study.
