Rapanea pearcei

Rapanea pearcei, commonly known as the Sierra Madre holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically oval-shaped and have a leathery texture. Its small, white flowers are followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Rapanea pearcei thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas at higher elevations.
