Raoulia parkii

Raoulia parkii, commonly known as the Solomon Islands orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that often display a combination of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, a common trait among orchids.

Raoulia parkii plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native ecosystem and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
