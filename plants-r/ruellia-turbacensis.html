Ruellia turbacensis

Ruellia turbacensis, commonly known as the mountain ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple tubular flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months.

Ruellia turbacensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
