Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. silesiacus

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

silesiacus, commonly known as the Silesian blackberry, is a shrub native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and have a tart flavor.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisine.
