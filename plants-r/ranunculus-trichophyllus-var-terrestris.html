Ranunculus trichophyllus var. terrestris

Ranunculus trichophyllus var. terrestris, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, spreading stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
