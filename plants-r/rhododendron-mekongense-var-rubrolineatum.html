Rhododendron mekongense var. rubrolineatum

Rhododendron mekongense var. rubrolineatum, commonly known as the Mekong Flame Rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its striking floral display.
