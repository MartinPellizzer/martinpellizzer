Rubus corylifolius proles callianthus

Rubus corylifolius proles callianthus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupes that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
