Riodocea pulcherrima

Riodocea pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Riodocea pulcherrima is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
