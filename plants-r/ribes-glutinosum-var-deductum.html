Ribes glutinosum var. deductum

Ribes glutinosum var. deductum, commonly known as sticky currant or red currant, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
