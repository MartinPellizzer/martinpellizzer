Rubus parvifolius var. subpinnatus

Rubus parvifolius var. subpinnatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with a pinnate structure, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
