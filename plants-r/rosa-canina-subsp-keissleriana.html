Rosa canina subsp. keissleriana

Rosa canina subsp.

keissleriana, commonly known as the Keissler's rose, is a subspecies native to Central Asia. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five to seven petals, and bloom in spring.

The plant produces red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
