Rhododendron oxycoccoides

Rhododendron oxycoccoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron oxycoccoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological niches.
