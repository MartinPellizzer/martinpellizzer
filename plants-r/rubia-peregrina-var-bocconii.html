Rubia peregrina var. bocconii

Rubia peregrina var. bocconii, commonly known as the Bocconii woodruff, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, creeping stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
