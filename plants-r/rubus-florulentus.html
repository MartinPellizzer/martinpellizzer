Rubus florulentus

Rubus florulentus, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, typically in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
