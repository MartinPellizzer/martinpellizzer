Ruizia integrifolia

Ruizia integrifolia, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its dense, rounded canopy. The leaves are compound, with long, slender leaflets that are smooth and entire, giving the plant its scientific name. It produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting birds and insects.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
