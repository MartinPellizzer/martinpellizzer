Rubus eurystachys

Rubus eurystachys, commonly known as the broad-stalked blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it flowers with white to pale pink flowers.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as woodlands and disturbed sites.
