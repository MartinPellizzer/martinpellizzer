Rhynchosia longipedunculata

Rhynchosia longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-peduncle cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
