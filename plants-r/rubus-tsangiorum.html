Rubus tsangiorum

Rubus tsangiorum, commonly known as the Yunnan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
