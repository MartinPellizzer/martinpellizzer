Rhysolepis dilloniorum

Rhysolepis dilloniorum, commonly known as the Dillon's lichen, is a unique organism that belongs to the lichen family, forming a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically an algae or cyanobacteria.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree bark or rocks in temperate forest regions. The plant's structure consists of a thallus, which is a composite body made up of both fungal and algal components. It plays a vital role in nutrient cycling and serves as a habitat for various microorganisms.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often used as an indicator species for environmental health.
