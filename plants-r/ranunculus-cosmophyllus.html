Ranunculus cosmophyllus

Ranunculus cosmophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with numerous petals and a hairy, lobed leaf structure. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for gardening purposes.
