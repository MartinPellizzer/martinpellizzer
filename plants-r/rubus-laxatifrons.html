Rubus laxatifrons

Rubus laxatifrons, commonly known as the broad-leafed blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological studies.
