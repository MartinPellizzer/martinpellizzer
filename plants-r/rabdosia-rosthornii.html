Rabdosia rosthornii

Rabdosia rosthornii, commonly known as the Malabar blue sage, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

Rabdosia rosthornii is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
