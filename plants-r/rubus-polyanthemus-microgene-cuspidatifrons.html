Rubus polyanthemus microgene cuspidatifrons

Rubus polyanthemus microgene cuspidatifrons, commonly known as the microgene thimbleberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, thimble-shaped fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valuable food source for wildlife and humans alike.
