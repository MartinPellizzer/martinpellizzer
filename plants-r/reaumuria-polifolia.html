Reaumuria polifolia

Reaumuria polifolia, commonly known as the many-leaved reaumuria, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish or greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its root system is extensive and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in harsh, sandy soils.

Reaumuria polifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
