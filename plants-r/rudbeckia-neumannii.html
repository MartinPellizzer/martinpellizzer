Rudbeckia neumannii

Rudbeckia neumannii, commonly known as the Neumann's coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow to orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk of dark brown or black tubular flowers, creating a striking visual display. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Rudbeckia neumannii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
