Rubus bhotanensis

Rubus bhotanensis, commonly known as the Bhutan raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly found in Bhutan, India, and Nepal. This shrub typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are serrated and have a hairy texture, while its flowers are usually white or pink.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
