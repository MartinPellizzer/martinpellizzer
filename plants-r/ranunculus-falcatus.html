Ranunculus falcatus

Ranunculus falcatus, commonly known as the sickleleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five sepals that are typically curved or sickle-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
