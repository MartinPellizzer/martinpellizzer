Rossioglossum splendens var. imschootianum

Rossioglossum splendens var. imschootianum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and sepals, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is prized in horticultural collections.
