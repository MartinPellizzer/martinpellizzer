Rosa transcaucasica

Rosa transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian rose, is a species native to the Caucasus region, including parts of Georgia, Armenia, and Azerbaijan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by oval, red-orange hips. Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat, and it is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in various horticultural settings.
