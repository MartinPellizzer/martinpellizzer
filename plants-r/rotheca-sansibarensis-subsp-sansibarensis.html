Rotheca sansibarensis subsp. sansibarensis

Rotheca sansibarensis subsp.

sansibarensis, commonly known as the Zanzibar rotheca, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shores.

The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
