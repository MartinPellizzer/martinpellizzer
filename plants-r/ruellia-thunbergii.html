Ruellia thunbergii

Ruellia thunbergii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Ruellia thunbergii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
