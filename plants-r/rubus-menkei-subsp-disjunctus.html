Rubus menkei subsp. disjunctus

Rubus menkei subsp.

disjunctus, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to China. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
