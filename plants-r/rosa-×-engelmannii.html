Rosa × engelmannii

Rosa × engelmannii, commonly known as the Engelmann rose, is a hybrid species originating from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa woodsii.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically borne on long pedicels. Its adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions makes it a popular choice for landscaping and hedges.

The Rosa × engelmannii is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
