Rosa afzeliana f. exarmata

Rosa afzeliana f. exarmata, commonly known as the naked-stemmed rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa afzeliana species.

It is characterized by its reduced or absent stipules, which distinguish it from other forms of the species. The plant typically features thorny stems and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red. It is native to parts of Asia, including China and India, and thrives in temperate climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
