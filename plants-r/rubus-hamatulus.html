Rubus hamatulus

Rubus hamatulus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically covered in a fine, whitish bloom. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and have a serrated margin.

The species is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade and moist soil.
