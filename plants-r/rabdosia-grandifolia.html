Rabdosia grandifolia

Rabdosia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered rabdosia, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rabdosia grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
