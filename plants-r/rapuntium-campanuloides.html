Rapuntium campanuloides

Rapuntium campanuloides, commonly known as the campanula-like rapuntius, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
