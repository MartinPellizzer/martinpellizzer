Rubus semitomentosus var. adenocladus

Rubus semitomentosus var. adenocladus, commonly known as the腺叶黑果莓, is a variant of the blackberry species native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The leaves are usually trifoliate, with a hairy underside, and the plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some use in traditional Chinese medicine.
