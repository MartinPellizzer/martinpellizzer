Rubus rosarius

Rubus rosarius, commonly known as the rose-bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically arranged alternately. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, edible berries in the summer. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
