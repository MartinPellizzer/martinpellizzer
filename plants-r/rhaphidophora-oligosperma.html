Rhaphidophora oligosperma

Rhaphidophora oligosperma, commonly known as the spider plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, cane-like stems and rosettes of waxy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by clusters of bright red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Rhaphidophora oligosperma is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
