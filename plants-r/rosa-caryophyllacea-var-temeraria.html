Rosa caryophyllacea var. temeraria

Rosa caryophyllacea var. temeraria, commonly known as the wild sweetbriar, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, oval leaves with serrated edges and produces red, hairy fruits known as hips. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is often found in woodlands and along streambanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
