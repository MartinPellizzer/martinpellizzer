Rennera

Rennera, commonly known as the false gentian, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gentianaceae.

It consists of several species, primarily found in arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plants are typically herbaceous, with showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. They have opposite, lance-shaped leaves and often grow in rocky or sandy soils.

Rennera species are known for their ornamental value and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their striking floral display.
