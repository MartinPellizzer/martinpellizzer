Rosa × dumetorum var. eulanceolata

Rosa × dumetorum var. eulanceolata, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, fragrant pink or white flowers, and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces rose hips that are typically red or orange, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable ornamental and a species of ecological interest.
