Rubus leucophyllus

Rubus leucophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with white or pale-colored undersides, giving it its common name. It produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color.

Rubus leucophyllus is often found in forests and along stream banks, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
