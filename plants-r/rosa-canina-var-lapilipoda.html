Rosa canina var. lapilipoda

Rosa canina var. lapilipoda, commonly known as the Lapland rose, is a variant of the dog rose native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

This shrub typically grows in alpine and tundra environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets with serrated edges. The flowers are pink to red, producing a sweet fragrance, and the plant is known for its hardy, thorny stems.

It plays a significant ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
