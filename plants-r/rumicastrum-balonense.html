Rumicastrum balonense

Rumicastrum balonense, commonly known as the Balon's burrobrush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with deep root systems and thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local wildlife.
