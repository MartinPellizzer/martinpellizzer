Rubus × semipapulosus

Rubus × semipapulosus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rubus genus in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, oval leaves with a rough, papular texture on the underside, contributing to its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The berries are edible, though they are generally less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties.
