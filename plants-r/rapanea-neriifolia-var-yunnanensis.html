Rapanea neriifolia var. yunnanensis

Rapanea neriifolia var. yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan evergreen laurel, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that remain throughout the year. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, and it produces small, dark purple berries that are edible.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
