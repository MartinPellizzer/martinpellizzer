Relbunium paulense

Relbunium paulense, commonly known as the Brazilian buttercup, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves with lobed margins. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
