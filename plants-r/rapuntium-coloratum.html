Rapuntium coloratum

Rapuntium coloratum, commonly known as the colorful cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to that of watercress.

Rapuntium coloratum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its culinary uses.
