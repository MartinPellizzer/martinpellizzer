Ribes rubrum subsp. petraeum

Ribes rubrum subsp.

petraeum, commonly known as the Sierra currant, is a shrub native to western North America. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant produces small, edible red berries that are similar in appearance to those of other currant species. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
