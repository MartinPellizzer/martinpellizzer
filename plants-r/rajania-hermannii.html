Rajania hermannii

Rajania hermannii, commonly known as the Hermann's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters of three to five. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, arranged in a fan-like pattern around the stem.

Rajania hermannii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
