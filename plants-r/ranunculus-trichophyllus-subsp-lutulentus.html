Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp. lutulentus

Ranunculus trichophyllus subsp.

lutulentus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and wetlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and erect.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
