Ranunculus pulchellus var. sajanensis

Ranunculus pulchellus var. sajanensis, commonly known as the Sajan ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, usually yellow or white flowers with five petals and a hairy, glandular stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with finely divided lobes, and it is often found in meadows and slopes at high elevations.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
