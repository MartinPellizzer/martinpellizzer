Ranunculus flammula var. arenarius

Ranunculus flammula var. arenarius, commonly known as the sand buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to sandy and rocky soils in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in open habitats such as dunes and gravelly meadows.

Due to its adaptability to harsh environments, it is considered a hardy and resilient species.
