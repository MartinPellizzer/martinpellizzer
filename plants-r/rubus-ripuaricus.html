Rubus ripuaricus

Rubus ripuaricus, commonly known as the ripuarian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in local cuisines.
