Ribes maximoviczii

Ribes maximoviczii, commonly known as the Maximovici gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, orange-red berries in late summer. These berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Ribes maximoviczii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
