Roscoea alpina f. pallida

Roscoea alpina f. pallida, commonly known as the pale form of the alpine roscoea, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are often pale green, giving it its distinguishing appearance. The plant produces showy, red-orange inflorescences with numerous small, tubular flowers that are adapted for pollination by birds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens with specific care requirements.
