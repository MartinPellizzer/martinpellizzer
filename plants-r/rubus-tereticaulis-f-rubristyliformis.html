Rubus tereticaulis f. rubristyliformis

Rubus tereticaulis f. rubristyliformis, commonly known as the red-styled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive red-colored inflorescence stems.

This subspecies is part of the Rubus genus, which includes numerous species of brambles and shrubs. It typically grows in temperate regions and is valued for its edible berries, which are similar in taste and texture to other blackberry varieties. The plant has a woody structure with thorny stems and produces aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets.

Its botanical features make it a notable subject in horticultural and ecological studies.
