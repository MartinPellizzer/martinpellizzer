Rubus lambertianus var. glaber

Rubus lambertianus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and dark purple to black berries that mature in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and its role in local ecosystems.
