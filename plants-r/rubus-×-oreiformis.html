Rubus × oreiformis

Rubus × oreiformis, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, resembling raspberries in appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
