Rubus × mariae

Rubus × mariae, commonly known as the Marie raspberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

The Marie raspberry is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
