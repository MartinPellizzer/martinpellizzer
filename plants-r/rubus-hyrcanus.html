Rubus hyrcanus

Rubus hyrcanus, commonly known as the Hyrcanian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
