Rosa villosa var. woodsiana

Rosa villosa var. woodsiana, commonly known as the Woods rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and foliage, which give it its scientific name "villosa.".

It is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
