Rosa setigera f. alba

Rosa setigera f. alba, commonly known as the white willow rose, is a rare and cultivated form of the willow rose species.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its foliage is finely divided, giving it a delicate, willow-like appearance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden settings for its unique aesthetic.
