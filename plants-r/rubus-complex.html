Rubus complex

Rubus complex, commonly known as brambles, is a large group of flowering plants in the Rosaceae family.

It includes over 400 species of shrubs and vines, characterized by their thorny stems and aggregate fruits. Many species within the complex are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plants typically have alternate, pinnately compound leaves and produce showy white or pink flowers.

They are often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.
