Rudbeckia occidentalis var. alpicola

Rudbeckia occidentalis var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine rudbeckia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations, thriving in full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in challenging alpine conditions.
