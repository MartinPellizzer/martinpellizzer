Rubus fairholmianus

Rubus fairholmianus, commonly known as the Fairholm blackberry, is a species of bramble native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
