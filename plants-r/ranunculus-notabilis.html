Ranunculus notabilis

Ranunculus notabilis, commonly known as the noble buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with numerous petals arranged in a radiant pattern. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Ranunculus notabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
