Rupicapnos sublaevis

Rupicapnos sublaevis, commonly known as the slender rock rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments, growing at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
