Rosa patrum

Rosa patrum, commonly known as the Patrum rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. Rosa patrum is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
