Rubus tephrodes

Rubus tephrodes, commonly known as the blackberry lichen, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its unique, shrub-like growth habit.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color, which are edible and have a tart flavor. The plant is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are usually hairy and serrated.

Rubus tephrodes plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitats.
