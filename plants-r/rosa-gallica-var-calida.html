Rosa gallica var. calida

Rosa gallica var. calida, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a hardy shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, thorny stems and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it is well adapted to cold climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.

The variety is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
