Rubus glandulosus subsp. schleicheri

Rubus glandulosus subsp.

schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
