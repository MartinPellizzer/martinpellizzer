Rhododendron brachycarpum var. rufescens

Rhododendron brachycarpum var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically dark green and leathery, while the stems exhibit a distinctive reddish hue, especially in younger growth. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
