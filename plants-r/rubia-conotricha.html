Rubia conotricha

Rubia conotricha, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rubia conotricha is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
