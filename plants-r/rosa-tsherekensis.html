Rosa tsherekensis

Rosa tsherekensis, commonly known as the Tsherekensis rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a serrated margin.

Rosa tsherekensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
