Rhamnus lycioides var. angustissima

Rhamnus lycioides var. angustissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some wildlife. It has a woody stem with thorny branches, and its leaves are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is part of the Rhamnaceae family, known for its medicinal and ecological significance.
