Rosa coriifolia var. saxetana

Rosa coriifolia var. saxetana, commonly known as the saxifrage rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers have five to seven petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in cooler climates.
