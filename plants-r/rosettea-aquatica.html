Rosettea aquatica

Rosettea aquatica, commonly known as the water rosette, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its dense, circular arrangement of leaves forming a rosette structure.

It thrives in shallow, still freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The leaves are typically narrow and strap-like, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for anchoring in soft substrates.

The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
