Rubus × parviflorus

Rubus × parviflorus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to maritime climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and it produces small, dark berries that are often used in local cuisines.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in coastal ecosystems.
