Rubus ruber

Rubus ruber, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically bright red when ripe. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Rubus ruber thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
