Rubus villicaulis var. leucandrus

Rubus villicaulis var. leucandrus, commonly known as the white-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale yellow fruits, which contrast with the typical dark berries of other Rubus species. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
