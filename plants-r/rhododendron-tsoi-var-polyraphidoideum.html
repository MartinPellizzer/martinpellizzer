Rhododendron tsoi var. polyraphidoideum

Rhododendron tsoi var. polyraphidoideum, commonly known as the Sichuan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is typically found at high altitudes with well-drained, acidic soils. Its floral structure is typical of the Ericaceae family, featuring showy bracts that often resemble petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
