Rubus cordialis

Rubus cordialis, commonly known as the heartleaf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves that are often heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, similar to other raspberries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The botanical features of Rubus cordialis make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
