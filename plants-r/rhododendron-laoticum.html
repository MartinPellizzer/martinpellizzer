Rhododendron laoticum

Rhododendron laoticum, commonly known as the Laotian rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in Laos and Vietnam.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Rhododendron laoticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
