Rubus tracyi

Rubus tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing features with other brambles and related plants.
