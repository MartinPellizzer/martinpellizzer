Roscoea purpurea f. alba

Roscoea purpurea f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered roscoea, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its ornamental, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with broad, strap-like leaves that are often variegated in color. This cultivar is prized for its beauty and ease of cultivation in shaded, moist garden environments.
