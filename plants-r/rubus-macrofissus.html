Rubus macrofissus

Rubus macrofissus, commonly known as the giant blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, thorny stems and clusters of dark purple to black berries. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small drupelets, which are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges and a rough texture.

Rubus macrofissus thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the southeastern regions of the United States.
