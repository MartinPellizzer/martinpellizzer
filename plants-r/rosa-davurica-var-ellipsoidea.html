Rosa davurica var. ellipsoidea

Rosa davurica var. ellipsoidea, commonly known as the Siberian rose or the shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink to white flowers. The leaves are pinnately compound, with 5 to 9 elliptical leaflets that are serrated along the edges. This species is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cold climates, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate zones.

It also has historical use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
