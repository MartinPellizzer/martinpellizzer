Rosa pomifera

Rosa pomifera, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its most distinctive feature is the large, apple-like fruit, which contains numerous seeds.

Rosa pomifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and historical significance.
