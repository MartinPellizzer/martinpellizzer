Rumex giganteus var. nelsonii

Rumex giganteus var. nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along stream banks and in wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
