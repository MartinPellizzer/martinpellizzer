Rosa viscaria var. hispidior

Rosa viscaria var. hispidior, commonly known as the hispid hillbilly rose, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardiness in temperate climates.
