Ruellia pauciflora

Ruellia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Ruellia pauciflora is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a relatively hardy species.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
