Rubus purpuratus f. latifolius

Rubus purpuratus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a variant of the common raspberry characterized by its broader, more robust leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically reddish or purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for cultivation in gardens.

Its foliage and fruiting habits make it an attractive and productive addition to horticultural landscapes.
