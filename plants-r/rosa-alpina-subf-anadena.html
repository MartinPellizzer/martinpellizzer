Rosa alpina subf. anadena

Rosa alpina subf.

anadena, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a subspecies of the alpine rose that thrives in high-altitude environments. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers.

The plant typically has narrow, hairy leaves and produces rose hips that are often red or orange in color. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it grows in rocky or alpine soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
