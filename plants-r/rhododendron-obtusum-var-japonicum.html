Rhododendron obtusum var. japonicum

Rhododendron obtusum var. japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers acidic, well-drained soil. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a distinctive red or purple glandular spot at the base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring bloom and evergreen foliage.
