Rosa rubiginosa var. silesiaca

Rosa rubiginosa var. silesiaca, commonly known as the Silesian rose, is a shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that contain seeds.

This variety is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
