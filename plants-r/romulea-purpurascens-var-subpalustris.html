Romulea purpurascens var. subpalustris

Romulea purpurascens var. subpalustris, commonly known as the marsh romulea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial bulbous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of striking pink to purple flowers. The species thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in marshy or wetland habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural environment.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
