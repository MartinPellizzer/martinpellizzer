Ranunculus auricomus subsp. prasinaster

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

prasinaster, commonly known as the green-stem buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its greenish stem and the presence of a greenish hue in its leaves and sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is often found in calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
