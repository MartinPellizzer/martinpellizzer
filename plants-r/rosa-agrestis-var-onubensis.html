Rosa agrestis var. onubensis

Rosa agrestis var. onubensis, commonly known as the Onubian brambles, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are edible and often used in traditional remedies. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and horticulture.
