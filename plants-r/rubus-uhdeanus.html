Rubus uhdeanus

Rubus uhdeanus, commonly known as the Uhde's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
