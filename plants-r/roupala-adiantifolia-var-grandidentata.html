Roupala adiantifolia var. grandidentata

Roupala adiantifolia var. grandidentata, commonly known as the large-toothed roupala, is a variant of the roupala genus native to certain regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves that are often divided into numerous narrow, feather-like segments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are known for their delicate, lace-like appearance, which gives it a distinctive aesthetic.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
