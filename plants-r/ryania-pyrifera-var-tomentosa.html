Ryania pyrifera var. tomentosa

Ryania pyrifera var. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose ryania, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with a dense, woolly underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, elongated, bean-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
