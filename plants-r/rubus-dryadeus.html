Rubus dryadeus

Rubus dryadeus, commonly known as the dryad's rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Rubus dryadeus is adapted to moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial shade.
