Ranunculus teneriffa

Ranunculus teneriffa, commonly known as the Teneriffe buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb with a short, thick rhizome and produces showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Ranunculus teneriffa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
