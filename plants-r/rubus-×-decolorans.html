Rubus × decolorans

Rubus × decolorans, commonly known as the white-berried blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus spectabilis.

It is characterized by its pale to white fruits, which contrast with the typical dark berries of its parent species. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, thriving in temperate climates. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are often used in culinary applications despite their less vibrant color.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to various soil conditions.
