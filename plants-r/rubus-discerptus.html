Rubus discerptus

Rubus discerptus, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Rubus discerptus is an important food source for various wildlife species.
