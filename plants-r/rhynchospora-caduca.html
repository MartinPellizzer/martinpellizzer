Rhynchospora caduca

Rhynchospora caduca, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Rhynchospora caduca produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
