Rosa parviflora var. setigera

Rosa parviflora var. setigera, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with slender stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs, adapting to cold and harsh environments.

Rosa parviflora var. setigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
