Ranunculus weberbaueri

Ranunculus weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic species. The plant typically features bright yellow petals and a hairy stem, with leaves that are deeply lobed and arranged alternately. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
