Rubus radula var. koehleroides

Rubus radula var. koehleroides, commonly known as the Koehler's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
