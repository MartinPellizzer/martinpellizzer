Rubus hexagynus

Rubus hexagynus, commonly known as the six-ovary blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. These fruits are edible and have a tart, sweet flavor.

Rubus hexagynus thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
