Rubus conothyrsus

Rubus conothyrsus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rubus conothyrsus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various animals.
