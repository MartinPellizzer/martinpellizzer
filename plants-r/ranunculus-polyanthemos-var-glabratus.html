Ranunculus polyanthemos var. glabratus

Ranunculus polyanthemos var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a hairy texture, while the variety 'glabratus' is distinguished by its smooth, hairless foliage.

It blooms in early spring and is often found in temperate regions with cool, moist climates.
