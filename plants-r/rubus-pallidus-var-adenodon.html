Rubus pallidus var. adenodon

Rubus pallidus var. adenodon, commonly known as the pale bramble or adenodonic raspberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow to reddish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, hairy berries in the summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
