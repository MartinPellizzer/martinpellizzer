Rhodamnia makumak

Rhodamnia makumak, commonly known as the Makumak tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
