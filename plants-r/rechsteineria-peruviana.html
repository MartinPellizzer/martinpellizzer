Rechsteineria peruviana

Rechsteineria peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or forested areas. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Rechsteineria peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
