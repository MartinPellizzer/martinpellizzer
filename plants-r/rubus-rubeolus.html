Rubus rubeolus

Rubus rubeolus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as raspberries, which are typically red when ripe. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary applications.

Rubus rubeolus thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.
