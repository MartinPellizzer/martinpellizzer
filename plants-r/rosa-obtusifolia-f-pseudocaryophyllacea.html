Rosa obtusifolia f. pseudocaryophyllacea

Rosa obtusifolia f. pseudocaryophyllacea, commonly known as the pseudocaryophyllaceous rose, is a cultivar of the shrub rose species.

It is characterized by its dense, thornless stems and compact growth habit. The foliage is dark green and glossy, with leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
