Rhododendron hispidum

Rhododendron hispidum, commonly known as the woolly rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
