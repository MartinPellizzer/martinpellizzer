Rubus rigidulatus

Rubus rigidulatus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like roses and raspberries.
