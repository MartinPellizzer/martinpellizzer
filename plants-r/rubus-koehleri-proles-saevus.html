Rubus koehleri proles saevus

Rubus koehleri proles saevus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine due to its tart flavor and nutritional content.
