Rubus × devius

Rubus × devius, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet-tart flavor. Rubus × devius thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a valuable crop for cultivation.
