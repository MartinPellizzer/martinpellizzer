Rosa rougeonensis

Rosa rougeonensis, commonly known as the Redonensis rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern France, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red petals and fragrant blooms, which typically appear in late spring. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces hip fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Rosa rougeonensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
