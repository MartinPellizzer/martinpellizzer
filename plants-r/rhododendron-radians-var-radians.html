Rhododendron radians var. radians

Rhododendron radians var. radians, commonly known as the crimson flame rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
