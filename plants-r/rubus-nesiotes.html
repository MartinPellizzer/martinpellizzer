Rubus nesiotes

Rubus nesiotes, commonly known as the Hawaiian raspberry, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in coastal forests and streamside areas. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces prickly stems covered in fine hairs.

Rubus nesiotes plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various insect and bird species.
