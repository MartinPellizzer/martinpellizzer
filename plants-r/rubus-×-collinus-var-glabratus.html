Rubus × collinus var. glabratus

Rubus × collinus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its berries are usually dark purple to black in color, with a sweet-tart flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a fruit crop.
