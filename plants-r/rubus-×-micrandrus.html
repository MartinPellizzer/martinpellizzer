Rubus × micrandrus

Rubus × micrandrus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in woodland habitats. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
