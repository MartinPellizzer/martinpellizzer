Rosa × dumetorum subvar. foliigera

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. foliigera, commonly known as the creeping evergreen rose, is a hybrid species characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features dark green, leathery leaves that are typically glossy and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, often blooming throughout the growing season. It is well adapted to coastal and rocky environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
