Rhinacanthus xerophilus

Rhinacanthus xerophilus, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Rhinacanthus xerophilus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
