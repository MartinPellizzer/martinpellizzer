Rosa mesatlantica

Rosa mesatlantica, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and it produces fragrant, edible fruits.

Rosa mesatlantica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming pattern.
