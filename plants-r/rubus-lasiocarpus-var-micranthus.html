Rubus lasiocarpus var. micranthus

Rubus lasiocarpus var. micranthus, commonly known as the smooth-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale green, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry. The plant prefers moist, wooded habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

It is valued for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor and are used in jams and desserts.
