Ranunculus marginatus var. marginatus

Ranunculus marginatus var. marginatus, commonly known as the marginated buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals, often with a distinctive yellow or greenish margin. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
