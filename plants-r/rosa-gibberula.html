Rosa gibberula

Rosa gibberula, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. Rosa gibberula is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique texture and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
