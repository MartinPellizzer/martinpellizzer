Rosa billetii

Rosa billetii, commonly known as the African rose or Billet's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth, with thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red berries that contain seeds.

Rosa billetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
