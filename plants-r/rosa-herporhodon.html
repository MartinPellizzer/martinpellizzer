Rosa herporhodon

Rosa herporhodon, commonly known as the sand rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Kazakhstan. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

Rosa herporhodon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.
