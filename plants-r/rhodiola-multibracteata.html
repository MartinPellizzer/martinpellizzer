Rhodiola multibracteata

Rhodiola multibracteata, commonly known as the many-bract rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in alpine and arctic environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions. Rhodiola multibracteata is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for its purported adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.

Its resilience and unique morphology make it a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticulture.
