Reseda Phyteuma Subsp. Aragonensis

Reseda phyteuma subsp. aragonensis is a lesser-known member of the Resedaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of Spain. This perennial herb is distinguished by its compact growth habit and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. Although it shares some traits with its more common relatives, it is uniquely adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its habitat. Its limited distribution makes it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated and often overlooked in the wild.
