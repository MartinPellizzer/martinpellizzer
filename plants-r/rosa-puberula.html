Rosa puberula

Rosa puberula, commonly known as the pubescent rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in the spring. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in temperate climates.

Rosa puberula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
