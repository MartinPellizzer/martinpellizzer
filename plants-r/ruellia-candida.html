Ruellia candida

Ruellia candida, commonly known as white ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Ruellia candida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and continuous flowering habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
