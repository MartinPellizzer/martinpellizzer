Rubus × gladiatus

Rubus × gladiatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest understories. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are popular in local cuisine.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to wildlife.
