Rhus succedanea

Rhus succedanea, commonly known as staghorn sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces bright red, fuzzy fruit clusters that appear in the fall, making it visually striking. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing a rugged appearance.

Staghorn sumac is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
