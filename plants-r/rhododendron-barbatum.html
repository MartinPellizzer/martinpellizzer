Rhododendron barbatum

Rhododendron barbatum, commonly known as the hairy leaved rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with hairy undersides, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
