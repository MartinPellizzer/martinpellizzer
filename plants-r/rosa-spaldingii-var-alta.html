Rosa spaldingii var. alta

Rosa spaldingii var. alta, commonly known as the Sierra foothill rose, is a shrub-like species native to the western United States.

It features woody stems with thorny branches and is characterized by its clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
