Rubus rivularis var. humilifrons

Rubus rivularis var. humilifrons, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a low-growing shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and flowers with five pink to white petals.
