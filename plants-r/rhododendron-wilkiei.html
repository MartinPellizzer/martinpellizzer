Rhododendron wilkiei

Rhododendron wilkiei, commonly known as the Wilkie's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats and thrives in acidic soils. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
