Rubus urbionicus

Rubus urbionicus, commonly known as the European blackberry or bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus urbionicus is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to different environments.
