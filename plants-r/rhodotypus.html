Rhodotypus

Rhodotypus, commonly known as the red-tipped plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking red-tipped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Rhodotypus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
