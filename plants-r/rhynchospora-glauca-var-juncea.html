Rhynchospora glauca var. juncea

Rhynchospora glauca var. juncea, commonly known as bluebeard grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or purple flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil in wetland environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
