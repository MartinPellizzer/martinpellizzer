Rubus silesiacus subsp. chloophyllus

Rubus silesiacus subsp.

chloophyllus, commonly known as the Silesian blackberry, is a subspecies native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically larger than those of the typical Rubus silesiacus.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species within the Rubus genus.
