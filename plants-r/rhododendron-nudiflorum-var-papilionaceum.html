Rhododendron nudiflorum var. papilionaceum

Rhododendron nudiflorum var. papilionaceum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
