Rubus koehleri microgene leptocercus

Rubus koehleri microgene leptocercus, commonly known as the Koehler's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but may have a tart flavor. It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
