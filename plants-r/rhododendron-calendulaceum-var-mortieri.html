Rhododendron calendulaceum var. mortieri

Rhododendron calendulaceum var. mortieri, commonly known as the Florida flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bright orange to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a slight pubescence. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
