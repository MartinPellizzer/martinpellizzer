Ribes uva-crispa var. subatlanticum

Ribes uva-crispa var. subatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic gooseberry, is a variant of the European gooseberry that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous shrub with upright, woody stems that bear alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green berries that mature to a red color, typically in late summer. Its flowers are actinomorphic, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Grossulariaceae family.

This variety is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
