Robinia aurea

Robinia aurea, commonly known as the yellow false acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a yellow-green hue. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Robinia aurea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
