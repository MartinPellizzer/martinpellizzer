Rubus ammobius var. pseudoplicatus

Rubus ammobius var. pseudoplicatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The leaves are narrow and serrated, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or disturbed soils.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor and are used in culinary and traditional medicinal practices.
