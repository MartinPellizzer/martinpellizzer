Rubus chenonii

Rubus chenonii, commonly known as the Chenon's blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are similar to other blackberry species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
