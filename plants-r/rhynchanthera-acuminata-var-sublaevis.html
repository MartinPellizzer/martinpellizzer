Rhynchanthera acuminata var. sublaevis

Rhynchanthera acuminata var. sublaevis, commonly known as the smooth-tipped rhynchanthera, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a prominent white lip, and they bloom in clusters during the wet season. This orchid prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in local ecosystems.
