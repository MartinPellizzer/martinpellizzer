Rubus hirtus var. ramiger

Rubus hirtus var. ramiger, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark red or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This species is valued for its edible berries and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
