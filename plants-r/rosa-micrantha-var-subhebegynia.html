Rosa micrantha var. subhebegynia

Rosa micrantha var. subhebegynia, commonly known as the dwarf rose or mountain rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in cooler climates.
