Rubus monongaliensis

Rubus monongaliensis, commonly known as the Monongahela blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the Appalachian region.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
