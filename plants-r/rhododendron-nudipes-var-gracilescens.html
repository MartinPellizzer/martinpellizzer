Rhododendron nudipes var. gracilescens

Rhododendron nudipes var. gracilescens, commonly known as the slender leaf rhododendron, is a rare and unique variant of the rhododendron species.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
