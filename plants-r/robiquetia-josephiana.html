Robiquetia josephiana

Robiquetia josephiana, commonly known as the Joseph's rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, ribbed leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
