Rubus trichopoides

Rubus trichopoides, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red berries. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky areas and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink and appear in the spring, followed by the development of its edible fruits.

The rough blackberry is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in local foraging and traditional medicine.
