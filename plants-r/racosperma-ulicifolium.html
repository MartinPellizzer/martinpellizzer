Racosperma ulicifolium

Racosperma ulicifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers in dense spikes, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds.

Racosperma ulicifolium is adapted to dry and arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian flora.
