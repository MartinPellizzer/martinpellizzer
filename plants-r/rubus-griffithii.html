Rubus griffithii

Rubus griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black berries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are armed with thorns.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its edible fruits in some regions.
