Ranunculus pseudolobatus

Ranunculus pseudolobatus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. It has basal leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

Ranunculus pseudolobatus is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.
