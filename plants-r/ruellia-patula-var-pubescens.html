Ruellia patula var. pubescens

Ruellia patula var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Ruellia patula var. pubescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
