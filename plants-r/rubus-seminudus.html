Rubus seminudus

Rubus seminudus, commonly known as the naked-stem blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white to pink and appear in the spring, followed by the development of its edible, dark purple berries in the summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
