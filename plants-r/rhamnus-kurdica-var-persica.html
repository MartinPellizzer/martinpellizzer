Rhamnus kurdica var. persica

Rhamnus kurdica var. persica, commonly known as Persian buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its fruit, which is a small, smooth berry, ripens to a dark purple or black color in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
