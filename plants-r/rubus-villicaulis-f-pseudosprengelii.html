Rubus villicaulis f. pseudosprengelii

Rubus villicaulis f. pseudosprengelii, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and fruit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of North America, particularly the eastern United States. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and produces small, dark berries that are edible. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a generally compact growth habit.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local ecosystems.
