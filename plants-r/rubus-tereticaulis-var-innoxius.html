Rubus tereticaulis var. innoxius

Rubus tereticaulis var. innoxius, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible but have a somewhat bland flavor. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
