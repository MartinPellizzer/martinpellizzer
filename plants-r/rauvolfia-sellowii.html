Rauvolfia sellowii

Rauvolfia sellowii, commonly known as the Brazilian snakeroot, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant contains alkaloids, particularly reserpine, which has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties.

Rauvolfia sellowii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is commonly found in the understory of rainforests.
