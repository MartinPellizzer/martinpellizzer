Rhododendron arboreum subsp. delavayi

Rhododendron arboreum subsp.

delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's laurel, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,500 meters.

The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The species is well adapted to cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.
