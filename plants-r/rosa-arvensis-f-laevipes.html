Rosa arvensis f. laevipes

Rosa arvensis f. laevipes, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed dog rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically smooth and oval in shape. The plant produces pink to red flowers with five petals, which bloom in the summer months. It is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This variety is distinguished by its relatively smooth stems compared to other forms of Rosa arvensis.
