Rubus condensiflorus

Rubus condensiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of many Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregated flowers that develop into edible berries, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and agricultural purposes.
