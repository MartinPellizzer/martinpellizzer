Rubus guentheri f. incisoserratus

Rubus guentheri f. incisoserratus, commonly known as the Guenther's raspberry.

This variant is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. The leaves are often serrated, with a distinctive incised pattern that gives it its specific epithet.

It is typically found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role.
