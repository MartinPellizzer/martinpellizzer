Rosa baicalensis

Rosa baicalensis, commonly known as the Chinese rose or Baikal rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountains of northeastern China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it is known for its aromatic foliage.

Rosa baicalensis is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.
