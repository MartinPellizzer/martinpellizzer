Rubus rubricolor

Rubus rubricolor, commonly known as the red-colored bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
