Rubus arduennensis subsp. moestus

Rubus arduennensis subsp.

moestus, commonly known as the Arduennes bramble, is a shrub native to the humid forests of Central Africa. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, typical of many Rubus species.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded undergrowth.
