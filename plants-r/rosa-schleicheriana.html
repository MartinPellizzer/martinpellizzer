Rosa schleicheriana

Rosa schleicheriana, commonly known as the Schleicher's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a shrub with upright, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces oval, glossy leaves with serrated edges, typical of many rose species. Its flowers are usually borne in groups of three to five, adding to its ornamental value.

Rosa schleicheriana is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
