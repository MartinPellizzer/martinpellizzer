Rosettea carrissoi

Rosettea carrissoi, commonly known as the Carrizo rosette plant, is a succulent native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It forms dense, compact rosettes of fleshy, spiny leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring or early summer.

This plant is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
