Rubus obscurus microgene opulentus

Rubus obscurus microgene opulentus, commonly known as the opulent blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full to partial sunlight for optimal growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other species within the Rubus genus.
