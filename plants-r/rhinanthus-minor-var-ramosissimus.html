Rhinanthus minor var. ramosissimus

Rhinanthus minor var. ramosissimus, commonly known as the lesser burnet, is a flowering plant belonging to the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, reddish-orange flowers that bloom from June to August. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. It has slender, branched stems and is often found in nutrient-poor soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
