Rubus swinhoei var. nesiotus

Rubus swinhoei var. nesiotus, commonly known as the Swinhoe's blackberry, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often described as having a tart and slightly astringent flavor.
