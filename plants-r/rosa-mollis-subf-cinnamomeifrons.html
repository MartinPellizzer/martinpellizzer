Rosa mollis subf. cinnamomeifrons

Rosa mollis subf.

cinnamomeifrons, commonly known as the cinnamon-leaf rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with a distinctive cinnamon-scented aroma.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in spring, which are followed by red, hairy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is a valued species in horticulture.
