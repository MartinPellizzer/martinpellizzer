Ranunculus cheesemanii

Ranunculus cheesemanii, commonly known as the Cheeseman's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or moist soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
