Rosa pimpinellifolia subsp. gentilis

Rosa pimpinellifolia subsp.

gentilis, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedgerow construction.
