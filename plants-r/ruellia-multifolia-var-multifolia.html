Ruellia multifolia var. multifolia

Ruellia multifolia var. multifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Ruellia multifolia var. multifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
