Rhododendron mayebarae

Rhododendron mayebarae, commonly known as the Mayebarae rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Its floral display, which ranges from pink to purple, makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.

The leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms.
