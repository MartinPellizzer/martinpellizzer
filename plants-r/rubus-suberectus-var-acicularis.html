Rubus suberectus var. acicularis

Rubus suberectus var. acicularis, commonly known as the needleberry raspberry, is a variant of the brambles family with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and acuminate, giving the plant a sharp, needle-like appearance. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, and the fruits are small, aggregate berries that mature to a red or black color.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in ecological restoration projects.
