Roella recurvata

Roella recurvata, commonly known as the narrowleaf roella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
