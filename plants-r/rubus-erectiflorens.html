Rubus erectiflorens

Rubus erectiflorens, commonly known as the erect-flowered blackberry, is a species of bramble native to southeastern China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
