Ranunculus reichertii

Ranunculus reichertii, commonly known as the Reichert's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves with lobed or divided foliage and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. Its flowers open in early spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Ranunculus reichertii is characterized by its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
