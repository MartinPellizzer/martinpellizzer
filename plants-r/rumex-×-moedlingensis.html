Rumex × moedlingensis

Rumex × moedlingensis, commonly known as the hybrid sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rumex acetosa (common sorrel) and Rumex acetobatus (water sorrel), resulting in a plant with distinctive botanical features. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy surface and a sour taste due to the presence of oxalic acid. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its reproductive strategy involves both self-fertilization and cross-pollination, contributing to its adaptability in various habitats.
