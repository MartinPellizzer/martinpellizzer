Rubus callistylus

Rubus callistylus, commonly known as the silky blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, glandular stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often described as having a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a distinctive silvery-white underside.

Rubus callistylus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
