Ribes magellanicum subsp. magellanicum

Ribes magellanicum subsp.

magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic gooseberry, is a species native to South America. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in temperate and subpolar regions.

The plant produces small, yellow or orange berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
