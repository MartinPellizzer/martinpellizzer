Rhododendron gratum

Rhododendron gratum, commonly known as the elegant rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Rhododendron gratum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to add vibrant color to landscapes.
