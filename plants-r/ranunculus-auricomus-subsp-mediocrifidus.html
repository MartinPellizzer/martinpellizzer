Ranunculus auricomus subsp. mediocrifidus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

mediocrifidus, commonly known as the lesser buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in early spring.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
