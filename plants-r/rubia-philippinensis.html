Rubia philippinensis

Rubia philippinensis, commonly known as Philippine madder, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its roots are used to extract a red dye, which has been historically used in traditional crafts.

Rubia philippinensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
