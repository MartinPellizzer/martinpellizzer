Rubus rudis var. oblongifolius

Rubus rudis var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctively rough texture. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically clustered together. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
