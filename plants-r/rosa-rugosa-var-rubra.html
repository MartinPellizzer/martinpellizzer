Rosa rugosa var. rubra

Rosa rugosa var. rubra, commonly known as the red rugosa rose, is a deciduous shrub native to coastal regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a deep red, hairy rosehip that persists through winter.

Rosa rugosa var. rubra is valued for its hardiness, adaptability, and ornamental value in gardens.
