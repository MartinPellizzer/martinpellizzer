Rottboellia protensa var. hamiltoniana

Rottboellia protensa var. hamiltoniana, commonly known as the Hamilton's bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are typically red or purple in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are arranged in elongated clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
