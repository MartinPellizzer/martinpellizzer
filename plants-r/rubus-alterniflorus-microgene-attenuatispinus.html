Rubus alterniflorus microgene attenuatispinus

Rubus alterniflorus microgene attenuatispinus, commonly known as the narrow-spined blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
