Rosa wichurana var. glandulifera

Rosa wichurana var. glandulifera, commonly known as the Chilean rose or the Chilean wild rose, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in spring and early summer. Its fruit is a woody follicle containing several oval seeds, and it is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
