Roegneria gmelinii var. tenuiseta

Roegneria gmelinii var. tenuiseta, commonly known as slender-stemmed goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, with inflorescences consisting of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This variety is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
