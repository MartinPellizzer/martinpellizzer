Rubus commiscibilis

Rubus commiscibilis, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into the familiar red, hairy berries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its growth habit and fruiting cycle make it a valuable plant in both natural ecosystems and agricultural settings.
