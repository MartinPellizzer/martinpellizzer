Rivea apoensis

Rivea apoensis, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Rivea apoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its historical use in herbal remedies.
