Rhododendron meagae

Rhododendron meagae, commonly known as the Meagae rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, preferring acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Rhododendron meagae is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler, humid climates.
