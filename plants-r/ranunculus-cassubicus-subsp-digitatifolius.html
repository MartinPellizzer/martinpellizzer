Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. digitatifolius

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

digitatifolius, commonly known as the digitate buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 sepals that are often mistaken for petals.

The plant has a basal rosette of trifoliate or digitate (finger-like) leaves, which are typically hairy and have a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and often ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
