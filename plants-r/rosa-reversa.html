Rosa reversa

Rosa reversa, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, single-layered pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces red, hairy fruits called hips. Rosa reversa is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
