Rubus eurythyrsiger

Rubus eurythyrsiger, commonly known as the broad-throated blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
