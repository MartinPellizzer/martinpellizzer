Rhododendron brachystylum

Rhododendron brachystylum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and dark green, providing dense foliage throughout the year.

Rhododendron brachystylum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
