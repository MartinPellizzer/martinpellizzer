Rubus flagellaris f. roseoplenus

Rubus flagellaris f. roseoplenus, commonly known as the roseleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive rose-colored underside. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. Its flowers are white and arranged in clusters, typical of the Rubus genus.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
