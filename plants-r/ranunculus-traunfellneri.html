Ranunculus traunfellneri

Ranunculus traunfellneri, commonly known as the Traunfellner's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations. It has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
