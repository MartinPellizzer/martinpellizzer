Rosa uriensis var. rufescens

Rosa uriensis var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed wild rose, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the stems are often covered in a reddish hue, especially when mature. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a food source for various pollinators.
