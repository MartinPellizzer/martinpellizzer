Rapistrum rugosum var. macrocarpum

Rapistrum rugosum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded wild radish, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant features erect, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lobed and hairy. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in racemes, and it produces large, round, hairy fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
