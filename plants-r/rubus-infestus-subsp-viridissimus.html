Rubus infestus subsp. viridissimus

Rubus infestus subsp.

viridissimus, commonly known as the greenleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
