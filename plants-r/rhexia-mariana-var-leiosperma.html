Rhexia mariana var. leiosperma

Rhexia mariana var. leiosperma, commonly known as smoothflower marshplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, thriving in swampy or marshy environments. The plant features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate saturated soil conditions and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
