Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. prorsidens

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

prorsidens, commonly known as the upright buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June.

The plant has finely divided, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Ranunculaceae family.
