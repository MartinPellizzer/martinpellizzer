Randia stenantha var. tomentosa

Randia stenantha var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems covered in a dense layer of soft, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are elliptical or ovate, with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its overall appearance.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
