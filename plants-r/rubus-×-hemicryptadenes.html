Rubus × hemicryptadenes

Rubus × hemicryptadenes, commonly known as the blackberry dewberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are usually dark purple to black in color, containing small drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
