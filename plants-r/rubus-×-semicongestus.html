Rubus × semicongestus

Rubus × semicongestus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and thickets. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically sweet and juicy, making it a popular fruit for both commercial and home cultivation. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it is known for its vigorous growth habit.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural practices.
