Racosperma maitlandii

Racosperma maitlandii, commonly known as Maitland's wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
