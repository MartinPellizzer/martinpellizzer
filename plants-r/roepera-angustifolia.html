Roepera angustifolia

Roepera angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf roepera, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or streambanks. The plant features slender, linear leaves that are often arranged alternately along its stem. Its small, white or pale pink flowers bloom in clusters during the spring or early summer.

Roepera angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
