Rhamnus calderoniae

Rhamnus calderoniae, commonly known as the Caldesia buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, black berries that are a food source for various bird species. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
