Rheum turkestanicum

Rheum turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan rhubarb, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces reddish-orange flowers in late spring, which are clustered in panicle-like inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their medicinal properties, containing compounds like anthraquinones.

Rheum turkestanicum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
