Ranunculus laetus

Ranunculus laetus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to ten petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges across temperate regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus laetus is an herbaceous perennial that blooms in spring and early summer.
