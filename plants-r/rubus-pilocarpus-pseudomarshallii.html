Rubus pilocarpus pseudomarshallii

Rubus pilocarpus pseudomarshallii, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
