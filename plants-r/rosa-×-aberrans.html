Rosa × aberrans

Rosa × aberrans, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often forming a shrub-like structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Rosa × aberrans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
