Rhabdotheca angustifolia

Rhabdotheca angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf rhabdotheca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying xerophytic plants.
