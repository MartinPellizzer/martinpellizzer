Ranunculus angustifidus

Ranunculus angustifidus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, ribbed stem. The leaves are typically divided into three segments, with a distinctive narrow shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.
