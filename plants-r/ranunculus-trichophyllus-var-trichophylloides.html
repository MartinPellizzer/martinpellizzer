Ranunculus trichophyllus var. trichophylloides

Ranunculus trichophyllus var. trichophylloides, commonly known as the hairy leaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem covered in fine, soft trichomes. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated edge and a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, daisy-like blooms.
