Rhynchosia argentea

Rhynchosia argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf clusterbean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name, and produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil, and its seeds are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a forage crop or for its ornamental value.

Rhynchosia argentea is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
