Rosa micrantha var. frankorum

Rosa micrantha var. frankorum, commonly known as the Frank's shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features upright, thorny stems and is characterized by its small, fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
