Rubia cordifolia subsp. pratensis

Rubia cordifolia subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as red root or greater cow-wheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, red or purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces a deep red root system, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal pharmacology.
