Rubus micans var. sublaevis

Rubus micans var. sublaevis, commonly known as the soft-leaved raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves that are less hairy compared to other Rubus species. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variant is valued for its culinary uses and is also appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
