Rosa × dumetorum var. complicata

Rosa × dumetorum var. complicata, commonly known as the creeping rose, is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. Its stems are covered in thorns, which are usually short and curved.

This variety is well-suited for ground cover in moist, partially shaded environments.
