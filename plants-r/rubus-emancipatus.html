Rubus emancipatus

Rubus emancipatus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions and river valleys.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
