Rubus cimbricus microgene schwenckfeldii

Rubus cimbricus microgene schwenckfeldii, commonly known as the lesser bramble, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are usually red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
