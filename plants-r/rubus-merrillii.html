Rubus merrillii

Rubus merrillii, commonly known as the Merrill's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
