Rubus menkei subsp. brachyadenes

Rubus menkei subsp.

brachyadenes, commonly known as the short-stalked blackberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically eaten raw or used in jams. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open woodland or scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
