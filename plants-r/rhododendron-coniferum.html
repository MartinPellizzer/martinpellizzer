Rhododendron coniferum

Rhododendron coniferum, commonly known as the dwarf laurel, is a evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It features dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year, giving it a lush, tropical appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping for its year-round visual interest.
