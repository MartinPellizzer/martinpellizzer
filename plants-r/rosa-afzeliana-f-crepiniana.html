Rosa afzeliana f. crepiniana

Rosa afzeliana f. crepiniana, commonly known as the Crepiniana rose, is a botanical variety native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral characteristics, it is often cultivated for both its beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
