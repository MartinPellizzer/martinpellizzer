Rubus pascuus

Rubus pascuus, commonly known as the common bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit, known as a raspberry, is aggregate fruit formed from multiple drupelets.

Rubus pascuus is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
