Rosa canina var. domini

Rosa canina var. domini, commonly known as the Domini dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa genus. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it produces red, hairy fruits known as hips.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
