Rosa grandiflora

Rosa grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 5 inches in diameter. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white petals and has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Rosa grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
