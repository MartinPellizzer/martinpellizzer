Rohrbachia martini

Rohrbachia martini, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the surrounding substrate, a common feature among orchids.

The botanical classification places it within the family Orchidaceae, highlighting its evolutionary adaptations for epiphytic or terrestrial growth.
