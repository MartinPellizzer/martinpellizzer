Ranunculus pentodon

Ranunculus pentodon, commonly known as the five-toothed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with numerous stamens and a distinct central pistil.

Ranunculus pentodon thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
