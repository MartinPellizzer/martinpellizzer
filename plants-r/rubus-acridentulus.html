Rubus acridentulus

Rubus acridentulus, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. Its leaves are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are white with five petals.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
