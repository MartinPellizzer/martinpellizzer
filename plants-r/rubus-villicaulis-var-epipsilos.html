Rubus villicaulis var. epipsilos

Rubus villicaulis var. epipsilos, commonly known as the hairy-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry genus characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges and a soft, downy texture.

The botanical features of this plant make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and ecological research.
