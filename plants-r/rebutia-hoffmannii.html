Rebutia hoffmannii

Rebutia hoffmannii, commonly known as the Hoffmann's moon cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features a thick, segmented stem covered in spines, which helps it retain water in arid environments. It produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage.

Rebutia hoffmannii is well-suited for rock gardens and container gardening due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
