Rosa montenegrina

Rosa montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegrin rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, pinnate leaves, and numerous stamens in its showy flowers.

Rosa montenegrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
