Rhodocolea racemosa f. microphylla

Rhodocolea racemosa f. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved rhodocolea, is a unique variant of the rhodocolea genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more densely packed leaves compared to the typical species. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

This cultivar is often appreciated for its adaptability and aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
