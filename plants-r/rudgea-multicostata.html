Rudgea multicostata

Rudgea multicostata, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its bark is often fissured and rough in appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Rudgea multicostata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
