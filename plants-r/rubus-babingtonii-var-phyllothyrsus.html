Rubus babingtonii var. phyllothyrsus

Rubus babingtonii var. phyllothyrsus, commonly known as the Babington's blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
