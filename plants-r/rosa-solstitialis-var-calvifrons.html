Rosa solstitialis var. calvifrons

Rosa solstitialis var. calvifrons, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to reddish-orange flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as fields and along roadsides.
