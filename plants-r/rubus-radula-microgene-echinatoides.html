Rubus radula microgene echinatoides

Rubus radula microgene echinatoides, commonly known as the spiny raspberry, is a species of bramble characterized by its woody stems and thorny growth.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, containing multiple drupes. The plant is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has a shallow root system that helps it anchor in rocky or disturbed areas.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
