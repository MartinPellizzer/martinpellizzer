Rubus utschii

Rubus utschii, commonly known as the Tibetan raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and eastern Tibet.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible berries. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are pink to white in color.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and are valued for their tart flavor and nutritional content.
