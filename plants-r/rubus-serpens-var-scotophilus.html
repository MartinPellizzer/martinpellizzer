Rubus serpens var. scotophilus

Rubus serpens var. scotophilus, commonly known as the shade raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant adapted to low-light conditions.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, aggregate fruits resembling raspberries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use in shaded gardens.
