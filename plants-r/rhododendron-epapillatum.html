Rhododendron epapillatum

Rhododendron epapillatum, commonly known as the Papillate Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its botanical features include large, woolly bracts that surround the flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rhododendron epapillatum is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
