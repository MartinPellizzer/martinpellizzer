Ranunculus oxynotus

Ranunculus oxynotus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus oxynotus is characterized by its hairy stems and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils.
