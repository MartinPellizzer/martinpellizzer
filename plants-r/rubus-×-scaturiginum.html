Rubus × scaturiginum

Rubus × scaturiginum, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between blackberries (Rubus fruticosus) and raspberries (Rubus idaeus).

It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, featuring thorny stems and aggregate fruits that are typically red to black in color. The plant is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces edible fruits that are often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is often cultivated as a decorative shrub or for its fruit yield.
