Rapuntium kohautianum

Rapuntium kohautianum, commonly known as the Kohautian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
