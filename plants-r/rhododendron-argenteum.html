Rhododendron argenteum

Rhododendron argenteum, commonly known as the silverleaf rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white undersides on its elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
