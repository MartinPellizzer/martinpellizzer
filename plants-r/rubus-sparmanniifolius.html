Rubus sparmanniifolius

Rubus sparmanniifolius, commonly known as the Sparmanniifolius blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect or sprawling growth habit and produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

The fruit is edible and is sometimes used in local foraging and culinary traditions.
