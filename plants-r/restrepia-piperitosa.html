Restrepia piperitosa

Restrepia piperitosa, commonly known as the Piperita orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments such as cloud forests and high-altitude grasslands. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a velvety texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Restrepia piperitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
