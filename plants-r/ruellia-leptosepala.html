Ruellia leptosepala

Ruellia leptosepala, commonly known as the slender-sepaled ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are usually blue or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated calyx.

Ruellia leptosepala is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
