Rubus macrostachys subsp. chaboissaei

Rubus macrostachys subsp.

chaboissaei, commonly known as the Chaboissai raspberry, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
