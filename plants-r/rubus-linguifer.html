Rubus linguifer

Rubus linguifer, commonly known as the tongue-shaped raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
