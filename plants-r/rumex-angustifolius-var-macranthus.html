Rumex angustifolius var. macranthus

Rumex angustifolius var. macranthus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock with large flowers, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish to reddish flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
