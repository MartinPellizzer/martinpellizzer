Rubus posoniensis

Rubus posoniensis, commonly known as the Poson Island blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the island of Poson in the Philippines.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in the late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels.

The fruit is valued for its high antioxidant content and is used in local traditional medicine and culinary practices.
