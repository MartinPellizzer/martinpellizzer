Rhynchosia desertorum

Rhynchosia desertorum, commonly known as the desert legume, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pod-like fruits and trifoliate leaves. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, featuring deep root systems and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss. It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, supporting other plant life in nutrient-poor soils.

Rhynchosia desertorum is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture and land rehabilitation projects.
