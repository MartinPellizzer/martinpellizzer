Rhododendron indicum var. kirishima

Rhododendron indicum var. kirishima, commonly known as the Kirishima rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
