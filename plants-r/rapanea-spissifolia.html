Rapanea spissifolia

Rapanea spissifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved evergreen, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, round berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage.
