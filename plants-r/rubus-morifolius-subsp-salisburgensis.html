Rubus morifolius subsp. salisburgensis

Rubus morifolius subsp.

salisburgensis, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a shrub native to Japan. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and desserts.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
