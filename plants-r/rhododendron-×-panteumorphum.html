Rhododendron × panteumorphum

Rhododendron × panteumorphum, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Rhododendron peregrinum and Rhododendron molle, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its botanical features include leathery leaves and inflorescences that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.

This hybrid is valued for its hardiness and adaptability in various climates.
