Reseda spathulata

Reseda spathulata, commonly known as broadleaf reseda, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in summer. The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers.

Reseda spathulata is also known for its historical use in dye production and as a source of natural pigments.
