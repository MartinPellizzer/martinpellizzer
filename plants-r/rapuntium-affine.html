Rapuntium affine

Rapuntium affine, commonly known as the slender cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, upright growth habit and features alternate, linear to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its fruits are short, cylindrical siliques that contain several seeds.

Rapuntium affine is typically found in moist, disturbed soils and is often seen along stream banks and in meadows.
