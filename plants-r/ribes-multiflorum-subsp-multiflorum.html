Ribes multiflorum subsp. multiflorum

Ribes multiflorum subsp.

multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, edible berries that range in color from red to orange, depending on the variety. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
