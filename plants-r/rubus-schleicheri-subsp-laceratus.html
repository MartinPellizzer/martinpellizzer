Rubus schleicheri subsp. laceratus

Rubus schleicheri subsp.

laceratus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
