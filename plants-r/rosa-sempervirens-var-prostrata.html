Rosa sempervirens var. prostrata

Rosa sempervirens var. prostrata, commonly known as the creeping evergreen rose, is a low-growing, shrub-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features dense, thornless foliage with small, dark green leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ground cover or rock gardens.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
