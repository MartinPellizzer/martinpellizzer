Rhododendron indicum var. eriocarpum

Rhododendron indicum var. eriocarpum, commonly known as the woolly-barked rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems that give it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and arranged in whorls along the stems.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.
