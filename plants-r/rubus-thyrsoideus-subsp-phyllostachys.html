Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. phyllostachys

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

phyllostachys, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a shrub-like plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically serrated.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are often red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
