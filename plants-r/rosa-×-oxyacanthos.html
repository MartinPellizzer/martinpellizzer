Rosa × oxyacanthos

Rosa × oxyacanthos, commonly known as the everblooming rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa × centifolia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and its ability to produce flowers throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has dark green, lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa × oxyacanthos is well-suited for coastal areas due to its salt tolerance and hardy nature.
