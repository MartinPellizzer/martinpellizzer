Rumex alpestris subsp. lapponicus

Rumex alpestris subsp.

lapponicus, commonly known as Arctic sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in tundra and mountainous areas where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
