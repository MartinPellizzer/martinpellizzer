Rubus trichocarpus

Rubus trichocarpus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria, typically ripening to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrubland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
