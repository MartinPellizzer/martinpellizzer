Rubus godronii var. kolmariensis

Rubus godronii var. kolmariensis, commonly known as the Kolmari raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color, with a tart flavor. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential use in local cuisine.
