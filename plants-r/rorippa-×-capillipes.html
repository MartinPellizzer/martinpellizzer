Rorippa × capillipes

Rorippa × capillipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a hybrid aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often floating on the water surface. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize sediment in shallow waters.

Rorippa × capillipes is often found in ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams, where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.
