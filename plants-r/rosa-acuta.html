Rosa acuta

Rosa acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a pointed tip, giving it its common name. Its fragrant flowers, typically pink or white, bloom in spring and summer.

Rosa acuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
