Rosa banksiae f. albiflora

Rosa banksiae f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered banksia rose, is a cultivar of the Banksia rose species.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which are more prominent than those of the typical Banksia rose. The plant features thorned stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
