Rosa coriifolia subsp. subcanina

Rosa coriifolia subsp.

subcanina, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with ovate to elliptic leaflets that have a slightly serrated margin. This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively disease-resistant.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
