Rhamnus biniflora

Rhamnus biniflora, commonly known as the two-flowered buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky soils, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark, leathery berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, often with a waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
