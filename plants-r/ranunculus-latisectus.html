Ranunculus latisectus

Ranunculus latisectus, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped yellow flowers and deeply lobed, palmately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually broad and have a distinctive, intricate pattern of lobes.

Ranunculus latisectus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
