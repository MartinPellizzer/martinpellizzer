Rosa uniserrata

Rosa uniserrata, commonly known as the single-toothed rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, serrated leaf margins and produces fragrant, single-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Rosa uniserrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
