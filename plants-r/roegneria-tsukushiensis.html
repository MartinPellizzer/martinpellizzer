Roegneria tsukushiensis

Roegneria tsukushiensis, commonly known as the Japanese wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Roegneria tsukushiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and delicate appearance.
