Reseda amblycarpa var. eritreae

Reseda amblycarpa var. eritreae, commonly known as the Eritrean sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
