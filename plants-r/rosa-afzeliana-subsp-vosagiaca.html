Rosa afzeliana subsp. vosagiaca

Rosa afzeliana subsp.

vosagiaca, commonly known as the Vosagian rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
