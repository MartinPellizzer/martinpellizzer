Rubus fuscus microgene armatipes

Rubus fuscus microgene armatipes, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy surface.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
