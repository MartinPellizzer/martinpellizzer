Roella decumbens

Roella decumbens, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils near the shoreline. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Roella decumbens is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate harsh coastal conditions.
