Reimaria brasiliensis

Reimaria brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it blooms during the dry season.

Reimaria brasiliensis is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
