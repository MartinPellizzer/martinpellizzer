Rubus × semifecundus

Rubus × semifecundus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant year-round. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
