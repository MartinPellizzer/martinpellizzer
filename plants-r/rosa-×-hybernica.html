Rosa × hybernica

Rosa × hybernica, commonly known as the Irish rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa canina.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a distinctive double or semi-double bloom. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs, while its fruit is a red, hairy rose hip.

Rosa × hybernica is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
