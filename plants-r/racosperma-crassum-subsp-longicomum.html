Racosperma crassum subsp. longicomum

Racosperma crassum subsp.

longicomum, commonly known as the long-stemmed wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits.

The plant features prominently branched stems and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowers are typically yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces long, slender pods.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
