Rubus suberectus f. crassifolius

Rubus suberectus f. crassifolius, commonly known as the thick-leaved raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its broad, leathery leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. Its foliage is dense and often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
