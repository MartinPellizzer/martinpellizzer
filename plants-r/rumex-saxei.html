Rumex saxei

Rumex saxei, commonly known as saxifrage sorrel, is a perennial plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Rumex saxei is adapted to grow in poor, well-drained soils and can thrive in harsh, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species often found in mountainous landscapes.
