Rubus × amictiformis

Rubus × amictiformis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is native to the western United States and thrives in moist, shady environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex genetic makeup and adaptability to various soil types.
