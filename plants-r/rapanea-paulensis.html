Rapanea paulensis

Rapanea paulensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically followed by dark blue berries. Its dense foliage and compact growth make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Rapanea paulensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its resistance to pests and diseases.
