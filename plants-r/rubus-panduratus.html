Rubus panduratus

Rubus panduratus, commonly known as the Japanese bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

Rubus panduratus is native to Japan and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
