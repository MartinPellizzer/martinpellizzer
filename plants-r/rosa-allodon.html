Rosa allodon

Rosa allodon, commonly known as the white-barked rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist woodlands and stream banks. The plant features fragrant white flowers with five petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The rose is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
