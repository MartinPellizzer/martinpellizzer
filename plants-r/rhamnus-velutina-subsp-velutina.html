Rhamnus velutina subsp. velutina

Rhamnus velutina subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, woodlands and has smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered with a fine, velvety pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark, glossy berries. Its berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
