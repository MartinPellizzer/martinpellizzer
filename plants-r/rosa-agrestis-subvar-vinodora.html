Rosa agrestis subvar. vinodora

Rosa agrestis subvar. vinodora, commonly known as the wild pink rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems typical of the Rosa genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and aromatic blooms.
