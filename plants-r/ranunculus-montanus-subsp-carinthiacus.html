Ranunculus montanus subsp. carinthiacus

Ranunculus montanus subsp.

carinthiacus, commonly known as the Carinthian crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps. It typically grows in moist, calcareous meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals, which are often mistaken for true petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive basal rosette structure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, spring-blooming flowers.
