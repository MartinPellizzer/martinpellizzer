Resia ichthyoides

Resia ichthyoides, commonly known as the fishbone plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that resemble fish bones. The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Resia ichthyoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
