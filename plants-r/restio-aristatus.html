Restio aristatus

Restio aristatus, commonly known as the common restia, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, often found in fynbos vegetation. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small white to pale blue flowers.

Restio aristatus plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
