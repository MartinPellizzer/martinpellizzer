Rubus praecox var. rhodanthus

Rubus praecox var. rhodanthus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red when ripe and contain multiple seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological systems.
