Rubus × minatorius

Rubus × minatorius, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically green with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries that ripen to a deep black or dark purple color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
