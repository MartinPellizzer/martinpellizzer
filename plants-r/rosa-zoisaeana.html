Rosa zoisaeana

Rosa zoisaeana, commonly known as the Zois's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its simple, thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, semi-double flowers that range in color from white to pink. Rosa zoisaeana typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for study in plant ecology and horticulture.
