Rosa × damascena var. densiflora

Rosa × damascena var. densiflora, commonly known as the Damask rose variety with dense flowering, is a hybrid rose species prized for its rich fragrance and ornamental value.

It is characterized by its dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems, typical of many rose species. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular watering and pruning for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens and used in the production of rose essential oils.
