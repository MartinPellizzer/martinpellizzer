Racosperma hakeoides

Racosperma hakeoides, commonly known as the Hakea-like acacia, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden planting in suitable climates.
