Ranunculus boreanus

Ranunculus boreanus, commonly known as the Arctic buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing herb with simple, alternate leaves and yellow flowers that typically have five to seven petals. The plant thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in tundra environments. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Ranunculus boreanus plays a role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for herbivores.
