Rinzia affinis

Rinzia affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rinzia, is a small flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

Rinzia affinis is an important component of native plant communities and supports various pollinators.
