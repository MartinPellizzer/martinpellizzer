Rosa agrestis subvar. belnensis

Rosa agrestis subvar. belnensis, commonly known as the Belnensian wild rose, is a shrub-like plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The fruit is a red, hairy rose hip that matures in autumn, containing seeds within its follicle.
