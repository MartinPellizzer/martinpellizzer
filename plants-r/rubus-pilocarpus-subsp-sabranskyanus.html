Rubus pilocarpus subsp. sabranskyanus

Rubus pilocarpus subsp.

sabranskyanus, commonly known as the Sabransky's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their flavor in local cuisines.
