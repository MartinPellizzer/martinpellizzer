Rosa × anisopoda f. grandiflora

Rosa × anisopoda f. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora rose, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It is known for its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink, with a subtle fragrance. The plant has a semi-woody stem structure with thornless or sparingly thorned branches, making it suitable for ornamental gardens. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and they provide a lush green backdrop to the blooms.

Rosa × anisopoda f. grandiflora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, demonstrating moderate to high drought tolerance once established.
