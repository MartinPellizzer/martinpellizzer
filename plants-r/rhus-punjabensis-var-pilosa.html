Rhus punjabensis var. pilosa

Rhus punjabensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the pilose buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in dry, rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
