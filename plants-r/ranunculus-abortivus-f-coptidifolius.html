Ranunculus abortivus f. coptidifolius

Ranunculus abortivus f. coptidifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a variant of the buttercup genus characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs and have a distinct, waxy texture.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
