Rapuntium brevifolium

Rapuntium brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved cress, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its flowering stems are usually short and branched, with flowers appearing at the tips.

Rapuntium brevifolium is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland floors and along stream banks.
