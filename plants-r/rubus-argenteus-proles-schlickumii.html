Rubus argenteus proles schlickumii

Rubus argenteus proles schlickumii, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
