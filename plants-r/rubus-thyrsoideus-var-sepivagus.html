Rubus thyrsoideus var. sepivagus

Rubus thyrsoideus var. sepivagus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a perennial shrub characterized by its erect, spiny stems and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, which are edible and have a tangy flavor. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cool, temperate climates. It is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
