Ranunculus auricomus subsp. tenuistylus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

tenuistylus, commonly known as the narrow-styled golden buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five to seven petals.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slender, narrow styles in the flower structure. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
