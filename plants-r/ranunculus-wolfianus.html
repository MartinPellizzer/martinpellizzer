Ranunculus wolfianus

Ranunculus wolfianus, commonly known as the Wolf's milk buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy, often toxic plants. The plant features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
