Rosa sempervirens var. dasoii

Rosa sempervirens var. dasoii, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a variant of the evergreen rose species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year, making it a popular choice for hedges and screens. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with thorny stems and glossy leaves.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
