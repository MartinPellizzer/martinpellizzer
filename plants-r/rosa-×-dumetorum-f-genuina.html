Rosa × dumetorum f. genuina

Rosa × dumetorum f. genuina, commonly known as the alpine rose or alpine dog rose, is a hybrid species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or low shrubs, and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is adapted to cold, rocky environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically dark green and slightly hairy, with serrated edges.

Rosa × dumetorum f. genuina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
