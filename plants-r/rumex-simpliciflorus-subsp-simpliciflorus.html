Rumex simpliciflorus subsp. simpliciflorus

Rumex simpliciflorus subsp.

simpliciflorus, commonly known as narrowleaf dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple midrib. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense, upright inflorescences.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing food for various wildlife species.
