Ranunculus auricomus subsp. venustus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

venustus, commonly known as the golden crowncup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking yellow petals that resemble a crown, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
