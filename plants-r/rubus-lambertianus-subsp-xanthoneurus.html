Rubus lambertianus subsp. xanthoneurus

Rubus lambertianus subsp.

xanthoneurus, commonly known as the yellow-tipped blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are usually armed with sharp prickles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive yellow sepals that persist on the fruiting stems.
