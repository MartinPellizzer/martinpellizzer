Roemeria argentea

Roemeria argentea, commonly known as the silver roemeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, hairy foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Roemeria argentea has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silver hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
