Rubus candicans var. sumugiensis

Rubus candicans var. sumugiensis, commonly known as the Japanese raspberry, is a shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, red-fruited flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and horticultural settings.
