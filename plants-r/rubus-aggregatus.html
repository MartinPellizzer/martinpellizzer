Rubus aggregatus

Rubus aggregatus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
