Rosa molliorbicantula

Rosa molliorbicantula, commonly known as the soft-ribbed rose, is a species characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers and soft, hairy stems.

It typically features pink to white petals with a subtle fragrance, and its leaves are ovate with a serrated edge. The plant is native to certain regions of Asia and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and mild growth habit.

However, it is less commonly found in horticultural collections compared to more widely cultivated rose species.
