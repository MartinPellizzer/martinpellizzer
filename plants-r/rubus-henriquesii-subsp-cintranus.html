Rubus henriquesii subsp. cintranus

Rubus henriquesii subsp.

cintranus, commonly known as the Cintranus blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
