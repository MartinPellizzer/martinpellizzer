Rosa longicruris var. typica

Rosa longicruris var. typica, commonly known as the long-stemmed wild rose, is a species native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Rosa longicruris var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
