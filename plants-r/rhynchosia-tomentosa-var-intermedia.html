Rhynchosia tomentosa var. intermedia

Rhynchosia tomentosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a legume for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural systems.
