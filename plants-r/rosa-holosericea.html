Rosa holosericea

Rosa holosericea, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish woolly hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Rosa holosericea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
