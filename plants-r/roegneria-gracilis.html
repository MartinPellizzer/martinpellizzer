Roegneria gracilis

Roegneria gracilis, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, along stream banks, and in open woodlands.

Roegneria gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
