Roettlera urticifolia

Roettlera urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing a skin reaction in humans. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Roettlera urticifolia is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with small greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.
