Rubus × bicolor

Rubus × bicolor, commonly known as the blackberry-loganberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus loganobaccus (loganberry), resulting in a plant with unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that range in color from dark purple to red.

The hybrid is valued for its vigorous growth and the flavor of its fruits, which are often used in culinary applications.
