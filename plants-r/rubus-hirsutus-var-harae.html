Rubus hirsutus var. harae

Rubus hirsutus var. harae, commonly known as the Japanese bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan, and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
