Ramotha vera

Ramotha vera, commonly known as the banana plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant belonging to the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces a fruiting stem called a "hand," which bears clusters of bananas. Its root system consists of a central rhizome with fibrous roots that help anchor the plant.

Ramotha vera is cultivated worldwide for its edible fruits and has significant economic and nutritional importance.
