Rosa stylosa var. duffouriana

Rosa stylosa var. duffouriana, commonly known as the Duffour's shrub rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorned stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets that are dark green and slightly hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
