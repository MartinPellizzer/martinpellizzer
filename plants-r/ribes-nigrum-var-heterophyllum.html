Ribes nigrum var. heterophyllum

Ribes nigrum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the blackcurrant hybrid, is a variant of the blackcurrant plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology, featuring a mix of different leaf shapes on the same plant, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

This variety is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
