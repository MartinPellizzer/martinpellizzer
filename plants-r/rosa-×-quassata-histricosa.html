Rosa × quassata histricosa

Rosa × quassata histricosa, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a hybrid species within the genus Rosa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces red or orange hips that contain seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
