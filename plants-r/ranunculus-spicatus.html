Ranunculus spicatus

Ranunculus spicatus, commonly known as the spiny buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically palmately lobed and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands.

The plant is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
