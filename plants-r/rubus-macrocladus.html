Rubus macrocladus

Rubus macrocladus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
