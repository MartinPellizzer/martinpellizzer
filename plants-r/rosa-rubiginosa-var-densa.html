Rosa rubiginosa var. densa

Rosa rubiginosa var. densa, commonly known as the dense rose or the red-brown rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, clustered flowers that range from pink to red in color. The plant produces rose hips that are typically orange-red and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
