Rosa dichroa

Rosa dichroa, commonly known as the Japanese rose or twinleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into 5-9 elliptical leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are thornless, and it prefers well-drained soil in partial shade.

Rosa dichroa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
