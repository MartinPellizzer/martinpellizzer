Rosa omissa subvar. tunoniensis

Rosa omissa subvar. tunoniensis, commonly known as the Tunon's rose, is a rare and delicate species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pale pink flowers and narrow, fragrant leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in early summer, adding a subtle beauty to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
