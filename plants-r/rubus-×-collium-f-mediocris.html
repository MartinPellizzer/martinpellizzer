Rubus × collium f. mediocris

Rubus × collium f. mediocris, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and serrated, while the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries, which are dark purple to black when mature, are edible and often used in culinary applications.
