Ratonia anodonta

Ratonia anodonta, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and pinnately arranged, with small, ovate leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
