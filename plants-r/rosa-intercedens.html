Rosa intercedens

Rosa intercedens, commonly known as the interceding rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems covered in thorns. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and serrated.

Rosa intercedens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
