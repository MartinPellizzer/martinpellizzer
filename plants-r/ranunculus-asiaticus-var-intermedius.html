Ranunculus asiaticus var. intermedius

Ranunculus asiaticus var. intermedius, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It features showy, double flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has finely divided, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming habit.
