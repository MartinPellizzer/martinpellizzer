Ribes tortuosum

Ribes tortuosum, commonly known as the creeping gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Grossulariaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Ribes tortuosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
