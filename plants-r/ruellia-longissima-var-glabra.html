Ruellia longissima var. glabra

Ruellia longissima var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and hairless. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
