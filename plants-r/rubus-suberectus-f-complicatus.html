Rubus suberectus f. complicatus

Rubus suberectus f. complicatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a botanical variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are usually compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and edible fruits.
