Rosa canina var. iurensis

Rosa canina var. iurensis, commonly known as the Iurensis rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers and thorny stems. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
