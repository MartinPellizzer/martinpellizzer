Rhynchosia gansole

Rhynchosia gansole, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod fruits containing angular, brown seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Rhynchosia gansole is valued for its nutritional content and use in traditional agriculture and medicine.
