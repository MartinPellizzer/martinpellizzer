Ranunculus calthifolius

Ranunculus calthifolius, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lobed, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from May to July. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Ranunculus calthifolius is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
