Racosperma porcatum

Racosperma porcatum, commonly known as the porcate acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Racosperma porcatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
