Rubus tomentosus f. calabarensis

Rubus tomentosus f. calabarensis, commonly known as the Calabar bramble, is a shrub native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are edible berries that are typically red or black when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.
