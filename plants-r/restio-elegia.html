Restio elegia

Restio elegia, commonly known as the silver restio, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is a member of the Restiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Restio elegia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its unique foliage and elegant flowering make it a valuable addition to botanic gardens and conservation areas.
