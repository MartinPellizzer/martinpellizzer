Racosperma spinescens

Racosperma spinescens, commonly known as the spiny wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellow floral spikes that bloom during the spring. The plant has compound leaves with linear, spiny leaflets, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Racosperma spinescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
