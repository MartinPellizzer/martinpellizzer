Rhodocolea racemosa var. pilosula

Rhodocolea racemosa var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy raceme rhodocolea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its varietal name "pilosula," which means "hairy" in Latin. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
