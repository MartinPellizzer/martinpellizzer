Rubus extensus f. major

Rubus extensus f. major, commonly known as the large-leafed blackberry.

It is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its larger, more robust leaves and thorny stems. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
