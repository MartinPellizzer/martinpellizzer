Rosa tomentella var. atroviridis

Rosa tomentella var. atroviridis, commonly known as the blackleaf rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, dark green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

This variety is well adapted to rocky soils and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
