Ranunculus variabilis

Ranunculus variabilis, commonly known as the variable buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has palmately divided leaves. It produces small, green fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Ranunculus variabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
