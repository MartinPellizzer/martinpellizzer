Rosa sericea

Rosa sericea, commonly known as the silvery rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa sericea is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with thorny stems and simple, alternate leaves.

It is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
