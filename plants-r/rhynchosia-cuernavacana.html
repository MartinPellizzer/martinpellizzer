Rhynchosia cuernavacana

Rhynchosia cuernavacana, commonly known as the Cuernavaca bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. Rhynchosia cuernavacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its seeds, which can be used in traditional cuisines.

Its growth is often associated with humid, tropical environments where it thrives in partial shade.
