Ranunculus mendax

Ranunculus mendax, commonly known as the false water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers usually have five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

Ranunculus mendax is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
