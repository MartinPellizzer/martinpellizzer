Rhamnus insularis

Rhamnus insularis, commonly known as the island buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, black berries that are edible for birds.

Rhamnus insularis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
