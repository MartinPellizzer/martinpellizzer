Rosa viscaria var. dispar

Rosa viscaria var. dispar, commonly known as the variable brambles, is a species of shrub in the rose family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable plant, often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
