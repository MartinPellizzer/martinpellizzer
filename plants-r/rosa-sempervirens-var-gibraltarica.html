Rosa sempervirens var. gibraltarica

Rosa sempervirens var. gibraltarica, commonly known as the Gibraltar rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which remains dense and dark green throughout the year. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in Mediterranean climates.
