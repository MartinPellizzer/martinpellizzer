Rubus haeupleri

Rubus haeupleri, commonly known as the Haeupler's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces red, hairy berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest edges and shrublands.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
