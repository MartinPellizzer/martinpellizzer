Rubia tetragona

Rubia tetragona, commonly known as Spanish bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, quadrangular stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Rubia tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
