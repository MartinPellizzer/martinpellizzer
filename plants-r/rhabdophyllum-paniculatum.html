Rhabdophyllum paniculatum

Rhabdophyllum paniculatum, commonly known as the zigzag or false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and palmate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in clusters during the warm seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
