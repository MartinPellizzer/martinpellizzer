Rubus vulgaris macrophyllus

Rubus vulgaris macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, serrated leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous small drupes, which are usually dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value in gardens.
