Rubus × flexuosifrons

Rubus × flexuosifrons, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its trailing or prostrate growth habit, which allows it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
