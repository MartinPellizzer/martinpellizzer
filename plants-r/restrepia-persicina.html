Restrepia persicina

Restrepia persicina, commonly known as the Persicana orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that supports its growth in cooler, high-altitude environments. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. Its leaves are typically elongated and grass-like, adapting to the humid, shaded conditions of its habitat.

Restrepia persicina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
