Rubus vestitus var. courtoisianus

Rubus vestitus var. courtoisianus, commonly known as the Courtoisian raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
