Ranunculus bonariensis subsp. bonariensis

Ranunculus bonariensis subsp.

bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with five to eight petals in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant has smooth, lobed leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
