Rosa rafinesqueiana

Rosa rafinesqueiana, commonly known as the Rafinesque's rose, is a rare and endangered species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. Its stems are woody and often covered in fine, downy hairs.

Due to habitat loss, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to survive.
