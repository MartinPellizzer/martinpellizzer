Rhus radicans f. intercursa

Rhus radicans f. intercursa, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a variant of the common sumac native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by orange-red, fuzzy berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
