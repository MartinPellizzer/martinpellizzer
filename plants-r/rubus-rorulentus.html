Rubus rorulentus

Rubus rorulentus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are typically red when ripe. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are widely cultivated for culinary and commercial purposes.

Rubus rorulentus thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
