Rosa lutea

Rosa lutea, commonly known as the yellow rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of down.

Rosa lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in horticulture.
