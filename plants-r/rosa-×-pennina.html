Rosa × pennina

Rosa × pennina, commonly known as the English rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa gallica and Rosa damascena.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Rosa × pennina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
