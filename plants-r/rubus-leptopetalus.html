Rubus leptopetalus

Rubus leptopetalus, commonly known as the slender-petaled blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
