Roupala brasiliensis var. macropoda

Roupala brasiliensis var. macropoda, commonly known as the Brazilian white cedar, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the shoots. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
