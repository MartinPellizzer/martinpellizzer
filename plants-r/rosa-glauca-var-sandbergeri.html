Rosa glauca var. sandbergeri

Rosa glauca var. sandbergeri, commonly known as the Sandberger's rose, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters of five. The plant has dark green, glossy foliage that remains attractive throughout the growing season. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
