Rosa spuria f. frondosa

Rosa spuria f. frondosa, commonly known as the frondose prickly rose, is a botanical variant of the prickly rose species.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
