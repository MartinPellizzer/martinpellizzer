Rubus transmontanus

Rubus transmontanus, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions and along riverbanks.

The Pacific blackberry is valued for its flavorful berries and its role in local ecosystems.
