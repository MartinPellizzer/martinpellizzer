Rumex arcticus var. kamtschadalus

Rumex arcticus var. kamtschadalus, commonly known as the Kamtschadal mountain sorrel, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.
