Rhododendron octandrum

Rhododendron octandrum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to China and the Himalayas.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers in early spring, which are highly fragrant. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron octandrum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in horticulture for its vibrant blooms.
