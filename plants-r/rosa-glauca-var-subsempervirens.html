Rosa glauca var. subsempervirens

Rosa glauca var. subsempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen rose, is a variant of the blue rose that exhibits persistent foliage throughout the year.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pale blue or lavender flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its delicate blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
