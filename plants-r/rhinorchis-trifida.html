Rhinorchis trifida

Rhinorchis trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and three distinct, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, pendant flower with a distinctive yellow lip and greenish-yellow petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rhinorchis trifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
