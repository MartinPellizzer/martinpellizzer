Rosa obtusifolia f. hispidula

Rosa obtusifolia f. hispidula, commonly known as the hispid rose, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its thorny stems and dense, foliage. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters. This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
