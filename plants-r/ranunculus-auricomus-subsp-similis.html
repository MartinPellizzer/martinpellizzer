Ranunculus auricomus subsp. similis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

similis, commonly known as the golden banner, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, saucer-shaped flowers with a distinctive orange center.

The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that aids in water retention.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often colorful blooms.
