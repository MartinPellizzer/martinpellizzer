Rosa cuneatula var. latesecta

Rosa cuneatula var. latesecta, commonly known as the late-sected shrub rose, is a variety of the shrub rose species characterized by its unique foliage and floral features.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its dense, thorny growth habit. The leaves are typically compound, with elongated leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, appearing in clusters during the late spring to early summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
