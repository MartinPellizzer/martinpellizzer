Rosa glandulosa var. diplacantha

Rosa glandulosa var. diplacantha, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and is known for its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant has glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
