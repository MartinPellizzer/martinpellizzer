Rosa × grisebachii

Rosa × grisebachii, commonly known as the pink sandalwood rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and have a strong, sweet fragrance.

Rosa × grisebachii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
