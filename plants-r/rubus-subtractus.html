Rubus subtractus

Rubus subtractus, commonly known as the lesser blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
