Rudgea magnoliifolia

Rudgea magnoliifolia, commonly known as the magnolia-leaf rudgea, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to cream-colored flowers that resemble those of magnolias, hence its common name. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Rudgea magnoliifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its striking floral display.
