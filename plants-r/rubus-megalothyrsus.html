Rubus megalothyrsus

Rubus megalothyrsus, commonly known as the large-throated blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
