Rubus schleicheri var. vitosensis

Rubus schleicheri var. vitosensis, commonly known as the Schleicher's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
