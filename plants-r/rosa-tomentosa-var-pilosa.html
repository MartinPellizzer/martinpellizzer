Rosa tomentosa var. pilosa

Rosa tomentosa var. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish woolly covering on both the stems and leaves, which provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically alternate, with a serrated margin and a soft, hairy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and showy blooms.
