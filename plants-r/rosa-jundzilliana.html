Rosa jundzilliana

Rosa jundzilliana, commonly known as the Jundzilliana rose, is a species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, fragrant flowers that are usually pink to red in color, with five to seven petals. Its foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves, and it produces orange to red hips in the fall.

Rosa jundzilliana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
