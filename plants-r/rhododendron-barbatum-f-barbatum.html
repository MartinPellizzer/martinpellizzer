Rhododendron barbatum f. barbatum

Rhododendron barbatum f. barbatum, commonly known as the woolly rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
