Rubus simplex

Rubus simplex, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically three to five leaflets in number. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are dark purple to black when ripe. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

Rubus simplex thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
