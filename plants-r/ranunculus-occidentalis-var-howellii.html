Ranunculus occidentalis var. howellii

Ranunculus occidentalis var. howellii, commonly known as Howell's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It has palmately divided leaves and produces small, greenish sepals beneath its petals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
