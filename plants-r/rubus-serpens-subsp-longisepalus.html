Rubus serpens subsp. longisepalus

Rubus serpens subsp.

longisepalus, commonly known as the long-sepaled blackberry, is a shrub native to certain regions of South America. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it flowers with white to pale pink petals.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
