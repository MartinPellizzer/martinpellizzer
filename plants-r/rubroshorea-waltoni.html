Rubroshorea waltoni

Rubroshorea waltoni, commonly known as the redshorea tree, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, glossy leaves that provide dense shade. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody fruits containing seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often displaying a reddish hue, which contributes to its common name.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
