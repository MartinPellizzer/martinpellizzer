Rosa communis var. tomentella

Rosa communis var. tomentella, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage.

It produces fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its hairy stems and leaves provide a unique textural contrast to its delicate blossoms.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
