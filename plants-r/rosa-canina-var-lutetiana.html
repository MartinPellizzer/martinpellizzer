Rosa canina var. lutetiana

Rosa canina var. lutetiana, commonly known as the French rose or the Lutetian rose, is a variety of the dog rose.

It is a shrub native to southern Europe, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces oval, red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C and often used in herbal remedies. Its leaves are pinnate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its medicinal properties.
