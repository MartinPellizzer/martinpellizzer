Rhododendron phaeochrysum var. levistratum

Rhododendron phaeochrysum var. levistratum, commonly known as the golden leaved rhododendron, is a variant of the phaeochrysum species within the Rhododendron genus.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which remains vibrant throughout the growing season. The plant typically features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its unique foliage and floral display, it is a prized addition to botanical collections and horticultural landscapes.
