Rhododendron superbum subsp. ibele

Rhododendron superbum subsp.

ibele, commonly known as the Appalachian rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern United States. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils of mountainous regions.

The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a waxy coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
