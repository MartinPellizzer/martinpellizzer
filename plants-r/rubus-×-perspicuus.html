Rubus × perspicuus

Rubus × perspicuus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus and Rubus pectoralis.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
