Rosa longicruris var. microphylla

Rosa longicruris var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf climbing rose, is a compact variety of the long-legged rose.

It features small, delicate leaves and produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both decorative and hedging purposes.
