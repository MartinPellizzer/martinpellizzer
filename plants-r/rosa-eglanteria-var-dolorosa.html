Rosa eglanteria var. dolorosa

Rosa eglanteria var. dolorosa, commonly known as the sorrowful rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has serrated, ovate leaves that are typically a deep green on top and paler underneath. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and historical landscapes.
