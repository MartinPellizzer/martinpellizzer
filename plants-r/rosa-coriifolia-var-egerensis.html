Rosa coriifolia var. egerensis

Rosa coriifolia var. egerensis, commonly known as the Egerensis shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Central Europe.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has thorny stems and elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements due to its delicate blossoms.
