Rhododendron glischrum subsp. glischroides

Rhododendron glischrum subsp.

glischroides, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
