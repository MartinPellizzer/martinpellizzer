Rubus trifoliatus

Rubus trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a triangular pattern. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

The plant has a woody stem with thorns and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
