Rosa setigera f. inermis

Rosa setigera f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed wild rose, is a flowering plant species native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, thornless stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are red, hairy rosehips that mature in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
