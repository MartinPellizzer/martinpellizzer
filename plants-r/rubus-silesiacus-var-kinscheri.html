Rubus silesiacus var. kinscheri

Rubus silesiacus var. kinscheri, commonly known as the Kinscheri blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are highly aromatic and often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
