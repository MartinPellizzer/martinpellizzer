Rosa communis var. nemophila

Rosa communis var. nemophila, commonly known as the wood rose, is a species of flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces oval, green leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its stems are usually covered in thorns. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers partial shade with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
