Rosa vigeriana

Rosa vigeriana, commonly known as the Siberian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems covered in thorny buds and produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are alternate along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Rosa vigeriana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
