Rhododendron arfakianum

Rhododendron arfakianum, commonly known as the Arfak rhododendron, is a species native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, humid climates at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. The plant features dark green, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers in the spring. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar ecological requirements.
