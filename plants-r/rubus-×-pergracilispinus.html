Rubus × pergracilispinus

Rubus × pergracilispinus, commonly known as the slender bramble, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are often red or black in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is known for its vigorous growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various parts of the world.
