Randia fragrantissima

Randia fragrantissima, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring aromatic, opposite leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.

Randia fragrantissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
