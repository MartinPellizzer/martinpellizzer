Ranunculus bulbosus subsp. broteri

Ranunculus bulbosus subsp.

broteri, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a short, rhizomatous rootstock and produces basal leaves that are palmately lobed with a hairy surface.

The plant bears showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along streambanks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
