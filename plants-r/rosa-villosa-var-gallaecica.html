Rosa villosa var. gallaecica

Rosa villosa var. gallaecica, commonly known as the Galician bramble, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance.

This variety is often found in coastal and woodland areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
