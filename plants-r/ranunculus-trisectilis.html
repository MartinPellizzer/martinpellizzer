Ranunculus trisectilis

Ranunculus trisectilis, commonly known as the three-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven sepals that resemble petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed, often divided into three parts. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It is characterized by its short stem and is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.

Ranunculus trisectilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
