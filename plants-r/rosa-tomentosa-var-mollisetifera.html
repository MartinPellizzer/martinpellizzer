Rosa tomentosa var. mollisetifera

Rosa tomentosa var. mollisetifera, commonly known as the woolly rose or soft-haired rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy gray foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
