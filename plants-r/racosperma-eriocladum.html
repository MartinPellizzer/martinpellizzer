Racosperma eriocladum

Racosperma eriocladum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, which are often visited by a variety of pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in hard, woody pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Racosperma eriocladum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is well-suited to coastal and semi-arid environments.
