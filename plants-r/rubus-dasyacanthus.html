Rubus dasyacanthus

Rubus dasyacanthus, commonly known as the rough-stalked blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. Its botanical features include a woody structure and a flowering period in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
