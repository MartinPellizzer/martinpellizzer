Rubus omalus var. scabidus

Rubus omalus var. scabidus, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a rough texture. This plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

It produces small, red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.
