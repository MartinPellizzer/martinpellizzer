Rosa × cantabrigiensis

Rosa × cantabrigiensis, commonly known as the Cambridge rose, is a hybrid rose species originating from crosses between Rosa damascena and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Rosa × cantabrigiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
