Rubia peregrina subsp. longifolia

Rubia peregrina subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved madder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are a key feature of its botanical appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its historical use in dye production and as a medicinal plant.
