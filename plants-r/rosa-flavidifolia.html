Rosa flavidifolia

Rosa flavidifolia, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces fragrant flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and leaves, as well as hips that are often orange or red in color.

Rosa flavidifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
