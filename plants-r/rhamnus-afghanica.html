Rhamnus afghanica

Rhamnus afghanica, commonly known as Afghan buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, black drupes that contain a single seed.

Rhamnus afghanica is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
