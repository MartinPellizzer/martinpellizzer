Rubus stenophyllidium

Rubus stenophyllidium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, aggregated fruits that are dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
