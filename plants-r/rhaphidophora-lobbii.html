Rhaphidophora lobbii

Rhaphidophora lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant features striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive, narrow petiole. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spathe, a common characteristic of the Araceae family.

Rhaphidophora lobbii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
