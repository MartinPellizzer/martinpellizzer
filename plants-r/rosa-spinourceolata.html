Rosa spinourceolata

Rosa spinourceolata, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features thorny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

Rosa spinourceolata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
