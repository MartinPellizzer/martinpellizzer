Rubus grossidentatus

Rubus grossidentatus, commonly known as the large-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves with serrated edges and its thorny stems. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as blackberries, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. These fruits are edible and often used in culinary applications.

Rubus grossidentatus thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and disturbed soils, and is known for its hardy, perennial growth habit.
