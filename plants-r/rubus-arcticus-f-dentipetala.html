Rubus arcticus f. dentipetala

Rubus arcticus f. dentipetala, commonly known as the Arctic raspberry, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces small, red fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have serrated edges.

It thrives in cold climates and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.
