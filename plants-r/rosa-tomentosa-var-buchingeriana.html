Rosa tomentosa var. buchingeriana

Rosa tomentosa var. buchingeriana, commonly known as the Buchinger's woolly rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features thorny stems and small, oval leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
