Rubus arduennensis var. mollis

Rubus arduennensis var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-fruited blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, soft-textured leaves and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are prized for their flavor and culinary use.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
