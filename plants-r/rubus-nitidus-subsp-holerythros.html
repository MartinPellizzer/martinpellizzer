Rubus nitidus subsp. holerythros

Rubus nitidus subsp.

holerythros, commonly known as the red-fruited blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems, which help it climb or spread along the ground.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on maturity. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its edible berries.
