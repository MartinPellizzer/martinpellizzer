Ranunculus triangularis

Ranunculus triangularis, commonly known as the triangular buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often with a red or orange center. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, which are typically hairy and slightly lobed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Ranunculus triangularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
