Ranunculus acriformis var. montanensis

Ranunculus acriformis var. montanensis, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is a characteristic species of the alpine flora.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic plant in its native range.
