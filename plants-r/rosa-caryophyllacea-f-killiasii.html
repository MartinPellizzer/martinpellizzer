Rosa caryophyllacea f. killiasii

Rosa caryophyllacea f. killiasii, commonly known as the Killiasii rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa caryophyllacea species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which contributes to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This form is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
