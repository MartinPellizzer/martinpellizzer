Rhamnus ulmoides

Rhamnus ulmoides, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including dry and rocky areas.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
