Rubus maltei

Rubus maltei, commonly known as the Maltei blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

The fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves.
