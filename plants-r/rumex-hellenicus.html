Rumex hellenicus

Rumex hellenicus, commonly known as Greek dock, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, wavy leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or woodland areas. Its leaves are edible and have been used historically in traditional medicine.

The species is notable for its adaptability and resilience in various environmental conditions.
