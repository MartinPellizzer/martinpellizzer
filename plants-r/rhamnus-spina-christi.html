Rhamnus spina-christi

Rhamnus spina-christi, commonly known as Christ's thorn or the crown of thorns, is a shrub or small tree native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its name is derived from its association with the thorns of the Crown of Thorns, a symbol in Christian tradition.
