Rosa kewensis

Rosa kewensis, commonly known as the Kew rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can be pink, red, or white. The plant has fragrant petals and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It produces hips, which are the fruit of the plant, and these can be used in culinary applications.

Rosa kewensis is also valued for its historical significance, as it was first discovered and cultivated at Kew Gardens in the 19th century.
