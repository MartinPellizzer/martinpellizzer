Rosa mollilaetecolorans

Rosa mollilaetecolorans, commonly known as the multicolored rose, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, from pink to white and even red. The plant produces rose hips, which are typically orange or red and contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and unique floral display, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
