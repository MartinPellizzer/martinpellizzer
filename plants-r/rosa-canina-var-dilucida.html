Rosa canina var. dilucida

Rosa canina var. dilucida, commonly known as the early dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of pink to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits known as hip fruits, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
