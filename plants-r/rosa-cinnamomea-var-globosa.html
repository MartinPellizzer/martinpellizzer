Rosa cinnamomea var. globosa

Rosa cinnamomea var. globosa, commonly known as the cinnamon rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its distinctive, fragrant flowers that range from pale pink to deep crimson. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange hips that are often used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, with serrated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of garden conditions.
