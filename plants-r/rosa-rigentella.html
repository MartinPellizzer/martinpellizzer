Rosa rigentella

Rosa rigentella, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and high elevation environments. The plant features woody stems with thorny branches and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a relatively compact growth habit.

Rosa rigentella is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
