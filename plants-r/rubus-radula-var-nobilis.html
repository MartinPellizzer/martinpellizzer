Rubus radula var. nobilis

Rubus radula var. nobilis, commonly known as the noble raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when ripe and contain multiple seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in woodland edges and open fields.

This variety is valued for its flavorful berries, which are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
