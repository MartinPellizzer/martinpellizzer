Rixea

Rixea, commonly known as the "Banyan of the South," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rixeiaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, the Pacific Islands, and parts of Africa. The plants typically have smooth, often reddish bark and produce small, inconspicuous flowers. Rixea species are often found in lowland rainforests and are characterized by their ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Some species are used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
