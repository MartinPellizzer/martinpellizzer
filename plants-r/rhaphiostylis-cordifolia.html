Rhaphiostylis cordifolia

Rhaphiostylis cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-shaped leaf cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
