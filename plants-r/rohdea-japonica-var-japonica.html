Rohdea japonica var. japonica

Rohdea japonica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese ginger or Japanese lily, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features strap-like, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and dramatic floral display.
