Rubus desarmatus

Rubus desarmatus, commonly known as the naked-stem blackberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are serrated and slightly hairy.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and they appear in the spring before the foliage.
