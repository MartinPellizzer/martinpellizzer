Ranunculus jugosus

Ranunculus jugosus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and hairy stems that rise from a short rhizome. Its leaves are palmately divided, with lobes that are often covered in fine hairs.

Ranunculus jugosus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
