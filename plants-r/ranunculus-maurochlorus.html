Ranunculus maurochlorus

Ranunculus maurochlorus, commonly known as the greenish buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, usually yellow or greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
