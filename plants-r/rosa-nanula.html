Rosa nanula

Rosa nanula, commonly known as the white campion, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

Rosa nanula is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it blooms in late spring to early summer.
