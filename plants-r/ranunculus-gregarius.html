Ranunculus gregarius

Ranunculus gregarius, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

The species is notable for its ability to form dense clusters of flowers, which attract various pollinators.
