Rhynchosia viscosa

Rhynchosia viscosa, commonly known as sticky crotalaria, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, often sticky stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals, typical of the genus Rhynchosia. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

Rhynchosia viscosa is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
