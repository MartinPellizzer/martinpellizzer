Rosa pimpinellifolia subalpina

Rosa pimpinellifolia subalpina, commonly known as the alpine strawberry rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces edible, strawberry-like fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
