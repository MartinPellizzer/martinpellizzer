Rosa aublancii

Rosa aublancii, commonly known as the white musk rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and produces rose hips that are reddish-brown when mature. Rosa aublancii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
