Rosa basilica

Rosa basilica, commonly known as sacred basil, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and containers. Its botanical name, Rosa basilica, reflects its historical association with religious and medicinal uses.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
