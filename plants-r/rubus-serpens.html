Rubus serpens

Rubus serpens, commonly known as the serpentine bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a woody shrub characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

Rubus serpens is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
