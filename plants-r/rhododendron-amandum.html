Rhododendron amandum

Rhododendron amandum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, primarily found in Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are usually dark green and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in the spring.

Rhododendron amandum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
