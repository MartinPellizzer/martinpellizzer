Rhododendron aischropeplum

Rhododendron aischropeplum, commonly known as the Aischropeplum rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from pink to purple, depending on the variety.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
