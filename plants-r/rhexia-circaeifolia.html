Rhexia circaeifolia

Rhexia circaeifolia, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a distinctive tuberous root system that gives it its common name. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.

Rhexia circaeifolia is a herbaceous perennial that plays an important role in its native ecosystem.
