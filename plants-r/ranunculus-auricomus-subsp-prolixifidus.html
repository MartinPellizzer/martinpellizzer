Ranunculus auricomus subsp. prolixifidus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

prolixifidus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its palmate, deeply lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader leaves and more elaborate leaf divisions compared to the typical form of Ranunculus auricomus.
