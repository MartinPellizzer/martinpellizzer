Rhinacanthus latilabiatus

Rhinacanthus latilabiatus, commonly known as the broad-lipped flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.
