Rosa rubrifolia f. diminuta

Rosa rubrifolia f. diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf red-leaf rose, is a compact shrub characterized by its small, rounded form and vibrant red foliage.

It features delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pink to white. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, fertile soil. It requires full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
