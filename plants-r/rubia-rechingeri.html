Rubia rechingeri

Rubia rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's madder, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family, which includes many species of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months.

The roots of Rubia rechingeri contain alkaloids and have been historically used in traditional medicine.
