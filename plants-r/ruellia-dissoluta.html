Ruellia dissoluta

Ruellia dissoluta, commonly known as the silverleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are tubular and usually blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ruellia dissoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
