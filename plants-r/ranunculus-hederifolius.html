Ranunculus hederifolius

Ranunculus hederifolius, commonly known as the ivy-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that resemble those of ivy, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Ranunculus hederifolius is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
