Ribes turbinatum

Ribes turbinatum, commonly known as the turbinated gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants and edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The fruit is a small, yellow berry that ripens in late summer and is often used in jams and preserves.
