Rhamnus staddo

Rhamnus staddo, commonly known as the African buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It is typically found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
