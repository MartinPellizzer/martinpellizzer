Rubeola angustifolia

Rubeola angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rubeola, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
