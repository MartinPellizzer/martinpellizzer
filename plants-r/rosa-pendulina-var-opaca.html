Rosa pendulina var. opaca

Rosa pendulina var. opaca, commonly known as the opaca climbing rose, is a variant of the pendulous rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems that can reach several meters in length, making it well-suited for trellises or arbors. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink to white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
