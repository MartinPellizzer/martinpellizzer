Rhizobolus butyrosus

Rhizobolus butyrosus, commonly known as the buttercup fungus, is a unique plant-like organism that exhibits both fungal and plant characteristics.

It is primarily found in moist, shaded environments, often near decaying organic matter. This organism is known for its ability to produce a yellowish, cup-shaped structure that resembles a buttercup, hence its common name. Rhizobolus butyrosus plays a role in nutrient cycling by decomposing organic material and releasing nutrients back into the soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its unusual morphology and its contribution to ecological balance in forest ecosystems.
