Rubus glaucifolius subsp. ganderi

Rubus glaucifolius subsp.

ganderi, commonly known as Gander's blackberry, is a subspecies native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are oval with a waxy coating, giving them a bluish-green appearance.

The plant has a woody stem with thorny growths, typical of the Rubus genus.
