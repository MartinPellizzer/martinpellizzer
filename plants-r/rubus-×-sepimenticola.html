Rubus × sepimenticola

Rubus × sepimenticola, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States and grows in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface.

The berries, which are usually dark purple to black, are edible and have a tart flavor.
