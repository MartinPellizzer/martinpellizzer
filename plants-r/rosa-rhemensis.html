Rosa rhemensis

Rosa rhemensis, commonly known as the Chilean rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa rhemensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
