Rosa viscaria var. ligustica

Rosa viscaria var. ligustica, commonly known as the Ligurian rose, is a compact shrub native to the coastal regions of Italy.

It features dense, thorny stems and small, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers with a delicate fragrance, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in saline environments.
