Rudgea recurva

Rudgea recurva, commonly known as the waxy vine or velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its waxy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Rudgea recurva thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and ecological research.
