Ranunculus meyerianus

Ranunculus meyerianus, commonly known as Meyer's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with 5 to 15 petals. The plant has palmately divided leaves and thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
