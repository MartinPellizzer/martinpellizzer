Rudbeckia aspera

Rudbeckia aspera, commonly known as rough coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and showy yellow to orange ray flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its foliage is alternate and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Rudbeckia aspera is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
