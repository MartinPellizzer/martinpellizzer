Rubus deliciosus

Rubus deliciosus, commonly known as the sweet raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red when mature. These fruits are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
