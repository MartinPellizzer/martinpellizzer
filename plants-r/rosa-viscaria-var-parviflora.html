Rosa viscaria var. parviflora

Rosa viscaria var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered prickly rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are often borne singly or in small clusters. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
