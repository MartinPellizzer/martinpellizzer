Rubus amiantinus

Rubus amiantinus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The silverleaf blackberry is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
