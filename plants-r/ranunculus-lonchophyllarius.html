Ranunculus lonchophyllarius

Ranunculus lonchophyllarius, commonly known as the long-leaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features distinctive, palmate leaves with a deep green color and a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, often accompanied by five sepals that are longer than the petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
