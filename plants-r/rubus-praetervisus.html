Rubus praetervisus

Rubus praetervisus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has a shrub-like growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them a valuable resource for both wildlife and humans.
