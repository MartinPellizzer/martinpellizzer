Ribes velutinum var. congdonii

Ribes velutinum var. congdonii, commonly known as the Congdon gooseberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and oval, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, tart berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
