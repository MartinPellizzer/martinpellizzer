Rubus thyrsiflorus var. heterotrichus

Rubus thyrsiflorus var. heterotrichus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
