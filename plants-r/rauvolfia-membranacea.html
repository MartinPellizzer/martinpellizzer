Rauvolfia membranacea

Rauvolfia membranacea, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a climbing shrub that typically grows along forest edges and in moist, shaded areas. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has elongated, thin leaves with a smooth texture. Its roots contain alkaloids, which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
