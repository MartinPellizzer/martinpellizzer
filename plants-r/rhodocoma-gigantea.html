Rhodocoma gigantea

Rhodocoma gigantea, commonly known as the giant rhodocoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spike-like structure. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces seeds within elongated, woody capsules that disperse through wind and water.

Rhodocoma gigantea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
