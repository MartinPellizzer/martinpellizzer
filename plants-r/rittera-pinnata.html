Rittera pinnata

Rittera pinnata, commonly known as the clustered milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Rittera pinnata is a member of the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
