Restio vimineus

Restio vimineus, commonly known as the slender restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a grass-like plant characterized by its narrow, grassy leaves and tall, flowering stems. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salty air. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small white flowers.

Restio vimineus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its elegant appearance and drought tolerance.
