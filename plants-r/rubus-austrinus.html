Rubus austrinus

Rubus austrinus, commonly known as the Australian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Australia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Rubus austrinus is often found in open woodlands and along the edges of forests.
