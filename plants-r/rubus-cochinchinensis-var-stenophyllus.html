Rubus cochinchinensis var. stenophyllus

Rubus cochinchinensis var. stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
