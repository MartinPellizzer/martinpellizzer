Rubus laciniosus

Rubus laciniosus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family, which includes other well-known fruits like strawberries and raspberries.
