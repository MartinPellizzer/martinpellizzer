Retanilla moelleri

Retanilla moelleri, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes. Retanilla moelleri is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
