Renealmia fragilis

Renealmia fragilis, commonly known as the brittle reed, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often fragile stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in humid, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering stems produce panicles of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are usually green or brown in color.

Due to its delicate structure, it is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and rivers.
