Rubus spoliatus

Rubus spoliatus, commonly known as the spangled blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color, with a tart flavor. Rubus spoliatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and small mammals.
