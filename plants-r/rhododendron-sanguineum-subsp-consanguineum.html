Rhododendron sanguineum subsp. consanguineum

Rhododendron sanguineum subsp.

consanguineum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are dark green and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports local wildlife.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
