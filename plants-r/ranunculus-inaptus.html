Ranunculus inaptus

Ranunculus inaptus, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features distinctive yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens, which are often surrounded by a ring of sepals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a slender, lance-shaped appearance.

Ranunculus inaptus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
