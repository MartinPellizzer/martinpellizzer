Ruellia patula var. dumicola

Ruellia patula var. dumicola, commonly known as the rock ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue to violet flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ruellia patula var. dumicola is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
