Rivea subincana

Rivea subincana, commonly known as the velvet tangle vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Rivea subincana is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
