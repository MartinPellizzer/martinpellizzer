Rostraria Parviflora

Rostraria parviflora is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is known for its unique, elongated flower structure that resembles a narrow beak, giving it its scientific name. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is now considered endangered. Its beauty and rarity make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
