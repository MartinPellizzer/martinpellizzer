Rubus × pseudosubhispidulus

Rubus × pseudosubhispidulus, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Rubus allegheniensis ( Allegheny blackberry) and Rubus strigosus (prickly blackberry). The plant features thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and produces aggregate fruits composed of small drupelets. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
