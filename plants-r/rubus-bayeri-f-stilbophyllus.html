Rubus bayeri f. stilbophyllus

Rubus bayeri f. stilbophyllus, commonly known as the Bayery's raspberry, is a unique botanical variant of the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features large, leathery leaves with a deep green color and a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow, depending on the season. It is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
