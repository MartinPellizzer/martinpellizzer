Rosa quelpaertensis

Rosa quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Jeju rose, is a species native to the Korean Peninsula, particularly Jeju Island.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on pedicels and attract pollinators. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in thorns.

Rosa quelpaertensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
