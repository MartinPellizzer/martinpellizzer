Rubus luteicaulis

Rubus luteicaulis, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed blackberry.

It is a shrub native to North America, particularly found in the southeastern United States. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. Its leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and the flowers are white or pale yellow.

The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
