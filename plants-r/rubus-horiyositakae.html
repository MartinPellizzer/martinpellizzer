Rubus horiyositakae

Rubus horiyositakae, commonly known as the Horiyositaka raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
