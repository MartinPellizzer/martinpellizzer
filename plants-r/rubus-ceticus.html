Rubus ceticus

Rubus ceticus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are not only edible but also rich in antioxidants and other beneficial compounds.

Rubus ceticus is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
