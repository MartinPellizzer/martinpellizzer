Rinorea welwitschii subsp. tanzanica

Rinorea welwitschii subsp.

tanzanica, commonly known as the Tanzanian rinorea, is a shrub native to Tanzania. It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are capsules that release small seeds when mature.

This species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
