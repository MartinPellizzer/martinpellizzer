Rosa multiflora var. pilosissima

Rosa multiflora var. pilosissima, commonly known as the silky rose, is a flowering shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a soft, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
