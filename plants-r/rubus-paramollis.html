Rubus paramollis

Rubus paramollis, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red berries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
