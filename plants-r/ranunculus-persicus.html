Ranunculus persicus

Ranunculus persicus, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals, often in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with Persia, where it has been cultivated for centuries.
