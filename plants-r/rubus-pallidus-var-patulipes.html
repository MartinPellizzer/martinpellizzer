Rubus pallidus var. patulipes

Rubus pallidus var. patulipes, commonly known as the pale raspberry or broad-leaved raspberry, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that develop into red, hairy berries. Its leaves are typically broad and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is native to parts of North America and is often found in woodland areas.
