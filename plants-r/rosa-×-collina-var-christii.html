Rosa × collina var. christii

Rosa × collina var. christii, commonly known as the Christ's Rosa or Christ's shrub rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage is dark green and aromatic, with thorny stems that provide structural support.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in garden settings.
