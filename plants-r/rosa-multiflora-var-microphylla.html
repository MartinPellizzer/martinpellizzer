Rosa multiflora var. microphylla

Rosa multiflora var. microphylla, commonly known as the Japanese rose or small-leaf multiflora rose, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
