Raphanus cornutus

Raphanus cornutus, commonly known as the horned radish, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. The plant features a fleshy, swollen root that is typically white or yellow in color. Its leaves are lobed and slightly hairy, and it produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

Raphanus cornutus is often cultivated for its edible root and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
