Rubus vallisparsus var. perambigens

Rubus vallisparsus var. perambigens, commonly known as the Florida blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage, which provide habitat for various wildlife species. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

This variety is often found in wetlands and along the edges of swamps, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
