Rudgea thyrsiflora

Rudgea thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian candlewood, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces woody, candle-like inflorescences, which are a notable feature in its botanical description. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Rudgea thyrsiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
