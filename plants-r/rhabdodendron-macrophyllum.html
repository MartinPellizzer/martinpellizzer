Rhabdodendron macrophyllum

Rhabdodendron macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
