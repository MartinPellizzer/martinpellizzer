Ranunculus muricatus var. emuricatus

Ranunculus muricatus var. emuricatus, commonly known as the rough meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with numerous petals.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features a short, stout stem with basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually solitary and bloom from late spring to early summer.

This variety is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
