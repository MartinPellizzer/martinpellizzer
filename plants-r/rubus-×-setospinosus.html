Rubus × setospinosus

Rubus × setospinosus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are not only edible but also contain high levels of antioxidants.

Rubus × setospinosus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
