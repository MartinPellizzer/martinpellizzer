Ranunculus parviflorus

Ranunculus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often showy flowers.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
