Rubus euchroanthus

Rubus euchroanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white, and arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees.

The fruit, a small, dark berry, is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.
