Rosa pendulina var. gentilis

Rosa pendulina var. gentilis, commonly known as the hanging rose or the weeping rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa pendulina species.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically has a shrub-like growth habit with thornless stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or as a specimen plant.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to add vertical interest to landscapes.
