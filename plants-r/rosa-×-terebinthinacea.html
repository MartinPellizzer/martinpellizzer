Rosa × terebinthinacea

Rosa × terebinthinacea, commonly known as the Turkish rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Balkans and Turkey.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form compact, bushy growth.
