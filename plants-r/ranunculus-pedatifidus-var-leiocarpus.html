Ranunculus pedatifidus var. leiocarpus

Ranunculus pedatifidus var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smooth-seeded buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a waxy surface and bright yellow flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have five sepals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The variety leiocarpus is distinguished by its smooth, hairless seeds compared to the more hairy seeds of the typical variety.
