Rhoicissus edulis

Rhoicissus edulis, commonly known as the sour fig or Natal fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Vitaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically sour but can be sweet when ripe. Its stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
