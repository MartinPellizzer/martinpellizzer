Racosperma gonophyllum

Racosperma gonophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with slender, lance-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, typical of many wattles, and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Racosperma gonophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
