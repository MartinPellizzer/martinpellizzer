Rubus × pseudohamulosus

Rubus × pseudohamulosus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a hybrid plant within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of edible, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thorny stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic resource for breeding programs aimed at improving fruit quality and disease resistance.
