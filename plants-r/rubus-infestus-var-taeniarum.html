Rubus infestus var. taeniarum

Rubus infestus var. taeniarum, commonly known as the Tasmanian blackberry, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, black fruits that are highly aromatic and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain environments.
