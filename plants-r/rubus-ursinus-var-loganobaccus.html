Rubus ursinus var. loganobaccus

Rubus ursinus var. loganobaccus, commonly known as the Loganberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to California.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupes that ripen in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The Loganberry is valued for its sweet-tart flavor and is often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
