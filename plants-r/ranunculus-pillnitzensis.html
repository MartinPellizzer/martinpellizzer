Ranunculus pillnitzensis

Ranunculus pillnitzensis, commonly known as the Pillnitz water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, floating leaves that are heart-shaped and have a waxy coating. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale yellow, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It plays a role in supporting aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
