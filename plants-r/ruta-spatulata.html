Ruta spatulata

Ruta spatulata, commonly known as broad-leaved rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, orange berries. Ruta spatulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and medicinal properties.
