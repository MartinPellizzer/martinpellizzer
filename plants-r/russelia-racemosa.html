Russelia racemosa

Russelia racemosa, commonly known as the red hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower clusters that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and can grow up to 2 meters in height. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Russelia racemosa is also valued for its long blooming period, which extends from late spring through early autumn.
