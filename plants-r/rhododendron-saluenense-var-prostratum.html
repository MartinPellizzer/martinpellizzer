Rhododendron saluenense var. prostratum

Rhododendron saluenense var. prostratum, commonly known as the prostrate saluenense rhododendron, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
