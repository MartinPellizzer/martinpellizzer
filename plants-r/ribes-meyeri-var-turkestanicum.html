Ribes meyeri var. turkestanicum

Ribes meyeri var. turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It features small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its culinary uses.
