Rubus hirtus var. coloratiformis

Rubus hirtus var. coloratiformis, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive reddish stems and foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of North America, particularly the eastern United States. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, making it a popular choice for both gardens and small-scale cultivation.
