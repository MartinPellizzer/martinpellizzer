Rubus hui

Rubus hui, commonly known as the Hui bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are similar in appearance to raspberries but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is sometimes grown in gardens.
