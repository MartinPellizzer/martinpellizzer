Rubus rivularis var. obovatus

Rubus rivularis var. obovatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves that are obovate in shape, with a serrated margin and a hairy surface. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus, with particular attention to its leaf morphology and fruit structure.
