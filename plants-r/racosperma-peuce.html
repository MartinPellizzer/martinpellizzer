Racosperma peuce

Racosperma peuce, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant is distinguished by its slender, often silver-haired stems and bipinnate leaves. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in racemes, typical of the genus.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
