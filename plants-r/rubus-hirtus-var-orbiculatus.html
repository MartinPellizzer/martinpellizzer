Rubus hirtus var. orbiculatus

Rubus hirtus var. orbiculatus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, thorny stems and broad, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its edible berries and its role in supporting local wildlife.
