Ranunculus monophyllus subsp. neumanii

Ranunculus monophyllus subsp.

neumanii, commonly known as the Neuman's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, often with a distinctive central stigma.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a delicate appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
