Reseda sesamoides var. spathulata

Reseda sesamoides var. spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf sesame or narrowleaf reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, branching stems and lanceolate to spatulate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
