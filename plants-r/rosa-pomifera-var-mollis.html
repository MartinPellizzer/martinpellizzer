Rosa pomifera var. mollis

Rosa pomifera var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-fruited rose, is a unique variant of the rose family characterized by its distinctive, soft, and edible fruits.

This plant is native to certain regions of Asia and is known for its large, fleshy, berry-like fruits that are often described as having a sweet and tangy flavor. The foliage consists of pinnately compound leaves, and the plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers during the spring. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique fruit structure and flavor, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
