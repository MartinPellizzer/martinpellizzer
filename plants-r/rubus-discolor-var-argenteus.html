Rubus discolor var. argenteus

Rubus discolor var. argenteus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically less flavorful compared to other blackberry varieties. Its foliage is covered in fine, silvery hairs, which give it its unique appearance.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking leaf coloration.
