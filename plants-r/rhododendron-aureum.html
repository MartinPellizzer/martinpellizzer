Rhododendron aureum

Rhododendron aureum, commonly known as the golden rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, broad leaves that are typically a vibrant green, turning yellow in the autumn. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences, and the plant is well adapted to high-altitude environments.

Rhododendron aureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
