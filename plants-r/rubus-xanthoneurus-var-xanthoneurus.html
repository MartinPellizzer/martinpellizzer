Rubus xanthoneurus var. xanthoneurus

Rubus xanthoneurus var. xanthoneurus, commonly known as the yellow-tipped bramble, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, hairy berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is notable for its distinctive yellow floral bracts and its role in local ecosystems.
