Rubus epipsilos subsp. holochlorus

Rubus epipsilos subsp.

holochlorus, commonly known as the yellow-tailed blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three leaflets.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink and are followed by edible, dark purple berries. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and thickets.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting pollinator populations.
