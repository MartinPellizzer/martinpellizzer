Rubus fuscus subsp. angustifolius

Rubus fuscus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with narrow, serrated leaflets.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
