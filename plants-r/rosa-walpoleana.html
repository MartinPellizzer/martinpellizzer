Rosa walpoleana

Rosa walpoleana, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with woody stems and compound leaves. Its flowers, which are pollinated by moths, have a unique ability to open and close in response to light.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
