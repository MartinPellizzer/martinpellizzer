Rhododendron microcarpum

Rhododendron microcarpum, commonly known as the dwarf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a slight pubescence.

Rhododendron microcarpum prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
