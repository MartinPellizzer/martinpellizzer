Rubus greinenis

Rubus greinenis, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Rubus greinenis is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
