Robinia pyramidalis

Robinia pyramidalis, commonly known as the false acacia, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rapid growth, pyramidal shape, and compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces fragrant, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance, while its flowers are typically pale yellow and bloom in spring.

Robinia pyramidalis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
