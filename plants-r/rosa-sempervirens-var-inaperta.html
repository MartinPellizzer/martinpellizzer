Rosa sempervirens var. inaperta

Rosa sempervirens var. inaperta, commonly known as the Evergreen Rose, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that retains its color throughout the year. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.

It is well-suited for coastal gardens due to its tolerance of salt spray and drought conditions.
