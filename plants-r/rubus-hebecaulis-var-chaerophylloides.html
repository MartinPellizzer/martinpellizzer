Rubus hebecaulis var. chaerophylloides

Rubus hebecaulis var. chaerophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark red to black berries. The leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin. This variety is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
