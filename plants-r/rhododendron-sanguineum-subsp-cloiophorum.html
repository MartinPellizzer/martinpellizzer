Rhododendron sanguineum subsp. cloiophorum

Rhododendron sanguineum subsp.

cloiophorum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a flowering shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its showy, clustered blooms that range from pink to red in color, typically appearing in late spring.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its leaves are typically elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
