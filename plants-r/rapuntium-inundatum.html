Rapuntium inundatum

Rapuntium inundatum, commonly known as the water cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with small, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or yellowish wax. Its flowers are typically small and white or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The plant thrives in moist environments, such as stream banks and wet meadows, and is often harvested for its peppery, edible leaves.
