Rosa graveolens var. fimbrisepala

Rosa graveolens var. fimbrisepala, commonly known as the fringed-leaf rose, is a species native to the Himalayas and parts of southern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, and the leaves of the varietal form are notably fringed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery.
