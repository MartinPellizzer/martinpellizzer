Rubus yakumontanus

Rubus yakumontanus, commonly known as the Yakumontan blackberry, is a species of brambles native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in woodland areas and moist soils, preferring partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Rubus species.
