Rubus ambiguus

Rubus ambiguus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when ripe. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

Rubus ambiguus is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
