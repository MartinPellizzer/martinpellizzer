Rubus thyrsoideus f. pulcherrimus

Rubus thyrsoideus f. pulcherrimus, commonly known as the showy blackberry.

This plant is a cultivated form of the common blackberry, characterized by its striking, pinkish-white flowers that appear in early summer. It produces dark purple berries that are typically smaller than those of the standard species. The plant is shrub-like, with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
