Rubus tereticaulis var. pseudobellardii

Rubus tereticaulis var. pseudobellardii, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, characterized by its slender stems and small, thorny branches. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often found in disturbed areas and along the edges of forests.
