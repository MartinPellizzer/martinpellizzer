Ranunculus apricus

Ranunculus apricus, commonly known as the spring buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five to ten petals. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and blooms from late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus apricus is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.
