Rhynchospora × hakkodensis

Rhynchospora × hakkodensis, commonly known as the Hakkoda marsh sedge, is a hybrid plant species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in humid environments with poor soil drainage. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, facilitating wind pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
