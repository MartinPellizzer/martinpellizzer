Rubus vulgaris f. macrophyllus

Rubus vulgaris f. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf bramble, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its larger, more elongated leaves compared to the typical Rubus vulgaris. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits.
