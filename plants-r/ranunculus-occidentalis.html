Ranunculus occidentalis

Ranunculus occidentalis, commonly known as the western buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, often surrounded by a ring of white or yellow sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It is an important plant in ecological studies due to its role in supporting pollinators and its sensitivity to environmental changes.
