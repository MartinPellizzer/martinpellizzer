Rosa elliptica subvar. pseudograveolens

Rosa elliptica subvar. pseudograveolens, commonly known as the fragrant elliptic rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers with a sweet scent, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild fragrance, though it is less commonly cultivated than other rose species.
