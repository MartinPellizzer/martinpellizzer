Rubus × tholiformis

Rubus × tholiformis, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a vigorous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, drupe-like berries. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Rubus × tholiformis is widely cultivated for its edible fruits and is also used in ornamental gardening.
