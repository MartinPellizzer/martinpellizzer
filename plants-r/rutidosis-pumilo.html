Rutidosis pumilo

Rutidosis pumilo, commonly known as the cushion plant, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

The plant's compact growth habit allows it to resist wind and reduce water loss, making it well-adapted to desert conditions.
