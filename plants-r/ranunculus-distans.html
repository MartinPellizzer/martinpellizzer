Ranunculus distans

Ranunculus distans, commonly known as the distant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers feature five to eight petal-like sepals.

Ranunculus distans is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
