Rubus argenteus var. tourletii

Rubus argenteus var. tourletii, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a variant of the silverleaf raspberry.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
