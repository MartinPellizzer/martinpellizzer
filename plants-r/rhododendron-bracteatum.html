Rhododendron bracteatum

Rhododendron bracteatum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in forested areas at moderate elevations. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive bract-like structure below the petals. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of wildlife.

Rhododendron bracteatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
