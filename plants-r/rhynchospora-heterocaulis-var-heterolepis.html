Rhynchospora heterocaulis var. heterolepis

Rhynchospora heterocaulis var. heterolepis, commonly known as the slender beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and three-ranked leaves. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem with a distinctive, elongated flowering spike that produces small, three-lobed flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and tapering, with a smooth, waxy surface adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

This variety is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
