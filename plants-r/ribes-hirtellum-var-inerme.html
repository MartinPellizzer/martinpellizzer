Ribes hirtellum var. inerme

Ribes hirtellum var. inerme, commonly known as smooth gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, tart berries in late summer. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the stems are generally smooth, giving it its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
