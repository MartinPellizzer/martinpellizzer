Ranunculus glabrifolius

Ranunculus glabrifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are smooth and glabrous, with a deep green color and a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Ranunculus glabrifolius is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
