Rungia parviflora var. muralis

Rungia parviflora var. muralis, commonly known as the wall rungia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub or vine that typically grows along walls or in shaded areas, where it can climb or trail. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
