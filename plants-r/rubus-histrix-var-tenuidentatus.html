Rubus histrix var. tenuidentatus

Rubus histrix var. tenuidentatus, commonly known as the narrow-toothed blackberry bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or hedgerow environments.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
