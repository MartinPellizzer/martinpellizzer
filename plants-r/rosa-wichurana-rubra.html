Rosa wichurana rubra

Rosa wichurana rubra, commonly known as the Chilean rose or the red rose of Chile, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Chile and is characterized by its vibrant red petals and strong fragrance. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers that bloom in spring. It has dark green, serrated leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Rosa wichurana rubra prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
