Ranunculus thoroides

Ranunculus thoroides, commonly known as the showy buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow petals with a distinctive black center, and its leaves are deeply lobed with a hairy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ranunculus thoroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy appearance.
