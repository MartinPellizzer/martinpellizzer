Rhododendron delavayi

Rhododendron delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay rhododendron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southwest China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in cool, moist climates with well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in spring and are highly ornamental.

Rhododendron delavayi is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to specific garden conditions.
