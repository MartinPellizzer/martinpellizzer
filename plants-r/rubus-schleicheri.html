Rubus schleicheri

Rubus schleicheri, commonly known as Schleicher's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is a shrub that produces small, dark purple to black berries, which are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The plant's foliage is characterized by compound leaves with serrated edges and a dense, thorny structure.
