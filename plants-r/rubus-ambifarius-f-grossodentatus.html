Rubus ambifarius f. grossodentatus

Rubus ambifarius f. grossodentatus, commonly known as the large-toothed blackberry, is a botanical variant of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and large, serrated leaves. The inflorescence consists of dense clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a dark purple to black berry, typically larger and more flavorful than the standard species.

This form is valued in horticulture for its improved yield and taste.
