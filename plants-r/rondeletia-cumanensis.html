Rondeletia cumanensis

Rondeletia cumanensis, commonly known as the Cumanensis flame flower, is a striking tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is less commonly found in non-native regions.
