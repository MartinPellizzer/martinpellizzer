Rubus × paniculatus

Rubus × paniculatus, commonly known as the wineberry, is a hybrid shrub native to China and introduced to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or purple in color. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in home gardens.
