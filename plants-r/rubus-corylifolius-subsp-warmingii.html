Rubus corylifolius subsp. warmingii

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

warmingii, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically borne on thorny stems.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its berries are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a culinary ingredient.
