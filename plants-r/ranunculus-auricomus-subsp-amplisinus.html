Ranunculus auricomus subsp. amplisinus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

amplisinus, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, broad, auricle-like leaf base.

The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces numerous small, yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
