Rubus celticus

Rubus celticus, commonly known as the Celtic bramble, is a species of shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and hedgerows, and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, Rubus celticus is a common sight in many temperate regions.
