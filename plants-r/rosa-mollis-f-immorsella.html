Rosa mollis f. immorsella

Rosa mollis f. immorsella, commonly known as the soft rose or velvet rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy texture on the foliage and stems, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
