Rhamnus kayacikii

Rhamnus kayacikii, commonly known as the Kayacik buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Turkey.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh conditions.

Rhamnus kayacikii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
