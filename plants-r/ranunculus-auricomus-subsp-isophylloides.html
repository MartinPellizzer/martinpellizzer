Ranunculus auricomus subsp. isophylloides

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

isophylloides, commonly known as the golden shield buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five broadly ovate petals, often with a distinctive golden or orange hue.

The leaves are palmately divided, typically with three to five lobes, and are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and streambanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
