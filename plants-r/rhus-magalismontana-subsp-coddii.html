Rhus magalismontana subsp. coddii

Rhus magalismontana subsp.

coddii, commonly known as the Codd's winged sumac, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into three to five pairs of leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fuzzy berries, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its limited range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
