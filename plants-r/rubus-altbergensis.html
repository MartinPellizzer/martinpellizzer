Rubus altbergensis

Rubus altbergensis, commonly known as the Altberg blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
