Rubus serpens var. aciculatus

Rubus serpens var. aciculatus, commonly known as the needleleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and small, dark berries that ripen in the summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its thorny stems and compact growth habit make it a resilient species in woodland habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and exhibits features typical of the Rubus genus, including aggregate fruits and persistent calyx structures.
