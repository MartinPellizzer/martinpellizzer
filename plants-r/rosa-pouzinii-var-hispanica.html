Rosa pouzinii var. hispanica

Rosa pouzinii var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves with a hairy underside, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Rosa pouzinii var. hispanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in Mediterranean climates.
