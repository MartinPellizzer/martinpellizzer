Rubus macrophyllus subsp. montanus

Rubus macrophyllus subsp.

montanus, commonly known as the mountain raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and prickly stems, typical of the Rubus genus.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a food source for wildlife.
