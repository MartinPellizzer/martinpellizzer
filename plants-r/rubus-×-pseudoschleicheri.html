Rubus × pseudoschleicheri

Rubus × pseudoschleicheri, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry raspberry, is a species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (common blackberry) and Rubus chilensis (chilean raspberry).

It is a thorny shrub native to South America, particularly Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart and slightly sweet flavor. It is valued for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries.

The hybrid is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
