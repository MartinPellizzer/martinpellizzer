Rhodax montanus var. paniculatus

Rhodax montanus var. paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled rhodax, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
