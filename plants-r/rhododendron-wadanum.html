Rhododendron wadanum

Rhododendron wadanum, commonly known as the Wadanum rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is well adapted to acidic soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
