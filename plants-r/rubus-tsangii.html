Rubus tsangii

Rubus tsangii, commonly known as the Tsang's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along mountain slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less commonly cultivated species.
