Rhodiola viridula

Rhodiola viridula, commonly known as the green rhodiola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and arctic environments, where it thrives in cold, rocky soils. It is known for its ability to tolerate extreme environmental conditions, including freezing temperatures and low light levels.

Rhodiola viridula is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
