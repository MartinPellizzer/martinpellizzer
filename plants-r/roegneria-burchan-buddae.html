Roegneria burchan-buddae

Roegneria burchan-buddae, commonly known as the Himalayan false brome, is a perennial grass native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
