Rosa moorcroftii

Rosa moorcroftii, commonly known as the Moorcroft's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Pamir and Tian Shan mountains. This perennial shrub is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has serrated, dark green leaves and is well adapted to high-altitude environments.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
