Rosa × litvinovii var. berehowiensis

Rosa × litvinovii var. berehowiensis, commonly known as the Berehowian hybrid rose, is a botanical variant native to Central Europe.

It is a cross between Rosa rugosa and Rosa canina, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems and dense, aromatic foliage. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in gardens and natural habitats.
