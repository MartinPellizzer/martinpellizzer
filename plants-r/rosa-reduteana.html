Rosa reduteana

Rosa reduteana, commonly known as the redutean rose, is a species native to parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of red and pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a woody structure and the ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny environments.

Rosa reduteana is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
