Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. suksdorfii

Ranunculus eschscholtzii var. suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suksdorf's buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with 5 to 15 petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the coastline, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.
