Ranunculus fallax subsp. orphnodes

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

orphnodes, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent, rounded centers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is characterized by its short stature and ability to withstand cold temperatures.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
