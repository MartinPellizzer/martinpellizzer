Rosa provincialis alba

Rosa provincialis alba, commonly known as the white provincial rose, is a species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its simple, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its botanical features include a woody stem, pinnate leaf structure, and fragrant petals.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its aesthetic appeal.
