Roxburghia baccata

Roxburghia baccata, commonly known as the red-fruited hibiscus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red, fleshy fruits that resemble small tomatoes, which are a key feature of the plant. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Roxburghia baccata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
