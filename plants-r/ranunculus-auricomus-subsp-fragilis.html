Ranunculus auricomus subsp. fragilis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

fragilis, commonly known as the fragrant buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more fragile stems and leaves compared to the typical form of Ranunculus auricomus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
