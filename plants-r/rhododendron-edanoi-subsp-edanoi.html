Rhododendron edanoi subsp. edanoi

Rhododendron edanoi subsp.

edanoi, commonly known as the Edanoi rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its floral display ranges from pink to red, with some cultivars exhibiting white or lavender hues.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
