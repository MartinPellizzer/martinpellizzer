Rosa pomifera var. proxima

Rosa pomifera var. proxima, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its fruit, known as a rose hip, is an ornamental feature that develops after flowering.

While it is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic value, it also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
