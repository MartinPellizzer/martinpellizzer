Ranunculus fallax subsp. porrectus

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

porrectus, commonly known as the straight-stemmed buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features erect, slender stems that can reach up to 60 cm in height, supporting large, showy yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive basal rosette and hairy surfaces.

The species is known for its early spring bloom and is often found in calcareous habitats.
