Rollinia dolichopetala var. suberecta

Rollinia dolichopetala var. suberecta, commonly known as the upright-leaf rollinia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces edible, sweet, and fleshy berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture in humid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
