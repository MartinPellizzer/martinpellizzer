Rhoicissus erythrodes

Rhoicissus erythrodes, commonly known as the red-flowered climbing vine, is a member of the Vitaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and gardens. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, making it suitable for trellises or arbors.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
