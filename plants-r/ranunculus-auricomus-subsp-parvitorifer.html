Ranunculus auricomus subsp. parvitorifer

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

parvitorifer, commonly known as the small-seeded golden buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy stem.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller seeds compared to other members of the species. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
