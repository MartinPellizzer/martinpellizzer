Rubus parviflorus f. paramicadenius

Rubus parviflorus f. paramicadenius, commonly known as the Micadens rosehip, is a botanical variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, hairy fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a desirable plant for gardens and conservation efforts.
