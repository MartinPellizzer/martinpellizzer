Randia gambica

Randia gambica, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
