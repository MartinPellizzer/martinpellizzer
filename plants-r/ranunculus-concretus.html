Ranunculus concretus

Ranunculus concretus, commonly known as the rock buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid regions.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in crevices and on slopes, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Ranunculus concretus is adapted to harsh environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
