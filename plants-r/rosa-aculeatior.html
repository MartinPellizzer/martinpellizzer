Rosa aculeatior

Rosa aculeatior, commonly known as the prickly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and its fruits are red, hairy berries.

Rosa aculeatior is native to parts of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.
