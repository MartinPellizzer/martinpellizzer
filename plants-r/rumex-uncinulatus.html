Rumex uncinulatus

Rumex uncinulatus, commonly known as curled dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, wavy, and often reddish leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender, erect stalks. It thrives in moist soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Rumex uncinulatus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
