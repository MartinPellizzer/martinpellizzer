Rosa afzeliana var. setosoglandulosa

Rosa afzeliana var. setosoglandulosa, commonly known as the hairy glandular rose, is a variant of the Rosa afzeliana species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and glandular structures, which are adaptations to its tropical and subtropical habitats. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and covered with fine, soft hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
