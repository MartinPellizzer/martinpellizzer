Rubus × spinescens

Rubus × spinescens, commonly known as the prickly blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

This species is often cultivated for its edible berries, though its spines make harvesting challenging.
