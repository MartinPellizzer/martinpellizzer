Ranunculus auricomus subsp. obtusidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

obtusidens, commonly known as the broad-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. Its flowering period occurs in spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies obtusidens is distinguished by its more rounded leaf shape compared to the typical form.
