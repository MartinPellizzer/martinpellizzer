Rubus × elongatus

Rubus × elongatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
