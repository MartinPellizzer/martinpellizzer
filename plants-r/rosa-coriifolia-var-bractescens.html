Rosa coriifolia var. bractescens

Rosa coriifolia var. bractescens, commonly known as the bracted shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, thornless stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has ovate, serrated leaves with a velvety texture, and its fruit is a red, fuzzy rose hip.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
