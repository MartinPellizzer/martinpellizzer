Rubus malgrangianus

Rubus malgrangianus, commonly known as the Malgrange bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small white or pink flowers. Its fruits, which are aggregate berries, are typically red or black when mature.

Rubus malgrangianus is often found in woodland edges and rocky slopes, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
