Rubus hirtus subsp. polytrichus

Rubus hirtus subsp.

polytrichus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its robust growth habit and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
