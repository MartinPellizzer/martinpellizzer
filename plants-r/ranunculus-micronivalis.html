Ranunculus micronivalis

Ranunculus micronivalis, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with a distinctive central pistil and numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.

Ranunculus micronivalis is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
