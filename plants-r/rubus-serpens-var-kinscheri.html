Rubus serpens var. kinscheri

Rubus serpens var. kinscheri, commonly known as the Kinscheri blackberry, is a variant of the creeping blackberry species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with thorny stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
