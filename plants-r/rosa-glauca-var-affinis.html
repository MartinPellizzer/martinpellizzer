Rosa glauca var. affinis

Rosa glauca var. affinis, commonly known as the blue rose or blue shrub rose, is a compact, shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and its most distinctive feature: flowers that range from pale blue to deep violet, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, oval fruits known as rose hips, which are typically red or orange in color. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique coloration, it is often cultivated for its visual interest in gardens and landscapes.
