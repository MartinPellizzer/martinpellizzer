Robinsonella brevituba

Robinsonella brevituba, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, adapting it to its often humid and shaded habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall streamlined appearance.

This orchid is often found growing in rocky crevices or on tree trunks, showcasing its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
