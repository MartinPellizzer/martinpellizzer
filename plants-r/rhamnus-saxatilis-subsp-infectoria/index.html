Rhamnus Saxatilis Subsp. Infectoria

Rhamnus saxatilis subsp. infectoria is a lesser-known member of the Rhamnaceae family. This subspecies is native to specific regions of southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean. It thrives in rocky and coastal environments, often growing on steep slopes and cliffs. Its small, dark green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers make it an interesting subject for botanists and horticulturists. Despite its ecological importance, it remains under-researched and largely overlooked in mainstream botanical studies.
