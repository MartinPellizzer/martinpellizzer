Ruelingia rufescens

Ruelingia rufescens, commonly known as the red-barked tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental bark and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
