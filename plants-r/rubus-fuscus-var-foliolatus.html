Rubus fuscus var. foliolatus

Rubus fuscus var. foliolatus, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry.

It is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia, characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The leaves are typically broad and have a distinctive lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows or woodland edges.
