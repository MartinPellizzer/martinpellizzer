Rosa morawskii

Rosa morawskii, commonly known as the Morawski rose, is a species of shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has dark green, leathery leaves with serrated edges. Rosa morawskii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
