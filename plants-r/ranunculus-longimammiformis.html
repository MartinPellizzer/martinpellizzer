Ranunculus longimammiformis

Ranunculus longimammiformis, commonly known as the long-mammiform buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, rounded center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture.

Ranunculus longimammiformis is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often bright-colored blooms.
