Rhus delavayi var. quinquejuga

Rhus delavayi var. quinquejuga, commonly known as the five-lobed Chinese gallnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets, each ending in a sharp tip. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles, followed by distinctive, spiny fruit clusters. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate forest ecosystems.
