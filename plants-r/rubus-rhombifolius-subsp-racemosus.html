Rubus rhombifolius subsp. racemosus

Rubus rhombifolius subsp.

racemosus, commonly known as the racemosus blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive rhomboid shape.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black drupelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
