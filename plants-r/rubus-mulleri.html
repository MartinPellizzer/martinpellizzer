Rubus mulleri

Rubus mulleri, commonly known as the Chinese blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The botanical features of Rubus mulleri make it an important species for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
