Rhamnus virgata var. hirsuta

Rhamnus virgata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark, glossy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The hairy buckthorn is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
