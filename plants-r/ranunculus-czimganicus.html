Ranunculus czimganicus

Ranunculus czimganicus, commonly known as the Czimgan's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has alternate, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
