Rumicastrum compositum

Rumicastrum compositum, commonly known as the composite rumicost, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically features a woody stem with alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
