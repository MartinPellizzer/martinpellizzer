Reseda alphonsi subsp. barbutii

Reseda alphonsi subsp.

barbutii, commonly known as the Barbut's sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, succulent stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
