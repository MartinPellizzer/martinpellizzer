Rhododendron zaleucum var. zaleucum

Rhododendron zaleucum var. zaleucum, commonly known as the white rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers acidic, well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
