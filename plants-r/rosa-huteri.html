Rosa huteri

Rosa huteri, commonly known as the Huter's rose, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in thorns. Rosa huteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of temperate climates.
