Reseda alba var. laetevirens

Reseda alba var. laetevirens, commonly known as yellow reseda, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
