Rubus dasyneurus

Rubus dasyneurus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

The hairy blackberry is valued for its edible berries and its role in local ecosystems.
