Rosa calva var. cathayensis

Rosa calva var. cathayensis, commonly known as the Chinese shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, usually in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a woody, perennial structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
