Rejoua aurantiaca

Rejoua aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The tree's rapid growth and vibrant floral display make it a notable species in botanical gardens and urban environments.
