Rhododendron sojolense

Rhododendron sojolense, commonly known as the Sojo's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, humid environments. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in high-altitude forests and is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
