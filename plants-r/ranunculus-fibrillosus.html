Ranunculus fibrillosus

Ranunculus fibrillosus, commonly known as the fibrous buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive, hairy center. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is characterized by its fibrous root system. Its flowers bloom in spring, and it is often found in rocky outcrops and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
