Rosa afzeliana f. subcomplicata

Rosa afzeliana f. subcomplicata, commonly known as the African rose or the Cape rose, is a hybrid rose species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
