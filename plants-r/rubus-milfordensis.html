Rubus milfordensis

Rubus milfordensis, commonly known as Milford bramble, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in coastal and forested areas, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
