Rhododendron tosaense f. duplex

Rhododendron tosaense f. duplex, commonly known as the Tosa double-flowered rhododendron, is a unique variant of the Tosa rhododendron.

It is characterized by its distinctive double blooms, which feature two layers of petals, creating a more elaborate floral display. This plant is native to Japan, specifically the Tosa region, where it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It typically grows as an upright shrub with dense, dark green foliage.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring, range in color from pink to white, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.
