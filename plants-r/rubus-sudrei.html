Rubus sudrei

Rubus sudrei, commonly known as the Sudetic raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
