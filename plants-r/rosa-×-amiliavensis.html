Rosa × amiliavensis

Rosa × amiliavensis, commonly known as the Amiliana rose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Rosa damascena and Rosa centifolia.

It is characterized by its strong, sweet fragrance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. The plant produces large, full blooms in shades of pink, red, and white, depending on the specific cultivar. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rosa × amiliavensis is also valued for its use in perfumery and as a source of rose otto oil.
