Rhus pentheri

Rhus pentheri, commonly known as the Cyprus buckthorn, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes plants like the poison ivy and cashew. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating. It produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are edible for some bird species.

Rhus pentheri is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
