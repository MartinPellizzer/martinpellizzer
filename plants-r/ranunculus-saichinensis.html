Ranunculus saichinensis

Ranunculus saichinensis, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive central tuft of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
