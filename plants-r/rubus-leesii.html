Rubus leesii

Rubus leesii, commonly known as the Lees' blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often described as tart and somewhat bland. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rubus genus within the Rosaceae family, which includes other brambles and shrubs.
