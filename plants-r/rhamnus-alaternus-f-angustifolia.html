Rhamnus alaternus f. angustifolia

Rhamnus alaternus f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed buckthorn, is a shrub characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and smooth, thornless stems.

It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue-black berries in the fall. The plant is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It has a dense growth habit, making it suitable for hedges or ornamental use in gardens.

Its foliage provides habitat for various insects and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.
