Rhododendron telmateium

Rhododendron telmateium, commonly known as the Andes mountain laurel, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

Rhododendron telmateium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its specific environmental requirements.
