Rosa pendulina

Rosa pendulina, commonly known as the weeping rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems that give it a drooping appearance, and its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Rosa pendulina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and delicate floral display.
