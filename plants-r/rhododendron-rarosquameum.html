Rhododendron rarosquameum

Rhododendron rarosquameum, commonly known as the rare scale-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically small, leathery, and covered with a distinctive scale-like texture. This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid climate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
