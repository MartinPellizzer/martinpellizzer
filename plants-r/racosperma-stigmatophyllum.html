Racosperma stigmatophyllum

Racosperma stigmatophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has thin, linear leaves and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.

Racosperma stigmatophyllum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
