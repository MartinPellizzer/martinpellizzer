Rubus oscillans

Rubus oscillans, commonly known as the wavy bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it typically grows in woodland edges and shrublands. The plant is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the fruit is a small, aggregate berry.

Rubus oscillans is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
