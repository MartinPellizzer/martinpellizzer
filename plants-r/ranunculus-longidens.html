Ranunculus longidens

Ranunculus longidens, commonly known as the long-stemmed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. The plant has a hairy, upright stem and palmate leaves that are deeply divided. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
