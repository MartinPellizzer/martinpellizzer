Rutidea landolphioides

Rutidea landolphioides, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
