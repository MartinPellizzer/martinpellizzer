Rubia peregrina var. intermedia

Rubia peregrina var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate wood rue, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers have been historically used in herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
