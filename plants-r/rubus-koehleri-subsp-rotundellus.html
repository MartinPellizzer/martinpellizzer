Rubus koehleri subsp. rotundellus

Rubus koehleri subsp.

rotundellus, commonly known as the roundleaf blackberry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and thorny stems, typical of many Rubus species.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible berries.
