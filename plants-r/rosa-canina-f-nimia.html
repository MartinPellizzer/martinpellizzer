Rosa canina f. nimia

Rosa canina f. nimia, commonly known as the "Crimson Bush Rose," is a botanical variant of the dog rose, characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit.

It features dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to crimson. The plant is notable for its abundant production of rose hips, which are rich in vitamin C and often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
