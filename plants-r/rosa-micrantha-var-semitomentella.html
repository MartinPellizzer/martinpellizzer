Rosa micrantha var. semitomentella

Rosa micrantha var. semitomentella, commonly known as the silky rose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit makes it well-suited for rock gardens and as a border plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
