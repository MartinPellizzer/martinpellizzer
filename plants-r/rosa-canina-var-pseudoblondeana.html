Rosa canina var. pseudoblondeana

Rosa canina var. pseudoblondeana, commonly known as the pale rose or blond rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which are often more subdued in color compared to the typical red of Rosa canina. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits known as hips, which are edible and used in culinary and medicinal applications. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rosaceae family.
