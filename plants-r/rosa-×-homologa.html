Rosa × homologa

Rosa × homologa, commonly known as the hybrid tea rose, is a widely cultivated ornamental plant characterized by its large, fragrant flowers and thorny stems.

It is a hybrid between Rosa damascena and Rosa gallica, resulting in a plant that exhibits a range of color variations from white to deep red. The botanical features include simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins and a growth habit that is typically shrub-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote flowering.

This species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is commonly used in gardens and floral arrangements.
