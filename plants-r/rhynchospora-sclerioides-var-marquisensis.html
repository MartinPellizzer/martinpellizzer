Rhynchospora sclerioides var. marquisensis

Rhynchospora sclerioides var. marquisensis, commonly known as the Marquis Island spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Marquesas Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or pale brown in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas. Its flowers are monoecious, with separate male and female inflorescences, and it reproduces through wind pollination.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
