Rhododendron × albescens

Rhododendron × albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered rhododendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rhododendron catawbiense and Rhododendron maximum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.

Rhododendron × albescens is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
