Ruschia vaginella

Ruschia vaginella, commonly known as the velvetleaf Ruschia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid climates.
