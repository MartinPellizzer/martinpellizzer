Rorippa pseudoboissieri

Rorippa pseudoboissieri, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and flooded meadows. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Rorippa pseudoboissieri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
