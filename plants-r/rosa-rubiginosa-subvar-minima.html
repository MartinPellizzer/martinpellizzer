Rosa rubiginosa subvar. minima

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. minima, commonly known as the dwarf rose or miniature wild rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This subspecies is prized for its hardiness and ornamental value in horticulture.
