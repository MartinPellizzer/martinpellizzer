Ranunculus auricomus subsp. vaginifer

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

vaginifer, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and finely divided, pinnate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. Its subspecies vaginifer is distinguished by its leaf morphology, with stipules that form a sheath around the stem.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
