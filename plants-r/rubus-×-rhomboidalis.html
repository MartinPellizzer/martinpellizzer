Rubus × rhomboidalis

Rubus × rhomboidalis, commonly known as the blackberry or raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chamaemorus (cloudberry). The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or forested areas.

The fruit is edible, with a tart flavor, and is used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
