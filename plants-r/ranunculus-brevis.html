Ranunculus brevis

Ranunculus brevis, commonly known as the short-stemmed buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, cup-shaped flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ranunculus brevis is often found in temperate regions and is noted for its delicate appearance and early spring blooming habit.
