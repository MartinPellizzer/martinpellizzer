Rhynchosia totta var. pilosa

Rhynchosia totta var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pilosa.". The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. It belongs to the legume family (Fabaceae) and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Rhynchosia totta var. pilosa is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
