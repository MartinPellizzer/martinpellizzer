Rosa canina var. swartzii

Rosa canina var. swartzii, commonly known as the Swartz's dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red-orange hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional herbal remedies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, supporting pollinators and providing food for wildlife.
