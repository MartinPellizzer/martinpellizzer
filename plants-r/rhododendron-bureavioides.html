Rhododendron bureavioides

Rhododendron bureavioides, commonly known as the Bornean rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Rhododendron bureavioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
