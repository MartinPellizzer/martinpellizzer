Rubus podophyllos var. dichroacanthus

Rubus podophyllos var. dichroacanthus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a variant of the bramble family with distinctive botanical features.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries in the summer. Its leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, giving it a dichroic appearance.

This species is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, wooded areas.
