Rosa pendulina subvar. diplotricha

Rosa pendulina subvar. diplotricha, commonly known as the weeping shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in an alternate pattern along the stems. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
