Ronaldella determannii

Ronaldella determannii, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ronaldella determannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
