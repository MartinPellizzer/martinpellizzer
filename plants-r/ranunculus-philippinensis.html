Ranunculus philippinensis

Ranunculus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus philippinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
