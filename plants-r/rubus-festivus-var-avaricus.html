Rubus festivus var. avaricus

Rubus festivus var. avaricus, commonly known as the showy raspberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun. Its flowers are pinkish-white with five petals and are visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in local cuisines and traditional herbal remedies.
