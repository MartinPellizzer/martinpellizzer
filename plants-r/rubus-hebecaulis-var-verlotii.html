Rubus hebecaulis var. verlotii

Rubus hebecaulis var. verlotii, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along streambanks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring.

The berries are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
