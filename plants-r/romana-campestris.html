Romana campestris

Romana campestris, commonly known as field pennycress, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves that are hairy to the touch. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in cultivated fields.

Romana campestris is also known for its ability to produce a toxic oil, which has been historically used in various applications.
