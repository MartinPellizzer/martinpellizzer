Rubus × parlinii

Rubus × parlinii, commonly known as the parlinii blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces edible berries that are popular for culinary use.

This species is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
