Rhododendron westlandii

Rhododendron westlandii, commonly known as the Westlandii rhododendron, is a species native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica and Panama.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of epiphytic plants.

Rhododendron westlandii thrives in cool, humid environments with high rainfall and is often found in shaded, mountainous regions.
