Racosperma acanthocladum subsp. glaucescens

Racosperma acanthocladum subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous acanthocladum, is a shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive glaucous foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in degraded areas.
