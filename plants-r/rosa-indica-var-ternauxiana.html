Rosa indica var. ternauxiana

Rosa indica var. ternauxiana, commonly known as the Ternauxiana rose, is a botanical variant of the Indian rose.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves, and it produces rose hips that are used in traditional medicine.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
