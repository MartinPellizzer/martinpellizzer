Rubus sirbenus

Rubus sirbenus, commonly known as the Serb raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Europe and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
