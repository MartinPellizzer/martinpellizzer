Rhododendron cerasiflorum

Rhododendron cerasiflorum, commonly known as the cherry-flowered rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers in late spring. The plant has elliptic to ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
