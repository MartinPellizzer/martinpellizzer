Rubus malaccensis

Rubus malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
