Rutidea glabra

Rutidea glabra, commonly known as the smooth rutidion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes. The plant has smooth, glabrous leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Rutidea glabra plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native ecosystems.
