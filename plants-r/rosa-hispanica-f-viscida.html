Rosa hispanica f. viscida

Rosa hispanica f. viscida, commonly known as the Spanish rose or the sticky rose, is a cultivar characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a hybrid of Rosa hispanica, which is native to the Iberian Peninsula, and is distinguished by its dense, sticky hairs on the stems and leaves. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and often covered with a fine, waxy coating, contributing to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various garden conditions.
