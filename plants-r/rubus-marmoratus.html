Rubus marmoratus

Rubus marmoratus, commonly known as the marbled raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its distinctive marbled pattern on its leaves and fruits. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are similar in appearance to raspberries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and showy white flowers.
