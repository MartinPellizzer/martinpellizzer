Rubus sellowii f. calvescens

Rubus sellowii f. calvescens, commonly known as the Sellow's blackberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
