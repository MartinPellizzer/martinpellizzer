Rhapidophyllum

Rhapidophyllum, commonly known as the giant saguaro cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the Sonoran Desert in North America, particularly in Arizona and southern California. The plant is characterized by its tall, branching stems that can grow up to 50 feet in height. Its thick, ribbed structure stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Rhapidophyllum plays a vital role in the desert ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
