Rhaphiolepis loureiroi

Rhaphiolepis loureiroi, commonly known as the Japanese laurel, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight serration along the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a versatile choice for landscaping.
