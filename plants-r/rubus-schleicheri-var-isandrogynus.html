Rubus schleicheri var. isandrogynus

Rubus schleicheri var. isandrogynus, commonly known as the Androgynous Blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaries. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This variety is notable for its unique reproductive features, including the presence of both male and female reproductive structures in the same flower.
