Rosa pliniana

Rosa pliniana, commonly known as the Pliocene rose, is a rare and ancient species of flowering plant.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in temperate climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are typically thorny.

Rosa pliniana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical significance in botanical studies.
