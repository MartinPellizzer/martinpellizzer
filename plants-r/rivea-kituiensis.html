Rivea kituiensis

Rivea kituiensis, commonly known as the Kitui morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pentagonal flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
