Rumex raulinii

Rumex raulinii, commonly known as the Raulin's sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features upright, branched stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish flowers are arranged in dense clusters and bloom in early spring.

Rumex raulinii is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
