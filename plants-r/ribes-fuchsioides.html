Ribes fuchsioides

Ribes fuchsioides, commonly known as the pink currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of currants and gooseberries. The plant is characterized by its small, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, edible berry that ripens to a dark purple or black color, typically harvested in late summer.
