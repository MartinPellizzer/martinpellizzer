Rutaea obtusifolia

Rutaea obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved rutaea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually leathery and have a blunt or rounded tip. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in cymes, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Rutaea obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
