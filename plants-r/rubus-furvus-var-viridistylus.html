Rubus furvus var. viridistylus

Rubus furvus var. viridistylus, commonly known as the green-styled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they give way to small, dark purple berries that are edible. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or thickets.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species within the Rubus genus.
