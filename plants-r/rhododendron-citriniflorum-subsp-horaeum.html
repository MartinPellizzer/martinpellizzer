Rhododendron citriniflorum subsp. horaeum

Rhododendron citriniflorum subsp.

horaeum, commonly known as the goldenflower azalea, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
