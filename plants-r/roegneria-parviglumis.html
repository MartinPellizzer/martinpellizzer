Roegneria parviglumis

Roegneria parviglumis, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are small and arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family.

Roegneria parviglumis is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soils and support local wildlife.
