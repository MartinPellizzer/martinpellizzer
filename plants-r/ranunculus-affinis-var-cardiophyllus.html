Ranunculus affinis var. cardiophyllus

Ranunculus affinis var. cardiophyllus, commonly known as the heart-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped, with a distinctive lobed appearance, and flowering stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, with both male and female reproductive structures, and are pollinated by insects.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
