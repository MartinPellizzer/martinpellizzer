Rosa brunonii var. nepalensis

Rosa brunonii var. nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal rose, is a shrub-like species native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated leaflets, and it produces rosehips that are rich in vitamin C. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
