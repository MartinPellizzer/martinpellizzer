Ranunculus pectinatus

Ranunculus pectinatus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus pectinatus is often found in temperate regions and is known for its early spring blooming period.
