Rubus centiformis var. adenothyrsus

Rubus centiformis var. adenothyrsus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and dark purple to black berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in temperate climates. It has a thorny stem structure typical of the Rubus genus, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This variant is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
