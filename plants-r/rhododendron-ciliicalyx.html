Rhododendron ciliicalyx

Rhododendron ciliicalyx, commonly known as the woolly rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in China and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Rhododendron ciliicalyx is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
