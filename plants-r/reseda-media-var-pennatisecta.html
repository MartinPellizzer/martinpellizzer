Reseda media var. pennatisecta

Reseda media var. pennatisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved madder, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are deeply divided into narrow segments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
