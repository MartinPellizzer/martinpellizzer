Ranunculastrum sparsipilum

Ranunculastrum sparsipilum, commonly known as the sparsely hairy ranunculus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes a variety of herbaceous plants known for their showy flowers. The plant typically features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
