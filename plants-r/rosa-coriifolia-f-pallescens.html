Rosa coriifolia f. pallescens

Rosa coriifolia f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale-flowered shrub rose, is a unique variant of the common shrub rose.

It is characterized by its pale pink to almost white flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. Its botanical features include simple, serrated leaves and fragrant, single or semi-double flowers.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
