Ranunculus acris subsp. japonicus

Ranunculus acris subsp.

japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy coating and produces showy yellow flowers with numerous stamens.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. It is characterized by its finely divided foliage and is often found in temperate climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth habit compared to the typical R. acris.
