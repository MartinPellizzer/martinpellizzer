Rubus lindblomii

Rubus lindblomii, commonly known as Lindblom's blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the mountainous regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are valued for their flavor and are sometimes used in local culinary traditions.
