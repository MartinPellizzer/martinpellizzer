Rhynchelythrum ramosum

Rhynchelythrum ramosum, commonly known as the branching thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of spiny, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear pink to purple flowers in late summer. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences, and the plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils.

Rhynchelythrum ramosum is often found in grasslands and wastelands, where it thrives in full sun.
