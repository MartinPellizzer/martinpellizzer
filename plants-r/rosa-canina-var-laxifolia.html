Rosa canina var. laxifolia

Rosa canina var. laxifolia, commonly known as the shrub rose or dog rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features arching stems with thorns and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces rosehips, which are small, orange-red fruits containing high levels of vitamin C. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with 5 to 9 elliptical leaflets.

Rosa canina var. laxifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
