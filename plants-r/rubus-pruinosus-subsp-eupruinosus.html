Rubus pruinosus subsp. eupruinosus

Rubus pruinosus subsp.

eupruinosus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its finely hairy, often reddish stems and large, trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark purple to black achenes surrounded by a fleshy, white or pinkish receptacle. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
