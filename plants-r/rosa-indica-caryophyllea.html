Rosa indica caryophyllea

Rosa indica caryophyllea, commonly known as the Damask rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western Himalayas and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant is known for its high essential oil content, which is extracted from its petals. The oil is widely used in perfumery and skincare products due to its rich aroma and therapeutic properties.

Rosa indica caryophyllea is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
