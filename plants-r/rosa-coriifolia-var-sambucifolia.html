Rosa coriifolia var. sambucifolia

Rosa coriifolia var. sambucifolia, commonly known as the sambucifolia rose, is a hybrid rose species characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features.

It is native to China and is known for its finely cut, maple-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early summer. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscape design for its visual appeal.
