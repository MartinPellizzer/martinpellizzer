Ranunculus reticulatus

Ranunculus reticulatus, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, bright yellow flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as ponds, streams, and wet meadows. Its roots are rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water conditions, making it a common sight in freshwater habitats.
