Rubus hirtus var. ravus

Rubus hirtus var. ravus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, fertile ground.

This species is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
