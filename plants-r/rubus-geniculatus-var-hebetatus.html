Rubus geniculatus var. hebetatus

Rubus geniculatus var. hebetatus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The botanical features of this variety make it distinct from other Rubus species, particularly in its leaf texture and fruit morphology.
