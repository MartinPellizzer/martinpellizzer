Rubus porphyropetalus

Rubus porphyropetalus, commonly known as the purple-petaled raspberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs with thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers and edible, raspberry-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
