Rhagodia candolleana var. parvifolia

Rhagodia candolleana var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved rhagodia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing, mat-forming habit, characterized by its narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
