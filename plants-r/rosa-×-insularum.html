Rosa × insularum

Rosa × insularum, commonly known as the Madeira rose, is a hybrid species native to the Azores and Madeira Islands.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Rosa × insularum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its beauty and hardiness.
