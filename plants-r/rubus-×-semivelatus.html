Rubus × semivelatus

Rubus × semivelatus, commonly known as the silverleaf blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries and silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its fruit is edible, though some varieties may have a tart or astringent flavor.
