Romulea requienii var. revelierei

Romulea requienii var. revelierei, commonly known as the Reveliere's glory-of-the-snow, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is particularly noted for its early spring blooming period, making it a sought-after addition to rock gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
