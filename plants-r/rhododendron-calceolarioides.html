Rhododendron calceolarioides

Rhododendron calceolarioides, commonly known as the alpine azalea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. Its leaves are generally small, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Rhododendron calceolarioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
