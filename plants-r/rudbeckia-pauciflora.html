Rudbeckia pauciflora

Rudbeckia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy yellow to orange daisy-like flowers with a dark central disk, typically blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Rudbeckia pauciflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
