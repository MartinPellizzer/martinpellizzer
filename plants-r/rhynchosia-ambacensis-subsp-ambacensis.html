Rhynchosia ambacensis subsp. ambacensis

Rhynchosia ambacensis subsp.

ambacensis, commonly known as the African burrbean, is a climbing legume native to sub-Saharan Africa. It features trifoliate leaves with pinnate venation and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant bears distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
