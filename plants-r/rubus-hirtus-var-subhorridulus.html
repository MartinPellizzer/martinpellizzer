Rubus hirtus var. subhorridulus

Rubus hirtus var. subhorridulus, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

This variety is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
