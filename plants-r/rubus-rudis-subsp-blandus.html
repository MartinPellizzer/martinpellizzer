Rubus rudis subsp. blandus

Rubus rudis subsp.

blandus, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy foliage and distinctive floral structure.
