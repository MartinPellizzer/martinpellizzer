Ranunculus auricomus subsp. singularis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

singularis, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided with a serrated edge, and the stems are often hairy.

It is native to Europe and has been introduced to parts of North America.
