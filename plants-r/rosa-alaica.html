Rosa alaica

Rosa alaica, commonly known as the Caucasian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It produces fragrant, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem covered in thorns. Rosa alaica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
