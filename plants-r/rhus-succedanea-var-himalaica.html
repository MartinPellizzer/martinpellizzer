Rhus succedanea var. himalaica

Rhus succedanea var. himalaica, commonly known as Himalayan sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with 11 to 31 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
