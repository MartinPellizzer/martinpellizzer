Ruellia californica subsp. californica

Ruellia californica subsp.

californica, commonly known as California ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and chaparral habitats.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that have a rough, hairy texture. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
