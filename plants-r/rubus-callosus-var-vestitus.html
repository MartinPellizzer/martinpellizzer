Rubus callosus var. vestitus

Rubus callosus var. vestitus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
