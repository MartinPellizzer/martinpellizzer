Rochea dejecta

Rochea dejecta, commonly known as the desert rose or false rose, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually small and scale-like, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Rochea dejecta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
