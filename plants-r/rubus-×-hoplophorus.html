Rubus × hoplophorus

Rubus × hoplophorus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between blackberry (Rubus fruticosus) and raspberry (Rubus idaeus).

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color, containing numerous small drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible berries.

The botanical features of Rubus × hoplophorus make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
