Rubus obscurus var. fulgens

Rubus obscurus var. fulgens, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by dark purple berries in the fall. Its foliage is typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
