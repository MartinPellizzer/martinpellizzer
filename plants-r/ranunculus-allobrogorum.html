Ranunculus allobrogorum

Ranunculus allobrogorum, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five to nine petals, and its leaves are deeply lobed and hairy. It has a short stem and produces small, greenish-brown achenes as its fruit.

Ranunculus allobrogorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
