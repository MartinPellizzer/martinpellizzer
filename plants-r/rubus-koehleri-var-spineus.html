Rubus koehleri var. spineus

Rubus koehleri var. spineus, commonly known as the spiny blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep red or black color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
