Rosa rubiginosa var. macrostyla

Rosa rubiginosa var. macrostyla, commonly known as the large-styled Rosa rugosa, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rugged, thorny stems and broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by orange-red hips that persist through the winter. It thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is highly tolerant of maritime conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife.
