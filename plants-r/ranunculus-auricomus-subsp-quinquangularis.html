Ranunculus auricomus subsp. quinquangularis

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

quinquangularis, commonly known as the five-angled buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features showy, yellow flowers with five distinct, angular petals that give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, glossy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
