Ranunculus peltatus subsp. pseudofluitans

Ranunculus peltatus subsp.

pseudofluitans, commonly known as the floating water crowfoot, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are palmately lobed and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and marshes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of aquatic life.
