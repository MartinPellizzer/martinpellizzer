Rubus hatsushimae

Rubus hatsushimae, commonly known as the Hatsushimae raspberry, is a species of bramble native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
