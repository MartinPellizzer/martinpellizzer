Rosa coriifolia var. valderecendens

Rosa coriifolia var. valderecendens, commonly known as the winter coral.

This variant is a deciduous shrub native to China, characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant features thorned stems and trifoliate leaves, with small, lance-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, creating a showy, clustered display.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
