Ranunculus auricomus subsp. magnitorifer

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

magnitorifer, commonly known as the large-flowered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, showy flowers with numerous petals and a distinctive central yellow stamen cluster.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are usually hairy.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
