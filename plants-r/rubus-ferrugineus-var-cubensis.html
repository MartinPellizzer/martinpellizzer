Rubus ferrugineus var. cubensis

Rubus ferrugineus var. cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban raspberry, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
