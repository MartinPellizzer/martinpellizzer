Rubus suberectus f. oblongatus

Rubus suberectus f. oblongatus, commonly known as the broadleaf blackberry, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and oblong, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually black when ripe, though they may vary in color depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various regions.
