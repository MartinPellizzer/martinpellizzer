Ranunculus areolatus

Ranunculus areolatus, commonly known as the spotted buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with distinctive black spots on the petals, which are arranged in a characteristic pattern. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and the stems are hairy and often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
