Rosa villosa var. tomentosa

Rosa villosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and stems, which give it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval-shaped leaflets that are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in garden settings.
