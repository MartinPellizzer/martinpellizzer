Ranunculastrum bulbosum

Ranunculastrum bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive central crown, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has a bulbous root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Ranunculastrum bulbosum is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
