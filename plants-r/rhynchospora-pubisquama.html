Rhynchospora pubisquama

Rhynchospora pubisquama, commonly known as the hairy bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges.

Rhynchospora pubisquama is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
