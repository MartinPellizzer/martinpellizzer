Rosa salicinella

Rosa salicinella, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are elliptical, with a silvery-white underside that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
