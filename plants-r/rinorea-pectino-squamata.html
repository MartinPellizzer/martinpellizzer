Rinorea pectino-squamata

Rinorea pectino-squamata, commonly known as the woolly rinorea, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in terminal inflorescences, and are usually white or pale pink in color.

Rinorea pectino-squamata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and foliage.
