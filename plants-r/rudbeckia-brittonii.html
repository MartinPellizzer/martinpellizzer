Rudbeckia brittonii

Rudbeckia brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow disk flowers surrounded by orange to red ray flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces a tall, branching stem with rough, hairy leaves and is known for its showy, composite flower heads.

Rudbeckia brittonii is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
