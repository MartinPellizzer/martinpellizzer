Ribes rubrisepalum

Ribes rubrisepalum, commonly known as the red-sepal currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red sepals that remain visible even after the flowers have faded. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Ribes rubrisepalum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
