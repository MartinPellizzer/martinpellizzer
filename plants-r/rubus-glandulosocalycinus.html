Rubus glandulosocalycinus

Rubus glandulosocalycinus, commonly known as the glandular calycinous raspberry.

This plant is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. It typically grows in temperate regions with moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are usually compound, with serrated edges and glandular hairs.

Its flowers are pink to white, and it produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.
