Rapanea diminuta

Rapanea diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf rapanea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, glossy dark green leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
