Rhytiglossa obtusifolia

Rhytiglossa obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhytiglossa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. The plant features broad, ovate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and a distinctive reddish or purple stem. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are usually pink or white in color.

Rhytiglossa obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
