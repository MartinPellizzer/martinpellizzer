Rubus imperiorum

Rubus imperiorum, commonly known as the imperial blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry and blackberry species.
