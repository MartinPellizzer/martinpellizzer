Rosa glauca f. pennina

Rosa glauca f. pennina, commonly known as the blue rose or blue-flowered rose, is a rare and striking variation of the Rosa glauca species.

This cultivar is characterized by its deep blue petals, which result from a unique combination of pigments and genetic traits. It is a shrub with thorny stems and fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often prized by horticulturists and botanical enthusiasts.
