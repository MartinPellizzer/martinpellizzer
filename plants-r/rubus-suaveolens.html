Rubus suaveolens

Rubus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented raspberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its hairy, reddish stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
