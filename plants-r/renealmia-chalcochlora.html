Renealmia chalcochlora

Renealmia chalcochlora, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist and well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like structure that produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Renealmia chalcochlora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
