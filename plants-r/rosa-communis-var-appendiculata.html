Rosa communis var. appendiculata

Rosa communis var. appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring appendages on the sepals that give it a unique appearance. This plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a fragrant aroma.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden settings.
