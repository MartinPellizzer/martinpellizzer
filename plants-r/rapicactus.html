Rapicactus

Rapicactus, commonly known as the rock cactus, is a genus of cacti native to Mexico.

These plants are characterized by their compact, often spiny stems that are adapted to store water. They typically grow in rocky or arid environments, showcasing a unique ability to thrive in harsh conditions. The stems are usually green and may bear small, spiny or woolly structures.

Rapicactus species are popular among cactus enthusiasts due to their striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
