Ribes leptanthum subsp. lasianthum

Ribes leptanthum subsp.

lasianthum, commonly known as the large-flowered currant, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which are often reddish in color.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red berries in the summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a hairy underside.

This subspecies is often found in moist, shady habitats such as forests and stream banks.
