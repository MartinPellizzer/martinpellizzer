Rosa psilophylla

Rosa psilophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in the spring. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
