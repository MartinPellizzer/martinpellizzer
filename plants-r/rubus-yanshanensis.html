Rubus yanshanensis

Rubus yanshanensis, commonly known as the Yanshan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit is valued for its flavor and is sometimes used in local cuisines.
