Ranunculus breyninus var. nemorosus

Ranunculus breyninus var. nemorosus, commonly known as the wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas, where it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a basal rosette of foliage.

The species is known for its early spring blooming habit, often emerging through leaf litter in late winter or early spring.
