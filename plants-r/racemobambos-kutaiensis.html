Racemobambos kutaiensis

Racemobambos kutaiensis, commonly known as the Kutai bamboo, is a unique member of the grass family (Poaceae) native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a hollow, segmented structure typical of bamboo species. The plant produces inflorescences in the form of racemes, which are clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a slight waxy coating, aiding in water retention in its humid habitat.

Despite its bamboo-like appearance, it is botanically distinct due to its specific genetic makeup and ecological adaptations.
