Rudbeckia serotina f. rubra

Rudbeckia serotina f. rubra, commonly known as the red-leaf coneflower, is a variant of the late coneflower native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red leaves, which contrast with its yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. It produces composite flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique foliage and is often cultivated in wetland gardens.
