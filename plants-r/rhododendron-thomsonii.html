Rhododendron thomsonii

Rhododendron thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and often have a slight pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in trusses and are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
