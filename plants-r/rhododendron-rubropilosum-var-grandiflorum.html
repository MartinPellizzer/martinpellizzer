Rhododendron rubropilosum var. grandiflorum

Rhododendron rubropilosum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese flame azalea, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and elliptical in shape.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
