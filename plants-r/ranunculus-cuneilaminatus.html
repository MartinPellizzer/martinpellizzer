Ranunculus cuneilaminatus

Ranunculus cuneilaminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are distinctive, with a narrow, wedge-shaped base and deeply lobed upper leaves.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are often accompanied by a pleasant, slightly sweet fragrance.
