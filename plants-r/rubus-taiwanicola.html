Rubus taiwanicola

Rubus taiwanicola, commonly known as the Taiwan bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Taiwan and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
