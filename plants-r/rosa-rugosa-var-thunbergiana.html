Rosa rugosa var. thunbergiana

Rosa rugosa var. thunbergiana, commonly known as the Thunberg rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rugged, thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its hips, which are orange-red berries, are edible and often used in jams and teas.

Rosa rugosa var. thunbergiana is highly adaptable and thrives in coastal areas, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
