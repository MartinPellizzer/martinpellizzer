Rubus idaeus var. canadensis

Rubus idaeus var. canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian raspberry, is a variant of the red raspberry plant native to North America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupes that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
