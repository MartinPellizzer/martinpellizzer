Rubus sabinus

Rubus sabinus, commonly known as the thimbleberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and pies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are hairy and have a distinctive serrated edge.

The thimbleberry is also valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
