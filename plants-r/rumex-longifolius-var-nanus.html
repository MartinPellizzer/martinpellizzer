Rumex longifolius var. nanus

Rumex longifolius var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf dock, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by reddish-brown seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and wet meadows.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden use in damp areas.
