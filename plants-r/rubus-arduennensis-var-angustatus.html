Rubus arduennensis var. angustatus

Rubus arduennensis var. angustatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves and small, dark berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and moist soils, preferring partial shade. Its botanical features include thorny stems and a compact growth habit.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
