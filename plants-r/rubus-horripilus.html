Rubus horripilus

Rubus horripilus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, juicy drupelets that are often eaten by various animals and birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in woodland areas and along hedgerows.

Rubus horripilus plays an important role in ecosystems by providing food and shelter for wildlife.
