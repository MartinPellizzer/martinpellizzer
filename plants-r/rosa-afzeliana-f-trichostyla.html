Rosa afzeliana f. trichostyla

Rosa afzeliana f. trichostyla, commonly known as the trichostyle rose, is a botanical variant of the species Rosa afzeliana.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy stems and foliage, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Africa and thrives in well-drained soils with partial sunlight.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
