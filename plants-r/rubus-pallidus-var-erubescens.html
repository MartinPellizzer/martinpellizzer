Rubus pallidus var. erubescens

Rubus pallidus var. erubescens, commonly known as the pale raspberry variant, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits are small, aggregate berries that turn red when ripe, often used in local cuisines.

This species is typically found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
