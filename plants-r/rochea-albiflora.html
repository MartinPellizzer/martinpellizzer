Rochea albiflora

Rochea albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered rock heath, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. The plant produces small, white, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Rochea albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
