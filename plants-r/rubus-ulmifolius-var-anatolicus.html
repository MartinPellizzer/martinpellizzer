Rubus ulmifolius var. anatolicus

Rubus ulmifolius var. anatolicus, commonly known as Anatolian blackberry, is a variant of the common bramble native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, edible berries and its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local cuisines.
