Rhododendron × bathyphyllum

Rhododendron × bathyphyllum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features dense, leathery evergreen leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
