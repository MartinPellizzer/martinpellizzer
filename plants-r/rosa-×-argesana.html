Rosa × argesana

Rosa × argesana, commonly known as the Argentinian rose, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina, and is characterized by its vigorous growth and thorny stems. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color, with five to seven petals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its adaptability, Rosa × argesana is often cultivated in temperate climates for its aesthetic value and hardiness.
