Rubus tereticaulis microgene subalpinus

Rubus tereticaulis microgene subalpinus, commonly known as the alpine blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the flowers are white with five petals.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in subalpine and alpine zones.
