Rubus pronus

Rubus pronus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in jams and pies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Rubus pronus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
