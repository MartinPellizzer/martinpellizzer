Renealmia mutica

Renealmia mutica, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands and marshes of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Renealmia mutica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
