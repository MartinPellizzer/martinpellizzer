Robinia schiedeana

Robinia schiedeana, commonly known as the Schiede's acacia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, which opens to release the seeds when mature.
