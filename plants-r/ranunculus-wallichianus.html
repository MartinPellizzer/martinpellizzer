Ranunculus wallichianus

Ranunculus wallichianus, commonly known as the Himalayan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps protect it from harsh weather.

Ranunculus wallichianus is a perennial herb that plays a role in the ecological diversity of its mountainous habitat.
