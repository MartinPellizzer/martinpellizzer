Rhododendron maddenii var. obtusifolium

Rhododendron maddenii var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf rhododendron, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests. Its leaves are thick and leathery, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
