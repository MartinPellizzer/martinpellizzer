Rubus etropicus

Rubus etropicus, commonly known as the tropical raspberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white or pink and appear in clusters.

The fruit, which resembles a raspberry, is edible and has a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
