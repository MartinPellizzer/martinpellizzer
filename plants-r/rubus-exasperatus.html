Rubus exasperatus

Rubus exasperatus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, edible blackberries. The plant produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white and clustered in umbels.

Rubus exasperatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
