Rubus vestitus var. subeglandulosus

Rubus vestitus var. subeglandulosus, commonly known as the hairy raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruits, though it can be somewhat invasive in certain environments.
