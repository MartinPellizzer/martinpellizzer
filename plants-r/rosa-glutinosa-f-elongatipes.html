Rosa glutinosa f. elongatipes

Rosa glutinosa f. elongatipes, commonly known as the sticky rose or sticky bush rose, is a unique hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Asia and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its beauty and resilience.
