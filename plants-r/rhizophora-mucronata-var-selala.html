Rhizophora mucronata var. selala

Rhizophora mucronata var. selala, commonly known as the Selala mangrove.

It is a variant of the mangrove species Rhizophora mucronata, characterized by its unique morphological features. This plant exhibits specialized adaptations for saltwater environments, including salt-excreting leaves and aerial roots. It thrives in tidal zones and estuaries, playing a crucial role in coastal ecosystem stability.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
