Rubus borreri var. brachytrichoides

Rubus borreri var. brachytrichoides, commonly known as the short-hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, erect shrubs with thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically pink or white, and the fruit is a small, dark purple to black berry. This variety is notable for its dense, short hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
