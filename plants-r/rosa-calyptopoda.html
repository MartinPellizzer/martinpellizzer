Rosa calyptopoda

Rosa calyptopoda, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and stems, which provide a unique texture and appearance. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are covered in fine hairs.

Rosa calyptopoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in suitable climates.
