Ranunculus illinoensis

Ranunculus illinoensis, commonly known as the Illinois buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has smooth, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

The Illinois buttercup is part of the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
