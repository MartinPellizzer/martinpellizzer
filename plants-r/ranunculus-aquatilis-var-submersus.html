Ranunculus aquatilis var. submersus

Ranunculus aquatilis var. submersus, commonly known as the submerged water crowfoot, is a variant of the water crowfoot genus native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its fully submerged leaves and flowers, which are adapted to aquatic conditions. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are usually narrow and ribbon-like, while its flowers are small and yellow with five petals.

This variant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
