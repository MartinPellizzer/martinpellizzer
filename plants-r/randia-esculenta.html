Randia esculenta

Randia esculenta, commonly known as wild tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. Its roots and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Randia esculenta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
