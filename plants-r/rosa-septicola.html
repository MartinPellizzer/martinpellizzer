Rosa septicola

Rosa septicola, commonly known as the swamp rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to wetland and marshy areas in North America, particularly in the eastern United States. The plant typically grows in waterlogged soils and has dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and it produces small, red hips that contain seeds.

Rosa septicola plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
