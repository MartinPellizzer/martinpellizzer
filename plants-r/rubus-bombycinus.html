Rubus bombycinus

Rubus bombycinus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of bramble in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and disturbed soils.

Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.
