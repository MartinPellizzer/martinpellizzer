Rhododendron vaseyi var. album

Rhododendron vaseyi var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white or pale pink blooms. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers are usually borne in clustered inflorescences and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its beauty and ecological benefits.
