Rubus hirtus var. lengyelii

Rubus hirtus var. lengyelii, commonly known as the Hungarian bramble, is a variant of the common bramble species.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its adaptability and ecological significance.
