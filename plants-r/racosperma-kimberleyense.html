Racosperma kimberleyense

Racosperma kimberleyense, commonly known as Kimberley acacia, is a flowering plant native to the Kimberley region of Western Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to the arid and semi-arid environments of its habitat. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.
