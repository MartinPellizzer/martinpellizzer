Rubus × piletopus

Rubus × piletopus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits, which consist of tiny drupelets. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces flowers that range from white to pink.

The fruit is edible, often used in jams, pies, and other culinary applications.
