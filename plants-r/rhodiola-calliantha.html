Rhodiola calliantha

Rhodiola calliantha, commonly known as the alpine rose or golden root, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant is characterized by its compact growth, with fleshy, dark green leaves and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Rhodiola calliantha is known for its ability to withstand extreme cold and low oxygen levels, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to enhance stamina and reduce fatigue.
