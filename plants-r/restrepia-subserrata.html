Restrepia subserrata

Restrepia subserrata, commonly known as the serrated restrepia, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. It thrives in cool, shaded environments with high humidity, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it is considered a challenging but rewarding orchid for experienced growers.
