Rosa tomentosa var. lamotteana

Rosa tomentosa var. lamotteana, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white fuzzy foliage and thorny stems. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for rocky or arid garden environments.
