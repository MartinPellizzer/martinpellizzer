Rorippa fluviatilis

Rorippa fluviatilis, commonly known as the watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a semi-aquatic species that typically grows in shallow water or damp soil along rivers, streams, and ponds. The plant has small, alternate leaves that are usually submerged or floating, and it produces tiny white or yellow flowers. Its roots are fibrous, and it spreads through both sexual reproduction via seeds and vegetative propagation.

Rorippa fluviatilis is known for its peppery flavor and is often used in salads or as a garnish.
