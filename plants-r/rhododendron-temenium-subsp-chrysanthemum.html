Rhododendron temenium subsp. chrysanthemum

Rhododendron temenium subsp.

chrysanthemum, commonly known as the golden rhododendron, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It features dense, dark green foliage and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the spring.

The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in shaded, moist forest environments. Its botanical features include hairy stems and small, leathery leaves.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
