Racosperma heteroclitum subsp. validum

Racosperma heteroclitum subsp.

validum, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to northern Australia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slender stems and smaller leaflets compared to other members of the species.
