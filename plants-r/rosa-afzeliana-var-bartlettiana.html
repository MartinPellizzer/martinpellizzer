Rosa afzeliana var. bartlettiana

Rosa afzeliana var. bartlettiana, commonly known as the Bartlett's rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and thornless stems. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double to double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
