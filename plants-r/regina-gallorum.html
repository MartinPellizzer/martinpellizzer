Regina gallorum

Regina gallorum, commonly known as the Queen of the Galleries, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that display a range of colors from deep purple to vibrant red. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique growth pattern, with leaves arranged in a spiral formation, makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Regina gallorum is considered a conservation concern.
