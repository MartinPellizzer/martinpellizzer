Rubroshorea almon

Rubroshorea almon, commonly known as the crimson shorea, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its thick, reddish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils along riverbanks and coastal areas.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.
