Ranunculus venustus

Ranunculus venustus, commonly known as the pretty buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with simple, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
