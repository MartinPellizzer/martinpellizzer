Rubus asperidens

Rubus asperidens, commonly known as the rough bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically serrated and hairy. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits called drupes, which are usually red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
