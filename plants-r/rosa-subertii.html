Rosa subertii

Rosa subertii, commonly known as the Sierra Rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets, and its stems are often armed with thorns.

Rosa subertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
