Rungia philippinensis

Rungia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine shrub or Philippine rungia, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rungia philippinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
