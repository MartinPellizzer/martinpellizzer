Rubus verticalis

Rubus verticalis, commonly known as the erect blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that ripen to a dark purple or black color, typically in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its roots system is fibrous and extensive.

Rubus verticalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
