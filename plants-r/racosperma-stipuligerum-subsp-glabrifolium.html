Racosperma stipuligerum subsp. glabrifolium

Racosperma stipuligerum subsp.

glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with a stipule at the base of each leaflet.

The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, typical of the genus Racosperma. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments.

The subspecies glabrifolium is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaflets, which set it apart from other members of the species.
