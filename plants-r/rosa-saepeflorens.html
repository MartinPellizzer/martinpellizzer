Rosa saepeflorens

Rosa saepeflorens, commonly known as the frequent-flowering rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom repeatedly throughout the growing season. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves and thorny stems typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, Rosa saepeflorens is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
