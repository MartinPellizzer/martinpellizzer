Rumex pulcher var. oligodonta

Rumex pulcher var. oligodonta, commonly known as the purple-stemmed sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, reddish-purple stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on terminal panicles. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
