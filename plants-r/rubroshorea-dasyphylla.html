Rubroshorea dasyphylla

Rubroshorea dasyphylla, commonly known as the redshore flameleaf, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant red and orange foliage.

It belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia. The plant features broad, leathery leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins that enhance its visual appeal. It produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
