Ranunculus fallax subsp. arvidii

Ranunculus fallax subsp.

arvidii, commonly known as the Arctic buttercup, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and deeply lobed leaves, which are adapted to cold climates.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a short-lived perennial with a compact growth habit.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
