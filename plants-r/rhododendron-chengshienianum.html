Rhododendron chengshienianum

Rhododendron chengshienianum, commonly known as the Chengshienian rhododendron, is a species native to China, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in the spring. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for acidic, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
