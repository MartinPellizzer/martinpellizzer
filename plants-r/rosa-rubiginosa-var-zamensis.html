Rosa rubiginosa var. zamensis

Rosa rubiginosa var. zamensis, commonly known as the Zambezi rose, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces oval, yellowish fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
