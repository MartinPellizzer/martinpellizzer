Ranunculus eschscholtzii

Ranunculus eschscholtzii, commonly known as the Eschscholtz's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to California.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to nine petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for studying native plant ecology in the western United States.
