Rauvolfia nitida

Rauvolfia nitida, commonly known as Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and have a distinctive yellowish-green hue.

Rauvolfia nitida is notable for containing alkaloids, such as reserpine, which have historically been used in medicinal applications.
