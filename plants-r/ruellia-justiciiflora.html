Ruellia justiciiflora

Ruellia justiciiflora, commonly known as the Mexican bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in whorls along the stem, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Ruellia justiciiflora prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
