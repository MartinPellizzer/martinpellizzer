Rubus bifrons var. duriminius

Rubus bifrons var. duriminius, commonly known as the Sierra raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or yellow in color. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, trifoliate leaves, and small white flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
