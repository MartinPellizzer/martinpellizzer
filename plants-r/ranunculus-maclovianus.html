Ranunculus maclovianus

Ranunculus maclovianus, commonly known as the Maclovian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has palmate leaves with lobes that are either entire or slightly toothed. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus maclovianus prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.
