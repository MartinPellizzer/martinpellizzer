Raphanis magna

Raphanis magna, commonly known as the giant radish, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and broccoli. The plant features large, fleshy roots that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, making it a notable species for its size. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it produces small, yellow flowers in clusters.

Raphanis magna is often cultivated for its edible roots, though it is less common in modern agriculture compared to other radish varieties.
