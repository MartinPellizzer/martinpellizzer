Rhodanthemum gayanum subsp. gayanum

Rhodanthemum gayanum subsp.

gayanum, commonly known as the Sri Lankan rhodanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sri Lanka. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
