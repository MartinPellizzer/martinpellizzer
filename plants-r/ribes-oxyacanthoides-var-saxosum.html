Ribes oxyacanthoides var. saxosum

Ribes oxyacanthoides var. saxosum, commonly known as the alpine gooseberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, red berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold temperatures.
