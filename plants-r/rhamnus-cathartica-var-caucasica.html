Rhamnus cathartica var. caucasica

Rhamnus cathartica var. caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
