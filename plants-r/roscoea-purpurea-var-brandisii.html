Roscoea purpurea var. brandisii

Roscoea purpurea var. brandisii, commonly known as the Brandis' rose ginseng, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a rhizomatous perennial with tall, upright stems that bear large, showy, violet-pink flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its lush, tropical appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
