Rubus chalaranthus

Rubus chalaranthus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a species of brambles native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature, though they may turn black depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its edible berries in some regions.
