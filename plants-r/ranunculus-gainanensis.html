Ranunculus gainanensis

Ranunculus gainanensis, commonly known as the Gainan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features simple, palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
