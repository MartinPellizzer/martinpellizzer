Ranunculus vertumnus

Ranunculus vertumnus, commonly known as the winter ranunculus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowers are showy, with five to eight petals that range in color from white to pink and yellow.

Ranunculus vertumnus is known for its ability to bloom in late winter or early spring, making it an early indicator of seasonal change.
