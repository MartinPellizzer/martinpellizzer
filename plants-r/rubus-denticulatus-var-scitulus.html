Rubus denticulatus var. scitulus

Rubus denticulatus var. scitulus, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

The species is valued for its edible fruit and its role in local ecosystems.
