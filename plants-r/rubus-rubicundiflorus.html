Rubus rubicundiflorus

Rubus rubicundiflorus, commonly known as the crimson-flowered bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a aggregate berry, typically red and edible, though often tart.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
