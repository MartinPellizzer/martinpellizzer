Rhododendron aiolosalpinx

Rhododendron aiolosalpinx, commonly known as the Aeolian or Greek rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Greece and the Balkans.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a slight pubescence. Its flowers are showy, with five to seven petals, ranging in color from pink to red, and are borne in clusters.

Rhododendron aiolosalpinx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
