Rhus lineatifolia

Rhus lineatifolia, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

Rhus lineatifolia is also noted for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
