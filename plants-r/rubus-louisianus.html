Rubus louisianus

Rubus louisianus, commonly known as the Louisiana blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark red to black berries that ripen in the summer. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, achenes, which are typically consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a source of food for wildlife.
