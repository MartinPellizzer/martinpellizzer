Rhus venulosa

Rhus venulosa, commonly known as smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with 11 to 31 leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in upright clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are a common source of berries for birds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including dry, rocky areas.

Rhus venulosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
