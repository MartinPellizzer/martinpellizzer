Ranunculus auricomus subsp. brachyschides

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

brachyschides, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with yellow centers and white to pale yellow petals.

The leaves are typically divided into three parts, with a distinctive narrowing at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
