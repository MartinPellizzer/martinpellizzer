Rutidea fuscescens subsp. fuscescens

Rutidea fuscescens subsp.

fuscescens, commonly known as the blackwood or black thorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
