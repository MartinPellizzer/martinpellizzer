Rosa ciliata var. glaucopsis

Rosa ciliata var. glaucopsis, commonly known as the glaucous bristly rose, is a compact shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, hairy leaves that are typically covered with fine, white to grayish hairs, giving them a distinctive glaucous appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in early summer. Its stems are usually covered with bristles, contributing to its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
