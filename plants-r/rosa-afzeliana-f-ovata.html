Rosa afzeliana f. ovata

Rosa afzeliana f. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the upper surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Asia, particularly China and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and fragrance.
