Rosa lyalliana

Rosa lyalliana, commonly known as the Lyall's rose, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of New Zealand's native flora, often found in coastal and forested areas.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
