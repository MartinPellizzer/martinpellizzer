Rubus × consimilis

Rubus × consimilis, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rubus genus in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas where it forms dense thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and used for erosion control in certain regions.
