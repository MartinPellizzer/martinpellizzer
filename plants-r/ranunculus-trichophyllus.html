Ranunculus trichophyllus

Ranunculus trichophyllus, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a hairy texture that gives it its common name.

The hairy buttercup is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
