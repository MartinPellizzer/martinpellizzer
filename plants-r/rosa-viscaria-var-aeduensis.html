Rosa viscaria var. aeduensis

Rosa viscaria var. aeduensis, commonly known as the Edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the high alpine regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white bracts that surround small yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 1,500 meters. It is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate, making it a rare and protected species.

Due to its beauty and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
