Rytigynia claviflora

Rytigynia claviflora, commonly known as the Brazilian whiteflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Rytigyniaceae and is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Rytigynia claviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
