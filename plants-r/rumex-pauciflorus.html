Rumex pauciflorus

Rumex pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats and sorrels. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in moist, acidic soils.
