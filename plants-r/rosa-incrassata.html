Rosa incrassata

Rosa incrassata, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a species native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa incrassata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
