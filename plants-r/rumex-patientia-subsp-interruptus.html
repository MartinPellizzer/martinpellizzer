Rumex patientia subsp. interruptus

Rumex patientia subsp.

interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged on the underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rumex genus.
