Rosa uriensis var. biserrata

Rosa uriensis var. biserrata, commonly known as the serrated shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are compound, typically with five to seven leaflets, and are often serrated along the edges. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also noted for its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
