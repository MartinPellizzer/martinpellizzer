Racosperma allenianum

Racosperma allenianum, commonly known as the Allen's wattle, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry sclerophyll forests.

Racosperma allenianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
