Ranunculus allegheniensis

Ranunculus allegheniensis, commonly known as the Allegheny buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a hairy, upright stem. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands and streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
