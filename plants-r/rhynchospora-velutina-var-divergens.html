Rhynchospora velutina var. divergens

Rhynchospora velutina var. divergens, commonly known as the hairy sand-plain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, slightly hairy leaves. It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded sandy soils, often found in marshes and wetlands.

The inflorescence consists of a spikelike cluster of small, three-lobed flowers that bloom in late summer.
