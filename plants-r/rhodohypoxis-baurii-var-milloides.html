Rhodohypoxis baurii var. milloides

Rhodohypoxis baurii var. milloides, commonly known as the golden bulbous plant, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and bulbous underground stems. The plant typically grows in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
