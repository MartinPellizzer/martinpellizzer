Rosa clavigera

Rosa clavigera, commonly known as the swordleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. Its botanical features include stipules that are often modified into small, leaf-like structures.

Rosa clavigera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
