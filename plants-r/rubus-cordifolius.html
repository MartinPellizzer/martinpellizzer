Rubus cordifolius

Rubus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which have heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Rubus cordifolius is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with thorny stems and clusters of white flowers in the spring.
