Ribes lacustre var. parvulum

Ribes lacustre var. parvulum, commonly known as the small-flowered gooseberry, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are clustered in cymes and are typically white or pale yellow, producing small, edible berries that ripen in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
