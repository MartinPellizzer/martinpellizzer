Rumex punjabensis

Rumex punjabensis, commonly known as the Punjab sorrel, is a perennial herb native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its rhombic leaves with reddish veins. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in grasslands and along water bodies. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional remedies.
