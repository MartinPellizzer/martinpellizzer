Rubus schleicheri microgene parvipetalus

Rubus schleicheri microgene parvipetalus, commonly known as the dwarf raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
