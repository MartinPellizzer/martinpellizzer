Roemeria dodecandra

Roemeria dodecandra, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, winged fruits that disperse its seeds by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Roemeria dodecandra is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
