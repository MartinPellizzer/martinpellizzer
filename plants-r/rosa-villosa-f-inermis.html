Rosa villosa f. inermis

Rosa villosa f. inermis, commonly known as the soft-hairy rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The leaves are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. It is native to regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant produces hips, which are the fruiting bodies containing seeds.
