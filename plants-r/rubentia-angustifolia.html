Rubentia angustifolia

Rubentia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rubentia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Rubentia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
