Rubus schlechtendalii subsp. macrophylloides

Rubus schlechtendalii subsp.

macrophylloides, commonly known as the large-leaf blackberry, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside.

The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red to black achenes surrounded by a fleshy, white receptacle. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and its role in ecological systems.
