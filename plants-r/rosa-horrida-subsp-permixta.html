Rosa horrida subsp. permixta

Rosa horrida subsp.

permixta, commonly known as the rough rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include stipules that are often fused to the stem and bracts that are glandular and hairy.

It is adapted to harsh, rocky environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
