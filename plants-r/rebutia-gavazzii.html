Rebutia gavazzii

Rebutia gavazzii, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, colorful flowers. The plant has a short, thick stem with areoles that bear spines and flowers. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

Rebutia gavazzii is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
