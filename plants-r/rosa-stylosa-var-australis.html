Rosa stylosa var. australis

Rosa stylosa var. australis, commonly known as the Australian sweet briar, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in coastal and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rosa genus, valued for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
