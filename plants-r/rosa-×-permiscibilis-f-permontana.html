Rosa × permiscibilis f. permontana

Rosa × permiscibilis f. permontana, commonly known as the climbing rose, is a hybrid species characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its thorned stems.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its large, fragrant flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins and a strong, woody stem structure that supports its climbing nature.
