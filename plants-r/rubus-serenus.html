Rubus serenus

Rubus serenus, commonly known as the serene raspberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of shrubs commonly referred to as blackberries, raspberries, and dewberries. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow in color, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in stinging hairs.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
