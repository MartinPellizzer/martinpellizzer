Rosa urbica var. baresanica

Rosa urbica var. baresanica, commonly known as the urban rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and urban environments, showing adaptability to human-altered landscapes. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets, and it produces red, hairy hips that contain seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
