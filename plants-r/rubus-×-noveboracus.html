Rubus × noveboracus

Rubus × noveboracus, commonly known as the New York raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit, which resembles a raspberry, is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a popular choice for jams and desserts.
