Racosperma maconochieanum

Racosperma maconochieanum, commonly known as the Macdonald's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters.

Racosperma maconochieanum is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
