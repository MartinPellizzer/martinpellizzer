Rhododendron odoratum

Rhododendron odoratum, commonly known as the sweet rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its flowers range in color from white to deep pink, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Rhododendron odoratum is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
