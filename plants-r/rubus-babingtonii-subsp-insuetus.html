Rubus babingtonii subsp. insuetus

Rubus babingtonii subsp.

insuetus, commonly known as the insuetus blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It produces small, dark berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant has thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Rubus genus.
