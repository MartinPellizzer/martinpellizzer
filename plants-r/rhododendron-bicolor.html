Rhododendron bicolor

Rhododendron bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its strikingly different flower colors, with the upper part of the flower being white and the lower part a vibrant pink or red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Rhododendron bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical gardens.
