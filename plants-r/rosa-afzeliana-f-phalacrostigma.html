Rosa afzeliana f. phalacrostigma

Rosa afzeliana f. phalacrostigma, commonly known as the dark-stemmed rose, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems that are often dark in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
