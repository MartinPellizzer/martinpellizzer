Rosa wittmannii

Rosa wittmannii, commonly known as the Wittmann's rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Argentina, Bolivia, and Chile. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

Rosa wittmannii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
