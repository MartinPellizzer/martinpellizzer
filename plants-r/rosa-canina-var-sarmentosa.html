Rosa canina var. sarmentosa

Rosa canina var. sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping dogrose, is a variant of the common rose that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a low-growing shrub with thorny stems that often trail along the ground, forming dense mats. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are typically oval and serrated. The flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Rosaceae family.

This variety is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its resilience and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
