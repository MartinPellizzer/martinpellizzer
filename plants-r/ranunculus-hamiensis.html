Ranunculus hamiensis

Ranunculus hamiensis, commonly known as the Hami buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc and numerous petal-like sepals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Ranunculus hamiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, daisy-like blooms.
