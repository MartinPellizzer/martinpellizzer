Rubus timbal-lagravei subsp. gracilior

Rubus timbal-lagravei subsp.

gracilior, commonly known as the slender blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
