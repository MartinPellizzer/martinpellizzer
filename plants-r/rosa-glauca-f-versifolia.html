Rosa glauca f. versifolia

Rosa glauca f. versifolia, commonly known as the blue rose with variegated foliage, is a botanical variant of the Rosa glauca species.

It is characterized by its striking blue-pink flowers and uniquely mottled, variegated leaves. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in cooler climates. Its foliage exhibits a mix of green, white, and yellow tones, adding visual interest.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
