Rhipsalis grandiflora

Rhipsalis grandiflora, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a unique epiphytic cactus native to Brazil.

It features long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often with a braided or twisted appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming during the winter months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular houseplant worldwide.
