Rosa mygindii

Rosa mygindii, commonly known as the Mygindii rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Tajikistan and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, red or orange fruits known as hips.

Rosa mygindii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
