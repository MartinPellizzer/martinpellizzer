Ribes indecorum

Ribes indecorum, commonly known as the purple-flowered gooseberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of flowering plants and some edible berries. The plant is characterized by its clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, edible berries in the fall.

Ribes indecorum is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.
