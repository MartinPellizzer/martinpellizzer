Ranunculus auricomus subsp. atrovirens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

atrovirens, commonly known as the black-veined buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features bright yellow flowers with deeply lobed petals and a distinctive blackish-green vein pattern.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
