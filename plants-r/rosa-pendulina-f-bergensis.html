Rosa pendulina f. bergensis

Rosa pendulina f. bergensis, commonly known as the Bergens rose, is a botanical variant of the pendulous rose.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to red petals. Native to the mountainous regions of Norway, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
