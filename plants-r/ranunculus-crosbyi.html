Ranunculus crosbyi

Ranunculus crosbyi, commonly known as Crosby's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant features delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
