Ranunculus porrectus

Ranunculus porrectus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture, and it spreads via rhizomes, forming dense mats.

Ranunculus porrectus is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
