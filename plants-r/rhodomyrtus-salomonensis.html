Rhodomyrtus salomonensis

Rhodomyrtus salomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands rose myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Solomon Islands.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. The plant has oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
