Rhododendron bauhiniiflorum

Rhododendron bauhiniiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a flowering shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers acidic, well-drained soils. It blooms in spring, producing clusters of pink to reddish flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
