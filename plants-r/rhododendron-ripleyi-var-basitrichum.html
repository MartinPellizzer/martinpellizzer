Rhododendron ripleyi var. basitrichum

Rhododendron ripleyi var. basitrichum, commonly known as the Mexican velvetleaf rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the highlands of Mexico.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in clusters. The plant is characterized by its short, stout stems and thick, leathery leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements in suitable climates.
