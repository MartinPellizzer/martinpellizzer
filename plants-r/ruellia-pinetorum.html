Ruellia pinetorum

Ruellia pinetorum, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. The plant produces showy blue to violet flowers that bloom from spring to summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
