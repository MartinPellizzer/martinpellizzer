Rumex bequaertii

Rumex bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and it produces elongated, ribbed fruits.

Rumex bequaertii is characterized by its robust rhizomatous growth and is often found in disturbed or wetland environments.
