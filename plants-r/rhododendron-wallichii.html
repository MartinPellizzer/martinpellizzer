Rhododendron wallichii

Rhododendron wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features broad, leathery leaves and produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Rhododendron wallichii is also known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
