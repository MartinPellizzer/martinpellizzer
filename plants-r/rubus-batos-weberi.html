Rubus batos-weberi

Rubus batos-weberi, commonly known as the Weber's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
