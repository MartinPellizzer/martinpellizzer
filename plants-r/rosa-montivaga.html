Rosa montivaga

Rosa montivaga, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated and hairy.

Rosa montivaga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
