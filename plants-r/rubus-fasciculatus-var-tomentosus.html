Rubus fasciculatus var. tomentosus

Rubus fasciculatus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and local biodiversity.
