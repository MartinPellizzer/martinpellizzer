Rhipsalis hookeriana

Rhipsalis hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's mistletoe cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing cactus that features long, slender stems covered in small, white spines. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are modified into short, pointed structures, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Rhipsalis hookeriana is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
