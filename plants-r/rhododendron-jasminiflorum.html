Rhododendron jasminiflorum

Rhododendron jasminiflorum, commonly known as the jasmine rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features large, showy flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with a strong, sweet fragrance reminiscent of jasmine. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its floral display. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
