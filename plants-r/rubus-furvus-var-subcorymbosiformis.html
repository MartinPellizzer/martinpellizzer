Rubus furvus var. subcorymbosiformis

Rubus furvus var. subcorymbosiformis, commonly known as the subcorymbous blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a deep black color, typically in late summer. Its flowers are white and clustered in corymbs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variant is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, thriving in partial shade.
