Rollandia kaalae

Rollandia kaalae, commonly known as the Kauaʻi ironwood, is a rare and endemic tree species native to the island of Kauaʻi in Hawaii.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rugged, twisted trunk, which provides habitat for various native birds and insects. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. Rollandia kaalae thrives in moist, shaded environments on rocky slopes and cliffs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is classified as endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
