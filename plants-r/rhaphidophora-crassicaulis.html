Rhaphidophora crassicaulis

Rhaphidophora crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its rhizomatous root system is thick and fleshy, which contributes to its common name.

Rhaphidophora crassicaulis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
