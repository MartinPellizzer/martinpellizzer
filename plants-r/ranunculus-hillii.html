Ranunculus hillii

Ranunculus hillii, commonly known as the Armenian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.

Ranunculus hillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
