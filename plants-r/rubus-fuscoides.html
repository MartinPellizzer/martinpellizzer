Rubus fuscoides

Rubus fuscoides, commonly known as the blackberry shrub, is a species of bramble native to parts of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, thickets, and moist soils, preferring partial to full shade. Its dark purple to black berries are edible when ripe and are often used in jams and pies.

The plant plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, providing food for birds and small mammals.
