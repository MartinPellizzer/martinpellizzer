Rubus × oplothyrsoides

Rubus × oplothyrsoides, commonly known as the blackberry-boysenberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological and genetic traits from its parent species.
