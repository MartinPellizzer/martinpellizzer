Rosa coriifolia subsp. cinerea

Rosa coriifolia subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its grayish-white, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has a compact, multi-stemmed growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. Its foliage turns a striking silver-gray in autumn, adding visual interest to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
