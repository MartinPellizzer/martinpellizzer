Ranunculus fluitans subsp. flabellifolius

Ranunculus fluitans subsp.

flabellifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved water buttercup, is a aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, with elongated stems that often float on the water surface.

The plant features palmately divided leaves that are broad and lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are bright yellow with five petals and are borne on long, slender stalks.

It plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
