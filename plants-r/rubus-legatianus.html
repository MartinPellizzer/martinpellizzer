Rubus legatianus

Rubus legatianus, commonly known as the Legatian blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is closely related to other members of the Rubus genus, which includes numerous species of raspberries and blackberries.
