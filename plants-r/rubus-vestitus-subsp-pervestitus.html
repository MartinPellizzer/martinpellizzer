Rubus vestitus subsp. pervestitus

Rubus vestitus subsp.

pervestitus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
