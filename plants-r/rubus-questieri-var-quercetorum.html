Rubus questieri var. quercetorum

Rubus questieri var. quercetorum, commonly known as the Quercetum raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
