Rapuntium cordatum

Rapuntium cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf rapunculus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its stems are hairy and often have a slightly bitter taste.

Rapuntium cordatum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
