Ranunculus dissectus subsp. napellifolius

Ranunculus dissectus subsp.

napellifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant has finely dissected, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
