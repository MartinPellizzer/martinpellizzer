Rumex bucephalophorus subsp. graecus

Rumex bucephalophorus subsp.

graecus, commonly known as the Greek dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems that are covered with rough, hairy texture.

The leaves are large, lance-shaped, and have a wavy margin, often with a reddish or purplish tinge on the underside. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This plant is typically found in moist soils along riverbanks, wet meadows, and other damp habitats.
