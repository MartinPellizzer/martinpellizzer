Rhaphidophora lacera

Rhaphidophora lacera, commonly known as the laceleaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fenestrated, and deeply lobed leaves that give it a unique, almost lace-like appearance. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its distinctive inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Rhaphidophora lacera thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
