Rubus meyeri

Rubus meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's blackberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rubus genus in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Rubus meyeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in garden settings.
