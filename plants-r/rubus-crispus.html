Rubus crispus

Rubus crispus, commonly known as sawtooth raspberries, is a shrub native to North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts. The plant has thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant is an important food source for various wildlife species.
