Ranunculus exiguus

Ranunculus exiguus, commonly known as the small water crowfoot, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it typically grows in moist, calcareous soils near water bodies. The plant's flowers are usually yellow or white, and they bloom from late spring to early summer.

Ranunculus exiguus is often found in wet meadows, streambanks, and marshy areas, where it thrives in partially shaded to full sunlight conditions.
