Ruellia aciculata

Ruellia aciculata, commonly known as the needleleaf ruellia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often clustered in loose inflorescences. Ruellia aciculata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a variety of growing conditions.
