Rubus × perspurius

Rubus × perspurius, commonly known as the European blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

This species is valued for its fruit, which is used in culinary and commercial applications.
