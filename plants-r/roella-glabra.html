Roella glabra

Roella glabra, commonly known as the smooth roella, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.
