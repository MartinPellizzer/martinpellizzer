Ramsdenia

Ramsdenia, commonly known as the "Australian sandalwood," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and includes species such as *Ramsdenia arguta* and *Ramsdenia ramosa*. These plants are characterized by their aromatic foliage and small, white or pink flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a pleasant, resinous scent.

Ramsdenia species are often found in sandy or coastal soils and are valued for their ornamental and aromatic properties.
