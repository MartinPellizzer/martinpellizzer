Rubus murinus

Rubus murinus, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to saline and windy coastal environments.

Rubus murinus plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
