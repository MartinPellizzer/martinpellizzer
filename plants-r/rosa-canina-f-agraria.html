Rosa canina f. agraria

Rosa canina f. agraria, commonly known as the agrarian rose, is a subspecies of the dog rose, characterized by its distinctive, clustered flowers and thorny stems.

It is native to southern Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, red-orange fruits known as hips, which are rich in vitamin C and used in herbal medicine. Its foliage is dark green and oval-shaped, with serrated edges.

This subspecies is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, where it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
