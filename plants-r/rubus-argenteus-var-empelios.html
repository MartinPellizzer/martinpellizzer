Rubus argenteus var. empelios

Rubus argenteus var. empelios, commonly known as silverleaf blackberry.

It is a species of brambles native to the western United States, characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically not eaten by humans but are used by wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its growth habit is shrub-like, with thorny stems and a dense foliage structure.
