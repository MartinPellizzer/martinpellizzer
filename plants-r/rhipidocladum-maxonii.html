Rhipidocladum maxonii

Rhipidocladum maxonii, commonly known as the desert holly, is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of Africa.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, spiny leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, adding a splash of color to the landscape. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Rhipidocladum maxonii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
