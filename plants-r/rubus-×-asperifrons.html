Rubus × asperifrons

Rubus × asperifrons, commonly known as the rough-stemmed blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces dark purple to black berries.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
