Rubus discolor pubescens

Rubus discolor pubescens, commonly known as the hairy everlet raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia, characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and thorned stems, making it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
