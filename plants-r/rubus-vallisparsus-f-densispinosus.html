Rubus vallisparsus f. densispinosus

Rubus vallisparsus f. densispinosus, commonly known as the dense-spined blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its dense, thorny growth.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of southern Africa. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. Its foliage is usually serrated, and the stems are covered in stiff, prickly hairs.

This form is noted for its vigorous growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
