Rudbeckia triloba var. triloba

Rudbeckia triloba var. triloba, commonly known as the black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a dark brown central disk, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its showy, long-lasting blooms.
