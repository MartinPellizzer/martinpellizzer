Rubus lobophyllus

Rubus lobophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
