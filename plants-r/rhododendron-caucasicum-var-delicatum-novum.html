Rhododendron caucasicum var. delicatum-novum

Rhododendron caucasicum var. delicatum-novum, commonly known as the Caucasian Delicate Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
