Rosa × dumetorum var. vodanensis

Rosa × dumetorum var. vodanensis, commonly known as the Vodanensian shrub rose, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, with dark green, oval-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. Its botanical features include a woody stem structure and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
