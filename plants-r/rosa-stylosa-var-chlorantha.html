Rosa stylosa var. chlorantha

Rosa stylosa var. chlorantha, commonly known as the green-stem rose, is a variant of the stylosa rose species characterized by its distinctive greenish stems.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China and Vietnam, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
