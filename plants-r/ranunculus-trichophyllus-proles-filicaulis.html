Ranunculus trichophyllus proles filicaulis

Ranunculus trichophyllus proles filicaulis, commonly known as the filicaulis buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves with a hairy underside, giving it the species name *trichophyllus*. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
