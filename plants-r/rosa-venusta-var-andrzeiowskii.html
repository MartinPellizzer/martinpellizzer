Rosa venusta var. andrzeiowskii

Rosa venusta var. andrzeiowskii, commonly known as the Andrzeiowskii rose, is a cultivar of the Rosa venusta species, native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticultural collections.
