Rubus × pilocarpifolius

Rubus × pilocarpifolius, commonly known as the blackberry-boysenberry hybrid, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically larger and more flavorful than those of its parent species. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique genetic makeup, it exhibits a range of morphological and biochemical traits that make it a subject of interest in horticultural research.
