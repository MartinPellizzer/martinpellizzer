Rosa molliglaucigera

Rosa molliglaucigera, commonly known as the soft bluish-gray rose, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy, bluish-gray stems and leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Rosa molliglaucigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
