Rosa santaecrucis

Rosa santaecrucis, commonly known as the Holy Cross rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and have cultural significance in local communities.

Rosa santaecrucis is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical association with religious symbolism.
