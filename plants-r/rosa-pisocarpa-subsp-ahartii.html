Rosa pisocarpa subsp. ahartii

Rosa pisocarpa subsp.

ahartii, commonly known as the Ahartii rose, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers.

The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible but typically consumed raw. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
