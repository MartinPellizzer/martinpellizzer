Rubus viridifrons

Rubus viridifrons, commonly known as the green-fronted blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically dark purple when ripe. These berries are often used in jams and desserts due to their sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant thrives in moist, wooded areas and is an important food source for various wildlife species.
