Ricinus morifolius

Ricinus morifolius, commonly known as the Castor oil plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive spiny capsules that contain toxic seeds. The plant is part of the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for producing castor oil, which has various industrial and medicinal uses. Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms depending on the climate.
