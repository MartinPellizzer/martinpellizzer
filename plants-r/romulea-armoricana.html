Romulea armoricana

Romulea armoricana, commonly known as the Armenian crown.

It is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are grass-like and form a dense rosette around the bulb.

Romulea armoricana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens.
