Ranunculus angustiscutatus

Ranunculus angustiscutatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often have a distinctive hairy texture.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
