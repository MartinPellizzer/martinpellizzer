Ranunculus amplexicaulis var. plantagineus

Ranunculus amplexicaulis var. plantagineus, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with basal leaves that wrap around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petal-like sepals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
