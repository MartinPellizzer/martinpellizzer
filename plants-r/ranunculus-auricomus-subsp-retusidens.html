Ranunculus auricomus subsp. retusidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

retusidens, commonly known as the broadleaf buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers with 5 to 10 petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are usually hairy.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae, known for its showy flowers and often toxic compounds.
