Rubus tomentosus f. glabratus

Rubus tomentosus f. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to various regions of the United States. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically smaller than those of the common blackberry. Its foliage is usually dense and has a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

The flowers are white and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
