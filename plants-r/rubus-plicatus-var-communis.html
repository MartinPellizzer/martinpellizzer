Rubus plicatus var. communis

Rubus plicatus var. communis, commonly known as the common blackberry.

It is a shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its fruit and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
