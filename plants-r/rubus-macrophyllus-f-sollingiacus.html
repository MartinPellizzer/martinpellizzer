Rubus macrophyllus f. sollingiacus

Rubus macrophyllus f. sollingiacus, commonly known as the Solling mountain raspberry, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and thorny stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces clusters of small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
