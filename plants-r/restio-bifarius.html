Restio bifarius

Restio bifarius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Restio bifarius is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
