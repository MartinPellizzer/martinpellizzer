Rhododendron suaveolens f. roseum

Rhododendron suaveolens f. roseum, commonly known as the Roseum Rhododendron, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features dense, leathery foliage that is dark green and glossy, with a fragrant, bell-shaped flower cluster. The flowers are typically pink to rose-purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
