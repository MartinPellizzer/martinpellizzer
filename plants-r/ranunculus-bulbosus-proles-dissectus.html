Ranunculus bulbosus proles dissectus

Ranunculus bulbosus proles dissectus, commonly known as the dissected buttercup, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, lobed leaves and showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

The species is noted for its finely divided foliage, which gives it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance.
