Ranunculus kangmaensis

Ranunculus kangmaensis, commonly known as the Kangmaensis buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy and often toxic species. The plant is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and brightly colored, showy flowers that typically range from yellow to red. It thrives in moist, cool environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
