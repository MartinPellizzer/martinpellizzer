Ritaia

Ritaia, commonly known as the red sanders, is a rare and valuable tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly prized for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Ritaia grows slowly and is often found in dry, rocky soils, making it a challenging species to cultivate.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is now critically endangered and protected under international conservation laws.
