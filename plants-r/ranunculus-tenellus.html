Ranunculus tenellus

Ranunculus tenellus, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its beauty and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
