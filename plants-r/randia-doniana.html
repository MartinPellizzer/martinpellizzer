Randia doniana

Randia doniana, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are often pink or red. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Randia doniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
