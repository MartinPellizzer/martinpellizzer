Rhodiola himalensis var. bouvieri

Rhodiola himalensis var. bouvieri, commonly known as the Himalayan rhodiola, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low oxygen levels.

Rhodiola himalensis var. bouvieri is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
