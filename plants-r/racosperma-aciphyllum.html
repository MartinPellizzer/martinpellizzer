Racosperma aciphyllum

Racosperma aciphyllum, commonly known as the leafless acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive appearance, featuring long, slender stems and the absence of leaves, which is an adaptation to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, attracting various pollinators. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Despite its lack of foliage, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals.
