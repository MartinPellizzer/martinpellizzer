Razumovia grandiflora

Razumovia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant razumovia, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant shades of pink and white. The plant has a woody stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade, making it a popular choice for botanical gardens.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
