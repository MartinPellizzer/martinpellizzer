Rosa afzeliana var. holmii

Rosa afzeliana var. holmii, commonly known as the Holm rose, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven elliptical leaflets, and the fruits are red, hairy rosehips that mature in autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
