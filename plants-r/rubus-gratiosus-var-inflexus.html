Rubus gratiosus var. inflexus

Rubus gratiosus var. inflexus, commonly known as the bent-stem blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often arching stems that grow close to the ground. The leaves are typically three-lobed with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, dark purple fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
