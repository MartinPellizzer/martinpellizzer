Rhododendron campanulatum

Rhododendron campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron campanulatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
