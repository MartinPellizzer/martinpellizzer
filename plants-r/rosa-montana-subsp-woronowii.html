Rosa montana subsp. woronowii

Rosa montana subsp.

woronowii, commonly known as the Siberian rose, is a shrub native to the cold regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to harsh climates and can thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate gardens.
