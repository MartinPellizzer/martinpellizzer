Rosa conyzoides

Rosa conyzoides, commonly known as the African rose or false rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring woody stems and alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. It is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

Rosa conyzoides is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in treating various ailments.
