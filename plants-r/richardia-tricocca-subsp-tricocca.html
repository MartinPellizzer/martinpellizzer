Richardia tricocca subsp. tricocca

Richardia tricocca subsp.

tricocca, commonly known as the three-lobed richardia, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves with three distinct lobes.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a terminal spike, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
