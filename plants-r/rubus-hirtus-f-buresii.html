Rubus hirtus f. buresii

Rubus hirtus f. buresii, commonly known as the rough blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically serrated and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its "hirtus" designation. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits and ornamental appeal in gardens.
