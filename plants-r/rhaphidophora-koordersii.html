Rhaphidophora koordersii

Rhaphidophora koordersii, commonly known as the Koorders' arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Rhaphidophora koordersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
