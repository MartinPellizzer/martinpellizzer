Rubus Subcanus Var. Supinus

Rubus subcanus var. supinus is a lesser-known species within the Rubus genus, commonly known as brambles or blackberries. This variant is characterized by its unique growth habit and distinctive floral and fruit morphology. Native to specific regions in the northern hemisphere, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. Despite its potential for cultivation, it remains underappreciated in both horticultural and ecological contexts. Its study offers insights into plant adaptation and biodiversity in understudied plant groups.
