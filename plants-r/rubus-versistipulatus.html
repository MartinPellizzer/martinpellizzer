Rubus versistipulatus

Rubus versistipulatus, commonly known as the Japanese bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are similar in appearance to raspberries but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
