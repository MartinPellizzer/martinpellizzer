Ranunculus claviformis

Ranunculus claviformis, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to nine petals and a distinctive, clustered arrangement of sepals. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
