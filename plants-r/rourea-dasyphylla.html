Rourea dasyphylla

Rourea dasyphylla, commonly known as the woolly rourea, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique foliage.
