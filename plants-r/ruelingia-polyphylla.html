Ruelingia polyphylla

Ruelingia polyphylla, commonly known as the Brazilian candlewood, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces fruit that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Ruelingia polyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
