Rubus calophyton

Rubus calophyton, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a species of brambles belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into bright red, hairy berries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Rubus calophyton thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
