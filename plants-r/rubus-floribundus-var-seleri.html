Rubus floribundus var. seleri

Rubus floribundus var. seleri, commonly known as the Seler's blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other bramble species.
