Rosa tomentosa var. pyrenaica

Rosa tomentosa var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean woolly rose, is a shrub native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its stems are covered in a thick layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
