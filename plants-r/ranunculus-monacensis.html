Ranunculus monacensis

Ranunculus monacensis, commonly known as the Monacan buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other buttercup species.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
