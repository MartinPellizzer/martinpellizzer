Rubus gremlii subsp. styriacus

Rubus gremlii subsp.

styriacus, commonly known as the Styrian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Alps and surrounding regions. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and mountainous areas.

The species is valued for its edible berries and is also of interest in ecological studies due to its role in local biodiversity.
