Rinorea congensis

Rinorea congensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf sterculia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Rinorea congensis typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
