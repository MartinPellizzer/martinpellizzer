Rubus plicatus var. nitidus

Rubus plicatus var. nitidus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, shiny berries that are typically larger and more flavorful than those of other blackberry varieties. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The flowers are white and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
