Rubus pruinosus var. warmingii

Rubus pruinosus var. warmingii, commonly known as the glossy raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, often reddish stems and leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are usually red or black when mature, containing multiple drupes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
