Rhododendron orbiculare subsp. maolingense

Rhododendron orbiculare subsp.

maolingense, commonly known as the Maoling rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and red.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
