Rosa tomentosa var. farinosa

Rosa tomentosa var. farinosa, commonly known as the farinose musk rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces hips, which are orange-red berries containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

This variety is particularly noted for its unique, musky scent and adaptability to various soil conditions.
