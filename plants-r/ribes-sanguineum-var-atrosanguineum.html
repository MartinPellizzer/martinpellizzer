Ribes sanguineum var. atrosanguineum

Ribes sanguineum var. atrosanguineum, commonly known as the dark red currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically harvested in late summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, often with a reddish or purple hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in culinary applications.
