Rubus hebecarpos var. cyclophyllus

Rubus hebecarpos var. cyclophyllus, commonly known as the red-berried blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark red fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant produces thorny stems and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by the development of its distinctive berries.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
