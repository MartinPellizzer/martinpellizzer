Ranunculus macrodon

Ranunculus macrodon, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow flowers with numerous petals that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, stout stem and basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for pollinators and its ornamental value in gardens.
