Rubus serpens subsp. angustifrons

Rubus serpens subsp.

angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically narrow and serrated.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark red or black color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in local ecosystems and for horticultural use.
