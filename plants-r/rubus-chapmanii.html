Rubus chapmanii

Rubus chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Botanically, it is closely related to other species in the Rubus genus, which includes many edible and ornamental berries.
