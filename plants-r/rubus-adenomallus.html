Rubus adenomallus

Rubus adenomallus, commonly known as the Florida blackberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along streams.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
