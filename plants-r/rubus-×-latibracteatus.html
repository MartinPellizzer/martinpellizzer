Rubus × latibracteatus

Rubus × latibracteatus, commonly known as the broad-bract raspberry, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, bright red drupes that are typically eaten by birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
