Rubus hirtus var. intermixtus

Rubus hirtus var. intermixtus, commonly known as the mixed-bristle blackberry, is a thorny shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has trifoliate leaves with hairy undersides. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and is often found in forest understories.

The variety intermixtus is distinguished by its mixed pubescence on the stems and leaves.
