Rubus discolor subsp. rusticanus

Rubus discolor subsp.

rusticanus, commonly known as the rusty blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs.

The plant produces small, usually red or yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries in the summer. Its foliage has a distinctive serrated edge and a slightly fuzzy texture.

This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas, where it thrives in partial shade.
