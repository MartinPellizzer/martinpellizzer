Ribes nevadense var. jasperae

Ribes nevadense var. jasperae, commonly known as the Sierra grape.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States, particularly found in high-elevation areas of California and Nevada. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, tart berry that ripens to a deep red color in the summer.

The species is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
