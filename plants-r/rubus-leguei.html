Rubus leguei

Rubus leguei, commonly known as the Guatemalan blackberry, is a species of bramble native to Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

Rubus leguei is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local agriculture.
