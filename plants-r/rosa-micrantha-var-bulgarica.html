Rosa micrantha var. bulgarica

Rosa micrantha var. bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers. The plant produces thorny stems and has dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
