Rosa communis subsp. glaucoformis

Rosa communis subsp.

glaucoformis, commonly known as the blue-green rose, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique coloration, it is a sought-after plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
