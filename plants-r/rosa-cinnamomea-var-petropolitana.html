Rosa cinnamomea var. petropolitana

Rosa cinnamomea var. petropolitana, commonly known as the Siberian rose or the Cinnamon rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, fragrant flowers that range from pink to deep red. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves and produces hips that are orange-red and covered in fine hairs. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in colder regions.
