Rubus reussii

Rubus reussii, commonly known as the California blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The botanical features of Rubus reussii make it an important plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
