Rubus ulmifolius f. genuinus

Rubus ulmifolius f. genuinus, commonly known as the common blackberry.

This subspecies is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the fruit is a dark purple to black berry. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedging and fruit production.
