Rhododendron planifolium

Rhododendron planifolium, commonly known as the flat-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a flat, horizontal manner. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
