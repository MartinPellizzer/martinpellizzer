Rubus obotriticus

Rubus obotriticus, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a species of bramble native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
