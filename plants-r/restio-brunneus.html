Restio brunneus

Restio brunneus, commonly known as the brown restio, is a perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes.

Restio brunneus is often found in coastal and sandy habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
