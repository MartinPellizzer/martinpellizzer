Rubus rosanthus var. eriocarpus

Rubus rosanthus var. eriocarpus, commonly known as the woolly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically edible, though they may have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

The botanical features of this variety make it both ornamental and useful in ecological settings.
