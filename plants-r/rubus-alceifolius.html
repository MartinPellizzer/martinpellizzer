Rubus alceifolius

Rubus alceifolius, commonly known as the alceifolius blackberry, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple or black when ripe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other blackberry and raspberry species.
