Ruttya tricolor

Ruttya tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly colorful flowers that display three distinct hues. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Ruttya tricolor is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
