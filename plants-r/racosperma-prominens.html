Racosperma prominens

Racosperma prominens, commonly known as the broad-leaved acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in spikes. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Racosperma prominens is used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
