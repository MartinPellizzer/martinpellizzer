Riedelia cordylinoides

Riedelia cordylinoides, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of Central America and northern South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant features showy, pentagonal flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Riedelia cordylinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
