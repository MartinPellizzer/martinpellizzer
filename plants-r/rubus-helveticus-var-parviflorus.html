Rubus helveticus var. parviflorus

Rubus helveticus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the alpine raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, pale pink flowers and prickly stems, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
