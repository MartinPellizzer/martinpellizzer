Rubus micans subsp. subsimilis

Rubus micans subsp.

subsimilis, commonly known as the silverleaf bramble, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, usually white or pale pink flowers.

The leaves are trifoliate, with a silvery-white underside that gives the plant its common name. It produces edible berries that are typically small and tart.

This subspecies thrives in woodland edges and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
