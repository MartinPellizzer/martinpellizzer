Reseda linifolia

Reseda linifolia, commonly known as white cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Reseda linifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas.

It is also cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
