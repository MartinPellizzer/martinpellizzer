Rubus hirtus var. axillaris

Rubus hirtus var. axillaris, commonly known as the hairy blackberry.

It is a shrub native to North America, characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
