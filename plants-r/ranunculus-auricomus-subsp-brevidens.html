Ranunculus auricomus subsp. brevidens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

brevidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features delicate, palmately lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and the species is notable for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also known for its toxic properties, containing protoanemonin, which can cause skin irritation.
