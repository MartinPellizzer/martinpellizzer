Rubus fruticosus variegatus

Rubus fruticosus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

This species is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed sites.
