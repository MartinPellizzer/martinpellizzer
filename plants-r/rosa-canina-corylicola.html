Rosa canina corylicola

Rosa canina corylicola, commonly known as the wild dog rose, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, red, hairy fruits called hips, which are rich in vitamin C. Rosa canina corylicola typically grows in open woodlands, hedgerows, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and historical contexts.
