Rosa lapwaiensis

Rosa lapwaiensis, commonly known as the Lapwai rose, is a species of wild rose native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces red-orange hips in the fall.

Rosa lapwaiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
