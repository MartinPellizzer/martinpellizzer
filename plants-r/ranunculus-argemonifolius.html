Ranunculus argemonifolius

Ranunculus argemonifolius, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear numerous small, yellow flowers with five petals. The leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive yellow or orange hue on the underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
