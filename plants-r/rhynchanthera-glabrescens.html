Rhynchanthera glabrescens

Rhynchanthera glabrescens, commonly known as the silky orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of South America.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
