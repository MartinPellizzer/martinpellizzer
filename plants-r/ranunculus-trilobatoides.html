Ranunculus trilobatoides

Ranunculus trilobatoides, commonly known as the three-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight sepals that are often mistaken for petals. The plant has a basal rosette of trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and have a distinctive three-leaf appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
