Rosa persica var. lutea

Rosa persica var. lutea, commonly known as the Persian yellow rose, is a variant of the Persian rose characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers.

This plant is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, where it thrives in temperate climates. It produces fragrant, single or semi-double blooms that typically appear in late spring to early summer. The leaves are dark green, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Rosa persica var. lutea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
