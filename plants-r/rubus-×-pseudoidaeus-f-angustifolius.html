Rubus × pseudoidaeus f. angustifolius

Rubus × pseudoidaeus f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant is often found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its edible berries, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
