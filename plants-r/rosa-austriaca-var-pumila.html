Rosa austriaca var. pumila

Rosa austriaca var. pumila, commonly known as the Austrian dwarf rose, is a compact shrub native to the Alps.

It features small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a dense, bushy growth habit with thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for alpine gardens.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in temperate climates.
