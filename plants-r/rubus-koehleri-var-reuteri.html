Rubus koehleri var. reuteri

Rubus koehleri var. reuteri, commonly known as the coastal blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to coastal regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in local cuisines. It has a thorny stem structure and compound leaves with serrated edges.

This species is adapted to tolerate salt spray and is an important part of coastal ecosystems.
