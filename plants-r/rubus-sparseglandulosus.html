Rubus sparseglandulosus

Rubus sparseglandulosus, commonly known as the sparsely glandular blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riparian habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
