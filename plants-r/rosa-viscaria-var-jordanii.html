Rosa viscaria var. jordanii

Rosa viscaria var. jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's rose, is a shrub-like species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
