Restio dimorphostachyus

Restio dimorphostachyus, commonly known as the two-formed rush, is a species of grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming during the warmer months. Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
