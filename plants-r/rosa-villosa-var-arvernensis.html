Rosa villosa var. arvernensis

Rosa villosa var. arvernensis, commonly known as the Arvernian bristly rose, is a shrub-like plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and numerous stamens. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
