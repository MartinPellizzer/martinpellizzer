Rubus henryi var. bambusarum

Rubus henryi var. bambusarum, commonly known as the bamboo raspberry, is a variant of the brambles native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are reddish in color and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental and edible qualities, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
