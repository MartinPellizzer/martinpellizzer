Rhododendron xiguense

Rhododendron xiguense, commonly known as the Xigu rhododendron, is a species native to China, particularly in the Xizang (Tibet) region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in mountainous and alpine environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a waxy coating. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron xiguense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
