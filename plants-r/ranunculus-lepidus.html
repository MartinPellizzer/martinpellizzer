Ranunculus lepidus

Ranunculus lepidus, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds.

Ranunculus lepidus is often found in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
