Rosa nutkana var. microcarpa

Rosa nutkana var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf rock rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which is often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly serrated.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
