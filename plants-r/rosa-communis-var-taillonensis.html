Rosa communis var. taillonensis

Rosa communis var. taillonensis, commonly known as the Taillon's rose, is a botanical variant of the common rose.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and semi-double to fully double flowers. The plant typically features dark green, serrated leaves and produces fragrant, pink to red blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.
