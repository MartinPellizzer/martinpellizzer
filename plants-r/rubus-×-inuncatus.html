Rubus × inuncatus

Rubus × inuncatus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between the common blackberry (Rubus fruticosus) and the evergreen bramble (Rubus ulmifolius). This plant is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are similar to blackberries in taste and texture.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.
