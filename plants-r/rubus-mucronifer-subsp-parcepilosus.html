Rubus mucronifer subsp. parcepilosus

Rubus mucronifer subsp.

parcepilosus, commonly known as the hairy bracted blackberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a dark purple to black berry. This subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy pubescence on the stems and leaves.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including rocky soils and forest edges.
