Rubus leucodermis var. nigerrimus

Rubus leucodermis var. nigerrimus, commonly known as blackbriar, is a thorny shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
