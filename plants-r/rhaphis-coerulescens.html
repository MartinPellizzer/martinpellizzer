Rhaphis coerulescens

Rhaphis coerulescens, commonly known as the blue bamboo or blue fan palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a fan shape, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Rhaphis coerulescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
