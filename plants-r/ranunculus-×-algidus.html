Ranunculus × algidus

Ranunculus × algidus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a hybrid species within the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with 5-15 petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps protect it from harsh weather.

Ranunculus × algidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
