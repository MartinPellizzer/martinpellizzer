Rubus thyrsoideus f. candicans

Rubus thyrsoideus f. candicans, commonly known as the shining blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its glossy, dark foliage and clusters of small, edible berries.

It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are white and appear in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, dark purple berries in the summer.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and fruit production.
