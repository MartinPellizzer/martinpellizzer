Ruppia cirrhosa var. truncatifolia

Ruppia cirrhosa var. truncatifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sea rocket, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It typically grows in intertidal zones and salt marshes, where it thrives in brackish water conditions. The plant has slender, linear leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its reproductive strategy involves producing copious amounts of buoyant seeds that can drift long distances in water.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
