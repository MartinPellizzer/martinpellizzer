Rumex hasslerianus

Rumex hasslerianus, commonly known as the hairy sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish color and a hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose clusters, producing yellowish-green blooms.

The plant is known for its edible leaves, which have a tangy, sour flavor similar to other sorrel species.
