Racosperma mutabile subsp. incurvum

Racosperma mutabile subsp.

incurvum, commonly known as the variable acacia, is a shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typical of many legumes. Its leaves are often variable in shape, which contributes to its subspecies name.

It is adapted to dry environments and plays a role in soil stabilization and habitat provision.
