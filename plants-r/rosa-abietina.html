Rosa abietina

Rosa abietina, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a serrated edge, while its stems are covered in fine, silvery hairs.

Rosa abietina is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
