Rottboellia japonica

Rottboellia japonica, commonly known as Japanese barnyardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes many economically important grasses.
