Rubus microphyllus var. subcrataegifolius

Rubus microphyllus var. subcrataegifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, dark red to black drupelets. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
