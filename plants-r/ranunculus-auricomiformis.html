Ranunculus auricomiformis

Ranunculus auricomiformis, commonly known as the golden shield or yellow shield buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive yellow center. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks.

Ranunculus auricomiformis is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
