Rosa canina var. reginae

Rosa canina var. reginae, commonly known as the Queen's rose, is a variant of the dog rose, characterized by its distinctive floral features.

It typically produces large, showy flowers with a rich pink hue, often with a white center. The plant is a deciduous shrub, native to Europe and parts of Asia, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its thorny stems and compound leaves are typical of the Rosa genus.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
