Rubus × demandtii

Rubus × demandtii, commonly known as the hybrid blackberry or loganberry, is a species resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (evergreen blackberry) and Rubus gebleri (red raspberry).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, oval drupelets that mature to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its flavorful berries.

The botanical features of Rubus × demandtii make it a popular choice for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
