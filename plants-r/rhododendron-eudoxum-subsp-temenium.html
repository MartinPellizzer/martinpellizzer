Rhododendron eudoxum subsp. temenium

Rhododendron eudoxum subsp.

temenium, commonly known as the Yunnan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and shrublands, preferring cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves with a leathery texture and a compact growth habit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
