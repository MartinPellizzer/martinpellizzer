Rubus procerus var. amiantinus

Rubus procerus var. amiantinus, commonly known as the silky blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, dark purple to black fruits that are typically small and firm. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and has thorny stems typical of the Rubus genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, making them suitable for jams and pies.
