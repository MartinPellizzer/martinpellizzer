Ruellia barbillana

Ruellia barbillana, commonly known as the redstem ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ruellia barbillana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded environments.
