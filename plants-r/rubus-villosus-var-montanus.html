Rubus villosus var. montanus

Rubus villosus var. montanus, commonly known as the mountain blackberry, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and small, dark red to black berries. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in local cuisine.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting wildlife and stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
