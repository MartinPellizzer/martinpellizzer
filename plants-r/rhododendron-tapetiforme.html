Rhododendron tapetiforme

Rhododendron tapetiforme, commonly known as the broadleaf rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and purple.

Rhododendron tapetiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural landscaping.
