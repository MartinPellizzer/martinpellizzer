Rubus makinoensis

Rubus makinoensis, commonly known as Makino's blackberry, is a species of shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rubus genus, which includes many species of brambles and berries. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark red to black berries. It typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to moist, temperate climates.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a subject of both ecological and culinary interest.
