Rubus diminutus

Rubus diminutus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry.

It is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States, characterized by its small, trailing growth habit. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and its flowers are white with five petals.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.
