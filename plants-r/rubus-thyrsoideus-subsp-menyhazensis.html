Rubus thyrsoideus subsp. menyhazensis

Rubus thyrsoideus subsp.

menyhazensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian blackberry, is a shrub native to Ethiopia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries.

The plant typically grows in highland areas with well-drained soils and is adapted to the local climate. Its leaves are alternate, serrated, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is edible and has been traditionally used in local cuisines and for its medicinal properties.
