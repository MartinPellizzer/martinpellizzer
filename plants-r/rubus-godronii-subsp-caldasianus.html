Rubus godronii subsp. caldasianus

Rubus godronii subsp.

caldasianus, commonly known as the Caldasian blackberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of Colombia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for local biodiversity and traditional uses.
