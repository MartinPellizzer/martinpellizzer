Rhododendron sanguineum var. didymum

Rhododendron sanguineum var. didymum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy beneath. It prefers acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
