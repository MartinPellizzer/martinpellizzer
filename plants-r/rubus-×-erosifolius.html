Rubus × erosifolius

Rubus × erosifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of small, dark red drupelets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable forage plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
