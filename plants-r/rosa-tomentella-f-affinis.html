Rosa tomentella f. affinis

Rosa tomentella f. affinis, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage that provides a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its thorny stems and aromatic leaves make it well adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
