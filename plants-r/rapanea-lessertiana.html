Rapanea lessertiana

Rapanea lessertiana, commonly known as the Madagascar laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns from green to red as it matures.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
