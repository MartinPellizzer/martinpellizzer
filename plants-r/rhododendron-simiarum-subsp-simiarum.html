Rhododendron simiarum subsp. simiarum

Rhododendron simiarum subsp.

simiarum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in cool, moist mountainous regions.

The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are often densely covered with glandular hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
