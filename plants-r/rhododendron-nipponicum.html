Rhododendron nipponicum

Rhododendron nipponicum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Rhododendron nipponicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
