Roegneria × pavlovii

Roegneria × pavlovii, commonly known as the hybrid wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species, typically wheatgrass and other Poa species.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and has been cultivated for its ornamental and forage value. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces spikelet-based inflorescences, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.

Roegneria × pavlovii is also valued for its potential in agricultural applications, particularly in sustainable farming practices.
