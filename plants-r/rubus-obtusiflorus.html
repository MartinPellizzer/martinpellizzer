Rubus obtusiflorus

Rubus obtusiflorus, commonly known as the blunt-flowered blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The botanical features of Rubus obtusiflorus make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
