Ruellia glabrata

Ruellia glabrata, commonly known as the smooth ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes, often in shades of blue, purple, or white, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ruellia glabrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
