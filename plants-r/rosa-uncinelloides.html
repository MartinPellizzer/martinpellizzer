Rosa uncinelloides

Rosa uncinelloides, commonly known as the hooked rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, leathery leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Rosa uncinelloides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
