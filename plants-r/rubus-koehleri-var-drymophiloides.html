Rubus koehleri var. drymophiloides

Rubus koehleri var. drymophiloides, commonly known as the drymophiloid raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated margins, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
