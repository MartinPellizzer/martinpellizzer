Rubus × disparatus

Rubus × disparatus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of numerous drupelets, which are typically bright red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for cultivation in gardens and for commercial raspberry production.
