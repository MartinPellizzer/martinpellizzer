Rhododendron platypodum

Rhododendron platypodum, commonly known as the flatpod rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

Rhododendron platypodum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
