Rosa mollis f. refragatula

Rosa mollis f. refragatula, commonly known as the fragrant rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa mollis species.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is native to regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its compact growth habit makes it ideal for borders, containers, and as a ground cover in landscaped areas.
