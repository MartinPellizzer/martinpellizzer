Raddia nana

Raddia nana, commonly known as the dwarf raddia, is a small herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Raddia nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
