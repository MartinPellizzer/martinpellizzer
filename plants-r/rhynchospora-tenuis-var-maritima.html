Rhynchospora tenuis var. maritima

Rhynchospora tenuis var. maritima, commonly known as the slender beak sedge, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, saline soils along shorelines and tidal flats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, greenish flowers. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and periodic flooding, making it a characteristic species of maritime habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing food and habitat for various coastal invertebrates and birds.
