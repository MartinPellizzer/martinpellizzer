Rhododendron scottiatum

Rhododendron scottiatum, commonly known as the Scottish rhododendron, is a deciduous shrub native to the Scottish Highlands.

It features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
