Ranunculus truniacus

Ranunculus truniacus, commonly known as the Truancy buttercup, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically has palmate leaves and produces yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
