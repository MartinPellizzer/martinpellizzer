Rumex aschabadensis

Rumex aschabadensis, commonly known as the Aschabad dock, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. The plant features large, arrow-shaped leaves with a wavy margin and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, producing a yellowish-green color.

Rumex aschabadensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
