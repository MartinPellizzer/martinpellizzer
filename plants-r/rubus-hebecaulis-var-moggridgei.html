Rubus hebecaulis var. moggridgei

Rubus hebecaulis var. moggridgei, commonly known as the Moggridge's bramble, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streamside habitats.

This variety is notable for its horticultural value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
