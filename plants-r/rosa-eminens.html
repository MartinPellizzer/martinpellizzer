Rosa eminens

Rosa eminens, commonly known as the elevated rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing showy, semi-double petals that attract pollinators. Its leaves are pinnate, with serrated edges, and the stems are covered in soft, white hairs.

Rosa eminens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
