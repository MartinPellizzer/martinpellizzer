Rosa beauvaisii

Rosa beauvaisii, commonly known as the Beaufort rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer. Rosa beauvaisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.
