Rhododendron rupicola

Rhododendron rupicola, commonly known as the rock rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in the Sichuan and Yunnan provinces.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows on rocky slopes and in well-drained soils, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded landscapes.

Rhododendron rupicola is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in challenging terrains.
