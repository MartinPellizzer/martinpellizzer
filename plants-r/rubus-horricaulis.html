Rubus horricaulis

Rubus horricaulis, commonly known as the hairy blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which includes other well-known fruit-bearing plants.
