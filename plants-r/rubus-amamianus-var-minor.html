Rubus amamianus var. minor

Rubus amamianus var. minor, commonly known as the Amamian blackberry, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant features thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are characteristic of the Rubus genus. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The berries are valued for their intense flavor and are often used in culinary applications.
