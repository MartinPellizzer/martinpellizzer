Rubus gracilis var. anoplos

Rubus gracilis var. anoplos, commonly known as the slender blackberry.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits composed of tiny, dark red to black drupelets. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and numerous stamens.

This variety is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
