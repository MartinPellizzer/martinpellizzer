Rheedia cupi

Rheedia cupi, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Rheedia cupi is an annual herb that thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed in agricultural settings and a subject of study in plant ecology.
