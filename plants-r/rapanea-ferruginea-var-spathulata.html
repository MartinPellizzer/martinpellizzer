Rapanea ferruginea var. spathulata

Rapanea ferruginea var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed red ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or broad-ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often in shaded, moist environments.

Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for traditional uses and craftsmanship.
