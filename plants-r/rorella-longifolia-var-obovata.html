Rorella longifolia var. obovata

Rorella longifolia var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed rorella, is a variant of the rorella plant characterized by its obovate leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Brazil. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and rounded, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer.
