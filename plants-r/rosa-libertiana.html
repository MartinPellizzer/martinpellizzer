Rosa libertiana

Rosa libertiana, commonly known as the "Libertarian rose," is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green foliage. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa libertiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and pleasant scent.
