Rosa viscaria var. pseudomentita

Rosa viscaria var. pseudomentita, commonly known as the Mexican rose, is a shrub-like plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
