Rhododendron rubiginosum

Rhododendron rubiginosum, commonly known as the rusty leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to reddish-brown, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
