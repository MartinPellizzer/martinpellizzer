Ribes carneum

Ribes carneum, commonly known as the red currant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It produces small, red, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and used in culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and agricultural purposes.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and pollinators.
