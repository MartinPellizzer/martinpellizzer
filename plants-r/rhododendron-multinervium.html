Rhododendron multinervium

Rhododendron multinervium, commonly known as the broad-leaved rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a prominent network of veins, contributing to its scientific name "multinervium.".

Rhododendron multinervium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
