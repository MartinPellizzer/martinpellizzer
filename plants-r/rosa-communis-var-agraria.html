Rosa communis var. agraria

Rosa communis var. agraria, commonly known as the common garden rose, is a cultivar of the rose plant native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with multiple stems and a dense, thorny structure. The plant produces fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
