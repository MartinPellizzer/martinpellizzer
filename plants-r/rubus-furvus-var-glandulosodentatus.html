Rubus furvus var. glandulosodentatus

Rubus furvus var. glandulosodentatus, commonly known as the glandular-toothed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant characterized by its distinctive foliage and fruit.

It is native to parts of North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The leaves are typically serrated with glandular hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. Its fruits are small, dark berries that ripen in late summer, often used in culinary applications.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
