Rubus × sphenophorus

Rubus × sphenophorus, commonly known as the blackberry raspberry or loganberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Rubus ursinus (blackberry) and Rubus chinensis (raspberry).

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark red to black in color, with a tart flavor. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications such as jams, pies, and beverages.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
