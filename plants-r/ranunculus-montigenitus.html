Ranunculus montigenitus

Ranunculus montigenitus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, hairy fruits that disperse its seeds.

Due to its habitat, it is often considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
