Rhododendron × alstroemerioides

Rhododendron × alstroemerioides, commonly known as the alstroemeria rhododendron, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species.

It is a broadleaf evergreen shrub with dense, leathery foliage that remains green throughout the year. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
