Rubus subinermis

Rubus subinermis, commonly known as the smooth blackberry shrub, is a species of brambles native to North America.

It is characterized by its thornless stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in the fall. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of white flowers in the spring. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Rubus subinermis is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.
