Rubus rhombifolius subsp. sueviacus

Rubus rhombifolius subsp.

sueviacus, commonly known as the Suevicus blackberry, is a subspecies native to the northern regions of Europe. It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with rhomboid-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species within its native habitat.
