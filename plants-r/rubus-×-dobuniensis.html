Rubus × dobuniensis

Rubus × dobuniensis, commonly known as the Dobunian blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of eastern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the berries are usually dark purple to black when mature.

This species is valued for its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor and are used in local cuisines and traditional remedies.
