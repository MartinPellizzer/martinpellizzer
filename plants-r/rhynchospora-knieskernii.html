Rhynchospora knieskernii

Rhynchospora knieskernii, commonly known as Knieskern's spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to humid environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale pink flowers. Its root system is fibrous, aiding in stabilizing the soil in wetland ecosystems.

Rhynchospora knieskernii plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
