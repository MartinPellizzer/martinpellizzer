Rosa canina var. euoxyphylla

Rosa canina var. euoxyphylla, commonly known as the European dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals, and bloom in early summer. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit and contain numerous seeds.

It is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates.
