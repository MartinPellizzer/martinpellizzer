Rubus elongatispinus subsp. clusii

Rubus elongatispinus subsp.

clusii, commonly known as the Clusii blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible berries, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
