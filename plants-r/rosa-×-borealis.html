Rosa × borealis

Rosa × borealis, commonly known as the Arctic rose, is a hybrid species native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late summer. It is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Rosa × borealis is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in cooler environments.
