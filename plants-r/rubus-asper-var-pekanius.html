Rubus asper var. pekanius

Rubus asper var. pekanius, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and river valleys.

Its berries are edible, though they are generally tart and often used in jams or wines.
