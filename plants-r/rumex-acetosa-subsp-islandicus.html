Rumex acetosa subsp. islandicus

Rumex acetosa subsp.

islandicus, commonly known as coastal sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of the North Atlantic. It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for treating ailments such as inflammation and digestive issues.
