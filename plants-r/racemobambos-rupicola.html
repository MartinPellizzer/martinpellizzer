Racemobambos rupicola

Racemobambos rupicola, commonly known as the rock bamboo, is a unique plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family and exhibits a clumping growth habit, forming dense clusters of slender, bamboo-like stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in elongated racemes, which are borne on the upper parts of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall bamboo-like appearance.

Racemobambos rupicola is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient and drought-tolerant species.
