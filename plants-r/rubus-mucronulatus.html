Rubus mucronulatus

Rubus mucronulatus, commonly known as the blunt-tipped blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many species of blackberries and raspberries.
