Rourea pulchella

Rourea pulchella, commonly known as the velvetleaf rourea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its woody stems and thick leaves make it a distinctive member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Rourea pulchella is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
