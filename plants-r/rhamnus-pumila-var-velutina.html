Rhamnus pumila var. velutina

Rhamnus pumila var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet buckthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red or black berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It is adapted to poor soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
