Rubus ulmifolius f. apiculifer

Rubus ulmifolius f. apiculifer, commonly known as the bramble rose or sweet briar, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves that resemble those of the elm, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by red or black berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedgerow construction.
