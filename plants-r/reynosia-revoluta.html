Reynosia revoluta

Reynosia revoluta, commonly known as the Pacific coast buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark, leathery berries. It thrives in coastal climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Reynosia revoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
