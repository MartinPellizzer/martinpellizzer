Rhododendron spinuliferum var. glabrescens

Rhododendron spinuliferum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rhododendron, is a variant of the spinuliferum species.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that contrast with the typical hairy foliage of the species. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring. It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like China and Vietnam.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
