Roegneria tsukushiensis var. transiens

Roegneria tsukushiensis var. transiens, commonly known as the Japanese meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often concealed within the foliage, making it distinctive.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
