Ranunculus corylisinus

Ranunculus corylisinus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features delicate, palmate leaves and showy yellow flowers with prominent, rounded sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. It has a short flowering period in spring and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
