Rubus schleicheri var. mucronipetaloides

Rubus schleicheri var. mucronipetaloides, commonly known as the blunt-petaled blackberry.

It is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes, while the fruit is a dark purple to black berry. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
