Rosa afzeliana var. aostana

Rosa afzeliana var. aostana, commonly known as the Aosta rose, is a compact shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dark green, finely toothed leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is well adapted to harsh mountain climates, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its historical significance in botanical studies and horticultural cultivation.
