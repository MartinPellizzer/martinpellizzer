Radermachera sibuyanensis

Radermachera sibuyanensis, commonly known as the Sibuyan glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem, making it a distinctive ornamental species. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and valuable plant in horticulture.
