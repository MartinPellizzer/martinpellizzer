Rosa cozza

Rosa cozza, commonly known as the Cozza rose, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dark green, lobed leaves and produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring. Its stems are covered in thorns, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Rosa cozza is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
