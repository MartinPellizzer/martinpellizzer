Rosa canina f. transsilvanica

Rosa canina f. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian dog rose, is a botanical variant of the dog rose species.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina genus. This subspecies is native to parts of Europe, particularly Romania and Hungary, where it thrives in woodland and scrub habitats.

Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces hips that are rich in vitamin C. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
