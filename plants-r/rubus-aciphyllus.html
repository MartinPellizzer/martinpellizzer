Rubus aciphyllus

Rubus aciphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces compound leaves with a narrow, leathery texture, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is edible but has a tart flavor and is sometimes used in local cuisines.
