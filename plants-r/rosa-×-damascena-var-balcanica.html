Rosa × damascena var. balcanica

Rosa × damascena var. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan damask rose, is a hybrid species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring thorny stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are widely used in the production of rose oil and essential oils due to their high concentration of aromatic compounds.

This variety is valued for both ornamental purposes and its economic importance in the perfume and cosmetics industries.
