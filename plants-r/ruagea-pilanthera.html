Ruagea pilanthera

Ruagea pilanthera, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its striking white, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. Ruagea pilanthera is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blossoms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
