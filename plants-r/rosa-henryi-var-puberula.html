Rosa henryi var. puberula

Rosa henryi var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent Henry rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and oval, pubescent leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant blooms and unique foliage texture.
