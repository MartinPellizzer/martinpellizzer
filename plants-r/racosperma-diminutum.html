Racosperma diminutum

Racosperma diminutum, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous tiny leaflets, and it produces slender, straight pods that contain edible seeds.

Racosperma diminutum is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
