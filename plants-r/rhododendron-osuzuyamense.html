Rhododendron osuzuyamense

Rhododendron osuzuyamense, commonly known as the Osuzuyamense azalea, is a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
