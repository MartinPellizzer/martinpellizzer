Rubus nyalamensis

Rubus nyalamensis, commonly known as the Nyalam blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in local traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
