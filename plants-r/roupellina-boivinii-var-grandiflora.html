Roupellina boivinii var. grandiflora

Roupellina boivinii var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant senna, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
