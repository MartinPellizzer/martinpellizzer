Ranunculus dyris var. pseudobulbosus

Ranunculus dyris var. pseudobulbosus, commonly known as the mountain buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of finely divided, lanceolate leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has a short, rhizomatous rootstock that allows it to survive in harsh alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
