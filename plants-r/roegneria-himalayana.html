Roegneria himalayana

Roegneria himalayana, commonly known as the Himalayan false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and arid conditions. The plant features slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

Roegneria himalayana plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
