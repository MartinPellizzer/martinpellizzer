Rhaphidophora wallichii

Rhaphidophora wallichii, commonly known as the Zebra Plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that feature alternating light and dark green stripes, giving it a distinctive zebra-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
