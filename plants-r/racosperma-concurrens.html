Racosperma concurrens

Racosperma concurrens, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods.

Racosperma concurrens is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
