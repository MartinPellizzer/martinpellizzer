Rubus × secundarius

Rubus × secundarius, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its berries are edible, though they are often described as having a tart and somewhat astringent flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
