Ranunculus tauricus

Ranunculus tauricus, commonly known as the Tauric buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many other buttercup species.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it can be toxic if ingested.
