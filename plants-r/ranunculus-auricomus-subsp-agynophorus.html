Ranunculus auricomus subsp. agynophorus

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

agynophorus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate along the stem. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family.
