Rubus commersonii

Rubus commersonii, commonly known as the Commerson's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including forests and shrublands, and is often found in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
