Riedelia lanata f. ligulata

Riedelia lanata f. ligulata, commonly known as the woolly riedelia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
