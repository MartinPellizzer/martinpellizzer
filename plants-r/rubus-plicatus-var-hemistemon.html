Rubus plicatus var. hemistemon

Rubus plicatus var. hemistemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.
