Rubus lineatus var. glandulosus

Rubus lineatus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular dewberry, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thorny stems. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and glandular hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
