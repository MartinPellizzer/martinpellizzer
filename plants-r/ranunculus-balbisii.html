Ranunculus balbisii

Ranunculus balbisii, commonly known as the Balbisii crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has a basal rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
