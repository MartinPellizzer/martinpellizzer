Rytidosperma clavatum

Rytidosperma clavatum, commonly known as the clustered feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Rytidosperma clavatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine, feathery appearance.
