Rubus wahlbergii var. vexatus

Rubus wahlbergii var. vexatus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The berries are edible, though some varieties may have a tart or slightly bitter flavor.
