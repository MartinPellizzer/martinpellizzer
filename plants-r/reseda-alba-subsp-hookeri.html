Reseda alba subsp. hookeri

Reseda alba subsp.

hookeri, commonly known as white knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.

Botanically, it is noted for its fibrous root system and its historical use in dye production.
