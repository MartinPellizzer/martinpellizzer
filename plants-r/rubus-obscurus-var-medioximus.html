Rubus obscurus var. medioximus

Rubus obscurus var. medioximus, commonly known as the dark bramble or lesser blackberry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and disturbed soils, preferring moist to moderately dry conditions. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins, and its flowers are usually pale pink or white.

The fruit, though not widely cultivated, is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor.
