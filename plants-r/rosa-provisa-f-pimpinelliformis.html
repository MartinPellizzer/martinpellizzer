Rosa provisa f. pimpinelliformis

Rosa provisa f. pimpinelliformis, commonly known as the creeping or trailing rose, is a shrub-like form of the species Rosa provisa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with stems that often lie close to the ground and produce numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually pink or white in color. It is well adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to form dense ground cover.
