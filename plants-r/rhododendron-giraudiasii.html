Rhododendron giraudiasii

Rhododendron giraudiasii, commonly known as the Giraudia's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool and moist environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually pink or purple, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
