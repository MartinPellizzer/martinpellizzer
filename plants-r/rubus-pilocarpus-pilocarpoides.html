Rubus pilocarpus pilocarpoides

Rubus pilocarpus pilocarpoides, commonly known as the red raspberry or trailing raspberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, red berries that are edible. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other raspberry species. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces fruit in the summer months.

The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade.
