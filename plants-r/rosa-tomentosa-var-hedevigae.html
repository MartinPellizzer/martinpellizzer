Rosa tomentosa var. hedevigae

Rosa tomentosa var. hedevigae, commonly known as the Hedevig's woolly rose, is a compact shrub with dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage.

Its flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces hips that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
