Rubus vespicum

Rubus vespicum, commonly known as the stinging raspberry.

It is a species of bramble native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, edible fruits. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including poor and rocky ground. Its flowers are typically white or pink, and they appear in early summer.

The plant is also notable for its defensive stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact.
