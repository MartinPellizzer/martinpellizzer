Ranunculus rionii

Ranunculus rionii, commonly known as the Rion Mountain buttercup, is a rare flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in shaded woodlands. It is part of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy and often toxic flowers.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in many states.
