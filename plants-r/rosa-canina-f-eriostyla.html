Rosa canina f. eriostyla

Rosa canina f. eriostyla, commonly known as the bristly dog rose, is a botanical variant of the common dog rose.

It is characterized by its distinctive bristly stems and thorns, which give it a rugged appearance. The plant produces pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and scrublands, thriving in well-drained soils.
