Rubus falcatus

Rubus falcatus, commonly known as the curved bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India and Malaysia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruits, known as blackberries, are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The botanical features of Rubus falcatus make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
