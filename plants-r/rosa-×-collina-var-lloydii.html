Rosa × collina var. lloydii

Rosa × collina var. lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's hill rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, adding texture to the landscape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
