Ranunculus auricomus subsp. triplex

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

triplex, commonly known as the three-lobed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It features delicate, yellow flowers with three prominent lobes, which are typically borne on slender stems.

The plant has palmately divided leaves with a waxy coating, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and leaf structure compared to other members of the Ranunculus genus.
