Rosa diffusa

Rosa diffusa, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often covered in thorns.

Rosa diffusa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
