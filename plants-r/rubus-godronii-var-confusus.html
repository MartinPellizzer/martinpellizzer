Rubus godronii var. confusus

Rubus godronii var. confusus, commonly known as the Godron's blackberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is notable for its genetic variability and is considered a subspecies of Rubus godronii.
