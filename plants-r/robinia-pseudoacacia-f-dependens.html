Robinia pseudoacacia f. dependens

Robinia pseudoacacia f. dependens, commonly known as the weeping false acacia, is a unique variant of the honeylocust tree.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets that are arranged in a feathery pattern. It produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by long, slender pods.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
