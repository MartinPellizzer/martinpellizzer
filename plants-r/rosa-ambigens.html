Rosa ambigens

Rosa ambigens, commonly known as the ambigous rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. Rosa ambigens typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
