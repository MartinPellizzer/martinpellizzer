Racosperma arafuricum

Racosperma arafuricum, commonly known as the Arafurum acacia, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it ecologically significant in arid regions.

The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils with minimal water requirements.
