Rosa rubiginosa var. permixta

Rosa rubiginosa var. permixta, commonly known as the hybrid pink rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, typical of many rose species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural landscapes.
