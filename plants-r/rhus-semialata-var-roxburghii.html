Rhus semialata var. roxburghii

Rhus semialata var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and turn vibrant red in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
