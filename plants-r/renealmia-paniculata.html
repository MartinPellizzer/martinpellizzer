Renealmia paniculata

Renealmia paniculata, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are typically bright yellow to golden in color. The plant produces inflorescences that are panicles, which are branched clusters of small, delicate flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Renealmia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its striking foliage.
