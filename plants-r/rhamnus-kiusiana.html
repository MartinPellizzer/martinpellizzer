Rhamnus kiusiana

Rhamnus kiusiana, commonly known as the Kiusian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by reddish or black berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Rhamnus kiusiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
