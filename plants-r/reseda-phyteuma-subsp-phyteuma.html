Reseda phyteuma subsp. phyteuma

Reseda phyteuma subsp.

phyteuma, commonly known as the red campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant produces striking pink to red flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a taproot system.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
