Russelia cuneata

Russelia cuneata, commonly known as the red-hot catarrh, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Russelia cuneata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
