Rymandra excelsa

Rymandra excelsa, commonly known as the towering spire plant, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are finely divided and resemble those of parsley, though they are more elongated.

Native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
