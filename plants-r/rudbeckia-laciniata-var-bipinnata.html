Rudbeckia laciniata var. bipinnata

Rudbeckia laciniata var. bipinnata, commonly known as the bi-pinnate rudbeckia, is a striking perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flower heads with dark brown centers, which bloom from midsummer to early fall. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are bipinnately lobed, giving it a delicate and intricate appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
