Ranunculus pedrottii

Ranunculus pedrottii, commonly known as the Pedrott's buttercup, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five to eight petals and numerous stamens.

Ranunculus pedrottii is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
