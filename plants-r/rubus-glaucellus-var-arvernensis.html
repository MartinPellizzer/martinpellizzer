Rubus glaucellus var. arvernensis

Rubus glaucellus var. arvernensis, commonly known as the Arvernian raspberry, is a shrub native to central Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubus genus.
