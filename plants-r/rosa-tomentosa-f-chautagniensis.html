Rosa tomentosa f. chautagniensis

Rosa tomentosa f. chautagniensis, commonly known as the Chautagne rose, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the Rosa tomentosa species.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. This form is native to the Pyrenees region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticultural gardens.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative shrub in smaller spaces.
