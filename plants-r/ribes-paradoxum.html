Ribes paradoxum

Ribes paradoxum, commonly known as the purple-flowered currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ribes paradoxum thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
