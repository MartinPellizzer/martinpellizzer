Rosa calostyla

Rosa calostyla, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has thorny stems covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of 5 to 9 oval-shaped leaflets.

Rosa calostyla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
