Rubus thyrsoideus var. laterum

Rubus thyrsoideus var. laterum, commonly known as the red-stemmed blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and dark purple, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
