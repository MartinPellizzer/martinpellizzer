Rosa canina var. vacciniifolia

Rosa canina var. vacciniifolia, commonly known as the red-berried rose, is a variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are bright red, aggregate berries that ripen in late summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
