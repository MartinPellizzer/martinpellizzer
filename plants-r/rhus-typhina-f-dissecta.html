Rhus typhina f. dissecta

Rhus typhina f. dissecta, commonly known as the Staghorn Sumac, is a cultivar of the staghorn sumac, characterized by its distinctive, finely dissected leaves that create a lacy appearance.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, typically growing in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features smooth, reddish-brown stems that become rugged and horn-like with age, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, fuzzy berries that provide food for birds.

The cultivar is valued for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
