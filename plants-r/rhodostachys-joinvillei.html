Rhodostachys joinvillei

Rhodostachys joinvillei, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a strong, sweet fragrance. Its fruit is a green, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe.

Rhodostachys joinvillei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
