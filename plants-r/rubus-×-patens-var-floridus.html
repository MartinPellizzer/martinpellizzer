Rubus × patens var. floridus

Rubus × patens var. floridus, commonly known as the Florida blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is adapted to humid, subtropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are white or pink, and the fruit, a dark purple to black berry, is edible and has a sweet-tart flavor.
