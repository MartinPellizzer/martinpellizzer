Racosperma isoneurum

Racosperma isoneurum, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale green flowers in compact globular or cylindrical clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dryland restoration.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable in soil rehabilitation and ecological landscaping.
