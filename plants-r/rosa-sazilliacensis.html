Rosa sazilliacensis

Rosa sazilliacensis, commonly known as the Sazilliac rose, is a rare and ancient species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark red, semi-double flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has upright, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by horticulturists and botanists alike.
