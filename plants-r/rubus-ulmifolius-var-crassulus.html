Rubus ulmifolius var. crassulus

Rubus ulmifolius var. crassulus, commonly known as the broad-leaved blackberry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable species within the Rubus genus.
