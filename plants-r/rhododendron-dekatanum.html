Rhododendron dekatanum

Rhododendron dekatanum, commonly known as the Japanese rhododendron, is a species native to Japan and China.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its evergreen foliage and long-lasting blooms.
