Rhododendron maximum

Rhododendron maximum, commonly known as the Great Rhododendron, is a large evergreen shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Rhododendron maximum is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
