Ranunculus thomasii

Ranunculus thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with five to eight petals. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has palmate leaves that are divided into three to five lobes, and its stems are usually hairy.

Ranunculus thomasii is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.
