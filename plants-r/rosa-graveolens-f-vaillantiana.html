Rosa graveolens f. vaillantiana

Rosa graveolens f. vaillantiana, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a fragrant, thorny shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in hedgerows and gardens. Its fragrance is often described as sweet and delicate, reminiscent of gardenias.

It is also notable for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
