Rosa arvensis var. buraeana

Rosa arvensis var. buraeana, commonly known as the Buraean dog rose, is a variant of the common dog rose, characterized by its distinctive floral and foliage features.

It is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe, typically found in open woodland and grassy areas. The plant produces pale pink to white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are covered in thorns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
