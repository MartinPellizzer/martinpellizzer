Ranunculus glaberrimus

Ranunculus glaberrimus, commonly known as the smooth meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers have numerous sepals that resemble petals.

It is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
