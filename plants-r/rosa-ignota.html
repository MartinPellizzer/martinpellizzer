Rosa ignota

Rosa ignota, commonly known as the unknown rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a strong, sweet aroma.

Rosa ignota is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and scent.
