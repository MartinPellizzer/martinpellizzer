Romulea filifolia

Romulea filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf glory-of-the-snow, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and delicate, nodding flowers. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers with three petals and three sepals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in rare and exotic plants.
