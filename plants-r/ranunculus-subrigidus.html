Ranunculus subrigidus

Ranunculus subrigidus, commonly known as the rigid buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
