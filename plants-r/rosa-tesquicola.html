Rosa tesquicola

Rosa tesquicola, commonly known as the Mexican wild rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows in dry, rocky soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant features thorny stems and small, fragrant pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its fruits are red, hairy berries.

Rosa tesquicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
