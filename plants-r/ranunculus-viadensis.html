Ranunculus viadensis

Ranunculus viadensis, commonly known as the Viadensis buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to nine sepals that resemble petals.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural habitats.
