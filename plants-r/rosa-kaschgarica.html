Rosa kaschgarica

Rosa kaschgarica, commonly known as the Karakorum rose, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Karakorum Mountains in Pakistan and India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to its harsh habitat. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Rosa kaschgarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
