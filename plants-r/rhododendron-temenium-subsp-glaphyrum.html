Rhododendron temenium subsp. glaphyrum

Rhododendron temenium subsp.

glaphyrum, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in Nepal, Bhutan, and northern India. It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows.

The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are often dark green and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, with five petals and a showy, funnel-shaped bloom.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
