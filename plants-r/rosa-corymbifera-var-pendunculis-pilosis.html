Rosa corymbifera var. pendunculis-pilosis

Rosa corymbifera var. pendunculis-pilosis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed shrub rose, is a variant of the shrub rose species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
