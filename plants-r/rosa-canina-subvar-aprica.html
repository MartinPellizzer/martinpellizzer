Rosa canina subvar. aprica

Rosa canina subvar. aprica, commonly known as the apricot rose, is a variant of the dog rose that features pale pink to white flowers.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
