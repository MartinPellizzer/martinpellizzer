Rosa canina var. monsignatica

Rosa canina var. monsignatica, commonly known as the Monsignaca rose, is a rare variant of the dog rose.

It is characterized by its deep pink to red flowers and thorny stems, typical of the Rosa canina species. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Its foliage consists of pinnate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces red-orange hips that are rich in vitamin C. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
