Rosa communis var. deseglisei

Rosa communis var. deseglisei, commonly known as the Deseglise rose, is a rare and ancient variety of rose with a rich botanical history.

It is characterized by its deep crimson petals and a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive, spicy scent. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
