Rosa canina var. lancifolia

Rosa canina var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dog rose, is a variant of the dog rose species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, hairy berries that are rich in vitamin C and are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and wildlife.
