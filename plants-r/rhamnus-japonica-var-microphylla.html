Rhamnus japonica var. microphylla

Rhamnus japonica var. microphylla, commonly known as Japanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length, giving it its variety name "microphylla," meaning "small leaf.". The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by small, black berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
