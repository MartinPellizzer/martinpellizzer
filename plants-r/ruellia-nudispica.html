Ruellia nudispica

Ruellia nudispica, commonly known as the naked-stem ruellia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features slender, hairless stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are tubular and typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Ruellia nudispica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
