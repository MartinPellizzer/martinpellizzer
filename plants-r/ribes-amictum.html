Ribes amictum

Ribes amictum, commonly known as the naked-stem gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the genus Ribes, which includes various species of gooseberries and currants. The plant is characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. Its fruits are typically small, tart berries that ripen in late summer.

Ribes amictum is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
