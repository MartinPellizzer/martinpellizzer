Romulea hirsuta var. aurantiaca

Romulea hirsuta var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the Orange Milkweed or Orange Romulea, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of vibrant orange to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a standout in botanical collections.
