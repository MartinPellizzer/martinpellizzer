Rubus pectinellus

Rubus pectinellus, commonly known as the European brambles or blackberry shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries in the summer. These berries are typically dark purple to black when ripe and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Rubus pectinellus is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
