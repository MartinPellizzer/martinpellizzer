Rhododendron tsusiophyllum

Rhododendron tsusiophyllum, commonly known as the Tsusi's rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, while its flowers range in color from pink to red, adding vibrant visual interest to the landscape.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
