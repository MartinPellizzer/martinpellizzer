Rubus nemorosus var. pseudonemorosus

Rubus nemorosus var. pseudonemorosus, commonly known as the common bramble, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early summer, followed by edible red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a common and adaptable species in temperate regions.
