Ranunculus waldronii

Ranunculus waldronii, commonly known as the Waldron's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to eight sepals that resemble petals.

It is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
