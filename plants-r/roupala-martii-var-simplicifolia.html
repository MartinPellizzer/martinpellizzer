Roupala martii var. simplicifolia

Roupala martii var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved roupala, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy on both surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes grown as a decorative plant in cooler climates.
