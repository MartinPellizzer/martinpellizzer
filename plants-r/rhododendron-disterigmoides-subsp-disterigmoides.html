Rhododendron disterigmoides subsp. disterigmoides

Rhododendron disterigmoides subsp.

disterigmoides, commonly known as the Himalayan rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
