Rhopalopilia verdickii

Rhopalopilia verdickii, commonly known as the velvetleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, fleshy leaves that give it a succulent appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along roadsides.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique morphology, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
