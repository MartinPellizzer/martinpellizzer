Rubus parvifolius var. adenochlamys

Rubus parvifolius var. adenochlamys, commonly known as the Adeno-chlamys raspberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it is notable for its hairy foliage and distinctive floral structure.
