Rhamnus alaternus var. porquerollensis

Rhamnus alaternus var. porquerollensis, commonly known as the Corsican buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, black berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
