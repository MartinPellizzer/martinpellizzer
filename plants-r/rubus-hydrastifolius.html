Rubus hydrastifolius

Rubus hydrastifolius, commonly known as the waterleaf raspberry, is a species of shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are white and have five petals, while its fruit is a reddish-black berry.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
