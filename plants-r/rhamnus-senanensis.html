Rhamnus senanensis

Rhamnus senanensis, commonly known as the Senanese buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
