Richea alpina

Richea alpina, commonly known as the alpine richea, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces delicate white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Richea alpina thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
