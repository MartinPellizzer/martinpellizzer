Rubus mitissimus

Rubus mitissimus, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a species of bramble native to North America.

It is characterized by its trailing or shrub-like growth habit and produces small, edible berries. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pinkish flowers in the spring. Its berries are typically bright red or orange when ripe and are often used in jams and desserts.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
