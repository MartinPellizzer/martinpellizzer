Rosa rubiginosa glabra

Rosa rubiginosa glabra, commonly known as the rough rose or the shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in perfumery and medicine.
