Rubus subornatus var. typicus

Rubus subornatus var. typicus, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a species of bramble native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its prickly stems, compound leaves, and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils, preferring partial shade to full sun. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit, which ripens in summer, is widely cultivated for its sweet, juicy berries.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is often found in a variety of ecological habitats.
