Rubus × falsicuneatus

Rubus × falsicuneatus, commonly known as the blackberry- raspberry hybrid, is a deciduous shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, commonly referred to as berries, which are typically dark purple to black in color. These berries are edible, though they often have a tart flavor and are used in jams and wines.

It is native to parts of North America and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.
