Rosa glandulicaulis

Rosa glandulicaulis, commonly known as the glandular-stemmed rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in glandular structures that secrete a sticky substance. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners interested in cultivating unique and resilient rose varieties.
