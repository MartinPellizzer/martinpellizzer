Rondeletia tinifolia

Rondeletia tinifolia, commonly known as the red spider flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, spider-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, elliptic leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Rondeletia tinifolia is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its striking floral display.
