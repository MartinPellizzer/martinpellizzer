Ruta dampatis

Ruta dampatis, commonly known as common rue, is a perennial herb native to Europe, western Asia, and northwestern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and small yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically green when immature and turn black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Ruta dampatis has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
