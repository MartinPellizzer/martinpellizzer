Rubus canescens

Rubus canescens, commonly known as the hoary bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and stems. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are edible and have a tart flavor.

Rubus canescens is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
