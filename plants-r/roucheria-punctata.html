Roucheria punctata

Roucheria punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features upright, slender stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly rough texture.

Roucheria punctata is often found in grassy areas, meadows, and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained soil.
