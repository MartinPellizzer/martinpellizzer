Rubus orotavensis

Rubus orotavensis, commonly known as the Orotava blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in coastal and mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
