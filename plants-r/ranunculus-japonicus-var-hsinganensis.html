Ranunculus japonicus var. hsinganensis

Ranunculus japonicus var. hsinganensis, commonly known as the Hsingan buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petals. The leaves are palmately divided, typically with three to five lobes, and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow or orange blooms.
