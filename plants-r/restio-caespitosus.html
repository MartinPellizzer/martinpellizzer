Restio caespitosus

Restio caespitosus, commonly known as the dense restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Restio caespitosus is valued for its ornamental appearance and its role in supporting local wildlife.
