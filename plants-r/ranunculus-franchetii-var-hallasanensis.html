Ranunculus franchetii var. hallasanensis

Ranunculus franchetii var. hallasanensis, commonly known as the Hallasan ranunculus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Korean Peninsula.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers with five to seven petals, typically in shades of yellow or white. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are finely toothed. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
