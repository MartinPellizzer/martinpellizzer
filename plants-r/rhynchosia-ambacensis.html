Rhynchosia ambacensis

Rhynchosia ambacensis, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Rhynchosia ambacensis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
