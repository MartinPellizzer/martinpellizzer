Rumex garipensis

Rumex garipensis, commonly known as the Turkish sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey and parts of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish coloration, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rhubarb-like leaves have been traditionally used in local cuisine and herbal medicine.
