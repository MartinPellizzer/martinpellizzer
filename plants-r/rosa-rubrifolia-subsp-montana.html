Rosa rubrifolia subsp. montana

Rosa rubrifolia subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny stems.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and often have a reddish hue on the underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
