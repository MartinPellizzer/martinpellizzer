Rogiera cordata

Rogiera cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in early summer. Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Rogiera cordata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
