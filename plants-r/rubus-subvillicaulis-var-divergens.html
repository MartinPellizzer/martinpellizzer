Rubus subvillicaulis var. divergens

Rubus subvillicaulis var. divergens, commonly known as the diverging bramble, is a shrub native to parts of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for local wildlife, particularly birds and small mammals.
