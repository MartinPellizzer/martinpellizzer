Rosa uriensis var. longipedunculata

Rosa uriensis var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long peduncled swamp rose, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
