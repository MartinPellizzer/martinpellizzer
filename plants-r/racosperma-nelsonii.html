Racosperma nelsonii

Racosperma nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for native wildlife.

Its seeds are often used in traditional Indigenous practices and for ornamental purposes.
