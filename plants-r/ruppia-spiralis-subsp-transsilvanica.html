Ruppia spiralis subsp. transsilvanica

Ruppia spiralis subsp.

transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Europe. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often ribbon-like and can reach up to 30 cm in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, followed by cylindrical fruits containing single seeds.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
