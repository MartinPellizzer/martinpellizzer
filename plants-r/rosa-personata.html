Rosa personata

Rosa personata, commonly known as the Japanese rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, serrated leaves. Rosa personata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
