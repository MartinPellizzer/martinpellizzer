Rosa sepium var. graveolens

Rosa sepium var. graveolens, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces reddish-brown, thorned stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
