Rosa seringeana var. karstiana

Rosa seringeana var. karstiana, commonly known as the Karstian rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are red, hairy achenes that mature in autumn.

Rosa seringeana var. karstiana is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
