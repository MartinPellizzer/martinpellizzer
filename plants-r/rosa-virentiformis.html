Rosa virentiformis

Rosa virentiformis, commonly known as the green-leaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in cool, temperate climates. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.
