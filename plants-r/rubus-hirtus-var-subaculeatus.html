Rubus hirtus var. subaculeatus

Rubus hirtus var. subaculeatus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The leaves are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving the plant a bristly appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

This plant is valued for its edible fruits and its role in supporting local wildlife.
