Racosperma stereophyllum var. cylindratum

Racosperma stereophyllum var. cylindratum, commonly known as the cylindrical wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, yellowish hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curved, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
