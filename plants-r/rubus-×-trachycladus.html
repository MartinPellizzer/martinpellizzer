Rubus × trachycladus

Rubus × trachycladus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces small, black berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and edible landscaping.
