Rodriguezia pulcherrima

Rodriguezia pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces woody, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and showy appearance make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
