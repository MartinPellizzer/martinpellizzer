Ratibida peduncularis

Ratibida peduncularis, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, showy yellow-orange flower heads that resemble sunflowers. The plant produces both ray and disk flowers, with the outer rays being the most prominent. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Ratibida peduncularis thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along stream edges.
