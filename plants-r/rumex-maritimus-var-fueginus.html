Rumex maritimus var. fueginus

Rumex maritimus var. fueginus, commonly known as the Falkland Island sorrel, is a perennial herb native to the southern coast of South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in saline and coastal environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions, including strong winds and salt spray.
