Rubus myricae var. hesperius

Rubus myricae var. hesperius, commonly known as the Pacific blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique genetic variation and ecological adaptations to its specific habitat.
