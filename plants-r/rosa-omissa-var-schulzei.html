Rosa omissa var. schulzei

Rosa omissa var. schulzei, commonly known as the Schulze's shrub rose, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It features upright, thornless stems and produces fragrant, semi-double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy underside, and it blooms in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in Mediterranean climates.
