Rhaphidophora typha

Rhaphidophora typha, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical evergreen herb native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It features a short, thick rhizome from which large, heart-shaped leaves arise. The leaves are typically broad, glossy, and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spathes containing berries.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and for the starchy roots used in food preparation.
