Rumex aquaticus var. helolapathum

Rumex aquaticus var. helolapathum, commonly known as the water sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along the edges of ponds and streams. It has a short stem and produces edible, sour-tasting leaves that are sometimes used in salads.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
