Rubus jactabundus var. fallax

Rubus jactabundus var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
