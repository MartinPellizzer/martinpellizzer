Rosa pimpinellifolia

Rosa pimpinellifolia, commonly known as the rock rose, is a species of shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found growing on rocky slopes and coastal areas. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are compound, with leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

Rosa pimpinellifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
