Rhamnus oleoides subsp. libyca

Rhamnus oleoides subsp.

libyca, commonly known as the Libyan buckthorn, is a shrub native to North Africa. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by reddish berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
