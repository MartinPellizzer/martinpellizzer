Restio triflorus

Restio triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered restio, is a member of the Restionaceae family, native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

Restio triflorus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
