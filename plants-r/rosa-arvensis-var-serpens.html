Rosa arvensis var. serpens

Rosa arvensis var. serpens, commonly known as the creeping dog玫瑰, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit, with stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

Rosa arvensis var. serpens is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in other open, sunny habitats.
