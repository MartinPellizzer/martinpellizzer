Rubus serpens f. lasiogynus

Rubus serpens f. lasiogynus, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed blackberry.

This plant is a subspecies of the common blackberry, characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive floral and fruit morphology. It is a shrub native to parts of North America, typically found in moist, shaded habitats. The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit, a aggregate of small drupes, is typically dark purple to black when ripe.
