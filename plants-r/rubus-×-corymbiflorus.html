Rubus × corymbiflorus

Rubus × corymbiflorus, commonly known as the bramble or blackberry bush, is a hybrid plant within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, that are usually dark purple to black in color. These fruits are not only edible but also rich in antioxidants and nutrients.

Due to its vigorous growth and adaptability, Rubus × corymbiflorus is often found in temperate regions across the northern hemisphere.
