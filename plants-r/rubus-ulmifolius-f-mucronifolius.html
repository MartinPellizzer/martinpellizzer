Rubus ulmifolius f. mucronifolius

Rubus ulmifolius f. mucronifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaved blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry.

It is characterized by its small, acuminate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a shrub with thorny stems and produces aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Rubus genus.
