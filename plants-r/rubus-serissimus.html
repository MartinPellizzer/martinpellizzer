Rubus serissimus

Rubus serissimus, commonly known as the red raspberry, is a perennial shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as raspberries, which are rich in antioxidants and vitamin C. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both its edible fruits and ornamental value.

Rubus serissimus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
