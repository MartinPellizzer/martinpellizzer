Rubus meridionalis var. supercalvus

Rubus meridionalis var. supercalvus, commonly known as the Southern raspberry variant, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
