Rhamnus glabrata

Rhamnus glabrata, commonly known as the smooth buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, dark red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Rhamnus glabrata is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in ornamental horticulture.
