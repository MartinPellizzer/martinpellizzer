Rhododendron japonicum f. glaucophyllum

Rhododendron japonicum f. glaucophyllum, commonly known as the Japanese azalea or Japanese rhododendron, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features broad, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy, bluish-gray coating. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the variety. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant blooms.
