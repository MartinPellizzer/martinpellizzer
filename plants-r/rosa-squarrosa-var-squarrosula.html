Rosa squarrosa var. squarrosula

Rosa squarrosa var. squarrosula, commonly known as the squarrosa rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces red or orange hips in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, rocky soils.

This variety is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
