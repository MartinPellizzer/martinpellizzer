Rubus brainerdii

Rubus brainerdii, commonly known as Brainerd's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas of Georgia and Florida. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits that resemble blackberries. It typically grows in moist, wooded habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and potential for use in horticulture.
