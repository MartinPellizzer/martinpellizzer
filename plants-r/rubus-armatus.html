Rubus armatus

Rubus armatus, commonly known as the bristly blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is native to western North America, particularly in regions with temperate climates. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy surface.

Rubus armatus is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.
