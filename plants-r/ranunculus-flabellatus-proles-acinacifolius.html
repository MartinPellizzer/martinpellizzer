Ranunculus flabellatus proles acinacifolius

Ranunculus flabellatus proles acinacifolius, commonly known as the acinacifolius ranunculus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and showy, typically yellow or orange flowers. Native to specific regions in South America, this species thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are often short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
