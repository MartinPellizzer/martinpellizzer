Rubus × miscellus

Rubus × miscellus, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color, containing numerous small drupes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
