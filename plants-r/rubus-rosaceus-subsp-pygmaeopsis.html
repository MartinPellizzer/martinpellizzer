Rubus rosaceus subsp. pygmaeopsis

Rubus rosaceus subsp.

pygmaeopsis, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are white with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
