Ribes grossularia var. munitum

Ribes grossularia var. munitum, commonly known as the Pacific blackcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and clusters of small, black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant belongs to the Grossularia species within the genus Ribes, which is part of the Saxifragaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in culinary and commercial uses.
