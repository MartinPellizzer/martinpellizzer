Rosa tomentella f. obornyana

Rosa tomentella f. obornyana, commonly known as the woolly rose, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, with a velvety texture.

Rosa tomentella f. obornyana is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine environments.
