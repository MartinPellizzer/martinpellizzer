Rubus virgultorum f. magnifica

Rubus virgultorum f. magnifica, commonly known as the showy blackberry, is a variant of the common blackberry plant.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink to white in color. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its fruit yield.
