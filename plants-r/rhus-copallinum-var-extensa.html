Rhus copallinum var. extensa

Rhus copallinum var. extensa, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by clusters of red, fuzzy berries that are a food source for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.
