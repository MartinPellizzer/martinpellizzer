Rubus × patens

Rubus × patens, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and evergreen foliage, which remain green throughout the year. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically harvested in the fall. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three to five leaflets and white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring.

Rubus × patens is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and thickets.
