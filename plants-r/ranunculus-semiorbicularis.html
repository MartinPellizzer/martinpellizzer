Ranunculus semiorbicularis

Ranunculus semiorbicularis, commonly known as the semicircular buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive semicircular leaves and bright yellow flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms in the spring and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
