Rhus osbeckii

Rhus osbeckii, commonly known as the Chinese sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, typically arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by red or orange berries that attract wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or open woodlands.

Rhus osbeckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
