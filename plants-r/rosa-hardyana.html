Rosa hardyana

Rosa hardyana, commonly known as the Hardy rose, is a species native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are usually covered in thorns. Rosa hardyana is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
