Rubus × recticuspis

Rubus × recticuspis, commonly known as the trailing blackberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
