Ruellia leucantha subsp. leucantha

Ruellia leucantha subsp.

leucantha, commonly known as white ruellia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal habitats. Ruellia leucantha subsp.

leucantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
