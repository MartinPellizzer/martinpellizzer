Rubus × areschougii

Rubus × areschougii, commonly known as the Areschoug's raspberry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color, resembling raspberries in appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats alike.
