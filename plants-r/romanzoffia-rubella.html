Romanzoffia rubella

Romanzoffia rubella, commonly known as the red water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It has dark green, oval leaves that are covered with a waxy layer, allowing it to float on water surfaces. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Romanzoffia rubella is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense mats on water bodies.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in non-native environments.
