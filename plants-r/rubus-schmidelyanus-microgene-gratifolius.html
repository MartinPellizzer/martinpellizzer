Rubus schmidelyanus microgene gratifolius

Rubus schmidelyanus microgene gratifolius, commonly known as the Schmidel's raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, exhibiting a compact growth habit. Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local horticulture.
