Rosa pouzinii var. yebalica

Rosa pouzinii var. yebalica, commonly known as the Yebalica rose, is a shrub-like species native to specific regions of South America.

It features dense, thorny stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically dark green, oval-shaped, and arranged alternately along the stems. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
