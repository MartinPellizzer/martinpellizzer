Rivina herbacea

Rivina herbacea, commonly known as redroot, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright red stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Rivina herbacea is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
