Rubus menkei var. distractus

Rubus menkei var. distractus, commonly known as the Himalayan blackberry, is a variant of the Rubus genus native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, often with a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental and edible qualities, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local use.
