Rosa inodora

Rosa inodora, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Rosa inodora thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
