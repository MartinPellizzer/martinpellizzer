Rosa montivaga var. subvirens

Rosa montivaga var. subvirens, commonly known as the greenleaf shrub rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features upright, thorny stems with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color. The flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with five petals and a subtle fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
