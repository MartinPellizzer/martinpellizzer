Rubus bellardii var. latipetalus

Rubus bellardii var. latipetalus, commonly known as the broad-petaled blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad petals and dark, almost black fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and compound leaves, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is edible, though often tart and used in jams or pies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and culinary potential in regional horticulture.
