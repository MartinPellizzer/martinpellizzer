Ruellia dissidens var. subglabra

Ruellia dissidens var. subglabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf ruellia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The leaves are typically smooth and glabrous, giving the plant its varietal name. It produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
