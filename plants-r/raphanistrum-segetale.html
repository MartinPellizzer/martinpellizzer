Raphanistrum segetale

Raphanistrum segetale, commonly known as the grain amaranth, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its seeds are small, oval, and often have a slightly prickly surface. It is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
