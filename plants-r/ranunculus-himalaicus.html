Ranunculus himalaicus

Ranunculus himalaicus, commonly known as the Himalayan buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow or white flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its habitat, it is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and high UV exposure.
