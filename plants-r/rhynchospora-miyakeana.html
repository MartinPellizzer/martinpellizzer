Rhynchospora miyakeana

Rhynchospora miyakeana, commonly known as Miyake's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in the understory of forested wetlands.

Rhynchospora miyakeana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
