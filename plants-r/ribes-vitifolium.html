Ribes vitifolium

Ribes vitifolium, commonly known as blackcurrant, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ribes vitifolium is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its culinary uses.
