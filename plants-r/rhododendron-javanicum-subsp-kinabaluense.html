Rhododendron javanicum subsp. kinabaluense

Rhododendron javanicum subsp.

kinabaluense, commonly known as the Kinabalu rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Borneo. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in montane rainforests. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological significance and is a symbol of the biodiversity found in the Malaysian rainforests.
