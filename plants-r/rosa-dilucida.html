Rosa dilucida

Rosa dilucida, commonly known as the dawn rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Rosa dilucida is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
