Rosa fimbriata

Rosa fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has compound leaves with finely serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Rosa fimbriata is considered a rare and endangered species.
