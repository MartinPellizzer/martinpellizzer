Rubus doggettii

Rubus doggettii, commonly known as Doggett's blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy texture.

Botanically, it is part of the Rubus genus, which includes many other species of brambles and blackberries.
