Rosa mohavensis

Rosa mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave rose, is a rare and flowering shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on slender pedicels. The plant has woody stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs and produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Rosa mohavensis is a member of the Rosaceae family and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.
