Rhododendron × eucallum

Rhododendron × eucallum, commonly known as the hybrid rhododendron, is a species resulting from the cross between Rhododendron eucallum and Rhododendron ferrugineum.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in areas with cool, moist climates.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in ecological systems where it provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.
