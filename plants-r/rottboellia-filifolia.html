Rottboellia filifolia

Rottboellia filifolia, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often mistaken for true grasses due to their similar appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense mats in disturbed soils.

Rottboellia filifolia is often found in open areas such as roadsides, fields, and disturbed woodlands.
