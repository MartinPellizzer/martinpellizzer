Rosa rubrifolia var. subinermis

Rosa rubrifolia var. subinermis, commonly known as the redleaf rose, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its vibrant red foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a distinctive reddish hue during the growing season. Its flowers are typically pink to white, appearing in clusters, and it produces hips that are orange-red in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest throughout the growing season.
