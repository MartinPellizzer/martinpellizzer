Rubus opulentus

Rubus opulentus, commonly known as the opulent blackberry, is a species of bramble native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into dark purple to black berries. These berries are typically small, firm, and have a tart flavor.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
