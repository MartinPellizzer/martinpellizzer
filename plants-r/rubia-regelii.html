Rubia regelii

Rubia regelii, commonly known as the Regel's madder, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces red-orange roots that have been historically used in traditional medicine and dye production. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Rubia regelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens.
