Rhododendron trichocalyx

Rhododendron trichocalyx, commonly known as the Japanese azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers acidic, well-drained soils. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing an attractive backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Rhododendron trichocalyx is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
