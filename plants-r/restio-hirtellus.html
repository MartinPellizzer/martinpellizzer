Restio hirtellus

Restio hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy restio, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Restionaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense spikes.

Restio hirtellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
