Ricinus hybridus

Ricinus hybridus, commonly known as the castor oil plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and distinctive spiny seed capsules. The plant produces a toxic, sticky substance called ricin, which is found in the seeds. Ricinus hybridus is cultivated for its oil-rich seeds, which have various industrial and medicinal uses.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
