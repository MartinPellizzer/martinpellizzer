Rhododendron barkamense

Rhododendron barkamense, commonly known as the Kashmir rhododendron, is a species native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded, mountainous environments. Its bark is thick and rough, often featuring a reddish-brown hue with longitudinal fissures.

Rhododendron barkamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
