Rhynchosia leucophylla f. elliptica

Rhynchosia leucophylla f. elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a variant of the cowpea species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its elliptical, narrow leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds used for food and fodder. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
