Ranunculus sobakus

Ranunculus sobakus, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange, with five to nine petals. The plant has finely divided, pinnately arranged leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Ranunculus sobakus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
