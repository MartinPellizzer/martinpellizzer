Rumex × kaschmirianus

Rumex × kaschmirianus, commonly known as the Kashmir rhubarb, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically reddish in color and have a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are followed by elongated, reddish fruits. It is native to the Himalayan regions and thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
