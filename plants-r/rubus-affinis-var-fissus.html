Rubus affinis var. fissus

Rubus affinis var. fissus, commonly known as the fissured blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value.
