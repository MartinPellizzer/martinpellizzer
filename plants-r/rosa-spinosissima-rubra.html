Rosa spinosissima rubra

Rosa spinosissima rubra, commonly known as the crimson prickly rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of deep red, fragrant flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. The plant is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in herbal medicine.
