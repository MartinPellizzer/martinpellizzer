Rinorea brownii

Rinorea brownii, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as cloud forests and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Rinorea brownii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
