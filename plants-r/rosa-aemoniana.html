Rosa aemoniana

Rosa aemoniana, commonly known as the Aemonian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are typically thornless.

Rosa aemoniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
