Rubus milliformis var. simulans

Rubus milliformis var. simulans, commonly known as the mimic raspberry, is a variant of the bramble genus Rubus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it identifiable through its unique leaf morphology and fruit structure.
