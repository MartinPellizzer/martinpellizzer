Rubus ulmifolius microgene lemaitrei

Rubus ulmifolius microgene lemaitrei, commonly known as the Lemaitre's bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, thorny growth and compound leaves that are often lobed and serrated. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as drupes, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and horticultural interest.
