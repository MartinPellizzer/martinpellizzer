Rubus purpuratus subsp. ettendorfensis

Rubus purpuratus subsp.

ettendorfensis, commonly known as the Ettendorf blackberry, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically small and firm.

The plant has trailing stems with thorny branches, and its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than other blackberry varieties.
