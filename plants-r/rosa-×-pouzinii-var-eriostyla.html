Rosa × pouzinii var. eriostyla

Rosa × pouzinii var. eriostyla, commonly known as the pink sand rose, is a hybrid rose species native to the coastal regions of southern France and northern Spain.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to maritime climates.
