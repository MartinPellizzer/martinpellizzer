Rubus rhamnifolius var. bakeri

Rubus rhamnifolius var. bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that ripen to a dark purple or black color, typically in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its horticultural value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
