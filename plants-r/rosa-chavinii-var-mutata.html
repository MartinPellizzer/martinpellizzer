Rosa chavinii var. mutata

Rosa chavinii var. mutata, commonly known as the Chavin rose, is a compact shrub native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in thorny buds. It is well adapted to high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Andean culture.
