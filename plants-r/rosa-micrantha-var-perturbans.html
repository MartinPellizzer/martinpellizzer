Rosa micrantha var. perturbans

Rosa micrantha var. perturbans, commonly known as the variable rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, thorny stems and dark green, doubly serrated leaves. It prefers partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and compact growth habit.
