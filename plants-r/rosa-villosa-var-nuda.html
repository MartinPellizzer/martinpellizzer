Rosa villosa var. nuda

Rosa villosa var. nuda, commonly known as the naked hairy rose, is a species of shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thornless stems and clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant produces oval, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens or borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
