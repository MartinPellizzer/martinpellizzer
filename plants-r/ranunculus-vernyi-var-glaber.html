Ranunculus vernyi var. glaber

Ranunculus vernyi var. glaber, commonly known as the glabrous meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with five to eight petals, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
