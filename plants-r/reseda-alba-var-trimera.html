Reseda alba var. trimera

Reseda alba var. trimera, commonly known as white cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Reseda alba var. trimera is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
