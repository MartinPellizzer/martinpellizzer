Rosa alpina f. reversa

Rosa alpina f. reversa, commonly known as the Alpine rose reverse form, is a botanical variant of the Alpine rose.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its compact, often prostrate growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

This form is notable for its unique floral orientation, with petals facing downward, giving it a distinctive appearance.
