Rapuntium bracteosum

Rapuntium bracteosum, commonly known as the bracted rapunculus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with four petals. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. Its fruit is a dry, segmented silique that contains several small, ovoid seeds.

Rapuntium bracteosum is typically perennial and can thrive in temperate climates with adequate moisture.
