Rabdosia nervosa

Rabdosia nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined rabdosia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. Rabdosia nervosa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
