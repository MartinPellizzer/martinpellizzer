Renealmia ventricosa

Renealmia ventricosa, commonly known as the pineapple grass, is a herbaceous plant native to the wet tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and produces a spiky, pineapple-like inflorescence. The plant features long, narrow leaves with a fibrous texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Renealmia ventricosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
