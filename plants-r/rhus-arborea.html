Rhus arborea

Rhus arborea, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with 11 to 31 leaflets. The plant produces bright red, fuzzy fruits that attract birds and other wildlife. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

Rhus arborea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
