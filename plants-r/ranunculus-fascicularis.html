Ranunculus fascicularis

Ranunculus fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, often surrounded by a ring of small, spiny sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are palmately arranged and have a hairy surface.

Ranunculus fascicularis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its cheerful appearance.
