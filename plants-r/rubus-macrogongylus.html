Rubus macrogongylus

Rubus macrogongylus, commonly known as the large-seeded blackberry, is a species of bramble native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and clustered in inflorescences, and it thrives in moist, woodland environments.

The fruit is edible, though often tart, and is used in jams and wines.
