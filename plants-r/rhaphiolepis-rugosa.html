Rhaphiolepis rugosa

Rhaphiolepis rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf rose.

It is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia, characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, fragrant pink or white flowers. The leaves are oval-shaped, rough to the touch, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds but not typically for humans.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
