Rubus schleicheri var. scaber

Rubus schleicheri var. scaber, commonly known as the rough raspberry, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of small, red berries. The plant typically grows in forested areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them a valued plant in both culinary and ecological contexts.
