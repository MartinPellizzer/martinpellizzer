Ranunculus valdivianus

Ranunculus valdivianus, commonly known as the Valdivian buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with high humidity. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are usually borne on long, slender stalks.

Ranunculus valdivianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
