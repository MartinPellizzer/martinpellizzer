Ranunculus collinus

Ranunculus collinus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer.

Ranunculus collinus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
