Rebutia totorensis

Rebutia totorensis, commonly known as the Totoro Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
