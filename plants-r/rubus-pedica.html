Rubus pedica

Rubus pedica, commonly known as the pedicellate blackberry, is a species of bramble native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregated fruits. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically consumed raw or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
