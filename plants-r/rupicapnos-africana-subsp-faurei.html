Rupicapnos africana subsp. faurei

Rupicapnos africana subsp.

faurei, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
