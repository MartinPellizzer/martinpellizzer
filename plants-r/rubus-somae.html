Rubus somae

Rubus somae, commonly known as the Somaliland raspberry, is a species of bramble native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, red, edible berries. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and is often found in rocky or arid environments. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it resilient in its native habitat.

The fruit is used both for consumption and in traditional medicine by local communities.
