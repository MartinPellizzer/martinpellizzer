Rhaponticum centauroides

Rhaponticum centauroides, commonly known as the Siberian chicory, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a single, terminal head. Its root system is extensive and fibrous, capable of reaching deep into the soil to access water and nutrients.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
