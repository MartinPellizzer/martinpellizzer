Rubus hybridus var. pentaphyllus

Rubus hybridus var. pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leaf blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when ripe. Its botanical features include a woody structure, persistent leaves, and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and thickets, contributing to local biodiversity.
