Rubia laurae

Rubia laurae, commonly known as Laura's Rubia, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland and shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Rubia laurae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
