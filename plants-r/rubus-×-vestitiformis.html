Rubus × vestitiformis

Rubus × vestitiformis, commonly known as the hairy raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, red, edible berries.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
