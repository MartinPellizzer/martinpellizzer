Rosa caninella var. delatula

Rosa caninella var. delatula, commonly known as the sweet briar rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces rose hips, which are the fruit containing the seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedging and as a source of rose oil.
