Rubus echincaulon

Rubus echincaulon, commonly known as the spiny bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its structure and growth habits make it a common component of hedgerows and shrublands.
