Rubus parvus

Rubus parvus, commonly known as the dwarf blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white with five petals.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
