Rubus efferatus

Rubus efferatus, commonly known as the red raspberry.

It is a species of bramble in the family Rosaceae, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant produces bright red, hairy berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

The botanical features of Rubus efferatus make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
