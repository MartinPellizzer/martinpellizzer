Rubus corylifolius subsp. pruinosus

Rubus corylifolius subsp.

pruinosus, commonly known as the glossy blackberry, is a shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its large, dark green, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, edible black berries.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are white and have five petals, and the fruit is often used in jams and wines.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, waxy leaves and robust growth habit.
