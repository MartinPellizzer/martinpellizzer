Rhododendron lopsangianum

Rhododendron lopsangianum, commonly known as the Lopsangian rhododendron, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while the flowers range in color from pink to red.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
