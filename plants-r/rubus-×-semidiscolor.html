Rubus × semidiscolor

Rubus × semidiscolor, commonly known as the pink willow or red raspberry.

It is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and the fruit, a cluster of drupes, can range in color from red to black.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
