Rhynchostylis garwalica

Rhynchostylis garwalica, commonly known as the Garwal orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically has lance-shaped leaves and a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Rhynchostylis garwalica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
