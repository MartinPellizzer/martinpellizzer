Ruellia biloba

Ruellia biloba, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-shaped growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and is drought-tolerant once established.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
