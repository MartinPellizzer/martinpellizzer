Rhus gracilipes

Rhus gracilipes, commonly known as the slender winged sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive reddish color in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fuzzy fruits that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Rhus gracilipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
