Ranunculus leptorrhynchus

Ranunculus leptorrhynchus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features delicate, palmately divided leaves and bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often found in association with other wetland flora.

Ranunculus leptorrhynchus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
