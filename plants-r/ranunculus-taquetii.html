Ranunculus taquetii

Ranunculus taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers feature five petals and numerous stamens, with a prominent central stigma.

Ranunculus taquetii is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in high-altitude environments.
