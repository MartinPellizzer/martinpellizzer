Ranunculus californicus var. rugulosus

Ranunculus californicus var. rugulosus, commonly known as the California buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, showy flowers with five to eight petal-like sepals that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The species is characterized by its hairy stems and its ability to thrive in cool, humid conditions.
