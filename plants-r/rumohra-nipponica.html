Rumohra nipponica

Rumohra nipponica, commonly known as Japanese wall grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological roles in native plant gardens.
