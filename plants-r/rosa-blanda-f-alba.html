Rosa blanda f. alba

Rosa blanda f. alba, commonly known as the white meadow rose, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, glossy leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is prized for its delicate appearance and is commonly cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
