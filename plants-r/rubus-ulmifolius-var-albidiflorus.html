Rubus ulmifolius var. albidiflorus

Rubus ulmifolius var. albidiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered bramble, is a variant of the common bramble.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, hairy berry that ripens in late summer, attracting various birds and small mammals.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.
