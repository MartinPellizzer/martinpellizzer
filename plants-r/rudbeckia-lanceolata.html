Rudbeckia lanceolata

Rudbeckia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, slender stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces bright yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by ray florets, typically blooming from midsummer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Rudbeckia lanceolata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
