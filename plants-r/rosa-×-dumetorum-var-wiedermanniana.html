Rosa × dumetorum var. wiedermanniana

Rosa × dumetorum var. wiedermanniana, commonly known as the Wiedermann's shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and dense, reddish-brown stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate blooms and hardy nature, which allows it to withstand colder climates.
