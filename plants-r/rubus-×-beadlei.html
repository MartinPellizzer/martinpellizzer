Rubus × beadlei

Rubus × beadlei, commonly known as the blackberry bramble, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, which are typically dark purple to black in color when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become invasive in certain regions.
