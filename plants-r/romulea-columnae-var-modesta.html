Romulea columnae var. modesta

Romulea columnae var. modesta, commonly known as the modest romulea, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers. The flowers, typically white or pale pink, are borne on slender stems and bloom in late summer. This variant is distinguished by its more subdued coloration compared to other varieties of Romulea columnae.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.
