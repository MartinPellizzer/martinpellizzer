Rosa tomentosa f. anceps

Rosa tomentosa f. anceps, commonly known as the woolly rose or the hairy rose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent foliage and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Rosa tomentosa f. anceps is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it suitable for a variety of garden environments.
