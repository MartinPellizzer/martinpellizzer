Rosa caesia

Rosa caesia, commonly known as the white garden rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorned stems and dark green, leathery leaves. Rosa caesia prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
