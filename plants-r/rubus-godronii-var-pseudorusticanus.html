Rubus godronii var. pseudorusticanus

Rubus godronii var. pseudorusticanus, commonly known as the Godron's raspberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
