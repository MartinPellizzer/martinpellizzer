Rosa rubella var. melanocarpa

Rosa rubella var. melanocarpa, commonly known as the black rasperry rose, is a cultivar of the rose family (Rosaceae) characterized by its deep red to almost black flowers.

It produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to raspberries but have a distinct, tart flavor. The plant is typically shrub-like, with thorny stems and pinnate leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary gardens.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
