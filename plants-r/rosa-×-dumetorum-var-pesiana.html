Rosa × dumetorum var. pesiana

Rosa × dumetorum var. pesiana, commonly known as the Italian rose, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces rose hips that are rich in vitamin C. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in perfumery and culinary applications.
