Rubus crassus var. peltifolius

Rubus crassus var. peltifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf raspberry, is a variant of the raspberry plant characterized by its broad, leathery leaves.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or orange in color. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rosaceae family.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
