Rhamnus infectoria subsp. saxatilis

Rhamnus infectoria subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the rock buckthorn, is a shrub native to rocky and arid environments. It typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to poor soils.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are greenish-yellow and clustered in small inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, smooth drupe.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
