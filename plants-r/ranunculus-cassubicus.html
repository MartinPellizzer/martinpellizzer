Ranunculus cassubicus

Ranunculus cassubicus, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers and delicate, trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy blooms and often toxic sap.

Ranunculus cassubicus is often found in meadows and open slopes at high altitudes.
