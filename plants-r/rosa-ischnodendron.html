Rosa ischnodendron

Rosa ischnodendron, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens.
