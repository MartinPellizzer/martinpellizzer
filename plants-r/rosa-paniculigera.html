Rosa paniculigera

Rosa paniculigera, commonly known as the panicled rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and the Tibetan Plateau. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in thorns.

Rosa paniculigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
