Rosa communis var. pseudopouzinii

Rosa communis var. pseudopouzinii, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a compact shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features fragrant, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves with a slightly serrated margin. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

The variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
