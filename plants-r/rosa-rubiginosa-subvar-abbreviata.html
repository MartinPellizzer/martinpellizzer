Rosa rubiginosa subvar. abbreviata

Rosa rubiginosa subvar. abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf shrub rose, is a compact, thorny shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves with a distinctive silvery-white underside, and its stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
