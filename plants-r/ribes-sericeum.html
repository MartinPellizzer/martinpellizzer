Ribes sericeum

Ribes sericeum, commonly known as silver currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
