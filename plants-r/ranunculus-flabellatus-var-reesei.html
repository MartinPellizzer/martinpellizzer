Ranunculus flabellatus var. reesei

Ranunculus flabellatus var. reesei, commonly known as the Reese's buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, fan-shaped (flabellate) petal structure. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-green seed pods.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
