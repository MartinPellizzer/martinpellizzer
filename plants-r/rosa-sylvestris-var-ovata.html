Rosa sylvestris var. ovata

Rosa sylvestris var. ovata, commonly known as the shrub rose or sweetbriar, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces oval, red-brown hips that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
