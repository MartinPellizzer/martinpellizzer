Rubus morifolius

Rubus morifolius, commonly known as the Japanese blackberry, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
