Randia oppositifolia

Randia oppositifolia, commonly known as the oppositifoliate randia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Randia oppositifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
