Rorippa palustris subsp. pusilla

Rorippa palustris subsp.

pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf watercress, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas. It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

The plant has slender, branching stems with small, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are yellow and borne in racemes, producing small, edible seeds.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and pungent odor.
