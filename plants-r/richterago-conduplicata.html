Richterago conduplicata

Richterago conduplicata, commonly known as the double-leafed fern, is a unique species characterized by its intricate frond structure.

Its leaves are bi-lobed, with each lobe resembling a separate leaf, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on spores for propagation.

This fern is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and ecological adaptations.
