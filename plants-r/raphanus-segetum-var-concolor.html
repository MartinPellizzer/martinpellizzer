Raphanus segetum var. concolor

Raphanus segetum var. concolor, commonly known as the smooth watercress, is a variant of the watercress species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its succulent, edible leaves and small white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows, where it thrives in shallow water. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a peppery flavor, making it a popular ingredient in salads and traditional dishes.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability to aquatic conditions contribute to its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
