Ranunculus spissus

Ranunculus spissus, commonly known as the dense buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features basal leaves that are palmately divided with a hairy surface, and its flowers are typically yellow with 5 to 15 petal-like sepals. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small, elliptical seeds.

Ranunculus spissus is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
