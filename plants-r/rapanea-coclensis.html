Rapanea coclensis

Rapanea coclensis, commonly known as the Madagascar laurustin, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctively fragrant scent when crushed.

Rapanea coclensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
