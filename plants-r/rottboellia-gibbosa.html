Rottboellia gibbosa

Rottboellia gibbosa, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture along the edges and upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, roadsides, and agricultural fields.

Rottboellia gibbosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
