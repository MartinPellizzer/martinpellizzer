Rosa cinerascens var. ovoidea

Rosa cinerascens var. ovoidea, commonly known as the oval-ovate rose, is a distinctive variety of shrub rose native to the western United States.

It features pale pink to white flowers with a slightly fragrant aroma, blooming in early summer. The plant has thorny stems and elliptic to oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained, sunny locations.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in arid climates.
