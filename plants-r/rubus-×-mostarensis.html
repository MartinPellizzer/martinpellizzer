Rubus × mostarensis

Rubus × mostarensis, commonly known as the Mostar raspberry, is a hybrid species originating from the Balkans.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its berries are valued for their tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.

Due to its ornamental and edible qualities, it is cultivated in gardens and small-scale farms.
