Rubus perpetiolulatus var. remotus

Rubus perpetiolulatus var. remotus, commonly known as the remote bramble, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
