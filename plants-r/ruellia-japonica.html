Ruellia japonica

Ruellia japonica, commonly known as Japanese ruellia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct grayish-green color. Its flowers are tubular and typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Ruellia japonica is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
