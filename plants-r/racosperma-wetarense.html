Racosperma wetarense

Racosperma wetarense, commonly known as the Wetar acacia, is a shrub native to the island of Wetar in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or sandy soils.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
