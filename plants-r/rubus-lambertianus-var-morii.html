Rubus lambertianus var. morii

Rubus lambertianus var. morii, commonly known as Lambert's blackberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and dense, dark foliage. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark red to black in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
