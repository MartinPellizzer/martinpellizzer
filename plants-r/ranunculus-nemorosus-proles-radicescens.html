Ranunculus nemorosus proles radicescens

Ranunculus nemorosus proles radicescens, commonly known as the creeping wood anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along streambanks, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its basal leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is part of the Ranunculaceae family.
