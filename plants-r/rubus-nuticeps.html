Rubus nuticeps

Rubus nuticeps, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple ovaria. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The berries, which are dark purple to black when mature, are edible and often used in jams and wines.
