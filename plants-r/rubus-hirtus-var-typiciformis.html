Rubus hirtus var. typiciformis

Rubus hirtus var. typiciformis, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, prickly bracts and small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges and a hairy texture.

The fruit is edible, though the plant's thorns make harvesting challenging.
