Rosa communis var. montivaga

Rosa communis var. montivaga, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically produces thorny stems and has dark green, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial suitable for alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative shrub.
