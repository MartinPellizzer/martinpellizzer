Rubus pseudovenustus

Rubus pseudovenustus, commonly known as the silky blackberry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of China and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically edible. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the fruiting bodies.

The silky blackberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
