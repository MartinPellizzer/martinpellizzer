Roepera cordifolia

Roepera cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf roepera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Roepera cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
