Rosa × dumetorum subvar. trichoneura

Rosa × dumetorum subvar. trichoneura, commonly known as the alpine rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered with a fine layer of white pubescence, giving them a silvery appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
