Rosa tomentosa subvar. zabelii

Rosa tomentosa subvar. zabelii, commonly known as the Zabel's woolly rose, is a shrub-like species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and fragrant pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its stems are covered in a thick layer of white tomentum, which helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and fragrance.
