Rosa mollis f. calycida

Rosa mollis f. calycida, commonly known as the Calycate Rosa Mollis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and fragrant, pink to white flowers. The plant is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its growth habit includes multiple stems that can reach heights of up to 3 feet, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
