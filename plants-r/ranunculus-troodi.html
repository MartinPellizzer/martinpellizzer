Ranunculus troodi

Ranunculus troodi, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers with numerous petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces a cluster of small, greenish flowers that bloom in early summer.

Ranunculus troodi is often found in high-altitude regions and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.
