Rosa sciochroma

Rosa sciochroma, commonly known as the silverleaf rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. Its foliage is evergreen, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

Rosa sciochroma is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
