Ranunculus cassubicus subsp. pawlowskii

Ranunculus cassubicus subsp.

pawlowskii, commonly known as the Pawlowski's buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features showy, yellow flowers with five to seven petals, typically appearing in early spring.

The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
