Rubus occitanicus var. umbrosus

Rubus occitanicus var. umbrosus, commonly known as the dark raspberry.

This variant is characterized by its dark, almost black fruits, which are smaller and more intensely flavored compared to the standard Rubus occitanicus. It is a shrub with thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves, typical of the Rubus genus. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the plant is often found in shaded, moist environments.

It is native to southern Europe and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary qualities.
