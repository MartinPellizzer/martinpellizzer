Rubus incisus var. conduplicatus

Rubus incisus var. conduplicatus, commonly known as the double-leaf blackberry, is a variant of the blackberry plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive double leaflets, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
