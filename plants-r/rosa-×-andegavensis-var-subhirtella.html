Rosa × andegavensis var. subhirtella

Rosa × andegavensis var. subhirtella, commonly known as the Andegavensis rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, thorny stems with dark green, lobed leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
