Rosa rubus f. glandulifera

Rosa rubus f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular rose, is a botanical variant of the Rosa rubus species.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland areas and along hedgerows.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
