Rhododendron macgregoriae var. glabrifilum

Rhododendron macgregoriae var. glabrifilum, commonly known as the Glabrous Filament Rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the wild.
