Rhododendron sanguineum var. cloiophorum

Rhododendron sanguineum var. cloiophorum, commonly known as the pink azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
