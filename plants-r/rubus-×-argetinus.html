Rubus × argetinus

Rubus × argetinus, commonly known as the evergreen blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and local wildlife habitats.
