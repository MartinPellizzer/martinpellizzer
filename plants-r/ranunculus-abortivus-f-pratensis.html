Ranunculus abortivus f. pratensis

Ranunculus abortivus f. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
