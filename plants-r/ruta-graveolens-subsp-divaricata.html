Ruta graveolens subsp. divaricata

Ruta graveolens subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the fragrant rue, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its aromatic, pinnately compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant contains volatile oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a pest repellent highlights its botanical and cultural significance.
