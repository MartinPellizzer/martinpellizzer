Rubus glanduliger

Rubus glanduliger, commonly known as the glandular blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. The plant has serrated leaves with glandular dots that secrete a sticky substance. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in jams and pies.

The botanical features of Rubus glanduliger make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
