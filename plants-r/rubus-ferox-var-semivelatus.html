Rubus ferox var. semivelatus

Rubus ferox var. semivelatus, commonly known as the hairy bramble, is a variant of the brambles native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its hairy foliage and robust growth make it a distinctive and adaptable species within the Rubus genus.
