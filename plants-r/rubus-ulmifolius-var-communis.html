Rubus ulmifolius var. communis

Rubus ulmifolius var. communis, commonly known as the common blackberry, is a shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces aggregate fruits, known as drupes, which are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

The berries are edible, though some varieties may have a tart or bitter taste.
