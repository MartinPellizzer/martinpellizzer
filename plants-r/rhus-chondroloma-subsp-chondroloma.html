Rhus chondroloma subsp. chondroloma

Rhus chondroloma subsp.

chondroloma, commonly known as the smooth sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring compound leaves with 15 to 31 leaflets that are alternate and serrated.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, which are followed by clusters of red, fuzzy fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it is often found in dry, open woods or along roadsides.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in providing habitat and food for wildlife.
