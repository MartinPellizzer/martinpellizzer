Rubus roetensis

Rubus roetensis, commonly known as the Roetense blackberry, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
