Ravenea albicans

Ravenea albicans, commonly known as the white-rimmed Ravenea, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a lush, tropical appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its unique leaf structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Ravenea albicans thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
