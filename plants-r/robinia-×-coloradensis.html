Robinia × coloradensis

Robinia × coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado redbud, is a deciduous tree native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is a hybrid between Robinia pseudoacacia (black locust) and Robinia humilis (dwarf redbud). The tree is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing shallow fissures with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
