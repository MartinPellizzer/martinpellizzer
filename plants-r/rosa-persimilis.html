Rosa persimilis

Rosa persimilis, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran, Afghanistan, and the Caucasus. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Rosa persimilis is typically a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

It produces rose hips, which are the fruiting bodies that contain the seeds.
