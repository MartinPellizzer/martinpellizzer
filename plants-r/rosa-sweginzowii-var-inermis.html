Rosa sweginzowii var. inermis

Rosa sweginzowii var. inermis, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a variety of the Rosa sweginzowii species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thornless stems and dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, single or semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
