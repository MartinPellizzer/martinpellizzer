Rourea rugosa

Rourea rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf rourea, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Rourea rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
