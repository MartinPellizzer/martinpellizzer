Rhododendron longiflorum var. bancanum

Rhododendron longiflorum var. bancanum, commonly known as the Bancan rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Nepal and India.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pink in color. The plant typically grows in high-altitude forests and shrublands, thriving in cool, moist climates with acidic soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
