Rubus uncinatus

Rubus uncinatus, commonly known as the trailing blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible blackberries, which are typically harvested in late summer. Its botanical features include a woody structure and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Rubus uncinatus plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
