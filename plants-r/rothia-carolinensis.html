Rothia carolinensis

Rothia carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina rothia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Rothia carolinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
