Rubus macrophyllus

Rubus macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant blackberry or large-leaf bramble, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its large, serrated leaves and thorny stems, which are typical features of the Rubus genus. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible berries that are similar in appearance to blackberries.

The plant is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
