Ranunculus auricomus subsp. accedens

Ranunculus auricomus subsp.

accedens, commonly known as the golden crowncup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive golden center.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are hairy and upright.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
