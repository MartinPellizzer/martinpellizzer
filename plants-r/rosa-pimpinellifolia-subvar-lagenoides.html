Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. lagenoides

Rosa pimpinellifolia subvar. lagenoides, commonly known as the pear-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, often thornless, leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky or coastal terrain.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
