Rosa turbinata

Rosa turbinata, commonly known as the turban rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, turban-shaped blooms that are typically pink to red in color. The plant produces fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. Rosa turbinata is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
