Rhododendron campylocarpum subsp. telopeum

Rhododendron campylocarpum subsp.

telopeum, commonly known as the New Guinea rhododendron, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Its botanical features include thick, leathery leaves and a compact, shrub-like growth habit.
