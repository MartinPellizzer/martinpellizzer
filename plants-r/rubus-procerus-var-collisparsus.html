Rubus procerus var. collisparsus

Rubus procerus var. collisparsus, commonly known as the hairy blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, often followed by the development of the edible berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its fruit in certain regions.
