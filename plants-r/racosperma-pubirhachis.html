Racosperma pubirhachis

Racosperma pubirhachis, commonly known as the woolly acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Racosperma pubirhachis is adapted to dry environments and is often found in sandy soils.
