Rubus rolfei

Rubus rolfei, commonly known as the Rolfe's blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits that develop from multiple small drupes. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.
