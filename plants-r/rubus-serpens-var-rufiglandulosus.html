Rubus serpens var. rufiglandulosus

Rubus serpens var. rufiglandulosus, commonly known as the red-hairy bramble, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits, known as raspberries, are aggregate fruits formed from multiple drupelets.

The variety is distinguished by its reddish glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name.
