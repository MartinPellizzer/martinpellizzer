Rosa stellata

Rosa stellata, commonly known as the starry rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub with thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers have five to seven petals and are often arranged in clusters, making it a striking addition to gardens.

Rosa stellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in horticultural research due to its adaptability to various soil types.
