Rubus allophyllus

Rubus allophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blackberry, is a species of bramble native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with narrow, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Rubus species and exhibits typical features of the Rubiaceae family.
