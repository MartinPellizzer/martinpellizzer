Restrepia lehmanniana

Restrepia lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's restia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically during the spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
