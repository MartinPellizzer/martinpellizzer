Rhaphidophora megasperma

Rhaphidophora megasperma, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in a spike, followed by berries that contain multiple seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its starchy roots, which are used in cooking.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible plants.
