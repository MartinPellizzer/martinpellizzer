Rosa cordifolia

Rosa cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf rose, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia and the Caucasus. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. Rosa cordifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
