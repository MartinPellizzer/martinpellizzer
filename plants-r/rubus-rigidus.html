Rubus rigidus

Rubus rigidus, commonly known as the prickly rose or brambles, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces red or black berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along disturbed ground.

Rubus rigidus plays an important ecological role by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
