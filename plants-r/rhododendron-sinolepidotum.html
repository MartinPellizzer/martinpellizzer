Rhododendron sinolepidotum

Rhododendron sinolepidotum, commonly known as the Chinese rhododendron, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring acidic, well-drained soils. It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes other popular ornamental shrubs.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
