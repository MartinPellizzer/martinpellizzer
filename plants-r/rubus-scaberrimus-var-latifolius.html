Rubus scaberrimus var. latifolius

Rubus scaberrimus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf bramble, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves with broad, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, raspberry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native habitat.
