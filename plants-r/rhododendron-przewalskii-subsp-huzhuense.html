Rhododendron przewalskii subsp.

huzhuense, commonly known as the Huzhu rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, humid environments. It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes many ornamental and ecologically important shrubs.
