Racosperma diaphanum

Racosperma diaphanum, commonly known as the translucent acacia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, delicate foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into two sets of smaller leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance.

Racosperma diaphanum is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
