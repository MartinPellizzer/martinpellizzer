Rhysolepis macrocalyx

Rhysolepis macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered rhododendron, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in dense forests, thriving in cool, moist environments with acidic soil. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms.

Rhysolepis macrocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
