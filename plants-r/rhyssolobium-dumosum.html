Rhyssolobium dumosum

Rhyssolobium dumosum, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils.

Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a range of colors from white to purple.
