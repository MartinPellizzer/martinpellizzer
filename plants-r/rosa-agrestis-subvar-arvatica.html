Rosa agrestis subvar. arvatica

Rosa agrestis subvar. arvatica, commonly known as the rough-stemmed wild rose, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, which are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces fragrant pink to red flowers in summer, followed by pinkish-red hips that persist into winter. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven elliptical leaflets with serrated edges.

This species is adapted to moist, woodland habitats and is often found in stream banks and along forest edges.
