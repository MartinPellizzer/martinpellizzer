Ranorchis

Ranorchis, commonly known as the marsh orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its terrestrial or epiphytic growth habit and is typically found in wet, marshy environments. The plants produce showy, often fragrant flowers that can range in color from white to pink and purple. Ranorchis species are known for their specialized root systems that help them absorb nutrients from their moist habitats.

These orchids play an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and supporting various pollinators.
