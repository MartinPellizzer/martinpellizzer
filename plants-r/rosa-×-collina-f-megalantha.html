Rosa × collina f. megalantha

Rosa × collina f. megalantha, commonly known as the giant rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of large, showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and rocky habitats. Its foliage consists of small, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
