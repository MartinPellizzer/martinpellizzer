Rosa rugosa alba

Rosa rugosa alba, commonly known as white rugosa rose, is a deciduous shrub native to coastal regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its rugged, thorny stems and large, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, lobed leaves that are covered in a fine layer of down. It is highly adaptable, thriving in sandy or saline soils and tolerating salt spray.

Rosa rugosa alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a hardy, low-maintenance garden plant.
