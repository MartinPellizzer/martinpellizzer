Rubus × assurgens var. arvinus

Rubus × assurgens var. arvinus, commonly known as the Arvin blackberry, is a hybrid species within the Rubus genus, characterized by its thorny stems and aggregate fruits.

It is native to California and thrives in coastal and valley regions with a Mediterranean climate. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are typically firm and have a high concentration of antioxidants. It is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for cultivation in home gardens.

The variety is also noted for its relatively disease-resistant properties compared to other blackberry species.
